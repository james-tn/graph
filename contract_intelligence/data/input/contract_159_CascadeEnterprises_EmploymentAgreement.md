## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK  

2.1 **Engagement of Service Provider.**  
The Company hereby engages the Service Provider, and the Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services during the Term in accordance with this Agreement and one or more SOWs. The Services shall include, without limitation, the provision of technology professionals, project managers, analysts, and related personnel to support the Company’s business operations and project initiatives.

2.2 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms of this Agreement, the Services may include the following categories of work, as specified in each SOW:

  (a) **Staff Augmentation and Employment-Type Assignments.** The Service Provider shall supply qualified personnel to perform full-time or part-time assignments at the Company’s premises or remotely, including software developers, quality assurance engineers, DevOps engineers, data analysts, business analysts, systems administrators, and project managers. Such personnel shall follow the Company’s reasonable directions with respect to day-to-day tasks, hours of work, and project requirements, provided that the Service Provider shall retain all employer-related obligations for such personnel.

  (b) **Project-Based Services.** The Service Provider may undertake defined projects, such as system implementations, integrations, data migrations, upgrades, and custom development efforts, pursuant to SOWs specifying scope, milestones, timelines, and acceptance criteria.

  (c) **Managed Services and Support.** The Service Provider may provide ongoing support, monitoring, and maintenance services for specified systems or applications, including incident response, ticket resolution, and performance optimization, in accordance with applicable Service Levels.

  (d) **Advisory and Consulting Services.** The Service Provider may deliver technology consulting, architecture design, process improvement, and strategic advisory services as requested by the Company and set forth in an SOW.

2.3 **Deliverables and Work Product.**  
Each SOW shall describe the Deliverables to be produced, including but not limited to:

  (a) functional and technical specifications;  
  (b) source code, configuration scripts, build artifacts, and deployment packages;  
  (c) user guides, training materials, and operational documentation;  
  (d) status reports, governance reports, and project documentation; and  
  (e) test plans, test cases, test results, and acceptance reports.  

Deliverables shall meet the specifications, quality standards, and acceptance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW and shall be fit for the purposes stated therein.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services (a) in a professional, timely, and workmanlike manner, (b) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for comparable services, (c) in compliance with Applicable Law, and (d) in accordance with the Company’s written policies reasonably provided to the Service Provider (including information security, health and safety, and workplace conduct, to the extent applicable to the Service Provider’s personnel). Without limiting the foregoing:

  (i) The Service Provider shall ensure that its personnel assigned to the Company are properly trained, qualified, and experienced for the tasks they are assigned.  

  (ii) The Service Provider shall promptly replace any assigned personnel who, in the reasonable opinion of the Company, fail to perform adequately, engage in misconduct, or otherwise present a risk to the Company; provided that the Company shall provide a reasonable written explanation for such request for replacement.

  (iii) The Service Provider shall implement quality assurance processes to verify that Deliverables conform to the applicable specifications and are free from material defects and errors.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
Each SOW shall set forth a project schedule or timeline, including start and end dates, key milestones, and dependencies. The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet such timelines and milestones. If the Service Provider becomes aware of any event or circumstance likely to cause a delay, it shall promptly notify the Company in writing, explain the cause of the delay, and propose a mitigation plan. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to adjust schedules through the Change Management process described in Article XIII.

2.6 **Location of Services.**  
Services may be performed (a) at the Company’s facilities, (b) at the Service Provider’s facilities, and/or (c) remotely, as specified in each SOW or as otherwise agreed in writing. The Service Provider shall ensure that remote work is performed in secure environments consistent with the data protection obligations in Article XI.

2.7 **Project Governance and Reporting.**  
The Parties shall designate project managers or engagement managers responsible for day-to-day coordination and communication. The Service Provider shall provide status reports as specified in the applicable SOW, which may include progress updates, risk and issue logs, resource utilization, and key performance indicators. Upon request, the Service Provider shall participate in governance meetings, steering committee sessions, and operational reviews, in person or via teleconference.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise specified in a SOW:

  (a) The Service Provider shall notify the Company in writing when a Deliverable is ready for review and acceptance.  

  (b) The Company shall have a reasonable period, not less than ten (10) Business Days, to review the Deliverable and either (i) accept it in writing, or (ii) reject it by providing a written notice identifying any material non-conformities.  

  (c) The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct any identified material non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance within a mutually agreed timeframe.  

  (d) A Deliverable shall be deemed accepted upon the earliest of (i) the Company’s written acceptance, (ii) the Company’s use of the Deliverable in a production environment for more than thirty (30) consecutive days without a written rejection notice, or (iii) the Company’s failure to provide a rejection notice within the acceptance period.

2.9 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
The Company is not obligated to purchase any minimum volume of Services under this Agreement, and the Service Provider acknowledges that the Company may engage other vendors to perform services similar to the Services. Likewise, the Service Provider may provide services to other clients, provided that such services do not breach the confidentiality, intellectual property, or other obligations under this Agreement.

2.10 **Compliance with Company Policies.**  
While on the Company’s premises or accessing the Company’s systems, the Service Provider’s personnel shall comply with all applicable Company policies communicated in writing, including security, safety, and access control policies. The Company reserves the right to remove or deny access to any individual who violates such policies, provided that the Company notifies the Service Provider and the Parties cooperate to ensure continuity of Services.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS  

3.1 **Fees.**  
In consideration for the Services and Deliverables, the Company shall pay the Service Provider fees as specified in each SOW, which may include:

  (a) **Time and Materials Fees.** Hourly or daily rates for personnel, multiplied by the actual hours or days worked, subject to any caps or budgets agreed in the SOW.  

  (b) **Fixed Price Fees.** Lump-sum amounts for defined Deliverables or project phases, payable upon completion of milestones or acceptance of Deliverables.  

  (c) **Retainer or Managed Service Fees.** Recurring monthly or quarterly fees for ongoing Services, support, or managed services, as specified in the SOW.

The aggregate fees across all SOWs issued under this Agreement shall not exceed the Contract Value of **US$4,644,928** without a written amendment or Change Order executed by both Parties.

3.2 **Invoices.**  
The Service Provider shall invoice the Company in accordance with the billing schedule specified in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated in an SOW:

  (a) Time and materials fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on time records approved by the Company.  

  (b) Fixed price fees shall be invoiced upon completion of associated milestones or Deliverables and acceptance (or deemed acceptance) in accordance with Section 2.8.  

  (c) Managed service fees shall be invoiced monthly in advance.

Each invoice shall itemize the Services performed, the applicable SOW, the billing period, and any approved expenses. Time records and supporting documentation shall be made available to the Company upon reasonable request.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Net 60.**  
Subject to the Company’s good faith review and verification of each invoice, the Company shall pay undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt of a correct and undisputed invoice (**“Net 60”**). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by electronic funds transfer or other method agreed by the Parties.

3.4 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice, it shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receiving the invoice, specifying the amounts in dispute and the reasons. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve disputes promptly. The Company may withhold payment of disputed amounts until resolution, but shall pay undisputed amounts in accordance with Section 3.3.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the date due until the date paid at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law. The Service Provider may not suspend or terminate the Services solely for late payment unless (i) such non-payment remains uncured for more than thirty (30) days after written notice, and (ii) the amounts are not subject to a good-faith dispute.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
Fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income or employment-related taxes arising from the Service Provider’s status as employer of its personnel. The Company shall be responsible for any applicable sales or use taxes that the Service Provider is legally required to collect, provided that such taxes are separately stated on the invoice. If the Company is exempt from certain taxes, it shall provide the Service Provider with appropriate exemption certificates.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing the Services, such as travel, lodging, meals, and third-party tools or licenses, in accordance with the Company’s expense policies provided to the Service Provider. Expense reimbursement shall be subject to the following:

  (a) Expenses must be pre-approved in writing by the Company’s designated representative.  

  (b) The Service Provider shall submit itemized expense reports with supporting receipts no later than sixty (60) days after the expenses are incurred.  

  (c) The Company may reject reimbursement for expenses that are not properly documented, exceed approved amounts, or do not comply with applicable policies.

3.8 **No Withholding from Service Provider Personnel.**  
The Service Provider is solely responsible for payment of all wages, salaries, benefits, taxes, and other compensation due to its personnel. The Company shall have no obligation to make any such payments directly to the Service Provider’s personnel.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION  

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated as provided herein, shall continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date of May 15, 2026** (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend the Term for additional periods, subject to negotiation of any revised terms, including pricing and scope. Any such renewal shall be documented in a written amendment signed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk allocation agreed by the Parties, the Company may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider. The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Company. Termination of the Agreement shall automatically terminate all SOWs, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, is subject to a petition for bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) The Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the terminated Services, except as necessary to provide any agreed transition assistance in accordance with Section 4.6.  

  (b) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for (i) all Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted through the effective date of termination, (ii) any non-cancellable, pre-approved expenses incurred prior to the termination, and (iii) reasonable wind-down costs agreed in advance in writing.  

  (c) Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information and Client Materials, in accordance with Section 5.5 and Section 11.7.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
At the Company’s written request, the Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition services for a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration, including knowledge transfer, documentation, and assistance in transitioning Services to the Company or a replacement vendor. Such transition assistance shall be provided at the rates set forth in the applicable SOW or, if not specified, at the Service Provider’s then-current standard rates, unless the Agreement is terminated by the Service Provider for uncured material breach by the Company, in which case the Parties will negotiate reasonable rates in good faith.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The Parties’ rights and obligations under Sections 3.5, 4.5, 4.6, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 14, 15, and 16, and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY  

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public or proprietary information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, visually, in writing, or in electronic form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, strategies, pricing, customer information, financial data, product roadmaps, trade secrets, software, source code, technical documentation, security processes, and any Personal Data.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  

  (b) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care;  

  (c) restrict access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, officers, agents, and Subcontractors who have a legitimate need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  

  (d) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party without the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent, except as expressly permitted herein.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
The obligations in Section 5.2 do not apply to any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  

  (a) was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction at the time of disclosure;  

  (b) becomes publicly known through no wrongful act or omission of the Receiving Party;  

  (c) is received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  

  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that, to the extent legally permissible, the Receiving Party (a) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of the requirement, (b) cooperates with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and (c) discloses only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or, at the Disclosing Party’s option, securely destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except for one archival copy that may be retained solely for legal, regulatory, or compliance purposes, subject to continuing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)  

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
For any managed services or hosted systems provided by the Service Provider under an SOW, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the covered systems achieve **99.9%** monthly uptime, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall meet the following target response and resolution times for incidents reported by the Company, unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical)** – System or major functionality is unavailable:

    - Response Time: within one (1) hour.  
    - Resolution or Workaround: within eight (8) hours.

  - **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant impairment with no viable workaround:

    - Response Time: within two (2) hours.  
    - Resolution or Workaround: within two (2) Business Days.

  - **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Moderate impairment or cosmetic issue:

    - Response Time: within one (1) Business Day.  
    - Resolution or Workaround: within ten (10) Business Days.

12.3 **Measurement and Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall implement monitoring tools to measure uptime and incident response performance and shall provide monthly SLA reports to the Company, including uptime percentages, incident volumes, response and resolution times, and any breaches of SLAs.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment or other SLAs in a given calendar month, the Company shall be entitled to service credits as set forth in the applicable SOW or, if not specified, as follows:

  (a) Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly fees for the affected service;  

  (b) Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly fees for the affected service;  

  (c) Uptime below 98.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly fees for the affected service.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall be the Company’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to the Company’s rights to terminate under Article IV in case of persistent failures.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
SLA obligations shall not apply to outages or performance issues caused by (a) the Company’s misuse or unauthorized modification of the systems, (b) third-party systems or networks not under the Service Provider’s control, or (c) Force Majeure Events.

## FORCE MAJEURE  

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond a Party’s reasonable control that renders performance impracticable, including, without limitation, acts of God, acts of government or regulatory authorities, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (not involving the non-performing Party’s own workforce), pandemics, epidemics, natural disasters, power outages, or widespread Internet or telecommunications failures.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature and expected duration of the event, (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable, and (c) keep the other Party informed of progress in resolving the event.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
Performance obligations affected by a Force Majeure Event shall be suspended for the duration of the event and extended for an equivalent period, provided that the affected Party complies with Section 15.2. The unaffected Party may reasonably adjust delivery dates and schedules to accommodate such suspension.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and substantially prevents performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or, if the entire Agreement is materially impacted, this Agreement, upon fifteen (15) days’ written notice.
