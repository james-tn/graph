# AMENDMENT TO DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT  
**Contract Reference Number: AMD-VER-202509-722**

This Amendment (“**Amendment**” or “**Amendment AMD-VER-202509-722**”) is entered into as of **August 29, 2026** (the “**Amendment Effective Date**”), by and between:

- **Contoso Enterprises, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at 4100 Contoso Way, New York, NY 10005, USA (“**Contoso**”, “**Client**” or “**Company**”); and  
- **Vertex Group LLC**, a New York limited liability company, with its principal place of business at 980 Vertex Plaza, Suite 2400, New York, NY 10019, USA (“**Vertex**”, “**Vendor**” or “**Service Provider**”).

Contoso and Vertex are each referred to herein individually as a “**Party**” and collectively as the “**Parties**”.

This Amendment bears **Contract Reference Number: AMD-VER-202509-722** and specifically relates to and **amends, supplements, and is entered into pursuant to** that certain **Data Processing Agreement** dated **September 29, 2025**, by and between the Parties, having **Contract Reference Number: DPA-VER-202509-641** and internal identifier **“contract_641”** (the “Parent Agreement” or “DPA”).

## PREAMBLE & RECITALS

**Contract Reference Number: AMD-VER-202509-722**

WHEREAS, the Parties previously entered into that certain **Data Processing Agreement** bearing **Contract Reference Number: DPA-VER-202509-641** and internal identifier **contract_641**, effective as of **September 29, 2025** (the “Parent Agreement” or “DPA”), pursuant to which Service Provider agreed to provide to Company certain enterprise-grade data processing, cloud-based software, and related managed services as more fully described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement;

WHEREAS, the Parent Agreement established, among other things, the Parties’ respective rights and obligations with respect to the processing of **Client Data**, including **Personal Data**, under applicable **Data Protection Laws**, as defined in **Article I (Definitions)** of the Parent Agreement, and the Parties’ obligations concerning **Data Protection and Privacy** under **Article XI** of the Parent Agreement;

WHEREAS, the Parties have categorized the engagement, including the processing of Personal Data and associated services, as “High Risk” from a data protection, operational, and business continuity perspective, and the Parent Agreement accordingly provides for **no contractual cap on liability** and broad allocation of risk under **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)**;

WHEREAS, Company desires to expand and further define specific data processing, cross-border transfer, and data protection obligations beyond those set forth in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, **Section 2.6 (Subprocessors and Third-Party Services)**, and related provisions of the Parent Agreement, and to introduce additional deliverables and service-level expectations in relation to data protection and privacy;

WHEREAS, Service Provider is willing to provide such expanded and clarified data processing and related services to Company under the terms and conditions of this Amendment, in consideration of additional fees and commitments set forth herein;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire that this Amendment expressly **updates data processing terms dated September 29, 2025 (Ref: DPA-VER-202509-641)** and shall, to the extent of any conflict, **control over** the Parent Agreement solely with respect to the subject matter addressed herein, while leaving all other terms of the Parent Agreement in full force and effect;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises, covenants, and agreements herein contained, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties **hereby agree** as follows:

## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  
This Amendment **updates data processing terms dated September 29, 2025 (Ref: DPA-VER-202509-641)** and supplements the **“Services”** as defined in **Section 1.17** and further described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly set forth herein, the nature of the Services remains as described in **Sections 2.1–2.6** of the Parent Agreement. This Amendment primarily:

- (a) adds **Enhanced Data Protection Services** to the existing Services;
- (b) refines and expands the Parties’ obligations under **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of the Parent Agreement; and
- (c) introduces **Enhanced SLA Commitments** specific to data protection functions, without reducing the **Parent SLA**.

2.2 **Enhanced Data Protection Services.**  
During the Amendment Term, Service Provider shall provide to Company the following Enhanced Data Protection Services, in addition to those described in the Parent Agreement:

(a) **Data Mapping and Records of Processing.** Service Provider shall, on at least an annual basis and upon Company’s reasonable request, assist Company in maintaining detailed records of processing activities related to Client Data, including categories of Personal Data, categories of Data Subjects, purposes of processing, retention periods, and transfers, by providing:

  - (i) A consolidated **Data Processing Schedule**, updated no more than once per calendar quarter unless required by changes in the Services or Applicable Law;  
  - (ii) A summary of Subprocessors and their geographic locations, supplementing the list available under **Section 11.3** of the Parent Agreement; and  
  - (iii) Documentation of the primary data flows between Company systems and Service Provider’s platform as provided in **Section 2.1(c)** of the Parent Agreement.

(b) **DPIA and Risk Assessment Support.** Taking into account the “High Risk” classification referenced in the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, Service Provider shall provide reasonable assistance, at the fee rates set forth in **Article III**, for Company’s preparation of DPIAs, including:

  - (i) Providing descriptions of the technical and organizational measures implemented pursuant to **Section 11.5 (Security Measures)** of the Parent Agreement and the Security Annex;  
  - (ii) Providing information concerning the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing as described in **Section 2.1 (General Description of Services)** of the Parent Agreement; and  
  - (iii) Identifying potential high-risk processing operations within Service Provider’s environment and recommending mitigation measures.

(c) **Cross-Border Data Transfer Support.** In addition to the obligations stated in **Section 11.9 (Cross-Border Transfers)** of the Parent Agreement, Service Provider shall:

  - (i) Implement and maintain appropriate **Data Transfer Mechanisms** for transfers of Personal Data from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or other jurisdictions requiring specific safeguards;  
  - (ii) Provide Company with copies or a description of the relevant Data Transfer Mechanisms upon reasonable written request, subject to redaction of confidential commercial information; and  
  - (iii) Promptly inform Company of any material changes in law or guidance of a Regulator that may render a Data Transfer Mechanism invalid or insufficient, and cooperate in good faith to implement alternative mechanisms.

(d) **Enhanced Incident Response and Reporting.** In addition to the breach notification obligations set forth in **Section 11.7 (Data Breach Notification)** of the Parent Agreement, Service Provider shall:

  - (i) Provide **Incident Response Deliverables**, including incident runbooks described in **Section 2.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement, tailored to Company’s environment;  
  - (ii) Participate in up to two (2) simulated incident response exercises per calendar year with Company’s security team, scheduled at mutually agreeable times;  
  - (iii) Provide detailed post-incident reports following any confirmed Personal Data breach affecting Client Data, including root cause analysis, remediation steps, and lessons learned.

(e) **Joint Security Reviews.** At least annually, and more frequently as mutually agreed, the Parties shall conduct a **Joint Security Review**, during which:

  - (i) Service Provider will present relevant third-party certifications and reports (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001) as contemplated by **Section 11.8 (Audits)** of the Parent Agreement;  
  - (ii) The Parties will review the Security Annex and consider updates in light of evolving threats and regulatory expectations; and  
  - (iii) The Parties will document any agreed changes as a change order pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement as supplemented herein.

(f) **Data Subject Request Facilitation.** To supplement **Section 11.4 (Data Subject Requests)** of the Parent Agreement, Service Provider shall:

  - (i) Provide a designated point of contact and process for Company to submit batched Data Subject requests;  
  - (ii) Implement reasonable tooling or queries within its platform to facilitate search, export, correction, or deletion of Personal Data, consistent with Company’s documented instructions; and  
  - (iii) Provide quarterly summary reports of Data Subject requests processed with Service Provider’s assistance during the preceding quarter.

2.3 **Deliverables and Milestones.**  
Without limiting **Section 2.2** above, the following **Deliverables** shall be provided under this Amendment, in addition to the Deliverables referenced in **Section 2.2** of the Parent Agreement:

(a) **Initial Data Processing Schedule.** Within sixty (60) days of the Amendment Effective Date, Service Provider shall deliver an initial Data Processing Schedule identifying, at a minimum, categories of Personal Data, Data Subjects, purposes of processing, storage locations, and Subprocessors.

(b) **Security Annex.** Within forty-five (45) days of the Amendment Effective Date, Service Provider shall provide a Security Annex that describes the technical and organizational measures required by **Section 11.5 (Security Measures)** of the Parent Agreement, cross-referenced to controls relevant for GDPR and CCPA compliance.

(c) **Incident Response Playbooks.** Within ninety (90) days of the Amendment Effective Date, Service Provider shall provide updated Incident Response Deliverables tailored to Company’s environment, incorporating categories of incidents referenced in **Section 2.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement.

(d) **Annual Reports.** For each full calendar year during the Amendment Term, Service Provider shall deliver an annual data protection and security report summarizing:

  - (i) Key security metrics;  
  - (ii) Summary of Personal Data breaches and security incidents (if any);  
  - (iii) Any material changes in Subprocessors pursuant to **Section 11.3 (Subprocessing)** of the Parent Agreement; and  
  - (iv) Material changes to Data Transfer Mechanisms.

Unless otherwise expressly set forth herein, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted upon delivery, subject to the acceptance framework in **Section 2.2** (final paragraph) of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Service Provider shall perform the Enhanced Data Protection Services:

(a) In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards, in alignment with **Section 2.3 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement;  

(b) In compliance with **Applicable Law** and **Data Protection Laws** as they apply to Service Provider’s role as a Processor or “service provider”; and  

(c) In reliance on Company’s timely cooperation and fulfillment of its responsibilities, consistent with **Section 2.4 (Company Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement, including providing accurate and complete information necessary for DPIAs and risk assessments.

2.5 **Company Responsibilities.**  
In addition to Company’s responsibilities under **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement, Company shall:

(a) Provide timely and accurate Supplemental Instructions for processing of Personal Data;  
(b) Promptly notify Service Provider of any changes in its processing activities or regulatory obligations that may affect this Amendment; and  
(c) Ensure that its own internal policies and notices to Data Subjects are consistent with the processing described in the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

2.6 **Subprocessors and Third-Party Services.**  
The provisions set forth in **Section 2.6 (Subprocessors and Third-Party Services)** and **Section 11.3 (Subprocessing)** of the Parent Agreement are hereby incorporated by reference and shall apply in full to the Enhanced Data Protection Services. Service Provider shall remain responsible for the acts and omissions of its Subprocessors in accordance with those sections.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Amendment Contract Value.**  
In consideration for the Enhanced Data Protection Services and Deliverables described in **Article II** of this Amendment, Company shall pay to Service Provider an additional contract value of **US $3,590,488** (the “**Amendment Contract Value**”), which is in addition to, and not in substitution for, the **Contract Value of US $4,287,004** specified in **Section 3.1 (Contract Value)** of the Parent Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Amendment Contract Value shall be allocated as follows:

(a) **Base Amendment Subscription Fees.** Recurring fees for ongoing Enhanced Data Protection Services, including ongoing data mapping, DPIA support, cross-border transfer support, and Enhanced SLA Commitments, billed annually in advance, in amounts specified in order forms referencing both **DPA-VER-202509-641** and **AMD-VER-202509-722**.

(b) **Incremental Professional Services Fees.** Fees for project-based activities such as initial creation of the Data Processing Schedule, development of customized Incident Response Deliverables, participation in simulated incident response exercises, Joint Security Reviews beyond those included in base services, and support for complex DPIAs or regulatory inquiries, to be billed on a time-and-materials or fixed-fee basis as set forth in applicable SOWs executed under the Parent Agreement and referencing this Amendment.

(c) **Usage-Based Fees (If Applicable).** To the extent that certain Enhanced Data Protection Services are tied to usage metrics (e.g., volume of records subject to Data Subject requests, number of regions involved in cross-border transfers), Service Provider may charge usage-based fees as further specified in relevant order forms or SOWs. Such usage-based fees are supplemental to the usage-based fees set forth in **Section 3.2(b)** of the Parent Agreement.

(d) **Travel and Expenses.** Travel and related expenses incurred by Service Provider personnel in connection with on-site Joint Security Reviews, workshops, or other on-premises Enhanced Data Protection Services requested by Company shall be reimbursed by Company in accordance with **Section 3.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
Except as expressly modified herein, the invoicing provisions of **Section 3.3 (Invoicing)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the fees under this Amendment. Unless otherwise specified in an order form or SOW:

(a) Base Amendment Subscription Fees shall be invoiced annually in advance on or about the Amendment Effective Date and each anniversary thereof during the Amendment Term;  
(b) Incremental Professional Services Fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears;  
(c) Any usage-based fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears; and  
(d) Expenses shall be invoiced monthly in arrears with supporting documentation.

3.4 **Payment Terms (Net 45).**  
Company shall pay all undisputed amounts invoiced under this Amendment in accordance with **Section 3.4 (Payment Terms (Net 45))** of the Parent Agreement, i.e., within **forty-five (45) days** from the invoice date by wire transfer or other agreed method.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
Any disputes regarding invoices issued under this Amendment shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.5 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent Agreement. Company shall timely pay all undisputed portions and cooperate in good faith to resolve disputes.

3.6 **Late Payments; Interest.**  
Any late payments under this Amendment shall accrue interest at the rate and on the terms described in **Section 3.6 (Late Payments; Interest)** of the Parent Agreement. Service Provider may, upon notice, suspend the Enhanced Data Protection Services for non-payment, without prejudice to its rights regarding the Parent Agreement.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All fees under this Amendment are exclusive of Taxes, which shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.7 (Taxes)** of the Parent Agreement.

3.8 **No Setoff.**  
The no setoff provision of **Section 3.8 (No Setoff)** of the Parent Agreement applies equally to amounts due under this Amendment.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Amendment Term.**  
This Amendment shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date and shall continue in effect until **March 27, 2028** (the “**Amendment Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent Agreement.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.**  
The Term of the Parent Agreement is defined in **Section 4.1 (Term)** as extending from the Effective Date to April 16, 2027, unless earlier terminated. The Parties expressly agree that:

(a) If the Parent Agreement is renewed by mutual agreement beyond April 16, 2027, this Amendment shall continue in force through the Amendment Expiration Date, unless otherwise modified by written agreement;  

(b) If the Parent Agreement is not renewed and expires on April 16, 2027, this Amendment shall automatically terminate on that same date, unless the Parties explicitly agree in writing to maintain certain Enhanced Data Protection Services for a limited wind-down period, subject to mutually agreed fees; and  

(c) In no event shall this Amendment survive the complete termination (for any reason) of the Parent Agreement, except as expressly stated in **Section 4.5** below.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The termination for convenience rights in **Section 4.2 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent Agreement apply to this Amendment. Accordingly, either Party may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**, provided that:

(a) Termination of the Parent Agreement for convenience by either Party shall automatically result in concurrent termination of this Amendment; and  
(b) If Company terminates this Amendment for convenience but the Parent Agreement remains in force, Company shall remain liable for all fees accrued as of the effective date of termination and for any minimum recurring charges committed under order forms referencing this Amendment, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
The termination for cause provisions set forth in **Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement are incorporated herein by reference and apply to this Amendment. A material breach of this Amendment by either Party shall be deemed a material breach of the Parent Agreement and vice versa, to the extent the breach relates to data processing obligations.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Amendment:

(a) The Enhanced Data Protection Services shall cease, and Service Provider’s obligations shall revert to those set forth exclusively in the Parent Agreement, to the extent it remains in force;  

(b) Company shall pay Service Provider all fees due and payable for services performed and expenses incurred under this Amendment up to the effective date of termination, including any early termination charges expressly agreed in order forms or SOWs referencing this Amendment;  

(c) Data export, retention, and deletion shall be handled in accordance with **Section 4.4(d)** and **Section 11.6 (Data Retention and Deletion)** of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented by any more specific requirements in this Amendment or the Data Processing Schedule; and  

(d) The provisions of this Amendment that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and Sections 4.5 and 4.6, shall so survive, subject to the survival provisions of the Parent Agreement.

4.6 **No Refunds.**  
Except as expressly provided otherwise in this Amendment or the Parent Agreement, all fees paid or payable to Service Provider under this Amendment are non-refundable, consistent with the high-risk, high-investment nature of the engagement.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Confidentiality.**  
The confidentiality obligations set forth in **Article V (Confidentiality)** of the Parent Agreement (Sections 5.1–5.6) apply fully to this Amendment and to all information exchanged in connection with the Enhanced Data Protection Services. This Article V clarifies and supplements those obligations.

5.2 **Confidential Information.**  
For purposes of this Amendment, “Confidential Information” shall have the meaning set forth in **Section 5.1** of the Parent Agreement and shall expressly include:

(a) The Data Processing Schedule, Security Annex, and any related documentation;  
(b) Incident Response Deliverables and post-incident reports;  
(c) Details regarding Data Transfer Mechanisms and Subprocessors; and  
(d) Any communications with Regulators concerning the Services, to the extent permitted by law.

5.3 **Additional Obligations.**  
In addition to the obligations under **Section 5.3 (Obligations)** of the Parent Agreement, the Receiving Party shall:

(a) Implement appropriate internal access controls to limit access to Confidential Information to those personnel who have a strict need to know for purposes of this Amendment;  

(b) Handle all information obtained during audits, Joint Security Reviews, and DPIA support engagements as Confidential Information; and  

(c) Not use any Confidential Information relating to security controls, vulnerabilities, or Data Transfer Mechanisms for any purpose other than fulfilling its obligations under the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures; Regulatory Inquiries.**  
The permitted disclosure provisions of **Section 5.4** of the Parent Agreement apply; additionally, either Party may disclose relevant portions of this Amendment and related documentation to a Regulator as reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with Data Protection Laws, provided that the disclosing Party, where legally permissible, notifies the other Party and cooperates in seeking confidential treatment.

5.5 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations under the Parent Agreement survive for **five (5) years** following termination or expiration, with trade secret and Personal Data protections lasting as long as required by law. These survival periods apply equally to Confidential Information under this Amendment.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Relationship to Parent SLA.**  
The **Parent SLA** set forth in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement remains in full force and effect. This Amendment introduces **Enhanced SLA Commitments** specifically related to data protection and privacy functions and does not reduce or diminish any rights or remedies under the Parent SLA.

12.2 **Enhanced SLA – Data Subject Request Handling.**  
Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to:

(a) Provide an initial response to Company’s properly submitted Data Subject request assistance tickets within:

  - (i) Four (4) Business Hours for high-priority requests involving regulatory deadlines or threatened litigation, when expressly identified by Company;  
  - (ii) One (1) Business Day for all other requests;

(b) Complete reasonable support tasks for Data Subject requests within timeframes mutually agreed in writing, considering regulatory requirements and the complexity of the requests.

12.3 **Enhanced SLA – Incident Notification.**  
Without limiting **Section 11.7** of the Parent Agreement, Service Provider shall:

(a) Provide Company with initial notice of a confirmed Personal Data breach affecting Client Data **without undue delay and, where feasible, within forty-eight (48) hours** after becoming aware, subject to applicable law; and  

(b) Provide periodic updates at intervals agreed with Company, and a final incident report within thirty (30) days of containment or as otherwise agreed.

12.4 **Enhanced SLA Remedies.**  
If Service Provider materially and repeatedly fails to meet the Enhanced SLA Commitments under this Amendment (excluding delays attributable to Company or Force Majeure Events), Company may, upon written notice and following a 30-day cure period:

(a) Request a remediation plan and dedicated remediation oversight for a mutually agreed period; and  

(b) If the failure persists, seek additional service credits not exceeding 5% of the applicable monthly Amendment fees for the affected services, in addition to any credits under the Parent SLA. Such credits shall be the sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failures to meet the Enhanced SLA Commitments, without limiting Company’s non-monetary remedies under the Parent Agreement.
