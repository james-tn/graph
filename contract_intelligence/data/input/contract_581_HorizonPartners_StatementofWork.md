## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Company a comprehensive suite of consulting, implementation, and managed services in support of the Company’s enterprise cloud migration and analytics modernization initiative (the “**Project**”). The Services shall include, without limitation: (a) strategic advisory services for cloud migration; (b) assessment and design of target-state architectures; (c) implementation and configuration of selected cloud platforms; (d) data migration and integration services; (e) development of analytics and reporting frameworks; and (f) ongoing managed services and support for the implemented solutions during the Term.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Services shall be structured into the following phases, each with associated Deliverables and milestones:

  2.2.1 **Phase I – Discovery and Assessment (Months 1–4).**  
  (a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and current-state assessments of the Company’s infrastructure, applications, and data landscape.  
  (b) Deliverables:  
   - Current State Assessment Report;  
   - Cloud Readiness Assessment;  
   - Data Inventory and Classification Report;  
   - Risk and Compliance Gap Analysis.  

  2.2.2 **Phase II – Architecture and Roadmap (Months 5–8).**  
  (a) Design target architecture for cloud infrastructure, data platforms, security controls, and integration patterns.  
  (b) Develop a phased migration roadmap with prioritized workloads, dependency mapping, and risk mitigation strategies.  
  (c) Deliverables:  
   - Target-State Architecture Blueprint;  
   - Migration Roadmap and Workload Prioritization;  
   - Security and Compliance Architecture Plan;  
   - High-Level Data Governance Framework.

  2.2.3 **Phase III – Implementation and Migration (Months 9–30).**  
  (a) Implement selected cloud infrastructure and services, including compute, storage, networking, and security controls.  
  (b) Migrate prioritized applications and datasets to the target environment, following a phased cutover strategy to minimize business disruption.  
  (c) Configure monitoring, logging, incident management, and backup/restore capabilities.  
  (d) Deliverables:  
   - Configured Cloud Environments (Dev, Test, Prod);  
   - Migrated Applications and Data Sets (as enumerated in migration plan);  
   - Operational Runbooks and Playbooks;  
   - Test Plans, Test Reports, and Cutover Reports.

  2.2.4 **Phase IV – Analytics Enablement (Months 18–42).**  
  (a) Implement data warehouses, data lakes, and/or analytics platforms as defined in the Target-State Architecture.  
  (b) Develop dashboards, reports, and analytic models for key business functions (e.g., sales, operations, finance).  
  (c) Provide knowledge transfer and training to the Company’s teams.  
  (d) Deliverables:  
   - Configured Analytics Platform(s);  
   - Standardized Dashboards and Reports;  
   - Machine Learning / Advanced Analytics Use Cases (up to a mutually agreed number);  
   - Training Materials and Training Sessions Summary.

  2.2.5 **Phase V – Managed Services and Optimization (Months 31–End of Term).**  
  (a) Provide ongoing monitoring, incident management, change management support, and performance optimization of the migrated environments and analytics platforms.  
  (b) Provide periodic architecture reviews, cost optimization recommendations, and security posture assessments.  
  (c) Deliverables:  
   - Monthly Operations Reports;  
   - Quarterly Optimization and Security Assessments;  
   - Incident and Problem Management Reports;  
   - Updated Runbooks and Knowledge Base Articles.

2.3 **Performance Standards and Service Levels.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services: (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services; (b) in accordance with the Specifications and Service Levels set forth in this Agreement, including **Article XII**; (c) using personnel who are appropriately qualified and experienced for the tasks assigned; and (d) in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. The Parties acknowledge that due to the high-risk nature of this Project, the Service Provider will exercise commercially reasonable efforts to meet the Project milestones, but certain dependencies (including, without limitation, the timely provision of Client Materials, decisions, and approvals by the Company) may impact the schedule. Any material changes to milestones or timelines shall be managed in accordance with **Article XIII** (Change Management).

2.4 **Company Responsibilities and Dependencies.**  
The Company acknowledges that the Service Provider’s ability to perform the Services in accordance with the timelines and performance standards described herein is contingent upon the Company fulfilling the following responsibilities, without limitation:

  (a) Providing timely access to relevant systems, documentation, networks, and personnel necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services;  
  (b) Designating a project sponsor and project manager who shall have authority to make decisions and provide approvals on behalf of the Company;  
  (c) Supplying accurate and complete Client Materials, including data extracts and system architectural diagrams, in a timely manner;  
  (d) Ensuring that necessary third-party licenses and consents are obtained to permit the Service Provider to perform the Services; and  
  (e) Providing timely feedback and approvals on Deliverables in accordance with agreed review cycles.

Any delay or failure by the Company to meet the foregoing obligations may result in adjustments to the Project schedule, Fees, or both, to be documented in a Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII**.

2.5 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise specified in a particular Change Order, each Deliverable shall be subject to an acceptance process as follows:

  (a) Upon completion of a Deliverable, the Service Provider shall notify the Company and provide the Deliverable for review.  
  (b) The Company shall have ten (10) Business Days (the “**Review Period**”) to review the Deliverable and either: (i) provide written acceptance; or (ii) provide a written notice of rejection specifying in reasonable detail the material deficiencies relative to the applicable Specifications.  
  (c) If the Company fails to provide a notice of rejection within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.  
  (d) If a Deliverable is rejected, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable, after which the review and acceptance process described in this Section shall be repeated.  

2.6 **Subcontractors.**  
The Service Provider may, in its discretion, engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (a) the Service Provider remains fully responsible and liable for the performance of such subcontractors; and (b) any subcontractors who will have access to the Company’s Confidential Information or Personal Data are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection that are at least as protective as those contained in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total Fees payable by the Company to the Service Provider for the Services and Deliverables under this Agreement shall be **$3,831,961** (three million eight hundred thirty-one thousand nine hundred sixty-one U.S. dollars), exclusive of applicable taxes, unless otherwise adjusted in accordance with this Agreement and any duly executed Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall be structured as follows:

  3.2.1 **Phase-Based Fixed Fees.**  
  - Phase I (Discovery and Assessment): $421,000  
  - Phase II (Architecture and Roadmap): $612,000  
  - Phase III (Implementation and Migration): $1,600,000  
  - Phase IV (Analytics Enablement): $850,000  
  - Phase V (Managed Services and Optimization – Baseline): $348,961  

  3.2.2 **Time and Materials Components.**  
  Certain Services, including out-of-scope enhancements, specialized analytics use cases, and major unplanned change requests, shall be billed on a time and materials basis at the Service Provider’s then-current standard rates, unless otherwise agreed in a Change Order. The Service Provider’s initial hourly rate card shall be provided as an Appendix and may be adjusted annually upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the Company.

3.3 **Milestone Payments.**  
The Parties agree to the following milestone-based payment schedule for the fixed-fee components:

  (a) **Milestone 1 – Completion of Phase I:** 100% of Phase I fees invoiced upon Company’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of all Phase I Deliverables.  
  (b) **Milestone 2 – Completion of Phase II:** 100% of Phase II fees invoiced upon Company’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of all Phase II Deliverables.  
  (c) **Milestone 3 – Implementation Midpoint:** 50% of Phase III fees invoiced upon completion of at least fifty percent (50%) of the workloads identified for migration, as confirmed in writing by the Parties.  
  (d) **Milestone 4 – Completion of Phase III:** Remaining 50% of Phase III fees invoiced upon Company’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of all Phase III Deliverables.  
  (e) **Milestone 5 – Completion of Phase IV Core Use Cases:** 70% of Phase IV fees invoiced upon Company’s acceptance of core analytics use cases as defined in the applicable requirements document.  
  (f) **Milestone 6 – Completion of Phase IV Extended Use Cases:** Remaining 30% of Phase IV fees invoiced upon completion and acceptance of all agreed analytics components.  
  (g) **Phase V Managed Services Fees:** The baseline Phase V fees shall be invoiced in equal monthly installments over the scheduled duration of Phase V.

3.4 **Payment Terms; Net 30.**  
All invoices issued by the Service Provider under this Agreement shall be due and payable by the Company within **thirty (30) calendar days** after the Company’s receipt of such invoice (“**Net 30**”). Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars, by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method. The Company shall not unreasonably withhold or delay payment of any undisputed portion of an invoice.

3.5 **Late Payment; Interest.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the highest rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower, computed from the due date until the date paid in full. The Service Provider shall have the right, upon written notice to the Company, to suspend performance of the Services in the event that any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the due date, provided that the Service Provider has given at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice and an opportunity to cure.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
The Fees are exclusive of all sales, use, value-added, excise, and other similar taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority. The Company shall be responsible for all such taxes associated with the Services and Deliverables, excluding taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or employees.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other related costs, provided that such expenses are in compliance with the Company’s then-current travel and expense policy (if provided in writing to the Service Provider). Expense invoices shall be accompanied by reasonable supporting documentation.

3.8 **Withholding and Setoff.**  
Except as required by applicable law, the Company shall not withhold or deduct any amounts from payments due to the Service Provider under this Agreement. The Company shall have no right of setoff or counterclaim against any amounts owed to the Service Provider, except to the extent expressly permitted by this Agreement or agreed in writing by the Parties.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in full force and effect until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Termination for Convenience by the Company.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement for its convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider. In the event of such termination for convenience:

  (a) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for: (i) all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted (or deemed accepted) through the effective date of termination; (ii) all authorized, non-cancellable commitments and expenses incurred by the Service Provider in reasonable reliance on this Agreement; and (iii) any early termination or wind-down fees expressly set forth in an applicable Change Order.  
  (b) The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to effect an orderly transition of Services, subject to additional fees and terms as may be agreed in a transition assistance plan.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.3.1 **By the Company.**  
  The Company may terminate this Agreement for cause if the Service Provider: (a) materially breaches any material term of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days following receipt of written notice thereof from the Company; or (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed over substantially all of its assets.  

  4.3.2 **By the Service Provider.**  
  The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for cause if the Company: (a) fails to pay any undisputed amount due under this Agreement within sixty (60) days after the due date and fails to cure such nonpayment within ten (10) Business Days following written notice from the Service Provider; (b) materially breaches any material term of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days following written notice thereof; or (c) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed over substantially all of its assets.

4.4 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) The Company shall promptly pay all amounts then due and payable to the Service Provider, including any amounts described in **Section 4.2** and any outstanding expenses.  
  (b) The Service Provider shall cease performance of the Services, except as may be reasonably necessary to effect a transition if so requested by the Company, subject to mutually agreed fees.  
  (c) Each Party shall promptly return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to the retention rights described in **Section 5.4** and **Article XI**.  
  (d) The Company’s license rights in any Deliverables or Work Product, as granted under **Article VI**, shall survive termination or expiration, subject to the Company’s full payment of applicable Fees.

4.5 **Survival.**  
Notwithstanding any expiration or termination of this Agreement, the following provisions shall survive: **Articles I, III (to the extent of outstanding fees and expenses), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and **Sections 2.7, 4.4, 4.5** and any other provision that by its nature is intended to survive.

SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For the managed services under Phase V, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production cloud environments and analytics platforms under its direct management are available at least **99.9%** of the time each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and downtime caused by: (a) Force Majeure Events; (b) failures of third-party networks or systems outside the Service Provider’s control; or (c) the Company’s acts or omissions.

12.2 **Incident Response and Resolution.**  
The Service Provider shall adhere to the following incident response times:

  - **Critical Severity:** Affects mission-critical business operations or causes complete system outage. Response within one (1) hour; target resolution or workaround within four (4) hours.  
  - **High Severity:** Significant degradation impacting multiple users. Response within four (4) hours; target resolution within one (1) Business Day.  
  - **Medium Severity:** Moderate impact or partial degradation. Response within one (1) Business Day; target resolution within three (3) Business Days.  
  - **Low Severity:** Minor issues or cosmetic defects. Response within three (3) Business Days; resolution on a best-effort basis in future maintenance releases.

12.3 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
In the event that the Service Provider fails to meet the Service Availability target in a given month, the Company shall be entitled to receive a service credit equal to a percentage of the monthly managed services fee for the affected environment, as follows:

  - Availability between 99.5% and 99.9%: 5% credit  
  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.49%: 10% credit  
  - Availability below 99.0%: 15% credit  

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute the Company’s sole monetary remedy for SLA breaches, without limiting the Company’s other remedies for material breach.
