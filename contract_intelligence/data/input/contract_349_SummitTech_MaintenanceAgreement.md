## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive **Maintenance Services** for Client Systems and related infrastructure as specified in this Agreement and any applicable SOW. Such Maintenance Services shall include, without limitation:

  (a) Preventive, corrective, and adaptive maintenance of Client Systems;  
  (b) Monitoring of system performance, capacity, and availability;  
  (c) Application of security patches, bug fixes, and version updates;  
  (d) Incident management and problem resolution;  
  (e) Technical support and help desk services for designated Client personnel;  
  (f) Preparation of periodic reports on performance, incidents, and maintenance activities; and  
  (g) Consultation and recommendations regarding performance optimization, capacity planning, and lifecycle management.

2.2 **Detailed Maintenance Activities.**  
Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.1**, Vendor’s Maintenance Services shall, at a minimum, include the following categories of work, to be performed in accordance with the timelines and Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**:

  (a) **Preventive Maintenance.** Vendor shall perform regularly scheduled preventive maintenance, including system checks, log reviews, security scans, capacity utilization assessments, and routine health checks at intervals agreed upon in writing, but no less than monthly. Preventive Maintenance shall be designed to proactively identify and remediate potential issues before they result in Incidents.

  (b) **Corrective Maintenance.** Vendor shall promptly investigate, diagnose, and correct Issues and Incidents impacting the operation or performance of the Client Systems. Corrective Maintenance includes applying temporary workarounds, permanent fixes, configuration changes, and, where necessary, escalation to third-party vendors under Vendor’s management.

  (c) **Adaptive Maintenance and Enhancements.** Vendor shall adjust or modify configurations, parameters, and integrations in response to changes in Client’s business requirements, operating environment, or third-party dependencies, to the extent such changes fall within the scope of standard maintenance. Material enhancements or development efforts may require a Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII**.

  (d) **Patch and Update Management.** Vendor shall review and evaluate vendor-issued patches, updates, firmware upgrades, and security advisories relevant to the Client Systems. Vendor shall recommend and, upon Client’s approval (unless otherwise agreed in writing), implement such patches or updates in accordance with industry best practices and within commercially reasonable timeframes, subject to risk assessments and testing within staging environments where feasible.

  (e) **Monitoring and Alerting.** Vendor shall implement and maintain 24x7 monitoring (unless otherwise specified in an SOW) of Client Systems within the scope of this Agreement, including monitoring of uptime, performance metrics, error logs, and security events. Vendor shall configure thresholds and alerts so that material deviations are promptly notified to Vendor’s support team and, as appropriate, to Client’s designated contacts.

  (f) **Backup and Recovery Support.** Where Client’s systems rely on backup processes within Vendor’s control as set forth in an SOW, Vendor shall monitor backup status, report backup success and failure, and support data restoration efforts upon Client’s request. Vendor shall maintain and periodically test recovery processes for systems under its management, consistent with the recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO) specified in any applicable SOW.

  (g) **Security Maintenance.** Vendor shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect the security, confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Client Systems within Vendor’s control, including patching of security vulnerabilities, threat monitoring, and escalation of critical security events to Client in accordance with **Article XI**.

2.3 **Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly stated in this Agreement or an SOW (or added by a Change Order), the Services expressly exclude:

  (a) Development of new software or applications;  
  (b) Major system redesigns or migrations;  
  (c) Procurement of hardware or software licenses on Client’s behalf;  
  (d) Onsite services at Client locations, except as agreed in an SOW;  
  (e) Remediation of issues arising from Client’s unauthorized modifications, negligence, or breach of this Agreement; and  
  (f) Support for systems, applications, or environments outside the agreed scope.

Any such excluded services, if requested by Client, shall be subject to a separate written agreement or Change Order and may be billed at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, training, and experience, and in accordance with:

  (a) generally accepted industry standards for IT maintenance services;  
  (b) the Service Levels and response times set forth in **Article XII**; and  
  (c) all applicable laws and regulations (including those governing data protection and cybersecurity).

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Vendor’s performance of the Services is expressly conditioned upon the following responsibilities of Client:

  (a) Providing Vendor with prompt and reasonable access to relevant Client Systems, environments, and information, including secure remote access where applicable;  
  (b) Designating one or more knowledgeable technical and business contacts to coordinate with Vendor;  
  (c) Maintaining appropriate hardware, network connectivity, and environmental conditions not under Vendor’s control;  
  (d) Notifying Vendor promptly of any Incidents, material changes, or issues affecting the Services; and  
  (e) Complying with all security and access procedures agreed by the Parties.

Vendor shall have no liability for any failure to meet Service Levels or other obligations to the extent caused by Client’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities under this Agreement.

2.6 **Deliverables.**  
Vendor shall provide the Deliverables described in any applicable SOW, which may include, without limitation:

  (a) Monthly performance and incident reports;  
  (b) Maintenance and patching logs;  
  (c) System configuration and architecture documentation;  
  (d) Security assessment summaries; and  
  (e) Root cause analysis reports for Severity 1 Incidents.

Unless otherwise agreed, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted upon delivery, unless Client provides written notice of material nonconformity within ten (10) Business Days following delivery, specifying the basis for rejection in reasonable detail. Vendor shall then use commercially reasonable efforts to cure such nonconformity within a reasonable period.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article, shall remain in full force and effect until **July 17, 2026** (the “Term”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew this Agreement for additional terms upon mutually agreed pricing and conditions. Any such renewal shall be set forth in a written amendment executed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Client.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for its convenience and without cause upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to Vendor. In the event of such termination for convenience:

  (a) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services rendered and Deliverables provided through the effective date of termination;  
  (b) Client shall pay any non-cancellable third-party costs incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf; and  
  (c) Client shall pay an early termination fee equal to **twenty percent (20%)** of the remaining unpaid Contract Value that would have been payable through the end of the Term, as liquidated damages and not as a penalty, in recognition of the high-risk nature of this engagement and Vendor’s infrastructure and staffing commitments.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Vendor.**  
Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon at least **two hundred ten (210) days’** prior written notice to Client. Vendor shall cooperate in good faith to provide reasonable transition assistance (at Client’s expense) during such notice period.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  

  (a) **By Either Party.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail, provided that for breaches that are not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have an additional period of up to thirty (30) days to cure, so long as it commences cure within the initial thirty (30) days and diligently pursues such cure.

  (b) **Non-Payment.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, Vendor may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Client fails to pay any undisputed Fees within sixty (60) days after the due date and fails to cure such non-payment within ten (10) Business Days after receiving written notice thereof.

  (c) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent, (ii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, (iii) has a receiver appointed, or (iv) files or has filed against it any petition under bankruptcy or insolvency laws that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) All rights and licenses granted to Client hereunder (other than any perpetual licenses expressly set forth in this Agreement or an SOW) shall terminate;  
  (b) Client shall immediately cease all use of any Vendor-provided tools or systems accessible solely under this Agreement;  
  (c) Client shall promptly pay Vendor all Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued and unpaid through the effective date of termination, plus any early termination fee due under **Section 4.3**, if applicable; and  
  (d) Each Party shall, upon the other Party’s written request, return or securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information, subject to the retention requirements of **Section 5.4** and **Article XI**.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon Client’s request made at least sixty (60) days prior to expiration or upon notice of termination, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to one hundred twenty (120) days following the expiration or termination of this Agreement, at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates, to facilitate a smooth transition of the Services to Client or a third-party provider.

4.8 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any payment obligations and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive.

12.1 **Service Availability.**

  (a) Provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for Planned Maintenance expected to cause service interruptions exceeding thirty (30) minutes; and  
  (b) Endeavor to limit Planned Maintenance causing service unavailability to no more than eight (8) hours per month.

Planned Maintenance shall not count against the uptime commitment, provided Vendor complies with the notice requirements.

12.3 **Incident Severity Levels and Response Times.**  

Incidents reported by Client or detected by Vendor shall be categorized as follows:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of a mission-critical service or severe degradation with no reasonable workaround, impacting a substantial number of users.  
  - Initial Response: within **1 hour** (24x7).  
  - Target Restoration or Workaround: within **4 hours**.

  (b) **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant functional impairment with a reasonable workaround, or performance issues affecting important but not mission-critical services.  
  - Initial Response: within **2 hours** during Business Days (with reasonable efforts outside Business Days).  
  - Target Restoration or Workaround: within **8 hours**.

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact issues, partial loss of non-critical functionality, or issues for which a workaround is available and business impact is moderate.  
  - Initial Response: within **4 Business Hours**.  
  - Target Restoration or Workaround: within **3 Business Days**.

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries and service requests.  
  - Initial Response: within **1 Business Day**.  
  - Target Resolution: within **10 Business Days** or as mutually agreed.

12.4 **Service Credits (If Provided).**  
In the event that Vendor fails to meet the 99.9% monthly uptime commitment (excluding Planned Maintenance and force majeure), Client may be eligible for service credits calculated as follows:

  (a) Availability < 99.9% but ≥ 99.0%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service;  
  (b) Availability < 99.0% but ≥ 98.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service;  
  (c) Availability < 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service.

Service credits, if any, shall be applied to the next invoice. Service credits are the sole contractual remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to other remedies for breach of this Agreement, subject to **Article IX**.

12.5 **Exclusions from SLA.**  
Service Levels shall not apply to interruptions or performance issues caused by:

  (a) Client’s acts or omissions or failure to comply with this Agreement;  
  (b) Client’s or third-party hardware, software, or services not under Vendor’s control;  
  (c) Force Majeure Events; or  
  (d) Planned Maintenance performed in accordance with this Article.
