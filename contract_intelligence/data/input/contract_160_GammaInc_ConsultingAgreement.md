## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
The Service Provider shall provide professional consulting and related Services to the Company, which may include, without limitation:

  a. **Strategic Consulting:** Assessment of the Company’s current technology landscape, operating model, and organizational structure; development of strategic roadmaps for digital transformation, cloud migration, and process optimization; and recommendations for technology investment and prioritization.

  b. **Process and Operational Consulting:** Analysis and redesign of key business processes, including but not limited to sales operations, customer support, supply chain, and finance functions; development of standardized procedures; and design of key performance indicators (KPIs) and governance frameworks.

  c. **Technology Implementation and Integration:** Planning, configuration, and implementation support for selected enterprise software platforms, including but not limited to CRM, ERP, analytics, and integration middleware; data migration and validation; and coordination with third-party vendors and internal IT teams.

  d. **Change Management and Training:** Development and execution of change management plans, stakeholder communication strategies, and training programs for end users and administrators, including training materials and train-the-trainer sessions.

  e. **Project Management and PMO Support:** Provision of project management services, including the establishment of project charters, schedules, risk registers, and status reporting; assistance with establishing or enhancing a Project Management Office (PMO) capability.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) The specific details of each engagement, including the scope of Services, Deliverables, milestones, acceptance criteria, and associated Fees, shall be set forth in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties under this Agreement.  

(b) Each SOW shall reference this Agreement and the contract reference number **CSA-GAM-202509-160**. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern, unless the SOW expressly states that it intends to supersede a particular provision of this Agreement.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Service Provider shall prepare and deliver to the Company the Deliverables identified in each SOW, which may include, by way of example and not limitation:

  a. Written assessment reports, strategy documents, and roadmaps;  
  b. Process flow diagrams, standard operating procedures (SOPs), and governance guidelines;  
  c. Configuration documentation, solution design documents, and integration specifications;  
  d. Training curricula, user guides, and reference materials; and  
  e. Periodic performance, progress, and status reports.

Each Deliverable shall conform in all material respects to the specifications, standards, and acceptance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW. The Parties acknowledge that the detailed description of Deliverables may be further elaborated through Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII**.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications, and in accordance with industry best practices and the standards specified in this Agreement and the applicable SOW.  

(b) The Service Provider shall comply with all written policies and procedures of the Company that are provided to the Service Provider in advance and that are reasonably applicable to the performance of the Services, including those relating to information security, data protection, and workplace conduct.

(c) The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet all agreed timelines and milestones set forth in the applicable SOW. The Parties acknowledge that timelines may be contingent upon timely performance by the Company of its obligations, including the provision of necessary Client Materials, access, and approvals.

2.5 **Project Governance and Reporting.**  
(a) The Parties shall designate project managers (or equivalent roles) who shall be responsible for day-to-day coordination and communication.  

(b) The Service Provider shall provide regular status reports as specified in the applicable SOW, which may include progress against milestones, issues, risks, mitigation plans, and any deviations from the agreed project plan.

(c) Upon reasonable request by the Company, the Service Provider shall participate in governance or steering committee meetings to review project status, key decisions, and performance against Service Levels.

2.6 **Company Obligations.**  
(a) The Company shall provide the Service Provider with timely access to relevant personnel, systems, premises, and Client Materials as reasonably required to perform the Services.  

(b) The Company shall review and provide feedback on Deliverables and other materials submitted by the Service Provider within the timeframes specified in the applicable SOW, or if not specified, within a reasonable period not to exceed ten (10) Business Days.

(c) The Company shall be responsible for decisions based on recommendations and Deliverables provided by the Service Provider, and for implementing such decisions within its organization, except to the extent the implementation is expressly part of the Services.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  
The Service Provider may not subcontract any material portion of the Services without the Company’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. The Service Provider shall remain fully responsible and liable for all acts and omissions of any approved subcontractors, and for ensuring that such subcontractors comply with the terms of this Agreement, including confidentiality and data protection obligations.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
(a) Unless otherwise provided in an applicable SOW, the Company shall have ten (10) Business Days from receipt of a Deliverable (the **“Review Period”**) to review such Deliverable and to notify the Service Provider in writing of its acceptance or of any material non-conformities with the applicable specifications.  

(b) If the Company notifies the Service Provider of any material non-conformities within the Review Period, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for re-review. The Company shall again have a Review Period to accept or reject the resubmitted Deliverable.  

(c) If the Company fails to provide a written notice of acceptance or rejection within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, except where the Deliverable contains a defect that could not reasonably have been discovered during such Review Period.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total contract value for all Services and Deliverables to be rendered under this Agreement is **Three Million Thirty Thousand Two Hundred Eighty-One U.S. Dollars (US$3,030,281)** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustments for approved Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII**.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall be comprised of one or more of the following components, as specified in the applicable SOW:

  a. **Fixed Fees:** Lump-sum or milestone-based Fees for defined Deliverables or phases.  

  b. **Time-and-Materials (T&M) Fees:** Fees based on actual hours worked multiplied by the applicable hourly or daily rates specified in the SOW for the Service Provider’s personnel.  

  c. **Retainer Fees:** Periodic retainer amounts for ongoing advisory or support Services, where applicable.

3.3 **Payment Schedule and Milestones.**  
(a) Unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW, the Contract Value shall be invoiced according to the following general schedule:  

  - Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value upon execution of this Agreement;  
  - Fifty percent (50%) of the Contract Value invoiced monthly in arrears based on Services performed and milestones achieved, as set forth in the SOW;  
  - Thirty percent (30%) of the Contract Value upon completion and final acceptance of the key Deliverables identified in the SOW.

(b) Each SOW shall specify any additional or alternative payment milestones, including milestone-based payments tied to acceptance of specific Deliverables.

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall submit invoices to the Company in accordance with the payment schedule set forth herein and in each SOW. Each invoice shall reference the contract reference number **CSA-GAM-202509-160** and the relevant SOW number, and shall itemize the Services performed, applicable milestones, and any approved expenses.  

(b) Payment of undisputed amounts shall be due within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of receipt of the invoice by the Company (**“Net 60”**).  

(c) The Company may withhold payment of any disputed amounts, provided that it notifies the Service Provider in writing of the basis for the dispute within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the applicable invoice. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any such dispute in accordance with **Article XIV**. The Company shall pay any portion of an invoice that is not disputed by the due date.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
(a) Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the date due until the date paid at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.  

(b) The Service Provider shall not suspend the performance of the Services for late payment unless (i) such payment is more than thirty (30) days past due, (ii) the Service Provider has provided at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice of its intent to suspend, and (iii) the Company has failed to cure the non-payment during such notice period. Any suspension must be proportionate, and the Service Provider shall resume Services promptly upon receipt of payment.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
(a) The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other incidentals in accordance with the Company’s travel and expense policy, as provided to the Service Provider in advance.  

(b) All reimbursable expenses shall be supported by appropriate receipts and documentation, and shall be invoiced in arrears.  

(c) The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize expenses and shall seek prior written approval from the Company for any individual expense in excess of US$2,500.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) The Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes. The Company shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or employment-related taxes), which shall be itemized on the Service Provider’s invoices, where applicable.  

(b) If withholding taxes are required by law to be deducted from any payments to the Service Provider, the Company shall withhold such amounts and shall remit them to the proper tax authority. The Company shall provide the Service Provider with an official receipt or other appropriate documentation to enable the Service Provider to claim a credit or refund for such withholding.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until **May 14, 2028** (the **“Expiration Date”**) (such period, the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for one or more additional terms under mutually agreed terms and conditions. Any renewal shall be documented in a written amendment executed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon providing the Service Provider with at least **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice**. The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience only upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** and only with the Company’s written consent, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld if no critical project phases or milestones (as identified in the applicable SOW) would be adversely impacted.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  a. Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or, if the breach is not reasonably capable of cure within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period) after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  b. Becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, is adjudicated bankrupt, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver or similar officer appointed for its business, property, or assets.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:

  a. The Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the terminated Services, except as reasonably necessary to ensure an orderly wind-down;  

  b. The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted prior to the effective date of termination, and for any reasonable, non-cancellable commitments incurred in reliance upon this Agreement (provided such commitments were pre-approved by the Company);  

  c. Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, in accordance with **Section 5.4**; and  

  d. The Service Provider shall, upon the Company’s request, provide reasonable transition assistance for up to thirty (30) days at the Service Provider’s then-current rates, to facilitate the transfer of the Services to the Company or to a third party.

4.6 **Survival.**  
Any provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination shall so survive, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (to the extent of accrued service credits), XIV, XV, and XVI**, as well as any payment obligations accrued prior to termination.

Confidential Information does not include information that:

  a. Is or becomes generally known to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  

  b. Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any duty of confidentiality;  

  c. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records; or  

  d. Is approved for release by the Disclosing Party in writing.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
(a) The Receiving Party shall: (i) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for purposes of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement; (ii) restrict disclosure of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, agents, contractors, and professional advisors (collectively, **“Representatives”**) who have a need to know such information for such purposes and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein; and (iii) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.  

(b) The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this **Article V** by its Representatives.

5.3 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permissible) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. The Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose and shall use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for any such information.

5.4 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information (including all copies), except that the Receiving Party may retain (i) one (1) archival copy solely for legal and compliance purposes, and (ii) copies stored in routine backup systems, provided such copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein until destroyed in the ordinary course.

5.5 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that Confidential Information constituting trade secrets shall be kept confidential for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
To the extent that a Party’s performance of its obligations (other than payment obligations) is prevented or delayed by a Force Majeure Event, such obligations shall be suspended during the period and to the extent of such prevention or delay, provided that the affected Party complies with **Section 15.2**.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**
