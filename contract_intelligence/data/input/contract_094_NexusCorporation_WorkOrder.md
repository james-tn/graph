## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 Relationship to Parent SOW

2.1.1 This Work Order **adds scope to the Statement of Work dated May 01, 2024 (Ref: SOW-NEX-202405-074)** and is **executed under and subject to** the terms of the Parent SOW, except to the extent explicitly modified in this Work Order.

2.1.2 The high-level Services described in **Section 2.1 (General Description of Services)** and **Section 2.3 (Phases of Work)** of the Parent SOW remain in effect and are hereby supplemented by the WO Services described in this **Article II**. In the event of a direct conflict between the Parent SOW and this Work Order with respect to the WO Services, this Work Order shall control solely with respect to such WO Services.

2.1.3 All performance standards in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** and all acceptance procedures in **Section 2.6 (Deliverables and Acceptance)** of the Parent SOW apply to the WO Services and WO Deliverables, except as enhanced or refined in this Work Order.

### 2.2 Overview of WO Services

2.2.1 Vendor shall provide to Client the following WO Services, in addition to the Services described in Article II of the Parent SOW:

(a) **Advanced Analytics Services:**  
Design, implement, and operationalize a set of advanced analytics capabilities on top of the Platform, including but not limited to:  
  - Predictive models for sales forecasting, churn, and operational risk;  
  - Machine learning pipelines for anomaly detection and fraud alerts;  
  - Self-service analytics enablement through curated semantic layers and governed data marts;  
  - Advanced KPI catalog and dimensional models for at least five (5) additional business domains (e.g., Supply Chain, Marketing Effectiveness, Customer Success, Risk & Compliance, Procurement).

(b) **Global Rollout Services:**  
Plan and execute a multi-region rollout of the Platform to support at least three (3) additional geographic regions beyond the initial scope of the Parent SOW, including:  
  - Multi-region data residency and localization configuration;  
  - Integration with region-specific source systems and identity providers;  
  - Time-zone aware scheduling and localized reporting;  
  - Regional training and enablement programs.

(c) **Enhanced Governance Framework:**  
Design and implement an end-to-end data governance framework that extends the baseline governance contemplated in Phase 4 of the Parent SOW (**Section 2.3.4(c)**), including:  
  - Enterprise data catalog configuration, classification, and lineage;  
  - Role-based access policy templates and approval workflows;  
  - Data quality scorecards, issue management workflows, and stewardship dashboards;  
  - Regulatory reporting data domains (e.g., for GDPR/CCPA, financial reporting, and audit support).

(d) **Additional Managed Services:**  
Extend the managed services described in **Phase 4 – Managed Services and Enhancements (Months 13–24)** of the Parent SOW (**Section 2.3.4**) to cover the Extended Term for those components activated under this Work Order, including:  
  - Level 2 and 3 support for the advanced analytics components;  
  - Proactive monitoring and performance optimization for the global deployment;  
  - Quarterly roadmap sessions specifically focused on advanced analytics and governance;  
  - Capacity planning and cost optimization advisory services.

2.2.2 Vendor acknowledges that timely execution of WO Services is critical to Client’s strategic analytics and compliance initiatives and shall allocate appropriately skilled **Key Personnel** (as defined in Section 1.11 of the Parent SOW) to meet the obligations set forth herein.

### 2.3 WO Phases and Milestones

2.3.1 The WO Services shall be organized into the following phases, which supplement and may run in parallel with the phases set forth in **Section 2.3 of the Parent SOW**:

(a) **WO Phase A – Advanced Analytics Design (Months 1–4 of WO):**  
  - Perform discovery workshops with global and regional stakeholders;  
  - Develop a **WO Advanced Analytics Design Document**, including target architecture, model inventory, and feature engineering approach;  
  - Identify required Third-Party Materials (as defined in Section 1.18 of the Parent SOW) specific to advanced analytics (e.g., ML libraries, cloud AI services).

(b) **WO Phase B – Build & Pilot (Months 4–12 of WO):**  
  - Implement and configure data pipelines, models, and analytics content for at least three priority advanced analytics use cases;  
  - Deploy pilot solutions in one primary region, with defined user acceptance criteria;  
  - Conduct performance tuning and security hardening for all pilot components.

(c) **WO Phase C – Global Rollout & Governance (Months 12–24 of WO):**  
  - Extend the pilot solutions to additional regions;  
  - Implement the Enhanced Governance Framework, including data catalog, access workflows, and stewardship dashboards;  
  - Complete training and enablement across all target regions.

(d) **WO Phase D – Extended Managed Services (Months 24–48 of WO):**  
  - Provide Additional Managed Services for all components delivered under Phases A–C;  
  - Conduct quarterly service reviews and roadmap sessions;  
  - Implement minor enhancements pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent SOW and this Work Order.

2.3.2 Target WO Milestones, which supplement the Milestones in **Section 2.4.1 of the Parent SOW**, are as follows (subject to adjustment via Change Orders):

- **WO Milestone A1 – Advanced Analytics Design Sign-Off:** Within 120 days of the Effective Date of this Work Order.  
- **WO Milestone B1 – Completion of Initial Advanced Analytics Pilot:** Within 270 days of the Effective Date.  
- **WO Milestone C1 – Completion of Global Rollout to All Target Regions:** Within 540 days of the Effective Date.  
- **WO Milestone C2 – Enhanced Governance Framework Operational:** Within 600 days of the Effective Date.  
- **WO Milestone D1 – Transition to Extended Managed Services:** Within 720 days of the Effective Date.

### 2.4 WO Deliverables

2.4.1 In addition to the Deliverables specified in **Section 2.6.1 of the Parent SOW**, Vendor shall provide at least the following WO Deliverables:

(a) **WO Advanced Analytics Design Document** (extending the Solution Design Document referenced in **Section 2.3.1(b)** of the Parent SOW);  
(b) **Model Inventory and Data Science Documentation**, including feature definitions, model governance notes, and retraining policies;  
(c) **Global Rollout Implementation Plan**, including region-by-region dependencies and cutover strategies;  
(d) **Enhanced Data Governance Charter**, policies, and process documentation;  
(e) **Configured Data Catalog and Lineage Views**, including role-based access setup;  
(f) **Stewardship & Data Quality Dashboards** for key domains;  
(g) **Regional Training Materials and Recordings** tailored to local operations;  
(h) **Extended Managed Services Runbooks**, including escalation procedures aligned with **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent SOW.

2.4.2 Acceptance of WO Deliverables shall follow the process set forth in **Section 2.6 (Deliverables and Acceptance)** of the Parent SOW, except that the review period for WO Advanced Analytics Design Document and Enhanced Data Governance Charter shall be **thirty (30) Business Days** due to their complexity. All other review periods remain **twenty (20) Business Days** unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### 2.5 Performance Standards for WO Services

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the WO Services:

(a) consistently with the performance standards in **Section 2.5 of the Parent SOW**;  
(b) in compliance with Client’s Security Policies and any additional requirements applicable to advanced analytics and data governance; and  
(c) in a manner that ensures the advanced analytics components do not materially degrade the Platform’s availability commitments under **Section 12.1 of the Parent SOW**, subject to Force Majeure Events.

2.5.2 The Parties acknowledge that this Work Order is designated **High Risk**, and therefore Vendor shall implement enhanced operational controls, including:  
(a) expanded monitoring coverage for advanced analytics and governance workflows;  
(b) monthly operational reporting to Client’s designated governance committee; and  
(c) participation in at least semi-annual security and compliance reviews initiated by Client.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value and Fee Structure

3.1.1 The total contract value for the WO Services and WO Deliverables under this Work Order shall be **One Million Nine Hundred Thirty Thousand Nine Hundred Fifty-Six United States Dollars (US $1,930,956)** (the “**Contract Value**” or “**WO Fees**”), exclusive of taxes.

(a) **WO Phases A–C (Design, Build, Rollout & Governance):** US $1,200,000 (fixed fee);  
(b) **WO Phase D – Additional Managed Services (Extended Term Support):** US $650,000 (recurring fees);  
(c) **WO Contingency/Change Budget:** US $80,956 (to be drawn down only upon execution of WO-specific Change Orders consistent with **Article XIII of the Parent SOW**).

3.1.3 The WO Fees are separate and in addition to the Contract Value of **US $2,592,793** specified in **Section 3.1.1 of the Parent SOW**, and no amounts under this Work Order shall be deemed included in or reduce the fees due under the Parent SOW.

### 3.2 Invoicing and Payment Milestones

3.2.1 Vendor shall invoice Client for the WO Fees in accordance with the following schedule:

(a) **WO Phases A–C (Fixed Fee Components):**  
  - 20% of US $1,200,000 (i.e., US $240,000) upon Client’s acceptance of **WO Milestone A1** (Advanced Analytics Design Sign-Off);  
  - 30% of US $1,200,000 (i.e., US $360,000) upon Client’s acceptance of **WO Milestone B1** (Initial Advanced Analytics Pilot);  
  - 50% of US $1,200,000 (i.e., US $600,000) upon Client’s acceptance of **WO Milestone C2** (Enhanced Governance Framework Operational).

(b) **WO Phase D – Additional Managed Services:**  
  - Billed in equal quarterly installments in arrears during the Extended Managed Services period for a total of US $650,000. The Parties will agree in writing, prior to commencement of Phase D, on a quarterly schedule that aligns with the transition date in **WO Milestone D1**.

(c) **WO Change Orders:**  
  - Invoiced in accordance with the payment schedule specified in the applicable Change Order referencing this Work Order, utilizing the WO Contingency/Change Budget where applicable.

3.2.2 Notwithstanding **Section 3.3.2 (Net 45)** of the Parent SOW, the Parties agree that, due to the High-Risk designation and the extensive Additional Managed Services, **all undisputed invoices under this Work Order shall be due and payable within sixty (60) days** from the date of Client’s receipt of such invoice (“**Net 60**”).

3.2.3 Client may continue to exercise its set-off and good-faith dispute rights under **Section 3.7 of the Parent SOW** with respect to WO Fees, provided that any disputed portion of an invoice shall not relieve Client of its obligation to pay the undisputed portion in accordance with this Section 3.2.

### 3.3 Late Payments

3.3.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due under this Work Order shall accrue interest from the date due until the date paid at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month** or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law. For clarity, this rate supersedes the rate specified in **Section 3.5.1 of the Parent SOW** solely with respect to WO Fees.

3.3.2 Vendor’s right to suspend WO Services for late payment shall be as described in **Section 3.5.2 of the Parent SOW**, provided that Vendor may, in its discretion, suspend only those WO Services associated with the overdue invoices while continuing to provide other Services under the Parent SOW.

### 3.4 Taxes and Expenses

3.4.1 Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)** of the Parent SOW. All WO Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, VAT, or similar taxes.

3.4.2 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, WO Fees are inclusive of ordinary travel and related expenses, consistent with **Section 3.6 of the Parent SOW**. Any extraordinary or international travel specifically required for WO Services shall be pre-approved in writing by Client and reimbursed at cost, subject to Client’s expense policies.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term; Extended Term

4.1.1 This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until **January 22, 2029** (the “**Expiration Date**” or “**Extended Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent SOW.

4.1.2 The Parties may extend the Extended Term only by a written amendment or new work order executed in accordance with **Section 16.3 of the Parent SOW** and **Section 16.3 of this Work Order**.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 In amendment to **Section 4.2.1 of the Parent SOW**, and reflecting the High-Risk and high-investment nature of this Work Order, **Client may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor. During such notice period, Client shall remain responsible for payment of all WO Fees for WO Services performed and non-cancellable commitments incurred by Vendor consistent with this Work Order.

4.2.2 Vendor may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon **ninety (90) days’ prior written notice** to Client, provided that Vendor continues to perform the WO Services diligently during such notice period and complies with its Transition Assistance Services obligations as described in **Section 4.5**.

4.2.3 Termination of this Work Order for convenience shall not, by itself, constitute termination of the Parent SOW, which shall remain in effect in accordance with its terms unless separately terminated.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 The Parties’ rights to terminate for cause under **Section 4.3 of the Parent SOW** apply to this Work Order. Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Work Order or the applicable portions of the Parent SOW and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of notice specifying the breach.

4.3.2 For material breach of WO-specific Service Levels, recurring critical security failures, or repeated unremedied failure to meet WO Milestones, Client may pursue termination for cause as described above, without prejudice to other remedies.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination; Relationship to Parent SOW

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order:

(a) Vendor shall cease the WO Services (except to the extent Transition Assistance Services are requested and agreed), and shall promptly deliver to Client any completed or in-progress WO Deliverables for which Client has paid;  
(b) Client shall pay Vendor for all undisputed WO Services properly performed and accepted, and for authorized expenses incurred, through the effective date of termination, consistent with **Section 4.4 of the Parent SOW**; and  
(c) the provisions of this Work Order which by their nature are intended to survive shall remain in full force and effect, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**.

4.4.2 Termination or expiration of the Parent SOW shall, unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing, automatically result in termination of this Work Order as of the effective date of such termination of the Parent SOW. In such case, Client shall compensate Vendor as set forth in Section 4.4.1(b) above for WO Services performed prior to termination.

4.4.3 Termination of this Work Order shall not automatically terminate the Parent SOW; however, if the circumstances giving rise to termination for cause relate to Vendor’s fundamental inability to perform managed services or maintain the Platform, Client may, at its discretion, initiate termination for cause of the Parent SOW pursuant to **Section 4.3 thereof**.

### 4.5 Transition Assistance

4.5.1 In addition to **Section 4.5 of the Parent SOW**, Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request made at any time during the 90-day period following termination or expiration of this Work Order, provide Transition Assistance Services for the WO Services for up to **one hundred and twenty (120) days** after such termination or expiration.

4.5.2 Unless termination results from Vendor’s uncured material breach, Transition Assistance Services shall be provided at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates. In the event of termination by Client for Vendor’s material breach, such Transition Assistance Services shall be provided at a mutually agreed discounted rate, not to exceed fifty percent (50%) of such rate.
