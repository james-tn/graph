## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 Phoenix shall sell, supply, and deliver to Contoso, and Contoso shall purchase from Phoenix, the Products, Deliverables, and Services described in this Agreement and any applicable SOWs or POs issued hereunder.

2.1.2 The Parties anticipate that the engagement will include the design, supply, configuration, and commissioning of a multi-site data center hardware refresh and expansion, including high-availability server infrastructure, associated storage and networking equipment, and ongoing maintenance and support throughout the Term.

### 2.2 Products

2.2.1 Phoenix shall supply enterprise-grade hardware Products, which may include, without limitation:

- Rack-mounted servers with redundant power supplies and hot-swappable drives;  
- Network switches, routers, and firewalls suitable for data center operations;  
- Storage arrays with RAID capabilities and associated controllers;  
- Power distribution units (PDUs) and related accessories; and  
- Embedded firmware and bundled management software necessary for operating the Products.

2.2.2 All Products shall conform in all material respects to the Specifications and to Phoenix’s published product descriptions at the time of the applicable PO, and shall be new, unused, and not previously deployed, unless otherwise agreed in writing by Contoso.

### 2.3 Services

2.3.1 Phoenix shall provide the following categories of Services:

(a) **Design and Planning Services**  
Phoenix shall collaborate with Contoso’s technical and business stakeholders to review Contoso’s capacity, performance, resilience, and security requirements; develop a detailed infrastructure design; and produce solution architecture documentation. These Services shall include network and rack layout design, capacity planning, redundancy strategy, and integration approach with Contoso’s existing systems.

(b) **Implementation and Installation Services**  
Phoenix shall perform on-site and/or remote installation and configuration of Products at data center locations specified by Contoso. Such Services shall include physical rack and stack, cabling, initial power-up, network configuration, operating system and firmware configuration (subject to licenses held by Contoso), and basic integration testing to verify connectivity and base functionality.

(c) **Configuration and Integration Services**  
Phoenix shall configure the Products in accordance with Contoso’s documented requirements and Phoenix’s best practices, including VLAN configuration, storage allocation, high-availability failover configuration, and integration with Contoso’s monitoring and logging systems, subject to access and information reasonably provided by Contoso.

(d) **Testing and Acceptance Support**  
Phoenix shall conduct initial system tests, including power, connectivity, and basic performance tests. Phoenix shall support Contoso’s own acceptance testing by providing technical resources to remedy any defects or non-conformities identified in accordance with Section 2.6.

(e) **Training Services**  
Phoenix shall deliver training sessions to designated Contoso staff, covering operation, monitoring, and basic troubleshooting of the supplied Products. Training may be delivered on-site or remotely and shall include supporting materials such as user guides and runbooks.

(f) **Maintenance and Support Services**  
Phoenix shall provide maintenance and support Services, including hardware break-fix, firmware updates, and technical support, subject to the Service Levels set out in Article XII and any detailed support description in an SOW.

### 2.4 Deliverables

2.4.1 Deliverables to be provided by Phoenix shall include, without limitation:

- Detailed infrastructure design documentation;  
- Rack diagrams, cabling plans, and IP addressing schemes;  
- Configuration files and scripts used for system setup;  
- System runbooks and operational manuals;  
- Training materials and session recordings (if applicable); and  
- Periodic maintenance and support reports.

2.4.2 Each Deliverable shall conform to the applicable Specifications, as defined in the relevant SOW or PO, and shall be suitable for use in Contoso’s environment as described in such SOW or PO.

### 2.5 Timeline and Milestones

2.5.1 The Parties anticipate that the primary implementation phase shall be substantially completed within twelve (12) months of the Effective Date, subject to timely cooperation by Contoso and timely issuance of applicable POs and SOWs.

2.5.2 Specific milestones, including but not limited to design completion, delivery of equipment, completion of installation at each site, and final acceptance, shall be defined in one or more SOWs, which shall include target dates and associated responsibilities.

2.5.3 Phoenix shall provide Contoso with a project plan and schedule within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, which schedule may be updated from time to time upon mutual written agreement.

### 2.6 Acceptance

2.6.1 Unless otherwise set forth in an applicable SOW, Contoso shall have a period of thirty (30) days following delivery and installation of the Products and Deliverables at each applicable site (the “Acceptance Period”) to test and evaluate the same for material conformity with the Specifications.

2.6.2 If the Products or Deliverables materially conform to the Specifications, Contoso shall provide written acceptance or shall be deemed to have accepted them upon expiration of the Acceptance Period without written notice of rejection.

2.6.3 If Contoso reasonably determines that the Products or Deliverables fail to conform in any material respect to the Specifications, Contoso shall, within the Acceptance Period, provide written notice to Phoenix specifying the nature of such non-conformity in reasonable detail. Phoenix shall, at its own cost and as Contoso’s sole remedy for such non-conformity, promptly correct, repair, or replace the non-conforming item and resubmit it for acceptance testing. The Parties shall repeat the acceptance process as described in this Section 2.6 for the corrected item.

2.6.4 In the event Phoenix fails to correct a material non-conformity after a reasonable number of attempts (not fewer than two (2)), Contoso may, at its option and in addition to other rights set forth herein, reject the affected Product or Deliverable and receive a refund of any amounts paid for such Product or Deliverable.

### 2.7 Performance Standards

2.7.1 Phoenix shall perform the Services (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards, (b) using personnel with suitable skills, experience, and qualifications, and (c) in accordance with this Agreement and all applicable laws, rules, and regulations.

2.7.2 Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the project timelines and Service Levels, recognizing that certain tasks may be dependent on Contoso’s timely cooperation, including provision of access, information, and approvals.

2.7.3 Phoenix shall promptly notify Contoso in writing if Phoenix becomes aware of any circumstances that are likely to materially delay the performance of any Services or the delivery of any Products or Deliverables, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate such delay.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The total estimated contract value for the Products, Deliverables, and Services contemplated under this Agreement is **Three Million Eight Hundred Thirty-Two Thousand Five Hundred Thirty-Three U.S. Dollars (US$3,832,533)** (the “Contract Value”), subject to adjustments via mutually agreed POs, SOWs, and Change Orders.

### 3.2 Fee Structure

3.2.1 The Fees shall consist of the following components:

(a) **Product Fees** – Fixed unit prices for each category of hardware and embedded software, as set forth in Schedule A or applicable POs;  

(b) **Implementation Services Fees** – Time-and-materials or fixed fees for design, installation, configuration, and integration services, as detailed in Schedule B or applicable SOWs;  

(c) **Maintenance and Support Fees** – Annual or multi-year support fees for hardware maintenance and technical support, as set forth in Schedule C; and  

(d) **Other Fees** – Any additional agreed charges, such as on-site emergency support, expedited shipping, or specialized training, as specified in applicable SOWs or POs.

### 3.3 Payment Terms

3.3.1 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW or PO, Fees shall be invoiced as follows:

(a) Product Fees:  
- Fifty percent (50%) of the Product Fees payable upon issuance of the applicable PO; and  
- Fifty percent (50%) of the Product Fees payable upon delivery of the Products to the applicable Contoso site.

(b) Implementation Services Fees:  
- Monthly in arrears based on actual Services performed, if on a time-and-materials basis; or  
- According to milestone-based payments as specified in the applicable SOW, if on a fixed-fee basis.

(c) Maintenance and Support Fees:  
- Annually in advance, unless otherwise specified in Schedule C.

3.3.2 All invoices shall be payable within **thirty (30) days** from the date of invoice (“Net 30”), provided that the invoice is accurate and issued in accordance with this Agreement. Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars by electronic funds transfer or as otherwise agreed in writing.

### 3.4 Late Payments

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate allowed by applicable law, from the date such payment was due until the date of actual payment.

3.4.2 Phoenix shall not suspend Services or delivery of Products for late payment unless (a) Contoso is more than forty-five (45) days past due on undisputed invoices totaling at least one hundred thousand U.S. dollars (US$100,000), and (b) Phoenix has provided Contoso with written notice of such delinquency and a ten (10) Business Day cure period.

### 3.5 Disputed Amounts

3.5.1 If Contoso disputes any invoiced amount in good faith, Contoso shall notify Phoenix in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice.

3.5.2 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts within thirty (30) days after Contoso’s notice. Any resolved amount shall be paid within fifteen (15) days after resolution.

### 3.6 Taxes

3.6.1 The Fees are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding Phoenix’s income, franchise, and employment taxes) that Phoenix is required to collect by law.

3.6.2 Phoenix shall itemize any such taxes on its invoices and shall provide reasonable documentation to support the tax amounts charged.

### 3.7 Expenses

3.7.1 Reasonable, pre-approved travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Phoenix in performing on-site Services shall be reimbursed by Contoso at cost, subject to Contoso’s then-current expense reimbursement policies, provided that Phoenix submits proper receipts and documentation with its invoices.

3.7.2 Phoenix shall obtain Contoso’s written approval (email being sufficient) prior to incurring any single expense in excess of one thousand U.S. dollars (US$1,000).

### 4.1 Term

4.2.1 The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, extend this Agreement for one or more additional periods, under such terms and conditions as the Parties may agree in writing.

4.3.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon at least **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Phoenix.

4.3.2 Phoenix may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon at least **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso, provided that no such termination shall relieve Phoenix of its obligation to complete any ongoing SOW or PO unless Contoso agrees otherwise in writing.

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment of undisputed Fees) following receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or for reorganization; (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) All outstanding and undisputed invoices shall become immediately due and payable;  
(b) Phoenix shall cease performance of Services and accept no further POs, except as mutually agreed in writing;  
(c) Contoso shall pay Phoenix for all Products delivered and accepted and Services properly performed through the effective date of termination, and any non-cancellable, pre-approved expenses actually incurred; and  
(d) Each Party shall, upon the other Party’s request, return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information, subject to Section 5.5 and applicable law.

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any rights, obligations, or liabilities that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including any rights to payment, indemnification, or remedies for breach.

4.5.3 Sections that by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination (including but not limited to Sections 3.4, 3.5, 4.5, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 12, 14, 15, and 16) shall so survive.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The Party claiming a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and estimated duration of the Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of such event and to resume performance as soon as practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

15.3.1 During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, except for any payment obligations accrued prior to such event.

15.4.1 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially impairs the ability of a Party to perform its obligations, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for any accrued payment obligations.
