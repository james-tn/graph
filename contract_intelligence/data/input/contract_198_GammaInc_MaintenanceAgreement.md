## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive maintenance and support services in respect of the Systems, which may include, without limitation:

  a. Preventative maintenance activities designed to minimize unplanned downtime and optimize performance of the Systems;

  b. Corrective maintenance to investigate, diagnose, and remediate Incidents affecting the Systems;

  c. Software maintenance, including the installation of patches, updates, hotfixes, and minor upgrades;

  d. Hardware maintenance, including inspection, diagnostics, and coordination of repair or replacement of hardware components (where Vendor is responsible or as otherwise agreed);

  e. 24x7 monitoring of critical Systems (if specified in an SOW), alerting, and event correlation;

  f. Knowledge transfer, technical advice, and operational guidance to Client’s authorized personnel;

  g. Preparation and delivery of maintenance reports, incident logs, root cause analyses, and capacity or performance reports, as agreed in an SOW.

2.2 **Detailed Scope.**  
The Maintenance Services under this Agreement shall be further described and specified in one or more SOWs or schedules referencing this Agreement and the Contract Reference Number **MNT-GAM-202411-198**. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services shall typically include:

  a. **Preventative Maintenance:**  
  - Scheduled system health checks (at least quarterly, unless otherwise set forth in an SOW);  
  - Performance benchmarking and capacity monitoring;  
  - Validation of backup routines and verification of restore procedures;  
  - Application of security patches and recommended vendor patches following Client approval where required;  
  - Maintenance of configuration baselines and asset inventories.

  b. **Corrective Maintenance and Incident Management:**  
  - Operation of a help desk or support desk accessible via email, telephone, or ticketing system during the support hours defined in the applicable SOW;  
  - Logging and triage of Incidents, prioritization, and assignment of severity levels;  
  - Investigation and diagnosis of root cause, including analysis of system logs and configuration;  
  - Implementation of temporary workarounds where immediate permanent remediation is not feasible;  
  - Implementation of permanent fixes as soon as reasonably practicable within the service levels in **Article XII**.

  c. **Software Maintenance and Configuration Management:**  
  - Management and deployment of software patches and minor releases for designated software components;  
  - Ensuring compatibility of patches with existing configurations (to the extent reasonably possible);  
  - Version control and record-keeping of all changes to the Systems implemented by Vendor;  
  - Maintenance of documentation reflecting the current operational configuration and topology.

  d. **Hardware Coordination (if applicable):**  
  - Coordination with third-party hardware vendors or original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) for warranty repairs or replacements;  
  - On-site intervention (if specified in an SOW) or remote assistance to support hardware diagnostics;  
  - Provision of temporary workarounds or alternative configurations to minimize Client’s downtime.

  e. **Reporting and Meetings:**  
  - Monthly or quarterly service review reports, summarizing Incidents, response and resolution times, trends, and outstanding issues;  
  - Periodic review meetings (in person or virtual) with Client’s technical and management representatives to review performance, upcoming changes, and risk assessments;  
  - Recommendations for enhancement or optimization of the Systems and the overall maintenance program.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  a. In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry best practices for system maintenance and support;  
  b. Using personnel who are appropriately skilled, trained, and experienced for their assigned tasks;  
  c. In compliance with all Applicable Laws and regulations;  
  d. In accordance with the service levels and response times specified in **Article XII** and any applicable SOW; and  
  e. In such a manner as to minimize disruption to Client’s normal business operations, including performing Planned Maintenance during agreed maintenance windows where reasonably practicable.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
In order for Vendor to perform the Services, Client shall:

  a. Provide Vendor with reasonable access (remote and/or on-site, as applicable) to the Systems, including necessary network connectivity and VPN access;  
  b. Designate one or more authorized representatives to serve as the primary contact(s) for coordination and escalation;  
  c. Provide all information, documentation, and cooperation reasonably requested by Vendor to perform the Services;  
  d. Ensure that all third-party licenses, consents, and approvals necessary for Vendor to access and maintain the Systems are in place and remain valid;  
  e. Refrain from making unauthorized changes to the Systems that may materially affect the Services; and  
  f. Promptly notify Vendor of any Incidents, anomalies, or suspected security breaches relating to the Systems.

2.5 **Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly set forth in an SOW or otherwise agreed in writing:

  a. Vendor shall not be responsible for maintenance or support of hardware or software not listed in the applicable SOW;  
  b. Vendor shall not be responsible for Incidents arising from unauthorized modifications or misuse of the Systems by Client or third parties;  
  c. Vendor shall not be required to provide custom development, major version upgrades, or large-scale system re-architecture as part of the Maintenance Services (such work may be subject to a separate project SOW); and  
  d. Vendor shall not be responsible for services outside the agreed support hours except on a time and materials basis as mutually agreed.

2.6 **Statements of Work.**  
The Parties may, from time to time, enter into additional SOWs under this Agreement. Each SOW shall reference this Agreement and Contract Reference Number **MNT-GAM-202411-198**, set forth the specific scope of Services, Deliverables, fees, timelines, and acceptance criteria, and shall be signed by authorized representatives of both Parties. In the event of conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail, unless the SOW expressly states that it overrides specific provisions of this Agreement.

All undisputed invoices issued by Vendor shall be payable by Client within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of invoice (**Net 45**). Payment shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) by bank transfer or such other method as may be agreed in writing.

Vendor shall be entitled, upon written notice and after providing Client with a ten (10) Business Day cure period, to suspend the provision of Services for as long as any undisputed invoice remains overdue by more than thirty (30) days, provided that such suspension shall not relieve Client from its obligation to pay all amounts due.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of an invoice if Client disputes any portion of such invoice, specifying the reasons for the dispute. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such dispute promptly. Client shall remain obligated to pay the undisputed portion of any invoice in accordance with **Section 3.4**.

All fees stated are exclusive of any applicable taxes, duties, or governmental charges (including, but not limited to, value-added tax, goods and services tax, and withholding tax) imposed in connection with the Services. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income. If Client is required by law to withhold taxes from payments to Vendor, Client shall provide Vendor with appropriate documentation and cooperate to minimize any such withholding where lawfully possible.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Reasonable and properly documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in providing on-site or other agreed Services, such as travel, accommodation, and per diem, shall be reimbursed by Client at cost, subject to Client’s prior written approval (email being sufficient). ## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **October 30, 2027** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** (the **“Initial Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Agreement for one or more additional terms, each for a period and on financial terms to be mutually agreed in writing (each a **“Renewal Term”**, and together with the Initial Term, the **“Term”**).

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. In the event of such termination for convenience:

  a. Client shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination; and  
  b. Any prepaid fees for Services not yet performed shall be refunded on a pro rata basis within thirty (30) days after the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice in the event that:

  a. The other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days following written notice specifying the breach; or  
  b. The other Party becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or is placed under receivership or liquidation.

4.5 **Termination for Persistent Service Level Failures.**  
Without prejudice to Client’s other rights and remedies, if Vendor fails to meet the core service levels under **Article XII** for three (3) consecutive months or for any four (4) months in a rolling twelve (12) month period, and such failures have a material adverse impact on Client’s operations, Client may terminate this Agreement or the affected SOW for cause upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, provided Vendor does not cure such failures within such period.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  a. Vendor shall promptly cease performing the Services, except for any Services reasonably necessary to ensure an orderly transition, where requested by Client;  
  b. Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed amounts due for Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination;  
  c. Vendor shall deliver to Client, at Client’s request and cost (if applicable), any work in progress and all Deliverables for which Client has paid; and  
  d. Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5**.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination, shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Availability.**  
Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Systems under Vendor’s direct management and monitoring are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Planned Maintenance, emergency maintenance, and outages caused by factors beyond Vendor’s reasonable control.

12.2 **Support Hours.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  a. Standard support shall be available from 09:00 to 18:00 (local time of the Client’s primary location) on Business Days; and  
  b. Critical Incident support for Severity 1 issues may be provided on a 24x7 basis as specified in the SOW.

12.3 **Incident Severity Levels and Response Times.**  
Vendor shall classify Incidents based on severity and respond within the following target Response Times:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Total outage or severe impact on critical business operations; Response Time: within 1 hour during support hours (or 24x7 if specified).  
  - **Severity 2 (High):** Significant functional impairment with no feasible workaround; Response Time: within 2 hours during support hours.  
  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial degradation or non-critical issue with a workaround; Response Time: within 4 hours during support hours.  
  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor impact, cosmetic issues, or general questions; Response Time: by next Business Day.

12.4 **Planned Maintenance.**  
Vendor may perform Planned Maintenance that may impact system availability, provided that:

  a. Vendor provides at least five (5) Business Days’ advance notice to Client (except in emergency situations);  
  b. Planned Maintenance is scheduled, where reasonably practicable, outside of Client’s normal business hours or at mutually agreed windows; and  
  c. Planned Maintenance lasting more than two (2) hours shall be coordinated with Client’s designated representative.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the availability target in **Section 12.1** in a given calendar month, Client may be entitled to Service Credits as follows (to be applied against the next invoice):

  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: Service Credit equal to 3% of the monthly maintenance fee;  
  - Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: Service Credit equal to 5% of the monthly maintenance fee;  
  - Availability below 98.0%: Service Credit equal to 10% of the monthly maintenance fee.

Service Credits shall not exceed 20% of the total monthly fees for the relevant month. Service Credits are Client’s sole monetary remedy for service level failures, without prejudice to Client’s right to terminate under **Section 4.5**.

12.6 **Reporting.**  
Vendor shall provide monthly or quarterly SLA reports (as agreed) summarizing uptime, Incidents, response and resolution times, and any Service Credits due. Client shall notify Vendor of any disputed SLA metrics within fifteen (15) Business Days of receiving such report.
