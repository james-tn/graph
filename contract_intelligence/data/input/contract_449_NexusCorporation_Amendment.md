## PREAMBLE & RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Parties entered into the **Software License Agreement** reference number **SLA-NEX-202406-435**, dated June 16, 2024 (also identified as **contract_435**), pursuant to which Nexus agreed to provide, and Contoso agreed to license and use, certain proprietary software solutions, related services, and Deliverables, all as further described in Article II (“Scope of Services / Work”) and Article VI (“Intellectual Property Rights”) of the Parent Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, the Parent Agreement currently has an Effective Date of June 16, 2024 (Section 1.9), an Expiration Date of November 26, 2026 (Section 1.19 and Section 4.1.1), a Contract Value of **US$1,479,219** (Recitals and Section 1.5), and **Net 30** payment terms (Recitals and Section 3.2.2);

**WHEREAS**, the Parties now desire to (a) extend and expand the scope of the Services and Software licensed under Article II and Article VI of the Parent Agreement, (b) increase the overall contract value, (c) modify certain payment terms and service levels, and (d) extend the term of their relationship, all on a **low-risk**, client-friendly basis consistent with the Parent Agreement’s risk allocation reflected in Article IV (Term and Termination), Article VIII (Indemnification), and Article IX (Limitation of Liability);

**WHEREAS**, the Parties intend that this Amendment shall (i) **modify** the Software License Agreement dated June 16, 2024 (Ref: **SLA-NEX-202406-435**, internal identifier **contract_435**), (ii) be **entered into pursuant to** and in accordance with Section 16.2 (“Amendments”) of the Parent Agreement, and (iii) be fully subject to the governing law and dispute resolution provisions of Article XIV and Section 16.9 of the Parent Agreement (Delaware law and jurisdiction);

**WHEREAS**, the Parties acknowledge and agree that, following this Amendment, the new aggregate **Contract Value** for the extended term of the relationship, including the incremental fees introduced by this Amendment, shall be **Four Million Eight Hundred Seventy-Nine Thousand One Hundred Eighty-One U.S. Dollars (US$4,879,181)**, subject to adjustment in accordance with Article III of this Amendment and Article III of the Parent Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties further desire to align the payment terms for the expanded Services with **Net 45** days from invoice date, in place of the Net 30 terms set forth in Section 3.2.2 of the Parent Agreement, as more fully described in Article III of this Amendment;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties wish to extend certain licenses and Service obligations beyond the original Expiration Date of November 26, 2026, and agree that, solely for the purposes of the expanded scope under this Amendment, the applicable Expiration Date for the additional Services and extended licenses shall be **January 31, 2029**, as set forth in Article IV of this Amendment;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties desire to confirm that all capitalized terms used but not defined herein shall have the meanings assigned to them in Article I of the Parent Agreement, and that, except as expressly modified by this Amendment, the Parent Agreement (SLA-NEX-202406-435 / contract_435) remains in full force and effect;

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Relationship to Parent Agreement

2.1.1 This Article II supplements and expands upon **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly stated herein, all obligations, performance standards, and Client responsibilities set forth in Article II of the Parent Agreement shall continue in full force and effect and shall apply equally to the Amended Services.

2.1.2 Pursuant to Section 2.1.1 and Section 2.1.2 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall continue to provide Client with access to and use of the Software on a hosted, SaaS basis, and shall now also provide additional modules, extended environments, and related professional services described in this Amendment, including implementation for new business units, geographies, and functional areas of Client.

### 2.2 Expansion of Software and Additional Modules

2.2.1 The Software defined in Section 1.17 and described in Section 2.2 of the Parent Agreement is hereby expanded to include the **Additional Modules**, which may include, by way of example:

- Additional financial operations sub-ledgers;  
- Advanced analytics and reporting capabilities;  
- Global tax and compliance modules;  
- Extended supply chain management modules; and  
- Enhanced API and integration toolkits.

2.2.2 Vendor shall ensure that all Additional Modules are provided in a version functionally equivalent to or better than the version generally made available to Vendor’s other customers for the same product tier, consistent with Section 2.2.3 of the Parent Agreement.

2.2.3 The Additional Modules shall be subject to the same License terms as set forth in Section 6.2 (“License Grant to Client”) of the Parent Agreement, as modified by Article VI of this Amendment, including restrictions on use set forth in Section 6.3 (“Use Restrictions”) of the Parent Agreement.

2.2.4 A detailed listing of the Additional Modules, including user counts, environments (production, staging, sandbox), and any usage limitations or parameters, may be set forth in a schedule or SOW issued under the Parent Agreement and this Amendment. Each such SOW will be governed by Article II and Article XIII of the Parent Agreement and Article XIII of this Amendment.

### 2.3 Transition Environment and Implementation Services

2.3.1 Vendor shall provision and maintain a **Transition Environment** to support:

(a) data migration from Client’s legacy systems to the Additional Modules;  
(b) end-to-end integration testing with Client’s systems;  
(c) user acceptance testing (UAT); and  
(d) phased cutover and “go-live” activities for new business units.

2.3.2 Implementation Services shall include, without limitation:

- requirements and discovery workshops with key stakeholders from newly onboarded regions or departments;  
- solution design artifacts, including architecture diagrams and configuration workbooks;  
- configuration of localized business rules (taxes, currencies, languages);  
- integration with external and internal systems using Vendor’s APIs or middleware, consistent with Section 2.3.1 of the Parent Agreement;  
- execution of multiple test cycles (system integration, UAT, regression); and  
- support during go-live and hypercare periods following go-live.

2.3.3 Vendor shall designate a **program manager** (in addition to the “project manager” referenced in Section 2.3.2 of the Parent Agreement) who shall:

(a) coordinate cross-SOW dependencies;  
(b) oversee the Transition Environment;  
(c) provide periodic status reporting (e.g., bi-weekly) to Client; and  
(d) serve as a senior escalation point for implementation and transition matters.

2.3.4 Client shall continue to comply with its responsibilities under Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement, including provision of timely access to personnel, systems, and data, and shall designate additional regional project managers where required for the Amended Services.

### 2.4 Additional Deliverables and Milestones

2.4.1 In addition to the Deliverables contemplated in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide the following categories of Deliverables under this Amendment:

(a) multi-region configuration documents;  
(b) integration mappings and data transformation specifications;  
(c) training content tailored to new user groups;  
(d) updated security roles and access control matrices; and  
(e) operational runbooks and playbooks for ongoing support.

2.4.2 Each SOW executed pursuant to this Amendment shall:

(a) identify specific milestones and associated Deliverables;  
(b) define clear acceptance criteria consistent with Section 2.4.3 of the Parent Agreement (15 Business Day acceptance period, acceptance or rejection with specified non-conformities, re-submission until acceptance); and  
(c) specify any milestone-based payment triggers, which shall be linked to milestone acceptance.

2.4.3 For complex, multi-wave rollouts, the Parties may agree to “release trains” or other structured deployment frameworks, with each “release” treated as a separate milestone for purposes of acceptance and invoicing.

### 2.5 Maintenance and Support Enhancements

2.5.1 Vendor shall continue to provide **Maintenance and Support Services** as defined in Section 1.13 and described in Section 2.5 of the Parent Agreement and, in addition, shall:

(a) extend support coverage to the Additional Modules;  
(b) provide extended support windows for critical go-live periods; and  
(c) escalate Priority 1 (“Critical”) issues in accordance with the Enhanced SLA defined in Article XII of this Amendment.

2.5.2 Vendor shall provide proactive monitoring and alerts for the Additional Modules in the production environment, including performance thresholds specifically tailored to Client’s increased transaction volumes and user base.

### 2.6 Performance Standards

2.6.1 Vendor reaffirms the performance standards set forth in Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement and agrees that the Amended Services shall be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skill and experience for large-scale enterprise deployments.

2.6.2 Where Vendor uses subcontractors to perform portions of the Amended Services, Vendor shall remain fully responsible for such subcontractors’ performance and compliance with this Amendment and the Parent Agreement.

2.6.3 Without limiting Client’s rights under Article VII (Representations and Warranties) and Article XII (SLA) of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, repeated failures to meet agreed milestones or SLA metrics may constitute a material breach, subject to the cure rights and termination provisions of Article IV of the Parent Agreement and Article IV of this Amendment.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value and Fee Structure

3.1.1 The Parties acknowledge that the **Contract Value** originally set forth in the Recitals and Section 1.5 of the Parent Agreement is US$1,479,219. By this Amendment, the Parties agree that the aggregate contract value for the Parent Agreement as amended is increased to **US$4,879,181**.

3.1.2 The **Incremental Contract Value** of **US$3,399,962** (US$4,879,181 minus US$1,479,219) shall cover, without limitation:

(a) license and subscription fees for the Additional Modules for the Extended Term;  
(b) implementation and configuration services under Article II of this Amendment;  
(c) additional professional services, including training and consulting; and  
(d) enhanced support and monitoring services as described in Section 2.5 of this Amendment.

3.1.3 Unless otherwise specified in a SOW, all fees shall continue to be denominated and payable in **U.S. Dollars (USD)**, consistent with Section 3.1.3 of the Parent Agreement.

### 3.2 Payment Terms; Modification of Section 3.2.2

3.2.1 **Modification of Payment Terms.** Effective as of the Amendment Effective Date, Section 3.2.2 of the Parent Agreement, which currently specifies **Net 30** terms, is hereby amended **solely with respect to invoices issued on or after the Amendment Effective Date** to read as follows:

> “Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of invoice (‘**Net 45**’), via wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed payment method.”

3.2.2 All other provisions of Section 3.2 of the Parent Agreement, including invoicing schedules and dispute procedures (notice of dispute within fifteen (15) Business Days, payment of undisputed portions), shall remain in full force and effect and apply equally to fees under this Amendment.

3.2.3 Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the schedules set forth in the applicable SOW(s) under this Amendment. In the absence of a specific schedule, Vendor may invoice:

(a) one-time implementation fees upon execution of the relevant SOW or upon completion of specified milestones; and  
(b) recurring fees for Additional Modules annually in advance.

### 3.3 Late Payment; Suspension

3.3.1 The late payment provisions of Section 3.3 of the Parent Agreement (interest at the lesser of 1% per month or the maximum legal rate, suspension after 30 days following notice) shall continue to apply to any undisputed amounts outstanding beyond the **Net 45** period.

3.3.2 Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to notify Client’s designated billing contact prior to exercising any rights of suspension and shall promptly resume Services upon receipt of all outstanding payments, consistent with Section 3.3.2 of the Parent Agreement.

### 3.4 Taxes

3.4.1 The tax provisions of Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to all fees under this Amendment. All fees are exclusive of Taxes, which shall be borne by Client, except for Taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees.

### 3.5 Expenses

3.5.1 Vendor shall continue to be reimbursed for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses in accordance with Section 3.5 of the Parent Agreement. For extended, multi-region deployments, the Parties may agree to per-diem or capped travel budgets in the applicable SOW(s).

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Extended Term; Relationship to Parent Agreement

4.1.1 The term of the Parent Agreement, as defined in Section 1.19 and Section 4.1.1 (Effective Date to November 26, 2026), remains unchanged with respect to the original scope of Services, unless otherwise amended in writing.

4.1.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Parties agree that the licenses to the Additional Modules and the obligations relating to the Amended Services under this Amendment shall continue in effect until **January 31, 2029** (the “**Extended Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or Article IV of the Parent Agreement.

4.1.3 The Parties may renew or further extend the Extended Term upon mutual written agreement executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to January 31, 2029, consistent with Section 4.1.2 of the Parent Agreement.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement (Termination for Convenience) shall apply to this Amendment. For clarity:

(a) Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice; and  
(b) Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon sixty (60) days’ prior written notice, subject to its cooperation in effecting an orderly wind-down and data transition as described in Section 4.5 of the Parent Agreement and Section 4.5 of this Amendment.

4.2.2 The Parties may agree in a SOW that certain non-recurring implementation services under this Amendment shall be non-cancellable once commenced, except as otherwise provided in Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause).

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 The rights of termination for cause set forth in Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement (material breach, insolvency, data protection or confidentiality breach) shall apply equally to this Amendment and the Amended Services.

4.3.2 In addition, repeated material failures by Vendor to meet the Enhanced SLA (as defined in Article XII) over three (3) consecutive months, or five (5) months in any rolling twelve (12) month period, following written notice and a thirty (30) day cure period, may be deemed a material breach, entitling Client to terminate the affected portion of the Amended Services.

### 4.4 Termination Upon Termination of Parent Agreement

4.4.1 This Amendment is an integral part of the Parent Agreement. Accordingly, if the Parent Agreement is terminated in its entirety pursuant to Article IV thereof, this Amendment shall automatically terminate as of the effective date of such termination, except as otherwise expressly provided in this Article IV and Article XVI with respect to surviving obligations.

4.4.2 If the Parent Agreement is terminated only with respect to specific SOWs or modules, the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to determine whether and to what extent the Additional Modules and Amended Services under this Amendment shall continue, be modified, or be terminated.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment for any reason:

(a) Client’s Licenses to the Additional Modules and any supplemental components granted under this Amendment shall immediately terminate, subject to any agreed-upon transition period (not to exceed sixty (60) days, unless mutually extended);  
(b) Vendor shall provide transition assistance and data export services in accordance with Section 4.5.2 of the Parent Agreement, subject to reasonable fees unless termination results from Vendor’s uncured material breach; and  
(c) Client shall pay Vendor all fees accrued and unpaid as of the effective date of termination, including any earned but unpaid milestone-based implementation fees and properly documented expenses.

4.5.2 The survival provisions set forth in Section 4.5.3 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Amendment, including the survival of provisions relating to fees and payment, confidentiality, IP, indemnification, limitation of liability, and data protection.

## ARTICLE IX  
## LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 9.1 Modified Liability Cap

9.1.1 Section 9.1.1 of the Parent Agreement limits each Party’s aggregate liability (subject to stated exceptions) to two (2) times the total fees paid or payable by Client during the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the Claim.

9.1.3 The exceptions to the limitation of liability in Section 9.1.1 (indemnification obligations, confidentiality breaches, and Client’s breach of license restrictions) remain unchanged and shall apply to the cap specified in Section 9.1.2 of this Amendment.

### 9.2 Exclusion of Consequential Damages

9.2.1 The exclusion of consequential, indirect, and special damages set forth in Section 9.2.1 of the Parent Agreement remains in full force and effect and applies equally to this Amendment, subject to the same exceptions (indemnification, confidentiality, and Client’s license restriction breach).

9.3.1 The Parties acknowledge that the increased limitation of liability (3x fees over 12 months), combined with the overall Contract Value and the low-risk designation of this engagement, represents a fair and reasonable allocation of risk and forms an essential basis of the bargain between the Parties.
