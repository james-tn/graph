## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services for the Company in accordance with this Agreement, the applicable Specifications, and generally accepted professional standards. The Services shall include, without limitation, the following key components:

  (a) **Technology Integration and Implementation.** Design, configuration, and implementation of an integrated enterprise data and workflow platform for the Company, including integration with existing CRM, ERP, and HR systems as specified by the Company; development of data pipelines, APIs, and middleware components; configuration of dashboards, analytics reports, and workflow automation rules; user acceptance testing support; and deployment to production environments.

  (b) **Managed Services and Support.** Provision of ongoing operational support, incident management, monitoring, and maintenance services for the integrated platform; provision of a staffed help desk with agreed response and resolution times as described in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**; and performance of routine system health checks, patching, and optimization.

  (c) **Data Migration and Validation.** Planning and execution of one-time and phased data migrations from legacy systems to the new integrated platform; data cleansing, validation, and reconciliation; and provision of migration reports documenting completeness, exceptions, and remediation steps.

  (d) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Development and delivery of training materials, including user guides, reference cards, and recorded training sessions; performance of train-the-trainer sessions for designated Company personnel; and documentation of operational runbooks and standard operating procedures for ongoing support.

  (e) **Project Management and Governance.** Provision of project management resources to plan, track, and report on the Project; maintenance of a project plan with milestones and dependencies; coordination of regular status meetings, risk/issue tracking, and change management activities; and preparation of governance reports for executive stakeholders.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
The Service Provider shall produce and deliver, at a minimum, the following Deliverables to the Company, subject to further detail and refinements agreed in writing:

  (a) **Initial Project Plan and Requirements Specification.** A detailed project plan, including tasks, dependencies, resource assignments, and a timeline extending from the Effective Date through the projected completion of the implementation phase; a written requirements specification capturing business, functional, and technical requirements validated by the Company.

  (b) **System Design Documentation.** Architecture diagrams, data flow diagrams, integration specifications, and configuration design documents describing how the Service Provider will implement the integrated platform in accordance with the Company’s requirements.

  (c) **Configured Platform and Integration Components.** Configured instances of the integrated platform in development, test, and production environments; developed or configured integration components (e.g., APIs, middleware adapters) connecting the Company’s systems; initially deployed workflows and automation rules.

  (d) **Data Migration Packages and Reports.** Scripts, mappings, and procedures for data migration; documented data quality checks; and reports that summarize data migrated, validation results, and any issues or anomalies requiring remediation.

  (e) **User and Administrative Documentation.** End-user guides, administrator manuals, configuration documentation, runbooks, and escalation procedures, all in reasonably detailed written and/or electronic form, suitable for use by the Company’s personnel.

  (f) **Training Materials.** Training presentations, course outlines, sample exercises, and recorded training sessions, as mutually agreed, that provide the Company’s operational and technical teams with the knowledge necessary to operate and maintain the integrated platform.

  (g) **Periodic Performance and SLA Reports.** Monthly or quarterly reports (as agreed) summarizing Service Levels, incidents, response/resolution times, uptime metrics, and recommendations for improvements.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a diligent, prompt, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry best practices and the care, skill, and diligence that would reasonably be expected of a provider of similar services operating under similar circumstances;

  (b) in conformity with the Specifications, timelines, and Milestones set forth in this Agreement, including those in any attached project plan or schedule, as may be updated via Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)**;

  (c) in compliance with all Applicable Laws, including without limitation data protection and privacy laws described in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**;

  (d) using personnel who are appropriately skilled, trained, and experienced in the tasks assigned to them, and who shall devote sufficient time and attention to the Project to meet the agreed timelines; and

  (e) in coordination with the Company’s designated project manager and other personnel, including participation in scheduled status meetings, governance reviews, and risk/issue resolution discussions.

2.4 **Timeline and Milestones.**  
Subject to reasonable adjustments pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)**, the Parties anticipate the following high-level timeline and Milestones:

  (a) **Phase 1 – Initiation and Planning (Effective Date + 60 days).**  
  - Completion of detailed requirements workshop and documentation;  
  - Delivery and Company review of the initial project plan and requirements specification;  
  - Formal sign-off of requirements and baseline project plan.

  (b) **Phase 2 – Design and Configuration (following 60–180 days).**  
  - Delivery of system design documentation;  
  - Configuration of core platform modules;  
  - Initial build of integration components and test environment.

  (c) **Phase 3 – Data Migration and Testing (approximately 6–12 months from Effective Date).**  
  - Completion of test cycles (unit, integration, user acceptance testing);  
  - Execution of initial data migration and validation;  
  - Resolution of critical and high-severity defects.

  (d) **Phase 4 – Go-Live and Stabilization (target by month 12–18).**  
  - Production deployment of the integrated platform;  
  - Final data migration and cutover;  
  - Stabilization period (e.g., 60–90 days post go-live) with enhanced support.

  (e) **Phase 5 – Ongoing Managed Services (from go-live through Expiration Date).**  
  - Continued provision of support, monitoring, and maintenance;  
  - Periodic improvements and optimizations;  
  - Regular Service Level reporting.

Timelines are estimates and shall be refined in the project plan. Any material deviation that affects scope, cost, or schedule shall be addressed through the Change Management provisions in **Article XIII**.

2.5 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall, at no cost to the Service Provider (except as otherwise expressly provided):

  (a) provide timely access to appropriate Company personnel, systems, and facilities as reasonably required for the Service Provider to perform the Services;

  (b) provide timely and accurate information, decisions, and approvals reasonably required for the Service Provider to meet its obligations and timelines;

  (c) ensure the availability of subject matter experts who can articulate business requirements and validate Deliverables;

  (d) comply with any mutually agreed prerequisites, including system prerequisites and third-party licenses, necessary to enable the Services; and

  (e) promptly notify the Service Provider of any issues or concerns regarding the Services or Deliverables, and cooperate in good faith to resolve such issues.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Except as otherwise agreed in writing:

  (a) The Service Provider shall deliver each Deliverable to the Company along with any applicable acceptance criteria.  

  (b) The Company shall have a reasonable period, not to exceed thirty (30) days (the **“Acceptance Period”**), to review and either accept or reject the Deliverable in writing.  

  (c) If the Company reasonably determines that the Deliverable does not conform to the applicable Specifications, it shall provide the Service Provider with written notice identifying the non-conformities in reasonable detail, and the Service Provider shall, at no additional cost, promptly correct such non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable.  

  (d) The Company shall then have an additional Acceptance Period to re-evaluate the corrected Deliverable.  

  (e) Any Deliverable shall be deemed accepted if the Company fails to provide written notice of acceptance or rejection within the applicable Acceptance Period, provided that such deemed acceptance shall not limit the warranties in **Article VII (Representations and Warranties)**.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
In consideration for the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Agreement, the Company shall pay to the Service Provider a total amount not to exceed **US$4,928,477** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustments for approved Change Orders and any other amounts payable pursuant to this Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Contract Value shall be allocated as follows (amounts approximate and subject to refinement in a mutually agreed fee schedule):

  (a) **Implementation and Integration Services:** US$2,200,000  
  (b) **Data Migration and Validation:** US$650,000  
  (c) **Training and Knowledge Transfer:** US$278,477  
  (d) **Managed Services and Support (through Expiration Date):** US$1,600,000  
  (e) **Contingency/Change Budget (to be drawn only upon written approval):** US$200,000

The Parties may attach a more detailed fee schedule as **Schedule A (Fee Schedule)**, which shall form an integral part of this Agreement.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in Schedule A, the following Milestone-based payment structure shall apply:

  (a) **Milestone 1 – Project Initiation:** 10% of the Contract Value invoiced upon Effective Date and commencement of Services.  

  (b) **Milestone 2 – Requirements and Design Completion:** 15% of the Contract Value invoiced upon Company’s acceptance of the requirements specification and system design documentation.  

  (c) **Milestone 3 – Configuration and Integration Completion:** 25% of the Contract Value invoiced upon completion of core configuration and integration components and Company’s acceptance of such Deliverables.  

  (d) **Milestone 4 – Successful Data Migration and Testing Completion:** 20% of the Contract Value invoiced upon completion of user acceptance testing and initial data migration, subject to Company’s acceptance.  

  (e) **Milestone 5 – Go-Live:** 10% of the Contract Value invoiced upon successful production go-live of the integrated platform.  

  (f) **Milestone 6 – Managed Services:** The remaining 20% of the Contract Value attributable to managed services shall be invoiced on a periodic basis (e.g., monthly or quarterly in advance or in arrears as specified in Schedule A) over the Term.

3.4 **Invoicing.**  
The Service Provider shall issue invoices to the Company upon achievement of the applicable Milestones and in accordance with any periodic invoicing schedule described in Schedule A. Each invoice shall:

  (a) reference this Agreement and the contract reference number **WO-NEX-202506-401**;  

  (b) describe in reasonable detail the Services and Deliverables to which the invoiced amounts relate;  

  (c) specify the applicable Milestone or period of managed services; and  

  (d) include any applicable supporting documentation for reimbursable expenses pursuant to **Section 3.7 (Expenses)**.

3.5 **Payment Terms.**  
Except as otherwise expressly provided in this Agreement, all undisputed amounts due and owing under this Agreement shall be paid by the Company within **sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt of a valid and undisputed invoice (**“Net 60”**). Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by electronic funds transfer or other mutually agreed method.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Company in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice, the Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute in reasonable detail. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. The Company shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with **Section 3.5**, and the payment of the disputed portion may be withheld pending resolution without constituting a breach. Any adjustments resulting from the resolution shall be reflected in subsequent invoices.

3.7 **Expenses.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred by the Service Provider in the performance of the Services, including but not limited to travel and lodging, shall be reimbursed by the Company at cost, provided that:

  (a) such expenses are pre-approved in writing by the Company’s project manager;  

  (b) expense reimbursements are documented with appropriate receipts; and  

  (c) travel expenses shall conform to the Company’s travel policies as communicated in writing to the Service Provider.

3.8 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the date due until the date paid. The imposition of interest shall not limit the Service Provider’s other rights or remedies under this Agreement; provided, however, that because this is a **Low Risk** and client-friendly arrangement, the Service Provider shall provide written notice of non-payment and a fifteen (15) Business Day cure period before suspending any Services for non-payment.

3.9 **Taxes.**  
The fees stated in this Agreement are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes (excluding taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income or payroll), which shall be the responsibility of the Company. The Service Provider shall separately itemize any such taxes on its invoices. If the Company is legally required to withhold any taxes from payments, it shall provide the Service Provider with appropriate documentation evidencing such withholding and shall use reasonable efforts to assist the Service Provider in obtaining any applicable tax credits or exemptions.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Initial Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **April 15, 2030** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew the Term for one or more additional periods under terms and conditions to be negotiated at that time. Any such renewal or extension shall be documented in a written amendment executed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for its convenience and without cause upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider. The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Company and only after the first twelve (12) months of the Term, so as to minimize disruption to the Company.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment) after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail. Material breaches include, but are not limited to:

  (a) the Service Provider’s failure to perform the Services in accordance with Article II, resulting in a material adverse impact on the Project;  

  (b) the Company’s failure to pay undisputed amounts when due and, after notice, within the specified cure period; or  

  (c) a material breach of confidentiality or data protection obligations set forth in **Article V** or **Article XI**.

4.5 **Termination for Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (d) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for (i) all Services duly performed and accepted and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, and (ii) any authorized, non-cancellable commitments actually incurred in reliance on this Agreement, provided the Service Provider uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such costs.  

  (b) The Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the Services (except for those reasonably necessary to effect an orderly transition) and shall cooperate with the Company in the transition of Services, including the transfer of relevant documentation, configurations, and Client Data as reasonably requested.  

  (c) Each Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the other Party’s option) the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4 (Return or Destruction)**.  

  (d) The Service Provider shall, at the Company’s written request and subject to mutually agreed fees, provide reasonable termination and transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days after the effective date of termination.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure) and XVI (General Provisions)**, as well as any payment obligations accrued prior to termination and any other provisions that by their nature should reasonably survive.

The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and survive for a period of five (5) years following the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall be protected for so long as they remain trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
For any hosted or managed components of the integrated platform operated by the Service Provider, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve a monthly uptime percentage of at least **99.9%**, excluding scheduled maintenance and any downtime caused by Force Majeure Events, Company systems, or third-party failures outside the Service Provider’s reasonable control.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Service Provider may perform scheduled maintenance during pre-agreed maintenance windows, provided that:

  (a) the Service Provider provides the Company with at least three (3) Business Days’ prior notice of any planned maintenance that is reasonably expected to result in service unavailability; and  

  (b) scheduled maintenance shall, where feasible, be performed during off-peak hours to minimize business disruption.

12.3 **Support Hours and Incident Classification.**  
The Service Provider shall provide support services during normal business hours (e.g., 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Pacific Time, Monday through Friday, excluding major holidays), and on a limited on-call basis outside such hours for critical issues. Incidents shall be classified as follows:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or significant business impact (e.g., system unavailable to a majority of users).  

  (b) **Severity 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired with moderate business impact; workaround may be available.  

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial, non-critical loss of functionality; minor business impact.  

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low):** Cosmetic, informational, or enhancement requests.

12.4 **Response and Resolution Targets.**  
The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the following initial response and resolution targets:

  (a) **Severity 1:**  
  - Initial response: within one (1) hour  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within eight (8) hours  

  (b) **Severity 2:**  
  - Initial response: within four (4) hours  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within two (2) Business Days  

  (c) **Severity 3:**  
  - Initial response: within one (1) Business Day  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within five (5) Business Days  

  (d) **Severity 4:**  
  - Initial response: within two (2) Business Days  
  - Resolution: to be addressed in a future release or maintenance cycle, as mutually agreed.

12.5 **SLA Remedies.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** in any calendar month, the Company shall be entitled to service credits, as follows:

  (a) Uptime < 99.9% but ≥ 99.0%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly managed services fee;  
  (b) Uptime < 99.0% but ≥ 98.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly managed services fee;  
  (c) Uptime < 98.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly managed services fee.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices. Service credits are the Company’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, but shall not preclude the Company from exercising termination rights for repeated material SLA breaches.
