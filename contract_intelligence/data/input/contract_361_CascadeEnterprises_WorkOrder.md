## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview and Relationship to Parent Agreement**

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide the Additional Services and Additional Deliverables described in this Article II in support of the Programme and the CRM solution implemented under the Parent Agreement. The Parties acknowledge and agree that:

(a) The Additional Services **add scope** to and **do not replace** the Services specified in **Article II of the Parent Agreement**;  
(b) All general obligations, performance standards, and Client responsibilities described in **Sections 2.4 (Client Responsibilities)** and **2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement apply equally to the Additional Services; and  
(c) The acceptance mechanism in **Section 2.6 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement shall govern acceptance of Additional Deliverables, except as expressly modified in this Work Order.

2.1.2 The Additional Services comprise four primary workstreams:

(a) **Workstream A – Multi-Region CRM Rollout**;  
(b) **Workstream B – Advanced Analytics and Reporting Implementation**;  
(c) **Workstream C – Optimisation and Continuous Improvement**; and  
(d) **Workstream D – Managed Support Plus (Extended Managed Support)**.

2.2 **Workstream A – Multi-Region CRM Rollout**

2.2.1 Vendor shall design, plan, and execute the rollout of the CRM solution (as configured and deployed under **Phases 1–4 of the Parent Agreement**) to the Rollout Regions. This shall include, at a minimum:

(a) **Regional Discovery and Fit/Gap** – Conduct region-specific workshops to identify localisation requirements, regulatory considerations, and process variations, building upon the “Requirements Specification Document” and “Current-State Process Maps” delivered under **Phase 1 (Section 2.2(a))** of the Parent Agreement;  
(b) **Regional Solution Design** – Prepare addenda to the “Solution Design Document” and “Integration Design” described in **Phase 2 (Section 2.2(b))** of the Parent Agreement, specifying region-specific configurations, integrations, and data migration variations;  
(c) **Regional Configuration and Build** – Configure the CRM platform for each Rollout Region using the global template established under **Phase 3 (Section 2.2(c))** of the Parent Agreement, with appropriate localisation (languages, currencies, regulatory fields, workflows);  
(d) **Regional Testing and Deployment** – Plan and execute region-specific system, integration and user acceptance testing, and coordinate deployment activities with Client’s regional IT and business teams, aligned to the approach in **Phase 4 (Section 2.2(d))** of the Parent Agreement; and  
(e) **Regional Enablement** – Deliver focused training and knowledge transfer for regional stakeholders consistent with **Phase 5 (Section 2.2(e))** of the Parent Agreement.

2.2.2 **Key Deliverables – Workstream A** (each an Additional Deliverable):

(a) Regional Fit/Gap Analysis Reports (per Rollout Region);  
(b) Regional Solution Design Addenda (UK, EU, North America at minimum);  
(c) Regional Data Migration Specifications and Test Plans;  
(d) Configured Regional CRM Environments (including UAT and Production);  
(e) Regional Deployment Runbooks and Go-Live Checklists;  
(f) Regional Training Materials and Quick Reference Guides.

2.2.3 **High-Level Timeline – Workstream A**  
Targeted durations per region (subject to adjustment via Change Order and consolidated project plan):

- UK rollout: Weeks 1–16 from Effective Date of this Work Order;  
- EU rollout: Weeks 10–28;  
- North America rollout: Weeks 22–40;  
- Additional regions (if any): as defined in applicable Change Orders.

2.3 **Workstream B – Advanced Analytics and Reporting Implementation**

2.3.1 Vendor shall design and implement the Analytics Solution to provide enhanced visibility into sales, marketing and customer service performance across the Programme. Services shall include:

(a) **Analytics Requirements & Data Assessment** – Building on the “High-Level Data Inventory” and “Test Strategy and High-Level Test Plan” developed under **Phases 1 and 2** of the Parent Agreement, conduct workshops to define KPIs, dashboards, data sources and data quality requirements;  
(b) **Analytics Architecture & Data Model Design** – Define an analytics architecture and data model aligned with the “Solution Design Document” and integration strategy in **Phase 2 (Section 2.2(b))** of the Parent Agreement, including star schemas, dimensional models and data refresh mechanisms;  
(c) **ETL/ELT and Integration Build** – Configure and/or develop data pipelines between the CRM platform, ERP, marketing automation and support ticketing systems identified under **Section 2.2(a)(iv)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(d) **Dashboard and Report Development** – Develop a suite of standard dashboards and reports for sales, marketing, service and executive stakeholders, plus a governed self-service analytics environment;  
(e) **Analytics Testing & Validation** – Define and execute test cases to validate data accuracy, completeness, performance and usability, aligned with acceptance criteria and test processes under **Section 2.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement; and  
(f) **Analytics Training & Handover** – Train Client’s analytics and business users and provide documentation consistent with the “User Training Materials” and “Administrator Handbook” referenced in **Phase 5 (Section 2.2(e))** of the Parent Agreement.

2.3.2 **Key Deliverables – Workstream B**

(a) Analytics Requirements and KPI Catalogue;  
(b) Analytics Solution Architecture and Data Model Design Document;  
(c) Configured ETL/ELT Jobs and Integration Mappings;  
(d) Standard Dashboard Suite (minimum of 20 core dashboards);  
(e) Data Quality and Validation Report;  
(f) Analytics Governance and Usage Guidelines;  
(g) Analytics Training Materials and Knowledge Transfer Session Records.

2.4 **Workstream C – Optimisation and Continuous Improvement**

2.4.1 Following completion of core rollouts and analytics implementation, Vendor shall provide Optimisation Services to enhance adoption, performance and value realisation. These services will be delivered on a time-and-materials basis within defined monthly and annual caps as set out in **Article III**.

2.4.2 Optimisation activities may include, but are not limited to:

(a) Fine-tuning of workflows, automation rules, dashboards and security roles originally implemented under **Phase 3 (Configuration and Build)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(b) Enhancements to reports, data visualisations and analytics models delivered under this Work Order;  
(c) Configuration of additional CRM features (e.g., advanced lead scoring, territory management, customer segmentation) within the boundaries of the existing platform;  
(d) Performance optimisation, including query tuning and configuration adjustments;  
(e) Process optimisation recommendations and documentation; and  
(f) Light integration enhancements that do not materially alter the architecture defined under **Phase 2** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4.3 **Key Deliverables – Workstream C**

(a) Quarterly Optimisation Roadmaps;  
(b) Optimisation Change Logs and Configuration Documentation;  
(c) Semi-Annual CRM Adoption and Value Realisation Reports;  
(d) Updated Process and Training Materials, as applicable.

2.5 **Workstream D – Managed Support Plus (Extended Managed Support)**

2.5.1 This Workstream extends and enhances the Managed Support defined under **Phase 6 – Managed Support (Section 2.2(f))** and the Service Levels set out in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement.

2.5.2 Under Managed Support Plus, Vendor shall:

(a) Provide second-line and third-line support across all Rollout Regions during Business Hours, with increased availability as detailed in **Article XII** of this Work Order;  
(b) Increase the allocation of monthly support hours beyond the estimate of 75 hours per month referenced in **Section 3.2.2(c)** of the Parent Agreement, as detailed in **Section 3.2.3** of this Work Order;  
(c) Provide extended coverage windows for Priority 1 incidents, potentially including optional after-hours coverage if agreed in writing;  
(d) Maintain an enhanced knowledge base, configuration repository, and support runbooks covering regional configurations and the Analytics Solution; and  
(e) Provide periodic service reviews aligned with and in addition to the “Monthly Support and Incident Reports” and “Quarterly Service Review Reports” listed in **Section 2.2(f)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.5.3 **Key Deliverables – Workstream D**

(a) Enhanced Support Operating Model Document;  
(b) Regionalised Support and Escalation Runbooks;  
(c) Monthly Support and Incident Reports (expanded format);  
(d) Quarterly Service Review Packs including SLA performance, optimisation recommendations and roadmap alignment.

2.6 **Performance Standards**

2.6.1 Vendor shall perform the Additional Services:

(a) With the same due skill, care and diligence described in **Section 2.5.1(a)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(b) In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards for similar services, as required by **Section 2.5.1(b)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(c) In accordance with the Specifications, including this Work Order, the Parent Agreement, and any approved Change Orders, consistent with **Section 2.5.1(c)** of the Parent Agreement; and  
(d) In compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including Data Protection Laws defined in **Section 1.6** and described in **Article XI** of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities**

2.7.1 In addition to Client’s responsibilities defined in **Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement**, Client shall:

(a) Designate regional business owners and subject matter experts for each Rollout Region;  
(b) Ensure timely completion of region-specific data cleansing and mapping activities;  
(c) Provide access to analytics-related data sources and systems;  
(d) Assign an executive sponsor and steering committee member to oversee multi-region rollout and analytics strategy; and  
(e) Perform user acceptance testing for regional deployments and analytics dashboards in accordance with agreed test plans.

2.8 **Acceptance of Additional Deliverables**

2.8.1 Acceptance of Additional Deliverables shall follow the process set out in **Section 2.6 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement. For clarity:

(a) The Review Period shall remain ten (10) Business Days unless otherwise agreed in writing;  
(b) Deliverables not rejected within the Review Period shall be deemed accepted; and  
(c) Vendor shall be entitled to correct and resubmit non-conforming Additional Deliverables in accordance with Section 2.6.3 of the Parent Agreement.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

- Data Integration & Modelling: USD $230,000  
- Dashboard & Reporting Suite: USD $180,000  
- Analytics Training & Handover: USD $70,000  

3.2.3 **Workstream C – Optimisation Services (Time-and-Materials, Capped)**

(a) Rates (aligned with and extending the T&M model in **Section 3.2.2(a)** of the Parent Agreement):

(d) Cap: The aggregate fees for Managed Support Plus over the Term shall not exceed **USD $350,000**, unless modified via Change Order.

3.3.1 Fixed-fee components for Workstreams A and B shall be invoiced as follows (mirroring, with appropriate adjustments, the milestone structure in **Section 3.3.1 of the Parent Agreement**):

- 20% of the EU Rollout fee upon commencement of EU regional configuration;

3.4 **Payment Terms**

3.4.1 All undisputed invoices under this Work Order shall be due and payable within **forty-five (45) calendar days** from the invoice date (**“Net 45”**). This constitutes a specific payment term for this Work Order and shall supersede **Section 3.4.1 (Net 30)** of the Parent Agreement solely with respect to invoices issued under this Work Order.

3.4.2 If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within ten (10) Business Days of receipt, specifying the amounts disputed and the reasons therefor, consistent with **Section 3.4.2 of the Parent Agreement**. Client shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.4.1.

3.5 **Late Payment**

3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of **1.5% per month** (or, if lower, the maximum rate permissible under applicable law), calculated on a daily basis from the due date until the date of actual payment. This provision is more Client‑favourable than the 2% per month interest rate set out in **Section 3.5.1 of the Parent Agreement** and applies solely to invoices under this Work Order.

3.5.2 Vendor may, upon no less than ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Additional Services if any undisputed invoice under this Work Order remains unpaid for more than forty-five (45) calendar days after the due date, provided that Vendor resumes performance promptly upon receipt of payment, consistent with the approach in **Section 3.5.2 of the Parent Agreement**.

3.6 **Taxes**

3.6.1 All fees under this Work Order are exclusive of VAT and any other applicable sales, use or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for such taxes, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, payroll, or property, consistent with **Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement**.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement**

3.7.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Additional Services, in line with **Section 3.7 of the Parent Agreement** and Client’s travel policy.

3.7.2 Vendor shall provide documentation for reimbursable expenses and invoice such expenses monthly in arrears.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term**

4.1.1 This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until **20 June 2030** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal / Extension**

4.2.1 The Parties may, by mutual written agreement no later than ninety (90) calendar days prior to the Expiration Date, extend all or part of this Work Order on terms to be agreed. Any such extension shall be documented by a written amendment or new work order referencing **WO-CAS-202508-361** and the Parent Agreement **SOW-CAS-202508-249 (contract_249)**, consistent with **Section 4.2.1 of the Parent Agreement**.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience**

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) calendar days’** prior written notice to the other Party. This shorter notice period is Client‑favourable and applies solely to this Work Order; it does not modify **Section 4.3.1 of the Parent Agreement** for other work.

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor:

(a) All fees and approved expenses incurred and properly invoiced under this Work Order up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) A pro rata portion of any fixed-fee components for work performed but not yet invoiced based on the percentage of completion; and  
(c) Any non-cancellable third-party costs that Vendor has reasonably incurred on Client’s behalf under this Work Order.

4.4 **Termination for Cause**

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) Materially breaches any provision of this Work Order or the Parent Agreement as it relates to this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days after receiving written notice thereof; or  
(b) Becomes insolvent or subject to insolvency-related events as described in **Section 4.4.1(b) of the Parent Agreement**.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement**

4.5.1 The Parties acknowledge that this Work Order is dependent upon the Parent Agreement. Accordingly:

(a) If the Parent Agreement is lawfully terminated pursuant to **Article IV of the Parent Agreement**, this Work Order shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue some or all of the Additional Services under modified terms;  
(b) Termination of this Work Order shall not in itself terminate the Parent Agreement, which shall continue in accordance with its terms unless separately terminated; and  
(c) Upon termination of the Parent Agreement, the rights and obligations relating to confidentiality, IP, data protection, limitation of liability and dispute resolution shall continue to apply to this Work Order as described in **Section 16.9 (Survival)** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 16.9** of this Work Order.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of this Work Order**

4.6.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Work Order:

(a) Vendor shall cease performing the Additional Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary to effect an orderly wind-down;  
(b) Vendor shall deliver to Client all completed Additional Deliverables and any partially completed work for which Client has paid;  
(c) Client shall pay Vendor for all Additional Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, in accordance with Section 4.3.2 or 4.4, as applicable; and  
(d) Each Party shall, upon request, return or securely destroy Confidential Information relating specifically to this Work Order, subject to any retention obligations under applicable law and **Article XI of the Parent Agreement**.

4.7 **Transition Assistance**

4.7.1 Upon Client’s request made prior to the effective date of termination or expiration of this Work Order, Vendor shall provide reasonable Transition Assistance for up to sixty (60) calendar days at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates (subject to the caps in **Sections 3.2.3 and 3.2.4**, unless otherwise agreed), consistent with **Section 4.6.1 of the Parent Agreement**.
