## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Vendor.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Client hereby engages Vendor, and Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to perform the Services and provide the Deliverables described herein and in any SOW executed by the Parties.

2.2 **General Description of Services.** Vendor shall provide strategic consulting and advisory services to support Client’s digital transformation and operating model optimization efforts. The Services shall include, but not be limited to, the following categories of work:

  2.2.1 **Strategic Assessment and Planning.** Vendor shall conduct a comprehensive assessment of Client’s current business processes, technology landscape, and organizational structure. This shall include interviews with key Client stakeholders; review of existing documentation, systems, and tools; and analysis of operational performance. Based on such assessment, Vendor shall prepare a strategic roadmap that identifies priority initiatives, business case justifications, risks, dependencies, and estimated timelines.

  2.2.2 **Operating Model and Process Design.** Vendor shall collaborate with Client to design target operating models, including process flows, governance structures, and roles and responsibilities. Vendor shall prepare detailed process documentation covering core business functions identified by Client (such as sales operations, customer support, finance operations, and technology delivery). Deliverables may include process maps, RACI matrices, and standard operating procedures.

  2.2.3 **Technology Advisory and Architecture.** Vendor shall provide guidance on the selection, integration, and optimization of technology platforms, including enterprise resource planning (ERP), customer relationship management (CRM), data analytics, and cloud infrastructure. Vendor will evaluate current systems, recommend enhancements or replacements, outline integration approaches, and prepare high-level solution architectures in alignment with Client’s strategic objectives.

  2.2.4 **Program Management and Change Enablement.** Vendor shall support Client in planning and managing specific transformation initiatives. This may include creating program charters, detailed project plans, risk and issue logs, communication plans, and change management strategies. Vendor may also provide training sessions, workshops, or presentations to Client’s employees to facilitate adoption of new processes and tools.

  2.2.5 **Performance Measurement and Optimization.** Vendor shall define key performance indicators (KPIs) and measurement frameworks to assess the success of Client’s transformation initiatives. Vendor shall assist in setting up dashboards or reporting mechanisms and provide periodic performance reviews, recommendations, and optimization proposals.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Without limitation of any specific SOW, Vendor shall prepare and deliver, in a professional and workmanlike manner, the following types of Deliverables:

  2.3.1 **Initial Assessment Report.** A written report summarizing the findings of the initial strategic assessment, including identified pain points, improvement opportunities, and high-level recommendations.

  2.3.2 **Strategic Roadmap.** A multi-phase roadmap detailing proposed initiatives over the Term of the Agreement, including sequencing, estimated durations, resource assumptions, and indicative budget ranges.

  2.3.3 **Process Documentation.** Documentation of key business processes as-is and to-be, including process flow diagrams and supporting narratives.

  2.3.4 **Technology Recommendations.** Recommendations regarding technology platforms and architecture, including pros/cons analysis, indicative implementation considerations, and high-level integration requirements.

  2.3.5 **Program Management Artifacts.** Project plans, status reports, risk and issue logs, and steering committee packs as reasonably required by Client.

  2.3.6 **Training and Communication Materials.** Presentation decks, training materials, and communication content to support change management activities.

2.4 **Timeline and Milestones.** The Parties anticipate the following high-level timeline, subject to refinement in SOWs and Change Orders:

  (a) **Phase 1 – Discovery & Assessment (First 3 months)**  
  – Conduct stakeholder interviews, data collection, and initial analysis.  
  – Deliver: Initial Assessment Report and draft Strategic Roadmap.

  (b) **Phase 2 – Design & Planning (Months 4-9)**  
  – Develop target operating model, process designs, and technology recommendations.  
  – Deliver: Final Strategic Roadmap, target process documentation, technology advisory deliverables.

  (c) **Phase 3 – Implementation Support (Months 10-24)**  
  – Provide program management support, change management, and performance measurement frameworks.  
  – Deliver: Program management artifacts, change enablement materials, performance dashboards.

  (d) **Phase 4 – Optimization & Handover (Remainder of Term)**  
  – Conduct periodic optimization reviews.  
  – Deliver: Optimization reports, updated KPIs, final handover documentation.

Specific milestones, acceptance criteria, and delivery dates shall be set forth in one or more SOWs.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**

  2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Services (i) in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, (ii) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for consulting services of similar nature, scope, and complexity, and (iii) in compliance with Applicable Law and this Agreement.

  2.5.2 Vendor shall assign personnel with appropriate skills and experience to perform the Services. Vendor may use subcontractors as permitted under Section 16.10, provided Vendor remains responsible for the performance of the Services and compliance with this Agreement.

  2.5.3 Vendor shall cooperate reasonably with Client’s personnel and other vendors engaged by Client, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid interference with Client’s business operations.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**

  2.6.1 Unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW, Client shall have **ten (10) Business Days** from receipt of any Deliverable to review such Deliverable and either (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide written notice of rejection that specifies in reasonable detail the basis for rejection, limited to material non-conformity with the applicable SOW or this Agreement.

  2.6.2 If Client timely rejects a Deliverable, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable within a mutually agreed period. The Parties shall repeat the review process described in Section 2.6.1 for any resubmitted Deliverable.

  2.6.3 If Client fails to respond within the applicable review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the expiration of such period.

2.7 **Project Governance.** The Parties shall establish a governance structure, which may include:

  (a) designation of a primary project manager for each Party;  
  (b) recurring status meetings (e.g., weekly or bi-weekly) to review progress, risks, and issues;  
  (c) escalation procedures for resolving disputes or roadblocks as per Article XIV; and  
  (d) documentation of key decisions and approvals.

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **September 11, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

  4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon providing at least **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

  4.3.2 If Client terminates for convenience, Client shall (a) pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted (or deemed accepted) through the effective date of termination, (b) reimburse Vendor for all approved, non-cancellable expenses incurred prior to the effective date of termination, and (c) pay any agreed early termination charges expressly stated in an applicable SOW (if any).

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice from the non-breaching Party describing the breach in reasonable detail. If the breach is not reasonably capable of cure within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have such additional reasonable time as necessary to cure, provided it commences cure within such thirty (30)-day period and diligently proceeds to completion.

  4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (c) has a receiver appointed over substantial assets, or (d) ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**

  4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
  (a) Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary for orderly wind-down;  
  (b) each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.6; and  
  (c) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed amounts due for Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, including any approved expenses and applicable milestone payments for Deliverables accepted or deemed accepted.

  4.5.2 Unless otherwise agreed, Vendor shall deliver to Client any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid and shall cooperate reasonably to transition the Services to Client or a third party designated by Client, at Client’s expense.

4.6 **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration of this Agreement shall so survive, including without limitation Article V (Confidentiality), Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights), Article VII (Representations and Warranties), Article VIII (Indemnification), Article IX (Limitation of Liability), Article X (Insurance), Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy), Article XIV (Dispute Resolution), Article XV (Force Majeure), and Article XVI (General Provisions), along with any payment obligations accrued as of the effective date of termination.
