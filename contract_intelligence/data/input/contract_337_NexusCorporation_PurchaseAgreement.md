## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **Overview.** Nexus shall supply to Contoso, on a non-exclusive basis, certain Products and Services to support Contoso’s enterprise technology infrastructure and related business operations, in accordance with the terms of this Agreement and the applicable SOWs and POs. The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement is intended as a master framework, and that the specific scope, timelines, and commercial details for each engagement will be defined in one or more SOWs and/or POs executed pursuant hereto.

2.2 **Products.**  
(a) **Hardware.** Nexus shall provide enterprise-grade hardware, which may include servers, storage arrays, networking equipment, security appliances, and related accessories. The specifications, part numbers, quantities, and configurations of such hardware shall be set forth in the applicable PO or SOW.  

(b) **Software.** Nexus shall provide software licenses, including proprietary Nexus software and, where specified, Third-Party Materials. The type (perpetual vs. subscription), license metrics (e.g., per user, per core, per device), license term, and any usage limitations shall be identified in the relevant SOW or PO and governed by this Agreement and, where applicable, any third-party license terms disclosed in writing to Contoso prior to or at the time of purchase.

2.3 **Professional Services.**  
Nexus shall perform professional Services, which may include, without limitation:

  (i) **Assessment & Design Services.** Analysis of Contoso’s current infrastructure, collection of requirements, and design of solution architecture, including capacity planning, network design, failover and redundancy strategies, and security controls;

  (ii) **Implementation & Configuration.** Installation and configuration of Products, integration with Contoso’s existing systems, creation of standard operating procedures, and configuration of monitoring and alerting tools;

  (iii) **Data Migration.** Planning, testing, and execution of data migration from legacy systems to the new environment, including validation of data integrity and rollback contingencies;

  (iv) **Customizations & Integrations.** Development of custom scripts, connectors, and integration components to enable interoperability with Contoso’s internal applications or third-party platforms, as further detailed in a SOW;

  (v) **Testing & Acceptance Support.** Conducting user acceptance testing support, performance testing assistance, and remediation of defects in accordance with the acceptance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW;

  (vi) **Training.** Delivery of training sessions (onsite or remote) and training materials for Contoso’s administrators and end users regarding use, configuration, and basic troubleshooting of the Products and Services; and

  (vii) **Project Management.** Provision of project management resources responsible for planning, tracking, and managing the Services, including status reporting and risk management.

2.4 **Support and Maintenance Services.**  
Subject to Contoso’s payment of any applicable support fees, Nexus shall provide support and maintenance Services for the Products as outlined in the applicable SOW or product support policy, which may include:

  (a) Help desk support during designated support hours;  
  (b) Incident management, including response and resolution in accordance with the Service Levels;  
  (c) Provision of patches, bug fixes, security updates, and minor upgrades;  
  (d) Access to online knowledge bases and Documentation.

The specific Service Levels (e.g., uptime commitments, response time targets) are set forth in **Article XII** and/or the applicable SOW.

2.5 **Project Plan and Timelines.**  
(a) For each SOW involving implementation or project-based Services, Nexus shall, in collaboration with Contoso, prepare a detailed project plan that includes milestones, timelines, resource allocations, and dependencies. The project plan shall be incorporated by reference into the SOW.  

(b) Nexus shall use commercially reasonable efforts to perform the Services in accordance with the timelines and milestones set forth in the applicable SOW. Contoso acknowledges that delays in providing requested information, access, or approvals may impact such timelines.  

(c) If Nexus anticipates that any milestone or delivery date may not be met, Nexus shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, provide an explanation for the delay, and propose a revised schedule. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate any adverse impact on the project schedule.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Nexus shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services, and with the degree of skill and care exercised by reputable providers of similar services.  

(b) Nexus shall ensure that all personnel assigned to perform Services under this Agreement are appropriately qualified and experienced. Nexus shall be responsible for the acts and omissions of its personnel and subcontractors to the same extent as if they were acts or omissions of Nexus.  

(c) Nexus shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations in the performance of the Services, including, where applicable, Data Protection Laws as further described in **Article XI**.

2.7 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Nexus’s ability to perform its obligations is dependent on Contoso’s timely cooperation. Accordingly, Contoso shall:

  (i) Provide Nexus with timely access to relevant systems, facilities, documentation, and personnel as reasonably required to perform the Services;  
  (ii) Designate a project manager or primary point of contact who shall be responsible for coordination and decision-making;  
  (iii) Provide timely feedback, reviews, and approvals of Deliverables and milestones;  
  (iv) Ensure that any necessary third-party consents or licenses (not provided by Nexus) are obtained; and  
  (v) Perform any tasks or responsibilities attributed to Contoso in the applicable SOW or project plan.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
(a) Each SOW shall set forth acceptance criteria and procedures for the Deliverables. In the absence of such specific criteria, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted if Contoso does not reject them in writing, with reasonable detail describing material non-conformities, within fifteen (15) Business Days after delivery.  

(b) If Contoso timely rejects a Deliverable, Nexus shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, promptly correct the non-conformities and re-deliver the Deliverable. Contoso shall then have a further review period of ten (10) Business Days to accept or reject the revised Deliverable.  

(c) If Nexus is unable to correct the non-conformities after a reasonable number of attempts, the Parties shall discuss in good faith an appropriate adjustment, which may include a reduction in Fees associated with the non-conforming Deliverable. This Section 2.8 is without prejudice to Contoso’s other rights and remedies under this Agreement.

2.9 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
Unless expressly stated otherwise in a SOW, Nexus’s engagement hereunder is non-exclusive and Contoso remains free to purchase similar products or services from other vendors, subject to any confidentiality obligations under **Article V**.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees and Contract Value.**  
(a) The Parties acknowledge that the total estimated contract value under this Agreement is **US$2,797,951**. This amount represents the aggregate of all anticipated Fees over the Term but is not a guaranteed minimum or maximum commitment unless expressly stated in an applicable SOW or PO.  

(b) The specific Fees applicable to particular Products and Services, including unit prices, recurring fees, and one-time charges, shall be set forth in one or more SOWs or POs.

3.2 **Invoicing.**  
(a) Nexus shall invoice Contoso for Products and Services in accordance with the billing schedule set forth in the applicable SOW or PO. Unless otherwise specified:  
  (i) Hardware and perpetual software licenses shall be invoiced upon shipment;  
  (ii) Subscription-based licenses and recurring support or maintenance Services shall be invoiced annually in advance; and  
  (iii) Professional Services shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on time and materials or upon completion of milestones, as indicated in the applicable SOW.  

(b) Each invoice shall reference this Agreement, the applicable SOW or PO number, and shall provide reasonable detail regarding the Products or Services billed, including quantities, unit prices, and applicable taxes.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) Contoso shall pay all undisputed Fees to Nexus within **thirty (30) days** from the date of the applicable invoice (“**Net 30**”), in U.S. dollars by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method.  

(b) If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice, Contoso shall notify Nexus in writing prior to the payment due date, specifying the disputed amounts and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute. Upon resolution, any amounts determined to be payable by Contoso shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of such resolution.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment. Nexus shall provide written notice to Contoso of any overdue amounts prior to taking further action. Without limiting Nexus’s other rights under this Agreement, if any undisputed Fees remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment, Nexus may suspend performance of the affected Services or deliveries of Products until all overdue amounts (including accrued interest) are paid, provided that Nexus acts reasonably and proportionately.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
(a) All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, excise, customs duties, or other similar taxes or charges imposed by any governmental authority (“**Taxes**”) (other than taxes based on Nexus’s net income). Contoso shall be responsible for paying any such Taxes properly invoiced by Nexus.  

(b) If Contoso is required by law to withhold any taxes from payments to Nexus, Contoso shall provide Nexus with official receipts or other evidence issued by the appropriate taxing authority and shall cooperate with Nexus in seeking to minimize any such withholding tax.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Nexus in connection with the performance of the Services (such as travel, lodging, and per diem) shall be reimbursed by Contoso at actual cost, without markup, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policies provided to Nexus in advance. Nexus shall provide appropriate documentation (e.g., receipts) with each request for reimbursement.

3.7 **No Set-Off.**  
Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or agreed in writing by Nexus, Contoso shall not set off, deduct, or withhold any amounts from payments due to Nexus based on any claim, counterclaim, or alleged liability.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until **December 2, 2028** (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew this Agreement for additional periods on terms to be negotiated in good faith. Any such renewal shall be documented in a written amendment executed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile of this engagement, Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Nexus. Nexus may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso. Any SOW or PO then in effect may be separately terminated or allowed to run to completion, as specified in the notice or mutually agreed by the Parties.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any affected SOW or PO) upon written notice to the other Party if:

  (a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) The other Party (i) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they become due, (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or for reorganization, (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (iv) has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets, and such proceeding is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination of Specific SOWs or POs.**  
Any SOW or PO may be terminated independently of this Agreement in accordance with the termination provisions set forth in such SOW or PO. Termination of an individual SOW or PO shall not automatically terminate this Agreement or any other SOW or PO, unless expressly stated.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
(a) Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
  (i) All outstanding unpaid invoices shall become immediately due and payable;  
  (ii) Nexus shall cease performance of the terminated Services;  
  (iii) Contoso shall cease use of any time-limited or subscription-based licenses that are terminated and shall, upon request, certify in writing that it has discontinued such use; and  
  (iv) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5.  

(b) Termination of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of any obligations that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including payment obligations.  

(c) In the event of termination for Nexus’s uncured material breach, Contoso shall be entitled to a pro rata refund of any prepaid Fees for Services or subscriptions not yet performed or delivered as of the effective date of termination.  

(d) In the event of termination for Contoso’s convenience, Contoso shall pay Nexus for (i) all Products delivered and Services performed up to the effective date of termination; and (ii) any non-cancellable commitments or costs reasonably incurred by Nexus in reliance on this Agreement, provided that Nexus uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such costs.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including, without limitation, **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and Sections 3.4, 3.5, 4.6, and 4.7.

## ARTICLE V  
### CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to business plans, financial information, product roadmaps, pricing, trade secrets, technical and operational information, software source code and object code, designs, processes, methods, policies, security information, and information relating to customers, suppliers, or employees. Confidential Information of Contoso includes its data, systems architecture, and any Personal Data provided to Nexus. Confidential Information of Nexus includes the Products, Documentation, and its proprietary methodologies.

5.2 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  

  (b) Not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement; and  

  (c) Protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but no less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.3 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:

  (a) To its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, contractors, advisors, and agents who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement; and  

  (b) To the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party (i) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted), and (ii) cooperates, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.4 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) Is or becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of a breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  

  (b) Was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession, without restriction on disclosure, prior to receipt from the Disclosing Party;  

  (c) Is lawfully received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  

  (d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that (a) the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for legal or compliance purposes, and (b) any Confidential Information contained in routine backup media shall be destroyed in the ordinary course of business according to the Receiving Party’s data retention policies.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that, with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, such obligations shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under applicable law.

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For any hosted or managed service components specified in a SOW, Nexus shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such services are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and emergency maintenance as described below.

Nexus shall use commercially reasonable efforts to respond to and resolve support incidents in accordance with the following target Service Levels (which may be further refined in a SOW):

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Production system is down or severely impacted.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours  

- **Severity 2 (High):** Major functionality is impacted, but system is operational.  
  - Initial Response: within 4 hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 24 hours  

- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issue, partial loss of functionality.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

- **Severity 4 (Low):** General inquiries, minor issues, or cosmetic defects.  
  - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
  - Target Resolution: next release or as agreed.

- Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% of the monthly Fees for the affected service;  
- Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% of the monthly Fees;  
- Availability below 98.0%: 20% of the monthly Fees.

Service credits shall be applied as a credit against future invoices and shall not exceed 20% of the monthly Fees for the affected service in any month. Service credits are Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the availability Service Levels.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
Service Levels shall not apply to, and Nexus shall have no liability for, failures or delays resulting from:

  (a) Contoso’s or its users’ misuse or unauthorized use of the services;  

  (b) Internet or network issues outside Nexus’s reasonable control;  

  (c) Force Majeure Events (as defined in Article XV); or  

  (d) Scheduled maintenance, provided such maintenance is carried out in accordance with Section 12.2.

## ARTICLE XV  
### FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics or pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving only the affected Party’s employees), acts of government, and failures of telecommunications or power supplies not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature and expected duration of the event;  

  (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the event on its performance; and  

  (c) Resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the period that such obligations are affected by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of a Party’s obligations, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or this Agreement (in whole or in part) upon written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination, except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
