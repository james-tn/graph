## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Summit Tech shall provide to Contoso comprehensive technology services, including design, development, implementation, integration, testing, deployment, support, and maintenance of a cloud-hosted enterprise platform (the “Platform”) and related systems, as more particularly described below. The Parties acknowledge that the Services are business-critical to Contoso and that Summit Tech shall act as a primary technology partner throughout the Term.

2.2 **Project Components.**  
The Services to be provided by Summit Tech shall include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 **Platform Design and Architecture.**  
  (a) Conduct discovery sessions with Contoso stakeholders to gather detailed business, functional, and technical requirements.  
  (b) Prepare a comprehensive solution design, including high-level and low-level architecture diagrams, data models, integration points, and security design.  
  (c) Submit the design documents to Contoso for review and written approval prior to commencing development activities.

  2.2.2 **Software Development and Configuration.**  
  (a) Develop or configure modules for customer management, order processing, billing, reporting, and analytics.  
  (b) Implement role-based access control, audit logging, and configurable workflows.  
  (c) Utilize industry-standard coding practices, version control, and code review processes.  
  (d) Comply with the Specifications and applicable regulatory and security standards agreed by the Parties.

  2.2.3 **Systems Integration.**  
  (a) Integrate the Platform with Contoso’s existing CRM, ERP, and authentication systems via APIs, middleware, or other integration mechanisms agreed by the Parties.  
  (b) Implement data synchronization routines, including initial data migration and ongoing data feeds.  
  (c) Provide integration documentation and test plans.

  2.2.4 **Testing and Quality Assurance.**  
  (a) Develop and execute test plans, including unit testing, integration testing, system testing, performance testing, and security testing.  
  (b) Provide test reports and remediation plans for any defects identified.  
  (c) Cooperate with Contoso’s project team in conducting user acceptance testing (“UAT”), and address defects within mutually agreed timeframes.

  2.2.5 **Deployment and Go-Live Support.**  
  (a) Prepare deployment scripts and instructions for moving from test to production environments.  
  (b) Support production deployment, including a contingency plan and rollback procedures.  
  (c) Provide hypercare support for at least thirty (30) days following initial go-live, focusing on critical and high-severity issues.

  2.2.6 **Support and Maintenance.**  
  (a) Provide ongoing support as set forth in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**, including incident management, problem resolution, and monitoring.  
  (b) Provide periodic updates, patches, and minor enhancements needed to maintain the Platform’s functionality and security.  
  (c) Provide technical support channels (e.g., email, ticketing system, and phone) and assign designated points of contact.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Summit Tech shall provide the following Deliverables (“Deliverables”) under this Agreement, at a minimum:

  2.3.1 Detailed solution design and architecture documentation.  
  2.3.2 Configured and/or custom-developed Platform modules in object code form.  
  2.3.3 Integration components, including APIs, connectors, adaptors, or middleware configurations.  
  2.3.4 Test plans, test scripts, and test result reports.  
  2.3.5 Deployment documentation, including runbooks, rollback plans, and environment configuration details.  
  2.3.6 User documentation, including user guides, administrator manuals, and training materials.  
  2.3.7 Support procedures, escalation matrix, and contact information.

The specific content, format, and delivery dates of each Deliverable may be further detailed in written exhibits or statements of work executed by the Parties and incorporated into this Agreement.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall collaborate to establish a mutually agreed project plan, including key milestones and target completion dates. By way of illustration, and not limitation:

  2.4.1 **Requirements and Design Phase:**  
  - Completion within approximately eight (8) weeks of the Effective Date, subject to Contoso’s timely provision of information and approvals.  

  2.4.2 **Development and Integration Phase:**  
  - Commencing promptly upon Contoso’s approval of the solution design, with an estimated duration of twelve (12) to sixteen (16) weeks, depending on complexity and scope.  

  2.4.3 **Testing and UAT Phase:**  
  - Commencing upon completion of development, with an estimated duration of six (6) to eight (8) weeks.  

  2.4.4 **Deployment and Go-Live:**  
  - Target go-live date to be mutually agreed by the Parties in writing, taking into account UAT results and readiness.

All timelines are estimates and may be adjusted by written agreement of the Parties, including via Change Orders under **Article XIII**. The Parties acknowledge that delays attributable to Contoso (including delays in providing information, access, or approvals) shall entitle Summit Tech to reasonable schedule extensions and, where applicable, additional Fees.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Summit Tech shall perform the Services:

  (a) In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  (b) Using appropriately skilled, qualified, and experienced personnel;  
  (c) In accordance with the Specifications and SLAs set forth in this Agreement; and  
  (d) In compliance with applicable laws, rules, and regulations as described in **Article VII**.

2.6 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Summit Tech’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Contoso’s timely cooperation and performance of its responsibilities, including:

  (a) Providing timely access to Contoso’s systems, facilities, data, and personnel, as reasonably required for Summit Tech to perform the Services;  
  (b) Reviewing and providing timely feedback on Deliverables, designs, and other materials submitted by Summit Tech;  
  (c) Designating a project manager and key stakeholders with authority to make decisions and provide approvals;  
  (d) Ensuring that its own systems and infrastructure are adequate to support the Platform and integrations.

Summit Tech shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performance to the extent caused by Contoso’s failure to fulfill its obligations under this Section 2.6.

2.7 **Subcontractors.**  
Summit Tech may, in its discretion, engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that Summit Tech remains fully responsible for the performance of such subcontractors and for compliance with this Agreement. Upon request, Summit Tech shall provide Contoso with a list of significant subcontractors involved in the performance of the Services.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the total estimated contract value for the baseline Services described in **Article II** is **US$95,657** (the “Contract Value”), exclusive of any applicable taxes and any additional fees arising from Change Orders under **Article XIII**.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Contract Value shall be invoiced in accordance with the following milestones:

  3.2.1 **Milestone 1 – Project Initiation (20%).**  
  - Amount: 20% of Contract Value (US$19,131.40).  
  - Trigger: Execution of this Agreement and commencement of the Requirements and Design Phase.

  3.2.2 **Milestone 2 – Completion of Design & Architecture (25%).**  
  - Amount: 25% of Contract Value (US$23,914.25).  
  - Trigger: Delivery and Contoso’s written acceptance (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed) of the solution design and architecture documentation.

  3.2.3 **Milestone 3 – Completion of Development & Integration (35%).**  
  - Amount: 35% of Contract Value (US$33,479.95).  
  - Trigger: Delivery of the developed and integrated Platform into the staging or pre-production environment for UAT.

  3.2.4 **Milestone 4 – Go-Live & Hypercare (20%).**  
  - Amount: 20% of Contract Value (US$19,131.40).  
  - Trigger: Successful deployment to production and completion of the initial thirty (30) day hypercare support period.

3.3 **Time-and-Materials and Additional Services.**  
Services beyond the baseline scope, including changes requested by Contoso and approved through Change Orders, shall be billed on a time-and-materials basis at Summit Tech’s then-current standard rates, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
Summit Tech shall invoice Contoso upon achievement of each milestone or monthly in arrears for time-and-materials services, as applicable. Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) calendar days** of the invoice date (“Net 60”). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the due date until the date of actual payment. Summit Tech shall be entitled, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, to suspend Services for any undisputed amount that remains unpaid more than thirty (30) days after the due date.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, withholding, and other similar taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes based on Summit Tech’s net income) and shall pay or reimburse Summit Tech for any such taxes assessed in connection with this Agreement.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Contoso shall reimburse Summit Tech for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and related expenses, in accordance with Contoso’s reasonable expense policies provided in advance. Summit Tech shall provide appropriate documentation (e.g., receipts) for reimbursable expenses.

3.8 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall notify Summit Tech in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall pay all undisputed portions in accordance with **Section 3.4**, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Failure to dispute an invoice within such time period shall be deemed acceptance of the invoice.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until **September 18, 2027** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “Term”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Agreement may be extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties executed prior to the Expiration Date. Any renewal term shall be subject to the then-current rates and terms as agreed in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Summit Tech. Summit Tech may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Contoso. In the event of termination under this Section 4.3:

  (a) Contoso shall pay Summit Tech all Fees and reimbursable expenses incurred through the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments entered into by Summit Tech in reliance on this Agreement; and  
  (b) Any prepaid but unearned Fees may be retained by Summit Tech as consideration for its pre-termination resource commitments and unrecoverable costs.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice to the other Party if:

  (a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) The other Party becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, and such condition is not cured within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Regulatory or Legal Reasons.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the continued performance of the Agreement would violate any applicable law, regulation, or binding order of a governmental authority, and the Parties are unable to modify the Agreement to avoid such violation.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) Summit Tech shall promptly cease performing the Services, except as reasonably necessary to wind down activities and facilitate an orderly transition;  
  (b) Contoso shall pay Summit Tech all Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued and unpaid as of the effective date of termination, and any additional amounts due under this Agreement;  
  (c) Summit Tech shall deliver to Contoso, to the extent feasible and subject to receipt of all outstanding payments, any completed or in-progress Deliverables in a reasonable state, along with any relevant documentation; and  
  (d) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information pursuant to **Section 5.4**, subject to any rights to retain copies for archival or legal purposes.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination shall so survive, including without limitation **Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI** and Sections **4.6** and **4.7**.

5.2 **Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
  (b) Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement;  
  (c) Restrict access to the Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein; and  
  (d) Exercise at least the same degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to any information that:

  (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) Is lawfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;  
  (c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records; or  
  (d) Was in the Receiving Party’s lawful possession without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted:

  (a) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of such requirement to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and  
  (b) Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required, and use reasonable efforts to ensure that it is treated confidentially.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain copies as required by law or for archival, audit, or dispute resolution purposes, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** from the date of disclosure of the Confidential Information, except that trade secrets shall be maintained in confidence for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Availability.**  
Subject to scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events, Summit Tech shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the Platform is available **99.9%** of the time measured on a monthly basis (“Availability Commitment”).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Planned maintenance windows (e.g., system upgrades, patching) shall not count as downtime for the purposes of calculating availability, provided that:

  (a) Summit Tech provides at least forty-eight (48) hours’ advance notice; and  
  (b) Maintenance is scheduled during low-usage periods where reasonably practicable.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Summit Tech shall respond to support incidents within the following target timeframes:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical):**  
  - Impact: Production system down or critical functionality unavailable with significant business impact.  
  - Initial Response Time: Within one (1) hour, 24x7.  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: Continuous efforts until resolved or mitigated.

  (b) **Severity 2 (High):**  
  - Impact: Major functionality impaired with moderate business impact; workaround available.  
  - Initial Response Time: Within four (4) hours during Business Days.  
  - Target Resolution: Within two (2) Business Days.

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium):**  
  - Impact: Non-critical functionality impaired or issues with limited business impact.  
  - Initial Response Time: Within one (1) Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution: Within five (5) Business Days.

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low):**  
  - Impact: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
  - Initial Response Time: Within two (2) Business Days.  
  - Target Resolution: As reasonably scheduled.

12.4 **SLA Remedies.**  
If Summit Tech fails to meet the Availability Commitment in any calendar month, Contoso may be entitled to service credits as follows, upon written request within thirty (30) days after the end of the month:

  (a) Availability between 99.5% and 99.9%: Service credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring Fees;  
  (b) Availability between 99.0% and 99.5%: Service credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring Fees;  
  (c) Availability below 99.0%: Service credit equal to 15% of the monthly recurring Fees.

Service credits shall be applied to future invoices and shall constitute Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the Availability Commitment, without limiting any other rights or remedies under the Agreement for material breach.

15.2 **Notice.**  
The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, describing the nature of the event, its expected duration, and the impact on performance.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party shall be excused from performance of the affected obligations, and the time for performance shall be extended for a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party:

  (a) Uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event; and  
  (b) Resumes performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
