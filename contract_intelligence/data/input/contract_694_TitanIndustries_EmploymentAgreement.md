## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Titan shall provide to Contoso comprehensive employment-related and professional services, including without limitation:

(a) **Staffing and Placement Services.** Identification, recruitment, screening, hiring, and assignment of qualified Personnel to perform designated functions for Contoso (e.g., software engineering, systems administration, project management, customer support, quality assurance, and related roles);

(b) **Onboarding and Administration.** Administration of background checks (where permitted by Applicable Law), employment eligibility verification, orientation, onboarding documentation, benefits administration for Titan’s employees, payroll processing, and ordinary employment-related administration for Titan’s Personnel assigned to Contoso;

(c) **Ongoing Management and HR Support.** Ongoing human resources management of Titan Personnel assigned to Contoso, including performance reviews (in coordination with Contoso’s designated manager), discipline, and termination decisions (subject to consultation with Contoso as appropriate);

(d) **Professional Consulting Services.** Provision of specialized consulting and advisory services (e.g., technology implementation, process optimization, project planning) as described in each SOW;

(e) **Transition and Knowledge Transfer.** Upon request by Contoso and subject to the terms of this Agreement, support for transitioning services or knowledge to Contoso or a replacement vendor at the end of an assignment or at termination or expiration of this Agreement.

2.1.2 The Services may be further detailed, customized, or supplemented by one or more SOWs that reference this Agreement by its reference number **AGR-TIT-202505-694**. Each SOW shall specify, as applicable, the job roles, number of Personnel, experience requirements, location of services (onsite, remote, hybrid), working hours, deliverables, milestones, and other relevant parameters.

### 2.2 Deliverables

2.2.1 Titan shall provide Deliverables as specified in each SOW, which may include:

- Staffing plans and candidate profiles;
- Periodic performance and utilization reports for assigned Personnel;
- Project plans, status reports, and implementation documentation;
- Technical documentation, design specifications, and knowledge base articles;
- Training materials and handover documentation at project completion.

2.2.2 Each Deliverable shall be deemed accepted by Contoso upon the earlier of: (a) Contoso’s written confirmation of acceptance; or (b) ten (10) Business Days after delivery, provided Contoso has not delivered a written notice of material non-conformance to Titan within such period specifying in reasonable detail the basis for rejection. In the event of a properly rejected Deliverable, Titan shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, promptly correct, re-perform, or replace the Deliverable and resubmit it for Contoso’s review. Acceptance of a Deliverable shall not waive any warranty rights under Article VII.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 Titan shall perform the Services (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, using Personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications; (b) in accordance with industry best practices for similar services; and (c) in compliance with all Applicable Laws and this Agreement.

2.3.2 Titan shall ensure that Personnel assigned to Contoso:

- possess the required technical and professional qualifications for their roles as described in the relevant SOW;
- receive necessary training regarding Contoso’s policies explicitly identified by Contoso as applicable to vendor personnel (e.g., information security, code of conduct, workplace safety); and
- adhere to reasonable work rules and site security policies communicated in writing by Contoso.

2.3.3 Contoso shall have the right to request the removal and prompt replacement of any Titan Personnel who, in Contoso’s reasonable judgment: (a) fail to meet performance requirements; (b) engage in misconduct; (c) present a security risk; or (d) otherwise fail to comply with Contoso’s applicable policies. Titan shall use commercially reasonable efforts to replace such Personnel as promptly as practicable, consistent with the skill, experience, and location requirements specified in the applicable SOW.

### 2.4 Locations, Remote Work, and Tools

2.4.1 Services may be performed at Contoso’s premises, Titan’s premises, or remotely, as stated in the applicable SOW. Titan shall ensure that Personnel comply with security and facility access procedures when working at Contoso’s sites.

2.4.2 Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Contoso shall provide Titan Personnel with necessary access to Contoso systems, facilities, and tools, subject to Contoso’s security requirements. Titan shall remain responsible for its Personnel’s adherence to Contoso’s security and acceptable use policies.

2.4.3 Titan shall supply ordinary tools, equipment, and materials needed for its Personnel to perform the Services, unless the Parties expressly agree that Contoso will provide specified equipment (e.g., laptops, access badges).

### 2.5 Co-Employment and Direction of Work

2.5.1 The Parties expressly acknowledge and agree that Titan shall be the sole employer (or contractual engager, in the case of independent contractors) of its Personnel. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship between Contoso and Titan’s Personnel.

2.5.2 Contoso may provide day-to-day direction regarding the tasks, priorities, work product requirements, and schedule of Titan Personnel assigned to Contoso projects. However, Titan shall retain responsibility for all other employment-related aspects, including hiring, firing, compensation, benefits, tax withholding, discipline, and compliance with employment-related laws, except as otherwise expressly provided herein.

### 2.6 Service Level Compliance

Services shall be performed in accordance with the Service Levels described in Article XII, as further detailed in any applicable SOW. The remedies for failure to meet the Service Levels are set forth in Section 12.4.

### 2.7 Changes to Services

Any change to the scope, nature, or timing of the Services or Deliverables shall be managed in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management). Titan shall have no obligation to perform any additional or modified Services unless and until a Change Order is executed.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The Parties acknowledge that the estimated total contract value for the Term, based on the anticipated scope of Services and staffing levels, is **Two Million Three Hundred Forty-Nine Thousand One Hundred Ninety-Four U.S. Dollars (US$2,349,194)** (the “**Contract Value**”).  

3.1.2 The Contract Value is an estimate and not a guaranteed minimum or maximum, unless otherwise specified in a particular SOW. Actual Fees shall be based on time and materials, fixed fees, or other pricing structures as set forth in each SOW.

### 3.2 Fee Structure

3.2.1 **Time and Materials.** For Personnel billed on a time-and-materials basis, the hourly or daily rates shall be set forth in the applicable SOW. Titan shall provide itemized invoices showing hours worked per individual, applicable rates, and a brief description of work performed.

3.2.2 **Fixed-Price Deliverables.** For fixed-price projects or Deliverables, the Fees and milestones shall be set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise agreed, Titan shall invoice upon completion and acceptance of each milestone or Deliverable.

3.2.3 **Retainer or Monthly Fees.** If an SOW provides for a monthly retainer or managed services fee, such fee shall be invoiced monthly in advance, unless otherwise specified.

### 3.3 Payment Terms

3.3.1 Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of Titan’s valid invoice (“**Net 60**”).

3.3.2 Invoices shall be submitted no more frequently than monthly, and must reference this Agreement (including the reference number **AGR-TIT-202505-694**) and the relevant SOW, and provide sufficient detail to allow Contoso to verify the amounts invoiced.

3.3.3 Contoso may withhold payment of any amounts disputed in good faith, provided that Contoso (a) pays all undisputed portions of the invoice; and (b) notifies Titan in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice of the basis for the dispute. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such disputes promptly. Any portion of an invoice that is determined to be payable shall be paid within thirty (30) days after such determination.

### 3.4 Late Payment

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount that remains unpaid after the applicable due date shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full.

3.4.2 Titan shall not suspend the Services solely for late payment unless: (a) such payment is more than sixty (60) days past due; (b) Titan has provided at least fifteen (15) days’ prior written notice of its intent to suspend; and (c) Contoso has not paid the overdue amount or provided a reasonable dispute explanation during such notice period.

### 3.5 Taxes

3.5.1 All Fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes, which shall be the responsibility of Contoso, except for taxes based on Titan’s income, property, or employment obligations.

3.5.2 If Applicable Law requires Contoso to withhold taxes from payments to Titan, Contoso shall (a) withhold such amounts as required; (b) remit such taxes to the appropriate tax authorities; and (c) provide Titan with appropriate documentation evidencing such remittance.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursement

3.6.1 Contoso shall reimburse Titan for pre-approved, reasonable, and necessary out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Titan’s Personnel in performing the Services (e.g., travel, lodging, meals), in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policies provided in writing to Titan.

3.6.2 Titan shall submit supporting documentation (e.g., receipts) for reimbursable expenses with its invoices. Contoso shall pay approved expenses in accordance with the payment terms set forth in Section 3.3.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until **April 16, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the **“Term”**).

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew this Agreement for one or more additional periods, subject to renegotiation of applicable terms as necessary.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience upon not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

4.2.2 Upon termination for convenience by Contoso, Contoso shall pay Titan: (a) all Fees for Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted through the effective date of termination; and (b) any non-cancellable commitments actually incurred in reliance on this Agreement or the applicable SOW, provided Titan uses reasonably diligent efforts to mitigate such costs.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) materially breaches this Agreement or any SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; or

(b) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.3.2 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Titan shall have the right to terminate this Agreement for cause if Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amounts within sixty (60) days after written notice of non-payment, subject to any good-faith dispute per Section 3.3.3.

### 4.4 Effects of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) Titan shall promptly cease all Services, except as necessary to wind down operations and transition as directed by Contoso;  
(b) Contoso shall pay Titan all undisputed amounts due and payable in accordance with Article III;  
(c) Titan shall promptly return or, at Contoso’s option, destroy Confidential Information and Client Materials, in accordance with Section 5.6 and Article XI; and  
(d) each Party shall cooperate in good faith to effect an orderly transition of Services to Contoso or to a successor vendor, subject to a mutually agreed transition plan and fees if such transition exceeds ordinary wind-down activities.

4.4.2 Termination or expiration shall not relieve either Party of any obligations that have accrued as of the effective date of termination, nor of obligations that by their nature are intended to survive, as specified in Section 16.10.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 “**Confidential Information**” means any non-public, proprietary, or confidential information, in whatever form (written, oral, electronic, visual, or otherwise) disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), including but not limited to: business plans, pricing, financial information, customer lists, marketing strategies, product roadmaps, trade secrets, technical data, software, source code, architectural designs, security information, and the terms of this Agreement.

5.1.2 Confidential Information does **not** include information that:  
(a) is or becomes generally available to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) is rightfully received from a third party without a duty of confidentiality;  
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
(d) was already known to the Receiving Party without confidentiality obligations at the time of disclosure, as evidenced by the Receiving Party’s contemporaneous written records.

### 5.2 Confidentiality Obligations

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  
(b) restrict disclosure of the Confidential Information to its Personnel and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those herein; and  
(c) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

### 5.3 Permitted Disclosures

5.3.1 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law or a valid court or governmental order, provided that, where legally permitted, the Receiving Party: (a) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice; and (b) cooperates with the Disclosing Party’s reasonable efforts to limit or contest such disclosure.

### 5.4 Return or Destruction

Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, except that the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for legal and compliance purposes and may retain copies stored in routine backup archives, which shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

### 5.5 Survival

The confidentiality obligations under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and remain in effect for a period of **five (5) years** after the termination or expiration of this Agreement, except that trade secrets shall be protected for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## FORCE MAJEURE

### 15.1 Definition

“**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding strikes or labor disputes involving only the affected Party’s employees), governmental actions or orders, and failures of telecommunications or utility services not caused by the affected Party.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

The affected Party shall:

(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event, including reasonable details of its impact;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event; and  
(c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension and Termination

Performance of the affected obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate the affected portions of the Services upon fifteen (15) days’ written notice, without penalty.
