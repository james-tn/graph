## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

### 2.1 Overview of the Project

2.1.1 The Service Provider shall provide to the Company a comprehensive suite of professional services relating to the design, implementation, integration, and ongoing support of a cloud-based enterprise operations platform (the “**Platform**”), including but not limited to customer relationship management (CRM), resource planning, and analytics modules.

2.1.2 The Project is intended to support the Company’s business operations by consolidating disparate systems, improving reporting capabilities, and providing scalable, secure infrastructure. The Services shall be provided in multiple phases, as described below, and shall align with the Specifications and business requirements documented and approved by the Parties.

### 2.2 Phases of Work

2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Estimated: Effective Date + 60 days)**  
(a) The Service Provider shall conduct discovery workshops with key stakeholders of the Company to document business processes, pain points, and system requirements.  
(b) Deliverables shall include:
   - Requirements Traceability Matrix (“RTM”);  
   - Current State Assessment Report;  
   - Future State Architecture Overview; and  
   - Initial Project Plan with Milestones and dependencies.

(c) The Company shall review and approve or provide written feedback on Phase 1 Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of delivery.

2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Solution Design (Estimated: Effective Date + 120 days)**  
(a) Based on the approved requirements, the Service Provider shall produce detailed solution and technical design documentation.  
(b) Deliverables shall include:
   - Detailed Solution Design Document;  
   - Data Model and Integration Design;  
   - Security and Access Control Design;  
   - Test Strategy and Acceptance Criteria Proposal.

(c) The Company’s written approval of the Solution Design Document is a prerequisite for commencement of Phase 3.

2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Configuration, Integration, and Development (Estimated: Effective Date + 240 days)**  
(a) The Service Provider shall configure the Platform in accordance with the approved design, develop any agreed custom integrations and extensions, and set up environments (development, test, production).  
(b) Services shall include:
   - Platform configuration;  
   - Development of integration interfaces (APIs, ETL jobs, middleware);  
   - Data migration scripts and test runs;  
   - Implementation of security and user access controls.

(c) Deliverables shall include:
   - Configured Platform in test environment;  
   - Integration components deployed to test;  
   - Data migration test results;  
   - Configuration and Technical Runbooks.

2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Testing, User Acceptance, and Go-Live (Estimated: Effective Date + 300 days)**  
(a) The Service Provider shall support and coordinate system testing, integration testing, and user acceptance testing (“UAT”) in accordance with the agreed Test Strategy.  
(b) The Service Provider shall remediate defects in accordance with the Service Levels and severity classifications agreed by the Parties.  
(c) Upon successful completion of UAT and resolution of all Priority 1 issues and mutually agreed Priority 2 issues, the Parties shall schedule and execute the production go-live.

(d) Deliverables shall include:
   - Test Plans and Test Cases;  
   - UAT Summary Report;  
   - Go-Live Readiness Checklist;  
   - Go-Live Completion Report.

2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Post-Go-Live Support and Optimization (From Go-Live to Expiration Date)**  
(a) Following Go-Live, the Service Provider shall provide ongoing support, incident management, minor enhancements, and optimization services as further described in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**.  
(b) Deliverables shall include:
   - Monthly performance and incident reports;  
   - Updated documentation reflecting changes;  
   - Quarterly optimization recommendations.

### 2.3 Detailed Description of Services

2.3.1 The Service Provider shall perform all Services in a diligent, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and Applicable Law, and in compliance with the Company’s reasonable written policies provided to the Service Provider in advance.

2.3.2 The Services shall include, without limitation:

(a) **Project Management**  
- Assignment of a project manager responsible for planning, status reporting, risk management, and stakeholder communication;  
- Weekly status meetings and written status reports;  
- Maintenance of a project plan with tasks, dependencies, and milestones;  
- Risk and issue logs, with mitigation and resolution plans.

(b) **Business Analysis and Process Design**  
- Analysis of the Company’s existing business processes;  
- Identification of opportunities to streamline and standardize;  
- Documentation of future state processes aligned with the Platform capabilities;  
- Alignment with internal control and compliance requirements of the Company.

(c) **Technical Design and Architecture**  
- Design of system architecture including integration with the Company’s existing systems (e.g., ERP, HR, data warehouse);  
- Definition of data flows, master data management approach, and data governance touchpoints;  
- Design of role-based security, authentication mechanisms, and audit controls;  
- Consideration of performance, scalability, and high availability, consistent with the Service Levels.

(d) **Configuration and Customization**  
- Configuration of Platform modules (e.g., CRM, resource planning, analytics) based on approved requirements;  
- Development of custom components only where necessary and documented in the design;  
- Use of best practices to minimize technical debt and maintain upgradeability.

(e) **Integration and Data Migration**  
- Implementation of agreed integration interfaces including mapping, transformation, and scheduling;  
- Design and execution of data migration strategy including data cleansing, validation, and reconciliation;  
- At least one full test migration and one production cut-over migration, with reconciliation reports.

(f) **Testing and Quality Assurance**  
- Development of test cases covering functional, integration, regression, and performance aspects;  
- Execution of test cycles, documentation of results, and tracking of defects;  
- Support to the Company during UAT, including triage and resolution of defects.

(g) **Training and Knowledge Transfer**  
- Development of training materials appropriate for end users and administrators;  
- Delivery of up to [X] training sessions (remote or on-site as mutually agreed);  
- Knowledge transfer sessions for the Company’s technical team regarding configuration, integration, and support procedures.

(h) **Support and Maintenance**  
- Provision of a support desk contact point for incident intake;  
- Classification and prioritization of incidents, in accordance with the Service Levels;  
- Resolution of incidents, patching, and minor configuration changes as part of managed services;  
- Coordination with third-party vendors where integrations are impacted, subject to access and cooperation by those vendors.

### 2.4 Performance Standards and Dependencies

2.4.1 The Service Provider shall meet or exceed the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**, including uptime commitments and response times.

2.4.2 The Service Provider’s performance is dependent upon the timely and accurate provision by the Company of Client Materials, access to systems and personnel, timely approvals, and other cooperation reasonably required. Any delay in such provision may impact the timeline, and the Parties shall address such impact in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.4.3 The Service Provider shall use personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications to perform the Services. The Service Provider may use subcontractors with the Company’s prior written consent, not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed, and shall remain fully responsible for the acts and omissions of such subcontractors.

### 2.5 Acceptance of Deliverables

2.5.1 For each Deliverable, the Service Provider shall submit such Deliverable to the Company for review and acceptance in accordance with the applicable acceptance criteria.

2.5.2 Unless otherwise specified, the Company shall have ten (10) Business Days from delivery to either: (a) provide written acceptance, which shall not be unreasonably withheld; or (b) provide written notice of material non-conformities with the acceptance criteria.

2.5.3 The Service Provider shall use reasonable efforts to correct any identified non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputes regarding acceptance, and unresolved disputes shall be handled pursuant to **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**.

12.3.1 The Service Provider shall classify incidents in accordance with the following severity levels:

- **Severity 1 (Critical)**: Complete outage or critical functionality unavailable.  
  - Response Time: within 1 hour.  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 8 hours.

- **Severity 2 (High)**: Major functionality impaired; significant impact but system not completely unavailable.  
  - Response Time: within 4 hours.  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 2 Business Days.

- **Severity 3 (Medium)**: Non-critical functions impaired or moderate business impact.  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 5 Business Days.

- **Severity 4 (Low)**: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or requests for information.  
  - Response Time: within 2 Business Days.

12.4.1 If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitments in **Section 12.1** in any given calendar month, the Company shall be entitled to a service credit, calculated as a percentage of the applicable monthly support fees, as follows (illustrative only):
