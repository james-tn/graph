## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW; Incorporation by Reference.**  
This Work Order **adds scope** to and **supplements** the Services and Deliverables described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent SOW. Except as expressly modified in this Work Order, all terms and conditions relating to Services, Deliverables, performance standards, Client Responsibilities, Acceptance of Deliverables, Exclusions, and Key Personnel set forth in **Article II** of the Parent SOW shall apply mutatis mutandis to the Extended Services and Extended Deliverables under this Work Order. For clarity, all Extended Deliverables are subject to the acceptance process set forth in **Section 2.5 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent SOW.

2.2 **Overview of Extended Services.**  
Subject to the terms of the Parent SOW and this Work Order, the Service Provider shall perform the following **Extended Services**:

  (a) Global rollout of the Platform to additional regions beyond Asia-Pacific, including EMEA and Americas, with necessary localization and compliance adaptations;  
  (b) Design, development, and deployment of Advanced Analytics Accelerators, including machine learning use cases, standardized dashboards, and self-service analytics capabilities;  
  (c) Implementation of additional integration packs with new Client systems and selected third-party systems;  
  (d) Delivery of Enhancement Packs (EPs) to incrementally improve platform functionality and user experience;  
  (e) Expansion of Managed Services to cover the extended global footprint and additional service levels (“SLA Extension”); and  
  (f) Program governance, training, and knowledge transfer to support Client autonomy and ongoing operation.

2.3 **Phasing and Major Workstreams.**  
The Extended Services shall be organized into the following phases and workstreams, which align with and extend the phased approach specified in **Section 2.2 (Phased Approach)** of the Parent SOW:

2.3.1 **Phase A – Global Rollout Preparation (Estimated: 4 months).**  
The Service Provider shall:

  (a) Conduct regional discovery workshops (EMEA, Americas) with Client stakeholders to identify localization, regulatory, data residency, and integration requirements (building upon Phase 1 of the Parent SOW);  
  (b) Update and extend the **Target-State Architecture and Integration Blueprint** originally delivered under Phase 1 and Phase 2 of the Parent SOW to reflect global deployment, multi-region architecture, and failover strategies;  
  (c) Produce an **Updated Global Program Plan**, including Milestones specific to this Work Order and dependencies on Parent SOW activities;  
  (d) Deliver the following **Extended Deliverables**:  
      - Global Rollout Requirements Traceability Addendum;  
      - Updated Global Architecture Diagram and Regional Deployment View;  
      - Global Data Residency and Compliance Matrix (referencing GDPR, CCPA, PDPA);  
      - Global Rollout Master Plan and Region-by-Region Cutover Strategy.

2.3.2 **Phase B – Global Design & Localization (Estimated: 5 months).**  
The Service Provider shall:

  (a) Extend the **Detailed Solution Design Document** and **Security and Compliance Design** provided under Phase 2 of the Parent SOW to address:  
      - multi-region tenancy or equivalent architecture;  
      - localized data retention, residency, and masking requirements;  
      - regional access control models and language/localization requirements;  
  (b) Define detailed **Regional Integration Specifications** for in-scope EMEA and Americas systems;  
  (c) Design a **Global Monitoring and Observability Framework** aligned with the Service Levels in **Article XII** of the Parent SOW and the SLA Extension in this Work Order;  
  (d) Deliver the following Extended Deliverables:  
      - Global Solution Design Addendum;  
      - Regional Integration Specification Packs (EMEA, Americas);  
      - Global Security and Privacy Addendum to the Security and Compliance Design;  
      - Global Monitoring and Observability Design Document.

2.3.3 **Phase C – Advanced Analytics Accelerators (Estimated: 7 months, overlapping with Phase B).**  
The Service Provider shall:

  (a) Identify and prioritize at least **ten (10)** high‐value analytics use cases jointly with the Client’s business units;  
  (b) Design, build, and deploy at least:  
      - Five (5) machine-learning based models (e.g., churn prediction, anomaly detection, demand forecasting);  
      - Fifteen (15) standardized cross-regional dashboards;  
      - A reusable analytics template library;  
  (c) Integrate the Advanced Analytics Accelerators into the existing Platform architecture established under Phases 2 and 3 of the Parent SOW;  
  (d) Provide **Model Governance and Lifecycle Documentation**, including versioning, retraining, and monitoring approaches;  
  (e) Deliver the following Extended Deliverables:  
      - Advanced Analytics Use Case Catalogue;  
      - Model Design Specifications and Feature Documentation;  
      - Pre-configured Dashboards and Analytics Templates;  
      - Analytics Governance and Operations Runbook.

2.3.4 **Phase D – Integration Packs & Enhancement Packs (Estimated: 8 months).**  
The Service Provider shall:

  (a) Implement integration packs with at least **six (6)** additional Client or third-party systems (e.g., new CRM instances, HRIS systems, marketing automation platforms), using APIs, message-based, or file-based integration consistent with Phase 3 of the Parent SOW;  
  (b) Deliver at least **four (4)** Enhancement Packs, each comprising a defined set of enhancements (e.g., additional workflow automations, UI improvements, new data transformations) prioritized by the Program Governance Board;  
  (c) Ensure all integrations and enhancements comply with the performance standards and security requirements specified in **Section 2.3 (Performance Standards)** and **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of the Parent SOW;  
  (d) Deliver the following Extended Deliverables:  
      - Integration Configuration Guides and Mapping Documents for each integration pack;  
      - Enhancement Pack Release Notes and Updated Configuration Guides;  
      - Regression Test Cases and Execution Reports for each Enhancement Pack.

2.3.5 **Phase E – Global Go-Live & Stabilization (Estimated: 4 months per major region).**  
Building on Phase 5 of the Parent SOW, the Service Provider shall:

  (a) Execute region-specific data migration and cutover plans for EMEA and Americas, aligned with the **Production Go-Live Checklist** and **Post-Implementation Review** templates established under the Parent SOW;  
  (b) Provide hypercare support for a minimum of **forty-five (45) days** post go-live in each new region, with extended coverage hours aligned to local time zones;  
  (c) Produce regional **Post-Implementation Review Reports** and a consolidated **Global Lessons-Learned Document**;  
  (d) Deliver the following Extended Deliverables:  
      - Regional Go-Live Checklists;  
      - Regional and Global Post-Implementation Review Reports;  
      - Updated Final As-Built Global Documentation.

2.3.6 **Phase F – Expanded Managed Services (through WO Term).**  
In addition to Phase 6 of the Parent SOW, the Service Provider shall:

  (a) Extend Managed Services to support the global footprint of the Platform, including EMEA and Americas, with coverage windows and incident response aligned to the SLA Extension described in Article XII of this Work Order;  
  (b) Provide enhanced monitoring, proactive performance optimization, and quarterly **Global Capacity and Optimization Reports**;  
  (c) Support the operation and maintenance of the Advanced Analytics Accelerators, including model retraining on agreed schedules;  
  (d) Deliver the following Extended Deliverables on an ongoing basis:  
      - Global Monthly Service Performance Reports;  
      - Incident and Problem Management Reports covering all regions;  
      - Quarterly Global Capacity, Cost Optimization, and Performance Recommendations.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Extended Services in accordance with:

  (a) The performance standards specified in **Section 2.3 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW;  
  (b) The Service Levels in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent SOW, as extended or adjusted under Article XII of this Work Order; and  
  (c) Any additional performance and acceptance criteria explicitly stated in this Work Order or its schedules.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client’s responsibilities set out in **Section 2.4 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent SOW apply to the Extended Services. Without limiting the foregoing, the Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to regional stakeholders and systems in EMEA and Americas;  
  (b) Secure all necessary third-party consents for integrations implemented under this Work Order, in accordance with Section 2.4.3 of the Parent SOW;  
  (c) Review and provide written feedback or approvals on Extended Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days, unless otherwise agreed.

2.6 **Acceptance of Extended Deliverables.**  
The acceptance process in **Section 2.5 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent SOW shall govern Extended Deliverables. For clarity:

  (a) The Client shall have ten (10) Business Days (or a period otherwise agreed in writing) to accept or reject each Extended Deliverable;  
  (b) Failure to respond within such period shall result in deemed acceptance;  
  (c) In case of rejection, Service Provider shall correct material non-conformities and resubmit for acceptance.

2.7 **Key Personnel.**  
The Key Personnel identified in **Section 2.7 (Key Personnel)** of the Parent SOW shall continue to have oversight of the Extended Services, supplemented by additional roles as necessary, such as a **Global Rollout Lead** and **Lead Data Scientist**, which shall be deemed Key Personnel and subject to the same restrictions on removal and reassignment as in the Parent SOW.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value for this Work Order.**  
The total maximum contract value for the Extended Services and Extended Deliverables under this Work Order shall be **US$2,168,873** (two million one hundred sixty-eight thousand eight hundred seventy-three United States dollars), exclusive of applicable taxes (“**WO Contract Value**”). This amount is **in addition to** the total Contract Value of **US$1,017,121** specified in **Section 3.1 (Contract Value)** of the Parent SOW.

3.2 **Fee Structure and Milestones (Work Order).**  
The WO Fees shall be structured as follows:

3.2.1 **Fixed Fees for Phases A–E.**  

  (a) Phase A – Global Rollout Preparation: **US$260,000**  
  (b) Phase B – Global Design & Localization: **US$410,000**  
  (c) Phase C – Advanced Analytics Accelerators: **US$650,000**  
  (d) Phase D – Integration Packs & Enhancement Packs: **US$540,000**  
  (e) Phase E – Global Go-Live & Stabilization: **US$148,873**

3.2.2 **Expanded Managed Services (Phase F).**  

  (a) Additional Monthly Managed Services Fee (global extension and SLA Extension) from the first global go-live under this Work Order through the WO Expiration Date: **US$13,000 per month**;  
  (b) For planning purposes only, Managed Services fees under this Work Order are estimated to total approximately **US$160,000**, but actual amounts will depend on the precise dates of regional go-lives and the duration of services up to the WO Expiration Date. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the aggregate WO Fees under this Work Order shall not exceed the WO Contract Value of US$2,168,873 without a signed Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** of this Work Order and **Article XIII** of the Parent SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
3.3.1 **Milestone-Based Invoicing (Phases A–E).**  
For fixed-fee Phases A–E, the Service Provider shall invoice the Client upon the completion of each Milestone as follows, which is consistent in structure (but not percentages) with **Section 3.2.1** of the Parent SOW:

  (a) **20%** of the fee for each Phase upon commencement of that Phase; and  
  (b) **80%** upon Acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the primary Extended Deliverables for that Phase under Section 2.6 of this Work Order and Section 2.5 of the Parent SOW.

3.3.2 **Managed Services Invoicing (Phase F).**  
The additional Managed Services fees under Phase F shall be invoiced **monthly in arrears**, consistent with **Section 3.2.2(b)** of the Parent SOW.

3.3.3 **Payment Terms (Net 60).**  
Notwithstanding **Section 3.3.3 (Net 30)** of the Parent SOW, the Parties hereby agree that all invoices issued under this Work Order shall be due and payable within **sixty (60) days** from the date of invoice (“**Net 60**”), without set-off or deduction except as expressly permitted under the Parent SOW or this Work Order.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
**Section 3.4 (Late Payment)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to all undisputed amounts payable under this Work Order. For clarity, late payment interest shall accrue on overdue undisputed WO Fees at the rate set out in Section 3.4.1 of the Parent SOW (the lesser of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law), and the Service Provider shall have the suspension rights in Section 3.4.2 if the Client fails to cure non-payment following notice.

3.5 **Expenses and Third-Party Costs.**  
The provisions of **Section 3.5 (Expenses and Third-Party Costs)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to the Extended Services under this Work Order. Any travel, lodging, or other reimbursable expenses specifically associated with the global rollout and analytics work under this Work Order shall:

  (a) Be pre-approved by the Client;  
  (b) Be invoiced in accordance with Section 3.5.1 of the Parent SOW; and  
  (c) Be in addition to the WO Contract Value, unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.6 (Taxes)** of the Parent SOW. The WO Fees are exclusive of Taxes, which shall be borne by the Client as specified therein.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.**  
**Section 3.7 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to invoices under this Work Order. The Client shall notify the Service Provider of any disputed portion of an invoice within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, and shall timely pay all undisputed amounts.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **WO Term.**  
The term of this Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date (10 December 2025) and continue until **8 June 2030** (the “**WO Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the termination provisions of the Parent SOW.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Term.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the **Parent Term** under **Article IV (Term and Termination)** of the Parent SOW currently ends on 17 January 2028. The Parties agree that, notwithstanding the earlier Expiration Date of the Parent SOW, this Work Order shall remain in full force and effect through the WO Expiration Date, and the relevant provisions of the Parent SOW (including but not limited to Articles III–XVI) are hereby **extended and incorporated by reference** for purposes of this Work Order for the duration of the WO Term, unless and until this Work Order is earlier terminated.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
4.3.1 **By Client.**  
Without limiting **Section 4.3 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent SOW, the Client may terminate this Work Order for convenience, in whole or in part, upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the Service Provider.

4.3.2 **By Service Provider.**  
The Service Provider may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the Client, provided that any such termination shall not affect the Parties’ rights and obligations under the Parent SOW that are unrelated to this Work Order, unless otherwise agreed.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
The termination for cause rights set forth in **Section 4.4 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to this Work Order mutatis mutandis. Either Party may terminate this Work Order (and, at its election, the Parent SOW) for material breach by the other Party, subject to applicable cure periods.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Work Order.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:

  (a) The Client shall pay the Service Provider all accrued and unpaid WO Fees for Extended Services performed and Extended Deliverables delivered up to the effective date of termination, plus all approved expenses, in accordance with **Section 4.5.1** of the Parent SOW;  
  (b) Each Party shall handle Confidential Information and Client Data as set forth in **Section 4.5.2** of the Parent SOW and **Articles V and XI** of both the Parent SOW and this Work Order;  
  (c) The Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate in transitioning Extended Services to the Client or a third party, consistent with **Section 4.5.3** of the Parent SOW, at rates to be mutually agreed, which shall not be less than the then-current time-and-materials rates;  
  (d) Licenses for Work Product and Extended Deliverables shall continue as described in **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)** of the Parent SOW and Article VI of this Work Order.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiration of Parent SOW.**  
If the Parent SOW is terminated or expires before the WO Expiration Date:

  (a) The Parties agree that, unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing, the provisions of the Parent SOW necessary to give effect to this Work Order (including Articles III–XVI) shall **survive** and continue to govern this Work Order for the remainder of the WO Term;  
  (b) Either Party may, upon ninety (90) days’ written notice following such termination or expiration, elect to also terminate this Work Order, in which case Section 4.5 of this Work Order shall apply;  
  (c) If the Parties wish to continue the Extended Services under a new master agreement, they shall negotiate in good faith to migrate this Work Order into such new framework.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Provisions of this Work Order which by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Articles III (as to unpaid WO Fees), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provision expressly stated to survive.
