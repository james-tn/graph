  (a) All rights and licenses granted to the Client under this Agreement shall immediately cease, except as expressly provided in Section 4.6.2;  
  (b) The Client shall immediately cease all access to and use of the Software and Documentation and shall, upon the Vendor’s request, certify in writing that it has deleted or destroyed any copies of the Software in its possession or control (except as permitted for backup or archival purposes under this Agreement);

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.** The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production instance of the Software is available to the Client with a monthly uptime percentage of at least **99.9%**, measured on a calendar-month basis, excluding Scheduled Downtime and Permitted Outages (as defined below).

12.2 **Scheduled Downtime and Permitted Outages.**  

  a. “Scheduled Downtime” means planned maintenance or upgrades to the Software or Vendor infrastructure, for which the Vendor provides at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice to the Client, and which are scheduled outside of peak business hours where reasonably practicable.  
  b. “Permitted Outages” include unavailability due to: (i) Force Majeure Events; (ii) Client’s or third-party’s hardware, software, or network failures; (iii) misuse or unauthorized use of the Software; or (iv) any other causes beyond the Vendor’s reasonable control.

12.3 **Incident Classification and Response Times.** The Vendor shall classify and respond to incidents as follows:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Software is unavailable or severely impacted for all or a majority of Users, with no workaround.  
    - Target Response Time: within 1 hour during Business Hours;  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround Time: within 4 hours.

  - **Priority 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired for a significant subset of Users, with a workaround available.  
    - Target Response Time: within 2 hours during Business Hours;  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround Time: within 1 Business Day.

  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical functionality issues or errors with minimal business impact.  
    - Target Response Time: within 1 Business Day;  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround Time: within 5 Business Days.

  - **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
    - Target Response Time: within 2 Business Days;  
    - Target Resolution: in a future Update at the Vendor’s discretion.

12.4 **SLA Credits.** If the Vendor fails to meet the monthly uptime commitment in Section 12.1, the Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  - Uptime < 99.9% but ≥ 99.0%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly Subscription Fees;  
  - Uptime < 99.0% but ≥ 98.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly Subscription Fees;  
  - Uptime < 98.0%: credit equal to 15% of the monthly Subscription Fees.

Service credits shall be applied against future Subscription Fees and shall constitute the Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet uptime commitments, without prejudice to the Client’s termination rights under Section 4.5.

12.5 **SLA Claims Process.** To receive a service credit, the Client must submit a written claim to the Vendor within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the SLA failure occurred, including reasonable details of the incident(s). The Vendor shall review and, if validated, issue the service credit within sixty (60) days.
