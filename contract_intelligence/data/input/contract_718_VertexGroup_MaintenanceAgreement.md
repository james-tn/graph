## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Vertex shall provide to Contoso comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Equipment and associated systems as identified in any applicable SOW. The Services shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **Preventive Maintenance.** Routine, scheduled inspection and servicing of Equipment to prevent breakdowns and extend operational life, including cleaning, lubrication, adjustments, calibration, testing, and replacement of wear-and-tear components consistent with manufacturer specifications and good industry practice.

  (b) **Corrective Maintenance.** Diagnosis, repair, and restoration of Equipment in response to Incidents, failures, malfunctions, or performance degradations, including replacement of defective parts, reconfiguration of systems, and hardware or software troubleshooting.

  (c) **Monitoring and Diagnostics.** Remote and/or on-site monitoring of system performance, logs, alerts, and key operational parameters, as well as proactive diagnostics to identify potential issues prior to failure, to the extent set forth in any applicable SOW.

  (d) **Emergency Support.** 24x7 or otherwise agreed emergency response for critical Incidents that significantly disrupt Contoso’s operations or pose a safety or security risk, subject to the Service Levels specified in Article XII.

  (e) **Patch Management and Updates (as applicable).** Implementation of firmware updates, security patches, minor software releases, and other vendor-recommended updates to supported systems and Equipment, where expressly included in the applicable SOW.

  (f) **Documentation and Reporting.** Maintenance of service logs, Incident reports, maintenance records, root-cause analyses, and performance trend reports, including the provision of periodic maintenance summaries to Contoso.

  (g) **Consulting and Advisory Services.** On an as-requested and mutually agreed basis, advisory services relating to optimization of maintenance schedules, asset lifecycle planning, spare parts strategies, and operational improvements.

2.2 **Specific Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, Vertex shall provide the following key Deliverables as part of the Services, as further detailed in applicable SOW(s):

  (a) **Maintenance Plan.** Within thirty (30) days following the Effective Date, Vertex shall deliver to Contoso a written Maintenance Plan for each major category of Equipment covered, specifying preventive maintenance tasks, frequencies, required downtime, and key milestones for the initial twelve (12) months of the Term.

  (b) **Incident Reports.** For each critical Incident (as defined in the Service Levels), Vertex shall provide a written Incident report within five (5) Business Days after resolution, describing the cause, steps taken, and recommended preventive actions.

  (c) **Quarterly Performance Reports.** On a quarterly basis, Vertex shall provide performance reports that include: (i) number and type of Incidents, (ii) compliance with Response and Resolution Times, (iii) summary of preventive maintenance activities, and (iv) any observed trends or recurring issues.

  (d) **Asset Condition Assessments.** At least annually, Vertex shall provide an asset condition assessment report for major Equipment categories, including recommendations for replacement, refurbishment, or upgrades.

2.3 **Locations.**  
The Services shall be provided for Contoso’s facilities and sites as specified in the initial SOW and any subsequent SOWs. Vertex shall not be obligated to provide Services at locations not identified in a signed SOW unless and until the Parties execute a Change Order or additional SOW reflecting such locations and any additional Fees.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  

  (a) **Industry Standards.** Vertex shall perform the Services in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for providers of similar services for similar equipment and facilities, and in accordance with all applicable manufacturer specifications and guidelines, except where Contoso has expressly instructed otherwise in writing.

  (b) **Compliance with Laws.** Vertex shall perform the Services in compliance with Applicable Laws, including applicable health, safety, and environmental regulations. Without limiting the foregoing, Vertex shall adhere to Contoso’s reasonable, written site safety rules and security requirements communicated to Vertex in advance.

  (c) **Qualified Personnel.** Vertex shall staff the Services with personnel who are properly trained, qualified, and experienced in the performance of such Services, and shall provide appropriate supervision.

  (d) **Service Levels.** Vertex shall meet or exceed the Service Levels specified in Article XII and any SOW. Contoso acknowledges that Service Levels are dependent on Contoso providing the cooperation, access, and information described in Section 2.7.

2.5 **Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly stated otherwise in an applicable SOW or Change Order, the Services and Fees do not include:

  (a) Major capital repairs or full replacement of Equipment;  
  (b) Structural modifications to buildings or facilities;  
  (c) Work required as a result of misuse, negligence, accidents, modifications, or repairs by parties other than Vertex;  
  (d) Third-party licenses, subscriptions, or maintenance contracts for software or hardware not procured by Vertex;  
  (e) Regulatory compliance upgrades driven by new or changed laws unless specifically contracted.

Any such excluded items shall, if requested by Contoso and accepted by Vertex, be subject to additional Fees and a Change Order.

2.6 **Project Management and Governance.**  

  (a) **Named Contacts.** Each Party shall designate a primary contact person who shall be responsible for day-to-day communications related to the Services.  

  (b) **Status Meetings.** The Parties shall conduct status calls or meetings at least monthly (or as otherwise agreed) to review performance, Incidents, upcoming Planned Maintenance, and any open issues.

  (c) **Escalation.** Escalation procedures for unresolved issues or SLA disputes shall be as set forth in Article XIV and further specified in applicable SOWs.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Vertex’s performance of the Services is dependent upon Contoso’s timely and effective cooperation. Contoso shall:

  (a) Provide Vertex with timely access to Equipment, facilities, systems, documentation, and relevant personnel;  

  (b) Maintain appropriate network connectivity, power, and environmental conditions as recommended by the manufacturer for the Equipment;  

  (c) Promptly notify Vertex of Incidents and material changes to the Equipment or operating conditions;  

  (d) Refrain from making modifications or repairs to the Equipment that may interfere with Vertex’s performance of the Services without Vertex’s prior written consent;  

  (e) Obtain and maintain any necessary third-party consents or licenses required for Vertex to perform the Services.

Vertex shall not be responsible for any failure to meet Service Levels or delays in performance caused by Contoso’s failure to comply with its obligations under this Section 2.7.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **May 16, 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Unless either Party provides written notice of its intention not to renew at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, the Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods on substantially the same terms, subject to Vertex’s right to revise Fees upon renewal, as notified to Contoso at least ninety (90) days prior to the renewal date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days** prior written notice to Vertex. Vertex may also terminate this Agreement for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Contoso. Upon termination for convenience by Contoso, Contoso shall pay Vertex: (a) all Fees and approved expenses accrued through the effective date of termination; and (b) any documented, non-cancellable commitments made by Vertex in reasonable reliance on this Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  (a) **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

  (b) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, is the subject of a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) All sums owed by Contoso to Vertex for Services performed and expenses incurred prior to the effective date of termination shall become immediately due and payable;  

  (b) Vertex shall cease performing the terminated Services and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide an orderly wind-down and transition assistance for a period up to ninety (90) days if requested by Contoso, at Vertex’s then-current rates;  

  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4; and  

  (d) Termination shall be without prejudice to any rights or remedies of the Parties that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature should survive termination or expiration (including Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination) shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, visually, in writing, or in any other tangible or intangible form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. 5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:  

  (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  

  (b) was rightfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to its disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  

  (c) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  

  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  

  (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement; and  

  (c) protect such Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information, and in no event less than reasonable care.

The Receiving Party may disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permitted) provide prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party to allow it to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. The Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for such information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that: (a) the Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for legal or compliance purposes; and (b) Confidential Information stored automatically in archival or backup systems may be retained until the ordinary deletion of such backups.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** after termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall remain confidential for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Availability / Uptime.**  
Vertex shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the critical systems and Equipment under its direct monitoring and control are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Planned Maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response Times.**  
Incidents shall be categorized as follows, with corresponding Response Times:

  (a) **Priority 1 – Critical:** Complete outage or major disruption significantly affecting core operations. Response Time: within one (1) hour, 24x7.  

  (b) **Priority 2 – High:** Significant degradation with a substantial impact but operations continue. Response Time: within four (4) hours during Business Days.  

  (c) **Priority 3 – Medium:** Partial disruption or non-critical issue. Response Time: within one (1) Business Day.  

  (d) **Priority 4 – Low:** Minor issues or general service requests. Response Time: within three (3) Business Days.

12.3 **Resolution Targets.**  

  (a) Priority 1 – Critical: Vertex shall use continuous commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within twenty-four (24) hours.  

  (b) Priority 2 – High: Vertex shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within three (3) Business Days.  

  (c) Priority 3 – Medium and Priority 4 – Low: Resolution timelines shall be agreed on a case-by-case basis, commensurate with the complexity of the issue.

12.4 **Planned Maintenance.**  
Vertex may schedule Planned Maintenance that may result in temporary unavailability or reduced performance of Equipment, provided that:

  (a) Vertex provides at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for non-emergency Planned Maintenance; and  

  (b) such Planned Maintenance is, to the extent reasonably practicable, scheduled outside of Contoso’s peak business hours.

Planned Maintenance shall not count as downtime for purposes of the uptime calculation.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If Vertex fails to meet the uptime or Response Time targets in a given calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits as specified in the applicable SOW. The Parties agree that service credits, if any, shall be Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for such SLA failures, without limiting Contoso’s right to terminate this Agreement in accordance with Article IV.

12.6 **Exclusions from SLA Calculations.**  
SLA measurements shall exclude downtime or delays caused by:

  (a) Force Majeure Events;  

  (b) acts or omissions of Contoso or its personnel;  

  (c) failures of Contoso’s third-party vendors or systems not under Vertex’s control; or  

  (d) any other factors expressly excluded in an applicable SOW.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate its effects and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are prevented by the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party continues to use commercially reasonable efforts to perform those obligations that are not impacted.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially prevents or delays the performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
