## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description.** Vendor shall provide to Client advanced data analytics, cloud integration, and managed support Services related to Client’s global operations, as more particularly described herein (the **“Project”**). Vendor’s Services shall include: (a) requirements analysis and solution design; (b) configuration and deployment of Vendor’s proprietary analytics platform; (c) integration with Client’s existing enterprise resource planning (ERP), customer relationship management (CRM), and human capital management (HCM) systems; (d) data ingestion, transformation, and visualization; (e) ongoing support and maintenance; and (f) other related tasks reasonably required to achieve the objectives of this Addendum.

2.2 **Detailed Work Components.** Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, Vendor shall perform the following:

2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (Estimated Duration: 8–10 Weeks).**  
(a) Conduct stakeholder interviews with Client’s business and technical teams to identify key use cases, data sources, and reporting requirements.  
(b) Review Client’s existing systems architecture, including but not limited to its ERP, CRM, and any existing analytics or data warehouse solutions.  
(c) Prepare a detailed **Solution Design Document** describing data models, integration points, system topology, security architecture, and user roles, which shall be submitted to Client for review and written approval.  
(d) Develop a high-level **Project Plan**, including milestones, dependencies, and resourcing assumptions.  
(e) Identify any Third-Party Materials or additional Client-side prerequisites required for successful implementation.

2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Configuration and Integration (Estimated Duration: 16–20 Weeks).**  
(a) Configure Vendor’s analytics platform in accordance with the approved Solution Design Document and Specifications.  
(b) Develop and test data connectors and APIs for integrating Client’s ERP, CRM, and HCM systems with Vendor’s platform, including batch and real-time data flows where applicable.  
(c) Implement data transformation logic, including data cleansing, normalization, and enrichment processes, as required to support agreed reporting and analytics use cases.  
(d) Establish role-based access controls, single sign-on (SSO) integration (if applicable), and audit logging in accordance with Client’s security policies, as provided to Vendor in writing.  
(e) Conduct unit, integration, and system testing and provide Client with test results, including test cases, defects logs, and remediation steps.

2.2.3 **Phase 3 – User Acceptance Testing and Go-Live (Estimated Duration: 6–8 Weeks).**  
(a) Support Client’s user acceptance testing (UAT) by providing test environments, sample test scripts, and guidance on test execution.  
(b) Promptly remediate defects identified during UAT that materially affect the functioning of the Services or Deliverables in accordance with the agreed Specifications.  
(c) Collaborate with Client to define the go-live strategy, including cut-over plans, data migration steps, backout procedures, and communication plans for end users.  
(d) Execute go-live activities and provide hypercare support for a period of no less than thirty (30) days following initial production deployment.

2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Managed Services and Support (Throughout Term).**  
(a) Provide ongoing system monitoring, incident management, and problem resolution for the production environment as set forth in Article XII (SLA).  
(b) Perform periodic maintenance, including updates to Vendor Technology, security patches, and performance optimizations, during agreed maintenance windows.  
(c) Provide second- and third-level support to Client’s internal helpdesk or designated support contact, including root-cause analysis reports for Severity 1 and Severity 2 incidents.  
(d) Provide up to ten (10) days per Contract Year of remote advisory services (e.g., analytics best practices, dashboard enhancements), included in the Fees, with additional advisory services billable on a time-and-materials basis.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Vendor shall deliver, at a minimum, the following Deliverables to Client:

2.3.1 Solution Design Document;  
2.3.2 Project Plan and milestone schedule;  
2.3.3 Configured analytics platform instance(s);  
2.3.4 Data integration connectors and API documentation;  
2.3.5 Security configuration and access control documentation;  
2.3.6 Test plans, test cases, and test summary reports;  
2.3.7 UAT support documentation;  
2.3.8 Go-live and cut-over plan;  
2.3.9 Root-cause analysis reports for critical incidents; and  
2.3.10 Periodic performance and utilization reports as reasonably requested by Client.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

2.4.1 With due skill, care, and diligence consistent with generally accepted industry standards for providers of similar services;  
2.4.2 In a professional and workmanlike manner by appropriately qualified personnel;  
2.4.3 In compliance with Applicable Law, including data protection and privacy laws, as further set forth in Article XI; and  
2.4.4 In accordance with the SLA in Article XII and any mutually agreed Specifications.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.** Vendor’s performance is conditioned upon Client’s timely fulfillment of the following responsibilities (without limitation):

2.5.1 Providing Vendor with timely access to relevant systems, environments, and Client personnel;  
2.5.2 Ensuring the accuracy, completeness, and lawfulness of Client Data provided to Vendor;  
2.5.3 Reviewing and approving or rejecting Vendor’s Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days (or such other period as agreed in writing) of receipt, with any rejection stating the specific reasons for non-acceptance;  
2.5.4 Maintaining and operating Client-controlled systems and equipment in a manner that is compatible with the Services and Vendor Technology; and  
2.5.5 Implementing and maintaining appropriate backup, redundancy, and disaster recovery mechanisms for Client’s own systems and Client Data, except to the extent specifically included in the scope of Services under this Addendum.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Addendum shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **March 23, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, the managed services portion of this Addendum shall automatically renew for additional one (1) year periods on Vendor’s then-current standard managed services fees, subject to a maximum annual increase of seven percent (7%), unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (High-Risk, Vendor-Favorable).** Client may terminate this Addendum for convenience and without cause only upon providing Vendor with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**. Vendor may terminate this Addendum for convenience upon ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to Client. In the event of Client’s termination for convenience, Client shall pay: (a) all Fees incurred and expenses reimbursable through the effective date of termination; and (b) an early termination fee equal to twenty percent (20%) of the remaining unbilled portion of the Contract Value, as reasonable compensation for Vendor’s resource commitments and lost opportunity.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Addendum, in whole or in part, upon written notice if:  
(a) Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of non-payment;  
(b) Client materially breaches any other provision of this Addendum and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof; or  
(c) Client experiences an insolvency event, including bankruptcy, receivership, or assignment for the benefit of creditors.

4.4.2 **By Client.** Client may terminate this Addendum, in whole or in part, upon written notice if:  
(a) Vendor materially breaches this Addendum and fails to cure such breach within forty-five (45) days after receiving written notice thereof; or  
(b) Vendor experiences an insolvency event, including bankruptcy, receivership, or assignment for the benefit of creditors.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Addendum for any reason:

4.5.1 All rights and licenses granted to Client under this Addendum shall automatically cease as of the effective date of termination, except as expressly stated otherwise in Article VI;  
4.5.2 Client shall promptly pay Vendor all Fees due and payable for Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, as well as any applicable termination fees under Section 4.3;  
4.5.3 Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request and at Vendor’s then-current professional services rates, provide reasonable transition assistance for a period not to exceed sixty (60) days, subject to mutually agreed scope;  
4.5.4 Each Party shall return or destroy Confidential Information of the other Party in accordance with Section 5.4 and Article XI; and  
4.5.5 Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive such expiration or termination.

4.6 **No Liability for Termination.** Subject to Article IX (Limitation of Liability), neither Party shall be liable to the other for damages of any kind (including loss of anticipated profits) solely as a result of the termination or expiration of this Addendum in accordance with its terms, except for: (a) unpaid Fees and expenses; and (b) early termination fee under Section 4.3, where applicable.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.** Vendor shall provide 24x7 production operations for the hosted components of the Services, with a target monthly service availability of **99.9%** (excluding scheduled maintenance windows and emergency maintenance).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may schedule maintenance windows during which the Services may be unavailable, provided that: (a) such windows occur outside of Client’s core business hours where reasonably practicable; and (b) Vendor gives at least three (3) Business Days’ prior notice for non-emergency maintenance.

12.3 **Incident Classification.** Service incidents shall be classified by Vendor in good faith as follows:

12.3.1 **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of Service or a critical function with no workaround.  
12.3.2 **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of Service or critical function with a workaround.  
12.3.3 **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical functionality impaired with workaround.  
12.3.4 **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general questions.

12.4 **Response and Resolution Targets.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the following response and resolution time targets:

- Severity 1: Response within 1 hour; initial workaround or mitigation within 4 hours; permanent fix as soon as reasonably practicable.  
- Severity 2: Response within 4 hours; mitigation within 8 Business Hours; permanent fix as soon as reasonably practicable.  
- Severity 3: Response within 1 Business Day; resolution in a future maintenance release.  
- Severity 4: Response within 2 Business Days; resolution as prioritized in Vendor’s product roadmap.

12.5 **Measurement and Reporting.** Vendor shall provide Client, upon request and no more than once per calendar quarter, with a report summarizing service availability, incident volume, and SLA compliance for the preceding quarter.

12.6 **SLA Remedies.** If Vendor fails to meet the 99.9% monthly uptime target in any calendar month (a **“Service Level Failure”**), Client’s sole and exclusive remedy (in addition to any termination rights) shall be a service credit calculated as follows:

- 99.0% – 99.89%: 5% of monthly managed services Fees;  
- 98.0% – 98.99%: 10% of monthly managed services Fees;  
- Below 98.0%: 20% of monthly managed services Fees.

Service credits may be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash. Client must request the service credit in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the Service Level Failure occurred.
