## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF LEASE AND SERVICES

### 2.1 Lease of Premises

2.1.1 **Grant of Lease.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Vendor hereby leases to Client, and Client hereby leases from Vendor, the Premises for the Term, together with the non-exclusive right to use the Common Areas, in accordance with the rules and regulations attached hereto as **Exhibit B** (Building Rules and Regulations), as may be reasonably amended by Vendor from time to time upon written notice to Client, provided such amendments do not materially and adversely affect Client’s use of the Premises.

2.1.2 **Condition of Premises.** Vendor shall deliver the Premises to Client on or before the Commencement Date in good working order, structurally sound, and in compliance with Applicable Law, with all base building systems (including HVAC, electrical, plumbing, fire and life safety systems, elevators, and security systems) in good operating condition. Vendor shall complete, at its cost (unless otherwise agreed), the Landlord Improvements described in **Exhibit C** prior to Substantial Completion.

2.1.3 **Use of Premises.** Client shall use the Premises solely for general office, administrative, software development, data analytics, and related lawful business activities and for no other purpose without Vendor’s prior written consent, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Client shall not use or permit the use of the Premises for any purpose that (a) violates Applicable Law, (b) constitutes a nuisance or waste, or (c) materially interferes with other tenants’ use and enjoyment of the Building.

### 2.2 Services Provided by Vendor

2.2.1 **Building Operations.** Vendor shall operate and maintain the Building and Common Areas in a manner consistent with that of comparable first-class commercial buildings in the Newark, Delaware area. Vendor shall keep the Common Areas clean, safe, and in good repair, including lighting, flooring, and signage.

2.2.2 **Utilities.** Vendor shall provide, either directly or through third-party providers, the following utilities to the Premises: (a) electricity for standard office use; (b) heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (“HVAC”) during standard Business Hours (8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday through Friday, excluding holidays), sufficient to maintain reasonable office comfort levels; (c) water for restroom and standard kitchen use; and (d) telecommunications infrastructure up to the point of demarcation. Client’s use of utilities exceeding standard building usage may be subject to additional charges as set forth in **Section 3.3**.

2.2.3 **Janitorial and Maintenance Services.** Vendor shall provide routine janitorial services to the Premises on Business Days, including vacuuming, trash removal, and cleaning of restrooms and common areas. Vendor shall also be responsible for maintenance and repair of (a) structural components of the Building (including foundations, roof, and load-bearing walls), (b) Building systems serving multiple tenants (including main HVAC units, electrical risers, plumbing mains, and fire safety systems), and (c) Common Areas.

2.2.4 **Security Services.** Vendor shall provide Building security consistent with industry standards, including monitored access control at main entrances, security cameras in Common Areas as appropriate, and after-hours access systems (e.g., key card access). Client shall be responsible for maintaining the security of the Premises (e.g., interior access control) and its own equipment and property therein.

2.2.5 **Technology Infrastructure.** Vendor shall provide, as part of the Services, the core technology infrastructure necessary to support Client’s operations in the Premises, including structured cabling to the Premises, secure network demarcation points, and access to a dedicated equipment room for Client’s telecommunications and network gear, subject to the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

### 2.3 Client’s Use and Obligations

2.3.1 **Compliance with Laws.** Client shall comply with all Applicable Laws relating to its use and occupancy of the Premises and shall not use the Premises in any unlawful manner or for any unlawful purpose.

2.3.2 **Alterations and Tenant Improvements.** Client shall not make any structural alterations or material improvements to the Premises without Vendor’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Non-structural, cosmetic alterations (e.g., painting, carpeting, interior glass walls) may be undertaken by Client upon prior written notice to Vendor and in accordance with **Exhibit D** (Alteration Requirements). Unless otherwise agreed, Tenant Improvements shall become part of the Premises and the property of Vendor upon installation, subject to Client’s right to remove its trade fixtures and equipment at the end of the Term.

2.3.3 **Client’s Equipment and Cabling.** Client may install, at its expense and risk, its own furniture, computer equipment, servers, telecommunications equipment, and specialized cabling within the Premises, provided such installations (a) do not adversely affect the Building’s systems, (b) are performed in accordance with Vendor’s reasonable requirements, and (c) comply with Applicable Law. Client shall be responsible for maintaining and insuring its own equipment.

2.3.4 **Access.** Client shall have access to the Premises 24 hours per day, 7 days per week, 365 days per year, subject to reasonable Building security procedures and any Force Majeure Event. Vendor shall ensure that Building systems (e.g., after-hours HVAC, access control) support such access in accordance with the Service Levels in **Article XII**.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 **Standard of Care.** Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for first-class commercial office buildings and in material compliance with this Agreement and Applicable Law.

2.4.2 **Service Failures.** To the extent Vendor fails to meet any Service Level commitments set forth in **Article XII**, Vendor shall (a) promptly investigate and identify the root cause of the issue; (b) promptly correct such failure; and (c) provide Client with any applicable service credits or other remedies as expressly set forth in this Agreement, without prejudice to Client’s other rights and remedies.

2.4.3 **Client-Friendly Protection.** In recognition of the low risk level of this engagement, Vendor acknowledges and agrees that repeated material failures to meet Service Levels, or any failure that materially impairs Client’s ability to conduct its ordinary business operations from the Premises for more than five (5) consecutive Business Days, shall constitute grounds for termination for cause by Client under **Section 4.3.2**, and the limitation of liability in **Article IX** shall not apply to Vendor’s willful misconduct or gross negligence in performing the Services.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The total estimated contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **Three Million Five Hundred Seventy-Two Thousand Two Hundred Six U.S. Dollars (US$3,572,206)** (the “**Contract Value**”), which includes Base Rent, estimated operating expenses, and technology and facility management services, subject to actual reconciliations as set forth herein.

### 3.2 Base Rent

3.2.1 **Base Rent Amount.** Client shall pay to Vendor monthly base rent in the amount of **US$[●]** (the “**Monthly Base Rent**”), such that the aggregate Base Rent over the Term aligns with the Contract Value, subject to any agreed escalations. The detailed rent schedule is attached as **Exhibit E** (Rent Schedule).

3.2.2 **Payment of Base Rent.** Base Rent shall be due and payable in advance on or before the first (1st) day of each calendar month during the Term. For any partial month at the beginning or end of the Term, Base Rent shall be prorated on a per diem basis.

### 3.3 Additional Rent; Operating Expenses; Utilities

3.3.1 **Operating Expenses.** In addition to Base Rent, Client shall pay as additional rent (“**Additional Rent**”) its proportionate share (based on rentable square footage of the Premises compared to the total rentable square footage of the Building) of Operating Expenses, which include, without limitation, costs and expenses relating to maintaining and operating the Building, Common Areas, insurance, property taxes, and utilities serving Common Areas, as further detailed in **Exhibit F** (Operating Expense Components).

3.3.2 **Utilities in Premises.** Standard building utility usage within the Premises (e.g., typical office lighting, standard computing load, HVAC during Business Hours) is included in Base Rent. Incremental utility costs attributable to Client’s extraordinary usage (e.g., high-density server racks, special equipment) shall be billed to Client as Additional Rent based on sub-metering or reasonable allocation methodologies.

3.3.3 **Reconciliation.** Vendor shall provide an annual statement of actual Operating Expenses for the prior calendar year and a reconciliation of Client’s estimated payments against actual amounts. Any overpayment by Client shall be credited against future Rent (or, if after the Term, refunded), and any underpayment shall be paid by Client within forty-five (45) days after receipt of the reconciliation statement.

### 3.4 Payment Terms and Invoicing

3.4.1 **Invoicing.** Vendor shall issue monthly invoices to Client for Base Rent and any Additional Rent, itemizing the components thereof. Invoices shall be sent electronically to the billing contact designated by Client pursuant to **Section 16.8**.

3.4.2 **Payment Terms – Net 45.** Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of Vendor’s invoice (“**Net 45**”). Client may withhold payment of amounts reasonably disputed in good faith, provided that Client (a) pays all undisputed portions of the invoice by the due date, and (b) provides Vendor with written notice of the basis for the dispute within such forty-five (45)-day period.

3.4.3 **Method of Payment.** Payments shall be made by electronic funds transfer, ACH, or wire transfer to the bank account designated by Vendor, or by such other method as may be agreed in writing between the Parties.

### 3.5 Late Payments

3.5.1 **Interest on Late Payments.** Any undisputed payment not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until the date paid. The imposition of interest shall not preclude Vendor from exercising any other rights or remedies under this Agreement.

3.5.2 **Client-Friendly Accommodation.** Vendor agrees that no late fees or default interest shall accrue on payments that are delayed due solely to a documented billing error by Vendor, provided that Client pays the corrected invoice within fifteen (15) days of resolution of the error.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursements

3.6.1 **Reimbursable Expenses.** Certain expenses relating specifically to Client’s use of the Premises, including after-hours HVAC requests, additional security services requested by Client, or special cleaning services, shall be reimbursed by Client as Additional Rent, provided such expenses are pre-approved by Client in writing (email sufficient) and documented with reasonable supporting detail.

3.6.2 **Taxes.** Client shall be responsible for any sales, use, excise, or similar taxes imposed on Rent or services provided under this Agreement (excluding Vendor’s income or franchise taxes). Vendor shall itemize such taxes on its invoices where applicable.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 **Initial Term.** The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Commencement Date and, unless earlier terminated as provided herein, shall continue in full force and effect until the Expiration Date, **February 8, 2026**.

4.1.2 **Holdover.** If Client remains in possession of the Premises after the Expiration Date or earlier termination of this Agreement with Vendor’s express written consent, Client’s occupancy shall be deemed a month-to-month tenancy, terminable by either Party upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, at a holdover base rent equal to one hundred twenty-five percent (125%) of the Monthly Base Rent then in effect, plus applicable Additional Rent.

### 4.2 Renewal Option

4.2.1 **Renewal Term.** Provided Client is not in material default of this Agreement beyond any applicable notice and cure period, Client shall have the option to renew the Term for one (1) additional period of twelve (12) months (the “**Renewal Term**”), on terms to be mutually agreed, with Base Rent not to exceed a five percent (5%) increase over the last year of the initial Term.

4.2.2 **Exercise of Renewal.** Client shall provide Vendor with written notice of its exercise of the renewal option no later than one hundred twenty (120) days prior to the Expiration Date.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience and for Cause

4.3.1 **Client Termination for Convenience.** Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience at any time after the first twelve (12) months of the Term upon not less than **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor. In such case, Client shall pay Vendor any accrued and unpaid Rent through the effective date of termination. Vendor shall not impose any early termination fee or penalty beyond actual, documented, and reasonable non-cancellable third-party costs incurred specifically for Client’s benefit and approved by Client in writing.

4.3.2 **Termination for Cause – By Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Vendor:  
(a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice from Client describing the breach in reasonable detail;  
(b) repeatedly fails to meet material Service Levels such that Client’s use of the Premises is materially impaired, and Vendor fails to implement a mutually acceptable remedial plan within thirty (30) days after notice from Client; or  
(c) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is the subject of any voluntary or involuntary bankruptcy or receivership proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.3.3 **Termination for Cause – By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement if Client:  
(a) fails to pay undisputed Rent when due and such failure continues for thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice from Vendor;  
(b) materially breaches any other material provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice; or  
(c) becomes insolvent or subject to similar financial distress events as described in **Section 4.3.2(c)**.

### 4.4 Effects of Termination

4.4.1 **Surrender of Premises.** Upon expiration or earlier termination of this Agreement, Client shall vacate and surrender the Premises to Vendor in good condition, ordinary wear and tear, casualty, and Vendor’s obligations excepted. Client shall remove its personal property, trade fixtures, and cabling (if required by Vendor), and repair any damage caused by such removal.

4.4.2 **Outstanding Payments.** Termination or expiration shall not relieve Client of its obligation to pay any Rent or other charges accrued and unpaid as of the effective date of termination. Vendor shall refund any prepaid amounts for periods after the effective date of termination within thirty (30) days.

4.4.3 **Survival.** The provisions of **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XII (as applicable to pre-termination Service Level breaches), XIII, XIV, XV, and XVI**, and any other provisions that by their nature should reasonably survive, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)

### 12.1 Uptime Commitments

12.1.1 **Building Systems Uptime.** Vendor shall ensure that critical Building systems supporting Client’s occupancy (including main electrical supply, HVAC, and security access control) are available not less than **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.1.2 **Technology Infrastructure Uptime.** Vendor’s core technology infrastructure (e.g., structured cabling, demarcation equipment, network access to Building services) shall have an uptime of not less than **99.5%** per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

### 12.2 Response and Resolution Times

12.2.1 **Incident Priority Levels.** Vendor shall categorize incidents as follows:  
- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of power, HVAC, or access to the Premises, or major safety issue.  
- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation of Services materially affecting Client’s operations (e.g., partial HVAC outage affecting a floor).  
- **Priority 3 (Normal):** Non-critical issues not materially affecting operations (e.g., single light fixture failure in a non-critical area).

12.2.2 **Response and Resolution Targets.**  
- Priority 1: Response within 1 hour; target resolution within 4 hours.  
- Priority 2: Response within 4 hours; target resolution within 1 Business Day.  
- Priority 3: Response within 1 Business Day; target resolution within 3 Business Days.

### 12.3 Remedies for SLA Breach

12.3.1 In the event Vendor fails to meet the uptime or response/resolution commitments in this **Article XII** in any given calendar month, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows:  
(a) For each cumulative hour of Priority 1 downtime beyond the SLA commitment, a credit equal to 1% of the monthly Base Rent, capped at 25% of such month’s Base Rent.  
(b) For repeated Priority 2 failures (three or more in a calendar month), an additional credit equal to 5% of the monthly Base Rent.

12.3.2 Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s). If the Agreement has expired or terminated, Vendor shall refund any outstanding credits within thirty (30) days.

12.3.3 If SLA breaches are systemic or recurring and materially impair Client’s operations, Client may treat such breaches as grounds for termination for cause under **Section 4.3.2(b)**.

### 15.1 Definition

15.1.1 “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including, without limitation, acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, governmental orders, labor strikes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), or failure of third-party utility providers.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and probable duration of the event;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the event; and  
(c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Effect on Obligations

15.3.1 The affected Party shall be excused from performance of its obligations under this Agreement to the extent and for the duration that such performance is prevented or delayed by the Force Majeure Event, except for Client’s obligation to pay Rent for Premises that remains reasonably usable. If the Premises become wholly unusable by Client for more than ten (10) consecutive Business Days due to a Force Majeure Event, Rent shall abate proportionally until the Premises are reasonably usable again.
