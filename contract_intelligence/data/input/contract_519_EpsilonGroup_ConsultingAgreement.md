## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **Engagement.** The Company hereby engages the Service Provider, and the Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to perform the Services described in this Agreement and in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties.

2.2 **General Description of Services.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall provide professional consulting services to support the Company’s business and technology initiatives. These Services may include, without limitation:

2.2.1 Strategic consulting and advisory services relating to digital transformation, operational excellence, and business process reengineering;

2.2.2 Assessment, analysis, and documentation of the Company’s current-state business processes, technology platforms, and organizational capabilities;

2.2.3 Design of target-state (“to-be”) processes, organizational structures, and technology architectures;

2.2.4 Development of implementation roadmaps, project plans, and change management strategies;

2.2.5 Assistance in the selection, configuration, and implementation of enterprise software tools and platforms (including, where applicable, ERP, CRM, analytics, and workflow solutions);

2.2.6 Project management services, including planning, coordination, and tracking of tasks, risks, issues, and dependencies;

2.2.7 Development and delivery of training materials, workshops, and knowledge transfer sessions to Company personnel;

2.2.8 Preparation of business cases, financial models, and benefit realization plans relating to the Project; and

2.2.9 Such other consulting, advisory, or related professional services as may be mutually agreed upon in an SOW.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of Section 2.2, the Parties anticipate that the Services will include the creation and delivery of certain Deliverables, which may include:

2.3.1 Written assessment reports summarizing findings and recommendations regarding the Company’s current-state processes and systems;

2.3.2 Process maps, workflow diagrams, and target operating model documentation;

2.3.3 Technology roadmaps and architecture diagrams;

2.3.4 Implementation project plans, including milestones, timelines, resource estimates, and risk registers;

2.3.5 Training materials, user guides, quick reference materials, and other documentation supporting end-user adoption;

2.3.6 Periodic status reports, executive summaries, dashboards, and performance metrics; and

2.3.7 Any other work products identified as Deliverables in the applicable SOW.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.** The Parties shall agree upon specific timelines, phases, and milestones for the Services and Deliverables in each SOW. Unless otherwise specified:

2.4.1 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the timelines and milestones specified in the applicable SOW.

2.4.2 If the Service Provider reasonably believes that any timeline or milestone may not be met due to factors beyond its reasonable control (including delays caused by the Company), it shall promptly notify the Company in writing, explaining the reasons for the delay and proposing a revised timeline.

2.4.3 The Company acknowledges that its timely provision of information, resources, access, and approvals (as further described in Section 2.6) is essential to the Service Provider’s ability to meet timelines.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** The Service Provider shall:

2.5.1 Perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry standards for consulting services of a similar nature;

2.5.2 Assign Consultants with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications to perform the Services;

2.5.3 Comply with all Applicable Laws and the Company’s reasonable written policies and procedures that have been provided to the Service Provider in advance;

2.5.4 Use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure continuity of key personnel assigned to the Project; provided, however, that the Service Provider may replace personnel with others of comparable qualifications and experience upon prior written notice to the Company.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.** The Company shall:

2.6.1 Provide the Service Provider with timely access to relevant information, systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services;

2.6.2 Designate a project sponsor and a primary point of contact for coordination and decision-making;

2.6.3 Review Deliverables, provide feedback, and issue approvals or rejections within the timeframes reasonably specified by the Service Provider in the SOW or in written communications;

2.6.4 Perform such tasks and responsibilities as may be identified in the applicable SOW or otherwise reasonably requested by the Service Provider to enable the performance of the Services;

2.6.5 Ensure that any third-party systems, platforms, or tools necessary for the Services are accessible and functioning, unless otherwise agreed in the SOW.

2.7 **Statements of Work.** Each SOW shall, at a minimum, identify:

2.7.1 A description of the Services and Deliverables to be provided;

2.7.2 The timeline and milestones for the Services and Deliverables;

2.7.3 The applicable Fees, including any fixed-fee, time-and-materials, or milestone-based components;

2.7.4 Any specific assumptions, dependencies, or Company responsibilities;

2.7.5 Any unique Service Levels or acceptance criteria applicable to the SOW.

In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and a SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement, in which case the SOW shall control solely with respect to the Services under such SOW.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.** Unless otherwise set forth in a SOW:

2.8.1 The Company shall have ten (10) Business Days following delivery of a Deliverable to review and either (a) accept the Deliverable in writing, or (b) provide written notice of any material deficiencies.

2.8.2 If the Company timely notifies the Service Provider of material deficiencies, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable within a mutually agreed period.

2.8.3 If the Company fails to respond within the aforementioned review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total estimated contract value for the initial Term of this Agreement is **Four Hundred Forty-Seven Thousand Eight Hundred Forty-Two U.S. Dollars (US$447,842)** (the “**Contract Value**”). The Parties acknowledge that the Contract Value is an estimate based on the anticipated scope of Services and may be subject to adjustment in accordance with executed SOWs or Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** Unless otherwise specified in an applicable SOW, the Fees shall consist of:

3.2.1 **Time-and-Materials Fees.** Professional services billed on a time-and-materials basis at the Service Provider’s then-current standard hourly or daily rates, as set forth in the SOW.

3.2.2 **Fixed Fees or Milestone Fees.** Fixed or milestone-based fees for specific Deliverables or phases of the Project, as specified in the SOW, which shall be payable upon completion and acceptance of the relevant Deliverable or milestone.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  

3.3.1 Unless otherwise stated in a SOW, the Service Provider shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears for time-and-materials Services and related expenses, and upon completion and acceptance of applicable milestones for milestone-based Fees.

3.3.2 Payment of all undisputed amounts shall be due **Net 30** days from the date of the Service Provider’s invoice.

3.3.3 All payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other mutually agreed electronic payment method to the account designated on the Service Provider’s invoice.

3.4 **Late Payments.**  

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted under Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full.

3.4.2 If any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the invoice date, the Service Provider may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ written notice to the Company, suspend performance of the Services until all past due amounts and applicable interest are paid in full; provided, however, that the Service Provider’s suspension rights shall not apply to amounts reasonably disputed by the Company in good faith.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.5.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for all reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and incidental expenses, in accordance with the Company’s expense policies provided to the Service Provider in advance.

3.5.2 The Service Provider shall provide reasonable documentation of expenses (e.g., receipts or expense reports) upon request.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

3.6.1 The Fees do not include any sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes (collectively, “**Taxes**”). The Company shall be responsible for all Taxes imposed on the Services or Fees, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or employees.

3.6.2 If the Company is exempt from any Taxes, it shall provide the Service Provider with a valid exemption certificate, and the Service Provider shall not invoice the Company for such exempt Taxes.

3.7 **No Setoff.** Except for amounts disputed in good faith, the Company shall not set off, withhold, or reduce any payments due under this Agreement based on any claim or dispute with the Service Provider.

4.1 **Term.** The term of this Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date** and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 20, 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.3.1 The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason by providing the Service Provider with at least **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice.

4.3.2 The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon at least **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Company.

4.4.1 Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or

4.4.2 Becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) The Service Provider shall cease performing the Services, except as reasonably requested by the Company to wind down the engagement;  
(b) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments made on the Company’s behalf with the Company’s prior approval; and

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not, by itself, operate to terminate any SOW then in effect unless the termination notice expressly states otherwise, provided that no SOW may extend beyond the Term unless the Parties explicitly agree in writing.

4.6 **Survival.** The rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections 3.4, 3.5, 4.5, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provisions which by their nature should reasonably be understood to survive, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.
