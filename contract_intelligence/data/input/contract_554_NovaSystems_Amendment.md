## PREAMBLE & RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties previously entered into that certain **Master Services Agreement** (the **“Master Agreement”**) effective as of a date prior to the Effective Date of this Amendment, pursuant to which Vendor agreed to provide certain technology, software implementation, integration, support, and related professional services to Client (the **“Existing Engagement”**);

WHEREAS, the Parties now desire to expand, clarify, and modify the terms and conditions of the Existing Engagement in order to (a) define more specifically the scope of services to be performed by Vendor, (b) establish a revised fee and payment structure reflecting the expanded engagement, (c) adjust the term and termination provisions, and (d) set forth comprehensive provisions related to confidentiality, intellectual property rights, risk allocation, data protection, service levels, and change management;

WHEREAS, the Parties acknowledge and agree that the **Contract Value** associated with this Amendment and the services contemplated herein is **Four Million Five Hundred Fifty-Four Thousand and Eighty U.S. Dollars (US $4,554,080)**, subject to adjustments only as expressly set forth in this Amendment;

WHEREAS, the Parties acknowledge that the **risk level** of the engagement contemplated by this Amendment has been assessed as **High**, and accordingly, the Parties have agreed to certain terms that are more favorable to Vendor, including broader indemnities by Client, restrictive intellectual property rights in favor of Vendor, extended termination notice periods, and an uncapped limitation of liability provision, all as set forth herein;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire that this Amendment shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **State of Texas**, without regard to its conflicts of law rules; and

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to set forth herein their mutual agreement with respect to the matters described above, which shall amend and supplement the Master Agreement, and, in the event of any conflict between the Master Agreement and this Amendment, the provisions of this Amendment shall control with respect to the subject matter hereof.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, **the Parties hereby agree as follows**:

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall provide to Client the Services and Deliverables described in this Article II and any applicable SOWs entered into pursuant to this Amendment. The Services relate primarily to the design, configuration, implementation, integration, and ongoing support of a multi-tenant, cloud-based enterprise platform enabling Client to manage operations, analytics, and reporting across its business units (the **“Platform”**).

2.2 **Service Components.** Vendor’s obligations shall include, without limitation, the following components:

2.2.1 **Assessment and Planning.** Vendor shall conduct an initial assessment of Client’s existing systems, infrastructure, processes, and data sources to define detailed requirements for the Platform implementation. This includes stakeholder interviews, business process mapping, gap analysis, and the preparation of a written Solution Design Document outlining architecture, integration points, and security considerations.

2.2.2 **Configuration and Customization.** Based on the Solution Design Document, Vendor shall configure the Platform to meet Client’s business requirements, including but not limited to:
   - Creation and configuration of data models, data schemas, and data ingestion pipelines;
   - Setup of business rules, workflows, and automation scripts;
   - Tailoring of user interface components and dashboards;
   - Implementation of user roles, permissions, and access controls aligned with Client’s policies.

2.2.3 **Integration Services.** Vendor shall integrate the Platform with Client’s designated internal and external systems (the **“Integrated Systems”**), which may include enterprise resource planning systems, customer relationship management solutions, human capital management tools, and third-party data sources. Such integration work shall include:
   - Designing and implementing APIs, connectors, or adapters;
   - Establishing secure data transfer mechanisms;
   - Configuring data synchronization schedules and error handling routines;
   - Coordinating with Client’s internal IT resources and third-party vendors.

2.2.4 **Data Migration.** Vendor shall assist in migrating designated Client Data from legacy systems into the Platform. Such assistance shall include development of migration scripts, execution of test migrations, reconciliation, and validation support. Client remains responsible for the accuracy, completeness, and legality of all Client Data.

2.2.5 **Testing and Acceptance.** Vendor shall perform unit testing, system integration testing, and user acceptance testing support in cooperation with Client. Vendor will provide test plans and scripts, log and remediate defects, and support Client in executing its acceptance tests. Deliverables shall be deemed accepted upon the earlier of: (a) Client’s written acceptance, or (b) thirty (30) days after delivery if Client has not provided written notice of material defects describing the failure in reasonable detail.

2.2.6 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall provide training sessions to Client’s designated personnel covering system usage, administration, and reporting features. Such training may consist of web-based training, on-site sessions, and/or training materials as agreed in a SOW. Vendor will provide up to the number of training hours specified in the applicable SOW; additional training shall be billed on a time and materials basis.

2.2.7 **Support and Maintenance Services.** During the Term, Vendor shall provide ongoing support services, including incident management, bug fixes, minor enhancements, and updates to the Platform components implemented by Vendor, in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII. Support shall be available during Vendor’s standard business hours, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.3 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

2.3.1 **Project Schedule.** Vendor and Client shall mutually develop a project schedule, including key milestones, which shall be set forth in one or more SOWs. The Parties acknowledge that timely performance of Client’s responsibilities, including provision of timely approvals, access, and information, is critical to Vendor’s ability to meet the agreed timelines.

2.3.2 **Delays.** If Vendor’s performance of the Services is delayed or impacted due to (a) Client’s failure to meet its obligations under this Amendment, (b) unavailability of Client personnel or systems, or (c) delays caused by third-party vendors contracted by Client, then Vendor shall be entitled to an equitable extension of time and adjustment to fees as reasonably necessary to account for such delay, as further implemented through the Change Management process in Article XIII.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  

2.4.1 **Professional Manner.** Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using resources with skills and experience commensurate with the requirements of the engagement.

2.4.2 **Compliance with Documentation.** Vendor shall ensure that the Services and Deliverables conform materially to the functional specifications set forth in the Solution Design Document, the applicable SOW, and the Documentation.

2.4.3 **Subcontractors.** Vendor may engage Subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that Vendor remains responsible for the performance of such Subcontractors and their compliance with this Amendment. Vendor shall ensure that any Subcontractors who have access to Client Data are bound by confidentiality and data protection obligations no less protective than those contained herein.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  

2.5.1 **Access and Cooperation.** Client shall provide Vendor with timely access to Client’s personnel, systems, facilities, and relevant documentation, as reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services. Client shall designate a primary project manager to act as a single point of contact.

2.5.2 **Technical Environment.** Client shall be responsible for ensuring that its technical environment, including hardware, operating systems, network connectivity, and security infrastructure, meets the minimum requirements specified by Vendor and is maintained in good working order.

2.5.3 **Client Data and Content.** Client represents and warrants that it has all necessary rights, licenses, and consents to provide Client Data to Vendor for use in connection with the Services and that such use by Vendor will not infringe or violate any third-party rights or applicable laws.

2.6 **Change in Scope.** Any material change to the scope, nature, or timing of the Services or Deliverables, including changes initiated by Client, shall be governed by the Change Management procedures set forth in Article XIII. Vendor shall have no obligation to perform any changed or additional Services until a written Change Order is executed by both Parties.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total contract value associated with this Amendment is **US $4,554,080** (the **“Contract Value”**), which includes all fixed fees, recurring charges, and estimated usage-based fees as specified in the applicable SOWs, subject to adjustments as expressly provided in this Amendment or any Change Order.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

3.2.1 **Implementation Fees.** A portion of the Contract Value, in the amount of **US $2,300,000**, is allocated to implementation Services, including assessment, configuration, integration, testing, and training (the **“Implementation Fees”**). Implementation Fees shall be invoiced in accordance with the milestone schedule set forth in Section 3.4.1.

3.2.2 **Subscription and Support Fees.** A portion of the Contract Value, in the amount of **US $1,754,080**, is allocated to subscription access to the Platform components, hosting, and support services for the Term (the **“Subscription Fees”**). Subscription Fees shall be invoiced in advance on a quarterly basis, unless otherwise specified in an SOW.

3.2.3 **Variable and Usage-Based Fees.** The remaining portion of the Contract Value, in the estimated amount of **US $500,000**, corresponds to variable usage-based fees, including additional storage, premium support escalations, and optional feature enablements (the **“Usage Fees”**). Actual Usage Fees may exceed this estimate, subject to Client’s actual usage, as documented in Vendor’s usage reports.

3.3 **Rates for Additional Services.** Any Services requested by Client that are outside the scope of the applicable SOW shall be billed on a time and materials basis at Vendor’s then-current standard hourly rates, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

3.4 **Payment Milestones and Invoicing.**  

3.4.1 **Implementation Milestones.** Implementation Fees shall be invoiced as follows:
   - **Twenty percent (20%)** of the Implementation Fees upon execution of this Amendment;
   - **Thirty percent (30%)** upon delivery of the Solution Design Document;
   - **Thirty percent (30%)** upon completion of system integration testing; and
   - **Twenty percent (20%)** upon Client’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the final Deliverables, as described in Section 2.2.5.

3.4.2 **Subscription Fees.** Subscription Fees shall be invoiced quarterly in advance. The first invoice for Subscription Fees shall be issued within ten (10) Business Days following the Effective Date and shall be prorated for any partial quarter, if applicable.

3.4.3 **Usage Fees.** Usage Fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on Vendor’s metering and usage records. Vendor shall provide Client with reasonable detail supporting such fees.

3.5 **Payment Terms.**  

3.5.1 **Net 60.** All invoices issued by Vendor under this Amendment shall be due and payable by Client within **sixty (60) days** from the date of invoice (**“Net 60”**), unless otherwise agreed in writing.

3.5.2 **Method of Payment.** Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method. Vendor’s banking details shall be provided on the invoice.

3.5.3 **Disputed Amounts.** If Client disputes any invoiced amount in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within thirty (30) days of the invoice date, specifying the nature of the dispute in reasonable detail. Client shall timely pay all undisputed amounts. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputes; however, nonpayment of disputed amounts shall not be deemed a material breach so long as Client complies with this Section 3.5.3.

3.6 **Late Payment and Interest.** Any amounts not paid when due (other than amounts validly disputed in accordance with Section 3.5.3) shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law. Vendor shall have the right, in its sole discretion, to suspend performance of the Services and access to the Platform if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of nonpayment, and such suspension shall not be deemed a breach of this Amendment by Vendor.

3.7 **Taxes.** All amounts payable under this Amendment are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, excise, or other taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income), and Vendor shall itemize any applicable taxes on its invoices.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.8.1 **Reimbursable Expenses.** Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with Client’s travel and expense policy, if provided and agreed in advance.

3.8.2 **Documentation.** Vendor shall provide reasonably detailed receipts or expense reports for reimbursable expenses. Reimbursable expenses shall be invoiced monthly and shall be payable in accordance with the Net 60 payment terms.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The term of this Amendment shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **June 4, 2025** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.** Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal to the other Party at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, the Term shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods on Vendor’s then-current terms and pricing, subject to annual price increases not to exceed five percent (5%), unless otherwise agreed in a subsequent amendment.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon providing Vendor with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**. Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon providing Client with at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Amendment upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Amendment and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Amendment immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (d) ceases to carry on its business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

4.5.1 **Accrued Obligations.** Upon any expiration or termination of this Amendment, Client shall promptly pay Vendor all amounts due and owing for Services performed and Deliverables delivered as of the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable third-party costs incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf.

4.5.2 **Early Termination Fee (Vendor-Favorable).** If Client terminates this Amendment for convenience pursuant to Section 4.3, or if Vendor terminates this Amendment for cause pursuant to Section 4.4 due to Client’s uncured breach, Client shall pay to Vendor, as liquidated damages and not as a penalty, an early termination fee equal to **fifty percent (50%) of the remaining unpaid Contract Value** that would have been payable through the remainder of the Term. The Parties acknowledge that actual damages in such circumstances would be difficult to ascertain and that this amount represents a reasonable estimate of such damages.

4.5.3 **Return or Deletion of Client Data.** Upon termination or expiration of this Amendment, Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request received within thirty (30) days following such termination or expiration, provide Client with a copy of Client Data in a commercially reasonable format. Thereafter, Vendor shall delete or anonymize Client Data in accordance with its data retention policies and Section 11.4, except to the extent retention is required by law.

4.5.4 **Survival.** The provisions of this Amendment that by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation: Articles III (Fees and Payment Terms), V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and any accrued payment obligations.

5.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party; (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Availability.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to make the Platform components implemented for Client available **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance, emergency maintenance, and downtime caused by factors outside Vendor’s reasonable control (the **“Service Availability Commitment”**).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance on the Platform, which may result in temporary downtime. Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to perform scheduled maintenance outside of normal business hours and shall provide Client with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance expected to impact availability.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve support incidents according to the priority levels described below:

12.3.1 **Priority 1 (Critical).** Complete loss of service or severe impact with no workaround. Vendor shall respond within one (1) hour and work continuously during business hours until the issue is resolved or a workaround is provided.

12.3.2 **Priority 2 (High).** Significant degradation of service with limited workaround. Vendor shall respond within four (4) business hours and use reasonable efforts to resolve within two (2) Business Days.

12.3.3 **Priority 3 (Medium).** Partial loss of service or non-critical issue. Vendor shall respond within one (1) Business Day and use reasonable efforts to resolve within five (5) Business Days.

12.3.4 **Priority 4 (Low).** Minor issues or general inquiries. Vendor shall respond within two (2) Business Days and use reasonable efforts to resolve in due course.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  

12.4.1 If Vendor fails to meet the Service Availability Commitment in a given calendar month, Client may be eligible for service credits. Service credits shall be calculated as a percentage of the monthly Subscription Fees applicable to the affected services, as follows (Vendor-favorable: service credits are Client’s exclusive remedy for availability issues):

- Availability of 99.0% to 99.9%: **3%** service credit;
- Availability of 98.0% to 98.9%: **5%** service credit;
- Availability below 98.0%: **10%** service credit.

12.4.2 To receive a service credit, Client must submit a written request within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the unavailability occurred, specifying the incidents and dates involved. Service credits shall be applied against future Subscription Fee invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash.

12.5 **Exclusive Remedies.** Except for claims arising from Vendor’s gross negligence or willful misconduct, the service credits described in this Article XII shall be Client’s **sole and exclusive remedy** for any failure by Vendor to meet the Service Levels.

## ARTICLE XV  
## FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure.** A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, governmental actions, labor disputes (other than those of the affected Party’s own workforce), failures of telecommunications or internet service providers, and power outages.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.** The Party whose performance is affected by a Force Majeure Event (the **“Affected Party”**) shall notify the other Party in writing as soon as reasonably practicable after becoming aware of the Force Majeure Event, describing the nature of the event, its expected duration, and the steps the Affected Party is taking to mitigate its effects.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** The obligations of the Affected Party under this Amendment shall be suspended to the extent necessary and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the Affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact and resume performance as soon as reasonably possible.

15.4 **Termination for Prolonged Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially prevents a Party from performing its obligations, either Party may terminate this Amendment upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination, except for Client’s obligation to pay for Services rendered and costs incurred prior to the effective date of termination.
