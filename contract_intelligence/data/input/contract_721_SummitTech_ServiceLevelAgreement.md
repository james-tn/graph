## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Client a comprehensive set of information technology services, including, without limitation:

  (a) Provision and hosting of a secure, multi-tenant SaaS platform for business process management and analytics (the **“Platform”**);  
  (b) Configuration, integration, and customization of the Platform to align with the Client’s business requirements as detailed in one or more SOWs;  
  (c) Ongoing system monitoring, performance optimization, and preventive maintenance;  
  (d) Help desk support, incident management, and technical assistance;  
  (e) Data backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity services; and  
  (f) Implementation, training, and advisory services designed to maximize Client’s use of the Platform.

2.2 **Detailed Responsibilities of the Service Provider.**  
Without prejudice to the generality of Section 2.1, the Service Provider shall:

  (a) Maintain the Platform in a production-ready state with a minimum uptime as stated in Article XII;  
  (b) Provide access to the Platform to authorized users of the Client via secure web interfaces and/or API endpoints;  
  (c) Implement and maintain security safeguards, access controls, encryption, and logging designed to protect the integrity and confidentiality of Client’s data;  
  (d) Perform regular data backups, at least on a daily basis for production data, and store such backups in secure, geographically separate locations;  
  (e) Provide standard support services during Business Hours, including Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 support, as described in Article XII;  
  (f) Provide corrective maintenance to remedy defects, bugs, or performance issues;  
  (g) Provide functional and security updates to the Platform, including patches and new releases;  
  (h) Provide a designated account manager as the primary point of contact for managing the relationship, escalations, and service review meetings; and  
  (i) Comply with Client’s reasonable policies and procedures provided in writing and acknowledged by the Service Provider, to the extent such policies are consistent with this Agreement.

2.3 **Detailed Responsibilities of the Client.**  
The Client shall:

  (a) Provide the Service Provider with timely access to relevant data, systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably necessary for the performance of the Services;  
  (b) Designate a project manager or primary contact responsible for coordination with the Service Provider;  
  (c) Promptly notify the Service Provider of any actual or suspected Incidents, vulnerabilities, or service issues;  
  (d) Use the Services in compliance with Applicable Law and refrain from using the Services for any illegal or unauthorized purpose;  
  (e) Provide timely approvals, sign-offs, and feedback required under any SOW; and  
  (f) Ensure that its authorized users maintain the confidentiality and security of their access credentials.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables under this Agreement shall be specified in the applicable SOW(s) and may include, without limitation:

  (a) Implementation plans, project schedules, and system design documents;  
  (b) Configured Platform instances with defined workflows, dashboards, reports, and integrations;  
  (c) Technical documentation, including architecture diagrams and integration specifications;  
  (d) Training materials, user guides, and knowledge base articles; and  
  (e) Periodic service performance reports, including uptime metrics, incident statistics, and capacity utilization.

Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, time schedules for Deliverables are estimates and not firm deadlines, provided that the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet agreed timelines.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  (a) In a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications;  
  (b) In accordance with generally accepted industry standards for comparable services;  
  (c) In compliance with the service level commitments set forth in Article XII; and  
  (d) In compliance with Applicable Laws and this Agreement.

Failure by the Service Provider to meet the performance standards and service levels shall entitle the Client to the remedies specifically set forth herein, including Service Credits, without prejudice to any other remedies available under this Agreement or at law.

2.6 **Location of Services.**  
The Services may be performed at the Service Provider’s facilities, data centers (including those of its hosting providers), remote locations, or at Client’s premises, as agreed in the SOW. The Service Provider shall remain fully responsible for all aspects of the Services regardless of the location of performance.

2.7 **Subcontractors.**  
The Service Provider may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that:

  (a) The Service Provider remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services and compliance with this Agreement;  
  (b) Any subcontractor with access to Client’s Confidential Information or Personal Data is bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth herein; and  
  (c) The Service Provider provides, upon Client’s reasonable request, a list of material subcontractors involved in providing the Services.

2.8 **Service Reviews and Reporting.**  
The Parties shall conduct service review meetings at least quarterly, or more frequently upon mutual agreement, to:

  (a) Review service performance, including uptime, incidents, and response/resolution times;  
  (b) Discuss ongoing projects, enhancements, and planned maintenance;  
  (c) Identify and address service issues or risks; and  
  (d) Plan improvements in service delivery and optimization.

The Service Provider shall provide periodic reports as reasonably requested by the Client, including performance metrics, incident summaries, and capacity reports.

2.9 **Scope Changes.**  
Any change to the scope of Services, Deliverables, timelines, or other material terms shall be handled in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management). Unless and until a change request is approved in writing, the Service Provider shall continue to perform the Services in accordance with the then-current scope.

  (c) The Parties cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly.  

No interest shall accrue on disputed amounts that are later determined to have been properly invoiced.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **November 15, 2025** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Initial Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Unless either Party provides written notice of its intent not to renew at least sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1)-year periods (each a **“Renewal Term”**) under the same terms and conditions, except for pricing adjustments pursuant to Section 3.8, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile and to provide flexibility to the Client, either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party if:

  (a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or  
  (b) The other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver or similar officer appointed for its business or assets and such proceeding is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Persistent SLA Failures.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 4.4, the Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if the Service Provider:

  (a) Fails to meet the uptime commitment set forth in Section 12.2 in any three (3) months during any rolling twelve (12)-month period; or  
  (b) Repeatedly fails to meet critical Response Time or Resolution Time commitments defined in Article XII and such failures materially affect the Client’s business operations.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) All rights and licenses granted to the Client hereunder shall immediately cease, except for any perpetual rights expressly stated as surviving;  
  (b) The Client shall promptly pay all undisputed amounts due for Services performed up to the effective date of termination;  
  (c) The Service Provider shall, upon Client’s written request and at Client’s cost (unless termination is due to the Service Provider’s uncured material breach), provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days following termination to enable the orderly transfer of the Services to the Client or to a third-party provider;  
  (d) Each Party shall return or, at the other Party’s written election, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5; and  
  (e) The Service Provider shall promptly provide to the Client a final backup of the Client’s data in a commonly used, machine-readable format, at no additional cost if termination is due to the Service Provider’s breach, or at the Service Provider’s then-current professional services rates otherwise.

4.7 **Suspension of Services.**  
The Service Provider may suspend the Services, in whole or in part, upon written notice to the Client:

  (a) For non-payment of undisputed amounts more than thirty (30) days past due, after at least ten (10) days’ prior written notice and an opportunity to cure;  
  (b) If the Client’s use of the Services poses a material security risk to the Platform or to other customers of the Service Provider; or  
  (c) If required by Applicable Law or by order of a governmental authority.

Suspension shall be limited to the extent reasonably necessary and shall not relieve the Client from its payment obligations. The Service Provider shall promptly restore the Services once the cause of suspension is remedied.

4.8 **Survival.**  
Provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration of this Agreement shall so survive, including, without limitation, Sections 3.4, 3.6, 4.6, 4.8, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 14, 15, and 16.

  (a) Use the Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
  (b) Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party, except as permitted under this Agreement;  
  (c) Limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and advisors who need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein; and  
  (d) Use at least the same degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar importance, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that:

  (a) Is or becomes generally known to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  (c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
  (d) Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any confidentiality obligation.

  (a) Provides the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement; and

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**

5.6 **Confidentiality Period.**

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
The Service Provider shall make the Platform available **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Downtime (as defined below) (**“Service Availability Commitment”**).

12.2 **Measurement and Exclusions.**  
  (a) Service availability shall be measured based on the total number of minutes in a calendar month during which the Platform is not reachable or usable by the Client, divided by the total number of minutes in that calendar month, excluding:  
   - **Scheduled Maintenance** (Section 12.4);  
   - Downtime caused by Force Majeure Events;  
   - Downtime attributable to the Client’s systems, networks, or misuse; and  
   - Downtime resulting from Client-approved tests or changes requested by the Client (**“Excluded Downtime”**).  
  (b) The Service Provider shall maintain logs and records to support the calculation of service availability and shall provide such information upon the Client’s reasonable request.

12.3 **Incident Prioritization and Response/Resolution Times.**  
Incidents shall be classified and handled based on the following severity levels:

  (a) **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Complete outage or severe degradation of the Services affecting a majority of users or critical business functions.  
   - Target Response Time: within **1 hour** (24x7).  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within **4 hours**.

  (b) **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant functionality impaired with no practical workaround, affecting a substantial subset of users.  
   - Target Response Time: within **2 hours** during Business Hours.  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within **1 Business Day**.

  (c) **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Non-critical functionality issues or performance degradation with a reasonable workaround.  
   - Target Response Time: within **4 Business Hours**.  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within **3 Business Days**.

  (d) **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
   - Target Response Time: within **1 Business Day**.  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: as mutually agreed, typically within the next planned release.

The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet these response and resolution targets.

12.4 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
  (a) The Service Provider may perform Scheduled Maintenance during predetermined maintenance windows (e.g., weekend or off-peak hours), which may involve temporary unavailability of the Services.  
  (b) The Service Provider shall provide the Client with at least **five (5) Business Days’** prior written notice of any Scheduled Maintenance expected to cause more than thirty (30) minutes of unavailability.  
  (c) Scheduled Maintenance shall not exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month, unless otherwise agreed by the Client.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the Service Availability Commitment in any calendar month (a **“Service Level Failure”**), the Client shall be entitled to Service Credits, calculated as follows:

  - Availability < 99.9% and ≥ 99.0%: Service Credit of **5%** of the monthly subscription Fees for the affected Services;  
  - Availability < 99.0% and ≥ 98.0%: Service Credit of **10%** of the monthly subscription Fees;  
  - Availability < 98.0%: Service Credit of **15%** of the monthly subscription Fees.

12.6 **Service Credit Conditions.**  
  (a) To receive a Service Credit, the Client must request it in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the Service Level Failure occurred.  
  (b) Service Credits shall be applied against future invoices for subscription Fees and are not redeemable for cash.  
  (c) Service Credits are cumulative but shall not exceed **25%** of the monthly subscription Fees for the affected Services in any calendar month.  
  (d) Service Credits shall be without prejudice to the Client’s right to terminate for persistent SLA failures under Section 4.5.
