## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the Enhanced Services described in this **Article II** are in addition to, and shall be deemed part of, the “Services” defined in Section 1.16 and described in **Article II (Scope of Services and Work)** of the Parent Agreement. All obligations, protections, and limitations applicable to the Services under the Parent Agreement (including, without limitation, those set forth in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**, and **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)**) shall apply mutatis mutandis to the Enhanced Services, except as expressly modified by this Amendment.

2.1.2 This Amendment updates the data processing terms dated May 16, 2025 (Ref: **DPA-BET-202505-120**) by expanding the categories of Personal Data Processed, the nature and purposes of Processing, and the volume and sensitivity of Processing activities, thereby necessitating enhanced security controls, monitoring, and reporting as set forth herein.

2.2 **General Description of Enhanced Services.**  

Without limiting the general Service description in Section 2.1 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide the following Enhanced Services:

(a) **Advanced Analytics and Risk‑Scoring.** Vendor shall design, configure, and operate an expanded suite of analytics and AI/ML models that Process Personal Data and Additional Personal Data Categories to generate risk scores, segmentation profiles, predictive performance indicators, and other derived metrics as documented in applicable Work Orders. Such Processing shall be carried out strictly on Client’s documented Instructions in accordance with Section 11.2 of the Parent Agreement and Section 11.2 of this Amendment.

(b) **High‑Volume Data Ingestion and Streaming.** In addition to the batch and API‑based data ingestion capabilities described in Section 2.2.2 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall enable and manage high‑throughput, near real‑time data streaming pipelines, including event‑driven ingestion from Client’s systems, with enhanced resiliency, back‑pressure handling, and monitoring.

(c) **Regulatory Reporting Support.** Vendor shall provide configurable data exports and Enhanced Reports and Dashboards tailored to support Client’s regulatory reporting obligations in respect of its own customers, employees, and other Data Subjects, including, where relevant, audit logs, enumerations of Processing activities, and evidence of Data Subject interaction history.

(d) **Security Enhancement Program Implementation.** Vendor shall implement the Security Enhancement Program described in Section 2.4 and Article XI of this Amendment, including enhanced logging, continuous vulnerability scanning, periodic penetration testing (at frequencies agreed in writing), and upgraded access controls (e.g., multi‑factor authentication enforcement).

(e) **Expert Advisory and Governance Support.** Vendor shall provide Client with advisory services relating to data governance, model governance, and privacy impact assessments, to the extent they relate to Vendor’s Services, consistent with Section 11.7 of the Parent Agreement, but on an expanded and more frequent basis as described in Section 2.5 below.

2.3 **Deliverables, Outputs, and Timelines.**  

2.3.1 **Enhanced Reports and Dashboards.** Vendor shall deliver Enhanced Reports and Dashboards, which build upon the Deliverables described in Section 2.2.4 of the Parent Agreement, and shall include, at a minimum:

- Predictive risk‑scoring dashboards updated at least daily;  
- Segmentation and behavioral analysis reports generated weekly;  
- Compliance and audit summary reports generated monthly; and  
- On‑demand data exports (e.g., CSV, JSON, XML) for regulatory, audit, or internal governance purposes.

2.3.2 **Implementation and Rollout Milestones.** Without limiting Section 2.3 of the Parent Agreement, the Parties agree to the following overarching milestones for the Enhanced Services, subject to further detail in applicable Work Orders:

(a) **Design and Configuration Phase.** Within thirty (30) Business Days of the Amendment Effective Date, Vendor shall complete the high‑level solution design, confirm Additional Personal Data Categories, and define initial models and reports.

(b) **Pilot Phase.** Within ninety (90) days of the Amendment Effective Date, Vendor shall deploy a pilot environment for at least one designated business unit, enabling end‑to‑end data ingestion, Processing, and generation of Enhanced Reports and Dashboards.

(c) **Production Rollout.** Within one hundred eighty (180) days of the Amendment Effective Date, Vendor shall complete production rollout of the Enhanced Services to all designated Client business units identified in writing.

2.3.3 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Enhanced Services:

(a) In a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards, consistent with Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement;  

(b) In compliance with Applicable Data Protection Law, the Parent Agreement, and this Amendment; and  

(c) In conformity with the Enhanced Service Levels set forth in Article XII of this Amendment, which supplement the Service Levels in Article XII of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Security Enhancement Program.**  

2.4.1 **Additional Security Measures.** In addition to the technical and organizational measures required by Section 11.4 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall:

(a) Implement enforced **multi‑factor authentication** for all administrative access to systems Processing Client Personal Data;  

(b) Maintain **continuous vulnerability scanning** of relevant external‑facing infrastructure, with remediation of high‑severity vulnerabilities within ten (10) Business Days of identification and critical vulnerabilities within five (5) Business Days;  

(c) Conduct at least **annual third‑party penetration tests** of the core platform environment used to provide the Services and Enhanced Services, and provide Client with executive summaries of such assessments, which shall constitute Vendor’s Confidential Information under Article V of the Parent Agreement;  

(d) Implement enhanced **security event and incident monitoring**, including near real‑time alerting to Vendor’s security operations team and documented runbooks for triage and escalation.

2.4.2 **Security Governance.** Vendor shall appoint a designated security contact for Client and participate in at least semiannual security review meetings, during which Vendor will present summaries of recent security tests, incidents, and improvements.

2.5 **Advisory and Governance Support.**  

(a) Vendor shall, upon Client’s reasonable request and subject to mutually agreed Work Orders, provide up to a defined number of advisory hours per quarter (as specified in a Work Order) to support Client’s **Data Protection Impact Assessments** (DPIAs) and other privacy assessments related to High‑Risk Processing Activities.  

(b) Such assistance shall be provided in accordance with Section 11.7 of the Parent Agreement, but with increased frequency and detail to reflect the high‑risk nature of the Enhanced Services.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities (Supplemental).**  

In addition to Client’s responsibilities set forth in Section 2.5 of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

(a) Clearly designate in writing any Processing that constitutes **High‑Risk Processing Activities**, and provide Vendor with sufficient detail on the lawful basis of such Processing under Applicable Data Protection Law;  

(b) Promptly review and approve configuration documents, model specifications, and Enhanced Reports and Dashboards, and notify Vendor of any requirements for modification;  

(c) Ensure that Authorised Users follow Client’s and Vendor’s documented security and acceptable use guidelines, including credential management and access controls.

2.7 **Sub‑processors for Enhanced Services.**  

Vendor may engage additional Sub‑processors to perform specific components of the Enhanced Services, subject to the conditions in Section 11.8 of the Parent Agreement and the supplemental provisions of Section 11.8 of this Amendment. Vendor’s use of Sub‑processors does not relieve Vendor of its obligations under the Parent Agreement or this Amendment.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Incremental Contract Value.**  

3.1.1 In consideration of Vendor’s performance of the Enhanced Services and delivery of related Deliverables, Client shall pay Vendor incremental fees not to exceed an aggregate amount of **USD $1,885,687** (the “**Amendment Contract Value**”) over the Amendment Term, in addition to all Fees and the Contract Value of **USD $494,248** set forth in Section 3.1 of the Parent Agreement.

3.1.2 For clarity, the Amendment Contract Value is separate from and **in addition to** the Contract Value referenced in Article III of the Parent Agreement. The combined potential maximum financial exposure for Services and Enhanced Services under the Parent Agreement and this Amendment shall therefore be **USD $2,379,935**, subject to any further Work Orders.

3.2 **Fee Components.**  

The Amendment Contract Value may be allocated across the following components (which may be further specified in Work Orders):

(a) **Enhanced Platform Subscription Fee.** A recurring subscription fee of **USD $24,500 per calendar month** for access to the Enhanced Services, including advanced analytics features, additional storage, and enhanced security capabilities.

(b) **Implementation and Configuration Fee.** A one‑time fee of **USD $420,000**, payable in accordance with the milestones in Section 3.3, for design, configuration, pilot, and production rollout of the Enhanced Services.

(c) **Professional Services and Advisory Fee.** A time‑and‑materials fee billed at **USD $225 per hour** for additional advisory, governance, training, and customization services beyond the scope of standard onboarding as set out in Section 2.2.7 of the Parent Agreement.

(d) **Optional Add‑Ons and Capacity Upgrades.** Additional fees for optional features, increased data storage, or higher throughput tiers, as may be set forth in Work Orders or order forms referencing this Amendment.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  

(a) **Implementation and Configuration Fee.**  
   - 40% payable upon execution of this Amendment;  
   - 40% payable upon successful completion of the Pilot Phase described in Section 2.3.2(b);  
   - 20% payable upon completion of production rollout in accordance with Section 2.3.2(c).

(b) **Enhanced Platform Subscription Fee.** Invoiced **monthly in advance**, commencing on the first day of the calendar month following the Amendment Effective Date (or such other date specified in a Work Order), and continuing throughout the Amendment Term unless terminated earlier in accordance with Article IV.

(c) **Professional Services and Advisory Fees; Add‑Ons.** Invoiced **monthly in arrears** based on time actually incurred and documented by Vendor, or usage recorded for add‑ons and capacity upgrades.

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

3.4.1 Vendor shall issue invoices referencing both **AMD-BET-202505-154** and **DPA-BET-202505-120**.  

3.4.2 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** of the invoice date (“**Net 60**”), superseding the Net 45 payment term in Section 3.3 of the Parent Agreement with respect to all Fees payable under this Amendment and any Work Orders referencing it.

3.4.3 Client’s right to dispute invoices and withhold the disputed portion, as well as the Parties’ good faith resolution process, shall follow Section 3.3 of the Parent Agreement, except that any undisputed portion of an invoice shall remain payable within the Net 60 period.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  

Any undisputed amount not paid when due under this Amendment shall accrue interest at the rate specified in Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement, applied from the due date until paid in full. Vendor may exercise its suspension rights under Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement (suspension for non‑payment) with respect to both Services and Enhanced Services, after providing at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice and opportunity to cure.

3.6 **Taxes and Expenses.**  

The tax provisions in Section 3.5 and expense reimbursement provisions in Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to all Fees under this Amendment. Any travel and related expenses incurred specifically for the Enhanced Services shall require prior written approval by Client and shall be reimbursed at cost, subject to documentation.

3.7 **No Set‑Off.**  

Section 3.7 of the Parent Agreement (No Set‑Off) is hereby incorporated by reference and shall apply equally to all Fees payable under this Amendment.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Amendment Term.**  

This Amendment shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date and continue in effect until **November 1, 2029**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or Article IV of the Parent Agreement.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.**  

4.2.1 The Term of the Parent Agreement, as defined in Section 4.1 thereof, runs through **November 28, 2029**, unless earlier terminated. The Amendment Term is currently scheduled to end earlier, on **November 1, 2029**.

4.2.2 If the Parent Agreement is terminated or expires for any reason, this Amendment shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, without any separate notice required, except as expressly otherwise agreed in writing. For clarity, this Amendment cannot continue in force independently of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement (Termination for Convenience) is amended, solely with respect to this Amendment and any Enhanced Services, as follows: either Party may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party.

4.3.2 During such notice period, the Parties shall continue to perform their obligations under this Amendment, and Client shall remain responsible for all Fees for Enhanced Services performed and committed obligations up to the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

The termination for cause provisions in Section 4.4 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Amendment, including material breach of the data protection provisions in Article XI (as amended hereby) and insolvency events. A material breach of this Amendment shall constitute a material breach of the Parent Agreement, and vice versa, where such breach directly affects the Enhanced Services.

4.5 **Effects of Termination or Expiration.**  

Upon termination or expiration of this Amendment:

(a) Vendor shall cease performing the Enhanced Services, except to the extent necessary to perform Transition Assistance Services or as required by Applicable Data Protection Law;  

(b) Client shall pay Vendor all Fees due and payable for Enhanced Services rendered through the effective date of termination, including any committed Fees reasonably and demonstrably incurred in reliance on the continuation of the Enhanced Services;  

(c) Vendor shall, at Client’s written request and at the then‑current professional services rates (unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach, in which case reasonable transition assistance of up to thirty (30) days shall be provided at no additional charge), provide Transition Assistance Services as described in Section 4.7 of the Parent Agreement and supplemented by Section 4.6 below;  

(d) Data export, deletion, or anonymization obligations shall be handled in accordance with Sections 4.6(d), 4.6(e), and 11.10 of the Parent Agreement, and Section 11.10 of this Amendment; and  

(e) Any provisions of this Amendment that by their nature are intended to survive termination (including Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, and XVI) shall so survive.

4.6 **Enhanced Transition Assistance.**  

4.6.1 In connection with termination of the Enhanced Services, Vendor shall provide transition assistance for up to **ninety (90) days** following the effective date of termination, upon Client’s written request received no later than thirty (30) days before termination, to facilitate the orderly transfer of data and functionality.

4.6.2 Such assistance may include: (a) exporting data and model configurations in mutually agreed formats; (b) providing technical documentation; and (c) reasonable coordination with Client’s new provider, subject to appropriate confidentiality obligations.

## ARTICLE IX  
### LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

9.1 **Modification of Limitation of Liability for High‑Risk Processing.**  

9.1.1 The Parties acknowledge the **High** risk profile of the Enhanced Services. Accordingly, the aggregate liability cap in Section 9.2 of the Parent Agreement **shall not apply** to claims arising out of or in connection with this Amendment or the Enhanced Services.

9.1.2 Each Party’s total aggregate liability arising out of or in connection with this Amendment, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), strict liability, or otherwise, shall be **uncapped**, subject only to applicable law and any mandatory statutory limitations.

9.2 **Consequential and Indirect Damages.**

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs) – ENHANCED SERVICES

12.1 **Service Availability.**  

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment supporting the Enhanced Services is available with a **monthly uptime percentage of at least 99.95%**, excluding scheduled maintenance, Force Majeure Events, and outages caused by factors outside Vendor’s reasonable control, supplementing the 99.9% uptime commitment in Section 12.1 of the Parent Agreement.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  

Vendor shall endeavor to schedule maintenance impacting the Enhanced Services outside of Client’s peak business hours, as reasonably defined by Client, and provide at least **three (3) Business Days’ prior notice** for planned maintenance that may significantly affect availability.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  

For the Enhanced Services, Vendor shall meet or exceed the following target response and resolution times, which supplement Section 12.3 of the Parent Agreement:

- **Priority 1 (Critical – High‑Risk Impact):**  
  - Initial response within 30 minutes;  
  - Continuous work until workaround or resolution;  
  - Hourly updates to Client during business hours (and as necessary outside business hours for severe incidents).

- **Priority 2 (High):**  
  - Initial response within 2 hours;  
  - Target workaround or resolution within 8 Business Hours.

- **Priority 3 (Medium):**  
  - Initial response within 1 Business Day;  
  - Target resolution or workaround within 2 Business Days.

- **Priority 4 (Low):**  
  - Initial response within 2 Business Days;  
  - Resolution scheduled in a subsequent regular release or as agreed.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 of this Amendment for the Enhanced Services in any calendar month, Client may be eligible for the following incremental service credits (in addition to any credits under Section 12.4 of the Parent Agreement):

- Uptime ≥ 99.5% but < 99.95%: credit of 5% of the monthly Enhanced Platform Subscription Fees;  
- Uptime ≥ 99.0% but < 99.5%: credit of 10% of the monthly Enhanced Platform Subscription Fees;  
- Uptime < 99.0%: credit of 25% of the monthly Enhanced Platform Subscription Fees.

Service credits must be requested by Client in writing within thirty (30) days following the month in which the SLA failure occurred and shall be applied to future invoices. Such credits shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures relating to the Enhanced Services, without prejudice to any other rights relating to material breach or persistent failures.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  

The SLA commitments do not apply to downtime or performance issues caused by: (a) factors outside Vendor’s reasonable control (including Force Majeure Events); (b) Client’s misuse or unauthorized modifications; (c) Client systems, networks, or third‑party services not controlled by Vendor; or (d) scheduled maintenance conducted in accordance with this Article XII.
