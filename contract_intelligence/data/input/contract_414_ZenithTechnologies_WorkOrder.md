## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client a suite of technology consulting, implementation, and managed support services pursuant to this Work Order. The Services shall include: (a) assessment and planning, (b) design and architecture of a scalable enterprise integration platform, (c) configuration and deployment of such platform, (d) integration of designated Client systems and applications, (e) user acceptance testing support, (f) knowledge transfer and training, and (g) ongoing managed support and maintenance during the Term.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Services will be organized into the following phases, with indicative timelines measured from the Effective Date. Such timelines may be refined in a separate SOW but shall be binding once agreed in writing:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Weeks 1–6).**  
  (a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, system inventory, and current-state analysis.  
  (b) Document business requirements, technical requirements, and integration points.  
  (c) Produce a comprehensive Requirements Specification Document (“RSD”), including data flows, interface definitions, security requirements, and Service Level expectations.  
  (d) Present RSD to Client for review and obtain written approval.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Solution Design and Architecture (Weeks 7–12).**  
  (a) Develop a high-level and detailed solution architecture for the integration platform, including network topology, security architecture, and system interfaces.  
  (b) Identify required Third-Party Materials, if any, and obtain Client’s written approval before procurement or use, in accordance with **Section 6.3**.  
  (c) Deliver a Solution Design Document (“SDD”) describing components, data models, error handling, logging, monitoring, and redundancy.  
  (d) Conduct design review workshops and incorporate Client feedback.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Implementation and Configuration (Weeks 13–32).**  
  (a) Install, configure, and integrate the agreed systems in accordance with the RSD and SDD.  
  (b) Develop integration workflows, transformation rules, and APIs required to connect identified Client systems.  
  (c) Implement security measures consistent with **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and logging.  
  (d) Provide periodic status reports at least bi-weekly, including progress against Milestones, issues, and risk mitigation plans.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Testing and User Acceptance (Weeks 33–40).**  
  (a) Develop and execute test plans and test cases for unit, integration, system, and performance tests.  
  (b) Remedy identified defects in a timely manner, consistent with the Service Levels in **Article XII**.  
  (c) Support Client in conducting User Acceptance Testing (“UAT”), including re-testing of resolved issues.  
  (d) Deliver a Testing Summary Report evidencing successful completion of testing and UAT.

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Go-Live and Stabilization (Weeks 41–48).**  
  (a) Support go-live activities, including cutover planning, data migration (if applicable), and system readiness checks.  
  (b) Provide enhanced support during a stabilization period of not less than thirty (30) days post go-live.  
  (c) Monitor system performance, identify defects, and resolve issues in accordance with **Article XII**.

  2.2.6 **Phase 6 – Ongoing Managed Support (Remainder of Term).**  
  (a) Provide Level 2 and Level 3 application and integration support during the Term, with response times and availability as set forth in **Section 12.2**.  
  (b) Perform routine maintenance, including security patches, minor updates, and configuration changes approved through the Change Management process in **Article XIII**.  
  (c) Provide quarterly performance and capacity reports, including recommendations for optimization.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Vendor shall deliver, at a minimum, the following Deliverables, each in a mutually agreed electronic format:

  2.3.1 Requirements Specification Document (RSD).  
  2.3.2 Solution Design Document (SDD).  
  2.3.3 Detailed Project Plan with Milestones and dependencies.  
  2.3.4 Configured integration platform environment (Development, Test, and Production).  
  2.3.5 Integration workflows and associated documentation.  
  2.3.6 Test Plans, Test Cases, and Testing Summary Reports.  
  2.3.7 User and Administrator Training Materials and Conducted Training Sessions (minimum of four (4) sessions).  
  2.3.8 Operations and Support Runbook, including incident handling procedures, escalation contacts, and standard operating procedures.  
  2.3.9 Quarterly Performance and Capacity Reports during the managed support phase.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skill, experience, and qualifications;  
  (b) materially in accordance with the Specifications, this Work Order, and any SOW;  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Laws, including Data Protection Laws; and  
  (d) in a manner reasonably designed to meet or exceed the Service Levels specified in **Article XII**.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client shall:

  (a) provide timely access to its systems, facilities, information, and personnel as reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  
  (b) designate a project manager or primary point of contact to coordinate with Vendor;  
  (c) review and provide feedback or approval on Deliverables in a timely manner, not to exceed ten (10) Business Days unless otherwise agreed; and  
  (d) be responsible for obtaining any necessary third-party consents for Vendor’s access to Client’s third-party systems, to the extent not otherwise arranged by Vendor with Client’s prior written consent.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Within ten (10) Business Days after Vendor’s delivery of a Deliverable (or such other period specified in a SOW), Client shall either: (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide a written notice of material non-conformity describing with reasonable specificity the manner in which the Deliverable fails to conform to the Specifications.   (a) **Milestone 1 – RSD Acceptance:** US$75,000.  
  (b) **Milestone 2 – SDD Acceptance:** US$100,000.  
  (c) **Milestone 3 – Completion of Implementation in Test Environment:** US$125,000.  
  (d) **Milestone 4 – Successful Completion of UAT:** US$100,000.  
  (e) **Milestone 5 – Go-Live and End of Stabilization Period:** US$100,000.

Vendor’s right to interest shall be Vendor’s sole monetary remedy for late payment, subject to Vendor’s rights under **Section 4.3** to suspend performance for material non-payment.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 27, 2030** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Any extension of the Term beyond the Expiration Date, including for continued managed support, shall be subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date. Absent such agreement, this Work Order shall simply expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Client may terminate this Work Order or any SOW, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason upon not less than thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Vendor. Vendor may terminate this Work Order for convenience only upon not less than sixty (60) days’ prior written notice and only with respect to periods beyond any committed Milestones already scheduled for completion. Upon termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor for (a) Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted as of the effective date of termination, and (b) any non-cancellable third-party commitments approved in writing by Client, but shall have no obligation to pay any early termination fees, lost profits, or similar charges.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any material term or condition of this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed over its assets, or experiences a similar event indicating financial distress such that it is unable to perform its obligations hereunder.

4.5 **Suspension for Non-Payment.**  
In addition to its rights under **Section 4.4**, if Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within fifteen (15) days after written notice of such failure, Vendor may, after providing an additional ten (10) days’ written notice, suspend performance of Services until all undisputed overdue amounts have been paid. Vendor shall promptly resume performance upon receipt of such amounts. Vendor shall not unreasonably suspend Services where such suspension would cause disproportionate harm relative to the overdue amounts, and shall coordinate with Client to minimize operational impact.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  

  (a) Upon termination or expiration of this Work Order, each Party shall return or, at the other Party’s written request, destroy any Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession or control, subject to **Section 5.4** and **Section 11.7** regarding retention of certain records.  
  (b) Client shall pay Vendor for all undisputed fees due and payable for Services performed and Deliverables accepted prior to the effective date of termination.  
  (c) Vendor shall reasonably cooperate with Client, for up to thirty (30) days after termination (at Client’s request and at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates, unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach), to transition the Services to Client or a third-party provider.  
  (d) Termination or expiration of this Work Order shall not affect any rights or obligations that have accrued as of the effective date of termination, nor any provisions that by their nature should survive, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**.

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For any managed services provided under this Work Order, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production integration platform is available not less than **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows agreed in advance with Client (not to exceed four (4) hours per month during off-peak hours) and permitted downtime due to Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Vendor shall respond to and resolve incidents in accordance with the following targets:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical – complete system outage or severe impact):**  
    - Initial Response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
    - Target Resolution: within 8 hours

  - **Priority 2 (High – significant functionality impaired with workaround):**  
    - Initial Response: within 4 Business Hours  
    - Target Resolution: within 24 Business Hours

  - **Priority 3 (Medium – minor impact or non-critical issue):**  
    - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
    - Target Resolution: within 5 Business Days

  - **Priority 4 (Low – cosmetic or informational request):**  
    - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
    - Target Resolution: within 10 Business Days

12.3 **Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the Service Availability target in **Section 12.1** in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to a service credit equal to five percent (5%) of the monthly managed support fee for each one-half percent (0.5%) of downtime below the 99.9% target, up to a maximum of twenty percent (20%) of the monthly managed support fee for the affected month. Service credits shall be applied to future invoices and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet Service Levels, without prejudice to Client’s termination rights under **Article IV**.

12.4 **Exclusions.**  
Service Level commitments shall not apply to downtime or service degradation resulting from: (a) Client’s acts or omissions; (b) failures of Client’s systems or networks; (c) use of the Services in breach of this Work Order; or (d) Force Majeure Events.

## ARTICLE XV. FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), epidemics, pandemics, governmental actions, or failures or interruptions of utilities or telecommunications services not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall notify the other Party in writing of a Force Majeure Event as soon as reasonably practicable and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations impacted by the Force Majeure Event shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. Payment obligations shall not be excused except to the extent Services are not delivered due to the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate this Work Order upon written notice to the other Party, without liability except for those obligations that have accrued as of the effective date of termination and as otherwise expressly provided in this Work Order.
