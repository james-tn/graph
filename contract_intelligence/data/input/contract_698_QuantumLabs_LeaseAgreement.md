## LEASE AGREEMENT

This LEASE AGREEMENT (this **“Agreement”** or **“Lease”**), Reference Number **LSE-QUA-202502-698**, is made and entered into as of **February 15, 2025** (the **“Effective Date”**), by and between:

- **Contoso Enterprises, Inc.**, a [Delaware] corporation, with its principal place of business at **1250 Hudson Plaza, 18th Floor, New York, New York 10019** (hereinafter referred to as **“Client”**, **“Company”**, or **“Tenant”**), and

- **Quantum Labs, LLC**, a New York limited liability company, with its principal place of business at **780 Quantum Drive, Suite 600, Brooklyn, New York 11201** (hereinafter referred to as **“Vendor”**, **“Service Provider”**, **“Landlord”**, or **“Lessor”**).

Client and Vendor may be referred to herein individually as a **“Party”** and collectively as the **“Parties.”**

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Vendor is the fee simple owner, ground lessee, or authorized master lessee of certain improved real property, including laboratory, office, and related support space located at **780 Quantum Drive, Brooklyn, New York 11201** (the **“Building”**), together with associated common areas, systems, equipment, and infrastructure suitable for scientific research and development activities;

WHEREAS, Client desires to lease from Vendor, and Vendor desires to lease to Client, certain premises located within the Building and associated services, equipment, and infrastructure for use as corporate offices, research and development laboratories, and related ancillary uses consistent with applicable laws, pursuant to the terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement;

WHEREAS, Vendor is also engaged in the business of providing specialized laboratory services, facilities management, building services, and related operational support, and Client desires to engage Vendor to provide certain services in connection with Client’s use of the leased premises;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to set forth in writing their respective rights, duties, and obligations with respect to the lease of the premises, the provision of services, payment of fees, risk allocation, confidentiality, intellectual property, data protection, and other matters, all as more fully described in this Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein contained, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF LEASED PREMISES AND SERVICES

2.1 **Lease of Premises.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Vendor hereby leases to Client, and Client hereby leases from Vendor, the Premises, together with the non-exclusive right, in common with others, to use the Common Areas, Building systems, elevators, stairways, corridors, lobbies, loading docks, and other facilities designated by Vendor for common use, solely for the Permitted Use described in Section 2.3.

2.2 **Premises Condition; Landlord’s Work.**

2.2.1 **“As-Is” Condition.** Except as specifically provided in Section 2.2.2, Client accepts the Premises in its “AS-IS,” “WHERE-IS,” and “WITH ALL FAULTS” condition as of the Commencement Date, with no representation or warranty, express or implied, as to condition, fitness for a particular purpose, or compliance with Applicable Law, except that Vendor represents that, as of the Commencement Date, the Building and base building systems (including HVAC, electrical, plumbing, fire and life safety systems) shall be in good working order and in material compliance with Applicable Law.

2.2.2 **Landlord’s Work.** Vendor shall, at its sole cost and expense (except to the extent specifically stated otherwise), perform the base building and fit-out work described in Exhibit B (“Landlord’s Work”), which may include installation of basic laboratory benches, fume hoods, core and shell utilities to lab points, and general office layout, as mutually agreed by the Parties. Landlord’s Work shall be substantially completed on or before the Commencement Date, subject to Force Majeure Events. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize interference with Client’s operations during any punch list or post-Commencement Date activities.

2.2.3 **Tenant Improvements.** Any additional tenant-specific improvements requested by Client beyond Landlord’s Work (“Tenant Improvements”) shall be subject to Vendor’s prior written approval (not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed) and shall be undertaken in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management) and all Applicable Law. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Tenant Improvements shall be at Client’s cost and shall, upon installation, become the property of Vendor, except for Client’s trade fixtures, movable equipment, and personal property.

2.3 **Permitted Use.** Client shall use the Premises solely for the purpose of conducting corporate office operations, research and development activities, data analytics, product development, testing, and related ancillary functions consistent with a modern life sciences, technology, and research organization, and for no other purpose without Vendor’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Client shall not use or occupy the Premises in any manner which constitutes a nuisance or which violates any Applicable Law or the rules and regulations of the Building attached hereto as Exhibit C.

2.4 **Services Provided by Vendor.** During the Term, Vendor shall provide the following Services to Client in connection with the Premises, subject to the Service Levels set forth in Article XII:

2.4.1 **Utilities.** Vendor shall provide, or cause to be provided, electricity, water, sewer, and other utilities reasonably necessary for office and laboratory operations of the nature contemplated herein. Unless otherwise stated in Article III, utilities shall be separately metered and billed to Client at cost plus a reasonable administrative fee not to exceed five percent (5%).

2.4.2 **HVAC.** Vendor shall furnish heating, ventilation, and air conditioning to the Premises during Business Hours (defined as 8:00 a.m. – 6:00 p.m., Monday through Friday, excluding holidays), sufficient for customary office and laboratory use. After-hours HVAC shall be provided upon reasonable prior request, and Client shall pay Vendor’s then-current charge for such after-hours usage.

2.4.3 **Janitorial and Cleaning.** Vendor shall provide routine janitorial services to the Premises on Business Days, including trash removal, vacuuming, restroom cleaning, and cleaning of common areas. Laboratory-specific cleaning (e.g., specialized disinfection, hazardous cleanup, or decontamination) shall be provided as mutually agreed and may be subject to additional charges as set forth in Article III.

2.4.4 **Security.** Vendor shall maintain Building-level security, including controlled access points, CCTV monitoring of common areas, and personnel or remote monitoring during evenings and weekends, consistent with a medium-security commercial laboratory building. Client shall be responsible for any interior security measures specific to its operations within the Premises.

2.4.5 **Maintenance and Repairs.** Vendor shall maintain in good order, condition, and repair the structural components of the Building (including foundation, roof, and exterior walls) and the Building’s base systems (including main HVAC, electrical risers, plumbing lines, fire alarm, and life safety systems), excluding any damage caused by Client’s negligence or willful misconduct. Client shall maintain and repair the interior, non-structural elements of the Premises, including any Tenant Improvements and Client’s equipment, in a clean and safe condition.

2.4.6 **Waste Management.** Vendor shall provide general trash removal for ordinary office and non-hazardous laboratory waste. Hazardous, biohazardous, regulated medical, radioactive, or other controlled substances shall be handled and disposed of by Client in accordance with Applicable Law, using Vendor-approved service providers or Vendor’s own hazardous waste service (if offered) at Client’s cost.

2.4.7 **Other Ancillary Services.** Vendor may offer, and Client may elect to purchase, additional services such as shared conference room facilities, reception services, mail and parcel handling, and laboratory support (e.g., shared equipment, autoclaving, glass wash). Such services, if utilized, shall be subject to additional fees and, where appropriate, documented in a separate Service Schedule or Change Order pursuant to Article XIII.

2.5 **Client’s Obligations Regarding Use.** Client shall:

(a) comply with all Applicable Laws and Building rules with respect to its use and occupancy of the Premises;

(b) not cause or permit any Hazardous Materials (as defined by Applicable Law) to be generated, stored, used, or disposed of in or about the Premises or Building except in strict compliance with all Applicable Law and in a manner consistent with industry standards;

(c) not make any alterations or improvements to the Premises without Vendor’s prior written consent, except for non-structural and cosmetic changes that do not affect building systems; and

(d) promptly notify Vendor of any condition within the Premises that could reasonably be expected to cause material damage to the Building or pose a material risk to health or safety.

2.6 **Deliverables and Performance Standards.** In addition to the physical lease of the Premises, Vendor shall provide the Deliverables described in this Article II and in the Service Level Agreement referenced in Article XII, including:

- Periodic maintenance and inspection reports for Building systems impacting the Premises;

- Incident and outage reports in the event of material interruptions to utilities or Services;

- Monthly billing statements detailing Base Rent, Additional Rent, utility charges, and any other fees or pass-through charges; and

- Upon Client’s reasonable request, documentation demonstrating Vendor’s compliance with applicable environmental, health, and safety standards in connection with the operation of the Building.

Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted standards for comparable laboratory and office buildings in New York City and in compliance with Applicable Law and the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES, RENT, AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that the estimated aggregate value of this Agreement, inclusive of Base Rent, Additional Rent, and recurring service fees, over the Initial Term is **Three Million Eight Hundred Forty-One Thousand Five Hundred Ninety-Five U.S. Dollars (US $3,841,595)** (the **“Contract Value”**). The Contract Value is an estimate only and shall not limit or cap Client’s actual payment obligations arising under this Agreement, except as otherwise expressly stated.

3.2 **Base Rent.**

3.2.1 **Amount.** Client shall pay to Vendor annual Base Rent for the Premises in the total estimated sum of **US $3,000,000** over the Initial Term, payable in equal monthly installments. For illustrative purposes, the Parties agree that the initial monthly Base Rent as of the Commencement Date shall be **US $83,333.33** (equivalent to US $1,000,000 per Lease Year), subject to any agreed escalations.

3.2.2 **Escalations.** Commencing on the first anniversary of the Commencement Date and on each anniversary thereafter during the Term, the monthly Base Rent shall increase by three percent (3%). Such escalation shall be automatically reflected in Vendor’s invoices without the need for amendment to this Agreement.

3.3 **Additional Rent and Service Fees.**

3.3.1 **Operating Expenses and Taxes.** In addition to Base Rent, Client shall pay as **“Additional Rent”** its proportionate share of (a) Taxes, and (b) Building operating expenses (including insurance, common area utilities, cleaning, maintenance, and security), calculated based on the ratio of the rentable square footage of the Premises to the rentable square footage of the Building. Vendor shall provide a reasonable annual estimate of such charges and reconcile actual costs at least annually, issuing credits or additional charges as appropriate.

3.3.2 **Utility Charges.** Client shall pay for its actual consumption of electricity, water, gas (if applicable), and other utilities, as measured by direct or sub-metering or reasonably allocated by Vendor. Utility charges shall be invoiced monthly in arrears at Vendor’s cost plus an administrative fee not exceeding five percent (5%).

3.3.3 **Ancillary Service Fees.** Any optional services elected by Client (e.g., hazardous waste management, shared equipment usage, specialized cleaning) shall be billed at Vendor’s then-current rates or as otherwise agreed in writing and shall constitute Additional Rent.

3.4 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**

3.4.1 **Invoicing.** Vendor shall issue invoices to Client on a monthly basis in advance for Base Rent and estimated Additional Rent, and in arrears for variable charges and ancillary service fees. Each invoice shall itemize, in reasonable detail, the nature of the charges, applicable dates, and calculation basis.

3.4.2 **Payment Terms; Net 60.** All undisputed amounts due and owing under this Agreement shall be payable by Client within **sixty (60) calendar days** (Net 60) from the invoice date. Payments shall be made in United States Dollars by wire transfer or other method specified by Vendor in writing.

3.4.3 **Disputed Amounts.** If Client in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within thirty (30) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature and basis of the dispute. Client shall timely pay all undisputed amounts. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts promptly. Any amounts eventually determined to be payable shall be due within thirty (30) days of resolution.

3.5 **Late Payment; Interest.** Any undisputed amount not received by Vendor by the due date shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law. The assessment of interest shall not limit Vendor’s other rights or remedies under this Agreement, including the right to declare an Event of Default under Article IV.

3.6 **Security Deposit.** Upon execution of this Agreement, Client shall deposit with Vendor a security deposit equal to **two (2) months’ then-current Base Rent** (the “Security Deposit”). Vendor may, but shall not be required to, apply the Security Deposit to any unpaid Rent or other sums due from Client following notice and the expiration of any applicable cure period. Vendor shall return any unapplied portion of the Security Deposit within sixty (60) days after expiration or termination of this Agreement, subject to the condition of the Premises and Client’s fulfillment of its obligations, including surrender obligations under Section 4.5.

3.7 **No Setoff.** Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or as ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction, Client shall not set off or deduct any amounts from Rent or other charges due hereunder. Any setoff or deduction requires Vendor’s prior written consent.

3.8 **Taxes on Rent.** Client shall pay any sales, use, or similar taxes (excluding taxes based on Vendor’s income or net profits) imposed on rent payments or Services under this Agreement. Vendor shall provide applicable tax documentation on request.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date (**February 15, 2025**) and shall continue through **May 1, 2028**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or extended pursuant to Section 4.2.

4.2 **Renewal Options.** Provided Client is not then in material default beyond any applicable cure period, Client shall have one (1) option to renew this Lease for an additional period of **three (3) years** (the “Renewal Term”), on the same terms and conditions contained herein, except that (a) Base Rent for the Renewal Term shall be at the then-prevailing market rate for comparable premises in the Building, as reasonably determined by Vendor and agreed by Client, and (b) there shall be no further renewal options unless expressly agreed in writing. Client shall exercise its renewal option, if at all, by providing written notice to Vendor at least nine (9) months prior to the expiration of the then-current Term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 **By Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience, without cause, upon prior written notice to Vendor of not less than **sixty (60) days**. In such event, Client shall (a) pay all amounts due and owing through the effective date of termination, and (b) reimburse Vendor for any unamortized, landlord-funded Tenant Improvement allowances or leasing commissions actually incurred in connection with this Lease, amortized on a straight-line basis over the Initial Term.

4.3.2 **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon at least **one hundred twenty (120) days’** prior written notice to Client. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide Client with relocation options within the Building or a nearby comparable building. If Vendor terminates for convenience (and not for cause), Vendor shall reimburse Client for reasonable, documented relocation costs, subject to a cap of two (2) months’ Base Rent then in effect.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if any of the following Events of Default by Client occurs and remains uncured following notice and expiration of the applicable cure period:

(a) Client fails to pay any undisputed Rent or other sums due hereunder within fifteen (15) days after written notice from Vendor that such amount is past due; or

(b) Client materially breaches any non-monetary obligation under this Agreement (including, without limitation, unlawful use, unauthorized alterations, or violation of building rules), and such breach continues for thirty (30) days after written notice from Vendor; provided that if such breach is not reasonably capable of cure within thirty (30) days, Client shall not be in default if it commences cure within such period and diligently prosecutes the cure to completion; or

(c) Client: (i) becomes insolvent, (ii) files, or has filed against it (and not dismissed within sixty (60) days), any petition in bankruptcy or for reorganization, (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (iv) has a receiver appointed over a substantial portion of its assets.

4.4.2 **By Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if:

(a) Vendor materially fails to provide access to the Premises or materially fails to provide essential Services (such as utilities and HVAC) in accordance with this Agreement and such failure materially interferes with Client’s operations for a continuous period of more than ten (10) Business Days, and Vendor fails to cure such failure within thirty (30) days after written notice from Client; or

(b) Vendor materially breaches any other obligation under this Agreement, and such breach continues for thirty (30) days after written notice from Client, subject to the same cure extension conditions described in Section 4.4.1(b); or

(c) Vendor experiences a bankruptcy or insolvency event analogous to those described in Section 4.4.1(c).

4.5 **Surrender of Premises.** Upon expiration or earlier termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) Client shall vacate and surrender the Premises to Vendor in good order and condition, ordinary wear and tear, casualty, and condemnation excepted, free and clear of all liens and encumbrances arising by, through, or under Client.

(b) Client shall remove all of its trade fixtures, equipment, personal property, and any Tenant Improvements designated by Vendor for removal at the time of approval (if such designation was made), and shall repair any damage caused by such removal.

(c) Any property of Client remaining in the Premises after the Term may, at Vendor’s option, be deemed abandoned and disposed of at Client’s expense, without liability to Vendor.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of any obligation accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including the payment of any Rent or other amounts due, nor shall it affect any rights or obligations which, by their nature or express terms, are intended to survive termination, including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by competent evidence: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party; (c) was lawfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or (d) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations of Confidentiality.** The Receiving Party shall: (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or exercising its rights and obligations under this Agreement; (b) restrict disclosure of the Confidential Information to its employees, agents, contractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those contained herein; and (c) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of like importance, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.4 ** compelled Disclosure.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law or court order, provided that (where legally permissible) the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates reasonably, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in any effort to limit or protect the disclosure.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible forms of Confidential Information, including copies, extracts, and summaries thereof, except that the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal or compliance purposes, which shall remain subject to this Article V.

5.6 **Survival.** The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall remain in effect during the Term and for a period of **five (5) years** following expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue for so long as such information constitutes a trade secret under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)

12.1 **General.** Vendor shall provide the Services in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in this Article XII. To the extent of any conflict between this Article XII and the main body of the Agreement, this Article XII shall govern with respect to performance standards and remedies.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that essential building services necessary for occupancy (including main utilities, HVAC during Business Hours, elevators, and security systems) are available at least **99.9%** of the time on a monthly basis, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.3 **Response Times.**

(a) **Critical Issues** (e.g., complete loss of power or HVAC, major water leak, life safety system malfunction): Vendor shall respond within one (1) hour of notice and commence remedial action, with continuous efforts until resolved or temporarily mitigated.

(b) **High Priority Issues** (e.g., partial loss of utility services, security system faults not impacting life safety): Vendor shall respond within four (4) hours of notice and commence remedial action within one (1) Business Day.

(c) **Standard Issues** (e.g., non-urgent maintenance requests): Vendor shall respond within two (2) Business Days of notice and schedule remediation within five (5) Business Days, subject to parts availability.

12.4 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may schedule periodic maintenance during off-peak hours. Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to provide Client with at least five (5) Business Days’ notice of any scheduled maintenance expected to materially affect Client’s use of the Premises. Scheduled maintenance windows shall not count against the uptime commitment in Section 12.2.

12.5 **SLA Remedies.** If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.2 in any calendar month (excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events), Client shall be entitled to a service credit equal to a percentage of the monthly Base Rent as follows:

- Uptime 99.0% to < 99.9%: 2% of monthly Base Rent;

- Uptime 97.0% to < 99.0%: 5% of monthly Base Rent;

- Uptime < 97.0%: 10% of monthly Base Rent.

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice(s) issued to Client and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA failures, subject to Client’s termination rights under Section 4.4.2 where the failure constitutes a material breach.

12.6 **Measurement and Reporting.** Vendor shall maintain records sufficient to demonstrate compliance with the Service Levels and shall provide Client with such records upon reasonable request, not more than quarterly, or in connection with a material SLA claim.

(a) promptly notifies the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event, describing its nature and expected duration; and

15.3 **Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event affecting either Party continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially impairs Client’s ability to use the Premises or Vendor’s ability to perform essential Services, either Party may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, terminate this Agreement without penalty.
