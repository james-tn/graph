NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, undertakings, and agreements herein contained, and intending to be legally bound, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND LEASED PROPERTY (AMENDMENT)

2.1 **Reference to Parent Scope.**  
This Amendment supplements and modifies **Article II (Scope of Services and Leased Property)** of the Parent Agreement. Pursuant to **Section 2.1 (General Scope)** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall continue to lease Leased Property and provide Services and Deliverables as described therein and in applicable Order Documents. The Parties agree that this Amendment constitutes an additional and modifying agreement with respect to such Leased Property and Services, specifically regarding the Expansion Premises and Enhanced Managed Services described below.

2.2 **Expansion of Leased Property – Expansion Premises.**  
2.2.1 **Additional Workspace.** Vendor shall make available to Client, as part of the Leased Property under **Section 1.11** and **Section 2.2.5 (Workspace and premises within facilities operated or managed by Vendor)** of the Parent Agreement, additional dedicated workspace located within Vendor-managed facilities (the “Expansion Premises”). The Expansion Premises shall include, at a minimum:

  (a) Up to **18,000 square feet** of open-plan office space suitable for up to **150** concurrent users;  
  (b) Not fewer than **10** enclosed conference rooms (capacity range 6–20 persons each);  
  (c) Dedicated secured storage area of not less than **500 square feet** for client equipment; and  
  (d) Associated common areas reasonably necessary for the use of the Expansion Premises (hallways, restrooms, shared kitchenettes).

2.2.2 **Additional Equipment and Infrastructure.** In addition to the categories of Leased Property described in **Section 2.2** of the Parent Agreement (e.g., commercial office furniture per Section 2.2.1, IT equipment per Section 2.2.2, telecommunications per Section 2.2.3, and access control and security systems per Section 2.2.4), Vendor shall provide, at a minimum:

  (a) Workstations (desk, ergonomic chair, monitor arms) for up to 150 users;  
  (b) Network racks, core and edge switches, structured cabling, and Wi-Fi access points sufficient to support a minimum of **1 Gbps** to each workstation;  
  (c) IP-based phone handsets or softphone licenses (or a combination thereof) for up to 150 users;  
  (d) CCTV cameras and badge-reader access control for all entrances to the Expansion Premises; and  
  (e) Redundant power distribution and UPS coverage for all critical network and server equipment.

2.3 **Enhanced Managed Services.**  
2.3.1 **Infrastructure Monitoring and Management.** Building upon **Section 2.3.3 (Managed Services)** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide 24x7 monitoring of the critical network, power, and security systems serving the Expansion Premises. Monitoring shall include automated alerts, incident ticket creation, and proactive remediation in accordance with the Amended Service Levels set forth in **Article XII** of this Amendment.

2.3.2 **Workspace Services.** In addition to the standard building services described in **Section 2.3.4 (Workspace Services)** of the Parent Agreement (including utilities, cleaning of common areas, security access, and structural maintenance), Vendor shall:

  (a) Provide extended HVAC coverage on Business Days from **6:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. local time**, with after-hours availability upon 24 hours’ notice;  
  (b) Perform daily cleaning of Client-occupied office areas (excluding desks with personal items) and twice-daily cleaning of restrooms serving the Expansion Premises;  
  (c) Maintain a staffed reception desk for visitor management during Business Days from 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. local time; and  
  (d) Coordinate with building security to ensure that only Client-authorized personnel and visitors have access to the Expansion Premises.

2.3.3 **Onboarding, Training, and Documentation.** Consistent with **Sections 2.3.5 and 2.3.6** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide:

  (a) Initial onboarding training for Client’s facilities and IT operations teams on the operation and support processes associated with the Expansion Premises;  
  (b) Updated floor plans, network diagrams, and rack elevation diagrams reflecting the deployed Leased Property; and  
  (c) Monthly utilization and incident reports specific to the Expansion Premises.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
The performance obligations of Vendor under **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement are hereby reaffirmed and extended to the Expansion Premises and Enhanced Managed Services. Vendor shall perform all Services required under this Amendment in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with: (a) the Specifications associated with the Expansion Premises; (b) the Amended Service Levels set forth in **Article XII** of this Amendment; (c) applicable industry standards; and (d) all applicable laws and regulations, including building codes, fire and life safety regulations, and data protection laws under **Article XI** of the Parent Agreement.

2.5 **Implementation Timelines and Milestones.**  
2.5.1 **Project Plan.** In furtherance of **Section 2.5 (Project Plans and Timelines)** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties shall agree upon and document in an Amendment Order Document a detailed project plan for the build-out and activation of the Expansion Premises, including the following key milestones:

  (a) Design and configuration sign-off: within **30 days** following the Amendment Effective Date;  
  (b) Completion of physical build-out, including furniture and cabling: within **120 days** following design sign-off;  
  (c) Network and security systems deployment: within **30 days** following completion of physical build-out;  
  (d) Go-live date for Client occupancy: no later than **180 days** following design sign-off, subject to any mutually agreed adjustments due to Client-caused delays or Force Majeure Events pursuant to **Article XV** of the Parent Agreement.

2.5.2 **Delay Attribution.** Delays attributable to Client’s failure to provide timely approvals, information, or access (per **Section 2.5** and **Section 2.6.1 (Access and Cooperation)** of the Parent Agreement), or to Force Majeure Events, shall excuse Vendor from performance deadlines to the extent impacted. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate delays and keep Client informed of revised timelines.

2.6 **Client Obligations for Expansion Premises.**  
The obligations of Client under **Section 2.6 (Client Obligations and Use of Leased Property)** of the Parent Agreement apply to the Expansion Premises and Enhanced Managed Services, including without limitation:

  (a) Providing Vendor with reasonable access to Client personnel and decision-makers;  
  (b) Complying with **Use Restrictions** under Section 2.6.2, including not modifying or subleasing the Expansion Premises or Leased Property without Vendor’s written consent;  
  (c) Ensuring the Client-supplied environment (if any co-located Client equipment is deployed) meets the environmental requirements set forth in the relevant Amendment Order Document; and  
  (d) Promptly notifying Vendor of any damage to or malfunction of the Leased Property in accordance with **Section 2.6.4 (Damage and Loss)**.

2.7 **Title to Leased Property.**  
The Parties reaffirm **Section 2.7 (Title to Leased Property)** of the Parent Agreement. All furniture, fixtures, networking equipment, telecommunications systems, security equipment, and other tangible property provided under this Amendment shall constitute “Leased Property” for purposes of the Parent Agreement, and title thereto shall remain with Vendor or its lessors at all times.

2.8 **Acceptance of Expansion Premises and Equipment.**  
Acceptance of the Expansion Premises and associated Leased Property shall follow the process described in **Section 2.8 (Acceptance)** of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented by the Parties in the applicable Amendment Order Document. Unless otherwise agreed:

  (a) Client shall have **thirty (30) days** following the Go-live date to test and inspect the Expansion Premises and equipment for material conformance to the Specifications;  
  (b) If Client does not reject the Expansion Premises or specific Leased Property by providing written notice of material non-conformance within such period, they shall be deemed accepted; and  
  (c) Client’s productive use of the Expansion Premises for its business operations shall constitute acceptance.

2.9 **Subcontractors.**  
Vendor may engage subcontractors in accordance with **Section 2.9 (Subcontractors)** of the Parent Agreement to perform portions of the build-out or Enhanced Managed Services for the Expansion Premises, provided Vendor remains responsible for their performance and ensures appropriate confidentiality and data protection obligations.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS (AMENDMENT)

3.1 **Amendment Contract Value; Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  
3.1.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the **Amendment Contract Value** for the Expansion Premises and Enhanced Managed Services under this Amendment is estimated at **US$1,986,787** for the Amendment Term. This amount is **in addition to**, and shall not limit or reduce, the Contract Value of **US$1,596,968** stated in the Parent Agreement (see Recitals and **Section 3.1** of the Parent Agreement).  

3.1.2 Unless expressly stated otherwise, all references to “Fees” and “Contract Value” in the Parent Agreement shall be deemed to include the fees payable under this Amendment.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees payable under this Amendment shall include:

  (a) **Base Lease Charges.** Monthly lease charges for the Expansion Premises (workspace and associated furniture) in the amount of **US$62,500** per month during the Amendment Term;  
  (b) **Infrastructure and Equipment Charges.** Monthly lease charges for network, security, and telecommunications equipment in the amount of **US$18,750** per month;  
  (c) **Enhanced Managed Services Fees.** Monthly recurring fees for monitoring, incident management, and workspace services in the amount of **US$21,500** per month;  
  (d) **One-Time Implementation Fees.** A one-time implementation and build-out fee of **US$375,000**, invoiced 40% upon design sign-off, 40% upon completion of build-out, and 20% upon Go-live acceptance; and  
  (e) **Change Orders and Additional Services.** Any additional fees associated with approved Change Orders under **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Net 45.**  
3.3.1 The **Payment Terms** under **Section 3.2 (Payment Terms; Net 45)** of the Parent Agreement are hereby reaffirmed and shall apply to all invoices issued under this Amendment. Vendor shall invoice Client:

  (a) Monthly in arrears for recurring lease and service fees; and  
  (b) In accordance with the milestone schedule in **Section 3.2(d)** above for one-time implementation fees.

3.3.2 Client shall pay all **undisputed** amounts within **forty-five (45) calendar days** from the date of receipt of each valid invoice (“Net 45”). Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed electronic method.

3.4 **Invoice Disputes.**  
The procedures in **Section 3.3 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent Agreement apply. Client shall provide written notice of any dispute in good faith within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the applicable invoice, specifying the basis and amounts disputed. Client shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Net 45, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the disputed portion.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest in accordance with **Section 3.4 (Late Payment)** of the Parent Agreement. Vendor shall have the right to suspend Services specific to the Expansion Premises and Enhanced Managed Services if undisputed amounts remain unpaid more than sixty (60) days after the due date, following at least ten (10) days’ prior written notice and an opportunity to cure, provided such suspension is proportionate and does not unreasonably affect unrelated Services under other Order Documents.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Vendor’s travel and lodging expenses incurred in connection with the implementation and ongoing support of the Expansion Premises shall be reimbursed in accordance with **Section 3.5 (Expenses and Reimbursements)** of the Parent Agreement. Vendor shall obtain Client’s prior written approval before incurring travel expenses in excess of **US$1,000** for a single trip.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
Taxes associated with the fees under this Amendment shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.6 (Taxes)** of the Parent Agreement. Vendor shall itemize all applicable Taxes on its invoices, and Client shall be responsible for such Taxes, except for Taxes based on Vendor’s net income, franchise, or property.

3.8 **No Set-Off.**  
The prohibition on set-off in **Section 3.7 (No Set-Off)** of the Parent Agreement applies equally to amounts due under this Amendment, except as may be expressly agreed by Vendor in writing.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION (AMENDMENT)

4.1 **Amendment Term.**  
The term of this Amendment (the “Amendment Term”) shall commence on the **Amendment Effective Date (June 20, 2026)** and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent Agreement, shall continue in effect until **January 15, 2028** (the “Amendment Expiration Date”). For clarity, the termination or expiration of this Amendment **does not by itself terminate** the Parent Agreement, except as expressly provided in Section 4.5 below.

4.2 **Renewal of Amendment Scope.**  
Any extension or renewal of the lease or services associated with the Expansion Premises beyond the Amendment Expiration Date shall be subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties, documented in a further amendment or Order Document executed pursuant to **Section 16.3 (Amendments)** of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Client-Friendly).**  
4.3.1 **By Client.** In addition to Client’s termination rights under **Section 4.2 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent Agreement and in light of the “Low” Risk Level designation, Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part (e.g., only the Enhanced Managed Services portion), for convenience and without cause upon not less than **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor.

4.3.2 **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Client, provided Vendor: (a) continues to perform all Services and maintain Leased Property in accordance with this Amendment during the notice period; and (b) reasonably cooperates with Client’s transition efforts pursuant to **Section 4.5** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 4.6** of this Amendment.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
The termination for cause provisions of **Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Amendment. Either Party may terminate this Amendment (and/or the Parent Agreement) for material uncured breach or insolvency as set forth in Sections 4.3.1 and 4.3.2 of the Parent Agreement. Termination for cause of the Parent Agreement automatically terminates this Amendment.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement on Amendment.**  
4.5.1 If the Parent Agreement **expires or is terminated** for any reason, this Amendment shall automatically terminate as of the same effective date, without the need for further action, and the cessation of Services and return of Leased Property shall be governed by **Section 4.4 (Effect of Termination)** of the Parent Agreement as supplemented by this Amendment.

4.5.2 If this Amendment is terminated independently (e.g., Client exercises its convenience termination rights solely with respect to the Expansion Premises), the Parent Agreement and other Order Documents shall remain in effect in accordance with their terms, except to the extent directly impacted by such termination.

4.6 **Effect of Termination; Return of Leased Property; Transition Assistance.**  
4.6.1 **Cessation of Services.** Upon termination or expiration of this Amendment, Vendor shall cease providing the affected Services related to the Expansion Premises and Enhanced Managed Services, and Client shall cease using such Leased Property, except as necessary to complete agreed Transition Assistance.

4.6.2 **Return of Leased Property.** The return and de-installation process shall follow **Section 4.4.2 (Return of Leased Property)** of the Parent Agreement. Vendor shall remove the Leased Property from the Expansion Premises within **thirty (30) days** following termination or expiration, at Vendor’s cost unless termination is due to Client’s uncured material breach, in which case Client shall bear reasonable direct de-installation and shipping costs.

4.6.3 **Accrued Rights and Payment.** Termination or expiration of this Amendment shall not relieve either Party of any accrued payment or other obligations incurred prior to the effective date of termination, in accordance with **Section 4.4.3** of the Parent Agreement.

4.6.4 **Transition Assistance (Client-Friendly).** In addition to **Section 4.5 (Transition Assistance)** of the Parent Agreement, and in recognition of the Low Risk Level, Vendor shall, upon Client’s request in connection with termination or expiration of this Amendment (other than termination by Vendor for Client’s uncured material breach), provide reasonable Transition Assistance for up to **ninety (90) days** (an extension from the sixty (60) days in the Parent Agreement) at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates, or at such other preferential rates as may be set forth in an Amendment Order Document.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The survival provisions in **Section 4.4.4** and **Article XVI** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Amendment. Without limiting the foregoing, the following provisions of this Amendment shall expressly survive termination or expiration: **Articles III (to the extent of payment obligations accrued), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (with respect to earned but unused service credits), XIV, XV, and XVI**, together with any other provisions that by their nature should survive.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY (AMENDMENT)

5.1 **Reference to Parent Agreement.**  
The Parties reaffirm the confidentiality obligations set forth in **Article V (Confidentiality)** of the Parent Agreement. For purposes of this Amendment, all Confidential Information (as defined in **Section 5.1** of the Parent Agreement) relating to the Expansion Premises, floor plans, security configurations, incident reports, and Client occupancy details shall be deemed Confidential Information of Client, unless clearly identified as Vendor’s Confidential Information (e.g., proprietary tools, methodologies).

5.2 **Additional Confidentiality Measures for Expansion Premises.**  
In recognition of the increased sensitivity of physical and logical layouts of the Expansion Premises:

  (a) Vendor shall restrict access to detailed floor plans, network diagrams, and security system configurations to only those of its personnel and subcontractors with a need to know such information;  
  (b) Vendor shall ensure that all such personnel are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in the Parent Agreement; and  
  (c) Vendor shall not use photos, videos, or descriptions of the Expansion Premises for marketing or promotional purposes without Client’s prior written consent.

5.3 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations in this Amendment shall be subject to the survival period set forth in **Section 5.7 (Survival)** of the Parent Agreement (five (5) years following termination or expiration, or longer for trade secrets), and such survival applies equally to Confidential Information exchanged under this Amendment.
