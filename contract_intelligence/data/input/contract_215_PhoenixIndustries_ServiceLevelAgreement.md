## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall provide to Client the Services described in this Article II and any applicable SOWs. The Services are high-availability, enterprise-grade, managed IT services and associated support for Client’s production systems, including, without limitation:

  2.1.1 Managed hosting of Client’s critical applications and databases on Vendor Systems in a secure, redundant data center environment;

  2.1.2 24x7x365 monitoring and incident management of hosted environments;

  2.1.3 Operating system, middleware, and application layer support for designated systems as specified in the applicable SOW;

  2.1.4 Routine maintenance, patching, and upgrades for systems under management;

  2.1.5 Backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity support as further defined in Section 2.5;

  2.1.6 Service desk and technical support for designated Client contacts, including incident, problem, and change management.

2.2 **Detailed Service Description.** Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Services shall include the following components:

  2.2.1 **Infrastructure Hosting.** Vendor shall provision and maintain server, storage, and network resources in a virtualized or dedicated configuration sufficient to support the workloads described in the applicable SOW. Vendor shall maintain redundant power, network connectivity, and environmental controls designed to achieve the Service Levels in Article XII, including a target system availability of 99.9% per calendar month, excluding Scheduled Downtime.

  2.2.2 **Monitoring and Incident Management.** Vendor shall deploy monitoring tools to track system performance, availability, and capacity for the managed environments. Monitoring shall include CPU, memory, disk utilization, network connectivity, and application health (where technically feasible). Vendor shall operate a 24x7x365 Network Operations Center (NOC) to receive and respond to alerts. Upon detection of an incident, Vendor shall log the incident, assign severity, and respond in accordance with the response and resolution times set forth in Section 12.3.

  2.2.3 **Application Support.** For those applications identified in an SOW as “Supported Applications,” Vendor shall provide Level 2 and Level 3 support, including troubleshooting, configuration changes, and deployment support, subject to the constraints of third-party licensing and Vendor’s reasonable technical capabilities.

  2.2.4 **Maintenance and Patching.** Vendor shall perform routine maintenance activities, including vendor-recommended security patches, operating system updates, and minor software upgrades, during Scheduled Downtime windows agreed with Client. Vendor shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ advance notice of non-emergency maintenance that may impact availability and may conduct emergency maintenance as reasonably required to address security vulnerabilities or system instability, using commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption.

  2.2.5 **Backup and Recovery.** Vendor shall perform daily incremental and weekly full backups of designated systems and Client Data, with retention periods defined in the applicable SOW (which shall be no less than thirty (30) days unless otherwise agreed). Vendor shall test restoration procedures at least annually and shall retain evidence of such tests upon Client request. Disaster recovery procedures, recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO) shall be defined in the SOW, and Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet such objectives under non-catastrophic conditions.

  2.2.6 **Capacity Management.** Vendor shall monitor capacity trends and shall notify Client when resource utilization regularly exceeds agreed thresholds. Any capacity upgrade requiring additional fees shall be documented in a Change Request pursuant to Article XIII.

  2.2.7 **Service Desk.** Vendor shall operate a service desk accessible by email, web portal, and telephone for designated Client contacts. The service desk shall be available 24x7x365 for Severity 1 incidents and at least during Business Hours for lower-severity requests, unless otherwise specified in the SOW.

2.3 **Deliverables.** In performing the Services, Vendor shall produce and deliver to Client certain Deliverables, which may include:

  2.3.1 System architecture diagrams, configuration documentation, and inventories of managed components;

  2.3.2 Monthly Service Level reports detailing uptime, incident volumes, response and resolution times, and any Service Level deviations;

  2.3.3 Security and compliance reports related to penetration testing (if performed), vulnerability scans, and access control reviews, as mutually agreed;

  2.3.4 Project plans and implementation documentation in connection with onboarding, migrations, or significant changes.

All Deliverables shall conform in all material respects to the Specifications defined in the applicable SOW.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.** Vendor’s performance of the Services is contingent upon Client’s timely performance of the following obligations:

  2.4.1 Providing Vendor with necessary access (remote and, where applicable, physical) to systems, networks, personnel, and facilities as reasonably required to perform the Services;

  2.4.2 Designating qualified personnel as primary and secondary contacts for operational and escalation matters;

  2.4.3 Providing accurate and complete information regarding Client’s existing systems, applications, and dependencies relevant to the Services;

  2.4.4 Obtaining and maintaining all licenses and consents required for Vendor’s use of third-party software or data within Client’s environment, except to the extent Vendor is expressly responsible for licensing under the SOW;

  2.4.5 Complying with Vendor’s reasonable security, access, and acceptable use policies, as communicated in writing and updated from time to time.

Vendor shall have no liability for failure to meet Service Levels or other obligations to the extent such failure is caused by Client’s breach of this Section 2.4 or other Client acts or omissions.

2.5 **Service Onboarding and Transition.** Vendor shall cooperate with Client to perform a structured onboarding process, including discovery, design, implementation, testing, and cutover activities. The Parties shall document onboarding tasks, responsibilities, and timelines in an SOW. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid unplanned disruptions to Client’s business during transition.

2.6 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry standards for providers of similar services, and in conformity with the Specifications and Service Levels. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Client acknowledges that no service can be entirely error-free or continuously available, and that Vendor’s obligations are to meet the Service Levels and performance commitments expressly stated in this Agreement.

2.7 **Subcontractors.** Vendor may use subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that Vendor shall remain fully responsible for the performance of such subcontractors and for compliance with the terms of this Agreement. Vendor shall ensure that any subcontractors who have access to Client Data are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth herein.

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **February 03, 2027** (the **“Expiration Date”**), (the **“Term”**).

  4.4.1 **By Either Party.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or (b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

  4.4.2 **By Vendor for Chronic Breach.** Vendor may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW if Client: (a) fails to pay undisputed amounts within sixty (60) days after the due date, and fails to cure such non-payment within ten (10) Business Days of notice; or (b) repeatedly fails to perform its obligations under Section 2.4, materially impeding Vendor’s ability to provide the Services.

  4.5.1 Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments entered into by Vendor with Client’s written consent.

  4.5.2 Vendor shall cooperate in good faith with Client to transition the Services to Client or a third-party designated by Client, on a time-and-materials basis at Vendor’s then-current professional services rates, pursuant to a transition plan mutually agreed in writing.

  4.5.3 Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s written election, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to Section 5.6 and Section 11.6 regarding retention of certain records.

4.6 **Survival.** The rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections 3.4, 3.5, 4.5, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (with respect to accrued Service Credits), XIII (to the extent applicable to unpaid Change Requests), XIV, XV, and XVI shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Availability Commitment.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to make the production hosting environment for the Services available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Downtime and Force Majeure Events, measured at the point of handover from Vendor Systems to Client’s network.

12.2 **Scheduled Downtime.** “Scheduled Downtime” means planned maintenance periods during which the Services may be unavailable. Vendor shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for Scheduled Downtime, which shall, where practicable, be scheduled during low-usage windows. Scheduled Downtime shall not exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month without Client’s written consent.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents in accordance with the following targets:

- **Severity 1 (Critical)** – complete loss of service or severe impact to Client’s production operations with no workaround available.  
  - Response: 30 minutes (24x7x365)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 4 hours

- **Severity 2 (High)** – significant degradation of service or impact to key functionality, workaround available but not sustainable.  
  - Response: 1 hour (24x7x365)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 8 hours

- **Severity 3 (Medium)** – partial or non-critical loss of functionality, workaround available.  
  - Response: 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 2 Business Days

- **Severity 4 (Low)** – minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
  - Response: 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution: Next scheduled maintenance or as agreed.

12.4 **Service Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the availability commitment in Section 12.1 in a given calendar month (excluding Scheduled Downtime and Force Majeure Events), Client shall be eligible for Service Credits as follows:

- Monthly Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service.  
- Monthly Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service.  
- Monthly Availability below 98.0%: 20% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service.

12.5 **Claiming Service Credits.** To receive Service Credits, Client must submit a written request within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the Service Level failure occurred, detailing the outages and requesting credits. Vendor shall review and, if validated, apply the Service Credits to the next invoice. Service Credits shall not result in cash payments and shall not be applied against non-recurring fees.

12.6 **Service Levels as Exclusive Remedy.** Except as otherwise expressly provided in this Agreement (including termination rights under Article IV), Service Credits represent Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for Vendor’s failure to meet the Service Levels set forth in this Article XII.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event.
