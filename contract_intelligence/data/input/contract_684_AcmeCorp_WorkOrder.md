## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General.**  
Pursuant to **Article II (Scope of Services)** of the Master Agreement, Client hereby engages Service Provider, and Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to perform the Services and provide the Deliverables described in this **Article II** and in any Project Plan agreed by the Parties. This Work Order constitutes a specific **Statement of Work** under **Section 2.3** and **Section 1.17** of the Master Agreement.

2.2 **Relationship to Master Agreement.**  
The Services and Deliverables under this Work Order are part of the broader categories of services contemplated in **Section 2.2** of the Master Agreement, including:

- **Consulting and Advisory Services** (Section 2.2(a));  
- **Implementation and Integration Services** (Section 2.2(b));  
- **Managed Services and Operations** (Section 2.2(c));  
- **Support and Maintenance Services** (Section 2.2(d)); and  
- **Training and Knowledge Transfer** (Section 2.2(e)).

This Work Order applies those general categories to a specific engagement focused on Client’s enterprise data platform and related business systems.

2.3 **Project Objectives.**  
The primary objectives of the Project are to:

(a) Design and implement a scalable, secure, and compliant **enterprise data platform** to support Client’s analytics, reporting, and regulatory requirements;  

(b) Integrate the data platform with Client’s existing ERP, CRM, and line-of-business systems, ensuring data consistency and integrity across the organization;  

(c) Provide **managed services**, including monitoring, incident management, capacity management, and routine maintenance of the data platform;  

(d) Provide **support and enhancement services** to enable ongoing improvements, new use cases, and performance optimization; and  

(e) Conduct **training and knowledge transfer** to Client’s teams to support operational self-sufficiency and governance.

2.4 **Phased Approach.**  
The Services will be performed in the following phases, each associated with specific Milestones and Deliverables:

(a) **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (Months 1–3).**  
- Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and requirements-gathering sessions;  
- Assess existing data sources, data quality, and integration points;  
- Produce a **Solution Architecture Document** (Deliverable D1);  
- Produce a **Data Governance and Security Framework** (Deliverable D2);  
- Develop an initial **Project Plan** including detailed timeline and resource allocations (Deliverable D3);  
- Milestone M1: Approval by Client of the Solution Architecture Document and Project Plan.

(b) **Phase 2 – Build and Integration (Months 4–10).**  
- Build foundational data platform components (ingestion, storage, processing, access layers);  
- Implement integrations with core Client systems (ERP, CRM, HR, finance);  
- Configure monitoring, alerting, and logging of the Data Environment;  
- Deliver **Integration Specifications, Interface Designs, and Mapping Documents** (Deliverable D4);  
- Milestone M2: Completion of core platform build and successful completion of **system integration testing** (SIT).

(c) **Phase 3 – Data Migration and Validation (Months 11–14).**  
- Define migration strategy and detailed migration runbook;  
- Execute initial and incremental migration waves;  
- Perform data validation, reconciliation, and remediation;  
- Deliver **Migration Runbook and Validation Report** (Deliverable D5);  
- Milestone M3: Acceptance of migration results by Client.

(d) **Phase 4 – Go-Live and Stabilization (Months 15–18).**  
- Support go-live cutover and hypercare activities;  
- Monitor system performance and address post-go-live defects;  
- Deliver **Operational Runbooks, Support Procedures, and Training Materials** (Deliverable D6);  
- Milestone M4: Completion of stabilization period and transition into steady-state managed services.

(e) **Phase 5 – Managed Services and Enhancements (Months 19–WO Expiration Date).**  
- Provide ongoing managed services and operations of the Data Environment;  
- Provide support, maintenance, minor enhancements, and optimization;  
- Provide monthly **Operational Performance Reports** (Deliverable D7 – recurring);  
- Milestone M5: Completion of each contract year, subject to annual review.

2.5 **Detailed Services.**  

(a) **Consulting and Advisory Services.**  
Service Provider shall deliver strategic guidance on data architecture, analytics, and governance, including:

- Business process analysis and mapping;  
- Recommendations on data modeling, master data management, and retention;  
- Advice on regulatory compliance (including data protection and audit requirements).

(b) **Implementation and Integration Services.**  
Service Provider shall:

- Configure the chosen data platform technologies (cloud or on-prem, as agreed in the Project Plan);  
- Develop interfaces, APIs, and workflows for data ingestion and distribution;  
- Configure third-party tools or **Third-Party Materials** (as defined in **Section 1.20** of the Master Agreement) as required.

(c) **Managed Services and Operations.**  
In line with **Section 2.2(c)** of the Master Agreement, Service Provider shall:

- Monitor availability and performance of the Data Environment;  
- Perform incident and problem management under the SLAs in **Article XII**;  
- Perform routine maintenance, patches, upgrades, and capacity management;  
- Maintain a 24x7 on-call capability for Severity 1 Incidents.

(d) **Support and Maintenance Services.**  
Service Provider shall:

- Provide technical support for issues raised by Client;  
- Apply bug fixes and patches;  
- Provide performance tuning and optimization;  
- Maintain a ticketing and tracking system, and provide regular status updates.

(e) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
Service Provider shall:

- Deliver structured training sessions (remote or on-site) covering architecture, operations, and governance;  
- Provide user guides, administration manuals, and quick-reference materials;  
- Conduct handover workshops prior to end of each major phase.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  
Service Provider shall perform the Services:

- In accordance with **Section 2.4** of the Master Agreement (Performance Standards);  
- In a timely, diligent, professional and workmanlike manner;  
- Using personnel who meet the competency standards set out in the Master Agreement, including any **Key Personnel** designated in the Project Plan; and  
- In compliance with all applicable laws, including **Data Protection Laws** as referenced in **Article XI** of the Master Agreement and of this Work Order.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client shall undertake the responsibilities set forth in **Section 2.6** of the Master Agreement, including:

- Providing timely access to relevant personnel, systems, and **Client Materials**;  
- Providing prompt decisions, inputs, and approvals in accordance with the Project Plan;  
- Ensuring that all data provided for migration and integration are accurate and lawfully collected; and  
- Providing suitable test environments and test data for SIT and UAT.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
The acceptance process in **Section 2.5** of the Master Agreement shall apply, with the following clarifications:

(a) Client shall have an **Acceptance Period** of **fifteen (15) Business Days** from delivery of each Deliverable to either (i) issue a written acceptance notice, or (ii) provide a written notice of material non-conformity.  

(b) Service Provider shall remedy any material non-conformities at no additional cost within a mutually agreed period, followed by a further Acceptance Period of **ten (10) Business Days**.  

(c) If Client does not respond within the Acceptance Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.  

(d) Acceptance of a Milestone is contingent upon acceptance or deemed acceptance of all Deliverables associated with that Milestone.

2.9 **Subcontracting.**  
Service Provider may subcontract portions of the Services in accordance with **Section 2.7** of the Master Agreement. Upon Client’s written request, Service Provider will provide a list of material subcontractors supporting this Work Order, including any sub-processors of Personal Data.

- Phase 2 (Build and Integration): US$1,200,000

Starting upon completion of Milestone M4, Client shall pay an annual managed services fee of **US$190,000** for Years 2–6 (pro-rated for any partial year), covering: monitoring, incident management, minor enhancements, and reporting.

(d) **Pass-Through Costs.**  
Any third-party license, hosting, or pass-through costs shall be separately itemized and pre‑approved by Client in writing, consistent with **Section 3.2(d)** of the Master Agreement.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  

(a) **Fixed Fees.**  
- 20% of Phase 1 fee upon Effective Date;  
- 80% of Phase 1 fee upon Milestone M1 acceptance;  
- 40% of Phase 2 fee upon completion of core platform build;  
- 60% of Phase 2 fee upon Milestone M2 acceptance;  
- 50% of Phase 3 fee upon completion of initial migration;  
- 50% of Phase 3 fee upon Milestone M3 acceptance;  
- 40% of Phase 4 fee upon go-live;  
- 60% of Phase 4 fee upon Milestone M4 acceptance.

(b) **T&M Fees.**  
Invoiced **monthly in arrears** based on actual hours worked, supported by detailed timesheets and descriptions of activities.

(c) **Managed Services Fees.**  
Invoiced **quarterly in advance** beginning on the first day of the quarter following Milestone M4, with the first invoice pro-rated if applicable.

3.4 **Payment Terms (Net 60).**  
Notwithstanding **Section 3.3(a)** of the Master Agreement, the Parties agree that for this Work Order, all undisputed amounts shall be payable within **sixty (60) calendar days** of the invoice date (**“Net 60”**). This constitutes a specific override of the “Net 45” term in the Master Agreement for purposes of this Work Order only.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  
In the event of late payment of undisputed amounts:

(a) Interest shall accrue in accordance with **Section 3.4(a)** of the Master Agreement (1.5% per month or the maximum legal rate, whichever is lower); and  

(b) Service Provider may, after providing ten (10) Business Days’ written notice and an opportunity to cure, suspend Services pursuant to **Section 3.4(b)** of the Master Agreement, provided such suspension is proportionate and does not jeopardize data integrity or regulatory compliance.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
Client may withhold payment of disputed amounts in accordance with **Section 3.5** of the Master Agreement, provided that Client:

- Notifies Service Provider within fifteen (15) Business Days of receiving the invoice; and  
- Pays any undisputed portion in accordance with **Section 3.4** above.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.6** of the Master Agreement. All fees under this Work Order are exclusive of VAT and similar taxes.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reimbursable expenses shall be governed by **Section 3.7** of the Master Agreement. Service Provider shall obtain Client’s **written pre-approval** for any travel or extraordinary third-party expenses and shall provide receipts upon request.

3.9 **No Withholding.**  
The no-withholding principle in **Section 3.8** of the Master Agreement shall apply, subject to any mandatory tax withholdings required by applicable law.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term of Work Order.**  
This Work Order shall commence on the WO Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **March 13, 2030** (the **“WO Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or **Article IV** of the Master Agreement.

(a) This Work Order shall continue in force until the WO Expiration Date solely for the purposes of completing the Services and Deliverables described herein;

(c) All other SOWs or work orders under the Master Agreement may expire or terminate in accordance with their terms without affecting this Work Order.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (WO-Override).**  

(a) **Client’s Right.** Notwithstanding **Section 4.3** of the Master Agreement (which provides for a 180-day notice period for Client), Client may terminate this Work Order for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Service Provider.  

(b) **Service Provider’s Right.** Notwithstanding **Section 4.3** of the Master Agreement (which allows Service Provider to terminate with 120 days’ notice), Service Provider may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon not less than **ninety (90) days’ prior written notice** to Client.  

This **Section 4.3** expressly overrides **Section 4.3** of the Master Agreement with respect to this Work Order only.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Termination for cause shall follow **Section 4.4** of the Master Agreement, including:

- Material breach with a thirty (30) day cure period;  
- Insolvency or similar events;  
- Persistent non-payment of undisputed amounts by Client;  
- Persistent or material failure to meet Service Levels by Service Provider, subject to applicable cure mechanisms.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

(a) Upon termination or expiration of this Work Order, Client shall pay Service Provider for:

- All Services performed and Deliverables delivered (or substantially completed) up to the effective date of termination; and  
- Any non-cancellable commitments and approved third-party costs incurred in reliance on this Work Order, consistent with **Section 4.5(b)** of the Master Agreement.

(b) Upon termination for Client convenience or Service Provider cause, Client shall additionally pay any agreed early termination charges set out in Schedule 2 (if any).  

(c) Upon termination for Service Provider convenience or Client cause, Service Provider shall refund any prepaid fees for Services not yet performed as of the effective date of termination.

(d) Service Provider shall provide **Transition Services** for up to ninety (90) days post-termination at T&M rates (subject to mutual agreement) to facilitate a smooth migration of services to Client or a third party.

(e) Sections regarding return or destruction of **Confidential Information** and **Personal Data** shall be governed by **Section 4.5(c)** and **Article XI** of the Master Agreement and **Article XI** of this Work Order.

4.6 **Termination of Master Agreement.**  

(a) If the Master Agreement is terminated for cause by Service Provider, Service Provider may, at its option, terminate this Work Order effective on the same date.  

(b) If the Master Agreement is terminated for cause by Client, this Work Order shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, without prejudice to Client’s rights to remedies and refunds as provided in this Work Order and the Master Agreement.  

(c) If the Master Agreement is terminated for convenience, this Work Order shall continue as set forth in **Section 4.2** above unless expressly terminated or superseded by written agreement of the Parties.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Any provisions of this Work Order which, by their nature, are intended to survive termination or expiration (including payment obligations, confidentiality, IP rights, indemnities, limitation of liability, data protection, and dispute resolution) shall so survive, consistent with **Section 4.6** and **Article XVI** of the Master Agreement.
