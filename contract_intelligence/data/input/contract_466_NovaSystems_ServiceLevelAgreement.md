## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Company the managed IT and cloud services described in this Article II and in any applicable SOWs. The Services shall be delivered in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards and in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

2.2 **Managed Cloud Hosting Services.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall provision, configure, operate, and maintain cloud-based infrastructure for the Company, which may include virtual machines, storage, databases, and network resources.  
(b) Such cloud hosting shall be deployed in geographically redundant data centers located in the United States, with appropriate physical and logical security controls.  
(c) The Service Provider shall ensure that the System is scalable to support anticipated volumes and workloads specified by the Company and shall monitor resource utilization to recommend capacity adjustments.  
(d) The Service Provider shall be responsible for system administration functions, including operating system patching, security updates, performance monitoring, backup scheduling, and routine maintenance.

2.3 **Application Support and Maintenance.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall provide Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 support for designated Company applications as described in applicable SOWs.  
(b) Support shall include incident triage, root cause analysis, bug fixes, minor configuration changes, and coordination with third-party vendors as necessary.  
(c) The Service Provider shall maintain a ticketing system accessible to the Company for logging, tracking, and reporting Incidents and service requests.  
(d) The Service Provider shall provide support on a 24x7x365 basis for production Incidents of Severity 1 and Severity 2, and during Business Hours for lower-priority issues unless otherwise specified in an SOW.

2.4 **Monitoring and Incident Management.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall implement continuous monitoring tools and processes to track system health, application performance, and security events.  
(b) Thresholds for alerts and escalation procedures shall be agreed upon in writing and documented in an SOW or an operational runbook.  
(c) When an Incident occurs, the Service Provider shall respond and work to resolve the Incident within the response and resolution times specified in Article XII.  
(d) The Service Provider shall maintain communication with the Company during Incidents, including status updates at intervals commensurate with the severity of the Incident.

2.5 **Backup and Disaster Recovery.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall perform regular data backups of Customer Data and system configurations according to a predefined schedule (e.g., daily incremental backups and weekly full backups) agreed by the Parties.  
(b) Backups shall be stored in secure, geographically separate locations to ensure resilience in the event of a localized outage.  
(c) The Service Provider shall develop and maintain a disaster recovery plan that includes Recovery Time Objectives (“RTOs”) and Recovery Point Objectives (“RPOs”) acceptable to the Company, which may be further detailed in an SOW.  
(d) The Service Provider shall conduct at least one disaster recovery test annually and shall provide the Company with a written report summarizing the results and any follow-up actions.

2.6 **Security and Compliance Services.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the Customer Data and the System, consistent with industry standards such as ISO 27001 or SOC 2 (if applicable).  
(b) Security measures shall include, at a minimum: access control and authentication, network segmentation, encryption in transit (e.g., TLS 1.2 or higher), and encryption at rest for sensitive data, vulnerability management, and logging and monitoring of security events.  
(c) The Service Provider shall cooperate with the Company in meeting applicable regulatory standards related to the Services, such as GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy and data security requirements, as described in Article XI.

2.7 **Professional Services and Projects.**  
(a) From time to time, the Company may request, and the Service Provider may agree to provide, additional professional services, including but not limited to consulting, system integration, migrations, custom development, and performance tuning.  
(b) Such professional services shall be described in SOWs, including scope, assumptions, Deliverables, schedule, fees, and acceptance criteria.  
(c) Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, professional services shall be performed on a time-and-materials basis at the rates set forth in Article III or the applicable SOW.

2.8 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall perform the Services in accordance with the Service Levels specified in Article XII, including uptime guarantees of at least 99.9% for covered production Services, except as otherwise agreed in writing.  
(b) The Service Provider shall ensure that personnel assigned to perform the Services are adequately trained, qualified, and experienced for the tasks assigned.  
(c) The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to the Company’s operations, including scheduling planned maintenance during Maintenance Windows and providing advance notice as required by Section 12.4.

2.9 **Deliverables and Timelines.**  
(a) For each SOW, the Parties shall agree on specific Deliverables, milestones, and timelines.  
(b) The Service Provider shall deliver each Deliverable by the agreed delivery dates, subject to any documented dependencies on the Company or third parties.  
(c) The Company shall review Deliverables within the time specified in the applicable SOW (or, if not specified, within ten (10) Business Days of delivery) and shall notify the Service Provider of acceptance or rejection, including a reasonably detailed description of any deficiencies.  
(d) The Service Provider shall promptly correct any non-conformities in the Deliverables and re-submit them for review and acceptance. Failing communication from the Company within the review period, a Deliverable may be deemed accepted.

2.10 **Company Responsibilities.**  
(a) The Company shall provide timely access to relevant systems, information, and personnel reasonably necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services.  
(b) The Company shall perform its responsibilities and obligations set forth in this Agreement and any SOW in a timely manner.  
(c) The Company shall designate a primary point of contact to coordinate with the Service Provider.  
(d) The Company acknowledges that delays in its performance of responsibilities may impact the Service Provider’s ability to meet timelines and Service Levels, and to that extent, the Service Provider shall not be held responsible for such delays.

(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (i) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof (or within such longer period as the non-breaching Party may reasonably agree in writing, provided that the breaching Party is diligently pursuing cure); or  
  (ii) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed under receivership, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.  
(b) Without limiting the foregoing, repeated failures to meet critical Service Levels, as defined in Article XII, may constitute a material breach if not cured in a commercially reasonable timeframe.

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) The Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the applicable Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary to support an orderly transition.  
(b) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and accepted and for any approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to the Company’s rights regarding disputed amounts under Section 3.5.  
(c) Each Party shall return or destroy (at the other Party’s option) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, in accordance with Section 5.6, subject to data retention requirements in Article XI.

11.2 **Roles of the Parties.**  
For purposes of data protection laws, the Company shall generally act as the data controller or business (as such terms are defined under Applicable Law), and the Service Provider shall act as the data processor or service provider with respect to Customer Data processed on behalf of the Company.

(a) The Service Provider shall process Customer Data only in accordance with the Company’s documented instructions, this Agreement, and any applicable data processing addendum executed by the Parties.  
(b) The Service Provider shall not “sell” or “share” (as such terms are defined under CCPA) Customer Data.  
(c) The Service Provider shall ensure that persons authorized to process Customer Data are subject to appropriate confidentiality obligations.

11.4 **Security Measures.**  
The Service Provider shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect Customer Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access, proportionate to the nature of the Customer Data and the risks involved in processing.

(b) Such notice shall describe, to the extent known, the nature of the Data Breach, the categories and approximate number of affected data subjects and records, and the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address or mitigate the Data Breach.  
(c) The Service Provider shall cooperate with the Company in complying with any legal obligations regarding Data Breach notifications to data subjects or authorities.

11.6 **Subprocessors.**  
(a) The Service Provider may engage third-party subprocessors to assist in providing the Services, provided that:  
  (i) The Service Provider enters into written agreements with such subprocessors imposing data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and  
  (ii) The Service Provider remains responsible for the acts and omissions of its subprocessors as if they were its own.  
(b) Upon the Company’s written request, the Service Provider shall provide a list of its current subprocessors involved in processing Customer Data.

11.7 **Data Subject Rights.**

11.8 **Data Retention and Deletion.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall retain Customer Data only for as long as necessary to provide the Services or as required by Applicable Law.

(c) Any retained Customer Data shall remain subject to the confidentiality and data protection obligations set forth in this Agreement until such data is securely deleted.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the core production Services are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance, emergency maintenance, and Force Majeure Events.  
(b) “Availability” is calculated as:  
> (Total minutes in month – Unscheduled Downtime) / Total minutes in month × 100%

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve Incidents in accordance with the following targets:

- **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of mission-critical functionality or outage affecting a majority of end users:  
  - Response Time: within 30 minutes (24x7x365)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround Time: within 4 hours  

- **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant impairment of functionality with no immediate workaround, affecting a substantial subset of users:  
  - Response Time: within 1 hour (24x7x365)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround Time: within 8 hours  

- **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Partial impairment with a reasonable workaround; limited impact:  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution Time: within 2 Business Days  

- **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general service requests:  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution Time: within 5 Business Days

12.3 **Service Credits.**  
(a) If the Service Provider fails to meet the monthly uptime commitment in Section 12.1, the Company shall be entitled to Service Credits as follows:  

- Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: Service Credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service;  
- Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: Service Credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service;  
- Availability below 98.0%: Service Credit equal to 20% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service.

(b) Service Credits shall be applied to the next invoice issued to the Company. Service Credits shall not exceed 30% of the total monthly recurring fees for the affected Services in any given month.  
(c) The Service Credits set forth in this Section 12.3 shall be the Company’s exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitments, without prejudice to the Company’s right to terminate this Agreement under Article IV for repeated material failures.

12.4 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
(a) The Service Provider may schedule routine maintenance during designated Maintenance Windows.  
(b) The Service Provider shall provide the Company with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice of scheduled maintenance that is expected to result in downtime.  
(c) Scheduled maintenance of up to four (4) hours per month shall not count as downtime for purposes of calculating Availability.

12.5 **Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall provide the Company with periodic reports (at least monthly) summarizing Service performance, including uptime statistics, Incidents, response and resolution times, and any Service Credits incurred.
