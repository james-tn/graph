## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Vendor shall provide to the Client a comprehensive suite of **employment and technology services**, which may include, without limitation:

  (a) recruitment, vetting, selection, and on-boarding of technical, professional, and administrative personnel who will perform services for the benefit of the Client, including but not limited to software engineers, data scientists, project managers, systems analysts, cybersecurity consultants, and support staff;

  (b) secondment and assignment of Vendor Personnel to work on-site at Client’s premises, remotely, or in hybrid arrangements, as specified in each applicable SOW;

  (c) management of Vendor Personnel, including performance monitoring, training, scheduling, leave management, and compliance with Vendor’s obligations as employer (where Vendor is the employer) under Applicable Law;

  (d) provision of technology consulting and managed services, including systems integration, application development and maintenance, infrastructure monitoring, and support services, as may be further defined in SOWs;

  (e) any additional services expressly set forth in a Work Order, SOW, or written change request mutually agreed upon by the Parties pursuant to Article XIII.

2.2 **Employment Relationship and Status.**  
The Parties expressly acknowledge and agree that:

  (a) Vendor shall at all times operate as an **independent contractor** of the Client. Nothing contained in this Agreement shall be construed as creating any partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship between the Client and Vendor, or between the Client and any Vendor Personnel (Section 16.10).

  (b) Vendor (or its duly authorized subcontractors) shall be solely responsible as employer (where applicable) for all compensation, benefits, tax withholdings, social security contributions, pension contributions, and other employer obligations with respect to Vendor Personnel, and Client shall bear no responsibility for such matters.

  (c) Client shall not: (i) supervise, control, or direct the manner or method by which Vendor or Vendor Personnel perform the Services, except to the limited extent necessary to specify business requirements, deliverables, and acceptance criteria; or (ii) represent to any person that Vendor Personnel are employees of Client.

2.3 **Specific Deliverables and Tasks.**  
Each SOW shall describe one or more projects, tasks, or functions to be performed by the Vendor. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services may include the following categories of Deliverables:

  (a) **Technical Deliverables**: Software modules, system integrations, configuration scripts, test plans, test results, technical architecture diagrams, and related documentation.

  (b) **Staffing Deliverables**: Provision of specified numbers and types of Vendor Personnel (e.g., five Senior Software Engineers, three Data Analysts) for defined periods, along with staffing reports, skills matrices, and onboarding/training records.

  (c) **Management Deliverables**: Regular status reports, performance dashboards, risk and issue logs, project plans, resource allocation plans, and meeting minutes.

  (d) **Compliance Deliverables**: Evidence of background checks where required, training records, regulatory compliance reports, and data protection audits applicable to the Services.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
Each SOW shall specify:

  (a) the commencement date for the particular Services;

  (b) any intermediate milestones, deliverable due dates, or phased deliveries;

  (c) any dependencies on Client inputs, approvals, or third-party systems;

  (d) any Service-specific acceptance criteria.

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts, in accordance with Article XII, to perform the Services and deliver the Deliverables in accordance with the timelines set forth in each SOW. Where the timeline is expressly described as “critical” or “time is of the essence” in an SOW, Vendor shall allocate appropriate resources to meet those obligations.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;

  (b) with due care, skill, and diligence as can reasonably be expected of a professional provider of employment and technology services;

  (c) in compliance with Applicable Laws (subject to Section 7.2) and Client’s reasonable site-specific security and safety policies as communicated to Vendor in writing.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Vendor’s performance is predicated upon the timely and effective cooperation of the Client. The Client shall:

  (a) provide Vendor with accurate and complete information, specifications, and access to relevant systems and premises reasonably required for the performance of the Services;

  (b) designate a primary contact and, where appropriate, a steering committee or governance structure to promptly review deliverables and make decisions;

  (c) provide feedback, approvals, and acceptances within the timeframes specified in the relevant SOW. Delays in Client approvals shall entitle Vendor to corresponding extensions of time and, where applicable, equitable adjustments in fees pursuant to Article XIII.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  
Vendor may, at its discretion and without the need for prior written consent of Client (unless otherwise expressly stated in any SOW), engage subcontractors to perform any part of the Services, provided that Vendor shall:

  (a) remain fully responsible for the acts and omissions of such subcontractors as though they were acts and omissions of Vendor; and  

  (b) ensure that any subcontractor engaged in the processing of Personal Data is bound by data protection obligations at least as protective as those in Article XI.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise set forth in an SOW:

  (a) Client shall have ten (10) Business Days after delivery of a Deliverable to review and either (i) accept it in writing, or (ii) reject it in writing by providing reasonably detailed written reasons for non-acceptance;

  (b) if Client fails to respond within such period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted on the next Business Day;

  (c) if Client rejects a Deliverable, Vendor shall, as a sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet acceptance criteria (subject to Article IX), use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance within a reasonable time.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value for all Services to be provided under this Agreement from the Effective Date through the Expiration Date (as defined in Section 4.1) is **US$2,002,493** (two million two thousand four hundred ninety-three U.S. dollars), exclusive of applicable taxes, fees, or duties, unless expressly stated otherwise in an SOW.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees may be structured as one or a combination of the following, to be detailed in each SOW:

  (a) **Time and Materials (T&M)**: Daily or hourly rates for categories of Vendor Personnel, multiplied by the actual time spent performing the Services;

  (b) **Fixed Price**: Fixed amounts for specified deliverables, phases, or projects;

  (c) **Retainer / Managed Services**: Recurring monthly or quarterly charges for ongoing services including staffing pools, management services, and operational support;

  (d) **Success-Based / Performance Fees**, if explicitly agreed in an SOW.

3.3 **Payment Terms (Net 30).**  
Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the billing schedule set forth in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated:

  (a) invoices shall be issued monthly in arrears for T&M Services and in accordance with agreed milestones for fixed price Services;

  (b) Client shall pay each undisputed invoice in full within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the date of invoice (“**Net 30**”);

  (c) all payments shall be made in U.S. dollars via bank transfer to the account designated by Vendor in writing or as specified in the SOW.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
If Client fails to pay any undisputed amount by the due date:

  (a) Vendor may charge interest on the overdue amount from the due date until the date of actual payment at a rate of **4% per annum above the Bank of England base rate**, accruing on a daily basis; and

  (b) Vendor may, upon written notice and without liability, suspend performance of the Services (including access to Deliverables and Vendor Personnel) under this Agreement until all overdue amounts and applicable interest have been paid in full, provided that Vendor gives at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice of its intention to suspend.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith:

  (a) Client shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3;

  (b) Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) calendar days of the invoice date, specifying the nature and basis of the dispute in reasonable detail;

  (c) the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve such dispute promptly; and

  (d) upon resolution, any amount determined to be payable by Client shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All fees and charges are exclusive of value added tax (VAT), sales tax, use tax, and other similar taxes, duties, or levies, which shall be charged where applicable and shall be payable by Client. Each Party shall be responsible for its own income taxes, payroll taxes, and other taxes arising from its own activities.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, properly documented **out-of-pocket expenses** directly incurred in providing the Services and pre-approved in writing by Client, including:

  (a) travel and lodging expenses for Vendor Personnel traveling at Client’s request;

  (b) costs of specialized third-party tools, licenses, or equipment specifically acquired for the Client’s project (where agreed);

  (c) any regulatory, permit, or certification costs specific to the Client’s industry or project, if agreed in writing.

Such expenses shall be invoiced at cost (or at any mark-up agreed in an SOW) with adequate supporting documentation, and shall be payable together with the invoice on which they are included.

3.8 **No Set-Off.**  
Except to the extent expressly permitted by Applicable Law, Client shall not be entitled to set-off, withhold, or deduct any sums claimed to be due to Client from Vendor against any sums due to Vendor under this Agreement, without Vendor’s prior written consent.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date** and shall continue in full force and effect until **20 April 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, upon mutual written agreement at least one hundred eighty (180) days prior to the Expiration Date, extend or renew this Agreement for one or more additional periods on terms to be mutually agreed.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Client.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement or any particular SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing **not less than one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor. Client acknowledges that this long notice period is a material inducement for Vendor to enter into this Agreement and allocate resources.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Vendor.**  
Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing not less than ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to Client.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any affected SOW) immediately by written notice if the other Party:

  (a) commits a material breach of this Agreement which (if capable of remedy) is not remedied within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or

  (b) becomes insolvent, admits inability to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into any composition or arrangement with its creditors, is the subject of a petition for winding up or administration, or has a receiver, administrative receiver, or similar officer appointed over its assets.

4.6 **Termination for Non-Payment.**  
Without prejudice to Section 4.5, Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to Client if Client fails to pay any undisputed amount due hereunder within sixty (60) days after the due date, and Client does not cure such non-payment within ten (10) Business Days after receiving written notice from Vendor.

4.7 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) all SOWs then in effect shall automatically terminate, unless expressly stated otherwise in the termination notice;

  (b) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, plus any committed costs or non-cancellable expenses incurred by Vendor;

  (c) Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable transition assistance requested by Client and agreed in writing, at Vendor’s then-current T&M rates;

  (d) each Party shall return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s option) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, in accordance with Section 5.5;

  (e) the rights and obligations which by their nature are intended to survive (including those in Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI and payment obligations accrued prior to termination) shall continue in full force and effect.

4.8 **No Penalty.**  
Termination of this Agreement or any SOW pursuant to this Article IV shall not in itself give rise to any liability or compensation obligation for either Party, save as expressly provided herein or in any SOW, and without prejudice to any other rights or remedies that may have accrued up to the date of termination.

## CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any and all information, in any form or medium, disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”) that is marked or otherwise identified as confidential at the time of disclosure, or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to:

  (a) business, financial, commercial, technical, and operational information;  

  (b) trade secrets, know-how, Intellectual Property, inventions, processes, and product plans;  

  (c) customer lists, supplier information, business plans, marketing strategies, and pricing;  

  (d) in the case of Client, Client Materials and all information relating to Client’s customers, employees, and business operations; and  

  (e) in the case of Vendor, Vendor Materials, methodologies, tools, and information about Vendor’s other clients.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  

  (b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  

  (c) is lawfully received from a third party without restriction on use or disclosure; or  

  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  

  (b) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than reasonable care;  

  (c) not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party, except as permitted in Section 5.4; and  

  (d) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing upon becoming aware of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information and cooperate with the Disclosing Party to mitigate the effects and prevent further unauthorized use or disclosure.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:

  (a) to its employees, officers, professional advisers, subcontractors, and agents who have a legitimate need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those in this Article V;  

  (b) as required by Applicable Law, court order, or regulatory authority, provided that (to the extent permitted by law) the Receiving Party promptly notifies the Disclosing Party and cooperates in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.5 **Return and Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall, at the Disclosing Party’s option:

  (a) return all Confidential Information (including any copies); or  

  (b) destroy the Confidential Information and certify in writing that such destruction has occurred,  

except that the Receiving Party may retain such copies as are required by Applicable Law or for archival, audit, or evidence purposes, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations in this Article V shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** from the date of termination or expiry of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets, for which the obligations shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.

15.2 **Notification.**  
The affected Party shall notify the other Party in writing as soon as reasonably practicable after becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event, describing the nature of the event, its expected duration, and its impact on the Party’s ability to perform its obligations.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event to the extent that the Party is prevented from performing such obligations. The affected Party shall use reasonable endeavors to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event prevents either Party from performing its material obligations under this Agreement for a continuous period of more than ninety (90) days, either Party may terminate this Agreement (or the affected SOW) by giving thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party.
