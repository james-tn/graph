## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.** Vendor shall provide to Client, on a managed services and project basis, a comprehensive suite of **cloud-based business process management, data analytics, and application support services** (collectively, the **“Services”**). The Services are designed to support Contoso’s mission-critical internal operations, including but not limited to order management, inventory tracking, customer support workflows, financial reporting integrations, and executive dashboards.

2.2 **Categories of Services.** The Services to be performed by Vendor shall include, without limitation, the following categories:

2.2.1 **Managed Application Hosting.** Vendor shall host Client’s designated business applications on Vendor-managed infrastructure, including production, staging, and test environments. Vendor shall provision and maintain appropriate compute, storage, and network resources to support agreed workloads, with scaling capabilities in accordance with the Service Levels.

2.2.2 **Platform Configuration and Customization.** Vendor shall configure and customize the hosted applications and related platforms in accordance with Client’s business requirements, including creation of workflows, forms, rules engines, dashboards, and role-based access controls. Customizations shall be implemented in a manner consistent with Vendor’s best practices and shall be documented as part of the Deliverables.

2.2.3 **Systems Integration.** Vendor shall design, implement, and maintain integrations between the hosted applications and Client’s internal systems and third-party services (including, by way of example only, ERP, CRM, HRIS, and payment platforms). Such integrations may be accomplished via APIs, middleware, or file-based interfaces, as mutually agreed.

2.2.4 **Data Migration and Transformation.** Vendor shall assist Client with the extraction, cleansing, transformation, and loading of historical and current data from Client’s legacy systems into the Vendor-hosted environment, in accordance with mutually agreed data mapping specifications. Vendor will provide sample test runs, validation reports, and reconciliation support as part of the Deliverables.

2.2.5 **Analytics and Reporting Services.** Vendor shall develop and maintain a suite of standardized and custom reports, dashboards, and analytics outputs for Client’s business units. This includes periodic scheduled reports and on-demand dashboards for monitoring key performance indicators, trend analysis, and operational metrics.

2.2.6 **Support and Maintenance Services.** Vendor shall provide ongoing technical support, incident management, problem resolution, patch management, and routine maintenance for the hosted applications and integrations, in accordance with the priority levels, response times, and resolution targets set forth in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**.

2.2.7 **Advisory and Consulting Services.** Vendor shall provide advisory services relating to process optimization, platform usage, governance, security posture, and roadmap planning, upon Client’s reasonable request and subject to mutually agreed statements of work where appropriate.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor shall provide the following **Deliverables** during the Term:

2.3.1 **Solution Design Documentation.** High-level and detailed design documents describing the application architecture, integrations, security model, and data flows, including any constraints or dependencies.

2.3.2 **Configuration Artifacts.** Exportable configuration files, scripts, or parameter sets representing the implemented workflows, forms, rules, and other configurable components, to the extent technically feasible.

2.3.3 **Test Plans and Results.** Test plans, test cases, and summary reports of testing outcomes for major releases, integrations, and data migration cycles.

2.3.4 **Operational Runbooks.** Operational runbooks and standard operating procedures documenting key processes for incident management, change control, release management, and backup and recovery procedures.

2.3.5 **User Guides and Training Materials.** End-user and administrator guides, quick reference materials, and training session content, as reasonably required to enable Client’s personnel to use the Services.

2.3.6 **Periodic Performance and SLA Reports.** Reports on system uptime, incident metrics, response times, and other Service Level indicators, as further described in **Article XII**.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.** The Parties agree to the following high-level timeline, subject to refinement in project plans and Change Orders:

2.4.1 **Initiation Phase (Effective Date + 30 Days).**  
(a) Project kickoff;  
(b) Requirements refinement and confirmation;  
(c) Finalization of solution design; and  
(d) Establishment of communication, governance, and escalation protocols.

2.4.2 **Implementation and Integration Phase (Effective Date + 120 Days).**  
(a) Configuration of core workflows and initial integrations;  
(b) Data migration test cycles;  
(c) User acceptance testing; and  
(d) Transition planning into production.

2.4.3 **Production Go-Live (Target: Effective Date + 150 Days).**  
(a) Final data migration;  
(b) Go-live of core solution in production; and  
(c) Hypercare support period for not less than thirty (30) days following go-live.

2.4.4 **Optimization and Expansion Phase (Remainder of Term).** Continuous improvements to workflows, incremental integrations, and periodic enhancements as agreed via Change Orders.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:  

(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner;  
(b) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards applicable to providers of similar services;  
(c) in conformity with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**; and  
(d) in material compliance with Applicable Law.  

Notwithstanding the foregoing, Client acknowledges that the Services involve complex dependencies, third-party systems, and networks outside of Vendor’s direct control, and Vendor’s obligations shall be subject to such limitations as explicitly stated in this Amendment.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.** As a condition to Vendor’s performance, Client shall:

2.6.1 Provide timely access to relevant personnel, systems, facilities, and information as reasonably required by Vendor to perform the Services;

2.6.2 Ensure the accuracy, completeness, and lawfulness of Client Data provided to Vendor;

2.6.3 Designate one or more project managers and primary contacts who are authorized to make day-to-day decisions and approvals on behalf of Client;

2.6.4 Perform any Client-side tasks, configurations, or approvals within the timeframes identified by Vendor, and acknowledge that delays in performing such responsibilities may directly impact the timeline and performance of the Services;

2.6.5 Obtain and maintain, at Client’s cost, any necessary licenses, access rights, or consents for third-party systems to interoperate with the Vendor-hosted environment.

2.7 **High-Risk Acknowledgment.** Client expressly acknowledges and agrees that:  
(a) the Services are mission-critical and high-risk;  
(b) Client has performed its own risk assessment; and  
(c) Vendor’s obligations and pricing, including the **uncapped liability** and enhanced data protection duties in this Amendment, are predicated upon Client’s strict adherence to its responsibilities and upon the risk allocation and indemnification provisions in **Articles VIII and IX**, which are intentionally favorable to Vendor.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that the total estimated contract value for the Services under this Amendment is **US$1,213,928**, exclusive of any applicable taxes, and subject to adjustment for approved Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Components.** The Fees shall consist of the following components:

3.2.1 **Implementation Fees.** A fixed fee of **US$312,500** for design, configuration, integration, and initial data migration activities, payable in accordance with the milestones described in **Section 3.3.1**.

3.2.2 **Recurring Managed Services Fees.** A recurring fee of **US$37,500 per month** during the Term for hosting, support, maintenance, and ongoing operations.

3.2.3 **Advisory / Professional Services Fees.** Time-and-materials fees for additional consulting, optimization, or project work requested by Client and agreed by the Parties in writing, billed at Vendor’s then-current standard hourly rates as specified in a schedule or Change Order.

3.2.4 **Pass-Through Third-Party Costs.** Any third-party license fees, cloud infrastructure costs beyond baseline committed capacity, or other external costs incurred by Vendor solely for the benefit of Client, provided such costs are pre-approved in writing by Client (email suffices unless otherwise required).

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**

3.3.1 **Implementation Milestones.**  
(a) **Forty percent (40%)** of the Implementation Fees (US$125,000) shall be invoiced upon Effective Date;  
(b) **Thirty percent (30%)** (US$93,750) shall be invoiced upon completion of configuration and successful completion of initial integration testing; and  
(c) **Thirty percent (30%)** (US$93,750) shall be invoiced upon production go-live, or upon the earlier of Client’s first productive use or sixty (60) days following substantial completion of implementation, whichever occurs first.

3.3.2 **Recurring Managed Services.** Vendor shall invoice Client in advance on a **monthly** basis for the recurring Managed Services fees.

3.3.3 **Advisory / Professional Services.** Vendor shall invoice monthly in arrears for time-and-materials services, accompanied by reasonably detailed timesheets or summaries.

3.3.4 **Pass-Through Costs.** Pass-through third-party costs shall be invoiced as incurred, with supporting documentation reasonably acceptable to Client.

3.4 **Payment Terms.** Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 60”**). Payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) by wire transfer or another mutually agreed method.

3.5 **Late Payment; Interest; Suspension.**

3.5.1 Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lower of:  
(a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or  
(b) the maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full.

3.5.2 If any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than **ninety (90) days** from the invoice date, Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, **suspend** the provision of some or all Services until such amounts, including accrued interest, are paid in full. Such suspension shall not be deemed a breach by Vendor, and all applicable Fees shall continue to accrue during the suspension.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.** Client shall notify Vendor in writing of any good-faith dispute with respect to an invoice within **fifteen (15) days** of the invoice date, specifying the nature and basis of the dispute. Client shall pay all undisputed portions in accordance with **Section 3.4**. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any such dispute promptly.

3.7 **Taxes.** All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes imposed in connection with this Amendment, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees.

3.8 **Expenses.** Reasonable, pre-approved travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with on-site Services shall be reimbursed by Client at cost, provided Vendor submits appropriate supporting receipts or documentation.

3.9 **No Withholding.** Except as required by Applicable Law, Client shall not set off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from payments due to Vendor, unless expressly permitted in this Amendment or agreed to in writing by Vendor.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Amendment shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **April 1, 2027** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend the Term for additional periods, subject to negotiation of applicable fees and terms. Unless and until such written agreement is executed, there shall be no automatic renewal.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor, provided that:  
(a) Client shall remain liable for all Fees accrued through the effective date of termination; and  
(b) Client shall pay Vendor an early termination fee equal to **three (3) months** of the average monthly Managed Services fees (calculated based on the preceding twelve (12) months or the period since Effective Date, whichever is shorter), as a reasonable estimate of Vendor’s unrecoverable set-up and ramp-down costs, and not as a penalty.

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Client. In such event, Vendor shall reasonably cooperate with Client’s transition of the Services, as described in **Section 4.6**, and shall not charge any early termination fee.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Amendment for material breach by the other Party if such material breach remains uncured thirty (30) days after the breaching Party’s receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail. If the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the cure period may be extended to sixty (60) days, provided the breaching Party is diligently pursuing cure.

4.4.2 Vendor may, in addition to other rights, terminate this Amendment upon written notice if:  
(a) Client fails to pay any undisputed amount that has been outstanding for more than ninety (90) days after the invoice date; or  
(b) Client becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, has a receiver appointed, or is the subject of any liquidation, bankruptcy, or similar proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Data Protection Reasons.** Either Party may immediately terminate this Amendment upon written notice if the other Party materially violates its data protection or security obligations under **Article XI** and such violation:  
(a) results in a material Security Incident; and  
(b) remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice (or such shorter time as may be required by Applicable Law or a regulator).

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment for any reason:

4.6.1 All rights and licenses granted by Vendor to Client hereunder shall terminate, except as otherwise expressly provided in **Article VI** with respect to perpetual rights or survival of certain license grants.

4.6.2 Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request received within thirty (30) days after the effective termination date, provide reasonable transition assistance (such as export of Client Data in a mutually agreed format and cooperation to facilitate transition to another provider) at Vendor’s then-current rates, plus expenses.

4.6.3 Vendor shall retain a copy of Client Data for a limited **data retention period** of ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination, unless otherwise required by Applicable Law, after which Vendor shall securely delete or anonymize Client Data in accordance with **Section 11.6**, subject to any ongoing legal holds or archival requirements.

4.6.4 Termination shall not relieve either Party of any payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination, nor shall it affect any rights, remedies, obligations, or liabilities that have accrued up to such date.

4.7 **Survival.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, **Articles III (Fees and Payment Terms), V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions)** and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Amendment.

(b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein; and  
(c) protect such Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.6 **Confidentiality of Vendor Materials.** Vendor Materials and Vendor’s non-public methodologies, security measures, and proprietary tools shall be deemed **Confidential Information of Vendor**.

(a) the Receiving Party may retain copies as required by Applicable Law; and

5.8 **Survival.** The obligations under this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and survive for **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Amendment; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Client Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or personal/sensitive data under Applicable Law.

12.1 **Service Availability.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the Services is available **ninety-nine point nine percent (99.9%)** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Permitted Downtime (the **“Uptime Commitment”**).

(a) Vendor provides at least seventy-two (72) hours’ advance notice; and

12.3 **Incident Severity and Response Times.** Vendor shall categorize and respond to incidents as follows:

12.3.1 **Severity 1 (Critical):** Production service outage or critical functionality unavailable with no workaround.  
- Initial response time: within **1 hour**;  
- Vendor shall work on a 24x7 basis until service is restored or a workaround is implemented.

12.3.2 **Severity 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired with significant business impact, but workaround available.  
- Initial response time: within **4 hours** during Business Days;  
- Resolution target: within **24 hours**, subject to complexity.

12.3.3 **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical functionality issue or performance degradation.  
- Initial response time: within **1 Business Day**;  
- Resolution target: within **5 Business Days** or next maintenance release.

12.3.4 **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issue, cosmetic defect, or general inquiry.  
- Response time: within **2 Business Days**;  
- Resolution: to be addressed in future releases as reasonably prioritized.

12.4 **SLA Reporting.** Vendor shall provide monthly SLA reports summarizing uptime, incident metrics, response times, and resolution performance, and shall make such reports available to Client upon request.

12.5 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.** If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in a given calendar month, Vendor shall provide Client with a **service credit** equal to a percentage of the monthly Managed Services fee for the affected month, as follows:

- Uptime ≥ 99.0% and < 99.9%: **5%** service credit  
- Uptime ≥ 98.0% and < 99.0%: **10%** service credit  
- Uptime < 98.0%: **20%** service credit  

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute Client’s sole remedy for SLA breaches, except where such breaches result from Vendor’s gross negligence or willful misconduct.
