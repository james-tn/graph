## ARTICLE I – DEFINITIONS

1.1 **“Affiliate”** means any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party, where “control” means ownership of more than fifty percent (50%) of the voting interests of such entity or the ability to direct the management or policies of such entity, whether through ownership, contract, or otherwise.

1.2 **“Agreement”** or **“DPA”** means this Data Processing Agreement, including its preamble, recitals, all Articles and Sections, and any attached schedules, annexes, or appendices, as may be amended from time to time in accordance with Article XVI.

1.3 **“Applicable Data Protection Laws”** means all data protection, privacy, and security laws and regulations applicable to the processing of Personal Data under this Agreement, including, without limitation, the UK GDPR, EU GDPR (if applicable), the Data Protection Act 2018 (UK), the CCPA (to the extent applicable), and any binding regulations and guidance issued by relevant supervisory authorities.

1.4 **“Business Day”** means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in England and Wales, on which banks in London are generally open for business.

1.5 **“Confidential Information”** has the meaning set forth in Section 5.1 and includes, without limitation, Personal Data, trade secrets, business plans, software, financial information, technical data, customer data, and other proprietary information disclosed by one Party to the other.

1.6 **“Contract Value”** means the total monetary value of this Agreement, equal to US$1,714,470, payable by the Client to the Service Provider in accordance with Article III.

1.7 **“Controller”** (or **“Data Controller”**) means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data, and, for purposes of this Agreement, shall mean the Client.

1.8 **“Data Breach”** or **“Personal Data Breach”** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed by the Service Provider or its Sub-processors in connection with this Agreement.

1.9 **“Data Subject”** means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed under this Agreement, as such term is defined under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

1.10 **“Deliverables”** means all work product, software configurations, reports, analyses, documentation, and other items to be developed or delivered by the Service Provider to the Client as part of the Services under Article II.

1.11 **“Force Majeure Event”** has the meaning set forth in Article XV and includes any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party that prevents or delays the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

1.12 **“Intellectual Property Rights”** or **“IPR”** means any and all intellectual property rights of any kind or nature worldwide, whether registered or unregistered, including copyrights, patents, trademarks, trade secrets, design rights, moral rights, database rights, and any applications or rights to apply for registration of such rights.

1.13 **“Personal Data”** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by the Service Provider on behalf of the Client in the performance of the Services, as such term is defined under Applicable Data Protection Laws, and includes “personal information” as defined under the CCPA.

1.14 **“Processing”** or **“Process”** means any operation or set of operations which is performed upon Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.15 **“Processor”** (or **“Data Processor”**) means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller, and, for purposes of this Agreement, shall mean the Service Provider.

1.16 **“Services”** means the data processing, hosting, managed services, support, and related professional services to be provided by the Service Provider to the Client under this Agreement, as more particularly described in Article II.

1.17 **“Service Level Agreement”** or **“SLA”** means the service level commitments and performance metrics, including availability and response times, described in Article XII.

1.18 **“Sub-processor”** means any third-party Processor engaged by the Service Provider to process Personal Data on behalf of the Client in connection with the Services.

1.19 **“Term”** means the period commencing on the Effective Date and ending on the Expiration Date specified in Section 4.1, including any extensions or renewals agreed in accordance with this Agreement.

1.20 **“Third-Party Claim”** means any claim, demand, action, charge, lawsuit, proceeding, regulatory investigation, or other assertion of liability brought by a third party (including a governmental authority) against a Party.

## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Description of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Client a suite of managed data processing and cloud-based services, including, without limitation:

  2.1.1 Hosting, storage, and maintenance of the Client’s data, databases, and application environments on infrastructure managed by the Service Provider or its Sub-processors;

  2.1.2 Data ingestion, transformation, and normalization services, including extraction from Client systems, validation, enrichment, and loading into agreed data repositories;

  2.1.3 Operation of analytics tools, dashboards, and reporting solutions for the Client’s internal business intelligence and operational reporting;

  2.1.4 Provision of Application Programming Interface (“API”) endpoints and middleware services to enable secure access to, and integration with, the Client’s internal and external systems;

  2.1.5 Ongoing technical support, monitoring, and incident management in accordance with the SLA set out in Article XII;

  2.1.6 Professional services for implementation, configuration, data migration, training, and advisory support as may be reasonably requested by the Client and accepted by the Service Provider in writing.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
The Service Provider shall deliver, as part of the Services, certain Deliverables, which may include:

  2.2.1 Implementation plans, data mapping documentation, and system architecture diagrams;

  2.2.2 Configured instances of the Service Provider’s software and services tailored to the Client’s requirements;

  2.2.3 Custom reports, dashboards, and data models, as mutually agreed in writing;

  2.2.4 Training materials, user guides, and administrative documentation for the Client’s personnel;

  2.2.5 Periodic performance, usage, and security reports demonstrating adherence to the SLA and Applicable Data Protection Laws.

All Deliverables shall be considered accepted upon the earlier of: (i) written confirmation of acceptance by the Client; or (ii) thirty (30) days after delivery if the Client fails to provide written rejection identifying material nonconformities, provided such failure is not due to a breach by the Service Provider.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  2.3.1 In a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, using suitably qualified personnel with appropriate skills, training, and experience;

  2.3.2 In accordance with industry best practices for security, availability, and performance applicable to similar services;

  2.3.3 In compliance with all Applicable Data Protection Laws and all other applicable laws and regulations, including those relating to data privacy, export controls, and information security;

  2.3.4 In accordance with the SLA (Article XII), this Agreement, and any additional specifications or statements of work mutually executed by the Parties.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall agree in writing on key implementation and delivery milestones, including:

  2.4.1 An initial project kick-off within ten (10) Business Days of the Effective Date;

  2.4.2 Delivery of an implementation plan and project schedule within fifteen (15) Business Days after the kick-off;

  2.4.3 Completion of core system configuration and initial data migration within ninety (90) days of the Effective Date, subject to timely provision of necessary information, access, and resources by the Client;

  2.4.4 User acceptance testing and go-live within one hundred twenty (120) days of the Effective Date, unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

Any failure or delay by the Service Provider to meet agreed milestones due to the Client’s delay in providing necessary cooperation, access, or information shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement by the Service Provider, and the affected milestones shall be extended proportionately.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall:

  2.5.1 Provide the Service Provider with necessary access to the Client’s systems, networks, and relevant data, subject to reasonable security and confidentiality requirements;

  2.5.2 Designate a project manager and other key personnel to coordinate with the Service Provider, facilitate decision-making, and provide timely approvals;

  2.5.3 Ensure the accuracy, completeness, and legality of the data provided to the Service Provider for processing and promptly notify the Service Provider of any required corrections or updates;

  2.5.4 Obtain any necessary consents, authorizations, and notices required under Applicable Data Protection Laws to enable the Service Provider to lawfully process Personal Data as instructed by the Client;

  2.5.5 Use the Services only for lawful purposes and in accordance with this Agreement and any use policies provided by the Service Provider.

2.6 **Sub-processing.**  
Subject to Article XI, the Service Provider may engage Sub-processors to perform certain aspects of the Services, provided that:

  2.6.1 Each Sub-processor is bound by written terms imposing data protection obligations no less protective than those set out in this Agreement;

  2.6.2 The Service Provider remains fully liable to the Client for the performance of such Sub-processors and their compliance with this Agreement.

2.7 **Service Modifications.**  
The Service Provider may make routine modifications or improvements to the Services provided they do not materially degrade the functionality, security, or performance thereof. Material changes affecting scope, functionality, or pricing shall be managed in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management).

3.8 **No Set-Off.**  
Except for amounts disputed in accordance with Section 3.6, the Client shall not withhold, set off, or deduct any amounts from payments due to the Service Provider under this Agreement, except as required by law or expressly permitted in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date** and shall remain in full force and effect until **January 8, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may agree in writing to renew or extend the Term for additional periods on mutually agreed terms, at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile, the Client shall have the right to terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider. The Service Provider may terminate for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Client. In either case, the Client shall pay all fees for Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect upon written notice if:

  4.4.1 The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or

  4.4.2 The other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or is subject to any other similar proceeding under applicable insolvency laws.

4.5 **Termination for Data Protection Breach.**  
Without limiting Section 4.4, the Client may terminate this Agreement, or any affected portion of the Services, immediately upon written notice to the Service Provider if the Service Provider materially violates Applicable Data Protection Laws or its obligations under Article XI and fails to cure such violation, where capable of cure, within ten (10) days of written notice.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  4.6.1 The Service Provider shall cease processing the Client’s Personal Data, except to the extent required by law or necessary to perform any post-termination assistance requested by the Client;

  4.6.2 The Client shall pay the Service Provider all undisputed fees and expenses accrued and owed up to the effective date of termination;

  4.6.3 Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4 and Article XI, as applicable.

4.7 **Post-Termination Assistance.**  
For a period of up to ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination or expiration, the Service Provider shall, upon the Client’s written request and at the Client’s expense, provide reasonable assistance to facilitate the orderly migration of the Client’s data and operations to the Client or a third-party provider.

4.8 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including Articles III (to the extent of any unpaid fees), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and Section 4.8.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“Confidential Information” means all information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”) in connection with this Agreement, whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, Personal Data, business plans, pricing, financial data, trade secrets, product designs, software (including source code), system configurations, and security information.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  5.2.1 Use Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;

  5.2.2 Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent;

  5.2.3 Limit access to Confidential Information to its employees, officers, contractors, and Sub-processors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein;

  5.2.4 Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information of similar importance, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  5.3.1 Was in its lawful possession or known to it prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without restriction on disclosure;

  5.3.2 Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

  5.3.3 Is lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party without restriction on disclosure;

  5.3.4 Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or a valid order of a court or governmental authority, provided that the Receiving Party (i) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement, to the extent legally permitted, and (ii) cooperates with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking protective measures or other remedies.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain copies as required by applicable law or for legitimate business continuity and archival purposes, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and survive for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information constitutes a trade secret or Personal Data under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Availability and Uptime.**  
The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the core production Services are available **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime. “Permitted Downtime” includes:

  12.1.1 Scheduled maintenance, provided that the Service Provider gives the Client at least forty-eight (48) hours’ advance notice and schedules such maintenance during off-peak hours wherever reasonably possible;

  12.1.2 Emergency maintenance required to address critical security vulnerabilities or system failures, with notice to the Client as soon as practicable;

  12.1.3 Downtime caused by factors outside the Service Provider’s reasonable control, including Force Majeure Events, failures of third-party networks, or Client systems.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall respond to and work to resolve incidents reported by the Client according to the following priority levels:

  12.2.1 **Priority 1 (Critical):**  
  - Definition: Complete outage or severe degradation of the core Services affecting all users, with no workaround available.  
  - Response Time: Within one (1) hour of notification.  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: Within four (4) hours.

  12.2.2 **Priority 2 (High):**  
  - Definition: Major functionality impaired, affecting a significant subset of users, with limited workaround.  
  - Response Time: Within four (4) Business Hours of notification.  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: Within one (1) Business Day.

  12.2.3 **Priority 3 (Medium):**  
  - Definition: Non-critical issues or defects with a reasonable workaround.  
  - Response Time: Within one (1) Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution: Within five (5) Business Days.

  12.2.4 **Priority 4 (Low):**  
  - Definition: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or enhancement requests.  
  - Response Time: Within two (2) Business Days.  
  - Target Resolution: As agreed in the normal course of product updates.

12.3 **Service Credits.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in a given calendar month (excluding Permitted Downtime), the Client shall be entitled to receive service credits, calculated as a percentage of the monthly recurring fees for the affected Services:

  12.3.1 Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% credit;  
  12.3.2 Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% credit;  
  12.3.3 Availability below 98.0%: 20% credit.

Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) due. Service credits are the Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitment, without prejudice to the Client’s other contractual rights for repeated or material SLA failures.

12.4 **Chronic SLA Failures.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment for three (3) consecutive calendar months, or for any four (4) months within a rolling twelve (12) month period, the Client may, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, terminate the affected Services without penalty and receive a pro rata refund of prepaid, unused fees.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, government actions, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), failure of public utilities, or widespread internet or telecommunications outages.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  15.2.1 Notify the other Party in writing without undue delay after becoming aware of the Force Majeure Event, describing its nature, expected duration, and impact on performance;

  15.2.2 Use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume full performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that they are prevented or delayed by the Force Majeure Event. The affected Party shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performing such obligations during this period.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability, except for fees and obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
