## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall provide to Client the Services described in this Agreement and in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. The Services may include, without limitation:  
(a) technology consulting, business analysis, and requirements gathering;  
(b) solution architecture, system design, configuration, and customization;  
(c) software integration, data migration, and testing;  
(d) deployment, implementation, and cutover support;  
(e) training and knowledge transfer;  
(f) ongoing managed services, monitoring, maintenance, and support; and  
(g) such other services as the Parties may agree to in an SOW.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
2.2.1 Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify: (a) the nature and scope of the Services; (b) the Deliverables to be developed or provided; (c) applicable Service Levels; (d) the project plan, milestones, and timelines; (e) the fees, expenses, and payment schedule; and (f) any special terms or assumptions applicable to such SOW.  
2.2.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control, except where the SOW expressly states that a specific provision of this Agreement is being superseded, in which case the SOW shall control solely with respect to that specific provision and that SOW.

2.3 **Project Management and Governance.**  
2.3.1 **Project Managers.** Each Party shall designate a project manager (“**Project Manager**”) who shall serve as the primary point of contact for day-to-day communications regarding the Services. The Project Managers shall meet (in person or via teleconference) at regular intervals agreed in the applicable SOW to review progress, discuss issues, and coordinate activities.  
2.3.2 **Steering Committee.** For larger engagements, the Parties may establish a steering committee consisting of representatives of each Party. The steering committee shall meet as specified in the SOW to review overall project status, address escalated issues, and make key decisions, including those related to scope changes per **Article XIII**.

2.4 **Deliverables and Acceptance.**  
2.4.1 **Development and Delivery.** Vendor shall develop and deliver the Deliverables in accordance with the Specifications, timelines, and milestones set forth in the applicable SOW. Vendor shall provide Deliverables in a form and format reasonably acceptable to Client.  
2.4.2 **Acceptance Testing.** Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Client shall have a period of **thirty (30) days** from delivery of each Deliverable or completion of each milestone (the “**Acceptance Period**”) to test such Deliverable or milestone for conformity with the applicable Specifications.  
2.4.3 **Acceptance Criteria.** A Deliverable or milestone shall be deemed accepted upon the earliest of: (a) Client’s written notice of acceptance; (b) expiration of the Acceptance Period without Client having provided a written rejection notice specifying material non-conformities; or (c) Client’s use of the Deliverable in production (other than testing or evaluation).  
2.4.4 **Rejection and Correction.** If Client reasonably determines that a Deliverable or milestone does not materially conform to the applicable Specifications, Client shall within the Acceptance Period notify Vendor in writing of such non-conformity, specifying the nature of the discrepancies in reasonable detail. Vendor shall, at no additional cost, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable or milestone within a mutually agreed timeframe for re-testing. Client shall then have a further reasonable period (not less than ten (10) Business Days) to re-test. If, after two (2) correction and re-testing cycles, the Deliverable still fails to meet the material Specifications, Client may: (i) accept the Deliverable “as-is” with an equitable fee reduction; or (ii) reject the Deliverable and terminate the relevant portion of the SOW upon written notice, subject to **Article IV**, with a refund of fees paid for the rejected Deliverable.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Services: (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry best practices and standards of care generally exercised by reputable providers of similar services; (b) in accordance with the Specifications and Service Levels; and (c) in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations.  
2.5.2 Vendor shall ensure that all personnel performing Services on its behalf are appropriately trained, qualified, and experienced, and Vendor shall remain responsible for the acts and omissions of such personnel as if they were Vendor’s own acts and omissions.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
2.6.1 Client shall provide timely access to Client Materials, personnel, systems, and facilities as reasonably required for Vendor to perform the Services.  
2.6.2 Client shall be responsible for: (a) providing accurate and complete information; (b) making timely decisions and approvals; and (c) fulfilling any assumptions or dependencies specified in the SOW.  
2.6.3 Vendor shall not be liable for delays or failures in performance to the extent caused by Client’s failure to fulfill its obligations under this Agreement; provided that Vendor promptly notifies Client of such issues and uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate any adverse impact.

2.7 **Subcontractors.** Vendor may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (a) Vendor remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services and compliance with this Agreement; (b) any subcontractor with access to Confidential Information or Personal Data is bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and (c) Vendor shall be liable for any act or omission of its subcontractors to the same extent as for its own acts or omissions.

2.8 **Risk Level; Balanced Terms.** The Parties acknowledge that the risk level associated with this Agreement is **Medium**, and that the terms of this Agreement, including with respect to termination, indemnification, and limitation of liability, are intended to be commercially balanced and reasonable, subject to negotiation as needed in each SOW.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees and Contract Value.**  
3.1.1 In consideration for the Services and Deliverables provided by Vendor, Client shall pay Vendor the fees set forth in the applicable SOW(s), which collectively are anticipated to total the Contract Value of **US $1,039,997** during the Term, subject to adjustments in accordance with this Agreement.  
3.1.2 Fees may include: (a) fixed-fee amounts for defined Deliverables or milestones; (b) time-and-materials (“**T&M**”) fees based on Vendor’s standard hourly or daily rates; and/or (c) recurring fees for ongoing managed services, as specified in the applicable SOW.

3.2 **Payment Terms.**  
3.2.1 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for Services performed and accepted Deliverables delivered during the preceding month.  
3.2.2 All undisputed amounts invoiced by Vendor shall be due and payable by Client within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of receipt of a valid invoice (“**Net 45**” payment terms).  
3.2.3 All payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars (USD) by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method to the account designated in writing by Vendor.

3.3 **Milestone Payments.** Where an SOW specifies milestone-based payments, Client shall pay the fees associated with each milestone upon acceptance of the corresponding Deliverable or completion of the milestone in accordance with **Section 2.4**. Vendor may issue invoices upon such acceptance or completion, and Client shall pay in accordance with the Net 45 payment terms.

3.4 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
3.4.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred by Vendor in connection with the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other expenses, provided that such expenses are: (a) in accordance with Client’s then-current travel and expense policies (as provided to Vendor); and (b) supported by appropriate documentation and receipts.  
3.4.2 Vendor shall seek Client’s written approval in advance for any single expense in excess of **US $1,000** or as otherwise set forth in the SOW.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
3.5.1 All fees and charges are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, or other similar taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority (“**Taxes**”). Client shall be responsible for paying all applicable Taxes resulting from this Agreement, other than taxes based on Vendor’s income, property, or employees.  
3.5.2 If Vendor is required to collect Taxes on Client’s behalf, such Taxes shall be separately stated on Vendor’s invoices and paid by Client in accordance with this **Article III**.

3.6 **Disputed Invoices.**  
3.6.1 If Client disputes any invoiced amount, Client shall, within **fifteen (15) Business Days** of receipt of the invoice, notify Vendor in writing of the nature of the dispute and the amount in dispute, with reasonable supporting details.  
3.6.2 Client may withhold payment of only the disputed portion of the invoice, and shall timely pay the undisputed portion. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly in accordance with **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**.  
3.6.3 Upon resolution, any amounts determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of such resolution.

3.7 **Late Payments.**  
3.7.1 Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the date due until the date paid, at the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.  
3.7.2 If Client fails to pay undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** after the due date, and fails to cure such non-payment within **ten (10) Business Days** after receipt of Vendor’s written notice, Vendor may, in addition to any other rights and remedies: (a) suspend performance of the affected Services until all overdue amounts (including interest) are paid in full; and/or (b) terminate the applicable SOW in accordance with **Section 4.3**.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The initial term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **April 23, 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Agreement (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew this Agreement for one or more additional periods, each up to three (3) years, on mutually agreed terms, including revised fees and Service Levels. Absent such written renewal, this Agreement shall expire automatically on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, without cause, upon providing the other Party at least **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice.  
4.3.2 Upon such termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor: (a) fees for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted up to the effective date of termination; (b) any approved, non-cancellable expenses actually incurred; and (c) reasonable wind-down costs mutually agreed in advance.  
4.3.3 Upon such termination for convenience by Vendor, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to Client’s operations and shall cooperate in transition activities in accordance with **Section 4.6**.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.  
4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors; (c) has a petition filed by or against it under any bankruptcy or insolvency law (which, if involuntary, is not dismissed within sixty (60) days); or (d) ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Termination of Individual SOWs.** Termination of a particular SOW shall not, by itself, result in termination of this Agreement or any other SOW, unless the notice of termination expressly states otherwise. However, termination of this Agreement shall automatically terminate all outstanding SOWs, except as otherwise agreed in writing.

4.6 **Effect of Termination; Transition Assistance.**  
4.6.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:  
(a) Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the terminated Services;  
(b) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed fees and reimbursable expenses earned and incurred through the effective date of termination; and  
(c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**.  
4.6.2 Upon Client’s request and at Client’s cost (unless termination is due to Vendor’s material breach), Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to **ninety (90) days** following the effective date of termination, to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Client or to a successor service provider.

4.7 **Survival.** The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive.

5.6 **Survival.** The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under applicable law.
