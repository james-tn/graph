## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
During the Term (as defined in Section 4.1), the Service Provider shall provide to the Company comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Systems, on a managed service and support basis. The Maintenance Services shall include, without limitation: (a) proactive monitoring; (b) preventive maintenance; (c) corrective maintenance; (d) incident management and resolution; (e) problem management; (f) patch management and updates; (g) limited enhancements as mutually agreed; and (h) technical advisory and reporting services, all in accordance with the Service Levels described in Article XII.

2.2 **Covered Systems.**  
The Systems covered under this Agreement shall include, at a minimum, the following categories, as further detailed in the SOW(s):

  2.2.1 Enterprise application servers (Windows and Linux) used to host the Company’s internal business applications;  

  2.2.2 Network infrastructure, including routers, switches, firewalls, load balancers, and VPN gateways, used to support the Company’s production and development environments;  

  2.2.3 Storage systems and associated backup infrastructure supporting the Company’s primary data centers and critical remote locations;  

  2.2.4 Selected end-user devices and peripherals to the extent specifically identified in an SOW; and  

  2.2.5 Associated operating systems, firmware, and third-party software components for which the Service Provider has been given administrative access or management responsibility.

The specific inventory of covered Systems, including device counts, locations, and technical specifications, shall be set forth in one or more SOWs. The Parties acknowledge that the covered Systems may evolve over time, and any significant additions, removals, or changes shall be processed through the change management procedure in Article XIII.

2.3 **Preventive Maintenance.**  
The Service Provider shall perform periodic preventive maintenance activities to reduce the likelihood of Incidents and to optimize System performance. Such preventive maintenance shall include:

  2.3.1 Regular review of system logs and performance metrics;  

  2.3.2 Installation of firmware and operating system updates recommended by the OEMs or software vendors, subject to the Company’s approval where required;  

  2.3.3 Capacity planning and trend analysis, with written recommendations to the Company regarding necessary upgrades or configuration changes; and  

  2.3.4 Quarterly health checks of the critical Systems, with corresponding reports summarizing findings, risks, and recommended remedial actions.

Preventive maintenance shall be scheduled, whenever practicable, during agreed maintenance windows to minimize impact on the Company’s operations, in accordance with Section 12.4.

2.4 **Corrective Maintenance and Incident Management.**  
The Service Provider shall provide corrective maintenance services to address Incidents affecting the Systems. Such services shall include:

  2.4.1 24x7 monitoring of critical Systems and reception of incident alerts;  

  2.4.2 Incident triage, classification of severity levels, and prioritization consistent with the SLA;  

  2.4.3 Remote diagnosis and troubleshooting using industry-standard tools and procedures;  

  2.4.4 On-site support where required and specified in an SOW; and  

  2.4.5 Escalation and coordination with third-party vendors or OEMs where the Company has active support contracts, subject to appropriate authorizations.

The Service Provider shall comply with the response and resolution targets set forth in Article XII. For Incidents requiring extended remediation, the Service Provider shall provide periodic status updates and an incident closure report upon resolution.

2.5 **Patch and Release Management.**  
The Service Provider shall manage patches, updates, and releases for the Systems within the scope of this Agreement, including:

  2.5.1 Evaluation of vendor-released patches for relevance, stability, and security impact;  

  2.5.2 Development and maintenance of a patch deployment plan and schedule, including testing in a staging environment where feasible;  

  2.5.3 Implementation of security patches within risk-based timeframes agreed with the Company; and  

  2.5.4 Maintenance of records of applied patches and versions, available for audit by the Company upon reasonable notice.

Patch deployment may be subject to the Company’s change approval process and shall be coordinated to avoid conflicting changes and minimize downtime.

2.6 **Reporting and Documentation.**  
The Service Provider shall prepare and deliver to the Company, at a minimum on a monthly basis (or more frequently as set out in an SOW), the following:

  2.6.1 Service performance reports, including incident volume, response and resolution times, SLA compliance, planned and unplanned outages, and major root causes;  

  2.6.2 System health and capacity reports, including resource utilization trends and potential bottlenecks;  

  2.6.3 Security-related reports relating to patching status, critical vulnerabilities identified, and remediation activities; and  

  2.6.4 A summary of any significant risks or recommendations relevant to the Company’s IT environment.

All documentation shall be provided in electronic format, in English, and in a form reasonably acceptable to the Company.

2.7 **Exclusions.**  
Unless specifically provided in an SOW or a duly executed Change Order, the Services shall not include:

  2.7.1 Development of new software applications or major re-engineering of existing applications;  

  2.7.2 Replacement of hardware components that are not covered by manufacturer warranty or separate contracts;  

  2.7.3 Support for third-party software not approved or designated by the Company as part of the covered Systems;  

  2.7.4 Support for Systems or components modified by the Company or a third party without the Service Provider’s prior knowledge or consent, where such modification materially hinders maintenance.

Any services requested by the Company that are outside the scope described in this Article II or an SOW shall be treated as additional services and may require a Change Order with corresponding fee adjustments.

2.8 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications;  
  (b) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar maintenance and support services;  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Laws and the Company’s reasonable written policies made available to the Service Provider; and  
  (d) in accordance with the Service Levels described in Article XII.

2.9 **Cooperation and Dependencies.**  
The Company acknowledges that the Service Provider’s performance of the Services is dependent upon the timely and effective performance by the Company of its responsibilities, including providing access, approvals, and necessary information. The Service Provider shall not be responsible for delays or failures in performing the Services to the extent caused by the Company’s failure to comply with its obligations under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties agree that the total estimated value of this Agreement for the initial Term is **Four Hundred Twelve Thousand Eight Hundred Ninety-Nine U.S. Dollars (US$412,899)** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to adjustments pursuant to Change Orders and any additional services requested by the Company under this Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise provided in an SOW:

  3.2.1 **Base Monthly Fee.** The Company shall pay to the Service Provider a fixed base monthly fee equal to the Contract Value divided by twelve (12), i.e., **US$34,408.25** per month, in consideration of the standard Maintenance Services described herein.

  3.2.2 **Time and Materials (T&M) Rates.** Additional services outside the agreed scope, including major enhancements, project work, or emergency support beyond agreed limits, shall be charged on a time and materials basis at the Service Provider’s standard hourly rates as set forth in the applicable SOW or Change Order.

  3.2.3 **Pass-Through Costs.** The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for any pre-approved third-party costs directly related to the Services, including OEM support escalations or specialized licenses, without markup, subject to Section 3.5.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

  3.3.1 **Invoicing.** The Service Provider shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears for the Base Monthly Fee and any approved additional charges. Each invoice shall reasonably itemize the Services performed and any reimbursable expenses.

  3.3.2 **Payment Terms.** The Company shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) calendar days** of the invoice date (“**Net 60**”). Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other method specified by the Service Provider and reasonably acceptable to the Company.

  3.3.3 **Disputed Amounts.** If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, it shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the nature of the dispute. The Company may withhold payment of the disputed portion, but shall timely pay the undisputed portion. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve invoice disputes promptly. Any resolution may be addressed in accordance with Article XIV.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid by the Company when due shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of payment at a rate equal to the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law. The Service Provider shall provide written notice of late payment and a fifteen (15)-day cure period prior to exercising any right to suspend Services for non-payment.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, documented, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals for on-site work, in accordance with the Company’s expense policies as provided to the Service Provider. The Service Provider shall include appropriate receipts and supporting documentation with reimbursement invoices.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All fees quoted are exclusive of Taxes. The Company shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes in connection with this Agreement, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income or property. If the Service Provider is required to collect Taxes, such Taxes shall be separately stated on the invoice and paid by the Company.

3.7 **Set-Off.**  
Neither Party shall have the right of set-off with respect to amounts due under this Agreement, except to the extent expressly permitted by this Agreement or agreed in writing by the Parties.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **August 18, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Unless either Party provides written notice of its intent not to renew at least sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods (each, a “**Renewal Term**”) under the same terms and conditions, except that the Service Provider may adjust its fees for each Renewal Term upon providing at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to the Company. The Initial Term and any Renewal Term(s) are collectively referred to as the “**Term**.”

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. In such case, the Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and approved expenses incurred through the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof, specifying the nature of the breach.

  4.4.2 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, is subject to a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or has a receiver or similar officer appointed over its business or assets.

4.5 **Suspension of Services.**  
Without limiting any other rights or remedies, the Service Provider may suspend the Services (in whole or in part) upon fifteen (15) days’ prior written notice if: (a) any undisputed amount remains unpaid more than fifteen (15) days after the due date; or (b) the Company materially interferes with the Service Provider’s performance. The Service Provider shall resume the Services promptly upon cure of the applicable issue.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination;  

  (b) Each Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) all Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession or control, in accordance with Section 5.4;  

  (c) The Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate with the Company, at the Company’s expense and subject to mutually agreed scope and rates, to transition the Services to the Company or another service provider, including providing relevant documentation, configuration data, and knowledge transfer; and  

  (d) Any licenses granted under this Agreement shall terminate, except as expressly provided otherwise in Article VI.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions), and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the covered Systems and associated maintenance and monitoring services are available at least **99.9%** of the time on a monthly basis, excluding Planned Maintenance and Force Majeure Events (“**Service Availability**”).

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall respond to and target resolution of Incidents in accordance with the following guidelines (unless otherwise agreed in an SOW):

- **Severity 1 – Critical:** Complete loss of a critical business function or major security incident.  
  - Response Time: within 1 hour (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Severity 2 – High:** Significant degradation affecting a large number of users but workarounds may exist.  
  - Response Time: within 2 hours (Business Days)  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 8 Business Hours  

- **Severity 3 – Medium:** Partial loss or reduction in functionality with limited user impact.  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 2 Business Days  

- **Severity 4 – Low:** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general service requests.  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 5 Business Days

12.3 **Service Credits.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the Service Availability or response targets in a given calendar month, the Company may be entitled to Service Credits against future fees, calculated as follows:

  (a) Service Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 2% of the monthly Base Monthly Fee;  

  (b) Service Availability between 98.0% and 98.9%: 5% of the monthly Base Monthly Fee;  

  (c) Service Availability below 98.0%: 10% of the monthly Base Monthly Fee;

provided that the total Service Credits in any month shall not exceed 15% of the monthly Base Monthly Fee. Service Credits shall be the Company’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to the Company’s right to terminate the Agreement for chronic failures in accordance with Article IV.

12.4 **Planned Maintenance.**  
Planned Maintenance that may impact Service Availability shall be scheduled during agreed maintenance windows and communicated to the Company in writing at least five (5) Business Days in advance, except in urgent situations where shorter notice is necessary to address security or stability issues. Planned Maintenance shall not count against Service Availability metrics, provided it is conducted within agreed windows.

12.5 **Reporting and Review.**  
The Service Provider shall provide monthly SLA reports to the Company summarizing Service Availability, incidents, and SLA compliance. The Parties shall review the SLA performance at least quarterly and may adjust Service Levels by mutual written agreement.

FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure Event.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes, labor disputes, epidemics, pandemics, governmental actions, interruption or failure of the internet or hosting infrastructure not caused by the affected Party, or other similar events.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, describing the nature and expected duration of the event and the anticipated impact on performance. The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such performance is prevented by the Force Majeure Event. The time for performance shall be extended by a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of a material obligation, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
