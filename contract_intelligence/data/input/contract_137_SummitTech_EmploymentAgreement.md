## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 The Company hereby engages the Service Provider, and the Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to perform and deliver the Services and Deliverables described in this Agreement and any Statements of Work executed by the Parties. The Services shall primarily concern the design, implementation, and ongoing support of advanced HR and workforce management solutions, including but not limited to employee onboarding workflows, time and attendance integrations, payroll system interfaces, HR analytics dashboards, and related middleware.

2.1.2 The Parties anticipate that the Services will involve multiple phases, including planning, requirements gathering, solution design, software development and configuration, integration, testing, deployment, and post-deployment support. Each phase may be further documented in one or more SOWs that reference Agreement No. AGR-SUM-202412-137 and incorporate the terms of this Agreement by reference.

### 2.2 Detailed Description of Services

2.2.1 **Consulting and Requirements Analysis.** The Service Provider shall consult with the Company’s designated project managers, business owners, IT staff, and HR stakeholders to identify functional, technical, and regulatory requirements for the Company’s HR technology ecosystem. This shall include conducting discovery workshops, stakeholder interviews, and analysis of existing systems and processes. The Service Provider shall document such requirements in a formal Requirements Specification, which shall be considered a Deliverable.

2.2.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.** Based on the Requirements Specification, the Service Provider shall design an architecture for the integrated HR solution, including data flows between systems, integration points with payroll, timekeeping, benefits, and identity systems, security controls, and performance requirements. The Service Provider shall create high-level and detailed design documents, including system diagrams, integration specifications, and data mapping documents, all of which shall constitute Deliverables.

2.2.3 **Software Development and Configuration.** The Service Provider shall develop, configure, and customize software components, scripts, configuration templates, and interfaces necessary to implement the solution design. This includes, without limitation, development of APIs, middleware configurations, data transformation scripts, and configuration of off-the-shelf HR applications where applicable. The Service Provider shall utilize commercially reasonable development practices and version control mechanisms.

2.2.4 **Integration and Testing.** The Service Provider shall integrate the developed components with the Company’s existing HR systems and third-party platforms as identified in the Requirements Specification and relevant SOWs. Integration efforts shall include unit testing, system testing, integration testing, and user acceptance test support. The Service Provider shall provide test plans and test results summaries as Deliverables. The Company shall be responsible for performing or coordinating user acceptance testing, but the Service Provider will provide reasonable support, troubleshooting, and remediation.

2.2.5 **Deployment and Transition to Production.** The Service Provider shall assist in deploying the solution into the Company’s production environment, including preparing deployment checklists, cut-over plans, rollback plans, and relevant documentation. The Service Provider shall support the Company’s technical staff during deployment windows and validate that the deployed solution is functioning in accordance with the approved test results and Service Levels outlined in Article XII.

2.2.6 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** The Service Provider shall provide training sessions for the Company’s HR, IT, and operations personnel on the operation, administration, and maintenance of the implemented solution. Such sessions may be conducted remotely or on-site as set forth in applicable SOWs. The Service Provider shall also provide user guides, administrator manuals, and knowledge base articles as Deliverables.

2.2.7 **Managed Support Services.** During the Term, and subject to applicable Fees, the Service Provider shall provide ongoing support for the implemented solution, including incident management, troubleshooting, minor enhancements, and performance optimization. The Service Provider shall provide support in accordance with the Service Levels described in Article XII and any applicable SOW. Support may be provided through a ticketing system, email, or other mechanisms agreed by the Parties.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services (i) in a professional and workmanlike manner, (ii) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services, (iii) subject to the Service Levels set forth in Article XII, and (iv) in compliance with Applicable Law.

2.3.2 The Company acknowledges that the Services may depend on timely access to Client Materials, personnel, systems, and third-party providers under the control of the Company. The Service Provider shall not be responsible for delays, failures, or defects in the Services or Deliverables to the extent caused by (i) the Company’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities under Section 2.4, (ii) unavailability or malfunction of the Company’s systems not caused by the Service Provider, or (iii) failure of third-party providers retained by the Company.

2.3.3 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, all timelines and Milestones shall be considered **reasonable estimates** and not absolute deadlines, provided that the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve Milestones on or before the dates specified in the applicable SOW.

### 2.4 Company Responsibilities

2.4.1 The Company shall provide the Service Provider with timely access to all Client Materials, systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably required for the Service Provider to perform the Services.

2.4.2 The Company shall designate a project manager or primary contact to coordinate communications and approvals and to provide prompt decisions and feedback.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Fees and Contract Value

3.1.1 In consideration for the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Agreement, the Company shall pay the Service Provider total Fees not to exceed **USD $611,590** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustments through Change Orders or additional SOWs executed in accordance with Article XIII.

3.1.2 The Contract Value may be allocated among various SOWs and project phases, including but not limited to design, development, integration, deployment, and support, as set forth in each applicable SOW.

### 3.2 Fee Schedule and Milestones

3.2.1 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Fees shall be invoiced based on the following Milestones:

  a. **Project Initiation / Mobilization:** Twenty percent (20%) of the applicable SOW value upon execution of the SOW and commencement of Services.

  b. **Completion of Requirements and Design Phase:** Twenty-five percent (25%) upon Company’s written acceptance of the Requirements Specification and design documentation.

  c. **Completion of Development and Integration Phase:** Thirty percent (30%) upon substantial completion of development and integration deliverables as evidenced by successful system and integration testing.

  d. **Deployment / Go-Live:** Fifteen percent (15%) upon successful deployment into production and Company’s written confirmation that the solution is functioning materially in accordance with the applicable SOW.

  e. **Transition and Initial Support Period:** Ten percent (10%) upon completion of initial post-go-live support period (typically thirty (30) days after go-live), unless otherwise set forth in the applicable SOW.

3.2.2 Managed support services, if any, may be billed on a time-and-materials basis or a fixed monthly recurring fee as specified in the applicable SOW.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment

3.3.1 The Service Provider shall issue invoices to the Company upon the achievement of the applicable Milestones, or monthly in arrears for time-and-materials work or recurring services, as specified in the relevant SOW.

3.3.2 The Company shall pay all undisputed invoiced amounts within **thirty (30) calendar days** of the invoice date (**“Net 30”**).

3.3.3 All payments shall be made in **United States Dollars (USD)** by wire transfer or such other method as may be specified by the Service Provider.

3.3.4 Any disputes regarding invoices shall be raised by the Company in writing within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of the invoice. The Company shall pay all undisputed portions of an invoice in accordance with the payment terms. The Parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any disputed portion of an invoice as promptly as practicable.

### 3.4 Taxes

3.4.1 The Fees do not include any sales, use, value-added, or other taxes (other than taxes on the Service Provider’s income). The Company shall pay or reimburse the Service Provider for any such taxes imposed on the provision of the Services or Deliverables, unless the Company provides a valid tax exemption certificate.

### 3.5 Late Payments

3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month**, or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is less, from the due date until paid in full.

3.5.2 In addition to its other rights and remedies, the Service Provider may, upon written notice to the Company and after providing ten (10) Business Days to cure, suspend performance of Services (including access to any hosted components) in the event that any undisputed amount remains past due.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursements

3.6.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services.
