## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK  

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

(a) This Work Order is issued **under and subject to the Parent Agreement** as a “Statement of Work” within the meaning of **Section 1.17** and **Section 2.2 (Statements of Work)** of the Parent Agreement. The Services and Deliverables described in this **Article II** fall within the general categories of Services set forth in **Section 2.1 (General Scope)** of the Parent Agreement, specifically:  
  - Strategic Consulting,  
  - Process and Operational Consulting,  
  - Technology Implementation and Integration,  
  - Change Management and Training, and  
  - Project Management and PMO Support.  

(b) Unless expressly modified in this Work Order, all terms and conditions of the Parent Agreement (including, without limitation, **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)**, **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**, **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, and **Article XIII (Change Management)**) shall govern the performance of the Services and the creation and acceptance of the Deliverables under this Work Order.

2.2 **Project Overview.**  

The Project under this Work Order consists of a multi-phase, enterprise-wide consulting and implementation engagement to support Contoso’s digital transformation, data and analytics modernization, and process optimization across multiple business functions. Building upon and extending the initiatives authorized under **CSA-GAM-202509-160**, the Service Provider shall:

(a) Conduct detailed current-state assessments of Contoso’s existing CRM, ERP, data warehouse, analytics, and integration landscape;

(b) Design a target-state architecture and roadmap for an integrated, cloud-based digital platform, including customer, finance, and supply chain domains;

(c) Implement and integrate selected enterprise systems (including CRM, ERP, analytics, and integration middleware) and associated process changes; and

(d) Provide change management, training, and PMO support necessary to enable sustained adoption and ongoing optimization.

2.3 **Detailed Services.**  

Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.1** above, the Services shall include, at a minimum, the following:

(a) **Strategic Consulting and Roadmapping (Phase 1):**  
   (i) Stakeholder interviews and workshops with business and IT leaders to refine strategic objectives.  
   (ii) Assessment of existing systems, processes, and data flows, including gap analysis against industry best practices.  
   (iii) Development of a comprehensive digital transformation roadmap covering a 3–4 year horizon, including prioritized initiatives, high-level business cases, and dependency mapping.  
   (iv) Delivery of a written **Digital Transformation Roadmap Report** and executive presentation, which shall constitute key Deliverables under this Work Order.

(b) **Process and Operational Consulting (Phase 2):**  
   (i) Detailed process mapping and redesign for core processes in sales operations, customer support, supply chain, and finance, aligned with **Section 2.1(b)** of the Parent Agreement.  
   (ii) Definition of key performance indicators (KPIs) and governance frameworks for each functional area.  
   (iii) Creation of standard operating procedures (SOPs), process flow diagrams, and RACI (Responsible-Accountable-Consulted-Informed) matrices.  
   (iv) Delivery of **Process Design Packages** and **KPI Catalogs** as Deliverables.

(c) **Technology Implementation and Integration (Phase 3):**  
   (i) Solution design for selected CRM, ERP, analytics, and integration middleware platforms, including integration with legacy systems as applicable, consistent with **Section 2.1(c)** of the Parent Agreement.  
   (ii) Configuration and customization of the selected platforms within the boundaries agreed by the Parties in this Work Order and any related Change Orders under **Article XIII**.  
   (iii) Data migration planning and execution, including data mapping, cleansing, validation, and cutover strategies.  
   (iv) Development and testing of integration interfaces and APIs; performance and security testing; and preparation of technical documentation.  
   (v) Delivery of **Solution Design Documents**, **Configuration Runbooks**, **Integration Specifications**, **Migration Reports**, and **Test Summary Reports**.

(d) **Change Management and Training (Phase 4):**  
   (i) Stakeholder impact assessments and change readiness surveys.  
   (ii) Development of change management strategy, communication plans, and training curricula, consistent with **Section 2.1(d)** of the Parent Agreement.  
   (iii) Delivery of train-the-trainer sessions, end-user training, and leadership workshops (in-person and/or virtual, as mutually agreed).  
   (iv) Preparation and delivery of **Training Materials**, **User Guides**, **Quick Reference Cards**, and **Change Communication Artifacts**.

(e) **Project Management and PMO Support (Throughout All Phases):**  
   (i) Establishment and operation of a dedicated project management office (PMO) staffed primarily by the Service Provider, with defined roles and responsibilities.  
   (ii) Maintenance of project plans, schedules, risk registers, issue logs, decision logs, and status reports, as contemplated by **Section 2.5 (Project Governance and Reporting)** of the Parent Agreement.  
   (iii) Weekly or bi-weekly status meetings, monthly steering committee meetings, and ad hoc governance sessions as reasonably requested by the Company.  
   (iv) Delivery of routine **Status Reports**, **Risk and Issue Logs**, and **Executive Dashboards**.

2.4 **Phases, Timelines, and Target Dates.**  

(a) The Services shall be organized into the following Phases with indicative timelines (subject to adjustment via Change Orders in accordance with **Article XIII** of both this Work Order and the Parent Agreement):

- Phase 1 – Strategic Consulting and Roadmapping: April 2026 – August 2026  
- Phase 2 – Process and Operational Consulting: September 2026 – March 2027  
- Phase 3 – Technology Implementation and Integration: January 2027 – December 2028  
- Phase 4 – Change Management, Training, and Stabilization: July 2027 – June 2029  
- Hypercare and Optimization: July 2029 – March 2030  

(b) The Parties acknowledge that some Phases overlap. Timelines are contingent upon the Company’s timely performance of its obligations and provision of Client Materials, as described in **Section 2.6 (Company Obligations)** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 2.8** of this Work Order.

2.5 **Milestones and Key Deliverables.**  

(a) The Parties identify the following **key Milestones** (non-exhaustive) for planning and payment purposes:

- M1: Completion and acceptance of Digital Transformation Roadmap Report  
- M2: Completion and acceptance of Process Design Packages and KPI Catalogs  
- M3: Completion of core CRM and ERP solution design and sign-off  
- M4: Completion of System Integration Testing (SIT) and User Acceptance Testing (UAT) for core releases  
- M5: First-wave go-live and stabilization period end  
- M6: Final program completion and acceptance of all critical Deliverables

(b) Additional detailed Milestones may be documented in a project plan approved in writing by the Parties and may be updated via Change Orders under **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement.

(c) Milestones will be subject to the **Deliverable acceptance procedures** set forth in **Section 2.8 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, including the ten (10) Business Day Review Period, unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing for specific Deliverables.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  

(a) The Service Provider shall perform the Services:  
   (i) in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, consistent with **Section 2.4(a)** of the Parent Agreement;  
   (ii) using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications for their assigned roles; and  
   (iii) in accordance with industry best practices for large-scale technology transformation programs.

(b) The Service Provider shall comply with Applicable Policies provided by the Company in advance in accordance with **Section 2.4(b)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **Project Governance.**  

(a) Each Party shall designate a **Project Manager** who will serve as the primary point of contact and will have the authority to make day-to-day decisions and to escalate issues.

(b) A **Project Governance Committee** shall be formed, composed of senior representatives from both Parties, which will meet at least monthly (or more frequently as agreed) to:

In addition to the obligations set forth in **Section 2.6 (Company Obligations)** of the Parent Agreement, the Company shall:

(c) **Retainer Fees:** The Parties may agree to a monthly retainer for ongoing advisory, PMO, and optimization services during the Hypercare and Optimization period, to be specified in a subsequent Change Order, if any.

(a) Unless otherwise agreed in a Change Order, the WO Contract Value shall be invoiced approximately as follows:

- **Forty-five percent (45%)** of the WO Contract Value invoiced monthly in arrears based on T&M services performed, as documented in approved timesheets and summary reports;  
- **Forty percent (40%)** of the WO Contract Value tied to Milestone completions (M1–M6) and acceptance of key Deliverables, as specified in a Milestone payment schedule agreed in writing by the Parties.

(b) The Company shall not unreasonably withhold or delay acceptance of Deliverables under this Work Order. Acceptance procedures shall be as defined in **Section 2.8 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement.

(a) **Invoicing.** The Service Provider shall submit invoices to the Company:

> “Payment of undisputed amounts shall be due within **forty-five (45) calendar days** from the date of receipt of the invoice by the Company (Net 45).”
