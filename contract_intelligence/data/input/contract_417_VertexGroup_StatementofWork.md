## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Vendor shall provide to the Client comprehensive technology consulting, software development, cloud infrastructure management, and data analytics services designed to support the Client’s digital transformation initiatives and operational platforms (collectively, the “**Services**”). The Services will be performed on a project-based and managed services basis and will include, without limitation:

  a. **Technology Consulting and Solution Architecture.** The Vendor shall perform assessments of the Client’s current systems, processes, and architecture, and shall design solution architectures, technology roadmaps, and implementation plans. The Vendor shall provide recommendations and decision support in connection with technology selection, cloud adoption, and platform modernization.

  b. **Software Development and Integration.** The Vendor shall design, develop, configure, and integrate custom software components, APIs, microservices, and related functionality in accordance with the Specifications. This includes the development of a cloud-native customer portal, internal management tools, and data integration pipelines between the Client’s existing ERP, CRM, and e‑commerce systems.

  c. **Cloud Infrastructure Management.** The Vendor shall provision, configure, monitor, and manage cloud infrastructure resources in a leading public cloud environment (e.g., Microsoft Azure or Amazon Web Services, as further agreed in writing). This includes compute, storage, networking, security configurations, and monitoring tools required to host the Client’s applications and data workloads.

  d. **Data Analytics and Reporting.** The Vendor shall design and implement data ingestion, data warehousing, and analytics solutions, including dashboards and reports, to provide the Client with insights into sales performance, customer behavior, and operational key performance indicators.

  e. **Support and Maintenance.** The Vendor shall provide ongoing support, incident management, and corrective maintenance services for the systems and Deliverables provided under this Agreement, in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Services will be delivered in the following high-level phases, which may overlap as mutually agreed:

  a. **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Estimated 3 months).**  
  - Conduct stakeholder interviews, business process analysis, and system inventory.  
  - Produce a Requirements Specification Document (“RSD”) capturing functional and non-functional requirements.  
  - Develop a high-level solution architecture and preliminary project plan.

  b. **Phase 2 – Design and Planning (Estimated 4 months).**  
  - Develop detailed technical design documents, including data models, interface specifications, security architecture, and infrastructure topology.  
  - Define test strategy, test cases, and acceptance criteria.  
  - Finalize the implementation roadmap, including milestone dates and resource planning.

  c. **Phase 3 – Build and Integration (Estimated 10 months).**  
  - Develop custom software components and integration services in accordance with the Specifications.  
  - Configure cloud infrastructure and security controls.  
  - Integrate with Client’s ERP, CRM, and e‑commerce platforms and execute unit, integration, and system testing.

  d. **Phase 4 – User Acceptance Testing and Go‑Live (Estimated 5 months).**  
  - Support Client in conducting user acceptance testing (“UAT”).  
  - Address defects and perform performance tuning.  
  - Plan and execute cut‑over and go‑live, including data migration and rollback procedures.

  e. **Phase 5 – Stabilization and Managed Services (Remainder of Term).**  
  - Provide post‑go‑live hyper‑care stabilization.  
  - Deliver ongoing managed services including monitoring, incident resolution, minor enhancements, and patching as specified in Article XII.

The Parties acknowledge that the above timelines are estimates and may be refined through the Change Management process in Article XIII.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Vendor shall provide the following key Deliverables (each to be further detailed in mutually agreed schedules or project plans):

  a. Requirements Specification Document (RSD).  
  b. Solution Architecture Document (SAD).  
  c. Detailed Technical Design Documents for all major system components.  
  d. Configured and provisioned cloud infrastructure environment, including deployment scripts and configuration documentation.  
  e. Developed and tested application modules for the customer portal and internal management tools.  
  f. Developed and tested integration components (APIs, ETL jobs, messaging integrations).  
  g. Data warehouse schema and dashboards/reports as identified in the RSD.  
  h. Test plans, test cases, test execution reports, and defect logs.  
  i. Operational runbooks, support procedures, and escalation matrix.  
  j. Training materials and knowledge transfer sessions for Client’s designated personnel.  
  k. Periodic status reports and service performance reports as specified in Article XII.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services:

  a. with due skill, care, and diligence, and in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  

  b. in accordance with the Specifications and any mutually agreed project plans;  

  c. using appropriately qualified, experienced, and trained personnel;  

  d. in compliance with Applicable Law and the Client’s reasonable written policies provided to the Vendor in advance, including information security and data protection policies; and  

  e. with a commitment to cooperate with the Client’s other vendors and internal teams to facilitate integration and successful completion of the project.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client acknowledges that its timely performance is essential to the Vendor’s ability to provide the Services and meet applicable timelines. The Client shall:

  a. designate a suitably knowledgeable project manager and key stakeholders to coordinate with the Vendor;  

  b. provide timely access to Client Materials, systems, facilities (if required), and information necessary for the Vendor’s performance;  

  c. review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days (or such other period agreed in writing), after which the Deliverables may be deemed accepted for project planning purposes (without prejudice to warranty rights);

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Availability Commitment.**  
For the production systems hosted and managed by the Vendor under this Agreement, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the systems are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Permitted Downtime (“**Availability SLA**”).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance on the systems during designated maintenance windows. The Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to notify the Client at least forty‑eight (48) hours in advance of any scheduled maintenance that may impact availability. Scheduled Maintenance, not exceeding eight (8) hours per calendar month, shall be excluded from the calculation of availability.

12.3 **Incident Response Times.**  
The Vendor shall respond to and work to resolve incidents according to the following severity levels:

  a. **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or critical failure affecting all users with no workaround.  
      - Initial Response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
      - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 4 hours  

  b. **Severity 2 (High):** Major functionality impacted causing significant degradation for many users.  
      - Initial Response: within 4 hours (Business Days)  
      - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 1 Business Day  

  c. **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non‑critical issues affecting limited functionality or subset of users.  
      - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
      - Target Resolution: within 5 Business Days  

  d. **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
      - Initial Response: within 3 Business Days  
      - Target Resolution: to be addressed in next planned release or maintenance cycle.

12.4 **SLA Credits.**  
If the Vendor fails to meet the Availability SLA in any calendar month, the Client shall be eligible to receive a service credit equal to a percentage of the monthly managed services Fees for the affected services, as follows:

  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% credit  
  - Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% credit  
  - Availability below 98.0%: 20% credit  

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service credits shall be the Client’s exclusive remedy for failures to meet the Availability SLA, without prejudice to other rights for material breach as provided in Article IV.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
SLA commitments shall not apply to failures caused by: (a) factors outside the Vendor’s reasonable control, including Force Majeure Events; (b) non‑compliance by the Client with the Agreement or reasonable use guidelines; (c) Client’s or third-party hardware, software, or network; or (d) Scheduled Maintenance or Permitted Downtime as defined herein.
