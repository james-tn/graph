WHEREAS, this Work Order is entered into **pursuant to**, and is intended to **supplement and form a part of**, the Parent SOW (Ref: **SOW-ZEN-202401-226**, **contract_226**) and to be governed by the terms and conditions of the Parent SOW except as expressly modified herein.

## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order is executed **under and subject to** the Parent SOW and **adds scope** to the Services and Deliverables described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent SOW. Except as expressly supplemented or modified in this Work Order, the Services and Deliverables, performance standards, and acceptance procedures set forth in the Parent SOW shall continue to apply without limitation.

2.1.2 For clarity, **Section 2.1 (Overview of Project)** of the Parent SOW describes the Solution as supporting Client’s finance, supply chain, sales, customer support, and reporting functions “across its U.S. operations.” Under this Work Order, Vendor shall design, configure, deploy, and support the Solution to additional Regions and shall implement Advanced Analytics & AI Components and Enhanced Managed Support as set forth herein.

2.1.3 The Deliverable Review and Acceptance process in **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW (including the thirty (30) day Review Period and correction of non‑conformities at no additional charge) shall apply equally to all WO Deliverables.

2.2 **Extended Rollout – Additional Regions.**

2.2.1 **Regional Assessment & Planning.** Vendor shall perform an assessment for each new Region selected by Client, including:

- review of local statutory and regulatory requirements impacting finance, tax, data protection, and reporting;
- analysis of local business processes and deviations from the U.S. baseline process designs developed under **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements** of the Parent SOW;
- identification of localization requirements (e.g., language, currency, tax rules, reporting formats); and
- preparation of a **Regional Rollout Assessment Report** for each Region, summarizing scope, gaps, risks, dependencies, and recommended rollout sequence.

2.2.2 **Regional Design & Configuration.** For each approved Region, Vendor shall:

- extend the **Requirements Specification Document (RSD)** and **High‑Level Solution Architecture** delivered under **Phase 1** of the Parent SOW to incorporate Region‑specific requirements;
- prepare **Regional Functional Design Specifications (R‑FDS)** and **Regional Technical Design Specifications (R‑TDS)** consistent with the FDS and TDS described in **Section 2.2.2** of the Parent SOW;
- configure the ERP/CRM Solution in non‑production environments for each Region, including localized charts of accounts, tax codes, languages, currencies, and user roles;
- configure additional interfaces to local banking, tax, or regulatory systems as required by Client; and
- deliver a **Regional Prototype Environment** per Region for Client review and acceptance.

2.2.3 **Regional Data Migration & Cutover.** Vendor shall:

- update the **Data Migration Runbook** referenced in **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW to include Region‑specific data sources, mapping rules, and ETL processes;
- coordinate Region‑specific test migrations, data validations, and reconciliations;
- support Region‑specific SIT and UAT activities aligned with **Phase 4 – Testing, Training & Go‑Live** in the Parent SOW; and
- develop **Regional Go‑Live Cutover Plans** and rollback procedures aligned with Client’s global deployment strategy.

2.2.4 **Regional Training & Change Management.** Vendor shall:

- adapt the **Training Materials** described in **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent SOW to each Region, including localized user manuals, quick reference guides, and, where applicable, translated content;
- conduct up to **six (6)** additional onsite or virtual training sessions per Region, in addition to those already contemplated in the Parent SOW;
- provide attendance reports and training feedback summaries for each Region.

2.3 **Advanced Analytics & AI Optimization.**

2.3.1 **Analytics Requirements & Design.** Vendor shall extend the Reporting scope referenced in **Section 2.2.2** of the Parent SOW by:

- conducting stakeholder workshops focused on advanced analytics use cases (e.g., predictive demand forecasting, cash‑flow prediction, churn analytics, supply chain risk scoring);
- documenting analytics and AI requirements, KPIs, and data model enhancements;
- preparing **Analytics & AI Functional Design Specifications (AA‑FDS)** and **Analytics & AI Technical Design Specifications (AA‑TDS)**.

2.3.2 **Development & Integration.** Vendor shall:

- design and build advanced dashboards and visualizations integrated into the Solution’s reporting layer;
- develop and configure machine‑learning models using data sourced from the ERP/CRM modules configured under the Parent SOW (e.g., sales pipeline, order history, inventory levels, customer support tickets);
- integrate the Advanced Analytics & AI Components with existing **Integration Components and Documentation** delivered under **Phase 3** of the Parent SOW;
- implement appropriate logging, monitoring, and exception handling consistent with **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW.

2.3.3 **Testing & Validation.** Vendor shall:

- prepare **Analytics Test Plans and Test Cases**, consistent with the Testing Plan framework described in **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW;
- conduct model validation sessions with Client’s designated subject matter experts;
- tune models and dashboards based on feedback and performance metrics.

2.3.4 **Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall:

- provide training to Client’s analytics team on model operation, tuning, and interpretation;
- deliver documentation describing configuration, data flows, and maintenance procedures for the Advanced Analytics & AI Components.

2.4 **Enhanced Managed Support & Optimization.**

This Work Order supplements **Phase 5 – Managed Support & Optimization** in **Section 2.2.5** and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent SOW as follows:

2.4.1 **Extended Coverage.** Vendor shall:

- extend Level 2 and Level 3 application support to include all Regions brought into scope under this Work Order;
- provide **extended support hours** to cover at least two (2) additional time zones (to be mutually agreed), including priority handling of high‑severity incidents impacting multi‑Region operations.

2.4.2 **Proactive Monitoring & Optimization.** Vendor shall:

- implement enhanced monitoring for key analytics jobs, integrations, and Region‑specific processes;
- conduct **bi‑monthly** optimization workshops (in place of the “quarterly optimization workshops” referenced in **Section 2.2.5** of the Parent SOW) for the first twelve (12) months after each Region’s go‑live, then quarterly thereafter, unless otherwise agreed in writing;
- deliver **Enhanced System Health Reports** that include: cross‑Region performance metrics, analytics model performance summaries, and recommendations for process or configuration improvements.

2.4.3 **Release & Change Management.** Vendor shall:

- coordinate regionalized testing for platform releases and patches, supplementing the “periodic releases and patch testing support” described in **Section 2.2.5** of the Parent SOW;
- maintain a consolidated **Change Calendar** reflecting changes under both the Parent SOW and this Work Order.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Additional Services in accordance with the performance standards set out in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW, including performing in a professional and workmanlike manner, complying with Specifications, timelines, and Service Levels, and ensuring material conformity of all WO Deliverables.

2.5.2 WO Deliverables shall be subject to the Deliverable review and acceptance procedures in **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW. Where this Work Order specifies a different Review Period for particular WO Deliverables, such specified period shall prevail.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  

Client’s responsibilities under **Section 2.7 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent SOW shall apply equally to this Work Order. Without limitation, Client shall:

- identify Regions and prioritize rollout sequence;  
- provide timely access to regional business and IT stakeholders;  
- ensure accuracy and completeness of Region‑specific data, processes, and regulatory requirements provided to Vendor; and  
- designate a global project manager and regional leads to support workshops, approvals, and testing.

2.7 **Key Personnel.**  

Key Personnel provisions under **Section 2.8** of the Parent SOW shall apply. Vendor shall, where practicable, maintain continuity of Key Personnel assigned to the Parent SOW for the Additional Services under this Work Order and shall notify Client of any proposed changes in accordance with **Section 2.8**.

## ARTICLE III. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  

The total contract value for the Additional Services and WO Deliverables under this Work Order shall not exceed **Three Million Three Hundred Thirty‑Four Thousand Three Hundred Four U.S. Dollars (US$3,334,304)**, exclusive of any applicable taxes (“**WO Contract Value**”), unless increased by a mutually executed Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII** of this Work Order and **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent SOW.

3.2 **Fee Structure and WO Milestones.**

3.2.1 **Fixed‑Price Components.** The following components under this Work Order shall be fixed‑price:

- **Extended Rollout – Design & Configuration (All Regions in Initial Scope):** US$1,200,000  
- **Advanced Analytics & AI Components – Design and Build:** US$950,000  
- **Regional Data Migration & Go‑Live Support (Initial Two Regions):** US$550,000  

3.2.2 **Time‑and‑Materials Components (T&M).**  

Enhanced Managed Support and any additional Regions beyond the initial two (2) Regions shall be billed on a time‑and‑materials basis under this Work Order, in alignment with the rate card established in **Section 3.2.2** of the Parent SOW:

- Senior Architect: US$230/hour  
- Senior Consultant: US$190/hour  
- Consultant: US$150/hour  
- Support Engineer: US$120/hour  

The total T&M fees under this Work Order shall be subject to a not‑to‑exceed (“**NTE**”) amount of **US$634,304** over the WO Term, unless increased by a Change Order. Vendor shall obtain Client’s written approval (email sufficient) prior to exceeding such NTE, consistent with the approach in **Section 3.2.2** of the Parent SOW.

3.2.3 **WO Milestone Payments – Fixed Price.**  

Fixed‑price fees under this Work Order shall be invoiced upon achievement of the following WO Milestones and acceptance of the applicable WO Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW:

- **WO Milestone A:** Completion and Client acceptance of Regional Rollout Assessment Report(s) and extended RSD/High‑Level Architecture for all initial Regions – **20%** of Extended Rollout – Design & Configuration component (US$240,000).  
- **WO Milestone B:** Client acceptance of R‑FDS and R‑TDS for initial Regions and delivery of Regional Prototype Environments – **40%** of Extended Rollout – Design & Configuration (US$480,000).  
- **WO Milestone C:** Client acceptance of Analytics & AI FDS and TDS – **30%** of Advanced Analytics & AI Components component (US$285,000).  
- **WO Milestone D:** Completion and Client acceptance of Advanced Analytics & AI Components in non‑production and successful completion of analytics SIT – **50%** of Advanced Analytics & AI Components component (US$475,000).  
- **WO Milestone E:** Completion of Regional Data Migration Runbook updates and successful initial test migration for first Region – **40%** of Regional Data Migration & Go‑Live Support component (US$220,000).  
- **WO Milestone F:** First Region Go‑Live completion and 30‑day post‑go‑live support – **30%** of Regional Data Migration & Go‑Live Support component (US$165,000).  
- **WO Milestone G:** Second Region Go‑Live completion and 30‑day post‑go‑live support – remaining **30%** of Regional Data Migration & Go‑Live Support component (US$165,000).  
- **WO Milestone H:** Client acceptance of completed Advanced Analytics dashboards and confirmed use in production for at least thirty (30) days – remaining **20%** of Advanced Analytics & AI Components component (US$190,000).  
- **WO Milestone I:** Completion of initial six (6) months of Enhanced Managed Support activities (if funded partially as fixed price under mutual agreement) – as may be agreed in a Change Order referencing this Work Order.

3.2.4 **T&M Invoicing.**  

Consistent with **Section 3.2.2** and **Section 3.3** of the Parent SOW, T&M Services under this Work Order (Enhanced Managed Support and additional Regions beyond initial scope) shall be invoiced monthly in arrears, based on actual hours worked by role, supported by reasonable time records.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  

3.3.1 All invoices under this Work Order shall be subject to the payment procedures set forth in **Section 3.3 (Payment Terms)** of the Parent SOW. Payment terms shall be **Net 30** days from Client’s receipt of a valid invoice, as required by the Contract Parameters and consistent with **Section 3.3.2** of the Parent SOW.

3.3.2 The dispute procedures in **Section 3.6 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to any disputed invoices under this Work Order. Client shall pay undisputed portions of invoices while the Parties work in good faith to resolve any disputes.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

Undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest as provided in **Section 3.4 (Late Payment)** of the Parent SOW (the lesser of one percent (1%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law). Vendor’s right to suspend Services for non‑payment shall be exercised only in accordance with **Section 3.4** of the Parent SOW.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.5.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred in connection with the Additional Services under this Work Order, in accordance with **Section 3.5 (Expenses and Reimbursement)** of the Parent SOW and Client’s then‑current travel and expense policy.

3.5.2 No other expenses shall be reimbursable unless specifically authorized in advance in writing by Client’s project manager, consistent with **Section 3.5.2** of the Parent SOW.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

The tax provisions of **Section 3.7 (Taxes)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to the WO Fees. Vendor shall itemize applicable taxes on its invoices and cooperate with Client regarding any exemptions or credits.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **WO Term.**  

The term of this Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 3, 2028** (the “**WO Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or the Parent SOW (the “**WO Term**”). For clarity, the WO Term is fully contained within the Parent Term, which under **Section 4.1** of the Parent SOW expires on **October 16, 2028**.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party, which is consistent with the termination for convenience notice in **Section 4.2** of the Parent SOW and appropriate for a Medium risk profile.

4.2.2 In the event of such termination:

- Client shall pay Vendor for all Additional Services performed and WO Deliverables delivered and accepted through the effective date of termination, plus any non‑cancellable, pre‑approved expenses; and  
- Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to effect an orderly wind‑down of the Additional Services and, at Client’s request and cost, provide transition assistance for up to thirty (30) days following termination, consistent with **Section 4.2(b)** of the Parent SOW.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days following written notice thereof, consistent with **Section 4.3.1** of the Parent SOW.

4.3.2 Material breach by Vendor shall include, without limitation: (i) repeated failure to meet WO‑specific Service Levels (as described in **Article XII** below) that materially impact Client’s multi‑Region operations; (ii) material failure to achieve WO Milestones without justification or Client‑caused delay; or (iii) failure to maintain required insurance as set forth in **Article X** of the Parent SOW, as applied to this Work Order.

4.3.3 Material breach by Client shall include, without limitation: (i) repeated failure to pay undisputed WO Fees when due, subject to **Section 3.6** of the Parent SOW and the cure periods herein; or (ii) material violation of Vendor’s Intellectual Property Rights with respect to WO Deliverables.

4.4 **Effect of Termination of Parent SOW.**  

4.4.1 This Work Order is subordinate to and dependent upon the Parent SOW. Accordingly, if the Parent SOW is terminated or expires for any reason, this Work Order shall automatically terminate as of the effective date of such termination or expiration, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue certain obligations under this Work Order.

4.4.2 Upon termination of the Parent SOW or this Work Order, the Parties shall follow the obligations in **Section 4.4 (Effect of Termination)** of the Parent SOW, including cessation of Services, return or destruction of Confidential Information, and continuation of licenses for Deliverables already provided and paid for, except for any time‑limited licenses.

4.5 **Survival.**  

All provisions of this Work Order that by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including, without limitation, Articles **V (Confidentiality)**, **VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**, **VII (Representations and Warranties)**, **VIII (Indemnification)**, **IX (Limitation of Liability)**, **XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, **XIV (Dispute Resolution)**, and **XVI (General Provisions)**, together with any corresponding survival clauses in **Section 4.5** of the Parent SOW.
