## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

This Addendum **supplements and amends** the Parent Agreement as follows:

  a. It expands the scope of the license granted under **Section 2.2 (Software License Scope)** of the Parent Agreement by increasing the number of licensed Users and by adding Additional Modules to the definition of **“Software”** in **Section 1.14** of the Parent Agreement solely as between the Parties for the Addendum Term.  

  b. It further specifies additional Services and Deliverables consistent with **Section 2.4 (Implementation and Configuration Services)** and **Section 2.5 (Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, tailored to the Additional Users and Additional Modules.  

  c. It establishes supplemental timelines, milestones, and performance standards that complement **Section 2.6 (Timeline and Milestones)** and **Section 2.7 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement.

Except as expressly stated herein, nothing in this Article II shall limit or diminish Vendor’s obligations under Article II of the Parent Agreement with respect to existing Users and modules.

2.2 **Expansion of Licensed Users.**  

2.2.1 **Baseline Users.** The Parties acknowledge that the Parent Agreement contemplated license rights for up to a certain number of Users (the exact baseline number to be confirmed between the Parties in writing at or shortly after execution of the Parent Agreement), with subscription license fees of **USD $38,000** for the Term, as set forth in **Section 3.2(a)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.2.2 **Additional Users under this Addendum.** Under this Addendum, Vendor hereby grants to Client, subject to Client’s timely payment of Addendum Fees and compliance with the Parent Agreement and this Addendum, a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable (except as provided in **Section 16.6** of the Parent Agreement), non-sublicensable license during the Addendum Term to authorize up to **1,250 Additional Users** to access and use the Software (including Additional Modules) for Client’s internal business operations only, in accordance with **Section 2.2(a)** and **Section 6.3 (Use Restrictions)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.2.3 **User Management.** Client shall be responsible for designating, managing, and terminating access credentials for Additional Users, consistent with Vendor’s administrative tools and **Section 2.8(e)** of the Parent Agreement. Vendor shall provide reasonable administrative interfaces to enable Client to manage Users, including bulk provisioning and de-provisioning of Additional Users.

2.3 **Addition of New Modules.**  

2.3.1 **Additional Modules Description.** Vendor shall make available to Client the following Additional Modules as part of the Software:

  a. **StellarOps Predictive Analytics Module** – provides advanced forecasting, trend analysis, and machine learning–based recommendations for Client’s operations data;  

  b. **StellarOps Capacity Planning Module** – enables detailed capacity modeling, scenario planning, and resource allocation optimization;  

  c. **StellarOps Compliance & Audit Module** – provides configurable compliance workflows, audit trails, automated evidence collection, and reporting for internal and regulatory audits.

2.3.2 **License to Additional Modules.** Subject to the same license conditions applicable to the Software under **Section 2.2** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor hereby grants Client the right to access and use the Additional Modules during the Addendum Term, solely for Client’s internal business operations and in accordance with the Documentation referenced in **Section 1.7 (Documentation)** and use restrictions in **Section 6.3** of the Parent Agreement.

2.3.3 **Integration with Existing Modules.** Vendor shall configure the Additional Modules to operate in conjunction with existing modules licensed under the Parent Agreement (e.g., operations analytics, workflow automation, reporting dashboards) and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Additional Modules function cohesively within the overall StellarOps Enterprise Suite environment.

2.4 **Enhanced Implementation and Configuration Services.**  

In addition to the Services described in **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide the following **Enhanced Services** with respect to the Additional Users and Additional Modules:

  a. **User Onboarding and Role Design.** Vendor shall conduct role-mapping workshops with Client’s designated project team to define appropriate roles, permissions, and segregation of duties for the Additional Users across all modules, including the Additional Modules.  

  b. **Module Configuration.** Vendor shall configure each Additional Module in accordance with Client’s agreed requirements, including custom dashboards, predictive models parameterization, compliance workflows, and alerts, to the extent supported by the standard configuration options of the Software.  

  c. **Data Model Extension.** Vendor shall assist Client in extending its existing data models to support the functionality of the Additional Modules, including mapping new data fields and entities as required for predictive analytics, capacity planning, and compliance tracking.  

  d. **Integration Extensions.** For up to **three (3)** additional systems (in addition to the two (2) systems referenced in **Section 2.4(d)** of the Parent Agreement), Vendor shall provide reasonable assistance in integrating the Additional Modules via APIs or other standard integration mechanisms supported by the Software. Any extensive custom integration work beyond these three systems shall be addressed via a separate SOW pursuant to **Section 1.15 (Statement of Work)** and **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement.  

  e. **Extended UAT Support.** Vendor shall assist Client in User Acceptance Testing specifically relating to the Additional Modules and Additional Users by providing test scripts, test data advisory, and issue triage, consistent with the level of support described in **Section 2.4(e)** of the Parent Agreement, but extended in scope to cover the newly added features.  

  f. **Additional Training.** Vendor shall provide up to **80 hours** of remote training sessions focused on the Additional Modules and scaled deployment to Additional Users. Training materials shall be considered **Deliverables** under **Section 1.5 (Deliverables)** and shall remain subject to Vendor’s Intellectual Property Rights as described in **Article VI** of the Parent Agreement and **Article VI** of this Addendum.

2.5 **Deliverables under this Addendum.**  

Without limiting **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall deliver to Client at minimum the following additional Deliverables under this Addendum:

  a. Updated and/or separate access credentials and provisioning configuration enabling up to 1,250 Additional Users to access the Software and Additional Modules;  

  b. An updated **implementation configuration document** that supplements the configuration document referenced in **Section 2.5(b)** of the Parent Agreement, describing all new system settings, roles, workflows, and module-specific configurations implemented under this Addendum;  

  c. Module-specific training materials, including user guides, reference cards, and recorded training sessions for the Additional Modules;  

  d. Integration configuration documentation for each additional system integrated under **Section 2.4(d)** above; and  

  e. Periodic reports, upon Client’s request, detailing usage, performance, and adoption metrics specifically for the Additional Users and Additional Modules, in addition to the system performance reports referenced in **Section 2.5(d)** and **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement.

2.6 **Timeline and Milestones for Addendum Scope.**  

2.6.1 **High-Level Timeline.** The Parties anticipate the following timeline for the implementation and rollout of the Additional Users and Additional Modules under this Addendum (to be further detailed in the Implementation Plan, **Schedule 2**):

  a. **Addendum Project Kickoff:** Within ten (10) Business Days from the Addendum Effective Date;  

  b. **Module Configuration & Setup:** Approximately eight (8) to ten (10) weeks from Addendum Project Kickoff, subject to timely provision of Client data and resources;  

  c. **Extended Integration & Data Model Updates:** Conducted in parallel with configuration, anticipated to complete within the same period;  

  d. **User Acceptance Testing:** Approximately three (3) weeks following completion of configuration and integration;  

  e. **Phased Go-Live for Additional Users:** Target initial rollout within one hundred twenty (120) days of the Addendum Effective Date, followed by staggered deployments, if agreed in writing;  

  f. **Full Operationalization:** Expected within one hundred eighty (180) days of the Addendum Effective Date.

2.6.2 **Adjustments.** The foregoing timeline is an estimate and may be adjusted by mutual written agreement of the Parties, including via the **Change Management** procedures in **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XIII** of this Addendum. Delays caused by Client’s failure to meet its responsibilities under **Section 2.8** of the Parent Agreement or corresponding provisions herein shall extend the timeline accordingly.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**  

Vendor shall perform all Enhanced Services under this Addendum in a **professional and workmanlike manner** and consistent with industry standards, as required under **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement. In addition:

  a. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the milestones described in **Section 2.6** of this Addendum and in the Implementation Plan;  

  b. Vendor shall provide the Additional Modules with the same **Uptime Commitment** (99.9% availability) and **Support Response Times** set forth in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement;  

  c. Vendor shall promptly notify Client of any material issues affecting the Additional Modules’ performance and shall work diligently to remediate such issues; and  

  d. Vendor shall maintain the Additional Modules in a manner consistent with **Section 2.7(d)** of the Parent Agreement, including applying Updates and security patches as reasonably necessary.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities for Addendum Scope.**  

In addition to the responsibilities in **Section 2.8** of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

  a. Designate a project manager specifically for the Addendum rollout to coordinate with Vendor and ensure effective communication between existing and new User groups;  

  b. Provide complete and accurate information regarding its predictive analytics, capacity planning, and compliance requirements to enable effective configuration of the Additional Modules;  

  c. Ensure that necessary Client-side systems, interfaces, and identity/access management (IAM) services are available and properly configured to support onboarding of Additional Users;  

  d. Facilitate timely access to relevant subject matter experts (operations, compliance, IT) for workshops and UAT; and  

  e. Enforce compliance by Additional Users with the license and use restrictions set forth in **Section 6.3 (Use Restrictions)** of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum.

2.9 **Change in Scope under this Addendum.**  

Any material changes to the scope of Additional Users, Additional Modules, Enhanced Services, or project timelines under this Addendum shall be handled pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XIII** of this Addendum, and may result in adjustments to the Addendum Fees, milestones, and timelines.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Addendum Term.**  

This Addendum shall commence on the **Addendum Effective Date** of March 30, 2024 and shall remain in full force and effect until **February 09, 2028**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Addendum Term”**).

4.2 **Relationship with Parent Agreement Term.**  

4.2.1 The Parties acknowledge that the Parent Agreement’s **Initial Term** under **Section 4.1** currently expires on **October 07, 2025**. The Parties agree that, solely for purposes of enabling the continued use of the Additional Users and Additional Modules under this Addendum, they shall enter into one or more renewals or extensions of the Parent Agreement or a successor agreement as may be required to align the operative framework of the Parent Agreement with the Addendum Term.

4.2.2 If the Parent Agreement is renewed or replaced (e.g., via a new master agreement) such that the substantive terms governing the Software and Services remain in effect through at least February 09, 2028, this Addendum shall be deemed to apply to such renewed or successor master agreement and shall continue in full force and effect for its Addendum Term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (High-Risk, Vendor-Favorable).**  

4.3.1 **By Client.** Client may terminate this Addendum for convenience, in whole or in part, upon no less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor. In the event of such termination for convenience, Client shall pay Vendor:

  a. All accrued but unpaid Addendum Fees up to the effective date of termination; and  
  b. All non-cancellable commitments made by Vendor specifically for Client under this Addendum with Client’s prior written approval (e.g., multi-year hosting or third-party data subscriptions procured solely for Client’s use of the Additional Modules).

4.3.2 **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Addendum for convenience upon no less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Client. In such case, Vendor shall refund to Client the pro-rata portion of any prepaid but unused Additional Subscription License Fees relating to the remaining portion of the Addendum Term after the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

Either Party may terminate this Addendum upon written notice to the other Party if:

  a. The other Party materially breaches this Addendum or the Parent Agreement with respect to the Additional Users or Additional Modules and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice specifying the nature of the breach; or  

  b. An insolvency or similar event described in **Section 4.4(b)** of the Parent Agreement occurs with respect to the other Party.

4.5 **Termination for Non-Payment.**  

Without limiting **Section 4.5** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor may terminate this Addendum if Client fails to pay any undisputed Addendum Fee within **forty-five (45) days** after its due date and does not cure such non-payment within **ten (10) days** after receipt of written notice from Vendor thereof.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  

4.6.1 **Automatic Termination.** Unless the Parties explicitly agree otherwise in writing, termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement for any reason shall automatically result in the simultaneous termination of this Addendum, without the need for additional notice.

4.6.2 **Survival of Payment and Data Obligations.** Upon termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum:

  a. All rights and licenses granted to Client under this Addendum shall immediately cease, and Client shall discontinue all access to and use of the Additional Modules and any functionality made available solely under this Addendum, consistent with **Section 4.6(a)** of the Parent Agreement;  

  b. Client shall promptly pay Vendor all accrued but unpaid Addendum Fees through the effective date of termination; and  

  c. Vendor’s obligations relating to Client Data (including data return and deletion) shall be governed by **Section 11.7 (Data Retention and Deletion)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XI** of this Addendum.

4.7 **No Liability for Termination.**  

Except as expressly provided in this Addendum or the Parent Agreement, neither Party shall be liable to the other for any compensation, reimbursement, or damages arising from the termination of this Addendum in accordance with its terms, including any loss of anticipated savings or business, provided that this Section shall not limit any rights or remedies for breach occurring prior to termination.
