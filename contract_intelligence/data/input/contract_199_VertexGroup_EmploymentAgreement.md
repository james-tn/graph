## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall provide to the Company professional staffing and Outsourced Employment Services, including but not limited to:

  2.1.1 Recruitment, selection, screening, and vetting of qualified personnel for positions specified by the Company, including but not limited to IT professionals, business analysts, project managers, administrative staff, and customer support resources;

  2.1.2 Engagement, onboarding, and assignment of Vendor Personnel to perform services for and under the direction of the Company (to the extent permitted by law) at Company’s locations or remotely, as specified in the applicable SOW;

  2.1.3 Ongoing personnel administration, including payroll processing for Vendor Personnel, benefits administration (where applicable), timekeeping, and compliance with Applicable Law related to such Vendor Personnel;

  2.1.4 HR support activities including performance tracking, disciplinary process assistance (in coordination with the Company as appropriate), offboarding, replacement, and succession planning for Vendor Personnel;

  2.1.5 Project-based consulting services, including but not limited to process optimization, systems implementation support, and business transformation projects as may be specified in SOWs;

  2.1.6 Preparation and delivery of Deliverables such as staffing plans, status reports, performance metrics, risk assessments, and knowledge transfer documentation.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Service Provider shall produce and deliver to the Company, as applicable:

  2.2.1 **Staffing Deliverables**, including candidate shortlists, interview schedules, background check summaries (subject to Applicable Law), and finalized assignment letters;

  2.2.2 **Operational Deliverables**, such as weekly or monthly progress reports, utilization reports for Vendor Personnel, time and attendance logs, and SLA reports;

  2.2.3 **Project Deliverables**, which may include project plans, design documents, test plans, implementation guides, training materials, and post-implementation review reports;

  2.2.4 **Compliance Documentation**, including certificates of insurance, regulatory compliance statements, data processing records, and evidence of training for Vendor Personnel on Company policies as required under this Agreement.

Each Deliverable shall conform to the specifications, acceptance criteria, and timelines described in the relevant SOW or, in the absence of such SOW-specific standards, shall be prepared in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar work.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  2.3.1 With due care, skill, diligence, and professionalism consistent with prevailing industry standards for similar services;

  2.3.2 In compliance with all Applicable Laws, including but not limited to labor and employment laws, immigration laws, anti-discrimination and anti-harassment laws, and Data Protection Laws;

  2.3.3 In accordance with the Service Levels detailed in **Article XII**, including any more specific metrics set forth in an applicable SOW;

  2.3.4 Using Vendor Personnel who are appropriately qualified, trained, experienced, and, where required, properly licensed or certified to perform the Services assigned to them;

  2.3.5 In a manner that, to the extent reasonably feasible, minimizes disruption to the Company’s ongoing operations.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services in accordance with the timelines and milestones set forth in the applicable SOWs. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing:

  2.4.1 For staffing requests, the Service Provider shall provide qualified candidate profiles within the response times set forth in the SLA (Article XII), which shall typically require initial candidate submissions within ten (10) Business Days of a written requisition, subject to complexity and market conditions;

  2.4.2 For project-based engagements, the Service Provider shall adhere to the project plan and milestone schedule agreed in the SOW, including but not limited to key phase completion dates, deliverable submission dates, and acceptance testing periods;

  2.4.3 Any delay in meeting a material milestone shall be promptly reported to the Company in writing, together with: (a) the reason for the delay, (b) the anticipated duration of the delay, and (c) a proposed mitigation plan. The Company may, at its sole discretion, accept such revised schedule or require corrective actions or alternative resources.

2.5 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  2.5.1 Unless otherwise specified in a SOW, the Company shall have ten (10) Business Days from receipt of a Deliverable to review and either: (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide written notice identifying in reasonable detail any material non-conformities with the applicable specifications.

  2.5.2 If the Company timely notifies the Service Provider of any material non-conformities, the Service Provider shall, at no additional cost to the Company, promptly correct such non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for review. The Company shall then have an additional review period as specified in Section 2.5.1.

  2.5.3 If the Company neither accepts nor rejects a Deliverable within the applicable review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted solely for billing purposes; such deemed acceptance shall not limit the Company’s rights to invoke warranties under **Article VII**.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall:

  2.6.1 Provide, on a timely basis, such access to its personnel, facilities, systems, and information as reasonably necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services;

  2.6.2 Designate a qualified representative to serve as the primary point of contact for day-to-day coordination with the Service Provider;

  2.6.3 Review and respond to Service Provider requests for approvals, instructions, and information in a timely manner;

  2.6.4 Comply with all Applicable Laws related to its own employees and operations, and with its obligations under this Agreement, including any data controller obligations under Data Protection Laws.

2.7 **No Employment Relationship with Company.**  
Except as may be expressly agreed in writing in a particular SOW (in the context of a co-employment or EOR arrangement compliant with Applicable Law), Vendor Personnel shall at all times remain employees or contractors of the Service Provider and not employees of the Company. The Service Provider shall be solely responsible for:

  2.7.1 Payment of all wages, salaries, bonuses, and other compensation owed to Vendor Personnel;

  2.7.2 Compliance with all payroll tax withholding and reporting obligations, and provision of any legally required benefits to Vendor Personnel;

  2.7.3 Handling of all employment-related claims and disputes involving Vendor Personnel, subject to the indemnification provisions in **Article VIII**.

Nothing herein shall be construed to create any employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Company and any Vendor Personnel.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge and agree that the estimated total contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **US $3,403,250**, based on the anticipated volume of Services and resource utilization described in the SOWs. 3.3 **Invoicing.**  

  3.3.1 The Service Provider shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears, unless otherwise specified in an applicable SOW.

  3.3.2 Each invoice shall include: (a) the Agreement reference number **AGR-VER-202404-199**, (b) the applicable SOW number, (c) a summary of Services and Deliverables provided, (d) the billing period, (e) itemized time entries or milestone completions, and (f) any pre-approved expenses.

  3.3.3 Invoices shall be submitted electronically to the Company’s designated accounts payable contact, or such other contact as the Company may specify in writing from time to time.

  3.4.1 The Company shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of a valid and correct invoice (“**Net 30**”).

  3.4.2 The Company may withhold payment of any disputed amount, provided that it: (a) notifies the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice of the nature and basis of the dispute, and (b) pays any undisputed amounts when due. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve any invoice disputes promptly.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  

  3.5.1 Any undisputed invoiced amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the date due until the date paid.

  3.5.2 The Service Provider shall not suspend the Services for late payment unless: (a) amounts are more than sixty (60) days past due, (b) the Service Provider gives the Company at least fifteen (15) days’ prior written notice of its intent to suspend, and (c) the Company fails to cure within such notice period. Any suspension under this Section shall not affect the Company’s rights or remedies for non-performance by the Service Provider.

3.6 **Expenses.**  

  3.6.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with the Company’s then-current expense policies, provided such policies have been shared with the Service Provider in advance.

  3.6.2 The Service Provider shall provide appropriate documentation (such as receipts and travel itineraries) with each expense reimbursement request.

3.7 **Taxes.**  

  3.7.1 Fees under this Agreement are exclusive of Taxes. The Company shall pay all applicable Taxes on the Services, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, which shall be the sole responsibility of the Service Provider.

  3.7.2 If the Company is required by Applicable Law to withhold Taxes from amounts payable to the Service Provider, the Company shall provide reasonable documentation of such withholding and shall cooperate with the Service Provider to minimize such withholding to the extent legally permissible.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in full force and effect until **March 02, 2028** (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement may be renewed for additional one (1) year periods (“**Renewal Terms**”) by mutual written agreement of the Parties, executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current Term. The Initial Term and any Renewal Terms are collectively the “**Term**.”

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any SOW), for convenience by providing the Service Provider at least **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice**.

  4.3.2 The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the Company, and only after completion of any critical ongoing milestones identified by the Company in good faith.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
   (a) materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach; or  
   (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed, or ceases to conduct its business in the ordinary course.

  4.4.2 A material breach by the Service Provider shall include, without limitation: (a) repeated or material failures to meet the SLAs, (b) material violations of the Company’s written policies, (c) intentional misuse or disclosure of the Company’s Confidential Information, or (d) violations of Data Protection Laws in connection with the Services.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
   (a) The Service Provider shall promptly cease all Services (except as necessary for transition assistance under Section 4.6);  
   (b) Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, securely destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party, in accordance with **Article V**; and  
   (c) All SOWs then in effect shall either terminate concurrently or continue in accordance with their terms if expressly so agreed in writing by the Parties.

  4.5.2 The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, including any approved expenses incurred prior to termination, subject to the Company’s right to withhold or set off amounts for any unresolved disputes.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
At the Company’s written request received prior to or within thirty (30) days after termination or expiration, the Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance (at the then-current time-and-materials rates or as otherwise agreed in writing) to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to the Company or a successor service provider, for a period not exceeding ninety (90) days, unless otherwise agreed.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under **Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), whether oral, written, visual, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:  
(a) business plans, strategies, and financial information;  
(b) customer lists, pricing, and marketing information;  
(c) technical data, trade secrets, know-how, methodologies, and software;  
(d) Personal Data; and  
(e) the terms and conditions of this Agreement, including pricing.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) was already known to the Receiving Party without confidentiality obligations prior to disclosure;  
(b) becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
(c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on use or disclosure; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  

  5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
   (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for purposes of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
   (b) restrict access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to its employees, agents, and Subcontractors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein; and  
   (c) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall: (a) notify the Disclosing Party promptly (to the extent legally permitted), (b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party at the Disclosing Party’s expense to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and (c) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or (at the Disclosing Party’s option) securely destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for legal and compliance purposes, subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For any systems or platforms operated by or on behalf of the Service Provider that are used to manage or deliver the Services (e.g., time tracking, ticketing, or resource management tools), the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve:

  12.1.1 **Uptime** of at least **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance (not to exceed eight (8) hours per month) and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall meet the following service desk response and resolution targets for issues raised by the Company relating to the Services:

  12.2.1 **Critical Issues** (Service outage or major business impact):  
   - Initial Response: within 1 hour (24x7);  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours.

  12.2.2 **High Priority Issues** (significant impact but workaround possible):  
   - Initial Response: within 4 Business Hours;  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 2 Business Days.

  12.2.3 **Medium Priority Issues** (limited impact):  
   - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day;  
   - Target Resolution: within 5 Business Days.

  12.2.4 **Low Priority Requests** (informational or minor enhancement):  
   - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days;  
   - Target Resolution: as agreed between the Parties.

12.3 **Staffing SLAs.**  

  12.3.1 For standard staffing requisitions, the Service Provider shall present a minimum of three (3) qualified candidate profiles within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of a complete and approved requisition, subject to market conditions and Available talent.

  12.3.2 For critical roles identified by the Company, the Service Provider shall prioritize such requisitions and use commercially reasonable efforts to shorten submission timelines as mutually agreed.

12.4 **SLA Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall provide the Company with monthly SLA reports summarizing performance metrics, including uptime, response and resolution times, and staffing fulfillment rates. The Parties shall review such reports in regular governance meetings and agree on corrective actions as necessary.

12.5 **SLA Remedies.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet any material SLA in a given calendar month, the Company may be entitled to service credits as follows (unless otherwise specified in a SOW):

  12.5.1 For system uptime between 99.0% and 99.5%: a service credit equal to 2% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services;

  12.5.2 For system uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: a service credit equal to 5% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services;

  12.5.3 For system uptime below 98.0%: a service credit equal to 10% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services.

The aggregate service credits in any month shall not exceed 15% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services. Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute the Company’s sole monetary remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to other termination rights under **Article IV**.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, or failures or interruptions of telecommunications or power.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  15.2.1 Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;

  15.2.2 Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement that are directly affected by the Force Majeure Event shall be suspended. The affected Party shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performance to the extent caused by the Force Majeure Event, provided it complies with Section 15.2.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Services or this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without further liability (except for payment for Services rendered prior to termination).
