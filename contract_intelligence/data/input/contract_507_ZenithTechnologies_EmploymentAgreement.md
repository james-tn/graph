## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Vendor shall provide to the Client, on a non-exclusive basis, comprehensive professional, technical, and managed services, including the provision of skilled Vendor Personnel, to support the Client’s software development, systems integration, infrastructure operations, application maintenance, and related functions. The Services shall be performed in accordance with this Agreement and the applicable SOW(s) executed under Article I and Article XIII.

2.2 **Nature of Engagement.**  
(a) The Parties acknowledge that the Services include an “employment-style” engagement model, whereby the Vendor will recruit, employ, manage, and supervise Vendor Personnel who will perform day-to-day work and tasks under guidance and direction relating to project priorities and functional requirements as reasonably provided by the Client.  
(b) Notwithstanding any such direction from the Client:  
   (i) Vendor Personnel shall remain employees or contractors of the Vendor or its subcontractors;  
   (ii) the Vendor shall retain exclusive responsibility for payment of all wages, benefits, taxes, social security, insurance, and other costs associated with Vendor Personnel; and  
   (iii) nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment, joint employment, partnership, or agency relationship between the Client and any Vendor Personnel.

2.3 **Types of Services.**  
The Services may include, without limitation, the following categories, which may be refined or extended in each SOW:

(a) **Software Engineering and Development.** Design, coding, configuration, customization, integration, testing, and deployment of software applications, APIs, and microservices in various technologies used by the Client.

(b) **Application Maintenance and Support.** Provision of L1, L2, and L3 support for designated applications, including incident management, problem resolution, patching, and minor enhancements.

(c) **Infrastructure and Cloud Operations.** Management and monitoring of servers, networks, cloud environments (e.g., Azure, AWS), databases, and related infrastructure, including performance tuning and capacity planning.

(d) **Business Analysis and Project Management.** Requirements gathering, documentation, functional and technical specifications, sprint planning, and coordination of project execution, using agile or waterfall methodologies as prescribed by the Client.

(e) **Quality Assurance and Testing.** Creation and execution of test plans, test scripts, automation frameworks, regression testing, performance testing, and related quality assurance activities.

(f) **Consulting and Advisory Services.** Advisory, architectural, and solution design services in connection with the Client’s strategic technology initiatives.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  
(a) For each SOW, the Parties shall define and agree upon specific Deliverables, including acceptance criteria, delivery milestones, and formats.  
(b) Deliverables may include, without limitation: source code repositories, build scripts, configuration files, test reports, architectural documents, user manuals, training materials, operational runbooks, and periodic status or performance reports.  
(c) Unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW, Deliverables shall be delivered electronically via secure channels designated by the Client.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) The Vendor shall perform the Services with due skill, care, and diligence, in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for providers of similar services.  
(b) The Vendor shall ensure that Vendor Personnel assigned to the Client possess the requisite qualifications, experience, and technical expertise specified by the Client in relevant SOWs or resource profiles.  
(c) The Vendor shall promptly replace any Vendor Personnel who, in the reasonable opinion of the Client, do not meet performance expectations or exhibit inappropriate conduct at any Work Location, provided that the Client first provides a written explanation of the grounds for such request. The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide a suitable replacement within thirty (30) days or such other period as agreed in the relevant SOW.

2.6 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
(a) Each SOW shall specify estimated timelines, milestones, sprint schedules, and delivery dates, along with any dependencies or assumptions.  
(b) The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet such timelines and milestones. The Vendor shall promptly notify the Client in writing upon becoming aware of any circumstances likely to cause a delay or impact the achievement of any milestone, together with a proposed mitigation plan.  
(c) Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, time shall not be of the essence; however, persistent or material delays that are attributable to the Vendor and not excused by a Force Majeure Event or Client-caused delays may constitute a material breach under Section 4.4(b).

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall, at its own cost (unless otherwise agreed in an SOW):  
(a) Provide the Vendor with timely access to relevant Client Materials, systems, and subject matter experts reasonably required for the Vendor to perform the Services;  
(b) Make timely decisions and approvals in connection with the Services and any Change Orders;  
(c) Provide Vendor Personnel with appropriate access badges, credentials, or VPN connections to access the Client’s systems and Work Locations, subject to Client’s security and access policies; and  
(d) Fulfill any specific client-side obligations described in the applicable SOW(s).

2.8 **Work Location and Remote Work.**  
(a) Services may be performed onsite at the Client’s premises, at Vendor’s premises, or remotely, as specified in the applicable SOW.  
(b) Vendor Personnel performing Services at any Client Work Location shall comply with all reasonable Client policies and procedures notified in writing, including security, health and safety, and information technology usage policies.

2.9 **Subcontracting.**  
The Vendor may subcontract portions of the Services to qualified subcontractors, provided that:  
(a) the Vendor remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services and compliance with this Agreement;  
(b) any subcontractors are bound by written agreements imposing obligations no less protective of the Client than those set out in this Agreement, especially with respect to confidentiality, data protection, and IP; and  
(c) the Vendor shall be liable for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were the Vendor’s own.

2.10 **Priority of Documents.**  
In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the provisions of this Agreement and any SOW, the following order of precedence shall apply, unless expressly stated otherwise in a given SOW:  
(a) The main body of this Agreement;  
(b) The SOW (most recent amendment taking precedence over prior versions);  
(c) Any other annexes or exhibits.

(c) Once the dispute is resolved, any amounts finally agreed or determined as payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of such resolution, with interest applied from the original due date for any amount adjudged to be properly due to the Vendor.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **September 08, 2026** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend the Term for additional periods upon such terms and conditions as may be agreed in a written amendment or new agreement. Unless expressly stated, no automatic renewal shall apply.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the high-risk, Vendor-favorable nature of this engagement, either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience **only** upon providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. For clarity, Vendor’s right to terminate for convenience on such notice is unrestricted and may be exercised without cause.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if:

(a) The other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

(b) The other Party (i) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they fall due; (ii) enters into liquidation or a winding-up; (iii) has a receiver, administrator, or similar officer appointed; or (iv) ceases or threatens to cease to carry on its business.

4.5 **Termination of Individual SOWs.**  
The Parties may terminate any SOW independently of this Agreement, including for convenience as provided in Section 4.3 or for cause as provided in Section 4.4, without automatically terminating this Agreement as a whole, unless the notice of termination expressly states otherwise.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement or an SOW:

(a) The Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the affected Services (except to the extent reasonably necessary to complete any agreed transition activities).  

(b) The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted up to the effective date of termination, plus any reasonable, non-cancellable commitments incurred by the Vendor on the Client’s behalf in reliance on this Agreement, including any agreed early termination fees set out in the SOW.  

(c) The Vendor shall, upon the Client’s request and at mutually agreed fees, provide reasonable transition assistance, including knowledge transfer and documentation, to facilitate migration of the Services to the Client or a replacement provider.  

(d) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Any provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and Sections 3.6, 3.7, 4.6, and 16.7, shall so survive.

(a) The Vendor provides at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice to the Client, except in emergency situations; and

The Vendor shall respond to incidents in accordance with the following target response times, measured from the time the Client logs a ticket in the agreed support system:

- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issue or degraded performance – response within 1 Business Day.

14.4 **Arbitration.**

(a) The seat of arbitration shall be Singapore.  
(b) The arbitration shall be conducted in the English language.  
(c) The arbitral tribunal shall consist of **three (3) arbitrators**, one appointed by each Party and the third appointed by the two party-appointed arbitrators, or failing agreement, by SIAC.  
(d) The arbitral award shall be final and binding on the Parties and may be enforced in any court of competent jurisdiction.

14.5 **Interim Relief.**  
Nothing in this Article XIV shall prevent either Party from seeking interim or conservatory measures, including injunctive relief, from any court of competent jurisdiction at any time, including in support of arbitration.

## ARTICLE XV  
FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **Force Majeure Event** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, embargoes, or failures of electricity or telecommunications not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Consequences.**  
If a Party is unable to perform any of its obligations under this Agreement due to a Force Majeure Event:

(a) The affected obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, and the time for performance shall be extended accordingly;  

(b) The affected Party shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performing such obligations during the period of the Force Majeure Event; and  

(c) The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Notice.**  
The affected Party shall notify the other Party in writing as soon as reasonably practicable after becoming aware of the Force Majeure Event, providing details of the nature and expected duration of the event and its impact on performance.

15.4 **Prolonged Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of a Party’s obligations, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or, if the entire Agreement is materially affected, this Agreement, by providing thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party.
