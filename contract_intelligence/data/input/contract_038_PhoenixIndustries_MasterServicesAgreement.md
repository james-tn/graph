## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Vendor.** Client hereby engages Vendor, and Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described in this Agreement and in one or more SOWs executed pursuant to this Agreement, subject to the terms and conditions herein.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) From time to time during the Term, the Parties may negotiate and execute SOWs that describe particular projects or engagements. Each SOW shall:  
  (i) reference this Agreement and Contract Reference Number **MSA-PHO-202402-038**;  
  (ii) specify the scope of Services to be performed;  
  (iii) describe the Deliverables to be provided;  
  (iv) set forth the applicable fees, payment schedule, and any expenses;  
  (v) establish key milestones, timelines, and acceptance criteria; and  
  (vi) incorporate any applicable Service Levels.

(b) Once executed by both Parties, each SOW shall be deemed incorporated into and governed by this Agreement. In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.3 **General Description of Services.**  
Without limiting the scope of any specific SOW, the Services may include, but are not limited to, the following categories:

(a) **Consulting and Advisory Services.** Vendor may provide strategic advisory services related to Client’s technology infrastructure, digital transformation initiatives, process optimization, and related business objectives. This may include assessments, gap analyses, recommendations, roadmaps, and architecture design.

(b) **Implementation and Integration Services.** Vendor may configure, implement, and integrate software platforms, applications, and third-party systems, including data migration, system interfacing, customization, user acceptance testing support, and go-live assistance.

(c) **Development Services.** Vendor may design, develop, and deliver custom software components, scripts, reports, and integrations based on functional and technical specifications agreed by the Parties, including prototyping, coding, unit testing, and documentation.

(d) **Managed Services and Support.** Vendor may provide ongoing support, monitoring, incident management, and maintenance services in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and the applicable SOW, including Tier 1-3 support, patching, and performance tuning.

(e) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor may provide training sessions, workshops, documentation, and other knowledge transfer activities to enable Client’s personnel to effectively use and maintain the Deliverables and any supported systems.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner in accordance with (i) generally accepted industry standards; (ii) the specifications, requirements, and timelines set forth in the applicable SOW; and (iii) all Applicable Laws.

(b) Vendor shall assign appropriately qualified, experienced, and trained personnel to perform the Services and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure continuity of key personnel over the course of each SOW. Any changes to key personnel identified in an SOW shall be communicated to Client in advance, and Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that replacement personnel have comparable skills and experience.

(c) Vendor shall comply with Client’s reasonable policies and procedures, including information security, data protection, and workplace conduct policies, to the extent provided to Vendor in writing and applicable to the Services.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
(a) Each SOW shall specify estimated or firm timelines and milestones for the performance of Services and delivery of Deliverables. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the specified timelines and milestones.

(b) In the event Vendor anticipates that it will be unable to meet a milestone or delivery date, Vendor shall promptly notify Client in writing, explain the reasons for the delay, and propose a remediation plan and revised timeline. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate the impact of any such delay.

(c) If delays are caused by Client’s failure to perform its obligations, including timely provision of information, access, approvals, or resources, Vendor shall be entitled to a reasonable extension of time and, if agreed in a Change Order pursuant to Article XIII, additional Fees to account for such delays.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
(a) Client shall provide Vendor with timely access to its facilities, systems, data, personnel, and other resources reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services, subject to Client’s security and access policies.

(b) Client shall designate a primary contact and project manager for each SOW, who shall have authority to provide direction to Vendor, review and approve Deliverables (subject to formal acceptance procedures set forth in the SOW), and act as the main liaison between the Parties.

(c) Client shall review and respond to Vendor’s requests for information, specifications, and approvals within a reasonable time, not to exceed the timeframe set forth in the applicable SOW. Delays in Client’s responses may impact timelines and may require a Change Order.

2.7 **Deliverable Acceptance.**  
(a) Each SOW shall specify acceptance criteria and an acceptance process for Deliverables, which may include testing, review periods, and written acceptance or rejection notices.

(b) Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, Client shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt of a Deliverable (the **“Review Period”**) to review and either: (i) provide written acceptance; or (ii) provide a written notice of rejection specifying in reasonable detail the material non-conformities with the acceptance criteria.

(c) If Client timely rejects a Deliverable, Vendor shall promptly use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformities at no additional cost and resubmit the Deliverable for Client’s review. Following resubmission, Client shall have a new Review Period to review the corrected Deliverable.

(d) If Client fails to provide acceptance or rejection within the applicable Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the expiration of such period, provided Vendor has provided written notice to Client that the Review Period has commenced.

2.8 **Subcontractors.**  
Vendor may use subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (a) Vendor remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services and compliance with this Agreement; (b) any subcontractor is bound by written obligations no less protective of Client than those set forth herein, including confidentiality and data protection obligations; and (c) Vendor remains Client’s sole point of contact with respect to the Services.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total estimated contract value for all Services and Deliverables to be performed under this Agreement shall be **Two Million One Hundred Thirty-One Thousand Five Hundred Seventeen U.S. Dollars (US$2,131,517)**, as further allocated and detailed in the applicable SOWs. The Parties acknowledge that individual SOWs may specify sub-budgets within this overall amount.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees under this Agreement may be structured as one or more of the following, as specified in the applicable SOW:

(a) **Time and Materials (T&M).** Fees based on hourly or daily rates for Vendor personnel, multiplied by the actual hours or days worked, subject to any not-to-exceed amounts stated in the SOW.

(b) **Fixed Price.** A fixed fee for defined Deliverables or phases of work, payable upon completion of milestones or acceptance of Deliverables as set forth in the SOW.

(c) **Retainer or Managed Services Fees.** Recurring monthly or annual fees for ongoing managed services, maintenance, and support, as described in the SOW.

(d) **Other Fee Types.** Any other fee models mutually agreed by the Parties in an SOW, such as success fees or incentive-based fees.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Invoices.**  
(a) Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for Fees incurred during the preceding month.

(b) Each invoice shall detail, as applicable: (i) the SOW number; (ii) the time period covered; (iii) a description of Services performed and Deliverables provided; (iv) applicable hourly rates and hours/days worked; (v) any approved expenses; and (vi) all applicable Taxes.

(c) **Payment Terms.** Client shall pay undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt of a valid and correct invoice (**“Net 60”**), via wire transfer, ACH, or other method agreed in writing.

3.4 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the nature and amount of the dispute. Client may withhold payment only of the disputed portion, and shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any invoice disputes promptly. If the Parties determine that any disputed amounts are owed to Vendor, Client shall pay such amounts within fifteen (15) Business Days of resolution.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one percent (1.0%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted under Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment. Vendor shall not exercise any right to suspend Services for late payment unless: (i) such amounts remain unpaid more than thirty (30) days after written notice of late payment; and (ii) Vendor has engaged in good faith discussions with Client to resolve the payment issue.

3.6 **Expenses.**  
(a) Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred in connection with the performance of the Services (such as travel, lodging, and meals), in accordance with Client’s then-current travel and expense policy, provided such policy has been made available to Vendor in writing.

(b) Vendor shall submit appropriate documentation (e.g., receipts) with its expense reimbursement requests. Client shall reimburse approved expenses in accordance with the invoicing and payment terms set forth in this Article III.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) All Fees are exclusive of Taxes. Client shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes arising out of or related to this Agreement, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees.

(b) If Client is required to withhold any Taxes on payments to Vendor, Client shall provide Vendor with appropriate documentation, and the Parties shall cooperate to minimize any such Taxes in compliance with Applicable Law.

3.8 **No Withholdings or Setoffs.**  
Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or required by Applicable Law, Client shall not withhold, set off, or deduct any amounts from payments due to Vendor without Vendor’s prior written consent; provided, however, that Client may set off any amounts finally determined to be owed by Vendor to Client by a court or arbitral tribunal of competent jurisdiction.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **December 31, 2026** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may agree in writing to extend or renew this Agreement for additional periods on mutually agreed terms. Any such extension or renewal shall be memorialized in a written amendment executed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Client).**  
Given the low risk profile of the engagement, Client may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for convenience and without cause upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor. Upon such termination, Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables provided (and accepted) up to the effective date of termination, and for any non-cancellable commitments expressly approved by Client in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience (Vendor).**  
Vendor may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client, and only after good faith discussions with Client regarding alternatives to termination, including resource reallocation or scope adjustments. Vendor shall cooperate with Client to facilitate an orderly transition of Services.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer cure period as the Parties may agree) after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; or

(b) becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, is the subject of a bankruptcy petition or similar proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

(a) Vendor shall promptly cease performing the applicable Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary to wind down and transition the Services as requested by Client.

(b) Client shall pay Vendor for all undisputed Fees and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination (and, in the case of termination for convenience, any non-cancellable commitments approved in writing), subject to Client’s rights of setoff or withholding for material breaches by Vendor.

(c) Each Party shall, at the other Party’s option, return or securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession, in accordance with Section 5.5.

(d) To the extent requested by Client, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance services (such as knowledge transfer, documentation, and support to transition to Client or a replacement vendor) for a period of up to ninety (90) days after termination, at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates (or as otherwise specified in the applicable SOW).

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Sections 3.5, 3.7, 4.6, and Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions).

“Confidential Information” means any non-public, confidential, or proprietary information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**) in connection with this Agreement, whether in oral, written, electronic, or other form, that is designated as confidential or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Client’s Confidential Information includes Client Materials and any Personal Data processed on Client’s behalf.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is received from a third party who did not acquire or disclose such information in violation of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  
(b) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event with less than reasonable care; and  
(c) disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, officers, directors, agents, and subcontractors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party and reasonably cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or other remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for compliance and legal purposes, subject to continued confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Equitable Relief.**  
The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, in the event of any breach or threatened breach of this Article V.

5.7 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.
