## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF PRODUCTS AND SERVICES

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Phoenix shall manufacture, supply, and deliver to Contoso the Products, and shall perform the Services, as described in this Agreement, the Specifications, and each applicable Purchase Order or Statement of Work. The Parties agree that the scope hereunder includes both (a) recurring supply of specified Products for Contoso’s production and deployment needs, and (b) related professional and technical Services necessary to enable integration, commissioning, and lifecycle support of such Products within Contoso’s infrastructure.

2.2 **Products.**  
(a) Phoenix shall supply industrial-grade hardware components and subassemblies, including but not limited to:  
   (i) rack-mounted chassis and power modules designed to meet Contoso’s data center requirements;  
   (ii) network interconnect components and related mechanical assemblies; and  
   (iii) any replacement or spare parts specified by Contoso.

(b) All Products shall conform in all material respects to the applicable Specifications, industry standards, and performance criteria agreed in writing by the Parties. Phoenix shall maintain appropriate quality control processes, including incoming material inspection, in-process testing, and final quality assurance checks, to ensure consistent conformity.

(c) Unless otherwise expressly stated in a Purchase Order, all Products shall be new, free from defects in materials and workmanship, and shall not be refurbished or reconditioned.

2.3 **Ordering Procedure.**  
(a) Contoso shall issue Purchase Orders specifying the Product type, description, quantities, pricing (consistent with Article III), required delivery dates, shipping locations, and any other relevant details.  

(b) Phoenix shall confirm acceptance of each Purchase Order in writing (including by email acknowledgment referencing the Purchase Order number) within five (5) Business Days of receipt. Failure to reject a Purchase Order within such period shall be deemed acceptance of such Purchase Order.  

(c) If Phoenix cannot meet the requested delivery date or other material terms, Phoenix shall promptly notify Contoso and propose alternative terms. Any deviation from the Purchase Order shall be effective only if mutually agreed in writing by the Parties.

2.4 **Services.**  
Phoenix shall perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills and experience. Services may include, without limitation:  

(a) **Engineering and Design Support.** Assistance in the design, customization, and optimization of Products to meet Contoso’s technical and operational requirements, including review of mechanical and electrical specifications, design for manufacturability, and reliability analysis.

(b) **Configuration and Integration.** Configuration of Products to integrate with Contoso’s existing systems, including hardware pre-assembly, cabling, labeling, and pre-configuration as agreed in an SOW.

(c) **Installation Assistance and Commissioning.** Remote or on-site support to assist Contoso’s personnel or contractors in installing, commissioning, and validating the Products at designated locations, including coordination of test plans, verification procedures, and acceptance protocols.

(d) **Maintenance and Support.** Ongoing support services, including troubleshooting, failure analysis, repair or replacement coordination, and proactive recommendations for product improvements, as specified in an applicable SOW.

(e) **Training.** Provision of training materials and sessions (remote or in-person) to Contoso’s designated staff on the use, installation, and maintenance of the Products, as may be agreed.

2.5 **Deliverables and Milestones.**  
(a) For Services governed by a Statement of Work, each SOW shall describe in reasonable detail the Deliverables, milestones, acceptance criteria, and associated timelines.  

(b) Phoenix shall provide Contoso with a delivery schedule and progress updates at intervals reasonably requested by Contoso. Phoenix shall promptly notify Contoso in writing of any circumstances that may materially impact the timely delivery of any Deliverable or Product.  

(c) Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted ten (10) Business Days after delivery if Contoso has not provided Phoenix with written notice of material non-conformity. Any notice of non-conformity shall describe the non-conformity in reasonable detail.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Phoenix shall perform the Services in accordance with (i) generally accepted industry standards, (ii) the Service Levels described in Article XII, and (iii) applicable laws and regulations.  

(b) Phoenix shall implement and maintain a quality management system reasonably acceptable to Contoso, including documented procedures for handling non-conforming Products, corrective and preventive actions, and traceability of critical components.  

(c) Delivery of Products shall be subject to on-time performance metrics. The Parties may agree on a target on-time delivery rate (e.g., 95% on-time delivery) in an SOW or Service Level Appendix. Persistent failure to meet agreed performance standards shall entitle Contoso to the remedies set forth in Article XII and, where applicable, Article IV.

2.7 **Inspection and Testing.**  
(a) Contoso shall have the right, upon reasonable advance notice and during normal business hours, to inspect Phoenix’s facilities and processes relevant to the manufacture and quality control of the Products, not more than twice per calendar year unless a material quality issue arises.  

(b) Phoenix shall cooperate with such inspections and provide access to relevant records, test reports, and quality data, subject to reasonable confidentiality and security restrictions.  

(c) Contoso may perform incoming inspection and testing upon receipt of Products. Non-conforming Products may be rejected and returned to Phoenix, at Phoenix’s expense, for repair, replacement, or credit, as further set forth in Article VII and Article VIII.

2.8 **Subcontractors.**  
Phoenix may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services or manufacture components of the Products, provided that: (i) Phoenix remains fully responsible for the performance of its subcontractors; (ii) such subcontractors are bound by written agreements consistent with Phoenix’s obligations under this Agreement, including confidentiality and data protection; and (iii) Contoso shall have the right to reasonably object to subcontractors that present a material risk to Contoso’s operations, security, or reputation.

2.9 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
This Agreement is non-exclusive. Contoso may purchase products and services similar to or competitive with the Products and Services from other suppliers. Phoenix may sell similar goods and services to other customers, provided that Phoenix complies with its confidentiality and non-use obligations under Article V.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the anticipated aggregate value of Products and Services purchased by Contoso under this Agreement is **US$4,520,155**, based on projected volumes and rate cards. Such value is an estimate only and does not constitute a purchase commitment by Contoso except as expressly set forth in specific Purchase Orders or SOWs.

3.2 **Pricing and Rate Schedules.**  
(a) The prices for specific Products and Services shall be set forth in Exhibit A (Pricing Schedule), each Purchase Order, and/or applicable SOWs. In the event of inconsistency, the pricing in the applicable Purchase Order or SOW shall prevail for that transaction.  

(b) Pricing shall remain firm and not subject to increase during the Term, except as expressly agreed in a written amendment or mutually agreed index-based adjustment mechanism.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
(a) Phoenix shall issue invoices to Contoso in accordance with the milestones and billing schedule specified in the relevant Purchase Order or SOW. Unless otherwise stated, Phoenix shall invoice upon:  
   (i) shipment of Products (for Products); and  
   (ii) completion and acceptance of milestones or monthly in arrears (for Services).  

(b) Each invoice shall reference the applicable Purchase Order number, SOW number (if any), description of Products or Services, quantities, unit prices, applicable taxes, and any agreed discounts.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) Except as otherwise set forth in a Purchase Order or SOW, Contoso shall pay undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt of a correct and complete invoice (**“Net 60”**).  

(b) Contoso may withhold payment of any amounts disputed in good faith, provided that Contoso notifies Phoenix of the nature of the dispute within a reasonable time. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve invoicing disputes promptly. Upon resolution, any undisputed amount shall be paid within thirty (30) days.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
(a) Any undisputed invoice amount not paid when due shall, upon written notice from Phoenix and subject to a five (5) Business Day cure period, accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the date such amount became due until the date of actual payment.  

(b) Phoenix’s right to interest shall be Phoenix’s sole monetary remedy for late payment, subject to Phoenix’s rights under Article IV (Termination) with respect to persistent non-payment.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
(a) Prices are exclusive of Taxes unless expressly stated otherwise. Phoenix shall itemize applicable Taxes on each invoice.  

(b) Contoso shall be responsible for any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar Taxes relating to the purchase of Products and Services, except for taxes based on Phoenix’s income, property, or employment obligations, which shall be the sole responsibility of Phoenix.  

(c) If Contoso is exempt from certain Taxes, it shall provide Phoenix with appropriate exemption certificates, and Phoenix shall not charge such Taxes on invoices.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Out-of-pocket expenses (such as travel, lodging, and meals) incurred by Phoenix in performing Services shall be reimbursed by Contoso only if: (i) such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Contoso; and (ii) Phoenix provides reasonable supporting documentation with the relevant invoice.  

(b) Reimbursable expenses shall be charged at actual cost and in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policy, which shall be provided to Phoenix upon request.

3.8 **Set-Off.**  
Contoso may set off or deduct from any amounts payable to Phoenix any amounts that are bona fide due and payable to Contoso from Phoenix under this Agreement, including without limitation service credits, refunds, or indemnification payments that have been finally agreed or adjudicated.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **February 17, 2028** (the **“Initial Term”**). The Initial Term and any Renewal Terms (if applicable) are collectively referred to as the **“Term.”**

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement may be renewed for one or more additional periods of up to two (2) years each (each a **“Renewal Term”**), on terms to be negotiated in good faith.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Client-Friendly).**  
(a) Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any specific Purchase Order or SOW), for any reason or no reason, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Phoenix.  

(b) Phoenix may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso, and only with respect to future Orders not yet accepted by Phoenix, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any affected Purchase Order or SOW) immediately upon written notice if:  

(a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment) after receiving written notice thereof describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

(b) The other Party (i) becomes insolvent, (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or for reorganization that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (iv) ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

4.5 **Termination of Individual Orders or SOWs.**  
Contoso may terminate any individual Purchase Order or SOW for convenience or cause as provided above, without terminating this entire Agreement. In such event, the rights and obligations of the Parties with respect to other ongoing Orders or SOWs shall not be affected.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any Purchase Order or SOW:  

(a) Phoenix shall promptly cease work under the terminated portions and use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate costs;  

(b) Contoso shall pay Phoenix for (i) Products delivered and accepted, and (ii) Services performed and accepted, up to the effective date of termination, and any reasonable, documented, non-cancellable commitments pre-approved in writing by Contoso;  

(c) Phoenix shall promptly return or, at Contoso’s written direction, destroy Contoso’s Confidential Information and Client Data in accordance with Section 11.7;  

(d) Any licenses granted under Article VI shall continue only to the extent expressly stated therein; and  

(e) Any accrued rights, obligations, or liabilities of either Party as of the effective date of termination shall survive.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of Sections 3.5, 3.8, 4.6, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (only as to accrued credits), XIII (with respect to completed Change Orders), XIV, XV, XVI, together with any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is received from a Third Party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) is developed independently by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;  
(b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any Third Party except as permitted herein; and  
(c) protect such Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to its employees, Affiliates, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement.

5.5 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice and cooperate reasonably with the Disclosing Party’s lawful efforts to limit or contest such disclosure. Any such disclosure shall be limited to the extent required by law.

5.6 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s option) all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for legal and compliance purposes, subject to continuing confidentiality obligations.

5.7 **Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall be protected for so long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XV  
FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event beyond a Party’s reasonable control that prevents or materially delays the performance of its obligations under this Agreement, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, riots, civil unrest, epidemics, pandemics, governmental orders, labor disputes (other than those of the affected Party’s own employees), and failures of third-party utilities or infrastructure not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of such event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that performance is prevented or materially delayed. The time for performance shall be extended by a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of critical obligations, either Party may terminate the affected Purchase Orders or SOWs upon written notice, without liability (except for payment of amounts accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
