## SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
During the Term, Acme shall provide to Contoso certain professional consulting and related Services, which may include, without limitation:

  2.1.1 Strategic advisory services relating to business transformation, process optimization, and organizational design;

  2.1.2 Technology consulting, including current-state assessments, solution design, architecture review, and roadmap development for enterprise systems;

  2.1.3 Implementation and configuration support for selected software platforms, including but not limited to ERP, CRM, and analytics tools, subject to the terms of the applicable SOW;

  2.1.4 Program and project management services, including governance support, risk management, stakeholder communications, and status reporting;

  2.1.5 Data analytics and reporting services, including design of dashboards, key performance indicators, and management reports;

  2.1.6 Training and knowledge transfer services for Contoso personnel, including preparation of training materials and delivery of workshops or remote sessions;

  2.1.7 Any other consulting tasks or professional services that the Parties may agree upon in writing in an SOW from time to time.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
The specific nature, scope, and details of the Services to be performed and Deliverables to be provided shall be described in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, set forth:

  (a) a description of the Services and Deliverables;  
  (b) project timelines, key milestones, and interim deliverables;  
  (c) the responsibilities of each Party;  
  (d) the applicable Fees, payment schedule, and any expenses;  
  (e) the Service Levels, if applicable; and  
  (f) any additional terms and conditions specific to the SOW.

In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to modify this Agreement and specifically references the Section(s) being modified.

2.3 **Deliverables and Milestones.**  
Acme shall develop and deliver the Deliverables in accordance with the timelines and milestones set out in the applicable SOW. Deliverables may include, by way of example:

  2.3.1 Written assessment reports summarizing current-state findings, gaps, and recommended remediation strategies;

  2.3.2 Detailed project plans, including work breakdown structures, schedules, resource plans, and risk registers;

  2.3.3 Solution design documents, including technical architectures, integration specifications, and configuration guides;

  2.3.4 Prototype or configured system environments for demonstration or user acceptance testing (where applicable);

  2.3.5 Training materials, user guides, standard operating procedures, and reference documentation;

  2.3.6 Periodic status reports, executive dashboards, and performance metrics.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Acme shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services;  
  (b) by appropriately qualified, experienced, and trained personnel;  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Law and this Agreement; and  
  (d) using reasonable efforts to meet the timelines and milestones set forth in the SOWs.

Acme does not warrant that any particular business result or outcome will be achieved, except as expressly set forth in this Agreement or the applicable SOW. Contoso acknowledges that consulting services are advisory in nature and that actual business outcomes are influenced by factors outside Acme’s control, including Contoso’s internal decisions, systems, and third-party dependencies.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Contoso shall:

  2.5.1 Provide Acme with timely access to Contoso’s personnel, information, systems, facilities, and resources as reasonably required by Acme to perform the Services;

  2.5.2 Designate a project sponsor and a project manager who will be responsible for day-to-day liaison with Acme, coordination of internal stakeholders, and timely review and approval of Deliverables;

  2.5.3 Review Deliverables within the review periods specified in the applicable SOW and provide consolidated feedback, approvals, or rejections in writing. If Contoso fails to respond within such review period, the applicable Deliverable may, at Acme’s election, be deemed accepted;

  2.5.5 Obtain and maintain all necessary internal approvals and third-party consents (other than those expressly allocated to Acme) required for the Project.

Acme shall not be liable for any delay or failure in the performance of the Services to the extent caused by Contoso’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities under this Section 2.5.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Each SOW may set forth specific acceptance criteria and procedures. Unless otherwise stated in the applicable SOW:

  2.6.1 Upon delivery of a Deliverable, Acme shall notify Contoso in writing (email acceptable) of such delivery and request acceptance.

  2.6.2 Contoso shall have ten (10) Business Days (or such other period as specified in the SOW) to review and test the Deliverable to verify that it materially conforms to the applicable specifications.

Acme reserves the right, upon written notice and after providing Contoso ten (10) Business Days to cure following such notice, to suspend performance of Services if any undisputed invoice remains past due.

If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith:

  3.4.1 Contoso shall notify Acme in writing within fifteen (15) days after receipt of the invoice, specifying the amount and basis of the dispute.

  3.4.2 Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion by the due date.

  3.4.3 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Any amounts subsequently determined to be owed shall be paid within ten (10) days of such determination.

3.5 **Expenses.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  3.5.1 Contoso shall reimburse Acme for reasonable, documented out-of-pocket expenses (including travel, lodging, meals, and incidental expenses) incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, provided that such expenses comply with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policy, as provided in writing to Acme in advance.

  3.5.2 Acme shall obtain Contoso’s prior written approval (email acceptable) for any single expense exceeding US$2,500.

  3.5.3 Expense reimbursement requests shall be supported by appropriate documentation, including receipts or equivalent evidence.

Contoso shall be responsible for any Taxes imposed on the Services or Deliverables, excluding taxes based on Acme’s net income, property, or employees. If Acme is required to collect and remit Taxes, such Taxes shall be itemized on the applicable invoice and paid by Contoso, unless Contoso provides a valid tax exemption certificate.

3.7 **No Setoff.**  
Except for undisputed credits or refunds owing to Contoso under this Agreement, amounts due under this Agreement shall be paid without any right of setoff or deduction, except to the extent required by law.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **November 13, 2025** (the “**Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew this Agreement for additional terms on such terms and conditions as may be agreed in writing. Unless and until such written extension or renewal is executed, this Agreement shall expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience without cause upon not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. Termination of an individual SOW shall not, in and of itself, terminate any other SOW or this Agreement unless specifically stated in the notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or the applicable SOW, and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, has a receiver appointed, or is subject to any bankruptcy or insolvency proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  

  4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement (or any SOW), Acme shall promptly cease performing the applicable Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary to effect an orderly wind-down.

  4.5.2 Contoso shall pay Acme for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination or expiration, including for any non-cancellable commitments made by Acme in reliance on this Agreement that cannot be mitigated using commercially reasonable efforts.

  4.5.3 If Contoso terminates for convenience pursuant to Section 4.3, Contoso shall also pay Acme any early termination or disengagement fees expressly set forth in the applicable SOW, if any.

  4.5.4 Each Party shall, at the other Party’s written request, return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s direction) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to the rights and obligations in Section 5.4 and Section 11.7 (Data Retention and Deletion).

4.6 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections 3.3, 3.6, 4.5, 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Intellectual Property Rights) (to the extent applicable), 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 14, 15, and 16, and any other provisions that by their nature should survive, shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

  (a) is or becomes generally known to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without duty of confidentiality;  
  (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous documentation; or  
  (d) was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party without an obligation of confidentiality.

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
  (b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein;  
  (c) restrict access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  
  (d) use at least the same degree of care to protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted):

  (a) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement;  
  (b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and  
  (c) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed.

5.5 **Injunctive Relief.**

5.6 **Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, such obligations shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law or Personal Data, respectively.

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence, nature, and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event on its performance; and  
  (c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable after the Force Majeure Event ceases.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability except for amounts accrued up to the effective date of termination.
