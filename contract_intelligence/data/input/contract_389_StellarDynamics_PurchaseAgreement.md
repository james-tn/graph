## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **Overview.**  
Subject to the terms of this Agreement, the Vendor shall supply to the Company the Products, perform the Services, and deliver the Deliverables specified in this Agreement, any SOW, and any Orders issued hereunder. The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement provides a master framework, and the specific scope for each engagement shall be further detailed in one or more SOWs or Orders.

2.2 **Primary Objectives.**  
The primary objectives of the engagement are to:

  2.2.1 Supply and configure enterprise-grade server, storage, and network infrastructure suitable for the Company’s production workloads, disaster recovery, and development environments;

  2.2.2 Provide software and associated subscription services, including virtualization, monitoring, backup, and security platforms;

  2.2.3 Integrate the Products with the Company’s existing infrastructure, applications, and identity management systems;

  2.2.4 Provide ongoing managed services, including monitoring, incident response, patch management, capacity management, and performance optimization;

  2.2.5 Provide training, knowledge transfer, and documentation sufficient for the Company’s personnel to effectively operate and manage the implemented solutions.

2.3 **Detailed Deliverables.**  
Without prejudice to the generality of Section 2.1, the Vendor shall, at a minimum, provide the following Deliverables, which may be further refined in one or more SOWs:

  2.3.1 **Infrastructure Design Documentation.** A detailed high-level and low-level design for the target environment, including network diagrams, sizing calculations, high-availability architecture, and security controls.

  2.3.2 **Configured Hardware and Software.** Delivery and configuration of server, storage, and networking devices, as well as installation and configuration of required software components, including hypervisors, management consoles, and monitoring tools.

  2.3.3 **Integration Components.** Development or configuration of integration components (such as APIs, connectors, or scripts) required for the implemented solution to interoperate with the Company’s identity management system, ticketing platform, and monitoring tools.

  2.3.4 **Testing and Acceptance Materials.** Test plans, test scripts, and test results demonstrating that the implemented solution meets the agreed acceptance criteria set forth in the relevant SOW.

  2.3.5 **Operational Documentation.** Standard operating procedures (SOPs), runbooks, escalation matrices, and other documentation necessary for day-to-day operations and support.

  2.3.6 **Training Materials.** A set of training materials and sessions for the Company’s designated personnel, which may include on-site or remote workshops, user guides, and reference materials.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform all Services:

  2.4.1 In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards, and using suitably qualified, experienced, and trained personnel;

  2.4.2 In accordance with the Project Plan and any milestones and Service Levels agreed in this Agreement or in applicable SOWs;

  2.4.3 In compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and professional standards, including applicable Data Protection Laws, export controls, and health and safety regulations;

  2.4.4 With due care to minimize disruption to the Company’s ongoing business operations and in coordination with the Company’s designated project manager.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall agree in writing on key milestones and associated dates in each SOW or Project Plan. Unless otherwise set forth therein:

  2.5.1 The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet all agreed milestone dates, time being of the essence in relation to critical milestones expressly designated as such in an SOW.

  2.5.2 If the Vendor becomes aware of any circumstances that may delay completion of any milestone, it shall promptly notify the Company in writing, provide a revised timeline, and propose remedial measures.

  2.5.3 The Company shall provide timely access to relevant systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably required for the Vendor to meet the agreed milestones. Any delay caused by the Company’s failure to fulfill its obligations shall entitle the Vendor to a reasonable extension of time, to be agreed in writing.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  2.6.1 For each Deliverable or milestone, the Vendor shall notify the Company in writing when such Deliverable or milestone is ready for acceptance testing.

  2.6.2 The Company shall have a period of fifteen (15) Business Days (or such other period as may be specified in the relevant SOW) from receipt of the Deliverable to test and either (a) accept the Deliverable in writing, or (b) reject it by providing a written notice describing in reasonable detail the non-conformities with the applicable specifications.

  2.6.3 If the Company rejects a Deliverable, the Vendor shall promptly, at its own cost, remedy the non-conformities and re-submit the Deliverable for re-testing. The Parties shall repeat this acceptance process until the Deliverable is accepted or the Agreement is otherwise terminated in accordance with Article IV.

  2.6.4 If the Company neither accepts nor rejects a Deliverable within the applicable acceptance period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, provided that the Vendor has given a written reminder and the Company has failed to respond within five (5) additional Business Days.

2.7 **Changes in Scope.**  
Any changes to the scope of Products, Services, or Deliverables shall be managed through the change control process set out in Article XIII. No change shall be binding unless documented in a Change Order duly executed by the authorized representatives of both Parties.

2.8 **Subcontracting.**  
The Vendor may subcontract portions of the Services to third parties, provided that:

  2.8.1 The Vendor remains fully responsible and liable for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were its own;

  2.8.2 Any subcontractor that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Company shall be bound by written obligations that are no less protective than those imposed on the Vendor under Article XI; and

  2.8.3 The Vendor shall not subcontract any material portion of the Services to a direct competitor of the Company without the Company’s prior written consent.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value for Products, Services, and Deliverables to be provided under this Agreement during the Term is **USD $4,351,035** (four million three hundred fifty-one thousand and thirty-five United States dollars), exclusive of any applicable taxes, unless otherwise expressly stated.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall consist of the following components (which may be detailed further in each SOW or Order):

  3.2.1 **Product Fees.** One-time fees for hardware, perpetual software licenses, and initial subscriptions, payable in accordance with Section 3.3.

  3.2.2 **Implementation and Integration Fees.** Fixed-price or time-and-materials fees for implementation, configuration, and integration Services.

  3.2.3 **Managed Services Fees.** Recurring monthly or annual fees for ongoing support, monitoring, and maintenance.

  3.2.4 **Training and Consulting Fees.** Fees for training, workshops, and additional consulting services provided at the Company’s request.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**  

  3.3.1 Unless otherwise set forth in an SOW or Order, the Vendor shall invoice the Company:

   - Fifty percent (50%) of Product Fees upon acceptance of the corresponding Order;  
   - The remaining fifty percent (50%) of Product Fees upon delivery of the Products;  
   - Implementation and Integration Fees monthly in arrears based on milestones achieved or time and materials incurred;  
   - Managed Services Fees monthly or annually in advance, as agreed;  
   - Training and Consulting Fees upon completion of the relevant sessions or monthly in arrears for time and materials engagements.

  3.3.2 All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable **Net 45 days** from the date of the invoice, by bank transfer or other payment method agreed in writing. Time for payment shall not, of itself, be of the essence.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
If the Company fails to pay any undisputed amount by the due date:

  3.4.1 The Vendor may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate equal to two percent (2%) per annum above the Bank of England base rate, calculated daily from the due date until the date of actual payment; and

  3.4.2 The Vendor shall provide written notice of late payment, and if the Company fails to pay the overdue amount within fifteen (15) Business Days of such notice, the Vendor may, upon written notice, suspend performance of non-critical Services until payment is received, provided such suspension shall not relieve the Vendor of its obligations in respect of critical services required to ensure business continuity and data integrity.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  

  3.5.1 The Company may in good faith dispute any portion of an invoice by providing written notice to the Vendor within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute.

  3.5.2 The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve such dispute promptly. The Company shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any applicable value-added, sales, use, or similar taxes. The Company shall be responsible for any such taxes properly chargeable in relation to the Products and Services, except for taxes imposed on the Vendor’s income. The Vendor shall itemize any applicable taxes on its invoices.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
The Company shall reimburse the Vendor for pre-approved, reasonable, and properly documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services (including travel, accommodation, and subsistence expenses), subject to the following:

  3.7.1 The Vendor shall comply with the Company’s then-current travel and expense policy, provided such policy has been made available to the Vendor in writing in advance;

  3.7.2 The Vendor shall submit receipts or other appropriate supporting documentation for all reimbursable expenses; and

  3.7.3 The Vendor shall not charge any mark-up or administrative fee on reimbursable expenses without the Company’s prior written consent.

3.8 **Set-Off.**  
Except as required by law, the Company shall not set off or withhold any amounts due to the Vendor under this Agreement against any amounts owed by the Vendor to the Company, unless such set-off or withholding is agreed in writing or arises from a final, unappealable judgment or arbitral award in favour of the Company.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **13 June 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, no later than six (6) months prior to the Expiration Date, negotiate in good faith a renewal or extension of this Agreement. Any such renewal or extension shall be set forth in a written amendment executed by both Parties in accordance with Section 16.2.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (in whole or, at the Company’s option, in respect of specific SOWs or Services) for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

  4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Company:

   - The Company shall pay the Vendor for (i) all Products delivered and accepted, (ii) all Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, and (iii) any non-cancellable commitments reasonably incurred by the Vendor in reliance on this Agreement, provided the Vendor uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such commitments.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (in whole or in part) with immediate effect upon written notice if the other Party:

   - Commits a material breach of this Agreement which is incapable of remedy; or  
   - Commits a material breach which is capable of remedy but fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach and requiring it to be remedied.

  4.4.2 Without limitation, the following shall constitute material breaches:

   - The Company’s failure to pay undisputed Fees that remain unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the due date;  
   - The Vendor’s repeated failure to meet critical Service Levels as set forth in Article XII;  
   - Any material breach of the confidentiality obligations in Article V or data protection obligations in Article XI.

4.5 **Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect upon written notice if the other Party:

  4.5.1 Becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they fall due;  

  4.5.2 Enters into liquidation (except for the purpose of a solvent amalgamation or reconstruction);

  4.5.3 Has a receiver, trustee, administrator, or similar officer appointed over its assets; or

  4.5.4 Enters into any arrangement or composition with its creditors.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.6.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:

   - All outstanding unpaid invoices shall become immediately due and payable, subject to any good-faith disputes;  
   - The Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the terminated Services and shall deliver to the Company all completed and in-progress Deliverables for which the Company has paid;  
   - Each Party shall return or destroy (at the other Party’s option) the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5, subject to any retention required by law.

  4.6.2 Termination or expiration shall not affect any rights, remedies, obligations, or liabilities of either Party that have accrued up to the date of termination, including any right to claim damages in respect of any breach of this Agreement which existed at or before the date of termination.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), together with any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination, shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“Confidential Information” means any and all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**), whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including business plans, financial information, technical data, product roadmaps, software, source code, security information, Personal Data, trade secrets, and know-how.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  5.2.1 Use the Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Products, Services, and Deliverables under this Agreement;

  5.2.2 Keep the Confidential Information strictly confidential and exercise at least the same degree of care in protecting it as the Receiving Party uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care;

  5.2.3 Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement; and

  5.2.4 Limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  5.3.1 Was already lawfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction at the time of disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

  5.3.2 Becomes publicly known through no wrongful act or omission of the Receiving Party;

  5.3.3 Is lawfully received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or

  5.3.4 Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that, to the extent legally permissible, the Receiving Party:

  5.4.1 Provides the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement; and

  5.4.2 Cooperates with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information (including all copies) in its possession or control, provided that:

  5.5.1 The Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for the purpose of demonstrating its compliance with this Agreement or as required by law; and

  5.5.2 Any retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall be protected for so long as such information remains a trade secret or is considered Personal Data under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For managed Services that involve the operation of hosted or managed systems, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the covered services achieve an average **monthly uptime of 99.9%**, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and events of Force Majeure as defined in Article XV.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Vendor may schedule maintenance windows during which the Services may be unavailable, provided that:

  12.2.1 The Vendor provides the Company with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice of any planned maintenance that is expected to materially impact the Services;

  12.2.2 Scheduled maintenance is, where reasonably possible, conducted outside of the Company’s core business hours; and

  12.2.3 The Vendor uses reasonable efforts to minimize the duration and impact of such maintenance.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution.**  
The Vendor shall provide incident management services in accordance with the following target response and resolution times (to be further detailed in any SOW):

  12.3.1 **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of system functionality or severe impact on the Company’s critical business operations:  
   - Response Time: within one (1) hour;  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within four (4) hours.

  12.3.2 **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant impairment of services with limited operational workaround:  
   - Response Time: within two (2) hours;  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within one (1) Business Day.

  12.3.3 **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Partial impairment with acceptable workaround:  
   - Response Time: within four (4) hours;  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within three (3) Business Days.

  12.3.4 **Priority 4 (Low)** – Non-critical issues, cosmetic defects, and minor queries:  
   - Response Time: within one (1) Business Day;  
   - Target Resolution: to be scheduled in the next planned release or maintenance cycle.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If the Vendor fails to meet the agreed Service Levels in a given calendar month, the Company shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  12.4.1 For each complete 0.1% by which the monthly uptime percentage falls below 99.9%, the Company shall be entitled to a service credit equal to two percent (2%) of the monthly managed services Fees for the affected Services, up to a maximum of twenty percent (20%) of such Fees for the month in question.

  12.4.2 Service credits shall be applied as a discount against the Fees payable for the next invoice period. Service credits shall not be redeemable for cash.

  12.4.3 The Company’s entitlement to service credits shall be conditional upon the Company notifying the Vendor in writing of the Service Level failure within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the failure occurred.

12.5 **Exclusive Remedy.**  
Subject to Article IX, the service credits described in Section 12.4 shall be the Company’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for the Vendor’s failure to achieve the Service Levels, except where such failure amounts to a material breach giving rise to a termination right under Section 4.4.

## ARTICLE XIV – DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14.1 **Good Faith Negotiations.**  
In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement (a **“Dispute”**), the Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute through negotiations between senior executives of each Party.

14.2 **Escalation.**  

  14.2.1 Either Party may initiate the escalation procedure by giving written notice of the Dispute to the other Party. Within five (5) Business Days of such notice, each Party shall designate a senior executive with decision-making authority to attempt to resolve the Dispute.

  14.2.2 The designated executives shall meet (in person or via teleconference) as soon as reasonably practicable and shall endeavor to resolve the Dispute within twenty (20) Business Days of the initial escalation notice.

14.3 **Mediation.**  
If the Dispute is not resolved through negotiation within the period specified in Section 14.2.2, the Parties shall consider in good faith resolving the Dispute by mediation. Unless otherwise agreed, mediation shall be conducted in London under the auspices of the Centre for Effective Dispute Resolution (CEDR) or another mutually agreed mediation provider, in accordance with its then-current mediation rules.

14.4 **Arbitration or Litigation.**  
If the Dispute has not been resolved by negotiation or mediation within sixty (60) days from the date of the initial escalation notice, either Party may refer the Dispute to final resolution as follows:

  14.4.1 Unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing, the Dispute shall be finally resolved by arbitration under the rules of the London Court of International Arbitration (LCIA), which rules are deemed to be incorporated by reference into this clause.

  14.4.2 The seat, or legal place, of arbitration shall be London, England.  

  14.4.3 The arbitration tribunal shall consist of a sole arbitrator appointed in accordance with the LCIA Rules.  

  14.4.4 The language of the arbitration shall be English.  

14.5 **Interim Relief.**  
Nothing in this Article XIV shall prevent either Party from seeking interim, injunctive, or conservatory relief in any court of competent jurisdiction, including the courts of England and Wales, to protect its Confidential Information, Intellectual Property Rights, or other legitimate interests.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including acts of God, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labour disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), epidemics or pandemics, failure of utilities or telecommunications, natural disasters, or acts of government.

15.2 **Relief from Obligations.**  
Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) to the extent such delay or failure is caused by a Force Majeure Event, provided that:

  15.2.1 The affected Party gives the other Party prompt written notice of the Force Majeure Event and its expected duration; and

  15.2.2 The affected Party uses reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Prolonged Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period of more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of this Agreement, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
