## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive managed IT services and application hosting services, together with associated support and professional services, as further described in this Article II and any applicable Statement of Work. The Services are intended to support Client’s enterprise business operations, including hosting of mission-critical business applications, databases, and associated middleware in Vendor’s cloud or data centre infrastructure.

2.2 **Core Services.**  
The Services shall include, without limitation, the following core components:

  2.2.1 **Hosting and Infrastructure Services.** Vendor shall provide virtualized or dedicated server infrastructure, storage, networking, and security controls to host Client’s designated applications and databases. Such infrastructure shall be deployed in Vendor’s primary data centre located within the United Kingdom (or another location agreed in writing), with redundancy and failover as described in Section 12.3.

  2.2.2 **Application Management.** Vendor shall provide application-level monitoring, configuration management, and routine administration for Client’s designated applications (the “Managed Applications”), including deployment of updates and patches approved by Client, as well as performance tuning, capacity planning, and log management.

  2.2.3 **Monitoring and Support.** Vendor shall provide 24x7x365 monitoring of the production environment, including automated alerts and dashboards. Vendor shall provide a help desk and technical support function to receive Incident reports from Client, categorize and prioritize Incidents, and respond and resolve such Incidents in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

  2.2.4 **Backup and Recovery.** Vendor shall implement daily backups of Client Data stored in the production environment, with backup retention periods and recovery point objectives as further specified in the applicable SOW. Vendor shall support disaster recovery procedures as set forth in Section 12.3, including periodic restoration tests.

  2.2.5 **Security Services.** Vendor shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect the security, confidentiality, and integrity of Client Data, including firewalls, access controls, encryption at rest and in transit (where technically feasible and agreed), antivirus protections, and vulnerability management.

  2.2.6 **Professional Services.** Vendor may provide project-based professional services, such as onboarding, migration of applications or data into the hosted environment, custom integrations, scripting, or consulting services, pursuant to an SOW describing such services, Deliverables, timelines, and fees.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of the Services, Vendor shall deliver to Client, as applicable:

  (a) A detailed implementation plan for initial onboarding, including project milestones, dependencies, and responsibilities of each Party;  
  (b) Configuration documentation for the production environment;  
  (c) Operational runbooks describing standard operating procedures, Incident management workflows, and escalation paths;  
  (d) Periodic performance and capacity reports; and  
  (e) Any other Deliverables specified in an SOW.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
2.4.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to complete the initial onboarding and transition to the production environment within the timelines set forth in the initial SOW. Unless otherwise agreed, onboarding shall be substantially completed within ninety (90) days from the Effective Date.

2.4.2 For each SOW, the Parties shall agree on applicable milestones, including (a) environment provisioning, (b) connectivity testing, (c) data migration, (d) user acceptance testing (UAT), and (e) go-live. Vendor shall notify Client promptly of any circumstances that may reasonably be expected to affect the timely completion of any milestone.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  
  (b) in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII; and  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Law and Client’s reasonable written security and compliance requirements communicated to Vendor and accepted in writing.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Vendor’s performance is expressly conditioned on Client’s timely performance of the following obligations, without limitation:

  2.6.1 Providing Vendor with accurate, complete, and up-to-date information reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;

  2.6.2 Ensuring that Client’s internal systems, networks, and endpoints that connect to the Services meet minimum requirements specified by Vendor and are properly maintained;

  2.6.3 Designating qualified personnel as primary and secondary points of contact for Incident reporting, approvals, and escalation;

  2.6.4 Obtaining and maintaining all necessary licences and rights to use any software or content that Client requests Vendor to host or manage;

  2.6.5 Notifying Vendor promptly of any suspected security incident, data breach, or material change in Client’s environment that may impact the Services.

Vendor shall have no liability for any failure or delay in the Services to the extent caused by Client’s breach of its obligations under this Agreement, including Section 2.6.

2.7 **Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly included in an SOW, the following are excluded from the Services:

  (a) On-site support at Client’s premises;  
  (b) End-user device support (e.g., desktops, laptops, mobile devices);  
  (c) Development of custom software or applications;  
  (d) Support for third-party software not listed in the applicable SOW;  
  (e) Data modelling or business analytics services beyond standard reporting.

Any such excluded services may be provided by Vendor, subject to mutual written agreement and additional fees.

2.8 **Changes to Scope.**  
Any expansion, reduction, or material modification of the scope of Services shall be governed by the change management process in Article XIII and, where appropriate, reflected in an amended or new SOW.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees.**  
Client shall pay Vendor the fees for the Services as set forth in this Agreement and each applicable SOW. The aggregate contract value for the initial term of this Agreement is USD $1,617,072 (one million six hundred seventeen thousand seventy-two dollars), exclusive of any applicable taxes, as further allocated and detailed in the SOWs.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Parties agree that fees may consist of one or more of the following components:

  3.2.1 **Recurring Service Fees.** Monthly or annual recurring fees for hosting, infrastructure, monitoring, and support, calculated based on agreed resource units (e.g., number of virtual machines, storage, bandwidth, number of Managed Applications, and support tier).

  3.2.2 **One-Time Implementation Fees.** Fixed or time-and-materials fees for onboarding, migration, and initial configuration.

  3.2.3 **Professional Services Fees.** Time-and-materials fees for additional consulting, integration, or project-based work, based on Vendor’s then-current standard rate card unless otherwise agreed in the relevant SOW.

  3.2.4 **Pass-Through Costs.** Reasonable and documented third-party costs incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf (such as software licence fees, domain registration, or dedicated connectivity), provided Client has approved such costs in advance in writing.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  (a) Recurring Service Fees shall be invoiced monthly in advance;  
  (b) One-Time Implementation Fees shall be invoiced sixty percent (60%) upon project commencement and forty percent (40%) upon substantial completion of onboarding;  
  (c) Professional Services Fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on actual hours incurred; and  
  (d) Pass-Through Costs shall be invoiced in arrears as incurred.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
All undisputed amounts due under this Agreement shall be paid by Client within sixty (60) calendar days from the date of invoice (**“Net 60”**). Payments shall be made in United States dollars (USD) by bank transfer or other methods approved by Vendor in writing. Time of payment is of the essence.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, from the due date until paid in full. In addition, Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend provision of the Services if Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after the due date. Suspension shall not relieve Client of its payment obligations.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall:

  (a) pay the undisputed portion by the due date; and  
  (b) notify Vendor in writing within thirty (30) days of the invoice date, specifying the nature and amount of the dispute.

The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Failure by Client to dispute an invoice in accordance with this Section 3.6 shall constitute acceptance of such invoice.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All fees are exclusive of any sales, value-added, goods and services, use, withholding or other taxes, duties, levies, or similar governmental assessments (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Client shall be responsible for all Taxes imposed on the Services, other than taxes based on Vendor’s net income. If Client is required by law to withhold Taxes from any payment, Client shall promptly provide Vendor with official receipts issued by the appropriate taxing authority.

3.8 **Expenses.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved, documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with on-site or project activities requested by Client (such as travel, accommodation, and subsistence), in accordance with Vendor’s travel and expense policy provided to Client upon request.

3.9 **Price Adjustments.**  
Vendor may adjust recurring fees once per calendar year, provided that (a) any increase shall not exceed five percent (5%) of the previous year’s recurring fees for the same Services, and (b) Vendor provides at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice. Any increase exceeding 5% shall require written agreement by Client.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until May 23, 2027 (the **“Initial Term”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods (each, a **“Renewal Term”**) unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred eighty (180) days before the end of the then-current term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, upon at least one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Vendor. Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Client. In the event of such termination:

  (a) Client shall remain liable for all fees accrued up to the effective date of termination; and  
  (b) unless otherwise agreed, Client shall also pay to Vendor an early termination fee equal to fifty percent (50%) of the remaining recurring fees that would have become due for the remainder of the Initial Term or current Renewal Term for the terminated Services, reflecting Vendor’s pricing concessions and resource commitments.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail (or such longer cure period as may be reasonably required if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days and the breaching Party has commenced and diligently continues to cure); or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into liquidation, receivership, or administration, or any analogous event occurs under Applicable Law.

4.5 **Suspension.**  
In addition to any other rights, Vendor may, upon written notice to Client, suspend provision of all or part of the Services if:

  (a) Client fails to pay any undisputed amount when due and does not cure within the period specified in Section 3.5;  
  (b) Vendor reasonably determines that Client’s use of the Services poses a material security risk, may subject Vendor or any third party to liability, or may be fraudulent or illegal; or  
  (c) Vendor is required to do so by Applicable Law or a governmental authority.

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to limit the scope and duration of any suspension.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) All SOWs shall automatically terminate, unless otherwise agreed;  
  (b) Client shall immediately cease use of the Services and Vendor’s materials, except as reasonably necessary to complete transition under Section 4.7;  
  (c) All amounts owed by Client to Vendor under this Agreement shall become immediately due and payable, including any early termination fees, if applicable; and  
  (d) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon Client’s request made prior to the effective date of termination or non-renewal, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days after termination or expiration, to facilitate the orderly transfer of Client Data and, where feasible, configurations to Client or a third-party service provider. Such assistance shall be provided at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates, unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach, in which case Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance at no additional recurring service fee (excluding reimbursable expenses).

4.8 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections 3.5, 3.7, 4.3(b), 4.6, 4.7, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement.

5.7 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, except that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
Vendor shall make the production environment for the Services available with **99.9% Uptime** in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Downtime and force majeure as described in this Agreement.

12.2 **Uptime Calculation.**  
Uptime shall be calculated as follows:

> Uptime % = (Total Minutes in Month – Unscheduled Downtime) / (Total Minutes in Month – Scheduled Downtime) × 100

“Unscheduled Downtime” means any time during which the production environment is unavailable due to Vendor’s failure not falling within Scheduled Downtime or a Force Majeure Event.

12.3 **Redundancy and Disaster Recovery.**  
Vendor shall maintain redundant infrastructure and backup procedures designed to achieve a recovery time objective (RTO) of four (4) hours and a recovery point objective (RPO) of twenty-four (24) hours for critical systems, unless otherwise specified in an SOW. Vendor shall conduct at least one disaster recovery test per year and share a summary report with Client upon request.

12.4 **Scheduled Downtime.**  
Vendor may schedule maintenance that may cause limited or no availability of the Services. Vendor shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for planned maintenance expected to cause more than fifteen (15) minutes of downtime. Scheduled Downtime shall typically occur outside of Client’s business hours (as communicated to Vendor), where reasonably practicable.

12.5 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
Vendor shall respond to and resolve Incidents in accordance with the following target times:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of Service or severe impact on mission-critical operations.  
  - Initial response: within 30 minutes (24x7).  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours.

- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation of Service or significant impact on key business operations.  
  - Initial response: within 1 hour (24x7).  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 8 hours.

- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Moderate impact on non-critical operations; workaround available.  
  - Initial response: within 4 Business Hours.  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 2 Business Days.

- **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor impact, requests for information or standard service requests.  
  - Initial response: within 1 Business Day.  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 5 Business Days.

12.6 **Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to Service Credits as follows:

- Uptime 99.0% – 99.89%: 5% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service;  
- Uptime 98.0% – 98.99%: 10% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service;  
- Uptime below 98.0%: 15% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service.

Service Credits:

  (a) shall be requested by Client in writing within thirty (30) days after the month in which the Service Level failure occurred;  
  (b) shall be applied as a credit against future invoices; and  
  (c) shall not entitle Client to any refund or cash payment.

12.7 **Exclusive SLA Remedies.**  
Except for any termination rights expressly provided in this Agreement and indemnification obligations in Article VIII, Service Credits shall be the sole and exclusive remedy for Service Level failures, including Uptime and Incident response/resolution targets.

14.4 **Litigation.**

Nothing in this Article XIV shall prevent either Party from seeking interim or injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, riots, civil unrest, strikes or other labour disputes (excluding those of the affected Party’s own workforce), epidemics or pandemics, government actions, embargoes, failures of utility services or telecommunications networks, or widespread Internet outages.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  
  (c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of developments.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are rendered impossible or impracticable by the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party complies with Section 15.2.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
