This PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT (the “**Agreement**” or this “**Partnership Agreement**”), bearing reference number **AGR-PHO-202510-036**, is made and entered into as of **17 October 2025** (the “**Effective Date**”), by and between:

and

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Contoso is engaged in the business of manufacturing and distributing advanced consumer electronics and related digital solutions and desires to enhance its operational efficiency, supply chain visibility, and technology-enabled services to its customers;

WHEREAS, Phoenix is engaged in the business of providing integrated technology, consulting, implementation, and managed services, including but not limited to systems integration, cloud-based platforms, analytics, and support services to corporate clients;

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Phoenix shall provide to Contoso, on a non-exclusive basis, comprehensive technology and consulting services designed to support Contoso’s operational, technological, and strategic objectives. Such Services shall include, without limitation:

  2.1.1 Business process analysis and consulting related to Contoso’s supply chain, logistics, inventory management, and customer engagement platforms;

  2.1.2 Design, configuration, and implementation of an integrated technology platform, including but not limited to enterprise resource planning (ERP) modules, customer relationship management (CRM) components, and data analytics tools;

  2.1.3 System integration services, including integration with Contoso’s existing internal systems, third-party platforms, and external service providers, utilizing appropriate APIs, middleware, and secure communication protocols;

  2.1.4 Development and delivery of customized reports, dashboards, and analytics outputs tailored to Contoso’s operational and management requirements;

  2.1.5 Data migration services, including mapping, cleansing, transformation, and validation of Contoso’s legacy data into the new platform;

  2.1.6 Training services for Contoso’s personnel, including the preparation and delivery of training materials, workshops, and knowledge transfer sessions;

  2.1.7 Ongoing maintenance and support services, including incident management, bug fixes, minor enhancements, updates, and upgrades, subject to the Service Levels set forth in Article XII; and

  2.1.8 Any other related or ancillary services agreed by the Parties in an SOW.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
Phoenix shall create, develop, and deliver to Contoso various Deliverables in connection with the Services. Without limitation, such Deliverables may include:

  2.2.1 Detailed project plan(s) outlining phases, timelines, milestones, dependencies, and resource allocations;

  2.2.2 Functional and technical design documents describing business requirements, system architecture, integration points, and configuration specifications;

  2.2.3 Configured system environments (development, testing, staging, and production) including customized workflows, business rules, and user interfaces;

  2.2.4 Integration components, including connectors, adapters, configuration scripts, and documentation for integration to and from Contoso’s existing systems;

  2.2.5 Data migration scripts, mapping files, and validation reports;

  2.2.6 User manuals, standard operating procedures (SOPs), and administration guides;

  2.2.7 Training materials (presentations, handouts, e-learning content) and training session records; and

  2.2.8 Periodic performance, status, and analytics reports.

All Deliverables shall be provided in English and in such electronic or physical format as agreed in the applicable SOW.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Phoenix shall perform the Services:

  2.3.1 In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with the highest industry standards applicable to providers of similar services;

  2.3.2 With due skill, care, and diligence, and in compliance with all Applicable Laws;

  2.3.3 In accordance with the timelines, milestones, and Service Levels set forth in this Agreement and any SOW; and

  2.3.4 Using personnel who are appropriately qualified, experienced, and trained for the tasks assigned.

2.4 **Project Governance and Reporting.**  

  2.4.1 Each Party shall appoint a primary project manager who shall serve as the principal point of contact for all operational and technical matters relating to the Services.

  2.4.2 Phoenix shall provide to Contoso, at least monthly (or at such other frequency as specified in an SOW), written status reports summarizing progress, milestones achieved, key issues, risk assessments, and proposed mitigation measures.

  2.4.3 The Parties shall hold regular project review meetings (in person or via teleconference or videoconference) to review progress, discuss issues, and agree on next steps and action items.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

  2.5.1 Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the timelines and milestones specified in the project plan and SOW(s). The Parties acknowledge that certain milestones may be dependent upon Contoso’s timely performance of its obligations as described in Section 2.6.

  2.5.2 If Phoenix reasonably anticipates that any milestone or delivery date will not be met, Phoenix shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, providing details of the delay, its causes, and a proposed revised schedule. The Parties shall in good faith discuss any necessary adjustments and document them via a Change Order in accordance with Article XIII.

2.6 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Phoenix’s ability to perform the Services in accordance with this Agreement is dependent upon Contoso’s timely cooperation and performance. Accordingly, Contoso shall:

  2.6.1 Provide Phoenix with timely access to accurate and complete information, documentation, data, and materials reasonably required for the performance of the Services;

  2.6.2 Make available suitable personnel, including subject matter experts and decision-makers, to provide direction, approvals, and feedback;

  2.6.3 Provide access, during normal Business Hours or as otherwise agreed, to relevant premises, systems, networks, and equipment, subject to Contoso’s security policies;

  2.6.4 Obtain any necessary third-party consents or licenses required to allow Phoenix to interface with or use third-party systems or data in connection with the Services; and

  2.6.5 Review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within the timelines specified in the relevant SOW, it being agreed that any delays caused by Contoso’s failure to provide timely feedback or approvals may entitle Phoenix to schedule extensions and/or additional fees via a Change Order.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  2.7.1 Each Deliverable shall be subject to an acceptance process as set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise agreed, Contoso shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery of any Deliverable (the “Review Period”) to review and either:  
  (a) provide written acceptance; or  
  (b) provide written notice of any material non-conformity with the specifications.

  2.7.2 If Contoso fails to provide such notice within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the expiry of the Review Period.

  2.7.3 In the event Contoso rejects a Deliverable due to material non-conformity, Phoenix shall, at no additional charge, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable within a mutually agreed period. The Deliverable shall then be subject to a further Review Period.

  2.7.4 Acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of a Deliverable shall not waive Contoso’s rights with respect to latent defects or Phoenix’s continuing obligations under this Agreement.

2.8 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
Unless expressly stated otherwise in an SOW, nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to prevent either Party from entering into similar agreements or relationships with any third party, subject to the confidentiality obligations in Article V.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value and Fee Structure.**  
The Parties acknowledge and agree that the aggregate maximum value of this Agreement (the “Contract Value”) is **USD $2,070,905**. Unless otherwise provided in an SOW, the Contract Value shall be comprised of:

  3.1.1 Fixed project implementation fees for design, configuration, integration, and initial deployment;

  3.1.2 Time-and-materials fees for additional consulting, customization, and enhancements requested by Contoso via Change Orders;

  3.1.3 Annual or periodic support and maintenance fees; and

  3.1.4 Reimbursable expenses, if any, incurred by Phoenix in the course of performing the Services.

3.2 **Fee Schedule and Milestones.**  
A detailed fee schedule, including payment milestones, shall be specified in the applicable SOW(s). By way of example, a typical fee schedule may include:

  3.2.1 Ten percent (10%) of the applicable project fees upon execution of the SOW;

  3.2.2 Twenty percent (20%) upon completion of design and delivery of the approved solution design document;

  3.2.3 Thirty percent (30%) upon completion of system configuration and successful completion of user acceptance testing;

  3.2.4 Twenty percent (20%) upon Go-Live of the solution in production; and

  3.2.5 Twenty percent (20%) upon completion of a post-implementation support period (e.g., 60 days following Go-Live).

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
Phoenix shall invoice Contoso in accordance with the applicable payment milestones, Service periods, and fee schedule set forth in the relevant SOW. Each invoice shall:

  3.3.1 Reference this Agreement and the relevant SOW;

  3.3.2 Itemize the Services performed, Deliverables delivered, and any reimbursable expenses;

  3.3.3 Indicate the period to which the invoice relates; and

  3.3.4 Be sent to the billing contact and address notified by Contoso in writing.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
All undisputed amounts due under Phoenix’s invoices shall be paid by Contoso within **thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (“**Net 30**”), by electronic bank transfer or such other method as the Parties may agree in writing.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
If Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amount when due:

  3.5.1 Phoenix may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate of one percent (1%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment; and

  3.5.2 Phoenix may, upon written notice to Contoso and after providing a ten (10) Business Day cure period, suspend performance of the Services if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after the due date. Phoenix’s suspension rights shall not apply to amounts reasonably disputed by Contoso in good faith in accordance with Section 3.7.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All fees and charges are exclusive of any applicable taxes, including but not limited to value-added tax (VAT), goods and services tax (GST), sales tax, withholding tax, or similar taxes (“Taxes”). Contoso shall be responsible for payment of all such Taxes, except for taxes based solely on Phoenix’s income, property, or employment of personnel. If Contoso is required to withhold any Taxes from payments due to Phoenix under Applicable Law, Contoso shall:

  3.6.1 Withhold only the required amount;  
  3.6.2 Pay such amount to the relevant tax authority; and  
  3.6.3 Provide Phoenix with an official receipt or other documentation evidencing such payment, to allow Phoenix to claim any available tax credits or refunds.

3.7 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall:

  3.7.1 Notify Phoenix in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of the invoice date, specifying the reasons for the dispute and the amount in dispute; and

  3.7.2 Pay any undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.4.

The Parties shall promptly confer in good faith to resolve any such dispute. Any amounts determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days after resolution.

3.8 **Expenses.**  
Subject to any limitations specified in an SOW, Phoenix shall be entitled to reimbursement of reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred in the performance of the Services (such as travel, accommodation, and subsistence expenses), provided that:

  3.8.1 Such expenses are supported by appropriate receipts or documentation; and

  3.8.2 Phoenix complies with Contoso’s travel and expense policy, as communicated to Phoenix from time to time.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **23 July 2029** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “Term”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing, no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, to renew or extend this Agreement for an additional term, on such terms and conditions as the Parties may then agree.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Because the Parties have designated the risk level of this engagement as **Low**, the Parties agree to the following client-friendly termination provisions:

  4.3.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience at any time by providing Phoenix at least **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice.

  4.3.2 Phoenix may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing Contoso at least **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  4.4.1 Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or an SOW and, where such breach is capable of remedy, fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; or

  4.4.2 Becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into any arrangement or composition with its creditors, has a receiver or administrator appointed over its assets, or is the subject of any winding up, liquidation, or similar proceeding (other than for the purpose of a solvent restructuring not prejudicial to creditors).

4.5 **Termination for Regulatory or Legal Reasons.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice if continued performance would contravene Applicable Law or a binding order of a competent governmental or regulatory authority, provided such Party provides reasonable detail of the legal basis for termination.

4.6 **Effects of Termination.**  

  4.6.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:
  - (a) Phoenix shall cease performance of the Services, except as may be reasonably necessary to wind down activities;  
  - (b) Contoso shall pay Phoenix for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered (and accepted or deemed accepted) up to the effective date of termination, plus any reasonable and documented wind-down costs agreed in advance; and  
  - (c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5.

  4.6.2 If Contoso terminates for convenience under Section 4.3.1, Contoso shall pay Phoenix for:  
  - (a) all Services duly performed and Deliverables accepted or deemed accepted up to the termination date; and  
  - (b) any non-cancellable commitments or third-party charges that Phoenix has reasonably and demonstrably incurred specifically for Contoso’s project, provided Phoenix uses reasonable efforts to mitigate such costs.

  4.6.3 If Contoso terminates for cause under Section 4.4 as a result of Phoenix’s material breach, Contoso shall have no obligation to pay for any Services or Deliverables not yet performed or delivered, and Phoenix shall promptly refund any prepaid amounts on a pro rata basis corresponding to unperformed Services.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Any provisions which by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination of this Agreement, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), shall so survive.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Availability.**  
For any hosted or managed services provided by Phoenix under this Agreement, Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such services achieve a monthly uptime percentage of at least **99.9%** (excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Permitted Downtime as defined below).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance and Permitted Downtime.**  

  12.2.1 “Scheduled Maintenance” means planned downtime for maintenance, upgrades, or other changes, for which Phoenix has provided Contoso with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice and which is conducted during off-peak hours where practicable.

  12.2.2 “Permitted Downtime” means downtime caused by:  
  - (a) Scheduled Maintenance (provided it does not exceed four (4) hours per month);  
  - (b) Force Majeure Events;  
  - (c) Acts or omissions of Contoso or its vendors; or  
  - (d) Failures of third-party services or networks outside Phoenix’s reasonable control.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution.**  
Phoenix shall comply with the following response and resolution times for incidents affecting the services:

  12.3.1 **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete service outage or major impact on business-critical functions with no workaround available.  
  - Response time: within one (1) hour of notification.  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within four (4) hours.

  12.3.2 **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation of service or impact on important business functions, with limited workaround.  
  - Response time: within four (4) hours of notification.  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within one (1) Business Day.

  12.3.3 **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Partial degradation or minor impact with acceptable workaround.  
  - Response time: within one (1) Business Day.  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within five (5) Business Days.

  12.3.4 **Severity 4 (Low)** – Non-critical issues, minor bugs, cosmetic issues, or general inquiries.  
  - Response time: within two (2) Business Days.  
  - Target resolution: best efforts; may be addressed in next scheduled release.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If Phoenix fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 for a given calendar month (excluding Permitted Downtime), Contoso shall be entitled to a service credit as follows:

  12.4.1 Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly fees for the affected service;

  12.4.2 Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly fees for the affected service;

  12.4.3 Uptime below 98.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly fees for the affected service.

Service credits shall be applied as a credit against future invoices and shall constitute Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitments.

12.5 **Reporting.**  
Phoenix shall provide Contoso, upon request, with monthly reports detailing service availability, incident statistics, and performance against the Service Levels.
