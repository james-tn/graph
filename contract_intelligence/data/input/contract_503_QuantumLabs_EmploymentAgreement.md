## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Quantum Labs shall provide to the Company comprehensive Employment Services and related professional services as described in this Article II (the “**Services**”). The Parties acknowledge that the nature of the engagement is to provide specialized technical, analytical, and managerial personnel on a project-based and time-and-materials basis, supplemented by defined deliverables and performance obligations.

2.2 **Assigned Personnel and Roles.**

2.2.1 Quantum Labs shall assign qualified personnel (the “**Assigned Personnel**”) to perform Services for the Company, which may include, without limitation:  
  (a) Senior Data Scientists;  
  (b) Machine Learning Engineers;  
  (c) Cloud Infrastructure Engineers;  
  (d) Software Developers;  
  (e) Business Analysts;  
  (f) Project Managers; and  
  (g) Quality Assurance Specialists.

2.2.2 The initial Assigned Personnel, their roles, and indicative allocation (e.g., full-time equivalent commitment) shall be mutually agreed in writing and may be attached as a schedule or statement of work. Quantum Labs shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that Assigned Personnel have the necessary skills and experience to perform the Services in accordance with this Agreement.

2.2.3 The Company shall have the right to reasonably request the removal or replacement of any Assigned Personnel on the grounds of lack of performance, misconduct, or failure to meet required qualifications. Upon such request, Quantum Labs shall, within a commercially reasonable period (typically not to exceed ten (10) Business Days), remove such Assigned Personnel from the Project and propose one or more suitable replacements.

2.3 **Nature of Services.**  

The Services to be performed by Quantum Labs shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **Technical and Analytical Services.** Development of advanced data analytics models, machine learning pipelines, and AI-driven features to support the Company’s enterprise products and internal decision-making processes.

  (b) **Software Design and Development.** Design, implementation, testing, and deployment of software components, microservices, APIs, integrations, and user interface elements, as directed by the Company’s product and engineering teams.

  (c) **Cloud Infrastructure & DevOps.** Design, configuration, and maintenance of cloud-based infrastructure (e.g., on Microsoft Azure, AWS, or similar platforms) to support staging, testing, and production environments, including CI/CD pipelines, monitoring, logging, and scalability support.

  (d) **Project Management & Business Analysis.** Provision of project management services, including planning, scheduling, coordinating sprints or work streams, and performing requirements elicitation, analysis, documentation, and stakeholder communication.

  (e) **Quality Assurance and Testing.** Development and execution of test plans, automated tests, performance and security testing, defect tracking, and supporting user acceptance testing activities.

  (f) **Knowledge Transfer and Training.** Preparation of documentation, conduct of workshops, and transfer of know-how to Company personnel to ensure continuity of operations and use of Deliverables.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  

2.4.1 Quantum Labs shall provide Deliverables as reasonably requested by the Company and as specified in applicable statements of work. Deliverables may include, but are not limited to:

  (a) Technical design documents, architecture diagrams, and system specifications;  
  (b) Source code repositories, scripts, models, and configuration files;  
  (c) Data models, dashboards, and analytical reports;  
  (d) Test plans, test cases, test results, and quality assurance reports;  
  (e) Project status reports, milestone reports, and risk assessments;  
  (f) User manuals, support guides, and training materials.

2.4.2 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Deliverables shall be provided in electronic format using widely accepted industry-standard tools and file formats (e.g., PDF, DOCX, XLSX, PPTX, or equivalent). Source code shall be provided via mutually agreed secure repositories (e.g., Git-based platforms) with appropriate access controls.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**

2.5.1 Quantum Labs shall perform the Services:  
  (a) in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  
  (b) using personnel with the requisite expertise, training, and qualifications;  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Laws, including but not limited to data protection and export control laws; and  
  (d) in accordance with reasonable instructions, policies, and procedures of the Company, including any security, facility, and code of conduct policies communicated in writing to Quantum Labs.

2.5.2 Quantum Labs shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet any timelines, milestones, or schedules agreed in writing between the Parties. If Quantum Labs reasonably believes that it will be unable to meet a deadline due to circumstances not attributable to the Company, it shall promptly notify the Company in writing, explaining the reasons for the delay and proposing a revised timeline.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.**

2.6.1 The Company shall:  
  (a) Provide Quantum Labs and Assigned Personnel with reasonable access to the Company’s systems, facilities, documentation, and personnel as necessary for the performance of the Services;  
  (b) Provide timely feedback, approvals, and decisions;  
  (c) Designate a primary point of contact responsible for coordinating with Quantum Labs;  
  (d) Ensure that its own employees cooperate reasonably with Assigned Personnel and do not interfere with or hinder the performance of the Services.

2.6.2 The Company acknowledges that Quantum Labs’ ability to perform the Services and meet timelines is dependent upon the Company fulfilling its responsibilities. Quantum Labs shall not be in breach of this Agreement to the extent its failure to perform is caused by the Company’s delay or failure to meet its obligations under this Agreement.

2.7 **Location and Hours.**

2.7.1 The Services may be performed remotely, on the Company’s premises, on Quantum Labs’ premises, or at other locations agreed by the Parties.

2.7.2 Assigned Personnel shall generally perform Services during the Company’s normal business hours for the relevant location, unless otherwise agreed. Quantum Labs will reasonably accommodate the Company’s requests for extended or off-hours support, which may be subject to additional fees as set forth in **Article III** or a Change Order.

2.8 **Medium Risk and Balanced Allocation.**  

Given the **Medium** Risk Level, the Parties acknowledge that this Agreement is structured to achieve a balanced allocation of responsibilities, liabilities, and protections, as reflected in **Article VIII (Indemnification)** and **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)**. The Parties agree to cooperate in good faith to manage and mitigate risks associated with the Project.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  

The aggregate value of this Agreement, inclusive of all Services and Deliverables but exclusive of reimbursable expenses and applicable taxes, shall not exceed **US$604,639** (the “**Contract Value**”), unless increased by a duly executed Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII**.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**

3.2.1 The Parties agree to a blended fee structure comprising:

  (a) **Time-and-Materials Fees:**  
  For Assigned Personnel billed on an hourly or daily basis at the rate card attached as a schedule (the “Rate Card”). In the absence of a schedule, example rates may include:
   - Senior Data Scientist: US$180/hour  
   - Machine Learning Engineer: US$160/hour  
   - Software Developer: US$140/hour  
   - Project Manager: US$150/hour  

  (b) **Fixed-Fee Components:**  
  Certain well-defined Deliverables (e.g., completion of a specific prototype, implementation of a defined analytics dashboard) may be charged on a fixed-fee basis, as set forth in applicable statements of work.

3.2.2 Quantum Labs shall not be entitled to exceed the Contract Value without the Company’s prior written approval, which shall be documented in a Change Order.

3.3 **Payment Terms (Net 30).**

3.3.1 Quantum Labs shall issue invoices monthly in arrears, detailing:  
  (a) hours worked by each Assigned Personnel and applicable rates;  
  (b) any fixed-fee milestones achieved;  
  (c) approved expenses; and  
  (d) applicable taxes, if any.

3.3.2 The Company shall pay undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) calendar days** of receipt of the invoice (“**Net 30**”). Invoices shall be sent electronically to the Company’s designated billing contact.

3.3.3 If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice, it shall notify Quantum Labs in writing within fifteen (15) calendar days of receipt, specifying the disputed amounts and reasons. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any such disputes. The Company shall timely pay all undisputed portions of an invoice.

3.4 **Late Payment.**

3.4.1 Any undisputed invoiced amount not paid by its due date shall accrue interest at a rate of the lesser of:  
  (a) one percent (1%) per month, or  
  (b) the maximum rate permitted under Applicable Law,  
from the date due until the date paid.

3.4.2 Quantum Labs shall not suspend or withhold Services solely due to late payment by the Company unless:  
  (a) the Company is more than sixty (60) days past due on an undisputed amount; and  
  (b) Quantum Labs has provided at least ten (10) Business Days’ written notice of its intent to suspend;  
and provided further that suspension is a last resort and that Quantum Labs will cooperate in good faith to avoid material disruption to the Company.

3.5 **Taxes.**

3.5.1 The fees set forth in this Agreement are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. The Company shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes based on Quantum Labs’ net income) that are legally imposed on the Services.

3.5.2 Quantum Labs shall itemize any applicable taxes on its invoices and shall cooperate reasonably with the Company in obtaining tax exemptions or refunds where applicable.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**

3.6.1 Quantum Labs shall be responsible for its own general overhead and administrative expenses, including but not limited to salaries, benefits, office rent, ordinary travel within the Assigned Personnel’s home city, and equipment required to perform the Services.

3.6.2 The Company shall reimburse Quantum Labs for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Services, including but not limited to:
  (a) travel and lodging for trips requested by the Company;  
  (b) specialized software licenses or tools requested by the Company and not otherwise part of Quantum Labs’ standard tooling;  
  (c) other expenses expressly authorized in writing by the Company.

3.6.3 Reimbursable expenses shall be documented with receipts or appropriate supporting documentation, and invoiced at actual cost without markup, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **January 04, 2029** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  

The Term may be extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties, executed not later than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date. Any renewal shall specify the renewed term, revised Contract Value, and any modifications to the Services.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any statement of work, in whole or in part, for convenience, without cause, by providing at least **forty-five (45) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. This notice period represents a balanced approach consistent with the Medium Risk Level.

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Company, the Company shall pay Quantum Labs for:
  (a) Services actually performed up to the effective date of termination;  
  (b) non-cancellable commitments entered into by Quantum Labs with the Company’s written approval; and  
  (c) reasonable demobilization efforts not to exceed ten percent (10%) of the remaining Contract Value for the affected scope, unless otherwise agreed.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:
  (a) materially breaches this Agreement, and such breach remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice specifying the breach; or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a petition filed for its bankruptcy or reorganization which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.4.2 Material breach by Quantum Labs shall include, without limitation:
  (a) repeated failure to meet material Service Levels;  
  (b) breach of confidentiality obligations under **Article V**; or  
  (c) material non-compliance with Applicable Law in the performance of Services.

4.4.3 Material breach by the Company shall include, without limitation:
  (a) failure to pay undisputed amounts due under this Agreement within sixty (60) days after the due date; or  
  (b) unauthorized use of Quantum Labs’ Background IP in violation of **Article VI**.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:
  (a) Quantum Labs shall promptly cease performance of the Services, except as necessary to wind down activities;  
  (b) Quantum Labs shall deliver to the Company any completed Deliverables and any work-in-progress reasonably capable of being delivered;  
  (c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5**; and  
  (d) the Company shall pay Quantum Labs all amounts due for Services performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination.

4.5.2 Termination shall not affect any rights or obligations of either Party that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, nor any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination, including, without limitation, **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  

“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, visually, in writing, electronically, or in any other form, including but not limited to: business plans, financial information, technical data, product designs, trade secrets, know-how, software, source code, algorithms, client lists, pricing, personnel information, and any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information or the circumstances of disclosure.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
  (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without restriction on use or disclosure;  
  (c) is received from a third party who did not acquire or disclose such information in violation of any legal or contractual obligation; or  
  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  
  (b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted under this Agreement; and  
  (c) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of like nature, but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, Affiliates, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less protective than those set forth herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or a valid court or governmental order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted:  
  (a) promptly notify the Disclosing Party;  
  (b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and  
  (c) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, except that:  
  (a) one archival copy may be retained solely for legal or compliance purposes; and  
  (b) Confidential Information that is automatically stored in routine backup systems may be retained until such backups are overwritten, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  

The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement and continue for a period of **five (5) years** thereafter, except that trade secrets shall be kept confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets under Applicable Law.

  (a) **Critical Issues** (system down, severe production impact): response within 1 hour;  
  (b) **High Priority Issues** (significant degradation, major feature impacted): response within 4 hours;  
  (c) **Medium Priority Issues** (limited impact, workaround available): response within 1 Business Day;  
  (d) **Low Priority Issues** (minor issues, cosmetic defects): response within 3 Business Days.

12.2.2 Resolution times shall be agreed on a case-by-case basis, taking into account the nature and complexity of the issue. Quantum Labs shall keep the Company informed of progress and expected time to resolution.

12.3 **SLA Credits.**

12.3.1 If Quantum Labs fails to meet the uptime commitment set forth in **Section 12.1** in any given month (other than due to the Company’s acts or omissions or a Force Majeure Event), the Company may be eligible for service credits as follows:

  (a) Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly fees for the affected services;  
  (b) Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly fees for the affected services;  
  (c) Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly fees for the affected services.

12.3.2 Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service credits shall constitute the Company’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet uptime Service Levels, subject to Quantum Labs’ continuing obligations under this Agreement.

Quantum Labs shall provide the Company with reasonable access to service performance metrics and reports demonstrating uptime, response times, and incident statistics, upon request and at least quarterly during the Term.
