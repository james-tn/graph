## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company desires to engage the Vendor to provide certain professional employment-related, staffing, managed workforce, consulting, and related services on an outsourced basis, including the provision, supervision, and management of designated personnel and deliverables in support of the Company’s operations;

WHEREAS, the Vendor is in the business of providing specialized professional services, including but not limited to staffing, managed services, consulting, program management, data and systems support, and related deliverables, and represents that it has the necessary expertise, personnel, and resources to perform such services;

WHEREAS, the Parties acknowledge that this Agreement involves a high degree of operational, financial, and legal risk and therefore are willing to accept certain aggressive terms that are more favorable to the Vendor, consistent with the risk designation of “High” for this engagement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises, covenants, representations, and warranties contained herein, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Engagement.** The Company hereby engages the Vendor, and the Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement. The Vendor shall perform the Services as an independent contractor, and nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an employer-employee relationship between the Company and any personnel engaged by the Vendor.

2.2 **Nature of Services.** The Services shall include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 **Staffing and Employment Services.**  
  (a) The Vendor shall recruit, vet, onboard, assign, supervise, and manage personnel with skills reasonably required by the Company for roles such as project management, business analysis, data analytics, IT operations support, administrative support, and other roles mutually agreed in writing.  
  (b) The Vendor shall be solely responsible for all employment-related obligations for its personnel, including payroll, benefits, tax withholding, workers’ compensation, unemployment insurance, and compliance with employment laws, except as otherwise expressly stated herein.  
  (c) The Vendor shall ensure that each assigned individual executes appropriate confidentiality obligations consistent with Article V before commencing work for the Company.

  2.2.2 **Managed Services.**  
  The Vendor shall provide managed services in one or more functional areas agreed by the Parties, which may include operational process management, service desk support, data processing, reporting services, and related operational tasks, with defined Service Levels as described in Article XII and any applicable SOW.

  2.2.3 **Consulting Services.**  
  The Vendor may provide consulting services in areas including process optimization, workflow design, reporting frameworks, analytics solutions, and implementation planning, as requested by the Company and agreed in an SOW.

2.3 **Statements of Work.**  

  2.3.1 From time to time, the Parties may enter into one or more SOWs under this Agreement. Each SOW shall specify, at a minimum: (a) the description of Services and Deliverables; (b) applicable timelines, milestones, and Service Levels; (c) applicable fees, rates, and payment terms consistent with Article III; and (d) any special terms and conditions applicable to such SOW.  

  2.3.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern and control, unless the SOW expressly states that a specific term is intended to supersede this Agreement and references the applicable section being superseded.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  

  2.4.1 The Vendor shall develop and deliver to the Company the Deliverables specified in Article II and in any applicable SOW. Deliverables may include periodic staffing reports, performance analytics, service-level reports, process documentation, training guides, or other specified materials.  

  2.4.2 Unless otherwise agreed in an SOW, Deliverables shall be provided in electronic form via secure means reasonably acceptable to both Parties.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

  2.5.1 **Professional Standards.** The Vendor shall perform the Services:  
  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner;  
  (b) with reasonable skill, care, and diligence consistent with industry standards for comparable services; and  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Law and applicable Company policies that are provided in writing to the Vendor and agreed by the Vendor.

  2.5.2 **Service Levels.** The Vendor shall meet or exceed the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and any applicable SOW. Failure to meet Service Levels shall give rise to the remedies specified in Article XII, subject to the limitations set forth in Article IX.

  2.5.3 **Personnel Qualifications.** The Vendor shall ensure that all personnel performing Services possess the necessary qualifications, experience, and training to perform their assigned tasks. The Vendor shall remove and replace, within a commercially reasonable period, any personnel who materially fail to perform their duties or who, in the reasonable written judgment of the Company, are unsuitable for continued assignment.

2.6 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

  2.6.1 The Parties acknowledge that the timely performance of the Services is a material term of this Agreement. Each SOW shall specify any critical milestones, delivery dates, or completion dates.  

  2.6.2 The Vendor shall promptly notify the Company in writing if the Vendor becomes aware of any event or circumstance that is likely to delay any material milestone or delivery date. The Parties shall use commercially reasonable efforts to agree in writing on any necessary adjustments to the timelines.

2.7 **Company Responsibilities.**  

  2.7.1 The Company shall provide the Vendor with reasonable access to information, facilities, systems, and personnel that are necessary for the Vendor to perform the Services, subject to the Company’s security, confidentiality, and compliance requirements.  

  2.7.2 The Company shall designate a project manager or primary contact to coordinate with the Vendor for purposes of providing instructions, approvals, and feedback.

  2.7.3 The Company acknowledges that delays or failures by the Company to fulfill its responsibilities may impact the Vendor’s performance. To the extent Vendor’s failure to meet a timeline or Service Level is directly and primarily caused by such delays or failures, the Parties shall adjust the timelines or Service Level measurements accordingly.

2.8 **High-Risk Acknowledgment.**  

  2.8.1 The Company acknowledges that the Services may be mission-critical or essential to certain Company operations, and that this Agreement is designated as involving a **High** level of risk.  

  2.8.2 In consideration of the pricing, the specialized nature of the Services, and the Vendor’s willingness to accept certain obligations, the Company agrees that this Agreement contains provisions that are more favorable to the Vendor than are typical for lower-risk engagements, including but not limited to termination provisions (Article IV), indemnification obligations (Article VIII), intellectual property ownership (Article VI), and liability (Article IX).

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total maximum aggregate amount payable by the Company to the Vendor for all Services and Deliverables under this Agreement shall not exceed **USD $1,670,646** (the **“Contract Value”**), unless increased by a written amendment or Change Order executed in accordance with Article XIII.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees for the Services shall be comprised of some or all of the following components, as specified in each SOW:

  3.2.1 **Base Staffing Fees.** An hourly, daily, or monthly rate for each category of personnel provided by the Vendor (e.g., project manager, senior analyst, junior analyst), as set forth in the applicable SOW.

  3.2.2 **Managed Service Fees.** Fixed monthly fees for managed services (e.g., service desk, back-office processing) based on agreed service capacity, transaction volume, or other metrics.

  3.2.3 **Project-Based Fees.** For discrete projects, a fixed fee or milestone-based fee schedule tied to completion of specified milestones or Deliverables.

  3.2.4 **Change Orders.** Adjustments to fees arising from approved Changes (as defined in Article XIII).

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  

  3.3.1 The Vendor shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears for Services performed and Deliverables accepted during the preceding period, unless otherwise specified in an SOW.  

  3.3.2 The Company shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** from the date of the Vendor’s invoice (**“Net 60”**).  

  3.3.3 Invoices shall reference this Agreement and any applicable SOW and shall provide reasonable detail regarding the Services performed, personnel utilized, hours or units charged, and any expenses, consistent with the fee structure.

3.4 **Late Payments.**  

  3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full.  

  3.4.2 If the Company fails to pay undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of non-payment from the Vendor, the Vendor may, in addition to any other rights, suspend performance of the Services (in whole or in part) until such amounts are paid.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice, the Company shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the basis for the dispute. The Company shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the disputed portion. Any resolution may be reflected in a future invoice or credit.

3.6 **Expenses.**  

  3.6.1 The Company shall reimburse the Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred by the Vendor in performing the Services (e.g., travel, lodging, per diem, specialized materials), in accordance with the Company’s written expense policies as provided to the Vendor.  

  3.6.2 Expense reimbursements shall be invoiced with reasonable supporting documentation.

3.7 **Taxes.**  

  3.7.1 Fees do not include applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. The Company shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes based on the Vendor’s net income), and the Vendor may invoice the Company for such taxes as a separate line item.  

  3.7.2 The Vendor shall cooperate with reasonable requests for tax documentation to enable the Company to claim exemptions or credits.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **March 15, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, the Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods on the same terms and conditions, except for pricing and Contract Value, which may be adjusted by mutual written agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Vendor.  

  4.3.2 The Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to the Company.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (a) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they become due, files for bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course; or  
  (b) commits a material breach of this Agreement that (i) is incapable of cure, or (ii) if capable of cure, remains uncured thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.5 **Vendor Suspension.**  
Without limiting Section 3.4, the Vendor may suspend performance of Services for material, undisputed non-payment, or for a material breach by the Company that materially impairs the Vendor’s ability to perform, after giving at least fifteen (15) days’ prior written notice and an opportunity to cure.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.6.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:  
  (a) The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to cease Services in an orderly manner and, if requested by the Company, provide reasonable transition assistance at then-current rates, subject to a separate written agreement if extensive transition is required.  
  (b) The Company shall pay the Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted up to the effective date of termination, plus any reasonable, non-cancellable commitments incurred by the Vendor in connection with the Services.  

  4.6.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or obligations of either Party that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including payment obligations, indemnification, and confidentiality.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement: Articles III (to the extent of accrued fees), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X (to the extent of claims arising during the Term), XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including business plans, financial data, customer information, technical data, trade secrets, software, personnel information, and the terms of this Agreement.

5.2 **Obligations.**  

  5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
  (a) use Confidential Information only for purposes of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
  (b) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care; and  
  (c) disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know such information and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Article V.

5.3 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
  (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;  
  (b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  (c) is received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality; or  
  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law or court order, provided that (to the extent legally permitted) the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates in any reasonable effort to limit or protect such disclosure.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s election) all Confidential Information and certify in writing that it has done so, except for one archival copy that may be retained solely for legal and compliance purposes.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall begin on the Effective Date and continue for **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets, which shall be protected for so long as they remain trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding strikes of a Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, failure of utilities or telecommunications, or other causes beyond such Party’s reasonable control.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall provide prompt written notice to the other Party upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of such event.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the Party continues to use reasonable efforts to resume performance as soon as practicable. In the event a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Services on ten (10) days’ written notice.
