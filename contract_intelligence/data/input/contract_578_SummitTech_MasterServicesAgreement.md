2.3 **General Description of Services.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services may include, but are not limited to:

  2.3.1 **Consulting and Advisory Services.**  
  (a) Technology strategy and architecture consulting;  
  (b) Systems assessment, gap analysis, and solution design;  
  (c) Project and program management services, including coordination with third-party vendors.

  2.3.2 **Software Development and Integration.**  
  (a) Custom application design, development, testing, and deployment;  
  (b) Integration of new and existing applications with third-party systems and APIs;  
  (c) Development of interfaces, middleware, and data transformation scripts;  
  (d) Configuration and customization of commercial off-the-shelf software, as permitted by license terms.

  2.3.3 **Implementation and Migration Services.**  
  (a) Installation, configuration, and tuning of software and systems;  
  (b) Data migration, data cleansing, and data validation activities;  
  (c) Cut-over planning and go-live support.

  2.3.4 **Maintenance, Support, and Managed Services.**  
  (a) Ongoing application support, including incident management and problem resolution;  
  (b) Preventive maintenance, patching, and upgrades;  
  (c) Monitoring of system performance and availability;  
  (d) Service desk and end-user support as described in the applicable SOW.

  2.3.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
  (a) Development and delivery of training materials and workshops;  
  (b) Knowledge transfer to Company personnel, including documentation and shadowing sessions.

2.4 **Deliverables; Acceptance.**  
(a) Each SOW shall describe the Deliverables to be produced and shall set forth objective acceptance criteria. The Service Provider shall provide the Deliverables to the Company in accordance with the schedule specified in the applicable SOW.

(d) If the Company fails to provide written notice of acceptance or non-conformity within the applicable Acceptance Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the last day of the Acceptance Period. For avoidance of doubt, “acceptance” shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed by the Company.

(a) The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices and the performance standards set forth in the applicable SOW and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**.  

(b) The Service Provider shall assign personnel with appropriate skills, training, and experience to perform the Services.   (a) Providing access to relevant systems, environments, facilities, and personnel;

(a) The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and third-party fees, in accordance with the Company’s then-current travel and expense policies, if provided to the Service Provider in writing.  

(b) The Service Provider shall provide supporting documentation (e.g., receipts) for expenses upon request. The Company shall reimburse approved expenses within forty-five (45) days of receipt of an invoice.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated as provided herein, shall remain in effect until **January 19, 2030** (the “**Expiration Date**”).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Term may be extended by written agreement of the Parties executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date. Any renewal shall be on terms mutually agreed by the Parties and may include revised pricing, SLAs, or other terms.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
(a) The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including with respect to one or more SOWs), for convenience at any time by providing the Service Provider with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice.  

(b) The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing the Company with at least one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW for material breach by the other Party, upon written notice specifying the nature of the breach and providing thirty (30) days to cure such breach (the “**Cure Period**”). If the breach is not cured within the Cure Period, termination shall be effective upon written notice following the expiration of the Cure Period.  

(b) Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may terminate immediately upon written notice if: (i) the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed into receivership, or ceases doing business in the ordinary course; or (ii) the other Party commits a material breach of **Article V (Confidentiality)** or **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:

  (a) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and Deliverables provided through the effective date of termination, including any unpaid Fees, expenses, and approved committed costs, and in the case of termination by the Company for convenience, any reasonable wind-down costs actually incurred by the Service Provider.  

  (b) Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s direction, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**, subject to any retention rights and obligations in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.  

  (c) The Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate with the Company, at the Company’s expense, to effect an orderly transition of the Services to the Company or a third-party service provider, as further described in any applicable SOW.

4.6 **Survival.** Any provision of this Agreement which, by its nature, is intended to survive expiration or termination, including but not limited to **Articles III (with respect to accrued payment obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.** The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;  

  (b) Not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party, except (i) to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those set forth herein; or (ii) as otherwise permitted under this Agreement;  

  (c) Protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3 **Exceptions.** Confidential Information shall not include information that:

  (a) Is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  

  (b) Is lawfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;  

  (c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records; or  

  (d) Was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or, at the Disclosing Party’s option, securely destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain: (a) copies required to comply with legal or regulatory obligations; and (b) copies maintained in routine backup or archival systems, provided that such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.** The confidentiality obligations in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that any obligations relating to trade secrets and Personal Data shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **General.** This **Article XII** sets forth the baseline Service Levels applicable to any managed services, hosting, or support Services provided under this Agreement, unless otherwise specified in an SOW.

12.2 **Availability/Uptime Commitment.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that any production systems or applications hosted or managed by the Service Provider for the Company are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and force majeure events as defined in **Article XV**.  

(b) “Scheduled Maintenance” means planned maintenance windows mutually agreed by the Parties and occurring during low-utilization periods where reasonably practicable.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution.** The Service Provider shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve support incidents in accordance with the following target timeframes, unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical)** – System down or severe impact on core business operations:  
    - Response Time: within 1 hour  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours  

  - **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant degradation or partial outage:  
    - Response Time: within 4 hours  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 24 hours  

  - **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Non-critical functionality impaired:  
    - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

  - **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries:  
    - Response Time: within 2 Business Days  
    - Target Resolution on a best-efforts basis.

12.4 **SLA Credits / Remedies.**  
(a) If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.2 in a given calendar month, the Company may be eligible for **service credits** as follows:  

  - Uptime between 99.0% and 99.89%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Service;  
  - Uptime between 98.0% and 98.99%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring Fees;  
  - Uptime below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly recurring Fees.

(b) Service credits shall be applied as a credit against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash. The Company must request service credits in writing within thirty (30) days following the month in which the SLA breach occurred.  

(c) Service credits shall be the Company’s exclusive monetary remedy for SLA breaches, without limiting the Company’s other contractual remedies for uncured material breaches under **Article IV** and **Article XIV**, subject to **Article IX**.

12.5 **Exclusions.** SLA commitments shall not apply to performance or availability issues resulting from: (a) factors outside the Service Provider’s reasonable control (including Force Majeure Events); (b) any actions or inactions of the Company or third parties; (c) the Company’s failure to use supported configurations; or (d) Scheduled Maintenance.
