## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Engagement.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Contoso hereby engages Gamma, and Gamma hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described herein and in one or more SOWs executed pursuant to this Agreement. Each SOW shall form an integral part of this Agreement, and in the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states otherwise with reference to the specific provision being modified.

2.2 **Nature of Services.**  
The Services to be provided by Gamma may include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 Technology consulting and business requirements analysis to support Contoso’s enterprise solutions, including stakeholder interviews, system assessments, and solution design workshops;

  2.2.2 Software implementation and configuration services for specified platforms, including system setup, integration with Contoso’s existing infrastructure, migration of legacy data, and end-to-end testing;

  2.2.3 Development and customization of certain software modules, interfaces, or scripts to enable specific functionality requested by Contoso and defined in an SOW;

  2.2.4 Ongoing support and maintenance services, including incident management, bug fixes, minor enhancements, and technical support through agreed-upon channels during defined support hours;

  2.2.5 Managed services operations, including performance monitoring, capacity planning, and routine administration of designated systems, as further detailed in applicable SOWs;

  2.2.6 Training services, including the preparation and delivery of user guides, training materials, and instructor-led or remote training sessions for Contoso’s personnel.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Gamma shall create, deliver, and, where applicable, install or configure the Deliverables described in each SOW. Deliverables may include:

  2.3.1 Design documents, solution architecture diagrams, and functional and technical specifications;

  2.3.2 Configured software instances, including implemented modules and integrations;

  2.3.3 Custom code components, scripts, and configuration files as designated in an SOW;

  2.3.4 User and system documentation, including installation guides, user manuals, and operations runbooks;

  2.3.5 Test plans, test cases, test scripts, and test results, including evidence of successful completion of agreed-upon test phases;

  2.3.6 Periodic status reports, project plans, risk registers, and other project management artifacts.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Gamma shall perform the Services:

  2.4.1 In a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services and in compliance with the Specifications and Service Levels set forth in this Agreement and applicable SOWs;

  2.4.2 In compliance with all Applicable Laws and Contoso’s written policies and procedures that are reasonably communicated to Gamma and that relate to data security, facility access, and acceptable use of systems;

  2.4.3 Using personnel who are suitably qualified, competent, and experienced for the tasks assigned, and Gamma shall ensure continuity of key personnel to the extent reasonably practicable, subject to Section 2.8.

2.5 **Timeline and Milestones.**  
Each SOW shall set forth the applicable timeline, project phases, and milestones for the Services and Deliverables. Unless otherwise stated in an SOW:

  2.5.1 Gamma shall provide a detailed project plan within fifteen (15) Business Days of the effective date of such SOW, including key milestones, dependencies, and resource allocations;

  2.5.2 Contoso shall review and provide feedback on the project plan within ten (10) Business Days of receipt. Any changes required by Contoso that materially affect scope, budget, or timeline shall be addressed in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management);

  2.5.3 Gamma shall report progress against milestones on at least a bi-weekly basis, including identification of risks, issues, and proposed mitigation strategies;

  2.5.4 If Gamma reasonably anticipates that it will be unable to meet a milestone or timeline, it shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, stating the reasons for the delay and proposing a revised schedule. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate delays.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  2.6.1 Unless otherwise stated in the relevant SOW, Contoso shall have a period of thirty (30) calendar days from delivery of a Deliverable (the “**Acceptance Period**”) to review and test the Deliverable against the applicable Specifications and acceptance criteria.

  2.6.2 If Contoso determines that the Deliverable materially conforms to the Specifications and acceptance criteria, Contoso shall provide Gamma with written notice of acceptance. If Contoso determines that the Deliverable does not materially conform, Contoso shall provide a written notice specifying in reasonable detail the non-conformities.

  2.6.3 Upon receipt of notice of non-conformity, Gamma shall promptly, at its own cost, correct the identified non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance. The Parties shall then follow the acceptance process set forth above, with a subsequent Acceptance Period of fifteen (15) calendar days.

  2.6.4 Acceptance of a Deliverable shall not be unreasonably withheld, delayed, or conditioned by Contoso. If Contoso fails to provide any written notice of acceptance or rejection within the applicable Acceptance Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the last day of such period, provided Gamma has sent a written reminder at least five (5) Business Days before the end of the Acceptance Period.

2.7 **Cooperation and Client Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Gamma’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon timely cooperation by Contoso. Accordingly:

  2.7.1 Contoso shall provide Gamma with timely access to relevant systems, facilities, personnel, and information reasonably required to perform the Services;

  2.7.2 Contoso shall designate a primary point of contact for communications with Gamma and shall ensure that necessary decisions and approvals are provided in a timely manner;

  2.7.3 Contoso is responsible for reviewing and providing feedback on Deliverables and project documentation within timeframes stated herein or in the applicable SOW.

Any failure by Contoso to meet its obligations under this Section 2.7 may result in delays in performance by Gamma. Gamma shall not be liable for such delays to the extent caused by Contoso’s failure to cooperate as required herein; however, Gamma shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate resulting impacts.

2.8 **Personnel; Subcontracting.**  

  2.8.1 Gamma shall be responsible for the direction and control of its employees, agents, and subcontractors in performing the Services.

  2.8.2 Gamma may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that (a) Gamma remains fully responsible for the performance of such subcontractors; (b) all subcontractors are bound by written agreements requiring confidentiality and data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and (c) subcontractors located outside the United States shall only process Personal Data in compliance with Data Protection Laws.

2.9 **Service Value and Overall Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the aggregate contract value of this Agreement, inclusive of all SOWs issued as of the Effective Date, is **Four Million Three Hundred Thirty-Eight Thousand Four Hundred Fifty U.S. Dollars (US$4,338,450)** (the “**Contract Value**”). The Contract Value is an estimate based on the initial scope and may be revised by mutual written agreement through additional SOWs or Change Orders.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees.**  
Contoso shall pay Gamma the Fees for the Services and Deliverables as described in this Agreement and in each SOW. Fees may include:

  3.1.1 Fixed-fee amounts for specific Deliverables or project phases;

  3.1.2 Time and materials charges based on agreed hourly or daily rates for specified personnel roles;

  3.1.3 Recurring Fees for ongoing managed services or support, billed monthly or annually as stipulated in the SOW.

3.2 **Fee Schedule and Payment Milestones.**  

  3.2.1 Each SOW shall specify the applicable fee structure, including any milestone-based payments tied to delivery or acceptance of Deliverables, and any recurring charges for ongoing services.

  3.2.2 By way of example, a typical implementation SOW may include (a) an initial mobilization payment of ten percent (10%) of the SOW value upon execution, (b) subsequent milestone payments upon completion and acceptance of major phases, and (c) a final payment upon final acceptance.

  3.2.3 For time and materials-based work, Gamma shall invoice monthly in arrears for the actual hours worked and expenses incurred during the preceding month, in accordance with the rates specified in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

  3.3.1 Gamma shall issue invoices to Contoso in accordance with the invoicing schedule specified in each SOW. If no schedule is specified, Gamma may invoice monthly in arrears.

  3.3.2 All invoices shall reference this Agreement, Contract Reference No. AGR-GAM-202406-221, and the applicable SOW, and shall include reasonable supporting detail.

  3.3.3 Unless otherwise stated in the applicable SOW, payment shall be due **Net 45** days from Contoso’s receipt of a valid, undisputed invoice (“**Payment Terms**”).

  3.3.4 Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other agreed method to the account specified by Gamma on its invoices.

3.4 **Disputed Amounts.**  

  3.4.1 If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall provide written notice of the dispute, including the basis therefor, within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, and the Parties shall cooperate to resolve the dispute promptly.

  3.4.2 Contoso shall timely pay all undisputed amounts in accordance with Section 3.3. Any disputed amounts determined to be payable after resolution of the dispute shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days of such resolution.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  

  3.5.1 Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law.

  3.5.2 Gamma may, upon at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice to Contoso, suspend performance of the Services if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid more than sixty (60) days after the due date, provided that Gamma resumes Services promptly upon payment of all outstanding amounts.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

  3.6.1 Contoso shall reimburse Gamma for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and per diem, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policy, as provided to Gamma.

  3.6.2 Gamma shall provide reasonable documentation supporting all reimbursable expenses, including receipts or equivalent proof of payment where required.

3.7 **Taxes.**  

  3.7.1 Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all such taxes imposed in connection with this Agreement, excluding taxes based on Gamma’s net income, property, or employees.

  3.7.2 If Gamma is required by Applicable Law to collect and remit taxes on the Fees, Gamma shall include such taxes on its invoices, and Contoso shall pay such amounts in accordance with the Payment Terms, unless Contoso provides a valid exemption certificate.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until March 25, 2027 (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew this Agreement for additional periods of one (1) year each, on terms to be negotiated in good faith. Any such renewal shall be documented in a written amendment executed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any or all SOWs), for convenience upon not less than thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Gamma.

  4.3.2 Gamma may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon not less than sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Contoso, provided that Gamma shall complete, to the extent reasonably practicable, any critical milestones or transition activities as requested by Contoso.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (and/or any applicable SOW) immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is subject to liquidation or receivership, or ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business; or (b) assigns this Agreement in violation of Section 16.7.

  4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (and/or any applicable SOW) for material breach by the other Party by providing written notice specifying the nature of the breach. The breaching Party shall have thirty (30) days from receipt of such notice to cure the breach (the “**Cure Period**”). If the breach is not cured within the Cure Period, the non-breaching Party may terminate this Agreement and/or the affected SOW upon written notice.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:  
  (a) All outstanding unpaid invoices shall become immediately due and payable;  
  (b) Gamma shall cease performing Services except as reasonably necessary to wind down activities and assist in transition as described in Section 4.5.3; and  
  (c) Each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.

  4.5.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Contoso, Contoso shall pay Gamma (a) all Fees for Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted (or deemed accepted) through the effective date of termination, (b) any non-cancellable commitments entered into by Gamma specifically for the benefit of Contoso and pre-approved in writing, and (c) reasonable wind-down costs not to exceed an amount mutually agreed in good faith.

  4.5.3 At Contoso’s request, Gamma shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days following termination (or such other period as the Parties may agree in writing) at Gamma’s then-current time and materials rates, to facilitate an orderly transition of Services to Contoso or a third-party provider.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and all payment obligations accrued prior to termination, shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”) in connection with this Agreement, whether oral, visual, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  5.1.1 Is or becomes generally available to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

  5.1.2 Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction on use or disclosure prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

  5.1.3 Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction on use or disclosure and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality owed to the Disclosing Party; or

  5.1.4 Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

  5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall: (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement; (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein; and (c) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

  5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its and its Affiliates’ employees, contractors, advisors, and agents who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less protective than those in this Article V. The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Article V by such persons.

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice and cooperate reasonably (at the Disclosing Party’s expense) in any effort to limit or contest the disclosure. 5.4 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) copies required to comply with legal or regulatory requirements, and (b) archival copies stored in automatic backup systems, provided that such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.5 **Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Availability Commitment.**  
For any hosted or managed services provided under this Agreement, Gamma shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the applicable systems are available to Contoso with a minimum uptime of **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excused Downtime.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Gamma may perform routine maintenance on the systems supporting the Services. Gamma shall endeavor to schedule such maintenance during off-peak hours and provide Contoso with at least three (3) Business Days’ prior notice for planned maintenance that is expected to result in service downtime. Scheduled Maintenance shall not exceed twelve (12) hours per calendar month.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Gamma shall comply with the following target response and resolution times for support incidents (measured from the time Gamma receives the incident report from Contoso):

  12.3.1 **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of service or severe business impact with no workaround:  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours

  12.3.2 **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant degradation with major impact, workaround available:  
  - Initial Response: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 2 Business Days

  12.3.3 **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact, partial loss of functionality:  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 5 Business Days

  12.3.4 **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries:  
  - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: to be determined in coordination with Contoso.

12.4 **SLA Credits.**  
If Gamma fails to meet the availability commitment in Section 12.1 in any given calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  12.4.1 Uptime < 99.9% but ≥ 99.5%: credit equal to 2% of the monthly Fees for the affected service;

  12.4.2 Uptime < 99.5% but ≥ 99.0%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly Fees for the affected service;

  12.4.3 Uptime < 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly Fees for the affected service.

To receive credits, Contoso must request them in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the service level failure occurred. Service credits shall be applied to future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service credits represent Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for service level failures, without prejudice to Contoso’s right to terminate for persistent or material breach per Article IV.

  15.2.2 Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.
