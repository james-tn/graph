## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 Relationship to Parent Agreement

2.1.1 This Maintenance Agreement is expressly **executed under and subject to the terms of the Parent Agreement (SLA-STE-202507-499 / contract_499)**, as further detailed in **Article XVII (Reference to Master/Parent Agreement)** below.

2.1.2 The Maintenance Services provided hereunder are in furtherance of and supplement the **“Maintenance and Support Services”** referenced in **Section 1.1.14** and **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement, and are subject to the **Service Level Agreements** of **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement, as enhanced herein.

2.1.3 Except as expressly modified or supplemented in this Maintenance Agreement, all rights, obligations, and limitations set forth in the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect and are incorporated herein by this reference.

### 2.2 General Description of Maintenance Services

2.2.1 Vendor shall provide to Client the Maintenance Services for the Licensed Software during the Term, including:

  (a) **Incident Management and Break/Fix Support**, including diagnosis, troubleshooting, and correction of Errors as defined in **Section 1.1.10 of the Parent Agreement**;  

  (b) **Corrective Maintenance**, including provision and deployment of error corrections, bug fixes, patches, and hotfixes;  

  (c) **Preventive Maintenance**, including periodic health checks, performance optimization, log reviews, and recommendations;  

  (d) **Release and Upgrade Management**, including provision of Maintenance Releases and Minor Upgrades at no additional license cost, and management of Major Upgrades as further described in Section 2.5;  

  (e) **Configuration and Administration Support**, providing guidance on configuration of workflows, dashboards, connectors, and alerts as initially implemented under **Section 2.4.1 (Implementation and Configuration)** of the Parent Agreement;  

  (f) **Knowledge Transfer and Training Refresh**, including periodic refresher sessions and documentation updates aligned with the Deliverables outlined in **Section 2.4.2 of the Parent Agreement**; and  

  (g) **Technical Account Management**, including assignment of a named Technical Account Manager (“TAM”) as further detailed below.

### 2.3 Specific Deliverables

2.3.1 During the Term, Vendor shall provide the following specific Deliverables (in addition to the core Maintenance Services):

  (a) **Quarterly System Health Report**: A written report summarizing system performance, incident trends, capacity metrics, and recommendations for improvements, delivered within fifteen (15) Business Days after the end of each calendar quarter.  

  (b) **Annual Architecture Review**: A workshop and written assessment reviewing the deployment architecture of the StellarOps Suite in the Client Environment, including recommendations for scaling, resilience, and security hardening.  

  (c) **Updated Runbook and Support Documentation**: At least annually, and after any Major Upgrade, Vendor shall update the operational runbook and support documentation originally described in **Section 2.4.2(e) of the Parent Agreement**, reflecting current best practices and Client-specific configurations.  

  (d) **Patch and Upgrade Schedule**: A rolling six (6)‑month schedule of planned Maintenance Releases and planned availability of Minor Upgrades for the Licensed Software.  

  (e) **Training Refresh Sessions**: Up to **twelve (12) hours per calendar year** of virtual training or Q&A sessions for Client’s administrators and key Users, building upon the training materials described in **Section 2.4.2(c) of the Parent Agreement**.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Maintenance Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards, as required by **Section 2.4.4 and Section 7.2.2** of the Parent Agreement;  

  (b) in compliance with applicable laws and regulations, including data protection laws as required under **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of the Parent Agreement and this Maintenance Agreement; and  

  (c) in accordance with the Service Levels specified in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement and this Maintenance Agreement.

2.4.2 Vendor shall assign personnel with the requisite skills and experience to perform the Maintenance Services, consistent with **Section 2.4.4** of the Parent Agreement.

### 2.5 Upgrades and New Releases

2.5.1 **Maintenance Releases and Minor Upgrades**. During the Term, Vendor shall, at no additional license fee:

  (a) make available to Client all Maintenance Releases and Minor Upgrades for the Licensed Software that Vendor makes generally available to its similarly situated enterprise customers with similar maintenance entitlements; and  

  (b) provide reasonable remote assistance to Client’s technical staff in planning and executing the installation of Maintenance Releases and Minor Upgrades in the Client Environment.

2.5.2 **Major Upgrades**. Major Upgrades may be subject to additional license/subscription fees and/or separate Order Forms under the Parent Agreement. Vendor shall:

  (a) notify Client at least ninety (90) days prior to end‑of‑support for any major version currently in use by Client; and  

  (b) provide a migration plan and estimated professional services effort for any Major Upgrade, to be contracted via a Statement of Work (“SOW”) under the Parent Agreement.

2.5.3 Client is responsible for promptly applying Maintenance Releases and Minor Upgrades designated by Vendor as security‑critical. If Client fails to implement such updates within a commercially reasonable timeframe after Vendor’s notice, Vendor shall have no liability for failures or security incidents to the extent they would have been avoided by timely installation of such updates.

### 2.6 Client Responsibilities

2.6.1 In addition to the responsibilities set forth in **Section 2.4.5 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

  (a) designate a primary and secondary support contact to interface with Vendor’s support team;  

  (b) provide remote access to the Client Environment as reasonably required to perform diagnostic and maintenance activities, subject to Client’s security policies;  

  (c) maintain up‑to‑date backups of Client Data, consistent with Client’s own disaster recovery policies; and  

  (d) implement Vendor’s reasonable recommendations for configurations necessary for stability and security.

2.6.2 Vendor shall not be responsible for failures to meet Service Levels or for delays in resolving Errors to the extent caused by (a) the Client Environment, (b) modifications not authorized by Vendor, or (c) Client’s failure to comply with Vendor’s documented prerequisites or its obligations under this Maintenance Agreement and **Section 2.4.5** of the Parent Agreement.

### 2.7 Exclusions

2.7.1 Unless otherwise set forth in an Order Form or SOW, Maintenance Services do not include:

  (a) development of custom features, integrations, or reports (which shall be performed, if agreed, under a separate SOW pursuant to the Parent Agreement);  

  (b) support for third‑party software or hardware not provided by Vendor;  

  (c) on‑site services (other than those specifically contracted); or  

  (d) remediation of issues caused by unauthorized modifications, negligence, or misuse of the Software in violation of **Section 2.3 (Use Restrictions)** of the Parent Agreement.

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Maintenance Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or as a result of termination of the Parent Agreement, shall continue in effect until **October 27, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**).

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Maintenance Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to Vendor, which is a longer period than the thirty (30) days provided for termination of the Parent Agreement under **Section 4.3.1** and is expressly agreed in light of the high‑risk and mission‑critical nature of the Maintenance Services.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Maintenance Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail, consistent with the cure periods specified in **Section 4.4.1 of the Parent Agreement** for material breach.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement immediately upon written notice if any of the events described in **Section 4.4.2 of the Parent Agreement** occur (insolvency, bankruptcy, cessation of business, unlawful or fraudulent conduct materially impacting the other Party).

### 4.5 Effect of Termination

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Maintenance Agreement for any reason:

  (a) Vendor’s obligations to provide Maintenance Services and Enhanced Support Services shall cease;  

  (b) Client shall remain licensed to use the Licensed Software in accordance with the Parent Agreement, subject to the license termination provisions in **Section 4.5.1(a) of the Parent Agreement**;  

  (c) Client shall remain responsible for all undisputed Maintenance Fees accrued through the effective date of termination; and  

  (d) any outstanding Deliverables (such as the Quarterly System Health Report) for which Client has fully paid shall be provided to the extent reasonably practicable.

4.5.2 Upon Client’s request made within thirty (30) days following termination or expiration, Vendor shall provide reasonable assistance, on a time‑and‑materials basis at Vendor’s then‑current rates, to transition knowledge and documentation to Client’s internal support teams or to a successor maintenance provider, consistent with the data handling provisions of **Section 4.5.3 and Section 11.4** of the Parent Agreement.

### 4.6 Interdependency with Parent Agreement

4.6.1 **Automatic Termination.** This Maintenance Agreement is dependent upon the continued validity of the Parent Agreement. If the Parent Agreement is terminated in its entirety for any reason, this Maintenance Agreement shall automatically terminate effective as of the effective date of such termination, without further notice, except that any provisions that by their nature should survive shall survive as set forth in Section 4.7.

4.6.2 **Partial Termination / Module‑Specific Termination.** If the Parent Agreement is terminated only with respect to certain modules of the StellarOps Suite, this Maintenance Agreement shall automatically be deemed terminated with respect to the corresponding modules, and the Maintenance Fees shall be adjusted on a going‑forward basis through mutual agreement, taking into account the removed scope.

### 4.7 Survival

4.7.1 Provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration of this Maintenance Agreement shall so survive, including without limitation: **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)** with respect to outstanding obligations, **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**, **Article VII (Representations and Warranties)**, **Article VIII (Indemnification)**, **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)**, **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**, **Article XV (Force Majeure)**, **Article XVI (General Provisions)**, and **Article XVII (Reference to Master/Parent Agreement)**.
