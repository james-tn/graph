## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Phoenix shall provide to Contoso a comprehensive program of industrial systems integration, automation configuration, data aggregation, and ongoing managed support services in connection with Contoso’s manufacturing and logistics operations within the United States (the **“Project”**). The Services shall be performed in accordance with the Specifications, this Work Order, and any written instructions reasonably provided by Contoso that are not inconsistent with this Work Order.

2.2 **Implementation and Integration Services.**  
(a) **Systems Assessment and Design.** Within thirty (30) days following the Effective Date, Phoenix shall conduct an initial assessment of Contoso’s relevant facilities, equipment, control systems, and data flows. Phoenix shall prepare a system design document that includes: (i) a network topology for industrial control systems, (ii) mappings between existing programmable logic controllers (PLCs) and the new supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) platform, (iii) data integration points with Contoso’s ERP and MES systems, and (iv) a phased implementation plan.  

(b) **Configuration and Integration.** Phoenix shall configure and integrate:  
  (i) up to ten (10) manufacturing lines across three (3) primary sites;  
  (ii) the SCADA platform with field devices and PLCs;  
  (iii) interfaces between the SCADA platform and Contoso’s ERP/MES systems; and  
  (iv) data aggregation and reporting dashboards for operations, quality, and maintenance teams.  

(c) **Testing and Validation.** Phoenix shall prepare and execute a test plan, including unit, integration, and user acceptance testing (UAT). Phoenix shall document test results and remediate material defects in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

2.3 **Managed Services and Support.**  
During the Term, Phoenix shall provide ongoing managed services, including:  
(a) **Monitoring and Incident Management.** 24x7 remote monitoring of the integrated systems, incident detection, triage, and remediation in accordance with response and resolution times set forth in **Article XII**;  

(b) **Preventive Maintenance.** Quarterly preventive maintenance on Phoenix-provided software components and configurations, including patch management, performance tuning, and capacity planning reviews;  

(c) **Change Implementation.** Implementation of minor configuration changes requested by Contoso that do not materially alter the Scope of Services or require a Change Order under **Article XIII**, subject to reasonable limits to be agreed in writing;  

(d) **Support Desk.** Provision of a support desk for Contoso’s designated technical contacts, available during Business Days from 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Central Time, with escalation to on-call support for critical incidents as further defined in **Article XII**.

2.4 **Project Management and Reporting.**  
(a) **Project Manager.** Phoenix shall assign a project manager who shall be the primary point of contact for Contoso regarding the Project. Contoso shall assign a corresponding project manager.  

(b) **Status Meetings.** The Parties shall conduct bi-weekly status meetings (which may be virtual) during the implementation phase and monthly meetings during the steady-state managed services phase.  

(c) **Reporting.** Phoenix shall provide monthly written reports to Contoso summarizing: (i) activities performed; (ii) Service Level performance; (iii) incidents and resolutions; (iv) upcoming milestones; and (v) any material risks or issues.

2.5 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of the Services, Phoenix shall produce and provide the following key Deliverables to Contoso, each in English and in electronic format unless otherwise specified:  
(a) **System Design Document** – including architecture diagrams, integration mappings, and security design;  
(b) **Configuration Specifications** – including PLC and SCADA configuration parameters, tagging schemes, and interfaces;  
(c) **Test Plan and Test Reports** – including documented test cases, results, and remediation status;  
(d) **Operations Manual** – user guides, operational procedures, and troubleshooting instructions;  
(e) **Training Materials** – training slides, reference guides, and recorded sessions (where applicable);  
(f) **Monthly Performance Reports** – as described in **Section 2.4(c)**.

2.6 **Timeline and Milestones.**  
Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the following timeline, subject to delays caused by Contoso, third parties, or Force Majeure Events:  
(a) **Phase 1 – Assessment and Design** (Effective Date + 0–45 days): completion of initial site assessments, design workshops, and delivery of System Design Document;  
(b) **Phase 2 – Pilot Implementation** (Effective Date + 46–150 days): implementation and testing at one pilot facility; completion of UAT;  
(c) **Phase 3 – Rollout Implementation** (Effective Date + 151–365 days): rollout to remaining designated facilities;  
(d) **Phase 4 – Steady-State Managed Services** (Effective Date + 366 days through Term): ongoing support, monitoring, and optimization.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**  
Phoenix shall perform the Services:  
(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards for similar services;  
(b) in compliance with applicable laws and regulations; and  
(c) in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII** and the Specifications.

2.8 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Phoenix’s performance is dependent on the timely and effective cooperation of Contoso, including:  
(a) providing reasonable access to facilities, systems, and personnel;  
(b) promptly reviewing and providing feedback on Deliverables;  
(c) ensuring that its equipment and systems meet reasonable technical prerequisites specified by Phoenix; and  
(d) supplying accurate and complete Client Materials. Phoenix shall not be liable for delays or non-compliance resulting from Contoso’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated value of this Work Order is **Two Million Three Hundred Sixty-Four Thousand Six Hundred Thirty-Two U.S. Dollars (US$2,364,632)** (the **“Contract Value”**), exclusive of any applicable taxes, duties, or withholdings, which shall be the responsibility of Contoso as set forth in **Section 3.7**.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees under this Work Order are structured as follows:

(a) **Fixed Implementation Fees.**  
- Assessment & Design (Phase 1): US$285,000  
- Pilot Implementation (Phase 2): US$625,000  
- Rollout Implementation (Phase 3): US$920,000  

(b) **Managed Services Fees (Phase 4).**  
- Monthly Managed Services Fee during the steady-state phase: US$52,000 per month, for an anticipated twenty (20) months during the Term, totaling an estimated US$1,040,000.  

(c) **Total.**  
The sum of the fixed implementation fees and estimated managed services fees equals the Contract Value, subject to adjustment pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** and any out-of-scope services approved by Contoso.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in a Change Order, Phoenix shall invoice Contoso according to the following schedule:

(a) **Phase 1 – Assessment & Design.**  
- 40% of Phase 1 Fee (US$114,000) upon Effective Date;  
- 60% of Phase 1 Fee (US$171,000) upon delivery of the System Design Document.

(b) **Phase 2 – Pilot Implementation.**  
- 30% of Phase 2 Fee (US$187,500) upon commencement of pilot deployment;  
- 40% of Phase 2 Fee (US$250,000) upon substantial completion of pilot configuration;  
- 30% of Phase 2 Fee (US$187,500) upon completion of UAT for pilot.

(c) **Phase 3 – Rollout Implementation.**  
- 30% of Phase 3 Fee (US$276,000) upon commencement of rollout;  
- 40% of Phase 3 Fee (US$368,000) upon completion of configuration at all designated sites;  
- 30% of Phase 3 Fee (US$276,000) upon successful completion of rollout UAT.

(d) **Phase 4 – Managed Services.**  
Phoenix shall invoice monthly in advance for the Managed Services Fee, beginning thirty (30) days after commencement of the steady-state managed services phase.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) All invoices shall be due and payable by Contoso **within sixty (60) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 60”**), without setoff or deduction except as required by law or expressly permitted under this Agreement.  

(b) Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other method specified on the invoice.  

(c) If Contoso disputes any amount in good faith, Contoso shall notify Phoenix within fifteen (15) days after receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute. Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts when due. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve disputed amounts promptly.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month** or the maximum interest rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less, from the due date until paid in full. In addition, if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after its due date, Phoenix may, upon written notice to Contoso, suspend performance of the Services until such amounts are paid, without liability for any resulting delays or non-performance.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Contoso shall reimburse Phoenix for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket travel and living expenses incurred by Phoenix personnel in connection with onsite Services, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policies provided in advance to Phoenix.  

(b) Phoenix shall submit documentation reasonably supporting such expenses, and Contoso shall reimburse such expenses within the same payment terms as apply to Fees.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees and other amounts payable under this Work Order are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, withholding, or similar taxes (**“Taxes”**). Contoso shall be responsible for all Taxes arising from the transactions under this Work Order, excluding taxes based on Phoenix’s net income or employment-related taxes for Phoenix personnel. If Contoso is required by law to withhold or deduct Taxes from payments to Phoenix, Contoso shall provide Phoenix with official receipts and shall reasonably cooperate with Phoenix in obtaining any available reductions or exemptions.

3.8 **No Withholding for Service Levels.**  
Except as expressly specified in a Change Order or in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**, no portion of the Fees shall be subject to withholding, offset, penalty, or reduction due to Service Level shortfalls, and the Parties agree that the credits and remedies set forth in **Article XII** shall be Contoso’s exclusive monetary remedy for Service Level breaches.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The Term of this Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 08, 2027**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend the Term for additional periods on terms and conditions to be negotiated in good faith. Absent a written renewal or extension, this Work Order shall automatically expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Contoso.**  
Contoso may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for its convenience and without cause, upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Phoenix. In such event, Contoso shall pay Phoenix:  
(a) all Fees for Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) all non-cancellable commitments and reasonable wind-down costs incurred by Phoenix as a result of the early termination; and  
(c) an early termination fee equal to **twenty percent (20%) of the remaining unpaid Contract Value** (excluding any amounts attributable to uncommitted future Managed Services), as liquidated damages and not as a penalty, in recognition of the high-risk allocation and foregone opportunity costs.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Phoenix.**  
Phoenix may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Contoso. In such event, Phoenix shall reasonably cooperate with Contoso to transition the Services to Contoso or a third party designated by Contoso, at Contoso’s expense. Phoenix shall refund any prepaid but unearned Fees for Services not yet provided as of the effective date of termination.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Work Order by written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.  

(b) Notwithstanding the foregoing, Phoenix may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if: (i) Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of non-payment; or (ii) Contoso’s breach of **Article V (Confidentiality)** or **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)** materially threatens Phoenix’s IPR or confidential information.  

(c) Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver or similar officer appointed for its business or assets, and such proceeding is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.6 **Effects of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:  
(a) Phoenix shall cease performing the Services (except as necessary to complete any agreed transition period);  
(b) Contoso shall pay Phoenix all amounts due and owing as of the effective date of termination or expiration, including any termination fees under **Section 4.3**, within thirty (30) days;  
(c) Phoenix shall, upon Contoso’s written request and at Contoso’s expense, use commercially reasonable efforts to assist in transitioning the Services to Contoso or another service provider, on terms to be mutually agreed;  
(d) Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, destroy Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession, subject to **Section 5.4**; and  
(e) The provisions of this Work Order that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to **Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and this **Section 4.6**, shall so survive.

11.1 **Compliance with Data Protection Laws.**  
Each Party shall comply with applicable Data Protection Laws in connection with the performance of this Work Order. To the extent Phoenix processes Personal Data on behalf of Contoso, Phoenix shall act as a “processor” or “service provider” (as such terms are defined under applicable law), and Contoso shall act as “controller” or “business.”

Phoenix shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Contoso, which are set forth in this Work Order and any related documents, unless otherwise required by applicable law. Phoenix shall promptly notify Contoso if it believes an instruction violates Data Protection Laws.

Phoenix shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access, commensurate with the nature of the data and the risks involved, including:  
(a) access controls;  
(b) encryption of Personal Data at rest and in transit where appropriate;  
(c) regular vulnerability assessments; and  
(d) incident response procedures.

11.4 **Subprocessors.**  
Phoenix may engage subcontractors to process Personal Data on its behalf (**“Subprocessors”**), provided that Phoenix:  
(a) ensures Subprocessors are bound by written agreements imposing data protection obligations substantially similar to those set out in this **Article XI**; and  
(b) remains responsible for Subprocessors’ acts and omissions to the same extent as if such acts and omissions were Phoenix’s own.

11.5 **Data Subject Rights.**  
Phoenix shall, to the extent feasible and at Contoso’s cost, assist Contoso in responding to data subject requests to exercise rights under Data Protection Laws (e.g., access, correction, deletion, portability).

11.6 **Data Breach Notification.**

(a) notify Contoso without undue delay and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours after becoming aware of the Data Breach;  
(b) provide information reasonably required by Contoso to meet its legal obligations, as such information becomes available; and  
(c) take reasonable steps to mitigate the effects of the Data Breach and prevent its recurrence.

11.7 **Data Retention and Deletion.**

Upon termination or expiration of this Work Order, or upon Contoso’s written request, Phoenix shall delete or return all Personal Data in its possession or control, except to the extent retention is required by law or necessary for legitimate business purposes (e.g., compliance documentation), in which case Phoenix shall continue to protect such data in accordance with this **Article XI**.

11.8 **CCPA-Specific Terms.**  
To the extent the CCPA applies, Phoenix shall:  
(a) act as a “service provider” and shall not sell or share Personal Information processed on behalf of Contoso;  
(b) not retain, use, or disclose Personal Information for any purpose other than providing the Services or for a business purpose as specified in this Work Order; and  
(c) notify Contoso if Phoenix determines it can no longer meet its obligations as a service provider.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Availability Commitment.**  
Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production SCADA monitoring and integration environment operated or managed by Phoenix for Contoso is available **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding:  
(a) scheduled maintenance (not exceeding eight (8) hours per month, with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice where feasible);  
(b) emergency maintenance required to address critical vulnerabilities;  
(c) downtime attributable to Contoso, third parties under Contoso’s control, or Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Incident Severity and Response Times.**  
Phoenix shall respond to incidents according to the following severity levels:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or material impact on safety or production.  
  - Response Time: within 1 hour (24x7)  
  - Target Restoration: within 8 hours  

- **Severity 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired with limited workaround.  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Restoration: within 24 hours  

- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial loss of non-critical functionality.  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Restoration: within 5 Business Days  

- **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, non-urgent requests.  
  - Response Time: within 2 Business Days  
  - Target Restoration: based on mutual agreement and prioritization.

12.3 **Measurement and Reporting.**  
Phoenix shall measure Service Levels using its monitoring tools and logs and shall report monthly Service Level performance as part of the reports described in **Section 2.4(c)**. Contoso may reasonable request additional information to validate reported Service Levels.

12.4 **Service Level Credits.**  
(a) If Phoenix fails to meet the Availability Commitment for a calendar month due to reasons attributable to Phoenix, Contoso shall be entitled to a service credit equal to **5% of the monthly Managed Services Fee** for each full 1% below the 99.9% availability target, up to a maximum of **25% of the monthly Managed Services Fee** for that month.  

(b) If Phoenix fails to meet the Response Time targets for more than three (3) Severity 1 incidents in any calendar quarter, Contoso shall be entitled to a one-time service credit equal to **10% of the Managed Services Fees** invoiced for that quarter.  

(c) Service credits shall be applied to future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash.

12.5 **Exclusive Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
The Parties agree that the service credits set forth in this **Article XII** shall be Contoso’s **sole and exclusive monetary remedy** for Service Level failures, without prejudice to Contoso’s right to terminate this Work Order for material breach under **Section 4.5** if Service Level failures are persistent and material.

## ARTICLE XIV – DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14.1 **Good Faith Negotiations.**  
In the event of any Dispute, the Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute through informal discussions between the project managers. If the Dispute is not resolved within fifteen (15) days, either Party may escalate the Dispute to senior management.

14.2 **Executive Escalation.**  
Upon escalation, each Party shall designate a senior executive with authority to resolve the Dispute. The executives shall meet (in person or by teleconference) within fifteen (15) days of escalation and shall attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute within thirty (30) days thereafter.

14.3 **Mediation.**  
If the Dispute is not resolved through executive escalation, the Parties agree to submit the Dispute to non-binding mediation before a mutually agreed mediator in Dallas County, Texas. The mediation shall commence within forty-five (45) days after either Party makes a written request for mediation. The Parties shall share equally the mediator’s fees and expenses, and each Party shall bear its own attorneys’ fees and costs.

14.4 **Arbitration or Litigation.**  
If the Dispute has not been resolved through mediation within sixty (60) days after the mediator’s appointment, either Party may initiate litigation as provided in **Section 14.5**. The Parties expressly agree **not** to require binding arbitration unless they mutually agree in writing to do so for a particular Dispute.

14.5 **Governing Law; Venue.**

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure Events.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event beyond a Party’s reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, fire, flood, earthquake, epidemic, pandemic, war, terrorism, riot, civil unrest, labor strike (excluding strikes limited to a Party’s own workforce), governmental action, embargo, or failure of utilities or telecommunications not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature and expected duration of the event;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the event and resume performance as soon as practicable; and  
(c) keep the other Party informed of progress in resolving the event.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Work Order shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are directly affected by the Force Majeure Event. The affected Party shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performing such obligations during such period.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate this Work Order upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination (except for payment of amounts accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
