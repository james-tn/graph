## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  
This Work Order constitutes a **Statement of Work** under the Parent Agreement within the meaning of **Section 1.17** and is governed by **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** thereof. In accordance with **Section 2.1 of the Parent Agreement**, the Client hereby engages the Vendor, and the Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Extended Services and Deliverables described in this Work Order. This Work Order **supplements** and, where expressly stated, **modifies** the general scope previously agreed in the Parent Agreement. Unless expressly overridden herein, the provisions of the Parent Agreement shall govern the performance of the Services under this Work Order.

2.2 **Purpose of the Work.**  
Under the Parent Agreement, Client is engaged in global commercial operations and seeks to enhance its technology infrastructure, business analytics, and digital transformation capabilities (Parent Agreement Recitals). This Work Order focuses on a **multi-wave digital transformation initiative** consisting of:

- Design and implementation of a **cloud-based data platform**,
- Integration of multiple legacy systems into a centralized analytics environment,
- Development of **enterprise-wide dashboards and advanced analytics**,
- Establishment of robust **governance and operating models** for ongoing data-driven decision making.

The Extended Services authorized by this Work Order are specifically intended to **extend and deepen** the Technology Consulting, Systems Integration, Data Analytics, and Project Management services described in **Sections 2.2(a)–(d)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.3 **Phased Services.**  
Vendor shall perform the Extended Services in the following Phases. Each Phase shall include Deliverables, Milestones, and acceptance criteria as described below.

2.3.1 **Phase 1 – Strategy & Assessment (Estimated Duration: 3 Months).**  
(a) Conduct comprehensive assessment of Client’s existing infrastructure, applications, and data architecture, in line with **Section 2.2(a) (Technology Consulting and Assessment)** of the Parent Agreement.  
(b) Produce the following Deliverables:  
  - Current-state architecture documentation;  
  - Gap analysis and risk assessment report;  
  - Cloud migration strategy and roadmap;  
  - Data governance framework proposal, including roles and responsibilities.  
(c) Milestone M1: Delivery and Acceptance of Strategy & Assessment Pack.  
(d) Vendor shall adhere to the performance standards in **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement and shall use senior consultants for key analysis activities, as listed in Schedule 1.

2.3.2 **Phase 2 – Design & Architecture (Estimated Duration: 4 Months).**  
(a) Design target-state architecture for the cloud data platform and integration layers, consistent with the systems integration scope outlined in **Section 2.2(b)** of the Parent Agreement.  
(b) Define detailed data models, integration patterns, and security design.  
(c) Deliverables:  
  - Target architecture blueprints;  
  - Detailed data model specifications;  
  - Interface and API specifications;  
  - Security design and controls catalogue.  
(d) Milestone M2: Acceptance of Design & Architecture Package and approval of Project Plan for Phase 3.  

2.3.3 **Phase 3 – Implementation & Integration (Estimated Duration: 12 Months).**  
(a) Implement cloud infrastructure, data ingestion pipelines, transformation logic, and storage layers;  
(b) Integrate at least eight (8) source systems (ERP, CRM, Finance, HR, and others to be agreed) using agreed workflows, consistent with **Section 2.2(b)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(c) Build and deploy analytics dashboards and reports per the analytics requirements in **Section 2.2(c)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(d) Deliverables:  
  - Configured cloud data platform;  
  - Deployed integrations to agreed source systems;  
  - Suite of priority dashboards and reports;  
  - Runbooks, technical documentation, and configuration guides.  
(e) Milestones:  
  - M3: Completion and Acceptance of “Minimum Viable Platform” (MVP) and first set of dashboards;  
  - M4: Completion and Acceptance of full integration scope and prioritized dashboards.

2.3.4 **Phase 4 – Stabilization, Training & Knowledge Transfer (Estimated Duration: 3 Months).**  
(a) Provide post-go-live stabilization support, incident resolution, and tuning of analytics performance, consistent with **Section 2.2(f) (Support and Maintenance)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(b) Deliver training, workshops, and documentation in line with **Section 2.2(e) (Training and Knowledge Transfer)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(c) Deliverables:  
  - Training materials and recordings;  
  - Knowledge transfer documentation;  
  - Final stabilization report, including incident statistics and performance metrics.  
(d) Milestone M5: Completion and Acceptance of Stabilization & Knowledge Transfer.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
In addition to the obligations in **Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement**, Vendor shall:

(a) Maintain a dedicated Program Manager and Technical Architect as Key Personnel;  
(b) Provide monthly written status reports including progress versus Milestones, risk and issue log, and updated Risk Register;  
(c) Attend steering committee meetings with Client’s senior stakeholders at least monthly, consistent with the governance referenced in **Section 2.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(d) Notify Client promptly of any circumstances likely to cause material delay to any Milestone, in accordance with **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement, and propose corrective actions.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client’s obligations under **Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement** apply in full and are supplemented as follows:

(a) Client shall provide timely access to subject matter experts from operations, finance, sales, and IT to support requirements gathering, design validation, and user acceptance testing;  
(b) Client shall make available non-production environments and sufficient test data, ensuring that any Client Personal Data used in lower environments is appropriately masked or pseudonymized, consistent with **Article XI of the Parent Agreement**;  
(c) Client shall provide decisions and approvals on key design choices within five (5) Business Days of Vendor’s request, failing which corresponding Milestones may be adjusted through a Change Order.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
The Deliverable acceptance mechanism in **Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement** applies and is further refined as follows:

(a) For each Milestone, Vendor shall submit the relevant Deliverables with an “Acceptance Package” summarizing scope, testing performed, and key outcomes;  
(b) Client shall have ten (10) Business Days (or such other period agreed in writing) to review Deliverables and either (i) accept in writing, or (ii) provide written notice of material non-conformities with reasonable details;  
(c) Vendor shall remedy any material non-conformities within a mutually agreed timeframe and resubmit for Acceptance;  
(d) If Client fails to respond within the review period, Acceptance shall be **deemed given** in accordance with **Section 2.7(d) of the Parent Agreement**, provided Vendor has issued a written reminder and five (5) additional Business Days have passed without response.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  
Vendor’s right to subcontract under **Section 2.8 of the Parent Agreement** applies. Given the high risk profile of this Work Order, Vendor shall seek Client’s prior written consent (email sufficient) before engaging any material subcontractor for work under this Work Order, and shall identify such subcontractors in Schedule 2 (if any).

2.8 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
This Work Order is non-exclusive as per **Section 2.9 of the Parent Agreement**. Client may engage other service providers for similar services, provided such engagement does not unreasonably interfere with Vendor’s performance.

To the extent SLA Services are provided under this Work Order, the Client’s right to terminate for persistent Service Level failures as described in **Section 4.5 of the Parent Agreement** shall apply specifically to the SLA Services under this Work Order.

(b) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information related to this Work Order in accordance with **Section 5.5 of the Parent Agreement**;

4.7 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  
If the Parent Agreement terminates or expires for any reason:

(a) This Work Order shall automatically terminate as of the effective date of termination of the Parent Agreement, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to maintain this Work Order under a successor or standalone agreement;  
(b) The provisions of **Section 4.6 of the Parent Agreement** and **Section 4.6 of this Work Order** shall govern payment and post-termination obligations with respect to this Work Order.

The survival provisions in **Section 4.7 of the Parent Agreement** apply to this Work Order.
