## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Addendum **supplements the Statement of Work dated January 27, 2024 (Ref: SOW-PIN-202401-521)** and is entered into **pursuant to and subject to** the terms and conditions of the Parent Agreement, except as expressly modified herein.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that **Article II (Scope of Services/Work)** of the Parent Agreement defines the baseline Services, including solution design, application development, data migration, testing, deployment, managed services, infrastructure support, and training (Sections 2.2.1 through 2.2.8). This Addendum adds a further layer of Extended Services and Enhanced Managed Services that (a) build upon the Deliverables and Milestones defined therein and (b) introduce additional capabilities, governance, and performance requirements.

2.2 **Description of Extended Services and Enhanced Managed Services.**  

2.2.1 **Advanced Platform Optimization and Automation.**  
In addition to the Services described in Section 2.2.2 (Application Development and Configuration) and Section 2.2.6 (Managed Services & Application Support) of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall:

  (a) Perform ongoing **performance profiling and optimization** of the enterprise application platform developed under SOW-PIN-202401-521, including code-level tuning, database query optimization, and infrastructure right-sizing, to maintain or improve performance benchmarks established during the Parent Agreement’s UAT (Section 2.2.4).

  (b) Design, develop, and implement **automation scripts and orchestration workflows** for repetitive operational tasks such as routine maintenance, patch deployment, backup validation, and log rotation, aiming to reduce manual intervention and incident frequency.

  (c) Implement **continuous integration and continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines**, where feasible, for enhancements and configuration changes, adhering to the change control procedures in **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement.

  (d) Provide **quarterly optimization reports** outlining actions taken, performance improvements achieved, and recommendations for further enhancements.

2.2.2 **Enhanced Monitoring, Observability, and Resilience.**  
Building on the infrastructure and hosting provisions in Section 2.2.7 of the Parent Agreement, if applicable, the Vendor shall:

  (a) Deploy and configure **advanced monitoring tools and observability frameworks** for the Client’s production, staging, and key non-production environments, including, at a minimum, metrics for CPU, memory, I/O, response times, transaction throughput, error rates, and integration health.

  (b) Establish and maintain **custom dashboards** for real-time visibility into system health, SLAs, and business-critical KPIs, accessible to designated Client stakeholders.

  (c) Implement and document **resilience patterns**, including failover configurations, redundancy, and automated recovery procedures, consistent with the disaster recovery and backup expectations referenced in Section 2.2.7(b) of the Parent Agreement.

  (d) Conduct **semi-annual resilience and disaster recovery exercises**, with pre-agreed test scenarios, and provide post-exercise reports including findings, issues identified, and remediation plans.

2.2.3 **Expansion of Managed Services & Application Support.**  
Further to Section 2.2.6 of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall:

  (a) Extend **support coverage** to include an agreed blend of Business Day and off-hours coverage (up to 24x7 for Priority 1 incidents, as further detailed in Article XII of this Addendum).

  (b) Introduce a **tiered incident triage and problem management framework**, including root cause analysis (RCA) for all recurring or severity-critical incidents, and maintain an RCA register shared with the Client.

  (c) Maintain and enhance a **knowledge base** for incident resolution, building upon the documentation described in Section 2.2.6(d) of the Parent Agreement, and ensure that new learnings are incorporated within ten (10) Business Days of incident closure.

  (d) Provide **monthly service review meetings** to review incident trends, SLA performance (as per Article XII of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum), and improvement initiatives.

2.2.4 **Extended Training and Adoption Services.**  
In addition to the training obligations described in Section 2.2.8 of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall:

  (a) Develop and deliver **advanced curriculum modules** for administrators, developers, and power users, including topics such as performance troubleshooting, release management, and advanced reporting.

  (b) Provide **onboarding training** for new Client personnel up to a quarterly cadence, either remotely or on-site as mutually agreed.

  (c) Facilitate **change adoption workshops** for business stakeholders to support adoption of new features and process changes implemented under the Extended Services.

2.2.5 **Strategic Advisory and Roadmap Services.**  

  (a) On at least a semi-annual basis, the Vendor shall conduct **strategic roadmap workshops** with the Client’s steering committee (as referenced in Section 2.2.1(d) of the Parent Agreement) to review current platform status, alignment with business priorities, and potential enhancements.

  (b) The Vendor shall produce a **rolling 12–18 month roadmap document** covering proposed enhancements, major releases, capacity forecasts, and risk mitigation actions.

2.3 **Additional Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.3 of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall provide at a minimum the following Additional Deliverables:

  (a) **Automation & Optimization Artifacts:** Automation scripts, runbooks, configuration templates, and CI/CD pipeline definitions, each with documentation sufficient for knowledgeable technical personnel of the Client to understand, operate, and maintain.

  (b) **Resilience and DR Documentation:** Updated disaster recovery plans, failover runbooks, architecture diagrams highlighting redundancy and failover flows, and reports from semi-annual resilience tests.

  (c) **Enhanced Reporting Pack:**  
   - Monthly SLA and incident performance reports (expanding Section 2.2.6(e) and Article XII of the Parent Agreement);  
   - Quarterly trend and RCA analysis reports; and  
   - Annual strategic review report summarizing overall health, risks, and recommendations.

  (d) **Training Materials:** Advanced training decks, e-learning modules, recordings (where permitted), and attendance records for all training and workshops delivered under this Addendum.

2.4 **Incremental Milestones and Timeline.**  

2.4.1 The Extended Services shall commence on the Addendum Effective Date and continue through the Addendum Term, subject to the Milestones below, which are in addition to (and do not replace) the Milestones set forth in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement:

  (a) **Incremental Milestone A – Monitoring & Observability Setup:**  
      - Target Completion: Within ninety (90) days of the Addendum Effective Date;  
      - Deliverables: Monitoring tools deployed, dashboards configured, initial baseline metrics defined.

  (b) **Incremental Milestone B – Automation Foundation:**  
      - Target Completion: Within one hundred eighty (180) days of the Addendum Effective Date;  
      - Deliverables: Initial automation scripts for core operational tasks, CI/CD pipeline prototype in non-production, and associated documentation.

  (c) **Incremental Milestone C – First Resilience Exercise & DR Plan Finalization:**  
      - Target Completion: Within two hundred seventy (270) days of the Addendum Effective Date;  
      - Deliverables: Completed test report, updated DR plan, remediation backlog.

  (d) **Incremental Milestone D – Enhanced Managed Services Steady State:**  
      - Target Completion: Within twelve (12) months of the Addendum Effective Date;  
      - Deliverables: Fully operational Enhanced Managed Services with agreed SLA Enhancements in production.

2.4.2 Milestone dates are **indicative** and may be adjusted by mutual agreement pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement and Article XIII of this Addendum.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

2.5.1 The Vendor shall perform the Extended Services in accordance with Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement and, in addition:

  (a) Ensure that automation and optimization activities do not materially disrupt production operations, unless scheduled and agreed as maintenance.

  (b) Conduct all resilience tests and training activities with prior written notice and in coordination with the Client’s designated project manager as identified under Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement.

2.5.2 In case of conflict between the performance standards in the Parent Agreement and this Addendum with respect to Extended Services, this Addendum shall prevail for such Extended Services only.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client’s obligations in Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement apply equally to the Extended Services, and, in addition, the Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to additional stakeholders as reasonably required for roadmap and strategic advisory sessions.

  (b) Ensure that key decision-makers attend the semi-annual strategic and DR/resilience workshops.

  (c) Review and provide feedback on optimization recommendations and RCAs in a timely manner to facilitate continuous improvement.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Addendum Contract Value.**  
The total additional contract value for the Extended Services and Additional Deliverables under this Addendum shall be **USD $3,194,730** (the **"Addendum Contract Value"**), exclusive of any applicable taxes and in addition to the **USD $2,907,957** Contract Value specified in Section 3.1 of the Parent Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure Under This Addendum.**  
The Addendum Contract Value shall be comprised of the following components:

  (a) **One-Time Setup and Enablement Fees:**  
      - Amount: **USD $1,250,000**  
      - Scope: Monitoring and observability setup, automation foundation, CI/CD pipelines, first resilience exercise, documentation, and initial advanced training.

  (b) **Recurring Enhanced Managed Services Fees:**  
      - Total Amount over Addendum Term: **USD $1,700,000**  
      - Structure: Recurring quarterly fees, as further detailed in Section 3.4 of this Addendum, for provision of Enhanced Managed Services described in Section 2.2.3.

  (c) **Contingency and Optional Services Pool (Addendum-Specific):**  
      - Amount: **USD $244,730**  
      - Purpose: To fund mutually agreed additional services, enhancements, or scope expansions related to Extended Services, to be documented via Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum.

3.3 **Milestone-Based Payments (One-Time Setup and Enablement).**  

3.3.1 The one-time setup and enablement fees specified in Section 3.2(a) shall be invoiced as follows, contingent on Client acceptance (in line with Section 2.5 of the Parent Agreement):

  (a) **Incremental Milestone A – Monitoring & Observability Setup:**  
      - Amount: 25% of setup fees = **USD $312,500** upon Client acceptance of monitoring tools and dashboards.

  (b) **Incremental Milestone B – Automation Foundation:**  
      - Amount: 35% of setup fees = **USD $437,500** upon Client acceptance of initial automation scripts and CI/CD pipeline prototype.

  (c) **Incremental Milestone C – First Resilience Exercise & DR Plan Finalization:**  
      - Amount: 25% of setup fees = **USD $312,500** upon Client acceptance of test report and updated DR plan.

  (d) **Incremental Milestone D – Enhanced Managed Services Steady State:**  
      - Amount: 15% of setup fees = **USD $187,500** upon Client confirmation that Enhanced Managed Services are fully operational.

3.4 **Recurring Enhanced Managed Services Fees.**  

3.4.1 The **USD $1,700,000** in recurring Enhanced Managed Services Fees shall be invoiced in equal quarterly installments over the Addendum Term, commencing on the later of:

  (a) The date of Incremental Milestone D acceptance; or  
  (b) The date mutually agreed by the Parties in writing if steady-state Enhanced Managed Services commence earlier.

3.4.2 For illustration only (and not as a waiver of exact proration), assuming a full three-year period of recurring services, quarterly invoices would approximate **USD $141,666.67** (exclusive of taxes), subject to actual start and end dates and any applicable Change Orders.

3.5 **Contingency and Optional Services.**  

3.5.1 The **Contingency and Optional Services Pool** defined in Section 3.2(c) shall be utilized only upon:

  (a) Submission of a Change Request by either Party;  
  (b) Issuance of a corresponding Change Estimate by the Vendor; and  
  (c) Execution of a Change Order in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement.

3.5.2 Fees drawn against the pool shall be invoiced as work is performed, with appropriate references to the applicable Change Order.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
The provisions of Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement regarding taxes and withholding shall apply mutatis mutandis to this Addendum. All amounts stated in this Addendum are exclusive of any applicable taxes.

3.7 **Invoicing and Payment Terms for This Addendum.**  

3.7.1 **Payment Terms.** Notwithstanding Section 3.7(b) of the Parent Agreement (Net 30), all invoices issued under this Addendum shall be payable by the Client within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of invoice (**"Net 45"**), unless otherwise agreed in a Change Order.

3.7.2 **Invoice References.** Each invoice relating to this Addendum shall:

  (a) Reference both the **Parent Agreement** (SOW-PIN-202401-521, contract_521) and this **Addendum (ADD-PIN-202401-572)**;  
  (b) Identify the Milestone, recurring services period, or Change Order to which the invoice relates; and  
  (c) Provide sufficient detail (including hours, resources, and service descriptions) to substantiate the amounts invoiced.

3.8 **Disputed Invoices.**  

3.8.1 Section 3.8 of the Parent Agreement applies equally to invoices under this Addendum, except that any reference therein to the Agreement shall be construed to include this Addendum.

3.9 **Late Payment.**  

3.9.1 Section 3.9 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to late payment of any undisputed amounts due under this Addendum. ## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Addendum Term.**  
This Addendum shall commence on the **Addendum Effective Date (September 09, 2024)** and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent Agreement, shall remain in effect until **August 15, 2027** (the "**Addendum Expiration Date**").

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.**  

4.2.1 If the Parent Agreement expires on January 20, 2027, as per Section 4.1 thereof, but is renewed pursuant to Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement, this Addendum shall continue throughout its Addendum Term, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.2.2 If the Parent Agreement is **not** renewed and expires, the Parties shall:

  (a) Cooperatively determine whether any Extended Services require continuation through the Addendum Expiration Date; and  
  (b) Either (i) allow this Addendum to continue as a standalone statement of work under the same legal and commercial terms of the Parent Agreement (as incorporated by reference), or (ii) mutually agree in writing to terminate or replace this Addendum, subject to Section 4.6.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Addendum, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause by providing not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party.

4.3.2 Upon termination for convenience by the Client:

  (a) The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Extended Services performed and Additional Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination; and  
  (b) The Client shall reimburse the Vendor for any non-cancellable commitments properly incurred in reliance on this Addendum, subject to the Vendor’s mitigation obligations.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Addendum immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Addendum or the Parent Agreement with respect to the Extended Services and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration of Parent Agreement.**  

4.5.1 If the Parent Agreement is terminated for cause or for persistent SLA failures under Sections 4.4 or 4.5 of the Parent Agreement, this Addendum shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue certain Extended Services under new or modified arrangements.

4.5.2 Upon any termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement and/or this Addendum:

  (a) The Vendor shall cease all Extended Services, except as reasonably required to provide Transition Services;  
  (b) The Vendor shall deliver to the Client all completed and in-progress Additional Deliverables, Client Materials, and relevant documentation relating to the Extended Services, subject to payment of undisputed amounts due; and  
  (c) Sections 4.6 and 4.7 of the Parent Agreement, together with Section 4.6 of this Addendum, shall govern Transition Services.

4.6 **Transition Services.**  

4.6.1 In addition to Section 4.7 of the Parent Agreement, upon expiration or termination of this Addendum (other than termination by the Vendor due to Client’s material breach), the Vendor shall, upon the Client’s written request, provide **Transition Services** for up to ninety (90) days (or such longer period as may be agreed in writing), at the Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates or as otherwise agreed.

4.6.2 Transition Services may include knowledge transfer, documentation handover, assistance with migration of monitoring tools and automation scripts, and reasonable cooperation with any successor provider.

4.7 **Survival.**  
All provisions of this Addendum that by their nature should survive expiration or termination (including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XII, XIV, XV, and Sections 3.6, 3.7, 4.5, and 4.6) shall survive and remain in full force and effect, in addition to the survival provisions in Section 4.8 of the Parent Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement SLAs.**  
The SLA commitments in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement remain in effect for the Services defined therein. This Article XII introduces **SLA Enhancements** applicable specifically to the Extended Services and Enhanced Managed Services.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment for Enhanced Managed Services.**  

12.2.1 For hosted or managed components under the Vendor’s control that are part of the Extended Services, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure system availability of at least **99.95%** per calendar month, measured at the primary production environment level.

12.2.2 This higher uptime commitment supplements, and for the Extended Services supersedes, the **99.9%** commitment in Section 12.1 of the Parent Agreement.

12.3 **Support Response and Resolution Targets.**  
For Enhanced Managed Services, the following response and resolution targets shall apply, refining the targets in Section 12.3 of the Parent Agreement:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):**  
    - Definition: Complete system outage or major business-critical functionality unavailable with no workaround, impacting a large proportion of users;  
    - Response: within **30 minutes**;  
    - Target resolution or workaround: within **4 hours**.

  - **Priority 2 (High):**  
    - Definition: Significant degradation of service or critical functionality impaired but with temporary workaround;  
    - Response: within **1 hour**;  
    - Target resolution: within **8 Business Hours**.

  - **Priority 3 (Medium):**  
    - Definition: Non-critical issue or partial impairment with acceptable workaround;  
    - Response: within **4 Business Hours**;  
    - Target resolution: within **3 Business Days**.

  - **Priority 4 (Low):**  
    - Definition: Cosmetic issues, minor inconveniences, or general inquiries;  
    - Response: within **1 Business Day**;  
    - Target resolution: in next scheduled maintenance window or as mutually agreed.

12.4 **Service Credits Under This Addendum.**  

12.4.1 If the Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.2 for the Extended Services in a given calendar month, the Client shall be entitled to the following **Addendum Service Credits**, which are in addition to any service credits under Section 12.5 of the Parent Agreement, provided that they shall not be duplicated for the same incident or outage:

  (a) For each 0.05% shortfall below 99.95% but at or above 99.5%, a service credit equal to **2%** of that month’s Enhanced Managed Services Fees;  

  (b) For availability below 99.5%, an additional service credit of **10%** of that month’s Enhanced Managed Services Fees.

12.4.2 Service credits shall be applied to future invoices under this Addendum and shall be the Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures related to Extended Services, without prejudice to the Client’s termination rights under Section 4.5 of the Parent Agreement and Section 4.3 of this Addendum.

12.5 **Reporting.**  
Monthly SLA reports required under Section 12.4 of the Parent Agreement shall be expanded to include all metrics relevant to the SLA Enhancements, including uptime, incident response and resolution performance, and details of any service credits earned.
