## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF MAINTENANCE SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  
2.1.1 This Maintenance Agreement is executed under and pursuant to the Parent Agreement (SLA-ACM-202507-430, contract_430), and is intended to supplement and, where expressly stated herein, clarify or refine the Maintenance and Support Services referred to in **Section 1.14**, **Article II**, and **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement.  

2.1.2 In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Maintenance Agreement and the Parent Agreement concerning Maintenance and Support Services, **the terms of this Maintenance Agreement shall prevail**, but solely with respect to Maintenance Services, Service Levels, and related Fees. All other terms of the Parent Agreement shall remain in full force and effect.

2.2 **Overview of Maintenance Services.**  
During the Term (as defined in **Article IV** of this Maintenance Agreement), Acme shall provide Maintenance Services for the Covered Software, including:

  (a) **Corrective Maintenance:** Identification, analysis, and resolution of Errors, including provision of patches, hotfixes, or configuration changes to restore materially conforming functionality as warranted in **Section 7.2(a)** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (b) **Preventive Maintenance:** Deployment of maintenance releases intended to prevent or reduce the occurrence of future Errors, performance issues, or security vulnerabilities, including tuning and optimization measures.  

  (c) **Updates and Upgrades:** Delivery and installation (in a SaaS context, deployment to the production environment) of new releases, minor and major updates, and enhancements to the Covered Software that Acme generally makes available to its enterprise customers under similar maintenance arrangements, consistent with **Section 6.1** and **Section 6.4** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (d) **Technical Support Services:** Provision of remote technical support, including Incident logging, troubleshooting, workarounds, root cause analysis, and advice on the use and configuration of the Covered Software.  

  (e) **Service Level Monitoring and Reporting:** Monitoring the availability and performance of the production environment as required to comply with the **99.9% availability** commitment in **Section 12.1** of the Parent Agreement (as further detailed herein), and providing periodic reports on Service Level attainment.  

  (f) **Knowledge Transfer and Documentation Updates:** Maintenance of up-to-date Documentation relevant to the Covered Software and its updates, and reasonable knowledge transfer to Contoso’s designated support personnel regarding new features or changes materially impacting operations.

2.3 **In-Scope Software and Environments.**  
2.3.1 Maintenance Services shall apply to the production instance(s) and agreed non-production instances (e.g., UAT, staging) of the Covered Software operated by Acme under the SaaS deployment model described in **Section 2.3** of the Parent Agreement.  

2.3.2 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Maintenance Services do not cover: (i) custom code or custom integrations developed by third parties without Acme’s involvement; (ii) Contoso’s internal systems not forming part of the Covered Software; or (iii) third-party software licensed directly by Contoso from other vendors.

2.4 **Incident Management Process.**  
Acme shall operate an Incident management process that includes:

  (a) **Logging and Acknowledgment:** Contoso shall log Incidents through the agreed support portal, email, or phone line. Acme shall acknowledge Incidents within the applicable Response Time based on the severity levels defined in **Section 12.4** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 12.4** of this Maintenance Agreement.  

  (b) **Classification and Prioritization:** Incidents shall be classified as Priority 1–4, consistent with **Section 12.4** of the Parent Agreement. Contoso and Acme shall cooperate in good faith to assign a reasonable priority level based on business impact.  

  (c) **Diagnosis and Resolution:** Acme shall diagnose the Incident, propose and implement workarounds where appropriate, and work toward permanent resolution within commercially reasonable Resolution Times, taking into account the complexity of the issue and dependencies on third-party components.  

  (d) **Escalation:** If Acme is unable to resolve a Priority 1 or Priority 2 Incident within the expected Resolution Time, the Incident shall be escalated internally within Acme and, if necessary, to joint senior management teams of both Parties in accordance with the escalation procedures in **Article XIV** of the Parent Agreement.

2.5 **Exclusions from Maintenance Services.**  
Maintenance Services do not include:

  (a) Development of new features or substantial enhancements requested solely by Contoso that are not included in Acme’s standard roadmap;  

  (b) Custom development, integration, or migration services that would ordinarily be delivered under a separate **Statement of Work** as described in **Section 1.19** and **Article II** of the Parent Agreement;  

  (c) Remediation of Incidents caused by: (i) Contoso’s misuse of the Software or use in violation of **Section 6.3 (License Restrictions)** of the Parent Agreement; (ii) unauthorized modifications made by Contoso or third parties; (iii) hardware, network, or third-party system failures outside Acme’s reasonable control; or (iv) failure by Contoso to implement updates or releases provided by Acme, in alignment with **Section 7.3** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (d) On-site support, which, if requested by Contoso, shall be provided as professional services under separate SOWs, subject to the fee structure described in **Sections 3.2(d)** and **3.5** of the Parent Agreement.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  
2.6.1 Acme shall perform the Maintenance Services:

  (a) In a professional and workmanlike manner and in accordance with **Section 2.6** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (b) Using personnel appropriately skilled and trained on Acme Enterprise Suite;  
  (c) In a manner designed to achieve or exceed the Service Levels in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement and this Maintenance Agreement; and  
  (d) In material compliance with the Documentation and applicable policies communicated to Contoso.

2.6.2 Contoso acknowledges, consistent with **Section 2.6** and **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement, that Acme’s ability to deliver effective Maintenance Services depends on Contoso’s timely cooperation, including providing accurate information, access to relevant log data, and prompt testing of fixes or workarounds in non-production environments.

2.7 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
In support of the Maintenance Services, Contoso shall:

  (a) Designate qualified personnel to act as primary contacts for Incident reporting and coordination;  
  (b) Provide timely and accurate information, logs, screenshots, and other data as reasonably requested by Acme to diagnose Incidents;  
  (c) Maintain compliant end-user devices, connectivity, and browser configurations as reasonably specified in the Documentation;  
  (d) Implement and test updates and releases in its UAT or staging environments within reasonable timelines following Acme’s notification, where applicable; and  
  (e) Escalate urgent business issues promptly through the agreed channels.

2.8 **Relationship to Implementation Services and Deliverables.**  
2.8.1 The Parties acknowledge that Acme has provided or will provide implementation services and Deliverables as described in **Sections 2.4 and 2.5** of the Parent Agreement. This Maintenance Agreement governs the ongoing maintenance of those implemented configurations and integrations that form part of the Covered Software and are supported by Acme as part of its standard offering.  

2.8.2 Any material re-implementation work, redesign of configurations, or development of new integration components requested by Contoso shall not be deemed Maintenance Services and shall be subject to a separate SOW under the Parent Agreement.

2.9 **Service Documentation and Reporting.**  
Acme shall, upon Contoso’s reasonable request and not more than once per calendar quarter (unless otherwise agreed or required by an SLA failure):

  (a) Provide a report summarizing Incidents logged, response and resolution performance, and Service Levels achieved;  
  (b) Document major root causes and agreed preventive actions for Priority 1 and Priority 2 Incidents; and  
  (c) Provide a forward-looking schedule of planned major updates or upgrades that may impact Contoso’s operations.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated value of this Maintenance Agreement (the **“Contract Value”**) is **Nine Hundred Fifty-Eight Thousand Two Hundred Thirty-Two United States Dollars (US$958,232)**, which relates primarily to ongoing Maintenance Services and associated Service Levels for the Covered Software during the Term, and is separate from (but complementary to) the **Contract Value of US$4,605,619** under **Section 3.1** of the Parent Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Components.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the Contract Value under this Maintenance Agreement shall be comprised of the following components (illustrative structure):

  (a) **Annual Maintenance Fees:**  
  A base annual Maintenance Fee of **US$191,646.40** per Contract Year for five (5) Contract Years, totaling approximately US$958,232, subject to standard indexation or adjustments, if any, agreed in writing.  

  (b) **Optional Extended Support Fees:**  
  Additional fees for any extended support services beyond the standard coverage described in this Maintenance Agreement, if requested by Contoso and agreed by the Parties through a Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII** of this Maintenance Agreement and **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (c) **Out-of-Scope Services:**  
  Any out-of-scope professional services requested by Contoso (e.g., on-site support, custom development, special consulting) shall be billed separately on a time-and-materials basis pursuant to **Sections 3.2(d)** and **3.5** of the Parent Agreement, and are not included in the Contract Value stated above.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
3.3.1 Unless otherwise agreed in a written schedule, Acme shall invoice the annual Maintenance Fees **annually in advance**, on or about the first day of each Contract Year during the Term, following the structure of **Section 3.4** of the Parent Agreement but with the modified payment terms below.  

3.3.2 All undisputed invoices issued under this Maintenance Agreement shall be payable by Contoso within **forty-five (45) calendar days** from the date of invoice (**“Net 45”**), which replaces the **“Net 30”** term provided in **Section 3.6** of the Parent Agreement solely with respect to invoices issued under this Maintenance Agreement.  

3.3.3 Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other method reasonably designated by Acme, consistent with **Section 3.6** of the Parent Agreement.

3.4 **Late Payments.**  
3.4.1 Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, in line with **Section 3.7** of the Parent Agreement. Interest will be calculated on a daily basis.  

3.4.2 If any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than **sixty (60) days** after its due date, Acme may, upon written notice to Contoso and without liability, suspend Maintenance Services and/or access to the Covered Software in accordance with **Section 3.7** of the Parent Agreement, provided that Acme shall resume Services promptly upon receipt of all overdue amounts and accrued interest.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
All Fees under this Maintenance Agreement are exclusive of Taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all Taxes (other than Taxes imposed on Acme’s income, property, or employees) in accordance with **Section 3.8** of the Parent Agreement. If withholding is required, Contoso shall comply with the obligations set forth in **Section 3.8**.

3.6 **Expenses.**  
Maintenance Services are expected to be performed remotely. Any travel or out-of-pocket expenses for on-site support or other activities requested by Contoso and approved by Contoso in advance shall be reimbursed in accordance with **Section 3.9** of the Parent Agreement.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, it shall notify Acme in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying in reasonable detail the basis for the dispute, as contemplated in **Section 3.10** of the Parent Agreement. Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with **Section 3.3** above, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Maintenance Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **March 04, 2030** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or as required by the Parent Agreement.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Maintenance Agreement for additional renewal terms on such terms and conditions as the Parties may agree in writing, consistent with the renewal process in **Section 4.2** of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
4.3.1 **By Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Maintenance Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience without cause by providing at least **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Acme.  

4.3.2 **By Acme.** Acme may terminate this Maintenance Agreement for convenience by providing at least **ninety (90) days’ prior written notice** to Contoso, consistent with **Section 4.4** of the Parent Agreement, provided that Acme shall refund any prepaid Maintenance Fees for Maintenance Services not yet performed as of the effective date of termination, prorated on a daily basis.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement for cause in accordance with the following:

  (a) If the other Party commits a material breach of this Maintenance Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail, the non-breaching Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement upon written notice, consistent with **Section 4.5(a)** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (b) Without limiting the foregoing, Acme may terminate this Maintenance Agreement immediately upon written notice if Contoso is in material breach of its payment obligations under this Maintenance Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice, consistent with **Section 4.5(b)(i)** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (c) Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, is placed in the hands of a receiver, enters bankruptcy or reorganization proceedings, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course, consistent with **Section 4.5(c)** of the Parent Agreement.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  
4.5.1 This Maintenance Agreement is contingent upon the continued effectiveness of the Parent Agreement. If the Parent Agreement expires or is terminated for any reason, this Maintenance Agreement shall automatically terminate on the effective date of such expiration or termination, without separate notice, except to the limited extent transition assistance is requested pursuant to **Section 4.7** of the Parent Agreement and mirrored in **Section 4.7** below.  

4.5.2 In the event of early termination of the Parent Agreement, any prepaid Maintenance Fees attributable to periods after such termination shall be refunded by Acme on a pro rata basis, except where termination was due to Contoso’s uncured material breach, in which case no refund shall be required except as mandated by applicable law.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of this Maintenance Agreement.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Maintenance Agreement for any reason:

  (a) Acme’s obligation to provide Maintenance Services and Service Levels shall cease as of the effective date of termination, but Contoso’s License to use the Software under **Article VI** of the Parent Agreement shall continue subject to the terms of the Parent Agreement, unless the Parent Agreement has also expired or been terminated;  

  (b) All amounts accrued or invoiced for Maintenance Services up to the effective date of termination shall become immediately due and payable, subject to any refunds expressly stipulated herein;  

  (c) The Parties shall, at Contoso’s request, cooperate in providing transition assistance as described in **Section 4.7** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 4.7** below; and  

  (d) The provisions of this Maintenance Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive (including Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and Sections 3.4, 4.5, 4.6, and 16.11) shall survive expiration or termination.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
At Contoso’s written request received at least thirty (30) days prior to expiration or termination of this Maintenance Agreement, Acme shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination or expiration, including export of relevant Client Data and reasonable support in handing over operational knowledge to Contoso or a successor service provider, in a manner consistent with **Section 4.7** of the Parent Agreement. Such assistance shall be provided at Acme’s then-current professional services rates unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Acme shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the Covered Software is available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Events, consistent with **Section 12.1** of the Parent Agreement. The calculation of uptime and exclusions shall follow **Sections 12.2 and 12.3** of the Parent Agreement.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
12.2.1 Acme may perform Scheduled Maintenance during agreed maintenance windows, typically outside Contoso’s core business hours where reasonably practicable.  

12.2.2 Acme shall provide Contoso with at least **forty-eight (48) hours’ advance notice** of any Scheduled Maintenance expected to cause a material service interruption, in accordance with **Section 12.2** of the Parent Agreement.

12.3 **Excluded Events.**  
The exclusions from uptime calculations set out in **Section 12.3** of the Parent Agreement (Scheduled Maintenance, Force Majeure Events, Contoso-caused downtime, third-party systems failures) shall apply equally here.

12.4 **Support Services – Priorities and Response.**  
Acme shall provide support as follows, consistent with and elaborating on **Section 12.4** of the Parent Agreement:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Production system down or severely impacted; no reasonable workaround exists.  
    - Initial Response Time: within **1 hour**, 24x7.  
    - Work Continuity: continuous efforts until resolution or workaround, with status updates at least every 2 hours.  

  - **Priority 2 (High):** Major functionality impacted but system is operational; reasonable workaround may exist.  
    - Initial Response Time: within **4 hours** during Business Hours.  
    - Work Continuity: work during Support Hours until resolution or workaround, with daily status updates.  

  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues; partial loss of non-essential functionality.  
    - Initial Response Time: within **1 Business Day**.  

  - **Priority 4 (Low):** General inquiries, cosmetic issues, or minor inconveniences.  
    - Initial Response Time: within **2 Business Days**.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
12.5.1 If Acme fails to meet the 99.9% monthly uptime commitment, Contoso shall be entitled to Service Credits as defined in **Section 12.5** of the Parent Agreement and calculated as follows:

  - 99.0%–99.9% availability: 5% of the monthly Maintenance Fee as credit;  
  - 98.0%–99.0% availability: 10% of the monthly Maintenance Fee as credit;  
  - Less than 98.0% availability: 20% of the monthly Maintenance Fee as credit.

12.5.2 Service Credits shall be applied against future Maintenance Fee invoices and shall constitute Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet uptime Service Levels, except in cases of Acme’s willful misconduct or gross negligence, consistent with **Section 12.5** of the Parent Agreement.

12.6 **Claims for Service Credits.**  
To receive Service Credits, Contoso must submit a written claim within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the SLA failure occurred, with reasonable supporting details, in accordance with **Section 12.6** of the Parent Agreement. Failure to submit in time constitutes a waiver of rights for that month.
