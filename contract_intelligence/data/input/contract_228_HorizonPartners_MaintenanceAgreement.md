## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Client comprehensive maintenance and support services for the Client’s facilities, equipment, and systems, which may include, without limitation: mechanical systems (HVAC, pumps, motors), electrical systems (power distribution panels, lighting, emergency power), building management systems (BMS), security systems (access control, CCTV), and related IT and monitoring infrastructure (collectively, the “Facilities and Systems”). The Services may be further defined and customized in one or more SOWs executed pursuant to this Agreement.

2.2 **Preventive Maintenance.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall design, implement, and maintain a preventive maintenance program for the Facilities and Systems. Such program shall be based on manufacturer recommendations, industry best practices, and the Client’s operational requirements.  
(b) Preventive maintenance activities shall include, as applicable: inspections, testing, cleaning, lubrication, calibration, adjustments, replacement of consumables, minor repairs, and such other tasks as are reasonably necessary to minimize downtime and ensure safe and efficient operation.  
(c) The Service Provider shall provide the Client with a written preventive maintenance schedule (monthly, quarterly, semiannual, and annual tasks) within thirty (30) days following the Effective Date of the applicable SOW. The schedule shall identify the equipment covered, the maintenance tasks to be performed, and the expected duration and any anticipated disruption to operations.  
(d) The Service Provider shall coordinate all preventive maintenance activities with the Client’s designated contact to minimize disruption to the Client’s operations, and shall, where practicable, perform such activities during off‑peak hours or as otherwise directed by the Client.

2.3 **Corrective and Remedial Maintenance.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall provide corrective and remedial maintenance services in response to failures, malfunctions, alerts, or other events affecting the Facilities and Systems.  
(b) Upon receiving a service request or incident ticket from the Client, the Service Provider shall respond, diagnose, and remediate the issue within the response and resolution times specified in Article XII.  
(c) Corrective maintenance may include: troubleshooting, repair, replacement of parts and components, emergency interventions, and system tuning.  
(d) Where immediate repair is not possible, the Service Provider shall provide temporary work‑around solutions designed to reduce operational impact until a permanent fix can be implemented.

2.4 **Monitoring and Support Services.**  
(a) Where specified in an SOW, the Service Provider shall remotely monitor key systems (e.g., BMS, critical environmental conditions, power quality) to proactively detect and address potential issues.  
(b) The Service Provider shall provide a helpdesk or support line available during the support hours set forth in the applicable SOW (which may include 24x7 support for critical systems), and shall maintain a ticketing system to log, track, and report incidents and requests.  
(c) The Service Provider shall provide the Client with access to periodic performance and incident reports, as specified in Section 2.7.

2.5 **Deliverables.**  
The Service Provider shall provide the following types of Deliverables, in such detail and frequency as may be specified in the applicable SOW:  
(a) **Maintenance Logs and Reports:** Detailed logs of preventive and corrective maintenance, including date, time, tasks performed, parts utilized, technician name, and observations.  
(b) **Asset Inventory and Condition Reports:** An up‑to‑date inventory of the covered equipment and systems, with condition assessments, risk ratings, and recommended actions.  
(c) **Service Level Reports:** Monthly or quarterly reports summarizing Service Level performance (uptime, response times, resolution times), incident trends, and any Service Credits earned.  
(d) **Compliance Documentation:** Where required, documentation of maintenance activities and system performance necessary for regulatory or internal compliance (e.g., environmental, safety, quality audits).  
(e) **Change and Improvement Recommendations:** Periodic recommendations for system upgrades, process improvements, and cost‑efficiency measures.

2.6 **Service Standards.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner consistent with (i) generally accepted industry standards for similar services, (ii) applicable laws and regulations, and (iii) the Client’s reasonable policies and procedures provided in writing to the Service Provider.  
(b) All personnel of the Service Provider assigned to perform the Services shall be appropriately trained, qualified, and, where required by law or regulation, licensed or certified to perform the tasks assigned.  
(c) The Service Provider shall use new or equivalent‑to‑new parts that meet or exceed manufacturer specifications, unless otherwise approved in writing by the Client.  
(d) The Service Provider shall comply with all security, safety, and site access protocols provided by the Client and shall ensure that its personnel and Subcontractors do the same.

2.7 **Reporting and Meetings.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall provide the Client with periodic reports (at least quarterly, unless otherwise agreed) detailing maintenance activities, incidents, Service Level performance, root‑cause analyses for major incidents, and continuous improvement actions.  
(b) The Parties shall participate in regular service review meetings (monthly or quarterly, as specified in the applicable SOW) to review performance, discuss upcoming maintenance activities, address any operational issues, and agree on action items.  
(c) The Service Provider shall maintain a complete record of all Services performed for a minimum of five (5) years after completion of the relevant Services and shall make such records available to the Client for inspection upon reasonable notice.

2.8 **Exclusions.**  
(a) Unless specifically included in an SOW, the Services do not include: (i) major capital replacements or upgrades; (ii) repairs required due to misuse, vandalism, or unauthorized modifications by the Client or third parties; (iii) services necessitated by Force Majeure Events; or (iv) design professional services such as architectural or engineering design.  
(b) Any services outside the defined Scope shall be considered out‑of‑scope and shall be subject to a Change Order under Article XIII before the Service Provider is obligated to perform them.

2.9 **Client Responsibilities.**  
(a) The Client shall provide the Service Provider with reasonable access to the Facilities and Systems during agreed times and shall ensure that any necessary permits, passes, or authorizations are obtained.  
(b) The Client shall provide relevant documentation, manuals, system information, and contact details reasonably required for the Service Provider to perform the Services.  
(c) The Client shall promptly notify the Service Provider of any issues or incidents affecting the Facilities and Systems that may require the Service Provider’s attention.  
(d) The Client shall not materially interfere with or impede the Service Provider’s performance of the Services, and any delays caused by the Client may be addressed through appropriate timeline and fee adjustments under Article XIII.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees.**  
(a) In consideration of the Services and Deliverables, the Client shall pay the Service Provider fees not to exceed the Contract Value of **US$4,053,158**, subject to any duly executed Change Orders.  
(b) The detailed fee schedule, including fixed fees, time-and-materials rates, and any unit pricing for specific services or components, shall be set forth in one or more SOWs referencing this Agreement.  
(c) Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, all hourly or daily rates shall be stated in U.S. Dollars and shall include all labor, overhead, and ordinary business costs.

3.2 **Payment Milestones.**  
(a) Fees may be structured as a combination of (i) recurring monthly or quarterly maintenance fees, and (ii) project‑based or milestone‑based fees for specific initiatives (e.g., major overhauls or upgrades).  
(b) Each SOW shall specify the payment schedule. By way of example:  
   - Twenty percent (20%) upon commencement of Services under the SOW;  
   - Sixty percent (60%) in equal monthly installments over the term of the SOW; and  
   - Twenty percent (20%) upon completion of the Services and acceptance of the Deliverables for the applicable period.  
(c) If an SOW does not specify a payment schedule, the default shall be monthly invoicing in arrears for Services rendered and expenses incurred during the prior month.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall submit detailed invoices to the Client in accordance with the applicable SOW. Each invoice shall reference this Agreement, the relevant SOW, the Contract Reference No. **MNT-HOR-202509-228**, and shall itemize Services and expenses.  
(b) The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** of the Client’s receipt of a correct and complete invoice (“**Net 30**”).  
(c) The Client shall have the right to withhold payment of any disputed portion of an invoice, provided that the Client (i) notifies the Service Provider in writing of the dispute within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, and (ii) pays the undisputed portion in accordance with the payment terms. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any invoice disputes promptly.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
(a) Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.  
(b) The accrual of interest shall not limit the Service Provider’s other rights and remedies under this Agreement; provided, however, that prior to suspending Services due to non‑payment, the Service Provider shall provide the Client with at least ten (10) Business Days’ written notice and an opportunity to cure.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) The Client shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket expenses actually incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and materials, to the extent specified in the applicable SOW.  
(b) The Service Provider shall comply with the Client’s travel and expense policies provided in advance in writing, and shall provide documentation and receipts for all reimbursable expenses.  
(c) No markup shall be applied to pass‑through expenses unless expressly stated in an SOW.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
(a) The fees payable under this Agreement do not include any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes, which, if applicable, shall be added to the invoices and paid by the Client, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s income, profits, or property.  
(b) Each Party shall be responsible for its own income and employment taxes.

3.7 **No Set‑Off.**  
Except as expressly permitted in this Agreement or agreed in writing by the Parties, the Client shall not set off, withhold, or otherwise deduct from any payment any amounts allegedly owed to it by the Service Provider; provided, however, that this Section 3.7 shall not limit the Client’s rights regarding disputed invoices as described in Section 3.3(c).

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in full force and effect until **July 30, 2030** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (such period, the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, upon mutual written agreement at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend this Agreement for additional periods on terms to be agreed in writing. Absent such written agreement, the Agreement shall expire automatically on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
(a) The Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider.  
(b) The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Client.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer period as the non‑breaching Party may authorize in writing) after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.  
(b) Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent, (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (iv) ceases to carry on its business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
(a) Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:  
   (i) the Service Provider shall cease performing the applicable Services;  
   (ii) the Client shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and expenses properly incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to any good faith disputes; and  
   (iii) the Service Provider shall promptly return or, at the Client’s written request, destroy the Client’s Confidential Information and other property, in accordance with Section 5.6.  
(b) If the Client terminates this Agreement or any SOW for cause, the Client shall have no obligation to pay for Services that are the subject of the breach to the extent not cured or properly performed.  
(c) If the Client terminates for convenience, the Client shall pay (i) all fees for Services properly performed to the termination date, and (ii) any non‑cancelable, pre‑approved costs that the Service Provider has incurred specifically for the Client and cannot reasonably mitigate.  
(d) Termination or expiration shall not affect any rights, remedies, obligations, or liabilities of either Party that have accrued up to the date of termination or expiration, nor any provisions which by their nature are intended to survive (including, without limitation, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and Sections 3.4, 4.5, and 16.6).

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
At the Client’s request, the Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days following termination or expiration, at the then‑applicable rates or as otherwise agreed in writing, to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to the Client or to a replacement service provider.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
Where the Services involve system monitoring, remote access, or other technology components, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the monitored systems or services under its control achieve **99.9%** uptime per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
(a) The Service Provider may schedule maintenance windows during which certain systems or Services may be unavailable, provided that the Service Provider:  
   (i) provides the Client with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of scheduled maintenance; and  
   (ii) schedules such maintenance during off‑peak hours where reasonably practicable.  
(b) Scheduled maintenance time shall not count as downtime for purposes of the uptime calculation.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times for incidents reported by the Client, unless more stringent Service Levels are specified in the applicable SOW:

- **Priority 1 (Critical)**: Complete system or service outage or major impact on critical operations.  
  - Response time: within 1 hour  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High)**: Significant degradation or partial outage impacting important operations.  
  - Response time: within 2 hours  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 8 hours  

- **Priority 3 (Medium)**: Limited impact issues with reasonable workarounds available.  
  - Response time: within 8 Business Hours  
  - Target resolution: within 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low)**: Minor issues, cosmetic issues, or routine requests.  
  - Response time: within 2 Business Days  
  - Target resolution: within 10 Business Days

12.4 **Measurement and Reporting.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall maintain accurate records of uptime, incident response, and resolution times, and shall provide periodic Service Level reports to the Client (at least quarterly) or as specified in the applicable SOW.  
(b) The Parties shall review performance against the SLAs during periodic service review meetings and agree on corrective actions as needed.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
(a) If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment or response/resolution targets set forth in this Article XII for a given month, the Client may be eligible for Service Credits, calculated as a percentage of the monthly recurring fees for the affected Services, as specified in the applicable SOW.  
(b) Service Credits shall be applied as a credit against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash.  
(c) The Service Provider’s obligation to provide Service Credits shall be contingent upon the Client notifying the Service Provider in writing of the SLA breach within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the breach occurred.

12.6 **Exclusive Remedies.**  
Service Credits shall be the Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches, subject to the overall limitations of liability in Article IX; provided, however, that repeated or material SLA failures may constitute a material breach of this Agreement for purposes of Section 4.4.

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil disturbances, strikes or labor disputes (excluding strikes or labor disputes involving the affected Party’s own workforce), embargoes, government actions, or failures of public utilities or telecommunications.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  
(c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of developments.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration such performance is prevented or materially hindered by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
