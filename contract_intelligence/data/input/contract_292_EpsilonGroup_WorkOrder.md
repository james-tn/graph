## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall perform for the Client a comprehensive set of **digital transformation and managed IT services** designed to modernize the Client’s infrastructure, enhance operational efficiency, and support data-driven decision making throughout the Client’s organization. The Services shall include, without limitation:

- Assessment and design of a cloud-based target architecture;  
- Implementation and migration of selected systems and data to cloud hosting environments;  
- Deployment and configuration of managed network, security, and endpoint management solutions;  
- Provision of ongoing managed support, monitoring, and incident management;  
- Implementation and support of a data analytics platform including data integration, data warehousing, and reporting; and  
- Program management, training, and knowledge transfer.

2.2 **Phase I – Discovery and Assessment.**

2.2.1 The Service Provider shall conduct a detailed discovery and assessment phase lasting approximately three (3) months from the Effective Date, during which it shall:

(a) Perform interviews and workshops with key stakeholders designated by the Client;  
(b) Review the Client’s current infrastructure, applications, data flows, security posture, and support processes;  
(c) Identify key business requirements, priorities, constraints, and success criteria; and  
(d) Produce an **Assessment Report** and **Target State Architecture Document** (collectively, the **“Phase I Deliverables”**).

2.2.2 The Phase I Deliverables shall include:

- A comprehensive inventory of systems, applications, data stores, and dependencies;  
- A risk assessment highlighting vulnerabilities and compliance gaps;  
- A prioritized roadmap outlining proposed initiatives over a 36–48 month horizon;  
- A high-level cloud architecture and reference design; and  
- Estimates of resource requirements and indicative timelines.

2.3 **Phase II – Design and Implementation.**

2.3.1 Following acceptance of the Phase I Deliverables pursuant to Section 2.7, the Service Provider shall proceed to a detailed design and implementation phase, expected to last twelve (12) to twenty-four (24) months, subject to Change Orders as appropriate.

2.3.2 The Services in Phase II shall include:

(a) **Detailed Solution Design.** Preparation of detailed technical designs, including infrastructure topology, security controls, data migration plans, and integration patterns;  
(b) **Cloud Infrastructure Provisioning.** Configuration of the agreed cloud environments (such as IaaS, PaaS, or SaaS platforms) in accordance with the detailed designs;  
(c) **System and Data Migration.** Planning and execution of migrations for agreed systems and data sets, including pilot migrations, cutover, and rollback strategies;  
(d) **Security and Compliance Controls.** Implementation of identity and access management, encryption, monitoring, alerting, and compliance controls aligned with the Client’s policies and Applicable Law;  
(e) **Testing and Validation.** Execution of system integration testing, performance testing, and user acceptance testing support, including remediation of defects; and  
(f) **Go-Live Support.** Coordination of go-live activities, hypercare support, and stabilization following major releases.

2.4 **Phase III – Managed Services and Support.**

2.4.1 Upon successful go-live of the relevant components of the Project, the Service Provider shall provide ongoing managed services and support until the Expiration Date, including:

(a) 24x7 monitoring of critical systems as defined in the SLAs (Article XII);  
(b) Incident management, problem management, and change management support;  
(c) Proactive capacity management, patching, and performance optimization;  
(d) Service reporting, including monthly performance, availability, and incident reports; and  
(e) Access to a service desk for logging, tracking, and resolving incidents and service requests.

2.4.2 Incident classification, response, and resolution targets shall be governed by the Service Levels set out in Article XII.

2.5 **Phase IV – Data Analytics and Business Intelligence.**

2.5.1 The Service Provider shall design and implement a data analytics platform, which shall include:

(a) Data ingestion pipelines from designated source systems;  
(b) A centralized data warehouse or data lake architecture;  
(c) Data quality and master data management controls;  
(d) Self-service reporting and dashboards for authorized business users; and  
(e) Governance mechanisms for data access, retention, and security.

2.5.2 The Deliverables for Phase IV shall include:

- Data model documentation;  
- Technical and user documentation;  
- At least ten (10) standard management dashboards agreed with the Client; and  
- Training sessions for the Client’s data and analytics team.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**

2.6.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

(a) With reasonable skill and care in accordance with good industry practice;  
(b) In compliance with the Specifications and Service Levels; and  
(c) In compliance with Applicable Laws and the Client’s written policies provided to the Service Provider in advance.

2.6.2 The Service Provider shall ensure that all personnel assigned to the Project are suitably qualified and experienced for the tasks assigned to them.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**

2.7.1 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the Client shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery of any Deliverable (the **“Review Period”**) to review and either:

(a) Provide written acceptance of the Deliverable; or  
(b) Provide written notice of defects or non-conformities in reasonable detail.

2.7.2 If the Client does not respond within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, provided that the Service Provider has expressly notified the Client in writing that the Review Period has commenced and has sent at least one written reminder at least three (3) Business Days before the end of the Review Period.

2.7.3 If the Client notifies the Service Provider of any defects or non-conformities, the Service Provider shall promptly, and at no additional cost to the Client, use commercially reasonable efforts to remedy such defects and resubmit the Deliverable. The Client shall then have a further Review Period of ten (10) Business Days to review the corrected Deliverable.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.**

2.8.1 The Client shall:

(a) Provide access to Client personnel, systems, and premises as reasonably required for performance of the Services;  
(b) Provide timely decisions, approvals, and feedback;  
(c) Ensure the accuracy and completeness of Client Materials; and  
(d) Designate a primary project manager to liaise with the Service Provider.

2.8.2 The Service Provider’s performance of the Services is dependent on the timely fulfillment of the Client’s responsibilities. The Service Provider shall not be liable for delays or failures in performance to the extent caused by the Client’s failure to meet its obligations under this Section 2.8.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total aggregate value of this Work Order shall be **US$3,997,439** (three million, nine hundred ninety-seven thousand, four hundred thirty-nine United States dollars) (the **“Contract Value”**), inclusive of all Services and Deliverables described herein, but exclusive of any applicable taxes and reimbursable expenses unless expressly stated otherwise.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall be structured as follows:

3.2.1 **Phase I – Discovery and Assessment:**  
A fixed fee of **US$475,000**, payable as follows:

(a) 30% (US$142,500) upon commencement of Phase I;  
(b) 40% (US$190,000) upon delivery of the draft Assessment Report; and  
(c) 30% (US$142,500) upon acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the Phase I Deliverables under Section 2.7.

3.2.2 **Phase II – Design and Implementation:**  
Fees for Phase II shall be **US$1,650,000**, comprising:

(a) A fixed design fee of US$450,000; and  
(b) A time-and-materials component capped at US$1,200,000, billed monthly in arrears based on agreed daily rates set out in a schedule to this Work Order.

3.2.3 **Phase III – Managed Services and Support:**  
For the period from completion of initial go-live to the Expiration Date, the Client shall pay a **monthly managed services fee** of **US$48,000** (forty-eight thousand US dollars), subject to reasonable annual indexation not to exceed three percent (3%) per annum.

3.2.4 **Phase IV – Data Analytics and Business Intelligence:**  
A fixed implementation fee of **US$822,439**, payable in Milestones to be detailed in a project schedule, with not less than 10% retained until final acceptance of the Phase IV Deliverables.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
Except as otherwise expressly stated in this Work Order:

3.3.1 The Service Provider shall invoice the Client upon achievement of the relevant Milestones, and for recurring managed services on a monthly basis in arrears.

3.3.2 All properly issued and undisputed invoices shall be due and payable **Net 60** days from the date of the invoice.

3.3.3 Invoices shall be submitted electronically to the Client’s designated accounts payable contact as notified in writing.

3.4 **Late Payment.**

3.4.1 If the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount by the due date, the Service Provider may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate equal to **3% per annum above the Bank of England base rate**, accruing daily from the due date until the date of actual payment, whether before or after judgment.

3.4.2 The Service Provider shall not suspend performance of the Services for non-payment unless:

(a) The amount is undisputed and overdue by more than thirty (30) days;  
(b) The Service Provider has given the Client at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice of its intention to suspend; and  
(c) The Client has failed to pay such overdue amount within such notice period.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**

3.5.1 If the Client in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice, the Client shall:

(a) Notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the amount in dispute and the reasons; and  
(b) Pay any undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3.

3.5.2 The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any invoice dispute promptly. Any resolved amounts shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days following resolution.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of value-added tax (VAT), sales tax, and any similar taxes. The Client shall be responsible for any such taxes properly chargeable in respect of the Services, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

3.7.1 Reasonable, pre-approved travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred by the Service Provider in connection with the Services shall be reimbursed by the Client at cost, subject to:

(a) The Service Provider providing appropriate receipts or documentation; and  
(b) The Client’s prior written approval for any individual expense exceeding US$2,000.

3.7.2 The Service Provider shall use reasonable efforts to minimize expenses and to use cost-effective travel and accommodation options.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience, without cause, by giving the other Party not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice**.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Client:

(a) The Client shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and accepted, and all approved expenses incurred, up to the effective date of termination; and  
(b) The Client shall additionally pay any non-cancellable third-party commitments reasonably incurred in compliance with this Work Order.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) Materially breaches any provision of this Work Order and, where such breach is capable of remedy, fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer period as is reasonable in the circumstances, not exceeding sixty (60) days) after receiving written notice specifying the nature of the breach; or  
(b) Becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation or administration (other than a solvent restructuring), or has a receiver appointed over any of its assets.

4.3.2 The Client may, without prejudice to any other rights, terminate this Work Order by written notice in the event of a persistent failure by the Service Provider to meet the Service Levels, as defined in Article XII, following the exhaustion of the remedy escalation process specified therein.

4.4 **Effect of Termination.**

4.4.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Work Order:

(a) The Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the Services, except as reasonably required to provide any agreed transition assistance;  
(b) Each Party shall promptly return or delete (at the disclosing Party’s option) the Confidential Information of the other Party, subject to Section 5.3 and Article XI regarding data retention; and  
(c) The Client shall pay all undisputed Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued up to the effective date of termination, in accordance with Article III.

4.4.2 The Service Provider shall provide reasonable cooperation to assist in the orderly transition of the Services to the Client or to a successor service provider, for a period not exceeding ninety (90) days following termination, at the Service Provider’s then-current time-and-materials rates unless otherwise agreed.

4.5 **Survival.**  
Provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration of this Work Order shall so survive, including, without limitation, Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions) and Sections 3.4, 3.6, 4.4.

(a) Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Work Order by the Receiving Party;  
(b) Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) Is received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality to the Disclosing Party; or  
(d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

12.1 **Service Availability.**

12.1.1 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the core production systems within its control under this Work Order achieve an **uptime of at least 99.9%** measured on a monthly basis, excluding agreed maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events.

12.1.2 Scheduled maintenance shall be performed during agreed maintenance windows and shall not exceed four (4) hours per month, unless otherwise agreed.

12.2 **Incident Response and Resolution.**

Incidents shall be classified and addressed in accordance with the following target response and resolution times:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):**  
  - Response time: within 30 minutes  
  - Resolution or workaround: within 4 hours  
- **Priority 2 (High):**  
  - Response time: within 1 hour  
  - Resolution or workaround: within 8 hours  
- **Priority 3 (Medium):**  
  - Response time: within 4 hours  
  - Resolution or workaround: within 3 Business Days  
- **Priority 4 (Low):**  
  - Response time: within 8 hours  
  - Resolution or workaround: within 5 Business Days

12.3 **Service Credits.**

12.3.1 If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1.1 in any given month, the Client shall be entitled to a service credit, calculated as a percentage of the monthly managed services fee for that month, as follows:

- Uptime 98.0%–98.99%: 10% credit  
- Uptime below 98.0%: 15% credit

12.3.2 Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice(s) issued to the Client. Service credits are the Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitment, without prejudice to the Client’s rights in case of persistent failures under Section 4.3.2.

12.4 **Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall provide monthly service reports to the Client detailing:

(a) Uptime performance;  
(b) Incident volumes and response/resolution times; and  
(c) Any major issues and improvement actions.

The Parties shall meet at least quarterly to review the SLAs, discuss performance, and identify opportunities for improvement. Any changes to SLAs shall be documented in a Change Order in accordance with Article XIII.

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, riots, strikes (other than strikes of the affected Party’s own workforce), pandemics (including related government restrictions), and failures of third-party telecommunications or hosting providers.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

(a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing upon becoming aware of the Force Majeure Event;  
(b) Use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event; and  
(c) Resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension and Termination.**  
Performance of the affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Work Order upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party.
