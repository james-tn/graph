## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Vendor.**  
The Client hereby engages the Vendor, and the Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described in this **Article II** and in one or more SOWs entered into pursuant to this Agreement. The Parties may execute multiple SOWs during the Term, each of which shall be governed by and incorporated into this Agreement.

2.2 **Nature of Services.**  
The Services shall include, without limitation, the following categories of work, subject to further detail in each SOW:

  2.2.1 **Staff Augmentation / Employment‑Type Services.** Vendor shall assign qualified personnel (including software engineers, QA engineers, DevOps specialists, data analysts, project managers, and other professionals) to work on Client projects under Client’s day‑to‑day direction and supervision. Such personnel shall remain employees or contractors of the Vendor, and Vendor shall be solely responsible for:

  - Recruitment, hiring, and onboarding;  
  - Payment of salaries, wages, bonuses, and benefits;  
  - Payment and withholding of all taxes and statutory contributions; and  
  - Compliance with labor and employment laws applicable to the Vendor’s personnel.

  2.2.2 **Managed Services.** In certain areas, the Vendor shall provide outcome‑oriented managed services, whereby Vendor manages defined deliverables and processes, including but not limited to:

  - Ongoing maintenance and support of specified applications;  
  - 24x7 monitoring and incident response for designated systems;  
  - Release management and deployment operations;  
  - Operational reporting and performance analysis.

  2.2.3 **Project‑Based Work.** The Vendor shall execute specific projects for the Client, which may include:

  - Design, development, and integration of software components;  
  - Migration of legacy systems to cloud infrastructure;  
  - Implementation of security enhancements and access controls;  
  - Development of technical documentation and training materials.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Vendor shall produce and deliver to the Client the following types of Deliverables, as specified in each SOW:

  2.3.1 **Software Artifacts.** Source code, compiled binaries, configuration files, scripts, application programming interfaces (APIs), and related artifacts necessary for the operation of Client systems.

  2.3.2 **Documentation.** Technical design documents, system architecture diagrams, runbooks, test plans, test cases, user guides, and release notes.

  2.3.3 **Reports and Analyses.** Periodic status reports, incident reports, capacity and performance analyses, risk assessments, and compliance reports.

  2.3.4 **Training Materials.** Training presentations, user manuals, and knowledge‑transfer sessions designed to enable the Client’s internal staff to operate and maintain the systems.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
Each SOW shall specify the expected timelines, milestones, and key delivery dates for the applicable Services and Deliverables, which may include:

  - Project initiation dates and transition periods;  
  - Milestone completion dates for design, development, testing, and deployment;  
  - Go‑live dates and post‑implementation support periods;  
  - SLA‑related measurement periods (e.g., monthly or quarterly).

The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet such timelines and milestones. If Vendor becomes aware of any circumstances that may materially delay performance, Vendor shall promptly notify the Client in writing, describing the reasons for delay and proposing a recovery plan. Any material adjustment to timelines or milestones shall be handled in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner;  
  (b) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  (c) using personnel who are appropriately trained, experienced, and qualified for their assigned tasks; and  
  (d) in compliance with Applicable Laws and the Client’s reasonable written policies, including security and access policies, provided such policies have been communicated in writing to the Vendor.

2.6 **Key Personnel.**  
Certain Vendor personnel may be designated as Key Personnel in the SOW. The Vendor shall not reassign or remove such Key Personnel from the project without providing reasonable prior written notice to the Client and securing Client’s prior consent (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed), except where such removal is required by law or due to such individual’s resignation, illness, or other circumstances beyond Vendor’s control. The Vendor shall promptly propose a replacement with comparable skills and experience.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall:

  (a) Provide Vendor with timely access to Client Materials and personnel as reasonably necessary to perform the Services;  
  (b) Designate a primary contact person to coordinate with Vendor;  
  (c) Review and provide timely feedback on Deliverables and reports; and  
  (d) Ensure that any prerequisites or dependencies identified in the SOW are fulfilled in a timely manner.

Vendor’s performance is conditioned upon the Client’s timely fulfillment of its responsibilities. Vendor shall not be liable for delays or failures to meet Service Levels to the extent caused by Client’s failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the following acceptance procedure shall apply:

  2.8.1 Upon completion of a Deliverable, Vendor shall notify the Client in writing and provide the Deliverable for review.

  2.8.2 The Client shall have **fifteen (15) Business Days** (the “Review Period”) to review and either:

  - Provide written acceptance; or  
  - Provide a written, reasonably detailed statement of non‑conformities with the applicable specifications.

  2.8.3 If the Client fails to respond within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted. If Client provides a non‑conformity notice, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the reported issues and re‑submit the Deliverable for Client review, subject to a further Review Period of ten (10) Business Days.

  2.8.4 The foregoing process shall repeat until the Deliverable is accepted or deemed accepted; provided that if, after two (2) re‑submissions, the Deliverable still materially fails to meet the agreed specifications, the Parties shall escalate the issue in accordance with **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**.

2.9 **Location and Remote Work.**  
Services may be performed at Client’s premises, Vendor’s premises, or remotely, as specified in the SOW. If Vendor personnel are required to work on Client premises, they shall comply with Client’s reasonable site rules and security procedures, provided such rules and procedures have been communicated in writing.

2.10 **Subcontractors.**  
Vendor may use subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that Vendor:

  (a) remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services;  
  (b) ensures that any subcontractor is bound by written obligations no less protective of the Client than those set forth in this Agreement, including confidentiality and data protection obligations; and  
  (c) obtains Client’s prior written consent for any subcontractor that will have access to Client Confidential Information or Personal Data, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value under this Agreement is **Two Million Nine Hundred Thirty‑Five Thousand Two Hundred Three United States Dollars (US $2,935,203)**, exclusive of any applicable taxes, as further detailed in the SOW(s). This total is an estimate based on projected volume, rates, and scope; actual amounts invoiced shall be based on Services actually rendered and expenses incurred in accordance with this Agreement and the SOW(s).

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees may include any combination of the following, as specified in each SOW:

  3.2.1 **Time and Materials (T&M).** Hourly or daily rates for Vendor personnel, multiplied by the actual hours or days worked. The SOW shall specify the applicable rates and any overtime rules.

  3.2.2 **Fixed Price.** A fixed fee for specific Deliverables or milestones, payable upon completion and acceptance of such Deliverables or milestones.

  3.2.3 **Retainer or Monthly Recurring Charges.** A recurring monthly fee for managed services, support services, or dedicated resources.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
Unless otherwise stated in the applicable SOW, the Vendor shall invoice the Client monthly in arrears for Services performed and accepted Deliverables. All undisputed amounts shall be due and payable **Net 60** days from the date of the invoice.

3.4 **Milestone Payments.**  
For fixed‑price or milestone‑based work:

  (a) Each SOW shall specify the deliverables, completion criteria, and corresponding payment amounts.  
  (b) Vendor shall be entitled to issue an invoice upon Client’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the applicable milestone or Deliverable, in accordance with **Section 2.8**.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
The Client shall reimburse the Vendor for reasonable, pre‑approved, out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred solely in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and related incidental expenses, subject to the following:

  (a) Expenses must comply with the Client’s travel and expense policy, as provided in writing to Vendor;  
  (b) Any individual expense item exceeding US $500 must be pre‑approved in writing by the Client;  
  (c) Vendor shall provide appropriate receipts or documentation with each expense claim.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value‑added, withholding, or other taxes or duties. The Client shall be responsible for all such taxes (except taxes based on Vendor’s net income), and Vendor shall itemize applicable taxes on its invoices where required by law. If Client is required by law to withhold any taxes from payments to Vendor, Client shall provide Vendor with official tax receipts or other appropriate documentation.

3.7 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one‑half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date payment is received. Vendor shall provide written notice of any late payment before applying interest.

3.8 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Client shall:

  (a) Notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the reasons for dispute; and  
  (b) Pay all undisputed amounts by the due date.

The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve disputed amounts promptly. Any amounts ultimately determined to be payable shall be due within fifteen (15) Business Days after resolution.

3.9 **No Withholdings for Performance Issues.**  
Except for amounts properly disputed pursuant to **Section 3.8**, Client shall not withhold or set off amounts due under this Agreement as a remedy for alleged performance issues; rather, such issues shall be handled through the remedies provided in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** and **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until **February 24, 2029** (the “Term”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew the Agreement for additional periods, on terms to be negotiated in good faith. Any such extension or renewal shall be documented by an amendment executed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile and client‑friendly nature of this engagement, the Client may terminate this Agreement, any SOW, or any portion thereof, for convenience and without cause, upon providing **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Vendor. The Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Client.

Upon termination for convenience by either Party, the Client shall pay Vendor for:

  (a) all Services performed and accepted Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination; and  
  (b) all approved, non‑cancelable expenses incurred prior to the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed for substantially all of its assets, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Partial Termination.**  
The Client may, at its discretion, terminate one or more SOWs or portions of the Services without terminating the entire Agreement, by providing the applicable notice in accordance with **Sections 4.3** or **4.4**.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the terminated Services and cooperate with Client in effecting an orderly transition, including reasonable knowledge transfer to Client or to a replacement provider;  
  (b) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed amounts due for Services performed and accepted Deliverables provided prior to the effective date of termination, and any approved, non‑cancelable expenses;  
  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5**; and  
  (d) The provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**) shall so survive.

4.7 **No Penalty.**  
Termination in accordance with this **Article IV** shall not be deemed a breach of contract by either Party and shall not entitle the other Party to any penalty or termination fee, except for payment obligations expressly set forth herein.

  (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
  (b) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;  
  (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
  (d) is approved in writing for release by the Disclosing Party.

The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
  (b) not disclose the Confidential Information to any person or entity except to its employees, officers, directors, agents, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those herein; and  
  (c) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information under its control (in any form or medium), except that:

  (a) The Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for legal or compliance purposes; and

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations relating to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for as long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control that prevents or materially impedes the performance of its obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to:

  (a) acts of God, natural disasters, or extreme weather;  
  (b) war, terrorism, civil unrest, or riots;  
  (c) strikes, labor disputes, or lockouts (excluding those involving a Party’s own workforce, unless industry‑wide);  
  (d) epidemics, pandemics, or public health emergencies;  
  (e) governmental actions, embargoes, or export restrictions; or  
  (f) failures of third‑party telecommunications or hosting providers, to the extent not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) Notify the other Party in writing as soon as reasonably practicable, describing the nature and expected duration of the event and its impact on performance; and  
  (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, to the extent performance is prevented or materially impeded. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to adjust timelines and deliverables as necessary.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW upon written notice, without liability other than payment for Services rendered and approved expenses incurred prior to the effective date of termination.
