- **Contoso Enterprises Pte. Ltd.**, a company incorporated under the laws of Singapore, with its registered office at 123 Marina View, #25-01, Singapore 018961 (hereinafter referred to as “**Contoso**”, the “**Client**” or the “**Company**”); and

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES  

2.1 **Engagement of Service Provider.** The Company hereby engages the Service Provider, on a non‑exclusive basis, to provide the Services described herein and in one or more SOWs, and the Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

2.2 **General Description of Services.**  

The Services to be provided by the Service Provider may include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 Strategic business consulting in relation to the Company’s technology roadmap, digital transformation initiatives, and market expansion plans;

  2.2.2 Technology assessment, architecture design, and integration advisory, including evaluation of existing systems, identification of gaps, and recommendation of appropriate technology solutions;

  2.2.3 Project and program management services, including planning, scheduling, resource allocation, risk management, and status reporting for technology and operational initiatives;

  2.2.4 Implementation support for selected technologies, platforms, and tools, including configuration, customization, testing support, and deployment advisory;

  2.2.5 Process optimization and operational excellence consulting, including analysis of current business processes, identification of improvement opportunities, and design of target operating models and governance structures;

  2.2.6 Training, workshops, and knowledge transfer sessions for the Company’s employees and representatives in relation to new processes, systems, and best practices;

  2.2.7 Data and analytics advisory, including the design of reporting frameworks, dashboards, and KPI measurement systems;

  2.2.8 Such other consulting and professional services as may be mutually agreed by the Parties and documented in an SOW or Change Order.

2.3 **Statements of Work.**  

  2.3.1 From time to time, the Parties may enter into one or more SOWs describing particular projects or engagements under this Agreement. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify: (a) the nature and scope of the Services; (b) any specific Deliverables; (c) key assumptions and dependencies; (d) project timelines, milestones, and deadlines; (e) fees, expenses, and payment schedules; and (f) any special Service Levels or acceptance criteria.

  2.3.2 Each SOW shall be in writing and shall be deemed incorporated by reference into this Agreement. In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and the terms of an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail, except where the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.4 **Deliverables and Milestones.**  

  2.4.1 The Service Provider shall develop and/or provide the Deliverables specified in each SOW in accordance with the timelines and milestones agreed therein. Deliverables may include, by way of illustration, (a) strategic assessment reports; (b) solution design documents; (c) implementation plans; (d) training materials; (e) process documentation; and (f) progress and performance reports.

  2.4.2 The Company shall, within the review period specified in the relevant SOW (and if none is specified, within ten (10) Business Days following delivery), review the Deliverables and either (a) provide written acceptance of the Deliverables, or (b) provide a written statement of deficiencies that render the Deliverables non‑conforming to the applicable SOW. Failure by the Company to respond within the review period shall be deemed acceptance of the Deliverables.

  2.4.3 In the event of non‑conformity, the Service Provider shall, at its own cost and as its sole obligation and the Company’s exclusive remedy for non‑conforming Deliverables (subject always to Article IX), use commercially reasonable efforts to promptly correct the deficiencies and re‑deliver the corrected Deliverables for review in accordance with this Section 2.4.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

  2.5.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner; (b) in accordance with generally accepted industry practices for similar services; and (c) in material compliance with Applicable Law.

  2.5.2 The Service Provider does not guarantee any specific business results or outcomes (such as increased revenue, cost savings, or market share) arising from the Services, and the Company acknowledges that many factors outside the Service Provider’s control may impact the success of any recommendations or Deliverables.

  2.5.3 The Parties may agree to specific Service Levels and performance metrics in an SOW. Any Service Levels shall be subject to the exclusions and remedies set forth in Article XII and the applicable SOW.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.**  

The Company acknowledges that the Service Provider’s performance is dependent on the timely and effective cooperation of the Company. Accordingly, the Company shall:

  2.6.1 Provide access to the Company’s personnel, facilities, systems, data, and information as reasonably necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services, subject to the Company’s security and access policies;

  2.6.2 Designate a project manager or primary contact person who will be responsible for day‑to‑day liaison with the Service Provider and for providing required approvals and decisions;

  2.6.3 Provide accurate, complete, and timely information and materials, and promptly advise the Service Provider of any changes to such information that may affect the Services;

  2.6.4 Perform any tasks or responsibilities expressly assigned to the Company in any SOW or Change Order, including making internal resources available and executing required dependencies in a timely manner.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  

The Service Provider may, at its discretion, engage subcontractors or independent consultants to perform all or part of the Services; provided that (a) the Service Provider shall remain fully responsible for the performance of the Services and the acts and omissions of its subcontractors, and (b) any subcontractor having access to the Company’s Confidential Information or Personal Data shall be bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection that are no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS  

3.1 **Fees.**  

  3.1.1 In consideration for the performance of the Services and provision of the Deliverables, the Company shall pay the Service Provider fees in the aggregate amount not to exceed the Contract Price, unless otherwise mutually agreed in writing. The specific fee structure, including time‑and‑materials rates, fixed fees, or milestone‑based fees, shall be set forth in each SOW.

  3.1.2 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Services shall be provided on a time‑and‑materials basis, at the Service Provider’s then‑current standard hourly or daily rates for the personnel involved. The Service Provider may adjust such rates on an annual basis upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the Company.

3.2 **Invoices and Payment Terms.**  

  3.2.1 The Service Provider shall invoice the Company for fees and reimbursable expenses on a monthly basis in arrears, or as otherwise specified in the applicable SOW (e.g., upon achievement of particular milestones or delivery of specific Deliverables).

  3.2.2 All invoices shall be due and payable by the Company **within sixty (60) days** from the date of the invoice (“**Net 60**”), without set‑off or deduction, except as expressly permitted herein or in the applicable SOW.

  3.2.3 All payments shall be made in United States dollars (USD) by wire transfer or such other payment method as may be agreed in writing by the Parties.

3.3 **Late Payments.**  

  3.3.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of one and one‑half percent (1.5%) per month (or the maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law, if lower), calculated from the due date of payment until the date of actual payment in full.

  3.3.2 In addition to interest, if the Company fails to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after written notice of non‑payment from the Service Provider, the Service Provider may, without liability and without prejudice to any other rights and remedies, suspend the performance of Services and/or the delivery of Deliverables until all outstanding amounts and applicable interest are paid in full.

3.4 **Disputed Invoices.**  

  3.4.1 If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute.

  3.4.2 The Company shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.2. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Any resolved amount shall be payable within ten (10) Business Days after resolution.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

  3.5.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, documented out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, local transportation, and other incidental expenses, provided that such expenses are in accordance with the Company’s expense policy as notified to the Service Provider in writing or are otherwise pre‑approved by the Company.

  3.5.2 The Service Provider shall submit documentary evidence, such as receipts or invoices, for all reimbursable expenses, in accordance with the Company’s reasonable requirements.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

  3.6.1 All fees and other amounts payable under this Agreement are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value‑added, withholding, or other taxes, duties, or similar charges (“**Taxes**”). The Company shall be responsible for the payment of all Taxes arising from or relating to this Agreement, other than taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or employees.

  3.6.2 If the Company is required under Applicable Law to withhold any Taxes from payments due to the Service Provider, the Company shall: (a) withhold the required amount; (b) remit such amount to the appropriate tax authority; and (c) furnish the Service Provider with valid tax receipts or certificates evidencing payment. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to minimize any Taxes that may be lawfully reduced or eliminated.

## TERM AND TERMINATION  

4.1 **Term.**  

The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **22 December 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  

The Agreement may be renewed or extended beyond the Expiration Date only by a written agreement signed by authorized representatives of both Parties, which may include a new or amended Statement of Work or a formal extension agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any or all SOWs), for convenience and without cause, by providing the other Party at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

  4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent; (b) is unable to pay its debts as they fall due; (c) enters into liquidation (voluntary or compulsory, other than for purposes of an amalgamation or reconstruction); (d) has a receiver, administrator, or similar officer appointed over any of its assets; or (e) enters into any arrangement or composition with its creditors generally.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
  (a) the Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, including any non‑cancelable commitments and any fees expressly stated as non‑refundable;  
  (b) all rights and licenses granted to the Company under this Agreement (other than fully‑paid, perpetual licenses expressly set forth) shall automatically terminate; and  
  (c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5.

  4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any SOW that has not been terminated, unless the termination notice expressly states otherwise.

  4.5.3 Termination of this Agreement shall be without prejudice to any rights, remedies, obligations, or liabilities of either Party that have accrued up to the date of termination.

4.6 **Survival.**  

The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: Sections 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, 3.6, 4.5, 4.6, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (to the extent applicable), XIV, XV, and XVI, together with any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY  

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  

“**Confidential Information**” means any and all non‑public, confidential, or proprietary information of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) disclosed or made available to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, in writing, electronically, visually, or in any other form, and whether disclosed before or after the Effective Date, including, without limitation:  
(a) business plans, strategies, and financial information;  
(b) product plans, designs, and roadmaps;  
(c) customer and supplier information;  
(d) technical data, trade secrets, know‑how, and research and development;  
(e) any information expressly designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure; and  
(f) the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes public knowledge through no fault or breach of the Receiving Party;  
(b) was already in the Receiving Party’s lawful possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without restriction on disclosure;  
(c) is lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  

The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
(b) restrict disclosure of the Confidential Information to those of its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, agents, subcontractors, and professional advisors (“**Representatives**”) who have a need to know such information for such purpose and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality that are no less protective than those set forth herein;  
(c) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of like nature, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care; and  
(d) not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  

If the Receiving Party is required by Applicable Law or by a valid order of a court or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permissible, provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement, and shall cooperate, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or other remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly (a) return to the Disclosing Party all tangible copies of the Confidential Information, or (b) destroy such Confidential Information and certify in writing that it has done so; provided that the Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information (i) as required by Applicable Law, or (ii) in its standard archival or backup systems, which shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein until destroyed in the ordinary course.

5.6 **Injunctive Relief.**  

The Parties acknowledge and agree that any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief, without the necessity of posting bond or proving actual damages, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

5.7 **Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.**  

The obligations of confidentiality and non‑use under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall be maintained in confidence for so long as they remain trade secrets under Applicable Law.

  15.2.1 Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, describing its nature and expected duration and the obligations affected;

  15.2.2 Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and

  15.2.3 Keep the other Party reasonably informed of the status of the Force Majeure Event and its impact on performance.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  

During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are prevented or delayed by the Force Majeure Event. The time for performance shall be extended accordingly.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  

If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period of more than ninety (90) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
