### Recitals

WHEREAS, the Parties previously entered into the **Statement of Work SOW-GAM-202509-217 (contract_217)** dated **17 September 2025** (the **“Parent Agreement”**), pursuant to which the Service Provider agreed to design, develop, implement, deploy, and provide ongoing managed services for a global, cloud-based enterprise analytics and workflow platform for the Company’s multi-regional operations (the **“Platform”**), as more particularly described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement;

WHEREAS, the Parent Agreement established, among other things, (a) definitions in **Article I**, (b) scope, Deliverables, Milestones, and phased project approach in **Article II**, (c) the fee structure and invoicing framework in **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)**, including a Contract Value of **USD $3,129,859** and **Net 60** payment terms, (d) term and termination provisions in **Article IV**, with an Expiration Date of **13 December 2027**, and (e) general contractual terms including **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**, **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**;

WHEREAS, the Company now desires to expand the scope of Services and Deliverables originally contemplated under the Parent Agreement in order to (i) introduce additional functional modules to the Platform, (ii) extend integrations to further enterprise systems, (iii) enhance analytics capabilities, and (iv) extend managed services beyond the original term of the Parent Agreement;  

WHEREAS, the Service Provider has agreed to provide such additional and extended Services and Deliverables, subject to the terms and conditions of the Parent Agreement as supplemented and refined by this Work Order;

WHEREAS, **this Work Order adds scope to the Statement of Work dated 17 September 2025 (Ref: SOW-GAM-202509-217, contract_217)**, and is intended to be read and construed together with the Parent Agreement, with this Work Order acting as an additional, stand-alone work order under and pursuant to the Parent Agreement;

## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order is entered into **pursuant to** and **supplements** the Parent Agreement. For the avoidance of doubt, this Work Order does not replace or novate the Parent Agreement but rather **adds scope** to the Statement of Work dated 17 September 2025 (Ref: **SOW-GAM-202509-217, contract_217**).  

2.1.2 The nature and type of services provided under this Work Order remain within the general categories contemplated in **Section 2.1 (General Description of Services)** of the Parent Agreement, namely information technology, software development, implementation, managed support services, and Platform-related services. However, this Work Order introduces additional scope, modules, integrations, and an extended support horizon as detailed below.  

2.1.3 Except as explicitly modified herein, the performance standards in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** and the acceptance procedures in **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply in full to the Extended Services and Additional Deliverables defined in this Work Order.

2.2 **Overview of Extended Services.**  

The Service Provider shall perform the following Extended Services for the Company under this Work Order:

(a) **Enhanced Analytics Module Design and Implementation.**  
- Design and implement an advanced analytics and reporting layer on top of the Platform’s existing data model defined under the Parent Agreement.  
- Introduce predictive analytics, machine learning-powered insights, and self-service dashboards targeted at business users across finance, operations, and compliance.  
- Integrate data from new structured and semi-structured sources into the Platform’s analytics layer, consistent with the **Data Model and Integration Specifications** Deliverable described in Section 2.3(d) of the Parent Agreement.  

(b) **Global Workflow Expansion.**  
- Extend workflow automation functionality for additional business units in Asia-Pacific, EMEA, and Latin America, building on the Platform workflows developed under **Phase 3 – Development and Integration** (Section 2.2.3 of the Parent Agreement).  
- Configure localized approval matrices, escalation paths, and business rules aligned with local regulatory and operational requirements.  
- Enhance role-based access control and segregation of duties for the newly onboarded user groups, consistent with the security architecture described in **Phase 2 – Design and Architecture** of the Parent Agreement.  

(c) **Extended Integrations.**  
- Develop and deploy new integrations between the Platform and:  
  1. Two additional ERP instances used by regional subsidiaries;  
  2. The Company’s global data lake;  
  3. The Company’s third-party vendor risk management platform; and  
  4. Additional HR and procurement systems designated by the Company.  
- Use secure APIs and ETL processes consistent with the integration approach defined in Section 2.2.3 of the Parent Agreement and applicable security standards described in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of the Parent Agreement.

(d) **Data Modernization and Migration.**  
- Assess legacy datasets and schemas for newly integrated systems and perform data mapping, cleansing, and migration into the Platform’s canonical data model.  
- Design and document any necessary extensions to the data model, subject to the Company’s approval, and ensure backward compatibility with existing reports and workflows.  

(e) **Extended Managed Services.**  
- Provide Level 2 and Level 3 support and maintenance for the Enhanced Analytics Module, Global Workflow Expansion, and Extended Integrations for the **Extended Managed Services Period**, including proactive monitoring, incident management, patching, performance tuning, and minor enhancements approved by the Company.  
- Align support activities with **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XII** of this Work Order.  

2.3 **Additional Deliverables.**  

Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.3 (Key Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, the Service Provider shall provide at least the following Additional Deliverables under this Work Order:

(a) **Extended Requirements Specification Addendum (WO-237).** A requirements document detailing the specific business and technical requirements for the Enhanced Analytics Module, Global Workflow Expansion, Extended Integrations, and data modernization activities, which shall supplement the **Requirements Specification Document** produced under the Parent Agreement.

(b) **Extended Solution Architecture Addendum (WO-237).** An architectural addendum describing changes or extensions to the Platform architecture, including data flows, integration topologies, and security updates, supplementing the **Solution Architecture Document** under the Parent Agreement.

(c) **Analytics Design Package.** Enhanced **High-Level Design (HLD)** and **Low-Level Design (LLD)** artifacts specific to the Enhanced Analytics Module, including data mart structures, model training workflows, and dashboard templates.

(d) **Integration Specifications (Extended).** Detailed technical design documents for new integration interfaces, including message formats, API specifications, error handling, and performance expectations.

(e) **Configuration and Deployment Packages.** Configured workflow templates, role-based access control schemes, dashboard definitions, and analytics components packaged for deployment across environments.

(f) **Testing Artifacts (Extended Scope).** Test plans, test cases, and test reports for the Extended Services, including performance and security testing, which shall be considered part of the **Test Plans, Test Cases, and Test Reports** Deliverables referenced in Section 2.3(h) of the Parent Agreement.

(g) **Operations and Support Addendum.** Updates to the **Operational Runbooks and Support Procedures** and Service Level reporting templates to incorporate Extended Services and Additional Deliverables.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones (Work Order Specific).**  

2.4.1 Within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date of this Work Order, the Parties shall mutually agree in writing on the **Project Plan (WO-237)**, specifying detailed Milestones, dependencies, and resource allocations for the Extended Services.  

2.4.2 The Parties acknowledge that the Parent Agreement’s **Section 2.4 (Timelines and Milestones)** shall apply mutatis mutandis to the Extended Services. Time shall remain of the essence; however, timelines may be adjusted by mutual agreement via a Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XIII** of this Work Order.

2.4.3 At a minimum, the following key Milestones shall be established:

- **Milestone 1:** Completion and acceptance of Extended Requirements Specification Addendum and Extended Solution Architecture Addendum.  
- **Milestone 2:** Completion and acceptance of Analytics Design Package and Integration Specifications (Extended).  
- **Milestone 3:** Completion and acceptance of core development and configuration of Enhanced Analytics Module and Global Workflow Expansion.  
- **Milestone 4:** Completion and acceptance of Extended Integrations into production-ready state.  
- **Milestone 5:** Successful completion of UAT for all Extended Services and readiness for production deployment.  
- **Milestone 6:** Completion of production deployment, stabilization, and commencement of Extended Managed Services.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

2.5.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Extended Services in accordance with the performance standards set out in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, including the obligation to perform Services with due skill, care, and diligence and in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

2.5.2 For Extended Managed Services, the Service Provider shall meet or exceed the **Service Levels** specified in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement and Article XII of this Work Order.  

2.5.3 Failure to meet Service Levels may give rise to Service Credits in accordance with Section 12.4 of the Parent Agreement and Section 12.4 of this Work Order, without prejudice to other contractual remedies.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.**  

The Company’s responsibilities under **Section 2.6 (Company Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to this Work Order. In particular, the Company shall:

(a) Provide timely access to additional systems, data sources, and business stakeholders relevant to the Extended Services;  

(b) Designate business owners for each new module or integration in order to facilitate design validation and UAT;  

(c) Review and provide feedback or approvals on Additional Deliverables in a timely manner consistent with the acceptance timeframes specified in Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **Acceptance of Additional Deliverables.**  

2.7.1 The acceptance procedure set out in **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to all Additional Deliverables under this Work Order. Unless otherwise agreed in the Project Plan (WO-237), the Company shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery to review and either accept or reject each Deliverable.  

2.7.2 Any rejection shall specify in reasonable detail the non-conformities with the agreed Specifications. The Service Provider shall, at its own cost, remediate and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance.  

2.7.3 If, after two (2) resubmissions, a Deliverable still materially fails to meet the Specifications, the Parties shall escalate pursuant to **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)** of the Parent Agreement and this Work Order to determine an appropriate remediation plan.

2.8 **Subcontractors.**  

Section 2.8 (Subcontractors) of the Parent Agreement is incorporated herein by reference and shall apply fully to the Extended Services, including the requirement that the Service Provider remains fully responsible for subcontractors and obtains the Company’s prior written consent for any subcontractor performing material portions of the Extended Services.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Work Order Contract Value.**  

3.1.1 The total maximum contract value for the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Work Order shall be **USD $2,141,991** (the **“WO Contract Value”**), exclusive of any applicable taxes, unless otherwise specified.  

3.1.2 The WO Contract Value is separate from, and in addition to, the **Contract Value of USD $3,129,859** under **Section 3.1** of the Parent Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure under this Work Order.**  

The WO Contract Value shall be comprised of the following components:

(a) **Fixed Implementation Fees (Extended Scope):** USD $1,250,000 for design, development, integration, testing, and deployment of the Extended Services (Milestones 1–5);  

(b) **Time-and-Materials (T&M) Enhancements and Change Requests:** USD $391,991 (estimated), for incremental enhancements and change requests specific to Extended Services, based on the prevailing rate card used under **Exhibit C** of the Parent Agreement, as may be updated;  

(c) **Extended Managed Services Fees:** USD $500,000 for Extended Managed Services during the Extended Managed Services Period, from the earlier of (i) the end of the Parent Agreement’s Expiration Date (13 December 2027) or (ii) the Go-Live date of Extended Services, through **22 November 2030**.

3.3 **Milestone Payments (Fixed Implementation Fees).**  

3.3.1 Fixed Implementation Fees under this Work Order shall be invoiced upon achievement and acceptance of the following Milestones, subject to the acceptance procedures in Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement and Section 2.7 of this Work Order:

(a) **Milestone 1 – Extended Requirements & Architecture:**  
Completion and acceptance of the Extended Requirements Specification Addendum and Extended Solution Architecture Addendum: **15%** of Fixed Implementation Fees.

(b) **Milestone 2 – Analytics & Integration Design:**  
Completion and acceptance of Analytics Design Package and Integration Specifications (Extended): **20%** of Fixed Implementation Fees.

(c) **Milestone 3 – Core Development & Configuration:**  
Completion and acceptance of core Enhanced Analytics Module and Global Workflow Expansion configurations: **30%** of Fixed Implementation Fees.

(d) **Milestone 4 – Extended Integrations Ready:**  
Completion and acceptance of all Extended Integrations in a production-ready state: **15%** of Fixed Implementation Fees.

(e) **Milestone 5 – UAT Completion & Deployment Readiness:**  
Successful completion of UAT for all Extended Services and readiness for production deployment: **10%** of Fixed Implementation Fees.

(f) **Milestone 6 – Production Deployment and Stabilization:**  
Completion of production deployment and the conclusion of a stabilization period of not less than sixty (60) days: **10%** of Fixed Implementation Fees.

3.4 **Extended Managed Services Fees.**  

3.4.1 Extended Managed Services Fees under this Work Order shall accrue monthly in equal installments over the Extended Managed Services Period and shall be invoiced monthly in arrears.

3.4.2 Any increase in scope of Extended Managed Services beyond what is described in Section 2.2(e) shall be subject to a Change Order in accordance with **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and this Work Order.

3.5 **Time-and-Materials Charges.**  

3.5.1 The T&M fee structure provided in **Section 3.5 (Time-and-Materials Charges)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Work Order, provided that any T&M effort estimated to exceed **USD $25,000** shall require the Company’s prior written approval (email being sufficient).

3.6 **Payment Terms (Modification of Parent Agreement).**  

3.6.1 Notwithstanding **Section 3.6 (Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement, which provides for **Net 60** payment terms, the Parties agree that for this Work Order **payment terms shall be Net 30**. Accordingly:

(a) The Service Provider shall issue invoices upon the occurrence of the relevant billing events described in this **Article III**;  

(b) The Company shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** from the date of receipt of a valid invoice (Net 30);  

(c) Invoices shall be sent electronically to the billing address designated by the Company and shall reference both **WO-GAM-202509-237** and the Parent Agreement **SOW-GAM-202509-217**.

3.6.2 In all other respects, Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement remains in effect.

3.7 **Late Payment.**  

Section 3.7 (Late Payment) of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Work Order. Interest shall accrue on undisputed overdue amounts at the rate set forth therein (1% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower), and the Service Provider may suspend performance of Extended Services upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, provided that critical production support is not unreasonably suspended.

3.8 **Taxes.**  

Section 3.8 (Taxes) of the Parent Agreement is incorporated herein and applies to WO Fees. The Company is responsible for applicable taxes (except those based on the Service Provider’s net income), and standard withholding and cooperation obligations shall apply.

3.9 **Expenses.**  

Section 3.9 (Expenses) of the Parent Agreement applies to this Work Order. Reasonable, pre-approved travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with on-site Extended Services shall be reimbursable at cost, subject to the Company’s travel and expense policies.

3.10 **Disputed Amounts.**  

Section 3.10 (Disputed Amounts) of the Parent Agreement applies to invoices under this Work Order. Non-payment of disputed amounts shall not constitute a breach pending resolution of the dispute, provided that undisputed amounts are timely paid.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term of Work Order.**  

The term of this Work Order (the **“WO Term”**) shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until **22 November 2030** (the **“WO Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or by operation of Section 4.5 below.

4.2 **Renewal.**  

Any renewal or extension of the Extended Managed Services beyond the WO Expiration Date shall be subject to mutual written agreement executed at least ninety (90) days prior to the WO Expiration Date, consistent with the approach in **Section 4.2 (Renewal)** of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by the Company.**  

The Company may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, by providing not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider, consistent with **Section 4.3** of the Parent Agreement. In such event:

(a) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Extended Services performed and Additional Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination;  

(b) The Company shall pay any non-cancellable expenses and commitments incurred in good faith in reliance on this Work Order, to the extent documented and substantiated.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by the Service Provider.**  

Given the high-risk profile recognized in both the Parent Agreement (Risk Level: High) and this Work Order, the Service Provider may terminate this Work Order for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to the Company, in alignment with **Section 4.4** of the Parent Agreement. Transition Assistance shall be provided as set forth in Section 4.6 below and in Section 4.6 of the Parent Agreement.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  

4.5.1 If the Parent Agreement **SOW-GAM-202509-217 (contract_217)** is terminated or expires for any reason prior to the WO Expiration Date, then, unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing:

(a) This Work Order shall **automatically terminate** on the effective date of termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement;  

(b) The Parties shall promptly meet to coordinate an orderly wind-down or transition of the Extended Services;  

(c) The payment obligations set forth in Sections 4.3 and 4.6 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the portion of the WO Fees accrued as of the effective date of such termination.

4.5.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Parties may mutually agree in writing that certain Extended Managed Services continue under this Work Order for a defined period following termination of the Parent Agreement, in which case selected provisions of the Parent Agreement necessary to support such continuation shall survive accordingly.

4.6 **Effects of Termination; Transition Assistance.**  

The effects of termination and Transition Assistance obligations set forth in **Section 4.6** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Work Order. In particular:

(a) The Company shall pay all undisputed WO Fees and reimbursable expenses due and payable as of the effective date of termination;  

(b) The Service Provider shall cease performance of Extended Services (except as necessary for Transition Assistance) and deliver any completed or in-progress Additional Deliverables for which the Company has paid;  

(c) For up to six (6) months following termination (or as otherwise agreed), the Service Provider shall provide Transition Assistance for the Extended Services at the T&M rates set out in Exhibit C of the Parent Agreement or such other rates as agreed.

4.7 **Termination for Cause and Insolvency.**  

The provisions of **Section 4.5 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement, including Sections 4.5.1 and 4.5.2, apply to this Work Order. Either Party may terminate this Work Order for material breach or insolvency events subject to the notice and cure rights described therein.
