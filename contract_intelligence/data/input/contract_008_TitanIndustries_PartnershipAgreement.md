## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement.** Contoso hereby engages Titan, and Titan hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described in this Agreement and in each SOW executed by the Parties. Each SOW shall be deemed incorporated into and governed by this Agreement. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern unless the SOW expressly states that it supersedes specific provisions of this Agreement.

2.2 **General Description of Services.** Titan shall provide to Contoso a range of professional and technical services which may include, without limitation:

  (a) **Consulting and Advisory Services.** Strategic planning, business process analysis, solution design, and architectural consulting in relation to Contoso’s industrial and commercial operations, systems, and infrastructure.

  (b) **Implementation and Integration Services.** Installation, configuration, customization, and integration of systems, platforms, equipment, and software identified in the applicable SOW, including integration with Contoso’s existing systems, third-party systems, and infrastructure.

  (c) **Managed Services and Operational Support.** Ongoing monitoring, management, and support of certain systems and processes as identified in the SOW, including incident management, problem resolution, and preventive maintenance.

  (d) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Development and delivery of training materials and training sessions for Contoso personnel regarding the use, operation, and maintenance of the Deliverables and any supported systems, as may be specified in the SOW.

  (e) **Project Management.** Project planning, coordination, reporting, and risk management for the Services, including the assignment of a project manager to act as Titan’s primary point of contact.

2.3 **Deliverables.** For each SOW, Titan shall produce and deliver the Deliverables identified therein, which may include, by way of example and not limitation:

  (a) Detailed requirements and design documents;  
  (b) Configuration and implementation documentation;  
  (c) System integration specifications and test plans;  
  (d) Test results, acceptance reports, and go-live readiness assessments;  
  (e) User guides, operational manuals, and training materials;  
  (f) Software configuration files, scripts, and related artifacts (excluding Titan Pre-Existing IP); and  
  (g) Periodic performance, capacity, and incident reports.  

All Deliverables shall conform in all material respects to the Specifications, the relevant SOW, and the applicable Service Levels.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Titan shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, with due skill, care, and diligence consistent with industry standards for providers of similar services;  

  (b) in accordance with the timetable, milestones, and deadlines specified in the applicable SOW, subject to any extensions permitted under this Agreement (including under Article XIII and Article XV);  

  (c) in compliance with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations, and Contoso’s reasonable written policies provided in advance, including without limitation safety, security, and access policies for Contoso’s facilities and systems; and  

  (d) using appropriately qualified, trained, and experienced personnel. Titan shall be solely responsible for the direction and control of its personnel.

2.5 **Project Governance and Cooperation.**  

  (a) **Project Management.** Titan shall designate a project manager who shall serve as the primary point of contact between the Parties for the Services. Contoso shall likewise designate a project manager or liaison with decision-making authority for approvals required under the SOW.  

  (b) **Meetings and Reporting.** Titan shall participate in regular status meetings (which may be in person or via teleconference/videoconference) at frequencies specified in each SOW. Titan shall prepare and deliver written status reports, including progress against milestones, key risks and issues, and proposed mitigation measures.  

  (c) **Cooperation and Access.** Contoso shall provide Titan with reasonable cooperation and access to facilities, systems, personnel, and Client Materials necessary to perform the Services, subject to Contoso’s security and safety policies. Titan shall not be liable for delays or failures in performance to the extent caused by Contoso’s failure to provide such cooperation or access.

2.6 **Assumptions and Dependencies.** Each SOW shall set forth any assumptions and dependencies on which the Services are based, including the availability of Client Materials, third-party products, or Contoso personnel. Titan’s ability to meet the schedule and Service Levels is expressly conditioned on the timely fulfillment of such assumptions and dependencies. If such assumptions prove incorrect or dependencies are not fulfilled, the Parties shall, in good faith, adjust the schedule, Service Levels, and fees through a Change Order in accordance with Article XIII.

2.7 **Subcontractors.** Titan may, in its discretion, engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services; provided, however, that Titan shall (i) remain fully responsible for the performance of the Services, (ii) ensure that any subcontractor is bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth in this Agreement, and (iii) not use any subcontractor that is reasonably objected to by Contoso on written grounds relating to security, compliance, or conflict of interest.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  (a) Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, Contoso shall have a period of thirty (30) days (the “**Acceptance Period**”) from delivery of each Deliverable to review and test such Deliverable for substantial conformity with the applicable Specifications.  

  (b) If Contoso determines in good faith that a Deliverable does not materially conform to the Specifications, Contoso shall provide Titan with written notice describing the nonconformities in reasonable detail. Titan shall, at no additional charge (except as otherwise provided in the SOW), promptly use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the nonconformities and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance.  

  (c) The corrected Deliverable shall be subject to a further Acceptance Period of fifteen (15) days. If, after a reasonable number of correction attempts (not less than two (2)), Titan fails to deliver a conforming Deliverable, Contoso may exercise its rights under Article IV and Article VIII, as applicable.  

  (d) A Deliverable shall be deemed accepted upon the earliest of (i) Contoso’s written acceptance, (ii) the expiration of the Acceptance Period without a written notice of nonconformity, or (iii) use of the Deliverable in production by Contoso (other than for testing purposes).

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge that the estimated aggregate value of all Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Agreement during the Term is **Three Million Seven Hundred Sixty-Two Thousand Five Hundred Eighty-One U.S. Dollars (US $3,762,581)**, as more particularly described in the SOWs executed hereunder. This is an estimate based on the scope currently contemplated and may be adjusted by the Parties through additional SOWs or Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** Fees for the Services may be structured as any combination of:

  (a) **Fixed Fees.** Fixed-price amounts for specified Deliverables or milestones, as set forth in the applicable SOW;  

  (b) **Time-and-Materials.** Hourly or daily rates for professional services rendered, based on the rate card attached to the applicable SOW; and  

  (c) **Recurring Fees.** Monthly, quarterly, or annual fees for managed services, hosting services, or support services, as specified in the SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

  (a) Titan shall invoice Contoso in accordance with the billing schedule set forth in each SOW. In the absence of such schedule, Titan may invoice monthly in arrears for Services performed and Deliverables delivered during the preceding month.  

  (b) All invoices shall be due and payable by Contoso within **sixty (60) days** of the date of the invoice (“**Net 60**”), without setoff or deduction, except to the extent that Contoso disputes an invoice in good faith pursuant to Section 3.5.  

  (c) Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method to the account designated by Titan in the invoice.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

  (a) Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.  

  (b) In addition, if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment, Titan may, upon written notice, suspend performance of the Services until such amounts, together with accrued interest, are paid in full, provided that Titan shall not suspend mission-critical services without giving Contoso at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice and an opportunity to cure.  

  (c) Titan’s right to interest and suspension under this Section 3.4 shall be without prejudice to any other rights or remedies available to Titan at law, in equity, or under this Agreement.

3.5 **Invoice Disputes.** If Contoso disputes any invoiced amount in good faith, Contoso shall notify Titan in writing within thirty (30) days of the invoice date, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute in reasonable detail. The Parties shall work together in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Contoso shall pay all undisputed portions of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3. Any resolved disputed amounts shall be paid within thirty (30) days after resolution.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

  (a) All fees are exclusive of Taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for and shall pay all applicable Taxes arising from or relating to this Agreement, except for Taxes based on Titan’s net income, property, or employees.  

  (b) If Titan is required to collect or remit any Taxes on behalf of Contoso, Titan shall itemize such Taxes in the invoice, and Contoso shall pay such Taxes in addition to the fees, unless Contoso provides Titan with a valid tax exemption certificate authorized by the appropriate taxing authority.  

  (c) Each Party shall provide reasonable cooperation to the other Party in connection with any tax audits or inquiries.

3.7 **Expenses.** Reasonable, documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Titan in the performance of the Services, including but not limited to travel, lodging, meals, and shipping, shall be reimbursed by Contoso at actual cost, provided that (i) such expenses are consistent with Contoso’s expense policies provided to Titan in advance, and (ii) any single expense in excess of US $2,500 shall require Contoso’s prior written approval, unless otherwise stated in the SOW.

3.8 **Adjustments and Increases.** For multi-year SOWs or recurring services, Titan may, upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Contoso, increase its standard rates annually by up to five percent (5%) or the percentage increase in the U.S. Consumer Price Index (CPI-U) for the preceding 12-month period, whichever is greater, unless a different rate adjustment mechanism is expressly agreed in the applicable SOW.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until May 24, 2026 (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, upon mutual written agreement executed at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Agreement for additional periods on such terms as the Parties may then agree. Unless and until any renewal is so agreed, this Agreement shall expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to Titan. Titan may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon at least one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Contoso.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  (a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course; or (ii) materially breaches this Agreement or an SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer period as may be specified in the notice, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days) after receiving written notice from the non-breaching Party describing the breach in reasonable detail.  

  (b) Without limiting the foregoing, repeated uncured failures by Contoso to pay undisputed amounts when due may constitute a material breach; repeated failure by Titan to achieve Service Levels may, depending on severity and duration, also constitute a material breach, subject to any cure and remediation provisions set forth in Article XII and the applicable SOW.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) Contoso shall pay Titan for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered (and accepted or deemed accepted) up to the effective date of termination, plus any non-cancellable commitments or termination fees for third-party products or services incurred by Titan in reliance on this Agreement or the SOW, provided Titan provides reasonable supporting documentation.  

  (b) To the extent permitted by law, all licenses granted to Contoso with respect to Titan Pre-Existing IP shall continue in accordance with their terms, except as expressly provided otherwise in this Agreement.  

  (c) Each Party shall, within thirty (30) days after termination, return or destroy, at the disclosing Party’s option, all Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession or control, subject to the retention right in Section 5.4 and data retention obligations under Article XI.  

  (d) Titan shall provide reasonable termination assistance services at Contoso’s written request and expense, at Titan’s then-current time-and-materials rates, for a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination, to facilitate transition of the Services to Contoso or a third party.

4.6 **Survival.** The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason: Articles I, III (with respect to payment obligations accrued prior to termination), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and this Section 4.6, as well as any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE V

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.** “**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”) in connection with this Agreement, whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, financial information, product plans, technical data, technology, software, trade secrets, know-how, customer information, pricing, and the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

5.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate: (a) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party; (b) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or (d) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party.

5.3 **Obligations.**  

  (a) The Receiving Party shall use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services and fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement.  

  (b) The Receiving Party shall protect the confidentiality of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.  

  (c) The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, officers, directors, agents, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.** If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement so that the Disclosing Party may seek an appropriate protective order or other remedy. The Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose and shall reasonably cooperate, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in any effort of the Disclosing Party to obtain confidential treatment or protective orders with respect to such Confidential Information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) copies required to comply with legal and regulatory obligations or for bona fide archival purposes, in which case such copies shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein, and (b) copies stored in routine backup systems that cannot feasibly be deleted, provided they are not accessed except for backup recovery purposes.

5.6 **Survival.** The obligations in this Article V shall remain in effect during the Term and for a period of **five (5) years** following expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall be protected for as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  

  (c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of progress in resolving the Force Majeure Event.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.** To the extent that a Party’s performance of its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) is prevented or delayed by a Force Majeure Event, such performance shall be excused for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. 15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination, except for payment of fees for Services performed and Deliverables delivered up to the effective date of termination.
