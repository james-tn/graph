## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
2.1.1 The Service Provider shall provide the Client with enterprise-level managed technology services, including, without limitation:

  (a) **Cloud Hosting and Infrastructure Services** – provisioning, configuration, monitoring, and maintenance of virtual servers, storage, network connectivity, load balancing, and backup infrastructure necessary to host the Client’s business applications;  

  (b) **Application Management Services** – deployment, patching, configuration management, and performance tuning of designated Client applications, including but not limited to ERP, CRM, and internal web portals specifically identified in an SOW;  

  (c) **Service Desk and Support** – operation of a multi-tier help desk providing Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 technical support for Authorized Users, including incident logging, troubleshooting, escalation management, and resolution;  

  (d) **Monitoring and Alerting Services** – 24x7x365 monitoring of the hosted infrastructure and critical applications, including automated alerts, incident detection, and response coordination;  

  (e) **Security Services** – implementation and maintenance of security controls, including firewalls, intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDPS), vulnerability scanning, anti-malware solutions, and security patch management within the Service Provider’s managed environment; and  

  (f) **Professional Services and Consulting** – periodic technology assessments, capacity planning, architecture reviews, and implementation or migration projects as mutually agreed in writing in an SOW.

2.1.2 The specific Services, project phases, Deliverables, and timelines shall be detailed in one or more SOWs, which shall be deemed incorporated into this Agreement by reference. In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede specific provisions of this Agreement.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
2.2.1 The Service Provider shall produce and provide to the Client the Deliverables as specified in applicable SOWs. By way of example and without limitation, such Deliverables may include:

  (a) **Architecture and Design Documents** – describing system topology, network diagrams, security configurations, and scalability considerations;  

  (b) **Implementation and Migration Plans** – documenting tasks, responsibilities, rollback plans, and testing procedures for system deployment or data migration;  

  (c) **Operational Runbooks** – containing standard operating procedures, escalation paths, recovery steps, and maintenance schedules;  

  (d) **Periodic Service Reports** – including monthly performance, uptime, incident, and capacity reports, as more fully described in Section 12.4; and  

  (e) **Training Materials** – user guides, FAQs, and training sessions for Authorized Users where agreed in an SOW.

2.2.2 Unless otherwise expressly stated in the applicable SOW, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted upon the earlier of: (i) the Client’s written acceptance; or (ii) ten (10) Business Days after delivery if the Client has not provided a written rejection specifying material non-conformities. If the Client timely rejects a Deliverable, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance.

2.3 **Service Levels and Performance Standards.**  
2.3.1 The Service Provider shall render the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry standards for managed services providers of similar size and resources, and in compliance with the service level commitments and uptime targets detailed in Article XII (Service Level Agreements).

2.3.2 The Service Provider shall perform the Services on a 24x7x365 basis for production environments, subject to Permitted Downtime as defined in Section 12.1.2. Non-production environments may be subject to less stringent service levels as set forth in the applicable SOW.

2.3.3 The Service Provider shall maintain a support structure that includes:  

  (a) A help desk reachable by phone and email during all hours of operation;  

  (b) Incident classification and prioritization based on impact and urgency, including defined response and resolution targets as set forth in Section 12.2; and  

  (c) Escalation procedures that ensure prompt attention by senior technical staff for high-severity Incidents.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
2.4.1 The Client shall:  

  (a) Provide accurate, complete, and timely information reasonably required by the Service Provider for the performance of the Services;  

  (b) Designate a primary point of contact responsible for coordination with the Service Provider;  

  (c) Ensure that Authorized Users comply with the usage policies, security requirements, and technical constraints communicated by the Service Provider;  

  (d) Provide on-site access, network connectivity, and any necessary hardware or software described as Client-provided in any SOW; and  

  (e) Promptly review Deliverables and provide acceptance or detailed feedback in accordance with Section 2.2.2.

2.4.2 The Service Provider’s performance is conditioned upon the Client’s timely discharge of its responsibilities. The Service Provider shall not be liable for any failure to meet service levels or timelines caused by the Client’s delay or omission.

2.5 **Subcontracting.**  
2.5.1 The Service Provider may subcontract portions of the Services to third parties, provided that: (a) the Service Provider remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors; and (b) any subcontractor who will have access to Client Data is bound by confidentiality and data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement.

2.5.2 The Client hereby consents to the Service Provider’s use of subcontractors and third-party cloud providers, including data centers located within the United States, for provision of the Services, subject to compliance with Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy).

2.6 **Risk Level and Operational Controls.**  
2.6.1 In view of the **High Risk** classification of the engagement, the Parties acknowledge that:

  (a) The Client’s business operations rely heavily on the availability and security of the Services;  

  (b) The Service Provider will implement advanced monitoring, change control, and incident management procedures to mitigate operational risks, in accordance with Articles XI and XII; and  

  (c) The contractual allocation of legal and financial risk shall be governed by Articles VIII, IX, and X, which are intentionally structured in a Vendor-favorable manner, consistent with the risk level designation.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **December 23, 2026** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Initial Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
4.2.1 The Agreement may be extended upon mutual written agreement of the Parties. Absent such written agreement, the Agreement shall automatically terminate on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
4.3.1 Given the high-risk nature of this engagement and the Service Provider’s commitments and investments in infrastructure and staffing, the Client may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW hereunder, for convenience only upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Service Provider.

4.3.2 The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to the Client, provided that the Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate with the Client during such notice period to facilitate transition as set forth in Section 4.6.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 Notwithstanding Section 4.4.1, the Service Provider may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the Client: (a) fails to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment; (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets; or (c) breaches its obligations under Article V (Confidentiality) or Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy).

4.5 **Termination for Regulatory or Legal Reasons.**  
4.5.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement effective immediately upon written notice if continued performance would result in a violation of Applicable Law or a directive of a governmental or regulatory authority with jurisdiction. The terminating Party shall provide as much prior notice as reasonably feasible under the circumstances.

4.6 **Effects of Termination.**  
4.6.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  

  (a) The Client shall immediately pay all fees and expenses accrued through the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments and early termination charges agreed in an SOW;  

  (b) The Service Provider shall cease performing the Services, except as may be expressly required under this Section 4.6 or an agreed transition plan;  

  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s option) any Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession, subject to Section 5.3; and  

  (d) The Service Provider shall, if requested by the Client within thirty (30) days after termination, provide commercially reasonable assistance for data export and transition to another provider, subject to the payment of mutually agreed professional services fees.

4.6.2 The termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not affect any rights, obligations, or liabilities that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, nor any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination, including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, and XVI.

  (b) Not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein; and

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
5.3.1 The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  

  (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  

  (b) Was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  

  (c) Is received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  

  (d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4.1 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law, regulation, or court order, provided that, to the extent legally permitted, it gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates reasonably (at the Disclosing Party’s expense) in any effort to limit or contest the disclosure.

5.5.1 The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Client Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Client Data respectively.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Availability and Uptime.**  
12.1.1 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the hosted Services is available **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month (**“Availability Commitment”**).

12.1.2 For purposes of calculating availability, the following shall not be considered Downtime (**“Permitted Downtime”**):  

  (a) Scheduled maintenance, provided that the Service Provider gives at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice and schedules such maintenance outside of peak usage hours where reasonably possible;  

  (b) Emergency maintenance in response to critical security threats or system failures, provided that the Service Provider uses reasonable efforts to minimize disruptions;  

  (c) Downtime attributable to the Client’s systems, networks, or third-party services not under the Service Provider’s control;  

  (d) Downtime caused by Force Majeure Events; or  

  (e) Downtime resulting from the Client’s breach of this Agreement.

12.2 **Incident Response and Resolution.**  
12.2.1 The Service Provider shall respond to Incidents according to the following target times:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Service completely unavailable or significantly degraded with major business impact – response within one (1) hour, resolution or workaround within four (4) hours.  
  - **Priority 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired with high business impact – response within two (2) hours, resolution or workaround within eight (8) hours.  
  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Limited functionality or performance issues with moderate business impact – response within four (4) hours, resolution or workaround within two (2) Business Days.  
  - **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries – response within one (1) Business Day, resolution on a best-effort basis.

12.2.2 The Client shall provide sufficient details and contact information when reporting Incidents to facilitate timely diagnosis and resolution.

12.3 **Service Credits (Exclusive SLA Remedy).**  
12.3.1 If the Service Provider fails to meet the Availability Commitment in a given calendar month, the Client may be eligible to receive Service Credits, calculated as a percentage of the monthly recurring fees for the affected Services as follows:

  - Uptime >= 99.0% and < 99.9%: 5% Service Credit  
  - Uptime >= 97.0% and < 99.0%: 10% Service Credit  
  - Uptime < 97.0%: 15% Service Credit

12.3.2 To receive Service Credits, the Client must submit a written request within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the Service Provider failed to meet the Availability Commitment. Service Credits will be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash.

12.3.3 **Service Credits constitute the Client’s sole and exclusive remedy** for any failure to meet service levels described in this Article XII, without prejudice to the general remedies available under Article IX, given the uncapped liability regime applicable to other forms of breach or harm.

12.4 **Reporting.**  
12.4.1 The Service Provider shall provide the Client with monthly reports summarizing uptime statistics, incident volumes, response times, and service performance.
