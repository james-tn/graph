## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Amendment **modifies and supplements** the scope of services set out in **Article II (Scope of Services/Work)** of the Parent Agreement, and is expressly stated to **amend the Statement of Work dated 21 September 2025 (Ref: SOW-PIN-202509-222)**.  

2.1.2 Unless expressly superseded herein, all descriptions of **Services** in **Section 2.1 through 2.7** of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect and shall be read together with this Article II. In case of conflict between the Parent Agreement and this Amendment with respect to Additional Services, **this Amendment shall prevail** for the subject matter of the conflict.

2.2 **Overview of Additional Services.**

2.2.1 The Vendor shall provide a comprehensive expansion of the Client’s existing enterprise business systems program originally described in **Section 2.1 (Overview)** and **Section 2.2 (Service Components)** of the Parent Agreement. The Additional Services under this Amendment include:

  (a) A **Second Implementation Wave** extending configuration and integration activities to additional business units and regions not covered under the Parent Agreement;

  (b) A **Data Integration and Advanced Analytics Program** expanding the “data integration” scope referenced in **Section 2.1** and **Section 2.2.3(b)** to include enterprise data warehouse (EDW) optimisation, advanced analytics, and self-service BI capabilities;

  (c) **Enhanced Managed Support Services and Capacity** building upon **Section 2.2.6 (Managed Support and Maintenance Services)** and the SLAs in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement, including better coverage and enhanced availability commitments;

  (d) **Continuous Optimisation and Improvement Services** to refine system performance, address evolving business requirements, and incorporate new regulatory or compliance mandates;

  (e) **Expanded Training and Knowledge Transfer Services** that supplement **Section 2.2.5** of the Parent Agreement for broader user populations and more advanced content.

2.2.2 All Additional Services shall be performed in accordance with the performance standards in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented by the more stringent or specific obligations set forth in this Amendment.

2.3 **Detailed Description of Additional Services.**

2.3.1 **Second Implementation Wave and Regional Roll-Out.**

  (a) The Vendor shall design and execute an extended implementation of the Client’s selected platforms (as referenced in **Section 2.2.3(a)** of the Parent Agreement, including but not limited to Microsoft Dynamics 365, SAP S/4HANA, or equivalent) across up to **four (4) additional business units** and **three (3) new geographic regions** designated by the Client in writing.

  (b) The Vendor shall develop an additional **thirty (30) integration workflows** beyond the original twenty (20) indicated in **Section 2.2.3(b)** of the Parent Agreement. These additional integrations shall include cross-region data synchronization, extended CRM–ERP–Data Warehouse flows, and customer portal integrations as requested through the Change Management process.

  (c) The Vendor shall create, modify, or optimise an additional **forty (40) reports and dashboards**, focused on region-specific regulatory reporting, executive analytics, and operational performance KPIs, supplementing the up to thirty (30) reports specified in **Section 2.2.3(c)** of the Parent Agreement.

  (d) The Vendor shall conduct expanded configuration of role-based access controls and security configurations in line with **Section 2.2.3(d)** and the Data Protection obligations in **Article XI** of the Parent Agreement and Article XI of this Amendment.

2.3.2 **Advanced Data Integration and Analytics.**

  (a) Building upon the **Data Migration Services** described in **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall design and implement an enhanced data integration framework, including:

   - Development of an additional **enterprise ETL layer** enabling near real-time data ingestion from legacy and third-party systems;
   - Creation of **canonical data models** and **data quality rules** for critical data domains (customer, product, finance, and operations);
   - Implementation of **data lineage and metadata management** capabilities for auditability and compliance.

  (b) The Vendor shall deliver an **Advanced Analytics Suite**, consisting of at least:

   - Ten (10) executive dashboards;
   - Twenty (20) advanced analytical reports;
   - Self-service BI templates and semantic models for at least three (3) departments.

  (c) The Vendor shall produce an **Analytics and Data Governance Runbook**, detailing ongoing procedures for data stewardship, monitoring, and periodic quality assessments, constituting Incremental Deliverables under this Amendment.

2.3.3 **Enhanced Managed Support Services and Capacity.**

  (a) The Vendor shall expand the baseline support scope defined in **Section 2.2.6** of the Parent Agreement by:

   - Providing support for all newly onboarded business units and regions;
   - Extending coverage to **24x7** for **Severity 1 (Critical)** incidents as defined in **Section 12.3** of the Parent Agreement, as modified in Section 12.3 of this Amendment;
   - Increasing the Vendor’s support team capacity to handle an anticipated additional **40% ticket volume** relative to baseline volumes under the Parent Agreement.

  (b) The Vendor shall continue to provide monthly **Service Performance Reports** as required under **Section 2.2.6(e)** of the Parent Agreement, enhanced to include coverage metrics, backlog analytics, and continuous improvement initiatives.

  (c) The Enhanced Support Services will operate under the **Enhanced Service Levels** described in **Article XII** of this Amendment, which shall supersede conflicting SLA provisions in **Article XII of the Parent Agreement** solely with respect to the Additional Services.

2.3.4 **Continuous Optimisation and Improvement.**

  (a) The Vendor shall conduct **quarterly optimisation assessments**, reviewing system performance, user adoption, incident trends, and alignment with Client’s evolving business strategy.

  (b) Following each assessment, the Vendor shall provide a **Quarterly Optimisation Report** containing:

   - Root cause analysis of recurring incidents;
   - Recommendations for configuration changes, process improvements, or additional enhancements;
   - A prioritised remediation plan, including estimated effort and timelines.

  (c) Optimisation activities identified in such reports that are within the defined scope and budget of this Amendment shall be executed without additional charge. Activities outside such scope shall be handled via **Change Requests** pursuant to **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

2.3.5 **Expanded Training and Knowledge Transfer.**

  (a) In addition to the up to six (6) training sessions described in **Section 2.2.5(b)** of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall deliver up to **twelve (12)** additional instructor-led training sessions (on-site or remote, as agreed) covering:

   - Advanced analytics usage;
   - Region-specific business processes;
   - Administrative operations for new regional teams.

  (b) The Vendor shall update and expand end-user training materials, including user guides and quick reference sheets, and prepare at least six (6) new video tutorials.

  (c) The Vendor shall deliver an **Advanced Administrative Handover Pack** for the extended solution footprint, complementing the administrative handover pack referenced in **Section 2.2.5(c)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Enhanced Milestones and Timelines.**

2.4.1 The following **Enhanced Milestones** shall apply to the Additional Services under this Amendment (timelines measured from the Amendment Effective Date), and are in addition to the original milestones in **Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement**:

  - **Enhanced Milestone A – Extended Design & Architecture Sign-Off:**  
    Completion of detailed design for additional business units and regions, including updated integration design and analytics architecture, within **one hundred eighty (180) days** from the Amendment Effective Date.

  - **Enhanced Milestone B – Second Implementation Wave Go-Live:**  
    Completion of configuration, integrations, and UAT for the second implementation wave and go-live for at least two (2) new regions within **twelve (12) months** from the Amendment Effective Date.

  - **Enhanced Milestone C – Full Regional Roll-Out Completion:**  
    Deployment to all in-scope additional regions and business units, including data cutover and hypercare, within **eighteen (18) months** from the Amendment Effective Date.

  - **Enhanced Milestone D – Advanced Analytics Suite Deployment:**  
    Delivery of the Advanced Analytics Suite described in **Section 2.3.2(b)** within **fourteen (14) months** from the Amendment Effective Date.

  - **Enhanced Milestone E – Transition to Extended Steady-State Support:**  
    Completion of at least **ninety (90) days** of post-wave-2 hypercare and formal transition to the Enhanced Support Services, anticipated within **twenty (20) months** of the Amendment Effective Date.

2.4.2 Time shall not be of the essence except where explicitly stated in this Amendment, consistent with **Section 2.5.3** of the Parent Agreement; however, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the Enhanced Milestones, and shall promptly notify the Client of anticipated delays, causes, and remediation.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

2.5.1 The Vendor shall provide the Additional Services with at least the level of skill, care, and diligence described in **Section 2.5.1** of the Parent Agreement and, where applicable, the more stringent Enhanced SLAs in **Article XII** of this Amendment.

2.5.2 The Incremental Deliverables shall conform in all material respects to the specifications and requirements agreed in writing by the Parties for the Additional Services, and non-conformities shall be corrected at the Vendor’s cost in accordance with **Section 2.5.2** and **Article VII (Representations and Warranties)** of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented herein.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**

2.6.1 In addition to the obligations set out in **Section 2.6** of the Parent Agreement, the Client shall:

  (a) Designate regional contacts and subject matter experts for each new region and business unit participating in the second implementation wave;

  (b) Ensure timely provision of local regulatory requirements and process documentation necessary for configuration and reporting;

  (c) Provide secure, timely access to any additional legacy systems or third-party applications required for data integration and analytics under **Section 2.3.2**;

  (d) Facilitate scheduling and participation of end-users and administrators in the expanded training sessions.

2.6.2 Delays in the Client’s fulfilment of these responsibilities may impact the Vendor’s ability to meet Enhanced Milestones and Enhanced SLAs and shall be treated in accordance with the principles described in **Section 2.6(d)** and **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **Out-of-Scope Services.**

2.7.1 The out-of-scope provisions in **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement continue to apply. Without limiting those provisions, the following are expressly **out of scope** for this Amendment unless incorporated via an approved Change Request:

  (a) Custom software development of entirely new standalone applications unrelated to the enterprise platforms contemplated in the Parent Agreement;

  (b) Hardware procurement, on-premise data center build-outs, or network infrastructure upgrades beyond advisory services;

  (c) Remediation of issues caused by unapproved third-party modifications to systems or configurations implemented by the Vendor;

  (d) Migration or integration of systems not reasonably identified in the impact assessments and design documentation for the Additional Services.

2.7.2 Any such out-of-scope services may be performed by the Vendor subject to separate Fees and timelines agreed through the Change Management process under **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

  (b) **Advanced Analytics and Data Governance Fees:**  
      **USD 850,000** for the Advanced Analytics Suite, data governance frameworks, and supporting materials described in **Section 2.3.2**.

      **USD 1,000,000** for the Enhanced Support Services provided during the Extended Term (and, if applicable, overlapping with the residual term of the Parent Agreement).

  (d) **Training, Optimisation, and Continuous Improvement Fees:**  
      **USD 200,000** for expanded training, knowledge transfer, and continuous optimisation services as described in **Sections 2.3.4 and 2.3.5**.

  (e) **Contingency and Change Reserve:**

3.3.1 The Vendor shall invoice the Client for the Wave 2 and Advanced Analytics components of the Amendment Contract Value based on the following milestones:

  (a) **Amendment Initiation (10% of 3.2.1(a) + 3.2.1(b)):**  
      Upon execution of this Amendment, the Vendor shall invoice **USD 365,000**.

  (b) **Enhanced Design & Architecture Sign-Off (20% of 3.2.1(a) + 3.2.1(b)):**

  (c) **Second Implementation Wave Go-Live (30% of 3.2.1(a)):**

  (d) **Full Regional Roll-Out Completion (25% of 3.2.1(a)):**  
      Upon completion of Enhanced Milestone C and Client’s conditional acceptance (subject to hypercare completion), the Vendor shall invoice **USD 700,000**.

  (e) **Advanced Analytics Suite Deployment (Remaining balance of 3.2.1(a) + 3.2.1(b)):**  
      Upon completion of Enhanced Milestone D and Client acceptance of the Advanced Analytics Suite, the Vendor shall invoice the remaining implementation and analytics Fees, being **USD 1,015,000**.

3.4 **Support Fees and Recurring Invoicing.**

3.4.1 The **Enhanced Managed Support and Capacity Fees** of **USD 1,000,000** shall be invoiced on a recurring basis as follows:

  (a) Quarterly in advance, in **equal instalments** over the Extended Term (from 25 December 2028 through 09 March 2030), prorated as necessary for any partial quarter, resulting in an approximate quarterly charge of **USD 142,857**, subject to a final true-up invoice or credit.

To the extent the Parties so agree in writing, the **Net 30** terms shall also apply prospectively to remaining invoices under the Parent Agreement.

3.5 **Contingency and Change Reserve.**

3.5.1 The **Contingency and Change Reserve** of **USD 104,810** shall be drawn down only upon the Client’s written approval of Change Requests in accordance with **Section 13.4 (Approval of Changes)** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

3.6.1 In the event the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount by the due date, such amount shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of actual payment at an annual rate equal to **three percent (3%) per annum above the Bank of England base rate**, or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower. 3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

3.7.1 The provisions of **Section 3.6 (Expenses and Reimbursement)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Amendment. In particular:

  (a) Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses, including travel, accommodation, and subsistence for on-site presence explicitly requested by the Client, shall be reimbursed by the Client;  

  (b) The Vendor shall obtain the Client’s written pre-approval for any single expense exceeding USD 1,000 and provide appropriate receipts;  

  (c) Approved expenses shall be invoiced monthly in arrears and paid on **Net 30** terms in line with this Amendment.

3.8 **Taxes.**

3.8.1 The taxes provisions in **Section 3.7** of the Parent Agreement apply equally to this Amendment. All amounts under this Amendment are exclusive of VAT and other applicable taxes, which shall be borne by the Client, except for taxes on the Vendor’s net income and employment-related taxes.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term and Extended Term.**

4.1.1 The original Term of the Parent Agreement, as described in **Section 4.1** thereof, remains unchanged for the existing scope and is set to expire on **24 December 2028**.

4.1.2 This Amendment shall commence on the **Amendment Effective Date** and shall remain in full force and effect until **09 March 2030**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent Agreement. For certain components (e.g., Wave 2 implementation), this Amendment will operate concurrently with the existing Parent Agreement Term.

4.1.3 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew the Extended Term or add further amendments, consistent with **Section 4.1.2** and **Section 16.2** of the Parent Agreement.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.2.1 Notwithstanding the sixty (60) days’ notice period in **Section 4.2.1** of the Parent Agreement, with respect to the Additional Services under this Amendment, either Party may terminate this Amendment for convenience, without cause, by providing the other Party with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Client of this Amendment:

  (a) The Client shall pay the Vendor all Fees earned and expenses incurred under this Amendment up to the effective date of termination, including a fair and reasonable proportion of milestone-based Fees for partially completed milestones, based on percentage of completion; and  

  (b) Any non-cancellable costs reasonably incurred by the Vendor in reliance on this Amendment and pre-approved by the Client shall be reimbursed in accordance with **Section 4.2.2** of the Parent Agreement.

4.2.3 In the event of termination for convenience by the Vendor of this Amendment, the Vendor shall take commercially reasonable steps to mitigate adverse impacts on the Client and, if requested, provide transition assistance as further described in **Section 4.5** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 4.5** of this Amendment.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**

4.3.1 The termination for cause rights in **Section 4.3** of the Parent Agreement apply equally to this Amendment. Either Party may terminate this Amendment (and, if so expressly stated, the Parent Agreement) with immediate effect upon a material breach by the other Party that remains uncured after thirty (30) days’ written notice specifying the breach.

4.4 **Termination Due to Parent Agreement.**

4.4.1 If the Parent Agreement is terminated in its entirety for any reason, this Amendment shall, unless otherwise agreed in writing, automatically terminate on the same effective date of termination.

4.4.2 Termination of this Amendment shall not, by default, terminate the Parent Agreement, unless the Party providing notice expressly elects to terminate both the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**

4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Amendment for any reason:

  (a) The Vendor shall cease performing the Additional Services (except as reasonably requested for transition assistance);  

  (b) Each Party shall return or destroy Confidential Information and Client Data associated with the Additional Services in accordance with **Article V** and **Article XI** of both the Parent Agreement and this Amendment;  

  (c) All accrued but unpaid Fees and approved expenses under this Amendment up to the effective date of termination shall become immediately due and payable, subject to the Client’s rights of dispute as set out in **Section 3.4.3** of the Parent Agreement.

4.5.2 Termination of this Amendment shall not affect any rights or obligations of the Parties that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, and shall be without prejudice to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

4.6 **Survival.**

4.6.1 The survival provisions in **Section 4.6** of the Parent Agreement shall apply. In addition, the following provisions of this Amendment shall survive its termination or expiration: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (ENHANCED SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.**

12.1.1 For systems and services in scope of the Enhanced Support Services, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable endeavours to ensure monthly Service Availability of at least **99.95%** per calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and agreed downtime. This obligation **supersedes the 99.9%** uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** of the Parent Agreement for the Additional Services.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**

12.2.1 The scheduled maintenance provisions in **Section 12.2** of the Parent Agreement apply, subject to the following enhancements:

  (a) For maintenance expected to impact production systems covered by the Enhanced SLAs, the Vendor shall give the Client at least **five (5) Business Days’** prior notice;  

  (b) Where reasonably practicable, maintenance shall be scheduled outside the Client’s local core business hours for the affected region.

12.3 **Incident Management and Response Times.**

12.3.1 The incident classification in **Section 12.3.1** of the Parent Agreement remains, but Enhanced Support Services shall adhere to the following **target response times**, measured during relevant support hours:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Initial response within **15 minutes**, with updates every **hour** until interim mitigation or resolution;  
  - **Severity 2 (High):** Initial response within **1 hour**, with daily updates;  
  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Initial response within **8 Business Hours**;  
  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Initial response within **2 Business Days**.

12.4 **Resolution Targets.**

12.4.1 The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents as follows:

  - Severity 1: within **4 Business Hours**;  
  - Severity 2: within **1 Business Day**;  
  - Severity 3: within **3 Business Days**;  
  - Severity 4: within the next scheduled release or maintenance window.

12.5 **Service Credits and Remedies.**

12.5.1 If the Vendor fails to meet the 99.95% monthly availability target or repeatedly fails to meet Severity 1 and 2 response/resolution targets for the Additional Services, the Client shall be entitled to **service credits** as follows (calculated as a percentage of the monthly Enhanced Support Fees):

  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.95%: **5%** credit;  
  - Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: **10%** credit;  
  - Availability below 98.0%: **20%** credit.

12.5.2 Service credits under this Amendment shall be cumulative with, and not exclusive of, other remedies, given the removal of liability caps in **Article IX**. However, the Client may not recover twice for the same loss.

12.6 **Reporting.**

12.6.1 The Vendor shall provide **monthly Service Performance Reports** in line with **Section 12.6** of the Parent Agreement, enhanced to include:

  - Global availability metrics;  
  - Incident trend analysis;  
  - SLA breach root cause analysis;  
  - Proposed remediation and improvement actions.
