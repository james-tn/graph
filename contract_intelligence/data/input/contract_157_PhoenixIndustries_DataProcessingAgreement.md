## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Appointment of Vendor.** The Client hereby appoints the Vendor, and the Vendor hereby accepts such appointment, to act as the Client’s Processor (or Service Provider) solely for the purpose of providing the Services specified in this Agreement, and to process Personal Data on behalf of the Client strictly in accordance with the Client’s documented instructions and this Agreement.

2.2 **Description of Services.** The Vendor shall provide a comprehensive suite of data processing and related services to support the Client’s business operations, which shall include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 **Data Hosting and Storage.** Vendor will host and store Client Data, including Personal Data, on Vendor’s managed cloud infrastructure. This includes provision of redundant storage, data backup and recovery, and secure access controls as further set forth in Article XI.

  2.2.2 **Data Integration and Ingestion.** Vendor shall provide tools and services for ingesting data from Client’s internal systems, third-party applications, and external data sources. This includes scheduled batch imports, real-time APIs, and secure file transfer protocols.

  2.2.3 **Data Transformation and Analytics.** Vendor shall provide data transformation, cleansing, normalization, and analytics services, including but not limited to generation of dashboards, reports, predictive models, and key performance indicator (KPI) analyses tailored to the Client’s specifications.

  2.2.4 **User Access and Interface.** Vendor will provide the Client with access to a web-based and/or API-based interface (“Platform”) through which authorized Client users can view, manage, and export data and reports, subject to role-based access control.

  2.2.5 **Support and Maintenance.** Vendor shall provide technical support and maintenance services, including resolution of incidents, bug fixes, performance tuning, and periodic updates to the Platform as further defined in Article XII (Service Levels).

  2.2.6 **Professional Services.** Upon Client’s request and subject to additional Fees, Vendor shall perform professional services such as data migration projects, custom integrations, bespoke analytics, configuration workshops, and training sessions for Client’s personnel.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of Section 2.2, Vendor shall provide, at a minimum, the following Deliverables during the Term:

  2.3.1 A secure, production-ready instance of the Platform configured for the Client, including environment setup, user provisioning, and initial data schema configuration.

  2.3.2 At least five (5) standardized dashboards and ten (10) custom reports defined by the Client and agreed upon in writing within sixty (60) days following the Effective Date.

  2.3.3 Monthly system performance and usage reports detailing uptime, latency, incident logs, and summary of support tickets.

  2.3.4 Documentation, including user guides, API documentation, and data mapping specifications sufficient for the Client to utilize and integrate the Services.

2.4 **Implementation Timeline.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the following indicative timeline:

  2.4.1 **Project Kickoff:** Within ten (10) Business Days following the Effective Date.

  2.4.2 **Initial Environment Provisioning:** Within twenty (20) Business Days following the Effective Date.

  2.4.3 **Initial Data Integration & First Dashboard Delivery:** Within sixty (60) days following receipt of all necessary Client input and access credentials.

  2.4.4 **Full Operational Readiness:** No later than ninety (90) days following the Effective Date, subject to Client’s timely cooperation and provision of required information and approvals.

Any failure to meet these target dates due to Client delays, change requests (see Article XIII), or circumstances beyond Vendor’s reasonable control shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement by Vendor.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.** The Client shall:

  2.5.1 Provide timely access to systems, data, network connections, and personnel reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services.

  2.5.2 Ensure that Client’s instructions regarding the processing of Personal Data are lawful and do not cause Vendor to violate Applicable Data Protection Law.

  2.5.3 Be solely responsible for the accuracy, quality, and legality of Client Data and the means by which Client acquired such data.

  2.5.4 Promptly review and approve or comment on Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of receipt. Failure to respond within such period shall constitute deemed acceptance.

2.6 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

  2.6.1 In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for managed data services;  

  2.6.2 In compliance with Applicable Data Protection Law and the security obligations set forth in Article XI; and  

  2.6.3 In accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

2.7 **Sub-processors.** Vendor may engage Sub-processors to perform the Services, provided that:

  2.7.1 Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of such Sub-processors;  

  2.7.2 Vendor enters into a written agreement with each Sub-processor imposing data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and  

  2.7.3 Vendor provides the Client with a list of current Sub-processors upon request and gives reasonable prior notice of any material changes, subject to Client’s rights under Applicable Data Protection Law.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total contract value for the Services under this Agreement is **US$51,474**, exclusive of any applicable taxes, duties, and similar governmental assessments.

3.2 **Fee Schedule.**

  3.2.1 **Base Platform Fee.** A fixed base fee of US$35,000 covering Platform access, hosting, standard support, and standard analytics during the Term.

  3.2.2 **Implementation and Onboarding Fee.** A one-time implementation fee of US$10,000 covering initial configuration, data migration, and training.

  3.2.3 **Professional Services Pool.** A pre-approved pool of US$6,474 for additional professional services (e.g., custom integrations, bespoke analytics). Such services shall be billed against this pool at Vendor’s then-current hourly rates (which shall be communicated to Client in writing), and any overages shall require written approval by the Client via a change order pursuant to Article XIII.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**

  3.3.1 **Invoicing Schedule.** Vendor shall invoice Client as follows:

   (a) 50% of the Implementation and Onboarding Fee (US$5,000) upon the Effective Date;  
   (b) The remaining 50% of the Implementation and Onboarding Fee (US$5,000) upon completion of initial environment provisioning under Section 2.4.2;  
   (c) The Base Platform Fee shall be invoiced annually in advance in equal installments over the Term unless otherwise mutually agreed in writing;  
   (d) Professional Services shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on time and materials, applied against the Professional Services Pool.

  3.3.2 **Payment Terms.** Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** of the invoice date (**Net 60**). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method.

3.4 **Late Payments.** Any undisputed invoice amount not received by Vendor by the due date shall accrue interest at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower, from the date such payment was due until the date paid. Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend the Services for persistent non-payment, provided that Client has not cured such payment default within such notice period.

3.5 **Taxes.** All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, or other taxes, duties, or governmental charges (collectively, “**Taxes**”). Client is responsible for paying all Taxes associated with the Services, excluding Taxes based on Vendor’s net income.

3.6 **Expenses.** Reasonable out-of-pocket travel and living expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with on-site services requested by Client shall be reimbursed by Client, provided that (a) such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Client and (b) Vendor provides reasonable supporting documentation.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.** Client must notify Vendor in writing of any good-faith dispute with an invoice within fifteen (15) days of the invoice date, specifying the reasons for the dispute. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of such invoice.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **April 30, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend the Term under mutually agreed terms and conditions no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** In light of the agreed high-risk nature of the engagement, and in consideration of Vendor’s commitment of resources, Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon providing **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor. Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Client.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice of such breach, except that the cure period for a material payment default shall be ten (10) Business Days.

  4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they mature; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**

  4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason: (a) all rights and licenses granted to Client hereunder shall immediately cease, except as expressly provided otherwise; and (b) Client shall promptly pay all Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued and payable through the effective date of termination.

  4.5.2 If Client terminates for convenience, Client shall also pay Vendor any non-cancellable costs, committed expenses, and a termination fee equal to twenty percent (20%) of the remaining contract value as a reasonable estimate of Vendor’s lost profits and administrative costs.

  4.5.3 Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request received within thirty (30) days after termination, provide Client with a one-time export of Client Data in a commonly used machine-readable format. Vendor may charge a reasonable fee for such export if it requires significant manual effort or professional services.

  4.5.4 Subject to Section 11.7 (Data Retention and Deletion), Vendor shall securely delete or anonymize Personal Data upon Client’s written instructions, except to the extent retention is required by applicable law.

4.6 **Survival.** The rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections that by their nature should survive, including without limitation Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

  5.4.1 Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;

## ARTICLE XI

11.4 **Security Measures.** Vendor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data, including:

  11.4.1 Encryption of Personal Data at rest and in transit where commercially reasonable;  

  11.4.2 Access controls, including role-based access and multi-factor authentication for administrative users;  

  11.4.3 Regular vulnerability assessments and patch management;  

  11.4.4 Business continuity and disaster recovery plans, including regular backup and recovery testing; and  

  11.4.5 Personnel training on data protection and information security.

11.5 **Data Subject Rights.** Taking into account the nature of the processing, Vendor shall assist Client, by appropriate technical and organizational measures, in fulfilling Client’s obligations to respond to Data Subject requests to exercise rights under Applicable Data Protection Law, including requests for access, rectification, erasure, restriction, or portability. 11.6 **Data Breach Notification.** Vendor shall notify Client without undue delay, and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours after becoming aware of a confirmed Data Breach affecting Personal Data processed under this Agreement. Such notification shall include:

  11.6.1 The nature of the Data Breach, including, where possible, categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned;  

  11.6.2 The likely consequences of the Data Breach; and  

  11.6.3 The measures taken or proposed to address the Data Breach and mitigate its possible adverse effects.

Vendor shall cooperate with Client in complying with Client’s obligations under Applicable Data Protection Law relating to Data Breaches, including any required notifications to supervisory authorities or Data Subjects.

### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Platform is available **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events (“**Availability Commitment**”).

  12.2.1 Vendor provides at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice to Client; and  

  12.2.2 Scheduled maintenance shall not exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month under normal circumstances.

12.3 **Incident Response Times.** Vendor shall respond to and work toward resolution of support incidents as follows (measured from the time Vendor receives notice):

  12.3.1 **Priority 1 (Critical – Platform Unavailable):** Initial response within one (1) hour, continuous efforts until resolution or workaround;  

  12.3.2 **Priority 2 (High – Major Functionality Impacted):** Initial response within four (4) hours, business-hours engagement until resolution or workaround;  

  12.3.3 **Priority 3 (Medium – Limited Functionality Impacted):** Initial response within one (1) Business Day;  

  12.3.4 **Priority 4 (Low – Minor Issues / Requests):** Initial response within three (3) Business Days.

12.4 **SLA Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the Availability Commitment in a given calendar month (excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events), Client shall be entitled to the following service credits, to be applied against future Fees:

  12.4.1 Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring Fee;  

  12.4.2 Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring Fee;  

  12.4.3 Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly recurring Fee.

12.5 **Exclusive Remedies for SLA Failures.** The service credits described in Section 12.4 shall be Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for Vendor’s failure to meet the Availability Commitment, except where such failure constitutes a material breach of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XV  
### FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.** A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control, including, without limitation, acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s workforce), failures of third-party hosting providers or telecommunications networks not under the Party’s control, and governmental actions or orders.

15.2 **Notice.** A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature of the event, its expected duration, and the steps being taken to mitigate its impact.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
