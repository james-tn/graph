## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide Client with professional and technology services focused on the design, configuration, integration, and optimization of Client’s enterprise data analytics, reporting, and operational platforms (the **“Services”**). The Services shall include, without limitation:

- Assessment of Client’s existing systems, data sources, and workflows;
- Design of target-state architecture and integration patterns;
- Implementation of data models, pipelines, and dashboards;
- Configuration of business intelligence tools and reporting automation;
- Limited customization of Vendor’s Pre-Existing IP to interface with Client’s environment;
- Support, maintenance, and advisory services as further described in this Article II and any SOW.

2.1.2 The Parties anticipate that, from time to time, they will enter into one or more Statements of Work that further specify the Services and Deliverables. Each SOW shall be governed by and subject to the terms of this Agreement. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that a particular provision supersedes a specific section of this Agreement.

### 2.2 Specific Deliverables

2.2.1 Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, Vendor shall provide the following categories of Deliverables, subject to the ownership and license provisions in Article VI:

- **Architecture & Design Documents:** High-level and detailed solution designs, integration diagrams, data flow maps, and security configuration documentation.
- **Configuration Artifacts:** System configuration files, parameter settings, and environment specifications for staging and production.
- **Data Models & Pipelines:** Logical and physical data models, ETL/ELT scripts or processes, and associated transformation rules and documentation.
- **Reports & Dashboards:** Configured business intelligence dashboards, key performance indicator (KPI) reports, and scheduled report definitions.
- **Operational Runbooks:** Procedural documentation for monitoring, maintenance, incident handling, and escalation pertaining to the Services.

2.2.2 Deliverables shall be delivered in electronic form via secure file transfer mechanisms, shared repositories, or other mutually agreed means. Client shall be responsible for maintaining its own backups and archives of Deliverables after delivery.

### 2.3 Timelines and Milestones

2.3.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to perform the Services and complete the Deliverables in accordance with the timelines and milestones set forth in any applicable SOW. The Parties acknowledge that timely performance requires Client’s full cooperation, including providing necessary information, access, and approvals.

2.3.2 Delays attributable to Client, including but not limited to failure to provide timely feedback, approvals, access to systems, or necessary resources, shall entitle Vendor to an equitable extension of the relevant timelines and may give rise to additional Fees as set forth in Article III and Article XIII.

2.3.3 Unless expressly guaranteed as firm dates in an SOW, all delivery and completion dates shall be deemed estimates only. Vendor shall not be liable for delays that arise from Client’s acts or omissions, Force Majeure Events, or third-party service providers’ failures.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services performed under similar circumstances.

2.4.2 Vendor does not warrant that the Services or Deliverables will be error-free or uninterrupted, or that they will meet all of Client’s business requirements, except as expressly set forth in Article VII or in a mutually signed SOW. Client acknowledges that complex technology projects inherently involve risks and dependencies on third-party systems.

2.4.3 Client shall be solely responsible for:

- Ensuring that Client’s internal policies, governance, and change management processes are followed;
- Providing accurate and complete Client Data and requirements;
- Ensuring that third-party systems, tools, and licenses are appropriately maintained and compatible with Vendor’s recommended configurations.

2.4.4 Vendor’s performance under this Article II shall be further supported by the Service Levels specified in Article XII.

### 2.5 Subcontracting

2.5.1 Vendor may, in its sole discretion, subcontract portions of the Services to qualified subcontractors, provided that Vendor shall remain primarily responsible for the performance of the Services and the confidentiality obligations in Article V.

2.5.2 Client hereby consents to Vendor’s use of subcontractors and Affiliates, including offshore resources, for the provision of Services, subject to Vendor’s compliance with the data protection and confidentiality provisions herein.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The Parties acknowledge that the aggregate estimated contract value for the initial Term of this Agreement is **Eight Hundred Forty-Two Thousand Six Hundred Fifty U.S. Dollars (US$842,650)** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustment through Change Orders and additional SOWs.

### 3.2 Fee Structure

3.2.1 Unless otherwise provided in an SOW, the Fees shall consist of:

- **Fixed Fees** for defined Deliverables or phases; and
- **Time-and-Materials (T&M) Fees** for ongoing consulting, support, and advisory Services, billed at Vendor’s then-current standard rates.

3.2.2 Vendor’s indicative rate card and fee schedule may be attached as an exhibit and updated by Vendor upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Client. Updated rates shall apply to new SOWs and any T&M work performed after such notice.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for T&M Services and in accordance with milestone achievements for fixed-fee Deliverables, as specified in the relevant SOW.

3.3.2 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) calendar days** after the invoice date (**“Net 30”**). Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed payment method.

3.3.3 Client shall notify Vendor in writing of any disputed amounts within ten (10) Business Days of receiving the invoice, specifying the basis for the dispute in reasonable detail. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve such disputes; however, Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice.

### 3.4 Late Payments

3.4.1 Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the due date until paid in full.

3.4.2 Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Services (including access to any hosted components) if Client fails to pay any undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days after the due date, and such failure continues during the notice period.

### 3.5 Taxes

3.5.1 All Fees and other amounts payable under this Agreement are exclusive of any applicable taxes, duties, and similar charges (other than taxes based on Vendor’s net income), and Client shall be responsible for paying any such taxes imposed on the transactions contemplated herein.

3.5.2 If Client is required by law to withhold or deduct any tax from any payment due to Vendor, Client shall promptly provide Vendor with official receipts or other documentation evidencing such withholding. The Parties shall reasonably cooperate to minimize any such withholding taxes, including by availing themselves of applicable tax treaties.

### 3.6 Expenses

3.6.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses necessarily incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and third-party tools or licenses reasonably required for project execution.

3.6.2 Vendor shall provide supporting documentation for such expenses upon request. Reimbursement shall be subject to the same Net 30 terms as set forth in Section 3.3.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **January 30, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend the Term for additional periods on such terms as they may agree.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for convenience upon providing the other Party at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**.

4.2.2 During such notice period, Vendor shall continue to perform the Services, and Client shall remain responsible for payment of all Fees and reimbursable expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable third-party costs and a reasonable wind-down period.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or any SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail (ten (10) days in the case of non-payment); or

(b) becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is the subject of any voluntary or involuntary proceeding relating to bankruptcy, receivership, liquidation, or similar proceedings, which proceeding is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.3.2 Vendor may, in addition to any other rights or remedies, immediately suspend performance of the Services upon written notice if Client’s use of the Services or Deliverables is illegal, fraudulent, or otherwise creates a material risk of legal liability or reputational harm to Vendor.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement:

(a) All SOWs shall automatically terminate unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing;

(b) Client shall promptly pay Vendor all outstanding Fees and approved expenses incurred through the effective date of termination, including any applicable early termination fees or recovery of non-cancellable costs specified in an SOW;

(c) Each Party shall, upon written request of the other Party, return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s election) all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, subject to the archival rights described in Section 5.4.

4.4.2 Termination shall not relieve either Party of any obligations that by their nature are intended to survive termination, including, without limitation, obligations under Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection), XIV (Dispute Resolution), and XVI (General Provisions).

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 **“Confidential Information”** means any and all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Disclosing Party to a Receiving Party, whether orally or in writing, visually or in other tangible form, and whether before, on, or after the Effective Date, including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, forecasts, and budgets;
- Financial information, pricing, and cost data;
- Technical information, including designs, specifications, and know-how;
- Client Data and Personal Data;
- Security protocols and system configurations;
- Any information that is identified as “confidential,” “proprietary,” or similar.

5.1.2 Confidential Information shall also include the existence and terms of this Agreement and each SOW, except to the extent disclosure is required by law or agreed in writing.

### 5.2 Exceptions

5.2.1 Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by competent written evidence:

(a) was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction at the time of disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

(b) becomes publicly known through no breach or fault of the Receiving Party;

(c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or

(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

### 5.3 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:

(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services or otherwise fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement;

(b) protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential or proprietary information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care;

(c) limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, Affiliates, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and

(d) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure

5.4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permissible) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing and cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.4.2 If such protective order or remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information which it is legally compelled to disclose, and shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that such information is treated as confidential.

### 5.5 Survival

5.5.1 The obligations of confidentiality and non-use under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that, for trade secrets, such obligations shall continue for as long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

5.5.2 Notwithstanding Section 4.4, each Party may retain archival copies of Confidential Information as required by law, regulation, or bona fide internal compliance policies, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.
