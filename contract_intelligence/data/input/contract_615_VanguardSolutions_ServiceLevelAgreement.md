## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Vendor shall provide the Client with comprehensive managed IT services designed to support the Client’s business operations, including but not limited to:

  (a) Cloud-hosted application management and support;  
  (b) Server and infrastructure monitoring and maintenance;  
  (c) Incident management and problem resolution;  
  (d) Change management support;  
  (e) Security patching and updates;  
  (f) Backup, recovery, and disaster recovery support; and  
  (g) Periodic reporting, performance reviews, and service optimization recommendations.

2.2 **Detailed Service Components.**

2.2.1 **Hosting and Infrastructure Services.**  
The Vendor shall host and manage the Client’s designated applications and systems in a secure, scalable environment (whether Vendor-owned, co-located, or public cloud-based). Responsibilities shall include:

  (a) Provisioning and configuration of virtual servers, storage, and network components;  
  (b) Operating system installation, hardening, and patch management;  
  (c) Monitoring of CPU, memory, storage, network throughput, and other performance metrics;  
  (d) Ensuring high availability and redundancy in accordance with the Service Levels in **Article XII**; and  
  (e) Implementing security measures such as firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, and access controls.

2.2.2 **Application Support and Maintenance.**  
The Vendor shall provide ongoing support for the Client’s designated applications, including:

  (a) Level 1 and Level 2 support for user-reported issues;  
  (b) Coordination with third-party software vendors for Level 3 escalations where applicable;  
  (c) Routine application health checks, log monitoring, and performance tuning;  
  (d) Deployment of agreed-upon application updates, patches, and minor enhancements; and  
  (e) Support for integration points between the hosted applications and the Client’s other systems, as described in any applicable SOW.

2.2.3 **Incident Management.**  
The Vendor shall operate an incident management process in accordance with IT service management best practices. This shall include:

  (a) A single point of contact (e.g., helpdesk or service desk) available during Business Hours, and on-call support for critical incidents outside Business Hours, as defined in an SOW;  
  (b) Logging, classification, and prioritization of Incidents based on severity and business impact;  
  (c) Response and Resolution Times aligned with the targets described in **Article XII**;  
  (d) Root cause analysis (“RCA”) for Priority 1 and Priority 2 Incidents, with documented reports shared with the Client within a reasonable timeframe; and  
  (e) Implementation of corrective and preventive actions to minimize incident recurrence.

2.2.4 **Backup and Disaster Recovery.**  
The Vendor shall implement and maintain backup and disaster recovery procedures as follows:

  (a) Daily backups of critical systems and data (or as otherwise specified in an SOW);  
  (b) Retention periods appropriate to the Client’s requirements (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly snapshots) as agreed in writing;  
  (c) Periodic testing of backup restoration procedures, at least annually, with summary results provided to the Client;  
  (d) Disaster recovery procedures designed to restore services within agreed recovery time objectives (RTOs) and recovery point objectives (RPOs); and  
  (e) Documentation of backup and restoration processes made available to the Client upon request.

2.2.5 **Security and Compliance Services.**  
The Vendor shall maintain security controls proportionate to the sensitivity of the Client’s data, including:

  (a) User authentication and authorization mechanisms;  
  (b) Role-based access controls;  
  (c) Encryption of data in transit using industry-standard protocols (e.g., TLS);  
  (d) Encryption of data at rest where feasible and agreed in writing;  
  (e) Vulnerability management and remediation; and  
  (f) Support for audits and compliance assessments relevant to the Services, subject to reasonable notice and confidentiality obligations.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.1**, the Vendor shall provide, at a minimum, the following deliverables:

  (a) **Service Implementation Plan**, detailing timelines, roles, dependencies, and cutover steps;  
  (b) **System Architecture and Design Documentation**, including network diagrams and data flow diagrams;  
  (c) **Monthly Performance Reports**, detailing uptime metrics, incident summaries, response and resolution performance, and capacity utilization;  
  (d) **Security and Compliance Reports**, including summaries of critical patches applied, security incidents (if any), and results of any periodic vulnerability scans;  
  (e) **Incident Reports and RCAs**, as applicable under **Section 2.2.3(d)**; and  
  (f) **Change Logs**, documenting significant changes to infrastructure and applications in accordance with **Article XIII**.

2.4 **Service Onboarding and Transition.**  
The Vendor shall manage onboarding activities, including:

  (a) Discovery and assessment of the Client’s existing systems and configurations;  
  (b) Migration of workloads and data to the Vendor’s environment with minimal disruption, following a mutually agreed migration plan;  
  (c) Knowledge transfer sessions with the Client’s designated personnel; and  
  (d) Transitional support during an agreed stabilization period after go-live.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) In a professional and workmanlike manner, using due skill, care, and diligence consistent with industry standards;  
  (b) In accordance with the Service Levels specified in **Article XII**;  
  (c) In compliance with Applicable Laws and Data Protection Laws; and  
  (d) In accordance with the Client’s reasonable written policies and security requirements provided to the Vendor in advance.

2.6 **Exclusions.**  
The following items are excluded from the scope of Services unless otherwise expressly agreed in an SOW:

  (a) End-user hardware support not under Vendor control;  
  (b) Onsite support at Client locations, unless specified;  
  (c) Major application development projects or custom software development beyond minor enhancements;  
  (d) Third-party license fees and subscriptions, unless explicitly included; and  
  (e) Any work resulting from unauthorized changes made by the Client or its third-party providers.

2.7 **Statements of Work.**  
Specific projects, detailed scope, and unique commercial terms may be set forth in one or more SOWs. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states otherwise and the Parties clearly indicate their intent to override this Agreement with respect to that SOW.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **May 13, 2030** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Unless either Party provides written notice of its intention not to renew at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods on the same terms and conditions, subject to any mutually agreed adjustments.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile and to provide client-friendly terms, the Client may terminate this Agreement or any specific SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Vendor. The Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Client.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any affected SOW) for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the cure period for breaches involving non-payment of undisputed fees shall be fifteen (15) days from notice.

4.4.3 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (a) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed over its assets; or  
  (b) Ceases to carry on business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:  
  (a) The Vendor shall cease providing the Services as of the effective date of termination, except for any transition assistance agreed under **Section 4.6**;  
  (b) The Client shall pay all fees due and payable for Services performed up to the effective date of termination, including any approved expenses; and  
  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5** and **Section 11.7** (for Personal Data).

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not automatically terminate any SOW unless such SOW is expressly terminated under the notice of termination.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon the Client’s request, and for a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration (or such other period as may be mutually agreed), the Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to the Client or a replacement provider, at the Vendor’s then-current T&M rates or such other rates as agreed in writing.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement: **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure the production environment of the Services is available **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and any Downtime caused by:

  (a) Force Majeure Events;  
  (b) Client’s acts or omissions, or those of its agents;  
  (c) Network or Internet outage outside the Vendor’s control; or  
  (d) Third-party service providers not under the Vendor’s control.

12.2 **Measurement of Availability.**  
Availability shall be calculated as follows:

> Availability % = (Total Minutes in Month – Downtime Minutes) / (Total Minutes in Month) × 100

Downtime shall not include periods of Scheduled Maintenance or exceptions listed in **Section 12.1**.

12.3 **Incident Severity Levels and Response Times.**  

- **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Complete system outage or major incident affecting core business operations with no workaround.  
  - Target Response Time: 30 minutes (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution Time: 4 hours

- **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant loss of functionality or performance degradation affecting multiple users, workaround available but unsatisfactory.  
  - Target Response Time: 1 hour (within Business Hours, and on-call outside Business Hours)  
  - Target Resolution Time: 8 Business Hours

- **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact issues affecting a subset of users, workaround available.  
  - Target Response Time: 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution Time: 2 Business Days

- **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general queries.  
  - Target Response Time: 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution Time: Next scheduled release or as mutually agreed.

12.4 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  

12.4.1 The Vendor may perform Scheduled Maintenance during agreed maintenance windows, generally outside Business Hours.

12.4.2 The Vendor shall provide the Client with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior written notice of Scheduled Maintenance expected to cause Downtime.

12.5 **Monitoring and Reporting.**  
The Vendor shall implement monitoring tools to track system availability and performance. Monthly performance and availability reports shall be provided to the Client, detailing:

  (a) Uptime metrics;  
  (b) Incident summaries by severity;  
  (c) Response and Resolution Time performance; and  
  (d) Any Service Level breaches and corrective actions taken.

12.6 **Service Credits.**  

12.6.1 In the event the Vendor fails to meet the monthly Uptime Commitment in **Section 12.1**, the Client shall be entitled to Service Credits as follows:

  - Availability 99.0% to < 99.9%: 5% credit of the monthly recurring fee;  
  - Availability 98.0% to < 99.0%: 10% credit of the monthly recurring fee;  
  - Availability < 98.0%: 20% credit of the monthly recurring fee.

12.6.2 To receive Service Credits, the Client must submit a written request within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the Service Level failure occurred. Service Credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash.

12.6.3 Service Credits shall be the Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for Service Level failures relating to availability, subject to the Client’s rights under **Section 4.3** and **Article IX** for repeated or material failures.

12.7 **Chronic Failure.**  
If the Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in three (3) or more months in any rolling six (6) month period, the Client may, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice and failure to cure, terminate the Agreement or the affected Services without penalty, in addition to any Service Credits earned.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to:

  (a) Acts of God, natural disasters, earthquakes, floods, fires, epidemics, or pandemics;  
  (b) War, terrorism, armed conflict, civil unrest, or riots;  
  (c) Government actions, embargoes, sanctions, or changes in law;  
  (d) Strikes, labor disputes, or industrial actions not limited to the affected Party’s employees;  
  (e) Failure of public utilities or widespread Internet outage not within the affected Party’s reasonable control.

15.2 **Notification.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature and expected duration of the event; and  
  (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that performance is prevented or materially hindered by the Force Majeure Event, provided that obligations for payment of fees already due are not excused.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the provision of essential Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Services or this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination.
