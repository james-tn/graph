## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Meridian shall provide Contoso with comprehensive managed IT services and support, including but not limited to:

  a. Infrastructure hosting and management services for selected Contoso business applications, including server hosting, storage, and network connectivity;  
  b. Application support services for designated applications, including incident management, problem management, patching, and minor enhancements;  
  c. Monitoring and alerting for critical infrastructure components and applications;  
  d. Service desk support and user support services at agreed service tiers;  
  e. Professional services related to migration, configuration, optimization, and documentation; and  
  f. Any additional services expressly set forth in a SOW executed under this Agreement.

2.2 **Infrastructure Hosting.**  
2.2.1 Meridian shall host Contoso’s designated applications and databases in a secure, high-availability environment using data centres located in the United Kingdom or within the European Economic Area, unless otherwise agreed in writing. Meridian will be responsible for:

  a. Provisioning virtual or physical servers according to the capacity, performance, and redundancy requirements agreed with Contoso;  
  b. Implementing and maintaining operating systems, security patches, and antivirus/anti-malware solutions;  
  c. Providing network connectivity, firewalls, load balancers, and related network infrastructure; and  
  d. Implementing backup and recovery procedures as described in Section 2.6.

2.2.2 Meridian shall ensure that the infrastructure meets the Service Levels defined in Article XII, including the uptime commitment of not less than **99.9% System Availability** in each calendar month, subject to the exclusions defined therein.

2.3 **Application Support.**  
2.3.1 Meridian shall provide Level 2 and Level 3 application support for the applications specified by Contoso, which may include, without limitation:

  a. Troubleshooting application errors and performance issues;  
  b. Applying vendor-supplied patches, updates, and upgrades in coordination with Contoso;  
  c. Monitoring application logs and performance metrics;  
  d. Supporting integration points between the applications and third-party systems; and  
  e. Providing minor configuration changes and enhancements that do not constitute a material change in scope.

2.3.2 Meridian shall work in coordination with Contoso’s internal IT team and any third-party vendors designated by Contoso, following agreed communication and escalation protocols.

2.4 **Monitoring and Incident Management.**  
2.4.1 Meridian shall implement proactive monitoring tools and processes to continuously monitor the health, performance, and availability of the infrastructure and applications within the scope of the Services.

2.4.2 Meridian shall provide 24x7x365 monitoring of critical components and shall respond to alerts in accordance with the incident response times specified in Article XII.

2.4.3 All Incidents shall be logged in Meridian’s ticketing system and categorized based on severity levels agreed with Contoso (e.g., Critical, High, Medium, Low). Meridian shall:

  a. Acknowledge Incidents within the response time targets in Article XII;  
  b. Provide regular status updates to Contoso; and  
  c. Use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve Incidents within the resolution time targets defined in Article XII.

2.5 **Service Desk and User Support.**  
2.5.1 Meridian shall provide a Service Desk accessible to Contoso’s authorized users via telephone, email, and a web-based portal during Business Hours, and shall provide on-call support for critical Incidents outside Business Hours.

2.5.2 The Service Desk shall be responsible for:

  a. Incident logging, initial triage, and assignment to appropriate support teams;  
  b. Providing first-line support for standard inquiries and known issues;  
  c. Coordinating with Contoso’s support teams for issues outside the scope of this Agreement; and  
  d. Communicating status updates and resolutions to the reporting users.

2.6 **Backup, Recovery, and Business Continuity.**  
2.6.1 Meridian shall perform regular backups of Client Data and system configurations in accordance with the following minimum standards, unless otherwise specified in a SOW:

  a. Daily incremental backups of critical data;  
  b. Weekly full backups;  
  c. Retention period of not less than thirty (30) days for backups;  
  d. Encryption of backup media or backup data in transit and at rest.

2.6.2 Meridian shall test restoration procedures at least annually and, upon Contoso’s reasonable request, shall provide Contoso with evidence of successful test results and any remedial actions taken.

2.6.3 Meridian shall maintain and periodically update a business continuity and disaster recovery plan relevant to the Services. In the event of a disaster or significant disruption, Meridian shall use commercially reasonable efforts to restore the Services within the recovery time objectives (RTOs) and recovery point objectives (RPOs) agreed in writing with Contoso.

2.7 **Professional Services and Projects.**  
2.7.1 Project-based activities, such as major migrations, system redesigns, large-scale deployments, or significant functional enhancements, shall be governed by one or more SOWs that reference this Agreement.

2.7.2 Each SOW shall specify:

  a. The scope and objectives of the project;  
  b. The Deliverables to be provided;  
  c. The project timeline and milestones;  
  d. The fees and payment milestones; and  
  e. Any project-specific Service Levels or acceptance criteria.

2.7.3 Meridian shall perform all professional services with due skill, care, and diligence, in accordance with generally accepted industry practices and the standards set forth in this Agreement.

2.8 **Performance Standards.**  
2.8.1 Meridian shall perform the Services:

  a. In a timely, professional, and workmanlike manner;  
  b. In accordance with the Service Levels and performance standards set forth in this Agreement and any SOW;  
  c. In compliance with Applicable Law and relevant industry standards (including ISO 27001 or comparable security frameworks, where applicable); and  
  d. Using appropriately skilled, qualified, and experienced personnel.

2.8.2 Meridian shall assign a designated Service Manager as the primary point of contact for Contoso regarding the Services. The Service Manager shall be responsible for service reviews, reporting, and escalation management.

2.9 **Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly included in this Agreement or a SOW, the following are excluded from the Services:

  a. Onsite support at Contoso premises (unless specifically agreed and charged separately);  
  b. Hardware procurement and maintenance (except where explicitly included);  
  c. Development of major new functionality or custom applications;  
  d. Support for software or systems not listed in the relevant SOW; and  
  e. Any services required due to unauthorized modifications, misuse, or negligence by Contoso or its third-party providers, except to the extent caused by Meridian.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value for the initial Term of this Agreement is **USD $4,403,752** (the “**Contract Value**”), which includes recurring service fees and any fixed project fees as specified in applicable SOWs.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
3.2.1 **Recurring Service Fees.**  
Meridian shall invoice Contoso monthly in advance for the recurring managed services, which may include:

  a. Fixed monthly fee for core hosting and infrastructure services;  
  b. Fixed or variable monthly fee for application support services, based on the number of applications and expected ticket volumes;  
  c. Per-user or per-device fees for service desk and endpoint support (if applicable).

3.2.2 **Project and Professional Service Fees.**  
Fees for project-based work and professional services shall be specified in the relevant SOW and may be structured as:

  a. Fixed price per project or milestone;  
  b. Time-and-materials (T&M) based on agreed hourly or daily rates; or  
  c. A combination of the above.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
3.3.1 Meridian shall issue invoices on a monthly basis, or as otherwise set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise stated in a SOW, all invoices shall be payable by Contoso within **sixty (60) days** from the date of the invoice (“**Net 60**”).

3.3.2 All amounts shall be invoiced and payable in United States Dollars (USD), unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
3.4.1 If Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amount by the due date, Meridian may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate equal to the lesser of:

  a. One percent (1%) per month; or  
  b. The maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law,

from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.4.2 Meridian shall not suspend the Services for non-payment unless (i) the amount is undisputed, (ii) such amount remains unpaid for at least thirty (30) days after Meridian has provided written notice to Contoso of such non-payment, and (iii) Meridian has used commercially reasonable efforts to resolve any payment issues with Contoso. Any suspension shall be limited in scope and duration to the minimum necessary and shall not relieve Contoso of its obligation to pay all undisputed amounts.

3.5 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall notify Meridian in writing within thirty (30) days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall promptly work together to resolve the dispute, and upon resolution, any amounts due shall be paid within thirty (30) days.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All fees and charges under this Agreement are exclusive of any applicable value added tax (VAT), sales tax, use tax, or other similar taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for any such taxes (excluding taxes based on Meridian’s net income), which shall be separately stated on Meridian’s invoices.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
3.7.1 Reasonable out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred by Meridian in the performance of onsite services or agreed project activities shall be reimbursable by Contoso, provided that:

  a. Such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Contoso; and  
  b. Meridian provides appropriate receipts or documentation.

3.7.2 Meridian shall use reasonable endeavours to minimize expenses and shall comply with any Contoso travel and expense policies provided to Meridian in advance.

3.8 **Service Credits and Fees.**  
Any Service Credits due under Article XII shall be applied as a credit against the next invoice(s) issued by Meridian. Service Credits shall not entitle Contoso to any cash refund, except upon expiration or termination of this Agreement where no further invoices are to be issued, in which case Contoso may offset such Service Credits against any amounts otherwise due or, absent such amounts, receive a refund of credited amounts.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **07 October 2026** (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon expiry of the Initial Term, this Agreement may be renewed for successive one (1) year periods (each a “**Renewal Term**”) by mutual written agreement of the Parties. Unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing, the terms and conditions of this Agreement shall continue to apply during any Renewal Term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
4.3.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Meridian.

4.3.2 Meridian may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso, provided that Meridian shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimise disruption and to assist in transition in accordance with Section 4.6.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  a. Commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  b. Becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation, has a receiver or administrator appointed over its assets, or is otherwise unable to pay its debts as they fall due, to the extent permitted by Applicable Law.

4.4.2 Without limiting the generality of Section 4.4.1, repeated failure by Meridian to meet critical Service Levels, coupled with failure to implement a mutually agreed remediation plan, may constitute a material breach.

4.5 **Termination for Regulatory or Legal Reasons.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect upon written notice if continued performance of this Agreement would cause such Party to be in violation of Applicable Law or binding regulatory requirements, and the Parties are unable to agree on modifications to the Agreement to remediate such violation within thirty (30) days.

4.6 **Effects of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  a. Meridian shall promptly cease providing the terminated Services, except as may be required to perform agreed transition activities;  
  b. Contoso shall pay Meridian for all Services properly performed and undisputed fees accrued up to the effective date of termination, and any agreed early termination charges, if expressly stated in an applicable SOW;  
  c. Meridian shall provide reasonable cooperation and assistance, for up to ninety (90) days following termination (or such longer period as mutually agreed, subject to fees at Meridian’s then-current rates), to transition the Services to Contoso or to a replacement service provider, including provision of documentation and data export;  
  d. Meridian shall return or securely delete Client Data in accordance with Article XI; and  
  e. The rights and obligations of the Parties that by their nature are intended to survive (including but not limited to payment obligations, confidentiality, intellectual property, warranties, indemnities, limitation of liability, and dispute resolution) shall survive in accordance with Section 16.10.

4.7 **No Fault of Customer.**  
If Contoso terminates this Agreement for convenience, Contoso shall not be liable for any early termination penalties or liquidated damages, except as expressly set out in a SOW for a specific project, and subject always to the limitation of liability in Article IX.

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information disclosed, directly or indirectly, by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that, given the nature of the information or the circumstances surrounding its disclosure, ought reasonably to be considered confidential, including without limitation: business plans, financial information, technical data, product plans, trade secrets, Client Data, security information, and Personal Data.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  a. Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  b. Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  c. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
  d. Is lawfully received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:

  a. Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
  b. Not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted herein; and  
  c. Protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:

  a. To its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less protective than those set forth herein; and  
  b. To the extent required by Applicable Law, regulation, or court order, provided that (where legally permissible) the Receiving Party promptly notifies the Disclosing Party in writing and cooperates with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking protective measures.

5.4 **Confidentiality of Personal Data and Client Data.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Meridian acknowledges that Client Data (including Personal Data) is Confidential Information of Contoso. Meridian shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect such data, in accordance with Article XI and Applicable Law.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon written request of the Disclosing Party, or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

  a. Return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, including any copies thereof, except as required to be retained by law or for legitimate archival purposes; and  
  b. If requested, certify in writing that it has complied with this Section 5.5.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations of confidentiality under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following the expiry or termination of this Agreement, except that obligations relating to trade secrets, Personal Data, and Client Data shall survive for so long as such information remains protected under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **System Availability.**  
12.1.1 Meridian shall ensure that the production environment of the Services has a monthly **System Availability** of at least **99.9%**, excluding Permitted Downtime.

12.1.2 “Permitted Downtime” includes:

  a. Planned Maintenance, provided that Meridian has given Contoso at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice and schedules such maintenance outside of Business Hours to the extent reasonably practicable;  
  b. Emergency maintenance required to address critical security vulnerabilities or threats, with as much advance notice as reasonably possible;  
  c. Downtime caused by Contoso’s misuse, negligence, or failure to comply with this Agreement; and  
  d. Downtime caused by Force Majeure Events, as defined in Article XV.

12.2 **Incident Severity and Response/Resolution Times.**  
Incidents shall be classified as follows, with Meridian using commercially reasonable efforts to meet the response and resolution targets during System unavailability not attributable to Permitted Downtime or Force Majeure:

  a. **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage of a critical production system impacting the majority of users.  
     - Response Time: within 30 minutes (24x7)  
     - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 4 hours  

  b. **Severity 2 (High):** Partial outage or severe degradation of a critical system impacting a significant subset of users.  
     - Response Time: within 1 hour (24x7)  
     - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 8 hours  

  c. **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues causing limited impact to business operations.  
     - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
     - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 2 Business Days  

  d. **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues or service requests with minimal business impact.  
     - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
     - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

12.3 **Service Credits for System Availability.**  
If monthly System Availability falls below the target of 99.9% (excluding Permitted Downtime), Contoso shall be entitled to Service Credits as follows:

  - 99.8% to < 99.9%: 2% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service  
  - 99.5% to < 99.8%: 5% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service  
  - 99.0% to < 99.5%: 10% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service  
  - < 99.0%: 15% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service  

The maximum Service Credits in any calendar month shall not exceed 25% of the monthly recurring fee for the affected Service.

12.4 **Service Credits for Response/Resolution Failures.**  
If Meridian fails to meet the response or resolution targets in Section 12.2 for more than five (5) Severity 1 or Severity 2 Incidents in a rolling three (3) month period, Contoso shall be entitled to additional Service Credits equal to 5% of the monthly recurring fee applicable to the affected Service for each such period.

12.5 **Claiming Service Credits.**  
To receive Service Credits, Contoso must submit a written claim to Meridian within thirty (30) days following the end of the month in which the Service Level failure occurred, providing reasonable details. Meridian shall validate the claim and apply any approved Service Credits to the next invoice.

12.6 **Chronic Service Level Failure.**  
If Meridian fails to meet the System Availability target in three (3) or more months in any rolling six (6) month period, or fails to meet critical response/resolution targets in a manner that materially impacts Contoso’s business and fails to cure such failure within thirty (30) days of notice, Contoso may treat such failure as a material breach and may terminate the affected Service or this Agreement pursuant to Section 4.4.
