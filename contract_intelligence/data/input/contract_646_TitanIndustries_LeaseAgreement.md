## LEASE AGREEMENT

This LEASE AGREEMENT (the “Agreement”), bearing reference number **LSE-TIT-202406-646**, is made and entered into as of **June 27, 2024** (the “Effective Date”), by and between:

**Contoso Enterprises**, a [jurisdiction of organization to be inserted] corporation, having its principal place of business at **1200 Contoso Way, Suite 500, Seattle, Washington 98101** (hereinafter referred to as “Contoso”, the “Client” or the “Company”); and

**Titan Industries**, a [jurisdiction of organization to be inserted] corporation, having its principal place of business at **4500 Titan Park Drive, Building A, Tacoma, Washington 98421** (hereinafter referred to as “Titan”, the “Vendor” or the “Service Provider”).

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Titan is in the business of owning, managing, operating, and maintaining commercial and industrial real property and related facilities, including certain office, warehouse, and light industrial spaces, and providing associated facility management, maintenance, and support services;

WHEREAS, Contoso desires to lease from Titan, and Titan desires to lease to Contoso, certain premises within the facility located at **4500 Titan Park Drive, Tacoma, Washington 98421** (the “Building”), together with related services, on the terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement;

WHEREAS, Titan, as service provider, will also provide certain facility-related services, including but not limited to building operations, maintenance, security, utilities management, and other support services more particularly described in **Article II (Scope of Services/Work)**;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to record the terms and conditions of their lease relationship in this Agreement, which shall govern the lease of the Premises (as defined below), as well as the services and obligations of each Party;

WHEREAS, the Parties agree that this Agreement is of **medium risk** to both Parties and seek to establish a balanced arrangement with mutually fair allocations of risk, liability, and responsibility;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Lease and Quiet Enjoyment.**  
(a) Titan hereby leases to Contoso, and Contoso hereby leases from Titan, the Premises, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.  
(b) Provided Contoso is not in material default beyond applicable cure periods, Contoso shall have quiet enjoyment and use of the Premises throughout the Term, without interference by Titan or any person claiming by, through, or under Titan, subject to Applicable Law and the rights of other tenants or occupants in the Building.

2.2 **Use of Premises.**  
(a) Contoso shall use the Premises primarily for general office operations, warehousing, light assembly, logistics, and related corporate activities consistent with a first-class commercial and industrial facility in Tacoma, Washington, and for no other purpose without Titan’s prior written consent, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed.  
(b) Contoso shall comply with all Applicable Laws, zoning ordinances, and reasonable rules and regulations promulgated by Titan and communicated in writing to Contoso from time to time (the “Building Rules”).

2.3 **Landlord’s Services (General).**  
Titan shall, at its own cost except as provided in **Article III**, provide and manage the following Services in a diligent, professional, and commercially reasonable manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for comparable facilities:

  2.3.1 **Building Operations and Maintenance.**  
  (a) Operation, maintenance, repair, and replacement, as needed, of the Building’s structural elements (foundations, load-bearing walls, roof), exterior façade, parking areas, elevator systems, and common areas.  
  (b) Maintenance and repair of base building systems serving the Premises, including HVAC systems, electrical supply systems up to the main panel within the Premises, plumbing risers, fire suppression systems, and life safety systems.  
  (c) Performance of scheduled preventive maintenance in accordance with manufacturer recommendations and industry best practices, with records made available upon reasonable request by Contoso.

  2.3.2 **Utilities.**  
  (a) Titan shall ensure that the Premises are supplied with electricity, water, sewer, and, where reasonably available, natural gas and telecommunications infrastructure to connection points at the Premises.  
  (b) Utilities serving the Premises may be separately metered to Contoso where practicable. If not separately metered, allocation shall be based on reasonable and equitable methods such as square footage or sub-metering estimates, as further described in **Section 3.5**.  
  (c) Titan shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize service interruptions. Planned outages affecting the Premises shall be scheduled outside normal business hours where feasible and with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ advance written notice to Contoso, except in emergencies.

  2.3.3 **Janitorial and Common Area Services.**  
  (a) Titan shall provide routine janitorial services for Building common areas (including lobbies, corridors, restrooms, and shared amenities) on Business Days, consistent with reasonable standards for similar buildings in the area.  
  (b) Contoso shall be responsible for janitorial services within the Premises, unless otherwise agreed in writing. If Titan provides janitorial services within the Premises at Contoso’s request, such services shall be billed as additional Services under **Section 3.6**.

  2.3.4 **Security and Access Control.**  
  (a) Titan shall provide building-level security measures, which may include access control systems (e.g., keycards, badges or codes), security cameras in common areas, and security patrols during designated hours.  
  (b) Titan does not warrant absolute security, but shall maintain commercially reasonable security consistent with comparable facilities.  
  (c) Contoso shall be responsible for internal security within the Premises (e.g., access controls to Contoso’s offices and internal IT rooms).

  2.3.5 **Parking.**  
  Titan shall make available to Contoso, on a non-exclusive basis, a reasonable number of unreserved parking spaces in the Building’s parking areas for Contoso’s employees, contractors, and visitors, consistent with the Building’s parking allocation ratios. Any reserved spaces shall be specified in a separate schedule with applicable fees.

2.4 **Tenant Improvements and Fit-Out.**  
(a) Contoso shall be responsible for all tenant improvements within the Premises, including interior partitioning, specialized electrical or data cabling, and fit-out of warehouse or assembly areas, subject to Titan’s review and approval (not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed) and compliance with Applicable Laws.  
(b) Any structural modifications or changes impacting base building systems shall require Titan’s prior written consent and compliance with Titan’s design and construction standards.  
(c) Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all permanent fixtures and improvements affixed to the Premises (other than Contoso’s trade fixtures and movable equipment) shall, at the expiration or earlier termination of this Agreement, become the property of Titan without compensation, except as provided in **Section 4.5** regarding removal and restoration obligations.

2.5 **Service Performance Standards.**  
Titan shall perform the Services in accordance with the Service Levels described in **Article XII**, including but not limited to:

  (a) **Response Times.** Titan shall respond to non-emergency maintenance requests from Contoso within the time frames specified in Section 12.2 (e.g., acknowledgment within four (4) Business Hours for standard issues, shorter for priority issues).  
  (b) **Resolution Times.** Titan shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve issues within the time frames set forth in the SLAs, subject to the nature of the issue and availability of necessary parts or third-party vendor support.  
  (c) **Quality of Work.** All maintenance and repair work shall be performed by qualified personnel in a good and workmanlike manner, in compliance with manufacturer instructions and Applicable Law.

2.6 **Additional Services.**  
Upon Contoso’s request, Titan may provide additional Services not included in the standard scope (e.g., specialized cleaning, dedicated security staff, additional build-out work, equipment installation assistance). Any such additional Services shall be subject to:

  (a) A written work order or change order pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** specifying scope, timing, and pricing; and  
  (b) Payment by Contoso of additional fees as agreed in writing, which may include time-and-materials charges or fixed fees.

2.7 **Operational Coordination.**  
(a) The Parties shall each designate a primary contact (a “Facility Representative”) for coordination of facilities issues, as set forth in the Notices section (**Section 16.7**).  
(b) The Facility Representatives shall meet (in person or by teleconference) at least quarterly to review service performance, upcoming maintenance, tenant requests, and any proposed changes or improvements.  
(c) Titan shall make reasonable efforts to schedule disruptive maintenance (e.g., HVAC shutdowns, major repairs) at times that minimize impact on Contoso’s operations, using good-faith coordination.

2.8 **Compliance and Safety.**  
(a) Titan shall ensure that the Building’s common areas and base building systems are maintained in compliance with Applicable Law and any applicable safety codes.  
(b) Contoso shall ensure that its use of the Premises, and any equipment, materials, or processes employed by Contoso, comply with Applicable Law, including applicable environmental, fire, and occupational health and safety requirements.  
(c) The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to address any safety concerns, including promptly remedying conditions that pose an unreasonable risk of harm to persons or property.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **General.**  
In consideration of the lease of the Premises and the provision of Services, Contoso shall pay to Titan the Base Rent, Operating Expenses, utility charges, and other amounts due under this Agreement, all in accordance with this **Article III**.

3.2 **Base Rent.**  
(a) The Parties acknowledge that the **Contract Value** of approximately **US $4,496,986** over the Term includes Base Rent and estimated Operating Expenses and other charges.  
(b) For the initial Term, Base Rent shall be payable in equal monthly installments, in advance, on or before the first (1st) day of each calendar month.  
(c) The initial annual Base Rent for the Premises shall be agreed and set forth in **Exhibit B (Rent Schedule)**, which may provide for annual escalations (e.g., 3% per Lease Year) depending on final negotiated terms.  
(d) If the Commencement Date is not the first day of a calendar month, the Base Rent for such partial month shall be prorated based on a thirty (30) day month and paid on or before the Commencement Date.

3.3 **Operating Expenses and Taxes.**  
(a) In addition to Base Rent, Contoso shall pay as additional rent its proportionate share of Operating Expenses and Taxes applicable to the Building.  
(b) Contoso’s proportionate share shall be determined as the ratio of the rentable square footage of the Premises to the total rentable square footage of the Building, as reasonably determined by Titan (e.g., per BOMA standards), and stated in **Exhibit B**.  
(c) Titan may invoice Operating Expenses and Taxes on an estimated monthly basis, with an annual reconciliation providing a statement of actual Operating Expenses and Taxes, accompanied by reasonable supporting documentation upon request. Any overpayment by Contoso shall be credited against future payments or refunded, and any underpayment shall be paid by Contoso within thirty (30) days of invoice.

3.4 **Utilities and Services Charges.**  
(a) Contoso shall be responsible for all utilities serving the Premises that are separately metered to Contoso and shall pay such utilities directly to the utility providers, where applicable.  
(b) For utilities that are centrally metered for the Building, Titan may allocate costs to Contoso based on reasonable methods (e.g., square footage, usage estimates). Contoso shall pay its allocated share as additional rent.  
(c) Charges for optional or additional Services requested by Contoso pursuant to **Section 2.6** shall be invoiced separately or included in Titan’s regular monthly invoice and shall be payable in accordance with the Payment Terms below.

3.5 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**  
(a) **Payment Terms.** All amounts due under this Agreement shall be payable within **thirty (30) days** of Contoso’s receipt of a correct and undisputed invoice from Titan (“Net 30”), unless otherwise specified.  
(b) **Invoices.** Titan shall invoice Contoso monthly in advance for Base Rent and estimated Operating Expenses, and in arrears for variable charges (e.g., additional Services, reconciled Operating Expenses).  
(c) Invoices shall be sent electronically (unless otherwise agreed) to the billing address or email designated by Contoso in **Section 16.7**, and shall reasonably itemize charges, including the period covered and the nature of each charge.

3.6 **Late Payment; Disputed Amounts.**  
(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid.  
(b) If Contoso in good faith disputes any invoiced amount, Contoso shall (i) pay the undisputed portion by the due date, and (ii) provide written notice to Titan within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the basis for the dispute. The Parties shall seek to resolve the dispute expeditiously, and any resolved amount shall be paid within fifteen (15) days after the resolution.  
(c) Titan shall not exercise its remedies for non-payment under **Article IV** with respect to any amounts that are subject to a good-faith dispute, provided Contoso pays all undisputed amounts in accordance with this Section.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
(a) Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, each Party shall bear its own costs and expenses relating to the negotiation and performance of this Agreement.  
(b) If Contoso requests extraordinary or non-standard work or services (e.g., after-hours HVAC for special events, custom build-outs beyond any tenant improvement allowance), Titan shall be entitled to reimbursement of its reasonable, documented costs, plus any agreed-upon mark-up, all as specified in the applicable work order or change order.  
(c) Titan shall obtain Contoso’s prior written approval (email sufficient) before incurring any pass-through costs for additional Services that exceed a threshold amount per request (to be agreed and included in **Exhibit B**), except in emergencies required to protect health, safety, or prevent significant property damage.

3.8 **Set-Off.**  
Contoso shall not have the right to set-off or withhold rent or other amounts due under this Agreement, except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or as required by Applicable Law or pursuant to a final, non-appealable order of a court or arbitrator of competent jurisdiction.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Commencement Date and continue until the Expiration Date, **January 08, 2027**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**.

4.2 **Renewal Options.**  
(a) Contoso shall have the option to renew this Agreement for up to one (1) additional term of three (3) years (a “Renewal Term”), upon written notice to Titan given not less than nine (9) months and not more than twelve (12) months prior to the Expiration Date.  
(b) The Renewal Term shall be on the same terms and conditions as this Agreement, except that Base Rent may be adjusted to then-prevailing market rates for comparable space in the Building (or comparable buildings in the area), subject to good-faith negotiation between the Parties.  
(c) If the Parties are unable to agree on the renewal Base Rent within sixty (60) days after Contoso’s renewal notice, this Agreement shall expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party, provided that such termination shall not be effective earlier than eighteen (18) months after the Commencement Date, unless otherwise agreed.  
(b) If Contoso terminates for convenience, Contoso shall pay all amounts due and payable up to the effective date of termination, but shall not owe any early termination penalty beyond such amounts, except for any specifically agreed early termination fee (if any) set forth in **Exhibit B**.  
(c) If Titan terminates for convenience, Titan shall cooperate with Contoso to enable an orderly transition and shall refund any pre-paid amounts for periods after the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof, or such longer cure period as may be reasonable if the breach is not capable of cure within thirty (30) days and the breaching Party commences and diligently pursues cure.  
(b) Material breaches by Contoso include, without limitation, failure to pay undisputed amounts due hereunder within the applicable cure period, unauthorized use of the Premises, or violation of Applicable Law that materially and adversely affects Titan or the Building.  
(c) Material breaches by Titan include failure to provide essential Services resulting in extended loss of use of the Premises, failure to maintain the Building in compliance with Applicable Law resulting in material adverse impact on Contoso’s operations, or repeated SLA failures, each subject to cure as specified above.

4.5 **Effect of Termination; Surrender.**  
(a) Upon expiration or earlier termination of this Agreement, Contoso shall vacate and surrender the Premises to Titan in substantially the same condition as received, reasonable wear and tear, casualty damage, and approved alterations excepted.  
(b) Unless otherwise agreed, Contoso shall remove its movable equipment, trade fixtures, and personal property from the Premises at its cost, and shall repair any material damage caused by such removal.  
(c) Titan may, upon written notice given at least thirty (30) days before the end of the Term, require Contoso to remove specified non-standard alterations and restore affected portions of the Premises to their prior condition, reasonable wear and tear excepted.  
(d) Any Contoso property remaining in the Premises after thirty (30) days following the expiration or termination of this Agreement may, at Titan’s option, be deemed abandoned, and Titan may remove or dispose of such property at Contoso’s cost, without liability to Contoso, subject to Applicable Law.

4.6 **Survival.**  
Provisions that by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination of this Agreement, including without limitation **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** and **Section 4.5**, shall so survive.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Confidential Information.**  
Each Party may disclose to the other Party Confidential Information in connection with this Agreement. The receiving Party agrees to keep all such Confidential Information confidential and to use it solely for purposes of performing its obligations, or exercising its rights, under this Agreement.

5.2 **Obligations.**  
The receiving Party shall:  
(a) exercise at least the same degree of care to protect the disclosing Party’s Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own confidential information of like importance, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care;  
(b) limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes consistent with this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those herein; and  
(c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the disclosing Party’s prior written consent, except as permitted in **Section 5.4**.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that the receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes generally known to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the receiving Party;  
(b) was rightfully known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure by the disclosing Party;  
(c) is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of or reference to the disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
(d) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:  
(a) to its affiliates, advisors, financing sources, and potential assignees, subject to confidentiality obligations; and  
(b) to the extent required by Applicable Law, regulation, or court order, provided that, where legally permissible, the receiving Party provides prompt written notice to the disclosing Party to allow the disclosing Party to seek protective measures.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the disclosing Party’s reasonable request or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) all copies of Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal or compliance purposes and as automatically stored in backup systems.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this **Article V** shall survive for a period of **three (3) years** from the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets (including security protocols) which shall be kept confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Titan shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that critical building systems serving the Premises (e.g., electricity, HVAC, water, and access control) are available **99.9%** of the time, measured on a monthly basis, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
(a) **Emergency Issues** (e.g., life safety, major utility outage affecting Premises): Titan shall respond within one (1) hour and use best efforts to initiate remediation immediately, continuing diligently until resolution or stabilization.  
(b) **High Priority Issues** (e.g., localized HVAC failure, significant water leak within Premises): Titan shall respond within four (4) Business Hours and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within twenty-four (24) hours.  
(c) **Standard Issues** (e.g., non-urgent maintenance requests): Titan shall respond within one (1) Business Day and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within three (3) Business Days.

12.3 **Monitoring and Reporting.**  
Titan shall maintain records of service requests, response times, and resolution times, and shall provide to Contoso quarterly reports summarizing service performance against the SLAs upon request.

12.4 **Service Credits and Remedies.**  
If Titan fails to meet the critical system availability SLA or repeatedly fails (e.g., three or more times in a quarter) to meet response/resolution SLAs without a valid excuse (e.g., Force Majeure or Contoso-caused delays):  
(a) Contoso shall be entitled to a **service credit** in the form of a reduction in the next monthly Base Rent installment, calculated as a negotiated percentage (e.g., 2–5% of the applicable month’s Base Rent), as specified in **Exhibit B**;  
(b) Persistent SLA failures (e.g., more than four consecutive months of material underperformance) shall constitute a material breach, subject to cure and potential termination under **Section 4.4**, and Contoso may seek further remedies consistent with **Articles VIII and IX**.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
SLAs shall not apply to:  
(a) outages or delays caused by Contoso’s equipment, personnel, or contractors;  
(b) scheduled maintenance with prior notice; or  
(c) Force Majeure Events as defined in **Article XV**.

## ARTICLE XV  
FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control that prevents or materially delays performance of its obligations (other than payment obligations), including, without limitation: acts of God, natural disasters, fires, floods, earthquakes, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, governmental actions or orders, labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce, unless industry-wide), or failures of utility or telecommunications infrastructure not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice.**  
The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, describing its nature, expected duration, and the obligations impacted, and shall update the other Party as reasonably necessary.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended to the extent they are directly impacted, and the time for performance shall be extended for a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided the affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate its effects and resume performance.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event affecting essential Services or occupancy of the Premises continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially impairs Contoso’s use of the Premises, Contoso may, upon written notice to Titan, either:  
(a) receive a proportionate abatement of Base Rent and Operating Expenses for the affected period; or  
(b) if the impairment is substantial and continuing, terminate this Agreement without penalty by providing thirty (30) days’ prior written notice.
