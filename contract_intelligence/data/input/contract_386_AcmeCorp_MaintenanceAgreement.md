## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 Relationship to Parent Agreement

2.1.1 This Maintenance Agreement **supplements and is executed pursuant to** the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly modified herein, all terms and conditions of the Parent Agreement (including but not limited to **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**, **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, and **Article XVI (General Provisions)**) shall apply to and govern the Maintenance Services.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that **Section 1.12 of the Parent Agreement** defines “Maintenance and Support Services,” and that **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement establishes certain baseline service level commitments. This Maintenance Agreement further **elaborates** and **extends** such commitments for the Covered Software during the term specified in **Article IV**.

2.1.3 To the extent of any conflict between this Maintenance Agreement and the Parent Agreement with respect to Maintenance and Support Services, **this Maintenance Agreement shall control** with respect to those maintenance-specific issues, while the Parent Agreement shall continue to govern all other matters (e.g., licensing, ownership, data protection, and general terms).

### 2.2 Description of Maintenance and Support Services

2.2.1 During the Term (as defined in **Section 4.1**), the Vendor shall provide the following Maintenance and Support Services for the Covered Software:

(a) **Incident Management and Error Correction**  
- Receive, log, categorize, and track Incidents reported by the Client.  
- Diagnose Errors and provide corrections, patches, or workarounds.  
- Prioritize Incidents according to severity levels aligned with **Section 12.3.1 of the Parent Agreement** (Severity 1–4).  
- Communicate status updates in accordance with **Section 12.3** of this Maintenance Agreement.

(b) **Updates, Upgrades, and Patches**  
- Provide to the Client all generally available updates, upgrades, bug fixes, patches, and new minor versions of the Covered Software released by the Vendor during the Term that are made available to similarly situated customers under maintenance.  
- Coordinate installation or deployment of such updates in the Client’s production, test, and development environments, subject to mutually agreed change windows and validation processes.

(c) **Preventive Maintenance**  
- Perform Planned Maintenance to maintain system stability and performance, including database tuning, index optimization, log management, and capacity planning, subject to the notice requirements in **Section 12.2**.  

(d) **Technical Support**  
- Provide remote technical support via email, web-based ticketing system, and telephone during the Vendor’s standard support hours, which shall at a minimum include **8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Eastern Time, Monday through Friday, excluding Vendor-observed holidays**.  
- Provide access to knowledge bases, FAQs, and documentation updates.

(e) **Monitoring and Reporting**  
- Monitor system health, performance, and key metrics for SaaS-deployed components of the Covered Software.  
- Provide monthly maintenance reports summarizing Incidents, response/resolution times, Uptime, and Planned Maintenance activities.

(f) **Configuration Assistance**  
- Provide reasonable assistance with configuration changes required to maintain the Software’s performance and operability, provided such assistance does not constitute new custom development beyond the scope contemplated in the Parent Agreement and applicable SOWs.

### 2.3 Specific Deliverables and Timelines

2.3.1 The Vendor shall provide the following **Deliverables** as part of the Maintenance Services:

(a) **Monthly Service Report** (delivered within ten (10) Business Days following the end of each calendar month) including:  
- Uptime statistics and measurements for the Covered Software, consistent with **Section 12.1.1 of the Parent Agreement**;  
- Incident summary by severity level (Severity 1–4);  
- Average Response Time and Resolution Time per severity level;  
- Summary of updates, patches, and Planned Maintenance deployed; and  
- Any known material risks or issues relating to the Covered Software.

(b) **Quarterly Maintenance Review** (delivered within fifteen (15) Business Days following the end of each calendar quarter), consisting of:  
- A written executive summary of the previous quarter’s maintenance activities;  
- Recommendations for performance optimization and risk mitigation; and  
- A forecast of planned major updates or upgrades for the upcoming quarter.

(c) **Emergency Incident Reports** for any Severity 1 Incidents that cause more than sixty (60) consecutive minutes of unplanned downtime, delivered within five (5) Business Days after incident closure, including root cause analysis and remediation steps.

2.3.2 Unless otherwise agreed in a Statement of Work executed pursuant to **Section 1.17 of the Parent Agreement**, all timelines set forth in this **Section 2.3** are **targets** and not strict deadlines; however, the Vendor shall use **commercially reasonable efforts** (as described in **Section 2.6.1 of the Parent Agreement**) to meet these timelines.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 The Vendor shall perform the Maintenance Services in a **professional and workmanlike manner**, consistent with **Section 2.6.1 of the Parent Agreement**, and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for enterprise software maintenance services.

2.4.2 The Vendor shall comply with and, in certain respects, enhance the service levels set forth in **Article XII of the Parent Agreement**, as further specified in **Article XII** of this Maintenance Agreement, including:

- Target Uptime commitment of **99.9%** for SaaS components (as per **Section 12.1.1 of the Parent Agreement**);  
- Response Times and Resolution Times for Incidents based on severity; and  
- Eligibility criteria and calculation methodologies for Service Credits.

2.4.3 In addition to the security requirements described in **Article XI of the Parent Agreement (Data Protection and Privacy)**, the Vendor shall ensure that all Maintenance Services, including remote access and log review, adhere to the security and data protection obligations applicable under the Parent Agreement, this Maintenance Agreement, and any Data Processing Addendum (“DPA”) executed pursuant to **Section 11.2.1 of the Parent Agreement**.

### 2.5 Client Responsibilities

2.5.1 The Client’s responsibilities under **Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement** are hereby incorporated by reference and shall apply to the Maintenance Services. Without limiting the foregoing, the Client shall:

(a) Provide timely access to relevant personnel, systems, and information, including knowledgeable staff for reproducing and diagnosing Incidents;  
(b) Maintain compliance with Vendor’s documented prerequisites and recommended configurations for the Covered Software;  
(c) Promptly install or allow the Vendor to deploy updates, patches, and upgrades that are reasonably necessary to correct Errors or protect security; and  
(d) Designate a primary contact person for maintenance matters, who shall coordinate all Incident reporting and communication with the Vendor.

2.5.2 The Client acknowledges that **failure to perform its responsibilities** in a timely manner may adversely affect the Vendor’s ability to meet performance standards and that the Vendor shall not be responsible for delays or failures to meet service levels caused by such Client failures.

### 2.6 Exclusions

2.6.1 The Maintenance Services do **not** include:  
(a) Development of new custom features or functionality not included in the standard Covered Software;  
(b) Support for third-party software, hardware, or network issues not under the Vendor’s control;  
(c) Data recovery services beyond restoration from existing backups maintained by the Vendor in accordance with its standard policies;  
(d) On-site support services, except as expressly agreed in a separate SOW; or  
(e) Maintenance or support for any Software deployed or used in a manner that violates the license terms or usage limitations set forth in **Section 6.3 (Restrictions) of the Parent Agreement**.

2.6.2 Any services falling within the exclusions described in **Section 2.6.1** may be provided by the Vendor subject to a separate SOW and additional Fees under **Article III**.

3.4.1 All Fees payable under this Maintenance Agreement are **exclusive of Taxes**, which shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement**.

### 3.5 Expenses and Reimbursements

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Maintenance Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date (February 10, 2025)** and shall continue in full force and effect until **October 06, 2027** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** (the **“Term”**).

4.1.2 The Term of this Maintenance Agreement is intended to be **co-extensive but slightly shorter** than the term of the Parent Agreement (which expires on **July 26, 2027**, per **Section 4.1 of the Parent Agreement**), recognizing that this Maintenance Agreement may provide extended support and transition services.

### 4.2 Renewal

4.2.1 Any renewal of Maintenance Services beyond the Expiration Date shall be subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties, which may be effected through a new maintenance agreement or an amendment referencing this Maintenance Agreement and the Parent Agreement.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience

4.3.1 After the first twelve (12) months of the Term, either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement for convenience **only upon not less than one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. This longer notice period reflects the **High Risk** classification and the Parties’ desire for stability in maintenance arrangements.

4.3.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, termination for convenience under this Maintenance Agreement **does not** terminate the Parent Agreement, unless separately terminated in accordance with **Article IV of the Parent Agreement**.

4.3.3 In the event of termination for convenience by the Client, the Client shall remain liable for:  
(a) all Fees accrued and payable up to the effective date of termination; and  
(b) any non-cancellable third-party commitments incurred by the Vendor on behalf of the Client for the Maintenance Services; and  
(c) **an early termination fee equal to twenty percent (20%) of the remaining Maintenance Contract Value** that would have been payable over the unexpired Term, which the Parties agree is a reasonable estimate of the Vendor’s lost profits and not a penalty.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Maintenance Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** (or **ten (10)** days for non-payment) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail, consistent with **Section 4.4.1 of the Parent Agreement**.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases business, as described in **Section 4.4.2 of the Parent Agreement**.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination or Expiration

4.5.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Maintenance Agreement:

(a) The Vendor’s obligation to provide Maintenance Services shall cease as of the effective date of termination, subject to any agreed transition services under **Section 4.6**;  
(b) The Client shall promptly pay all undisputed amounts due and payable under this Maintenance Agreement up to the effective date of termination, including any applicable early termination fee under **Section 4.3.3**;  
(c) The Parties shall each return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4 of the Parent Agreement**, subject to any retention obligations under **Article XI of the Parent Agreement**.

4.5.2 Termination or expiration of this Maintenance Agreement shall **not automatically terminate** the Parent Agreement. However, termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement (for any reason) shall, unless otherwise agreed in writing, automatically terminate this Maintenance Agreement, effective as of the effective date of termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement.

4.5.3 The provisions of this Maintenance Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and Sections 3.3, 4.3.3, 4.5**) shall so survive.

### 4.6 Transition Assistance

4.6.1 Upon the Client’s written request made at least **thirty (30) days** prior to termination or expiration, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide transition assistance services similar to those described in **Section 4.6 of the Parent Agreement**, including data export and knowledge transfer, at the Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates, subject to a mutually agreed SOW.

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

12.1.1 For SaaS or hosted components of the Covered Software, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure production environment availability of at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Permitted Downtime, consistent with **Section 12.1.1 of the Parent Agreement**.

12.2.1 The Vendor may perform Planned Maintenance during off-peak hours. The Vendor shall provide at least **forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice** for any Planned Maintenance expected to cause more than fifteen (15) minutes of downtime, and such Planned Maintenance shall not exceed **eight (8) hours per month**, consistent with **Section 12.2.1 of the Parent Agreement**.

### 12.3 Incident Severity and Response

12.3.1 The Vendor shall categorize and respond to Incidents in accordance with the following target Response Times (as per **Section 12.3.1 of the Parent Agreement**, with further detail):

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of production service or severe business impact with no workaround.  
  - Target initial Response Time: **1 hour** from ticket submission.  
  - Target Resolution Time: Vendor will work continuously (24x7) until a workaround or fix is implemented or the severity is downgraded.

- **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of service with limited workaround.  
  - Target initial Response Time: **4 hours**.  
  - Target Resolution Time: Vendor will use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within **two (2) Business Days**.

- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial, non-critical functionality impacted.  
  - Target initial Response Time: **1 Business Day**.  
  - Target Resolution Time: Vendor will use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within **ten (10) Business Days** or in the next scheduled release.

- **Severity 4 (Low):** General questions, enhancement requests, or cosmetic issues.  
  - Target initial Response Time: **2 Business Days**.  
  - Resolution Time: As agreed between the Parties, typically in a future release.

### 12.4 Service Credits

12.4.1 If the Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month, the Client shall be eligible for Service Credits calculated as described in **Section 12.4.1 of the Parent Agreement** (5%, 10%, or 20% of the monthly subscription Fees for the affected Software, depending on Uptime), applied proportionally to the maintenance fees for the corresponding period.

12.4.2 The Client must request Service Credits in writing within **thirty (30) days** after the end of the month in which the failure occurred, consistent with **Section 12.4.2 of the Parent Agreement**. Service Credits are the Client’s **sole and exclusive monetary remedy** for failure to meet Uptime Commitments, without limiting other non-monetary remedies expressly provided in this Maintenance Agreement.
