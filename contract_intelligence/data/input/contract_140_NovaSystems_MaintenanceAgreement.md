## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client ongoing Maintenance Services and related support for the Client Systems identified by the Parties in one or more SOWs. The Services shall be rendered in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices and in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

2.1.2 The Services under this Agreement are intended to ensure the operational stability, security, and performance of the Client Systems and to minimize downtime and disruptions to Client’s business. Vendor shall cooperate in good faith with Client’s internal IT personnel and any third-party vendors engaged by Client, subject to Section 2.9 below.

### 2.2 Types of Maintenance Services

2.2.1 **Corrective Maintenance.** Vendor shall diagnose and correct reported Defects, Errors, and Incidents affecting the covered Client Systems. Corrective Maintenance includes troubleshooting, root cause analysis, application of bug fixes, replacement of faulty components where within scope, and restoration of operations to normal Service Levels.

2.2.2 **Preventive Maintenance.** Vendor shall perform periodic health checks, apply security patches, firmware updates, and configuration reviews designed to prevent Incidents and security vulnerabilities, and shall recommend improvements to Client Systems to enhance stability and performance.

2.2.3 **Adaptive Maintenance.** Vendor shall make necessary changes to the Client Systems to keep them compatible with evolving operating systems, third-party software versions, and infrastructure components, to the extent within the scope defined in applicable SOWs.

2.2.4 **Perfective Maintenance.** Vendor shall, upon request and subject to Client’s approval, perform performance tuning, optimization, and minor enhancements to improve system responsiveness, capacity, and usability.

### 2.3 Support Services

2.3.1 Vendor shall provide remote and, where specified in an SOW, on-site support during the support hours defined in Article XII. Support Services include:

  (a) Incident logging, classification, and prioritization;  
  (b) Remote diagnostics and troubleshooting;  
  (c) Guidance and instructions to Client’s personnel; and  
  (d) Escalation and coordination with third-party vendors as needed for resolution.

2.3.2 Vendor shall maintain a help desk or ticketing system through which Client may report Incidents, request assistance, and track the status of open tickets. Access protocols and contact information shall be set forth in an SOW or support handbook provided by Vendor and reasonably accepted by Client.

### 2.4 Monitoring Services

2.4.1 Vendor shall implement monitoring tools and processes, reasonably acceptable to Client, to monitor the availability, performance, and capacity of designated Client Systems.  

2.4.2 Monitoring shall include, as applicable: CPU and memory utilization, disk capacity, network connectivity, application response times, and log analysis. Vendor shall alert Client promptly upon detection of conditions that could result in a Service Level breach or system outage.

2.4.3 Vendor shall provide periodic monitoring reports (monthly, unless otherwise agreed) summarizing system health, major events, Incidents, and recommended preventive actions.

### 2.5 Deliverables and Documentation

2.5.1 Vendor shall produce Deliverables as specified in each SOW, which may include:

  (a) Maintenance logs and change records;  
  (b) Incident reports and root cause analyses;  
  (c) System configuration documentation;  
  (d) Security patch inventories;  
  (e) Performance and capacity reports; and  
  (f) Updated runbooks and support procedures.

2.5.2 Vendor shall maintain accurate and contemporaneous records of all material maintenance activities and make such records available to Client upon reasonable request.

### 2.6 Timelines and Response

2.6.1 Vendor shall meet or exceed the response and resolution timelines defined in Article XII.  

2.6.2 Vendor acknowledges that timely response and resolution are critical to Client’s operations and shall prioritize Critical and High severity Incidents in accordance with the Service Levels.

### 2.7 Exclusions

2.7.1 Unless otherwise expressly stated in an SOW, the Services do **not** include:

  (a) Development of new custom software or major functionality;  
  (b) Major hardware upgrades or replacements not related to maintenance;  
  (c) Implementation of entirely new systems or platforms;  
  (d) On-site staffing beyond agreed support hours; or  
  (e) Remediation of issues caused by unauthorized changes made by Client or third parties.

2.7.2 Any excluded services may be provided by Vendor under a separate SOW or change order in accordance with Article XIII.

### 2.8 Client Responsibilities

2.8.1 Client shall:

  (a) Provide Vendor with reasonable access (remote and, where applicable, on-site) to Client Systems, facilities, and relevant personnel as necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  
  (b) Maintain appropriate backup procedures and disaster recovery measures, except to the extent such measures are explicitly part of Vendor’s Services;  
  (c) Not make material changes to Client Systems under Vendor’s management without coordinating with Vendor, except in emergencies; and  
  (d) Designate a primary and backup point of contact to coordinate communications with Vendor.

2.8.2 Vendor shall not be responsible for Service Level failures or Incidents arising from Client’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities under this Agreement, provided Vendor has notified Client of the requirement and the potential impact.

### 2.9 Third-Party Vendors

2.9.1 Client may engage third-party vendors for certain components of the Client Systems. Vendor shall reasonably cooperate with such third parties but shall not be liable for delays, errors, or additional costs arising from such third-party acts or omissions.

2.9.2 Where the Services require Vendor to interface with Third-Party Materials, Vendor’s obligations shall be limited by the applicable terms and conditions of such third parties to the extent such terms are disclosed or made available to Vendor.

4.3.2 Without limiting Section 4.3.1, the following shall constitute material breaches:

  (b) Vendor’s repeated failure to meet critical Service Levels in a manner that materially impacts Client’s operations, after written notice and a reasonable opportunity to cure; or  
  (c) A Party’s violation of its confidentiality obligations under Article V or data protection obligations under Article XI.

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files for bankruptcy or is the subject of an involuntary bankruptcy petition not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) Vendor shall promptly cease performing Services, except as necessary to assist with transition as set forth in Section 4.5.3;  
  (b) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed Fees and expenses accrued but unpaid as of the effective date of termination; and  
  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5.

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall automatically terminate all then-existing SOWs, unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

4.5.3 Upon request and subject to payment of Vendor’s then-current standard rates (unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach, in which case reasonable transition assistance shall be provided at no additional charge for up to thirty (30) days), Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide transition assistance to Client or Client’s designee to ensure an orderly transfer of the Services.

Those provisions of this Agreement which by their nature should survive expiration or termination, including without limitation Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), and XVI (General Provisions), shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

12.1.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the critical Client Systems under Vendor’s direct control achieve a minimum **monthly uptime of 99.9%**, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.1.2 Uptime shall be calculated as:

> Uptime % = (Total minutes in month – Unscheduled Downtime) / (Total minutes in month – Scheduled Maintenance minutes) × 100

### 12.2 Support Hours

12.2.1 Vendor shall provide standard support during **Normal Business Hours**, defined as 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Pacific Time, Monday through Friday, excluding U.S. federal holidays.

12.2.2 For Priority 1 (Critical) Incidents affecting production systems, Vendor shall provide 24x7 on-call support.

### 12.3 Incident Prioritization and Response Times

Incidents shall be classified as follows:

  (a) **Priority 1 – Critical:** Complete outage or severe impact to core business operations with no workaround.  
  (b) **Priority 2 – High:** Major functionality impaired with significant business impact; workaround may exist.  
  (c) **Priority 3 – Medium:** Non-critical issues affecting limited users or minor features.  
  (d) **Priority 4 – Low:** Cosmetic issues or general inquiries.

Vendor shall meet the following target response and initial action times:

  - Priority 1: Response within 1 hour; continuous work until resolution or workaround.  
  - Priority 2: Response within 4 hours; work during Business Hours until resolution.  
  - Priority 3: Response within 1 Business Day.  
  - Priority 4: Response within 2 Business Days.

### 12.4 Scheduled Maintenance

12.4.1 Vendor may perform Scheduled Maintenance during designated windows communicated to Client at least five (5) Business Days in advance, except for emergency maintenance.

12.4.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to schedule non-emergency maintenance outside of Client’s peak business hours, as reasonably identified by Client.

### 12.5 Remedies for SLA Breaches

12.5.1 If Vendor fails to meet the monthly uptime commitment in Section 12.1 by more than 0.5%, Client shall be entitled to a service credit equal to a percentage of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services, as follows:

  - Uptime ≥ 99.4% and < 99.9%: 5% credit of monthly recurring Fee for that month.  
  - Uptime ≥ 98.9% and < 99.4%: 10% credit.  
  - Uptime < 98.9%: 15% credit.

12.5.2 Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice. Service credits are Client’s exclusive monetary remedy for SLA-related performance shortfalls, without prejudice to Client’s right to terminate for repeated material SLA failures under Section 4.3.
