## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
Service Provider shall provide to Client the Services described in this Article II and in each applicable SOW executed by the Parties. The Services are anticipated to include, without limitation:

  (a) **Cloud Platform Hosting.** Provision of a secure, multi-tenant or dedicated (as specified in the SOW) production environment to host Client’s designated applications and databases, including compute, storage, and network resources.

  (b) **Application and Infrastructure Management.** Ongoing operation, monitoring, patching, performance tuning, and maintenance of the hosted environment and supported applications, in accordance with the Service Levels described in Article XII.

  (c) **Support Services.** Tiered helpdesk and technical support to Client’s designated administrators and, if agreed, to Authorized Users, including incident logging, triage, troubleshooting, escalation, and resolution.

  (d) **Professional Services.** As requested by Client and set forth in an SOW, implementation, configuration, migration, integration, customization (if applicable), training, and advisory or consulting services.

  (e) **Backup and Recovery.** Regular data backups, disaster recovery planning, and, where applicable, execution of recovery procedures to meet the Recovery Time Objectives (“RTO”) and Recovery Point Objectives (“RPO”) specified in the relevant SOW or Documentation.

2.2 **Detailed Description of Services.**  
Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with suitable training and experience. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services shall include:

  (a) **Environment Provisioning.** Service Provider will provision development, testing, staging, and production environments as specified in the applicable SOW. Each environment shall be configured based on Client’s documented requirements and industry best practices for security and performance. Service Provider shall maintain detailed environment configuration documentation and shall provide Client with access to such documentation upon request.

  (b) **Monitoring and Incident Management.** Service Provider shall implement 24x7 automated monitoring of system health, resource utilization, and key performance indicators. Monitoring shall include alerts for CPU usage, memory utilization, disk space, database performance, network latency, and application errors. For Incidents identified via monitoring tools or reported by Client, Service Provider shall classify such Incidents according to severity levels (e.g., P1–Critical, P2–High, P3–Medium, P4–Low) and respond and resolve in accordance with the response and resolution times set forth in Article XII. Service Provider shall maintain an incident management system and provide Client with periodic incident reports.

  (c) **Change and Release Management.** Service Provider shall implement a formal change management process consistent with Article XIII. Production deployments, patches, upgrades, and configuration changes shall be planned, tested, and implemented in a controlled manner. Service Provider shall coordinate with Client to schedule changes during maintenance windows to minimize business impact. All changes shall be logged, and rollback plans shall be documented where reasonably appropriate.

  (d) **Security Measures.** Service Provider shall implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect Client Data and Personal Data against unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. Such safeguards shall include, at a minimum: (i) role-based access control; (ii) multi-factor authentication for administrative access where technically feasible; (iii) encryption in transit using industry-standard protocols; (iv) data center security controls (if applicable); and (v) vulnerability management, including periodic vulnerability scans and timely remediation of high-risk vulnerabilities. More detailed data protection obligations are set forth in Article XI.

  (e) **Backups and Disaster Recovery.** Service Provider shall perform regular backups of Client Data at intervals specified in the applicable SOW. Backup schedules shall be designed to meet agreed RPOs. Service Provider shall maintain documented disaster recovery procedures and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to restore Services within the agreed RTOs in the event of a disaster affecting the primary hosting environment. Upon request, Service Provider shall provide summary results of its most recent disaster recovery test.

  (f) **Service Reports and Reviews.** Service Provider shall provide Client with monthly service reports summarizing System Availability, Incidents, response and resolution times, Service Level attainment, and Service Credits (if any). The Parties shall conduct periodic (at least quarterly) Service review meetings to discuss performance, upcoming changes, and continuous improvement initiatives.

2.3 **Deliverables and Milestones.**  
Certain Services, particularly Professional Services, will result in Deliverables and associated milestones, as set forth in an SOW. Each SOW shall specify:

  (a) A description of the Deliverables and acceptance criteria;  
  (b) The project schedule and key milestones;  
  (c) The responsibilities of each Party; and  
  (d) Any dependencies, assumptions, and constraints.

Client shall review Deliverables within the review period specified in the SOW (or, if none is specified, within ten (10) Business Days of receipt) and shall either (i) accept the Deliverables in writing, or (ii) provide written notice of any material non-conformities with the applicable acceptance criteria. If Client fails to respond within the review period, the Deliverables shall be deemed accepted. Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct any identified non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverables for acceptance.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Service Provider’s ability to perform the Services may depend upon Client’s cooperation and timely provision of information and resources. Accordingly, Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to necessary systems, facilities, personnel, and information;  
  (b) Designate a primary contact and an escalation contact for coordination of the Services;  
  (c) Ensure that Authorized Users use the Services in compliance with this Agreement and Applicable Law;  
  (d) Promptly notify Service Provider of any Incidents, security concerns, or suspected unauthorized access; and  
  (e) Be responsible for the accuracy, quality, integrity, and legality of Client Data, and the means by which Client acquires such data.

2.5 **Service Modifications.**  
Service Provider may make changes to the Services that (a) are required by Applicable Law, (b) do not materially degrade the overall functionality, security, or performance of the Services, or (c) are part of regular updates, improvements, or enhancements. Material changes that could adversely affect Client’s use of the Services shall be communicated to Client in advance in accordance with Article XIII.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **September 04, 2028** (the “**Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew this Agreement for one or more additional renewal terms, each of which shall be for a period of one (1) to three (3) years, on terms to be negotiated and documented in a written amendment or new agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any SOW), for convenience and without cause upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. For clarity, “medium risk” balance is intended: either Party has this right, and early termination consequences shall be as set forth below.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) Materially breaches this Agreement (including failure to meet Service Levels on a persistent basis) and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) Becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy (that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days), makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Termination for Regulatory or Legal Reasons.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice if Client determines in good faith, based on advice of counsel, that continued performance of this Agreement would violate Applicable Law or create a material regulatory risk.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

  (a) **Payment Obligations.** Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Client shall pay Service Provider all undisputed Fees accrued and payable for Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination. In the event of termination for Service Provider’s uncured material breach, Client shall not be obligated to pay for Services that were not rendered or that materially failed to conform to the Service Levels.

  (b) **Transition Assistance.** For a period of up to ninety (90) days following expiration or termination (the “Transition Period”), upon Client’s request, Service Provider shall provide commercially reasonable transition assistance to support migration of Client Data and, to the extent applicable, transition of services to Client or a replacement provider. Such assistance shall be at Service Provider’s then-current time-and-materials rates, unless termination is due to Service Provider’s uncured material breach, in which case reasonable transition assistance shall be provided at no additional charge.

  (c) **Return or Deletion of Client Data.** Within thirty (30) days after expiration or termination of this Agreement, Service Provider shall, at Client’s option and request, (i) make available to Client a complete export of Client Data in a commonly used, machine-readable format, and/or (ii) securely delete or destroy all Client Data and Personal Data in its possession or control, except to the extent retention is required by Applicable Law or necessary for legitimate business purposes (such as maintaining standard backup tapes for a limited retention period).

  (d) **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination (including payment obligations, confidentiality, intellectual property, indemnities, limitations of liability, data protection obligations applicable to retained data, dispute resolution, and governing law) shall survive in accordance with Section 16.9.

4.7 **No Further Liability.**  
Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, neither Party shall be liable to the other for any damages, costs, or losses resulting solely from a permitted termination of this Agreement pursuant to this Article IV.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the Services achieves a minimum **System Availability** of **99.9%** during each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime as defined in Section 12.2.

12.2 **Permitted Downtime.**  
“Permitted Downtime” means:

  (a) Scheduled maintenance windows, provided that Service Provider gives Client at least three (3) Business Days’ prior notice for non-emergency maintenance and schedules such maintenance during off-peak hours where reasonably possible;  
  (b) Emergency maintenance required to address critical security vulnerabilities or imminent failures, for which Service Provider shall provide as much advance notice as practical;  
  (c) Downtime or performance degradation caused by factors beyond Service Provider’s reasonable control, including Force Majeure Events;  
  (d) Downtime attributable to Client’s systems, networks, or internet connectivity; or  
  (e) Downtime resulting from Client’s unauthorized or improper use of the Services.

12.3 **Measurement and Reporting.**  
System Availability shall be measured as the total minutes during a calendar month during which the Services are available divided by the total minutes in that month, less Permitted Downtime, expressed as a percentage. Service Provider shall maintain logs and reports sufficient to verify System Availability and shall provide such reports to Client upon request.

12.4 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the following response and resolution targets for Incidents:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):**  
  - Description: Complete outage or severe impact on critical business operations with no workaround.  
  - Initial Response Time: 1 hour (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High):**  
  - Description: Major functionality impaired; workaround available but business impact is significant.  
  - Initial Response Time: 2 hours (Business Days)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 1 Business Day  

- **Priority 3 (Medium):**  
  - Description: Partial loss of non-critical functionality; minor business impact.  
  - Initial Response Time: 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low):**  
  - Description: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general questions.  
  - Initial Response Time: 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: To be addressed in the normal development cycle.

12.5 **Service Credits for Uptime Failures.**  
If System Availability in any calendar month falls below 99.9% (excluding Permitted Downtime), Client shall be eligible for Service Credits as follows:

  - **99.0% to < 99.9%:** Service Credit equal to 5% of the monthly subscription Fees for the affected Services;  
  - **98.0% to < 99.0%:** Service Credit equal to 10% of the monthly subscription Fees for the affected Services;  
  - **< 98.0%:** Service Credit equal to 15% of the monthly subscription Fees for the affected Services.

To receive a Service Credit, Client must submit a written request to Service Provider within thirty (30) days following the end of the month in which the Service Level Failure occurred, including reasonable supporting information. Service Credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive financial remedy for Service Level Failures, subject to any termination rights under this Agreement.

12.6 **Chronic Service Level Failures.**  
If Service Provider fails to meet the System Availability commitment in three (3) or more consecutive months, or in four (4) or more months in any rolling twelve (12) month period, Client may treat such failures as a material breach and may, at its option, terminate the affected Services upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice if the Service Level Failures remain uncured.
