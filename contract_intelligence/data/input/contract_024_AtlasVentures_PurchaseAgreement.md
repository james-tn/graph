## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall provide to the Company a managed technology solution that includes: (a) assessment and design of the Company’s target-state systems architecture; (b) implementation and configuration of certain software platforms identified in the applicable SOW(s); (c) integration of such platforms with the Company’s existing systems; (d) data migration services; (e) testing, user acceptance support, and go-live assistance; and (f) ongoing maintenance, monitoring, and technical support services, all as further detailed in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties under this Agreement.

2.2 **Specific Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Services shall include the creation and delivery of the following types of Deliverables, which shall be described with specificity in the SOW(s):

  a. **Solution Design Documentation.** A comprehensive solution design document, including architecture diagrams, data flow mappings, integration specifications, and security controls recommended for the Company’s environment.

  b. **Configuration Artifacts.** Configured software instances, environment settings, parameter files, and other configuration-related artifacts necessary for the operation of the implemented solution in the Company’s production, testing, and development environments.

  c. **Integration Components.** Custom connectors, APIs, middleware configurations, and integration scripts required to interface the implemented solution with the Company’s existing systems, including but not limited to ERP, CRM, and data warehouse platforms.

  d. **Migration Documentation and Scripts.** Data mapping documentation, transformation rules, migration scripts, and migration runbooks for the orderly migration of historical and operational data from legacy systems to the new solution.

  e. **Testing Artifacts.** Test plans, test cases, test scripts, and test result summaries for system integration testing, user acceptance testing, and performance testing.

  f. **Operational Runbooks and User Guides.** Administrative runbooks, standard operating procedures, and user-facing guides describing the operation, configuration, and maintenance of the implemented solution.

  g. **Support and Maintenance Deliverables.** Periodic status reports, incident and problem logs, root cause analyses, and updated documentation arising from ongoing support activities.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  a. In a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices for providers of similar services;

  b. In accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and any SOW;

  c. In compliance with all applicable Laws, including Data Protection Laws and applicable export control regulations;

  d. Using personnel who are appropriately trained, experienced, and qualified in their respective areas of responsibility; and

  e. In a manner designed to minimize disruption to the Company’s ongoing business operations.

2.4 **Project Management and Governance.**  

  a. **Project Manager.** Each Party shall designate a project manager (“Project Manager”) who shall serve as the primary point of contact for day-to-day communications and coordination. The Parties’ Project Managers shall meet (in person or via teleconference or videoconference) at least monthly during active phases of the project, and quarterly during steady-state support phases, to review status, issues, and upcoming milestones.

  b. **Project Plan.** The Service Provider shall develop and maintain a detailed project plan, including milestones, dependencies, resource assignments, and risk mitigation activities. The initial project plan shall be submitted to the Company for review and approval within thirty (30) days after the Effective Date or such other period as may be specified in the relevant SOW.

  c. **Status Reporting.** The Service Provider shall provide written status reports at least monthly (or more frequently as set forth in an SOW), summarizing progress against the project plan, key milestones achieved, upcoming activities, risks and issues, and any actions required from the Company.

  d. **Escalation.** The Parties shall establish an escalation matrix for timely resolution of critical issues. Issues that materially impact project timelines or Service Levels shall be escalated to senior management representatives of both Parties.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

  a. The Parties acknowledge that the timely delivery of the Services and Deliverables is of material importance. Each SOW shall specify target dates and milestones. The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed such dates and milestones.

  b. Delays caused by the Company’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities (including providing necessary information, access, decisions, or approvals) shall extend the applicable timelines on a day-for-day basis and may result in reasonable adjustments to Fees, subject to prior written notification by the Service Provider and the Company’s written approval (which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed).

  c. If the Service Provider becomes aware of any circumstance that may materially delay performance, it shall promptly notify the Company in writing, describe the cause of the delay, and propose a recovery plan.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall:

  a. Provide the Service Provider with reasonable access to its relevant personnel, systems, facilities, and information as necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services, subject to the Company’s internal security and access protocols;

  b. Designate a Project Manager and such other personnel as may be reasonably required to support the project governance structure;

  c. Promptly review and respond to the Service Provider’s reasonable requests for information, feedback, and approvals; and

  d. Ensure that any third-party systems or services on which the Deliverables depend are available and properly licensed for use in connection with the Services.

2.7 **Subcontractors.**  
The Service Provider may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (a) the Service Provider remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors; (b) such subcontractors are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and (c) any subcontracting of material portions of the Services is disclosed to the Company upon request. The Company may reasonably object to any subcontractor handling highly sensitive Company data, and the Parties shall work in good faith to address such objections.

2.8 **Statements of Work.**  
Each SOW executed under this Agreement shall, at a minimum, specify: (a) a description of the Services and Deliverables; (b) the applicable project schedule and milestones; (c) the applicable Fees and payment schedule; (d) any special Service Levels or acceptance criteria; and (e) any assumptions or dependencies. In the event of a conflict between the terms of an SOW and this Agreement, this Agreement shall control, except where the SOW expressly states that it is intended to override a specific provision of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees and Contract Value.**  
In consideration of the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Agreement, the Company shall pay to the Service Provider the Purchase Agreement Value of US $1,496,964, subject to any additions or reductions mutually agreed in writing pursuant to Change Orders or additional SOWs.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall consist of the following components (as further detailed in applicable SOWs):

  a. **Implementation Fees.** A fixed-fee amount allocated to design, configuration, integration, data migration, and go-live activities.

  b. **Recurring Support Fees.** Monthly or annual fees for ongoing maintenance, monitoring, and support services, which may be subject to standard annual increases not to exceed three percent (3%) per year following the first contract year, upon sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to the Company.

  c. **Time-and-Materials (T&M) Fees.** For any additional consulting or development work requested by the Company outside the original scope, calculated based on the Service Provider’s then-current standard hourly or daily rates, as approved in writing by the Company in advance.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**  

  a. The Service Provider shall invoice the Company in accordance with the payment schedule set forth in the applicable SOW(s). Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, implementation Fees may be invoiced based on milestones (e.g., 30% on project initiation, 40% on completion of user acceptance testing, and 30% on production go-live), and recurring Fees shall be invoiced monthly in advance.

  b. All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable within thirty (30) days from the invoice date (“**Net 30**”).

  c. The Company shall have the right to dispute in good faith any portion of an invoice by providing written notice to the Service Provider within fifteen (15) days of invoice receipt, specifying the amount in dispute and the reasons therefor. The Company shall timely pay the undisputed portion, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed Fees not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at the lower of: (a) one percent (1.0%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Law. The Service Provider shall provide written notice of late payment and allow an additional fifteen (15) days’ cure period before assessing interest or exercising any other remedies.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, and other similar taxes (collectively, “Taxes”). The Company shall be responsible for all Taxes arising from this Agreement, excluding taxes based on the Service Provider’s income, property, or employment of personnel. The Service Provider shall itemize applicable Taxes on each invoice. If the Company is exempt from any Taxes, it shall provide a valid exemption certificate.

3.6 **Expenses.**  
The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred by the Service Provider in connection with the performance of the Services, in accordance with the Company’s then-current travel and expense policies, provided that: (a) all such expenses are approved in writing by the Company in advance; and (b) the Service Provider furnishes appropriate receipts and documentation with its expense reimbursement requests.

3.7 **No Withholding / Setoff.**  
Except as required by Law or expressly permitted under this Agreement (including in connection with a good-faith dispute), the Company shall not withhold, offset, or deduct any amounts from the Fees without the Service Provider’s prior written consent.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until March 15, 2027 (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be renewed for additional periods of one (1) year each (each, a “Renewal Term”) upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date or the end of any Renewal Term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience at any time by providing at least thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the Service Provider. The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to the Company.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  a. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

  b. Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent; (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (iv) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

  a. Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason, the Service Provider shall promptly: (i) cease performance of the applicable Services; (ii) deliver to the Company all Deliverables (completed or in-progress) for which the Company has paid; and (iii) return or, at the Company’s written direction, securely destroy the Company’s Confidential Information and Personal Data in accordance with Section 11.5.

  b. The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and accepted, and reimbursable expenses properly incurred, up to the effective date of termination. In the case of termination for convenience by the Company, the Company shall also pay any non-cancellable third-party costs reasonably incurred by the Service Provider on the Company’s behalf, provided such costs were authorized by the Company in writing.

  c. In the event of termination by the Company for the Service Provider’s uncured material breach, the Company shall not be obligated to pay for any Services not yet performed or Deliverables not yet provided as of the effective date of termination.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon the Company’s request made prior to or within thirty (30) days following expiration or termination, the Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition services (e.g., knowledge transfer, documentation, data export) for up to ninety (90) days to support the orderly transfer of Services to the Company or a replacement provider. Such transition services shall be provided at the Service Provider’s then-current T&M rates, unless termination is due to the Service Provider’s uncured material breach, in which case the Parties shall negotiate in good faith a fair and reasonable fee.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under those provisions of this Agreement which by their nature should survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI, and Sections 3.4, 3.5, 4.5, and 4.7) shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all nonpublic information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (“Receiving Party”) in connection with this Agreement, whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, financial information, product roadmaps, technical specifications, source code, trade secrets, customer lists, pricing, and Personal Data. The Deliverables and all nonpublic information relating to the Company’s systems and operations shall be deemed the Company’s Confidential Information.

5.2 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  a. Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;

  b. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly authorized by this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent;

  c. Limit access to Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and

  d. Protect the confidentiality of the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate: (a) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any duty of confidentiality; or (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by Law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permissible) promptly notify the Disclosing Party and provide reasonable cooperation in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s earlier written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) one archival copy of Confidential Information solely for record-keeping and compliance purposes, and (b) any Confidential Information that cannot be reasonably deleted from electronic backup systems, provided that such retained Confidential Information remains subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall be protected for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable Law or as required by Data Protection Laws, respectively.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For any hosted or managed services provided by the Service Provider under this Agreement, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such services are available at least ninety-nine point nine percent (99.9%) of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Service Provider may perform scheduled maintenance that may affect service availability, provided that: (a) the Service Provider provides at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice to the Company; and (b) scheduled maintenance is performed during off-peak hours where reasonably practicable. Scheduled maintenance shall not exceed eight (8) hours in any calendar month, unless otherwise agreed.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve service incidents according to the following guidelines:

  a. **Priority 1 (Critical):** System outage or major functional impact causing critical business disruption. Initial response within one (1) hour; target resolution or workaround within eight (8) hours.

  b. **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation of service with material business impact but no total outage. Initial response within four (4) hours; target resolution or workaround within two (2) Business Days.

  c. **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues, limited business impact. Initial response within one (1) Business Day; target resolution or workaround within five (5) Business Days.

  d. **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries. Initial response within two (2) Business Days; resolution as reasonably scheduled.

12.4 **SLA Credits.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the service availability commitments set forth in Section 12.1 in any calendar month (excluding Force Majeure Events and the Company-caused outages), the Company shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  a. Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service;

  b. Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service;

  c. Availability below 98.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service.

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice. Service credits are the Company’s sole and exclusive remedy for service availability failures, without prejudice to any termination rights or remedies for uncured material breach.

12.5 **SLA Reporting.**  
Upon the Company’s request, the Service Provider shall provide monthly SLA reports summarizing service performance, incidents, and Service Level attainment. The Parties shall review SLA performance periodically and may agree on appropriate corrective actions or adjustments.

## ARTICLE XV  
## FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, riots, civil disturbances, strikes or labor disputes (excluding strikes or labor disputes involving the affected Party’s own workforce), acts of government, failure of utilities or telecommunications, or widespread internet outages.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall: (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event; (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects and duration of the Force Majeure Event; and (c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration affected by the Force Majeure Event. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to adjust project schedules and Service Levels as appropriate.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially prevents performance of material obligations, either Party may terminate the affected Services or this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without liability (except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
