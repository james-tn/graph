## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive professional, technical, and managed services in connection with the design, configuration, deployment, integration, and ongoing support of an enterprise application and accompanying infrastructure (the “**Project**”). The Services shall include, without limitation:

(a) Requirements gathering and analysis;  
(b) Solution design and architecture;  
(c) Software configuration and customization;  
(d) Integration with Client’s existing systems and third-party applications;  
(e) Data migration planning and execution;  
(f) User acceptance testing support;  
(g) Go-live support and stabilization; and  
(h) Post-implementation support, maintenance, and monitoring in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

2.1.2 The Parties anticipate that the Services may be further detailed in one or more Statements of Work or Scope of Work documents, collectively attached as **Exhibit A** (each an “SOW”), which shall be deemed incorporated into this Work Order by reference. In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Work Order and an SOW, the terms of this Work Order shall prevail, unless the SOW expressly states that it supersedes specific provisions of this Work Order.

### 2.2 Deliverables

2.2.1 Vendor shall provide the following categories of Deliverables, which shall be further detailed in Exhibit A:

(a) **Design Documentation** – including high-level and detailed architecture diagrams, data models, integration designs, and configuration blueprints;  

(b) **Configuration and Customization Artifacts** – including software configuration files, scripts, parameter settings, business rules, and custom code to the extent agreed in writing;  

(c) **Integration Components** – including APIs, connectors, adapters, and mapping documents enabling the interoperability between Client’s systems and the Vendor-implemented solution;  

(d) **Migration Artifacts** – including migration scripts, mapping tables, and data quality and validation reports;  

(e) **Testing Documentation** – including test plans, test cases, test results, defect logs, and remediation plans;  

(f) **Training Materials** – including user guides, administrator manuals, training presentations, and knowledge base articles; and  

(g) **Operational Runbooks** – including standard operating procedures, incident handling guidelines, and escalation workflows relating to the ongoing operation and support of the solution.

2.2.2 Each Deliverable shall conform to the Specifications and acceptance criteria defined in Exhibit A. Client shall have the right to review and accept or reject each Deliverable in accordance with Section 2.5.

### 2.3 Project Timeline and Milestones

2.3.1 The Parties shall mutually agree to a detailed project plan (“**Project Plan**”), which shall describe the key phases, tasks, and milestones for the Project. The Project Plan may be included in Exhibit A and may be updated from time to time by mutual written agreement (which may take the form of a Change Order pursuant to Article XIII).

2.3.2 The Project shall include at least the following major phases and indicative timelines, subject to refinement in the Project Plan:

(a) **Discovery and Requirements Phase** – Approximately 6–8 weeks from the Effective Date;  
(b) **Design and Architecture Phase** – Approximately 8–10 weeks following completion of discovery;  
(c) **Configuration, Customization, and Integration Phase** – Approximately 16–20 weeks following completion of design;  
(d) **Testing and User Acceptance Phase** – Approximately 8–10 weeks following completion of configuration; and  
(e) **Go-Live and Stabilization Phase** – Approximately 4–6 weeks following successful user acceptance testing.

2.3.3 The Parties acknowledge that the foregoing timeframes are good faith estimates and may be adjusted based on Client’s timely provision of inputs, approvals, and resources, as well as any Change Orders.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a diligent, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with (i) generally accepted industry standards for similar services, (ii) the Specifications, and (iii) Applicable Law.

2.4.2 Vendor shall assign appropriately qualified and experienced personnel to perform the Services. Vendor shall be responsible for the supervision, direction, and control of its personnel and Subcontractors.

2.4.3 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and any associated SLA schedules. Failure to meet such Service Levels may entitle Client to certain credits or remedies, as further specified in Article XII, but shall not, by itself, constitute a breach of this Work Order, provided that Vendor diligently and promptly implements corrective actions.

2.4.4 Vendor shall comply with Client’s reasonable policies, procedures, and security requirements provided to Vendor in writing, including any cyber-security, physical security, and workplace conduct policies, while on Client’s premises or accessing Client systems.

### 2.5 Acceptance of Deliverables

2.5.1 Upon delivery of each Deliverable, Vendor shall notify Client in writing (which may include email) and provide any associated documentation necessary for Client to review and test such Deliverable.

2.5.2 Client shall have a reasonable period, not to exceed thirty (30) days from receipt of the Deliverable (the “**Acceptance Period**”), to review and test the Deliverable to determine whether it materially conforms to the applicable Specifications and acceptance criteria.

2.5.3 If Client determines that the Deliverable materially conforms to the applicable Specifications, Client shall promptly notify Vendor in writing of its acceptance of the Deliverable (“**Acceptance**”). Acceptance may be deemed if Client fails to provide written notice of acceptance or rejection within the Acceptance Period, provided that Vendor’s delivery notice clearly indicated the commencement of the Acceptance Period.

2.5.4 If Client reasonably determines that the Deliverable does not materially conform to the Specifications, Client shall notify Vendor in writing of such nonconformity, specifying in reasonable detail the nature of the deficiencies. Vendor shall, at no additional cost to Client, promptly correct or re-perform the Deliverable and resubmit it to Client for review in accordance with this Section 2.5.

2.5.5 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputes regarding Deliverable acceptance. If, after two (2) rounds of remediation, the Deliverable still fails to materially conform to the Specifications, Client may, at its option and as a sole and exclusive remedy with respect to that particular Deliverable: (i) accept the Deliverable with a mutually agreed equitable reduction in Fees; or (ii) reject the Deliverable and receive a refund of any Fees already paid for such Deliverable.

### 2.6 Client Responsibilities

2.6.1 Client shall timely provide Vendor with all information, data, access, and reasonably necessary cooperation required for Vendor to perform the Services and deliver the Deliverables, including access to relevant personnel, systems, and facilities.

2.6.2 Client shall designate a project manager who will serve as the primary point of contact with Vendor and who shall be responsible for providing decisions, approvals, and directions on behalf of Client.

2.6.3 Client acknowledges that delays in providing requested information, approvals, or access may impact the Project timelines and milestones. Vendor shall not be liable for delays to the extent caused by Client’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities under this Section 2.6.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The total contract value under this Work Order shall be **One Million Eight Hundred Fifty-Eight Thousand Three Hundred Forty-Seven U.S. Dollars (US$1,858,347)** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to adjustment pursuant to Article XIII (Change Management) and any additional Change Orders executed by the Parties.

### 3.2 Fee Structure

3.2.1 The Fees shall be comprised of the following components:

(a) **Fixed Implementation Fee** – A fixed fee of US$1,200,000 for the design, configuration, customization, integration, testing, and go-live activities described in Article II;  

(b) **Subscription / Managed Services Fee** – Recurring fees totaling approximately US$558,347 over the Term for ongoing support, maintenance, hosting, and monitoring services, billed as specified in Section 3.2.2; and  

(c) **Time and Materials (T&M) Fee** – Any additional services requested by Client and approved in a Change Order shall be charged on a T&M basis at the rates specified in Exhibit B (Fee Schedule).

3.2.2 Unless otherwise set forth in Exhibit B, the Subscription / Managed Services Fees shall be invoiced annually in advance in equal installments throughout the Term, prorated for any partial periods as applicable.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Client for the Fixed Implementation Fee in accordance with the following milestone-based schedule:

(a) **20%** upon execution of this Work Order;  
(b) **30%** upon completion of the Design and Architecture Phase;  
(c) **30%** upon completion of Configuration, Customization, and Integration Phase; and  
(d) **20%** upon successful Go-Live Acceptance of the core solution.

3.3.2 Subscription / Managed Services Fees shall be invoiced thirty (30) days prior to the beginning of each applicable service period.

3.3.3 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** from the date of Vendor’s invoice (“**Net 30**”). Payment shall be made by wire transfer, ACH, or such other method as the Parties may mutually agree in writing.

3.3.4 Client shall notify Vendor in writing of any disputed invoice amounts within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying in reasonable detail the nature of the dispute. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such disputes. Client may withhold only the disputed portion of an invoice; the undisputed portion shall be paid in accordance with this Section.

### 3.4 Late Payment

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until the date such amount is paid in full.

3.4.2 Vendor shall provide written notice of non-payment to Client. If Client fails to cure such non-payment within fifteen (15) days after receipt of Vendor’s written notice, Vendor may, in addition to any other rights or remedies it may have at law or in equity, suspend performance of the Services until such time as all undisputed overdue amounts and any applicable late fees are paid.

### 3.5 Taxes

3.5.1 Fees set forth in this Work Order are exclusive of all sales, use, value-added, withholding, or similar taxes imposed by any governmental authority (“**Taxes**”). Client shall be responsible for all Taxes associated with the Services and Deliverables, other than Taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees.

3.5.2 If Vendor is required to collect Taxes from Client, Vendor shall itemize such Taxes on the invoice and Client shall pay such Taxes in addition to the Fees.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursement

3.6.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred by Vendor in the performance of the Services, including transportation, lodging, and meals, in accordance with Client’s then-current travel and expense policy, provided in writing to Vendor.

3.6.2 Vendor shall submit appropriate documentation (e.g., receipts, expense reports) to substantiate all reimbursable expenses. Client shall reimburse such expenses within thirty (30) days of receipt of a properly documented invoice.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date of November 06, 2028** (the “**Initial Term**”), unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

4.1.2 Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Work Order may be renewed for additional one (1) year periods (each a “**Renewal Term**”) by mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current Term.

4.1.3 The Initial Term and any Renewal Term(s) are collectively referred to as the “**Term**.”

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience by providing the other Party with at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice of termination.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor for (i) all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted through the effective date of termination, (ii) any non-cancellable, third-party expenses expressly approved by Client, and (iii) any Fees that are stated as non-refundable in this Work Order or an applicable SOW, to the extent such Fees were paid or committed by Vendor prior to Client’s notice of termination.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for cause upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the nature of the breach (the “**Cure Period**”).

4.3.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, Client may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if Vendor (i) repeatedly fails to meet critical Service Levels in a manner that materially impairs Client’s operations, despite Vendor’s efforts to cure such failures; or (ii) engages in willful misconduct or gross negligence causing material harm to Client.

4.3.3 Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent, (ii) files for bankruptcy or has a petition in bankruptcy filed against it, which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

### 4.4 Effects of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:

(a) Vendor shall promptly cease performing the Services, except to the extent reasonably requested by Client to assist with transition services as described in Section 4.4.2;  
(b) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, in accordance with Article III; and  
(c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.

4.4.2 At Client’s written request and subject to payment of reasonable fees to be agreed in writing, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance to Client for a period of up to ninety (90) days after termination or expiration, including providing copies of relevant documentation and coordinating knowledge transfer.

4.4.3 Termination of this Work Order shall not affect any rights or obligations of either Party that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, nor any provisions that expressly or by their nature are intended to survive termination, including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, and XVI.

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:

(a) Use the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Work Order;  
(b) Not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted under this Work Order; and

5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, agents, and Subcontractors who need to know such information for purposes of this Work Order, provided that such individuals or entities are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein.

### 5.3 Exceptions

5.3.1 The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) Is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Work Order by the Receiving Party;  
(b) Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) Is received by the Receiving Party from a third party that is not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or  
(d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4.1 Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination or expiration of this Work Order, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible and electronic copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that:

### 5.5 Survival

5.5.1 The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Work Order, except with respect to trade secrets, for which the obligations shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

### 15.1 Definition

15.1.1 A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, riots, strikes or other labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s employees), governmental actions, pandemic, epidemic, failure of third-party telecommunications or hosting providers, or other similar events.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of such event on the performance of its obligations.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

15.3.1 During the period of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Work Order shall be suspended to the extent such obligations are directly affected by the Force Majeure Event, and the time for performance shall be extended for a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

### 15.4 Extended Force Majeure

15.4.1 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and substantially prevents performance of the Services, either Party may terminate this Work Order upon written notice to the other Party without liability, except for payment of Fees for Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination.
