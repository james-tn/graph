## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client the Services set forth in this Article II and in any SOW issued under the MSA and this Amendment. The Services primarily comprise:  
(a) managed cloud hosting and infrastructure services;  
(b) application management and support for Client’s core business systems;  
(c) 24x7 monitoring and incident management for critical systems;  
(d) project-based professional services for system enhancements and integrations; and  
(e) transition and knowledge transfer services as may be required during onboarding and expiry or termination.  

2.1.2 The Parties agree that the scope defined herein supersedes any inconsistent scope description in prior SOWs, to the extent expressly identified in such SOWs as being superseded by this Amendment.

2.2 **Managed Cloud Hosting Services.**  

2.2.1 Vendor shall design, provision, configure, and operate a secure, scalable cloud-based infrastructure environment to host specified Client applications, including production, pre-production, and test environments as set forth in the applicable SOW(s).  

2.2.2 Vendor shall provide:  
(a) virtual or physical servers as required;  
(b) storage capacity, backup and archival services;  
(c) network connectivity, including VPN and firewall configurations;  
(d) environment segmentation and security controls; and  
(e) capacity planning to ensure performance in line with Service Levels.  

2.2.3 Vendor shall be responsible for patching and maintaining operating systems, virtualisation layers, and middleware under its direct control, following a documented patch management process agreed in writing with Client.

2.3 **Application Support and Maintenance.**  

2.3.1 Vendor shall provide Level 2 and Level 3 support services for the applications identified in the SOW, including incident diagnosis, bug fixes, problem management, and minor enhancements, subject to the change control procedures in Article XIII.  

2.3.2 Vendor shall:  
(a) provide a help desk contact point as described in Article XII;  
(b) log, classify, and prioritise incidents and service requests;  
(c) deploy fixes and workarounds in accordance with agreed response and resolution times; and  
(d) maintain up-to-date technical documentation relating to application configurations, dependencies, and known issues.  

2.3.3 Vendor shall conduct periodic preventive maintenance, including log reviews, performance tuning, database optimisation, and health checks, and shall provide Client with quarterly reports summarising key activities, findings, and recommendations.

2.4 **Monitoring and Incident Management.**  

2.4.1 Vendor shall implement and maintain monitoring tools for system availability, performance, capacity and security indicators for the systems under its management.  

2.4.2 Vendor shall ensure proactive alerting to its operations team and shall initiate incident response procedures without undue delay upon detection of any material degradation or outage.  

2.4.3 Vendor shall manage incidents through their lifecycle, from detection and logging through investigation, remediation, and closure, and shall keep Client informed through timely status updates, as specified in Article XII (Service Level Agreements).  

2.4.4 For Severity 1 (critical) incidents, Vendor shall provide an initial response within the timeframes specified in Section 12.4 and shall coordinate with Client and any third-party providers as reasonably necessary to restore normal service.

2.5 **Professional Services and Projects.**  

2.5.1 From time to time, Client may request that Vendor perform specific project work, such as:  
(a) system upgrades or migrations;  
(b) implementation of new modules or integrations;  
(c) security hardening and compliance initiatives; or  
(d) custom development and configuration.  

2.5.2 Such projects shall be set out in one or more SOWs referencing this Amendment, detailing scope, timelines, milestones, resource allocations, dependencies, assumptions, acceptance criteria, and Fees.  

2.5.3 Unless otherwise agreed, professional services shall be performed on either a fixed-price or time-and-materials basis, as indicated in the relevant SOW, subject to the change management provisions in Article XIII.

2.6 **Transition and Knowledge Transfer.**  

2.6.1 Vendor shall provide reasonable transition-in services, including knowledge acquisition, documentation review, and shadow/support activities, to onboard the Services under this Amendment without material disruption.  

2.6.2 Upon expiration or termination (in whole or in part) of this Amendment, Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request, provide transition-out services for a period of up to six (6) months (or such other period as agreed in writing), to enable the orderly transfer of services to Client or a replacement provider. Such services may include:  
(a) transfer of documentation and relevant data;  
(b) reasonable assistance with knowledge transfer; and  
(c) coordination of cutover activities.  

2.6.3 Fees for transition services shall be as set forth in the SOW or, if not specified, shall be on a time-and-materials basis at the then-current rates, subject to prior Client approval.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**  

2.7.1 Vendor shall perform the Services:  
(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with best industry practices for services of a similar nature;  
(b) in compliance with all Applicable Laws and regulatory requirements; and  
(c) in accordance with the Service Levels specified in Article XII and any applicable SOW.  

2.7.2 Vendor shall allocate appropriately skilled and experienced personnel to perform the Services and shall ensure continuity of key personnel, subject always to Vendor’s general staffing discretion and obligations under Applicable Law. Vendor will use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid frequent or disruptive changes to such personnel.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.**  

2.8.1 Client shall provide Vendor with timely access to information, systems, facilities, and personnel reasonably required for Vendor to perform the Services, subject to Client’s security and access policies.  

2.8.2 Client shall:  
(a) designate primary and secondary points of contact;  
(b) promptly review and provide feedback or approvals on deliverables; and  
(c) maintain responsibility for business decisions based on the Deliverables.  

2.8.3 Vendor’s performance obligations are conditioned upon Client’s fulfilment of its responsibilities. Vendor shall not be liable for failure to meet Service Levels or other obligations to the extent caused by Client’s delays, omissions, or breaches, provided Vendor promptly notifies Client of the issue and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate impact.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  

3.1.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the total estimated Contract Value for the Services and Deliverables under this Amendment is **USD $4,936,448** for the Term, subject to adjustments for Change Orders, additional SOWs, indexation (if expressly provided in an SOW), or other agreed variations.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

3.2.1 The Fees shall comprise:  
(a) a one-time implementation and transition-in fee, as specified in the relevant SOW;  
(b) recurring monthly managed services fees for cloud hosting, monitoring, and application support; and  
(c) project-based professional services fees pursuant to SOWs.  

3.2.2 Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, all Fees shall be denominated and invoiced in US Dollars (USD).

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  

3.3.1 Client shall pay undisputed invoiced amounts within **forty-five (45) calendar days** from the date of receipt of a correct and valid invoice ("**Net 45**").  

3.3.2 Invoices shall be issued:  
(a) monthly in arrears for recurring services;  
(b) in accordance with milestone achievements for fixed-price project work; and  
(c) monthly in arrears for time-and-materials work and reimbursable expenses.  

3.3.3 Each invoice shall include reasonable detail, including reference to this Amendment (AMD-ZEN-202411-410), the applicable SOW, billing period, description of Services, and applicable taxes.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of actual payment at a rate of the lesser of:  
(a) two percent (2%) per month; or  
(b) the maximum rate permitted under Applicable Law.  

3.4.2 Before charging interest or exercising any rights under this Section 3.4, Vendor shall provide written notice to Client of the overdue amount and grant a grace period of ten (10) Business Days to cure the non-payment.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  

3.5.1 Client may withhold payment of any portion of an invoice that Client reasonably and in good faith disputes, provided that Client:  
(a) notifies Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the reasons for the dispute; and  
(b) pays the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3.  

3.5.2 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any invoice disputes promptly. Upon resolution, any amount determined to be payable shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days, together with any applicable interest under Section 3.4.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

3.6.1 All Fees are exclusive of VAT and other applicable sales, use, or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s income, property, or employees.  

3.6.2 Where VAT is chargeable, Vendor shall provide a valid VAT invoice, and Client shall pay such VAT in addition to the Fees.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.7.1 Vendor shall be reimbursed for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, accommodation, and subsistence, in accordance with Client’s then-current travel and expense policy (as notified to Vendor).  

3.7.2 Vendor shall provide appropriate supporting documentation for all reimbursable expenses. Client shall reimburse approved expenses within the payment terms stated in Section 3.3.

3.8 **Set-Off.**  

3.8.1 Subject to Applicable Law, neither Party shall set off any amounts due under this Amendment against any amounts owing by the other Party under any other agreement, unless such set-off has been agreed in writing by the Parties or ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  

4.1.1 This Amendment shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **05 January 2029** (the "**Term**"), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Article IV.  

4.1.2 The Parties may agree in writing to extend the Term by mutual consent, subject to negotiation of applicable commercial terms.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing the other Party with not less than **sixty (60) days** prior written notice.  

4.2.2 Where this Amendment is terminated for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor:  
(a) all Fees for Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination; and  
(b) any reasonable and demonstrable non-cancellable commitments incurred by Vendor specifically for Client’s benefit, provided that Vendor uses reasonable efforts to mitigate such costs.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Amendment immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
(a) commits a material breach of this Amendment or the MSA, which, if remediable, is not cured within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or  
(b) becomes insolvent, enters into administration, receivership, or liquidation (other than for a solvent restructuring), or has an administrator or receiver appointed over a substantial portion of its assets.  

4.3.2 Without limiting the generality of Section 4.3.1, repeated failure by Vendor to meet critical Service Levels (as defined in Article XII), resulting in material business impact, may be deemed a material breach, subject to the cure provisions above.

4.4 **Suspension of Services.**  

4.4.1 Vendor may suspend provision of the Services in whole or in part upon written notice to Client if:  
(a) Client fails to pay any undisputed amounts more than thirty (30) days after the due date, and Vendor has provided a ten (10) Business Day cure notice under Section 3.4; or  
(b) Vendor reasonably determines that suspension is necessary to prevent or mitigate a security incident or to comply with Applicable Law.  

4.4.2 Vendor shall promptly lift any suspension once the underlying cause is resolved and shall use reasonable efforts to minimise any adverse impact on Client.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment for any reason:  
(a) all rights and licences granted by one Party to the other shall terminate, except as expressly stated otherwise in this Amendment or the MSA;  
(b) each Party shall promptly return or, at the Disclosing Party’s direction, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to archival requirements under Applicable Law and Section 5.6; and  
(c) Vendor shall promptly cease performing the terminated Services, except to the extent required to provide transition services under Section 2.6.  

4.5.2 Termination or expiration shall not relieve either Party of any obligation accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including payment obligations.  

4.5.3 Sections that by their nature are intended to survive (including Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any accrued rights to payment) shall survive termination or expiration.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  

12.1.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the managed hosting and application Services are available at least **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime.  

12.1.2 "**Permitted Downtime**" shall mean:  
(a) scheduled maintenance windows, not exceeding six (6) hours per month, for which Vendor has provided at least five (5) Business Days’ notice to Client;  
(b) emergency maintenance necessary to address urgent security or stability issues, provided Vendor notifies Client as soon as practicable;  
(c) downtime caused by Client’s systems, third party providers, or Client’s misuse of the Services; and  
(d) downtime due to a Force Majeure Event.

12.2 **Incident Classification.**  

12.2.1 Incidents shall be classified as follows (details may be further refined in an SOW):  

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or severe degradation of critical production system affecting a material portion of Client’s end-users with no acceptable workaround.  
- **Severity 2 (High):** Significant impairment of functionality with substantial business impact, but system remains operational with workarounds.  
- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Limited impairment with moderate business impact; workaround available.  
- **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general service requests.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  

12.3.1 Vendor shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents within the following target times (measured during 24x7 coverage for Severity 1 and 2, and Working Hours for Severity 3 and 4, unless otherwise agreed):  

- Severity 1: Initial response within 30 minutes; target resolution or workaround within 4 hours.  
- Severity 2: Initial response within 1 hour; target resolution or workaround within 8 hours.  
- Severity 3: Initial response within 4 hours; target resolution within 3 Business Days.  
- Severity 4: Initial response within 1 Business Day; target resolution within 10 Business Days.  

12.3.2 Vendor shall keep Client informed of progress on incident resolution through regular status updates commensurate with the severity level.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  

12.4.1 If Vendor fails to meet the availability commitment in Section 12.1.1 in a given calendar month (an "**SLA Failure**"), Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows:  

- Availability 99.0% – 99.89%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service(s);  
- Availability 98.0% – 98.99%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service(s);  
- Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 15% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service(s).  

12.4.2 Service credits shall be applied as a deduction from the next invoice(s). Service credits shall not, in any event, exceed 15% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected service(s) in any month.  

12.4.3 Service credits are Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA Failures, without prejudice to Client’s rights under Article IV to terminate for material breach in case of persistent failures.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  

12.5.1 Service Levels shall not apply, and Vendor shall have no liability for SLA Failures, to the extent caused by:  
(a) factors outside Vendor’s reasonable control, including Force Majeure Events;  
(b) Client’s equipment, software, or network connectivity;  
(c) Client’s failure to implement recommended updates or configurations; or  
(d) any unauthorised or improper use of the Services by Client.

## ARTICLE XV  
## FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Force Majeure Events.**  

15.1.1 A "**Force Majeure Event**" shall mean any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, which prevents or materially delays the performance of its obligations, including, without limitation: acts of God; natural disasters; epidemics or pandemics; war, terrorism, or civil unrest; strikes or labour disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce, unless illegal or industry-wide); governmental acts or restrictions; failure of utilities or telecommunications networks (not caused by the affected Party); or any other similar events beyond the affected Party’s control.

15.2 **Notice.**  

15.2.1 The affected Party shall:  
(a) as soon as reasonably practicable, notify the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event, describing its nature, expected duration, and impact on performance; and  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  

15.3.1 The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, to the extent that such obligations cannot reasonably be performed due to such event.  

15.3.2 The affected Party shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations caused by a Force Majeure Event, provided that it complies with Section 15.2.

15.4 **Prolonged Force Majeure.**  

15.4.1 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate the affected Services or the Amendment in whole or in part upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability (other than for Fees accrued up to the date of termination and any obligations intended to survive).
