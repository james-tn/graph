## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
Phoenix shall provide to Contoso a suite of managed IT services (the “**Services**”) comprising, at a minimum:

  (a) **Infrastructure Hosting and Management** for Contoso’s production and non-production environments, including virtual servers, storage, and networking;  

  (b) **Application Hosting and Support** for Contoso’s primary business applications, including but not limited to ERP, CRM, and analytics platforms, as identified in the applicable SOWs;  

  (c) **Monitoring and Incident Management** including 24x7x365 monitoring, alerting, incident triage, escalation, and resolution;  

  (d) **Backup, Recovery, and Business Continuity Support** including scheduled backups, periodic recovery testing, and support for disaster recovery events; and  

  (e) **Service Desk and End-User Support** including helpdesk services, ticket management, and user support within defined service windows.

2.2 **Detailed Description of Services.**

  2.2.1 **Infrastructure Services.**  
  Phoenix shall:

  - Provide virtual compute, storage, and network infrastructure sufficient to support the workloads identified in SOWs, including capacity planning, resource allocation, and performance tuning;  
  - Maintain operating system software, security patches, and firmware at levels that are currently supported by the OEMs or software vendors;  
  - Implement redundancy at the network and storage layers consistent with “medium risk” enterprise standards, such that single component failures do not result in complete service outages; and  
  - Provide secure remote access capabilities for authorized Contoso personnel, subject to multifactor authentication and appropriate access controls.

  2.2.2 **Application Services.**  
  Phoenix shall host and support those applications identified in SOWs. Phoenix’s responsibilities shall include:

  - Deployment, configuration, and routine maintenance of application instances;  
  - Coordination with relevant software vendors for escalated issues and patches;  
  - Application performance monitoring, including thresholds and alerts for key performance indicators; and  
  - Support for Contoso’s periodic release cycles, including coordination of deployments, rollback plans, and validation of system stability.

  2.2.3 **Monitoring and Incident Management.**  
  Phoenix shall:

  - Monitor core infrastructure, application availability, and critical performance metrics on a 24x7x365 basis;  
  - Maintain an incident management process including classification (e.g., Severity 1–4), triage, escalation, and communication;  
  - Provide Contoso with access to a ticketing system for submitting incidents and service requests and for tracking progress;  
  - Adhere to the response and resolution times set forth in **Article XII**; and  
  - Provide incident reports for Severity 1 and Severity 2 incidents within the timelines specified in **Section 12.4**.

  2.2.4 **Backup and Disaster Recovery.**  
  Phoenix shall:

  - Perform daily incremental and weekly full backups of identified systems, with retention periods as specified in the applicable SOW (typically 30–90 days);  
  - Store backups in geographically redundant locations or, at minimum, in a logically segregated environment distinct from the primary production environment;  
  - Conduct disaster recovery tests at least annually, or more frequently if agreed in a Change Order, and provide Contoso with test reports detailing objectives, results, and remedial actions;  
  - Support Contoso’s business continuity planning efforts, including providing relevant documentation, architectures, and recovery time objectives (“RTO”) and recovery point objectives (“RPO”).

  2.2.5 **Service Desk and End-User Support.**  
  Phoenix shall:

  - Operate a service desk during agreed service windows (e.g., 7:00 a.m.–7:00 p.m. Central Time on Business Days for standard support, with on-call support for critical incidents outside such hours);  
  - Provide multiple contact channels (e.g., phone, email, web portal) for incident reporting and service requests;  
  - Offer Level 1 and Level 2 support directly, and coordinate Level 3 support through internal experts or third-party vendors as necessary;  
  - Perform root cause analysis for recurring issues and chronic incidents and propose remediation measures.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Deliverables under this Agreement may include, without limitation:

  (a) **Architecture and Design Documentation** describing the infrastructure and application hosting environment;  

  (b) **Runbooks and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)** for routine operations and incident handling;  

  (c) **Monthly Service Reports** summarizing SLA performance, incidents, changes, and capacity metrics;  

  (d) **Disaster Recovery Test Reports** as described in **Section 2.2.4**; and  

  (e) Any other deliverables set forth in an SOW or Change Order.

2.4 **Service Locations.**  
The primary data center(s) used by Phoenix to provide the Services shall be located in the United States unless otherwise agreed in writing. Phoenix shall not change the data center’s country of location without Contoso’s prior written consent, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Phoenix shall perform the Services:

  - In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards;  
  - In compliance with Applicable Law, including relevant data protection and cybersecurity regulations; and  
  - In accordance with the service levels and targets set forth in **Article XII** and any applicable SOW.

2.6 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso shall:

  - Provide Phoenix with timely access to relevant personnel, systems, and information required for Phoenix to perform the Services;  
  - Be responsible for the accuracy and completeness of Client Data and for obtaining all necessary rights, consents, and approvals to provide such data to Phoenix;  
  - Designate a primary contact and escalation contacts for service management; and  
  - Promptly notify Phoenix of any issues, incidents, or changes in requirements that may affect the Services.

2.7 **Statements of Work.**  
From time to time, the Parties may execute SOWs that reference this Agreement. Each SOW will, at a minimum, specify the scope of Services, Deliverables, acceptance criteria (if applicable), timelines, and fees. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to override specific provisions of this Agreement.

Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any specific SOW for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.   4.4.1 **By Contoso.**  
  Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW, in whole or in part, upon written notice to Phoenix if:

  (a) Phoenix materially breaches this Agreement (including repeated material failures to meet service levels under **Article XII**) and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) Phoenix becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

  4.4.2 **By Phoenix.**  
  Phoenix may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW upon written notice to Contoso if:

  (a) Contoso materially breaches this Agreement, including by failing to pay undisputed amounts when due, and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice; or  

  (b) Contoso becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Suspension of Services.**  
Without limiting other rights or remedies, Phoenix may suspend all or part of the Services upon at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice to Contoso if Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after the due date, provided that: (a) Phoenix has made reasonable efforts to notify Contoso of the delinquency; and (b) Phoenix resumes Services promptly upon receipt of all overdue amounts. Any suspension shall be conducted in a manner designed to minimize disruption to Contoso’s critical business operations.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) Contoso shall pay all fees due for Services performed and Deliverables delivered up to the effective date of termination, and any other amounts due in accordance with this Agreement;  

  (b) Phoenix shall cease performing the Services (except as requested under **Section 4.7**), cooperate with Contoso to ensure an orderly transition, and deliver to Contoso all completed Deliverables as of the termination date;  

  (c) Phoenix shall return or, at Contoso’s written direction, securely destroy all Client Data in its possession or control, subject to any retention requirements under **Article XI** and Applicable Law; and  

  (d) Those provisions which by their nature or express terms are intended to survive termination (including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**) shall so survive.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
For a period of up to sixty (60) days following expiration or termination (or longer as mutually agreed), Contoso may request that Phoenix provide reasonable transition services to facilitate migration of the Services to Contoso or a new service provider. Transition services shall be provided at Phoenix’s then-current standard rates (or as otherwise agreed) and subject to the terms of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Phoenix shall provide an overall monthly system uptime of not less than **99.9%** for the production environment hosting Contoso’s critical applications, excluding Planned Maintenance and Force Majeure Events. Uptime shall be calculated as:

> Uptime % = [ (Total Minutes in Month – Unplanned Downtime Minutes) / Total Minutes in Month ] × 100

12.2 **Incident Severity and Response/Resolution Times.**  

Phoenix shall classify Incidents and respond/resolve within the following targets:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or severe degradation of a critical business function affecting a large number of users.  
  - Initial response: 30 minutes  
  - Workaround / substantial mitigation: 4 hours  
  - Target resolution: 12 hours  

- **Severity 2 (High):** Significant impact on business operations with no feasible workaround.  
  - Initial response: 1 hour  
  - Workaround / substantial mitigation: 8 hours  
  - Target resolution: 24 hours  

- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Limited impact; workaround available; non-critical functions affected.  
  - Initial response: 4 hours  
  - Target resolution: 3 Business Days  

- **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries / service requests.  
  - Initial response: 1 Business Day  
  - Target resolution: 5 Business Days

12.3 **Planned Maintenance.**  
Phoenix may schedule Planned Maintenance during designated maintenance windows. Phoenix shall:

  (a) provide Contoso with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of Planned Maintenance expected to cause service unavailability;  

  (b) use reasonable efforts to perform Planned Maintenance during off-peak hours; and  

  (c) ensure that Planned Maintenance does not exceed eight (8) hours per month, absent Contoso’s prior written consent.

Planned Maintenance properly noticed in accordance with this **Section 12.3** shall not be counted as downtime for uptime calculations.

12.4 **Incident Reporting.**  
For all Severity 1 and Severity 2 Incidents, Phoenix shall provide:

  (a) an initial incident report within twenty-four (24) hours of resolution, including root cause analysis (if available); and  

  (b) a final incident report within five (5) Business Days of resolution, including confirmed root cause, corrective actions, and preventive measures.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If Phoenix fails to meet the uptime service level in **Section 12.1**, Contoso shall be entitled to Service Credits as follows (per calendar month):

- Uptime ≥ 99.9%: No credit  
- 99.5% ≤ Uptime < 99.9%: 5% of Monthly Service Fee  
- 99.0% ≤ Uptime < 99.5%: 10% of Monthly Service Fee  
- Uptime < 99.0%: 20% of Monthly Service Fee

Service Credits shall be applied against the next invoice or, if no further invoices are due, refunded to Contoso. Service Credits shall not exceed twenty percent (20%) of the Monthly Service Fee for any month.

12.6 **Chronic Failure.**  
If Phoenix fails to meet the uptime service level in **Section 12.1** for three (3) or more months in any rolling six (6)-month period, such chronic failure shall constitute a material breach, and Contoso may, in addition to Service Credits, exercise its rights under **Section 4.4.1(a)**, including termination for cause, following the applicable cure period.

12.7 **Exclusive SLA Remedy.**  
Service Credits provided under this **Article XII** are intended to compensate Contoso for unavailability and degraded performance and shall be Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for service level failures, except where such failures constitute a material breach giving rise to additional rights under **Article IV** or where excluded from the limitation of liability under **Section 9.3**.
