## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive Maintenance Services for Client’s designated facilities, equipment, and systems, as set forth in this Agreement and any applicable SOW. The Maintenance Services shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **Preventive Maintenance.** Regular scheduled inspections, testing, calibration, cleaning, lubrication, adjustments, and replacements of parts necessary to maintain system reliability, in accordance with manufacturer’s recommendations, industry best practices, and any mutually agreed specifications.

  (b) **Corrective Maintenance.** Diagnosis, repair, replacement, and restoration activities undertaken in response to identified faults, failures, or degradation in the performance of the covered equipment and systems.

  (c) **Emergency Maintenance.** On-call response to urgent issues that materially affect safety, business continuity, or critical operations, subject to the response and resolution times set forth in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**.

  (d) **Technical Support.** Remote technical support (via phone, email, or ticketing system) during the support hours defined in the applicable SOW, including troubleshooting, configuration assistance, and guidance regarding system usage.

  (e) **Reporting and Documentation.** Preparation and delivery of periodic maintenance reports, incident logs, and Work Product as described in the applicable SOW, including documentation of all material maintenance activities performed.

2.2 **Covered Equipment and Facilities.**  
The specific equipment, components, systems, and facilities covered by this Agreement (collectively, the “**Covered Assets**”) shall be identified in one or more SOWs. Each SOW shall specify, as applicable:

  (a) the location(s) of the Covered Assets;  
  (b) the type, make, model, and serial number(s), where available;  
  (c) the maintenance schedule and required Service Levels; and  
  (d) any special operating requirements, safety procedures, or regulatory constraints.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional, diligent, timely, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices for maintenance service providers;  

  (b) in compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, codes, and ordinances, including occupational safety and health requirements;  

  (c) in compliance with the manufacturer’s specifications and recommendations for the Covered Assets, to the extent such specifications have been made available to Vendor; and  

  (d) in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII** and the applicable SOW.

2.4 **Service Hours and On-Site Presence.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  (a) **Standard Service Hours.** Vendor’s standard on-site and remote Services shall be available during Business Days between 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. local time of the applicable Client facility.

  (b) **After-Hours and Weekend Work.** Services performed outside Standard Service Hours shall be subject to additional Fees, as specified in **Article III** or an applicable SOW, except where such work is necessary to resolve a Critical Issue (as defined in **Article XII**) caused solely by Vendor’s negligence or breach.

  (c) **On-Site Presence.** Vendor shall ensure that qualified Personnel are available to perform Services on-site within the response times set forth in **Article XII**, subject to proper access being provided by Client.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Client’s timely cooperation and fulfillment of certain obligations, including but not limited to:

  (a) providing Vendor with reasonable access to the Covered Assets, facilities, and necessary areas, subject to Client’s security policies;  

  (b) providing accurate and complete information regarding the Covered Assets, including any known defects, hazards, or limitations;  

  (c) designating a primary point of contact for coordination of the Services;  

  (d) ensuring that all Client-controlled environments (e.g., electrical supply, network connectivity, physical access) are suitable for the proper operation and maintenance of the Covered Assets; and  

  (e) promptly notifying Vendor of any observed malfunction, abnormal condition, or safety concern related to the Covered Assets.

Failure by Client to fulfill its obligations under this **Section 2.5** may excuse Vendor from performance to the extent Vendor’s performance is adversely affected thereby, and may result in additional Fees as mutually agreed in writing.

2.6 **Subcontractors.**  
Vendor may utilize subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that:

  (a) Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors;  

  (b) such subcontractors are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and  

  (c) Vendor shall not subcontract any material portion of the Services to a competitor of Client without Client’s prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed).

2.7 **Service Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly included in an SOW, the Services do not include:

  (a) major capital repairs or replacements (e.g., complete system replacements or major retrofits);  

  (b) building structural modifications;  

  (c) services required as a result of misuse, negligence, or improper operation of the Covered Assets by Client or its Personnel;  

  (d) services to correct issues caused by Third-Party vendors not under Vendor’s control; or  

  (e) any other services beyond those expressly described in this Agreement or an SOW.

Any excluded services requested by Client may be provided by Vendor pursuant to a mutually executed Change Order in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.8 **Deliverables and Reporting.**  
Vendor shall provide Deliverables and reports as specified in each SOW. At a minimum, unless otherwise agreed:

  (a) Vendor shall maintain logs of all maintenance visits, including date, time, description of work performed, parts replaced, and observations;  

  (b) Vendor shall provide Client with a monthly summary report detailing preventive maintenance completed, incidents logged, response and resolution times, and any open issues or recommendations; and  

  (c) Vendor shall furnish any required regulatory or compliance reports related to the Services, to the extent specifically agreed in the applicable SOW.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the estimated total contract value for the Services to be performed under this Agreement during the Term is **Fifty-Six Thousand One Hundred Sixty-Eight U.S. Dollars (US$56,168)** (the “**Contract Value**”), unless otherwise adjusted by mutual written agreement or by executed Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Fees shall consist of:

  (a) **Base Maintenance Fee.** A recurring base fee for standard preventive and corrective maintenance, payable monthly in equal installments, which collectively shall not exceed the Contract Value during the Term absent a Change Order.

  (b) **Time and Materials (“T&M”) Fees.** Additional Services outside the agreed scope (e.g., after-hours emergency work, excluded services per **Section 2.7**) may be billed on a T&M basis at Vendor’s then-current rates, as set forth in the applicable SOW.

  (c) **Parts and Materials.** Replacement parts, consumables, and other materials may be invoiced separately if not included in the Base Maintenance Fee, as specified in an SOW.

3.3 **Invoices.**  
Vendor shall invoice Client for Fees:

  (a) monthly in arrears for Base Maintenance Fees and any T&M Fees incurred during the preceding month; and  

  (b) promptly upon completion or delivery of any milestone-based Deliverables specified in an SOW.

Each invoice shall reference the Contract Reference Number **MNT-CAS-202407-144**, the applicable SOW (if any), and shall include reasonable supporting detail.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of receipt of Vendor’s invoice (“**Net 45**”). Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars by electronic funds transfer, check, or such other method as mutually agreed.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice:

  (a) Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and basis for the dispute;  

  (b) Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion; and  

  (c) the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Upon resolution, any amount due shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days.

3.6 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the original due date until the date paid. Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice and failure by Client to cure such non-payment, suspend Services until all undisputed past due amounts are paid, provided that Vendor’s suspension rights are subject to the dispute procedure in **Section 3.5**.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income or payroll. If Vendor is required to collect such taxes, Vendor shall itemize them on the applicable invoice.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable, documented, out-of-pocket travel and living expenses incurred by Vendor’s Personnel in performing Services at Client sites outside the normal service region specified in an SOW shall be reimbursed by Client, provided that:

  (a) Vendor obtains Client’s prior written approval for any single expense exceeding a threshold agreed in the SOW; and  

  (b) Vendor provides appropriate receipts or supporting documentation.

3.9 **No Setoff.**  
Except with respect to amounts properly disputed in accordance with **Section 3.5**, Client shall not withhold, set off, or reduce any sums due to Vendor under this Agreement by reason of any claim or alleged claim against Vendor, without Vendor’s prior written consent, subject to applicable law and the dispute resolution provisions of **Article XIV**.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **October 25, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement may be renewed for additional one (1)-year periods on such terms and conditions, including pricing, as the Parties may agree in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. For any SOW terminated for convenience by Client:

  (a) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and non-cancellable expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination; and  

  (b) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate any additional costs and shall not incur further expenses that are not reasonably necessary for an orderly wind-down.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail (or, if the breach is not capable of cure within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period); or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases to carry on business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) all rights and obligations of the Parties under this Agreement shall cease, except for those that expressly or by their nature survive, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI** and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination;  

  (b) Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the Services (except as necessary for any agreed transition assistance);  

  (c) Vendor shall deliver to Client all Work Product and Deliverables completed and paid for as of the effective date of termination; and  

  (d) upon Client’s request, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance, at Vendor’s then-standard rates, to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Client or a successor provider.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement that by their terms or nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including, without limitation, the provisions relating to confidentiality, IP rights, payment, indemnification, limitation of liability, and dispute resolution.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
If the Services include remote monitoring systems or software support, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such monitored systems are available 99.9% of the time during each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events (“**Uptime Commitment**”).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance on monitored systems during off-peak hours, which shall not exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month. Vendor shall provide Client with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance that is reasonably expected to affect system availability.

12.3 **Incident Classification and Response Targets.**  
Vendor shall respond to and work to resolve incidents according to the following categories, unless modified in an SOW:

  (a) **Critical Incident** (complete outage or severe impact on critical operations):  
   - Initial response within 1 hour from Client’s notification during Standard Service Hours;  
   - Continuous efforts until resolution or temporary workaround is implemented.  

  (b) **High Priority Incident** (major degradation with significant impact):  
   - Initial response within 4 hours during Standard Service Hours;  
   - Vendor shall target resolution or workaround within 24 hours.  

  (c) **Medium Priority Incident** (limited impact on non-critical functions):  
   - Initial response within 1 Business Day;  
   - Vendor shall target resolution within 3 Business Days.  

  (d) **Low Priority Incident** (minor issues, cosmetic defects, or inquiries):  
   - Initial response within 3 Business Days;  
   - Resolution on a best-efforts basis.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment or response times in a material and repeated manner, Client may be entitled to Service Credits, as specified in the applicable SOW. Service Credits shall be applied as a credit against future invoices and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches, subject to **Article IX**.

12.5 **Exclusions from SLAs.**  
The Service Levels shall not apply to downtime or delays resulting from:

  (a) Client’s acts or omissions, including failure to provide required access or information;  

  (b) issues with Client’s or Third-Party networks, hardware, or software not under Vendor’s control;  

  (c) Force Majeure Events; or  

  (d) scheduled maintenance conducted in accordance with **Section 12.2**.
