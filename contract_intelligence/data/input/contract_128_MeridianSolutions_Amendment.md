## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Scope.**  
This **Article II** supplements and modifies **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly expanded, clarified, or modified herein, all provisions of **Article II** of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect. The Extended Services under this Amendment are a continuation and expansion of the Project described in **Section 1.9 (“Project”)** and **Section 2.2 (Project Objectives)** of the Parent Agreement, with a particular focus on:

- Rolling out the target-state architecture and process model to additional business units and geographies beyond the initial scope of Finance, Supply Chain, and Customer Operations;
- Enhancing data analytics capabilities and self-service reporting;
- Extending implementation, integration, training, and hypercare support beyond the baseline assumptions of the Parent Agreement.

2.2 **Objectives of Extended Services.**  
In addition to the objectives set forth in **Section 2.2 (Project Objectives)** of the Parent Agreement, the Extended Services under this Amendment aim to:

(a) **Geographic & Functional Rollout.** Extend the implemented solutions and process optimizations to additional legal entities and regions (initially EMEA and APAC divisions), including localization of configurations, reporting, and training where required by local regulations or business practices.

(b) **Advanced Analytics Enablement.** Design and implement advanced analytics and reporting capabilities leveraging the data structures established under the Parent Agreement, including coordinated data models, dashboards, and key performance indicators (KPIs) tailored to executive and operational users.

(c) **Integration Expansion.** Extend the existing integration landscape defined under **Work Stream 3 – Implementation, Configuration & Integration** in the Parent Agreement to additional upstream and downstream systems (e.g., vendor portals, third‑party logistics providers, and HR systems), ensuring consistent data flows and strong controls.

(d) **Extended Hypercare & Stabilization.** Provide a structured Hypercare Period beyond the initial go-live of the extended rollout, with enhanced SLAs and on-site/remote support to drive adoption and minimize disruption, in alignment with **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement.

(e) **Additional Training & Change Management.** Develop and deliver additional training curricula and change management interventions tailored to the newly in-scope regions and user groups, supplementing **Deliverables D11–D13** under **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement.

2.3 **Extended Work Streams.**  
The following Extended Services are organized as supplemental work streams that build upon the four Work Streams defined in **Section 2.3** of the Parent Agreement:

(a) **Work Stream 5 – Multi-Region Rollout & Localization**  
   - Analyze additional regional requirements (EMEA and APAC) for finance, supply chain, and customer operations processes.  
   - Localize configurations in line with local tax, regulatory, and reporting needs.  
   - Coordinate regional pilots, phased go-lives, and cutover activities.  
   - Collaborate with local stakeholders and change champions identified by Client.  

(b) **Work Stream 6 – Advanced Analytics & Reporting**  
   - Extend the data architecture designed under **Work Stream 2 – Target-State Design & Roadmap** in the Parent Agreement.  
   - Define and implement standardized KPIs, dashboards, and drill-down reports for executive, regional, and functional stakeholders.  
   - Provide self-service analytics enablement, including data catalogs and semantic layers.  
   - Deliver detailed documentation for analytics models and governance procedures.

(c) **Work Stream 7 – Integration Extensions & Ecosystem Connectivity**  
   - Identify integration requirements with additional systems (e.g., vendor portals, logistics providers, HRIS, and legacy databases).  
   - Design and build additional integration interfaces consistent with the **Integration Specifications** under **Deliverable D8** in the Parent Agreement.  
   - Update **Test Plans & Results** (D9) and **Go-Live Readiness Assessments** (D10) to incorporate extended integrations.  
   - Support data migration and reconciliation activities related to newly integrated systems.

(d) **Work Stream 8 – Extended Hypercare, Training & Adoption**  
   - Provide Hypercare support for post-go-live stabilization in newly onboarded regions, for a period to be defined in the **Updated Project Plan (Exhibit A-1)** but anticipated to last a minimum of ninety (90) days per major rollout wave.  
   - Enhance service coverage and response targets under **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** as further detailed in **Section 12.3** of this Amendment.  
   - Develop region-specific training materials and conduct train-the-trainer sessions, building on **Deliverable D11 (Training Curriculum and Materials)** and **Deliverable D13 (Knowledge Transfer Runbooks and Support Playbooks)**.  
   - Conduct post-implementation reviews for each major rollout, which will supplement **Deliverable D14 (Post-Implementation Review & Lessons Learned Report)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Extended Deliverables.**  
The Extended Services will result in the following additional Deliverables (collectively, the **“Extended Deliverables”**), which are cumulative to the Deliverables D1–D14 listed in **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement:

- **ED1 – Regional Rollout Strategy & Localization Matrix**

- **ED3 – APAC Configuration & Localization Package**  
- **ED4 – Advanced Analytics Design & KPI Catalogue**  
- **ED5 – Executive and Operational Dashboard Suite**  
- **ED6 – Extended Integration Specifications & Interface Inventory**  
- **ED7 – Extended Test Plans, Test Scripts, and Test Results (Rollout Phases)**  
- **ED8 – Hypercare Support Plan & Incident Management Playbook**  
- **ED9 – Region-Specific Training Materials (EMEA and APAC)**  
- **ED10 – Additional Post-Implementation Review Reports (per rollout wave)**  

Each Extended Deliverable shall conform to specifications and acceptance criteria to be set forth in the **Updated Project Plan (Exhibit A-1)**, and **Section 2.5 (Deliverables and Acceptance)** of the Parent Agreement shall govern the acceptance process, subject to the modifications retained from **Section 2.6 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement (e.g., fifteen (15) Business Day initial review period and ten (10) Business Day re-review period).

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

(a) This Amendment is dependent upon, and may not extend beyond, the continued validity of the Parent Agreement and the Master Consulting Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
(a) In addition to any termination rights under **Section 4.2 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent Agreement, Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Service Provider.

(a) Either Party may terminate this Amendment for cause in accordance with **Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement (e.g., uncured material breach, insolvency event).  

(b) For material breaches relating solely to Extended Services (e.g., repeated failure to meet agreed SLAs for Hypercare), the Parties shall follow the notice and cure procedures specified in **Section 4.3** of the Parent Agreement, and only this Amendment (and not necessarily the entire Parent Agreement) may be terminated if the breach is confined to Extended Services.

Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment:

(a) Service Provider shall cease providing the Extended Services under this Amendment, except as required for any agreed wind-down or transition period;  

(b) Client shall pay Service Provider for all Extended Services properly performed and authorized expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination;  

(c) The Parties shall return or destroy Confidential Information related to this Amendment in accordance with **Section 5.6 (Return or Destruction)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article V** of this Amendment; and  

(d) Any obligations that by their nature should survive (including payment obligations for accrued Fees, confidentiality, intellectual property, limitations of liability, and indemnities) shall survive in accordance with **Section 4.7 (Survival)** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 16.9** of this Amendment.
