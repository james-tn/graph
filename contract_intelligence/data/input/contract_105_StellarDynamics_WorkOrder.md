## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.** Vendor shall perform for Client a comprehensive set of technology implementation, integration, and managed support services aimed at modernizing Client’s enterprise operations, including but not limited to:

  a. Assessment, planning, and design of a cloud-enabled enterprise architecture;  
  b. Configuration and integration of Client’s selected platforms and applications;  
  c. Data migration from legacy systems;  
  d. Development of custom workflows, scripts, and automation;  
  e. Testing, training, and knowledge transfer; and  
  f. Ongoing support, maintenance, and performance monitoring.

2.2 **Project Phases.** The Project will be conducted in the following primary phases, each of which shall be further detailed in project plans mutually agreed by the Parties:

  a. **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Estimated Duration: 8 weeks).**  
  Vendor will conduct stakeholder interviews, requirements workshops, and system assessments to document business, functional, and technical requirements. Vendor shall produce (i) a Requirements Specification Document, (ii) an Integration Architecture Diagram, and (iii) a Data Migration Strategy. Client shall review and provide feedback within ten (10) Business Days of receipt. Vendor shall incorporate reasonable Client feedback and produce a final Requirements and Design Package.

  b. **Phase 2 – Design and Configuration (Estimated Duration: 12 weeks).**  
  Based on the approved Requirements and Design Package, Vendor shall configure the agreed platforms in Client’s designated environments (development, test, and production, as applicable). Deliverables shall include: configuration scripts, environment build documentation, and security and access control settings consistent with Client’s policies. Vendor shall ensure that configurations align with industry best practices and applicable compliance requirements, as communicated by Client.

  c. **Phase 3 – Integration and Data Migration (Estimated Duration: 10 weeks).**  
  Vendor shall implement integrations between Client’s core systems and ancillary applications using agreed integration methods (e.g., APIs, middleware). Vendor shall map data fields, perform data cleansing (within reasonable scope), and execute test and production data migration activities. Deliverables include integration mappings, migration runbooks, and migration validation reports. Vendor shall perform at least one full test migration prior to production cutover, with defects tracked and remediated.

  d. **Phase 4 – Testing and User Acceptance (Estimated Duration: 6 weeks).**  
  Vendor shall prepare test plans, test cases, and scripts, and shall conduct system, integration, and performance testing. Vendor will support Client’s user acceptance testing (“**UAT**”), including defect triage, resolution, and retesting. Vendor shall provide a consolidated Test Summary Report and UAT Sign-Off Package for Client’s approval. Client shall use commercially reasonable efforts to complete UAT and provide formal acceptance or rejection within ten (10) Business Days of delivery of each major deliverable.

  e. **Phase 5 – Go-Live and Stabilization (Estimated Duration: 4 weeks).**  
  Vendor shall support go-live activities, including cutover planning, execution, real-time monitoring, and post-go-live issue resolution. Vendor shall deploy final configurations to production, confirm service stability, and provide a Go-Live Completion Report. During stabilization, Vendor shall provide enhanced support, including daily check-ins and prioritized incident handling.

  f. **Phase 6 – Managed Support Services (Through Expiration Date).**  
  From the date of production go-live until the Expiration Date, Vendor shall provide ongoing support as defined in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**, including incident management, problem resolution, minor enhancements (within the agreed monthly support allocation), and periodic health checks and optimization recommendations.

2.3 **Detailed Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor shall deliver at least the following Deliverables:

  a. Requirements Specification Document;  
  b. System Architecture and Integration Design Documents;  
  c. Data Migration Strategy and Mapping Documents;  
  d. Configuration and Build Documentation for all environments;  
  e. Test Plans, Test Cases, and Test Summary Reports;  
  f. UAT Sign-Off Package and Go-Live Checklist;  
  g. Go-Live Completion Report and Stabilization Summary;  
  h. Knowledge Transfer and Training Materials; and  
  i. Monthly Support and Performance Reports.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform all Services:

  a. In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices;  
  b. In compliance with Applicable Law and Client’s written policies that are provided to Vendor in advance and agreed upon;  
  c. Using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications; and  
  d. In accordance with the timelines, milestones, and Service Levels agreed in this Agreement and any project plans adopted by the Parties in writing.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.** Client acknowledges that Vendor’s performance is dependent upon timely and effective cooperation by Client. Client shall:

  a. Provide timely access to relevant systems, data, and personnel reasonably required by Vendor;  
  b. Designate a project manager with authority to make day-to-day decisions and approvals;  
  c. Review Deliverables and provide feedback or approvals within the time frames specified herein;  
  d. Promptly notify Vendor of any issues or concerns that may affect the Services; and  
  e. Ensure that any third-party vendors under Client’s control reasonably cooperate with Vendor as necessary.

Vendor shall not be liable for delays or failures to perform to the extent caused by Client’s failure to perform its obligations under this Section 2.5, provided that Vendor has promptly notified Client of such issues and used commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate delay.

2.6 **Changes to Scope.** Any changes to the scope of Services, Deliverables, or timelines shall be managed in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**. Vendor shall not be obligated to perform out-of-scope work unless and until a Change Order is executed by both Parties.

2.7 **Acceptance Criteria.** Deliverables shall be deemed accepted when Client provides written acceptance or, unless expressly stated otherwise, ten (10) Business Days after delivery of the applicable Deliverable, provided that Client has not delivered a written notice of material nonconformity specifying the respects in which the Deliverable fails to meet the Specifications.   a. **Milestone 1 – Project Initiation:** 20% of the Fixed Implementation Fees (US$59,014.60) upon Effective Date;  
  b. **Milestone 2 – Completion of Phase 1 (Discovery and Requirements):** 20% of Fixed Implementation Fees (US$59,014.60) upon Client’s acceptance of the Requirements and Design Package;  
  c. **Milestone 3 – Completion of Phase 2 (Design and Configuration):** 20% of Fixed Implementation Fees (US$59,014.60) upon Client’s acceptance of key configuration Deliverables;  
  d. **Milestone 4 – Completion of Phase 3 & 4 (Integration, Migration, and UAT):** 25% of Fixed Implementation Fees (US$73,768.25) upon Client’s UAT sign-off;  
  e. **Milestone 5 – Go-Live and Stabilization Completion:** 15% of Fixed Implementation Fees (US$44,260.95) upon acceptance of Go-Live Completion Report;  
  f. **Managed Support Services:** Monthly in arrears, beginning the first full month after production go-live;  
  g. **Change Orders/Contingency:** As provided in the applicable Change Order or, absent such provisions, monthly in arrears.

3.4 **Payment Terms.** Unless otherwise specified in this Agreement or an applicable invoice, all undisputed amounts invoiced by Vendor shall be due and payable by Client within **sixty (60) days** of Client’s receipt of the invoice (“**Net 60**”).   b. Becomes the subject of a petition in bankruptcy or any other proceeding relating to insolvency, receivership, liquidation, or assignment for the benefit of creditors, which is not dismissed within ninety (90) days.

4.5 **Termination for Non-Payment.** Subject to Section 3.6, Vendor may terminate this Agreement if Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within sixty (60) days after the due date, and such failure remains uncured for thirty (30) days following Vendor’s written notice describing the unpaid amounts and expressing Vendor’s intent to terminate if not cured.

  a. Vendor shall promptly cease performing the Services and shall take commercially reasonable steps to minimize additional costs;  
  b. Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and approved Deliverables provided through the effective date of termination, and for any non-cancellable commitments made in reliance on this Agreement with Client’s prior written approval;  
  c. Vendor shall promptly deliver to Client all completed and in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid or will pay in accordance with this Section;

  e. Vendor shall provide reasonable termination assistance services, if requested by Client, for up to sixty (60) days after termination, at Vendor’s then-standard rates (or, if this Agreement is terminated for Vendor’s uncured material breach, at no additional charge to the extent such assistance mitigates the effects of such breach).

4.7 **Survival.** Any provision of this Agreement that, by its nature, should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation provisions relating to payment obligations (to the extent of accrued but unpaid fees), confidentiality, intellectual property, indemnification, limitation of liability, data protection, dispute resolution, and general provisions.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Applicability.** This Article XII applies to the managed support services and any ongoing operational Services provided by Vendor to Client during the Term.

12.2 **Service Availability.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the supported systems and Services under Vendor’s operational control meet or exceed the following availability target:

  a. **Monthly Uptime Percentage:** At least **99.9%** during each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and events beyond Vendor’s reasonable control (including Force Majeure Events and failures in Client-controlled or third-party systems not under Vendor’s management).

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents according to the following severity levels:

  a. **Severity 1 (Critical):** Production system outage or critical functionality unavailable with no workaround.  
     - Initial Response: within 1 hour, 24x7  
     - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 4 hours  

  b. **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation or loss of key functionality, workaround available but not acceptable for prolonged use.  
     - Initial Response: within 2 business hours  
     - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 1 business day  

  c. **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issue with limited impact; workaround generally available.  
     - Initial Response: within 1 business day  
     - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 5 business days  

  d. **Severity 4 (Low):** Cosmetic or minor issues, requests for information, or enhancement requests.  
     - Initial Response: within 2 business days  
     - Target Resolution: as mutually agreed, typically in next scheduled release.

12.4 **Support Hours.** Standard support shall be provided during Vendor’s normal business hours (8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Pacific Time, Monday through Friday, excluding public holidays). Severity 1 incidents shall receive 24x7 coverage.

12.5 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may schedule maintenance windows (e.g., for upgrades, patches, or infrastructure work) and shall use reasonable efforts to schedule such maintenance outside of normal business hours. Vendor shall provide at least seventy-two (72) hours’ prior notice of scheduled maintenance expected to result in any material downtime.

12.6 **Service Credits.** In the event Vendor fails to meet the Monthly Uptime Percentage or the response/resolution times for Severity 1 incidents in a material and repeated manner, Client shall be entitled to service credits, as follows:

  a. For each full 0.1% below 99.9% Monthly Uptime (excluding permitted exclusions), Client shall be entitled to a credit equal to 2% of the monthly managed support fees, up to a maximum of 25% of such fees for the month;  
  b. For more than three (3) Severity 1 incidents in a month where Vendor fails to meet both the response and target resolution times due to reasons within Vendor’s control, Client shall be entitled to an additional 5% of the monthly managed support fees as a credit.

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice. Service credits are Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, without limiting any other contractual remedies (such as termination rights) that Client may have.
