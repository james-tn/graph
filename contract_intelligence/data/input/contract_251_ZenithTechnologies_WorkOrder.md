## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order is executed under and subject to the terms of the Parent SOW (Ref: **SOW-ZEN-202401-226**, contract_226) and **adds scope** to the Services and Deliverables described therein. Except as expressly modified in this Work Order, all provisions of the Parent SOW, including but not limited to **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)**, **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**, **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**, and **Article XIII (Change Management)**, shall apply to the WO Services and WO Deliverables.

2.1.2 This Work Order specifically extends:  
(a) Phase 3 – Development, Integration & Data Migration (**Section 2.2.3** of Parent SOW) by adding data analytics and automation development activities;  
(b) Phase 4 – Testing, Training & Go-Live (**Section 2.2.4** of Parent SOW) by adding additional testing cycles and advanced user training for analytics and automations; and  
(c) Phase 5 – Managed Support & Optimization (**Section 2.2.5** of Parent SOW) by expanding ongoing support scope for the Enhanced Analytics Components and Automation & Workflow Enhancements.

2.2 **WO Services Overview.**  

Vendor shall provide the following categories of WO Services:

2.2.1 **Advanced Analytics Design & Implementation.**  
Vendor shall:

(a) Assess Client’s existing reporting and analytics requirements in the context of the Solution implemented pursuant to Article II of the Parent SOW;  
(b) Conduct up to eight (8) focused analytics workshops with Business Stakeholders to identify KPIs related to finance, supply chain, sales, customer support, and reporting, building upon the Requirements Specification Document (RSD) produced under **Section 2.2.1** of the Parent SOW;  
(c) Design and document at least ten (10) critical KPIs and associated data models supporting cross-functional reporting;  
(d) Configure and/or develop up to fifteen (15) analytics dashboards and reports (the “**Enhanced Analytics Components**”) across ERP and CRM modules, including drill-down and filtering capabilities;  
(e) Integrate dashboard access control with the role-based access controls and security roles configured under **Section 2.2.2** of the Parent SOW; and  
(f) Prepare and deliver an **Analytics Design & Configuration Document** capturing architecture, data sources, transformation logic, security mapping, and usage guidelines.

2.2.2 **Automation & Workflow Enhancements.**  
Vendor shall:

(a) Review current Solution workflows and manual processes documented under the RSD and Functional Design Specifications (FDS) from **Sections 2.2.1 and 2.2.2** of the Parent SOW;  
(b) Identify and prioritize up to twenty (20) workflows suitable for automation (e.g., approval routing, notifications, exception handling, integration-triggered events);  
(c) Configure or develop Automation & Workflow Enhancements, including:  
   - Up to twenty (20) new or modified workflow rules;  
   - Up to ten (10) integration-triggered automation actions connecting with HR, payroll, and e-commerce systems as originally scoped under **Section 2.2.2** and **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW;  
(d) Implement logging and monitoring for such automations, consistent with logging and monitoring requirements described in **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW; and  
(e) Deliver an **Automation Configuration Runbook** detailing rules, triggers, dependencies, and rollback procedures.

2.2.3 **Extended Testing & Validation.**  
In addition to testing obligations under **Section 2.2.3 and 2.2.4** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

(a) Develop supplemental test cases focused on analytics accuracy, workflow functioning, and performance impacts, to be incorporated into the “Testing Plan and Test Cases” Deliverables defined in **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW;  
(b) Execute at least two (2) additional cycles of system integration testing (“SIT”) and support Client in extended user acceptance testing (“UAT”) specifically for the WO Deliverables;  
(c) Provide defect tracking and remediation for issues arising from WO Deliverables until UAT sign-off; and  
(d) Deliver a **WO UAT Summary Addendum**, which supplements the “UAT Summary Report” deliverable under **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent SOW.

2.2.4 **Advanced Training & Knowledge Transfer.**  
In addition to the twelve (12) training sessions described in **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

(a) Develop specialized training materials (slides, quick reference guides, and up to five (5) short training videos) focused on the Enhanced Analytics Components and Automation & Workflow Enhancements;  
(b) Conduct up to six (6) additional training sessions (onsite or virtual) for Client’s power users, analytics teams, and IT administrators;  
(c) Provide role-based training aligned with the security and access configurations defined under **Section 2.2.2** of the Parent SOW; and  
(d) Update the Training Materials deliverable required by **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent SOW with an **Analytics & Automation Training Addendum**.

2.2.5 **Uplifted Support & Optimization.**  
Supplemental to Phase 5 – Managed Support & Optimization under **Section 2.2.5** and the SLA framework in **Article XII** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

(a) Provide second‑line specialized support for the Enhanced Analytics Components and Automation & Workflow Enhancements during the WO Term;  
(b) Participate in quarterly optimization workshops (in addition to those described in **Section 2.2.5** of the Parent SOW) specifically focused on performance, adoption, and accuracy of WO Deliverables;  
(c) Provide quarterly **Analytics & Automation Health Reports** as an addendum to the “System Health Reports” referenced in **Section 2.2.5** of the Parent SOW;  
(d) Implement minor enhancements (not exceeding eight (8) hours per discrete change) to dashboards and automations based on agreed optimization recommendations, within the WO Contract Value; and  
(e) Coordinate with Client to include WO Deliverables in the periodic releases and patch testing support described in **Section 2.2.5** of the Parent SOW.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  

2.3.1 Vendor shall perform all WO Services in accordance with the performance standards set forth in **Section 2.5** of the Parent SOW, namely, in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and in compliance with all applicable Specifications and Service Levels.

2.3.2 WO Deliverables shall conform in all material respects to the Acceptance Criteria and Specifications agreed by the Parties. Client shall have the right to review and accept or reject WO Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.6 (Deliverable Review and Acceptance)** of the Parent SOW, which is hereby incorporated by reference and applied mutatis mutandis to WO Deliverables.

2.3.3 For avoidance of doubt, acceptance of WO Deliverables shall not constitute a waiver of warranty rights under **Article VII** of the Parent SOW, nor limit Client’s rights with respect to latent defects or non-compliance.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  

In addition to Client’s responsibilities under **Section 2.7** of the Parent SOW, Client shall:

(a) Ensure timely availability of Business Stakeholders for analytics and workflow workshops;  
(b) Provide Vendor with access to relevant data, reporting tools, and environments required to design and validate the Enhanced Analytics Components;  
(c) Review and provide feedback on WO Deliverables within the Review Period specified in **Section 2.6.1** of the Parent SOW; and  
(d) Ensure that Client’s internal policies applicable to analytics, access controls, and data usage are communicated to Vendor in writing.

2.5 **Key Personnel.**  

The provisions of **Section 2.8 (Key Personnel)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to WO Services. Vendor shall designate a lead analytics architect and a workflow specialist as Key Personnel for this Work Order; any changes to these roles shall follow the same process described in **Section 2.8** of the Parent SOW.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **WO Term.**  

The term of this Work Order (the “**WO Term**”) shall commence on the WO Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **April 3, 2026** (the “**WO Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or the Parent SOW.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.2.1 Notwithstanding **Section 4.2 (60-day notice)** of the Parent SOW, and in light of the high-risk and specialized nature of the WO Services, either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

4.2.2 In the event of such termination for convenience:

(a) Client shall pay Vendor for all WO Services performed and WO Deliverables delivered (and accepted or deemed accepted) through the effective date of termination, plus any non‑cancellable, pre‑approved expenses, calculated in accordance with the fees and milestones specified in **Article III**; and  
(b) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to effect an orderly wind-down of the WO Services and, at Client’s request and expense, provide reasonable transition assistance for a period not to exceed thirty (30) days following the effective date of termination.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.3.1 The termination for cause provisions of **Section 4.3** of the Parent SOW shall apply equally to this Work Order. A material breach of this Work Order shall be deemed a material breach of the Parent SOW, and vice versa, where such breach directly affects WO Services or WO Deliverables.

4.3.2 Without limiting the generality of **Section 4.3.1 of the Parent SOW**, material breach by Client under this Work Order shall additionally include repeated failure to comply with the WO Payment Terms (Net 60) for undisputed amounts.

4.4 **Effect of Termination of Parent SOW.**  

4.4.1 If the Parent SOW is terminated for any reason, this Work Order shall automatically terminate effective as of the Parent SOW termination date, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue this Work Order under a successor or replacement agreement.

4.4.2 Upon termination of the Parent SOW and/or this Work Order:

(a) Vendor shall cease performing WO Services, except as reasonably necessary to wind down activities and provide requested transition assistance;  
(b) The provisions of **Section 4.4 (Effect of Termination)** of the Parent SOW regarding return or destruction of Confidential Information and continuation of licenses for paid Deliverables shall apply to WO Deliverables; and  
(c) Client shall remain obligated to pay all fees properly accrued and invoiced under this Work Order prior to the effective date of termination.

4.5 **Survival.**  

In addition to the surviving provisions listed in **Section 4.5** of the Parent SOW, the following provisions of this Work Order shall survive expiration or termination: **Articles III (with respect to accrued payment obligations), V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure)**, and **XVI (General Provisions)**.
