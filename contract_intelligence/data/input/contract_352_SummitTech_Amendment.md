## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

(a) This Amendment **amends, updates, and supplements** the Services described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. The Enhanced Data Protection Services are provided **in addition to** the Platform, hosting, integration, support, and professional services previously described in **Sections 2.1 and 2.2** of the Parent Agreement.  

(b) Except as expressly modified herein, all obligations of Summit Tech under **Article II** of the Parent Agreement (including the performance standards in **Section 2.3**, the timelines in **Section 2.4**, and the client responsibilities in **Section 2.5**) continue to apply to the Enhanced Data Protection Services.

2.2 **Overview of Enhanced Data Protection Services.**  

Summit Tech shall provide to Contoso the following Enhanced Data Protection Services under this Amendment, collectively forming the **Data Protection Project**:

(a) **Data Protection Gap Analysis and Design (Phase 1).**  
   - Conduct a detailed assessment of the current Processing activities, as described in **Section 2.2 (Detailed Description of Processing Activities)** of the Parent Agreement, across relevant systems, data flows, and geographies.  
   - Review existing technical and organizational measures referenced in **Section 11.4 (Security Measures)** and Contoso’s internal policies to identify gaps against applicable Data Protection Laws (including UK GDPR, EU GDPR, UK Data Protection Act 2018, CCPA/CPRA).  
   - Deliver a **Gap Analysis Report** outlining identified gaps, risk ratings, and recommended remediation steps, together with a proposed **Enhanced Security Measures Design**.

(b) **Implementation of Enhanced Security Measures (Phase 2).**  
   - Implement the Enhanced Security Measures agreed by the Parties (see **Section 2.4** below), including but not limited to: advanced encryption standards, role-based access controls, continuous vulnerability scanning, and enhanced logging and monitoring.  
   - Configure the Platform (as defined in **Section 2.1(a)** of the Parent Agreement) to support data minimization, pseudonymization, and configurable data retention periods per category of Personal Data.  
   - Integrate incident detection and alerting mechanisms consistent with and improving upon the requirements of **Section 11.6 (Data Breach Notification)** of the Parent Agreement.

(c) **Data Subject Rights Support (Phase 3).**  
   - Design and implement standardized workflows and tooling within the Platform and associated support channels to facilitate Contoso’s response to data subject requests in accordance with **Section 11.7 (Data Subject Rights)** of the Parent Agreement.  
   - Provide templates and guidance for handling rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.  
   - Train Summit Tech support personnel to recognize and appropriately handle data subject requests, ensuring alignment with Contoso’s documented instructions under **Section 11.2 (Processing Instructions)**.

(d) **DPIA, TIA, and Regulatory Support (Phase 4).**  
   - Assist Contoso in conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (“**DPIAs**”) and transfer impact assessments (“**TIAs**”) relating to the Services, in line with **Section 11.3 (Compliance with Data Protection Laws)** and **Section 11.9 (International Transfers)** of the Parent Agreement.  
   - Prepare and maintain **Joint Records of Processing**, including details of Processing purposes, categories of Data Subjects, categories of Personal Data, recipients, and transfer mechanisms.  
   - Provide reasonable assistance in responding to Regulatory Inquiries that concern Summit Tech’s Processing under the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

(e) **Ongoing Monitoring, Reporting and Review (Phase 5).**  
   - Provide quarterly security and data protection reports summarizing security events, vulnerabilities, remediation activities, and any Data Breaches or Incidents as defined in **Section 1.12** of the Parent Agreement.  
   - Participate in at least one (1) annual data protection review meeting with Contoso to discuss regulatory developments, audit results, and potential improvements.  
   - Support audits and inspections as further described in **Section 11.12 (Audit Rights)** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 11.6** of this Amendment.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  

In addition to any Deliverables defined in **Section 1.9 (Deliverables)** and **Article II** of the Parent Agreement, Summit Tech shall provide the following Deliverables under this Amendment (collectively, the “**Data Protection Deliverables**”):

(a) **Gap Analysis Report** and **Enhanced Security Measures Design Document**;  
(b) **Updated Data Flow Diagrams** and **Records of Processing**;  
(c) **Data Protection Incident Response Plan** (updated and enhanced);  
(d) **Data Subject Rights Handling Playbooks** and related configuration documents;  
(e) **DPIA/TIA Support Documentation**, including templates and guidance;  
(f) **Quarterly Data Protection Reports**; and  
(g) Any additional documents or tools specified in the applicable Project Plan or Data Protection Schedule.

All such Deliverables shall be considered “Deliverables” for purposes of **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

2.4 **Enhanced Security Measures.**  

(a) **Update to Section 11.4.** The Parties agree that, as of the Amendment Effective Date, **Section 11.4 (Security Measures)** of the Parent Agreement is hereby **supplemented** (but not replaced) by the following additional commitments:

   i. Summit Tech shall maintain **encryption in transit and at rest** for all Personal Data using industry-standard algorithms (e.g., AES-256, TLS 1.2 or higher), except where technically infeasible for limited and documented legacy integrations agreed in writing with Contoso.  
   ii. Summit Tech shall implement **multi-factor authentication (MFA)** for all administrative access to systems Processing Personal Data on behalf of Contoso.  
   iii. Summit Tech shall maintain a **documented vulnerability management program**, including monthly vulnerability scans, timely patch management, and risk-based remediation timelines.  
   iv. Summit Tech shall maintain **centralized logging** of security-relevant events and shall retain such logs for a minimum of twelve (12) months, unless longer retention is required by Applicable Law or specific Contoso instructions.  
   v. Summit Tech shall perform at least one (1) **independent third-party security assessment or penetration test** annually, and shall provide Contoso, upon reasonable request, with summary reports or attestation of such assessments (subject to confidentiality obligations).  

(b) In the event of any conflict between this **Section 2.4** and **Section 11.4** of the Parent Agreement, the provisions of this **Section 2.4** shall prevail with respect to the Enhanced Data Protection Services.

2.5 **Data Subject Rights Support Services.**  

(a) This Section supplements **Section 11.7 (Data Subject Rights)** of the Parent Agreement by specifying additional operational support to be provided by Summit Tech, including:  

   i. Configuring the Platform to flag, locate, and export Personal Data associated with specific Data Subjects identified by Contoso;  
   ii. Implementing configurable workflows permitting Contoso to trigger erasure, restriction, or anonymization operations within the Platform;  
   iii. Establishing secure communication channels for Contoso to submit and track data subject-related requests directed to Summit Tech.  

(b) Summit Tech shall respond to Contoso’s documented instructions concerning data subject requests **without undue delay** and in any event within timelines reasonably necessary for Contoso to meet its own legal obligations under Data Protection Laws.

2.6 **Timelines and Delivery Milestones.**  

(a) **Project Plan.** Within thirty (30) days of the Amendment Effective Date, the Parties shall agree a Project Plan consistent with the phased structure set out in **Section 2.2** above and the general implementation framework described in **Section 2.4 (Timelines and Delivery Milestones)** of the Parent Agreement.  

(b) **Illustrative Milestones.** Unless otherwise agreed in the Project Plan:

   - Phase 1 (Gap Analysis and Design): completed within sixty (60) days of the Amendment Effective Date;  
   - Phase 2 (Implementation of Enhanced Security Measures): completed within one hundred twenty (120) days of completion of Phase 1;  
   - Phase 3 (Data Subject Rights Support): completed within sixty (60) days of completion of Phase 2;  
   - Phase 4 (DPIA/TIA and Regulatory Support): initiated concurrently with Phases 2 and 3 and completed within one hundred eighty (180) days of completion of Phase 3;  
   - Phase 5 (Ongoing Monitoring): continues for the remainder of the Term of this Amendment.  

(c) Acceptance procedures for Deliverables shall follow the “phase-based deliverables” framework in **Section 2.4(b)** of the Parent Agreement, including the ten (10) Business Day review period and deemed acceptance mechanism, unless otherwise stated in a Data Protection Schedule.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**  

Summit Tech shall perform the Enhanced Data Protection Services:

(a) In accordance with the general performance standards set forth in **Section 2.3 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, including professional, diligent, and workmanlike performance, use of appropriately trained personnel, and compliance with Applicable Law;  

(b) In a manner consistent with “low risk” data processing posture agreed by the Parties for this Amendment, including proactive risk identification and remediation; and  

(c) While maintaining Service Levels and Availability Commitments as set forth in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XII** of this Amendment, it being understood that certain security changes may require carefully planned maintenance windows in accordance with **Section 12.2 (Scheduled Maintenance)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.**  

In addition to the responsibilities listed in **Section 2.5 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement, Contoso shall:

(a) Provide timely and accurate information about its Processing activities, data flows, and business requirements necessary for the Data Protection Project;  

(b) Designate a data protection contact and key stakeholders to review and approve Deliverables, risk assessments, and remediation priorities;  

(c) Implement any Contoso-side technical or organizational measures identified in the Gap Analysis Report as necessary for compliance, recognizing that Summit Tech’s obligations are limited to systems and processes under its control; and  

(d) Ensure that its own internal policies and notices align with the functionalities and security controls implemented under this Amendment.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Updated Contract Value for Enhanced Services.**  

The total estimated contract value for the Enhanced Data Protection Services to be provided under this Amendment is **USD $3,236,979** (the “**Updated Contract Value**”), which is **separate from and in addition to** the **Contract Value of USD $4,058,584** specified in **Section 3.1** of the Parent Agreement. The Updated Contract Value may be adjusted in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)** of this Amendment and **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

Unless otherwise specified in a Data Protection Schedule, the Fees under this Amendment shall consist of:

(a) **Project Fees.** Fixed fees for Phases 1–4 of the Data Protection Project, allocated as follows (illustrative, subject to detailed schedule):

   - Phase 1 (Gap Analysis and Design): USD $612,000  
   - Phase 2 (Enhanced Security Implementation): USD $1,435,000  
   - Phase 3 (Data Subject Rights Support Implementation): USD $489,000  
   - Phase 4 (DPIA/TIA and Regulatory Support Setup): USD $325,000  

(b) **Ongoing Support Fees.** Recurring annual fees for Phase 5 (Ongoing Monitoring, Reporting, and Reviews) equal to the balance of the Updated Contract Value, payable in equal annual installments over the Term (as defined in **Section 4.1**).

(c) **Additional Services Fees.** Time-and-materials fees for any additional consultancy, custom development, or bespoke regulatory projects requested by Contoso beyond the scope described in **Article II**, at rates to be agreed in a Data Protection Schedule.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

(a) **Invoicing Schedule.** Subject to any Data Protection Schedule:

   i. Fifty percent (50%) of the Phase 1 fee shall be invoiced on the Amendment Effective Date; the remaining fifty percent (50%) upon delivery of the Gap Analysis Report.  
   ii. For Phases 2–4, thirty percent (30%) of the applicable phase fee shall be invoiced upon commencement of the phase, and the remaining seventy percent (70%) upon phase completion (as accepted per Section 2.6).  
   iii. Ongoing Support Fees for Phase 5 shall be invoiced annually in advance on each anniversary of the Amendment Effective Date.  

(b) **Payment Terms – Net 30.** Notwithstanding **Section 3.3(b) (Net 60)** of the Parent Agreement, for all Fees invoiced under this Amendment, Contoso shall pay all amounts properly invoiced **within thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (“**Net 30**”), without set-off or deduction except as required by law or as otherwise expressly provided in this Amendment. For the avoidance of doubt, the “Net 60” payment term of the Parent Agreement shall continue to apply to Fees invoiced under the Parent Agreement other than this Amendment.

(c) **Payment Method and Currency.** Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars (USD) by bank transfer or such other payment method as mutually agreed in writing, consistent with **Section 3.3(c)** of the Parent Agreement.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

Any undisputed amount not paid when due under this Amendment shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at a rate of the lesser of: (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, consistent with **Section 3.4** of the Parent Agreement. Summit Tech may, upon written notice to Contoso and consistent with the low-risk posture of this engagement, suspend provision of non-critical Enhanced Data Protection Services (but not core security controls) if any undisputed amount remains overdue for more than thirty (30) days after written notice, provided that Summit Tech resumes such services promptly upon receipt of payment.

3.5 **Disputed Invoices.**  

Contoso may dispute any invoice in good faith in accordance with **Section 3.5 (Disputed Invoices)** of the Parent Agreement, which is incorporated herein by reference and applies equally to invoices under this Amendment.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

The provisions of **Section 3.6 (Taxes)** of the Parent Agreement apply mutatis mutandis to all Fees under this Amendment.

3.7 **Expenses.**  

Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Summit Tech in connection with on-site data protection workshops, audits, or regulatory meetings specifically requested by Contoso shall be reimbursed by Contoso at cost, in accordance with **Section 3.7 (Expenses)** of the Parent Agreement and subject to Contoso’s standard travel and expense policies.

3.8 **Adjustments to Fees.**  

Summit Tech may adjust its standard fees for the ongoing Phase 5 services upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice before any Renewal Term (as defined in **Section 4.2** of this Amendment), in line with **Section 3.8 (Adjustments to Fees)** of the Parent Agreement. Any such increase shall be commercially reasonable and consistent with the low-risk classification of this engagement.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term of Amendment.**  

This Amendment shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date (**26 March 2025**) and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until **26 November 2029** (the “**Amendment Expiration Date**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  

Upon the Amendment Expiration Date, this Amendment may be renewed for successive periods of one (1) year (each a “**Renewal Term**”) by mutual written agreement of the Parties at least sixty (60) days prior to the Amendment Expiration Date or any then-current Renewal Term. The term of this Amendment, together with any Renewal Terms, is herein referred to as the “**Amendment Term**”.

4.3 **Interaction with Parent Agreement Term.**  

(a) This Amendment is **entered into pursuant to** and **supplements** the Parent Agreement. If the Parent Agreement is terminated or expires in accordance with **Article IV (Term and Termination)** thereof, this Amendment shall automatically terminate on the effective date of such termination or expiration, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to maintain certain aspects of this Amendment (e.g., post-termination data protection assistance) for a defined period.  

(b) Termination or expiration of this Amendment shall not, by itself, terminate the Parent Agreement, which shall continue in accordance with its terms unless separately terminated.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience.**  

Notwithstanding **Section 4.3 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent Agreement (which provides for ninety (90) days’ prior written notice), because this Amendment is designated as **low risk**, either Party may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the other Party, provided that:

(a) Such termination shall not relieve Contoso of its obligation to pay any Fees accrued or committed under duly accepted Project Plans or Data Protection Schedules; and  

(b) Termination of this Amendment for convenience shall not affect the Parent Agreement, which shall remain in full force and effect.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  

Either Party may terminate this Amendment immediately upon written notice if:

(a) The other Party commits a material breach of this Amendment (including a persistent failure to comply with Enhanced Security Measures or data protection obligations) and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof; or  

(b) An event described in **Section 4.4(b)** of the Parent Agreement (insolvency, bankruptcy, etc.) occurs with respect to the other Party.

4.6 **Termination for Data Protection Reasons.**  

In addition to **Section 4.5** of this Amendment and **Section 4.5 (Termination for Data Protection Reasons)** of the Parent Agreement, Contoso may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, with immediate effect upon written notice if Summit Tech:

(a) Is in persistent or material breach of its data protection obligations under **Article XI** of the Parent Agreement as supplemented by this Amendment; or  

(b) Is unable or unwilling to implement or maintain the Enhanced Security Measures required under this Amendment;  

and, in each case, fails to cure such breach (if curable) within a reasonable period not exceeding thirty (30) days after written notice.

4.7 **Effect of Termination.**  

(a) **Accrued Rights.** Termination or expiration of this Amendment shall be without prejudice to any rights or obligations that have accrued prior to such termination or expiration, including payment obligations.  

(b) **Cessation of Enhanced Services.** Upon the effective date of termination or expiration of this Amendment, Summit Tech shall cease providing the Enhanced Data Protection Services, except as necessary to perform termination assistance under **Section 4.8**. Core Services under the Parent Agreement shall continue unless the Parent Agreement is also terminated.  

(c) **Return or Deletion of Data.** The obligations under **Section 4.6(c) (Return or Deletion of Data)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to any Personal Data processed under this Amendment, including any additional data sets or derived data created in the course of the Data Protection Project.

4.8 **Termination Assistance.**  

Upon Contoso’s request made on or before the effective date of termination or expiration of this Amendment, Summit Tech shall provide reasonable termination assistance services related to the Enhanced Data Protection Services, including:

(a) Provision of final Records of Processing, security configurations, and data subject rights workflow documentation;  

(b) Assistance with transfer of data protection-related configurations and documentation to an alternative provider or to Contoso;  

(c) Reasonable support in facilitating the secure handover of any tooling or scripts developed as part of the Data Protection Deliverables.

Such services shall be provided for a Transition Period of up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration, at Summit Tech’s then-current time-and-materials rates, unless termination is due to Summit Tech’s uncured material breach under this Amendment, in which case reasonable termination assistance directly related to remedial data protection tasks shall be provided at no additional charge, consistent with the client-friendly approach for low risk engagements.

4.9 **Survival.**  

The rights and obligations of the Parties under **Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI** of this Amendment, together with any provisions expressly stated to survive, and the corresponding provisions of the Parent Agreement (including but not limited to Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI), shall survive termination or expiration of this Amendment to the extent necessary to give effect to their intended purpose.

## ARTICLE XI – DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY (UPDATED TERMS)

11.1 **Role of the Parties.**  

The roles of the Parties as Controller (Contoso) and processor/service provider (Summit Tech) under **Section 11.1** of the Parent Agreement remain unchanged. This Amendment clarifies and enhances Summit Tech’s obligations without altering the fundamental allocation of roles.

11.2 **Processing Instructions.**  

Summit Tech shall continue to Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Contoso as described in **Section 11.2** of the Parent Agreement. The Project Plan, Data Protection Schedules, and written approvals of Deliverables under this Amendment shall constitute part of Contoso’s documented instructions.

11.3 **Compliance with Data Protection Laws.**  

Each Party shall continue to comply with Data Protection Laws as required by **Section 11.3** of the Parent Agreement. Summit Tech’s obligation to provide assistance now explicitly includes support for DPIAs, TIAs, and Regulatory Inquiries as described in **Section 2.2(d)**.

11.4 **Security Measures – Supplement.**  

The Enhanced Security Measures in **Section 2.4** of this Amendment supplement the requirements in **Section 11.4** of the Parent Agreement. In the event of conflict, the more protective measure shall apply.

11.5 **Personnel and Confidentiality.**  

Summit Tech shall ensure that all personnel involved in the Data Protection Project receive appropriate data protection and security training and are subject to confidentiality obligations, consistent with **Section 11.5** of the Parent Agreement.

11.6 **Data Breach Notification – Clarification.**  

The notification obligations in **Section 11.6** of the Parent Agreement (including notification “without undue delay” and where feasible within 48 hours) apply fully to this Amendment. In addition, Summit Tech shall:

(a) Provide Contoso with interim updates on the status of the investigation at reasonable intervals;  

(b) Cooperate in good faith with Contoso to prepare drafts of any required regulatory or data subject notifications; and  

(c) Include in its Data Protection Incident Response Plan clearly defined communication channels and points of contact for Contoso.

11.7 **Data Subject Rights.**  

Summit Tech’s obligations under **Section 11.7** of the Parent Agreement are reinforced by the operational support described in **Section 2.5** of this Amendment. Summit Tech shall not respond directly to data subject requests except in accordance with Contoso’s documented instructions or where required by law.

11.8 **Sub-processors.**  

The requirements for Sub-processors in **Section 11.8** of the Parent Agreement remain in effect. Summit Tech shall ensure that all Sub-processors involved in the Enhanced Data Protection Services are bound by contractual obligations consistent with the Enhanced Security Measures where relevant.

11.9 **International Transfers.**  

Summit Tech shall continue to ensure appropriate safeguards for international transfers under **Section 11.9** of the Parent Agreement. Any additional or modified transfer mechanisms required as a result of the Data Protection Project (e.g., updated standard contractual clauses) shall be documented in a Data Protection Schedule.

11.10 **Data Retention and Deletion.**  

Summit Tech shall configure the Platform to support customized retention periods as part of the Enhanced Data Protection Services. However, the general obligations in **Section 11.10** of the Parent Agreement regarding retention and deletion shall continue to apply.

11.11 **Aggregated and Anonymized Data.**  

Summit Tech may continue to generate and use aggregated/anonymized data as described in **Section 11.11** of the Parent Agreement, provided that the enhanced controls do not cause such data to re-identify individuals.

11.12 **Audit Rights.**  

Contoso’s audit rights under **Section 11.12** of the Parent Agreement extend to the Enhanced Security Measures and Data Protection Project activities. Summit Tech may, where appropriate, satisfy such audit rights by providing relevant third-party reports (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001) and summaries of penetration test results.

11.13 **CCPA/CPRA Obligations.**  

Summit Tech’s obligations under **Section 11.13** of the Parent Agreement (including not selling or sharing Personal Data and limiting use to specified purposes) are reaffirmed and apply to any processing carried out under this Amendment.
