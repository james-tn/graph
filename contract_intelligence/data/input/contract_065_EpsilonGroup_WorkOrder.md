## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order **adds scope to the Statement of Work dated 14 March 2025 (Ref: SOW-EPS-202503-045, ID: contract_045)**. The Services and Deliverables under this Work Order are **Additional Services** and **Enhanced Deliverables** that supplement, extend, and refine the scope described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent SOW.

2.1.2 Unless explicitly stated otherwise, all obligations, assumptions, dependencies, Client responsibilities, and Performance Standards defined in **Sections 2.4, 2.5, and 2.6** of the Parent SOW shall apply, mutatis mutandis, to the Additional Services and Enhanced Deliverables under this Work Order.

2.2 **Overview of Additional Services.**  

Vendor shall provide the following Additional Services to Client, which are in addition to and integrated with the **Project** and **Services** described in **Sections 1.1.15 and 1.1.17** and **Article II** of the Parent SOW:

(a) **Advanced Security Services**, as described in **Section 2.4**;  
(b) **Automation and Observability Services**, as described in **Section 2.5**;  
(c) **Expanded Managed Services**, as described in **Section 2.6**; and  
(d) **Strategic Roadmap and Governance Enhancements**, as described in **Section 2.7**.

2.3 **Program Structure and Timelines.**  

2.3.1 The Additional Services under this Work Order shall be organized into the following workstreams and indicative timelines, which align with the Parent SOW phases:

- **Workstream A – Security Hardening & Zero-Trust Extension (Months 1–12 of WO Term)**  
  Extending the “network segmentation and zero-trust security principles” described in **Section 2.2.1(c)** of the Parent SOW to additional Client environments and business units, including on-premises legacy systems and remote workforce segments.

- **Workstream B – Automation & IaC Expansion (Months 6–24 of WO Term)**  
  Building upon the “Infrastructure-as-Code framework” described in **Section 2.2.2(b)** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall extend automation templates to cover additional environments, disaster recovery configurations, and multi-cloud deployments.

- **Workstream C – Advanced Observability & Analytics (Months 9–30 of WO Term)**  
  Enhancing “logging, monitoring, SIEM integration and incident response framework” described in **Section 2.2.1(c)** of the Parent SOW, including advanced telemetry, application performance monitoring, and cost analytics dashboards.

- **Workstream D – Extended Managed Services and Optimization (Throughout WO Term)**  
  Extending and supplementing the activities described in **Section 2.2.3 (Phase 3 – Managed Services and Optimization)** of the Parent SOW, including higher-frequency reviews, continuous compliance monitoring, and capacity/finops improvements.

2.3.2 The Parties acknowledge that these timelines are indicative and may be refined by mutual written agreement in a project plan. Any changes with material impacts to scope, cost or schedule shall be handled via the **Change Management** processes under **Article XIII** of both this Work Order and the Parent SOW.

2.4 **Advanced Security Services.**  

Vendor shall perform the following Advanced Security Services, which extend the cybersecurity operations and security controls contemplated in **Sections 2.2.1(c), 2.2.2(a), and 2.2.3(c)** of the Parent SOW:

(a) **Zero-Trust Architecture Extension.**  
Design and implement an extended zero-trust framework across additional Client environments, including but not limited to:

- Segmentation of legacy networks and applications;  
- Micro-segmentation of critical workloads in the hybrid cloud;  
- Conditional access policies for remote workforce and third-party vendors;  
- Integration of identity-aware proxies and just-in-time access processes.

(b) **Threat Hunting and Red Team Exercises.**  
Conduct periodic (at least **two (2) per Program Year**) threat hunting campaigns and red team exercises, delivering:

- Threat hunting playbooks;  
- Reports on lateral movement paths, privilege escalation vectors, and control gaps;  
- Recommendations and prioritized remediation plans.

(c) **Security Runbook Enhancements.**  
Update and expand the **Runbooks and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)** originally described in **Section 2.3.5** of the Parent SOW to incorporate:

- Enhanced incident response procedures for advanced persistent threats;  
- Integration with new logging and SIEM sources;  
- Updated escalation matrices and RACI charts.

(d) **Compliance Support.**  
Provide advisory and operational assistance to support Client’s compliance with GDPR, CCPA and other Applicable Laws referenced in **Article XI** of the Parent SOW, including:

- Continuous controls monitoring;  
- Preparation of security and compliance evidence for audits;  
- Quarterly compliance posture reports.

2.5 **Automation and Observability Services.**  

Vendor shall expand Clients’ automation and observability capabilities as follows:

(a) **IaC Template Expansion.**  
Building upon the “Infrastructure-as-Code Artifacts” Deliverable described in **Section 2.3.4** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

- Develop additional reusable templates for multi-region scaling, disaster recovery failover, and blue/green deployments;  
- Implement parameterized modules to support multiple business units and environments;  
- Provide updated documentation of all IaC artefacts, consistent with the IP framework in **Article VI** of the Parent SOW.

(b) **CI/CD Integration.**  
Integrate IaC and configuration management scripts into Client’s CI/CD toolchains, ensuring:

- Automated validation, linting, and security scanning of IaC;  
- Controlled promotion through non-production to production environments;  
- Audit trails aligned with the security obligations in **Article XI** of the Parent SOW.

(c) **Advanced Observability.**  
Design and implement a comprehensive observability stack that complements the “logging, monitoring, SIEM integration” mentioned in **Section 2.2.1(c)** of the Parent SOW, including:

- Distributed tracing across core business applications;  
- Application and infrastructure performance dashboards;  
- Cost and performance analytics dashboards for hybrid cloud resources;  
- Integration with Vendor’s 24x7 monitoring under **Section 2.2.3(a)** of the Parent SOW.

2.6 **Expanded Managed Services.**  

In addition to the Phase 3 managed services described in **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall provide:

(a) **Enhanced Incident Management.**  
- Dedicated senior incident manager for Priority 1 incidents as defined in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent SOW;  
- Post-incident root cause analysis (RCA) reports for all Priority 1 and Priority 2 incidents, with preventive actions tracked to closure.

(b) **Proactive Capacity and Cost Optimization.**  
- Monthly finops review meetings (an increase on the quarterly reviews referenced in **Section 2.2.3(e)** of the Parent SOW);  
- Implementation of agreed cost optimization recommendations within defined timelines;  
- Quarterly “optimization scorecards”.

(c) **Extended Support Coverage.**  
- Support for an expanded set of application components as agreed in a services catalog;  
- Additional language and region coverage, where feasible, consistent with the **Territory** defined in **Section 1.1.20** of the Parent SOW.

2.7 **Strategic Roadmap and Governance Enhancements.**  

Vendor shall provide ongoing strategic guidance and governance, including:

(a) **Strategic Roadmap.**  
Within the first six (6) months of the WO Term, Vendor shall deliver an initial **Strategic Roadmap** outlining recommended initiatives across infrastructure, security, automation, and compliance for the duration of the WO Term.

(b) **Quarterly Governance Forums.**  
In addition to the “monthly service reviews” referenced in **Section 2.2.3(d)** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall participate in **quarterly executive governance forums** covering:

- Progress against the Strategic Roadmap;  
- Risk register review and mitigation strategies;  
- Alignment with Client’s business and regulatory environment.

(c) **Enhanced Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Additional Services in accordance with the general Performance Standards set forth in **Section 2.4** of the Parent SOW and the WO SLA Enhancements described in **Article XII** of this Work Order.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities; Assumptions and Dependencies.**  

The Client responsibilities in **Section 2.5** and the assumptions and dependencies in **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW apply equally to this Work Order. Without limitation, the Parties acknowledge that:

(a) Timely provision of access, decisions, and approvals by Client shall be critical;  
(b) Availability and suitability of third-party tools (e.g., SIEM, CI/CD platforms, observability tooling) are Client responsibilities unless expressly stated otherwise;  
(c) Any material deviation from assumptions or dependencies impacting cost or schedule shall be addressed via a WO Change Request in accordance with **Article XIII** hereof and **Article XIII** of the Parent SOW.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **WO Contract Value.**  

The total contract value for the Additional Services and Enhanced Deliverables under this Work Order shall be **US$4,609,604** (the “**WO Contract Value**”), exclusive of any applicable taxes, and in addition to the **Contract Value of US$4,977,036** specified in **Section 3.1** of the Parent SOW.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

The WO Contract Value is comprised of the following components:

3.2.1 **Project-Based Fees (Security, Automation, Observability).**  
A fixed-fee component totaling **US$2,100,000**, covering:

(a) Advanced Security Services (Section 2.4): **US$950,000**;  
(b) Automation and Observability Services (Section 2.5): **US$1,150,000**.

These amounts shall be invoiced based on the following milestones:

- **20%** upon WO Effective Date;  
- **40%** upon completion and Client sign-off (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed) of the core design deliverables for security and automation;  
- **30%** upon completion of implementation of at least 75% of the agreed scope;  
- **10%** upon final sign-off of project deliverables and documentation.

3.2.2 **Expanded Managed Services Fees.**  

A recurring fees component totaling **US$2,509,604** over the WO Term for Expanded Managed Services described in **Section 2.6** and governance described in **Section 2.7**, payable in equal monthly installments.

For avoidance of doubt, Expanded Managed Services fees under this Work Order are **in addition to** the Phase 3 managed services fees of **US$2,350,036** described in **Section 3.2.3** of the Parent SOW.

(a) The monthly fee for Expanded Managed Services shall be **US$45,600**, commencing on **1 July 2026** or the date on which the Parties mutually agree that the Extended Managed Services go live, whichever is earlier, and continuing through the WO Expiration Date.  
(b) In the final month of the WO Term, the monthly amount may be pro-rated based on the actual days of service.

3.3 **Expenses.**  

Consistent with **Section 3.3** of the Parent SOW, Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and living expenses incurred in the performance of the Additional Services. Vendor shall provide appropriate receipts and documentation. Any deviation from Client’s travel and expense policy must be pre-approved in writing.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  

3.4.1 **Invoices.**  

Vendor shall issue invoices in accordance with the milestone and recurring schedules in **Sections 3.2.1 and 3.2.2** of this Work Order. Invoices shall reference both **WO-EPS-202503-065** and **SOW-EPS-202503-045**.

3.4.2 **Payment Deadline (Net 30).**  

Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) calendar days** of the invoice date (“**Net 30**”), consistent with **Section 3.4.2** of the Parent SOW.

3.4.3 **Disputed Amounts.**  

Disputed amounts shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.4.3** of the Parent SOW. Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions while the Parties work in good faith to resolve any disputes.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  

Any undisputed amount not paid by the due date shall accrue interest at the rate specified in **Section 3.5** of the Parent SOW (1.5% per month or the maximum allowed by Applicable Law). Vendor may exercise its suspension rights under **Section 3.5** of the Parent SOW with respect to the Additional Services and Expanded Managed Services if undisputed amounts remain unpaid.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.6** of the Parent SOW. The WO Contract Value is exclusive of all applicable taxes which shall be borne by Client.

3.7 **No Set-Off.**  

Client’s rights and restrictions regarding set-off, withholding, or deduction shall be as provided in **Section 3.7** of the Parent SOW, and shall apply equally to this Work Order.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **WO Term.**  

This Work Order shall commence on the **WO Effective Date** and, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent SOW, shall continue in full force and effect until **23 February 2030** (the “**WO Expiration Date**”).

4.2 **Relationship to Parent SOW Term.**  

This Work Order is executed under and subject to the Parent SOW, which has a Term ending on **10 July 2029** as specified in **Section 4.1** of the Parent SOW. If the Parent SOW is renewed, extended, or otherwise continued pursuant to **Section 4.2** thereof, this Work Order shall continue until the WO Expiration Date, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties in writing. If the Parent SOW is not renewed and expires on its Expiration Date, the Parties shall agree in writing whether this Work Order continues as a standalone agreement under the same terms and conditions as the Parent SOW, except as modified herein.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

Consistent with **Section 4.3** of the Parent SOW, either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience, in whole or in part, upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. Termination of the Parent SOW for convenience shall, unless expressly agreed otherwise by the Parties in writing, be deemed to constitute termination for convenience of this Work Order as of the same effective date.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Work Order or the Parent SOW with respect to the Additional Services and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days of receiving written notice describing the breach, consistent with **Section 4.4.1** of the Parent SOW.

4.4.2 The insolvency-related termination rights in **Section 4.4.2** of the Parent SOW shall apply to this Work Order.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order:

(a) Client shall pay Vendor for (i) all Additional Services properly performed and Enhanced Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, (ii) all incurred but unpaid Expanded Managed Services fees through the effective date of termination, and (iii) any committed or non-cancellable costs, in line with **Section 4.5(a)** of the Parent SOW.

(b) Vendor shall provide **Transition Assistance Services** as reasonably requested by Client for up to ninety (90) days following termination, subject to mutually agreed fees and a written transition plan, consistent with **Section 4.5(b)** of the Parent SOW.

(c) Rights and licenses granted under **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)** of this Work Order shall terminate or survive in the same manner and to the same extent as described in **Section 4.5(c)** and **Article VI** of the Parent SOW.

(d) Termination of this Work Order shall be without prejudice to accrued rights and obligations, and survival of relevant provisions shall be in accordance with **Section 4.6** of the Parent SOW and **Section 16.9** of this Work Order.

4.6 **Termination of Parent SOW.**  

If the Parent SOW **SOW-EPS-202503-045** is terminated for any reason:

(a) This Work Order shall automatically terminate as of the Parent SOW’s effective termination date, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing that this Work Order shall survive as a standalone agreement;  
(b) The provisions of **Section 4.5** above shall apply; and  
(c) Any survival of terms will mirror the survival concepts in **Section 4.6** of the Parent SOW.
