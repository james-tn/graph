## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 Relationship to Parent Agreement

2.1.1 This Amendment **supplements and, where expressly indicated, modifies** the scope of Services described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly modified herein, the description of Services, Deliverables, and performance standards in Article II of the Parent Agreement shall remain in full force and effect and shall apply equally to the Additional Services.

2.1.2 Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1.1, the Parties confirm that Vendor shall continue to perform:  
(a) cloud-based data hosting and storage, as described in Section 2.1.2 of the Parent Agreement;  
(b) data ingestion and integration as described in Section 2.1.3;  
(c) analytics and reporting as described in Section 2.1.4;  
(d) professional services and configuration as described in Section 2.1.5; and  
(e) support and maintenance as described in Section 2.1.6 of the Parent Agreement, in each case as extended and enhanced under this Amendment.

### 2.2 Additional Services and Enhancements

2.2.1 **Enhanced Hosting and Segregation.**  
In addition to the obligations in Section 2.1.2 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall:

(a) Provide a **logically segregated virtual private cloud (VPC)** environment for Client within the Vendor’s hosting infrastructure, configured with dedicated network security groups and access control lists tailored to Client’s risk profile (which the Parties classify as **“Medium Risk”** for purposes of this Amendment);

(b) Support **multi-region hosting** within the United States and, subject to Section 11.4 of this Amendment (International Transfers), certain Expanded Territories (e.g., EU/EEA and UK regions), ensuring that Personal Data subject to GDPR is stored and Processed in compliance with Section 11.5 of the Parent Agreement;

(c) Implement **enhanced encryption** measures, such that:  
   - all Client Data at rest is encrypted using industry-standard algorithms (e.g., AES-256),  
   - all network communications are secured using TLS 1.2 or higher, and  
   - encryption keys are managed using a hardened key management service with role-based access controls.

2.2.2 **Advanced Data Ingestion and Quality Management.**  
Building upon Section 2.1.3 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall:

(a) Implement **automated data quality rules**, including validation of field formats, referential integrity checks, and anomaly detection thresholds, with configurable exception handling workflows;

(b) Provide **near real-time ingestion** capabilities for designated data sources, using event-driven architectures, with processing latency targets not to exceed fifteen (15) minutes for 95% of ingestion events under normal operating conditions;

(c) Support **data lineage tracking** for key Client Data sets, including a record of source systems, transformation rules, and timestamps for major Processing steps, in order to support Client’s compliance with Privacy Laws and audit obligations;

(d) Provide Client with **self-service tools or APIs** enabling Client to submit schema changes, mappings, and ingestion configurations, subject to Vendor’s reasonable review and approval, and consistent with Vendor’s platform limitations.

2.2.3 **Advanced Analytics, Reporting, and Privacy Controls.**  
In addition to Section 2.1.4 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall:

(a) Provide **role-based access control (RBAC)** down to the field and metric level, allowing Client to restrict viewing of certain Personal Data or sensitive fields to designated user roles, and to configure differential access based on jurisdictions and business units;

(b) Support **pseudonymization** and **tokenization** of selected Personal Data fields in analytics views and exports, as reasonably requested by Client, to align with data minimization and privacy-by-design principles under GDPR;

(c) Deliver a set of **privacy and compliance dashboards** as Deliverables under Section 2.2 of the Parent Agreement, providing metrics such as Data Subject access requests processed, Data Breach incident counts, data retention status by dataset, and Sub-processor usage (using the Sub-processor List);

(d) Enable **audit logging** of user access, data exports, configuration changes, and administrative actions related to Client Data, with retention of such logs for at least twelve (12) months, or longer if required by Privacy Laws or Client’s documented instructions.

2.2.4 **Professional Services – Regulatory Readiness.**  
In addition to Section 2.1.5 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide professional services to support Client’s regulatory and compliance initiatives, including:

(a) Assisting Client in mapping data flows to support data protection impact assessments (DPIAs) required under GDPR and similar laws, subject to Client’s responsibility for conducting and documenting such assessments;

(b) Configuring and, as mutually agreed, customizing workflows for handling Data Subject requests (e.g., access, erasure, restriction), consistent with Section 11.4 of the Parent Agreement and Section 11.3 of this Amendment;

(c) Providing up to **ten (10)** hours per calendar quarter of consultative sessions with Vendor’s privacy and security subject matter experts (SMEs) to review regulatory changes, platform capabilities, and recommended configurations.

2.2.5 **Support and Maintenance Enhancements.**  
The support obligations in Section 2.1.6 of the Parent Agreement are supplemented as follows:

(a) Vendor shall provide an **enhanced support window** for Severity 1 (Critical) incidents, extending coverage to **24x7** for initial incident triage and acknowledgement, with the response and resolution targets as set forth in Article XII of this Amendment;

(b) Vendor shall designate a **named technical account manager (TAM)** for Client, responsible for coordinating escalations, periodic service reviews, and SLA reporting;

(c) Vendor shall provide Client with quarterly **service performance and security posture reports**, including SLA compliance details and a summary of any Security Incidents (as defined in the Parent Agreement) related to Client Data.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 Vendor reaffirms the performance standards set out in Section 2.3 of the Parent Agreement and agrees that all Additional Services shall be performed:

(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  

(b) in compliance with Privacy Laws and applicable regulations, as updated from time to time; and  

(c) in accordance with the Enhanced SLA described in Article XII of this Amendment.

2.3.2 The Parties acknowledge that the **risk level** for the engagement as updated under this Amendment is **classified as “Medium”**, and the scope, security measures, and SLA commitments set forth herein reflect such risk classification.

### 2.4 Client Responsibilities

2.4.1 The Client responsibilities set forth in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect and apply to the Additional Services.

2.4.2 In addition, Client shall:

(a) Provide Vendor with timely and accurate information regarding applicable Privacy Laws, regulatory obligations, and Client’s internal policies that may materially impact the configuration of the Services;

(b) Review and promptly either approve or request changes to configuration proposals, data mappings, and role-based access models provided by Vendor, within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, to avoid delays in implementation;

(c) Maintain accurate records of instructions provided to Vendor concerning Processing of Personal Data, including jurisdiction-specific requirements, retention periods, and data subject rights.

Vendor may provide general information about Privacy Laws and industry practices, but Client remains solely responsible for obtaining legal advice concerning Client’s own compliance obligations.

## ARTICLE III. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value and Fee Structure

3.1.1 **Contract Value under this Amendment.**  
The total contract value associated with the Additional Services and enhancements described in this Amendment for the Amendment Term is **US$1,896,631** (the “**Amendment Contract Value**”), which is **in addition to** the contract value of **US$1,604,146** specified in Section 3.1.1 of the Parent Agreement, subject to adjustments under this Article III and Article XIII (Change Management) of both the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

3.1.2 **Fee Components.**  
The Fee components described in Section 3.1.2 of the Parent Agreement remain applicable and are expanded for this Amendment as follows:

(a) **Subscription Fees – Enhanced Platform:** Additional recurring fees for access to the enhanced hosting, security, and analytics capabilities described in Article II, based on Usage Metrics (e.g., number of active users, data storage capacity, data transfer volume, and analytical workload units);  

(b) **Implementation and Onboarding Fees:** One-time fees for implementation of Enhanced Security Measures, configuration of multi-region hosting, and deployment of advanced analytics and privacy dashboards;  

(c) **Professional Services Fees – Regulatory and Customization:** Fees for professional services related to regulatory readiness, custom workflows, and Data Subject request process configuration, generally on a time-and-materials basis at Vendor’s then-current rates, as agreed in writing; and  

(d) **Other Charges:** Any third-party costs (e.g., additional cloud infrastructure costs, data residency surcharges, specialized security tooling) expressly approved in advance by Client.

### 3.2 Invoicing and Payment

3.2.1 Section 3.2 of the Parent Agreement (Invoicing and Payment) shall continue to apply, except as modified below.

3.2.2 **Invoicing Schedule under this Amendment.**  
Unless otherwise set forth in a statement of work or order form executed under the Parent Agreement and referencing this Amendment:

(a) **Subscription Fees – Enhanced Platform:** Invoiced **annually in advance** for each 12-month period during the Amendment Term, based on projected Usage Metrics agreed by the Parties, with a true-up mechanism as described in Section 3.2.4 below;

(b) **Implementation Fees:** Invoiced **fifty percent (50%)** upon the Amendment Effective Date and **fifty percent (50%)** upon completion of the major implementation milestones set forth in an agreed implementation plan, which shall be deemed a Change Order under Article XIII of the Parent Agreement;

(c) **Professional Services Fees:** Invoiced **monthly in arrears**, based on actual hours worked and authorized expenses incurred by Vendor, with itemized time entries and descriptions provided.

3.2.3 **Payment Terms.**  
Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the invoice date (“**Net 45**”), consistent with Section 3.2.2 of the Parent Agreement. Payment shall be made by electronic funds transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed methods.

3.2.4 **Usage True-Up.**  
On a semi-annual basis during the Amendment Term, the Parties shall review actual Usage Metrics and compare them against the projected Usage Metrics used for Subscription Fee calculations. If actual usage exceeds the projected usage tier, Vendor may invoice Client for the incremental Subscription Fees associated with the higher tier for the applicable period, subject to Client’s right to review and validate the usage data.

3.2.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
Client’s right to dispute invoice amounts under Section 3.2.3 of the Parent Agreement applies equally to invoices issued under this Amendment. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such disputes.

### 3.3 Late Payment; Suspension

3.3.1 The late payment charges and suspension rights set forth in Section 3.3 of the Parent Agreement apply fully to Fees under this Amendment. Vendor’s right to suspend Additional Services shall be exercised in a manner that minimizes disruption, in particular to security-related services, and Vendor shall provide prior notice consistent with Section 3.3.2 of the Parent Agreement.

### 3.4 Taxes

Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement (Taxes) shall apply to all Fees under this Amendment.

### 3.5 Expenses

Section 3.5 of the Parent Agreement (Expenses) shall apply to travel and other expenses associated with on-site professional services necessary for the implementation and support of the Additional Services.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Amendment Term

4.1.1 This Amendment shall commence on the **Amendment Effective Date** and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until **October 20, 2027** (the “**Amendment Term**”).

4.1.2 For the avoidance of doubt, the Term of the Parent Agreement (as defined in Section 4.1 of the Parent Agreement) remains as stated therein (Initial Term through January 06, 2027, with potential Renewal Terms). If the Parent Agreement is renewed, this Amendment may also be renewed by mutual written agreement of the Parties, which may include a written extension, renewal order, or Change Order explicitly referencing this Amendment.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 The termination rights in Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement are hereby supplemented as follows:

(a) Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor;  

(b) Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client; and  

(c) Termination of this Amendment for convenience shall not, by itself, terminate the Parent Agreement unless expressly stated in the notice of termination and accepted in writing by the other Party.

4.2.2 Upon any termination for convenience, Client shall remain obligated to pay all accrued but unpaid Fees for Additional Services performed and accepted prior to the effective date of termination, consistent with Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 The termination for cause provisions in Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Amendment. In particular, either Party may terminate this Amendment upon written notice for the other Party’s material breach of this Amendment, subject to a thirty (30) day cure period, or upon insolvency events described in Section 4.3.2 of the Parent Agreement.

4.3.2 For purposes of this Amendment, **Vendor’s material breach of Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** or the Enhanced SLA under Article XII may constitute grounds for termination of this Amendment and may, depending on severity and scope, constitute grounds for termination of the Parent Agreement, subject to the cure provisions of Section 4.3.1.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement

4.4.1 This Amendment is **dependent upon** the continued existence of the Parent Agreement. If the Parent Agreement is terminated or expires for any reason, this Amendment shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to extend certain provisions (such as data return, deletion, and confidentiality) beyond such date.

4.4.2 Upon expiration or termination of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, Vendor’s obligations regarding payment, return or deletion of Client Data (per Section 4.4.2 of the Parent Agreement), and transition assistance (per Section 4.4.3) shall apply to the Additional Services.

### 4.5 Transition Assistance

4.5.1 During the **Transition Period** described in Section 4.4.3 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide reasonable assistance specific to the Additional Services, including:

(a) exporting configuration settings, role-based access models, and data quality rules;  

(b) coordinating data migration from the Expanded Territories to Client or a third-party provider designated by Client, in a manner consistent with Privacy Laws; and  

(c) providing documentation reasonably necessary for Client to understand and, where applicable, replicate the solution.

4.5.2 Fees for transition assistance shall be as set forth in Section 4.4.3 of the Parent Agreement (time-and-materials unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach).
