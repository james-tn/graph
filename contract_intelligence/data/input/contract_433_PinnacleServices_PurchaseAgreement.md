## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Pinnacle shall provide to Contoso comprehensive professional and managed services, including but not limited to: (a) technology implementation and systems integration; (b) data migration and transformation; (c) configuration of certain software systems and related infrastructure; (d) ongoing support and maintenance services; and (e) associated project management, training, and documentation (collectively, the “**Services**”). The Services shall be provided in accordance with this **Article II**, the applicable SOW(s), and the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

2.2 **Specific Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.1**, Pinnacle shall develop and deliver to Contoso one or more of the following Deliverables, as specified in the applicable SOW:

  (a) **System Integration Blueprints.** Detailed technical design documents, including architectural diagrams, data flow maps, integration specifications, and interface control documents covering the integration of Contoso’s legacy systems with new software platforms identified by Contoso.

  (b) **Configuration and Customization Packages.** Configuration scripts, parameter settings, business rules, and related artifacts required to tailor the implemented systems to Contoso’s business processes.

  (c) **Data Migration Tooling and Scripts.** Data extraction, transformation, and loading (ETL) scripts, mapping specifications, and reconciliation reports required to migrate data from Contoso’s legacy systems to the target environment.

  (d) **Operational Runbooks and Procedures.** Documentation of system operating procedures, incident response playbooks, escalation guidelines, and standard operating procedures for ongoing support.

  (e) **Training Materials and Sessions.** Training manuals, user guides, and delivery of remote or on-site training sessions for Contoso’s personnel, as specified in the SOW.

  (f) **Support and Maintenance Services.** Ongoing support services, incident management, and problem resolution, including defined response and resolution times as set forth in **Article XII** and the applicable SOW.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Pinnacle shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications;

  (b) in accordance with (i) the Project Plan and milestones; (ii) the Service Levels; and (iii) generally accepted industry standards for similar services;

  (c) in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and ordinances, including applicable privacy and data protection laws as further described in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**;

  (d) in a manner that reasonably cooperates with Contoso’s internal teams and other third-party vendors, as directed by Contoso.

2.4 **Project Plan and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall mutually agree upon a detailed Project Plan, which may be set forth in the relevant SOW or attached as an exhibit thereto. The Project Plan shall identify key milestones, acceptance checkpoints, and dependencies. Each milestone shall be associated with specific Deliverables and may be linked to payment events in accordance with **Article III**. Pinnacle shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet all milestone dates and shall promptly notify Contoso in writing if Pinnacle becomes aware of any circumstances likely to cause a material delay, including but not limited to delays attributable to Contoso’s failure to provide required information, resources, or decisions.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Pinnacle’s performance of the Services is dependent upon Contoso’s timely performance of its responsibilities, including:

  (a) providing Pinnacle with timely access to relevant systems, facilities, documentation, and personnel;

  (b) designating a project manager or primary point of contact with decision-making authority or appropriate escalation paths;

  (c) providing timely review, feedback, and approvals of Deliverables and other submissions from Pinnacle;

  (d) ensuring the accuracy and completeness of Client Data provided to Pinnacle and promptly notifying Pinnacle of any errors or discrepancies; and

  (e) providing or procuring any necessary licenses, consents, or authorizations for Pinnacle’s use of Contoso’s third-party systems or software in connection with the Services.

Any failure by Contoso to fulfill its responsibilities under this **Section 2.5** may result in delays or additional costs, and Pinnacle shall be entitled to relief in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Each SOW shall define acceptance criteria and procedures for the Deliverables. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW:

  (a) Pinnacle shall notify Contoso in writing when a Deliverable is ready for acceptance testing (“**Delivery Notice**”).

  (b) Contoso shall have a period of fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt of the Delivery Notice (the “**Acceptance Period**”) to test and evaluate the Deliverable.

  (c) If the Deliverable materially conforms to the acceptance criteria, Contoso shall promptly issue a written acceptance notice.

  (d) If the Deliverable fails to conform in any material respect to the acceptance criteria, Contoso shall provide a written rejection notice describing the deficiencies in reasonable detail. Pinnacle shall thereafter promptly correct such deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance testing. The Parties shall repeat this process in good faith until the Deliverable is accepted.

  (e) If Contoso neither accepts nor rejects a Deliverable in writing within the Acceptance Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the expiration of the Acceptance Period, except in cases of Contoso’s documented good-faith efforts to test where Pinnacle’s access limitations or failures prevent testing.

2.7 **Subcontractors.**  
Pinnacle may, in its discretion, engage Subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that:

  (a) Pinnacle remains fully responsible and liable for the acts and omissions of its Subcontractors;

  (b) any Subcontractor with access to Confidential Information or Personal Data shall be bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth in **Articles V** and **XI**; and

  (c) upon Contoso’s reasonable written request, Pinnacle shall identify its Subcontractors involved in performing material portions of the Services.

2.8 **Risk Profile.**  
The Parties acknowledge and agree that the project governed by this Agreement is characterized as **High Risk**. Accordingly, the Parties have negotiated and agreed to certain terms that are more favorable to Pinnacle, including, without limitation, long termination notice periods for convenience, broad indemnification obligations on the part of Contoso, and certain restrictive intellectual property terms, all as further set forth in **Articles IV, VI, VIII, and IX**.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
Subject to the provisions of this Agreement and any applicable SOW(s), Contoso agrees to pay Pinnacle an aggregate amount of **US$1,734,947** (the “**Contract Value**”) for the performance of the initial scope of Services and provision of the Deliverables contemplated as of the Effective Date. The Contract Value may be adjusted through Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII**.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Fees shall be structured as follows:

  (a) **Fixed-Fee Components.** Certain defined Deliverables and milestones shall be billed on a fixed-fee basis, as set forth in the relevant SOW.

  (b) **Time-and-Materials Components.** Additional services (e.g., out-of-scope enhancements, advisory services, or extended support) may be provided on a time-and-materials basis at the hourly or daily rates specified in the SOW or Pinnacle’s then-current rate card, as agreed by the Parties.

  (c) **Support and Maintenance Fees.** Recurring support and maintenance services, if applicable, shall be billed monthly or annually in advance in accordance with the SOW.

3.3 **Sample Milestone-Based Payment Schedule.**  
For illustrative purposes only (actual percentages and milestones to be defined in SOW):

  (a) Ten percent (10%) of the Contract Value payable upon execution of this Agreement and issuance of an initial invoice (mobilization fee).

  (b) Twenty-five percent (25%) upon completion and acceptance of the Systems Integration Blueprint Deliverables.

  (c) Thirty percent (30%) upon completion and acceptance of configuration and customization Deliverables in the staging environment.

  (d) Twenty percent (20%) upon successful data migration and user acceptance testing in the production environment.

  (e) Fifteen percent (15%) upon completion of training, knowledge transfer, and commencement of steady-state support.

3.4 **Invoices; Payment Terms.**  
Pinnacle shall invoice Contoso for amounts due under this Agreement in accordance with the payment schedule and billing frequency specified in the applicable SOW. Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the invoice date (“**Net 30**”). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer or other method reasonably designated by Pinnacle.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, it shall provide written notice to Pinnacle within fifteen (15) days of the invoice date reasonably describing the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall timely pay the undisputed portion of such invoice. The Parties shall use good-faith efforts to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days. Any amounts determined to be payable following resolution shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of such resolution.

3.6 **Late Payment; Interest.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the date due until paid in full. Pinnacle may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice to Contoso, suspend performance of the Services or withhold Deliverables if any undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than forty-five (45) days after the due date.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of all applicable taxes, levies, and duties, including sales, use, value-added, and similar taxes, other than taxes based on Pinnacle’s income, net worth, or employment obligations. Contoso shall be responsible for all such taxes payable in connection with the Services or Deliverables, and Pinnacle shall itemize such taxes on the relevant invoice to the extent legally required.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Contoso shall reimburse Pinnacle for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current corporate travel and expense policy, if provided to Pinnacle in writing. Pinnacle shall provide appropriate documentation for all reimbursable expenses upon request.

3.9 **No Withholding.**  
Unless otherwise required by applicable law, all payments by Contoso shall be made without set-off, counterclaim, or any deduction or withholding. If any tax withholding is required by law, Contoso shall gross up the payment such that Pinnacle receives the full amount it would have received absent such withholding.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until **October 06, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Agreement may be extended beyond the Expiration Date only by mutual written agreement of the Parties. Any renewal shall specify the renewed term, applicable Fees, and any changes to the scope of Services.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Contoso may, at its discretion, terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing Pinnacle with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**. Pinnacle may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience only upon mutual written agreement with Contoso.

Upon termination for convenience by Contoso:

  (a) Contoso shall pay Pinnacle for all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, including any accrued but unpaid Fees and approved expenses;

  (b) Contoso shall also pay any reasonable and documented wind-down costs incurred by Pinnacle that are directly attributable to such termination, including but not limited to non-cancellable commitments to Subcontractors and third-party providers, to the extent such commitments were reasonably incurred in reliance on this Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  (a) **By Either Party.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice of such breach (the “**Cure Period**”). If the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, but the breaching Party has commenced and is diligently pursuing cure, the Cure Period may be extended by an additional thirty (30) days at the non-breaching Party’s reasonable discretion.

  (b) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent; (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (iv) ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) Contoso shall promptly pay all Fees and reimbursable expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, including any amounts due under **Section 4.3** for termination for convenience;

  (b) Pinnacle shall, subject to **Article III** and **Article VI**, deliver to Contoso any completed Deliverables and any work-in-progress for which Contoso has fully paid and, if requested by Contoso and subject to mutual agreement on Fees, provide reasonable transition assistance for a period not to exceed ninety (90) days;

  (c) Each Party shall promptly return or certify destruction of the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**;

  (d) Any rights or obligations that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**.

4.6 **Suspension.**  
In addition to its termination rights, Pinnacle may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ written notice, suspend the performance of Services in the event of: (a) Contoso’s material breach of its payment obligations under **Article III** that remains uncured beyond the applicable Cure Period; or (b) Contoso’s material breach of its data protection or security obligations that materially and adversely affects Pinnacle’s ability to perform the Services safely or in compliance with law.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public or proprietary information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), whether orally or in writing, and whether before or after the Effective Date, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business plans, financial information, pricing, trade secrets, technical data, product roadmaps, software, source code, system architecture, security information, Client Data, Personal Data, and the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

5.2 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) is or becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of a disclosure by the Receiving Party in violation of this Agreement;

  (b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction as to confidentiality prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

  (c) is received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services and fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement;

  (b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent;

  (c) protect the confidentiality of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable care;

  (d) limit access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, officers, directors, Affiliates, Subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those contained herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permissible) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. The Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to preserve the confidentiality of the remaining Confidential Information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or, at the Disclosing Party’s option, destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) one archival copy solely for legal and compliance purposes; and (b) Confidential Information stored in routine backup or archival systems that is not readily accessible, provided that any such retained Confidential Information remains subject to the confidentiality obligations herein until destroyed in the ordinary course.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**, except with respect to trade secrets, Client Data, and Personal Data, which shall remain subject to confidentiality obligations for so long as such information constitutes a trade secret or is subject to applicable privacy and data protection laws.
