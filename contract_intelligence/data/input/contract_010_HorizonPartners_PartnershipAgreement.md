## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
During the Term, Horizon shall provide to Contoso the Services described herein and in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. The Services are expected to include, without limitation:  
(a) strategic consulting and advisory services related to [insert subject matter, e.g., digital transformation, systems integration, or process optimization];  
(b) design, configuration, and implementation services for specified systems, platforms, or solutions identified by Contoso;  
(c) project management, coordination, and reporting services;  
(d) training and knowledge transfer for Contoso’s designated personnel;  
(e) ongoing support and maintenance services, where applicable; and  
(f) such other services as the Parties may agree in writing from time to time.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) Each SOW shall, at a minimum, reference this Agreement, describe the specific Services and Deliverables, set forth the applicable Service Levels (if any), identify key milestones, specify the applicable Fees and payment schedule, and list assumptions and dependencies.  
(b) In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement and is signed by duly authorized representatives of both Parties.

2.3 **Specific Deliverables and Milestones.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Horizon anticipates delivering, subject to further detail in each SOW, the following categories of Deliverables and milestones:

(a) **Assessment and Planning Deliverables.**  
   - A current-state assessment report describing existing processes, systems, and organizational capabilities relevant to the project.  
   - A gap analysis, identifying differences between Contoso’s current state and desired future state.  
   - A detailed project plan, including timelines, resource requirements, key dependencies, and risk mitigation strategies.  
   - Requirements documentation reflecting jointly agreed functional and non-functional requirements.

(b) **Design and Implementation Deliverables.**  
   - Solution design documents (e.g., architecture diagrams, configuration workbooks, data models, integration specifications).  
   - Configured environments (development, test, and production, as applicable) reflecting the approved design.  
   - Migration scripts, integration components, and associated technical documentation.  
   - Test plans, test cases, and test results, including user acceptance testing (UAT) documentation.

(c) **Training and Knowledge Transfer Deliverables.**  
   - Training materials, including user guides, quick reference guides, and presentation decks.  
   - Conducting training sessions, workshops, or webinars for Contoso personnel, as specified in the SOW.  
   - Knowledge transfer sessions to designated Contoso personnel covering administration, configuration, and operation of the delivered solution.

(d) **Support and Maintenance Deliverables (if applicable).**  
   - Ongoing incident management, including ticket intake, triage, and resolution.  
   - Minor enhancements and configuration changes within agreed limits.  
   - Periodic performance and utilization reports.  
   - Regular status meetings, including documented minutes and action items.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Horizon shall perform the Services:  
(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services;  
(b) by personnel who are appropriately trained, skilled, and experienced for the tasks assigned;  
(c) in accordance with the project plan and milestones set forth in the applicable SOW;  
(d) in compliance with all Applicable Laws and with Contoso’s reasonable written policies and procedures that have been provided to Horizon in advance; and  
(e) in a manner that reasonably seeks to minimize any material disruption to Contoso’s operations.

2.5 **Cooperation and Dependencies.**  
(a) Contoso shall provide Horizon with reasonable cooperation, access to relevant personnel, systems, and facilities, and timely decisions and approvals as may be reasonably required for Horizon to perform the Services.  
(b) Horizon’s performance may depend on the accuracy and completeness of information and instructions provided by Contoso, as well as on the timely performance of Contoso’s responsibilities.  
(c) Horizon shall promptly notify Contoso in writing if it becomes aware of any material issue, dependency, or delay that may adversely affect the timeline, cost, or quality of the Services, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such issues.

2.6 **Project Governance.**  
(a) The Parties shall each appoint a project manager who will serve as the primary point of contact for day-to-day communications regarding the Services.  
(b) The project managers shall meet (in person, by telephone, or via videoconference) at regular intervals, as specified in the SOW, to review progress, discuss issues and risks, and agree on any necessary corrective actions.  
(c) Material issues that cannot be resolved at the project manager level shall be escalated in accordance with the dispute resolution and escalation procedures set forth in Article XIV.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
(a) Each SOW may specify acceptance criteria and acceptance procedures for the relevant Deliverables. In the absence of specific criteria, Deliverables will be deemed accepted when they materially conform to the applicable specifications and requirements set forth in the SOW.  
(b) Upon delivery of a Deliverable, Contoso shall have the period specified in the SOW (or, if none is specified, fifteen (15) Business Days) to review and either accept or reject the Deliverable by providing written notice of any material non-conformities.  
(c) If Contoso timely rejects a Deliverable, Horizon shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified material non-conformities and re-deliver the Deliverable for further review and acceptance in accordance with this Section.  
(d) If Contoso fails to provide written notice of acceptance or rejection within the applicable review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the expiration of such period, provided that Horizon has expressly notified Contoso in writing that the Deliverable is submitted for acceptance and Contoso has had a reasonable opportunity to review it.

Horizon may not subcontract any material portion of the Services without Contoso’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Horizon shall remain fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if such acts or omissions were those of Horizon.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total not-to-exceed value of this Agreement, inclusive of all SOWs executed hereunder, shall be Three Hundred Eighty-Seven Thousand Three Hundred Forty-Three U.S. Dollars (US$387,343), unless otherwise agreed in a duly executed amendment.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees payable under this Agreement may include a combination of (a) fixed-price amounts for specified Deliverables or project phases, and (b) time and materials charges for additional Services, as detailed in the applicable SOW. Each SOW shall specify the fee structure applicable to the Services described therein.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise set forth in an SOW, the Fees shall be invoiced and payable according to the following general milestones:

(a) **Project Initiation Fee.** Ten percent (10%) of the total SOW value upon execution of the applicable SOW and commencement of the Services.  

(b) **Interim Milestones.** Forty percent (40%) of the total SOW value, invoiced in increments aligned with the completion of key milestones (e.g., completion of requirements, completion of design, completion of configuration), as identified in the SOW and upon Contoso’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the corresponding Deliverables.  

(c) **Final Acceptance.** Forty percent (40%) of the total SOW value upon final acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of all Deliverables under such SOW.  

(d) **Retainage (if applicable).** Ten percent (10%) of the total SOW value may be retained by Contoso, if explicitly provided in the SOW, and shall be payable upon completion of a ninety (90) day post-go-live stabilization period and resolution of any critical defects.

3.4 **Time and Materials Services.**  
For Services billed on a time and materials basis, the applicable SOW shall specify the hourly or daily rates, billing increments, and any caps or not-to-exceed amounts. Horizon shall maintain accurate and complete records of time spent and materials used in performing such Services and shall provide Contoso with detailed time sheets or other supporting documentation upon request.

3.5 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) Horizon shall invoice Contoso for all Fees due under this Agreement in accordance with the applicable SOW.  
(b) Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, all properly issued, undisputed invoices shall be due and payable within sixty (60) calendar days from the date of receipt by Contoso (“**Net 60**”).  
(c) Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed electronic payment method.

3.6 **Late Payments.**  
(a) If any undisputed amount is not paid when due, Horizon may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.  
(b) Horizon shall not suspend the Services for late payment unless (i) the underlying amount is undisputed, (ii) such amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment, and (iii) Horizon has engaged in good-faith discussions with Contoso to resolve the issue. Any suspension of Services under this Section shall be limited in scope and duration as reasonably necessary.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall notify Horizon in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.5. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute as promptly as reasonably practicable, and upon resolution, any amounts determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
(a) All Fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, goods and services, and other similar taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority (“**Taxes**”), except for taxes based on Horizon’s net income, property, or employees.  
(b) Contoso shall be responsible for any applicable Taxes arising from the Services or Deliverables, provided that Horizon shall (i) separately state any such Taxes on the invoices, and (ii) reasonably cooperate with Contoso to enable Contoso to claim exemptions or recover Taxes where permissible.  
(c) If Applicable Law requires Contoso to withhold any taxes from payments to Horizon, Contoso shall (i) withhold and remit such taxes to the appropriate authority, (ii) provide Horizon with appropriate documentation of such remittance, and (iii) use reasonable efforts to reduce such withholding if allowed by law or applicable tax treaty.

3.9 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Horizon shall be responsible for its own overhead and administrative expenses.  
(b) Contoso shall reimburse Horizon for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and related expenses actually incurred by Horizon in the performance of the Services, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policy, provided that Horizon submits appropriate receipts and supporting documentation.  
(c) Any single trip expense in excess of [insert threshold, e.g., US$2,000] shall require prior written approval by Contoso.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until July 31, 2026 (the “**Expiration Date**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be renewed for additional one (1) year periods upon mutual written agreement of the Parties, which agreement shall be executed at least thirty (30) days prior to the Expiration Date or the end of any renewal term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low-risk nature of this engagement, Contoso may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for convenience upon not less than thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Horizon. Horizon may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for convenience upon not less than sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Contoso.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof from the non-breaching Party describing the breach in reasonable detail.  
(b) Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent; (ii) files a petition in bankruptcy or has a petition filed against it that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (iv) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Horizon shall promptly discontinue all Services (except for any Services reasonably necessary to facilitate an orderly transition, as may be requested by Contoso and agreed in writing by the Parties) and deliver to Contoso any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which Contoso has paid or will pay in accordance with this Agreement;  
(b) Contoso shall pay Horizon for all Services properly performed and reimbursable expenses properly incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to any good-faith dispute;  
(c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5; and  
(d) the rights and obligations of the Parties under those provisions which by their nature are intended to survive (including, without limitation, Sections 3.6, 3.7, 4.5, 5.0–5.6, 6.0–6.5, 7.0–7.4, 8.0–8.4, 9.0–9.4, 10.0–10.3, 11.0–11.6, 14.0–14.5, 15.0–15.4, and 16.0–16.9) shall survive such expiration or termination.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
At Contoso’s request made prior to the effective date of termination or expiration, Horizon shall provide reasonable transition services to facilitate the orderly transfer of the Services to Contoso or a third party designated by Contoso. Such transition assistance shall be provided on a time and materials basis at the rates specified in the applicable SOW (or, if none, at Horizon’s then-current standard rates), subject to a mutually agreed cap, and shall not exceed ninety (90) days unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, strategies, financial information, pricing, customer or supplier information, trade secrets, technical information, software, source code, systems, methodologies, know-how, and all copies and derivatives of the foregoing. Confidential Information of Contoso includes Client Materials and Personal Data. Confidential Information of Horizon includes Horizon Pre-Existing IP, proprietary methodologies, and pricing.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;  
(b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent; and  
(c) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.3 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, subcontractors, and professional advisors (collectively, “**Representatives**”) who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein. The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Article V by its Representatives.

5.4 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was rightfully known to the Receiving Party, without restriction, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is received from a third party who lawfully acquired such information and is not under any confidentiality obligation with respect to such information; or  
(d) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.5 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permissible) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing and cooperate, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or other remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that its legal counsel advises is legally required to be disclosed and shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that confidential treatment is afforded to any such Confidential Information.

5.6 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request at any time, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except to the extent that the Receiving Party is required by law to retain such information or that such information is contained in automatic electronic backup systems. Any retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.7 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of five (5) years following the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, the obligations shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law or as required by Data Protection Laws, respectively.

12.6 **Exclusions.**

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure Event.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), governmental actions or orders, failure of third-party telecommunications or hosting providers (not caused by the affected Party), or other events of a similar nature.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event and its expected duration and impact;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  
(c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of developments.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are affected by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement or the affected SOW upon written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination (except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
