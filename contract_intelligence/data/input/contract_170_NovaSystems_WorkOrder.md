## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW and Consulting Services Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order is executed under and subject to the terms of the Parent SOW (SOW-NOV-202312-124 / contract_124) and the Consulting Services Agreement (CSA-NOV-202312-100 / contract_100). Pursuant to **Section 2.1.1 (Relationship to Parent Agreement)** of the Parent SOW and **Section 1.18 (Statement of Work)** of the Consulting Services Agreement, this Work Order describes particular additional Services, Deliverables, scope, schedule, Service Levels, and Fees that are part of the same overarching Project.

2.1.2 This Work Order **adds scope to the Statement of Work dated March 18, 2024 (Ref: SOW-NOV-202312-124, contract_124)** by extending the Post‑Implementation Support and Optimization activities described in **Section 2.3.4 (Phase 4 – Post‑Implementation Support and Optimization)** of the Parent SOW for an additional period and by introducing new Enhancement and Continuous Improvement streams not expressly included therein.

2.1.3 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the terms of this Work Order and the Parent SOW, the following order of precedence shall apply: (a) the express terms of this Work Order shall control with respect to the Services and Deliverables described herein (including, without limitation, **Payment Terms** in Article III and **Limitation of Liability** in Article IX of this Work Order); and (b) for all other matters, the terms of the Parent SOW shall control over the Consulting Services Agreement, consistent with **Section 16.13 (Order of Precedence)** of the Parent SOW.

2.2 **Overall Description of Work Order Scope.**  

2.2.1 **Extended Support Services.**  

Building on **Phase 4 – Post‑Implementation Support & Optimization** in **Section 2.3.4** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall provide Extended Support Services throughout the WO Term, including:

- Application and integration support for the ERP, CRM, and integrations implemented pursuant to **Sections 2.3.2 and 2.3.3** of the Parent SOW, aligned with the SLA framework in **Article XII** of the Parent SOW and **Article XII** of this Work Order.
- Incident management, including logging, triage, root‑cause analysis, and resolution for issues reported by Client, categorized according to Priority Levels defined in **Section 12.3** of the Parent SOW and Section 12.3 of this Work Order.
- Ongoing configuration tuning and minor enhancements (e.g., new lookup values, minor field additions, adjustments to non‑custom code) within an agreed monthly effort envelope included in the retainer‑style fee structure described in Section 3.2 below.
- Monitoring of critical integrations, batch jobs, and scheduled tasks to proactively identify and remediate failures or performance degradations.

2.2.2 **Enhancement Services.**  

Complementing the Deliverables in **Section 2.3.2 (Phase 2 – Configuration and Build)** and **Section 2.3.3 (Phase 3 – Data Migration, Testing, and Go‑Live)** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall perform Enhancement Services including:

- Design and development of up to **thirty (30)** additional custom reports and dashboards (beyond the twenty (20) referenced in Section 2.3.2 of the Parent SOW), focused on advanced analytics, performance KPIs, and cross‑functional dashboards.
- Implementation of up to **fifteen (15)** new or materially modified integrations with external systems, including third‑party logistics providers, banking interfaces, and customer portals, consistent with the integration approach described in **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent SOW.
- Delivery of up to **twenty (20)** workflow enhancements or new workflow automations to streamline approvals, exception handling, and notifications not previously covered in the Parent SOW.
- Periodic enhancement releases (no less than quarterly) following a standard release management process, including design, build, system testing, user acceptance testing, and controlled deployment.

2.2.3 **Continuous Improvement Services.**  

In addition to the optimization workshops and annual health check described in **Section 2.3.4 (Phase 4)** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall perform Continuous Improvement Services as follows:

- Conduct at least **two (2)** structured optimization workshops per calendar year during the WO Term, focused on process improvements, feature adoption, and roadmap alignment with Client’s strategic objectives.
- Provide an annual comprehensive “ERP/CRM Health and Value Realization Report” evaluating system usage, process cycle times, error rates, SLA performance, and recommending specific initiatives for the subsequent year.
- Deliver semi‑annual training refresh programs, including updated user guides and e‑learning modules, consistent with the training framework in **Section 2.4 (Training and Knowledge Transfer)** of the Parent SOW.
- Provide advisory support for aligning system capabilities with evolving regulatory and compliance requirements relevant to Client’s industry, in coordination with **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW.

2.3 **Specific Deliverables.**  

Without limiting the foregoing, Vendor shall provide at least the following Work Order Deliverables:

2.3.1 **Support and Operations Deliverables.**

- Monthly SLA and incident reports detailing incident volumes, priorities, root causes, resolution times, and SLA attainment, building on the reporting expectations in **Section 2.3.4(b)** and **Section 2.3.7** of the Parent SOW.
- Updated configuration documentation and operational runbooks reflecting changes implemented under this Work Order.
- A maintained issues and enhancement backlog, with prioritization agreed between the Client Project Manager and Vendor Project Manager.

2.3.2 **Enhancement Deliverables.**

- Functional and technical design documents for each High‑Priority Enhancement.
- Updated configuration workbooks, integration design documents, and deployment plans aligned with the methodology used in **Section 2.3.2(b)** of the Parent SOW.
- Test plans, test cases, and test summary reports for each enhancement release, consistent with the testing framework in **Section 2.3.3(b)** of the Parent SOW.

2.3.3 **Continuous Improvement Deliverables.**

- Workshop materials, process maps, and documented recommendations from each optimization workshop.
- The annual Health and Value Realization Report described in Section 2.2.3 above.
- Updated training materials and knowledge transfer documents as referenced in **Section 2.4** of the Parent SOW.

2.4 **Timelines and Performance Standards.**

2.4.1 **Commencement.** Extended Support Services shall commence upon the later of: (a) the WO Effective Date; or (b) the completion of the Production Support Period defined in **Section 1.19** of the Parent SOW. For clarity, this Work Order is intended to seamlessly follow the completion of **Milestone 4 – Completion of Post‑Implementation Support Period** referenced in **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW.

2.4.2 **Enhancement Prioritization and Release Cadence.** Client shall propose and prioritize Enhancement Services in collaboration with Vendor via a rolling three‑month roadmap. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to deliver:

- High‑Priority Enhancements in a release cycle not to exceed **four (4) months** from requirements sign‑off; and
- Medium‑ and Low‑priority enhancements within **six (6) months** of prioritization, subject to agreed capacity and any Change Orders under **Article XIII**.

2.4.3 **Service Levels.** Vendor shall perform the Extended Support Services in accordance with the Service Levels in **Article XII** of this Work Order, which are aligned with but may further specify or refine the SLA framework in **Article XII** of the Parent SOW.

2.4.4 **Professional Standards.** Vendor shall perform all Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills and experience, and in compliance with all Applicable Laws, consistent with **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**

In addition to Client’s obligations in **Section 2.7 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent SOW, Client shall:

- Allocate appropriate internal resources to validate and accept enhancement Deliverables in a timely manner, following the acceptance process in **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent SOW as applicable.
- Maintain necessary third‑party licenses and infrastructure required to host and operate the ERP/CRM solution and related integrations, consistent with **Section 2.7** and **Section 2.5.5** of the Parent SOW.
- Provide Vendor with timely access to relevant production and non‑production environments, subject to Client’s security policies.

2.6 **Subcontractors.**  

Vendor may use subcontractors in accordance with **Section 2.8 (Subcontractors)** of the Parent SOW. Vendor shall remain fully responsible for the performance of subcontractors and shall ensure that each subcontractor is bound by obligations at least as protective as those imposed on Vendor under this Work Order and the Parent SOW.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Total Work Order Value.**  

The Parties acknowledge that the total estimated contract value for this Work Order is **US$1,312,082** (the “WO Fees”), exclusive of Taxes and reimbursable expenses, and separate from the SOW Fees of US$4,295,126 under **Section 3.1** of the Parent SOW.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

Consistent with the fee structures described in **Sections 3.2 and 3.3** of the Parent SOW, the WO Fees shall be structured as follows (exclusive of Taxes and reimbursable expenses):

3.2.1 **Extended Support Retainer.**

- Monthly retainer for Extended Support Services: **US$18,750** per month.  
- Retainer term: **36 months** (initial period from anticipated end of Production Support Period through the third anniversary), totaling **US$675,000**.

3.2.2 **Enhancement Services (T&M with Annual Cap).**

- Time‑and‑materials (T&M) for Enhancement Services billed at the rates agreed under the Parent SOW and Consulting Services Agreement, subject to an annual not‑to‑exceed cap of **US$180,000** per contract year during the WO Term (for a total estimated allocation of **US$540,000** over three years).
- Any increase to the annual cap shall be subject to a mutually executed Change Order per **Article XIII**.

3.2.3 **Continuous Improvement Services (Fixed Fee).**

- Fixed fee for annual optimization, health checks, training refresh, and advisory services: **US$32,027.33** per year (approx.), for a total of **US$96,082** over three years.

Total estimated Fees under this Work Order:  
US$675,000 (retainer) + US$540,000 (enhancements cap) + US$96,082 (continuous improvement) = **US$1,312,082**.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Milestones.**

3.3.1 **Invoicing.**

(a) Extended Support Retainer shall be invoiced **monthly in advance** during the WO Term.  

(b) Enhancement Services T&M shall be invoiced **monthly in arrears** based on actual hours incurred, supported by detailed timesheets and activity reports, and shall not exceed the applicable annual cap absent a Change Order.  

(c) Continuous Improvement Services fixed fees shall be invoiced **annually in advance** on or about the anniversary of the WO Effective Date.

3.3.2 **Payment Terms (Net 30).**  

Notwithstanding the **Net 45** payment term set forth in **Section 3.3.2** of the Parent SOW, the Parties expressly agree that, for this Work Order only, Client shall pay all **undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (“Net 30”). This Work Order‑specific payment term is intended to supplement and, solely for the Services under this Work Order, supersede the Payment Terms in the Parent SOW and Consulting Services Agreement pursuant to the order‑of‑precedence principles in **Section 2.1.3** above.

3.4 **Late Payments; Interest.**

The late payment provisions of **Section 3.6** of the Parent SOW shall apply to this Work Order, except that Vendor shall not suspend Services hereunder unless: (a) any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice from Vendor; and (b) the Parties have attempted in good faith to resolve the non‑payment via the dispute resolution steps in **Article XIV** of this Work Order and **Article XIV** of the Parent SOW.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  

Client may withhold payment of disputed amounts in accordance with **Section 3.7 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent SOW, provided Client pays all undisputed portions when due and supplies written notice of the basis for dispute with reasonable detail.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.8 (Taxes)** of the Parent SOW. All WO Fees are exclusive of any applicable Taxes.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

Reimbursable expenses shall be governed by **Section 3.9 (Expenses and Reimbursement)** of the Parent SOW, except that for this Work Order:

- Vendor shall obtain Client’s written pre‑approval for any individual travel expense projected to exceed **US$2,000** (slightly more restrictive than the US$2,500 threshold in the Parent SOW, in light of the Low Risk, client‑friendly designation).
- Vendor shall submit itemized expense reports with supporting documentation within thirty (30) days following the month in which expenses are incurred.

3.8 **Price Adjustments.**  

The annual rate adjustment provisions in **Section 3.10 (Price Adjustments)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to T&M rates and retainer fees under this Work Order, provided that no rate increase shall take effect prior to **January 1, 2026**.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term of Work Order.**  

This Work Order shall commence on the WO Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in effect until the WO Expiration Date of **March 16, 2029**.

4.2 **Renewal; Extension.**

Any extension or renewal of the WO Term, including extended support or additional enhancement phases beyond March 16, 2029, shall be subject to mutual written agreement and may be documented via an amendment to this Work Order or a new work order, consistent with **Section 4.2 (Renewal)** of the Parent SOW.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 **Client Termination.** Notwithstanding **Section 4.3 (Termination for Convenience – Client)** of the Parent SOW (which provides for sixty (60) days’ notice), Client may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor, reflecting the Low Risk, client‑friendly nature of this Work Order.

4.3.2 **Vendor Termination.** Vendor may terminate this Work Order for convenience in accordance with **Section 4.4 (Termination for Convenience – Vendor)** of the Parent SOW, upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

Termination for cause shall be governed by **Section 4.5 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent SOW and the corresponding provisions of the Consulting Services Agreement, including for uncured material breach or persistent non‑payment. Prior to exercising termination rights, the Parties shall follow the dispute escalation process under **Article XIV** of the Parent SOW and this Work Order, unless the breach is not capable of cure.

4.5 **Effect of Termination; Fees Payable.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order:

- Client shall pay Vendor all Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued through the effective date of termination, including fees for work‑in‑progress and non‑cancellable commitments, consistent with **Section 4.7.1** of the Parent SOW.
- Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance and knowledge transfer at mutually agreed T&M rates to ensure an orderly wind‑down or transition to Client or a third party, consistent with **Section 4.7.2** of the Parent SOW.
- Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 4.7.3** and **Section 5.6** of the Parent SOW and **Article V** of this Work Order.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiration of Parent SOW or Consulting Services Agreement.**  

If the Parent SOW or the Consulting Services Agreement terminates or expires for any reason:

- This Work Order shall automatically terminate effective as of the effective date of termination or expiration of the Parent SOW or Consulting Services Agreement, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing that specific obligations under this Work Order shall continue (e.g., extended support under a successor master agreement).
- The Parties’ respective obligations upon such termination shall be governed by **Section 4.7 (Effect of Termination)** and **Section 4.8 (Survival)** of the Parent SOW and the Consulting Services Agreement, as supplemented by this Article IV.

4.7 **Survival.**  

Any provisions of this Work Order which by their nature should survive expiration or termination, including, without limitation, Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XII, XIV, XVI, and any payment obligations accrued but unpaid, shall survive, together with the corresponding provisions of the Parent SOW and Consulting Services Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Applicability.**  

This Article XII sets forth the Service Levels applicable to the Extended Support Services under this Work Order and supplements **Article XII** of the Parent SOW. To the extent there is any conflict, the more protective SLA to Client shall apply.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment.**  

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the supported ERP/CRM systems and integrations achieve a monthly availability of at least **99.9%**, measured in accordance with the methodology in **Section 12.2** of the Parent SOW, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excused Downtime.

12.3 **Support Response and Resolution Times.**  

Priority levels and target response and resolution times for incidents shall be as follows:

- **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Complete production outage or critical business process unavailable with no workaround:  
  Response within **1 hour**; continuous efforts to resolve; target resolution within **4 hours**.  
- **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant degradation with major business impact or no acceptable workaround:  
  Response within **4 hours**; commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within **1 Business Day**.  
- **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Limited degradation or acceptable workaround available:  
  Response within **1 Business Day**; reasonable efforts to resolve within **3 Business Days**.  
- **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or enhancement requests:  
  Response within **3 Business Days**; resolution via future scheduled release or as mutually agreed.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month during the WO Term, Client shall be entitled to service credits equal to a percentage of the Extended Support monthly retainer for that month:

- Uptime 99.0% to < 99.9%: **5%** credit  
- Uptime 98.0% to < 99.0%: **10%** credit  
- Uptime < 98.0%: **20%** credit  

Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) and constitute Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA shortfalls, except in cases of Vendor’s gross negligence or willful misconduct.

12.5 **Chronic SLA Failures.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in three (3) or more months in any rolling twelve (12) month period, Client shall have the right, in addition to service credits, to (a) require a corrective action plan, and (b) if such plan is not implemented and SLA performance does not materially improve within ninety (90) days, terminate this Work Order for cause upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice.
