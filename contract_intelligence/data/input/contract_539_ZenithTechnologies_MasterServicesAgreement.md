## ARTICLE XII

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.** For any managed services or hosted solutions provided by Zenith under this Agreement, Zenith shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the applicable systems and services are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and excused downtime resulting from: (a) Force Majeure Events; (b) Contoso’s acts or omissions; or (c) third-party network failures outside Zenith’s reasonable control.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Zenith may perform scheduled maintenance that may impact service availability, provided that Zenith: (a) uses commercially reasonable efforts to schedule such maintenance during off-peak hours; and (b) provides Contoso with at least forty-eight (48) hours prior notice of any maintenance reasonably expected to affect service availability.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.** Zenith shall respond to and work to resolve incidents in accordance with the following target timeframes (which may be further refined in an SOW):

  - **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of a critical business function: initial response within one (1) hour; continuous work until resolution or workaround; target resolution within four (4) hours.  
  - **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation or impact on core functions: initial response within two (2) hours; target resolution within one (1) Business Day.  
  - **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Limited or minor impact: initial response within one (1) Business Day; target resolution within three (3) Business Days.  
  - **Severity 4 (Low)** – General inquiries or cosmetic issues: initial response within two (2) Business Days; resolution as prioritized and agreed.

12.4 **SLA Credits.** If Zenith fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** for a given calendar month, Contoso may be entitled to service credits as specified in the applicable SOW (for example, a credit equal to a percentage of the monthly Fees for that service, based on the amount of downtime). Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall be Contoso’s exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to Contoso’s right to terminate for chronic SLA breaches.

12.5 **Chronic SLA Failures.** If, in any rolling three (3) month period, Zenith fails to meet the uptime commitment or other critical Service Levels in two (2) or more months, Contoso may, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, terminate the affected SOW without penalty, in addition to any SLA credits.
