## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 The Vendor shall provide to the Client comprehensive employment, staffing, and workforce management services, including but not limited to sourcing, recruiting, screening, hiring, onboarding, managing, and offboarding Vendor Personnel who shall perform specified work assignments for the Client (collectively, the “Services”).  

2.1.2 The Services may also include project-based consulting, workforce planning, skills-gap analysis, training program development, performance management support, and such other related services as the Parties may agree to in writing.

### 2.2 Staffing and Employment Model

2.2.1 The Vendor shall be the employer (or contractor-of-record) of all Vendor Personnel and shall retain full responsibility for all employer obligations in respect of Vendor Personnel, including but not limited to the payment of salaries, wages, mandatory benefits, social security contributions, tax withholdings, and compliance with Applicable Law relating to employment, except to the extent expressly assumed by the Client in any SOW.  

2.2.2 All Vendor Personnel shall remain under the general supervision, direction, and control of the Vendor with respect to HR, disciplinary, and payroll matters. Operationally, Vendor Personnel shall follow the Client’s reasonable instructions relevant to the Services and the Client’s worksite rules and policies, provided such instructions and policies are communicated in writing or via the Client’s standard employee handbook.

2.2.3 The Parties acknowledge and agree that Vendor Personnel are not employees, agents, or representatives of the Client, and nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship between the Client and Vendor Personnel.

### 2.3 Detailed Description of Services and Deliverables

2.3.1 **Recruitment & Sourcing Services.** The Vendor shall:  
(a) Work with the Client to define role descriptions, required qualifications, skills, and experience for each position;  
(b) Source candidates through multiple channels, including job boards, professional networks, referral programs, and the Vendor’s internal talent pools;  
(c) Conduct initial screening, including CV review, preliminary interviews, and basic background checks (where permitted by Applicable Law);  
(d) Present a shortlist of qualified candidates to the Client for final selection, along with candidate summaries and relevant documentation.

2.3.2 **Onboarding & Deployment.** Once the Client selects candidates from the Vendor’s shortlist, the Vendor shall:  
(a) Prepare and issue employment or engagement contracts with Vendor Personnel in accordance with Applicable Law;  
(b) Coordinate onboarding activities such as documentation collection, orientation, and initial training as agreed with the Client;  
(c) Arrange deployment of Vendor Personnel to the Client’s designated locations or remote assignments on the agreed start date(s);  
(d) Ensure Vendor Personnel are briefed on the Client’s internal policies, security requirements, and confidentiality obligations, and obtain written acknowledgments where requested by the Client.

2.3.3 **Ongoing Workforce Management.** The Vendor shall:  
(a) Manage attendance, timesheets, and leave requests of Vendor Personnel;  
(b) Administer payroll, benefits, and statutory contributions for Vendor Personnel;  
(c) Address HR issues, including performance counseling, grievances, disciplinary actions, and termination of Vendor Personnel;  
(d) Coordinate performance evaluations with the Client’s line managers as reasonably requested;  
(e) Provide periodic reports on headcount, turnover, performance trends, and other workforce metrics as agreed with the Client.

2.3.4 **Training & Development.** To maintain quality and enhance performance, the Vendor shall:  
(a) Identify training needs in collaboration with the Client;  
(b) Develop or procure training programs (technical and soft skills) suitable for Vendor Personnel;  
(c) Organize training sessions, workshops, and e-learning modules as agreed in a SOW;  
(d) Track training completion and provide training records to the Client upon request.

2.3.5 **Compliance & Risk Management.** The Vendor shall:  
(a) Comply with all Applicable Laws relating to employment, health and safety, immigration, and data protection in the Territory, to the extent related to the Vendor’s role as employer;  
(b) Maintain internal policies and procedures in line with industry best practices;  
(c) Inform the Client in a timely manner of any material compliance issues that may impact the Services or the Client’s operations;  
(d) Cooperate with the Client’s audits and reviews relating to compliance, within reasonable limits and subject to confidentiality and security requirements.

2.3.6 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Vendor shall provide Deliverables that may include:  
(a) Monthly staffing and performance reports;  
(b) Quarterly workforce analytics and recommendations;  
(c) Onboarding and offboarding checklists and documentation;  
(d) Training materials and attendance records;  
(e) Any other documentation or work product specifically identified in an SOW.

### 2.4 Service Performance Standards

2.4.1 The Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications.  

2.4.2 The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the timelines and Service Levels set forth in **Article XII** and any SOW.  

2.4.3 The Vendor shall ensure that Vendor Personnel comply with all reasonable workplace rules, security procedures, and policies provided by the Client in writing.  

2.4.4 In the event of material underperformance of any Vendor Personnel, the Client may request replacement of such Vendor Personnel. The Vendor shall, at its expense, use commercially reasonable efforts to replace such Vendor Personnel within a reasonable timeframe (taking into account the applicable labor market and role complexity).

### 2.5 Timelines and Milestones

2.5.1 Specific timelines, milestones, and staffing numbers shall be set forth in applicable SOWs.  

2.5.2 Unless otherwise agreed in a SOW, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to present shortlisted candidates to the Client within fifteen (15) Business Days after receipt of a complete job requisition.  

2.5.3 The Parties acknowledge that timelines are estimates and are subject to factors outside the Vendor’s reasonable control (e.g., labor market conditions, candidate availability, Client’s response times).

### 2.6 Changes in Scope

Any changes to the scope of Services, including increases or decreases in headcount, changes in role profiles, or significant changes in deliverables, shall be addressed through the change management process set out in **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The total estimated contract value for the Term (as defined in **Section 4.1**) is **Nine Hundred Five Thousand Six Hundred Nineteen U.S. Dollars (USD 905,619)** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to adjustments as provided in this Agreement and any SOW.  

3.1.2 The Contract Value is an estimate for budgeting purposes and does not constitute a cap or limit on the Fees payable by the Client, unless expressly stated otherwise in a specific SOW.

### 3.2 Fee Structure

3.2.1 **Base Service Fees.** The Client shall pay the Vendor a recurring service fee for each Vendor Personnel assigned to the Client, as specified in the applicable SOW. Such fees may be structured as:  
(a) A fixed monthly fee per Vendor Personnel; and/or  
(b) Time-and-materials fees based on hourly or daily rates; and/or  
(c) Project-based milestone payments.

3.2.2 **Recruitment / Placement Fees.** For recruitment-related activities, the Vendor may charge:  
(a) A one-time placement fee per successful hire, expressed as a percentage of the annualized salary or a fixed amount; and/or  
(b) Retainer fees for retained search engagements, as specified in an SOW.

3.2.3 **Additional Services.** Any additional services requested by the Client (such as custom training, specialized compliance consulting, or out-of-scope reporting) shall be charged at the rates specified in an SOW or otherwise agreed in writing.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.3.1 The Vendor shall invoice the Client monthly in arrears for Services rendered and Deliverables provided during the preceding month, unless an SOW specifies different invoicing intervals.  

3.3.2 All invoices shall detail, as applicable:  
(a) The number of Vendor Personnel, their names or IDs, and corresponding fees;  
(b) Any applicable milestone payments;  
(c) Approved expenses (if any);  
(d) Applicable taxes; and  
(e) Reference to the applicable SOW and this Agreement (AGR-ACM-202311-493).

3.3.3 The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** of the invoice date (“**Net 60**”), in accordance with the Payment Terms defined in **Section 1.13**.  

3.3.4 Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars (USD) by wire transfer or other mutually agreed electronic payment method to the bank account designated by the Vendor.

### 3.4 Disputed Amounts

3.4.1 If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice, the Client shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the reasons for such dispute.  

3.4.2 The Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with **Section 3.3**. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any dispute within thirty (30) days.  

3.4.3 Any amounts ultimately determined to be payable by the Client shall be paid within fifteen (15) days after resolution of the dispute.

### 3.5 Late Payments

3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of:  
(a) One and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or  
(b) The maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law,  
from the due date until the date of actual payment.  

3.5.2 Without limiting any other rights or remedies, if the Client fails to pay undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days after receiving a written reminder from the Vendor, the Vendor may suspend performance of the Services until all overdue amounts and applicable interest are paid.

### 3.6 Taxes

3.6.1 The Fees are exclusive of any sales, value-added, goods and services, withholding, or other taxes (collectively, “Taxes”), unless expressly stated otherwise.  

3.6.2 The Client shall be responsible for all Taxes arising from or relating to this Agreement, except for taxes based on the Vendor’s net income, property, or employees.  

3.6.3 If any Taxes are required to be withheld from payments made by the Client under Applicable Law, the Client shall gross up the payment so that the Vendor receives the full amount it would have received in the absence of such withholding, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### 3.7 Expenses

3.7.1 Reasonable, out-of-pocket expenses incurred by the Vendor in the performance of the Services (such as travel, lodging, and meals) shall be reimbursed by the Client, **provided** that:  
(a) Such expenses are pre-approved in writing by the Client; and  
(b) The Vendor provides reasonable supporting documentation.  

3.7.2 Reimbursable expenses shall be invoiced together with the monthly Fees and shall be payable in accordance with **Section 3.3**.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **17 September 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).  

4.1.2 Any extension or renewal of this Agreement beyond the Expiration Date shall be subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 The Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing the Vendor with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days** prior written notice.  

4.2.2 The Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing the Client with at least ninety (90) days prior written notice.  

4.2.3 Upon termination for convenience by the Client, the Client shall be liable to pay:  
(a) All Fees for Services rendered and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) All non-cancellable commitments entered into by the Vendor in reliance on this Agreement (including but not limited to lease obligations, long-term employment contracts of Vendor Personnel, and training costs), to the extent such commitments cannot reasonably be mitigated; and  
(c) Any early termination or severance costs incurred by the Vendor in connection with the demobilization or termination of Vendor Personnel assigned to the Client.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.  

4.3.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Vendor may, at its discretion, immediately suspend Services or terminate this Agreement if:  
(a) The Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within sixty (60) days after the due date;  
(b) The Client becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, enters into any composition with creditors, or ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business; or  
(c) There is a material breach by the Client of its confidentiality or data protection obligations hereunder.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) The Client shall promptly pay the Vendor all Fees due and payable, including any amounts specified under **Section 4.2.3**;  
(b) Each Party shall return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) the other Party’s Confidential Information, subject to **Section 5.4**;  
(c) The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to cooperate with the Client to transition Services to the Client or a replacement provider, subject to mutually agreed fees for such transition assistance.  

4.4.2 Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall be without prejudice to any accrued rights, remedies, obligations, or liabilities of either Party as of the effective date of termination, including any right to claim damages arising from any prior breach.

### 4.5 Survival

The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including without limitation **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions)** shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.
