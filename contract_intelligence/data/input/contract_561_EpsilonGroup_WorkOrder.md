## ARTICLE II  
# SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW and CSA.**

2.1.1 This Work Order is **executed under and subject to** the terms and conditions of the Parent SOW (SOW‑EPS‑202407‑545, contract_545) and the CSA (CSA‑EPS‑202407‑536, contract_536), including without limitation Article II (**Scope of Services / Work**), Article III (**Fees and Payment Terms**), Article V (**Confidentiality**), Article VI (**Intellectual Property Rights**), Article XI (**Data Protection and Privacy**), Article XII (**Service Level Agreements**), and Article XIII (**Change Management**) of the Parent SOW and corresponding provisions of the CSA.

2.1.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between this Work Order and the Parent SOW or CSA, the following order of precedence shall apply: (a) CSA; (b) Parent SOW; (c) this Work Order; except that this Work Order is intended to **supplement and add scope** to the Parent SOW and shall not be interpreted as limiting any rights of Contoso under the CSA or Parent SOW, unless this Work Order expressly states that a specific provision is superseded and references such provision by Article and Section number.

2.1.3 For clarity, this Work Order **adds scope** to the Statement of Work dated 3 October 2024 (Ref: SOW‑EPS‑202407‑545, contract_545) by defining additional Services and Deliverables that fall primarily within Phase 3 and Phase 4 of the Parent SOW and by allocating additional Fees and timelines in connection therewith.

2.2 **Overview of the Additional Project Scope.**

2.2.1 Under this Work Order, Epsilon shall provide an extended **Enterprise Transformation Roll‑Out and Advanced Analytics Extension** program focusing on:

  (a) global and regional roll‑out of solutions designed during Phase 2 of the Parent SOW, including multi‑country deployment of Target Systems;  

  (b) advanced analytics and reporting use-case implementation, including design and deployment of predictive models, executive dashboards, and operational analytics;  

  (c) optimisation and stabilisation of post‑go-live environments, including performance tuning, process optimisation, and configuration refinements; and  

  (d) structured knowledge transfer and capacity building for Contoso’s internal teams, building upon the enablement activities described in Section 2.3.3(e) and Phase 4 activities in Section 2.3.4 of the Parent SOW.

2.2.2 The additional scope under this Work Order shall continue to focus on the **Target Systems** as described in Section 2.2.2 of the Parent SOW, including Contoso’s CRM, ERP, analytics, reporting stack, and integration middleware. This Work Order may prioritise specific regions, business units, or modules (e.g., Sales, Finance, Operations) as defined in the Project Plan.

2.3 **Detailed Services.**

2.3.1 **Stream A – Global Roll‑Out and Localisation.**  
Epsilon shall perform the following services, extending the implementation support in Section 2.3.3 of the Parent SOW:

  (a) **Roll‑Out Planning and Readiness:** Develop detailed country/region‑specific roll‑out plans, including cut‑over timelines, local data migration plans, localisation requirements, and resource plans, building upon the Project Plan defined under Section 2.3.1(b) of the Parent SOW.  

  (b) **Configuration and Localisation Support:** Provide configuration guidance and quality review of localised designs to ensure alignment with the global **Technology Architecture Blueprint** and **Future State Process Maps** delivered under Phase 2 (Sections 2.3.2(b) and (c) of the Parent SOW).  

  (c) **Integration and Data Validation:** Assist in validating cross‑system integrations and data flows for each roll‑out wave, including test execution support, defect triage, and performance verification.  

  (d) **Go‑Live and Hypercare:** Provide focused, time‑bound post‑go-live support (“Hypercare”) for each major roll‑out wave, including incident resolution guidance, root-cause analysis, and stabilisation recommendations.

2.3.2 **Stream B – Advanced Analytics and Reporting.**  
Epsilon shall provide enhanced analytics and reporting services consistent with the **Data and Analytics Maturity Assessment** and roadmap produced under the Parent SOW (Section 2.4.1):

  (a) **Use Case Prioritisation:** In collaboration with the Contoso Project Manager (as defined in Section 1.1.4 of the Parent SOW), prioritise analytics use cases across sales, finance, operations, and customer experience.  

  (b) **Data Model and Pipeline Design:** Design and document data models and ETL/ELT pipelines required to support prioritised use cases, building upon the **Technology Architecture Blueprint** delivered under Phase 2.  

  (c) **Dashboard and Report Development:** Develop analytics dashboards, reports, and scorecards for executive, management, and operational audiences, following Contoso’s design and branding standards.  

  (d) **Predictive and Prescriptive Analytics:** Where agreed in the Project Plan, design and prototype predictive models (e.g., churn prediction, demand forecasting) and prescriptive analytics (e.g., recommendations) with appropriate documentation and knowledge transfer.  

  (e) **Analytics Operating Model:** Update or extend Contoso’s analytics operating model, including governance, data stewardship roles, and performance metrics for analytics outcomes.

2.3.3 **Stream C – Process Optimisation and Stabilisation.**  
Epsilon shall:

  (a) Conduct periodic **Process Health Checks** on key business processes (e.g., order‑to‑cash, procure‑to‑pay, lead‑to‑opportunity) implemented under the Parent SOW to identify bottlenecks, control gaps, and user adoption issues.  

  (b) Propose and document process and configuration optimisations, including recommendations for automation, workflow streamlining, and control enhancements.  

  (c) Support Contoso in the implementation and testing of agreed optimisations, including updates to training materials and operating procedures.  

  (d) Provide structured **Quarterly Review Workshops**, complementing those in Section 2.3.4(b) of the Parent SOW, to review performance against KPIs and recommend further optimisation initiatives.

2.3.4 **Stream D – Training, Knowledge Transfer and Enablement.**  
In alignment with Section 2.3.3(e) of the Parent SOW, Epsilon shall:

  (a) Develop additional training materials, user guides, and knowledge articles reflecting new functionality, roll‑outs, and optimisation changes introduced under Streams A–C.  

  (b) Conduct **Train‑the‑Trainer** sessions for Contoso’s internal teams, including system administrators, super‑users, and analytics champions.  

  (c) Establish a structured **Capability Transfer Plan** to progressively reduce reliance on Epsilon resources and increase Contoso’s internal competency, including success criteria and transition milestones.  

  (d) Maintain training delivery records as part of the Deliverables under Section 2.4.

2.3.5 **Enhanced Advisory Scope.**  
Beyond the retainer-based advisory services in Section 2.3.4 of the Parent SOW, Epsilon shall:

  (a) Provide targeted executive advisory sessions (e.g., quarterly strategy briefings) on roadmap refinement, technology trends, and regulatory changes impacting the Target Systems.  

  (b) Participate, upon Contoso’s reasonable request, in steering committee meetings described in Section 2.8.3 of the Parent SOW to present progress, risks, and recommendations relating to this Work Order.  

  (c) Support Contoso in preparing business cases for incremental investments or changes, building upon the **Cost–Benefit Analysis and Business Case** Deliverables under the Parent SOW.

2.4 **Deliverables.**

Key Deliverables under this Work Order shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **Global Roll‑Out Plans** for each defined region or business unit, including cut‑over and hypercare plans;  

  (b) **Localisation Configuration Specifications** and integration validation reports;  

  (c) **Analytics Use Case Catalogue and Prioritisation Matrix**;  

  (d) **Data Model and Pipeline Design Documents**;  

  (e) **Analytics Dashboards and Reports**, including accompanying design documentation and user guides;  

  (f) **Predictive Model Design and Validation Documentation** (where applicable);  

  (g) **Process Health Check Reports** and **Optimisation Recommendations**;  

  (h) Updated **Training Materials, User Guides, and Knowledge Base Articles**;  

  (i) **Capability Transfer Plan** and **Training Delivery Records**;  

  (j) **Quarterly Optimisation and Advisory Reports**, complementing the “Quarterly Strategic Advisory Reports and Roadmap Updates” under Section 2.4.1 of the Parent SOW.

2.5 **Milestones and Timelines.**

2.5.1 The Parties shall develop a detailed Milestone schedule in the Project Plan, consistent with Section 2.4.2 of the Parent SOW. Indicative Milestones for this Work Order include:

  - **MW1:** Completion of initial Global Roll‑Out Plan and first regional roll‑out readiness assessment – within 90 days of WO Effective Date.  
  - **MW2:** Delivery of first set of Analytics Dashboards and Reports for at least two priority use cases – within 150 days of WO Effective Date.  
  - **MW3:** Completion of first Process Health Check and Optimisation Recommendations for at least two core processes – within 210 days of WO Effective Date.  
  - **MW4:** Completion of Capability Transfer Plan and initial Train‑the‑Trainer cycle – within 270 days of WO Effective Date.  
  - **MW5:** Completion of at least one end‑to‑end global roll‑out wave and associated Hypercare Exit Report – within 15 months of WO Effective Date.  
  - **MW6:** Delivery of final Quarterly Optimisation and Advisory Report prior to the expiration of this Work Order.

2.5.2 Time is not of the essence unless expressly designated in the Project Plan and agreed in writing by both Parties, consistent with Section 2.5.2 of the Parent SOW.

2.6 **Acceptance Process.**

Unless otherwise set out in a Change Order, the acceptance process in Section 2.4.3 of the Parent SOW shall govern Deliverables under this Work Order. In particular:

  (a) Contoso shall have ten (10) Business Days following delivery to review each Deliverable and either accept it or provide a written notice of material non‑conformance;  

  (b) Epsilon shall correct any material non‑conformities and resubmit the Deliverable within a mutually agreed period; and  

  (c) If Contoso fails to respond within the applicable review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**

Epsilon shall perform the Services under this Work Order in a diligent, professional, and workmanlike manner, consistent with the standards described in Section 2.5.1 of the Parent SOW and the Service Levels in Article XII of this Work Order and the Parent SOW.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.**

In addition to the responsibilities in Section 2.6 of the Parent SOW, Contoso shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to business stakeholders, subject-matter experts, and technical personnel necessary to define local requirements, analytics use cases, and optimisation priorities;  

  (b) Ensure that appropriate test environments, data sets, and tools are available to support roll‑outs and analytics development;  

  (c) Provide timely feedback on Deliverables and participation in workshops and training sessions.

## ARTICLE III  
# FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**

3.1.1 The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables under this Work Order is **Two Million Six Hundred Forty‑One Thousand Two Hundred Twenty United States Dollars (US$2,641,220)** (the **“WO Contract Value”**).

3.1.2 The WO Contract Value is **in addition to** the SOW Contract Value of US$1,977,028 stated in Section 3.1 of the Parent SOW and represents incremental scope and funding. The Parties acknowledge that, for purposes of the overall Agreement, the aggregate Fees under the CSA will reflect the combination of (a) the SOW Contract Value under the Parent SOW and (b) the WO Contract Value under this Work Order, as adjusted by any Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**

3.2.1 **Fixed‑Fee Components.**  
Certain Deliverables under Streams A–C shall be provided on a fixed‑fee basis as follows (indicative allocation):

  - Global Roll‑Out Planning Deliverables (MW1): US$320,000  
  - Initial Analytics Use Cases and Dashboards (MW2): US$410,000  
  - Initial Process Health Checks and Optimisation Recommendations (MW3): US$285,000  
  - Capability Transfer Plan and Initial Training Cycle (MW4): US$210,000  

The above fixed fees are payable upon completion and acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the associated Milestones pursuant to Section 3.3.2(a).

3.2.2 **Time‑and‑Materials Components.**  
Additional implementation, optimisation, analytics development, and advisory work beyond the fixed‑fee components shall be charged on a time‑and‑materials basis, using the role-based day rates defined below (which are consistent with, but may update, the rates in Section 3.2.2 of the Parent SOW):

  - Senior Consultant / Architect: US$1,650 per day  
  - Consultant: US$1,250 per day  
  - Analyst / Junior Consultant: US$920 per day  
  - Project Manager: US$1,450 per day  

The Parties estimate, for planning purposes only, an initial baseline of **US$1,110,000** in time‑and‑materials Fees over the Term of this Work Order, subject to adjustment via Change Orders.

3.2.3 **Retainer Components.**  
To support the Enhanced Advisory Scope in Section 2.3.5, Contoso shall pay a monthly advisory retainer of **US$18,000** per calendar month during the Term of this Work Order, unless otherwise adjusted by Change Order. This retainer is in addition to the Phase 4 retainer described in Section 3.2.3 of the Parent SOW and is intended to cover incremental executive advisory and analytics governance support.

3.2.4 **Not-to-Exceed.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in a Change Order, the aggregate of fixed‑fee, time‑and‑materials, and retainer components under this Work Order shall not exceed the WO Contract Value of US$2,641,220.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**

3.3.1 **Net 60 Terms for This Work Order.**  
Notwithstanding Section 3.3.1 of the Parent SOW (which specifies Net 30 for that SOW) and Section 3.3.1 of the CSA (Net 60), the Parties agree that, for this Work Order:

> **All undisputed invoices issued under this Work Order shall be payable by Contoso within sixty (60) days of the invoice date (“Net 60”).**

This Section 3.3.1 is intended to align with the standard payment terms under the CSA and **supersedes** the Net 30 provision in Section 3.3.1 of the Parent SOW solely with respect to invoices issued pursuant to this Work Order.

3.3.2 **Invoicing.**

  (a) **Fixed‑Fee Components:** Epsilon shall invoice upon achievement and acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the relevant Milestone, referencing the Milestone number (e.g., MW1–MW4) and associated Deliverables.  

  (b) **Time‑and‑Materials Components:** Epsilon shall invoice monthly in arrears, providing a breakdown of time spent by resource, role, and activity category (e.g., roll‑out support, analytics development, optimisation).  

  (c) **Retainer Components:** Epsilon shall invoice monthly in advance for the advisory retainer, on or about the first day of each calendar month.

3.4 **Taxes.**

Fees are exclusive of VAT and other applicable taxes, which shall be invoiced and paid in accordance with Section 3.4 of the Parent SOW and Section 3.3.2 of the CSA.

3.5 **Late Payment.**

If any undisputed amount is not paid by Contoso by the due date, Epsilon may charge interest and, after prior written notice and a reasonable cure period, suspend Services in accordance with Section 3.5 of the Parent SOW and Section 3.4 of the CSA. Epsilon shall exercise any right of suspension in a manner designed to minimise disruption to Contoso’s critical business operations.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**

3.6.1 Contoso shall reimburse Epsilon for reasonable, pre‑approved T&E incurred in performing the Services under this Work Order, in accordance with Section 3.6 of the Parent SOW and Section 3.5 of the CSA.

3.6.2 Any individual expense in excess of GBP £1,000 (or equivalent) shall require prior written approval from the Contoso Project Manager or delegate.

3.6.3 Epsilon shall itemise T&E and provide supporting documentation upon request. Reimbursement shall be subject to the Net 60 terms in Section 3.3.1.

3.7 **Currency.**

All Fees and reimbursable expenses under this Work Order shall be invoiced and paid in **United States Dollars (USD)**, consistent with Section 3.7 of the Parent SOW and Section 3.6 of the CSA.

## ARTICLE IV  
# TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**

This Work Order shall commence on the WO Effective Date (20 May 2025) and shall continue in force until **3 March 2027** (the **“WO Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, the Parent SOW, or the CSA.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent SOW and CSA Term.**

4.2.1 This Work Order may not extend beyond the term of the Parent SOW or CSA. To the extent the Parent SOW or CSA expires or is terminated before the WO Expiration Date, this Work Order shall automatically terminate on the effective date of such expiration or termination, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing that certain portions of this Work Order shall survive.

4.2.2 For the avoidance of doubt, if the CSA expires on 15 March 2027 (as referenced in Section 4.2 of the Parent SOW), the Term of this Work Order shall not extend beyond that date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. This shorter notice period is more favourable to the Client than the sixty (60) days stated in Section 4.3.1 of the Parent SOW and shall apply solely to this Work Order.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

This Work Order may be terminated by either Party for cause in accordance with the termination for material breach and insolvency provisions in Section 4.4 of the Parent SOW and the CSA, provided that any cure rights referenced therein shall apply equally to breaches under this Work Order.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**

Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order:

  (a) Epsilon shall cease performing the Services, except as requested for transition assistance pursuant to Section 4.5(c).  

  (b) Contoso shall pay Epsilon for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered (and accepted or deemed accepted) up to the effective date of termination, plus approved non‑cancellable commitments and expenses incurred in reliance on this Work Order, consistent with Section 4.3.2 of the Parent SOW.  

  (c) At Contoso’s request, Epsilon shall provide reasonable transition assistance on a time‑and‑materials basis at the rates set out in Section 3.2.2, to facilitate an orderly handover to Contoso or a third party. 

  (d) Both Parties shall return or destroy Confidential Information as required by Article V of this Work Order and the corresponding confidentiality provisions in the Parent SOW and CSA.

4.6 **Survival.**

Any provisions of this Work Order which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration (including payment obligations, IP provisions, confidentiality, data protection, limitation of liability, and dispute resolution) shall survive, consistent with Section 4.7 of the Parent SOW and Article XVI of this Work Order.

If a Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose Confidential Information, it shall (where legally permissible) give prompt written notice to the other Party and cooperate in any reasonable efforts to limit or protect the disclosure.

5.5 **Survival.**

The confidentiality obligations hereunder shall survive for the period set forth in Section 5.3 of the Parent SOW and Section 5.7 of the CSA (typically five (5) years for non‑trade secret information, and for so long as trade secrets or Personal Data remain such under applicable law).
