## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.**  

This Work Order supplements the **Scope of Services / Work** set out in **Article II of the Parent SOW** by adding new Services relating to:

- Advanced analytics, dashboards, and reporting across the ERP and CRM environments;  
- Data quality, governance, and monitoring instrumentation over data migrated and configured under **Phases 2–3** of the Parent SOW; and  
- Extended support and optimisation services beyond the baseline managed support described in **Phase 7 – Ongoing Support and Maintenance (Section 2.2.7)** and **Article XII** of the Parent SOW.

For the avoidance of doubt, the WO Services form part of the broader “Services” (as defined in **Section 1.1.16** of the Parent SOW), and shall be performed in accordance with the performance standards set out in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW, in addition to the specific performance commitments in this Work Order.

2.2 **Relationship to Parent SOW.**

2.2.1 All phases, activities, and Deliverables specified in the Parent SOW remain in full force and effect. This Work Order does not replace or supersede any element of the Parent SOW; rather, **it adds scope to the Statement of Work dated 20 June 2024 (Ref: SOW-SUM-202406-236, contract_236)** by:

  (a) Extending the design and architecture scope to include analytics and reporting layers that consume ERP and CRM data (building upon **Phase 2 – Solution Design and Architecture, Section 2.2.2** of the Parent SOW);

  (b) Extending configuration and integration scope to cover data warehouses, reporting tools, and dashboard frameworks (building upon **Phase 3 – Implementation, Configuration, and Integration, Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW);

  (c) Adding specific testing, UAT support, and training activities focused on analytics and reporting (supplementing **Phase 4 – Testing and User Acceptance, Section 2.2.4** and **Phase 5 – Training and Knowledge Transfer, Section 2.2.5** of the Parent SOW); and

  (d) Expanding ongoing and hypercare support (in addition to the minimum hypercare period in **Section 2.2.6(b)** of the Parent SOW and the recurring support described in **Section 2.2.7** and **Article XII**).

2.2.2 Unless expressly stated otherwise herein, all obligations of the Parties under **Sections 2.5 (Performance Standards), 2.6 (Client Responsibilities), and 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent SOW shall apply equally to the WO Services and WO Deliverables under this Work Order.

2.3 **WO Project Phases.**

The WO Services will be organised into the following phases, which are aligned and integrated with the existing phases under **Section 2.2 of the Parent SOW**:

2.3.1 **WO Phase A – Analytics Requirements and Design.**

  (a) Conduct additional stakeholder workshops focused on analytics, reporting, and KPI requirements across finance, sales, operations, and executive management.

  (b) Review the Requirements Specification Document and Solution Design Document delivered under **Sections 2.3.1 and 2.3.2** of the Parent SOW, identify gaps related to analytics and reporting, and produce a **Supplemental Analytics Requirements Addendum**.

  (c) Define data models, metrics, and aggregation layers required to support the Client’s reporting and dashboard needs, including regulatory and audit reporting where applicable.

  (d) Deliver a **WO Analytics & Reporting Design Document**, including data architecture diagrams, integration patterns with ERP/CRM, security model for analytics users, and performance considerations.

2.3.2 **WO Phase B – Implementation of Analytics and Reporting.**

  (a) Configure agreed reporting tools and dashboard platforms (for example, but not limited to, Power BI, Tableau, or similar tools agreed by the Parties) to integrate with ERP/CRM systems implemented under the Parent SOW.

  (b) Develop ETL/ELT processes, data transformations, and aggregations required to populate analytics data stores, ensuring consistency with the data migration strategy and mapping documents delivered under **Section 2.3.4** of the Parent SOW.

  (c) Implement role-based access controls for analytics users in alignment with the security controls and access management implemented under **Section 2.2.3(d)** of the Parent SOW.

  (d) Produce **Updated Integration Design Specifications** and **Runbook Enhancements** (as defined in Section 1.2.13), documenting the analytics components and support procedures.

2.3.3 **WO Phase C – Testing, UAT, and Go-Live for Analytics.**

  (a) Prepare test plans and test cases specifically for data accuracy, performance, and usability of analytics dashboards, extending the testing framework described in **Section 2.2.4(a)** of the Parent SOW.

  (b) Execute system and integration testing of analytics components, log defects in the defect tracking tool agreed under **Section 2.2.4(b)** of the Parent SOW, and remediate such defects.

  (c) Support Client-led UAT for analytics dashboards and reports; coordinate sign-off of WO Deliverables under the acceptance mechanism in **Section 2.7 of the Parent SOW**.

  (d) Assist with go-live planning for analytics, including updates to the **Go-Live Cutover Plan** referenced in **Section 2.3.9** of the Parent SOW, and participate in an additional analytics-specific hypercare period of sixty (60) days following analytics go-live.

2.3.4 **WO Phase D – Extended Support and Optimisation.**

  (a) Provide Extended Support Services for analytics and reporting components, integrated with the support processes, ticketing system, and reporting mechanisms specified in **Section 2.2.7** and **Section 2.3.11** of the Parent SOW.

  (b) Monitor performance and usage of analytics dashboards, identify optimisation opportunities, and implement minor enhancements as agreed via the **Change Management** process set forth in **Article XIII of the Parent SOW** and this Work Order.

  (c) Provide quarterly service review sessions dedicated to analytics performance, usage, and roadmap planning, in addition to the monthly service review meetings described in **Section 2.2.7(d)** of the Parent SOW.

2.4 **WO Deliverables.**

In addition to the Deliverables listed in **Section 2.3** of the Parent SOW, the Vendor shall deliver the following WO Deliverables under this Work Order:

2.4.1 **Supplemental Analytics Requirements Addendum** (extending the Requirements Specification Document under **Section 2.3.1** of the Parent SOW);

2.4.2 **WO Analytics & Reporting Design Document** (supplementing the Solution Design Document under **Section 2.3.2** of the Parent SOW);

2.4.3 **Updated Integration Design Specifications for Analytics** (supplementing **Section 2.3.3** of the Parent SOW);

2.4.4 **Analytics Data Model and ETL/ELT Specifications**;

2.4.5 **Configured Analytics Dashboards and Standard Reports** (a minimum of fifteen (15) standard dashboards and twenty (20) standard reports, as mutually defined in WO Phase A);

2.4.6 **Runbook Enhancements and Support Procedures** for analytics (extending the documentation under **Section 2.3.5** of the Parent SOW);

2.4.7 **Analytics Test Plans, Test Cases, and Test Reports** (supplementing **Section 2.3.6** of the Parent SOW);

2.4.8 **Analytics Training Materials** and a minimum of four (4) training sessions (supplementing **Section 2.3.7** of the Parent SOW);

2.4.9 **Analytics Hypercare Report and Optimisation Recommendations**;

2.4.10 **Quarterly Analytics Service Review Reports** forming part of the monthly service and performance reports referenced in **Section 2.3.11** of the Parent SOW.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**

2.5.1 The Parties acknowledge the importance of timely completion of the WO Services. Without prejudice to the high-level timeline in **Section 2.4** of the Parent SOW, the following WO Milestones apply:

- **WO Milestone A:** Completion of WO Phase A and delivery of the Supplemental Analytics Requirements Addendum and WO Analytics & Reporting Design Document – within ninety (90) days of the Effective Date of this Work Order.

- **WO Milestone B:** Completion of WO Phase B (implementation of analytics and reporting) and delivery of configured dashboards and Updated Integration Design Specifications – within one hundred eighty (180) days of the Effective Date.

- **WO Milestone C:** Completion of WO Phase C (testing, UAT, and analytics go-live), including acceptance of key analytics Deliverables – within two hundred seventy (270) days of the Effective Date.

- **WO Milestone D:** Completion of the initial sixty (60)-day analytics hypercare period – within three hundred thirty (330) days of the Effective Date.

- **WO Milestone E:** Extended Support and Optimisation – from the end of the analytics hypercare period until the Expiration Date of this Work Order.

2.5.2 Each WO Milestone shall be deemed achieved upon the Client’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the associated WO Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent SOW.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**

2.6.1 The Vendor shall perform the WO Services in accordance with **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW, including the obligations to exercise due skill, care, and diligence and to use suitably qualified personnel.

2.6.2 With respect to Extended Support Services, the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII of the Parent SOW (Service Level Agreements)** shall apply, including the service availability commitment of 99.9% in **Section 12.1**, the incident response and resolution times in **Section 12.3**, and the Service Credits in **Section 12.4**.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**

2.7.1 In addition to the Client responsibilities set out in **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW, the Client shall:

  (a) Designate business owners for each analytics domain (e.g., finance, sales, operations) to participate in workshops, reviews, and UAT;

  (b) Provide timely access to data sources, metadata, and any third-party reporting tools required for the WO Services;

  (c) Validate and sign off on key metric definitions and data mappings within ten (10) Business Days of Vendor submission, unless otherwise agreed.

2.7.2 Delays in fulfilling Client responsibilities may impact the achievement of WO Milestones and associated payment events; any such impacts shall be handled in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent SOW and this Work Order.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **WO Contract Value and Not-to-Exceed Amount.**

3.1.1 The total aggregate amount payable by the Client to the Vendor for all WO Services and WO Deliverables under this Work Order shall not exceed **USD $587,583** (the “**WO Contract Value**”), unless otherwise agreed in a written amendment to this Work Order executed by both Parties.

3.1.2 The WO Contract Value is separate from and in addition to the **Contract Value of USD $1,661,342** specified in **Section 3.1** of the Parent SOW. For clarity, the combined not-to-exceed exposure of the Client under the Parent SOW and this Work Order shall be the sum of their respective contract values, unless adjusted in accordance with the applicable change control provisions.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**

The Fees payable under this Work Order shall consist of the following components:

3.2.1 **WO Fixed Fees – Analytics Implementation.** A fixed-fee amount of **USD $385,000** for WO Phases A–C (analytics requirements, design, implementation, testing, and go-live), inclusive of all labour, materials, and overhead costs, payable according to the WO Milestones specified in Section 3.3.

3.2.2 **WO Extended Support Fees.** A recurring monthly fee of **USD $6,250** for WO Phase D (Extended Support and Optimisation) from completion of the analytics hypercare period through the Expiration Date of this Work Order, subject to early termination or extension as set forth in **Article IV**. These fees are in addition to the recurring support fees of **USD $18,000 per month** specified in **Section 3.2.2** of the Parent SOW.

3.2.3 **WO Time-and-Materials (“T&M”) Enhancements.** Any additional enhancements, change requests, or out-of-scope work relating to analytics, reporting, or Extended Support Services requested by the Client shall be performed on a T&M basis at the Vendor’s then-current standard rates, as may be updated in accordance with the approach described in **Section 3.2.3** of the Parent SOW, provided that:

  (a) No such T&M work shall be undertaken without a written Change Request approved under **Article XIII**; and  

  (b) All T&M charges under this Work Order shall count towards the WO Contract Value unless expressly agreed otherwise.

3.3 **WO Payment Milestones.**

3.3.1 The WO Fixed Fees under Section 3.2.1 shall be invoiced upon achievement of the following WO Milestones:

- **WO Milestone A Completion (Phase A – Requirements & Design):** 20% of WO Fixed Fees (USD $77,000);

- **WO Milestone B Completion (Phase B – Implementation):** 40% of WO Fixed Fees (USD $154,000);

- **WO Milestone C Completion (Phase C – Testing, UAT & Go-Live):** 30% of WO Fixed Fees (USD $115,500);

- **WO Milestone D Completion (Initial Analytics Hypercare Completion):** 10% of WO Fixed Fees (USD $38,500).

3.3.2 Each WO Milestone shall be deemed achieved upon the Client’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the associated WO Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.7 of the Parent SOW**.

3.4 **Invoice and Payment Terms.**

3.4.1 The Vendor shall issue invoices to the Client:

  (a) Upon achievement of each WO Milestone as described in Section 3.3;  

  (b) Monthly in arrears for Extended Support Fees under Section 3.2.2; and  

  (c) Monthly in arrears for any T&M work approved under the **Change Management** process in **Article XIII** of the Parent SOW and this Work Order.

3.4.2 All invoices under this Work Order shall reference both:

  - The Work Order Contract Reference No. **WO-SUM-202406-258**; and  
  - The Parent SOW Contract Reference No. **SOW-SUM-202406-236 (contract_236)**, 

and shall include reasonable supporting documentation, consistent with **Section 3.4.2** of the Parent SOW.

3.4.3 The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** of the date of a valid invoice (“**Net 30**”).

3.4.4 If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice, it shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the amount and basis of the dispute, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute. The Client shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.4.3. This process is aligned with **Section 3.4.4** of the Parent SOW.

3.5 **Late Payment.**

In the event that the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within the time required by Section 3.4.3, the Vendor may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate equal to the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until the date of actual payment. Late payment interest shall not apply to amounts disputed in good faith. This provision is consistent with **Section 3.5** of the Parent SOW.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

3.6.1 The Fees under this Work Order are exclusive of reasonable out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred by the Vendor in connection with on-site work specifically requested by the Client, consistent with **Section 3.6** of the Parent SOW.

3.6.2 Any such expenses shall be pre-approved in writing by the Client and reimbursed at cost, subject to the Client’s reasonable expense policies.

3.6.3 The Vendor shall provide receipts or other reasonable documentation for all reimbursable expenses.

3.7 **Taxes.**

Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.7** of the Parent SOW. For the avoidance of doubt, the Fees under this Work Order are exclusive of any applicable VAT or similar taxes, which shall be added where required by Applicable Law and paid by the Client.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **WO Term.**

This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until **25 January 2029** (the “**WO Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent SOW.

4.2 **Renewal.**

The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the WO Expiration Date, renew or extend the WO Term on such terms as the Parties may agree in writing. Any such renewal will be subject to the Parent SOW (as amended) remaining in force or being renewed on mutually agreed terms.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 The Client may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Vendor, consistent with the client-friendly right in **Section 4.3** of the Parent SOW.

4.3.2 The Vendor may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Client, provided that such termination shall not take effect prior to the completion of any critical analytics go-live or hypercare period then in progress, unless otherwise agreed by the Client, mirroring the intent of **Section 4.3** of the Parent SOW.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if:

4.4.1 The other Party commits a material breach of this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or

4.4.2 The conditions of insolvency, liquidation, administration, or cessation of business described in **Section 4.4.2** of the Parent SOW occur with respect to the other Party.

4.5 **Non-Payment and Suspension.**

If the Client fails to pay undisputed amounts owed under this Work Order and such failure continues for more than thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice from the Vendor, the Vendor may suspend performance of the WO Services and, if the failure is not cured within an additional thirty (30) days, may terminate this Work Order upon written notice, in line with **Section 4.5** of the Parent SOW. Any suspension shall be carried out in accordance with Applicable Law and with reasonable efforts to minimise disruption.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of Parent SOW.**

4.6.1 This Work Order is executed **under and subject to** the Parent SOW. In the event the Parent SOW is terminated or expires for any reason:

  (a) This Work Order shall automatically terminate on the same effective date of such termination or expiration, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to maintain this Work Order in force under an alternative governing agreement; and  

  (b) The effect of termination provisions in **Section 4.6** of the Parent SOW shall apply mutatis mutandis to this Work Order and the WO Services.

4.6.2 Upon termination or expiration of the Parent SOW and/or this Work Order, the Vendor shall cease all WO Services, except as necessary to provide any agreed transition assistance as described in Section 4.7.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**

At the Client’s request and expense (at T&M rates), the Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration of this Work Order, to facilitate the orderly transfer of the analytics and Extended Support Services to the Client or a replacement provider, including documentation, knowledge transfer, and data export. This is consistent with **Section 4.7 (Transition Assistance)** of the Parent SOW.

4.8 **Effects of Termination.**

Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:

4.8.1 The Client shall pay the Vendor for all WO Services duly performed and accepted and for approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to the WO Contract Value cap;

4.8.2 Each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information, in accordance with **Section 5.5** of the Parent SOW and Section 5.5 of this Work Order;

4.8.3 All licenses granted under **Article VI** of this Work Order and under **Article VI of the Parent SOW** shall terminate to the extent applicable to WO Deliverables, except as expressly stated to survive.

4.9 **Survival.**

The provisions of **Articles V–XI, XIV–XVI** of the Parent SOW and Articles V–XI, XIV–XVI of this Work Order, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive termination or expiration of this Work Order.
