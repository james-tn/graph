## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.** This Article II supplements and, to the extent inconsistent, supersedes the service descriptions in the Original Agreement. Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive systems integration, application development, data migration, and ongoing managed support services in connection with Client’s enterprise resource planning (ERP), customer relationship management (CRM), and associated cloud-based platforms (the **“Program”**).

2.2 **Detailed Description of Services.** The Services under this Amendment include, without limitation, the following components, each of which shall be further specified in one or more SOWs:

2.2.1 **Solution Design and Architecture.** Vendor shall:

  (a) Perform detailed requirements gathering, business process analysis, and system landscape assessments to understand Client’s current environment and target state;

  (b) Develop a comprehensive solution architecture and design documents, including integration diagrams, data flow maps, and security architecture incorporating industry-standard practices, with particular attention to compliance with Data Protection Laws;

  (c) Participate in design workshops with Client’s designated stakeholders and technical teams, and incorporate Client’s feedback into the final design documents;

  (d) Identify dependencies, constraints, and critical path elements, and propose risk mitigation strategies, including contingency plans for potential schedule or resource impacts.

2.2.2 **Implementation and Configuration Services.** Vendor shall:

  (a) Install, configure, and customize the agreed software platforms and related components in Client’s production and non-production environments, in accordance with the approved solution design;

  (b) Develop and configure necessary interfaces and integrations with Client’s existing systems, including legacy applications, data warehouses, and external third-party services, using agreed protocols and standards;

  (c) Implement role-based access controls, security policies, and audit logging consistent with Client’s internal policies and Applicable Law;

  (d) Conduct unit testing, system integration testing, and coordinate user acceptance testing (UAT) with Client, including the development of test scripts and documentation of test results.

2.2.3 **Data Migration Services.** Vendor shall:

  (a) Analyze Client’s legacy data structures, data quality, and data volumes relevant to the Program;

  (b) Design and implement data extraction, transformation, and loading (ETL) processes to migrate historical and current data from legacy systems into the target ERP and CRM systems;

  (c) Develop and execute data validation and reconciliation plans, ensuring that migrated data is accurate, complete, and consistent with Client’s specifications;

  (d) Provide data cleansing recommendations and, where agreed in writing, perform data cleansing activities, subject to additional fees if outside the initially agreed scope.

2.2.4 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall:

  (a) Develop training materials, user guides, configuration documentation, and operational runbooks tailored to Client’s environment;

  (b) Deliver training sessions (onsite or remote, as mutually agreed) for Client’s end users, super users, and technical support personnel;

  (c) Provide knowledge transfer workshops focusing on system administration, monitoring, and incident management processes, enabling Client to perform day-to-day operational tasks or to manage Vendor’s performance effectively.

2.2.5 **Managed Support and Maintenance.** For the duration of the Service Term, Vendor shall provide managed support and maintenance services, including:

  (a) Incident management and problem resolution in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII;

  (b) Monitoring, alerting, and performance tuning of the supported systems, including proactive identification of potential issues and recommended remediation steps;

  (c) Deployment of patches, updates, and minor releases to the supported systems as reasonably necessary to maintain security, functionality, and compliance, subject to Client’s change control procedures;

  (d) Periodic service reviews (at least quarterly) with Client to review performance reports, incident trends, and continuous improvement opportunities.

2.2.6 **Project Management and Governance.** Vendor shall:

  (a) Assign a project manager and, where appropriate, a service delivery manager as primary points of contact for Client;

  (b) Develop and maintain a project plan and timeline, including key milestones, deliverables, and dependencies, and provide regular status reports to Client;

  (c) Participate in regular governance meetings (e.g., steering committee, operational review) with Client to discuss risk, issues, changes, and performance against KPIs;

  (d) Coordinate with Client’s vendors and internal teams as reasonably required to deliver the Services.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the foregoing, the Deliverables under this Amendment include:

  (a) Requirements and solution design documents;  
  (b) System configuration and integration artifacts, including scripts, code repositories, and configuration logs;  
  (c) Data migration plans, ETL scripts, and validation reports;  
  (d) Test plans, test cases, test scripts, and test summary reports;  
  (e) Training materials, user guides, and runbooks;  
  (f) Monthly or quarterly performance and incident reports as specified in Article XII.

All Deliverables shall be provided in the formats and by the deadlines specified in the relevant SOWs, subject to any changes approved pursuant to Article XIII.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) In a professional and workmanlike manner, with due skill, care, and diligence consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;

  (b) In compliance with Applicable Law, Client’s reasonable written policies provided in advance, and the security and data protection requirements set forth in Article XI;

  (c) In accordance with the Service Levels and KPIs defined in Article XII and applicable SOWs. Persistent or material failure to meet Service Levels may constitute a material breach, subject to notice and cure under Section 4.4.

2.5 **Timeline and Milestones.** The Parties shall define detailed timelines, milestones, and acceptance criteria for the Services in each SOW. Unless otherwise specified:

  (a) Vendor shall provide to Client a proposed project schedule within fifteen (15) Business Days after the Effective Date or SOW execution;  

  (b) Milestones shall include, at a minimum: completion of solution design, completion of initial configuration, completion of data migration, UAT completion, go-live, and transition to steady-state operations;  

  (c) Delays caused primarily by Vendor shall be remedied at Vendor’s cost through additional resources or adjusted working hours, subject to mutual agreement and provided that such measures are commercially reasonable;  

  (d) Delays caused primarily by Client (including failure to provide timely approvals, access, or resources) shall entitle Vendor to equitable schedule and, where appropriate, fee adjustments in accordance with Article XIII.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total Contract Value for all Services and Deliverables provided under this Amendment is **US $3,790,464**, inclusive of all fixed and recurring fees agreed as of the Effective Date, but exclusive of any taxes, pass-through charges, and reimbursable expenses as described herein.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** Fees under this Amendment are comprised of:

  (a) **Implementation Fees.** A fixed-fee amount for design, implementation, configuration, integration, and data migration, as specified in the applicable SOW(s);  

  (b) **Managed Services Fees.** Recurring monthly or annual fees for ongoing support, maintenance, monitoring, and related services, as set forth in the applicable SOW(s);  

  (c) **Time-and-Materials Fees.** Fees for additional services not included in the fixed-fee scope, to be billed on a time-and-materials basis per Vendor’s rate card or as specified in the relevant SOW or Change Order.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.** Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Contract Value shall be invoiced as follows:

  (a) **Implementation Fees:**  
  - Twenty percent (20%) upon execution of this Amendment;  
  - Thirty percent (30%) upon completion and Client’s deemed acceptance of the solution design phase;  
  - Thirty percent (30%) upon completion of data migration and successful UAT;  
  - Twenty percent (20%) upon go-live and commencement of steady-state operations.

  (b) **Managed Services Fees:**  
  - Invoiced monthly in advance, based on the agreed monthly fee, commencing on the earlier of (i) the date of go-live or (ii) a date specified in the applicable SOW.

  (c) **Time-and-Materials Fees:**  
  - Invoiced monthly in arrears, accompanied by a summary of hours, roles, and tasks performed.

3.4 **Payment Terms.** All invoiced amounts are due and payable by Client within **thirty (30) calendar days** of the invoice date (**“Net 30”**), without setoff or withholding, except as expressly permitted under Section 3.6 (Disputed Amounts).

3.5 **Late Payment.** Any undisputed invoice amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid. Vendor shall provide written notice to Client of any past due amounts prior to imposing any suspension remedies. If Client fails to pay undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days after receiving such notice, Vendor may, upon an additional ten (10) days’ written notice, suspend provision of the Services until payment is received, provided such suspension is proportionate and Vendor resumes Services promptly following payment.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.** Client may in good faith dispute any portion of an invoice by providing written notice to Vendor within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days. Upon resolution, any disputed amount determined to be payable shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days, without interest if paid within such period.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.** Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in performing the Services (such as travel, lodging, and subsistence for onsite work) shall be reimbursed by Client at cost, subject to the following:

  (a) All travel must be pre-approved in writing by Client;  
  (b) Vendor shall comply with Client’s travel and expense policies as provided in advance in writing;  
  (c) Expense reimbursement requests shall be accompanied by reasonable supporting documentation, including receipts for individual expenses exceeding a threshold agreed by the Parties.

3.8 **Taxes.** All fees are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, and other similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for any such taxes arising from or in connection with the Services, excluding taxes on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees. If Vendor is required under Applicable Law to collect any such taxes, Vendor shall itemize them on its invoices.

3.9 **No Other Fees.** Except as expressly provided in this Amendment or in an SOW or Change Order executed by both Parties, no additional fees, charges, or costs shall be payable by Client. Vendor acknowledges that the Contract Value includes all fees necessary for Vendor to perform its obligations as described herein, subject to mutually agreed changes.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The Agreement, as amended by this Amendment, shall continue in full force and effect from the Effective Date through the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV. Certain Services may have shorter or longer service periods as specified in the applicable SOW, provided that no SOW shall extend beyond the Expiration Date unless the Parties execute a further written amendment.

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, engage in good faith discussions regarding an extension or renewal of the Agreement. Any renewal or extension must be set forth in a written amendment executed by both Parties. Absent such amendment, the Agreement shall automatically terminate on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate the Agreement, in whole or in part (including any or all SOWs), for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days** prior written notice to the other Party. Such termination shall not relieve Client of its obligation to pay for Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments incurred by Vendor with Client’s written approval.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

  (a) **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate the Agreement or any affected SOW for material breach by the other Party, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, specifying the breach in reasonable detail. The termination shall become effective at the end of such thirty (30) day period unless the breaching Party cures the breach within such period (or, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, begins curing during such period and diligently pursues cure to completion within an additional thirty (30) days).

  (b) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate the Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they become due; (ii) files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or similar proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of the Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) Vendor shall promptly cease all affected Services and shall not incur additional expenses or commitments on Client’s behalf, except as necessary for an orderly wind-down or as expressly requested by Client;  

  (b) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed fees accrued and payable under the Agreement for Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted up to the effective date of termination, subject to Section 3.6;  

  (c) Each Party shall return or, at the Disclosing Party’s option, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to any rights or obligations to retain data under Applicable Law or Article XI;  

  (d) Vendor shall cooperate reasonably with Client, at Client’s request and expense, to effectuate an orderly transition of Services to Client or a replacement provider, including limited transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days following termination, unless otherwise agreed in a transition SOW.

4.6 **Survival.** The rights and obligations of the Parties that by their nature should survive termination or expiration of the Agreement shall so survive, including without limitation accrued payment obligations, and the provisions of Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions).

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production systems supporting the managed Services achieve **99.9% Uptime** during each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and any downtime resulting from Client-caused issues or Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance during maintenance windows agreed with Client in advance. Vendor shall provide Client with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice of any scheduled maintenance that is reasonably expected to impact the availability or performance of the Services.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall respond to and work to resolve incidents in accordance with the following target timeframes:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical):**  
  - Description: Complete outage or critical impact on Client’s production operations with no workaround.  
  - Response Time: within one (1) hour of incident report.  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within four (4) hours.

  (b) **Severity 2 (High):**  
  - Description: Significant degradation of functionality with major impact on business operations; workaround available but not sustainable.  
  - Response Time: within two (2) hours.  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within one (1) Business Day.

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium):**  
  - Description: Partial loss of non-critical functionality or issues with limited business impact.  
  - Response Time: within four (4) Business Hours.  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within three (3) Business Days.

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low):**  
  - Description: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general service requests.  
  - Response Time: within one (1) Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within ten (10) Business Days or within a future scheduled release.

12.4 **Service Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  (a) Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly managed Services fees;  

  (b) Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly managed Services fees;  

  (c) Uptime below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly managed Services fees.

Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) issued to Client. Service credits are Client’s sole monetary remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to Client’s right to terminate for persistent or material breaches under Section 4.4.

12.5 **Exclusions.** SLA calculations shall exclude:

  (a) Downtime during Scheduled Maintenance;  

  (b) Downtime caused by Client’s acts or omissions or third-party services under Client’s control;  

  (c) Downtime due to Force Majeure Events.

### FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.** A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes (excluding strikes or labor disputes involving a Party’s own employees), embargoes, governmental restrictions or actions, failure of utilities or telecommunications, or widespread internet outages.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.** The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, its expected duration, and the anticipated impact on the Party’s ability to perform its obligations;  

  (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under the Agreement (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration affected by the event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance of key obligations, either Party may terminate the affected portions of the Services upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability (except for payment for Services performed prior to the effective date of termination).
