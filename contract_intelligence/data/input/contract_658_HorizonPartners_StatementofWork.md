## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Engagement.**  
Service Provider shall provide professional services to support Client’s digital transformation initiative, including but not limited to business process analysis, solution design, configuration and integration of selected technology platforms, data migration and analytics enablement, user training, and post-implementation support. The Project is intended to improve operational efficiency, reporting, and customer experience across Client’s organization.

2.2 **Detailed Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms of this SOW, Service Provider shall perform the following Services:

  2.2.1 **Discovery and Assessment.**  
  (a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and document reviews to understand Client’s current-state processes, systems, and pain points.  
  (b) Perform a gap analysis between current-state operations and Client’s stated business objectives.  
  (c) Produce a written **Current State Assessment & Gap Analysis Report** (a Deliverable) summarizing findings, risks, and recommendations.

  2.2.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.**  
  (a) Design a target-state solution architecture that addresses identified gaps and aligns with Client’s strategic objectives.  
  (b) Identify required systems, integrations, data flows, and security controls.  
  (c) Develop detailed functional and technical Specifications, including data models, integration specifications, and process flows.  
  (d) Deliver a **Solution Design & Architecture Document** for Client review and approval.

  2.2.3 **Configuration, Development, and Integration.**  
  (a) Configure the agreed technology platforms and tools based on the approved Specifications.  
  (b) Develop integrations between Client’s existing systems and the new solution, including APIs, ETL processes, and messaging interfaces.  
  (c) Implement agreed data transformation rules and mapping logic.  
  (d) Adhere to secure coding practices and Client’s security requirements, as reasonably communicated in writing.  
  (e) Provide periodic technical documentation, including configuration guides, integration diagrams, and deployment instructions.

  2.2.4 **Data Migration and Validation.**  
  (a) Assist Client in extracting data from legacy systems, cleansing and validating such data, and migrating it into the new environment.  
  (b) Design and document migration strategy, including phases, rollback procedures, and data quality checkpoints.  
  (c) Execute pilot migrations and final cutover migrations in coordination with Client.  
  (d) Provide a **Data Migration Summary Report** documenting migrated datasets, exceptions, and quality metrics.

  2.2.5 **Testing and Quality Assurance.**  
  (a) Develop test plans and test cases in collaboration with Client.  
  (b) Conduct unit, integration, system, and user acceptance testing support.  
  (c) Log, track, and remediate defects in coordination with Client’s teams.  
  (d) Provide a **Testing & Defect Resolution Report** summarizing test coverage, defects, and resolutions.

  2.2.6 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
  (a) Develop training materials, including user guides, quick reference documents, and training presentations.  
  (b) Conduct up to [__] on-site or virtual training sessions for Client’s end-users and administrators, as mutually agreed.  
  (c) Provide a **Knowledge Transfer & Training Completion Report** confirming topics covered and attendance.

  2.2.7 **Go-Live Support and Stabilization.**  
  (a) Provide go-live support during the initial production launch, including issue triage and incident resolution.  
  (b) Support system stabilization, performance tuning, and configuration adjustments as reasonably required.  
  (c) Prepare a **Go-Live & Stabilization Report** documenting issues encountered and resolutions.

  2.2.8 **Ongoing Support and Maintenance (Managed Services).**  
  (a) For the duration of the Term, provide Level 2 and Level 3 support for the implemented solution during agreed Business Hours, and after-hours emergency support as described in **Article XII**.  
  (b) Provide periodic maintenance releases, updates to configurations, and enhancements as requested and approved under **Article XIII (Change Management)**.  
  (c) Maintain documentation of support tickets, resolutions, and root-cause analyses.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Deliverables shall include, at a minimum, the following:

  (a) Current State Assessment & Gap Analysis Report  
  (b) Solution Design & Architecture Document  
  (c) Configuration & Integration Documentation  
  (d) Data Migration Strategy & Data Migration Summary Report(s)  
  (e) Testing & Defect Resolution Report  
  (f) Training Materials and Knowledge Transfer & Training Completion Report  
  (g) Go-Live & Stabilization Report  
  (h) Periodic Support Reports (e.g., monthly or quarterly), including SLA performance as per **Article XII**

Each Deliverable shall conform in all material respects to the Specifications, be internally coherent, and be delivered in industry-standard electronic formats (e.g., PDF, MS Office formats) unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.4 **Project Phases and Milestones.**  
The Project shall be structured in phases, with indicative timelines and Milestones as follows, subject to adjustment via Change Order:

  - Phase 1 – Discovery & Assessment: Approx. 6–8 weeks from Effective Date.  
    - Milestone 1: Delivery and Client acceptance (not unreasonably withheld) of Current State Assessment & Gap Analysis Report.

  - Phase 2 – Solution Design & Architecture: Approx. 8–10 weeks following Milestone 1.  
    - Milestone 2: Delivery and Client approval of Solution Design & Architecture Document.

  - Phase 3 – Configuration, Development & Integration: Approx. 16–20 weeks following Milestone 2.  
    - Milestone 3: Completion of core configurations and integrations and successful completion of system integration testing.

  - Phase 4 – Data Migration & Testing: Approx. 8–12 weeks following Milestone 3.  
    - Milestone 4: Successful completion of user acceptance testing and data migration pilots.

  - Phase 5 – Go-Live & Stabilization: Approx. 4–6 weeks following Milestone 4.  
    - Milestone 5: Production go-live and stabilization complete.

  - Phase 6 – Ongoing Support & Maintenance: Remainder of the Term following Milestone 5.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Service Provider shall:

  (a) Perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services;  
  (b) Use personnel with appropriate skill, experience, and qualifications to perform the Services;  
  (c) Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards as further described in **Article VII**;  
  (d) Use reasonable efforts to meet or exceed the Service Levels in **Article XII**; and  
  (e) Coordinate with Client’s designated project manager and stakeholders in good faith.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to relevant personnel, systems, facilities, and data as reasonably required for Service Provider to perform the Services;  
  (b) Designate a project manager to act as primary point of contact;

Either Party may terminate this SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) Materially breaches this SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or, if the breach is not reasonably capable of cure within thirty (30) days, fails to commence cure within such period and diligently pursue such cure) after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or  
  (b) Becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they become due, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed over substantially all of its assets, subject to applicable law.

Without limiting **Section 4.4**, if Service Provider fails to meet the Service Levels in **Article XII** in a material and repeated manner (e.g., failure to meet critical uptime or response time commitments in three (3) consecutive months or any four (4) months in a twelve (12)-month period), Client may, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice and opportunity to cure, terminate the Support & Maintenance portion of the Services without penalty.

4.6 **Effects of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this SOW for any reason:

  (a) Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the Services (except as reasonably required for transition assistance) and deliver to Client all completed and in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid or will pay in accordance with this SOW;

  (d) At Client’s request and subject to a mutually agreed fee schedule (unless termination is due to Service Provider’s uncured material breach), Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days to facilitate the orderly transfer of services to Client or a third-party provider.

4.7 **Survival.**

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Applicability.**  
This Article applies to the ongoing Support & Maintenance Services provided under **Section 2.2.8** for the production environment of the implemented solution.

12.2 **Availability/Uptime.**  
Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the supported production environment is available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding:

  (a) Scheduled maintenance windows, not to exceed four (4) hours per month, with at least seventy-two (72) hours’ advance notice to Client;  
  (b) Emergency maintenance required to address critical security vulnerabilities, with as much advance notice as reasonably practicable; and  
  (c) Downtime caused by Client’s acts or omissions, third-party vendors, or Force Majeure Events.

12.3 **Incident Classification and Response Times.**  
Service Provider shall respond to support incidents submitted by Client in accordance with the following guidelines:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of core production functionality impacting a majority of users with no workaround.  
    - Initial response within 1 hour; continuous work until workaround or resolution; status updates at least every 2 hours.

  - **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation of core functionality or major feature not working; workaround available but inefficient.  
    - Initial response within 4 hours; work during Business Hours until resolution; daily status updates.

  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical functionality issue or defect with limited business impact.  
    - Initial response within 1 Business Day; status updates as mutually agreed.

  - **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or enhancement requests.  
    - Initial response within 2 Business Days; resolution on a best-efforts basis and/or via planned releases.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.2** in any given calendar month (excluding permitted downtime), Client shall be entitled, upon written request within thirty (30) days of the end of that month, to the following service credits:

  - Uptime 99.0%–99.89%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly Support & Maintenance Fees;  
  - Uptime 98.0%–98.99%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly Support & Maintenance Fees;  
  - Uptime below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly Support & Maintenance Fees.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service credits are Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to Client’s termination rights under **Section 4.5**.

12.5 **Reporting.**  
Service Provider shall provide monthly reports summarizing system availability, incident volumes by priority, average response and resolution times, and any SLA breaches, as part of the support reports identified in **Section 2.3(h)**.

14.1 **Good Faith Negotiations.**  
In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this SOW (a **“Dispute”**), the Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute through informal discussions between the Parties’ project managers.

14.2 **Escalation.**  
If the Parties are unable to resolve the Dispute within fifteen (15) Business Days after written notice of the Dispute, either Party may escalate the matter to senior management. 14.3 **Mediation.**  
If the Dispute is not resolved through escalation within thirty (30) days, the Parties agree to attempt to resolve the Dispute through non-binding mediation before a mutually acceptable mediator located in **Dallas, Texas**. The costs of the mediator shall be shared equally, and each Party shall bear its own legal and other costs.

14.4 **Arbitration or Litigation.**  
If the Dispute is not resolved by mediation within sixty (60) days after appointment of the mediator, either Party may pursue its remedies:

  (a) Either by filing an action in the courts specified in **Section 16.4**; or  
  (b) By mutual written agreement, by submitting the Dispute to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) under its Commercial Arbitration Rules, with a single arbitrator, in Dallas, Texas.  

In the absence of such mutual agreement to arbitrate, the Dispute shall be resolved by litigation in accordance with **Section 16.4**.

14.5 **Injunctive Relief.**  
Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief, or other equitable relief, in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent or curtail actual or threatened misuse or disclosure of Confidential Information or infringement of Intellectual Property Rights.

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure Event.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving a Party’s own employees), acts or omissions of government authorities, failures of third-party telecommunications or hosting providers (not caused by the affected Party), or other similar events beyond the affected Party’s reasonable control.

15.2 **Effect of Force Majeure.**  
If a Party is prevented from performing its obligations under this SOW due to a Force Majeure Event, such obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that:

  (a) The affected Party gives written notice to the other Party within a reasonable time, describing the nature and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event; and  
  (b) The affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this SOW upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability except for payment of Fees for Services performed and Deliverables accepted prior to the effective date of termination.
