## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Vendor.**  
Client hereby engages Vendor, and Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described in this Agreement and in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties from time to time during the Term. Each SOW shall be deemed incorporated herein by reference upon execution.

2.2 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide professional consulting services to support Client’s strategic and operational initiatives. By way of illustration and not limitation, such Services may include:

  a. **Strategic Advisory Services.** Analysis of Client’s business operations, markets, and competitive landscape; development of strategic roadmaps; recommendations regarding operational improvements, cost efficiencies, and growth opportunities; and facilitation of workshops with Client’s executive and management teams.

  b. **Technical Consulting and Implementation.** Assessment of Client’s technology infrastructure and systems; design and configuration of solutions; integration with third-party systems; development of implementation plans; support for user acceptance testing; and assistance with go-live and post-implementation stabilization activities.

  c. **Process Optimization and Change Management.** Mapping and analysis of Client’s key business processes; identification of bottlenecks and inefficiencies; design and documentation of improved processes; and support for change management, including communication plans, training content, and operational handbooks.

  d. **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Development of training curricula, user guides, and reference materials; delivery of in-person and remote training sessions; and structured knowledge transfer to Client’s designated personnel to facilitate ongoing support and internal ownership of implemented solutions.

  e. **Project Management.** Provision of project management resources to coordinate activities, manage tasks and timelines, track dependencies, report status, identify and manage risks and issues, and collaborate with Client’s stakeholders and third-party vendors.

2.3 **Specific Deliverables and Milestones.**  
The specific Deliverables and associated milestones for each engagement shall be defined in one or more SOWs. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify:

  a. The detailed description of each Deliverable to be produced, including format, content, and acceptance criteria;

  b. The timeline and key milestones for completion of the Services and delivery of Deliverables;

  c. Any dependencies on Client resources, systems, or third-party participation;

  d. The Fees and payment schedule applicable to the Services and Deliverables under such SOW; and

  e. Any additional assumptions, constraints, or special terms applicable to that SOW.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  a. In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry best practices and standards of care generally applicable to reputable consulting firms providing similar services;

  b. In accordance with the timeline and milestones specified in the applicable SOW, subject to **Article XIII (Change Management)** and any Force Majeure Events described in **Article XV (Force Majeure)**;

  c. In compliance with all Applicable Laws and all reasonable written policies and procedures of Client provided to Vendor in advance, including security, health and safety, and workplace conduct policies, when Vendor’s personnel are on Client’s premises or accessing Client systems;

  d. Using appropriately skilled and qualified personnel, including consultants, employees, and subcontractors (if any), who have adequate training and experience relevant to the Services.

2.5 **Personnel and Subcontractors.**  
Vendor shall be responsible for the acts and omissions of all its personnel and subcontractors. Vendor shall not subcontract any material portion of the Services without Client’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Any approved subcontracting shall not relieve Vendor of any of its obligations or liabilities under this Agreement.

2.6 **Client Cooperation and Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to successfully perform the Services and meet the timelines may depend upon Client’s timely cooperation and the accuracy and completeness of information supplied by Client. Accordingly, Client shall:

  a. Provide Vendor with access to relevant personnel, systems, facilities, and information as reasonably required for Vendor to perform the Services;

  b. Designate a primary contact person or project manager to coordinate with Vendor and facilitate communications;

  c. Review Deliverables and other materials provided by Vendor in a timely manner and provide feedback, approvals, or instructions as reasonably requested by Vendor;

  d. Obtain, at its own cost, any third-party licenses, consents, or approvals necessary for Vendor to perform the Services using Client’s systems or data, unless expressly stated otherwise in the applicable SOW.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless an SOW specifies a different acceptance process, the following shall apply:

  a. Upon delivery of a Deliverable, Vendor shall notify Client in writing (email sufficient) that such Deliverable has been delivered and is ready for review.

  b. Client shall have **ten (10) Business Days** (the “Review Period”) from receipt of the Deliverable to review and either (i) provide written acceptance, or (ii) provide a written notice of rejection specifying in reasonable detail the material deficiencies in the Deliverable with reference to the applicable acceptance criteria.

  c. If Client fails to respond within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the last day of the Review Period.

  d. If Client properly rejects a Deliverable, Vendor shall, at no additional cost to Client, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified deficiencies and re-deliver the Deliverable for Client’s review within a mutually agreed timeframe. The foregoing review and acceptance process shall then reapply.

2.8 **Location of Services.**  
Vendor may perform the Services remotely, at Vendor’s facilities, at Client’s premises, or at other locations as reasonably necessary and agreed between the Parties. When performing Services at Client’s premises, Vendor personnel shall comply with all reasonable site rules and safety requirements communicated to Vendor in advance.

2.9 **Statements of Work Hierarchy.**  
In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of the SOW shall control solely with respect to the subject matter of such SOW, unless explicitly stated otherwise in the SOW. In all other respects, the terms of this Agreement shall govern.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the estimated total value of this Agreement is **US$3,126,526**. This amount represents the aggregate Fees for all Services and Deliverables anticipated as of the Effective Date. Actual Fees incurred may vary based on the scope of Services actually ordered and performed under executed SOWs and any Change Orders, provided that the total contracted value shall not exceed US$3,126,526 without a signed amendment or Change Order.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Fees may consist of a combination of:

  a. **Fixed-Price Fees** for specific Deliverables or defined work packages;

  b. **Time-and-Materials Fees** based on Vendor’s hourly or daily rates for personnel, as specified in the applicable SOW; and

  c. **Performance-Based or Milestone Fees** contingent upon Vendor’s completion of defined milestones or achievement of agreed performance targets.

3.3 **Rate Schedules.**  
Vendor’s standard consulting rates as of the Effective Date may be included in an exhibit or SOW. Vendor may propose adjustments to such rates on an annual basis; however, any rate increase shall not apply unless agreed in writing by Client through an amendment or new SOW.

3.4 **Invoicing.**  
Vendor shall invoice Client for Fees:

  a. Monthly in arrears for time-and-materials Services, based on documented hours or days worked;

  b. Upon completion and Client’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of specific Deliverables or milestones for fixed-price or milestone-based components, as specified in the applicable SOW; and

  c. For reimbursable expenses, in accordance with **Section 3.7 (Expenses and Reimbursements)**.

Each invoice shall reference this Agreement, the relevant SOW, the billing period, and provide reasonable detail regarding the Services performed, personnel involved, hours worked (for time-and-materials), Deliverables completed, and expenses incurred.

3.5 **Payment Terms; Net 30.**  
Client shall pay all undisputed amounts on each invoice within **thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (“Net 30”), via wire transfer, ACH, check, or other mutually agreed payment method. Payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) unless otherwise specified.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within **fifteen (15) days** of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute and the amount in dispute. Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such disputes within a reasonable period. Any amounts ultimately agreed or determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) days after resolution.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  a. Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, actual, out-of-pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred in performing the Services, provided such expenses are incurred in accordance with Client’s travel and expense policies communicated to Vendor in advance and are pre-approved in writing by Client for any single trip anticipated to exceed US$2,500.

  b. Vendor shall submit itemized expense reports with supporting receipts and documentation along with or separate from its invoices.

  c. Vendor shall not be reimbursed for overhead costs such as office rent, standard equipment, normal commuting expenses, or general administrative expenses.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
Fees exclude all Taxes. Client shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes arising from the Services or Deliverables, excluding any Taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employment-related taxes for Vendor’s personnel. If Vendor is required under Applicable Law to collect or withhold Taxes from amounts payable by Client, Vendor may add such Taxes to the invoice, and Client shall pay such amounts, unless Client provides a valid tax exemption certificate.

3.9 **Late Payment; Interest.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month**, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until the date of payment. Vendor’s right to interest shall be without prejudice to any other rights or remedies it may have under this Agreement or at law.

3.10 **No Setoff.**  
Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or required by Applicable Law, neither Party shall set off, withhold, or reduce any payment or other amount owed to the other Party under this Agreement based on any alleged claim, counterclaim, or set-off.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **September 25, 2026** (the “Initial Term” or “Expiration Date”). Any extension or renewal of this Agreement shall require a written amendment executed by both Parties.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including with respect to any SOW), for convenience and without cause upon not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. The Parties agree that this notice period is intended to be commercially reasonable and balanced given the medium risk profile of this engagement.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  a. Materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or

  b. Becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed for its assets, or otherwise ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

4.4 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:

  a. Vendor shall promptly cease performing the applicable Services, except to the extent reasonably requested by Client to assist in transitioning Services in accordance with **Section 4.5 (Transition Assistance)**.

  b. Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted (or deemed accepted) up to the effective date of termination, and for all approved, unreimbursed expenses incurred prior to such date, in accordance with **Article III**.

  c. Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession as set forth in **Section 5.5 (Return or Destruction)**.

4.5 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon Client’s request in connection with any termination or expiration, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to **sixty (60) days** (or such shorter period as Client may request) to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Client or a third-party service provider. Such assistance shall be provided at Vendor’s then-current standard rates (or as otherwise agreed) and subject to the terms of this Agreement.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions)**, together with any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“Confidential Information” means any non-public or proprietary information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (the “Receiving Party”), whether orally or in writing, and whether disclosed before or after the Effective Date, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business and marketing plans, financial information, pricing, customer and supplier lists, technical data, product plans, software, source code, inventions, know-how, processes, designs, trade secrets, and any information concerning the Disclosing Party’s operations, strategies, or personnel.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  a. Is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

  b. Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

  c. Is received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or

  d. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  a. Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;

  b. Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent; and

  c. Protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information:

  a. To its and its Affiliates’ employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those set forth herein; and

  b. To the extent required by Applicable Law, court order, or governmental authority, provided that (to the extent legally permitted) the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates reasonably with the Disclosing Party’s efforts (at the Disclosing Party’s cost) to seek protective measures or otherwise limit the scope of disclosure.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control and certify in writing such destruction, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) copies required to be retained under Applicable Law or professional standards, and (b) archival copies maintained solely for legal, regulatory, or compliance purposes. Any retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The obligations in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets, which shall remain subject to confidentiality obligations for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, which could not have been prevented by reasonable precautions, and which materially interferes with or delays such Party’s performance of its obligations under this Agreement. Force Majeure Events may include, but are not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own employees), acts of government, widespread internet or telecommunications failures not caused by the affected Party, and other similar events.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  a. Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, including reasonable details of its nature, expected duration, and impact on performance;

  b. Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and

  c. Keep the other Party reasonably informed regarding progress in mitigating the Force Majeure Event.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations that accrued prior to the Force Majeure Event) shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are directly affected by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Prolonged Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event affecting a Party continues for more than **sixty (60) consecutive days**, either Party may terminate the affected Services or SOW by providing written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for payment of Fees due for Services performed prior to the effective date of termination.
