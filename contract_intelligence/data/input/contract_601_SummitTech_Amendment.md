# AMENDMENT TO MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT  
**Contract Reference Number: AMD-SUM-202508-601**

This Amendment, reference number **AMD-SUM-202508-601** (this “**Amendment**”), is made and entered into as of **February 17, 2026** (the “**Amendment Effective Date**”), by and between:

- **Contoso Enterprises, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at 4100 Contoso Parkway, Wilmington, DE 19801, USA (“**Contoso**”, the “**Client**” or the “**Company**”), and  

- **Summit Tech Solutions, LLC**, a Delaware limited liability company, with its principal place of business at 920 Summit Avenue, Suite 1400, Newark, DE 19711, USA (“**Summit Tech**”, the “**Vendor**” or the “**Service Provider**”).

Contoso and Summit Tech are sometimes referred to individually as a “**Party**” and collectively as the “**Parties**.”

This Amendment relates to and modifies that certain **Master Services Agreement** bearing Contract Reference Number **MSA-SUM-202508-578**, file identifier **contract_578** (the “**Master Services Agreement**,” “**MSA**” or the “**Parent Agreement**”), dated **August 15, 2025** (the “Effective Date” of the Parent Agreement), entered into by the same Parties.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties entered into the **Master Services Agreement**, reference number **MSA-SUM-202508-578**, file identifier **contract_578**, effective as of **August 15, 2025**, pursuant to which the Service Provider agreed to provide certain information technology, software development, systems integration, maintenance, support, and related professional services to the Company, as more particularly described in **Article II (Scope of Services/Work)** of the Parent Agreement and the Statements of Work (“SOWs”) executed thereunder;

WHEREAS, the Parent Agreement established an initial aggregate contract value of **US $2,355,293** pursuant to **Section 3.1 (Contract Value)**, subject to adjustment through additional SOWs and Change Orders entered into under **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement;

WHEREAS, the Parties now desire to (a) augment and expand the scope of Services under the Parent Agreement, (b) establish an additional committed program of work with an incremental contract value of **US $4,497,607**, (c) update certain commercial and risk allocation provisions as they apply specifically to this Amendment, and (d) clarify the relationship of such work to the Parent Agreement, including the **High-Risk Engagement** designation in **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement;

WHEREAS, the Parties intend that this Amendment shall constitute a written modification executed pursuant to **Section 16.3 (Amendments)** of the Parent Agreement, and that it shall be read and construed together with the Parent Agreement, except as expressly modified herein;

WHEREAS, the Parties further acknowledge that this Amendment establishes a distinct program with a reduced overall risk profile (designated **“Low Risk”** for purposes of this Amendment only) relative to the “High Risk” designation under **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement, and therefore the Parties desire to adopt more **Client-favorable terms**, including a contractual liability cap, a narrower exclusion of damages, and shorter termination notice periods, solely with respect to Services and Deliverables performed and provided under this Amendment;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained in this Amendment, the Parent Agreement, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Reference to Master/Parent Agreement.** The general scope framework, engagement model, and definitions of “Services,” “Deliverables,” and “SOW” set forth in **Article II (Scope of Services/Work)** of the Parent Agreement are hereby incorporated by reference and shall apply to the Program Services and Program Deliverables, except as modified herein.

2.2 **Program Objective.** The purpose of this Amendment is to establish a multi-phase, enterprise-wide modernization and optimization initiative for the Company’s line-of-business applications, analytics infrastructure, and support processes (the “**Program**”). The Program shall include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 The design, development, and deployment of a consolidated application platform integrating existing Contoso systems and select third-party systems;

  2.2.2 The implementation of enhanced monitoring, incident management, and support processes to deliver improved availability and performance of critical applications;

  2.2.3 The provision of knowledge transfer, documentation, and training to enable the Company to effectively use, administer, and support the Program Deliverables.

2.3 **Components of Program Services.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Program Services shall include the following categories, each to be further detailed in one or more Program SOWs:

  2.3.1 **Program Governance & Advisory Services.**  
  (a) Establishment of a joint governance structure, including steering committees and project management offices;  
  (b) Roadmap definition, prioritization of workstreams, and alignment with Contoso’s strategic objectives;  
  (c) Risk, dependency, and stakeholder management.

  2.3.2 **Software Development and Integration.**  
  (a) Design and development of new application modules, microservices, APIs, and interfaces to integrate with existing Contoso systems and third-party platforms, consistent with **Section 2.3.2** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (b) Configuration and customization of commercial off-the-shelf software (where permitted under applicable license terms), consistent with **Section 2.3.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (c) Development of data transformation scripts, middleware, and connectors to support end-to-end business processes.

  2.3.3 **Implementation, Migration, and Cut-over.**  
  (a) Environment setup, configuration, and hardening in accordance with Company security policies and **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment;  
  (b) Data migration, data cleansing, data quality checks, and validations, consistent with **Section 2.3.3(b)** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (c) Cut-over planning, rehearsals, and go-live support for transitions from legacy systems to Program Deliverables.

  2.3.4 **Managed Services, Maintenance, and Support.**  
  (a) Provision of Level 2 and Level 3 support services for Program Deliverables and designated legacy systems, including incident management and problem resolution, consistent with **Section 2.3.4(a)** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (b) Preventive maintenance, patching, minor enhancements, and continuous improvement;  
  (c) Monitoring of system performance and availability, with targets and remedies established in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of this Amendment;  
  (d) Operation of a service desk interface or integration to the Company’s service desk tools, as further defined in the applicable Program SOW.

  2.3.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
  (a) Development and delivery of training curriculum, manuals, and e-learning modules tailored to end-users, administrators, and support personnel, consistent with **Section 2.3.5** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (b) Conduct of train-the-trainer sessions and shadowing programs;  
  (c) Delivery of operational runbooks and system administration guides.

2.4 **Program SOWs; Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

  2.4.1 Each Program SOW shall: (a) reference both the Parent Agreement by its contract reference number **MSA-SUM-202508-578** and this Amendment by its contract reference number **AMD-SUM-202508-601**; (b) specify the applicable Program Services and Program Deliverables; (c) define milestones, acceptance criteria, and timelines consistent with **Section 2.2 (Statements of Work)** and **Section 2.4 (Deliverables; Acceptance)** of the Parent Agreement; and (d) set forth Fees, rate cards, and payment terms consistent with **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

  2.4.2 In the event of conflict between (i) the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, this Amendment shall control solely with respect to the Program and only to the extent of such conflict; and (ii) this Amendment and any Program SOW, the Program SOW shall control **only if** it expressly states that a specific provision of this Amendment is intended to supersede such provision and references the relevant section, consistent with **Section 2.2(b)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

  2.5.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Program Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards, consistent with **Section 2.5(a)** of the Parent Agreement, and in compliance with Applicable Law and the Company’s documented security, privacy, and compliance policies where provided in writing.

  2.5.2 The Service Provider shall assign appropriately skilled personnel and may use subcontractors in accordance with **Section 2.5(b)** of the Parent Agreement; provided, however, that (a) no subcontractor shall materially increase the risk to Client Data or Personal Data; and (b) any subcontractors with access to Client Data shall be subject to written obligations no less protective than those in **Articles V and XI** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.** The Company’s responsibilities under **Section 2.6 (Company Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to the Program, including timely provision of information, access, approvals, and decisions. The Service Provider shall not be liable for delays or failures in performance to the extent caused by the Company’s failure to fulfill its responsibilities, provided the Service Provider uses reasonable efforts to mitigate resulting impacts.

2.7 **Risk Designation for Program.** Notwithstanding the “High Risk” designation in **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties agree that the Program performed under this Amendment is designated **“Low Risk”**. As a result, and solely with respect to the Program:  

  (a) The **liability framework** in **Article IX** of the Parent Agreement (including the absence of a contractual cap and the availability of consequential and punitive damages) is hereby replaced by the Updated Liability Regime in **Article IX** of this Amendment; and  

  (b) The Parties acknowledge that the Fees and Amendment Contract Value reflect this revised risk allocation and that, absent the liability cap and related provisions in this Amendment, the commercial terms (including pricing) would have been materially different.

2.8 **Changes to Scope.** Any change to the Program scope, including new workstreams, modified timelines, or incremental budget, shall be processed via the Change Management process in **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XIII** of this Amendment. The Service Provider shall have no obligation to commence work on any requested change until a written Change Order is executed by both Parties.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Amendment Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that the aggregate committed value for the Program under this Amendment is **Four Million Four Hundred Ninety-Seven Thousand Six Hundred Seven U.S. Dollars (US $4,497,607)** (the “**Amendment Contract Value**”). This amount is in addition to, and shall not limit or reduce, the initial aggregate contract value of **US $2,355,293** set forth in **Section 3.1** of the Parent Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure for Program.** Unless otherwise specified in a Program SOW, Fees under this Amendment may include:

  3.2.1 **Fixed-Price Components.** Fixed fees for defined Program Deliverables or phases (e.g., platform build, initial integrations), payable upon completion and acceptance of corresponding milestones as defined in the relevant Program SOW.

  3.2.2 **Time and Materials (T&M).** T&M charges for advisory, change-requested work, and incremental enhancements, calculated based on the Service Provider’s standard rates as stated in the applicable Program SOW. Rate escalations, if any, shall be expressly set forth in such Program SOW and shall not exceed an annual increase of [●]% without mutual written agreement.

  3.2.3 **Milestone-Based Fees.** Milestone payments tied to achievements such as completion of design, code freeze, system integration testing, and production go-live, as further specified in each Program SOW.

  3.2.4 **Recurring Fees.** Monthly or annual recurring Fees for managed services, hosting, maintenance, and support, in each case as described in the applicable Program SOW and subject to adjustment in accordance with the Change Management provisions of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**  

  3.3.1 **Net 45.** In accordance with **Section 3.3(a)** of the Parent Agreement and the Contract Parameters, all undisputed invoices issued under this Amendment shall be payable under **Net 45** terms, i.e., within forty-five (45) calendar days from the invoice date.

  3.3.2 **Invoicing.** The Service Provider shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears for T&M Fees and recurring Fees, and upon achievement of defined milestones for fixed-price components, subject to any acceptance criteria and Acceptance Periods consistent with **Section 2.4 (Deliverables; Acceptance)** of the Parent Agreement, as modified or supplemented in the applicable Program SOW.

  3.3.3 **Disputed Amounts.** The Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing of any disputed portion of an invoice within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the reasons for the dispute, consistent with **Section 3.3(c)** of the Parent Agreement. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such disputes. The Company shall timely pay all undisputed portions of such invoices.

3.4 **Late Payment.** Any undisputed amount that remains unpaid after the due date shall accrue interest at the rate specified in **Section 3.4 (Late Payment)** of the Parent Agreement (i.e., the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law) until paid in full. The Service Provider’s right to suspend Program Services and withhold Program Deliverables for non-payment, as set forth in **Section 3.4** of the Parent Agreement, shall apply equally to this Amendment; provided that the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize adverse impacts to critical business operations.

3.5 **Taxes.** The tax allocation provisions in **Section 3.5 (Taxes)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to all Fees invoiced under this Amendment. The Company shall remain responsible for applicable taxes (other than taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or employees), subject to the Company’s provision of valid exemption certificates where applicable.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.** The Parties incorporate by reference **Section 3.6 (Expenses and Reimbursement)** of the Parent Agreement. The Company shall reimburse reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Program Services. Each Program SOW may define specific travel policies, per diems, or caps on reimbursable expenses.

3.7 **No Withholding as Exclusive Remedy.** The prohibition on withholding, offsetting, or delaying payment of undisputed Fees as a remedy, as set forth in **Section 3.7** of the Parent Agreement, shall apply to all Fees under this Amendment, without prejudice to the Company’s rights and remedies under **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Amendment Term.** This Amendment shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated as provided herein, shall remain in effect until **May 19, 2027** (the “**Amendment Expiration Date**”).

4.2 **Renewal of Program.** The Parties may extend the Amendment Term or agree on successor terms for the Program by executing a written amendment or renewal agreement not later than sixty (60) days prior to the Amendment Expiration Date. Any such renewal may include revised pricing, SLAs, or other terms, consistent with **Section 4.2 (Renewal)** of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 **By the Company.** Notwithstanding the one hundred eighty (180) day notice period for termination for convenience in **Section 4.3(a)** of the Parent Agreement, the Company may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part (including with respect to one or more Program SOWs), for convenience at any time upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider.

  4.3.2 **By the Service Provider.** The Service Provider may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing the Company with at least **ninety (90) days’** prior written notice, which represents a shorter notice period than that set forth in **Section 4.3(b)** of the Parent Agreement and reflects the Low Risk Designation and the Client-favorable nature of this Amendment.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Amendment or any affected Program SOW for material breach by the other Party, upon written notice specifying the nature of the breach and providing thirty (30) days to cure such breach (the “**Amendment Cure Period**”). If the breach is not cured within the Amendment Cure Period, termination shall be effective upon written notice following the expiration of such period. The immediate termination rights for insolvency, bankruptcy, or material breaches of **Article V (Confidentiality)** or **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, as described in **Section 4.4(b)** of the Parent Agreement, shall apply equally to this Amendment.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment or any Program SOW for any reason:

  4.5.1 The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Program Services performed and Program Deliverables provided through the effective date of termination, including unpaid Fees, approved expenses, and reasonable committed costs, consistent with **Section 4.5(a)** of the Parent Agreement. In the case of termination by the Company for convenience, the Company shall also reimburse reasonable wind-down costs actually incurred by the Service Provider, not to exceed an amount agreed in the applicable Program SOW or, absent such agreement, a reasonable amount determined in good faith by the Parties.

  4.5.2 Each Party shall return or securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control in accordance with **Section 5.5 (Return or Destruction)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article V** of this Amendment, subject to any legal retention requirements and data protection obligations in **Article XI**.

  4.5.3 The Service Provider shall provide Transition Services to effect an orderly transition of the Program Services to the Company or a third-party provider, consistent with **Section 4.5(c)** of the Parent Agreement. The scope, duration, and Fees for Transition Services shall be defined in a written plan agreed by the Parties, which may be attached to the applicable Program SOW or a separate transition SOW.

4.6 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Termination.**  

  4.6.1 If the **Parent Agreement** is terminated or expires, this Amendment shall automatically terminate as of the effective date of such termination or expiration, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing that certain Program SOWs shall survive under alternative arrangements.

  4.6.2 If this Amendment is terminated in whole, such termination shall not, by itself, terminate the Parent Agreement or any other SOWs entered into under the Parent Agreement that are not designated as Program SOWs, unless expressly stated otherwise in the termination notice or a subsequent written agreement executed by both Parties.

4.7 **Survival.** Any provision of this Amendment that by its nature should survive expiration or termination, including but not limited to accrued payment obligations, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and Sections 4.5–4.7, shall survive in accordance with their terms and in alignment with **Section 4.6 (Survival)** of the Parent Agreement.

12.1 **General.** The baseline Service Levels described in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement apply to the Program, subject to the enhancements and Updated SLA Credits described in this Article XII.

  (a) For any production systems or applications hosted, managed, or supported by the Service Provider as part of the Program, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure availability of at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure Events as defined in **Article XV** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.  

  (b) “Scheduled Maintenance” shall, where practicable, be conducted during off-peak hours and shall be communicated to the Company at least five (5) Business Days in advance.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution.** The response and resolution targets in **Section 12.3** of the Parent Agreement apply, with the following clarifications:

  - Priority 1 (Critical): initial response within 1 hour, target resolution or workaround within 8 hours;  
  - Priority 2 (High): response within 4 hours, target resolution or workaround within 24 hours;  
  - Priority 3 (Medium): response within 1 Business Day, target resolution within 5 Business Days;  
  - Priority 4 (Low): response within 2 Business Days; resolution on a commercially reasonable efforts basis.

Each Program SOW may refine or supplement these targets for specific services or systems.

12.4 **SLA Credits / Remedies (Updated for Program).**  

  (a) If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitments under Section 12.2 for a given calendar month for a Program Service, the Company shall be eligible for **service credits** calculated as a percentage of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Program Service as follows:

  - Uptime between 99.0% and 99.89%: **5%** credit;  
  - Uptime between 98.0% and 98.99%: **10%** credit;  
  - Uptime below 98.0%: **20%** credit.

  (b) Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and are not redeemable for cash. The Company must request credits in writing within thirty (30) days following the month in which the SLA breach occurred.

  (c) Service credits constitute the Company’s exclusive monetary remedy for SLA breaches related to availability and response/resolution times for the Program, but do not limit the Company’s rights to terminate for chronic SLA failures that constitute a material breach under **Article IV**.

12.5 **Exclusions.** SLA commitments do not apply to issues resulting from factors outside the Service Provider’s reasonable control (including Force Majeure), the Company’s actions or inactions, unsupported configurations, or Scheduled Maintenance, consistent with **Section 12.5** of the Parent Agreement.
