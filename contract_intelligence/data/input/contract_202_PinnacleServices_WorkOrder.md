## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 The Service Provider shall perform, on a professional and diligent basis, a combination of consulting, implementation, integration, and managed services in support of the Company’s enterprise digital platform and associated systems (collectively, the **“Services”**). The Services are intended to support the Company’s operations in North America and, where mutually agreed, other jurisdictions.

2.1.2 The Services under this Work Order **WO-PIN-202506-202** shall include, without limitation:

- Enterprise platform assessment and solution design;
- Systems integration services, including API development and middleware configuration;
- Data migration and transformation services;
- Implementation and configuration of monitoring and alerting tools;
- Ongoing managed services and support, including Level 1–3 support;
- Periodic performance optimization and capacity planning; and
- Knowledge transfer and training to Company personnel.

### 2.2 Detailed Service Components

2.2.1 **Phase I – Discovery and Design (Months 1–3 after Effective Date)**  
The Service Provider shall:

(a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, system audits, and requirements workshops to document business and technical requirements;  
(b) Produce a detailed **Solution Architecture Document**, including integration diagrams, data flow maps, and security controls;  
(c) Deliver a **Project Plan** setting forth milestones, dependencies, resource assignments, and risk mitigation strategies; and  
(d) Provide a **Requirements Traceability Matrix** linking business requirements to proposed technical solutions.

All outputs under Phase I shall be considered Deliverables and subject to the review and acceptance process set forth in **Section 2.6**.

2.2.2 **Phase II – Implementation and Integration (Months 4–14)**  
The Service Provider shall:

(a) Implement and configure integration components, including the development of APIs and middleware connectors to the Company’s CRM, ERP, and data warehouse systems;  
(b) Perform data migration, mapping, and validation activities, ensuring integrity and completeness of migrated data;  
(c) Configure monitoring tools and dashboards to track system performance and log key events;  
(d) Conduct unit, integration, and user acceptance testing (UAT) pursuant to test plans approved by the Company; and  
(e) Prepare and deliver **Operational Runbooks** and **Support Procedures** for ongoing managed services.

2.2.3 **Phase III – Transition to Managed Services (Months 15–21)**  
The Service Provider shall:

(a) Establish a Service Desk function conforming to the SLAs set forth in **Article XII**, including incident intake, triage, escalation, and resolution processes;  
(b) Provide onboarding, shadowing, and reverse-shadowing sessions to transition activities from the implementation teams to the managed services teams;  
(c) Deliver a **Transition Completion Report** summarizing completion criteria, outstanding risks, and remediation plans; and  
(d) Commence steady-state support in accordance with **Section 2.3**.

2.2.4 **Phase IV – Steady-State Managed Services (Months 22–Expiration Date)**  
The Service Provider shall:

(a) Provide continuous monitoring, incident management, problem management, and service request fulfillment;  
(b) Conduct periodic preventive maintenance and system health checks;  
(c) Perform capacity planning, performance tuning, and cost optimization analyses, providing quarterly recommendations;  
(d) Support minor enhancements and configuration changes approved through the change management process under **Article XIII**; and  
(e) Participate in quarterly governance meetings with the Company to review performance, risks, and improvement initiatives.

### 2.3 Service Hours and Service Locations

2.3.1 **Service Hours.** Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the Service Provider shall provide:

- Standard support coverage during **Business Days, 8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Pacific Time** (“Standard Hours”); and  
- On-call coverage outside Standard Hours for Severity 1 incidents, as further detailed in **Article XII**.

2.3.2 **Service Locations.** The Services shall be performed remotely from the Service Provider’s facilities and, as reasonably requested by the Company, on-site at Company locations, including but not limited to the San Jose, California headquarters. On-site work shall be scheduled in advance and subject to the expense reimbursement provisions of **Section 3.5**.

### 2.4 Deliverables

The Service Provider shall produce and deliver at least the following key Deliverables (non-exhaustive list):

(a) Solution Architecture Document;  
(b) Detailed Project Plan and Requirements Traceability Matrix;  
(c) Test Plans and Test Summary Reports;  
(d) Operational Runbooks and Support Procedures;  
(e) Transition Completion Report;  
(f) Quarterly Performance Reports and Capacity Planning Analyses;  
(g) Training materials and documentation for Company personnel; and  
(h) Any additional Deliverables defined in mutually executed Change Orders.

Each Deliverable shall conform to the Specifications agreed upon by the Parties and be provided in industry-standard formats (e.g., PDF, Word, Excel, or other formats as agreed).

### 2.5 Performance Standards

2.5.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for providers of similar services, and in accordance with the Specifications, Service Levels, and timelines set forth in this Agreement.

2.5.2 The Service Provider shall comply with all Applicable Laws, including Data Protection Laws, in the performance of the Services and handling of any Client Materials or Personal Data.

2.5.3 The Service Provider shall ensure that all personnel assigned to provide the Services possess appropriate skills, training, and experience for the tasks assigned.

2.5.4 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet all milestones and deadlines specified herein. If any delays are anticipated, the Service Provider shall notify the Company promptly in writing under **Section 16.7 (Notices)**, including a root cause analysis and proposed mitigation plan.

### 2.6 Acceptance of Deliverables

2.6.1 Upon delivery of any Deliverable, the Company shall have **fifteen (15) Business Days** (the **“Review Period”**) to review and either:

(a) Provide written notice of acceptance; or  
(b) Provide written notice of material non-conformity with the Specifications, including reasonably detailed descriptions of such non-conformities.

2.6.2 If the Company fails to provide any such notice within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the end of the Review Period.

2.6.3 In the event of non-acceptance, the Service Provider shall, at no additional cost to the Company, promptly correct the identified non-conformities and resubmit the corrected Deliverable. The Company shall have an additional **ten (10) Business Days** to review the corrected Deliverable, following the same acceptance process.

2.6.4 Persistent failure (more than two (2) unsuccessful correction attempts) to deliver a conforming Deliverable may constitute a material breach, subject to **Article IV (Term and Termination)** and **Article XII (SLA remedies)** where applicable.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The total contract value for this Work Order **WO-PIN-202506-202** is **US$4,979,327** (the **“Contract Value”**), representing the aggregated maximum fees payable for all Services and Deliverables described herein, exclusive of applicable taxes and reimbursable expenses.

3.1.2 The Contract Value is allocated across phases and service components as follows (approximate):

- Phase I – Discovery and Design: US$624,000  
- Phase II – Implementation and Integration: US$2,450,000  
- Phase III – Transition to Managed Services: US$605,327  
- Phase IV – Steady-State Managed Services (base): US$1,300,000  

The above allocations are indicative and may be adjusted by mutual written agreement via Change Order.

### 3.2 Fee Structure

3.2.1 **Fixed-Fee Components.** Phases I, II, and III shall be billed on a fixed-fee basis, payable against milestones in accordance with **Section 3.3**.

3.2.2 **Recurring Fees.** Phase IV steady-state managed services shall be billed as a recurring monthly fee of **US$27,083** (annualized US$325,000), subject to adjustment as contemplated under **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

3.2.3 **Time-and-Materials (T&M).** Additional services requested by the Company and not included in the defined scope may be delivered on a T&M basis, subject to a written Change Order specifying hourly or daily rates, estimated hours, and not-to-exceed amounts.

### 3.3 Payment Milestones

The Company shall pay the Service Provider in accordance with the following milestone schedule for the fixed-fee components:

(a) **Phase I – Discovery and Design (US$624,000)**  
- 30% upon Effective Date and commencement of Phase I;  
- 40% upon delivery of the Solution Architecture Document and Project Plan;  
- 30% upon acceptance of all Phase I Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.6**.

(b) **Phase II – Implementation and Integration (US$2,450,000)**  
- 20% upon commencement of Phase II;  
- 40% upon completion of core integrations and successful system integration testing;  
- 40% upon completion of UAT and acceptance of key Deliverables.

(c) **Phase III – Transition to Managed Services (US$605,327)**  
- 50% upon commencement of transition activities;  
- 50% upon delivery and acceptance of the Transition Completion Report.

(d) **Phase IV – Steady-State (US$1,300,000)**  
- Billed in equal monthly installments in arrears, commencing the first full month after Transition Completion, and continuing until the Expiration Date or earlier termination.

### 3.4 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.4.1 The Service Provider shall invoice the Company upon achievement of each payment milestone and monthly in arrears for recurring and T&M Services.

3.4.2 All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable **Net 60** days from the date of invoice. This Net 60 term shall apply to all fees and reimbursable expenses under this Agreement.

3.4.3 The Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing of any disputed amounts within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the applicable invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve invoice disputes promptly. The Company shall pay the undisputed portion of any invoice in accordance with this Section.

### 3.5 Expenses and Reimbursement

3.5.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred in connection with on-site performance of the Services, in accordance with the Company’s then-current travel and expense policies, provided such expenses are itemized and supported by appropriate receipts.

3.5.2 The Service Provider shall obtain the Company’s written approval prior to incurring any single expense, or series of related expenses, exceeding **US$2,500**.

### 3.6 Taxes

3.6.1 The Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes. The Company shall be responsible for all such taxes, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income or employment-related taxes.

3.6.2 The Service Provider shall itemize any applicable taxes on its invoices in compliance with Applicable Law.

### 3.7 Late Payment

3.7.1 Any undisputed amount not received by the Service Provider by the due date shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the day after the due date until the date of payment.

3.7.2 The Service Provider may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Services if any undisputed amount remains unpaid more than sixty (60) days after its due date, provided that the Service Provider shall resume performance promptly upon receipt of such overdue amounts.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 26, 2030** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend the Term, modify the scope, or enter into subsequent work orders, subject to negotiated terms.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon providing the other Party with not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** of termination.  

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Company:

(a) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) The Company shall reimburse any non-cancellable, pre-approved expenses incurred by the Service Provider; and  
(c) If termination occurs during a fixed-fee phase, the Parties shall in good faith apportion the phase fee based on the percentage of completion as of the termination date.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receiving written notice specifying the breach; or  
(b) Becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, appoints or has appointed a receiver, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.3.2 Without limiting the foregoing, repeated or significant failure by the Service Provider to meet agreed Service Levels, or persistent delivery of non-conforming Deliverables, may constitute a material breach under this Section, subject to notice and cure.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) The Service Provider shall cease performance of the Services, except as necessary to transition the Services as described below;  
(b) The Company shall pay the Service Provider all undisputed Fees and reimbursable expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination; and  
(c) Each Party shall promptly return or, upon written request, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information, subject to **Section 5.4 (Survival)** and any legal retention obligations.

4.4.2 At the Company’s written request, the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to plan and execute an orderly transition of the Services (including transition to the Company or another service provider), for a period not exceeding **ninety (90) days** following termination, under commercially reasonable terms and at agreed T&M rates.

### 4.5 Survival

4.5.1 The provisions of **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions which by their nature should survive termination, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

### 12.1 Uptime Guarantee

12.1.1 For any monitoring, support, or managed services provided under this Agreement involving the Service Provider’s systems or tools, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure a minimum of **99.9%** monthly system availability, excluding maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events in accordance with **Article XV**.

### 12.2 Incident Severity Levels and Response Times

12.2.1 Incidents shall be classified by the Parties as follows:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Total loss of service or major system outage impacting business-critical operations.  
  - Response Time: within **1 hour**, 24x7  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within **8 hours**

- **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of service with no acceptable workaround, but core operations remain functional.  
  - Response Time: within **2 hours** during Standard Hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within **24 hours**

- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial loss of functionality with a reasonable workaround available; non-critical features affected.  
  - Response Time: within **4 Business Hours**  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within **5 Business Days**

- **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general service requests.  
  - Response Time: within **1 Business Day**  
  - Target Resolution: as scheduled in consultation with the Company.

### 12.3 Service Credits

12.3.1 If the Service Provider fails to achieve the uptime or incident response targets in a given calendar month (a **“Service Level Failure”**), the Company shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

(a) For each full one percent (1.0%) by which monthly uptime falls below 99.9%, a credit equal to **3%** of the monthly recurring Fees for that month, up to a maximum of 15%; and  
(b) For repeated failures (three (3) or more consecutive months) to meet Severity 1 response targets, an additional monthly credit of **5%** of the monthly recurring Fees.

12.3.2 Service credits shall be applied to future invoices and shall not constitute liquidated damages. Service credits shall be the Company’s exclusive monetary remedy for Service Level Failures, subject to the limitations and exceptions set forth in **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)**.
