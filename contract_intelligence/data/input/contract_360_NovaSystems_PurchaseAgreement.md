## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General.** Vendor shall provide to Client the Products, Services, and Deliverables described in this Article II and in each applicable SOW or purchase order issued under this Agreement. This Agreement establishes the general terms and conditions governing all such transactions between the Parties.

2.2 **Products.** Vendor shall supply to Client, on a non‑exclusive basis, the following categories of Products (collectively, the **“System”**):

  (a) **Hardware.** Enterprise‑grade servers, networking equipment, storage devices, and related components sufficient to support Client’s projected usage volumes, as further detailed in the applicable SOWs, including any replacement or upgraded hardware ordered during the Term.

  (b) **Software Licenses and Subscriptions.** Vendor’s proprietary system management software, analytics modules, middleware, and associated client access licenses, as well as any hosted or software‑as‑a‑service (SaaS) solutions, including but not limited to:  
  (i) Nova Core Platform;  
  (ii) Nova Analytics Suite;  
  (iii) Nova Integration Engine;  
  (iv) User access licenses as specified in the SOW.

  (c) **Third‑Party Materials.** Where identified in the SOW, Vendor may resell or provide access to third‑party operating systems, databases, security tools, or other software necessary for the System. Client acknowledges that third‑party terms may apply as described in Section 6.5.

2.3 **Implementation and Configuration Services.** Vendor shall perform professional Services to implement and configure the System in accordance with agreed specifications. Such Services may include:

  (a) **Discovery and Design.** Conducting discovery workshops, requirements analysis, and solution design sessions with Client’s stakeholders to document business requirements, functional specifications, and technical architecture (“Design Documentation”).

  (b) **Installation and Configuration.** Installing hardware and software at Client’s designated locations or configuring and provisioning cloud services, configuring integrations, user roles, access controls, and security parameters.

  (c) **Customization and Integration.** Developing custom interfaces, data mappings, and workflows, and integrating the System with Client’s existing platforms (e.g., ERP, CRM, HR, or other legacy systems) as described in the SOW.

  (d) **Data Migration.** Planning and executing data migration activities, including extraction, transformation, and loading (ETL) of Client Data into the System, subject to data quality and format constraints as set forth in the SOW.

  (e) **Testing and Acceptance Support.** Supporting Client during unit, integration, system, and user acceptance testing, addressing defects, and providing updated versions of the Deliverables until the System meets the applicable acceptance criteria.

2.4 **Support and Maintenance Services.** During the Term, Vendor shall provide ongoing support and maintenance for the System, which shall include:

  (a) **Help Desk Support.** Providing remote technical support via email, web portal, or phone during Vendor’s standard support hours, as specified in the SOW or Service Level Exhibit.

  (b) **Error Correction.** Using commercially reasonable efforts to correct reproducible errors in the System, providing patches, workarounds, or updates in accordance with the Service Levels.

  (c) **Updates and Upgrades.** Providing Client with access to generally available updates, enhancements, and new versions of the Vendor software components included in the System, subject to any additional fees specified in the SOW and to third‑party licensing terms.

  (d) **Preventive Maintenance.** Performing scheduled maintenance, including system monitoring, backups, and performance tuning, as further detailed in Article XII.

2.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall provide training to Client’s designated personnel on the operation, administration, and use of the System. Training may include:

  (a) On‑site or remote instructor‑led training sessions;  
  (b) Training materials and user guides;  
  (c) Knowledge transfer sessions for Client’s technical staff.

The scope, duration, and locations of training will be specified in the SOW. Additional training beyond the initial scope may be ordered at the rates set forth in Article III or the applicable SOW.

2.6 **Project Management and Governance.** Vendor shall assign a project manager to coordinate the Services, and Client shall designate a project manager to serve as the primary point of contact. The Parties shall establish a governance structure, which may include steering committee meetings, progress reporting, and escalation protocols, as described in the SOW and Article XIV.

2.7 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards, (b) by appropriately qualified and experienced personnel, and (c) in compliance with applicable Laws. Client acknowledges that successful implementation depends on Client’s timely cooperation, access to systems and personnel, and provision of accurate and complete information. Vendor shall not be responsible for delays, deficiencies, or failures to meet schedules or performance standards to the extent caused by Client’s acts or omissions or those of its Affiliates, contractors, or vendors.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.** In addition to any responsibilities specified elsewhere in this Agreement or in the SOW, Client shall:

  (a) Provide Vendor with timely access to Client’s facilities, systems, networks, data, and personnel, as reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  

  (b) Obtain and maintain all required consents and approvals from third parties (including landlords, licensors, or other vendors) necessary for Vendor to access and integrate with Client’s systems and to perform the Services;

  (c) Ensure that Client Data provided to Vendor is accurate, complete, and in a usable format, and that its collection, disclosure, and use complies with applicable Laws and Client’s internal policies;

  (d) Designate qualified personnel to participate in requirements gathering, testing, training, and acceptance activities, and to provide timely decisions, approvals, and feedback;

  (e) Maintain appropriate backup copies of Client Data and system configurations, except to the extent Vendor explicitly undertakes such backup responsibilities under the SOW.

2.9 **Milestones and Timeline.** The Parties shall agree upon specific project milestones, deliverable due dates, and implementation phases in the SOW. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve such milestones; however, such dates are estimates only unless expressly stated as “firm” in the SOW. Any adjustments to the timeline due to scope changes, Client delays, or unforeseen technical issues shall be managed in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management).

2.10 **Acceptance.** Unless otherwise specified in a SOW:

  (a) Upon delivery of a Deliverable, Vendor shall notify Client in writing. Client shall then have the period specified in the SOW (or, if not specified, thirty (30) days) (the **“Acceptance Period”**) to test and evaluate the Deliverable.

  (b) If the Deliverable materially conforms to the applicable specifications, Client shall provide written acceptance. If Client fails to provide written notice of rejection within the Acceptance Period, stating specific material non‑conformities, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

  (c) If Client timely rejects a Deliverable due to material non‑conformity, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such non‑conformity and re‑deliver the Deliverable. Client shall have a further Acceptance Period for re‑testing. If, after two (2) re‑delivery attempts, the Deliverable still does not materially conform, the Parties shall escalate the matter under Article XIV.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **June 01, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**) (the **“Term”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** Upon the Expiration Date, this Agreement may renew for additional periods as mutually agreed in writing by the Parties. Any renewal shall be subject to revised pricing and terms as agreed by the Parties at the time of renewal.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause, upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor. In such event, Client shall pay Vendor (a) all Fees accrued and unpaid through the effective date of termination, (b) any non‑cancelable or committed costs incurred by Vendor in reliance on this Agreement, and (c) any early termination or cancellation fees expressly set forth in the SOW. Client acknowledges that the 180‑day notice constitutes a material inducement for Vendor to enter into this Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause, upon at least one hundred twenty (120) days’ prior written notice to Client, provided that Vendor shall reasonably cooperate with Client to facilitate an orderly transition as described in Section 4.7.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) Materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (ten (10) days in the case of non‑payment) after receiving written notice of the breach from the non‑breaching Party; or  

  (b) Becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, has a receiver appointed, or becomes subject to any bankruptcy or insolvency proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.6 **Rights and Obligations Upon Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) All rights and licenses granted to Client under this Agreement (including Article VI) shall immediately terminate, except for any perpetual licenses expressly granted and fully paid, if any;

  (b) Client shall immediately cease all use of the Vendor software and Services and, at Vendor’s option, return or destroy all copies of Vendor Confidential Information and software in Client’s possession or control, certifying such destruction upon Vendor’s written request;

  (c) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed amounts due and payable under this Agreement through the effective date of termination, including any termination‑related charges under Section 4.3;

  (d) Each Party shall return or destroy Confidential Information of the other Party as described in Section 5.4.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.** At Client’s written request, Vendor shall, for a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration (or longer as mutually agreed in a separate SOW), provide reasonable transition assistance services to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Client or a third‑party service provider. Such services shall be provided at Vendor’s then‑current standard rates unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.8 **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature should survive expiration or termination (including, without limitation, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XVI, and Sections 3.4, 3.7, 4.6, and 4.8) shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.
