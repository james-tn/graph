## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Vendor.**  
Contoso hereby engages Delta, and Delta hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Personnel described in this Article II and in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. Delta shall perform the Services as an independent contractor, in accordance with the terms of this Agreement and any applicable SOW.

2.2 **General Description of Services.**  
Delta shall provide Contoso with professional staffing and related consulting services, including, without limitation:

  2.2.1 Recruitment, selection, and placement of qualified Personnel in the areas of software engineering, quality assurance, data analytics, IT operations, cybersecurity, technical project management, and related roles;

  2.2.2 Provision of managed teams and/or individual resources to support Contoso’s product development, systems integration, and operational support initiatives;

  2.2.3 Onboarding and administrative support for Personnel provided by Delta, including background checks where legally permissible, verification of credentials, and coordination with Contoso’s HR and security teams;

  2.2.4 Ongoing performance monitoring, coaching, and, where necessary, replacement of Personnel who do not meet performance standards agreed in this Agreement or an applicable SOW;

  2.2.5 Delivery of project-based Services, including analysis, design, development, testing, deployment, and knowledge transfer for specific projects identified by Contoso and described in applicable SOWs;

  2.2.6 Ancillary consulting services, such as process improvement recommendations, documentation of workflows, and recommendations for technology or organizational improvements.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables may include, but are not limited to:

  (a) Detailed project plans and staffing plans;  
  (b) Technical design documents, architecture diagrams, and configuration guides;  
  (c) Source code, scripts, configuration files, and related technical artifacts developed specifically for Contoso;  
  (d) Test plans, test cases, test scripts, and test results;  
  (e) Status reports, performance dashboards, and executive summaries;  
  (f) Training materials, user guides, and operational runbooks;  
  (g) Final project closure reports and handover documentation.

Each SOW shall specify the particular Deliverables to be provided, including format, level of detail, acceptance criteria, and delivery dates or milestones.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Delta shall perform the Services and provide the Deliverables:

  2.4.1 In a professional, diligent, timely, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;

  2.4.2 In compliance with all Applicable Laws, including those relating to employment, immigration, workplace safety, data protection, and intellectual property;

  2.4.3 In accordance with Contoso’s reasonable policies, procedures, and security requirements as communicated to Delta, including any policies related to remote work, use of Contoso systems, and information security;

  2.4.4 Using Personnel with the appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications to perform the assigned tasks. Delta shall ensure that all Personnel assigned to Contoso have been adequately trained and are capable of performing the Services to Contoso’s reasonable satisfaction.

2.5 **Timeline and Milestones.**  
The Parties acknowledge that timely completion of the Services and Deliverables is critical to Contoso’s business objectives. Accordingly:

  2.5.1 Each SOW shall include a detailed timeline, including start dates, major milestones, interim deadlines, and final delivery dates for the Services and Deliverables;

  2.5.2 Delta shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the timeline set forth in the applicable SOW. If Delta becomes aware of any event or circumstance that may delay performance, Delta shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, explaining the cause of the delay and proposing a mitigation plan and revised schedule;

  2.5.3 Contoso shall cooperate with Delta and provide necessary information, access, and approvals in a timely manner so as not to unreasonably delay Delta’s performance;

  2.5.4 Any material changes to the agreed timeline shall be managed through the change management process set forth in Article XIII and documented in a Change Order.

2.6 **Staffing and Resource Management.**  
To ensure successful delivery of the Services:

  2.6.1 Delta shall designate a **Vendor Project Manager** (or account manager) who will be the primary point of contact for Contoso and will be responsible for day-to-day oversight of the Services and coordination of Personnel;

  2.6.2 Contoso shall designate a **Client Project Manager** or representative who will be Delta’s primary point of contact for decisions, approvals, and escalations;

  2.6.3 Delta shall not substantially reduce or replace key Personnel identified in an SOW without prior written notice to Contoso and, where practicable, at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior notice. Contoso shall have the right to reasonably object to any proposed replacement Personnel who do not meet the required qualifications;

  2.6.4 If Contoso reasonably determines that any Delta Personnel fails to perform to requisite standards, engages in misconduct, or poses a risk to Contoso’s operations or reputation, Contoso may request in writing that such Personnel be removed. Delta shall promptly remove such Personnel from the assignment and provide a suitable replacement as soon as reasonably practicable, at no additional cost to Contoso.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW:

  2.7.1 Delta shall submit each Deliverable to Contoso for review and acceptance;

  2.7.2 Contoso shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt of a Deliverable to review it and either (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide written notice of rejection stating in reasonable detail the nonconformities with the applicable SOW or this Agreement;

  2.7.3 If Contoso timely rejects a Deliverable, Delta shall, at its sole cost and expense, promptly correct the identified nonconformities and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance. The acceptance procedure described in Section 2.7.2 shall then reapply;

  2.7.4 If Contoso fails to provide acceptance or rejection within the specified review period, the Deliverable shall not be deemed accepted by default; instead, Contoso shall be deemed to have requested an extension of time and the Parties shall promptly confer to finalize acceptance.

2.8 **No Employment Relationship with Contoso.**  
The Parties expressly acknowledge and agree that:

  2.8.1 Personnel provided by Delta shall remain employees or contractors of Delta (or of its subcontractors, where applicable) and shall not be considered employees of Contoso for any purpose;

  2.8.2 Delta shall be solely responsible for all compensation, benefits, tax withholdings, and other obligations relating to its Personnel, including compliance with all wage and hour laws, unemployment insurance, workers’ compensation, and employment tax obligations;

  2.8.3 Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create any employer-employee relationship, agency, partnership, or joint venture between Contoso and Delta or between Contoso and any Personnel of Delta, except as specifically provided otherwise in a written amendment signed by both Parties.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees and Contract Value.**  
In consideration of the Services and Deliverables to be provided by Delta hereunder, Contoso shall pay to Delta fees (the “**Fees**”) in accordance with this Article III and the applicable SOW(s). The Parties acknowledge that the aggregate contract value under this Agreement, including all SOWs, shall not exceed **Four Million Eight Hundred Fifty-Four Thousand Nine Hundred Eleven U.S. Dollars (US$4,854,911)**, unless increased by mutual written agreement.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees may include one or more of the following:

  3.2.1 **Time and Materials (T&M):** Hourly or daily rates for Personnel as set forth in the applicable SOW, based on actual hours worked, subject to any not-to-exceed amounts;

  3.2.2 **Fixed Price:** Fixed Fees for specific Deliverables, milestones, or projects, as described in the applicable SOW;

  3.2.3 **Retainer / Managed Services:** Monthly or periodic Fees for ongoing managed services or dedicated teams;

  3.2.4 **Success-Based or Incentive Fees:** Additional fees contingent upon achieving defined performance metrics or project outcomes, if expressly stated in an SOW.

3.3 **Invoices.**  

  3.3.1 Delta shall submit invoices to Contoso in accordance with the invoicing schedule set forth in the applicable SOW, or, if no schedule is provided, on a monthly basis in arrears;

  3.3.2 Each invoice shall reference the Agreement number **AGR-DEL-202501-474**, the relevant SOW number, the period covered, and a detailed description of the Services performed, Personnel utilized, hours worked (for T&M), and any applicable expenses;

  3.3.3 Contoso may reasonably request additional supporting documentation, and Delta shall provide such documentation promptly.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
Subject to Section 3.5, Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts on each invoice within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of receipt of a valid invoice (“**Net 60**”). Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars via wire transfer or other method agreed by the Parties.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  

  3.5.1 If Contoso disputes any invoiced amount, Contoso shall notify Delta in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature and basis of the dispute;

  3.5.2 Contoso shall pay all undisputed portions of the invoice in accordance with the payment terms, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the disputed amounts as soon as reasonably practicable;

  3.5.3 Upon resolution, any agreed adjustments shall be reflected in a subsequent invoice or credit note.

3.6 **Late Payment.**  

  3.6.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of payment at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one percent (1.0%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law;

  3.6.2 Interest shall not apply to any amount disputed in good faith by Contoso in accordance with Section 3.5;

  3.6.3 Delta shall provide Contoso with written notice of any overdue amount and allow at least ten (10) Business Days after such notice for cure before suspending Services, and any suspension shall be limited to the Services directly affected by the non-payment.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

  3.7.1 Contoso shall reimburse Delta for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred by Delta in performing the Services, including travel, lodging, and related expenses, provided that such expenses are in accordance with Contoso’s travel and expense policy as provided to Delta in writing;

  3.7.2 Delta shall obtain written approval from Contoso in advance for any single expense in excess of five hundred U.S. Dollars (US$500);

  3.7.3 Reimbursable expenses shall be itemized on Delta’s invoices and supported by appropriate receipts and documentation.

3.8 **Taxes.**  

  3.8.1 Fees do not include any sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes imposed by any governmental authority, except for taxes based on Delta’s income;

  3.8.2 Where required by law, Contoso shall withhold applicable taxes from payments to Delta and shall provide Delta with appropriate documentation regarding such withholding;

  3.8.3 The Parties shall cooperate to minimize taxes to the extent legally permitted.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **May 09, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Agreement may be renewed for additional periods upon mutual written agreement of the Parties prior to the Expiration Date. Any renewal shall be documented in a written amendment specifying the renewal term and any revised terms.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing Delta with at least **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice;

  4.3.2 Delta may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing at least **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso, to ensure continuity and minimize disruption to Contoso’s operations;

  4.3.3 In the event of termination for convenience, Contoso shall pay Delta for all Services performed and authorized expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, but shall have no obligation to pay any early termination fees, lost profits, or other cancellation charges.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of the breach, specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable detail;

  4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (c) ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business;

  4.4.3 For the avoidance of doubt, repeated or serious breaches of confidentiality, data protection, or non-compliance with Applicable Laws may constitute grounds for immediate termination for cause.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason, Delta shall promptly cease performing the Services (except to the extent reasonably requested by Contoso to assist with transition as described below);

  4.5.2 Within thirty (30) days after the effective date of termination or expiration, Delta shall deliver to Contoso all completed Deliverables and any work-in-progress for which Contoso has paid or is obligated to pay, together with relevant documentation and materials reasonably necessary for Contoso to use such Deliverables;

  4.5.3 Contoso shall pay Delta for all undisputed Services performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, in accordance with Article III;

  4.5.4 The termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not affect the survival of any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions).

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon Contoso’s request made prior to or within thirty (30) days after termination or expiration, Delta shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days to facilitate a smooth transition of the Services to Contoso or a third-party service provider designated by Contoso. Such assistance shall be provided at Delta’s then-current Time and Materials rates, unless the termination results from Delta’s uncured material breach, in which case such assistance shall be provided at a mutually agreed discounted rate.

CONFIDENTIALITY

For clarity, Client Materials shall be deemed Confidential Information of Contoso.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  

  5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement and for no other purpose;

  5.2.2 The Receiving Party shall not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party, except to its own employees, contractors, or professional advisors who have a “need to know” such information for the permitted purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those contained herein;

  5.2.3 The Receiving Party shall protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar importance, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
The obligations set forth in Section 5.2 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) Was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction on use or disclosure prior to receipt from the Disclosing Party;  
  (b) Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (c) Is received from a third party who did not acquire or disclose such information in violation of any legal or contractual obligation; or  
  (d) Was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party (to the extent legally permitted) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in any lawful efforts by the Disclosing Party to limit or contest such disclosure.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s option) all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in the Receiving Party’s possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) copies required to comply with Applicable Law, and (b) archival copies maintained in accordance with its standard backup procedures, which shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that trade secrets shall be protected for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under Applicable Law.

Delta shall respond to and resolve incidents in accordance with the following guidelines, or as otherwise specified in the applicable SOW:

  (a) **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of a critical service or major outage; initial response within one (1) hour, with continuous work until resolution or workaround;  
  (b) **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant degradation of service; initial response within four (4) hours;  
  (c) **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Non-critical issues or partial loss of functionality; initial response within one (1) Business Day;  
  (d) **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues or general service requests; initial response within two (2) Business Days.

  (c) Scheduled maintenance shall not exceed eight (8) hours per month unless otherwise agreed in writing.

If Delta fails to meet the Uptime Commitment or other material SLA metrics in a given calendar month, Delta shall provide Contoso with service credits as specified in the applicable SOW. 12.5 **Exclusions.**  
SLA commitments shall not apply to downtime or issues arising from: (a) Contoso’s misuse of the Services, (b) failures of Contoso’s systems or networks, (c) Third-Party Materials not under Delta’s control, or (d) Force Majeure Events.

## ARTICLE XV

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics or pandemics, acts of war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, or failures of utilities or telecommunications beyond the affected Party’s reasonable control.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
  (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are directly affected by the Force Majeure Event, provided that the unaffected Party may reasonably adjust project schedules and deadlines.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Services or this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without penalty.
