## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Client Facilities as more specifically described in this Article II and any applicable SOW. The Services shall include, without limitation: (a) preventive maintenance; (b) corrective and repair services; (c) emergency maintenance; (d) inspections and assessment services; (e) parts and materials management; (f) documentation, reporting, and recommendations; and (g) related consulting and advisory services.

2.2 **Preventive Maintenance.**  

2.2.1 Vendor shall perform preventive maintenance on equipment, systems, and infrastructure at the Client Facilities in accordance with: (a) manufacturer specifications; (b) industry best practices; and (c) any maintenance schedules agreed upon in an SOW.  

2.2.2 Preventive maintenance shall include, as applicable, lubrication, calibration, cleaning, testing, firmware upgrades, replacement of consumable parts, and other tasks necessary to minimize downtime and ensure the continued safe and efficient operation of the Client Facilities.  

2.2.3 Vendor shall prepare and maintain detailed preventive maintenance schedules for each major system (e.g., HVAC, electrical distribution, backup generators, UPS systems, security systems, IT infrastructure) and shall provide such schedules to Client upon request.

2.3 **Corrective and Repair Services.**  

2.3.1 Vendor shall diagnose and remedy malfunctions, defects, failures, and other issues reported by Client or detected by Vendor during preventive maintenance activities.  

2.3.2 Corrective services shall include troubleshooting, repair, replacement of parts, reconfiguration, recalibration, and testing of the affected equipment or systems.  

2.3.3 Vendor shall respond to service requests within the Response Times set forth in Article XII and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve issues within the applicable Resolution Times.  

2.3.4 Vendor shall notify Client promptly if Vendor determines that any particular repair or replacement is likely to exceed the cost thresholds or decision criteria specified in an SOW, and shall obtain Client’s approval prior to proceeding, except in an emergency where immediate action is required to protect safety or prevent material damage to Client’s property.

2.4 **Emergency Maintenance Services.**  

2.4.1 Vendor shall provide twenty-four (24) hour, seven (7) day per week emergency response availability for critical issues that materially impair the operation, safety, or security of the Client Facilities.  

2.4.2 For such emergency events, Vendor shall respond within the critical Response Times set forth in Article XII.  

2.4.3 Vendor shall coordinate with Client’s designated representatives to determine the most appropriate remedial measures and shall prioritize the restoration of critical operations, taking into account health and safety considerations and any applicable regulatory requirements.

2.5 **Inspections and Assessments.**  

2.5.1 Vendor shall conduct periodic inspections of the Client Facilities, at a minimum quarterly unless otherwise specified in an SOW, to assess the condition, performance, and remaining useful life of key equipment and systems.  

2.5.2 Vendor shall deliver written inspection reports, including findings, photographs (where reasonably practicable), and recommendations for remediation or improvement, within ten (10) Business Days following each inspection.  

2.5.3 Vendor’s assessments shall include recommendations regarding capital improvements, risk mitigation measures, and potential energy efficiency enhancements where applicable.

2.6 **Parts, Materials, and Subcontractors.**  

2.6.1 Vendor shall supply or procure all parts, consumables, and materials reasonably necessary for the performance of the Services, except as explicitly stated otherwise in an SOW.  

2.6.2 Parts and materials shall be new and of merchantable quality, or refurbished if expressly approved by Client in writing.  

2.6.3 Vendor may engage qualified subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (a) Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors; (b) any subcontractors are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection at least as protective as those set forth in this Agreement; and (c) subcontractors are properly insured as required under Article X.

2.7 **Documentation and Reporting.**  

2.7.1 Vendor shall maintain accurate and complete maintenance records, including service tickets, logs, inspection reports, parts used, labor hours, and any deviations from standard procedures.  

2.7.2 Vendor shall provide monthly summary reports to Client describing the Services performed during the preceding month, recurring issues, pending service requests, parts consumption, and any recommended improvements.  

2.7.3 Vendor shall maintain all maintenance records for at least five (5) years following the date of creation or for such longer period as may be required by Applicable Law.

2.8 **Performance Standards.**  

2.8.1 Vendor shall perform all Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards, Applicable Law, and Client’s reasonable policies provided to Vendor in writing.  

2.8.2 Vendor shall ensure that all personnel assigned to perform the Services are appropriately trained, qualified, and licensed (where required), and have the necessary expertise to perform the assigned tasks.  

2.8.3 Vendor shall meet or exceed the Service Levels and KPIs specified in Article XII and any SOW. Vendor’s failure to meet the Service Levels shall entitle Client to the remedies described in Article XII, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, subject to the limitations of Article IX.

2.9 **Project Planning and Transition.**  

2.9.1 Within thirty (30) days after the Effective Date, the Parties shall cooperate to finalize an initial SOW describing the Client Facilities, equipment lists, and baseline conditions.  

2.9.2 Vendor shall develop, in consultation with Client, a transition plan for any onboarding or handover from existing maintenance providers, including data and documentation transfer, as required.  

2.9.3 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance to Client or to any successor service provider designated by Client, subject to mutually agreed scope and Fees, as further described in Section 4.5.

2.10 **Exclusions from Scope.**  

2.10.1 Unless explicitly included in a SOW or Change Order, the Services shall not include: (a) major capital projects or full system replacements; (b) structural modifications; (c) architectural redesign; (d) IT software development unrelated to maintenance; or (e) any services requiring specialized third-party certifications not commonly held in the maintenance industry.  

2.10.2 Any services outside the defined Scope under this Article II and the relevant SOW shall require a Change Order in accordance with Article XIII.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total estimated contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **US $3,385,635** (the “Contract Value”), subject to adjustments for changes in scope, inflationary adjustments if specified in an SOW, and any additional services provided under Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

3.2.1 **Base Maintenance Fee.** Client shall pay Vendor a recurring base maintenance fee for preventive and routine maintenance services. Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Base Maintenance Fee shall be invoiced in equal quarterly installments over the Term.  

3.2.2 **Time and Materials for Non-Routine Work.** Non-routine repairs, emergency interventions beyond a specified threshold, and requested additional services not included in the Base Maintenance Fee shall be charged on a time-and-materials basis at the hourly rates and material mark-ups set forth in Exhibit A (Fee Schedule) or the applicable SOW.  

3.2.3 **Pass-Through Costs.** Large replacement components or third-party specialized services (e.g., crane rentals, specialized calibrations) shall be charged to Client at cost plus a handling fee or percentage markup specified in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  

3.3.1 Vendor shall issue invoices to Client on a monthly basis, unless otherwise set forth in an SOW. Each invoice shall separately itemize: (a) Base Maintenance Fees; (b) time-and-materials charges; (c) pass-through costs; (d) applicable taxes; and (e) any Service Credits applied pursuant to Article XII.  

3.3.2 All invoices shall reference the Contract Reference Number MNT-DEL-202404-699 and any relevant SOW number, and shall include a summary of Services performed, including maintenance ticket or work order numbers.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  

3.4.1 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts on each invoice within **sixty (60) days** after the invoice date (“Net 60”).  

3.4.2 Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by electronic funds transfer or such other method as mutually agreed in writing.  

3.4.3 If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of such invoice. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts, and any amounts ultimately determined to be payable by Client shall be paid within fifteen (15) days after resolution.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  

3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full.  

3.5.2 Vendor shall provide Client with written notice of late payment prior to suspending any non-critical Services. Vendor shall not suspend Services if the Parties are actively and in good faith resolving a dispute relating to such invoice.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

3.6.1 Fees exclude any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes imposed by any governmental authority. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes (except taxes based on Vendor’s net income), provided that Vendor shall itemize such taxes on its invoices.  

3.6.2 If Client is exempt from any such taxes, Client shall provide Vendor with a valid tax exemption certificate, and Vendor shall not charge such taxes to Client.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.7.1 Reasonable travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in the performance of the Services (e.g., airfare, lodging, ground transportation, meals, specialized equipment rentals) shall be reimbursed by Client at cost, provided such expenses are: (a) pre-approved in writing by Client, except for minor incidental expenses; and (b) supported by appropriate receipts.  

3.7.2 Vendor shall comply with Client’s reasonable travel and expense policies, as provided to Vendor in writing in advance.

3.8 **No Set-Off.** Except as expressly permitted pursuant to Service Credits or as otherwise expressly set forth herein, neither Party shall have the right to set off any amounts owed to the other Party under this Agreement without the other Party’s prior written consent or a final non-appealable order of a court of competent jurisdiction.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **May 28, 2027** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  

4.2.1 The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, extend or renew this Agreement for additional periods and under mutually agreed terms.  

4.2.2 Absent such written agreement, this Agreement shall automatically expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Vendor.  

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **ninety (90) days’** prior written notice to Client, provided that Vendor shall reasonably cooperate with Client to ensure an orderly transition of Services.  

4.3.3 In the event of termination for convenience, Client shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, plus any reasonable, documented demobilization and transition assistance costs agreed in advance, but shall not be liable for any other damages, early termination fees, or lost profits.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any material term of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the nature of the breach.  

4.4.2 Notwithstanding Section 4.4.1, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have an additional reasonable cure period (not to exceed sixty (60) days in total) if it commences cure within the initial thirty (30) days and diligently pursues such cure thereafter.  

4.4.3 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or any proceeding relating to insolvency, receivership, or liquidation that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  

4.5.1 Upon any termination or expiration of this Agreement:  
(a) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed Fees and approved expenses accrued through the effective date of termination, subject to any rights of set-off permitted under this Agreement;  
(b) Vendor shall cease performing the Services (except as necessary to wind down operations) and shall promptly (and in any event within thirty (30) days) deliver to Client all completed Deliverables and maintenance records existing as of the effective date of termination; and  
(c) each Party shall return or destroy Confidential Information of the other Party in accordance with Section 5.5.

4.5.2 At Client’s request, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days after termination or expiration, at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates (or as otherwise agreed in writing), to facilitate the transfer of Services to Client or a successor provider.

4.6 **Survival.** The rights and obligations of the Parties that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties) to the extent stated, VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination.

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime and Availability.**  

12.1.1 For any remote monitoring systems, maintenance portals, or other systems operated by Vendor and used by Client to log service requests or access maintenance data (the “Maintenance Platform”), Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Maintenance Platform is available **99.9%** of the time each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows.  

12.1.2 Scheduled maintenance windows shall not exceed eight (8) hours per month and shall be communicated to Client at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance, except in cases of emergency maintenance.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall adhere to the following target service levels for onsite and remote responses, measured from the time Client logs a service request through the agreed channel:

- **Critical Issues** (safety hazards, facility-wide outages, or regulatory violations):  
  - Response Time: within 2 hours (24x7)  
  - Resolution Time Target: within 24 hours, or temporary workaround within 8 hours  

- **High Priority Issues** (major system degradation, significant production impact):  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Resolution Time Target: within 2 Business Days  

- **Medium Priority Issues** (moderate impact, no immediate safety risk):  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Resolution Time Target: within 5 Business Days  

- **Low Priority Issues** (minor issues, cosmetic defects, non-urgent):  
  - Response Time: within 2 Business Days  
  - Resolution Time Target: within 10 Business Days

12.3 **Service Credits.**  

12.3.1 If Vendor fails to meet the uptime or response Service Levels in a given calendar month, Client may be eligible for Service Credits.  

12.3.2 Service Credits shall be calculated as a percentage reduction of the monthly Base Maintenance Fee, as follows (indicative, subject to any SOW):  
(a) Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: 2% credit  
(b) Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: 5% credit  
(c) Uptime below 98.0%: 10% credit  

12.3.3 Service Credits shall be applied to the invoice for the month following the month in which the Service Level failure occurred and shall not be redeemable for cash.

12.4 **Service Credit Claims.** Client must request Service Credits in writing within thirty (30) days following the end of the month in which the Service Level breach occurred, providing reasonable supporting documentation. Vendor shall review and confirm applicable credits in good faith.

12.5 **Exclusive Remedies.** Service Credits shall be Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for Vendor’s failure to meet the Service Levels, except where such failure constitutes a material breach of this Agreement or is due to Vendor’s gross negligence or willful misconduct.
