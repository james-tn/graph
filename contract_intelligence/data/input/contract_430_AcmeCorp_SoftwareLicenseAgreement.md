## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.** Acme shall provide to Contoso the Software, Services, and Deliverables described in this Article II and in any SOW executed pursuant to this Agreement. The Parties acknowledge that the Services are intended to support Contoso’s global operations and may include complex implementation and integration activities.

2.2 **Software Provision.** Beginning on the Effective Date and continuing during the Term, Acme shall grant to Contoso the License set forth in Article VI for **Acme Enterprise Suite**, including at a minimum the following core modules:

  (a) **Customer Relationship Management (CRM) Module** – for managing customer data, sales pipelines, and customer engagement records.  
  (b) **Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) Module** – for financials, procurement, inventory, and supply chain management.  
  (c) **Analytics and Reporting Module** – for dashboards, real-time analytics, and scheduled reporting.  
  (d) **Integration APIs** – to connect with third-party systems (e.g., HRIS, payment gateways, legacy ERP).

2.3 **Deployment Model.** Unless otherwise agreed in an SOW, the Software shall be provided via a multi-tenant SaaS model hosted by or on behalf of Acme in data centers located in Singapore and/or other regions as reasonably determined by Acme, subject to data localization requirements agreed in writing. Acme shall be responsible for the hosting infrastructure and system administration of the production environment.

2.4 **Implementation Services.** Within thirty (30) days after the Effective Date, Acme and Contoso shall jointly develop an implementation plan, to be attached to an SOW, covering at least the following phases:

  (a) **Requirements & Design Phase (Estimated 60–90 days).**  
  - Conduct workshops with Contoso’s stakeholders to capture business requirements, process flows, and integration points.  
  - Produce a solution design document outlining configuration, integrations, data migration approach, and security roles.

  (b) **Configuration & Development Phase (Estimated 90–120 days).**  
  - Configure the core modules to align with Contoso’s business processes.  
  - Develop any custom fields, workflows, and business rules as agreed in the SOW.  
  - Build and test integrations with Contoso’s designated third-party systems.

  (c) **Testing & User Acceptance Phase (Estimated 60–90 days).**  
  - Perform system integration testing and performance testing.  
  - Support Contoso in conducting user acceptance testing (UAT).  
  - Address defects identified during UAT in accordance with severity levels and timelines specified in the SLA in Article XII.

  (d) **Go-Live & Stabilization Phase (Estimated 30–60 days).**  
  - Execute data migration from Contoso’s legacy systems.  
  - Deploy the Software to the production environment.  
  - Provide hypercare support for at least thirty (30) days post go-live, including prioritized incident response.

2.5 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of the Services, Acme shall deliver the following key Deliverables (collectively, the **“Deliverables”**):

  (a) Solution design documentation;  
  (b) Configured instance(s) of Acme Enterprise Suite;  
  (c) Developed integration components and interface specifications;  
  (d) Data migration scripts and mapping documentation;  
  (e) End-user training materials and administrator guides;  
  (f) Final acceptance report post-UAT, as executed by Contoso (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed).

2.6 **Performance Standards.** Acme shall perform the Services:

  (a) In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards;  
  (b) With personnel who are appropriately skilled, trained, and experienced;  
  (c) In accordance with the timelines and milestones specified in the applicable SOW and this Article II, subject to mutually agreed Change Orders;  
  (d) In material compliance with the Documentation for the Software.

Contoso acknowledges that effective performance by Acme is dependent upon Contoso’s timely provision of accurate information, resources, access, and decision-making, including prompt responses to Acme’s requests.

2.7 **Contoso Responsibilities.** Contoso shall, at its own expense and in a timely manner:

  (a) Provide Acme with access to Contoso’s relevant personnel, systems, and facilities as reasonably required;  
  (b) Assign a qualified project manager and project team to coordinate and oversee implementation activities;  
  (c) Obtain all necessary consents from third parties (e.g., licensors of legacy systems) for data migration and integrations;  
  (d) Ensure the availability and suitability of hardware, network connectivity, and end-user devices, if any on-premise or hybrid components are required;  
  (e) Review and provide feedback on Deliverables within the response times specified in the SOW.

2.8 **Acceptance.** Unless otherwise stated in an SOW:

  (a) Each Deliverable shall be deemed accepted upon the earlier of (i) Contoso’s written acceptance; or (ii) fourteen (14) calendar days following delivery, if Contoso fails to provide Acme with written notice of material non-conformity;  
  (b) If Contoso provides written notice of material non-conformity within such period, Acme shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such non-conformities and re-submit the Deliverable for acceptance;  
  (c) If the Deliverable continues to materially fail to conform after a reasonable number of re-submissions, the Parties shall escalate the matter in accordance with Article XIV.

2.9 **Changes to Scope.** Any changes to the scope, timeline, or Fees, including material changes arising from new requirements, regulatory changes, or delays attributable to Contoso, shall be addressed through the Change Management process in Article XIII, and shall not be binding unless set forth in a signed Change Order.

  (c) **Twenty-five percent (25%)** upon completion of UAT and issuance of the final UAT acceptance (or deemed acceptance) by Contoso;

Contoso shall make all payments in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other payment method reasonably designated by Acme.

3.7 **Late Payments.** Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law. In addition, if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after its due date, Acme may, upon written notice to Contoso and without liability, suspend performance of the Services and/or access to the Software until all outstanding undisputed amounts, including interest, are paid in full.

3.8 **Taxes.** Fees are exclusive of all applicable taxes, duties, levies, and other governmental charges (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Contoso shall be responsible for and shall pay all Taxes imposed on or with respect to the Fees under this Agreement, except for Taxes imposed on Acme’s income, property, or employees. 3.9 **Expenses.** Contoso shall reimburse Acme for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of on-site Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel policy, provided that Acme submits appropriate receipts and documentation with its invoices.

3.10 **Disputed Amounts.** If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall notify Acme in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature and basis of the dispute. Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.6. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts promptly. ## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **April 26, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Agreement for additional renewal terms on such terms and conditions as the Parties may agree in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience without cause by providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Acme. Contoso shall remain liable for all Fees and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination and any non-cancellable commitments made by Acme in reliance on this Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Acme.** Acme may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience by providing at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to Contoso, provided that such termination shall not relieve Acme of its obligation to refund any prepaid Fees for Services not yet performed as of the effective date of termination, prorated on a daily basis.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**

  (a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement for material breach by the other Party if such breach remains uncured thirty (30) days after the breaching Party’s receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.  
  (b) Without limiting the foregoing, Acme may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if (i) Contoso is in material breach of its payment obligations and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice; or (ii) Contoso materially breaches the License restrictions in Article VI.  
  (c) Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, is placed in the hands of a receiver, enters into bankruptcy or reorganization proceedings, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) All Licenses granted to Contoso under Article VI shall automatically terminate, and Contoso shall immediately cease all use of the Software and Documentation, except as otherwise expressly provided in Section 4.7;  
  (b) Contoso shall promptly return or, at Acme’s option, destroy all copies of the Software and Documentation in its possession or control and certify such destruction in writing within thirty (30) days;  
  (c) All outstanding and unpaid Fees and expenses accrued through the effective date of termination shall become immediately due and payable;  
  (d) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.** At Contoso’s written request received at least sixty (60) days prior to termination or expiration, Acme shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination or expiration, including export of Client Data in a mutually agreed format. Such assistance shall be provided at Acme’s then-current professional services rates, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.8 **Survival.** Articles I, III (with respect to unpaid Fees), V, VI (to the extent indicated therein), VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (with respect to accrued credits only), XIV, XVI, and Sections 4.6, 4.7, and 17.8 shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

5.2 **Obligations.** The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
  (b) Not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party, except to its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement;  
  (c) Protect the confidentiality of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3 **Exceptions.** Confidential Information shall not include information that:

  (a) Is or becomes generally available to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  (c) Is received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
  (d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that, to the extent legally permitted, the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in any effort to obtain a protective order or otherwise limit such disclosure.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s election) all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, and shall certify such destruction in writing, except that the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal and compliance purposes.

5.6 **Survival.** The obligations in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and survive for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall be kept confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

12.1 **Service Availability.** Acme shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the SaaS Software is available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Events (as defined below).

12.3 **Excluded Events.** The following shall be excluded from the calculation of uptime:

  (a) Scheduled Maintenance;  
  (b) Downtime caused by Force Majeure Events as described in Article XV;  
  (c) Downtime caused by Contoso’s acts or omissions, or its failure to use the Software in accordance with the Documentation;  
  (d) Downtime resulting from third-party systems or networks not controlled by Acme.

12.4 **Support Services.** Acme shall provide support services during Business Days and Business Hours (as defined in the Documentation), including:

  (a) **Incident Priorities and Response Times:**  
  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Production system down or severely impacted; initial response within 1 hour; continuous work until resolution or reasonable workaround.  
  - **Priority 2 (High):** Major functionality impacted; initial response within 4 hours; work during Business Hours until resolution.  
  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues; initial response within 1 Business Day.  
  - **Priority 4 (Low):** General inquiries and minor issues; initial response within 2 Business Days.

12.5 **Service Credits.** If Acme fails to meet the 99.9% monthly uptime commitment, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  (a) **99.0%–99.9% availability:** 5% of the monthly subscription Fees as credit;  
  (b) **98.0%–99.0% availability:** 10% of the monthly subscription Fees as credit;  
  (c) **Less than 98.0% availability:** 20% of the monthly subscription Fees as credit.

Service credits shall be applied to future invoices and shall constitute Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet service availability levels, except in cases of willful misconduct or gross negligence by Acme.

12.6 **Claims for Credits.** To receive service credits, Contoso must submit a written claim within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the SLA failure occurred. The claim must contain reasonable details of the incident(s). Failure to submit such claim within the timeframe shall result in waiver of the right to service credits for that month.
