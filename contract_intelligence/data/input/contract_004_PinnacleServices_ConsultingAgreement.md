## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services  

2.1.1 The Service Provider shall provide strategic, operational, and process-improvement consulting services to the Company, which may include, without limitation:  

(a) assessment and analysis of the Company’s existing business processes, organizational structure, technology stack, and operational workflows;  

(b) development of recommendations and roadmaps for improving efficiency, reducing operational risk, enhancing customer experience, and optimizing resource allocation;  

(c) design and documentation of target-state processes, including detailed process maps, role definitions, RACI matrices, and standard operating procedures;  

(d) support for implementation planning, including prioritization of initiatives, resource and budget planning, and proposed implementation timelines;  

(e) limited assistance with change management, stakeholder communication planning, and training material recommendations; and  

(f) such additional consulting, advisory, or related professional services as may be described in any SOW.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the Services are of a consulting and advisory nature and that the Service Provider does not guarantee specific business outcomes, financial results, or regulatory approvals. The Company remains solely responsible for all decisions made based on the Deliverables and recommendations provided by the Service Provider.

### 2.2 Specific Deliverables  

2.2.1 Without limiting the foregoing, and unless otherwise set forth in an applicable SOW, the Deliverables under this Agreement may include:  

(a) **Initial Assessment Report.** A written report summarizing the findings of the Service Provider’s initial assessment of the Company’s current processes and operations, including identified issues, risks, and opportunities for improvement;  

(b) **Process Documentation.** Revised or newly created process documentation, including process flow diagrams, narrative descriptions, and relevant supporting documentation for key operational processes identified by the Parties;  

(c) **Recommendations and Roadmap.** A structured set of recommendations addressing strategic priorities, process improvements, technology enhancements, and organizational adjustments, together with a high-level roadmap that sequences recommended initiatives over time;  

(d) **Performance Metrics and KPIs.** Proposals for relevant metrics and key performance indicators (KPIs) to measure the effectiveness of implemented changes;  

(e) **Status Reports.** Periodic written status reports, at intervals agreed by the Parties (e.g., bi-weekly or monthly), summarizing project progress, key activities completed, upcoming milestones, and identified risks or issues; and  

(f) **Final Summary Presentation.** A concluding presentation or executive summary report outlining the Services performed, the Deliverables created, and the Service Provider’s final recommendations.

2.2.2 The specific Deliverables, format, and level of detail may be further refined in each SOW. To the extent of any conflict between this Article II and an SOW, the SOW shall control with respect to the Services covered by that SOW.

### 2.3 Performance Standards  

2.3.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a timely, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with:  

(a) generally accepted industry standards for consulting services of a similar nature;  

(b) the terms and conditions of this Agreement and any applicable SOW; and  

(c) all applicable laws and regulations.

2.3.2 The Service Provider shall designate a qualified project manager as the primary point of contact for the Company. The project manager shall be responsible for day-to-day coordination, status reporting, and resolving project-level issues. The Company shall also designate a project manager or primary contact to facilitate communication, provide necessary information, and make timely decisions or escalate them internally.

2.3.3 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the timelines and milestones set forth in this Agreement or any SOW. The Parties acknowledge that certain tasks may depend on the Company’s timely provision of information, access, or approvals. The Service Provider shall not be liable for delays caused by the Company’s failure to fulfill its obligations under Section 2.4.

### 2.4 Company Responsibilities  

2.4.1 The Company shall provide the Service Provider with timely and reasonable access to information, personnel, systems, and facilities as may be necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services, subject to the Company’s reasonable security, confidentiality, and safety policies.

2.4.2 The Company shall:  

(a) appoint a project sponsor and project manager with decision-making authority to oversee the Project;  

(b) review and provide feedback on Deliverables within reasonable timeframes specified in the applicable SOW or otherwise agreed in writing;  

(c) promptly notify the Service Provider of any known issues that may materially affect the Services; and  

(d) ensure that all information and materials provided to the Service Provider are accurate and complete to the best of the Company’s knowledge.

2.4.3 The Company acknowledges that the Service Provider is entitled to rely on the accuracy and completeness of information provided by the Company without independent verification, unless specifically agreed otherwise in writing. The Service Provider shall not be responsible for any failure or delay in performing the Services to the extent caused by inaccurate or incomplete information from the Company.

### 2.5 Subcontractors  

2.5.1 The Service Provider may, with the Company’s prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed), engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services. The Service Provider shall remain fully responsible for the acts and omissions of any subcontractors as if they were the acts and omissions of the Service Provider.

2.5.2 The Service Provider shall ensure that any subcontractors performing the Services are bound by written obligations of confidentiality, data protection, and intellectual property assignment that are at least as protective of the Company as those set forth in this Agreement.

### 2.6 Statements of Work  

2.6.1 From time to time, the Parties may enter into one or more SOWs describing specific Services and Deliverables. Each SOW shall:  

(a) reference this Agreement;  

(b) be signed by authorized representatives of both Parties; and  

(c) set forth, as applicable, the scope of work, Deliverables, project schedule, milestones, fees, expenses, key assumptions, and other relevant terms.

2.6.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the terms of this Agreement and the terms of any SOW, the terms of the SOW shall govern solely with respect to the Services and Deliverables described in that SOW, unless the SOW expressly states otherwise.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value; Fee Structure  

3.1.1 The Parties agree that the total contract value for the Services to be performed during the Term (as defined in Section 4.1) shall be **Seventy-Three Thousand Four Hundred Seventy-Eight U.S. Dollars (US$73,478)** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to adjustments for approved Change Orders.

3.1.2 Unless otherwise specified in a particular SOW, the Contract Value shall be structured as a combination of fixed-fee and time-and-materials components, as follows:  

(a) **Fixed-Fee Components.** Certain Deliverables (e.g., Initial Assessment Report, Final Summary Presentation) may be billed on a fixed-fee basis as specified in the SOW; and  

(b) **Time-and-Materials Components.** Advisory and iterative support services (e.g., ongoing process workshops, ad hoc analysis) may be billed at agreed hourly or daily rates, with an aggregate cap such that the total fees do not exceed the Contract Value without a signed Change Order.

3.1.3 The Service Provider shall invoice the Company in accordance with the milestones and schedule set forth in Section 3.2 and any SOW.

### 3.2 Payment Milestones  

Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Contract Value shall be invoiced in the following manner:

(a) **Milestone 1 – Project Initiation (20%).** Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value upon execution of this Agreement and delivery of a mutually agreed project plan outlining key phases and timelines;  

(b) **Milestone 2 – Completion of Initial Assessment (30%).** Thirty percent (30%) of the Contract Value upon delivery of the Initial Assessment Report and the Company’s acceptance (such acceptance not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed);  

(c) **Milestone 3 – Delivery of Interim Deliverables (30%).** Thirty percent (30%) of the Contract Value upon delivery of key interim Deliverables, including updated process documentation and the draft recommendations and roadmap; and  

(d) **Milestone 4 – Final Deliverables (20%).** Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value upon delivery of all final Deliverables, including the Final Summary Presentation, and the Company’s acceptance thereof.

### 3.3 Invoices; Payment Terms  

3.3.1 The Service Provider shall invoice the Company upon achievement of each milestone described in Section 3.2 or as otherwise provided in an SOW. Each invoice shall reference this Agreement, the applicable SOW (if any), and sufficiently detail the Services and Deliverables covered by such invoice.

3.3.2 All undisputed amounts properly invoiced under this Agreement are payable by the Company within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of the applicable invoice (“**Net 60**”). Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or such other method as the Parties may agree in writing.

3.3.3 If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, it shall provide written notice of the dispute to the Service Provider, specifying in reasonable detail the basis of the dispute, within fifteen (15) calendar days of receipt of such invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. The Company shall timely pay all undisputed portions of such invoice.

### 3.4 Late Payments  

3.4.1 Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one percent (1%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower, from the date such payment was due until the date paid in full.

3.4.2 The Service Provider may, upon written notice to the Company, suspend performance of the Services in the event that any undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of such non-payment, provided that the Service Provider resumes performance promptly following the Company’s full payment of all outstanding undisputed amounts and accrued interest.

### 3.5 Expenses and Reimbursements  

3.5.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other related expenses, provided that:

(a) any individual trip with estimated expenses exceeding US$1,500 shall require the Company’s prior written approval; and  

(b) the Service Provider shall comply with the Company’s then-current travel and expense policies, as provided to the Service Provider in writing.

3.5.2 The Service Provider shall submit supporting documentation (e.g., receipts) with any reimbursement request. The Company shall reimburse properly documented and approved expenses in accordance with the Net 60 payment terms in Section 3.3.2.

### 3.6 Taxes  

3.6.1 The fees stated in this Agreement and any SOW are exclusive of Taxes. The Company shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, excluding taxes based on the Service Provider’s income, property, or employees.

3.6.2 If the Service Provider is required to collect or pay any Taxes on the Company’s behalf, the Service Provider shall itemize such Taxes on the relevant invoice, and the Company shall reimburse the Service Provider accordingly, except to the extent the Company provides a valid tax exemption certificate.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term  

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until March 1, 2029 (the “**Expiration Date**”) (such period, the “**Term**”).

4.1.2 The Parties may extend the Term by mutual written agreement prior to the Expiration Date.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience  

4.2.1 The Company may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon not less than thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the Service Provider.

4.2.2 The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to the Company, provided that no such termination shall take effect prior to completion of any critical Deliverables due within such 60-day period, unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Company.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause  

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  

(a) materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of the breach (or within such longer cure period as the non-breaching Party may authorize in writing); or  

(b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, or has a substantial portion of its assets assigned for the benefit of creditors.

4.3.2 For clarity, repeated non-trivial breaches may constitute a material breach. Non-payment by the Company of undisputed amounts in accordance with Article III shall be deemed a material breach if not cured within the applicable cure period.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination  

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) the Service Provider shall cease performing the Services (except to the extent necessary to wind down in an orderly manner or as otherwise directed by the Company);  

(b) the Company shall promptly pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted up to the effective date of termination, plus any approved expenses incurred and non-cancellable commitments entered into in reliance on this Agreement; and  

(c) each Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s option) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to Section 5.5.

4.4.2 In the event of termination by the Company for cause pursuant to Section 4.3.1(a), the Company shall not be obligated to pay for any Services or Deliverables that are materially deficient or non-conforming and not remedied within the applicable cure period.

4.4.3 In the event of termination by the Service Provider for cause, the Service Provider shall be entitled to payment for all Services properly performed through the effective date of termination, plus any approved expenses and non-cancellable commitments.

4.4.4 Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not affect any accrued rights or obligations of either Party, including payment obligations, nor shall it affect any provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination, as provided in Section 16.9.

5.1.1 “**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, visually, in writing, electronically, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. ### 5.2 Exclusions  

5.2.1 Confidential Information shall not include information that:  

(a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  

(b) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information;  

(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous documentation; or  

(d) was already known to the Receiving Party at the time of disclosure without an obligation of confidentiality, as evidenced by the Receiving Party’s records.

### 5.3 Confidentiality Obligations  

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:

(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  

(b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted under this Agreement; and  

(c) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party may disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, advisors, and Affiliates who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein. The Receiving Party shall remain responsible for any breach of this Article V by any such persons.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure  

5.4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing and cooperate with the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed, provided that the Receiving Party uses reasonable efforts to ensure that the disclosed information is treated as confidential.

### 5.5 Return or Destruction  

5.5.1 Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain:  

(a) one (1) archival copy solely for purposes of legal or regulatory compliance; and  

(b) copies that are automatically created through electronic backup systems, to the extent such copies are not readily accessible in the ordinary course of business and remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

### 5.6 Survival  

5.6.1 The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and survive for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that any trade secrets shall be maintained as confidential for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.
