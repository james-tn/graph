## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Phoenix shall manufacture, supply, and deliver to Contoso, and Contoso shall purchase from Phoenix, the Products and related Services set forth herein and in the applicable POs and SOWs. The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement establishes a framework under which Contoso may, but is not obligated to, issue POs and SOWs from time to time. No minimum purchase obligation is created unless explicitly stated in a specific SOW.

2.2 **Products.**

2.2.1 **Description.** The Products to be supplied by Phoenix shall primarily consist of high-performance industrial components and assemblies, including, without limitation, precision-machined parts, control modules, sensor arrays, mounting hardware, and related accessories necessary for integration into Contoso’s technology-enabled industrial equipment. Each PO or SOW shall identify the specific Product models, part numbers, technical specifications, and quality standards.

2.2.2 **Specifications.** Phoenix shall manufacture and supply Products in accordance with (a) the written specifications, drawings, and technical documentation provided by Contoso and accepted by Phoenix; (b) Phoenix’s standard product specifications where Contoso has not provided specific requirements; and (c) all applicable laws and regulations. In the event of a conflict between Contoso’s specifications and Phoenix’s standard specifications, the Parties shall resolve such conflict in writing prior to commencement of manufacture.

2.2.3 **Packaging and Labeling.** Phoenix shall package and label the Products in a manner suitable for domestic shipment to the delivery locations specified by Contoso, consistent with industry standards, and in accordance with any packaging or labeling requirements set forth in the applicable SOW or PO.

2.3 **Services.**

2.3.1 **Implementation and Integration Services.** Phoenix shall, as requested by Contoso in applicable SOWs, provide implementation, installation, and integration Services to assist Contoso in incorporating the Products into Contoso’s manufacturing lines or equipment. Such Services may include site surveys, integration design, configuration, testing, commissioning, and support of Contoso’s internal engineering teams.

2.3.2 **Support and Maintenance Services.** Where specified in an SOW, Phoenix shall provide ongoing maintenance and support Services, including preventive maintenance, troubleshooting assistance, repair or replacement of defective Products, and remote technical support. Service Levels applicable to support Services shall be set forth in **Article XII** and any applicable SOW.

2.3.3 **Training and Documentation.** Upon Contoso’s reasonable request and subject to applicable Fees set forth in the relevant SOW, Phoenix shall provide training sessions (on-site or remote) to Contoso’s personnel on the operation, maintenance, and troubleshooting of the Products. Phoenix shall also provide user manuals, technical documentation, maintenance guides, and other supporting materials in English, in electronic or hard copy format, as reasonably required by Contoso.

2.4 **Deliverables.** Phoenix shall provide all Deliverables identified in each SOW, which may include engineering drawings, configuration files, test reports, training materials, or other documentation and work product. Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Deliverables shall be provided in industry standard electronic formats (e.g., PDF, DOCX, XLSX, CAD formats) and shall be delivered via secure electronic transmission or other method agreed by the Parties.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**

2.5.1 **Professional Manner.** Phoenix shall perform the Services in a prompt, diligent, and professional manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for providers of similar products and services and shall exercise at least the same degree of care and skill that Phoenix exercises for its own customers of similar size and importance.

2.5.2 **Personnel.** Phoenix shall assign appropriately skilled and qualified personnel to perform the Services. Phoenix shall be solely responsible for the direction, control, and compensation of its personnel, and nothing herein shall be construed as creating an employment relationship between Contoso and any Phoenix personnel.

2.5.3 **Compliance with Laws.** Phoenix shall perform the Services and supply the Products in compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances, including safety, environmental, export control, and labor laws.

2.6 **Project Planning and Timelines.**

2.6.1 **Project Plans.** For complex implementations or large-volume orders, the Parties may agree in an SOW upon a written project plan, including milestones, responsibilities of each Party, dependencies, and timelines. Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the project milestones and delivery dates set forth in the applicable SOW.

2.6.2 **Delay and Notice.** Phoenix shall promptly notify Contoso in writing if Phoenix becomes aware of any actual or anticipated delay in the delivery of Products, Deliverables, or performance of Services. Phoenix’s notice shall include the reason for the delay and the anticipated revised schedule. Phoenix shall not be liable for delays caused by Contoso’s acts or omissions, including failure to provide timely approvals, information, access, or materials.

2.6.3 **Contoso Responsibilities.** Contoso shall provide Phoenix, on a timely basis, with all information, data, access, and cooperation reasonably required for Phoenix to perform its obligations under this Agreement, including access to Contoso’s facilities, systems, and personnel, subject to Contoso’s reasonable security policies.

2.7 **Acceptance.**

2.7.1 **Products.** Unless otherwise stated in an SOW or PO, Products shall be deemed accepted upon delivery to Contoso’s designated facility, subject to Contoso’s right to inspect for obvious shipping damage or manifest nonconformities. Contoso shall notify Phoenix in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of delivery of any Products that Contoso reasonably alleges to be nonconforming. Failure to so notify Phoenix within such period shall constitute acceptance of the Products, except with respect to latent defects.

2.7.2 **Services and Deliverables.** Where acceptance criteria or procedures are specified in an SOW, Contoso shall review and test the Deliverables and Services in accordance with such procedures. If no acceptance criteria are specified, Services and Deliverables shall be deemed accepted ten (10) Business Days after delivery or completion unless Contoso notifies Phoenix in writing of a material nonconformity within such period. Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct any material nonconformities identified by Contoso.

2.8 **Non-Exclusivity.** Unless expressly stated otherwise in an SOW, this Agreement is non-exclusive and does not restrict either Party from entering into similar agreements with third parties. Contoso acknowledges that Phoenix may provide similar products and services to other customers, including competitors of Contoso.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge that the anticipated aggregate value of purchases under this Agreement is **Four Million Seven Hundred Fifty-Nine Thousand Two Hundred Eighty-One U.S. Dollars (US$4,759,281)** (the “**Contract Value**”). This amount is an estimate based on Contoso’s projected purchase volumes and is not a guaranteed minimum commitment unless explicitly stated otherwise in an SOW.

3.2 **Pricing and Fee Schedule.**

3.2.1 **Product Pricing.** Prices for Products shall be as set forth in the applicable SOW or PO. Unless otherwise specified, prices are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, and other taxes (collectively, “**Taxes**”), which shall be borne by Contoso, except for taxes based on Phoenix’s net income.

3.2.2 **Service Fees.** Fees for Services, including implementation, integration, maintenance, support, and training, shall be set forth in each SOW. Service Fees may be based on fixed price, time and materials, or a combination thereof, as agreed in writing.

3.2.3 **Adjustments.** Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Phoenix may propose adjustments to its standard pricing once per contract year to reflect material changes in raw material costs, labor rates, or regulatory requirements. Any such adjustments shall be effective for future POs and SOWs upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Contoso and Contoso’s acceptance via continued issuance of POs.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**

3.3.1 **Invoices.** Phoenix shall invoice Contoso for Products upon shipment (or as otherwise specified in an SOW) and for Services in accordance with the milestones or billing schedule set forth in the applicable SOW. All invoices shall reference this Agreement and, where applicable, the relevant PO or SOW.

3.3.2 **Net 30.** Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts due under each invoice within **thirty (30) days** after the invoice date (“**Net 30**”), by wire transfer or other method agreed by the Parties.

3.3.3 **Disputed Amounts.** If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall provide written notice to Phoenix within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute. Any resolved amounts shall be promptly paid within ten (10) days of resolution.

3.4 **Payment Milestones.** If an SOW includes payment milestones, Phoenix shall invoice Contoso upon completion of each milestone, as certified by Phoenix and, where applicable, acknowledged by Contoso. Milestone invoices shall be payable in accordance with **Section 3.3.2**.

3.5 **Late Payment.**

3.5.1 **Interest.** Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of payment at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

3.5.2 **Suspension of Performance.** In addition to other rights and remedies, Phoenix may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice and failure by Contoso to cure within such period, suspend future deliveries of Products and performance of Services until all overdue amounts and applicable interest are paid in full.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.** Contoso shall reimburse Phoenix for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Phoenix in the performance of Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and materials, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current expense reimbursement policies provided to Phoenix in advance. Phoenix shall provide reasonable supporting documentation with any request for reimbursement.

3.7 **Set-Off.** Subject to applicable law, Phoenix shall be entitled, in its sole discretion and without prior notice, to set off any amounts owed by Phoenix to Contoso against any amounts owed by Contoso to Phoenix, whether under this Agreement or any other agreement between the Parties.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall remain in full force and effect until **November 02, 2025** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (such period, the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend this Agreement on such terms and for such additional period(s) as they may mutually agree.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. For clarity, this extended notice period is favorable to Phoenix as Vendor and is a material term of this high-risk Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent or is generally unable to pay its debts as they become due; (b) files or has filed against it a petition under any bankruptcy or insolvency law that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of its creditors or has a receiver appointed over its assets.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**

4.5.1 **Accrued Obligations.** Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of any obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including payment obligations for Products delivered and Services performed.

4.5.2 **Existing Orders and SOWs.** Unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing, termination of this Agreement shall not automatically terminate any existing POs or SOWs, which shall remain in effect in accordance with their terms and subject to this Agreement. However, either Party may, to the extent permitted in such POs or SOWs, terminate them in accordance with their terms.

4.5.3 **Return of Materials.** Upon termination or expiration, each Party shall, upon the other Party’s written request and subject to applicable law, return or destroy (and certify destruction of) any Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession, as provided in **Section 5.4**.

4.5.4 **Transition Assistance.** Upon Contoso’s written request received within thirty (30) days following termination or expiration, Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide limited transition assistance Services (subject to mutually agreed scope and Fees) for up to ninety (90) days to facilitate Contoso’s orderly transition of Services to another provider or in-house solution.

4.6 **Survival.** The following provisions shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement: **Articles I, III (as to accrued payment obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and all other provisions which, by their nature, are intended to survive.

5.3 **Permitted Disclosures.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, Affiliates, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein. The Receiving Party shall remain responsible for any breach of this **Article V** by any such persons.

5.4 **Exceptions.** The obligations in this **Article V** shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by competent evidence: (a) was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction at the time of disclosure; (b) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party; (c) is lawfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.5 **Required Disclosures.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that (to the extent legally permissible) the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.6 **Return or Destruction.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain one copy in its legal files for record-keeping and compliance purposes, subject to continuing confidentiality obligations.

5.7 **Survival.** The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** after termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information qualifies as a trade secret or is subject to protection under applicable Data Protection Laws.
