## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK  

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive employment and staffing services, which may include, without limitation: (a) recruitment, sourcing, and placement of candidates for temporary, contract, and permanent positions; (b) provision of managed services for contingent workforce programs; (c) onboarding and offboarding support; (d) payroll administration and related HR support for Vendor Personnel assigned to Client; (e) workforce planning and consulting; and (f) such other related services as may be described in one or more SOWs (collectively, the **“Employment Services”**).

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) Each SOW shall, at a minimum, set forth:  
   (i) a description of the specific positions or categories of roles to be filled;  
   (ii) the scope of Employment Services to be provided;  
   (iii) applicable Deliverables and milestones;  
   (iv) estimated or fixed timelines for recruitment, onboarding, and project completion (if applicable);  
   (v) applicable Service Levels; and  
   (vi) the fees, payment schedule, and any special expense or travel arrangements.

(b) In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to override a specific provision of this Agreement, in which case such specific provision of the SOW shall govern solely for that SOW.

2.3 **Recruitment and Selection.**  
(a) Vendor shall, at its own expense, conduct candidate sourcing through appropriate channels, including but not limited to online job postings, professional networks, and recruitment agencies.  
(b) Vendor shall screen and evaluate candidates for skills, experience, and suitability based on criteria provided by Client. Such evaluation may include interviews, skills assessments, background checks (where permissible by law and as requested by Client), and reference checks.  
(c) Vendor shall present to Client only those candidates who reasonably meet the position requirements, in Vendor’s professional judgment, and shall provide relevant candidate profiles, resumes, and supporting documentation as reasonably requested by Client.  
(d) Client shall retain ultimate authority to approve or reject any proposed candidate. Vendor does not guarantee that any particular candidate will meet Client’s performance expectations, but Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts and industry-standard practices to identify suitable candidates.

2.4 **Onboarding and Assignment.**  
(a) Upon Client’s acceptance of a candidate, Vendor shall coordinate with Client to onboard the selected candidate as Vendor Personnel assigned to work for Client. Vendor shall ensure that Vendor Personnel complete Vendor’s standard employment documentation and any Client-specific forms reasonably requested by Client and agreed by Vendor.  
(b) Vendor shall be the employer of record for Vendor Personnel unless otherwise expressly stated in an SOW. Client shall not be deemed the employer of Vendor Personnel and shall have no responsibility for payroll taxes, benefits, or other employer obligations with respect to Vendor Personnel, except as required by Applicable Law or expressly set forth in this Agreement or an SOW.  
(c) Vendor shall assign Vendor Personnel to perform Services at Client premises, Client-designated sites, or remotely, as specified in the applicable SOW. Client shall provide Vendor Personnel with reasonable access to Client’s facilities, systems, and tools to the extent necessary for performance of the Services, subject to Client’s security and access policies.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform the Employment Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with (i) generally accepted industry standards, (ii) Applicable Law, and (iii) any Service Levels set out in Article XII or in an SOW.  
(b) Vendor shall implement appropriate management and oversight practices to monitor the performance of Vendor Personnel, including periodic performance reviews, performance issue escalation processes, and corrective action plans where necessary.  
(c) Vendor shall promptly address any performance issues involving Vendor Personnel. At Client’s reasonable request, Vendor shall remove or reassign Vendor Personnel whose continued assignment is deemed unsatisfactory by Client, provided that Client provides reasonable explanation to Vendor. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to replace such Vendor Personnel in a timely manner.

2.6 **Deliverables and Timelines.**  
(a) The specific Deliverables (such as periodic staffing reports, hiring metrics, candidate pipeline data, compliance reports, or project deliverables associated with specific roles) shall be described in the applicable SOW.  
(b) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet any target timelines for candidate submissions, hiring, and project completion described in an SOW. However, the Parties acknowledge that actual timelines may be influenced by labor market conditions, candidate availability, and Client’s internal decision-making processes.  
(c) Vendor shall provide Client with regular status updates, at intervals specified in the applicable SOW (or at least monthly absent such specification), summarizing progress against staffing targets, candidate pipeline status, and any issues that may materially affect timelines.

2.7 **Compliance and Work Authorization.**  
(a) Vendor shall be responsible for ensuring that Vendor Personnel assigned to Client have the legal right to work in the applicable jurisdiction, including securing any required work permits or visas, except where the Parties mutually agree that Client will sponsor certain work authorizations.  
(b) Vendor shall maintain accurate employment and assignment records, including timesheets, employment contracts, and proof of work authorization, and shall make such records available to Client upon reasonable request and subject to Applicable Law.

2.8 **Tools, Equipment, and Training.**  
(a) Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Vendor shall be responsible for providing Vendor Personnel with necessary personal tools and equipment for performance of the Services. If Vendor Personnel are required to use Client-owned equipment or systems, such use shall be subject to Client’s policies and shall not confer any ownership or lease rights on Vendor or Vendor Personnel.  
(b) Vendor shall ensure that Vendor Personnel have the necessary skills and training to perform their assigned roles. Client may, at its discretion, provide Client-specific systems or process training. Vendor shall ensure that Vendor Personnel complete any mandatory training required by Client or Applicable Law, including data privacy and security training.

2.9 **High-Risk Allocation and Assumption of Risk.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the Services may involve high operational and business risk given the volume and criticality of personnel placements. Vendor agrees to undertake such risk in reliance on the commercial terms and limitations set forth herein, and Client acknowledges that Vendor’s pricing reflects this allocation of risk and the broad protections afforded to Vendor under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS  

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The aggregate maximum contract value for Services to be performed under this Agreement during the initial Term, inclusive of all SOWs, shall be **One Million Two Hundred Twenty-Three Thousand Two Hundred Sixty-Four U.S. Dollars (US $1,223,264)**, unless otherwise increased by written amendment or additional SOWs duly executed by the Parties.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
(a) **Recruitment and Placement Fees.** For permanent placements, Vendor shall be entitled to a placement fee as specified in the applicable SOW, which may be structured as a percentage of the placed candidate’s first-year annualized base salary, a fixed fee, or another mutually agreed structure.  
(b) **Hourly / Daily / Project-Based Fees.** For temporary, contract, or project-based assignments, Vendor shall charge Client the hourly, daily, or project-based rates set forth in the applicable SOW.  
(c) **Retainer or Management Fees.** For managed services or recruitment process outsourcing engagements, Vendor may charge a recurring monthly or quarterly management fee as specified in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment.**  
(a) Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the schedule and invoicing procedures specified in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Vendor shall invoice monthly in arrears for Services performed and Deliverables provided during the preceding month.  
(b) All undisputed amounts set forth in Vendor’s invoices shall be due and payable by Client within **sixty (60) days** from the date of invoice (**“Net 60”**).  
(c) Client shall make all payments in U.S. Dollars by electronic funds transfer or other payment method reasonably acceptable to Vendor.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of payment at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law.  
(b) Vendor may, upon written notice to Client, suspend performance of Services in the event that Client fails to pay any undisputed fees within thirty (30) days following the due date, and such failure continues for ten (10) Business Days after Vendor provides written notice thereof. Any suspension shall not relieve Client of its payment obligations.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
Client may withhold payment of amounts reasonably and in good faith disputed by Client, provided that Client: (a) pays all undisputed portions of the invoice; (b) notifies Vendor in writing of the disputed portion and the reasons therefor within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice; and (c) cooperates with Vendor to promptly resolve the dispute. Upon resolution, Client shall pay any previously withheld amounts determined to be owed within fifteen (15) days of such resolution.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
(a) The fees payable to Vendor under this Agreement do not include any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, except for taxes on Vendor’s net income, employment-related taxes for Vendor’s direct employees, and any taxes that are by law imposed on Vendor and may not be passed through to Client.  
(b) If Vendor is required to collect and remit any such taxes on Client’s behalf, Vendor shall itemize such taxes on its invoices, and Client shall reimburse Vendor for such amounts.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other business expenses, in accordance with Client’s then-current travel and expense policies provided to Vendor in advance.  
(b) Vendor shall provide appropriate documentation, including receipts, for all reimbursable expenses.  
(c) Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Vendor shall not be obligated to incur expenses exceeding the amounts specified in the applicable SOW without Client’s prior written approval.

3.8 **No Setoff.**  
Except for amounts disputed in good faith in accordance with Section 3.5, Client shall pay all amounts due under this Agreement without setoff, counterclaim, deduction, or withholding of any kind, except as may be required by Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION  

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date of July 11, 2026** (the **“Term”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV. Any SOW in effect as of the Expiration Date shall continue in accordance with its terms, unless otherwise agreed in writing, provided that the terms of this Agreement shall continue to govern such SOW until its completion or termination.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for additional periods under terms and conditions to be mutually agreed upon at the time of renewal.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing Vendor with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**. Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon the same one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Client. The Parties acknowledge that this extended notice period is a material inducement for Vendor’s willingness to enter into this high-risk engagement.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
   (i) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or the applicable SOW and such breach remains uncured thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice of such breach (or such longer cure period as may be reasonably required, provided the breaching Party diligently and continuously pursues cure); or  
   (ii) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed for substantial portions of its assets, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.  
(b) Without limiting Vendor’s other rights and remedies, Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after its due date and such failure is not cured within ten (10) Business Days after Vendor’s written notice.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
(a) Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:  
   (i) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and authorized expenses incurred through the effective date of termination;  
   (ii) all licenses granted by Vendor to Client shall terminate as set forth in Article VI, except for any licenses expressly stated to survive;  
   (iii) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days after termination, at Vendor’s then-current rates, if requested by Client; and  
   (iv) each Party shall, upon written request, return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.  
(b) Termination or expiration shall not relieve either Party of any obligations that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination or any obligations that by their nature are intended to survive, as set forth in Section 16.10.

4.6 **Survival.**  
Without limitation, the following provisions shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement: Articles III (to the extent of accrued payment obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provisions which by their terms or nature should survive.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY  

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public, confidential, or proprietary information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**), whether orally, in writing, visually, or by inspection of tangible objects, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, strategies, financial information, pricing, customer data, vendor lists, know-how, trade secrets, technical information, processes, software, Personal Data, and the terms of this Agreement and any SOW.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that:  
(a) is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was rightfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction on use or disclosure prior to receipt from the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction on use or disclosure; or  
(d) is independently developed by employees or contractors of the Receiving Party without access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
(a) The Receiving Party shall:  
   (i) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;  
   (ii) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted by this Agreement; and  
   (iii) implement reasonable and appropriate safeguards to protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information from unauthorized use or disclosure, which safeguards shall be no less protective than those the Receiving Party uses to protect its own similar information of like importance, but in no event less than a commercially reasonable standard of care.  
(b) The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, advisors, and Affiliates who have a strict need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing so that the Disclosing Party may seek an appropriate protective order or waive compliance with this Agreement. The Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of Confidential Information that is legally required and shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that such information is treated confidentially.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible forms of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) one archival copy solely for legal or compliance purposes and (b) any copies stored in routine backup systems that are not readily accessible in the ordinary course of business, provided that any retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations under this Article V shall remain in effect during the Term and for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.

(c) Service credits, if provided, shall be Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA breaches, without limiting Client’s other rights under this Agreement.

In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve the matter through informal negotiations. ## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE  

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes, labor disputes (other than those involving the non-performing Party’s own workforce), epidemics or pandemics, governmental actions, embargoes, widespread utility failures, and internet or telecommunications outages not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature, extent, and anticipated duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  
(c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of ongoing developments.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. The affected Party’s failure to perform due to a Force Majeure Event shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement; provided that Client shall remain obligated to pay for any Services actually performed.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or, if the Force Majeure Event materially affects the entire Agreement, the Agreement itself, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice.
