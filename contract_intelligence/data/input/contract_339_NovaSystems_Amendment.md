## PREAMBLE & RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Parties entered into that certain **Software License Agreement**, reference number **SLA-NOV-202412-265**, dated **December 23, 2024**, identified internally as **“contract_265”** (the “**Parent Agreement**” or “**Master Agreement**”), pursuant to which Nova Systems licenses to Contoso certain software and provides related services, including the “NovaCore Platform,” as more particularly described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, Section **16.2 (Amendments)** of the Parent Agreement provides that no amendment or modification of the Parent Agreement shall be valid or binding unless it is in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties desire to enter into this Amendment, which **modifies and supplements the Software License Agreement dated December 23, 2024 (Ref: SLA-NOV-202412-265, contract_265)** in order to (a) expand the scope of Services and Deliverables related to the NovaCore Platform, (b) adjust certain Fees and payment terms under **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement, (c) specify additional Service Levels in alignment with **Article XII (Service Level Agreements (SLA))** of the Parent Agreement, and (d) clarify the relationship of such additional Services to the Term and termination provisions set forth in **Article IV (Term and Termination)** of the Parent Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties acknowledge that both the Parent Agreement and this Amendment are designated as **High Risk**, and consistent with **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)** and **Article VIII (Indemnification)** of the Parent Agreement, this Amendment continues the Parties’ agreement to operate without a monetary cap on liability and to provide broad indemnification obligations, particularly in favor of Vendor;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties desire to set forth their respective rights, obligations, and responsibilities with respect to the additional licensing, configuration, and professional services to be provided by Vendor under this Amendment, which shall be performed in conjunction with and under the framework of the Parent Agreement;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and promises contained in this Amendment and in the Parent Agreement, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 Relationship to Parent Agreement

2.1.1 This Amendment **amends and supplements** the scope of the Software and Services described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Unless expressly modified herein, all provisions of Article II of the Parent Agreement shall continue to apply to the Software, Services, and Deliverables, including those added under this Amendment.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that Section 2.1.2 of the Parent Agreement identifies the “NovaCore Platform” and associated modules as the Software. The Extended Modules defined in Section 1.4 of this Amendment are hereby incorporated into that definition and shall be deemed part of the “Software” for all purposes of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

2.1.3 For clarity, **Section 2.2 (Specific Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement is expanded to include the Amendment Deliverables described in this Article II. Where a conflict exists, the explicit provisions of this Amendment shall control with respect to the subject matter hereof, consistent with Section 2.2.2 of the Parent Agreement.

### 2.2 Description of Amendment Services

2.2.1 Vendor shall provide the **Enhancement Services** necessary to implement and operationalize the Extended Modules, including the following categories of work (which are in addition to, and not in limitation of, the Services described in the Parent Agreement):

  (a) **Requirements and Design Workshops**  
  Vendor shall conduct detailed workshops with Client’s finance, HR, procurement, and risk teams to gather business and technical requirements specific to the Extended Modules. The workshops will refine use cases, reporting requirements, data sources, and integration points beyond those contemplated in Section 2.2.1(b) and (d) of the Parent Agreement.

  (b) **Configuration of Extended Modules**  
  Vendor shall configure the Advanced Financial Analytics Module, Predictive Workforce Planning Module, and Supplier Risk Scoring Module in Client’s non-production and production environments. Such configuration shall include set-up of algorithms, scoring models, dashboard layouts, and thresholds tailored to Client’s business rules.

  (c) **Integration Services**  
  Building on the integration work described in Section 2.2.1(d) of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall design and implement new or extended integrations between the Extended Modules and Client’s HR, ERP, procurement, and third‑party risk solutions, including secure API-based data exchanges and ETL processes as mutually agreed in writing.

  (d) **Data Migration and Historical Loading**  
  Vendor shall assist Client with mapping, cleansing, and loading up to five (5) years of historical financial, workforce, and supplier data into the Extended Modules, subject to data availability and quality. Client remains responsible for data accuracy per Section 2.6.2 of the Parent Agreement.

  (e) **Testing and Validation Support**  
  Vendor shall provide unit testing, system testing, and support for User Acceptance Testing (“UAT”) related to the Extended Modules, including creation of sample test cases, expected results, and UAT guidance documentation.

  (f) **Training and Knowledge Transfer**  
  Vendor shall provide up to six (6) training sessions (remote or onsite as mutually agreed) to Client’s business users, analysts, and administrators to support adoption of the Extended Modules. Training materials will form part of the Amendment Deliverables.

  (g) **Post-Go-Live Hypercare**  
  For a period of sixty (60) days after the initial production go-live of each Extended Module, Vendor shall provide “hypercare” support, consisting of prioritized incident handling and configuration tweaks within scope, in addition to the Maintenance and Support Services described in Section 2.5 of the Parent Agreement.

### 2.3 Additional Users and Usage Parameters

2.3.1 In addition to the Users licensed under the Parent Agreement, Client is hereby authorized to designate up to **150 Additional Users** to access and use the Extended Modules, subject to Client’s compliance with the license restrictions set forth in **Section 6.2 (License Grant and Restrictions)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.3.2 The Additional Users shall be treated as “Users” for all purposes of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, and Client shall remain responsible for their compliance in accordance with Section 2.6.1(b) of the Parent Agreement.

2.3.3 Any use of the Extended Modules by more than 150 Additional Users shall require a mutually executed Change Order pursuant to Article XIII of the Parent Agreement and Article XIII of this Amendment, and will involve incremental Amendment Fees.

### 2.4 Timelines and Milestones

2.4.1 The Parties acknowledge the anticipated initial implementation period of six (6) to nine (9) months for core modules described in Section 2.3.3 of the Parent Agreement. The Enhancement Services under this Amendment are expected to occur concurrently with, or subsequent to, that initial period and shall be performed in phases aligned with the following target milestones (subject to Change Orders):

  - Completion of requirements and design workshops: within sixty (60) days of the Amendment Effective Date;  
  - Completion of initial configuration and non-production deployment of all Extended Modules: within one hundred twenty (120) days of the Amendment Effective Date;  
  - Completion of integration build and data migration: within one hundred eighty (180) days of the Amendment Effective Date;  
  - Production go-live of Extended Modules: within two hundred ten (210) days of the Amendment Effective Date;  
  - Completion of hypercare: sixty (60) days after each respective production go-live.

2.4.2 All timelines are subject to Client fulfilling its responsibilities under Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement and Section 2.6 of this Amendment. In particular, delays caused by Client in providing data, resources, or approvals shall extend the above milestones on a day‑for‑day basis and may result in additional Amendment Fees pursuant to Article III of this Amendment and **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement.

### 2.5 Performance Standards

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Amendment Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with Section 2.4.1 of the Parent Agreement and with generally accepted industry standards.

2.5.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Extended Modules substantially conform to the applicable Documentation and any configuration specifications agreed in writing. The uptime and support performance for the Extended Modules shall be subject to the SLA provisions in Article XII of the Parent Agreement as enhanced by Article XII of this Amendment.

### 2.6 Client Responsibilities (Amendment-Specific)

2.6.1 In addition to Client’s responsibilities under **Section 2.6 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to all relevant data sources, including HR, finance, procurement, and supplier risk systems, and ensure appropriate credentials and permissions;  
  (b) Assign business owners and subject-matter experts to participate in design workshops, testing, and sign-off activities;  
  (c) Validate the accuracy and completeness of the data mappings and configurations proposed by Vendor; and  
  (d) Ensure that its systems continue to meet the minimum technical requirements for the Extended Modules.

2.6.2 Vendor shall not be responsible for any delays, inaccuracies, or failures in achieving business outcomes resulting from Client’s failure to perform the foregoing obligations, consistent with Section 2.6.2 of the Parent Agreement.

### 2.7 Acceptance of Amendment Deliverables

2.7.1 The acceptance process in **Section 2.7 (Acceptance)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to all Amendment Deliverables. Where no specific acceptance period is stated in an SOW related to this Amendment, the default acceptance period shall be fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery.

2.7.2 If Client fails to notify Vendor of material non-conformities within the applicable acceptance period, the applicable Amendment Deliverables shall be deemed accepted.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Amendment Term

4.1.1 This Amendment shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date and continue until **June 13, 2026** (the “**Amendment Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

### 4.2 Relationship to Parent Agreement Term

4.2.1 The Term of the Parent Agreement, as defined in **Section 4.1 (Term)** thereof, currently extends through the **Expiration Date of June 18, 2026** and may automatically renew for successive Renewal Terms.

4.2.2 The Amendment Term shall at all times be coterminous with and subject to the Term of the Parent Agreement. If the Parent Agreement expires or is terminated for any reason, this Amendment shall automatically expire or terminate on the same effective date, without need for separate notice, subject to Section 4.4 of this Amendment.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience

4.3.1 Consistent with **Section 4.2 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent Agreement, either Party may terminate this Amendment for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. Such termination for convenience of this Amendment shall not, by itself, terminate the Parent Agreement unless expressly stated in the notice.

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall remain liable for:

  (a) All unpaid Amendment Fees for Services performed and Extended Modules already provided up to the effective date of termination;  
  (b) All non-cancellable commitments made by Vendor in reliance on this Amendment; and  
  (c) All remaining Extended Module Subscription Fees for the then-current Subscription Term applicable to the Extended Modules, which the Parties agree are a reasonable approximation of Vendor’s damages, consistent with Section 4.2.3(c) of the Parent Agreement.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause; Effect

4.4.1 The termination for cause provisions in **Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Amendment. Either Party may terminate this Amendment for cause if the other Party materially breaches this Amendment or the Parent Agreement (as it relates to the Extended Modules or Amendment Services) and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice.

4.4.2 Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment for any reason:

  (a) All rights and licenses granted to Client with respect to the Extended Modules shall immediately cease;  
  (b) Client shall cease use of the Extended Modules and shall comply with Section 4.4.1 of the Parent Agreement with respect to return or destruction of Vendor Confidential Information and any software components;  
  (c) Client shall remain obligated to pay all Amendment Fees accrued or payable prior to the effective date of such expiration or termination; and  
  (d) The survival provisions in Section 4.4.3 of the Parent Agreement shall apply, including the survival of Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI, as well as the corresponding Articles of this Amendment that by their nature are intended to survive.

4.4.3 **Transition Assistance.** At Client’s written request received within thirty (30) days after termination or expiration of this Amendment, Vendor shall provide limited Transition Assistance, including exporting Client Data from the Extended Modules and providing configuration documentation. Such assistance shall be provided on a time-and-materials basis at Vendor’s then-current professional services rates, subject to the confidentiality and data protection provisions of this Amendment and the Parent Agreement.
