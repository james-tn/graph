## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall perform for the Company ongoing, comprehensive **Maintenance Services** in respect of the Company’s designated facilities, systems, and related infrastructure. Such Services shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **Preventive Maintenance**: Scheduled inspections, tests, calibrations, adjustments, cleaning, lubrication, and parts replacement of critical equipment and systems (including but not limited to HVAC, electrical systems, backup power, building automation systems, production equipment, and IT infrastructure components) performed in accordance with industry best practices, manufacturer recommendations, and any specific schedules agreed upon in an applicable SOW;

  (b) **Corrective Maintenance**: Diagnosis and rectification of faults, failures, or malfunctions in the Company’s systems or equipment, including repair or replacement of defective parts or components, troubleshooting, and restoration of affected systems to normal operating conditions;

  (c) **Emergency Maintenance & Incident Response**: Priority response to unplanned outages, safety-critical incidents, or critical system failures that significantly impact facility operations, with response times and escalation procedures as set forth in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**;

  (d) **Monitoring and Reporting**: Collection and analysis of operational and maintenance data, including periodic status reports, performance metrics, failure analysis, and recommendations for improvements, as further described in **Section 2.5**;

  (e) **Planning and Advisory Services**: Technical consultation and recommendations regarding maintenance strategies, replacement schedules, risk mitigation, and lifecycle management for covered equipment and systems; and

  (f) **Other Related Services**: Such other services as may be set forth in one or more SOWs or mutually agreed Change Orders in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.2 **Facilities and Systems Covered.**  
The specific facilities, buildings, locations, and systems to be covered by the Maintenance Services shall be described in one or more SOWs, which shall incorporate, by reference, the Contract Reference Number **MNT-HOR-202502-566** and this Agreement. Each SOW shall specify, at a minimum:

  (a) the physical locations and/or systems covered;  
  (b) the nature and frequency of preventive maintenance activities;  
  (c) any special conditions (e.g., cleanroom standards, 24/7 operational requirements, security constraints); and  
  (d) applicable Service Levels, if different from those set forth in **Article XII**.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services and in compliance with applicable laws and regulations;  

  (b) in accordance with all safety regulations applicable to the facilities, including the Company’s reasonable site-specific safety policies communicated in writing to the Service Provider;  

  (c) in a manner that uses Personnel who are appropriately trained, experienced, and qualified for the tasks assigned to them; and  

  (d) in compliance with the Service Levels, including response times, resolution times, and availability targets, as specified in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** and any applicable SOW.

The Service Provider does not guarantee error-free operation or uninterrupted availability of any system and shall not be responsible for failures attributable to (i) Client Materials; (ii) third-party products or services not under the Service Provider’s control; or (iii) misuse or unauthorized modification of equipment by the Company or third parties, except to the extent expressly assumed by the Service Provider in a SOW.

2.4 **Maintenance Schedules and Windows.**  
The Parties shall mutually agree on a preventive maintenance schedule for each covered facility or system. Absent an emergency, the Service Provider shall conduct preventive maintenance during **Planned Maintenance Windows** that are scheduled in writing (including email) with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice to the Company. The Company shall not unreasonably withhold or delay consent to reasonably necessary Planned Maintenance Windows.

2.5 **Reporting and Documentation.**  
The Service Provider shall maintain accurate and complete records of all Maintenance Services performed, including:

  (a) work orders, service tickets, and incident logs;  
  (b) dates, times, and locations of maintenance activities;  
  (c) identification of equipment serviced and parts replaced;  
  (d) test results, performance measurements, and safety checks; and  
  (e) recommended follow-up actions or improvements.

The Service Provider shall provide to the Company, on at least a quarterly basis (or as otherwise set forth in a SOW), written reports summarizing the Maintenance Services performed, key incidents, root cause analyses (where applicable), and recommendations for improvements. Such reports shall constitute **Deliverables** under this Agreement.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall:

  (a) provide the Service Provider with reasonable access to facilities, equipment, networks, and relevant information necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services;  

  (b) ensure that its Personnel cooperate with the Service Provider and follow reasonable instructions intended to facilitate performance of the Services;  

  (c) promptly notify the Service Provider of any known issues, incidents, or conditions that may affect the performance of the Services or the operation of covered systems;  

  (d) obtain and maintain any necessary licenses, permits, or authorizations for the Company’s use of equipment and facilities, except to the extent such responsibility is expressly assumed by the Service Provider in a SOW; and  

  (e) be responsible for back-up and recovery of its data, except to the extent the Service Provider expressly undertakes data backup obligations in a SOW.

2.7 **Subcontractors.**  
The Service Provider may engage subcontractors to perform parts of the Services, provided that the Service Provider remains fully responsible for the performance of such subcontractors and for compliance with this Agreement. Upon written request, the Service Provider shall identify its material subcontractors to the Company, subject to reasonable confidentiality protections.

2.8 **Changes to Scope.**  
Any change to the scope, nature, or volume of the Services, including additional facilities or systems, shall be requested and processed in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**. Unless and until a Change Order is executed, the Service Provider shall have no obligation to perform the requested change, and any performance prior to execution of a Change Order shall be at the Service Provider’s discretion and at the Company’s cost and risk as provided in **Article III**.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge and agree that the expected aggregate value of this Agreement, inclusive of all committed Fees across the Term (as defined in **Section 4.1**), is **Three Million Seven Hundred Fourteen Thousand Nine Hundred Sixty-One U.S. Dollars (US$3,714,961)** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to increases or decreases pursuant to executed SOWs and Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an applicable SOW:

  (a) **Base Recurring Fee**: The Company shall pay a fixed monthly base fee covering standard preventive and corrective Maintenance Services, calculated pro rata based on the Contract Value and anticipated scope, as specified in an initial SOW.  

  (b) **Time-and-Materials Charges**: For Services outside the base scope (including emergency work outside standard hours, non-covered systems, or additional advisory work), the Company shall pay time-and-materials fees at the Service Provider’s then-current rates as specified in the applicable SOW or Change Order.  

  (c) **Pass-Through Costs**: The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved third-party costs and expenses incurred in performing the Services, including but not limited to replacement parts, specialized testing, and third-party technician costs, without markup unless expressly stated in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
Except as otherwise stated in an SOW, the Service Provider shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears for all Fees and reimbursable expenses. Each invoice shall be sufficiently detailed to reasonably enable the Company to verify the charges. The Company shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) calendar days** from the date of the invoice (“**Net 45**”). Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other method specified by the Service Provider.

3.4 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Company disputes in good faith any portion of an invoice, the Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. The Company shall timely pay the undisputed portion. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve any dispute within thirty (30) days. Upon resolution, any additional amounts due shall be paid within ten (10) days.

3.5 **Late Payment; Interest.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less. In addition, if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the due date, the Service Provider may, upon ten (10) days’ prior written notice and without liability, suspend performance of the Services until all overdue amounts, together with applicable interest, are paid in full.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, withholding, or other taxes or duties imposed by any governmental authority (other than taxes on the Service Provider’s net income). The Company shall be responsible for all such taxes associated with the Services, except to the extent the Company provides a valid exemption certificate.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, documented out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, provided such expenses are incurred in accordance with the Company’s then-current travel and expense guidelines communicated in writing and are pre-approved by the Company for any single trip expected to exceed US$5,000.

3.8 **No Set-Off.**  
Except as expressly provided in **Section 3.4**, the Company shall not set off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from any invoice due to the Service Provider under this Agreement, except to the extent required by applicable law.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date** and shall continue in full force and effect until **December 23, 2029** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be renewed upon mutual written agreement of the Parties for additional periods of one (1) year each, on terms and conditions to be negotiated in good faith, including adjusted fees reflecting then-current market conditions.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience without cause by providing the Service Provider with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days** prior written notice. The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon providing the Company with one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice. During any such notice period, the Company shall continue to pay all Fees due under this Agreement, including Fees for the remainder of any non-cancellable commitments undertaken by the Service Provider on the Company’s behalf.

4.4 **Termination for Cause by Service Provider.**  
The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, upon written notice to the Company if:

  (a) the Company fails to pay any undisputed amount due under this Agreement and such failure continues for more than thirty (30) days after the Company’s receipt of written notice thereof; or  

  (b) the Company materially breaches any other provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.5 **Termination for Cause by Company.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the Service Provider if the Service Provider materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within forty-five (45) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.6 **Termination for Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent or is generally unable to pay its debts as they become due; (b) files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or similar relief that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or has a receiver appointed over substantially all of its assets.

4.7 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) The Company shall promptly pay to the Service Provider all Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued but unpaid as of the effective date of termination and any non-cancellable commitments undertaken on the Company’s behalf.  

  (b) The Service Provider shall, upon the Company’s written request and at the Company’s cost, provide reasonable transition assistance for a period not to exceed ninety (90) days after termination, subject to mutual agreement on scope and applicable fees.  

  (c) Each Party shall, upon request and subject to **Article V (Confidentiality)** and **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession, except to the extent retention is required by law, regulation, or reasonable archival practices.

4.8 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: **Articles I, III (with respect to accrued payment obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Availability/Uptime.**  
Subject to the exclusions in **Section 12.4**, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that critical systems under its direct operational control, as specified in applicable SOWs, achieve a minimum uptime of **99.9%** per calendar month (“**Target Availability**”).

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall meet the following targets for Incidents reported by the Company through the agreed support channels:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical)** – complete failure or outage affecting core operations:  
   - Initial response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
   - Workaround or temporary fix: within 4 hours  
   - Target resolution: within 24 hours  

  (b) **Severity 2 (High)** – significant degradation affecting key business processes:  
   - Initial response: within 2 hours during Business Days  
   - Workaround or temporary fix: within 8 hours  
   - Target resolution: within 3 Business Days  

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium)** – functional impairment with acceptable workaround:  
   - Initial response: within 4 Business Hours  
   - Target resolution: within 5 Business Days  

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low)** – minor issues, cosmetic problems, or service requests:  
   - Initial response: within 1 Business Day  
   - Target resolution: as reasonably scheduled, generally within 10 Business Days.

12.3 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
If, in any calendar month, (i) Target Availability is not met, or (ii) the Service Provider fails to meet the applicable resolution targets for more than 10% of Severity 1 and Severity 2 Incidents, the Service Provider shall, as the Company’s sole and exclusive remedy for such SLA breaches (except for any rights under **Article VIII**), provide a **service credit** equal to a percentage of the monthly Fees for the affected Services, as follows:

  (a) Target Availability below 99.9% but at least 99.5%: 5% service credit;  
  (b) Target Availability below 99.5% but at least 99.0%: 10% service credit;  
  (c) Target Availability below 99.0%: 15% service credit;  

plus an additional 5% credit if the Service Provider fails the Severity 1 and Severity 2 resolution targets as described above. In no event shall total service credits for a month exceed 25% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services.

To receive service credits, the Company must request them in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the SLA failure occurred. Unused credits shall not be refundable in cash but shall be applied to future invoices.

12.4 **SLA Exclusions.**  
SLA calculations and obligations shall exclude downtime or delays resulting from: (a) Planned Maintenance Windows; (b) Force Majeure Events; (c) failures or delays caused by the Company’s acts or omissions or by third parties not under the Service Provider’s control; or (d) issues with third-party systems or networks not managed by the Service Provider.
