Contoso and Delta may be referred to herein individually as a “**Party**” and collectively as the “**Parties**.”

## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF GOODS AND SERVICES

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Delta shall supply to Contoso, and Contoso shall purchase from Delta, during the Term, (a) the Goods, and (b) the Services, as more specifically described in one or more SOWs entered into by the Parties under this Agreement. Each SOW shall be deemed incorporated into and governed by this Agreement. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail, unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.2 **Goods.**  
(a) **Specifications.** The Goods to be supplied by Delta shall conform in all material respects to the specifications, configurations, and features set forth in the applicable SOW or as otherwise agreed in writing by the Parties. Delta shall ensure that all Goods are new (not refurbished or used), of merchantable quality, free from material defects in design, materials, and workmanship, and suitable for the intended purpose described in the applicable SOW.  

(b) **Quantity and Delivery.** Contoso shall issue Purchase Orders referencing this Agreement and the relevant SOW(s) for the Goods. Delta shall accept each conforming PO in writing or via electronic confirmation and shall deliver the Goods to the delivery location(s) specified in such PO. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW or PO, all Goods shall be delivered DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) to Contoso’s designated facility within the Territory, in accordance with Incoterms® 2020.  

(c) **Packaging and Labeling.** Delta shall package and label the Goods in accordance with industry standards and any specific requirements set forth by Contoso in the applicable SOW or PO, ensuring adequate protection against damage during transportation and handling.

2.3 **Services.**  
Delta shall perform the Services described in the applicable SOWs, which may include, without limitation:

- **2.3.1 Installation and Configuration.**  
Delta shall install and configure the Goods at Contoso’s designated locations in accordance with the manufacturer’s instructions, applicable industry standards, and any project-specific requirements described in the SOW. Delta shall coordinate with Contoso’s IT and facilities teams to minimize disruption to Contoso’s business operations, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to perform installation activities during pre-agreed maintenance windows.

- **2.3.2 Integration and Testing.**  
Delta shall integrate the Goods with Contoso’s existing network, systems, and applications as specified in the SOW. Integration activities may include network configuration, software setup, security hardening, and data migration, as applicable. Upon completion of installation and integration, Delta shall conduct acceptance testing in accordance with written test plans agreed by the Parties. Contoso shall have a reasonable period (not less than ten (10) Business Days, unless otherwise agreed) to review the test results and either accept the Deliverables or provide written notice of material non-conformities.

- **2.3.3 Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
Where specified in the SOW, Delta shall provide training sessions for Contoso’s personnel on the operation, administration, and basic troubleshooting of the Goods and related software. Training may consist of on-site sessions, remote/web-based training, or provision of training materials. Delta shall ensure that trainers are adequately qualified and familiar with the Goods and the specific deployment for Contoso.

- **2.3.4 Maintenance and Support.**  
Delta shall provide ongoing maintenance and support services, including periodic updates, patches, and bug fixes for any embedded or related software supplied under this Agreement, and break/fix support for the Goods, all in accordance with the Service Levels described in Article XII and the applicable SOW. Support may be provided via telephone, email, web portal, and, where necessary, on-site support.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
Each SOW shall include an implementation schedule with key milestones, including delivery dates, installation dates, testing periods, and go-live dates. Delta shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed all agreed timelines. If Delta reasonably anticipates that any milestone will not be met, Delta shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, stating the reasons for the delay and proposing a recovery plan. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate delay, and any material impact on the overall project schedule shall, where appropriate, be documented in a Change Order pursuant to Article XIII.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Delta shall:  
(a) perform all Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, with due skill, care, and diligence, and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and best practices;  
(b) ensure that its personnel, contractors, and agents engaged in the performance of the Services are properly trained, qualified, and supervised;  
(c) comply with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations in the Territory, including health and safety requirements and reasonable security policies provided by Contoso; and  
(d) ensure that the Goods, when installed and configured, meet the functional and performance requirements specified in the applicable SOW.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
(a) **Deliverable Review Period.** Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Contoso shall have ten (10) Business Days following delivery of a Deliverable (including completion of testing, if applicable) to review and evaluate the Deliverable against the acceptance criteria set forth in the SOW (the “**Review Period**”).  

(b) **Acceptance or Rejection.** If the Deliverable materially conforms to the applicable acceptance criteria, Contoso shall issue a written notice of acceptance or deemed acceptance shall occur upon expiry of the Review Period without written rejection. If Contoso reasonably determines that the Deliverable does not materially conform, Contoso shall provide a written rejection notice specifying the non-conformities. Delta shall, at no additional cost to Contoso and within a reasonable period, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct or re-perform the Deliverable and resubmit it for review.  

(c) **Final Acceptance.** Acceptance of Deliverables does not waive Contoso’s rights under warranties (Article VII), indemnities (Article VIII), or other remedies for latent defects or non-conformities not reasonably discoverable during the Review Period.

2.7 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
This Agreement is non-exclusive. Unless expressly stated otherwise in an SOW, nothing herein shall prevent Contoso from purchasing goods or services similar or identical to the Goods or Services from third parties, nor prevent Delta from providing similar goods or services to other customers.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value of this Agreement, inclusive of all Goods, Services, and other charges anticipated under the initial SOWs, is **Two Million Seven Hundred Fourteen Thousand Nine Hundred Sixteen U.S. Dollars (US$2,714,916)** (the “**Contract Value**”). The Contract Value is an estimate for planning purposes and may be adjusted by mutual written agreement through additional or amended SOWs and Change Orders executed pursuant to Article XIII.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
(a) **Goods Fees.** The purchase price for the Goods shall be as set forth in the applicable SOW and/or PO. Prices may be specified on a unit basis, volume tier, or project basis. Unless otherwise agreed in the SOW, such prices are firm and fixed for the duration of the applicable SOW and shall include all costs of packaging, insurance during transit, and delivery to the agreed delivery location within the Territory.  

(b) **Services Fees.** Services may be charged on a time-and-materials basis, fixed-fee basis, or a combination thereof, as specified in the applicable SOW. Time-and-materials rates shall be specified in a rate card attached to the SOW, and any annual adjustments to such rates shall be subject to mutual written agreement.  

(c) **Maintenance and Support Fees.** Ongoing maintenance and support Fees (if any) shall be set forth in the applicable SOW, which may specify annual or periodic fees and the scope of covered Services in accordance with Article XII.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**  
(a) **Payment Terms.** Unless otherwise agreed in an SOW, all undisputed invoices shall be payable **Net sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt of a valid, correct, and undisputed invoice by Contoso (“**Net 60**”).  

(b) **Invoicing for Goods.** Delta shall invoice Contoso for Goods upon delivery and, where applicable, acceptance in accordance with Article II. Each invoice shall reference this Agreement (PUR-DEL-202507-178), the applicable SOW, and the relevant PO number.  

(c) **Invoicing for Services.** For Services, Delta shall invoice (i) for fixed-fee engagements, upon completion of the associated milestone or Deliverable as specified in the SOW; and (ii) for time-and-materials engagements, monthly in arrears, accompanied by a summary of hours, personnel categories, and a brief description of the work performed.  

(d) **Invoice Disputes.** If Contoso in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice, Contoso shall notify Delta in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts in accordance with this Article III. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Any amounts determined to be owing by Contoso following resolution shall be paid within twenty (20) days of such resolution.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed invoice amounts that remain unpaid after the due date shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of: (i) one percent (1.0%) per month; or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the date due until the date paid in full. The accrual of interest shall not limit any other rights or remedies available to Delta for late payment, provided that Delta shall not suspend Services or withhold delivery of Goods due to late payment unless: (a) such undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of delinquency; and (b) Delta has provided Contoso a final ten (10) Business Day cure period.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of sales, use, excise, value-added, or similar taxes, duties, or assessments imposed by any governmental authority in connection with the transactions under this Agreement (collectively, “**Taxes**”), except for taxes based on Delta’s net income, property, or employment obligations. Contoso shall be responsible for any such Taxes properly chargeable to Contoso, and Delta shall itemize such Taxes on its invoices where applicable. If Contoso provides Delta with a valid exemption certificate or other documentation demonstrating that certain transactions are exempt from Tax, Delta shall not charge such Taxes on the exempted transactions.

3.6 **Expenses.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Delta in performing the Services (such as travel, lodging, and meals) shall be reimbursable by Contoso at actual cost, provided that such expenses: (a) are authorized in writing by Contoso in advance; (b) comply with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policies, which Delta acknowledges having received or to which Contoso shall provide access; and (c) are supported by documentation reasonably acceptable to Contoso.

3.7 **Set-Off.**  
Contoso may not set off any amounts due from Delta against amounts payable by Contoso under this Agreement, except as expressly permitted by law or as mutually agreed in writing. Delta shall not set off amounts it may owe to Contoso against any amounts payable by Contoso under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in full force and effect until **January 16, 2030** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Agreement may be extended beyond the Expiration Date by mutual written agreement of the Parties, which may include one or more renewal terms and any mutually agreed changes to pricing, scope, or other terms. Any such extension shall be set forth in a written amendment executed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. Termination of the Agreement for convenience shall automatically terminate all then-effective SOWs as of the effective date of such termination unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.  

(b) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent; (ii) files a petition for bankruptcy or is the subject of an involuntary petition that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (iii) ceases to carry on its business in the ordinary course; or (iv) assigns assets for the benefit of creditors.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Delta shall cease performing Services and shall promptly discontinue all work being performed under this Agreement and any affected SOW;  
(b) each Party shall promptly return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party in accordance with Section 5.4;  
(c) Contoso shall pay Delta for (i) all Goods delivered and accepted by Contoso prior to the effective date of termination; (ii) all Services properly performed and accepted in accordance with this Agreement prior to the effective date of termination; and (iii) any non-cancellable, reasonable, documented costs or commitments incurred by Delta specifically for Contoso’s benefit under an SOW, provided that Delta uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such costs; and  
(d) all licenses granted under Article VI that are expressly stated to survive termination shall so survive.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under Articles I, III (with respect to unpaid Fees), IV.5, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (with respect to post-termination obligations, if any), XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provisions that by their nature should reasonably survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business plans, financial information, pricing, customer lists, technical data, product plans, software (including source code and object code), designs, inventions, know-how, trade secrets, and this Agreement and its terms. Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes generally available to the public without breach of this Agreement;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.2 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
(b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted in this Agreement; and  
(c) take measures to protect the confidentiality of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information that are at least as protective as those it uses to protect its own similar information, and in no event less than commercially reasonable care.

5.3 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:  
(a) to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, contractors, and professional advisors (collectively, “**Representatives**”) who have a legitimate need to know such information for purposes consistent with this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein; and  
(b) to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that, to the extent legally permissible, the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement and cooperates with the Disclosing Party (at the Disclosing Party’s expense) in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.4 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible materials containing the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, and certify in writing that it has complied with this requirement; provided, however, that the Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or bona fide internal record-keeping policies (in which case such retained information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein).

5.5 **Survival.**  
The obligations under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that with respect to trade secrets, such obligations shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XV

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including, without limitation: acts of God; natural disasters; epidemics or pandemics; war, terrorism, or civil unrest; acts of government or regulatory authorities; strikes or other labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce); failures of public utilities or telecommunications; or other similar events.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event on its performance; and  
(c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event to the extent they are prevented or delayed by such event. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to reschedule or otherwise adjust the impacted obligations.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects performance under this Agreement or any SOW, either Party may, upon written notice, terminate the affected SOW and, if the event materially affects the entire Agreement, this Agreement, without liability other than obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
