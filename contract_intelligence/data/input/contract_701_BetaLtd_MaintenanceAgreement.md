## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall provide comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Supported Environment of the Company. The Maintenance Services shall include, without limitation:

  2.1.1 **Preventive Maintenance** consisting of regularly scheduled inspection, testing, cleaning, calibration, and optimization activities designed to reduce the risk of failure, extend the useful life of the Supported Systems, and maintain optimal performance;

  2.1.2 **Corrective Maintenance** consisting of troubleshooting, repair, replacement, and restoration activities performed in response to Incidents, failures, errors, or performance degradation affecting the Supported Environment;

  2.1.3 **Adaptive Maintenance** including configuration changes, updates, minor enhancements, and compatibility adjustments to ensure that the Supported Systems remain compatible with evolving operating environments, interfaces, and dependent systems as mutually agreed by the Parties;

  2.1.4 **Monitoring and Alerting Services** including 24x7 automated or semi-automated monitoring of critical components, generation of alerts upon detection of threshold breaches or potential failures, and proactive intervention to mitigate risks;

  2.1.5 **Patch and Update Management** including deployment of vendor-released patches, updates, firmware upgrades, and hotfixes for hardware and software components within the Supported Environment, subject to compatibility and prior approval by the Company, where applicable;

  2.1.6 **Technical Support and Help Desk Services** including remote and, when necessary, on-site technical support through the Service Provider’s designated support channels, for logging, triaging, and resolving Incidents in accordance with the Service Levels defined in Article XII;

  2.1.7 **Reporting and Documentation** including the production of periodic maintenance reports, Incident logs, root cause analyses for Severity 1 Incidents, and asset inventory updates, as well as maintenance of accurate configuration and technical documentation reasonably required by the Company.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
The Service Provider shall deliver to the Company, in connection with the Maintenance Services:

  2.2.1 Monthly or quarterly **Maintenance Reports** summarizing preventive and corrective activities performed during the period, including Incidents handled, Resolution Times, and any recurring issues or risks identified;

  2.2.2 **Incident Reports** for all Severity 1 and Severity 2 Incidents (as classified in the SLA), including timelines, root causes to the extent reasonably ascertainable, corrective actions, and recommendations for preventive measures;

  2.2.3 Updated **Configuration and Asset Documentation**, including version information for software components, firmware levels, inventory of key hardware components, and network topology diagrams where applicable;

  2.2.4 **Change Logs** describing applied patches, upgrades, configuration changes, and other modifications to the Supported Environment;

  2.2.5 Any other documents or artifacts expressly identified as Deliverables in this Article II or in a Change Order.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Maintenance Services:

  2.3.1 In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards for maintenance of systems of similar complexity;

  2.3.2 In compliance with applicable laws and regulations, including relevant safety requirements;

  2.3.3 Using personnel who are appropriately trained, qualified, and experienced in the maintenance of systems similar to the Supported Systems;

  2.3.4 In accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and the timelines specified in this Agreement, subject to Force Majeure Events and delays caused by the Company.

2.4 **Service Availability and Coverage.**  
Unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing:

  2.4.1 The Service Provider shall provide monitoring and Incident intake on a 24x7 basis for Severity 1 Incidents affecting production operations;

  2.4.2 For lower severity Incidents and routine requests, the Service Provider shall provide support during the standard support window of 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday through Friday (Company local time), excluding public holidays in the Company’s primary location, unless otherwise agreed;

  2.4.3 On-site support shall be provided where remote resolution is not commercially reasonable, and the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to dispatch qualified personnel to the Company’s site within the Response Times specified in Article XII, subject to local availability.

2.5 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company acknowledges that the Service Provider’s ability to perform the Maintenance Services is dependent on the Company’s reasonable cooperation. Accordingly, the Company shall:

  2.5.1 Provide timely access (remote and/or on-site, as applicable) to the Supported Environment, including necessary infrastructure, networks, systems, and facilities;

  2.5.2 Provide accurate and up-to-date information regarding the Supported Systems, including system configurations, known issues, operating conditions, and any changes implemented by the Company or third parties;

  2.5.3 Designate one or more Authorized Representatives with decision-making authority regarding Incident prioritization, scheduling of maintenance windows, and approval of Changes that may affect business operations;

  2.5.4 Promptly notify the Service Provider of any Incident, suspected vulnerability, or material security issue affecting the Supported Environment, and cooperate with the Service Provider’s reasonable requests for information and assistance in diagnosing and resolving such issues;

  2.5.5 Obtain and maintain any third-party licenses, consents, or permissions necessary to permit the Service Provider to access and maintain the Supported Systems.

2.6 **Exclusions from Scope.**  
Unless expressly included by a Change Order or by written agreement of the Parties, the Maintenance Services shall not include:

  2.6.1 Major system upgrades or replacements requiring capital investment or large-scale project services;

  2.6.2 Development of new software applications or major feature enhancements unrelated to maintenance and support;

  2.6.3 Maintenance of systems, equipment, or components not designated as part of the Supported Environment or modified by the Company or third parties without the Service Provider’s prior knowledge and approval;

  2.6.4 Support for products or components that are at “end of life” or no longer supported by their original manufacturers or licensors, unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing.

2.7 **Timelines.**  
The Parties acknowledge the Term set forth in Section 4.1. Within thirty (30) days following the Effective Date, the Parties shall agree on an initial maintenance plan and schedule, identifying key milestones such as completion of an initial assessment of the Supported Environment, establishment of monitoring integrations, and baseline performance measurements. All timelines shall be subject to the Change Management process in Article XIII.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 20, 2029** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal to the other Party at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods on the same terms and conditions, except that the Service Provider may adjust the fees for any renewal term upon at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice, subject to the Company’s right to decline renewal.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider. In such event:

  4.3.1 The Company shall remain liable for all fees and reimbursable expenses accrued through the effective date of termination; and  

  4.3.2 If termination for convenience is effective prior to the Expiration Date, the Company shall, as an early termination charge, pay the Service Provider an additional amount equal to twenty percent (20%) of the remaining base maintenance fees that would have been payable from the effective date of termination through the Expiration Date, as a genuine pre-estimate of the Service Provider’s costs of redeployment and lost capacity.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

  4.4.2 Without limiting Section 4.4.1, the Service Provider may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if any undisputed amount remains unpaid more than ninety (90) days after its due date and the Company has failed to cure such payment default within such thirty (30) day period.

  4.4.3 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) enters into bankruptcy or similar proceedings that are not dismissed within ninety (90) days, or (c) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  

  4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:

  - All rights and licenses granted to the Company under this Agreement shall cease, except as expressly stated otherwise in Article VI;  
  - The Service Provider shall cease providing the Services as of the effective date of termination, except as necessary to provide agreed transition assistance under Section 4.5.3;  
  - The Company shall pay all amounts due and payable through the effective date of termination, including any applicable early termination charges under Section 4.3.2.

  4.5.2 Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s written request, securely destroy any Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession or control, subject to Section 5.4 and Section 11.5 and subject to any legal retention requirements.

  4.5.3 At the Company’s written request received prior to the effective date of expiration or termination, the Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance services for up to ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination at its then-current T&M rates, under a separate written statement of work if required.

4.6 **Survival.**  
Any provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination, including without limitation Sections 3.3, 3.5, 4.5, 5.3–5.5, Articles VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI (to the extent stated), XIV, XV, and Sections 16.1–16.9, shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production Supported Systems are available **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding:

  12.1.1 Planned Maintenance (with at least seventy-two (72) hours’ prior notice to the Company);  

  12.1.2 Downtime caused by Force Majeure Events or Company acts or omissions;  

  12.1.3 Downtime resulting from third-party service provider failures outside the reasonable control of the Service Provider.

12.2 **Incident Severity and Response Times.**  
Incidents shall be classified, in good faith, into severity levels as follows:

  12.2.1 **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of production functionality for a material portion of the Supported Environment, with no available workaround. Target Response Time: **1 hour**; Target Resolution Time: **8 hours**.

  12.2.2 **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of performance, or partial outage affecting production operations, with a limited workaround. Target Response Time: **2 hours**; Target Resolution Time: **24 hours**.

  12.2.3 **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical incident causing limited impact with a reasonable workaround. Target Response Time: **4 Business Hours**; Target Resolution Time: **3 Business Days**.

  12.2.4 **Severity 4 (Low / Service Request):** Minor issues or routine service requests. Target Response Time: **1 Business Day**; Resolution on a mutually agreed schedule.

The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed the above Response and Resolution Targets.

12.3 **Service Credits.**  
If in any calendar month the Service Provider fails to meet the monthly uptime commitment in Section 12.1 for production Supported Systems, the Company shall be eligible for Service Credits calculated as follows:

  12.3.1 Uptime 99.0%–99.89%: Service Credit equal to 5% of the monthly base maintenance fee.

  12.3.2 Uptime 98.0%–98.99%: Service Credit equal to 10% of the monthly base maintenance fee.

  12.3.3 Uptime below 98.0%: Service Credit equal to 15% of the monthly base maintenance fee.

Service Credits shall be applied as a credit against the next invoice(s) issued by the Service Provider. Service Credits shall constitute the Company’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitment, except where such failure is due to the Service Provider’s gross negligence, willful misconduct, or a repeated pattern of material non-performance.

12.4 **Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall provide periodic SLA reports (monthly or quarterly) detailing uptime, Incident volumes by severity, Response and Resolution Times, and Service Credits (if any) accrued during the period.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics or pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes, governmental actions, power or utility failures, or failures of third-party telecommunications or hosting providers.

15.2 **Notice.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  15.2.1 Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, describing the nature, cause, and anticipated duration of the event; and

  15.2.2 Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are directly affected by the Force Majeure Event. Payment obligations shall not be excused due to Force Majeure, except to the extent that Services are not delivered.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially impairs the performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Services upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
