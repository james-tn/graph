## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 Atlas shall provide to Contoso professional staffing, employment-related, and managed workforce Services designed to support Contoso’s technology, operations, project management, and administrative functions (collectively, the “Services”).  

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that the Services may be provided through multiple SOWs issued under this Agreement. In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement, in which case the SOW shall govern only with respect to that provision and that SOW.

### 2.2 Types of Services

2.2.1 **Staff Augmentation and Employment Services.** Atlas shall source, recruit, vet, and employ or contract with Personnel possessing skills and experience identified by Contoso, including but not limited to:

- Software engineers and developers;  
- Data analysts and data engineers;  
- Project managers and business analysts;  
- Quality assurance and testing professionals;  
- IT support and helpdesk personnel;  
- Administrative and back-office staff.  

2.2.2 **Managed Services.** At Contoso’s request, Atlas may provide managed services teams responsible for delivering defined outputs or outcomes, such as:  

- Application development and enhancement projects;  
- System integration and maintenance;  
- Data migration and reporting;  
- Business process outsourcing tasks.  

2.2.3 **Onboarding and Offboarding.** Atlas shall manage all onboarding and offboarding of Personnel assigned to Contoso, including background checks where legally permissible and requested by Contoso, verification of work authorization, and any training reasonably required for Personnel to comply with Contoso’s policies.

### 2.3 Deliverables

2.3.1 Atlas shall prepare and provide Deliverables as reasonably required by Contoso and specified in applicable SOWs, which may include:  

- Project plans, staffing plans, and resource allocation matrices;  
- Weekly or monthly status reports covering Personnel assignments, hours worked, and task status;  
- Skills assessments or personnel profiles;  
- Documentation, code, technical specifications, or other work product produced by assigned Personnel in the performance of Services;  
- Knowledge transfer documents and training materials upon completion or transition of Services.

2.3.2 All Deliverables shall be provided in a format agreed in the relevant SOW (e.g., electronic documents, code repositories, presentations) and delivered via secure channels reasonably acceptable to Contoso.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Atlas shall perform the Services, and shall cause its Personnel to perform the Services, in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with:

- Generally accepted industry standards;  
- Applicable Law;  
- Contoso’s reasonable policies and procedures provided in writing to Atlas in advance, including any information security, data protection, and workplace conduct policies.

2.4.2 Atlas shall ensure that all Personnel assigned to Contoso possess the requisite qualifications, skills, and experience necessary to perform the assigned tasks. Contoso shall have the right, acting reasonably, to reject or request replacement of any Personnel who, in Contoso’s good faith judgment:

- Fail to meet Contoso’s standards of performance;  
- Engage in misconduct or violate Contoso policies; or  
- Otherwise prove unsuitable for the assigned role.

Atlas shall provide a replacement resource within a commercially reasonable period, typically within ten (10) Business Days of such request, subject to market conditions and mutual cooperation.

### 2.5 Timelines and Milestones

2.5.1 Each SOW shall specify the start date and expected duration of the Services under that SOW, any key milestones or delivery dates, and any applicable performance metrics.

2.5.2 Atlas shall diligently use commercially reasonable efforts to meet all agreed schedules and milestones. If Atlas becomes aware of any circumstances that may delay performance or jeopardize timely completion of Services or Deliverables, Atlas shall promptly notify Contoso in writing and propose a corrective action plan.  

2.5.3 Contoso shall cooperate reasonably with Atlas and provide timely feedback, approvals, and access to relevant systems or information to facilitate Atlas’s performance. Any delays caused by Contoso’s failure to perform its obligations may result in adjustment to the timeline or milestones, subject to mutual written agreement.

### 2.6 Location and Method of Performance

2.6.1 The Services may be performed at Contoso’s premises, Atlas’s facilities, remotely, or at any other location mutually agreed by the Parties, in accordance with any location-specific requirements set forth in an SOW.

2.6.2 When Services are performed at Contoso’s premises, Personnel shall comply with all reasonable workplace rules, access control procedures, and security protocols communicated to Atlas. Contoso may deny access to its premises to any Personnel deemed unsafe, disruptive, or non-compliant, provided that Contoso promptly notifies Atlas of the reasons for such denial so that Atlas may address or replace such Personnel.

### 2.7 Service Management and Reporting

2.7.1 Atlas shall designate a primary **Engagement Manager** responsible for overall coordination of Services, and Contoso shall designate a primary **Client Manager** to serve as the point of contact for Contoso.

2.7.2 The Parties shall meet (in person or via teleconference) at least monthly, or as otherwise agreed, to review progress, staffing levels, open issues, and upcoming needs. Atlas shall provide reasonable periodic reporting on utilization of Personnel, major activities completed, and upcoming deliverables.

2.7.3 Atlas shall maintain accurate and complete records of hours worked by time-and-materials Personnel and shall provide such records to Contoso upon request for reconciliation with invoices.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value and Pricing Structure

3.1.1 The aggregate amount payable by Contoso to Atlas for all Services and Deliverables under this Agreement shall not exceed the **Contract Value** of **US$1,014,792**, unless otherwise agreed in a written amendment signed by both Parties.

3.1.2 Fees may be structured on a time-and-materials basis, fixed-fee basis, or a combination thereof, as specified in each SOW. Typical categories include:  

- Hourly or daily rates for Personnel by role and seniority;  
- Fixed-fee amounts for defined milestones or deliverables;  
- Monthly retainer or managed service fees.

3.1.3 The fee schedule and applicable billing rates for each SOW shall be set forth in that SOW or in an attached pricing schedule.

### 3.2 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.2.1 Atlas shall invoice Contoso monthly in arrears unless another invoicing interval is specified in the applicable SOW. Each invoice shall include:

- The Agreement reference number **AGR-ATL-202501-131**;  
- The applicable SOW reference;  
- Itemized description of Services and Deliverables;  
- Hours worked by each Personnel (if time-and-materials);  
- Any approved reimbursable expenses with supporting documentation.

3.2.2 Payment terms shall be **Net 45** days from Contoso’s receipt of a valid and undisputed invoice. Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer or other method agreed by the Parties.

3.2.3 Contoso may dispute in good faith any portion of an invoice by providing written notice to Atlas within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the reasons for the dispute. Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with the payment terms, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the disputed portion. Any adjustment shall be reflected in a subsequent invoice or credit note.

### 3.3 Late Payment

3.3.1 Any undisputed amount not paid within the stated Net 45 payment period may, at Atlas’s option, accrue interest at a rate not exceeding the lesser of: (a) one percent (1.0%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.3.2 Atlas shall not suspend or withhold Services solely for late payment unless (i) amounts are more than sixty (60) days overdue, (ii) Atlas has provided at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice of its intent to suspend, and (iii) Contoso has not cured the non-payment during such period. Any suspension shall be limited to the portion of Services directly affected by the unpaid invoices.

### 3.4 Expenses and Reimbursements

3.4.1 Contoso shall reimburse Atlas for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses that are: (a) incurred by Atlas in connection with the performance of Services; (b) in accordance with Contoso’s travel and expense policies provided in advance; and (c) supported by proper receipts or documentation.

3.4.2 No expenses shall be reimbursable unless Contoso has provided prior written approval, except for minor incidental expenses under a threshold specified by Contoso (e.g., US$50 per expense) as may be set forth in an SOW.

3.4.3 Atlas shall not charge any administrative or overhead mark-up on reimbursable expenses unless expressly agreed in an SOW.

### 3.5 Taxes

3.5.1 The Fees do not include any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar Taxes (other than Taxes based on Atlas’s income, property, or employment obligations), which shall be stated separately on invoices and paid by Contoso where required by law.

3.5.2 Each Party shall be responsible for its own income and employment-related Taxes. Atlas shall be solely responsible for payment of all salaries, wages, benefits, and employment taxes for its Personnel.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date**, **August 05, 2027**, unless earlier terminated pursuant to this Article IV (the “Term”).

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend the Term, subject to such terms and conditions (including adjustments to Contract Value and Fees) as may be agreed in a written amendment.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Atlas.

4.2.2 Atlas may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Contoso, provided that Atlas continues to perform all Services diligently during the notice period and cooperates in good faith to transition Services to Contoso or a replacement provider.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment of any undisputed Fees) after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.3.2 Either Party may immediately terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party:

- Becomes insolvent or is generally unable to pay its debts as they become due;  
- Files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or reorganization;  
- Makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or  
- Has a receiver appointed for substantially all of its assets.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) Atlas shall cease performing Services (except as necessary for transition as provided below);  
(b) Atlas shall promptly return or destroy (at Contoso’s election) all Contoso Confidential Information and any Contoso-owned materials, in accordance with Section 5.4;  
(c) Contoso shall pay Atlas for all undisputed Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to the Contract Value and the terms of Article III.

4.4.2 At Contoso’s request in connection with any termination (other than termination by Atlas for Contoso’s uncured material breach), Atlas shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to sixty (60) days after termination, at then-current time-and-materials rates or as otherwise agreed, to enable an orderly transfer of Services to Contoso or to a third party.

### 4.5 Survival

4.5.1 Any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination of this Agreement shall so survive, including Sections and Articles relating to **Confidentiality (Article V)**, **Intellectual Property Rights (Article VI)**, **Representations and Warranties (Article VII)**, **Indemnification (Article VIII)**, **Limitation of Liability (Article IX)**, **Data Protection and Privacy (Article XI)**, **Dispute Resolution (Article XIV)**, **Force Majeure (Article XV)**, and **General Provisions (Article XVI)**, as well as all payment obligations accrued prior to termination.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 “Confidential Information” means any non-public information disclosed, directly or indirectly, by or on behalf of one Party (“Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (“Receiving Party”) in connection with this Agreement, whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, including but not limited to: business plans, financial information, technology, software, source code, trade secrets, know-how, processes, customer data, personnel information, and any information marked or otherwise identified as confidential.

5.1.2 Confidential Information of Contoso includes, without limitation, any data, records, or information relating to Contoso’s customers, employees, systems, and business operations. Confidential Information of Atlas includes, without limitation, its pricing models, methodologies, internal processes, and non-public information about its Personnel.

### 5.2 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:

(a) Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  
(b) Restrict disclosure of Confidential Information to those employees, contractors, or agents who have a need to know such information for such purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those set forth herein;  
(c) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

### 5.3 Exceptions

5.3.1 The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) Was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction at the time of disclosure;  
(b) Was or becomes generally available to the public through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
(c) Was rightfully received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) Was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law or court order, provided that the Receiving Party (to the extent legally permitted) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates reasonably in any effort to seek protective measures.

### 5.4 Return or Destruction

5.4.1 Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of Confidential Information in its possession or control, except as required to be retained under Applicable Law or bona fide record-keeping policies. Any retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

### 5.5 Survival

5.5.1 The obligations of confidentiality under this Article V shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except that trade secrets shall be protected for so long as they remain trade secrets under Applicable Law.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, including its anticipated impact and duration, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects and resume full performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

15.3.1 During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations accrued before the event) shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration impaired by the event.

15.3.2 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and substantially prevents performance, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this Agreement upon ten (10) Business Days’ written notice, without liability, except for payment of amounts already due.
