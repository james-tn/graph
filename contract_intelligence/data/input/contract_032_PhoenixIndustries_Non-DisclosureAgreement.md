## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Phoenix shall provide to Contoso comprehensive technology integration, data analytics, and related consulting and support Services to enable Contoso to enhance its enterprise data platform, reporting capabilities, and operational decision‑making processes. The Services shall be performed in accordance with this Agreement and any applicable SOWs executed by the Parties. In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.2 **Types of Services.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services may include any or all of the following:

  2.2.1 **Discovery and Assessment.** Phoenix will perform an initial discovery and assessment phase, including stakeholder interviews, current‑state architecture review, data quality assessment, and identification of business requirements. Phoenix shall deliver assessment reports and recommendations concerning architecture, data pipelines, and reporting frameworks.

  2.2.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.** Based on the discovery phase, Phoenix shall design a target‑state data architecture encompassing data ingestion, transformation, storage, cataloging, security, and analytics enablement. Phoenix shall provide high‑level and detailed design documentation, including diagrams, interface specifications, and data models.

  2.2.3 **Implementation and Integration.** Phoenix shall configure, implement, and integrate the agreed‑upon solution components. This may include: (a) building ETL/ELT pipelines; (b) integrating with Contoso’s existing systems (e.g., ERP, CRM, line‑of‑business applications); (c) implementing data warehouses or data lakes; and (d) configuring analytics tools, dashboards, and self‑service reporting.

  2.2.4 **Data Analytics and Reporting.** Phoenix shall develop analytics use cases as specified in the SOW, including reports, dashboards, predictive models, and performance scorecards. Phoenix shall work with Contoso’s stakeholders to define KPIs and success metrics and shall configure appropriate reporting and visualization layers.

  2.2.5 **Support and Maintenance.** During the Term and as detailed in the applicable SOW, Phoenix will provide support and maintenance Services, which may include incident management, bug fixes, performance tuning, and deployment of updates. Service Levels for these support Services are set out in **Article XII**.

  2.2.6 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Phoenix shall conduct training sessions for Contoso personnel on the implemented solution, including system use, reporting capabilities, and basic troubleshooting. Training may be provided via live, virtual, and/or recorded sessions, as mutually agreed.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables under this Agreement may include, without limitation:

  (a) written assessment reports and recommendations;  
  (b) architecture and design documentation;  
  (c) configuration scripts, code, and integration components;  
  (d) data models, schema definitions, mapping documents, and transformation logic;  
  (e) dashboards, reports, and analytics models;  
  (f) operational run‑books, support documentation, and user guides; and  
  (g) training materials and knowledge transfer documents.

Each SOW shall specify the particular Deliverables, acceptance criteria, and delivery timelines. Unless expressly stated otherwise in an SOW, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted ten (10) Business Days after delivery if Contoso has not provided Phoenix with a written rejection detailing material non‑conformities with the applicable specifications.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Phoenix shall perform the Services (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services, (b) using personnel with the requisite skill, experience, and qualifications, and (c) in compliance with Applicable Laws. Phoenix does not warrant that the Services or Deliverables will be error‑free or will achieve any specific business result, except as expressly set forth in **Article VII**.

2.5 **Cooperation and Dependencies.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Phoenix’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Contoso’s timely cooperation and the accuracy and completeness of information provided by Contoso. Without limitation, Contoso shall:

  (a) provide Phoenix with timely access to relevant personnel, systems, facilities, and data as reasonably required by Phoenix to perform the Services;  
  (b) promptly review and provide feedback on Deliverables and documentation;  
  (c) designate a primary point of contact with appropriate authority to make decisions on behalf of Contoso; and  
  (d) obtain all necessary consents and authorizations for Phoenix to access and use third‑party systems or data as contemplated by this Agreement.

Phoenix shall not be responsible for delays or failures to perform to the extent caused by Contoso’s failure to meet the obligations in this **Section 2.5**, and any impacted timelines shall be equitably extended.

2.6 **Statements of Work.**  
From time to time, the Parties may execute one or more SOWs referencing this Agreement. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, describe: (a) the specific Services and Deliverables; (b) associated milestones and applicable Service Levels, if any; (c) the fees, expenses, and payment schedule; and (d) any additional or modified terms agreed upon by the Parties. All SOWs shall be governed by this Agreement and incorporated herein by reference.

2.7 **No Exclusivity.**  
Unless expressly stated otherwise in a particular SOW, nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an exclusive relationship between the Parties. Phoenix may perform similar services for other clients, and Contoso may engage other vendors for services similar to the Services.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Out‑of‑pocket expenses reasonably and necessarily incurred by Phoenix in the performance of the Services (including travel, lodging, and meals) shall be reimbursed by Contoso at cost, provided that: (a) such expenses are pre‑approved in writing by Contoso, and (b) Phoenix furnishes reasonable documentation evidencing such expenses. 3.9 **Set‑off.**  
Contoso shall not set‑off, counterclaim, or withhold any amounts due under this Agreement based on any claim or alleged claim against Phoenix, except as expressly permitted by this Agreement or by written agreement of the Parties.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall remain in full force and effect until **November 22, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no less than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend the Term of this Agreement, subject to such terms and conditions (including pricing) as the Parties may then agree in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (High‑Risk – Vendor‑Favorable).**  

  4.3.1 **By Phoenix.** Phoenix may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience, upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Contoso.  

  4.3.2 **By Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience only upon the prior written consent of Phoenix, which Phoenix may grant or withhold in its sole discretion; provided, however, that if Phoenix consents, such termination shall not be effective until at least one hundred eighty (180) days after Phoenix’s receipt of Contoso’s written notice of termination. In the event of termination for convenience by Contoso, Contoso shall pay Phoenix for: (a) all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination; (b) any non‑cancelable commitments; and (c) an early termination fee equal to fifteen percent (15%) of the remaining fees that would have been payable under the terminated SOW(s).

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 **By Phoenix.** Phoenix may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, upon written notice if Contoso: (a) fails to pay any undisputed amount within forty‑five (45) days after the due date and fails to cure such non‑payment within ten (10) Business Days after receipt of written notice; (b) materially breaches any other provision of this Agreement or any SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice; or (c) becomes insolvent, admits inability to pay debts as they become due, is subject to bankruptcy or similar proceedings, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

  4.4.2 **By Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice if Phoenix materially breaches this Agreement or any SOW and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days after receipt of written notice, provided that such breach is directly and materially detrimental to the core functionality of the Services or Deliverables. For avoidance of doubt, minor delays or defects that can be remedied within a reasonable time shall not constitute grounds for termination for cause.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  

  (a) all SOWs shall automatically terminate, unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing;  
  (b) Contoso shall promptly pay Phoenix all fees and reimbursable expenses accrued but unpaid as of the effective date of termination, including any applicable early termination fees as set forth in **Section 4.3.2**;  
  (c) each Party shall, at the request of the other, return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s option) all Confidential Information of the other Party as described in **Section 5.6**, subject to any legal retention requirements; and  
  (d) the provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (with respect to accrued service credits), XIII (for pending Change Orders), XIV, XV, and XVI**, and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
At Contoso’s written request made at least sixty (60) days prior to the effective date of termination or expiration, Phoenix shall make commercially reasonable efforts to provide limited transition assistance (e.g., knowledge transfer, data export) at Phoenix’s then‑current time and materials rates, under a separate SOW or Change Order.

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For any hosted or managed components of the Services, Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the relevant systems are available at least **99.9% of the time** during each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Phoenix may schedule periodic maintenance windows, during which Services may be unavailable, provided that Phoenix gives Contoso at least forty‑eight (48) hours’ prior notice and uses reasonable efforts to schedule such maintenance outside of peak business hours.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the following target response and resolution times for support incidents:

  (a) **Priority 1 – Critical:** System down or severe impact on production operations. Initial response within 1 hour; target resolution or workaround within 8 hours.  
  (b) **Priority 2 – High:** Significant degradation of functionality with substantial impact. Initial response within 4 hours; target resolution or workaround within 24 hours.  
  (c) **Priority 3 – Medium:** Limited or minor impact on operations. Initial response within 1 Business Day; target resolution or workaround within 5 Business Days.  
  (d) **Priority 4 – Low:** General questions or cosmetic issues. Initial response within 2 Business Days; resolution as reasonably scheduled.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If Phoenix fails to meet the **99.9%** monthly availability target for a particular managed Service, Contoso may be eligible for a service credit as follows (to be applied against future invoices):

  (a) Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly fee for the affected Service;  
  (b) Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly fee for the affected Service;  
  (c) Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly fee for the affected Service.

Service credits shall be Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the Service Levels, except that persistent failures (three consecutive months below 98% availability) may constitute a material breach for purposes of **Section 4.4.2**.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
Service Levels shall not apply, and Phoenix shall have no liability, to the extent that any failure to meet Service Levels is caused by: (a) Contoso’s systems or networks; (b) misuse of the Services by Contoso; (c) Third‑Party Materials or third‑party services; or (d) Force Majeure Events.

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including, but not limited to, acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, acts of war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes, labor disputes, governmental actions, power failures, telecommunications or internet outages, and failures of third‑party hosting providers or data centers.

The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event that materially affects its ability to perform its obligations under this Agreement, specifying the nature of the event, the anticipated duration, and the steps being taken to mitigate its impact.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuation of the Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are affected by the Force Majeure Event. The Parties shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects performance of key obligations, either Party may terminate the affected SOW upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability (other than obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
