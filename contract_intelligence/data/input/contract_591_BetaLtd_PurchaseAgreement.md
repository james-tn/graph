## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** The Service Provider shall supply, implement, configure, and support an enterprise data analytics and reporting solution (the “Solution”) for the Company, which shall be comprised of (i) the grant of access to and use of Vendor Materials forming the core analytics platform; (ii) delivery of hardware components (if any) set forth herein; and (iii) provision of related professional, implementation, and ongoing support Services. The Solution shall enable the Company to ingest, process, analyze, and visualize large volumes of operational and financial data across multiple business units.

2.2 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Deliverables shall include, at a minimum:

  2.2.1 **Core Analytics Platform.** A license (as more particularly set forth in Article VI) to the Service Provider’s proprietary cloud‑hosted analytics platform, including data ingestion pipelines, transformation tools, analytics engines, dashboards, and reporting modules, configured for the Company’s environment.

  2.2.2 **Custom Integrations.** Development and delivery of application programming interface (“API”) integrations or data connectors required to interface the Solution with the Company’s existing systems, including but not limited to enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, customer relationship management (CRM) platforms, and internal data warehouses as identified by the Company and agreed in writing.

  2.2.3 **Configuration and Customization.** Configuration of user roles, permissions, dashboards, alerts, and reporting templates specific to the Company’s use cases, including at least three (3) executive-level dashboards, five (5) operational dashboards, and ten (10) standard reports as detailed in the Project Plan.

  2.2.4 **Documentation.** Delivery of user manuals, administrator guides, and technical documentation sufficient for the Company to effectively use and manage the Solution on a day-to-day basis.

  2.2.5 **Training.** Provision of training sessions for key Company personnel, including (a) up to three (3) virtual training sessions for end users; and (b) up to two (2) administrator training sessions covering configuration, security, and basic troubleshooting.

  2.2.6 **Support and Maintenance.** Provision of ongoing support and maintenance during the Term, including incident management, bug fixes, updates, and upgrades, subject to the Service Levels in Article XII.

2.3 **Project Phases and Timelines.** The Parties agree that the implementation of the Solution shall be structured into the following phases. The specific dates shall be set forth in the Project Plan, but the Parties anticipate the following approximate timelines, subject to mutual adjustment via Change Orders:

  2.3.1 **Phase 1 – Initiation and Design (Estimated 6–8 Weeks).** During this phase, the Parties shall (i) finalize the Project Plan; (ii) conduct discovery workshops to define data sources, use cases, and integration requirements; (iii) confirm user roles and security model; and (iv) finalize the initial Specifications. The Company shall promptly provide access to relevant stakeholders and documentation necessary for this phase.

  2.3.2 **Phase 2 – Build and Integration (Estimated 12–16 Weeks).** During this phase, the Service Provider shall (i) configure the core platform; (ii) develop and test integrations; (iii) build initial dashboards and reports; and (iv) establish test environments. The Company shall provide sample data and system access required for integration and testing.

  2.3.3 **Phase 3 – User Acceptance Testing (Estimated 4–6 Weeks).** The Parties shall jointly conduct user acceptance testing (“UAT”) based on mutually agreed test cases. The Company shall designate UAT participants, promptly review Deliverables, and provide written acceptance or detailed written rejection specifying non-conformities. Any issues identified during UAT shall be addressed by the Service Provider within a mutually agreed timeframe.

  2.3.4 **Phase 4 – Production Deployment (Estimated 2–4 Weeks).** Following successful completion of UAT, the Service Provider shall deploy the Solution to the production environment and perform cutover activities as specified in the Project Plan. The Company shall provide reasonable assistance with change management, communication to end users, and any dependencies necessary for a successful go‑live.

  2.3.5 **Phase 5 – Post‑Go‑Live Stabilization (Estimated 4 Weeks).** After go‑live, the Service Provider shall provide heightened support to address stabilization issues, performance tuning, and minor adjustments. Thereafter, support shall continue as set forth in Article XII for the remainder of the Term.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** The Service Provider shall perform all Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  (b) in accordance with all applicable laws and regulations;  
  (c) in conformity with the Specifications, Project Plan, and Service Levels; and  
  (d) using personnel with appropriate skills, training, and experience.

2.5 **Company Responsibilities.** The Company acknowledges that its timely cooperation is critical to the success of the project. Accordingly, the Company shall:

  (a) provide the Service Provider with reasonable access to necessary systems, facilities, and personnel;  
  (b) designate a project sponsor and project manager to serve as primary points of contact;  
  (c) provide timely feedback and approvals, including with respect to UAT and Change Orders;  
  (d) ensure that any Company Data supplied for use in the Solution is accurate to the best of the Company’s knowledge; and  
  (e) comply with any reasonable technical and security requirements communicated by the Service Provider and agreed by the Company.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.** Unless otherwise specified in the Project Plan, each Deliverable shall be deemed accepted upon the earliest of: (i) the Company’s written acceptance; or (ii) the date that is fifteen (15) Business Days after delivery of the Deliverable, provided that the Company has not provided written notice of material non‑conformance during such period. If the Company timely rejects a Deliverable, the rejection notice shall specify the non‑conformities in reasonable detail, and the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such non‑conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance. The Parties shall repeat this procedure until acceptance.

2.7 **High‑Risk Allocation.** In light of the high value, complexity, and integration dependencies of the Solution, and as further reflected in Article IX (Limitation of Liability) and Article VIII (Indemnification), the Parties acknowledge and agree that certain risk allocations are weighted in favor of the Service Provider, including extended termination notice periods and expansive rights for the Service Provider with respect to Vendor Materials.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total Contract Value under this Agreement shall be **US $3,305,639**, which amount is exclusive of any applicable taxes, duties, or similar governmental charges unless expressly stated otherwise.

3.2 **Fee Components.** The Contract Value shall be composed of the following elements:

  3.2.1 **One‑Time Implementation Fee:** US $1,250,000 for design, implementation, integration, configuration, initial training, and launch activities described in Article II.

  3.2.2 **License and Subscription Fees (Fixed for Term):** US $1,600,000, payable in equal annual installments of US $400,000 over four (4) contract years, for the Company’s right to access and use the Solution as described in Article VI.

  3.2.3 **Support and Maintenance Services:** US $300,000 for support and maintenance, payable in equal annual installments of US $75,000 over four (4) contract years.

  3.2.4 **Contingency and Reserved Services Pool:** US $155,639 allocated for optional professional services (e.g., additional integrations, training, or enhancements) to be performed at the Company’s request and approved via Change Order, at the rates set forth in Section 3.4.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**

  3.3.1 **Implementation Fees.** The implementation fee shall be invoiced as follows: (a) forty percent (40%) upon Effective Date; (b) thirty percent (30%) upon completion of Phase 2 (Build and Integration) as reasonably determined by the Service Provider; and (c) thirty percent (30%) upon go‑live in production.

  3.3.2 **License and Subscription Fees.** The annual license and subscription fees shall be invoiced **annually in advance** on or about each anniversary of the Effective Date, except that the first annual fee shall be invoiced on the Effective Date.

  3.3.3 **Support and Maintenance Fees.** Support and maintenance fees shall be invoiced annually in advance together with the license and subscription fees.

  3.3.4 **Contingency and Reserved Services Pool.** Reserved services shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on actual hours incurred, at the rates set forth in Section 3.4, until the pool is fully utilized. Any unused portion of the pool at the Expiration Date shall be forfeited without refund, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

  3.3.5 **Payment Terms.** Except as otherwise stated in this Agreement, all undisputed invoices are payable within **forty‑five (45) days** from the date of the invoice (“Net 45”). Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars via wire transfer or other method specified in the applicable invoice.

3.4 **Rate Card for Additional Services.** For any additional Services not included in the Contract Value and requested by the Company and agreed by the Service Provider, the following hourly rates shall apply (subject to reasonable annual increases not exceeding five percent (5%) per year):

  (a) Senior Architect: US $275/hour  
  (b) Integration Specialist: US $225/hour  
  (c) Data Analyst: US $200/hour  
  (d) Project Manager: US $210/hour  
  (e) Trainer/Consultant: US $180/hour  

3.5 **Late Payment; Suspension.** Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one and one‑half percent (1.5%) per month or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the due date until paid in full. In addition to any other rights, the Service Provider may, upon written notice to the Company, suspend provision of the Services or access to the Solution if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the due date, until all such amounts and applicable interest are paid in full.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.** The Company may in good faith dispute any portion of an invoice by providing written notice of such dispute, with reasonable details, no later than fifteen (15) Business Days after receipt of the invoice. The Company shall timely pay any undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts within thirty (30) days of the Company’s notice. Failure to notify the Service Provider of a dispute within such fifteen (15) Business Day period shall constitute acceptance of the invoice.

3.7 **Taxes.** All fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value‑added, withholding, or other taxes, duties, or similar governmental charges (collectively, “Taxes”). The Company shall be responsible for all Taxes imposed on the transactions contemplated by this Agreement, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or employment. If the Company is required by law to withhold any Taxes from payments due to the Service Provider, the Company shall provide reasonable assistance to the Service Provider to obtain any available exemptions or reductions and shall provide the Service Provider with official receipts or other appropriate documentation evidencing such payment.

3.8 **Expenses.** Reasonable, pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred by the Service Provider’s personnel in connection with on‑site Services shall be reimbursed by the Company at cost, provided that: (a) the Service Provider obtains the Company’s written approval for any single trip expected to incur expenses exceeding US $3,000; and (b) the Service Provider provides reasonable supporting documentation (e.g., receipts) with its expense invoices.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until **April 24, 2028** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, the Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods on the same terms and conditions, except that fees may be adjusted by the Service Provider upon ninety (90) days’ prior written notice.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Favoring Vendor).** The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience at any time upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Service Provider. The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon at least **ninety (90) days’ prior written notice** to the Company.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days after receiving written notice of the breach from the non‑breaching Party; or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is the subject of any voluntary or involuntary petition in bankruptcy (which petition is not dismissed within sixty (60) days).

4.5 **Termination for Non‑Payment.** Without limiting Section 3.5, the Service Provider may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than ninety (90) days after the due date and the Company fails to cure such non‑payment within such thirty (30) day notice period.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) all rights and licenses granted to the Company under this Agreement shall immediately cease, except as expressly provided otherwise in Article VI;  
  (b) the Company shall immediately discontinue use of the Solution and Vendor Materials, and, at the Service Provider’s election, return or destroy any tangible copies thereof in the Company’s possession or control, certifying such destruction in writing upon request;  
  (c) the Company shall pay to the Service Provider all undisputed fees and expenses accrued up to the effective date of termination;  
  (d) if the Company terminates for convenience under Section 4.3, the Company shall pay (i) all fees accrued up to the effective date of termination; and (ii) as an early termination charge, the lesser of (x) thirty percent (30%) of the remaining license and support fees that would have been payable through the end of the then‑current Term; or (y) US $500,000; and  
  (e) Sections which by their nature should survive termination (including but not limited to Articles III (to the extent of accrued obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI) shall survive such termination.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.** Upon expiration or any termination (other than for the Service Provider’s uncured material breach), the Service Provider shall, upon the Company’s written request and at the Service Provider’s then‑current time and materials rates, provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days to facilitate migration of the Company’s data to an alternative solution.

  - **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant impairment of core functionality with limited workaround: initial response within four (4) hours; target resolution or workaround within twenty‑four (24) hours.
