## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of managed technology services (collectively, the **“Services”**) in support of Client’s business-critical application platform. The Services shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **Hosting and Infrastructure Services**  
  - Provision, operation, and management of production, staging, and test environments in a secure data center or cloud environment.  
  - Provisioning of compute, storage, network, and security resources sufficient to handle expected and peak loads, in accordance with the Specifications.  
  - Implementation and management of firewalls, load balancers, intrusion detection/prevention systems, and related security controls.

  (b) **Application Management Services**  
  - Deployment, configuration, and ongoing operational management of the hosted application, including patching, updates, and performance tuning.  
  - Monitoring of application health, transaction throughput, and error rates, including alerts and notifications to designated Client contacts.  
  - Coordination with Client’s internal teams in connection with integration interfaces, APIs, and third-party system connections.

  (c) **Support and Help Desk Services**  
  - Provision of Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 technical support for incidents and service requests related to the Services.  
  - Operation of a help desk accessible via email, web-based ticketing system, and telephone, during the support hours defined in Section 12.4.  
  - Logging, categorization, prioritization, and tracking of all Incidents through resolution, including communication with Client regarding status and next steps.

  (d) **Maintenance and Upgrades**  
  - Regular application of security patches, hotfixes, and releases to maintain the security and stability of the Services.  
  - Performance of routine preventative maintenance to optimize system performance and minimize downtime.  
  - Participation in scheduled maintenance windows, with prior notice to Client as set forth in Section 12.3.

  (e) **Backup and Disaster Recovery**  
  - Performance of regular backups of Client Data at intervals consistent with industry standards and the data retention requirements in Article XI.  
  - Maintenance and testing of disaster recovery capabilities enabling restoration of Services within target recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO) agreed between the Parties in writing.  
  - Execution of recovery procedures following a disaster or severe Incident, and coordination with Client on business continuity plans.

  (f) **Professional and Advisory Services**  
  - Architectural consulting and capacity planning to support Client’s anticipated growth and changing usage patterns.  
  - Assistance with migrations, onboarding, and offboarding activities, as may be requested by Client via a Change Order.  
  - Preparation of periodic reports regarding system performance, incident metrics, and recommended improvements.

2.2 **Specific Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, Vendor shall deliver the following tangible and intangible Deliverables:

  (a) **Implementation Plan.** A detailed implementation project plan within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, including major milestones, dependencies, roles and responsibilities, and acceptance criteria.

  (b) **System Architecture and Configuration Documentation.** Documentation describing the infrastructure topology, network design, security controls, data flows, backup procedures, and configuration parameters.

  (c) **Operational Runbook.** A runbook describing standard operating procedures, incident handling processes, escalation paths, routine maintenance tasks, and contact points for both Parties.

  (d) **Service Level Reports.** Monthly service level performance reports summarizing uptime, response times, incidents, root cause analyses (where applicable), and any Service Credits earned.

  (e) **Training Materials.** Basic user and administrator training materials, which may include manuals, quick reference guides, or recorded sessions, as agreed in writing.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  
  (b) using personnel with appropriate skills, training, and experience;  
  (c) in compliance with the Service Levels specified in Article XII;  
  (d) in compliance with all Applicable Laws, including data protection and privacy laws, as further set forth in Article XI; and  
  (e) in accordance with the Specifications, this Agreement, and any applicable Change Orders.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges and agrees that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Client’s timely performance of certain obligations, including:

  (a) providing accurate and complete requirements, information, and access credentials necessary to enable Vendor to perform the Services;  
  (b) designating a primary contact and an escalation contact for operational and incident-related communications;  
  (c) ensuring that Client’s systems and interfaces under Client’s control are properly configured and maintained;  
  (d) promptly reviewing and providing feedback on Deliverables and Service Level reports; and  
  (e) complying with Vendor’s reasonable security and access control procedures.

Vendor shall have no liability for performance issues to the extent caused by Client’s failure to perform its responsibilities in this Section 2.4, provided that Vendor has given prompt written notice of such failure.

2.5 **Exclusions.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in a Change Order, the Services expressly exclude:  
  (a) end-user training beyond the standard training materials described in Section 2.2(e);  
  (b) custom application development unrelated to the hosted solution;  
  (c) on-site support at Client locations (unless specifically agreed); and  
  (d) data cleansing, data normalization, or data modeling services.

2.6 **Changes to Scope.**  
Any changes to the scope, nature, or volume of the Services shall be managed via the Change Management process set forth in Article XIII. Vendor is not obligated to commence performance of any change until a corresponding Change Order has been executed by both Parties.

## ARTICLE III. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees.**  
In consideration for the Services and Deliverables provided by Vendor, Client shall pay Vendor an aggregate contract value of **USD $1,229,803** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to increases or decreases pursuant to duly executed Change Orders. The Contract Value shall be allocated as follows:

  (a) **Initial Implementation & Onboarding Fee:** USD $229,803, payable in accordance with Section 3.2(a);  
  (b) **Recurring Service Fees:** USD $1,000,000, allocated on a monthly basis over the Term, unless otherwise adjusted by Change Order;  
  (c) **Out-of-Scope Professional Services:** Billed on a time-and-materials basis at Vendor’s then-current standard hourly rates, as agreed in writing in each Change Order.

3.2 **Payment Milestones.**  

  (a) **Implementation & Onboarding Fee.**  
  - Fifty percent (50%) of the Implementation & Onboarding Fee shall be invoiced on the Effective Date.  
  - Fifty percent (50%) shall be invoiced upon completion of the initial production deployment or ninety (90) days after the Effective Date, whichever occurs first.

  (b) **Recurring Service Fees.**  
  - Recurring Service Fees shall be invoiced monthly in advance, based on the contracted capacity and service tiers.  
  - Any variable usage (if applicable) above baseline capacity shall be invoiced monthly in arrears.

  (c) **Professional Services.**  
  - Professional services under Change Orders shall be invoiced monthly in arrears, based on actual hours incurred and documented.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
All undisputed amounts invoiced by Vendor shall be due and payable within **thirty (30) days** of the date of the applicable invoice (**“Net 30”**). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method. Client shall be responsible for all bank or transfer charges associated with its payment.

3.4 **Late Payment; Interest.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, from the due date until the date of payment. Vendor may, upon written notice and after providing at least fifteen (15) days to cure, suspend performance of the Services in the event of Client’s failure to pay undisputed amounts when due.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
All fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for any such taxes (excluding taxes on Vendor’s income), and Vendor shall itemize any such taxes on its invoices where applicable. If Client is exempt from such taxes, it shall provide Vendor with a valid tax exemption certificate.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Vendor shall bear its own ordinary business expenses in connection with the performance of the Services. However, Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor at Client’s written request, provided that such expenses are incurred in accordance with Client’s then-current travel and expense policy, if provided to Vendor in advance.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of the invoice date, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Non-payment of a disputed amount shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement so long as Client complies with this Section.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until **January 09, 2028** (the **“Initial Term”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV. Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods (each, a **“Renewal Term”**) unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current Term.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement for its convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to Vendor. Vendor may terminate this Agreement for its convenience upon at least one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Client, provided that Vendor reasonably cooperates in transition assistance as described in Section 4.6.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail (or, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period); or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is the subject of a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver or similar officer appointed for its business, property, or assets.

4.4 **Termination for Non-Payment.**  
Vendor may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within forty-five (45) days after the applicable due date and fails to cure such non-payment within fifteen (15) days after receiving written notice from Vendor.

4.5 **Termination for Legal or Regulatory Reasons.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if continued performance would cause such Party to be in material violation of Applicable Law and the Parties are unable to agree on modifications to the Services or this Agreement to avoid such violation within sixty (60) days after good faith negotiations.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

  (a) **Accrued Obligations.** Termination shall not relieve either Party of any payment or other obligation accrued prior to the effective date of termination.  

  (b) **Wind-Down and Transition.** Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement (other than by Vendor for Client’s uncured material breach), Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance, including making Client Data available for export and cooperating in transition to Client or a replacement vendor, at Vendor’s then-standard rates (unless otherwise agreed).  

  (c) **Return or Deletion of Client Data.** Within sixty (60) days after termination, Vendor shall, at Client’s written direction, either return to Client or securely delete and destroy all Client Data in Vendor’s possession or control, subject to Section 11.6.  

  (d) **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including but not limited to Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI, and all payment obligations accrued but unpaid.

4.7 **No Refunds.**  
Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, including in the case of Vendor’s uncured material breach leading to termination by Client, all fees paid or payable are non-refundable.

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **General.**  
Vendor shall provide the Services in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in this Article XII. Failure to meet the Service Levels may entitle Client to Service Credits as set forth in Section 12.6, which shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for such failures, without prejudice to other remedies for material breach.

12.2 **Service Availability (Uptime).**  
Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the Services is available **99.9%** of the time each calendar month (the **“Uptime Commitment”**), excluding permitted downtime as defined below.

  (a) **Calculation of Uptime Percentage.**  
  Uptime Percentage = (Total Minutes in Month – Excluded Downtime – Unplanned Downtime) / (Total Minutes in Month – Excluded Downtime) × 100%.

  (b) **Excluded Downtime.**  
  Excluded Downtime shall include:  
    (i) scheduled maintenance within the System Maintenance Window as per Section 12.3;  
    (ii) emergency maintenance with at least two (2) hours’ notice to Client;  
    (iii) downtime caused by Client’s equipment or third-party systems under Client’s control;  
    (iv) Force Majeure Events; and  
    (v) incidents attributable to Client’s misuse or unauthorized modification of the Services.

12.3 **Maintenance Windows.**  
Vendor may schedule routine maintenance during the System Maintenance Window of Saturdays from 1:00 AM to 5:00 AM Eastern Time, or at other times mutually agreed in writing. Vendor shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ notice prior to any scheduled maintenance likely to cause service unavailability.

12.4 **Support Hours and Contact.**  
Vendor shall provide support for Incidents and service requests as follows:

  (a) **Standard Support Hours:** 24 hours per day, 7 days per week, for Severity 1 and Severity 2 Incidents;  
  (b) **Business Hours Support:** 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM Eastern Time, Monday through Friday (excluding public holidays), for Severity 3 and Severity 4 issues;  
  (c) Support may be accessed via web ticketing system, email, or phone, as specified in the operational documentation.

12.5 **Incident Severity, Response, and Resolution Targets.**  

Incidents shall be classified and responded to as follows:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):**  
    - Definition: Complete outage or failure of the production Services, or severe issue causing material business disruption with no workaround.  
    - Initial Response Time: 30 minutes.  
    - Target Resolution / Workaround: 4 hours.

  - **Severity 2 (High):**  
    - Definition: Significant degradation or partial unavailability impacting major functionality, with limited workaround.  
    - Initial Response Time: 1 hour.  
    - Target Resolution / Workaround: 8 hours.

  - **Severity 3 (Medium):**  
    - Definition: Non-critical issue affecting some features or a subset of users, workaround available.  
    - Initial Response Time: 4 Business Hours.  
    - Target Resolution / Workaround: 3 Business Days.

  - **Severity 4 (Low):**  
    - Definition: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general service requests.  
    - Initial Response Time: 1 Business Day.  
    - Target Resolution / Workaround: As reasonably scheduled, typically within 15 Business Days.

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the above response and resolution targets, but such targets are not guaranteed, and failure to meet them shall be considered in determining Service Credits under Section 12.6.

12.6 **Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to Service Credits calculated as follows:

  - Uptime ≥ 99.9%: No Service Credit.  
  - 99.0% ≤ Uptime < 99.9%: Service Credit of 5% of monthly recurring fees for the affected Services.  
  - 98.0% ≤ Uptime < 99.0%: Service Credit of 10% of monthly recurring fees for the affected Services.  
  - Uptime < 98.0%: Service Credit of 20% of monthly recurring fees for the affected Services.

Service Credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash, except where there are no further invoices, in which case Vendor shall refund the applicable amount.

12.7 **Conditions for Service Credits.**  
To be eligible for a Service Credit, Client must submit a written request within thirty (30) days of the end of the month in which the Service Level failure occurred, providing reasonable details. Vendor shall review and, if validated, apply the Service Credit to the next applicable invoice.

## ARTICLE XV. FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding strikes or labor disputes of the affected Party’s own workforce), pandemics, epidemics, governmental orders, failures of telecommunications or utility services not caused by the affected Party, and other similar events.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event and its expected impact on performance;  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event; and  
  (c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Excused Performance.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration necessary due to the Force Majeure Event. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith any appropriate adjustments to timelines, milestones, or fees necessitated by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially impairs the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
