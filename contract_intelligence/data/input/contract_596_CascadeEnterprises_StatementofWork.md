## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall design, implement, configure, integrate, and support a cloud-based enterprise data analytics and workflow automation platform (the “**Platform**”) for the Client’s operations in the Asia-Pacific region, with future scalability to global operations. The Services shall include project management, solution architecture, system configuration, data migration, integration with specified systems, testing, training, documentation, and post-go-live managed support.

2.2 **Phased Approach.**  
The Services shall be performed in the following phases, each of which shall consist of clearly defined activities and Deliverables:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Estimated: 3 months).**  
  (a) Conduct stakeholder workshops with Contoso’s business and IT teams to capture detailed business requirements, current-state processes, data sources, and technical constraints.  
  (b) Perform system landscape assessment, including existing ERP, CRM, HR, and legacy data warehouse systems.  
  (c) Develop and deliver the following Deliverables:  
  - Requirements Traceability Matrix;  
  - Current-State Architecture Diagram;  
  - Target-State Architecture and Integration Blueprint;  
  - High-Level Project Plan with work breakdown structure and Milestones.  

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Design and Architecture (Estimated: 4 months).**  
  (a) Define detailed technical specifications for the Platform, including data models, ETL/ELT processes, workflow definitions, role-based access controls, and security design.  
  (b) Select, in consultation with the Client, the chosen cloud infrastructure and major third-party components.  
  (c) Deliver the following Deliverables:  
  - Detailed Solution Design Document;  
  - Data Model and Dictionary;  
  - Integration Specifications for all in-scope systems;  
  - Security and Compliance Design, including GDPR/CCPA/PDPA alignment.  

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Build and Configuration (Estimated: 8 months).**  
  (a) Configure and develop the Platform environment(s) including development, test, staging, and production environments.  
  (b) Build ETL/ELT pipelines, data transformations, dashboards, reports, and workflow automation scripts.  
  (c) Configure integration interfaces (API-based, message-based, and file-based) to agreed source and target systems.  
  (d) Deliver the following Deliverables:  
  - Configured Platform in test and staging environments;  
  - Integration Connectors and Interfaces;  
  - Initial Data Loads and Data Validation Reports;  
  - Configuration Guides and Runbooks.  

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Testing and User Acceptance (Estimated: 3 months).**  
  (a) Develop and execute test plans, including system testing, integration testing, performance testing, and security testing.  
  (b) Support Client-led User Acceptance Testing (“**UAT**”) and remediation of defects.  
  (c) Deliver the following Deliverables:  
  - Test Strategy and Detailed Test Cases;  
  - Test Execution Reports and Defect Logs;  
  - Performance Benchmark Report;  
  - UAT Sign-Off Documentation (subject to Client’s approval).  

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Go-Live and Stabilization (Estimated: 2 months).**  
  (a) Execute final data migration strategy and cutover plan.  
  (b) Provide hypercare support for at least sixty (60) days post go-live.  
  (c) Deliver the following Deliverables:  
  - Production Go-Live Checklist;  
  - Post-Implementation Review and Lessons-Learned Document;  
  - Final As-Built Documentation.  

  2.2.6 **Phase 6 – Managed Services and Support (Remainder of Term).**  
  (a) Provide ongoing Level 2 and Level 3 support for the Platform, in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.  
  (b) Perform routine maintenance, monitoring, performance tuning, and minor enhancements as requested and approved via Change Order(s).  
  (c) Deliver the following Deliverables:  
  - Monthly Service Performance Reports;  
  - Incident and Problem Management Reports;  
  - Quarterly Capacity and Optimization Recommendations.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, with due skill, care, and diligence, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services. Without limiting the foregoing, the Service Provider shall:

  2.3.1 Use personnel with appropriate qualifications, experience, and training;  
  2.3.2 Comply with the Service Levels specified in Article XII;  
  2.3.3 Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards, including but not limited to data protection and cybersecurity standards;  
  2.3.4 Use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the agreed project timelines; and  
  2.3.5 Cooperate with the Client’s project management and other vendors to minimize disruptions.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client acknowledges that the Service Provider’s performance depends upon the timely and effective performance of the Client’s responsibilities, including:

  2.4.1 Providing timely access to Client personnel, systems, facilities, networks, and relevant documentation;  
  2.4.2 Providing all necessary Client Data in a clean, readable, and agreed format;

  2.5.2 The Client shall, within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of a Deliverable (or such other period as the Parties may agree in writing), either (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide a written list of material non-conformities with the Specifications.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production Platform is available **99.9%** of the time per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events (“**Service Availability**”).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Scheduled maintenance shall:

  12.2.1 Be performed during off-peak hours where reasonably practicable;  
  12.2.2 Not exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month without the Client’s prior written consent; and  
  12.2.3 Be notified to the Client at least five (5) Business Days in advance, except in emergency situations.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution.**  
The Service Provider shall provide incident management in accordance with the following severity levels:

  12.3.1 **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of a business-critical function with no workaround.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour (24x7).  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 4 hours.  

  12.3.2 **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of service with high business impact; workaround available but not sustainable.  
  - Initial Response: within 2 hours (Business Days, 8:00–18:00 SGT).  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours.  

  12.3.3 **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial degradation or non-critical issue; workaround readily available.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution: within 5 Business Days.  

  12.3.4 **Severity 4 (Low):** Cosmetic or minor issues; no significant business impact.  
  - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days.  
  - Target Resolution: as mutually agreed, typically in the next minor release.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
In the event that Service Availability in any calendar month falls below the 99.9% uptime commitment, the Client shall be entitled to the following non-refundable, non-transferable service credits (applied to the next Managed Services invoice):

  12.4.1 Availability 99.0% to <99.9%: 3% of the monthly Managed Services Fee;  
  12.4.2 Availability 98.0% to <99.0%: 7% of the monthly Managed Services Fee;  
  12.4.3 Availability <98.0%: 12% of the monthly Managed Services Fee.

Service credits shall be the Client’s sole monetary remedy for failure to meet Service Availability targets; however, this does not limit the Client’s rights with respect to material breach under this Agreement.

  15.2.1 Promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature, expected duration, and impact of the Force Majeure Event;  
  15.2.2 Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  
  15.2.3 Keep the other Party reasonably informed of developments and the expected resumption of performance.

15.3 **Suspension and Termination.**
