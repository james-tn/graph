## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General

2.1.1 Titan shall provide to Contoso, during the Term, such Services as Contoso may request and Titan may agree to perform, as detailed in this **Article II** and one or more SOWs executed pursuant to this Agreement. Each SOW shall be incorporated by reference into and governed by the terms of this Agreement.

2.1.2 The Services are expected to include, without limitation: (a) strategic consulting and advisory services in relation to Contoso’s enterprise systems and digital transformation initiatives; (b) design, configuration, and implementation of certain technology solutions; (c) integration of third‑party systems and data sources; (d) migration, quality assurance, and testing activities; (e) ongoing support and maintenance services; and (f) related project management, documentation, and training services for Contoso personnel.

### 2.2 Description of Services

2.2.1 Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.1**, the Services shall encompass the following categories of work, subject to refinement and elaboration in each SOW:

  (a) **Discovery and Requirements Gathering**  
  Titan shall conduct interviews, workshops, and technical assessments with Contoso’s subject matter experts, stakeholders, and IT personnel to identify requirements, constraints, and objectives. Titan shall prepare and deliver requirements documentation, including process flows, data mapping, and system interface specifications.

  (b) **Solution Design and Architecture**  
  Titan shall design an overall technical and functional solution architecture that meets the agreed requirements. Such design shall consider scalability, performance, security, regulatory compliance, and integration with Contoso’s existing infrastructure. Titan shall create solution design documents, conceptual models, and technical architecture diagrams.

  (c) **Configuration, Customization, and Development**  
  Titan shall configure standard modules and components of the selected technologies and, where necessary and agreed, design and develop custom components, scripts, or integrations. All such work shall be documented in sufficient detail to permit ongoing maintenance and support.

  (d) **Testing and Quality Assurance**  
  Titan shall perform unit, integration, system, and user acceptance testing (UAT support) as described in the relevant SOW. Titan shall provide test plans, test scripts, defect logs, and test reports, and shall address defects in accordance with agreed resolution timeframes.

  (e) **Deployment and Transition to Production**  
  Titan shall assist with the deployment of the Deliverables into Contoso’s production environment, including support for cut‑over planning, data migration, and rollback strategies. Titan shall coordinate with Contoso’s personnel to ensure minimal disruption to business operations.

  (f) **Training and Knowledge Transfer**  
  Titan shall provide training sessions for Contoso’s key users, administrators, and support staff, which may include on‑site or remote sessions, training materials, and user guides, as further specified in the SOWs.

  (g) **Ongoing Support and Maintenance**  
  Subject to the applicable SOW, Titan shall provide post‑deployment support, incident management, bug fixing, and minor enhancements, consistent with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

### 2.3 Deliverables

2.3.1 Titan shall deliver to Contoso the Deliverables described in this Article II and in each SOW, which may include, without limitation:

  - Requirements specifications;  
  - Architecture and design documentation;  
  - Configured system environments and code repositories;  
  - Integration components and interface specifications;  
  - Test plans and reports;  
  - User manuals, training materials, and operating procedures;  
  - Operational run books and support guides.

2.3.2 Deliverables shall be deemed accepted by Contoso upon the earlier of: (a) Contoso’s written acceptance; (b) Contoso’s use of the Deliverable in a production environment; or (c) ten (10) Business Days after delivery if Contoso has not provided a written rejection specifying material, reproducible non‑conformities with the applicable SOW. Any rejection must identify the non‑conformities in reasonable detail to permit Titan to address them.

2.3.3 In the event of a valid rejection of a Deliverable, Titan shall, at no additional charge (except where the non‑conformity arises from Contoso Materials or instructions), correct the non‑conformities within a commercially reasonable period, and the acceptance procedure in **Section 2.3.2** shall be repeated for the corrected Deliverable.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Titan shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications. Titan shall employ reasonable efforts to meet the timelines, milestones, and Service Levels set forth in this Agreement and any SOW.

2.4.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Parties acknowledge that project timelines may be affected by dependencies on Contoso’s actions (including provision of approvals, information, access, and resources) and third‑party factors outside Titan’s control. Titan shall not be liable for delays or failures to meet non‑mandatory deadlines to the extent caused or materially contributed to by such factors.

2.4.3 Contoso shall provide Titan with timely access to relevant personnel, systems, and facilities, and shall ensure that all necessary information and decisions are provided in a timely manner. Failure of Contoso to fulfill its obligations may result in adjustments to the timelines, fees, and Deliverables, as addressed in **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

### 2.5 Statements of Work

2.5.1 Each SOW shall, as appropriate, include: (a) a detailed description of the Services and Deliverables; (b) timelines, milestones, and acceptance criteria; (c) the associated fees and payment schedule; (d) any Service Levels applicable to such Services; and (e) any assumptions, dependencies, or special terms.

2.5.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to override this Agreement with respect to a particular provision.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date** and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until **19 November 2028** (the **“Expiration Date”**).

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend or renew this Agreement beyond the Expiration Date, on such terms and conditions as the Parties may agree.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience, by providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Titan, specifying the effective date of termination.

4.2.2 Titan may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience, by providing at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to Contoso.

4.2.3 In the event of a termination for convenience by Contoso, Contoso shall pay Titan for: (a) all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination; (b) all unrecoverable, non‑cancellable commitments and third‑party costs incurred by Titan in reliance on this Agreement or the applicable SOW; and (c) a reasonable wind‑down fee for Titan’s redeployment costs, if expressly provided in the SOW.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement or such SOW and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach.

4.3.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation other than for a solvent amalgamation or reconstruction, has a receiver or administrator appointed over any of its assets, or ceases to carry on business.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) All SOWs shall automatically terminate, unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing;  
  (b) Contoso shall immediately pay all undisputed amounts due to Titan under this Agreement and any SOW, including any amounts payable pursuant to **Section 4.2.3** and **Section 3.4**;  
  (c) Each Party shall, upon request, return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4 (Return or Destruction)**; and  
  (d) The rights, licenses, and obligations that expressly or by their nature are intended to survive (including Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI and payment obligations accruing as of the date of termination) shall so survive.

4.4.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of any liability that has accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including any payment obligations or indemnity claims under **Article VIII**.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 **“Confidential Information”** means any and all non‑public information, in whatever form or medium, disclosed by or on behalf of a Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, whether orally, visually, in writing, or in any other tangible or intangible form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

  (a) business plans, strategies, forecasts, financial information, and pricing;  
  (b) technical information, system architectures, source code, object code, algorithms, and documentation;  
  (c) customer lists, supplier lists, marketing plans, and market analyses;  
  (d) trade secrets, know‑how, inventions, discoveries, and methodologies;  
  (e) Personal Data and other data sets; and  
  (f) the terms and conditions of this Agreement and any SOW.

### 5.2 Exclusions

5.2.1 Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party on a non‑confidential basis prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  (c) is lawfully received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect thereto; or  
  (d) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

### 5.3 Confidentiality Obligations

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
  (b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement; and  
  (c) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, Affiliates, contractors, and professional advisers who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure

5.4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted, promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.4.2 If the Disclosing Party is unable to obtain such protective order, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose, provided that it uses reasonable efforts to ensure that confidential treatment is afforded to such disclosed information.

### 5.5 Return or Destruction

5.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, including all copies, summaries, and extracts, except that:

  (a) the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for the purpose of establishing its compliance with this Agreement; and  
  (b) the Receiving Party shall not be required to delete Confidential Information stored in routine back‑up media, provided that such Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein until deleted in the ordinary course.

### 5.6 Survival

5.6.1 The obligations in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall be protected for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under applicable law.
