## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

  3.2.2 **Project or Milestone Fees.** For specific projects or Deliverables, fixed fees or milestone‑based payments as identified in project‑specific documentation or Change Orders;

  3.2.3 **Additional Services Fees.** Charges for any additional services, enhancements, or out‑of‑scope work requested by Contoso and agreed in writing.

  3.3.1 Nova shall invoice Contoso monthly in arrears for all Services performed and Deliverables provided during the preceding month, unless a different invoicing schedule is specified in a Change Order for particular work.

  3.3.2 Each invoice shall be itemized in reasonable detail and shall reference the Contract Reference Number **AGR-NOV-202407-705**. Supporting documentation (such as timesheets or milestone achievement confirmations) shall be provided on request.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  

  3.4.1 All undisputed invoices shall be payable by Contoso within **forty‑five (45) days** from the date of receipt of the invoice (“**Net 45**”).

  3.4.2 Payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) by wire transfer to the bank account designated in writing by Nova.

  3.4.3 Contoso shall not be entitled to set‑off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from payments due to Nova except as may be required by Applicable Law or expressly agreed in writing.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  

  3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid by the due date shall accrue interest from the due date until payment in full at a rate equal to the greater of (i) four percent (4%) per annum above the Bank of England base rate, or (ii) ten percent (10%) per annum.

  3.6.1 The Fees are exclusive of Taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes arising from the Services and Deliverables, except for Nova’s income, payroll, or corporate taxes.

  3.6.2 If Contoso is required by Applicable Law to withhold any Taxes from payments to Nova, Contoso shall (a) promptly remit such Taxes to the appropriate authority, (b) provide Nova with official receipts or other documentation evidencing such payment, and (c) increase any payment so that Nova receives the full amount it would have received but for the withholding.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

  3.7.1 Reasonable, documented out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred by Nova in the performance of the Services (including travel, accommodation, and subsistence) shall be reimbursable by Contoso, provided that such expenses are pre‑approved in writing by Contoso.

  3.7.2 All expense claims shall be invoiced together with appropriate supporting receipts, in accordance with Contoso’s reasonable expense policies as notified to Nova in advance.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **7 December 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, by mutual written agreement at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend the Term of this Agreement, subject to revised commercial terms and conditions as agreed in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Client.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason by providing Nova with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** of termination. During such notice period:

  4.3.1 Contoso shall continue to pay all Fees and reimbursable expenses in accordance with Article III; and

  4.3.2 Nova shall continue to perform the Services, subject to any agreed ramp‑down plan, and shall reasonably cooperate with Contoso to transition the Services.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Vendor.** Nova may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing Contoso with at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice of termination.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**

  4.5.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a **material breach** which (if capable of remedy) is not remedied within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach.

  4.5.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into administration, or has a receiver, liquidator, or analogous officer appointed over all or a substantial part of its assets.

  4.5.3 Without limiting the foregoing, Nova may terminate this Agreement on written notice if Contoso fails to pay any undisputed invoice within sixty (60) days after the due date and does not cure such payment default within ten (10) Business Days following written notice.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  4.6.1 Contoso shall pay Nova all outstanding Fees and reimbursable expenses for Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, together with any early termination charges or wind‑down costs expressly agreed (if any);

  4.6.2 Nova shall, upon written request and subject to payment of applicable Fees, provide Contoso with reasonable cooperation for a transition of the Services to Contoso or a third‑party provider, on mutually agreed terms;

  4.6.3 Each Party shall return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s option) all Confidential Information and Client Materials, subject to the archival rights in Section 5.4 and any Applicable Law requiring retention;

  4.6.4 All licenses granted under this Agreement shall terminate, except for any perpetual licenses expressly set out in Article VI (if applicable).

4.7 **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions), and all payment obligations accrued up to the date of termination.

15.1 **Definition.** A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including:

  15.1.1 Acts of God, natural disasters, or extreme weather events;

  15.1.2 War, terrorism, civil unrest, riots, or insurrections;

  15.1.3 Strikes, lockouts, or other industrial disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce to the extent within its reasonable control);

  15.1.4 Epidemics, pandemics, or public health emergencies;

  15.1.5 Failure or interruption of telecommunications, power supply, or infrastructure not under the control of the affected Party;

  15.1.6 Acts or omissions of any governmental authority, including export/import restrictions and changes in law.

15.2 **Notice.** The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  15.2.1 Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event, its expected duration, and the obligations affected; and

  15.2.2 Use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration affected by the Force Majeure Event. Performance deadlines shall be extended by a period equivalent to the period of delay caused by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Prolonged Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Services or, if the Force Majeure Event materially affects the Agreement as a whole, terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice.
