## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overall Scope.** Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive technology consulting, software development, systems integration, and managed IT support services (collectively, the **“Services”**) to support Client’s internal business systems, customer-facing applications, and related infrastructure, in accordance with the terms of this Agreement, the Specifications, and any applicable SOWs. Vendor’s obligations shall be performed in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, exercising the degree of skill and care expected of a reputable provider of similar services.

2.2 **Description of Services.** Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Services shall include, as applicable:

2.2.1 **Consulting and Requirements Analysis.** Vendor shall:

  (a) conduct workshops and interviews with Client stakeholders to gather business and technical requirements;  

  (b) analyze Client’s existing systems, architecture, integration points, and data flows;  

  (c) develop a comprehensive solution design and architecture documents; and  

  (d) provide recommendations on best practices, technology stack, scalability, security, and compliance with Applicable Law.

2.2.2 **Software Development and Integration.** Vendor shall:

  (a) design, develop, configure, and customize software modules, microservices, APIs, and user interfaces as specified in the SOWs;  

  (b) integrate developed components with Client’s existing applications, databases, identity and access management systems, and third‑party services;  

  (c) adhere to agreed coding standards, version control practices, and documentation requirements; and  

  (d) perform unit, integration, system, and regression testing prior to delivery to Client.

2.2.3 **Data Migration and Transformation.** Where applicable, Vendor shall:

  (a) assess legacy data sources and formats;  

  (b) design and implement data migration scripts and processes;  

  (c) perform data cleansing, transformation, and validation to ensure integrity and accuracy; and  

  (d) coordinate trial and production data migrations with Client to minimize business disruption.

2.2.4 **Deployment and Transition.** Vendor shall:

  (a) prepare deployment plans, release notes, and rollback procedures;  

  (b) support deployment of Deliverables into Client’s test, staging, and production environments;  

  (c) assist Client’s internal teams with transition, including knowledge transfer sessions, training materials, and operational documentation; and  

  (d) provide post‑go‑live stabilization support for a period specified in the relevant SOW.

2.2.5 **Managed Support and Maintenance.** Vendor shall:

  (a) provide Level 2 and Level 3 technical support for the Deliverables and related systems during the Term;  

  (b) monitor system availability and performance in accordance with the Service Levels;  

  (c) provide bug fixes, patches, updates, and maintenance releases as necessary to maintain functionality and security; and  

  (d) maintain an incident management and escalation process as described in Article XII.

2.3 **Deliverables.** The Deliverables to be provided by Vendor under this Agreement may include, without limitation:

  (a) functional and technical specification documents;  

  (b) architecture diagrams and data models;  

  (c) software source code, compiled binaries, configuration files, and deployment scripts;  

  (d) test plans, test cases, and test result reports;  

  (e) user guides, system administration manuals, and training materials; and  

  (f) periodic performance, usage, and support reports.

Each SOW shall specify the applicable Deliverables, including detailed descriptions, dependencies, acceptance criteria, and delivery milestones.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall:

2.4.1 perform the Services in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices and with reasonable skill, care, and diligence;

2.4.2 ensure that all personnel assigned by Vendor are suitably qualified, experienced, and trained to perform the Services and meet the Service Levels;

2.4.3 perform the Services in compliance with Applicable Law, including all laws relating to data protection, privacy, information security, and export control;

2.4.4 use tools, methodologies, and processes that are consistent with good practice in the IT and consulting industry.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

2.5.1 Each SOW shall include a detailed project plan that identifies key activities, milestones, and delivery dates for the Services and Deliverables. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to such timelines.

2.5.2 Vendor shall promptly notify Client in writing if Vendor becomes aware of any circumstances that may materially impact the agreed timelines or milestone dates, stating the reasons for the delay and proposing remedial actions. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate any schedule impact, including considering a Change Order under Article XIII where appropriate.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

2.6.1 Upon delivery of any Deliverable, Vendor shall notify Client in writing. Client shall have a review and testing period (the **“Acceptance Period”**) as specified in the applicable SOW, or, if not specified, ten (10) Business Days from receipt of the Deliverable.

2.6.2 During the Acceptance Period, Client shall test the Deliverable against the applicable Specifications and acceptance criteria. Client shall provide Vendor with written notice of acceptance or a written description of any material non‑conformities.

2.6.3 If Client identifies material non‑conformities, Vendor shall, at no additional cost to Client, promptly correct such non‑conformities and re‑submit the Deliverable for Client’s review. The Parties shall repeat the acceptance process until the Deliverable is accepted. Failure by Client to provide notice of non‑conformity within the Acceptance Period shall be deemed acceptance, provided that this shall not limit Client’s rights under the warranties set out in Article VII.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  

Client acknowledges that Vendor’s performance of the Services is dependent upon timely and effective cooperation by Client. Accordingly, Client shall:

  (a) provide Vendor with access to relevant systems, facilities, personnel, and information as reasonably required for the performance of the Services;  

  (b) designate a project manager or primary point of contact to coordinate with Vendor;  

  (c) review and respond to Vendor’s requests for information, feedback, and approvals in a timely manner; and  

  (d) perform any Client responsibilities expressly set forth in this Agreement or applicable SOW.  

Any delay or failure by Client to fulfill its responsibilities may impact the timelines and may require a Change Order under Article XIII.

2.8 **Subcontractors.** Vendor may use subcontractors in the performance of the Services, provided that Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors and for the performance of the Services in accordance with this Agreement. Vendor shall ensure that any subcontractor engaged in the provision of the Services is bound by confidentiality and data protection obligations that are no less protective than those set out in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total estimated contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **Three Million Twenty‑One Thousand Four Hundred Seventy‑Four United States Dollars (US$3,021,474)** (the **“Contract Value”**). The Contract Value may be adjusted through duly executed Change Orders in accordance with Article XIII.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Fees shall consist of:

3.2.1 **Fixed Project Fees.** Fees for defined project phases, including requirements analysis, design, development, testing, and deployment, shall be charged on a fixed‑fee basis as set out in the applicable SOW.

3.2.2 **Time and Materials Fees.** For additional services requested by Client that are not included in the fixed project scope, Vendor may charge time and materials fees at the rates specified in the applicable SOW or fee schedule.

3.2.3 **Managed Services Fees.** Ongoing support, maintenance, and managed services shall be charged on a recurring monthly or annual basis as specified in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  

3.3.1 **Invoicing.** Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the milestone and billing schedule set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless expressly stated otherwise, Vendor may issue invoices:

  (a) upon completion and acceptance of specified milestones;  

  (b) monthly in arrears for time and materials services; and  

  (c) monthly or annually in advance for recurring managed services.

3.3.2 **Payment Due Date.** All properly issued and undisputed invoices shall be payable by Client within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the invoice date (**“Net 30”**).

3.3.3 **Disputed Amounts.** If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receiving the invoice, specifying the grounds for the dispute. Client shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.3.2. The Parties shall cooperate to resolve the dispute promptly. Any amount subsequently determined to be payable shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days of resolution.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

3.4.1 Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid by Client when due may, at Vendor’s discretion, accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate equal to the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law.

3.4.2 Vendor shall not suspend or reduce the Services solely due to late payment unless: (a) the amount is undisputed; (b) such amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of non‑payment; and (c) Vendor has provided at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice of its intention to suspend. Any suspension shall be proportionate and shall not relieve Client of its payment obligations.

3.5 **Taxes.**  

3.5.1 All Fees are exclusive of VAT and other applicable indirect taxes, which shall be charged in addition where applicable and paid by Client in accordance with Applicable Law.

3.5.2 Each Party shall be responsible for its own taxes based on its income or profits. If Client is required by law to withhold any taxes from payments to Vendor, Client shall provide Vendor with appropriate documentation of such withholding, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to minimize any such taxes, including by applying any applicable double taxation treaties.

3.6 **Expenses.** Vendor shall be reimbursed for reasonable, pre‑approved, out‑of‑pocket expenses actually incurred in the course of providing the Services, including travel, accommodation, and subsistence, in accordance with Client’s then‑current travel and expense policies as provided to Vendor in writing. Vendor shall provide supporting documentation (such as receipts) with any expense claims. Expenses shall be invoiced separately and paid in accordance with Section 3.3.

3.7 **No Set‑Off.** Except as otherwise expressly permitted under this Agreement or required by law, Client shall not be entitled to set off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from fees or other sums due to Vendor under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **12 October 2028** (the **“Initial Term”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may agree in writing, no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, to renew or extend this Agreement for one or more additional periods on terms to be agreed. Any such renewal or extension shall be documented by a written amendment executed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause by providing Vendor with not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice.

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience by providing Client with not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice.

4.3.3 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor for: (a) all Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination; (b) any non‑cancellable commitments incurred prior to notice of termination, provided Vendor uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such costs; and (c) any agreed termination fees expressly stated in an applicable SOW. Client shall not be liable for any loss of profit or other consequential loss resulting solely from such termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW with immediate effect by written notice if the other Party:

  (a) commits a material breach of this Agreement or any SOW which, if capable of remedy, is not remedied within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach and requiring it to be remedied; or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation, is placed into administration, has a receiver or similar officer appointed over its assets, or ceases or threatens to cease to carry on business in the ordinary course, subject to Applicable Law protecting insolvent entities.

4.4.2 Without limiting Section 4.4.1, repeated failure to meet key Service Levels, or repeated material non‑conformities in Deliverables, may constitute a material breach if not cured within the applicable cure period, subject to the dispute resolution procedure in Article XIV.

4.5 **Termination for Data Protection or Security Breach.** Client shall have the right to terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if Vendor experiences a material data breach or persistent information security deficiencies that materially compromise Client Data and Vendor fails to remedy such breach or deficiencies promptly and in any event within the period specified by Client acting reasonably, taking into account Applicable Law and supervisory authority requirements.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

4.6.1 Vendor shall promptly cease all work under the Agreement, except to the extent reasonably requested by Client to assist with orderly transition;

4.6.2 Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, subject to any rights of set‑off in respect of proven damages resulting from Vendor’s breach;

4.6.3 Vendor shall, at Client’s written direction, return or securely destroy all Client Data and Confidential Information in accordance with Article V and Article XI, subject to any retention obligations under Applicable Law;

4.6.4 Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance (at rates not exceeding those set out in this Agreement) for up to ninety (90) days following termination, if requested by Client, to facilitate migration to another service provider or back to Client, provided that Client pays Vendor’s Fees for such assistance.

4.7 **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including, without limitation, Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

  - Target Response Time: within 4 hours.  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within 24 hours.

  - Target Response Time: within 1 Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within 3 Business Days.

- **Priority 4 (Low)**: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or informational requests.  
  - Target Response Time: within 2 Business Days.
