## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client the Services described in this Article II and in any SOW executed under this Addendum. The Services are directed primarily toward the design, implementation, integration, and ongoing managed support of a cloud-hosted, business-critical application platform for Client’s enterprise operations, together with associated data migration, reporting, and training services.

2.1.2 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor shall:  
(a) assess Client’s existing systems, processes, and data flows;  
(b) design a solution architecture leveraging Vendor Materials and Third-Party Materials where appropriate;  
(c) configure, integrate, and customize the platform as necessary to meet mutually agreed specifications;  
(d) perform data migration and validation activities;  
(e) provide ongoing managed services, including monitoring, incident management, and support; and  
(f) deliver periodic performance reports and strategic recommendations.

### 2.2 Detailed Services Description

2.2.1 **Discovery and Requirements Gathering.** Vendor shall conduct an initial discovery phase of approximately six (6) to eight (8) weeks commencing on or about March 15, 2025, during which Vendor will interview Client stakeholders, review existing documentation, evaluate current systems, and capture business, technical, and security requirements. Vendor shall produce a Requirements & Architecture Specification (“**RAS**”) document, which shall be reviewed and approved in writing by Client. Client’s approval of the RAS shall serve as the baseline for scope, and any deviation thereafter shall be handled via the Change Management process in Article XIII.

2.2.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.** Based on the RAS, Vendor shall design the target solution architecture, including:  
(a) system components, integrations, and data flows;  
(b) roles and access control models;  
(c) high-level disaster recovery and business continuity approach;  
(d) non-functional requirements such as performance and scalability; and  
(e) high-level data retention and privacy controls.  
Vendor shall present the design to Client for review. Client may propose reasonable modifications, which Vendor shall incorporate, to the extent technically feasible, subject to schedule and fee adjustments as may be agreed in a Change Order.

2.2.3 **Platform Configuration and Integration.** Vendor shall configure its proprietary platform and related Vendor Materials to meet the requirements specified in the RAS and architecture. This includes:  
(a) setting up environments (development, testing, staging, production);  
(b) configuring workflows, dashboards, and user roles;  
(c) integrating with Client systems (e.g., ERP, CRM, identity management) using APIs or other agreed methods; and  
(d) implementing data import/export processes.  
Vendor shall conduct unit and integration testing and shall remediate material defects discovered in such testing prior to Client’s user acceptance testing (“**UAT**”).

2.2.4 **Data Migration and Validation.** Vendor shall assist with the extraction, transformation, and loading (“**ETL**”) of historical and active Client Data from designated legacy systems into the new platform. Client shall remain responsible for the accuracy and completeness of the source data, but Vendor shall:  
(a) define data mapping and transformation rules;  
(b) perform sample data loads and reconciliation; and  
(c) coordinate with Client to validate data integrity and resolve identified discrepancies.  
Vendor shall provide a Data Migration Completion Report, which shall be subject to Client review and sign-off.

2.2.5 **User Acceptance Testing.** Client shall conduct UAT upon notification from Vendor that the configuration and integrations are ready for testing. Vendor shall support UAT by:  
(a) addressing reported defects;  
(b) providing clarifications and guidance; and  
(c) preparing a consolidated defect log and remediation plan.  
Client’s written UAT acceptance shall mark the completion of the Implementation Phase for the relevant scope.

2.2.6 **Go-Live and Stabilization.** Following successful UAT, the Parties shall mutually agree on a go-live date for the production environment. Vendor shall support the go-live and provide an initial stabilization period of not less than thirty (30) calendar days, during which Vendor shall prioritize resolution of production issues and performance tuning.

2.2.7 **Managed Services and Support.** For the duration of the Term, Vendor shall provide ongoing managed services and support, including:  
(a) 24x7 monitoring of platform availability and performance;  
(b) incident and problem management in accordance with the SLA in Article XII;  
(c) deployment of patches, updates, and minor enhancements to Vendor Materials, at Vendor’s discretion;  
(d) periodic performance and usage reports; and  
(e) access to Vendor’s support desk for authorized Client users.

2.2.8 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall provide Client with reasonable training and knowledge transfer sessions, including at least:  
(a) two (2) administrator training sessions;  
(b) three (3) end-user training sessions; and  
(c) access to online documentation and user guides.  
Additional training sessions may be provided at Vendor’s then-current rates.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services and in substantial conformity with the specifications set forth in the applicable SOWs.

2.3.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the timelines, milestones, and delivery dates specified in the SOWs. However, Client acknowledges that timelines may be affected by Client’s timely provision of information, personnel availability, decisions, and approvals. Client’s failure to fulfill its obligations under Section 2.4 may result in schedule slippage, and Vendor shall not be liable for any resulting delays.

2.3.3 Vendor shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations in performing the Services, including Data Protection Laws, subject to Client’s obligations set forth in Article XI.

### 2.4 Client Responsibilities

2.4.2 Client shall be responsible for:

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the platform made available to Client is available not less than **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime (“**Uptime Commitment**”).

“**Permitted Downtime**” means:  
(a) scheduled maintenance (for which Vendor provides at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice and which occurs during off-peak hours);  
(b) emergency maintenance required to address security vulnerabilities or system instability;  
(c) downtime caused by Client’s acts or omissions; and  
(d) downtime caused by Force Majeure Events.

### 12.2 Incident Response and Resolution Times

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the following response and resolution targets for support incidents reported by Client:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Platform unavailable or severely degraded, with significant impact on Client’s operations.  
  - Initial Response: within one (1) hour.  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within four (4) hours.

- **Severity 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired, but core operations continue.  
  - Initial Response: within four (4) hours during Business Days.  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within one (1) Business Day.

- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issue, partial loss of functionality, or performance issue.  
  - Initial Response: within one (1) Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within five (5) Business Days.

- **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issue, cosmetic defect, or general inquiry.  
  - Initial Response: within two (2) Business Days.  
  - Resolution as reasonably scheduled.

### 12.3 Remedies for SLA Breaches

12.3.1 If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month (excluding Permitted Downtime), Client shall be entitled to a service credit equal to a percentage of the monthly Managed Services Fee for that month, as follows:

- Uptime ≥ 99.5% but < 99.9%: credit of 5%  
- Uptime ≥ 99.0% but < 99.5%: credit of 10%  
- Uptime < 99.0%: credit of 20%

12.3.2 Service credits shall be applied to the invoice for the month following the month in which the breach occurred, and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service credits shall not exceed twenty percent (20%) of the Managed Services Fees for any given month.

12.3.3 The foregoing service credits shall be Client’s sole and exclusive remedies for SLA breaches, except in cases of repeated or persistent failures where Client may have additional rights under Article IV.
