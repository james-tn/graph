## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services and provide the Deliverables described in this Article II and in any attached exhibits or schedules (collectively, the “Project”). The Project broadly consists of (a) assessment and design services; (b) implementation and integration services for selected applications and systems; (c) data migration and testing; (d) training and knowledge transfer; and (e) ongoing managed services and support following go-live.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Services shall be organized into the following phases, each of which may contain multiple tasks and sub-tasks:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Assessment (Estimated Duration: 6–8 Weeks).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and requirements-gathering sessions with designated Client personnel;  
  - Review the Client’s existing systems, infrastructure, processes, and related documentation;  
  - Analyze gaps between existing capabilities and desired future-state functionality;  
  - Produce an **Initial Assessment Report** detailing findings, recommendations, and identified risks; and  
  - Develop a **High-Level Solution Architecture** and **Project Plan** including timelines, milestones, resource allocations, and dependencies.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Design and Specification (Estimated Duration: 8–10 Weeks).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Develop detailed **Functional Specifications** and **Technical Specifications** for the proposed solution, including data models, integration points, workflows, and user interactions;  
  - Define and document **Security, Access Control, and Compliance Requirements** consistent with Applicable Law and the Client’s policies;  
  - Create **Interface and Integration Design Documents** describing interfaces between internal systems and any third-party platforms;  
  - Present the design to the Client for review, comment, and written approval (such approval not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed); and  
  - Update the Specifications based on Client feedback and secure formal sign-off in writing or by email acknowledgment.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Build, Configuration, and Integration (Estimated Duration: 16–20 Weeks).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Configure the agreed platforms and tools in accordance with the Specifications;  
  - Develop required custom components, scripts, or integrations necessary to meet the Client’s requirements;  
  - Integrate the solution with relevant internal systems, external partners, and third-party services;  
  - Implement security configurations, role-based access controls, audit logging, and encryption options consistent with the Specifications;  
  - Conduct unit testing and system integration testing to verify functional and technical performance; and  
  - Maintain a **Configuration Management Record** documenting all configurations, parameters, and customizations.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Data Migration and Testing (Estimated Duration: 10–12 Weeks).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Develop a **Data Migration Plan** describing source systems, data mapping rules, transformation logic, and validation criteria;  
  - Execute trial runs of data extraction, transformation, and loading (“ETL”) to test migration logic and performance;  
  - Remediate data-quality issues identified during trial runs in collaboration with the Client;  
  - Conduct **User Acceptance Testing (“UAT”)** with designated Client stakeholders, providing test scripts and expected results;  
  - Document and track defects, issues, and remediation actions; and  
  - Achieve UAT sign-off from the Client, which shall not be unreasonably withheld if the solution materially conforms to the Specifications.

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Deployment, Transition, and Go-Live (Estimated Duration: 6–8 Weeks).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Deploy the approved solution to the production environment, in accordance with the Client’s change management procedures;  
  - Execute the final data migration and cutover activities in coordination with the Client;  
  - Provide go-live support for a period of not less than thirty (30) calendar days following production deployment;  
  - Deliver a **Production Runbook**, operations manuals, and system topology documentation; and  
  - Assist in the transition of operational responsibilities to either the Client’s internal team or to the Service Provider’s Managed Services team, as applicable.

  2.2.6 **Phase 6 – Managed Services and Support (From Go-Live Through Expiration Date).**  
  Subject to Article XII (Service Level Agreements), following successful go-live, the Service Provider shall provide ongoing managed services, which may include:
  - System monitoring, incident management, and problem resolution;  
  - Routine maintenance, patching, and minor updates;  
  - Performance tuning and capacity planning recommendations;  
  - User support and helpdesk services during agreed coverage hours;  
  - Periodic reporting on system availability, incident metrics, and Service Level performance; and  
  - Coordination with third-party vendors where required for resolution of issues.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables under this Work Order shall include, without limitation:

  2.3.1 **Documentation Deliverables:**  
  - Initial Assessment Report;  
  - High-Level Solution Architecture;  
  - Detailed Functional and Technical Specifications;  
  - Interface and Integration Design Documents;  
  - Data Migration Plan;  
  - Test Plans, Test Scripts, and UAT Summary Report;  
  - Production Runbook and Operations Manuals;  
  - Training Materials and User Guides; and  
  - Monthly or quarterly service performance reports during the Managed Services phase.

  2.3.2 **Technical Deliverables:**  
  - Configured instances of the selected applications and platforms;  
  - Custom integrations, APIs, and automation scripts as defined in the Specifications;  
  - Configured monitoring, logging, and alerting set-ups;  
  - Implemented security controls and access management configurations; and  
  - Any other Work Product expressly identified in writing as a Deliverable.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client acknowledges that its timely cooperation is critical to the successful completion of the Project. The Client shall:

  - Provide access (physical and virtual) to relevant systems, environments, data, and personnel as reasonably requested by the Service Provider;  
  - Designate a project sponsor and a project manager to coordinate with the Service Provider and to provide required approvals;

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless a specific acceptance procedure is otherwise set forth in the Specifications, the following general procedure shall apply:

  2.6.4 A Deliverable shall be deemed accepted if the Client fails to respond in writing within the applicable review period, provided that the Service Provider has expressly stated in the submission notice that the Deliverable will be deemed accepted absent written comments within such period.

2.7 **Change in Scope.**  
Any changes to the scope, timing, or nature of the Services or Deliverables, including changes to the Specifications, shall be handled in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management).

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Total Contract Value.**  
The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Work Order is **US $3,933,176** (Three Million Nine Hundred Thirty-Three Thousand One Hundred Seventy-Six Dollars), subject to adjustments pursuant to approved Change Orders in accordance with Article XIII.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall be structured as follows, it being understood that the Parties may further detail the breakdown in an attached schedule:

  3.2.1 **Fixed-Fee Components.**  
  - Phases 1 through 5 (Discovery, Design, Build/Integration, Data Migration/Testing, and Deployment/Go-Live) shall be provided on a fixed-fee basis, collectively in the amount of **US $2,600,000**.  
  - Such fixed-fee amount includes project management, analysis, design, development/configuration, testing support, documentation, and training services, excluding any travel or other pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses.

  3.2.2 **Managed Services Fees.**  
  - Phase 6 (Managed Services and Support) shall be provided on a recurring fee basis, at an estimated amount of **US $90,000 per calendar quarter**, for the expected duration of the managed services term (from go-live through the Expiration Date), amounting to **US $1,260,000** assuming a fourteen (14) month post–go-live managed support period.  
  - Managed Services Fees may be adjusted based on actual consumption (e.g., ticket volumes, supported users, or environments) as mutually agreed in writing.

  3.2.3 **Contingency and Miscellaneous.**  
  - **US $73,176** is reserved as an estimated contingency amount for additional work reasonably required to address scope gaps, minor enhancements, or other Client-requested adjustments, subject to prior written approval by the Client and a documented Change Order where applicable.

3.3 **Milestone Payments.**  
The fixed-fee portion of the Fees shall be invoiced upon completion of the following milestones:

  - **Milestone 1 – Completion of Phase 1 (Discovery and Assessment):** 10% of fixed fee (US $260,000).  
  - **Milestone 2 – Completion and Approval of Detailed Specifications (Phase 2):** 20% of fixed fee (US $520,000).  
  - **Milestone 3 – Completion of Build and Integration (Phase 3) and Successful System Integration Testing:** 30% of fixed fee (US $780,000).  
  - **Milestone 4 – Completion of UAT and Data Migration (Phase 4) with Client Acceptance:** 20% of fixed fee (US $520,000).  
  - **Milestone 5 – Successful Go-Live and Completion of 30-Day Stabilization Period (Phase 5):** 20% of fixed fee (US $520,000).

Managed Services Fees for Phase 6 shall be invoiced quarterly in advance.

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

  3.4.1 **Invoicing.**  
  The Service Provider shall issue invoices upon achievement of the applicable milestone, and quarterly in advance for Managed Services. Each invoice shall reference the Contract Reference Number **WO-PHO-202504-269**, specify in reasonable detail the Services performed and/or the period for which Managed Services are billed, and include any applicable taxes.

  3.4.2 **Payment Terms – Net 60.**  
  The Client shall pay all **undisputed** amounts due under each invoice within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of the applicable valid invoice (“Net 60”). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars via wire transfer or other method mutually agreed in writing.

  3.4.3 **Disputed Amounts.**  
  If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Client shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the basis for the dispute. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute. The Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.4.2.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the date payment was due until the date payment is actually received. The Service Provider shall not suspend Services for non-payment without first providing written notice and a cure period in accordance with Section 4.3.3.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses (including travel, lodging, and meals, if applicable) incurred by the Service Provider in connection with the performance of the Services shall be reimbursed by the Client at cost, without markup, subject to the Client’s then-applicable travel and expense policy, provided that:

  - The Service Provider obtains prior written approval from the Client for any single expense above US $1,000;  
  - The Service Provider provides appropriate receipts and supporting documentation with its expense invoices; and  
  - Any dispute regarding reimbursable expenses shall be handled in accordance with Section 3.4.3.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
The Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, or similar taxes imposed by any governmental authority (other than taxes based on the Service Provider’s income). The Client shall be responsible for paying any such taxes that are properly invoiced and supported, or shall provide a valid exemption certificate.

This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the earlier of: (a) the Expiration Date of **January 22, 2027**; or (b) termination of this Work Order in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

The Parties may agree in writing to extend the Term or renew any Managed Services beyond the Expiration Date on mutually agreed terms and conditions, including updated Service Levels and Fees.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 The Client may terminate this Work Order for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider.

  4.3.2 The Service Provider may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon not less than **ninety (90) days’** prior written notice to the Client, provided that such termination shall not be effective earlier than the completion of any in-progress critical go-live or production cutover activities, unless otherwise agreed by the Client.

  4.3.3 In the event of termination for convenience by either Party, the Client shall pay the Service Provider: (a) all undisputed Fees due for Services satisfactorily performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination; and (b) any non-cancellable third-party commitments incurred with the Client’s prior written approval (to the extent they cannot reasonably be mitigated).

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Work Order for cause if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice thereof (or such longer cure period as the non-breaching Party may reasonably grant in writing, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days).

  4.4.2 **Insolvency Events.** Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (a) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they become due;  
  (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days;  
  (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or  
  (d) has a receiver appointed for its assets.

  4.4.3 **Non-Payment.** The Service Provider may, upon not less than thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of Services or terminate this Work Order for cause if the Client fails to pay any undisputed amounts when due and such failure is not cured within such thirty (30) day period.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  

  4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Work Order for any reason:  
  - The Service Provider shall cease performing Services except as necessary for an orderly wind-down;  
  - Each Party shall return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) Confidential Information of the other Party in accordance with Section 5.4; and  
  - Any licenses granted by the Client to the Service Provider shall terminate, except as necessary to perform post-termination assistance.

  4.5.2 **Payments Upon Termination.**  
  The Client shall pay the Service Provider for:  
  - All Services performed and Deliverables provided and accepted up to the effective date of termination;  
  - Any pre-approved expenses and non-cancellable commitments incurred prior to termination that cannot be mitigated through commercially reasonable efforts; and

Upon request of the Client made prior to or within thirty (30) days after the effective date of termination or expiration (other than for cause due to a material breach by the Client), the Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition services to facilitate the orderly transfer of services to the Client or to a third-party service provider, for up to ninety (90) days after termination or expiration, subject to the payment of mutually agreed, reasonable fees.
