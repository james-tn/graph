## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK  

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall provide to the Client professional services of a nature typically associated with employment, staffing, and outsourced project execution, including but not limited to:

  (a) provision of skilled personnel in the fields of technology development, business analysis, project management, customer support, operations, and related disciplines;  
  (b) management of such personnel, including recruitment, onboarding, performance oversight, and offboarding;  
  (c) delivery of specified project-based Deliverables, including documentation, software, reports, and analyses; and  
  (d) ongoing advisory and consulting services to support the Client’s internal teams.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
The specific details of the Services (including detailed descriptions of roles, duties, Deliverables, milestones, and acceptance criteria) shall be set forth in one or more SOWs executed by both Parties. In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states that it supersedes specific provisions of this Agreement.

2.3 **Nature of Engagement.**  
The Parties acknowledge that Employees / Assigned Personnel engaged under this Agreement shall remain employees, contractors, or agents of the Service Provider and shall not be deemed employees of the Client for any purpose (subject to Section 16.10). The Service Provider shall be solely responsible for payment of all wages, benefits, taxes, and other employment-related obligations with respect to such personnel.

2.4 **Specific Deliverables and Timelines.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, during the initial Term, the Service Provider shall:

  (a) Provide a core team of no fewer than eight (8) full‑time equivalent Assigned Personnel, including at least:  
   - two (2) senior software engineers;  
   - one (1) data engineer;  
   - one (1) business analyst;  
   - one (1) project manager;  
   - three (3) support/operations specialists;  

  (b) Within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, complete a discovery and planning phase, resulting in a written **Project Implementation Plan** (“PIP”) that includes:  
   - a detailed work breakdown structure;  
   - a prioritized backlog of tasks;  
   - defined Deliverables by quarter;  
   - risk analysis and mitigation plan; and  
   - staffing forecast.

  (c) Deliver on at least the following milestone-based Deliverables (to be more fully defined in the initial SOW):  
   - **Milestone 1 – Discovery & Design (by June 15, 2025):** Delivery of the PIP, system architecture documentation, and high-level process maps.  
   - **Milestone 2 – Initial Build & Pilot (by November 30, 2025):** Delivery of a working pilot solution for one business unit, including training materials and configuration documentation.  
   - **Milestone 3 – Expansion & Optimization (by May 31, 2026):** Delivery of expanded functionality covering additional business units, optimization reports, and operational runbooks.  

  (d) Provide monthly status reports summarizing:  
   - activities performed;  
   - milestone progress;  
   - issues and risks;  
   - staffing changes; and  
   - any budget or timeline variances.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall:

  (a) perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  

  (b) ensure that Assigned Personnel possess appropriate qualifications, experience, and skills for the tasks to which they are assigned;  

  (c) comply with all Applicable Laws, including but not limited to employment, anti‑discrimination, and data protection laws; and  

  (d) meet or exceed the Service Levels described in Article XII and any SOW.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall:

  (a) provide timely access to Client Materials, systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services;  

  (b) designate a primary contact person to coordinate with the Service Provider and facilitate decisions;  

  (c) respond reasonably promptly to requests for information or approvals, it being understood that delays in such responses may impact project timelines; and  

  (d) ensure that any instructions or directions given to Assigned Personnel regarding work product, priorities, or deliverables are consistent with this Agreement and Applicable Law.

2.7 **Location and Remote Work.**  
The Services may be performed onsite at the Client’s premises, at the Service Provider’s offices, or remotely, as specified in the relevant SOW. The Service Provider shall ensure that any remote work arrangements comply with the Client’s information security and confidentiality requirements.

2.8 **Subcontracting.**  
The Service Provider may not subcontract any material portion of the Services without the prior written consent of the Client, which shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed. The Service Provider shall remain fully responsible for the acts and omissions of any approved subcontractors as if they were its own.

2.9 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
For each Deliverable, the Client shall have a review and acceptance period as specified in the applicable SOW, or, if none is specified, ten (10) Business Days from delivery. If the Client reasonably determines that a Deliverable does not conform to the applicable specifications, the Client shall notify the Service Provider in writing, specifying the non‑conformities. The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such non‑conformities within a mutually agreed reasonable timeframe and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance. If the Client fails to respond within the applicable review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS  

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value for the initial Term of this Agreement is **Two Million Eight Hundred Seventy‑Five Thousand Nine Hundred Six U.S. Dollars (US$2,875,906)** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to adjustments pursuant to Change Orders and any additional SOWs.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise specified in a SOW:

  (a) Core team Services shall be billed on a time-and-materials basis with blended daily or hourly rates per role category;  

  (b) Certain Deliverables tied to milestones may be billed on a fixed-fee basis; and  

  (c) Any additional services beyond the scope defined in the applicable SOW shall be subject to prior written approval by the Client and documented in a Change Order.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  
By way of example and without limitation, the following payment milestones shall apply to the initial SOW:

  (a) **Milestone 1 Payment:** 20% of the Contract Value upon acceptance of Milestone 1 Deliverables as described in Section 2.4(c);  

  (b) **Milestone 2 Payment:** 35% of the Contract Value upon acceptance of Milestone 2 Deliverables;  

  (c) **Milestone 3 Payment:** 35% of the Contract Value upon acceptance of Milestone 3 Deliverables; and  

  (d) **Retainage:** 10% of the Contract Value, payable upon completion of all Services and Deliverables, resolution of all material issues, and submission of a final invoice.

Hourly or monthly resource-based charges, if any, shall be invoiced monthly in arrears and reconciled against the Contract Value and milestone payments as specified in the SOW.

3.4 **Invoicing.**  
The Service Provider shall issue detailed invoices to the Client specifying, as applicable:

  (a) the SOW and milestone to which the invoice relates;  
  (b) the time period covered;  
  (c) the names and roles of Assigned Personnel and hours worked (for time-and-materials);  
  (d) a description of Deliverables provided; and  
  (e) any approved reimbursable expenses.

Invoices shall be sent electronically to the Client’s designated accounts payable address or portal, as may be specified in writing.

3.5 **Payment Terms.**  
All undisputed amounts invoiced by the Service Provider shall be due and payable by the Client within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the date of receipt of a valid invoice (“**Net 30**”), unless otherwise set forth in the relevant SOW.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Client shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) calendar days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute. The Client shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.5. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any such dispute promptly.

3.7 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (i) one percent (1%) per month; or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment. The Service Provider shall not suspend provision of Services due to late payment unless: (a) such late payment has continued for more than forty‑five (45) days after written notice; and (b) the amount in question is not subject to a good‑faith dispute.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
The Fees are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value‑added, or similar taxes. The Client shall be responsible for any such taxes (excluding taxes based on the Service Provider’s income, property, or employment obligations), provided that the Service Provider properly itemizes and identifies such taxes on its invoices. The Service Provider shall be solely responsible for all employment‑related taxes for its Employees / Assigned Personnel.

3.9 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
The Client shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, such as travel and lodging. All reimbursable expenses must be supported by customary receipts or documentation. Any single expense exceeding US$1,000 shall require the Client’s prior written approval unless otherwise specified in a SOW.

3.10 **No Withholding for Set‑off (Subject to Disputes).**  
Except for amounts disputed in good faith pursuant to Section 3.6, the Client shall not withhold or set‑off any amounts owed to the Service Provider under this Agreement without the Service Provider’s prior written consent, provided that this shall not limit any rights of set‑off expressly granted in this Agreement or under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION  

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **July 10, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, extend or renew this Agreement for additional term(s) on such terms as the Parties may agree in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, by providing the other Party with not less than **forty‑five (45) days’** prior written notice. During such notice period, the Parties shall cooperate to effect an orderly wind‑down or transition of the Services.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail;  

  (b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial part of its assets; or  

  (c) engages in fraud, gross negligence, or willful misconduct in connection with the performance of this Agreement.

4.5 **Termination for Regulatory or Legal Reasons.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if continued performance would violate Applicable Law or a binding order of a governmental authority, and the Parties are unable to modify the Agreement to comply with such law or order.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) The Service Provider shall promptly cease performance of the terminated Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary to effect an orderly transition;  

  (b) The Client shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and accepted, and approved expenses incurred, up to the effective date of termination, subject to any remedy rights the Client may have for breach;  

  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information as provided in Section 5.5; and  

  (d) The Parties shall reasonably cooperate to transition the Services to the Client or its designee, including making Assigned Personnel reasonably available during the notice period or a mutually agreed transition period.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XII (as to past performance), XIV, XV, XVI, and Sections 3.7, 3.8, 4.6, and 4.7) shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

  (a) to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information and are bound by obligations of confidentiality at least as protective as those contained herein; and

5.6 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall remain in full force and effect for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret or constitutes Personal Data under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XV  
## FORCE MAJEURE  

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving a Party’s own workforce), epidemics or pandemics, government actions or restrictions, failures of utilities or telecommunications, and widespread internet outages.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and estimated duration of the Force Majeure Event;  

  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event on its performance; and  

  (c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable after the Force Majeure Event ceases.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the obligations of the affected Party under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended to the extent such obligations are affected by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Agreement upon fifteen (15) days’ written notice, without liability for such termination except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
