## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Beta shall provide to Contoso professional and managed services in support of Contoso’s technology and business operations. The Services shall include, without limitation:

  (a) assessment and analysis of Contoso’s existing systems, applications, and processes;  
  (b) design of system architectures and solution roadmaps;  
  (c) configuration, customization, and implementation of designated software platforms;  
  (d) integration of selected systems and data sources;  
  (e) testing, quality assurance, and validation of implemented solutions;  
  (f) training and knowledge transfer to Contoso personnel; and  
  (g) ongoing support, maintenance, and optimization services, as further detailed herein.

2.2 **Initial Scope and Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the initial scope of the Agreement shall encompass the following Deliverables and related Services:

  (a) **Discovery and Requirements Documentation.** Beta shall conduct discovery workshops with key Contoso stakeholders to capture and document functional and technical requirements. Deliverables shall include:  
  - a current-state assessment report;  
  - a detailed requirements specification; and  
  - a gap analysis document identifying discrepancies between current capabilities and desired future state.

  (b) **Solution Design and Architecture.** Based on the approved requirements, Beta shall produce:  
  - a solution architecture document outlining system components, interfaces, data flows, and security controls;  
  - configuration and customization design documents; and  
  - a data migration and integration design, including mapping of source and target data fields.

  (c) **Implementation and Configuration.** Beta shall configure and, where applicable, customize the identified software platforms in accordance with the approved design. Deliverables shall include:  
  - configured application instances aligned with Contoso’s requirements;  
  - integration scripts, APIs, connectors, or middleware configurations; and  
  - user profiles, roles, and permissions consistent with Contoso’s security policies.

  (d) **Testing and Quality Assurance.** Beta shall prepare and execute test plans and cases to validate that the implemented solution meets the agreed requirements. Deliverables shall include:  
  - a master test plan;  
  - test cases, test scripts, and test data;  
  - test execution reports and defect logs; and  
  - a final testing summary and sign-off recommendations.

  (e) **Training and Documentation.** Beta shall train designated Contoso personnel on the use, administration, and support of the implemented solution. Deliverables shall include:  
  - user guides and administrator manuals;  
  - training materials (slides, reference sheets, or e-learning content);  
  - training session agendas and attendance records; and  
  - post-training Q&A documentation.

  (f) **Support and Stabilization.** Following go-live, Beta shall provide stabilization support for a mutually agreed period, addressing defects, minor enhancements, and optimization recommendations in accordance with the Service Levels in Article XII.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Beta shall perform the Services and provide the Deliverables:

  (a) in a timely, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry best practices and standards applicable to similar services;  
  (b) by personnel who are appropriately qualified, trained, and experienced;  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Law and Contoso’s reasonable written policies and standards provided in advance; and  
  (d) in accordance with any Project Plan, SOW, or schedule agreed by the Parties.

Beta acknowledges that time is of the essence with respect to the key milestones and delivery dates. Any anticipated delay shall be promptly notified to Contoso, with a proposed mitigation or recovery plan for Contoso’s review and approval.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall agree upon specific timelines and milestones in one or more SOWs or Project Plans. Such documents shall include:

  (a) **Phase-based Milestones**, such as completion of discovery, completion of design, completion of configuration, completion of testing, go-live date, and completion of stabilization;  
  (b) **Acceptance Criteria** for each Deliverable or phase, including objective measures for Contoso’s review; and  
  (c) **Dependencies**, including Contoso’s obligations to provide data, access to systems, subject matter experts, and other resources necessary for Beta to perform the Services.

2.5 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  (a) Upon delivery of each Deliverable, Beta shall provide written notice to Contoso. Contoso shall have a period of **ten (10) Business Days** (or such other period as set forth in the applicable SOW) from receipt of the Deliverable to review and either:  
   - (i) provide written acceptance; or  
   - (ii) provide written notice of rejection, specifying any material non-conformities with the applicable requirements.

  (b) If Contoso fails to provide either acceptance or rejection within the acceptance period, the Deliverable shall not be deemed automatically accepted; rather, Beta shall send a reminder, and Contoso shall respond within five (5) Business Days. The Parties expressly agree that no Deliverable shall be deemed accepted by silence.

  (c) In the event of rejection, Beta shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, promptly correct the identified non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance in accordance with this Section 2.5.

2.6 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso shall:

  (a) provide Beta with reasonable access to relevant personnel, facilities, systems, and data required for Beta to perform the Services;  
  (b) designate a project manager or primary contact with decision-making authority or access thereto;  
  (c) review Deliverables in a timely manner and provide prompt feedback to avoid undue delays; and  
  (d) fulfill any other responsibilities set forth in the applicable SOW or Project Plan.

2.7 **Subcontractors.**

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees and Contract Value.**  
In consideration for the Services and Deliverables provided by Beta under this Agreement, Contoso agrees to pay Beta Fees not to exceed the **Contract Value of US$131,090**, unless increased by mutual written agreement under Article XIII. 3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in a specific SOW, the Fees shall be structured as follows:

  (a) **Fixed Implementation Fee.** A fixed fee for core implementation and configuration services, representing the majority of the Contract Value.  
  (b) **Time and Materials (T&M).** A T&M component for optional or additional services beyond the defined scope, billed at agreed hourly or daily rates.  
  (c) **Support and Maintenance Fee.** A recurring monthly fee, if applicable, for post-go-live support services, as specified in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise provided in a SOW, the Contract Value shall be invoiced as follows:

  (a) **Twenty percent (20%)** upon execution of this Agreement and issuance of the initial SOW;  
  (b) **Thirty percent (30%)** upon completion and acceptance of the Design and Architecture Deliverables;  
  (c) **Thirty percent (30%)** upon completion and acceptance of the Implementation and Testing Deliverables; and  
  (d) **Twenty percent (20%)** upon final acceptance and successful go-live of the solution.

3.4 **Invoicing.**  
Beta shall issue invoices to Contoso in accordance with the payment milestones and any recurring charges described in the applicable SOW. Each invoice shall:

  (a) reference this Agreement number **AGR-BET-202408-526**;  
  (b) identify the relevant SOW and milestone or period;  
  (c) itemize the Services and Deliverables billed; and  
  (d) include any required supporting documentation for T&M or reimbursable expenses.

3.5 **Payment Terms; Net 45.**  
Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of receipt of a valid invoice (“Net 45”). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by electronic funds transfer or such other method as mutually agreed.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Contoso disputes any amount on an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall notify Beta in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature and basis of the dispute. Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts in accordance with Section 3.5, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Any adjustment shall be reflected in a subsequent invoice or credit memo.

3.7 **Late Payments.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of:

  (a) one percent (1%) per month; or  
  (b) the maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law,

from the due date until the date of actual payment. Beta’s right to interest shall be without prejudice to any other rights or remedies under this Agreement.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Beta in connection with the performance of the Services (including travel, lodging, and meals where on-site work is required) shall be reimbursed by Contoso at cost, provided that:

  (a) such expenses are approved in writing in advance by Contoso;  
  (b) Beta submits appropriate receipts or supporting documentation; and  
  (c) such expenses comply with Contoso’s travel and expense policy, as provided to Beta in writing.

3.9 **Taxes.**  
The Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all such taxes, except for taxes based on Beta’s income, property, or employment-related obligations. Beta shall itemize taxes on invoices where applicable and cooperate with Contoso in providing tax documentation or certificates reasonably requested by Contoso.

3.10 **No Withholding of Services.**  
Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement, Beta shall not suspend or withhold the performance of Services or delivery of Deliverables solely due to any good faith dispute regarding invoiced amounts that Contoso has properly contested under Section 3.6.

4.1 **Term.**  
The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until **November 27, 2025** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

Beta may terminate for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso. In the event of termination for convenience:

  (a) Contoso shall pay Beta for Services satisfactorily performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, plus any non-cancellable commitments incurred with Contoso’s prior written approval; and  
  (b) Beta shall use commercially reasonable efforts to wind down the Services and provide reasonable transition assistance as requested by Contoso on a time-and-materials basis.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy (which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days), makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or otherwise ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Termination for Persistent SLA Failures.**  
In addition to the rights under Section 4.4, if Beta materially and repeatedly fails to meet the Service Levels (as defined in Article XII) in three (3) or more consecutive calendar months, Contoso may, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice and failure by Beta to implement an acceptable remediation plan, terminate the Agreement or the affected Services for cause.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) all rights and licenses granted hereunder shall cease, except as expressly provided otherwise in Article VI;  
  (b) Beta shall promptly cease performing the Services, except as necessary to effectuate an orderly transition if requested by Contoso;  
  (c) each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5; and  
  (d) Contoso shall pay Beta any undisputed Fees due and payable for Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Any provisions of this Agreement which, by their nature, should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI, and Sections 3.7, 3.9, 4.6, and 4.7.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Availability.**  
Where Beta provides hosted or managed services under this Agreement, Beta shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment for such services is available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance periods and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Beta may perform scheduled maintenance on the systems supporting the Services during windows agreed by the Parties, typically outside of normal business hours. Beta shall provide Contoso with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of scheduled maintenance that may impact availability.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution.**  

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of service or mission-critical impact. Beta shall respond within one (1) hour and shall work continuously until service is restored or a workaround is provided.  
  (b) **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation of service with major business impact. Beta shall respond within four (4) hours and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within one (1) Business Day.  
  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Partial degradation with limited business impact. Beta shall respond within one (1) Business Day and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve within five (5) Business Days.  
  (d) **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues or requests. Beta shall respond within two (2) Business Days and address in the normal course of maintenance.

12.4 **SLA Credits.**  
If Beta fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 for any calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits calculated as a percentage of the monthly Fees for the affected service, as follows:

  (a) Availability between 99.0% and 99.9% – credit of 5%;  
  (b) Availability between 98.0% and 99.0% – credit of 10%;  
  (c) Availability below 98.0% – credit of 15%.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute Contoso’s sole monetary remedy for SLA breaches, without prejudice to Contoso’s right to terminate for persistent SLA failures under Section 4.5.

## ARTICLE XV  
## FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party that prevents or delays performance of its obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes, labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), epidemics, pandemics, governmental actions, or widespread network or utility failures.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event, including a description of the event and its expected duration;  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably possible; and  
  (c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of the status of the Force Majeure Event and the efforts to resume performance.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended to the extent and for the period that such performance is prevented or materially hindered by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement or the affected Services upon written notice, without liability other than payment for Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination.
