## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Project.**  
The Service Provider shall design, implement, configure, integrate, and provide ongoing support and managed services for a cloud-based enterprise analytics and operations optimization platform (the **“Platform”**) for the Company. The Platform will consolidate data from multiple source systems, including but not limited to ERP, CRM, HRIS, and supply chain systems, and provide advanced analytics, dashboards, and process automation capabilities to support the Company’s operational, financial, and strategic decision-making.

2.2 **Phased Implementation.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the Project will be executed in multiple phases, which may overlap, and which are generally described as follows:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Estimated Duration: 3 months).**  
  (a) Conduct stakeholder interviews and workshops with the Company’s designated business and IT representatives.  
  (b) Document current-state architecture, data sources, data quality issues, operational processes, reporting requirements, and regulatory constraints.  
  (c) Produce a comprehensive **Requirements Specification Document** describing functional, technical, data, security, and compliance requirements for the Platform.  
  (d) Develop a **Solution Architecture Blueprint**, including data model, integration design, hosting architecture, access control model, and high-level disaster recovery approach.  
  (e) Prepare a **Project Plan** with detailed tasks, dependencies, resource allocations, and Milestones.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Design and Build (Estimated Duration: 9–12 months).**  
  (a) Design and configure the core data ingestion and ETL (extract, transform, load) processes to integrate data from at least five (5) primary source systems identified by the Company.  
  (b) Build a unified data warehouse or data lake environment, including schema, indexing strategy, and data governance rules.  
  (c) Develop a set of core analytics dashboards and reports covering financial performance, sales pipeline, supply chain KPIs, headcount and HR metrics, and operational efficiency metrics.  
  (d) Configure user roles, permissions, and SSO integration with the Company’s existing identity management solution, as reasonably specified by the Company.  
  (e) Implement data validation, reconciliations, and automated quality checks according to thresholds agreed between the Parties.  
  (f) Deliver **Technical Design Documents**, data dictionaries, integration specifications, and configuration guides.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Testing, Training, and Deployment (Estimated Duration: 4–6 months).**  
  (a) Develop and execute test plans covering unit, system, integration, performance, and user acceptance testing (**“UAT”**).  
  (b) Coordinate with the Company’s team to define test cases and acceptance criteria, which shall be reasonably aligned with the Requirements Specification Document.  
  (c) Remediate defects identified during testing in accordance with the severity levels and timelines set forth in Article XII.  
  (d) Conduct end-user and administrator training sessions, provide training materials, and create a knowledge base for ongoing support.  
  (e) Plan and execute production cutover, including data migration, final validations, and go-live readiness checks.  
  (f) Provide hypercare support during the initial ninety (90) days following go-live.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Managed Services and Continuous Improvement (From Go-Live through Expiration Date).**  
  (a) Provide ongoing monitoring, incident management, and problem resolution for the Platform in accordance with the SLAs set forth in Article XII.  
  (b) Perform routine maintenance, patching, upgrades, and performance tuning of the Platform components under the Service Provider’s control.  
  (c) Implement minor enhancements, reports, and configuration changes requested by the Company through the Change Management process in Article XIII.  
  (d) Provide quarterly business reviews, system health assessments, and recommendations for optimization and new capabilities.  
  (e) Support audit, compliance, and security review activities reasonably requested by the Company, subject to Article XI.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Service Provider shall provide at least the following Deliverables to the Company, in both electronic and (where requested) hardcopy format:

  (a) **Requirements Specification Document** (Phase 1).  
  (b) **Solution Architecture Blueprint** (Phase 1).  
  (c) **Project Plan and Milestone Schedule** (Phase 1).  
  (d) **Technical Design Documents and Integration Specifications** (Phase 2).  
  (e) **Data Model and Data Dictionary** (Phase 2).  
  (f) **Configured and Deployed Platform**, including integrations and dashboards (Phase 2–3).  
  (g) **Test Plans, Test Cases, and Test Results Reports** (Phase 3).  
  (h) **Training Materials and Knowledge Base Content** (Phase 3).  
  (i) **Operations Runbooks, Support Procedures, and Escalation Paths** (Phase 4).  
  (j) **Quarterly Health and Optimization Reports** (Phase 4).  

Additional Deliverables may be defined under Change Orders pursuant to Article XIII.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and training;  
  (b) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  (c) in material compliance with the Project Plan, Milestones, and SLAs;  
  (d) in compliance with Applicable Law, including Data Protection Laws; and  
  (e) in a manner that does not intentionally introduce any malicious code, backdoors, or security vulnerabilities into the Platform.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to Client Materials, source systems, and subject-matter experts reasonably necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services.  
  (b) Review and provide feedback on Deliverables within reasonable timeframes specified in the Project Plan; failure to do so may result in schedule impacts reflected via Change Order.  
  (c) Designate a primary project sponsor and project manager to coordinate with the Service Provider’s project manager.  
  (d) Ensure that the use of the Platform by the Company’s end users complies with Applicable Law and the Company’s internal policies.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise agreed, the Company shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery of a Deliverable to review and either:

  (a) provide written acceptance; or  
  (b) provide a written notice of material non-conformity with the applicable specifications.

If the Company does not provide such notice within the review period, the applicable Deliverable shall be deemed accepted. Upon notice of non-conformity, the Service Provider shall promptly correct and resubmit the Deliverable for re-testing and acceptance. This process may be repeated until acceptance is obtained, provided that repeated failure to achieve acceptance may constitute a material breach pursuant to Section 4.3.

  (b) **Milestone 2 – Core Data Integration and Data Model Complete**

  - Amount: **US $95,000**.

  (d) **Milestone 4 – Go-Live and Hypercare Commencement**  
  - Trigger: Successful go-live of the Platform and commencement of the ninety (90) day hypercare period.  
  - Amount: **US $63,268**.

Following go-live, the Company shall pay a monthly managed services fee of **US $6,250** for thirty-two (32) consecutive months (US $200,000) and thereafter **US $5,000** per month for the remaining ten (10) months (US $50,000), totaling US $250,000 across the Term for Phase 4, subject to any adjustments via Change Order.

All undisputed amounts invoiced under this SOW shall be due and payable within **thirty (30) days** from the date of the applicable invoice (**“Net 30”**), in U.S. dollars, by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method. Any disputed amounts shall be identified by the Company in writing within fifteen (15) days of invoice receipt, with a reasonably detailed description of the dispute. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve any invoice disputes promptly, and the Company shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with this Section 3.5.

Any undisputed invoice amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one percent (1.0%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, calculated from the date due until the date paid. The Service Provider may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the Company and failure by the Company to cure such non-payment within such period, suspend the performance of Services, provided that such suspension shall not relieve the Company of its payment obligations.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

Either Party may terminate this SOW for cause upon written notice to the other Party if:

  (a) the other Party materially breaches any provision of this SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (ten (10) days for non-payment) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course, and such condition is not remedied within thirty (30) days.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider. Upon such termination, the Company shall pay the Service Provider: (a) all fees earned for Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination; and (b) any non-cancellable commitments (including minimum commitments to third parties) reasonably incurred for the Project prior to the notice of termination.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this SOW:

  (a) The Service Provider shall promptly cease performing Services, except for any wind-down or transition services requested by the Company at mutually agreed fees.  
  (b) The Company shall pay all amounts due and owing for Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to Section 3.5.  
  (c) Each Party shall promptly return or, at the disclosing Party’s election, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information, subject to any backup and archival obligations, in accordance with Section 5.4 and Article XI.  
  (d) The Service Provider shall provide to the Company, upon request, a copy of all Work Product and Deliverables in its possession, in a reasonable format, and reasonable assistance in transitioning services to the Company or a third-party provider, at the Service Provider’s then-current time and materials rates, unless termination is due to the Service Provider’s uncured material breach, in which case such transition assistance shall be provided at no additional charge.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this SOW that by their nature should survive expiration or termination, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and Section 4.5, shall survive any expiration or termination of this SOW.

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Availability.**  
For managed services under Phase 4, the Service Provider shall ensure that the Platform components within the Service Provider’s control are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows agreed upon with the Company.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall meet the following response and resolution targets for support incidents:

- **Severity 1 (Critical – system down):**  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours
- **Severity 2 (High – major functionality impaired):**  
  - Initial Response: within 4 hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 24 hours
- **Severity 3 (Medium – minor impact / workaround available):**  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution: within 5 Business Days
- **Severity 4 (Low – informational / cosmetic):**  
  - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
  - Target Resolution: next planned release or as mutually agreed

12.3 **Measurement and Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall:

  (a) continuously monitor the Platform’s availability and performance;  
  (b) maintain records sufficient to verify compliance with SLAs; and  
  (c) provide monthly SLA compliance reports to the Company, including uptime, incident counts by severity, and mean time to resolve incidents.

12.4 **SLA Remedies.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the availability SLA in Section 12.1 in any calendar month, the Company shall be entitled to a service credit equal to five percent (5%) of the monthly managed services fee for each one-tenth of one percent (0.1%) below the 99.9% uptime commitment, up to a maximum of fifty percent (50%) of the monthly managed services fee for that month. Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice. Service credits are the Company’s primary remedy for SLA failures but shall not limit the Company’s rights under other provisions of this SOW, including Article IX.
