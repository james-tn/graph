## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.** The Vendor shall provide to the Client a comprehensive suite of technology implementation, integration, and managed support services relating to the Client’s enterprise resource planning (“ERP”) and customer relationship management (“CRM”) environments, together with associated data migration, testing, training, and ongoing Level 1–3 support services (collectively, the “Services”). The Services are intended to modernize the Client’s existing systems, improve data integrity, and enhance operational efficiency across the Client’s key business functions.

2.2 **Project Phases.** The Parties agree that the Services will be organized into the following main phases, each of which may include sub-phases or workstreams as described in the detailed project plan to be agreed between the Parties within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements Gathering.**  
  (a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and discovery sessions with designated Client personnel to identify current business processes, pain points, and objectives.  
  (b) Review existing system architecture, integrations, data model, and infrastructure.  
  (c) Document functional and technical requirements, including regulatory and audit requirements applicable to the Client’s operations.  
  (d) Produce a detailed **Requirements Specification Document** and a high-level solution architecture for Client review and approval.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Solution Design and Architecture.**  
  (a) Design a target solution architecture for the ERP and CRM systems, including integration points with existing Client systems such as HR, finance, and data warehouse platforms.  
  (b) Define data migration strategy, including mapping from legacy systems and data cleansing protocols.  
  (c) Produce a **Solution Design Document**, integration design specifications, and data migration approach for Client approval.  
  (d) Identify any required Third-Party Materials and obtain Client approval for their use.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Implementation, Configuration, and Integration.**  
  (a) Configure the agreed ERP and CRM platforms in accordance with the Solution Design Document and Specifications.  
  (b) Develop and implement system integrations using secure APIs, middleware, or other integration technologies.  
  (c) Execute data migration in iterative batches, including validation and reconciliation processes.  
  (d) Implement necessary security controls, user roles, and access management consistent with the Client’s information security policies.  
  (e) Prepare environment build documentation and configuration runbooks.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Testing and User Acceptance.**  
  (a) Develop test plans and test cases covering unit, system, integration, performance, and security testing.  
  (b) Execute testing and log all defects in a mutually agreed defect tracking tool.  
  (c) Support Client-led user acceptance testing (“UAT”) including defect remediation and regression testing.  
  (d) Provide test reports and quality assurance sign-off documentation to the Client.

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
  (a) Develop training materials, including user guides, quick reference guides, and e-learning modules, tailored to different user groups.  
  (b) Conduct live training sessions, workshops, and train-the-trainer programmes for Client personnel.  
  (c) Deliver technical knowledge transfer sessions to the Client’s IT and operations teams covering architecture, configuration, and support processes.  
  (d) Provide a knowledge base or FAQ documentation to support ongoing operations.

  2.2.6 **Phase 6 – Go-Live and Stabilisation.**  
  (a) Assist in cutover planning and execution, including go/no-go criteria and rollback procedures.  
  (b) Provide hypercare support for a minimum period of ninety (90) days following the first production go-live.  
  (c) Monitor system performance and resolve high-priority defects in collaboration with the Client.  
  (d) Provide a post-implementation review report, including lessons learned and recommended optimisations.

  2.2.7 **Phase 7 – Ongoing Support and Maintenance.**  
  (a) Provide managed support services including incident management, problem management, and minor enhancements, in accordance with the Service Levels in Article XII.  
  (b) Maintain a ticketing system for logging, tracking, and resolving incidents and service requests.  
  (c) Provide monthly service reports detailing ticket volumes, response/resolution times, system availability, and root cause analyses.  
  (d) Participate in monthly service review meetings with the Client.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Vendor shall produce and deliver to the Client the following Deliverables (each of which shall be subject to Client review and acceptance under Section 2.7):

  2.3.1 Requirements Specification Document;  
  2.3.2 Solution Design Document;  
  2.3.3 Integration Design Specifications;  
  2.3.4 Data Migration Strategy and Mapping Documents;  
  2.3.5 Configuration Documentation and Runbooks;  
  2.3.6 Test Plans, Test Cases, and Test Reports;  
  2.3.7 Training Materials and E-Learning Content;  
  2.3.8 Knowledge Base / FAQ Documentation;  
  2.3.9 Go-Live Cutover Plan;  
  2.3.10 Post-Implementation Review Report;  
  2.3.11 Monthly Service and Performance Reports.

2.4 **Project Timeline and Milestones.** The Parties acknowledge that timely completion of the Services is of critical importance. The Vendor shall perform the Services in accordance with the high-level timeline below, subject to change pursuant to Article XIII:

  - **Milestone 1:** Completion of Phase 1 and delivery of Requirements Specification Document – within sixty (60) days of the Effective Date.  
  - **Milestone 2:** Completion of Phase 2 and delivery of Solution Design Document – within one hundred twenty (120) days of the Effective Date.  
  - **Milestone 3:** Completion of Phase 3 (core configuration and integrations) – within two hundred forty (240) days of the Effective Date.  
  - **Milestone 4:** Completion of Phase 4 (testing and UAT support) – within three hundred (300) days of the Effective Date.  
  - **Milestone 5:** Initial Go-Live and completion of Phase 6 stabilisation – within three hundred sixty-five (365) days of the Effective Date.  
  - **Milestone 6:** Ongoing Support and Maintenance – from initial Go-Live through the Expiration Date.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** The Vendor shall perform the Services:

  2.5.1 With due skill, care, and diligence, in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  2.5.2 In compliance with all Applicable Laws and the Client’s reasonable written policies provided to the Vendor;

  4.4.1 The other Party commits a material breach of this SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or

  4.6.1 The Vendor shall cease all Services, except as necessary to provide any agreed transition assistance under Section 4.7;  

  4.6.2 The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services duly performed and accepted and for approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to the Contract Value cap;

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.** For the managed support Services and any hosted components managed by the Vendor, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve **monthly service availability of at least 99.9%** during each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** The Vendor may schedule maintenance windows that may affect service availability, provided that:

  12.2.1 Routine maintenance shall be conducted outside of normal UK business hours where reasonably practicable; and  

  12.2.2 The Vendor shall provide the Client with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of any Scheduled Maintenance expected to impact service availability.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.** The Vendor shall respond to and aim to resolve incidents based on their severity as follows:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of core system functionality impacting a material portion of users with no workaround.  
    - Response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
    - Target Resolution: within 8 hours  

  - **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of functionality or major feature not working with no immediate workaround.  
    - Response: within 2 Business Hours  
    - Target Resolution: within 1 Business Day  

  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial loss of functionality with a reasonable workaround available.  
    - Response: within 4 Business Hours  
    - Target Resolution: within 3 Business Days  

  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general enquiries.  
    - Response: within 1 Business Day  
    - Target Resolution: within 10 Business Days

12.4 **Service Credits.** If the Vendor fails to meet the service availability commitment in Section 12.1 in any calendar month (excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure Events), the Client shall, upon written request, be entitled to service credits as follows:

  12.4.1 Availability between 99.0% and 99.89%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly support Fees;  

  12.4.2 Availability between 98.0% and 98.99%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly support Fees;  

  12.4.3 Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly support Fees.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute the Client’s sole monetary remedy for failure to meet the availability commitment, without prejudice to the Client’s right to terminate for persistent SLA breaches.

12.5 **Chronic SLA Failures.** If the Vendor fails to meet the service availability commitment in three (3) or more months in any rolling twelve (12) month period, the Client shall have the right to terminate the ongoing support Services upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice without penalty, in addition to receiving any accrued service credits.
