## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Project**

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide professional services to design, configure, integrate, and support an enterprise CRM solution for Client, including associated business process optimisation and user enablement. The Project is intended to support Client’s global sales, marketing, and customer support operations, and shall be delivered in phases as set forth in this Article II.

2.1.2 The Services will be provided on a combination of fixed-fee and time-and-materials basis, as described in Article III, and will be performed primarily remotely, with on-site work at Client’s offices in London and other locations as agreed in writing.

2.2 **Project Phases**

Vendor shall perform the following phased activities, which shall constitute the Services under this SOW:

**(a) Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Approx. 8 Weeks)**  
i. Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and process walkthroughs with Client’s designated business and IT representatives.  
ii. Document current-state processes for sales lead management, opportunity management, account management, case management, and marketing campaigns.  
iii. Elicit and document detailed business, functional, and technical requirements for the CRM solution, including integration and reporting needs.  
iv. Identify data sources, existing systems (including ERP, marketing automation, and support ticketing), and any dependencies or constraints.  
v. Produce the following Deliverables:  
- Requirements Specification Document  
- Current-State Process Maps  
- High-Level Data Inventory  
- Initial Risk and Issues Log  

**(b) Phase 2 – Solution Design (Approx. 6 Weeks)**  
i. Develop a target-state solution architecture aligned with Client’s technology standards.  
ii. Define entity model, data structures, user roles, and permissions.  
iii. Design key workflows, automations, and integration flows.  
iv. Develop a data migration strategy, including data cleansing and mapping rules.  
v. Produce the following Deliverables:  
- Solution Design Document  
- Integration Design (including interface specifications)  
- Data Migration Plan and Mapping Specifications  
- Test Strategy and High-Level Test Plan  

**(c) Phase 3 – Configuration and Build (Approx. 16 Weeks)**  
i. Configure the selected CRM platform (to be specified by Client) in accordance with the Solution Design Document.  
ii. Develop custom workflows, automation rules, and configuration of forms, dashboards, and reports.  
iii. Build and configure interfaces with agreed third‑party systems (including, where applicable, ERP, marketing automation tools, and support ticketing systems).  
iv. Implement agreed security roles, permission sets, and authentication mechanisms.  
v. Produce the following Deliverables:  
- Configured CRM Environment (Dev, Test, and UAT instances)  
- Custom Workflow and Automation Scripts  
- Integration Components and Connectors  
- Technical Configuration Documentation  

**(d) Phase 4 – Testing and Deployment (Approx. 10 Weeks)**  
i. Develop detailed test cases and scripts for system, integration, and user acceptance tests.  
ii. Support Client-led testing, including triage and remediation of defects.  
iii. Execute technical deployment into production environment in coordination with Client’s IT team.  
iv. Provide go-live support during the initial launch period (no less than 4 weeks).  
v. Produce the following Deliverables:  
- Detailed Test Cases and Test Results Report  
- Deployment Runbook and Rollback Plan  
- Production Environment Configuration Record  
- Go-Live Support Summary and Lessons Learned  

**(e) Phase 5 – Knowledge Transfer and Training (Approx. 4 Weeks)**  
i. Prepare and deliver training to Client’s key user groups (sales, marketing, support, and system admins).  
ii. Provide administrator-level documentation and handover sessions to Client’s internal IT support team.  
iii. Produce the following Deliverables:  
- User Training Materials (slides, guides, and reference cards)  
- Administrator Handbook  
- Knowledge Transfer Workshops (minimum of four (4) sessions)  

**(f) Phase 6 – Managed Support (Remainder of Term)**  
i. Provide second-line and third-line CRM support services during Business Hours, as per the SLAs defined in Article XII.  
ii. Perform minor configuration changes, user management, and routine maintenance within an agreed allocation of hours per month.  
iii. Provide periodic health checks, performance reviews, and recommendations for optimisations.  
iv. Produce the following Deliverables:  
- Monthly Support and Incident Reports  
- Quarterly Service Review Reports  
- Updated Configuration Documentation as changes are implemented  

2.3 **Timelines and Milestones**

2.3.1 The high-level timeline for the Project is as follows (subject to adjustment via Change Orders):

- Phase 1: Discovery and Requirements – Weeks 1–8 from Effective Date  
- Phase 2: Solution Design – Weeks 9–14  
- Phase 3: Configuration and Build – Weeks 15–30  
- Phase 4: Testing and Deployment – Weeks 31–40  
- Phase 5: Knowledge Transfer and Training – Weeks 41–44  
- Phase 6: Managed Support – From week 45 until Expiration Date, unless terminated earlier.

2.3.2 The Parties shall agree in writing on detailed Milestones and associated dates in a project plan to be completed within ten (10) Business Days following the Effective Date. Such project plan shall be incorporated by reference into this SOW and may be adjusted in accordance with Article XIII.

Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Client fulfilling its responsibilities in a timely manner. Client shall:

(a) Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days after receiving written notice thereof (or, if the breach is not capable of cure within such period, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period); or  
(b) Becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into liquidation (other than for a solvent amalgamation or reorganization), or has a receiver, administrator, or similar officer appointed over its assets.

4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:

(a) Vendor shall cease performing the Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary to effect an orderly wind-down;

12.2.1 During Business Hours, Vendor shall acknowledge and respond to support requests in accordance with the following target response times:

- Priority 1 (Critical – system down or major business impact):  
  - Initial response: 1 hour  
  - Workaround or resolution target: 8 hours  

- Priority 2 (High – significant functionality impaired):  
  - Initial response: 4 hours  
  - Workaround or resolution target: 2 Business Days  

- Priority 3 (Medium – non-critical issues, limited impact):  
  - Initial response: 1 Business Day  
  - Resolution target: 5 Business Days  

- Priority 4 (Low – minor issues, general queries):  
  - Initial response: 2 Business Days  
  - Resolution target: As scheduled in next maintenance cycle.

12.3.1 If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in any calendar month due solely to Vendor’s causes, Client shall be entitled to a service credit, calculated as a percentage of the monthly managed support Fees, as follows:

- Availability 98.0% to 98.99%: 10% service credit  
- Availability below 98.0%: 15% service credit  

12.3.2 Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches, without prejudice to Client’s other rights in the event of persistent or material SLA failures.
