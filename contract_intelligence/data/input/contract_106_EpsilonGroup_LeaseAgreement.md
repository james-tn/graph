## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF LEASE AND SERVICES

2.1 **Demise of Premises.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Epsilon hereby leases to Contoso, and Contoso hereby leases from Epsilon, the Premises, together with the non-exclusive right to use the building’s common areas (the “Common Areas”), including lobbies, hallways, elevators, stairways, restrooms, and parking areas, as designated by Epsilon, for the Term and for the permitted use set forth in **Section 2.3**.

2.2 **Permitted Use.** The Premises shall be used solely for general administrative offices, corporate operations, professional services, and related lawful business activities consistent with a first-class office environment (the “Permitted Use”). Contoso shall not use the Premises for any purpose that violates Applicable Law, constitutes a nuisance, or is inconsistent with the building’s character or any reasonable written rules and regulations promulgated by Epsilon from time to time.

2.3 **Condition of Premises; Landlord’s Work and Tenant’s Work.**

2.3.1 **As-Is Condition.** Except as expressly set forth in this Agreement or any attached work letter, Contoso accepts the Premises in its “AS-IS, WHERE-IS” condition as of the Commencement Date, subject to Epsilon’s obligation, if any, to complete certain initial improvements (“Landlord’s Work”) set forth in **Exhibit B** (if attached).

2.3.2 **Landlord’s Work.** Epsilon shall perform Landlord’s Work, if any, in a good and workmanlike manner, in compliance with Applicable Law. Substantial completion of Landlord’s Work shall be a condition to the Commencement Date, as further described in **Section 2.4**.

2.3.3 **Tenant’s Work.** Any additional interior improvements, cabling, signage, or alterations desired by Contoso (“Tenant’s Work”) shall be at Contoso’s sole cost and expense, subject to Epsilon’s prior written approval not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed, and in accordance with **Section 2.9**.

2.4 **Commencement Date and Possession.** The Commencement Date shall be the Effective Date, provided that if Landlord’s Work (if any) is not substantially complete as of the Effective Date, then the Commencement Date shall be postponed to the date Epsilon delivers the Premises with Landlord’s Work substantially complete and reasonably suitable for occupancy for the Permitted Use. Epsilon shall notify Contoso in writing of the Commencement Date. Delay in completion caused by Contoso’s acts or omissions, including changes to Landlord’s Work, shall not postpone the Commencement Date.

2.5 **Services to be Provided by Epsilon.** During the Term, Epsilon shall provide or cause to be provided the following Services, subject to **Article XII** (SLAs):

2.5.1 **Building Operations and Maintenance.** Epsilon shall operate, maintain, and repair the building structure, roof, foundation, load-bearing walls, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and HVAC systems serving the Premises (other than systems exclusively serving Contoso’s equipment) in a manner consistent with comparable commercial office buildings in the area.

2.5.2 **Common Area Maintenance.** Epsilon shall maintain the Common Areas in a clean, orderly, and safe condition, including lighting, landscaping, parking areas, sidewalks, entrances, elevators, and corridors.

2.5.3 **Janitorial Services.** Epsilon shall provide routine janitorial services to the Premises on Business Days, including trash removal, vacuuming, and restroom cleaning, consistent with prevailing standards in similar buildings. Any specialized cleaning requested by Contoso shall be at Contoso’s expense.

2.5.4 **Security Services.** Epsilon shall provide building access control (e.g., key cards, access badges), and may provide on-site or remote monitoring services, consistent with a medium-risk office environment, but does not guarantee against criminal acts of third parties. Contoso shall cooperate with all security protocols and is responsible for its own internal security.

2.5.5 **Utilities.** Epsilon shall provide electricity, water, and HVAC service to the Premises in sufficient capacity for typical office use during standard business hours (e.g., 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. on Business Days). After-hours HVAC or above-standard usage shall be subject to additional charges as set forth in **Article III**.

2.6 **Contoso’s Obligations Regarding the Premises.** Contoso shall:

(a) Keep the Premises in a neat, clean, and safe condition;  
(b) Promptly notify Epsilon of any damage, defect, or needed repair in the Premises or building systems;  
(c) Be responsible for repair or replacement of any damage caused by Contoso’s employees, contractors, or visitors, normal wear and tear excepted;  
(d) Comply with all Applicable Laws relating to its use and occupancy of the Premises; and  
(e) Not install any equipment or systems that materially increase building utility loads without Epsilon’s prior written consent.

2.7 **Use Restrictions.** Contoso shall not:

(a) Use the Premises for any unlawful purpose or for the storage of hazardous materials (other than typical office supplies used in compliance with law);  
(b) Create noise, vibration, odors, or conditions materially interfering with other tenants;  
(c) Place any signage visible from the exterior without Epsilon’s prior written approval; or  
(d) Overload any floor or building system beyond its rated capacity.

2.8 **Compliance with Rules and Regulations.** Epsilon may promulgate reasonable rules and regulations for the building (the “Building Rules”), which may be amended from time to time upon reasonable written notice. Contoso shall comply with the Building Rules, provided such rules do not unreasonably interfere with Contoso’s Permitted Use and are applied in a non-discriminatory manner.

2.9 **Alterations and Improvements.** Contoso shall not make any material alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises without Epsilon’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. All approved Tenant’s Work shall:

(a) Be performed at Contoso’s sole cost and expense by licensed contractors;  
(b) Comply with Applicable Law and Epsilon’s reasonable construction rules;  
(c) Be coordinated with Epsilon to minimize interference with building operations; and  
(d) Become part of the Improvements and property of Epsilon upon installation, except for Contoso’s trade fixtures and equipment, which Contoso may remove upon expiration or earlier termination, subject to repairing any resulting damage.

2.10 **Deliverables and Documentation.** To the extent applicable, Epsilon shall provide Contoso with the following Deliverables, in addition to the provision of the Premises and Services:

(a) As-built floor plans of the Premises, if available;  
(b) Copies of Building Rules and any changes thereto;  
(c) Maintenance logs and incident reports related to significant building issues materially affecting the Premises, upon reasonable request;  
(d) Written notices of scheduled maintenance that may materially interrupt Services, in accordance with **Article XII**.

2.11 **Performance Standards.** Epsilon shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for commercial property management and as further specified in the Service Levels. Contoso acknowledges that certain aspects of building operations may be subject to temporary interruptions due to maintenance, repairs, or Force Majeure Events, as contemplated in **Article XII** and **Article XV**.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Commencement Date and continue until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article.

4.2 **Renewal Options.** Provided Contoso is not in material default under this Agreement beyond any applicable cure period, Contoso shall have the option to renew the Lease for up to **one (1) additional period of three (3) years** (the “Renewal Term”), upon providing written notice to Epsilon not less than **nine (9)** and not more than **twelve (12)** months prior to the Expiration Date. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith the Base Rent and other economic terms for the Renewal Term, at then-prevailing market rates for comparable space, reasonably adjusted for the terms and creditworthiness of Contoso.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 **By Contoso.** After the first twelve (12) months of the Initial Term, Contoso may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Epsilon, provided that Contoso pays (a) all Fees due through the effective date of termination, and (b) an early termination fee equal to **three (3) months’ then-current Base Rent**, representing an agreed reasonable estimate of Epsilon’s costs and damages.

4.3.2 **By Epsilon.** Epsilon may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon not less than **ninety (90) days’** prior written notice to Contoso, and shall refund any prepaid Rent covering periods after termination, subject to setoff for amounts due.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if:

(a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; provided that if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have such additional time (not exceeding sixty (60) days total) as is reasonably necessary to cure, so long as it commences cure within the initial thirty (30) days and diligently pursues completion; or  

(b) The other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is the subject of a receivership or similar proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Unavailability or Condemnation.** If the Premises are rendered substantially unusable due to (a) condemnation, governmental taking, or (b) casualty damage not reasonably repairable within one hundred eighty (180) days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice. Rent shall be abated in proportion to the extent of untenantability from the date of such event until restoration or termination, as applicable.

4.6 **Surrender of Premises.** Upon expiration or earlier termination of the Term, Contoso shall:

(a) Vacate and surrender the Premises in substantially the same condition as of the Commencement Date, reasonable wear and tear and damage by casualty excepted;  
(b) Remove its trade fixtures, furniture, equipment, and personal property;  
(c) Repair any damage caused by such removal; and  
(d) Return all keys, access cards, and passes to Epsilon.

Any personal property not removed within thirty (30) days after termination may be deemed abandoned and may be disposed of by Epsilon, at Contoso’s expense, without liability.

4.7 **Effect of Termination.** Termination of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including payment of any Fees due, or of any obligations expressly stated to survive termination, including but not limited to those in **Sections 3.6, 4.6, 5.3, 6.4, 7.4, 8.4, 9.3, 11.6, 14.6, and 16.8**.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Availability of Building Systems.** Epsilon shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that critical building systems (HVAC, electricity, elevator service, building access control) are available **99.9%** of the time, calculated on a monthly basis, excluding:

(a) Scheduled maintenance notified to Contoso at least seventy-two (72) hours in advance;  
(b) Emergency maintenance necessary to prevent imminent harm or comply with law; and  
(c) Downtime caused by Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response Times.** Epsilon shall respond to service requests from Contoso as follows:

(a) **Critical Issues** (e.g., total loss of power, HVAC failure materially impairing operations, leaks, safety hazards): Acknowledgement within one (1) hour and commencement of remedial action within four (4) hours;  
(b) **High Priority Issues** (e.g., partial system failures not causing immediate safety risk): Acknowledgement within four (4) hours and commencement of remedial action within one (1) Business Day;  
(c) **Normal Issues** (e.g., minor repairs, comfort adjustments): Acknowledgement within one (1) Business Day and commencement of remedial action within three (3) Business Days.

12.3 **SLA Credits.** If Epsilon fails to meet the availability target in **Section 12.1** in any given month for reasons within its reasonable control, Contoso shall be entitled, upon written request within thirty (30) days after the end of such month, to a service credit equal to a percentage of the monthly Base Rent as follows:

- Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: **2%** of monthly Base Rent  
- Availability between 98.0% and 98.9%: **5%** of monthly Base Rent  
- Availability below 98.0%: **10%** of monthly Base Rent

SLA credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) and shall constitute Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for such SLA failures, without prejudice to Contoso’s rights under **Article IV** (Termination) in the event of chronic or material failures.

12.4 **Exclusions.** The SLAs shall not apply to:

(a) Failures caused by Contoso’s equipment or actions;  
(b) Contoso’s failure to provide necessary access or cooperation;  
(c) Third-party utility outages or network failures beyond Epsilon’s reasonable control; or  
(d) Force Majeure Events under **Article XV**.

15.1 **Force Majeure Events Defined.** A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to:

(a) Acts of God, natural disasters, earthquakes, floods, fires;  
(b) War, terrorism, civil unrest, riots, or insurrection;  
(c) Strikes, lockouts, or other labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce to the extent within its control);  
(d) Epidemics, pandemics, or public health emergencies;  
(e) Governmental orders, laws, or regulations restricting access or use of the Premises; or  
(f) Failure of utilities or infrastructure not caused by the affected Party’s negligence.

15.2 **Suspension of Obligations.** If a Party is prevented or delayed from performing any obligation under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) due to a Force Majeure Event, such obligation shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided the affected Party:

(a) Provides prompt written notice to the other Party describing the Force Majeure Event and its anticipated impact; and  
(b) Uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects use of the Premises, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without liability other than payment of amounts accrued prior to termination.
