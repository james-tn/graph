## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall provide comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Equipment and Facilities identified in the applicable SOWs. Such Services shall include, without limitation: (a) preventive maintenance; (b) corrective and breakdown repairs; (c) emergency and on‑call support; (d) periodic inspections and testing; (e) performance monitoring and reporting; and (f) related technical advisory and training services, each as further described in this Article II.

2.2 **Preventive Maintenance.**  
(a) Vendor shall develop and maintain a **Preventive Maintenance Program** tailored to Client’s Equipment, taking into account OEM recommendations, regulatory requirements, historical failure data, and Vendor’s industry best practices.  

(b) Preventive maintenance activities will include, as applicable: lubrication, cleaning, calibration, adjustment, software patching, firmware updates, replacement of consumable components, testing of safety systems, and functional verification of key subsystems.  

(c) Vendor will prepare and maintain a **Preventive Maintenance Schedule** specifying the periodicity (e.g., weekly, monthly, quarterly, annually) of each preventive task for each Equipment category or asset, and will provide such schedule to Client for review. Client may, acting reasonably, request adjustments to the schedule with at least ten (10) Business Days’ notice, provided that any adjustments that increase Vendor’s workload shall be subject to a Change Order and additional Fees in accordance with Article XIII.

2.3 **Corrective and Breakdown Maintenance.**  
(a) Upon notification by Client of an Incident, or upon Vendor’s detection of an Incident through remote monitoring (if applicable), Vendor shall diagnose and correct failures, malfunctions, and defects in the Equipment.  

(b) Vendor shall dispatch qualified personnel to Client’s site as reasonably necessary to restore the Equipment to operational status, taking into account the Incident’s severity classification (e.g., critical, major, minor) as agreed in the SOW.  

(c) Corrective actions may include, without limitation, replacement of parts, reconfiguration, repairs, software corrections, and implementation of temporary workarounds pending availability of parts or final solutions.  

(d) Vendor shall document each Incident and the corresponding corrective actions in a **Maintenance Log** and shall make such log available to Client upon request or as specified in Section 2.8.

2.4 **Emergency and On‑Call Support.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide 24x7x365 telephone and remote support for critical Incidents affecting safety or significantly impacting Client’s operations, as further defined in the SOW.  

(b) For critical Incidents, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the accelerated Response Times and on‑site arrival times specified in Article XII.  

(c) Emergency on‑site visits requested by Client outside of standard working hours (as defined in the SOW) shall be billed at the emergency or overtime rates specified in Article III or the applicable SOW.

2.5 **Inspection, Testing, and Compliance.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform periodic inspections, tests, and calibrations required to maintain the Equipment in good working order and to comply with applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards.  

(b) Such activities may include safety inspections, regulatory compliance tests, vibration analysis, thermal imaging, and other diagnostic procedures.  

(c) Where mandated by law or regulation, Vendor shall issue inspection or compliance certificates, which Client may use for regulatory audits, subject to any limitations of liability set forth in this Agreement.

2.6 **Spare Parts and Materials.**  
(a) Unless expressly stated otherwise in the SOW, Client shall be responsible for procuring and maintaining an adequate inventory of spare parts and consumables necessary for the performance of the Services.  

(b) Upon Client’s request, Vendor may procure parts and materials on Client’s behalf, in which case Vendor may charge a reasonable handling fee as specified in Article III. Title to such parts shall pass directly to Client upon purchase, and Client shall bear the risk of loss from that time.  

(c) Vendor is not responsible for delays in the Services attributable to unavailability of required parts, provided Vendor has timely notified Client of the need for such parts.

2.7 **Exclusions.**  
The Services do not include, unless expressly stated in an SOW: (a) major capital upgrades or replacements of Equipment; (b) structural modifications to Client’s Facilities; (c) design or engineering of new production lines; (d) remediation of pre‑existing defects, improper installation, or latent conditions not reasonably discoverable by Vendor during ordinary maintenance; (e) any work required due to Client’s misuse, negligence, or failure to follow Vendor or OEM recommendations; or (f) services expressly designated as excluded in the SOW. Any such excluded services, if requested by Client, shall be subject to a mutually agreed Change Order.

2.8 **Reporting and Documentation.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide Client with **Monthly Maintenance Reports** summarizing work performed, Incidents addressed, preventive tasks completed, pending issues, and recommendations for improvement.  

(b) Vendor shall maintain detailed records of all Services, including work orders, parts used, labor hours, and test results, for at least five (5) years after completion of the relevant Services, or such longer period as required by applicable law.  

(c) Upon reasonable prior notice, Client shall have the right to review such records during normal business hours, provided that Vendor may redact proprietary or third‑party confidential information not specifically related to Client’s Equipment.

2.9 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for maintenance of comparable industrial equipment, and in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.  

(b) Vendor shall ensure that its personnel assigned to perform the Services are adequately trained, qualified, and supervised, and that they comply with Client’s reasonable site rules, safety policies, and security protocols communicated in writing in advance.  

(c) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and the applicable SOW; provided, however, that Service Levels are targets, not guarantees, except where expressly stated otherwise, and Vendor’s failure to meet Service Levels shall give rise only to the remedies expressly stated in Article XII.

2.10 **Client Obligations.**  
(a) Client shall provide Vendor with safe and reasonable access to the Facilities and Equipment as necessary to perform the Services, including access outside normal business hours where required.  

(b) Client shall maintain appropriate environmental conditions (e.g., temperature, humidity, cleanliness) and electrical supply for the Equipment, as recommended by the OEM and Vendor.  

(c) Client shall promptly notify Vendor of any Incidents, anomalies, or safety concerns, and shall follow Vendor’s reasonable instructions for mitigating potential harm or further damage.  

(d) Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services and meet Service Levels is dependent on Client’s timely cooperation, access, and information, and Vendor shall not be liable for any failure to perform to the extent caused by Client’s acts or omissions.

(a) Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for all Fees due for Services performed during the preceding month, including any T&M and pass‑through costs.

(a) Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (i) one and one‑half percent (1.5%) per month; or (ii) the maximum rate allowable by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment.  

(b) Vendor may, upon at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice and without liability, suspend performance of the Services if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than forty‑five (45) days following the invoice date, and may condition resumption of Services upon full payment of past due amounts and, in Vendor’s discretion, prepayment of one (1) month of anticipated Fees.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
(a) Client shall notify Vendor in writing of any disputed invoice amounts within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the basis for the dispute in reasonable detail.  

(b) Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute.  

(c) If the dispute is resolved in Vendor’s favor, Client shall pay the disputed amount, together with any accrued interest, within ten (10) Business Days of resolution.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
(a) All Fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, and other similar taxes, duties, or governmental charges (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Client shall be responsible for payment of all Taxes arising out of this Agreement, excluding Taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees.  

(b) If Vendor is required by law to collect Taxes from Client, Vendor shall itemize such Taxes on the applicable invoice, and Client shall pay them unless Client provides valid exemption documentation.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre‑approved travel and lodging expenses incurred by Vendor’s personnel in connection with on‑site Services, in accordance with Client’s travel policy provided to Vendor in advance.  

(b) Vendor shall provide copies of receipts or reasonable documentation for reimbursable expenses upon request.  

(c) Reimbursable expenses shall be invoiced monthly and shall be payable in accordance with the Net 30 terms set forth above.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 15, 2027** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Unless either Party provides written notice of non‑renewal at least **one hundred eighty (180) days** prior to the Expiration Date, the Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods on the same terms and conditions, except that pricing may be adjusted by Vendor in accordance with Section 3.2 and as specified in the renewal notice.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Vendor‑Favorable).**  
(a) **By Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor.  

(b) **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing at least **ninety (90) days’ prior written notice** to Client.  

(c) In the event of termination for convenience by either Party, Client shall pay Vendor: (i) all Fees accrued through the effective date of termination; (ii) all non-cancellable commitments and documented wind‑down costs; and (iii) an early termination fee equal to **twenty percent (20%) of the Fees that would have been payable for the remaining Term** absent such termination, as liquidated damages and not as a penalty.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.  

(b) Notwithstanding the foregoing, Vendor may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if Client fails to pay any undisputed amounts due hereunder within forty-five (45) days of the due date and fails to cure such non‑payment within ten (10) Business Days following Vendor’s written notice.

4.5 **Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) is the subject of a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or has a receiver appointed for a substantial part of its assets.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Vendor shall cease providing the Services, except as necessary to perform any mutually agreed transition assistance under Section 4.7.  

(b) Client shall immediately pay all amounts due and payable to Vendor, including any early termination fee as applicable.  

(c) Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s written election, destroy any Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession, subject to the retention obligations in Section 5.5.  

(d) All licenses granted to Client under Article VI shall terminate, except as expressly stated otherwise therein.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
At Client’s written request received at least sixty (60) days prior to termination or expiration, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance, for up to ninety (90) days following the end of the Term, to facilitate the orderly transition of Services to Client or a third-party provider. Such assistance shall be provided at Vendor’s then‑current T&M rates and subject to a separate SOW.

4.8 **Survival.**  
The provisions of Sections 3.4, 3.6, 4.6, 4.7, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (to the extent of accrued Service Credits or obligations), XIII (for completed Change Orders), XIV, XV, and XVI shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics or pandemics, acts of government, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor strikes (excluding strikes of the affected Party’s own workforce), fires, floods, explosions, or failures of utilities or telecommunications not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party claiming relief due to a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature and expected duration of the event, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate its effects and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration impacted by the event, provided that the other Party’s payment obligations for Services already rendered shall not be excused.

15.4 **Prolonged Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially prevents performance of a material obligation under this Agreement, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party, without liability other than payment for Services performed and costs incurred prior to the effective date of termination.
