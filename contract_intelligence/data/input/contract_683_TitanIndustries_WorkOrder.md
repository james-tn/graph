## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive industrial systems integration, equipment procurement, installation, commissioning, and ongoing support Services for the modernization and expansion of Client’s automated material handling and packaging lines at the Service Locations. The Services shall include, without limitation, design, engineering, project management, procurement of specified equipment, mechanical and electrical installation, software configuration, testing, commissioning, training, and post-go-live support, all in accordance with the Specifications, this **Article II**, and the project schedule mutually agreed by the Parties.

2.2 **System Design and Engineering.** Vendor shall:

  a. Conduct detailed site assessments, including review of current mechanical, electrical, and control systems, and perform load, throughput, and capacity analyses;

  b. Develop system-level and subsystem-level design documentation, including process flow diagrams, equipment layout drawings, network architecture diagrams, I/O lists, bill of materials, and integration specifications with Client’s existing ERP and warehouse management systems;

  c. Prepare and deliver finalized design documents for Client’s review and written approval prior to commencement of major procurement and installation activities; and  

  d. Ensure that all designs conform to applicable industry codes, safety standards, and Client’s internal engineering guidelines, to the extent such guidelines are provided to Vendor in writing.

2.3 **Equipment Procurement.** Vendor shall:

  a. Procure, as Client’s designated prime contractor, the equipment and materials specified in the approved bill of materials, which may include conveyors, sorters, palletizers, robotic cells, sensors, control panels, PLCs, HMIs, servers, network switches, and cabling;

  b. Use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain competitive pricing and favorable commercial terms with suppliers, provided that Vendor shall not be obligated to disclose supplier margin, pricing, or other confidential commercial information to Client;

  c. Arrange for shipment, customs clearance (if applicable), and delivery of equipment to the Service Locations, and coordinate inspection and acceptance of equipment deliveries; and  

  d. Maintain adequate records of all procured equipment and materials for audit by Client pursuant to **Section 3.8**, subject to reasonable notice and confidentiality restrictions.

2.4 **Installation and Integration.** Vendor shall:

  a. Provide qualified personnel, including licensed electricians and certified technicians where required, to perform mechanical and electrical installation in accordance with applicable local codes and safety regulations;

  b. Install equipment, control panels, cabling, and ancillary infrastructure necessary to integrate the new systems with existing infrastructure and IT systems;

  c. Configure and program PLCs, HMIs, and related control systems to achieve the throughput, error rates, safety interlocks, and other performance criteria set forth in the Specifications, subject to Client’s timely cooperation and provision of necessary interfaces;  

  d. Conduct integration testing, including I/O checks, functional tests, safety tests, and end-to-end system validation, and remedy any non-conformities at Vendor’s cost (except to the extent caused by Client’s systems or failures as further addressed in **Section 2.9**).

2.5 **Commissioning and Acceptance.** 

  a. **Commissioning Activities.** Upon completion of installation, Vendor shall commence commissioning, which shall include: (i) system startup; (ii) tuning and optimization to reach target throughput parameters; (iii) safety validation; and (iv) performance testing under typical and peak load conditions mutually defined in writing.

  b. **Acceptance Criteria.** The system shall be deemed to have achieved **Substantial Completion** when (i) the system operates in production mode for at least seven (7) consecutive days meeting at least ninety-seven percent (97%) of the agreed throughput and uptime targets, and (ii) no material safety or functionality defects remain unresolved. Final acceptance shall occur when Vendor has resolved all material defects identified by Client in a written punch list generated no later than fifteen (15) Business Days after Substantial Completion.

  c. **Deemed Acceptance.** If Client fails to provide a punch list or written rejection specifying material defects within fifteen (15) Business Days following the date Vendor notifies Client of Substantial Completion, the system and associated Deliverables shall be deemed accepted as of the date of Substantial Completion.

2.6 **Training.** Vendor shall provide training sessions at each Service Location for Client’s designated technical and operations personnel. Training shall cover system operation, basic troubleshooting, safety procedures, and preventive maintenance routines. Vendor shall deliver training materials in electronic format and, upon Client’s request, conduct additional training sessions at the hourly rates set forth in **Section 3.4**.

2.7 **Ongoing Support and Maintenance.** From the date of Final Acceptance (as defined in **Section 2.5(b)**) through the Expiration Date, Vendor shall provide:

  a. **Remote Support:** Telephone, email, and remote diagnostic support during Standard Support Hours (8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Eastern Time, Monday through Friday, excluding Vendor-recognized holidays), with enhanced response times as specified in **Article XII**.

  b. **On-Site Support:** Dispatch of service technicians to the Service Locations when remote resolution is not commercially feasible, with dispatch and arrival targets outlined in **Section 12.3**.

  c. **Preventive Maintenance:** Scheduled preventive maintenance visits, including inspection, cleaning, lubrication, adjustments, and replacement of wear items as specified in Vendor’s maintenance guidelines.

  d. **Spare Parts Management:** Guidance on recommended critical spare parts inventory and, where agreed, provision of Vendor-managed consignment stock subject to separate pricing and terms.

2.8 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

  a. In a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for industrial systems integration and maintenance;

  b. In compliance with the Specifications, applicable laws, and the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**; and  

  c. Using personnel with appropriate training, skill, and experience, and subject to Vendor’s right to determine the specific personnel and subcontractors performing the Services, as long as Vendor remains responsible for their acts and omissions.

2.9 **Client Responsibilities.** Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services depends on Client’s timely cooperation. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Client shall:

  a. Provide Vendor and its personnel with timely access to the Service Locations, including access to necessary equipment, systems, data, and utilities;

  b. Ensure that all pre-existing equipment, systems, and infrastructure that Vendor must interface with are in good working order and compliant with applicable safety and regulatory requirements;

  c. Designate a project manager and technical contacts to act as points of coordination and decision-making, and to review and approve deliverables within a reasonable timeframe;

  d. Obtain any necessary internal approvals and permits not expressly assumed by Vendor; and  

  e. Promptly notify Vendor of any incidents, system failures, or conditions that may materially impact Vendor’s ability to perform the Services.

Delays or additional costs resulting from Client’s failure to comply with its responsibilities under this **Section 2.9** shall be addressed in accordance with **Article XIII** and may result in schedule extensions and equitable adjustments to fees.

2.10 **High-Risk Classification.** The Parties acknowledge that the Services involve critical production infrastructure. In light of this **High Risk** classification:

  a. Vendor will use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate operational risks;  

  b. Client assumes responsibility for (i) overall facility operations, workplace safety policies, and compliance programs; and (ii) ensuring that systems are operated only by properly trained personnel; and  

  c. The allocation of risk and liability is governed by **Articles VIII and IX**, which the Parties recognize as reflecting a negotiated allocation of risk under this High Risk engagement.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** Subject to the terms herein, the total aggregate amount payable by Client to Vendor for the Services and Deliverables under this Work Order shall not exceed **US$2,201,169** (the **“Contract Value”**), exclusive of any applicable taxes, unless increased by one or more Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII**.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** The Contract Value is comprised of the following components:

  a. **Design and Engineering Services:** US$340,000  
  b. **Equipment and Materials Procurement:** US$1,350,000  
  c. **Installation and Integration Services:** US$285,000  
  d. **Commissioning and Acceptance Support:** US$80,000  
  e. **Training Services:** US$46,169  
  f. **Ongoing Support and Maintenance (Base):** US$100,000

3.3 **Payment Milestones.** Fees shall be invoiced and payable upon achievement of the following Milestones:

  a. **Milestone 1 – Project Initiation (15%):** Upon Effective Date and Vendor’s mobilization, Client shall pay fifteen percent (15%) of the Contract Value.

  b. **Milestone 2 – Design Approval (20%):** Upon Client’s written approval (or deemed approval) of the finalized design package under **Section 2.2**, Client shall pay an additional twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value.

  c. **Milestone 3 – Equipment Delivery (30%):** Upon delivery of at least eighty percent (80%) of the primary equipment to the Service Locations, as confirmed by delivery documentation, Client shall pay an additional thirty percent (30%) of the Contract Value.

  d. **Milestone 4 – Substantial Completion (25%):** Upon achievement of Substantial Completion under **Section 2.5(b)**, Client shall pay an additional twenty-five percent (25%) of the Contract Value.

  e. **Milestone 5 – Final Acceptance (10%):** Upon Final Acceptance under **Section 2.5(b)** or deemed acceptance under **Section 2.5(c)**, Client shall pay the remaining ten percent (10%) of the Contract Value.

3.4 **Time-and-Materials (T&M) Rates.** Any Services outside the defined scope, including additional training, consulting, or requested modifications not covered by existing Milestones, shall be billed on a T&M basis at the following rates (exclusive of taxes):

  a. Project Manager: US$225/hour  
  b. Senior Engineer: US$250/hour  
  c. Field Technician: US$185/hour  
  d. Trainer: US$175/hour

These rates may be adjusted annually on January 1 by up to three percent (3%) upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Client.

3.5 **Payment Terms.** Unless otherwise specified herein, invoices issued by Vendor shall be due and payable by Client within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the invoice date (**“Net 30”**). Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars by electronic funds transfer or other mutually agreed method. Client shall not unreasonably withhold or delay payment of any undisputed amounts.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.** If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within ten (10) Business Days of receipt, specifying the reasons for the dispute. Client shall pay the undisputed portion by the due date and the Parties shall cooperate to resolve the dispute promptly. Any portion determined to be payable shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days of resolution.

3.7 **Late Payment.** Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower, calculated from the due date until payment in full. Without prejudice to Vendor’s other rights and remedies under this Agreement or at law, if any undisputed payment is more than forty-five (45) days past due, Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of all or part of the Services until all overdue amounts (including interest) are paid in full.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.** Client shall reimburse Vendor for all reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, shipping, customs duties, and permits, provided that: (a) such expenses are consistent with Vendor’s standard travel policy (made available to Client upon request); and (b) Vendor obtains Client’s prior written approval for any single expense exceeding US$5,000.

3.9 **Taxes.** All fees and charges are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, withholding, or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes on Vendor’s net income), and Vendor shall itemize any such taxes on its invoices as required by law. If Client is required by law to withhold taxes from any payment, Client shall remit the withholding tax to the appropriate tax authority and provide Vendor with supporting documentation, while making reasonable efforts to minimize such withholding.

3.10 **No Set-Off.** Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or required by applicable law, Client shall not set off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from payments due to Vendor on account of any alleged amounts owed to Client.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until the Expiration Date (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, upon mutual written agreement not less than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend the Term for successive renewal periods, subject to negotiation of applicable fees, scope, and Service Levels.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor. Client shall remain liable for (a) all fees and expenses incurred or committed by Vendor up to the effective date of termination, including non-cancellable commitments, and (b) an additional early termination fee equal to five percent (5%) of the remaining unpaid portion of the Contract Value as of the effective date of termination, in recognition of the High Risk classification and Vendor’s allocation of resources.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon at least one hundred twenty (120) days’ prior written notice to Client; provided that Vendor shall not exercise such right in a manner that unreasonably jeopardizes the safe operation of Client’s facilities. Vendor shall cooperate in good faith to transition the Services in accordance with **Section 4.7**.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**

  a. **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to Client if: (i) Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within sixty (60) days after the due date, and fails to cure such non-payment within ten (10) Business Days after receiving written notice; or (ii) Client materially breaches any other provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach.

  b. **By Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to Vendor if Vendor materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach. For clarity, failure to meet any single Service Level does not, by itself, constitute a material breach; instead, materiality shall be assessed considering the duration, frequency, severity, and impact of the Service Level failures.

4.6 **Termination for Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files a petition in bankruptcy or is adjudicated bankrupt; (c) has a receiver or trustee appointed for it or its assets; or (d) executes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, to the extent permitted by applicable law.

4.7 **Effect of Termination.**

  a. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement: (i) all rights and licenses granted by Vendor to Client under this Agreement shall cease, except as expressly provided otherwise in **Article VI**; (ii) Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the Services; and (iii) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, including any applicable early termination fees under **Section 4.3**.

  b. At Client’s written request, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance (for up to sixty (60) days after termination) at Vendor’s then-current T&M rates, to facilitate the orderly transition of the Services to Client or a third-party provider.

  c. Termination of this Agreement shall be without prejudice to any rights or remedies that may have accrued prior to the effective date of termination.

4.8 **Survival.** The provisions of **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**, together with those provisions that by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **System Uptime.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the automated systems installed under this Agreement achieve **at least 99.9% uptime** during production hours, measured on a rolling calendar month basis at each Service Location, excluding: (a) scheduled maintenance windows agreed in advance with Client; (b) outages caused by Client’s systems, equipment, or network; (c) Force Majeure Events; and (d) downtime attributable to third-party providers not under Vendor’s control.

12.2 **Support Response Times.** During Standard Support Hours, Vendor shall adhere to the following initial response time targets after receipt of a support request from Client:

  a. **Critical Issue (Priority 1):** System outage or severe degradation materially impacting production operations – initial response within **one (1) hour**.  

  b. **High Issue (Priority 2):** Significant functionality impairment without total outage – initial response within **four (4) hours**.  

  c. **Medium Issue (Priority 3):** Non-critical issues affecting performance but not production-critical – initial response within **one (1) Business Day**.  

  d. **Low Issue (Priority 4):** Minor issues or cosmetic defects – initial response within **three (3) Business Days**.

12.3 **On-Site Response Times.** For Critical Issues that cannot be resolved remotely, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to dispatch qualified personnel to the relevant Service Location within **twenty-four (24) hours** of determining that on-site intervention is required, subject to travel feasibility and safety conditions.

12.4 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.** If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** for a given Service Location in any calendar month (excluding permitted exceptions), Client shall be entitled to the following service credits:

  a. Uptime between 99.5% and 99.9%: Service credit equal to 2% of the monthly support fee for that location;  
  b. Uptime between 99.0% and 99.5%: Service credit equal to 5% of the monthly support fee for that location;  
  c. Uptime below 99.0%: Service credit equal to 10% of the monthly support fee for that location.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be refundable in cash. Service credits shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, without limiting Client’s other rights for material breaches under **Section 4.5(b)**.

12.5 **Reporting.** Upon Client’s request, Vendor shall provide monthly reports summarizing uptime performance, incident response times, and any SLA breaches, together with root-cause analyses and corrective actions for significant incidents.
