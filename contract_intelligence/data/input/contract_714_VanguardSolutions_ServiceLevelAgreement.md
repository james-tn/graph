## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
During the Term of this Agreement, Vanguard shall provide to Contoso the Services described in this **Article II** and in any applicable SOW(s). The Services include, without limitation:

  a. **Managed Cloud Hosting Services**, consisting of:
  - Hosting of Contoso’s business applications and data on Vanguard’s cloud infrastructure;  
  - Provision of virtual servers, storage, backup services, and network connectivity;  
  - Monitoring of system performance, capacity, and security;  
  - Implementation of redundancy and failover measures consistent with the Service Levels.

  b. **Application Support and Maintenance**, consisting of:
  - Level 1 and Level 2 help desk support for designated Contoso applications;  
  - Incident management, including ticket intake, classification, escalation, and resolution;  
  - Routine maintenance, patching, and updates of supported applications;  
  - Root cause analysis for recurring or critical issues, with documented reports.

  c. **System Monitoring and Alerting**, consisting of:
  - 24x7 automated monitoring of servers, network devices, and relevant application components;  
  - Proactive alerting to Vanguard’s support team for anomalies or performance thresholds;  
  - Dashboard access for Contoso’s designated personnel, if applicable, to view system health and incident status.

  d. **Security and Compliance Services**, consisting of:
  - Implementation of industry-standard security controls (e.g., firewalls, access controls, encryption);  
  - Vulnerability assessments of the hosted environment on at least a quarterly basis;  
  - Support in audits or assessments required by Contoso related to the Services, subject to reasonable notice and scope;  
  - Assistance to Contoso in meeting data protection obligations under Applicable Law as described in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.

  e. **Professional Services and Consulting**, consisting of:
  - Migration support for Contoso’s existing systems and data to Vanguard’s cloud environment;  
  - Architecture and capacity planning consulting in coordination with Contoso’s IT team;  
  - Training sessions for Contoso’s administrators on the use of the System, if reasonably requested;  
  - Preparation of periodic reports and strategic recommendations for optimization of the Services.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables under this Agreement may include, but are not limited to:

  a. **Technical Design and Architecture Documentation**, detailing the overall architecture of the hosted environment, network topology, security measures, and integration points;

  b. **Migration Plans and Runbooks**, describing the steps, sequencing, and rollback strategies for migration of applications and data to the System;

  c. **Operational Procedures and Support Guides**, including documented incident management workflows, escalation matrices, and contact points;

  d. **Periodic Performance Reports**, including monthly or quarterly reports (as agreed) documenting uptime, incidents, response and resolution times, capacity usage, and security events.

Each Deliverable shall conform to any specifications stated in the applicable SOW and shall be subject to review and acceptance by Contoso, acting reasonably and in good faith. Unless otherwise specified, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted if Contoso does not reasonably object in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days following delivery, specifying the material non-conformities.

2.3 **Service Onboarding and Transition.**  
Within thirty (30) days following the Effective Date, or such other period as the Parties may agree in writing, Vanguard shall perform onboarding activities, which may include:

  a. Discovery and assessment of Contoso’s current IT environment and applications;  
  b. Agreement on a detailed transition plan and schedule;  
  c. Configuration of the System in accordance with Contoso’s agreed requirements;  
  d. Coordination with Contoso’s IT team for data migration and cutover to the System;  
  e. Initial training sessions on the use of the Services and support procedures.

Vanguard shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to Contoso’s business operations during transition, and Contoso shall provide reasonable cooperation, including access to systems, personnel, and relevant information.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Vanguard shall perform the Services:

  a. In a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry best practices for managed IT service providers;  
  b. In accordance with the Service Levels and metrics specified in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**;  
  c. In compliance with Applicable Law and with Contoso’s applicable policies and procedures that have been communicated in writing to Vanguard and accepted by Vanguard in writing;  
  d. Using personnel who are appropriately skilled, trained, and experienced in providing services of a similar nature.

2.5 **Exclusions.**  
Unless explicitly stated in this Agreement, an SOW, or a Change Order, the Services shall not include:

  a. Support for hardware or software not under Vanguard’s control or not expressly identified as in-scope;  
  b. Development of new custom applications or substantial customization of third-party applications (unless separately contracted);  
  c. On-site services at Contoso’s facilities, except as agreed in writing;  
  d. Services required due to (i) unauthorized modifications by Contoso or third parties, (ii) use of the Services not in accordance with the Documentation or this Agreement, or (iii) failures of third-party networks or services beyond Vanguard’s reasonable control.

Any such excluded services, if requested by Contoso, shall be subject to a separate SOW and additional Fees, as mutually agreed.

2.6 **Responsibilities of Contoso.**  
Contoso shall:

  a. Provide Vanguard with timely access to relevant information, systems, personnel, and resources as reasonably necessary for Vanguard to perform the Services;  
  b. Designate a primary contact and, as applicable, technical contacts who shall coordinate with Vanguard;  
  c. Maintain appropriate backup copies of its data (in addition to those maintained by Vanguard under this Agreement) as a prudent business practice;  
  d. Ensure that its users comply with any acceptable use policies or other usage guidelines provided by Vanguard and incorporated into the Agreement;  
  e. Notify Vanguard promptly of any suspected security incident, misuse, or unauthorized access involving the Services or Client Data.

Vanguard’s performance under this Agreement is dependent on Contoso’s timely and effective performance of its responsibilities. Vanguard shall not be liable for delays or service degradation to the extent caused by Contoso’s failure to fulfill its obligations as set forth herein.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees.**  
In consideration of the Services and Deliverables provided under this Agreement, Contoso shall pay Vanguard a total contract value not to exceed **USD $605,658** during the Initial Term, subject to any increases or adjustments made in accordance with this Agreement. The Fees shall consist of:

  a. **Recurring Service Fees.** Monthly recurring fees for managed hosting, monitoring, and support services, as detailed in the applicable SOW (e.g., a fixed monthly fee plus variable charges based on resource usage);

  b. **One-Time Implementation Fees.** One-time fees for onboarding, migration, and initial configuration activities, as specified in the applicable SOW;

  c. **Professional Services Fees.** Time-and-materials or fixed-fee charges for additional consulting, training, or project-based work, as agreed in a separate SOW or Change Order.

3.2 **Payment Terms.**  
Unless otherwise provided in an SOW, Vanguard shall invoice Contoso on a monthly basis in arrears for recurring Services and in accordance with agreed milestones for one-time or project-based Services. All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable within **sixty (60) days** from the invoice date (“**Net 60**”). Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or another method agreed in writing by the Parties.

3.3 **Invoices and Disputes.**  
Each invoice shall reference this Agreement and provide reasonable detail regarding the Services billed. In the event Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice, Contoso shall notify Vanguard in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of the invoice date, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve such disputes promptly. Contoso shall timely pay any undisputed portion of the invoice.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment. Vanguard shall provide written notice of late payment before charging interest, and no interest shall accrue if payment is made within ten (10) Business Days of such notice.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
The Fees are exclusive of all sales, use, excise, value-added, or other taxes or duties imposed by any governmental authority, excluding taxes based on Vanguard’s net income, property, or employees. Contoso shall be responsible for all such applicable taxes, which shall be separately itemized on the invoices when imposed, provided Vanguard furnishes documentation supporting such taxes where required.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vanguard in the performance of Services (such as travel, lodging, and per diem expenses for on-site services requested by Contoso) shall be reimbursed by Contoso at actual cost, without markup, upon submission of appropriate receipts. Vanguard shall obtain Contoso’s prior written approval for any single expense expected to exceed USD $1,000.

3.7 **Fee Adjustments.**  
For any renewal Term (as described in **Section 4.2**), Vanguard may propose reasonable adjustments to the Fees, not to exceed a five percent (5%) increase per renewal year, upon at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice before the end of the then-current Term. Any such adjustments shall be effective only upon mutual written agreement of the Parties.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Initial Term.**  
The initial term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **October 07, 2026** (the “**Initial Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement may be renewed for successive one-year periods (each, a “**Renewal Term**”) upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least sixty (60) days prior to the expiration of the then-current Term. If the Parties do not execute a renewal agreement, this Agreement shall expire at the end of the then-current Term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile and to provide flexibility to Contoso, either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. For clarity, termination of a specific SOW for convenience shall not automatically terminate this Agreement unless expressly stated in the notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any applicable SOW) immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  a. Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  b. Becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, is subject to a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or has a receiver appointed for substantially all of its assets.

4.5 **Termination for Chronic SLA Failure.**  
Without limiting other remedies, Contoso may terminate this Agreement or the relevant SOW, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, if Vanguard fails to meet the critical Service Level regarding system uptime (as defined in **Article XII**) in three (3) or more calendar months in any rolling twelve (12) month period, provided that Contoso provides reasonable documentation and such breaches are not due to Excluded Downtime or Force Majeure Events.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:

  a. Vanguard shall immediately cease performing the Services (except as may be required for transition assistance under Section 4.7) and shall invoice Contoso for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination;  
  b. Contoso shall pay Vanguard for all undisputed amounts due within the time period specified in **Article III**;  
  c. Each Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, in accordance with **Section 5.4**; and  
  d. All rights and licenses granted to Contoso under **Article VI** with respect to Deliverables fully paid for by Contoso as of the effective date of termination shall continue in full force and effect in accordance with their terms.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
At Contoso’s written request, Vanguard shall, for up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration, provide reasonable transition services to assist Contoso in migrating the Services to another provider or back in-house. Such transition services shall be provided at Vanguard’s then-current time-and-materials rates, unless termination is due to Vanguard’s uncured material breach, in which case transition services for up to forty (40) hours shall be provided at no additional charge.

4.8 **Survival.**  
Any provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration of this Agreement shall so survive, including without limitation **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, as well as any payment obligations accrued but unpaid as of termination.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business plans, financial information, pricing, technical data, software, system designs, security information, Client Data, and trade secrets. For clarity, Client Data shall be deemed Confidential Information of Contoso.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  a. Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  
  b. Not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted herein;  
  c. Limit access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and Affiliates who have a need to know such information and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein;  
  d. Use at least reasonable care, and in any event not less than the standard of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, to protect the confidentiality and security of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that:

  a. Is or becomes generally known to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  b. Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;  
  c. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous records;  
  d. Is approved in writing for release by the Disclosing Party.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing and cooperate, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or other remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally compelled to disclose.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain such copies as are required by Applicable Law or for archival purposes in accordance with its reasonable document retention policies, subject to the ongoing confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations under this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for **five (5) years** after termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets and Client Data, which shall remain confidential for so long as they constitute trade secrets or Client Data under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Availability Commitment.**  
Vanguard shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production System supporting the Services is available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Excluded Downtime (“**Availability**”). “Available” means that the primary production environment for the Services is accessible and operational for use by Contoso.

12.2 **Excluded Downtime.**  
The following shall be excluded from the calculation of Availability:

  a. Scheduled maintenance performed during the Maintenance Window (with at least seventy-two (72) hours prior notice to Contoso);  
  b. Emergency maintenance required to address critical vulnerabilities or threats, provided Vanguard uses reasonable efforts to minimize disruption and, where practicable, provides prior notice;  
  c. Downtime caused by Force Majeure Events;  
  d. Downtime caused by Contoso’s acts or omissions, including misuse or unauthorized modifications;  
  e. Downtime attributable to third-party network or telecommunications failures outside Vanguard’s reasonable control.

12.3 **Maintenance Window.**  
Routine maintenance shall generally be conducted during the following standard window (subject to change upon prior notice): Saturdays, 1:00 AM to 5:00 AM Central Time. Vanguard shall endeavor to schedule disruptive maintenance during this window and to minimize any impact on Availability.

12.4 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
Vanguard shall respond to and work to resolve Incidents according to the following guidelines (business impact and severity to be reasonably determined by Contoso and Vanguard):

  a. **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete service outage or critical business impact:
     - Initial response within 1 hour, 24x7  
     - Continuous work until resolution or workaround  
  b. **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant loss of functionality affecting many users:
     - Initial response within 2 hours during Business Days  
     - Target resolution or workaround within 8 Business Hours  
  c. **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Partial loss of functionality or minor degradation:
     - Initial response within 4 Business Hours  
     - Target resolution or workaround within 3 Business Days  
  d. **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, requests, or questions:
     - Initial response within 1 Business Day  
     - Resolution in the normal course of business

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If Vanguard fails to meet the Availability commitment in **Section 12.1** in any given calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to a service credit (the “**Service Credit**”) as follows:

  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services;  
  - Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services;  
  - Availability below 98.0%: 20% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services.

To receive a Service Credit, Contoso must notify Vanguard in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the failure occurred, providing reasonable details of the incident. Service Credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) issued by Vanguard and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service Credits are Contoso’s exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the Availability commitment, subject to Contoso’s right to terminate for chronic SLA failure under **Section 4.5**.

## ARTICLE XV  
FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), governmental orders, failures of third-party telecommunications or hosting providers, and other similar events.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  a. Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, describing the nature and expected duration of the event and its impact on performance;  
  b. Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration impacted by the Force Majeure Event. The time for performance shall be extended by a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
