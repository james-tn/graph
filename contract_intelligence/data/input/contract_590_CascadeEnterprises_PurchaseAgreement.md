## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF PRODUCTS, SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
During the Term, Cascade shall supply to Contoso the Products, Services, and Deliverables described in this Agreement and in one or more SOWs or Purchase Orders executed pursuant hereto. The Parties anticipate that Cascade will provide a combination of (a) infrastructure hardware, (b) software licenses, (c) systems configuration and implementation services, and (d) ongoing support and maintenance, as more specifically set forth below and in the applicable SOW(s).

2.2 **Products.**  
(a) Cascade shall provide enterprise-grade computing, networking, and storage hardware, including but not limited to servers, switches, routers, firewalls, storage arrays, and related accessories, as specified in each SOW or PO.  
(b) Products shall be new (not refurbished) unless expressly agreed otherwise in an SOW, conform to the manufacturer’s specifications, and be suitable for the intended purposes described by Contoso in the applicable SOW.  
(c) Cascade shall package, mark, and ship Products in accordance with industry standards and any packaging instructions reasonably specified by Contoso, with risk of loss and title passing as set forth in **Section 3.7**.

2.3 **Software and Licenses.**  
(a) Cascade shall supply to Contoso certain proprietary and/or Third-Party software licenses as specified in the SOW(s), which may include system management tools, monitoring software, security software, and other enterprise applications.  
(b) Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, software licenses granted under this Agreement are non-exclusive, non-transferable (except as permitted under **Section 16.7**), and limited to Contoso’s internal business purposes.  
(c) Any additional license terms applicable to Third-Party Products will be provided to Contoso in writing and shall be binding upon Contoso to the extent accepted by Contoso, provided that such terms do not conflict with or diminish Contoso’s rights under this Agreement. In case of conflict, the terms of this Agreement shall govern, except where Third-Party licensor terms are mandated by law.

2.4 **Implementation and Configuration Services.**  
(a) Cascade shall perform the implementation, installation, configuration, and integration Services necessary to deploy the Products and software within Contoso’s environment, as further detailed in each SOW.  
(b) Such Services may include requirements gathering, solution design, environmental readiness assessments, installation of hardware and software, network configuration, security hardening, testing, user acceptance support, and handover.  
(c) Cascade shall assign appropriately skilled personnel, including project managers, engineers, and support staff, and shall ensure that such personnel perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.  
(d) Cascade shall prepare and maintain a project plan for each SOW (if applicable), including milestones, dependencies, and estimated completion dates. Cascade shall review progress with Contoso on a regular basis (at least monthly during active project phases) and promptly notify Contoso of any issues likely to impact timelines or costs.

2.5 **Support and Maintenance Services.**  
(a) Cascade shall provide ongoing support for the Products and software as specified in **Article XII** and in the SOW(s), which may include help desk support, remote diagnostics, patches, updates, preventive maintenance, and on-site assistance where necessary.  
(b) Cascade shall provide Contoso with access to a support portal, telephone or ticketing system, and escalation paths as described in the applicable SOW.  
(c) Cascade shall maintain adequate staffing to meet the Service Levels and shall ensure continuity of knowledge regarding Contoso’s environment.

2.6 **Deliverables and Documentation.**  
(a) Cascade shall deliver to Contoso all Deliverables specified in each SOW, which may include:  
   - System architecture diagrams  
   - Configuration documentation  
   - Test plans and test reports  
   - Standard operating procedures (SOPs)  
   - Admin and user manuals, training materials, and “as-built” documentation.  
(b) Deliverables shall be subject to acceptance testing and criteria as set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise specified, Contoso shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery to review each Deliverable, notify Cascade in writing of any material non-conformities, and request corrections.  
(c) If Contoso timely notifies Cascade of non-conformities, Cascade shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such issues at no additional cost and resubmit the Deliverable for re-acceptance. If Contoso fails to reject a Deliverable within the specified review period, such Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, provided that such deemed acceptance shall not waive Contoso’s warranty rights under **Article VII**.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Cascade shall perform all Services with the degree of professional skill, care, diligence, and timeliness that would reasonably be expected from a leading provider of similar services.  
(b) Cascade shall comply with all reasonable technical standards, policies, and security requirements provided by Contoso in writing, provided such requirements are commercially reasonable and do not materially deviate from the agreed scope.  
(c) Time is of the essence with respect to critical milestones identified as such in an SOW. If Cascade becomes aware that it is likely to miss any such critical milestone, Cascade shall promptly notify Contoso, explain the causes, and propose a mitigation plan.  
(d) If Cascade repeatedly fails to meet material performance standards or critical milestones, Contoso may exercise its rights under **Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause)** and/or seek other remedies as provided herein.

2.8 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
(a) Contoso shall provide Cascade with timely access to relevant personnel, systems, facilities, and information reasonably necessary for Cascade to perform the Services.  
(b) Contoso shall perform any Contoso obligations specified in each SOW, including provision of test data, approvals, and decisions in a timely manner.  
(c) Contoso acknowledges that delays or failures by Contoso to perform its responsibilities may impact the schedule or cost; Cascade shall promptly notify Contoso of any such impacts, and the Parties shall address such impacts through the change management procedures in **Article XIII**.

2.9 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
Unless expressly stated in an SOW, this Agreement is non-exclusive. Contoso may procure similar products or services from other vendors, and Cascade may provide products and services to other customers, provided that Cascade complies with its confidentiality obligations under **Article V**.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The aggregate not-to-exceed value of this Agreement, across all SOWs and POs, shall be **USD $4,500,110**, unless increased by a written amendment signed by both Parties.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
(a) Fees for Products shall be set forth in each applicable SOW or PO, typically as unit prices multiplied by the quantity ordered.  
(b) Fees for Services may be structured as fixed-price amounts, time-and-materials (T&M) rates, or a combination thereof, as set forth in each SOW.  
(c) Any recurring fees (e.g., support, maintenance, subscription licenses) shall be clearly identified in the SOW, including billing periods and renewal terms.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
(a) Cascade shall invoice Contoso in accordance with the schedule set forth in each SOW or PO. In the absence of specific invoicing terms, Cascade may invoice:  
   - For Products: upon shipment (or upon delivery, if specified in the SOW);  
   - For fixed-price Services: upon completion of defined milestones;  
   - For T&M Services: monthly in arrears, based on actual hours worked and expenses incurred.  
(b) Each invoice shall reference this Agreement (PUR-CAS-202510-590), the applicable SOW or PO number, provide reasonable detail of the Products, Services, and expenses, and include any supporting documentation reasonably requested by Contoso.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) Contoso shall pay undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** of the date of receipt of a valid invoice (“**Net 60**”), via ACH, wire transfer, or other mutually agreed method.  
(b) Payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD), unless otherwise specified in an SOW.  
(c) Contoso may withhold payment of any disputed amounts, provided that Contoso (i) pays the undisputed portion of the invoice, and (ii) provides written notice of the dispute, including reasonable detail, within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any invoice disputes.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due may accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one percent (1.0%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted under applicable law.  
(b) Cascade shall not suspend Services for late payment unless (i) any undisputed amount is more than sixty (60) days past due, (ii) Cascade has provided written notice of such delinquency, and (iii) Contoso has failed to cure within fifteen (15) days after such notice. Any suspension shall be limited, proportionate, and shall not relieve Cascade of its confidentiality or data protection obligations.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
(a) Fees are exclusive of sales, use, value-added, and similar taxes (collectively, “**Taxes**”). Contoso shall be responsible for any Taxes assessed on its purchases, excluding taxes based on Cascade’s net income, property, or employees.  
(b) Where applicable, Cascade shall separately state Taxes on invoices. If Contoso provides Cascade with a valid exemption certificate or other evidence of tax-exempt status, Cascade shall not charge the exempt Taxes.

3.7 **Shipping, Delivery, and Title.**  
(a) Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Products shall be delivered **FOB Destination** (Incoterms 2020) to the Contoso location designated in the PO or SOW. Title and risk of loss shall pass to Contoso upon delivery at such location.  
(b) Shipping and handling charges, if any, shall be identified in the SOW or invoice. Cascade shall use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain competitive shipping rates.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred by Cascade in performing on-site Services shall be reimbursed by Contoso at actual cost, without markup, in accordance with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policies provided to Cascade in advance.  
(b) Cascade shall provide original or electronic receipts for all reimbursable expenses in excess of USD $25, or such other threshold as Contoso may reasonably specify.

3.9 **Setoff.**  
Contoso may set off any amounts due from Cascade to Contoso against amounts otherwise payable to Cascade under this Agreement, provided that Contoso notifies Cascade of the basis for such setoff and such amounts are not disputed in good faith by Cascade.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **July 09, 2027** (the “**Initial Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
(a) The Agreement may be renewed for additional periods of one (1) year each (each, a “**Renewal Term**”) upon mutual written agreement of the Parties.  
(b) Any renewal shall be documented in writing and shall specify any revised pricing or other terms agreed upon by the Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
(a) Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any SOW), for any reason or no reason, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Cascade.  
(b) Cascade may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso, provided that Cascade shall honor its obligations under any ongoing SOWs during such notice period unless Contoso agrees otherwise.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer cure period as may be reasonable given the nature of the breach, not to exceed sixty (60) days) after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.  
(b) Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent, (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (iv) ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) All outstanding unpaid invoices for Products delivered or Services performed up to the effective date of termination shall become due and payable in accordance with **Article III**.  
(b) For any SOW terminated for convenience by Contoso, Contoso shall pay Cascade for: (i) Products delivered and accepted; (ii) Services performed and accepted through the effective date of termination; and (iii) any documented, non-cancellable commitments made by Cascade specifically for Contoso’s project, to the extent unable to be mitigated.  
(c) Each Party shall promptly return or, at the disclosing Party’s written instruction, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**, subject to any retention obligations under **Article XI** or applicable law.  
(d) All licenses granted to Contoso that are perpetual in nature shall survive termination of this Agreement, subject to Contoso’s continued compliance with applicable license terms and payment obligations.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under **Sections 3.4–3.9**, **4.5**, and **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions)** shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is received from a third party who acquired or disclosed such information without breach of any confidentiality obligation; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
(a) The Receiving Party shall: (i) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement; and (ii) protect such Confidential Information from unauthorized use, access, or disclosure using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.  
(b) The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein. The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this **Article V** by such persons.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permissible) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other remedy. The Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such information is treated confidentially.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain: (a) one archival copy for legal and compliance purposes; and (b) any copies stored in routine backup media that are not readily accessible, provided that any retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein until destroyed.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this **Article V** shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years** from the date of termination or expiration; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under applicable law.
