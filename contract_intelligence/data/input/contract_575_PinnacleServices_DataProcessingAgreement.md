## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide data processing and related Services to Client for the **Contract Value** of **$1,910,768** over the Term (as defined in **Section 4.1**), in accordance with the terms of this Agreement and any associated Master Agreement or statement of work (“SOW”). In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and the Master Agreement regarding data protection or processing, this Agreement shall control to the extent of such conflict.

2.1.2 Vendor’s Services primarily consist of:  
(a) secure hosting of Client Personal Data in Vendor Systems;  
(b) processing, transformation, and aggregation of data to support Client’s analytics, reporting, and operational needs;  
(c) providing secure access interfaces (e.g., APIs, web portals, dashboards) to Client and its authorized users;  
(d) backup, disaster recovery, and data resilience services; and  
(e) support and maintenance services.

### 2.2 Detailed Description of Services

2.2.1 **Data Hosting and Storage.** Vendor shall host Client Personal Data on Vendor Systems or on environments provided by Vendor’s approved Subprocessors. Hosting shall include:  
(a) storage of structured and unstructured data repositories;  
(b) redundancy across at least two distinct data centers or availability zones;  
(c) implementation of Security Measures to protect Client Personal Data in transit and at rest; and  
(d) scheduled backups in accordance with **Section 11.4**.

2.2.2 **Data Processing and Transformation.** Vendor shall perform data processing operations, including:  
(a) ingestion of Client Personal Data from Client’s systems via secure transfer mechanisms (e.g., SFTP, HTTPS, VPN);  
(b) normalization, cleansing, and transformation of data to agreed data models;  
(c) generation of aggregated and anonymized datasets and reports as may be requested by Client, provided such aggregation or anonymization complies with Applicable Law;  
(d) execution of business rules, filters, and enrichment logic as documented in the relevant SOW; and  
(e) generation of Deliverables such as dashboards, scheduled reports, and data extracts.

2.2.3 **Access and Interface Services.** Vendor shall provide Client and its authorized users with access to Vendor’s web-based portals and APIs, enabling Client to:  
(a) query and retrieve data;  
(b) configure analytics and reporting;  
(c) manage user accounts and permissions; and  
(d) monitor system performance and usage metrics.

2.2.4 **Support and Maintenance Services.** Vendor shall provide support and maintenance in accordance with **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**, including:  
(a) a helpdesk or ticketing system for support requests;  
(b) defined response and resolution times;  
(c) system monitoring and performance optimization; and  
(d) routine maintenance, patching, and updates, scheduled to minimize impact on Client’s operations.

2.2.5 **Implementation and Onboarding.** Vendor shall cooperate with Client to implement the Services, including:  
(a) initial data migration from Client’s legacy systems;  
(b) configuration of Vendor Systems to Client’s specifications;  
(c) user training for Client’s designated personnel, as reasonably requested; and  
(d) acceptance testing as described in the applicable SOW.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications, and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards.

2.3.2 Vendor shall comply with the service levels detailed in **Article XII**, including any uptime commitments, incident response times, and performance metrics, subject to the remedies and exclusions therein.

2.3.3 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid and minimize any disruption to the Services. Planned maintenance windows that may affect availability shall be communicated to Client in accordance with **Section 12.4**.

### 2.4 Service Locations and Data Transfers

2.4.1 The primary data processing and hosting locations shall be within the United States, unless otherwise agreed in writing by Client. Vendor shall not transfer Client Personal Data outside of the country or region specified by Client (if any) without Client’s prior written authorization and subject to appropriate data transfer safeguards under Applicable Law.

2.4.2 To the extent that GDPR applies, any transfer of Client Personal Data from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland to a third country shall be governed by appropriate transfer mechanisms (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses or other lawful transfer tools) as further described in **Section 11.7**.

### 2.5 Subprocessors

2.5.1 Vendor may engage Subprocessors to assist in the performance of the Services, provided that:  
(a) Vendor enters into written agreements with Subprocessors that impose data protection obligations no less protective than those contained in this Agreement;  
(b) Vendor remains fully liable to Client for the performance of such Subprocessors; and  
(c) Vendor provides Client with a list of current Subprocessors upon request.

2.5.2 Vendor shall provide prior written notice (which may be by email or via a client portal) of any intended addition or replacement of Subprocessors that will process Client Personal Data. Client may reasonably object to such change on legitimate data protection grounds within thirty (30) days of such notice. In such case, the Parties shall discuss in good faith to address Client’s concerns; if no resolution is reached, Client may, as its sole and exclusive remedy, terminate the portion of the Services affected by the objectioned Subprocessor on thirty (30) days’ written notice without penalty.

### 2.6 No Sale of Personal Data

2.6.1 Vendor shall not “sell” or “share” (as those terms are defined under CCPA) Client Personal Data, nor retain, use, or disclose Client Personal Data for any purpose other than to perform the Business Purpose, except as otherwise permitted by Applicable Law.

2.6.2 Vendor certifies that it understands and shall comply with the obligations and restrictions imposed on “service providers” and “contractors” under CCPA, to the extent applicable.

3.2.1 Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the schedule set forth in the applicable SOW or order form. Unless otherwise specified, recurring fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears, and implementation fees shall be invoiced upon completion of agreed milestones.

### 3.3 Late Payments

3.3.1 Any undisputed amounts not paid by the due date may accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of:

3.3.2 Vendor shall not exercise its right to suspend the Services for late payment under this **Section 3.3** unless:  
(a) Vendor has provided Client with at least fifteen (15) days’ prior written notice; and  
(b) Client fails to pay the undisputed outstanding amounts within such notice period.

3.4.1 All fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or other taxes, duties, or governmental charges (collectively, “**Taxes**”), except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income. Client shall be responsible for payment of all applicable Taxes, which may be separately stated on Vendor’s invoices.

3.4.2 If Client is required by law to withhold any Taxes from fees payable to Vendor, Client shall provide Vendor with reasonable documentation of such withholding and shall cooperate with Vendor in obtaining any available exemptions or reductions.

### 3.5 Expenses and Reimbursement

3.5.1 Vendor shall be responsible for all ordinary and necessary business expenses incurred in providing the Services, unless otherwise expressly stated in an SOW.

3.5.2 Any out-of-pocket expenses reasonably incurred by Vendor at Client’s written request (e.g., travel, lodging, special materials) shall be reimbursed by Client at cost, provided that Vendor obtains Client’s prior written approval for any single expense in excess of $1,000.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **May 04, 2028** (the “**Initial Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**.

4.1.2 Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement may be renewed for additional one (1) year renewal terms (each, a “**Renewal Term**”) upon the Parties’ mutual written agreement at least sixty (60) days prior to the expiration of the then-current Term. The Initial Term together with any Renewal Terms shall be referred to as the “**Term**.”

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor, reflecting Client-friendly flexibility consistent with the low risk level of this Agreement.

4.2.2 Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client, provided that Vendor shall reasonably cooperate with Client to transition Services in accordance with **Section 4.5**.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.3.2 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the following shall constitute material breaches:  
(a) Vendor’s repeated or material failure to meet the service levels in **Article XII**, resulting in substantial adverse impact on Client’s operations;  
(b) Vendor’s unauthorized sale, sharing, or disclosure of Client Personal Data in violation of this Agreement;  
(c) Client’s failure to pay undisputed amounts that remain unpaid for more than ninety (90) days after the due date; and  
(d) either Party’s violation of Applicable Law relating to data protection or privacy that materially affects the other Party.

### 4.4 Termination for Insolvency

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
(a) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they become due;  
(b) files a petition or has a petition filed against it under bankruptcy or insolvency laws and such petition is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or  
(c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or is placed in the hands of a receiver or trustee.

### 4.5 Effects of Termination

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Vendor shall cease processing Client Personal Data except as necessary to perform any agreed transition or data return/destruction activities;  
(b) all rights and licenses granted to Vendor by Client shall terminate, except as expressly provided otherwise in this Agreement; and  
(c) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination.

4.5.2 **Data Return and Deletion.** Within thirty (30) days following the effective date of expiration or termination, Client may request in writing that Vendor:  
(a) return to Client a copy of all Client Personal Data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format; and/or  
(b) securely delete or destroy all Client Personal Data in Vendor’s possession or control, except to the extent retention is required by Applicable Law.  
Vendor shall provide written certification of such deletion or destruction upon Client’s request.

4.5.3 Any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination (including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI** and any related payment obligations) shall survive in accordance with their terms.

5.1.1 “**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (the “**Receiving Party**”), whether orally or in writing, and whether before or after the Effective Date, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information or the circumstances of disclosure.

(a) Client Personal Data;  
(b) business plans, strategies, forecasts, financial information, and pricing;  
(c) technical information, system designs, architectures, software, and documentation;  
(d) security policies and procedures; and  
(e) trade secrets and know-how.

### 5.2 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
(b) not disclose Confidential Information to any Third Party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement; and  
(c) protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but no less than reasonable care.

5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement.

### 5.3 Exceptions

5.3.1 Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes generally known to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction on disclosure prior to receipt from the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
(d) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a Third Party without duty of confidentiality.

5.4.1 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law or court order, provided that, where legally permissible, the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement and reasonably cooperates with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.5.1 The confidentiality obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and survive for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Client Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law or is considered Personal Data under Applicable Law, respectively.

  - Initial response: within 4 hours

## FORCE MAJEURE

### 15.1 Definition

15.1.1 A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control, including:  
(a) acts of God, natural disasters (e.g., floods, earthquakes, storms);  
(b) war, terrorism, civil unrest, or acts of public enemies;  
(c) strikes, labor disputes, or industrial actions (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce);  
(d) governmental actions, embargoes, or blockades;  
(e) widespread Internet or telecommunications failures; and  
(f) pandemics or epidemics and related government-imposed restrictions.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event that materially affects its ability to perform its obligations, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension and Termination

15.3.1 The obligations of the affected Party (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event to the extent they are affected by such event.

15.3.2 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Services upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without penalty.
