## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**

2.1.1 This Work Order is executed **under and subject to** the terms of the Parent Agreement, including, without limitation, **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** and **Article XIII (Change Management)**. Except as expressly supplemented or modified herein, the provisions of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect and are hereby incorporated by reference.

2.1.2 The Additional Services and Additional Deliverables defined in this Article II constitute an extension of the Project described in **Section 2.1 (Overview of Project)** of the Parent Agreement. In particular, this Work Order builds upon the outcomes and Deliverables of:
- **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements**,  
- **Phase 2 – Solution Design**,  
- **Phase 3 – Configuration and Build**,  
- **Phase 4 – Testing and Deployment**,  
- **Phase 5 – Knowledge Transfer and Training**, and  
- **Phase 6 – Managed Support**,  

as set out in **Section 2.2(a)–(f)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.1.3 This Work Order adds scope, but does not alter the fundamental nature of the Services, the Parties’ responsibilities, or the standards of performance set forth in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** and **Section 2.6 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, except as expressly stated herein.

2.2 **Overview of Additional Services.**

Vendor shall provide the following Additional Services under this Work Order:

2.2.1 **Global Roll‑Out Preparation.**  
Building on the “Solution Design Document”, “Integration Design”, “Data Migration Plan”, and other Deliverables defined in **Section 2.2(b) and (c) of the Parent Agreement**, Vendor shall:

(a) Analyse and document regional business and regulatory requirements for each Roll‑Out Region;  
(b) Prepare regional solution design addenda, including localisation for language, currency, and regional data privacy requirements;  
(c) Update the Test Strategy and High‑Level Test Plan developed under **Section 2.2(b)(v)** of the Parent Agreement to address multi‑region testing; and  
(d) Define Operational Readiness Criteria for each Roll‑Out Region and obtain Client approval.

2.2.2 **Regional CRM Roll‑Outs (Europe ex‑UK, North America, Asia‑Pacific).**  
For each Roll‑Out Region, Vendor shall:

(a) Configure and extend the CRM platform, based on the “Configured CRM Environment” and “Custom Workflow and Automation Scripts” defined in **Section 2.2(c)** of the Parent Agreement, to reflect regional sales, marketing, and support processes;  
(b) Implement Localisation Enhancements, including regional currencies, time zones, language packs (where supported by the underlying platform), and regional regulatory compliance configurations;  
(c) Design and configure regional dashboards and reports, leveraging the reporting capabilities established in the original Project;  
(d) Plan and execute regional data migration activities, including cleansing and mapping, in accordance with the “Data Migration Plan and Mapping Specifications” from **Section 2.2(b)(v)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(e) Conduct system, integration, and user acceptance testing for each region, in line with **Phase 4 – Testing and Deployment** under **Section 2.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(f) Support regional go‑live events, including cutover support for no less than three (3) weeks per region; and  
(g) Produce region‑specific documentation and knowledge transfer materials as described in **Section 2.3** below.

2.2.3 **Advanced Analytics Workstream.**  
Vendor shall design and implement an Advanced Analytics Workstream layered on top of the core CRM solution, including:

(a) A CRM analytics architecture aligned with Client’s enterprise analytics standards;  
(b) Data models and ETL (extract, transform, load) specifications integrating CRM data with existing data warehouses and marketing systems identified under **Section 2.1.1(iv) of the Parent Agreement**;  
(c) A set of standardised dashboards and KPIs covering lead conversion, pipeline health, customer lifetime value, marketing campaign effectiveness, and support case resolution performance;  
(d) Optional predictive modelling templates for lead scoring and churn risk (using platform-native or Client‑provided tools only; no separate license procurement is included unless agreed via WO Change Request); and  
(e) Documentation and training for Client’s business analytics and CRM teams on how to use and extend the analytics artefacts.

2.2.4 **Extended Support Services.**  
Vendor shall provide Extended Support Services that:

(a) Continue and expand the Managed Support Services set out in **Section 2.2(f) (Phase 6 – Managed Support)** and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement, for the Extended Term,  
(b) Cover all Roll‑Out Regions and Additional Deliverables under this Work Order;  
(c) Include second‑line and third‑line support, minor configuration changes, user management, and periodic health checks, consistent with **Section 2.2(f)(i)–(iii)** of the Parent Agreement; and  
(d) Provide the Enhanced SLA Commitments described in **Article XII of this Work Order**, which shall be no less favourable to Client than the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII of the Parent Agreement**.

2.3 **Additional Deliverables.**

Without limiting the definition of Deliverables in **Section 1.7 of the Parent Agreement**, Vendor shall provide, at a minimum, the following Additional Deliverables under this Work Order:

2.3.1 **Global Roll‑Out Preparation Deliverables.**

(a) Regional Requirements Addenda (one per Roll‑Out Region);  
(b) Regional Solution Design Addenda, including localisation specifications;  
(c) Updated Test Strategy and High‑Level Test Plan (multi‑region); and  
(d) Documented Operational Readiness Criteria and sign‑off templates.

2.3.2 **Regional Roll‑Out Deliverables.**

For each Roll‑Out Region:

(a) Configured Regional CRM Environment (including Dev, Test, and UAT instances where applicable);  
(b) Updated integration configurations and mapping for regional systems;  
(c) Regional data migration execution plan and completion report;  
(d) Test Cases, Test Results Summary, and Defect Logs specific to the region;  
(e) Regional Deployment Runbook and Rollback Plan; and  
(f) Regional Go‑Live Summary and Lessons Learned report.

2.3.3 **Advanced Analytics Deliverables.**

(a) CRM Analytics Architecture Document;  
(b) Analytics Data Model and ETL Specifications;  
(c) A minimum of eight (8) standard dashboards, with definitions for metrics and KPIs;  
(d) Lead Scoring and Churn Risk Model Specifications (where agreed); and  
(e) Analytics User Guide and Training Materials.

2.3.4 **Extended Support Deliverables.**

(a) Monthly Support and Incident Reports for the Extended Term, building on those described in **Section 2.2(f)(iv)** of the Parent Agreement;  
(b) Quarterly Service Review Reports including region‑specific performance and recommendations;  
(c) Updated Configuration Documentation reflecting changes performed as part of Extended Support Services; and  
(d) An End‑of‑Term Support Summary and Transition Report, as further detailed in **Section 4.5**.

2.4 **Timelines and WO Milestones.**

2.4.1 The high‑level schedule for the Additional Services is as follows (subject to adjustment via WO Change Requests consistent with **Article XIII** of this Work Order and **Article XIII of the Parent Agreement**):

(a) Global Roll‑Out Preparation: WO Months 1–3 from the WO Effective Date;  
(b) Europe ex‑UK Roll‑Out: WO Months 4–9;  
(c) North America Roll‑Out: WO Months 10–15;  
(d) Asia‑Pacific Roll‑Out: WO Months 16–22;  
(e) Advanced Analytics Workstream: WO Months 6–18 (overlapping with regional roll‑outs);  
(f) Extended Support Services: From the completion of the final regional go‑live until the WO Expiration Date.

2.4.2 The Parties shall, within twenty (20) Business Days of the WO Effective Date, agree in writing on a detailed WO project plan, including WO Milestones and associated dates, which shall be incorporated by reference into this Work Order and deemed an update to the project plan contemplated in **Section 2.3.2 of the Parent Agreement**.

2.4.3 WO Milestones shall include, at a minimum:

(a) Completion and acceptance of Global Roll‑Out Preparation Deliverables;  
(b) Operational Readiness sign‑off and go‑live for each Roll‑Out Region;  
(c) Initial deployment of core analytics dashboards;  
(d) Completion of Advanced Analytics Workstream; and  
(e) Quarterly service review checkpoints during the Extended Support Services.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**

The Client responsibilities in **Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement** apply equally to this Work Order. In addition, Client shall:

2.5.1 Designate regional business owners and subject matter experts for each Roll‑Out Region to participate in workshops, testing, and training;

2.5.2 Provide timely access to regional systems, data sources, and local IT teams as reasonably required for regional integrations and data migration;

2.5.3 Perform regional data cleansing activities and user acceptance testing in accordance with Vendor’s guidance and the agreed WO project plan;

2.5.4 Confirm regional Operational Readiness Criteria and provide timely approvals or reasons for non‑acceptance, in accordance with the acceptance procedures described in **Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement**; and

2.5.5 Ensure that any third‑party vendors under Client’s control cooperate with Vendor, to the extent reasonably necessary, in the execution of this Work Order.

2.6 **Performance Standards and Acceptance.**

2.6.1 Vendor shall perform the Additional Services:

(a) With due skill, care, and diligence, in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with the standards set out in **Section 2.5.1 of the Parent Agreement**;  
(b) In accordance with the Specifications, including the regional and analytics specifications agreed under this Work Order; and  
(c) In compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including Data Protection Laws and any region‑specific regulations applicable to the Roll‑Out Regions.

2.6.2 Acceptance of Additional Deliverables shall follow the procedures set out in **Section 2.6 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, provided that the Review Period shall be **ten (10) Business Days**, unless otherwise agreed in writing for a specific Deliverable.

2.6.3 Any dispute regarding acceptance of a Deliverable shall be escalated and resolved in accordance with **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XIV of this Work Order**.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Work Order Contract Value and Fee Structure.**

3.1.1 The total not‑to‑exceed value of this Work Order is **USD $662,757** (the **“Work Order Contract Value”**), exclusive of any applicable taxes.

(a) **Fixed‑Fee Components** for Global Roll‑Out Preparation, Regional Roll‑Outs, and Advanced Analytics Workstream, in an aggregate amount of **USD $472,000**; and  
(b) **Extended Support Services (Time‑and‑Materials)** in an estimated, capped amount of **USD $190,757** over the Extended Term, based on agreed hourly rates and estimated utilisation of hours.

3.1.3 The Fee structure in this Work Order supplements, and shall be read consistently with, **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement, except that where this Work Order expressly specifies different payment terms or caps, this Work Order shall prevail for the Additional Services.

(a) Global Roll‑Out Preparation: USD $78,000  
(b) Europe ex‑UK Roll‑Out: USD $148,000

(d) Asia‑Pacific Roll‑Out: USD $60,000  
(e) Advanced Analytics Workstream: USD $40,000  

Total Fixed‑Fee: **USD $472,000**

3.2.2 **Extended Support Services (Time‑and‑Materials):**

(a) Standard Support Hours (Business Hours), consistent with **Section 3.2.2(a) of the Parent Agreement**:

- Senior Consultant: USD $190/hour  
- Consultant: USD $150/hour  
- Support Engineer: USD $120/hour  

(b) After‑Hours or Emergency Support, if requested and pre‑approved by Client, consistent with **Section 3.2.2(b) of the Parent Agreement**:

- Senior Consultant: USD $250/hour  
- Consultant: USD $200/hour  
- Support Engineer: USD $160/hour  

(c) The Parties estimate an average of **65 billable hours per month** over the Extended Support Services period, resulting in an estimated total of **USD $190,757**, which shall constitute a not‑to‑exceed cap for Extended Support Services under this Work Order, unless increased via WO Change Request and Change Order.

3.3 **Payment Milestones for Fixed‑Fee Components.**

3.3.1 Fixed‑fee components shall be invoiced upon completion of specified WO Milestones, as follows:

(a) **Global Roll‑Out Preparation (USD $78,000):**  
   - 40% upon completion and acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of Global Roll‑Out Preparation Deliverables;  
   - 60% upon Client’s confirmation of readiness to commence the first regional roll‑out.

(b) **Europe ex‑UK Roll‑Out (USD $148,000):**  
   - 30% upon commencement of configuration for Europe ex‑UK;  
   - 40% upon completion and acceptance of regional testing (system, integration, and UAT);  
   - 30% upon successful go‑live and acceptance of post go‑live support summary.

(c) **North America Roll‑Out (USD $146,000):**  
   - 30% upon commencement of configuration for North America;  
   - 40% upon completion and acceptance of regional testing;  
   - 30% upon successful go‑live and acceptance of post go‑live support summary.

(d) **Asia‑Pacific Roll‑Out (USD $60,000):**  
   - 40% upon commencement of configuration for Asia‑Pacific;  
   - 60% upon successful go‑live and acceptance of regional Go‑Live Summary and Lessons Learned report.

(e) **Advanced Analytics Workstream (USD $40,000):**  
   - 25% upon approval of CRM Analytics Architecture Document;  
   - 25% upon delivery of initial data models and ETL specifications;  
   - 25% upon deployment of core dashboards into production;  
   - 25% upon completion of analytics training and acceptance of Analytics User Guide.

3.3.2 For the avoidance of doubt, the invoicing pattern for fixed‑fee components under this Work Order is separate from, and in addition to, the payment milestones set out in **Section 3.3.1 of the Parent Agreement**, and shall not affect any payment obligations arising under the Parent Agreement.

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**

3.4.1 Vendor shall invoice Client for Fixed‑Fee components upon achievement of the relevant Milestones described in **Section 3.3**.

3.4.2 Extended Support Services shall be invoiced **monthly in arrears** based on actual hours worked, supported by timesheets reasonably acceptable to Client, consistent with **Section 3.3.2 of the Parent Agreement**.

This Net 45 term is specific to this Work Order and supersedes the Net 30 payment term set out in **Section 3.4.1 of the Parent Agreement** for invoices issued under this Work Order.

3.4.4 If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within **ten (10) Business Days** of receipt of the invoice, specifying the amounts disputed and the reasons therefor, consistent with **Section 3.4.2 of the Parent Agreement**. Client shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.4.3 while the Parties work in good faith to resolve the dispute.

3.5 **Late Payment.**

3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due under this Work Order shall accrue interest at the rate specified in **Section 3.5.1 of the Parent Agreement** (2% per month or the maximum rate permissible under applicable law), calculated on a daily basis from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.5.2 Vendor may exercise its right to suspend performance of the Additional Services under this Work Order in accordance with **Section 3.5.2 of the Parent Agreement**, provided that Vendor resumes performance promptly upon receipt of the overdue payment.

3.6 **Taxes.**

3.6.1 All Fees under this Work Order are exclusive of VAT and any other applicable taxes. Client shall be responsible for such taxes in accordance with **Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement**.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

3.7.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre‑approved travel and out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Additional Services (including travel required for regional roll‑outs), in accordance with **Section 3.7 of the Parent Agreement** and Client’s travel policy.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term of Work Order.**

4.1.1 This Work Order shall commence on the WO Effective Date and shall continue in effect until the WO Expiration Date, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Article IV.

4.1.2 For the avoidance of doubt, the Term of this Work Order is partially concurrent with, and partially extends beyond, the Term of the Parent Agreement as defined in **Section 4.1.1 of the Parent Agreement**.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.**

4.2.1 The Parties acknowledge that, unless otherwise agreed in writing, the termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement shall automatically result in the termination of this Work Order with effect from the same effective date of termination or expiration, without the need for any additional notice, subject to **Section 4.5** below.

4.2.2 If the Parties wish to maintain this Work Order in effect beyond the Term of the Parent Agreement, they shall execute an amendment to the Parent Agreement or a replacement master agreement that expressly continues or re‑establishes the contractual framework for this Work Order.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) calendar days’** prior written notice to the other Party. This 30‑day period reflects a Client‑friendly approach consistent with the low risk classification of this Work Order and is without prejudice to the longer notice period in **Section 4.3.1 of the Parent Agreement** (45 days) with respect to the Parent Agreement itself.

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client under this Work Order:

(a) Client shall pay Vendor all Fees and approved expenses incurred and properly invoiced up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) For fixed‑fee components, Client shall pay a pro rata portion of any fixed‑fee components for work performed but not yet invoiced, based on the percentage of completion of the relevant Milestones, consistent with **Section 4.3.2 of the Parent Agreement**; and  
(c) Client shall reimburse Vendor for any non‑cancellable third‑party costs reasonably incurred on Client’s behalf in connection with this Work Order.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) Materially breaches any provision of this Work Order or the Parent Agreement as applied to this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days after receiving written notice thereof; or  
(b) Experiences an insolvency event as described in **Section 4.4.1(b) of the Parent Agreement**.

4.4.2 Termination for cause of this Work Order shall not, by itself, terminate the Parent Agreement, unless the underlying breach also entitles the terminating Party to terminate the Parent Agreement under **Article IV of the Parent Agreement**.

4.5 **Effect of Termination; Transition Activities.**

4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Work Order:

(a) Vendor shall cease performing the Additional Services, except as reasonably necessary to effect an orderly wind‑down or to complete agreed Transition Activities;  
(b) Vendor shall deliver to Client all completed Additional Deliverables and any partially completed work for which Client has paid;  
(c) Client shall pay Vendor for all Additional Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, in accordance with **Section 4.3.2 or 4.4 of the Parent Agreement**, as applicable; and  
(d) Each Party shall return or destroy Confidential Information in accordance with **Article V of the Parent Agreement** and **Article V of this Work Order**.

4.5.2 Upon Client’s request made prior to the effective date of termination or expiration of this Work Order, Vendor shall provide Transition Activities for up to **sixty (60) calendar days**, in line with **Section 4.6.1 (Transition Assistance) of the Parent Agreement**, at Vendor’s then‑current time‑and‑materials rates or rates otherwise agreed in writing.

4.6 **Survival.**

4.6.1 The provisions of this Work Order that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to Articles III (to the extent of accrued payment obligations), V, VI, VII (to the extent of disclaimers), VIII, IX, X, XI, XII, XIV, XV, and XVI, shall survive such termination or expiration, together with the corresponding provisions of the Parent Agreement.
