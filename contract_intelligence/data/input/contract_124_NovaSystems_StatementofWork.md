## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This SOW is executed under and subject to the terms of the Parent Agreement, Reference No. **CSA-NOV-202312-100** (contract_100). Pursuant to **Section 1.18 (Statement of Work)** and **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement, this SOW describes particular Services, Deliverables, scope, schedule, milestones, Service Levels, and Fees.  

2.1.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the terms of this SOW and the Parent Agreement, the order of precedence in **Section 16.13 (Order of Precedence)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply; provided, however, that for the limited purpose of the Services under this SOW, the **liability cap and risk allocations** set forth in **Article IX** of this SOW shall govern and are expressly intended to supersede the uncapped liability provisions in **Article IX of the Parent Agreement** to the extent of any conflict.

2.2 **Overall Project Description.**  

Vendor shall provide an end-to-end ERP and digital transformation program for Client, leveraging the general categories of Services enumerated in **Section 2.2** of the Parent Agreement, specifically:

- Enterprise architecture assessment and roadmap design (**Section 2.2.1**);  
- Business requirements gathering and documentation (**Section 2.2.2**);  
- Design, configuration, and implementation of ERP and CRM applications (**Section 2.2.3**);  
- Migration of legacy systems and data to cloud platforms (**Section 2.2.4**);  
- Development of custom modules, dashboards, and reports (**Section 2.2.5**);  
- Project management services (**Section 2.2.6**);  
- Training and knowledge transfer (**Section 2.2.7**); and  
- Post-implementation support (**Section 2.2.8**).

The Project will modernize Client’s finance, supply chain, sales, and customer service processes by implementing a cloud-based ERP/CRM solution integrated with Client’s existing data warehouse and analytics tools.

2.3 **Detailed Services and Deliverables.**  

2.3.1 **Phase 1 – Assessment and Solution Design (Estimated: March 2024 – June 2024)**  

(a) **Services:**

- Conduct enterprise architecture assessment of current systems, including core ERP, CRM, legacy line-of-business systems, and integrations, consistent with **Section 2.2.1** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Facilitate requirements workshops with finance, operations, sales, and IT stakeholders (referencing Client’s responsibilities in **Section 2.5.2** of the Parent Agreement).  
- Define future-state process maps and target application architecture.  
- Prepare an implementation roadmap, including sequence of releases, environments strategy, and high‑level resource plan.

(b) **Deliverables:**

- Current-state assessment report (in line with **Section 2.3.1** of the Parent Agreement).  
- Future-state architecture diagrams and solution blueprints (**Section 2.3.2**).  
- High-level implementation roadmap and risk register.  
- Requirements traceability matrix linking business requirements to proposed solution components.

2.3.2 **Phase 2 – Configuration and Build (Estimated: July 2024 – January 2025)**  

(a) **Services:**

- Configure ERP modules (General Ledger, Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, Procurement, Inventory, Order Management) and CRM modules (Lead Management, Opportunity Management, Case Management) per Client-approved requirements.  
- Design and build integrations with Client’s data warehouse and external systems using APIs and middleware, pursuant to **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Develop up to twenty (20) custom reports and dashboards and up to ten (10) custom workflow extensions.  
- Establish Testing, Staging, and Production environments in Client’s chosen cloud infrastructure.

(b) **Deliverables:**

- Configured ERP and CRM environments, documented configuration workbooks.  
- Integration design documents and implemented integration services.  
- Custom reports, dashboards, and extensions with technical and user documentation.  
- Data migration strategy and mapping documents (**Section 2.3.4** of the Parent Agreement).

2.3.3 **Phase 3 – Data Migration, Testing, and Go-Live (Estimated: February 2025 – July 2025)**  

(a) **Services:**

- Design and execute data migration scripts, including up to three (3) trial migrations and one (1) production migration.  
- Develop and execute system integration testing and user acceptance testing plans, consistent with **Section 2.3.5** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Coordinate cutover planning and Go-Live readiness assessments.  
- Provide hypercare support for four (4) weeks following Go-Live.

(b) **Deliverables:**

- Data migration scripts, mapping documents, and validation reports (**Section 2.3.4**).  
- Test strategy, test plans, test cases, and summarized test results (**Section 2.3.5**).  
- Cutover plan, Go-Live checklist, and signed Go-Live readiness report.  
- Hypercare incident summary and stabilization report.

2.3.4 **Phase 4 – Post-Implementation Support and Optimization (Estimated: August 2025 – February 2026)**  

(a) **Services:**

- Provide application and integration support in accordance with **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement and the SLAs in Article XII of this SOW.  
- Undertake minor configuration changes and enhancements (up to an agreed T&M cap) to address post‑Go-Live optimization requests.  
- Conduct two (2) optimization workshops and one (1) annual health check.  
- Provide ongoing knowledge transfer and documentation updates.

(b) **Deliverables:**

- Monthly SLA and performance reports (**Section 2.3.7** of the Parent Agreement).  
- Updated configuration documentation and operational runbooks.  
- Workshop materials and optimization recommendations.  
- Updated user guides and training materials (**Section 2.3.6**).

2.4 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  

Vendor shall deliver a comprehensive training program consistent with **Section 2.2.7** of the Parent Agreement, including:

- “Train-the-trainer” sessions for Client’s super users across finance, operations, and sales;  
- End‑user training sessions (remote and on-site) as mutually agreed;  
- Provision of user manuals, quick reference guides, and e-learning content; and  
- Knowledge transfer sessions to Client’s IT team for ongoing administration.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

Consistent with **Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement**, Vendor shall:

- Perform Services in a professional and workmanlike manner;  
- Use personnel adequately skilled and experienced for their responsibilities;  
- Meet the timelines and Milestones set forth in this SOW, subject to Client’s cooperation and any Change Orders; and  
- Comply with all Applicable Laws.

2.6 **Milestones and High-Level Schedule.**  

The Parties anticipate the following Milestones (subject to adjustment via Change Order):

- **Milestone 1 – Completion of Assessment & Design**: Delivery and Client acceptance of assessment report, future-state architecture, and implementation roadmap. Target: June 30, 2024.  
- **Milestone 2 – Completion of Core Configuration**: Configured ERP/CRM environments and integration designs delivered and approved. Target: January 31, 2025.  
- **Milestone 3 – Successful Completion of UAT and Go-Live**: Client sign-off on UAT and achievement of Go-Live. Target: July 31, 2025.  
- **Milestone 4 – Completion of Post-Implementation Support Period**: Completion of Production Support Period and delivery of optimization recommendations. Target: February 1, 2026.

Acceptance of Milestones shall follow the process in **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, unless modified in writing by the Parties.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  

In addition to the obligations under **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

- Assign a Client Project Manager with decision-making authority;  
- Ensure timely availability of key stakeholders for workshops, reviews, and approvals;  
- Provide access to relevant systems, data, and documentation;  
- Provide or procure necessary third-party licenses required for the Project, consistent with **Section 2.5.5** of the Parent Agreement; and  
- Complete agreed tasks and approvals within timelines specified in the project plan.

2.8 **Subcontractors.**  

Vendor may use subcontractors as permitted under **Section 2.8** of the Parent Agreement, provided Vendor remains responsible for their performance. Vendor shall disclose, upon Client’s request, the primary subcontractors engaged for material portions of the Project.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value Under This SOW.**  

The Parties acknowledge that the total estimated contract value for this SOW is **US$4,295,126** (the “SOW Fees”), which constitutes part of, and is consistent with, the overall contract value structure contemplated in **Section 3.1** of the Parent Agreement, and which may be allocated among fixed‑fee, T&M, and retainer-based components as set forth below.

3.2 **Fee Structure for This SOW.**  

Consistent with **Section 3.2** and **3.3** of the Parent Agreement, the SOW Fees shall be structured as follows (amounts are exclusive of Taxes and reimbursable expenses):

- **Phase 1 – Assessment & Design (Fixed Fee):** US$450,000  
- **Phase 2 – Configuration & Build (Mixed Fixed + T&M Cap):**  
  - Fixed component: US$1,700,000  
  - T&M component (capped): up to US$600,000  
- **Phase 3 – Data Migration, Testing & Go-Live (Fixed Fee):** US$900,000  
- **Phase 4 – Post-Implementation Support & Optimization (Retainer):**  
  - Monthly retainer (Aug 2025 – Jan 2026, 6 months): US$74,187.67/month (approx.)  
  - Total retainer: US$445,126  

Total estimated Fees: **US$4,295,126**.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Milestones.**  

3.3.1 **Invoicing.**  

(a) Phase 1 fixed fee shall be invoiced 40% upon SOW Effective Date, 40% upon completion of assessment workshops, and 20% upon Client’s acceptance of the Phase 1 Deliverables.  

(b) Phase 2 fixed component shall be invoiced 30% upon configuration design sign-off, 40% upon completion of initial build, and 30% upon completion of system integration testing. The T&M component shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on actual hours incurred, not to exceed the cap absent a Change Order.  

(c) Phase 3 fixed fee shall be invoiced 30% upon approval of data migration design, 40% upon successful completion of UAT, and 30% upon Go-Live.  

(d) Phase 4 monthly retainer shall be invoiced monthly in advance during the Production Support Period.

3.3.2 **Payment Terms.**  

Notwithstanding **Section 3.5 (Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement (which specifies Net 30), the Parties agree that, for this SOW only, Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **Net 45** days of the invoice date. This SOW-specific term is intended to supplement and, solely for this SOW, supersede the Payment Terms in the Parent Agreement pursuant to **Section 16.13** thereof.

3.4 **Late Payments; Interest.**  

Late payment provisions in **Section 3.6** of the Parent Agreement shall apply, except that Vendor shall not suspend Services under this SOW unless: (a) any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice; and (b) the Parties have attempted in good faith to resolve the non-payment via the dispute resolution steps in **Section 3.7** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XIV** of this SOW.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  

Client may withhold disputed amounts in accordance with **Section 3.7** of the Parent Agreement, provided Client pays undisputed portions when due and supplies written notice of the dispute with reasonable detail.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.8** of the Parent Agreement. The SOW Fees are exclusive of all applicable Taxes.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

Reimbursable expenses shall be governed by **Section 3.9** of the Parent Agreement. In addition, for this SOW:

- Vendor shall obtain Client’s written pre‑approval for any individual travel expense projected to exceed US$2,500.  
- Vendor’s use of business-class airfare requires prior written approval by Client’s Client Project Manager.  
- Vendor shall submit itemized expense reports with supporting documentation; Client shall reimburse approved expenses within Net 45 days.

3.8 **Price Adjustments.**  

The annual rate adjustment provisions in **Section 3.10** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to T&M rates and retainer fees under this SOW, except that no price increase shall take effect prior to January 1, 2025.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term of SOW.**  

This SOW shall commence on the SOW Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent Agreement, shall continue in effect until the SOW Expiration Date of **February 1, 2026** (the “SOW Term”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  

Any extension of the SOW Term, including extended support or additional phases, shall be subject to mutual written agreement and may be documented via an amendment or new SOW pursuant to **Section 4.2** of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Notwithstanding **Section 4.3 (Termination for Convenience – Client)** of the Parent Agreement (which provides for 180 days’ notice), Client may terminate this SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Vendor.  

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this SOW for convenience in accordance with **Section 4.4** of the Parent Agreement, upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

Termination for cause shall be governed by **Section 4.5** of the Parent Agreement, including for uncured material breach or non-payment, provided that prior to exercising termination rights as to this SOW, the Parties shall follow the dispute escalation process in **Article XIV** of the Parent Agreement and this SOW, unless the breach is not capable of cure.

4.5 **Effect of Termination; Fees Payable.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this SOW:

- Client shall pay Vendor all Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued through the effective date of termination, including fees for work-in-progress and non-cancellable commitments, consistent with **Section 4.7.1** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Vendor shall provide an orderly wind-down and, if requested, reasonable transition assistance at mutually agreed T&M rates, consistent with **Section 4.7.2**.  
- Each Party shall handle Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 4.7.3** and **Section 5.6** of the Parent Agreement.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  

If the Parent Agreement terminates or expires for any reason:

- This SOW shall automatically terminate effective as of the effective date of termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing that certain obligations under this SOW shall continue.  
- In such event, the Parties’ respective obligations shall be as set forth in **Section 4.7 (Effect of Termination)** and **Section 4.8 (Survival)** of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented by the specific payment and wind-down provisions of this SOW.

4.7 **Survival.**  

Any provisions of this SOW which by their nature should survive expiration or termination, including without limitation the payment obligations for accrued Fees, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, and XVI, shall survive, together with the corresponding provisions of the Parent Agreement as referenced in **Section 4.8** of the Parent Agreement.
