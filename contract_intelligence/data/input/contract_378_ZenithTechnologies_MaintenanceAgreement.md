## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

(a) This Maintenance Agreement is **entered into pursuant to and in supplementation of** the Parent Agreement. It is intended to provide a detailed framework for Maintenance and Support Services related to the **Zenith Enterprise Suite** licensed under **Article II** and **Article VI** of the Parent Agreement.  

(b) The Parties acknowledge that Section **2.5 (Maintenance and Support Services)** and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement provide high-level descriptions and commitments that are further elaborated, extended, and, where expressly stated, **superseded or supplemented** by the terms of this Maintenance Agreement.  

(c) In the event of conflict between the Parent Agreement and this Maintenance Agreement with respect to maintenance- and support‑specific obligations, the terms of this Maintenance Agreement shall prevail, except where the Parent Agreement explicitly states otherwise or where a conflict relates to liability, governing law, dispute resolution, or data protection, in which case the Parent Agreement shall govern.

2.2 **General Description of Services.**  

During the Term, Vendor shall provide the following Maintenance and Support Services for the Software:

(a) **Corrective Maintenance.** Diagnosis, troubleshooting, and remediation of reproducible errors, bugs, and defects in the Software that cause it to deviate materially from the functional specifications in the Documentation referenced in Section 1.8 of the Parent Agreement. This includes:  
  (i) error isolation and root-cause analysis;  
  (ii) provision of workarounds where prompt permanent resolution is not feasible; and  
  (iii) deployment of patches, hotfixes, and Maintenance Releases to restore the Software’s material conformity with the Documentation and Service Levels.

(b) **Adaptive Maintenance.** Modifications and adjustments necessary to maintain compatibility with:  
  (i) supported operating systems, databases, browsers, and middleware;  
  (ii) third‑party components as defined in Section 1.21 of the Parent Agreement; and  
  (iii) security and compliance requirements reasonably necessary for the continued lawful operation of the Software, in each case as reasonably determined by Vendor, consistent with Section 2.5(b) of the Parent Agreement.

(c) **Enhancements and Upgrades.** Provision to Client of any generally available new versions, releases, and enhancements of the Software that Vendor makes available to its enterprise customers under standard maintenance offerings, including feature improvements and performance optimizations, as contemplated in Section 2.5(c) of the Parent Agreement, but excluding new products or separately priced modules.

(d) **Technical Support and Helpdesk.** Access to Vendor’s support services as follows:  
  (i) **Standard Support Hours:** 09:00 to 18:00 Singapore time, Monday–Friday, excluding public holidays in Singapore, for Severity 2–4 Incidents;  
  (ii) **24x7 Critical Support:** continuous support coverage for Severity 1 Incidents, in alignment with Section 2.5(d) and Section 12.3(a) of the Parent Agreement;  
  (iii) multi-channel access via email, web-based ticketing system, and telephone, as detailed in Vendor’s support handbook reasonably acceptable to Client; and  
  (iv) escalation pathways and communication protocols consistent with the High Risk Profile described in Section 2.9 of the Parent Agreement.

(e) **Preventive Maintenance and Health Checks.** Quarterly health checks on the production environment, including review of system logs, performance metrics, capacity utilization, and security posture. Vendor shall provide written reports and recommendations (Deliverables) and, upon Client’s approval, may implement recommended changes under this Maintenance Agreement or a separate SOW executed pursuant to the Parent Agreement.

(f) **Release and Change Management Support.** Assistance in planning, scheduling, and executing Software Updates and configuration changes, consistent with the conservative change and release practices contemplated by Section 2.9(a) of the Parent Agreement, including:  
  (i) coordination of staging and production deployment windows;  
  (ii) regression testing support related to Vendor-delivered Updates; and  
  (iii) rollback planning and execution for failed or problematic releases.

(g) **Knowledge Transfer and Documentation.** Provision of updated release notes, known issues lists, configuration guides, and operational runbooks for client operations teams, supplementing the Documentation as defined in Section 1.8 of the Parent Agreement.

2.3 **Service Coverage and Environments.**  

(a) Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the Maintenance and Support Services shall cover the following environments:  
  (i) one (1) production instance of the Software hosted, as applicable, in Vendor’s or its cloud provider’s infrastructure pursuant to Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement;  
  (ii) one (1) non‑production test or staging instance; and  
  (iii) any expressly designated disaster recovery instance managed by Vendor.

(b) Additional environments requested by Client (e.g., extra development, training, or sandbox environments) may be supported subject to payment of additional Fees as specified in Article III or in a separate SOW under the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  

(a) Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner in accordance with generally accepted industry standards, consistent with Section 2.3(b) and the warranty in Section 7.2(b) of the Parent Agreement.  

(b) Vendor shall meet or exceed the uptime commitments and response targets set forth in Article XII of this Maintenance Agreement and **Article XII of the Parent Agreement**, subject to Permitted Downtime as defined in Section 12.2 of the Parent Agreement and Section 12.2 of this Maintenance Agreement.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  

Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to provide the Services is dependent on Client’s timely and effective performance of its responsibilities as set out in Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement and, in addition:

(a) Client shall provide Vendor with timely access to Client Systems, logs, network paths, and relevant personnel as reasonably necessary for Incident diagnosis and remediation.

(b) Client shall promptly install or allow Vendor to deploy Updates and Maintenance Releases that are designated as **critical** or **security-related**, understanding that failure to do so may relieve Vendor from certain Service Level obligations for Incidents caused by such failure.

(c) Client shall maintain up‑to‑date contact information for designated support liaisons and escalation contacts.

2.6 **Out-of-Scope Services.**  

(a) The following are expressly excluded from the Maintenance and Support Services under this Maintenance Agreement unless separately contracted under a Statement of Work pursuant to the Parent Agreement:  
  (i) major functional enhancements or custom development beyond generally available features;  
  (ii) data migration from third‑party or legacy systems;  
  (iii) extensive training programs beyond reasonable knowledge transfer;  
  (iv) on‑site support services at Client locations; and  
  (v) development or support for third‑party applications not supplied by Vendor.

(b) Any Services outside the scope defined in this Article II shall be handled through the **Change Management** procedures in Article XIII of this Maintenance Agreement and/or Article XIII of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **High‑Risk Engagement.**  

In line with **Section 2.9 (High Risk Acknowledgment)** of the Parent Agreement, Client reaffirms the High Risk Profile of this engagement. Accordingly:

(a) Vendor may apply conservative change controls, including extended testing windows and phased rollouts.  

(b) Client shall commit adequate internal resources to risk mitigation, including business continuity planning and validation of Updates in non-production environments.  

(c) The **uncapped liability** and **inclusion of consequential damages** provisions in **Article IX of the Parent Agreement** and Article IX of this Maintenance Agreement are an integral, bargained-for aspect of the Parties’ risk allocation for Maintenance and Support Services.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  

The total contract value for Maintenance and Support Services under this Maintenance Agreement is **USD $4,886,864** (four million eight hundred eighty‑six thousand eight hundred sixty‑four United States dollars), exclusive of Taxes, for the Term, subject to adjustments pursuant to Article XIII (Change Management) of this Maintenance Agreement.

3.2 **Fee Components.**  

The Fees payable by Client to Vendor under this Maintenance Agreement consist of the following components, which are separate and in addition to any fees payable under **Article III of the Parent Agreement**:

(a) **Base Maintenance Fee.**  
Annualized base maintenance and standard support fee for the Software: **USD $1,800,000 per annum**, allocated as follows:  
  (i) Core Platform & Standard Modules (as described in Section 2.2(b)(i), (iv), (vi) of the Parent Agreement): USD $1,200,000 per annum;  
  (ii) Advanced Analytics & Compliance Module (referenced in Section 3.2(a)(i) of the Parent Agreement): USD $600,000 per annum.

(b) **Premium and Extended Support Fee.**  
An incremental **premium support surcharge** to provide 24x7 global coverage, accelerated response and resolution targets, and quarterly on‑site or virtual service reviews: **USD $1,086,864** over the Term, payable as specified in Section 3.3.

(c) **Optional Services.**  
Any optional or out‑of‑scope services requested by Client under Article II or Article XIII shall be charged on a time-and-materials basis at Vendor’s then‑current professional services rates, or as otherwise agreed in a Statement of Work executed under the Parent Agreement.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Schedule.**  

(a) **Invoicing Frequency.** Unless otherwise agreed in writing:  
  (i) Base Maintenance Fees shall be invoiced **annually in advance** in equal installments on or about each anniversary of the Effective Date; and  
  (ii) Premium and Extended Support Fees shall be invoiced **quarterly in advance**.

(b) **Payment Terms.** All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable by Client within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of invoice (**"Net 60"**), consistent with **Section 3.3(a) of the Parent Agreement**.

(c) **Disputed Amounts.** Any invoice disputes shall be handled in accordance with the principles set forth in **Section 3.5 of the Parent Agreement**, which shall apply mutatis mutandis to this Maintenance Agreement.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at the lesser of:  
  (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or  
  (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, consistent with **Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement**.

(b) Vendor may, after giving **ten (10) Business Days’** prior written notice and an opportunity to cure, suspend Maintenance and Support Services (including service ticket handling and provision of Updates) if any undisputed amounts remain past due, without prejudice to its other rights under the Parent Agreement and this Maintenance Agreement.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  

(a) Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, documented, out‑of‑pocket travel, lodging, and related expenses incurred in connection with providing on‑site Services at Client’s request, consistent with **Section 3.7 of the Parent Agreement**.  

(b) Vendor shall obtain Client’s prior written approval for any single trip likely to incur expenses exceeding USD $5,000.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

All Fees are exclusive of Taxes. Client shall be responsible for all Taxes associated with the Services, consistent with **Section 3.8 of the Parent Agreement**. Where Client must withhold Taxes, Client shall gross up the payment so that Vendor receives the full amount it would have received absent such withholding.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  

This Maintenance Agreement shall commence on the **Effective Date (May 04, 2025)** and continue in full force and effect until **October 09, 2027** (the **"Expiration Date"**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  

(a) Unless either Party provides written notice of non‑renewal at least **ninety (90) days** prior to the Expiration Date, this Maintenance Agreement shall automatically renew for successive **twelve (12)‑month** periods on Vendor’s then‑current maintenance pricing, subject to a maximum annual increase of **eight percent (8%)**, consistent with **Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement**.

(b) Any renewal shall be deemed to be on the same terms and conditions as this Maintenance Agreement except for pricing and any mutually agreed amendments.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

(a) Client may terminate this Maintenance Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to Vendor, consistent with **Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement**.  

(b) Vendor may terminate this Maintenance Agreement for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Client.  

(c) In the event of termination for convenience by Client:  
  (i) all Fees paid are non‑refundable; and  
  (ii) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and non‑cancellable commitments incurred up to the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

(a) materially breaches this Maintenance Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** (or ten (10) days in the case of non‑payment) after receiving written notice specifying the breach, in line with **Section 4.4(a) of the Parent Agreement**; or  

(b) becomes insolvent or enters into bankruptcy or similar proceedings, as described in **Section 4.4(b) of the Parent Agreement**.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  

(a) This Maintenance Agreement is **ancillary to and dependent upon** the Parent Agreement. If the Parent Agreement expires or is terminated for any reason, this Maintenance Agreement shall automatically terminate on the effective date of such expiration or termination, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to maintain this Maintenance Agreement in force on a stand‑alone basis.

(b) Upon any termination of this Maintenance Agreement resulting from termination of the Parent Agreement, Client shall pay all accrued and outstanding Fees up to the effective date of termination, subject to any specific remedies or refunds expressly provided in the Parent Agreement.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Maintenance Agreement for any reason:

(a) Vendor’s obligation to provide Maintenance and Support Services hereunder shall cease.  

(b) Client’s right to receive Updates, Maintenance Releases, and support shall cease, but Client’s License to use the Software shall continue or cease in accordance with **Article IV and Article VI of the Parent Agreement**.  

(c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Article V of this Maintenance Agreement and Article V of the Parent Agreement.  

(d) The provisions of this Maintenance Agreement that by their nature should survive (including, without limitation, Articles III (as to accrued payment obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI) shall survive expiration or termination.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  

If Client requests transition assistance, Vendor shall provide such assistance in a manner consistent with **Section 4.7 (Transition Assistance)** of the Parent Agreement, including exporting relevant support artifacts and providing reasonable cooperation to Client or its replacement provider, at Vendor’s then‑current professional services rates.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production instance of the Software achieves an average monthly uptime availability of **99.9%**, measured over each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime, consistent with **Section 12.1 of the Parent Agreement**.

12.2 **Permitted Downtime.**  

"Permitted Downtime" shall have the meaning given in **Section 12.2 of the Parent Agreement**, including scheduled maintenance and emergency maintenance. Vendor shall, where commercially reasonable, schedule maintenance to minimize disruption to Client’s operations and provide at least forty‑eight (48) hours’ notice for scheduled maintenance.

12.3 **Incident Severity and Response Targets.**  

Vendor shall adhere to the following Severity Levels and target response times, which extend the framework of **Section 12.3 of the Parent Agreement**:

(a) **Severity 1 – Critical.**  
  - Impact: Production system outage or severe degradation preventing all or a majority of users from accessing core functionality.  
  - Target Initial Response: within **1 hour**, 24x7.  
  - Target Workaround or Action Plan: within **4 hours**.  

(b) **Severity 2 – High.**  
  - Impact: Significant impact on functionality or business processes with no acceptable workaround.  
  - Target Initial Response: within **2 Business Hours**.  
  - Target Workaround or Action Plan: within **1 Business Day**.  

(c) **Severity 3 – Medium.**  
  - Impact: Moderate impact with available workaround; non‑critical functionality affected.  
  - Target Initial Response: within **1 Business Day**.  
  - Target Workaround or Action Plan: within **5 Business Days**.  

(d) **Severity 4 – Low.**  
  - Impact: Minor impact, general inquiries, cosmetic issues, or enhancement requests.  
  - Target Initial Response: within **3 Business Days**.  
  - Resolution: As reasonably scheduled; may be incorporated into future Updates.

12.4 **Remedies for SLA Failures.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the 99.9% uptime target in any calendar month (excluding Permitted Downtime):

(a) Client shall be entitled to service credits calculated in accordance with the tiers set forth in **Section 12.4 of the Parent Agreement**;  

(b) such service credits shall constitute Client’s **sole and exclusive remedy** for uptime SLA failures, without limiting other rights for breaches of other obligations (including data protection obligations or willful misconduct); and  

(c) Client must request service credits in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the SLA failure occurred.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  

Vendor shall have no obligations to provide service credits or be deemed to have failed Service Levels where the failure results from:

(a) circumstances constituting Permitted Downtime;  

(b) Client’s misuse or unauthorized modification of the Software;  

(c) failure of Client Systems, third‑party networks, or infrastructure not under Vendor’s control; or  

(d) Client’s failure to implement critical Updates where Vendor has advised that such failure may impact availability or security.
