## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of information technology and related professional services aimed at designing, developing, implementing, and supporting a customized enterprise software platform (the **“Platform”**) to support Client’s internal business operations and its service offerings to its own customers.  

2.1.2 The Services will include, without limitation:  
(a) requirements gathering and analysis;  
(b) solution architecture and technical design;  
(c) software configuration, customization, and integration with Client’s existing systems;  
(d) development of custom modules, interfaces, and APIs;  
(e) data migration and validation;  
(f) testing (unit, integration, system, user acceptance support);  
(g) deployment and go-live support;  
(h) ongoing maintenance, incident management, and technical support;  
(i) performance monitoring and optimization; and  
(j) training of Client’s designated personnel in the use and administration of the Platform.  

2.1.3 The precise scope, including feature sets, technical specifications, and project phases, shall be set forth in one or more Statements of Work. Each SOW shall be deemed incorporated herein by reference and shall be governed by the terms of this Agreement, except as otherwise expressly stated in such SOW.

### 2.2 Deliverables

2.2.1 Vendor shall create and deliver the Deliverables identified in each SOW, which may include, but are not limited to:  
(a) detailed requirements specifications and functional design documents;  
(b) technical design documents, architecture diagrams, and configuration guides;  
(c) executable software components, modules, and services;  
(d) database schemas and related scripts;  
(e) interface and integration specifications, including API definitions;  
(f) test plans, test cases, and test results reports;  
(g) training materials, user manuals, and administration guides; and  
(h) operational runbooks and standard operating procedures for ongoing support.

2.2.2 Each Deliverable shall conform in all material respects to the specifications, requirements, and acceptance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, each Deliverable shall be considered accepted upon the earlier of:  
(a) Client’s written acceptance; or  
(b) the expiration of ten (10) Business Days following delivery without Client providing written notice of a material nonconformity.

2.2.3 If Client notifies Vendor within the applicable acceptance period of a material nonconformity, Vendor shall, at no additional cost to Client, promptly correct such nonconformity and redeliver the affected Deliverable for re-testing and acceptance, in accordance with the process set forth in the SOW.

### 2.3 Project Phases and Timelines

2.3.1 The Parties anticipate that the Services will be performed in multiple phases, which may include:  
(a) Discovery and Requirements Phase;  
(b) Design and Architecture Phase;  
(c) Development and Configuration Phase;  
(d) System Integration and Data Migration Phase;  
(e) User Acceptance Testing and Stabilization Phase; and  
(f) Production Roll-out and Transition to Support Phase.

2.3.2 Each SOW shall specify the expected start date, key milestones, completion criteria, and indicative completion dates for each phase. The Parties acknowledge that timelines may be adjusted through the Change Management process described in Article XIII.

2.3.3 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the agreed milestones and completion dates. Time is not of the essence unless expressly stated as such with respect to specific milestones in a SOW. Client shall provide timely feedback, approvals, and resources reasonably required for Vendor to meet such milestones.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a diligent, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with:  
(a) generally accepted industry standards and best practices for similar services;  
(b) the specifications, requirements, and Service Levels set forth in this Agreement and the applicable SOW; and  
(c) all applicable laws and regulations in the Territory, including Data Protection Laws.

2.4.2 Vendor shall ensure that all personnel assigned to perform the Services are properly trained, qualified, and experienced, and shall be responsible for the acts and omissions of its employees, subcontractors, and agents as if they were Vendor’s own acts and omissions.

2.4.3 Vendor shall designate a project manager as the primary point of contact for Client for each major project under a SOW. Client shall likewise designate a project manager. The project managers shall meet (in person or virtually) at intervals agreed by the Parties to review progress, resolve issues, and manage Change Requests.

### 2.5 Client Responsibilities

2.5.1 Client shall provide Vendor with timely access to relevant information, systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services, including access to subject matter experts, test data, test environments, and such other resources as may be specified in the SOW.

2.5.2 Client shall be responsible for:  
(a) the accuracy and completeness of all information and data provided to Vendor;  
(b) making timely decisions and providing approvals reasonably required for Vendor to proceed with the Services; and  
(c) ensuring that its own systems, infrastructure, and third-party services upon which the Services or Deliverables depend are properly maintained and available.

2.5.3 Vendor shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performing the Services to the extent caused by Client’s failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement, provided that Vendor promptly notifies Client of such failures and uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate any resulting impact.

### 2.6 Subcontracting

2.6.1 Vendor may subcontract portions of the Services to Third Parties, provided that:  
(a) Vendor remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services;  
(b) such subcontractors are bound by confidentiality and data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and  
(c) Client’s prior written consent is obtained for any subcontractor performing material components of the Services or having access to Client’s Confidential Information or Personal Data, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value and Fee Structure

3.1.1 The total estimated contract value for the initial Term of this Agreement is **Two Million One Hundred Thirty-Two Thousand Seven Hundred Ninety-Five U.S. Dollars (USD 2,132,795)** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustments arising from approved Change Requests, additional SOWs, or other written amendments.

3.1.2 The Fees payable under this Agreement shall be comprised of one or more of the following components, as set forth in the applicable SOW:  
(a) fixed project fees, based on defined milestones or deliverables;  
(b) time-and-materials fees, based on hourly or daily rates for Vendor personnel;  
(c) recurring service fees, such as hosting, support, and maintenance charges; and  
(d) license fees for use of Vendor’s proprietary software or third-party software made available by Vendor, if any.

### 3.2 Payment Milestones

3.2.1 Each SOW shall specify the applicable payment schedule, which may include milestone-based payments. By way of example, a typical schedule may be:  
(a) ten percent (10%) of the applicable SOW value upon SOW execution;  
(b) twenty percent (20%) upon completion of the Design and Architecture Phase;  
(c) thirty percent (30%) upon completion of the Development and Configuration Phase;  
(d) twenty percent (20%) upon successful completion of User Acceptance Testing; and  
(e) twenty percent (20%) upon production go-live and acceptance of final Deliverables.

3.2.2 For time-and-materials work, Vendor shall invoice monthly in arrears based on actual hours expended, at the rates specified in the SOW, together with any approved expenses.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.3.1 Vendor shall issue invoices in accordance with the applicable payment schedule or, in the case of recurring or time-and-materials Fees, on a monthly basis. Each invoice shall reference the relevant SOW, describe the Services or Deliverables provided, and include reasonable supporting detail.

3.3.2 All undisputed amounts due under this Agreement shall be payable by Client within **thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 30”**), in U.S. Dollars, by wire transfer or such other method as the Parties may agree.

3.3.3 If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Client shall pay all undisputed amounts by the due date. The Parties shall work in good faith to promptly resolve any disputed amounts.

### 3.4 Taxes

3.4.1 The Fees are exclusive of all applicable taxes, including but not limited to goods and services tax (GST), value-added tax (VAT), sales tax, and similar levies, which shall be charged in addition where applicable and shall be paid by Client, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income.

3.4.2 Each Party shall be responsible for its own tax obligations arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, in accordance with applicable laws.

### 3.5 Late Payment

3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of one percent (1%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower, from the due date until paid in full.

3.5.2 Without prejudice to any other rights or remedies, if Client fails to pay undisputed amounts within thirty (30) days after written notice of such failure, Vendor may, upon an additional ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Services until all overdue amounts (including interest) are paid, provided that Vendor continues to comply with its confidentiality and data protection obligations.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursement

3.6.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, such as travel, accommodations, and subsistence, in accordance with Client’s then-current travel and expense policy (to the extent provided to Vendor).

3.6.2 Vendor shall provide reasonable documentary evidence (such as receipts) for any reimbursable expenses. Such expenses shall be invoiced monthly in arrears.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **27 March 2028** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend the Term for additional periods, on terms and conditions to be agreed in writing.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or with respect to any SOW, upon not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. The Parties acknowledge that this notice period represents a balanced approach for a medium-risk engagement.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor:  
(a) all Fees due and payable for Services performed and Deliverables delivered up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) all approved, non-cancellable commitments made by Vendor on Client’s behalf; and  
(c) any reasonable wind-down costs expressly agreed in writing.

4.2.3 In the event of termination for convenience by Vendor, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to Client’s business and shall cooperate in any transition plan reasonably requested by Client.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any SOW) immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
(a) materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer period as may be reasonably required for cure, not exceeding sixty (60) days) after receiving written notice of the breach; or  
(b) becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into liquidation, receivership, judicial management, or similar proceedings, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.3.2 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, repeated failures by Vendor to meet Service Levels, if not remedied in accordance with Article XII, may constitute a material breach. Likewise, repeated failures by Client to pay undisputed amounts when due may constitute a material breach.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) all rights and licenses granted by one Party to the other under this Agreement shall immediately cease, except as expressly provided otherwise;  
(b) Vendor shall promptly cease all Services (except as necessary for transition) and shall deliver to Client any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid or is obligated to pay;  
(c) each Party shall, upon request, return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) the other Party’s Confidential Information, subject to any legal retention requirements and the provisions of Article XI; and  
(d) Client shall pay Vendor all Fees due for Services performed and Deliverables delivered up to the effective date of termination, as well as any other amounts due under Section 4.2, if applicable.

4.4.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any accrued rights or liabilities of either Party as of the effective date of termination, nor any provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination, including Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination.

(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;

5.5.1 The obligations of confidentiality under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for **five (5) years** following the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall be kept confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets or as required under applicable Data Protection Laws, respectively.

15.1.1 A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party that prevents or delays the performance of its obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to:  
(a) acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, or pandemics;  
(b) war, terrorism, civil unrest, riots, or armed conflict;

(e) failures of telecommunications or internet service providers, power outages, or other infrastructure failures not caused by the affected Party.

### 15.2 Notification

15.2.1 The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, describing the nature of the event, its anticipated duration, and the obligations affected.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

15.3.1 The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the event and resume performance as soon as practicable.
