## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of technology integration, infrastructure migration, managed support, and ongoing optimization services designed to support Client’s enterprise operations. The Services under this Work Order **WO-PIN-202410-475** shall include, without limitation:

  a. Assessment and design of Client’s target hybrid cloud infrastructure;  
  b. Migration of designated applications, databases, and workloads to the target environment;  
  c. Configuration and hardening of core systems, including identity and access management, network segmentation, and monitoring tools;  
  d. Provision of Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 support and incident management;  
  e. Capacity planning, performance tuning, and cost-optimization consulting; and  
  f. Periodic reporting, governance, and continuous improvement services.

2.2 **Detailed Phases and Deliverables.**

2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Assessment (Months 1–3).**  
During this phase, Vendor shall perform discovery activities to understand the Client’s current environment, dependencies, and operational requirements. Deliverables include, at a minimum:

  (i) **Current State Assessment Report** detailing infrastructure, applications, integrations, and security posture;  
  (ii) **Gap Analysis and Risk Register**, including identified vulnerabilities and recommended remediation steps;  
  (iii) **Target Architecture Blueprint**, including network diagrams, component catalog, and integration points; and  
  (iv) **Migration Roadmap and Project Plan**, defining prioritized workloads, dependencies, and wave-based migration approach.

Vendor shall conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and technical analyses. Client shall provide reasonable access to systems and personnel as necessary. Vendor shall deliver the above Deliverables within sixty (60) days from the Effective Date, subject to Client’s timely cooperation.

2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Implementation and Migration (Months 3–18).**  
Vendor shall implement the Target Architecture and migrate designated workloads in accordance with the Migration Roadmap. Key activities include:

  (i) Provisioning and configuration of compute, storage, networking, and security components;  
  (ii) Implementation of identity and access management integrations (e.g., SSO, MFA);  
  (iii) Configuration of monitoring, logging, and alerting solutions;  
  (iv) Execution of migration waves (test, pilot, production), including data migration, cutover planning, and rollback procedures; and  
  (v) Conducting functional, performance, and security testing, and supporting UAT (user acceptance testing).

Deliverables for Phase 2 include:

  - **Migration Runbooks** for each application/workload;  
  - **Configuration Documentation** for all core components;  
  - **Test Plans and Test Results**; and  
  - **Go-Live Sign-Off Documentation** per wave.

2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Managed Services and Optimization (Months 6–End of Term).**  
Following successful migration of initial workloads, Vendor shall provide ongoing managed services, including:

  (i) 24x7 monitoring and incident management for in-scope systems;  
  (ii) Routine maintenance, patching, and backup management;  
  (iii) Security event triage and coordination with Client’s security team;  
  (iv) Capacity and performance management; and  
  (v) Quarterly optimization assessments and executive reports.

Managed services will be provided in accordance with the Service Levels set out in **Article XII**. During this phase, Vendor will also support incremental enhancements and minor change requests, subject to the Change Management process under **Article XIII**.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  

2.3.1 Vendor shall perform the Services:  
  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  (b) in accordance with the Specifications, Service Levels, and agreed timelines; and  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Law and Client’s reasonable security and access policies, as communicated in writing.

2.3.2 Where specific performance metrics are set out in **Article XII**, such metrics shall be binding Service Levels. Any failure by Vendor to meet such Service Levels shall be addressed in accordance with **Section 12.4 (Remedies)**.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Client’s timely cooperation and performance of the following:

  (a) Providing, in a timely manner, all Client Materials, access credentials, approvals, and information reasonably required by Vendor;  
  (b) Ensuring the availability of key Client stakeholders for workshops, design reviews, UAT, and governance meetings;  
  (c) Making timely decisions and providing sign-offs within five (5) Business Days of reasonable request, unless otherwise agreed; and  
  (d) Maintaining appropriate backup and disaster recovery capabilities for Client systems not explicitly included within Vendor’s scope.

Any delays or failures by Client under this Section 2.4 may result in adjustments to the timetable, Deliverables, and Fees through the Change Management process.

2.5 **Out-of-Scope Activities.**  
Unless expressly included in this Work Order or a mutually executed Change Order, the following are out of scope:

  (a) Custom application development beyond configuration and scripting necessary for integration;  
  (b) Physical hardware procurement and on-premises datacenter build-out;  
  (c) Legal, regulatory, or tax advisory services; and  
  (d) Support for systems and applications not identified in the Migration Roadmap or any subsequent Change Orders.

2.6 **Risk Level and High-Risk Acknowledgment.**  
Client acknowledges that the Services involve complex migration and managed services with a **High** risk profile. Client agrees to allocate appropriate internal resources and risk mitigation measures and acknowledges the corresponding allocation of contractual risk in Vendor’s favor as reflected in **Articles VIII, IX, and VI**.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the in-scope managed services environment is available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events. “Availability” shall be measured at the primary system interfaces under Vendor’s control.

12.2.1 Vendor shall respond to and work to resolve incidents in accordance with the following target response and resolution times (Business Days unless otherwise noted):

- **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Production outage or severe degradation:  
  - Response Time: 30 minutes (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant business impact:  
  - Response Time: 2 hours (Business Hours)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 1 Business Day  

- **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Moderate impact or non-urgent issue:  
  - Response Time: 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues or service requests:  
  - Response Time: 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: 5 Business Days  

12.3 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Vendor may schedule routine maintenance windows not to exceed four (4) hours per week, during which Services may be unavailable. Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to schedule maintenance during off-peak hours and shall provide Client with at least three (3) Business Days’ prior notice, except in the case of emergency maintenance.

If Vendor fails to meet the Service Availability commitment set forth in **Section 12.1** in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to a service credit calculated as a percentage of the monthly managed services Fees for that month, as follows:

- Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% credit  
- Availability between 98.0% and 98.99%: 10% credit  
- Availability below 98.0%: 20% credit  

Service credits shall be applied to the invoice for the next billing period. Service credits are Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the Service Availability commitment, without prejudice to Client’s rights for material breach under **Article IV**.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
Service Level commitments shall not apply to any unavailability or performance issues resulting from:  
  (a) Client’s acts or omissions or those of its third-party vendors;  
  (b) Client’s failure to use a supported configuration;  
  (c) Third-party network or Internet issues outside Vendor’s reasonable control;  
  (d) Force Majeure Events; or  
  (e) Scheduled maintenance in accordance with **Section 12.3**.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control that prevents or delays the performance of its obligations under this Work Order, including, by way of example, acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, riots, civil unrest, labor disputes (excluding disputes involving a Party’s own workforce), pandemics, epidemics, acts of government, or failures of telecommunications or Internet service providers not under a Party’s control.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event (“**Affected Party**”) shall:  
  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  
  (c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of the status of the Force Majeure Event and its efforts to overcome it.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the Affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that performance is prevented or delayed by the Force Majeure Event. The corresponding obligations of the other Party shall likewise be suspended.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Work Order upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability (other than for amounts accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
