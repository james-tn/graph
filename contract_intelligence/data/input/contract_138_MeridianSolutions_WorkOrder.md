## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 The Service Provider shall design, implement, and support an enterprise technology solution for the Client, focused on: (a) systems integration; (b) cloud migration of selected applications; (c) deployment of monitoring and incident management capabilities; and (d) provision of ongoing managed services for the supported environment.

2.1.2 The Services shall be performed in a phased manner, as described in **Sections 2.2–2.5**, and shall be conducted using the Service Provider’s standard methodologies and best practices, subject to the requirements of this Agreement and the Specifications agreed by the Parties.

### 2.2 Phase 1 – Discovery and Assessment

2.2.1 The Service Provider shall perform a discovery and assessment of the Client’s current environment, including but not limited to: infrastructure, application portfolio, network topology, security posture, identity and access management, and current operational processes.

2.2.2 Deliverables for Phase 1 shall include:

- A Current State Assessment Report, summarizing the existing environment and identifying risks and dependencies;
- A Gap Analysis Document, identifying key gaps between the current environment and the desired target state;
- A Preliminary Migration and Integration Strategy, outlining recommended approaches and sequencing.

2.2.3 Phase 1 shall commence within fifteen (15) Business Days after the Effective Date and is expected to be completed within sixty (60) calendar days, subject to the Client’s timely provision of necessary information, access, and approvals as described in **Section 2.6**.

### 2.3 Phase 2 – Design and Planning

2.3.1 Based on the outputs of Phase 1, the Service Provider shall design the target architecture and operational model, including:

- Target cloud architecture (including compute, storage, network, security controls, and monitoring components);
- Integration architecture for identified systems and applications;
- Identity management and access control design;
- High-level disaster recovery and business continuity considerations.

2.3.2 Deliverables for Phase 2 shall include, at a minimum:

- A Target Architecture Design Document;
- A Detailed Implementation Plan, including milestones and resource plans;
- A Risk Register and Mitigation Plan for the proposed implementation.

2.3.3 Phase 2 is expected to commence immediately upon completion of Phase 1 and Client’s written approval of the Phase 1 deliverables, and to be completed within ninety (90) calendar days thereafter.

### 2.4 Phase 3 – Implementation, Integration, and Migration

2.4.1 The Service Provider shall implement the approved design and perform the necessary integration and migration activities, which may include:

- Provisioning of cloud resources and configuration of security groups, firewalls, and access controls;
- Installation, configuration, and integration of specified software components;
- Migration of selected applications and data to the target environment in accordance with the Implementation Plan;
- Execution of functional, performance, and security testing, and remediation of material defects.

2.4.2 Deliverables for Phase 3 shall include:

- Configured and operational target environment as defined in the Specifications;
- Migration Completion Report summarizing applications successfully migrated, issues encountered, and residual risks;
- Test Plans and Test Results Documentation for executed test cycles;
- Runbooks and operational documentation for ongoing support.

2.4.3 The Parties acknowledge that certain activities in Phase 3 may be highly interdependent on the Client’s internal teams and third-party providers. Accordingly, the Service Provider’s timelines and responsibilities for Phase 3 shall be subject to timely cooperation from the Client as specified in **Section 2.6**.

### 2.5 Phase 4 – Managed Services and Support

2.5.1 Following completion of the initial implementation and migration activities and acceptance of the corresponding Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.7**, the Service Provider shall provide ongoing managed services for the supported environment through the Expiration Date, including:

- 24x7 monitoring of the supported infrastructure and applications;
- Incident detection, triage, and escalation in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**;
- Routine maintenance, patching, and updates as required to maintain system stability and security, subject to Client change management controls;
- Technical support and issue resolution, including remote support and coordination with third-party vendors as reasonably necessary.

2.5.2 Specific operational responsibilities, including incident severity definitions, escalation paths, and maintenance windows, shall be documented in operational runbooks and procedures approved by the Client, which shall be incorporated by reference into this Agreement upon mutual written consent.

### 2.6 Client Responsibilities

2.6.1 The Client shall provide the Service Provider with timely access to its facilities, systems, personnel, and information as reasonably required for the Service Provider to perform the Services. Any delay or failure by the Client to provide such access may result in corresponding delays in the Project schedule, for which the Service Provider shall not be liable.

2.6.2 The Client shall designate a primary project manager, who shall be responsible for decision-making on behalf of the Client, coordination of Client resources, and review and acceptance of Deliverables.

2.6.3 The Client shall be responsible for obtaining and maintaining all necessary licenses, approvals, and consents from third parties (including software vendors and landlords) required to permit the Service Provider to perform the Services and access Client systems, except to the extent expressly agreed otherwise in writing.

2.6.4 The Client acknowledges that its timely provision of information and feedback is critical to the success of the Project. If the Client fails to review and respond to Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of submission, the Deliverables shall be deemed accepted for purposes of continuing work and scheduling (but not for purposes of payment if the Client provides written notice of material defects within fifteen (15) Business Days).

### 2.7 Acceptance of Deliverables

2.7.1 For each Deliverable, the Service Provider shall submit the Deliverable to the Client for review and acceptance. The Client shall have ten (10) Business Days (the “**Review Period**”) from receipt of the Deliverable to:

- (a) accept the Deliverable in writing; or  
- (b) reject the Deliverable by providing a written notice specifying in reasonable detail any material non-conformities with the applicable Specifications.

2.7.2 If the Client does not provide any written response within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

2.7.3 In the event of a timely rejection, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified material non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable within a mutually agreed period. The Client shall then have an additional Review Period to review the revised Deliverable. If, after two (2) resubmissions, the Deliverable is still materially non-conforming in the reasonable, good-faith judgment of the Client, the matter shall be escalated in accordance with **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**.

### 2.8 Performance Standards

2.8.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications, and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards.

2.8.2 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Service Provider shall:

- Adhere to the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**;
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations in the performance of the Services, as further described in **Article VII**;
- Use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid material disruption to the Client’s ongoing business operations.

2.8.3 The Parties agree that the Service Levels and any related credits or remedies set forth in **Article XII** shall be the Client’s sole and exclusive remedies with respect to the quality, timeliness, and availability of the managed services, except as otherwise expressly provided in this Agreement.

3.3.1 Any undisputed amounts not received by the Service Provider by the due date shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

## ARTICLE IV

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **July 3, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”).

4.1.2 The Term may be extended upon mutual written agreement of the Parties, which may include revised Fees, scope, and other terms.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Client:

- The Client shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and Deliverables completed up to the effective date of termination;  
- The Client shall also pay any reasonable, non-cancellable commitments incurred by the Service Provider in reliance on this Agreement and approved by the Client;  
- Any prepaid amounts for Services not yet performed as of the effective date of termination shall be applied to amounts due or, if excess, refunded within forty-five (45) days, subject to any setoff for non-cancellable commitments and termination-related costs.

4.2.3 Termination for convenience by the Client shall not relieve the Client of its obligation to pay any Fees or expenses accrued prior to the effective date of termination.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

- (a) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver or trustee appointed for substantially all of its assets; or  
- (b) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.3.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for material breach by the other Party, provided that:

- (a) the non-breaching Party provides written notice specifying the nature of the material breach in reasonable detail; and  
- (b) the breaching Party fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of such notice, or within such longer reasonable period (not to exceed sixty (60) days) as may be agreed in writing, provided the breaching Party has commenced and is diligently pursuing cure.

4.3.3 Without limiting any other rights it may have, the Service Provider may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within sixty (60) days after the due date and such failure remains uncured for an additional ten (10) Business Days following written notice.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

- The Client shall promptly pay the Service Provider all Fees and reimbursable expenses due and payable as of the effective date of expiration or termination;  
- The Service Provider shall cease performing the Services (except for any agreed transition assistance described in **Section 4.4.3**);  
- Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**.

4.4.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or obligations of either Party that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination or that, by their nature, are intended to survive termination, including without limitation those set forth in **Sections 4.4, 5.4, 6.3–6.5, 7.3, 8.1–8.4, 9.1–9.3, 11.5, 16.3, 16.7**, and any payment obligations.

4.4.3 At the Client’s request, and subject to mutual agreement on the scope, duration, and applicable Fees, the Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days following termination, to facilitate the orderly transition of the Services to the Client or a third-party provider.
