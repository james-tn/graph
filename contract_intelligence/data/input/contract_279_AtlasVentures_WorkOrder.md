## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order **adds scope to the Statement of Work dated February 25, 2024 (Ref: SOW-ATL-202402-246)**. It is **entered into pursuant to and supplements** the Parent SOW in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)** thereof.

2.1.2 Except as expressly modified in this Work Order, the scope, Deliverables, performance standards, and acceptance procedures set forth in **Article II** and **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent SOW shall apply to the Extended Services and Extended Deliverables under this Work Order.

2.1.3 For clarity, the Extended Services described herein are **in addition to** the Services described in **Section 2.2 (Project Phases)** of the Parent SOW and are not intended to reduce or replace any obligations of Vendor under the Parent SOW.

2.2 **Overview of Extended Services.**  

Vendor shall provide the following Extended Services, which are organized into four workstreams and associated Milestones. The estimated durations are provided for planning purposes and may be adjusted via the Change Management process under **Article XIII** of the Parent SOW as applied herein:

  2.2.1 **Workstream A – Advanced Analytics Enablement (Estimated: 20 weeks).**  
  Building on the data analytics enablement activities performed under **Section 2.2.3 (Phase 3 – Implementation & Migration)** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

  - Assess the current analytics and reporting capabilities implemented under Deliverable **D9 – Data Models, Data Pipelines, and Analytics Dashboard Specifications**;  
  - Design and implement additional data models to support advanced use cases, including predictive analytics, customer segmentation, and operational performance analytics;  
  - Develop and deploy at least ten (10) new analytics dashboards tailored to key stakeholder groups identified by Client, aligning with the **Multi-Year Transformation Roadmap (D5)** produced under the Parent SOW;  
  - Implement data quality and data lineage monitoring to ensure reliable and auditable analytics outputs; and  
  - Document the extended analytics architecture and patterns, to be appended to **D4 – Target Enterprise Architecture Diagrams and Specifications**.

  2.2.2 **Workstream B – Observability and Reliability (Estimated: 16 weeks).**  
  In extension of the platform configuration work described in **Section 2.2.3** and Deliverable **D8 – Configuration and Deployment Documentation** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

  - Design an **Observability Framework** encompassing centralized logging, distributed tracing, infrastructure and application metrics, and alerting rules;  
  - Implement observability tooling within Client’s agreed cloud environment, consistent with Vendor’s configurations for identity and access management, logging, monitoring, and backup under the Parent SOW;  
  - Create standard operational dashboards for system health, performance, and capacity utilization;  
  - Integrate observability alerts with Client’s incident management processes and tooling; and  
  - Produce detailed observability runbooks to be integrated with **D11 – Operational Runbooks and Support Procedures** from the Parent SOW.

  2.2.3 **Workstream C – Optimization Waves (Estimated: 3 waves over 18 months).**  
  Following the initial implementation and migration phases under the Parent SOW, Vendor shall conduct **three (3) Optimization Waves** focused on performance, cost, and usability of the Enhanced Analytics Platform:

  - **Wave 1 – Performance Optimization (Estimated: 4 months).**  
    - Analyze workload patterns and system performance metrics;  
    - Optimize data pipelines, queries, and dashboard rendering times;  
    - Implement caching, indexing, partitioning, or architectural refinements as appropriate;  
    - Provide a **Performance Optimization Report** summarizing improvements and recommendations.

  - **Wave 2 – Cost Optimization (Estimated: 4 months).**  
    - Review cloud resource utilization and cost drivers;  
    - Identify and implement cost reduction measures (e.g., rightsizing, reserved instances, storage tiering);  
    - Provide a **Cost Optimization Report** including before/after cost benchmarks and projected savings.

  - **Wave 3 – User Experience & Adoption Optimization (Estimated: 6 months).**  
    - Conduct user interviews and usage analytics to identify adoption barriers and enhancement opportunities;  
    - Recommend and implement changes to dashboards, navigation, and training materials;  
    - Facilitate up to six (6) user enablement workshops;  
    - Deliver an **Adoption & UX Improvement Report** and updated training materials that supplement **D12 – Training Materials and Knowledge Transfer Sessions** from the Parent SOW.

  2.2.4 **Workstream D – Extended Stabilization & Knowledge Transfer (Estimated: 24 weeks).**  
  Extending **Phase 4 – Stabilization & Knowledge Transfer** from the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

  - Provide extended hypercare support for the Enhanced Analytics Platform and Observability Framework, aligned with the Service Levels in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent SOW and the SLA Addendum in this Work Order;  
  - Coordinate with Client and any third‑party managed service providers to plan and execute an orderly transition of operational responsibilities;  
  - Deliver advanced training sessions for Client’s data engineering, analytics, and operations teams;  
  - Update and extend existing operational runbooks and support procedures (Deliverable **D11** in the Parent SOW), incorporating optimization and observability practices; and  
  - Provide transition assistance as requested by Client, consistent with **Section 4.6 (Transition Assistance)** of the Parent SOW and **Section 4.6** of this Work Order.

2.3 **Extended Deliverables.**  

Without limiting Vendor’s obligations under **Article II** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall deliver the following Extended Deliverables under this Work Order. Each Extended Deliverable shall be subject to Client’s review and acceptance pursuant to **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent SOW, as supplemented in **Section 2.7 of this Work Order**:

- **ED1 – Advanced Analytics Design & Use Case Catalogue.** Detailed design document describing additional analytics use cases, data models, and technical specifications, to be cross‑referenced with **D9** and incorporated into the Target Enterprise Architecture (D4).  

- **ED2 – Extended Data Models and Pipelines.** Implemented and documented data models and pipelines supporting the advanced use cases outlined in ED1, including technical documentation and data lineage diagrams.  

- **ED3 – Analytics Dashboards Pack.** A suite of at least ten (10) production‑ready dashboards with accompanying configuration documentation and user guides.  

- **ED4 – Observability Framework Design Document.** Architecture and design document for the Observability Framework, including tooling choices, integration points, and security considerations, building upon configuration documentation in **D8**.  

- **ED5 – Observability Implementation & Runbooks.** Deployed observability tooling, dashboards, alert configurations, and detailed operational runbooks to be appended to **D11**.  

- **ED6 – Performance Optimization Report.** Post‑Wave 1 report summarizing performance baselines, improvements achieved, and recommendations.  

- **ED7 – Cost Optimization Report.** Post‑Wave 2 report summarizing cloud cost baselines, optimization measures implemented, and projected annual savings.  

- **ED8 – Adoption & UX Improvement Report and Updated Training Pack.** Post‑Wave 3 report summarizing adoption metrics, usability improvements, and updated training materials supplementing **D12**.  

- **ED9 – Extended Stabilization & Transition Plan.** Detailed plan for extended hypercare, runbook updates, training, and transition of responsibilities, aligned with **Section 4.6** of the Parent SOW.  

- **ED10 – Final Extended Services Completion Report.** Consolidated summary of all Extended Services performed, Deliverables delivered, SLAs achieved, and outstanding recommendations.

2.4 **Milestones and Timeline.**  

The Parties shall agree, within thirty (30) days of the WO Effective Date, on a detailed Milestone schedule linking Extended Deliverables (ED1–ED10) to specific dates and payment events, consistent with **Section 3.2 (Fee Structure and Milestones)** of the Parent SOW. In the absence of a signed supplemental schedule, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to:

- Complete Workstream A and deliver ED1–ED3 within approximately twenty (20) weeks of the WO Effective Date;  
- Complete Workstream B and deliver ED4–ED5 within approximately thirty‑six (36) weeks of the WO Effective Date;  
- Complete Wave 1, Wave 2, and Wave 3 (ED6–ED8) within approximately twenty‑four (24) months of the WO Effective Date; and  
- Complete Workstream D and deliver ED9–ED10 no later than the WO Expiration Date.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

In addition to the performance standards set forth in **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW, Vendor shall:

2.5.1 Perform the Extended Services in a manner that does not materially degrade any Service Levels committed under **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent SOW.

2.5.2 Use personnel with advanced skills and experience in data analytics, observability, and cloud optimization to perform Workstreams A–C.

2.5.3 Ensure that the Observability Framework and Enhanced Analytics Platform adhere to the **Security and Compliance Framework Documentation (D6)** produced under the Parent SOW and comply with applicable **Data Protection Laws** as defined in **Section 1.7** of the Parent SOW and further addressed in **Article XI** of this Work Order.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  

The Client responsibilities set forth in **Section 2.5** of the Parent SOW apply equally to the Extended Services, and, in addition, Client shall:

2.6.1 Designate business owners and data stewards for the advanced analytics use cases to provide timely input and approvals.

2.6.2 Ensure access to cloud cost management tools and billing data required for the cost optimization activities in Wave 2.

2.6.3 Facilitate access to relevant user groups for interviews, workshops, and adoption assessments in Wave 3.

2.7 **Acceptance of Extended Deliverables.**  

The acceptance process in **Section 2.7** of the Parent SOW shall apply to ED1–ED10, provided that:

2.7.2 If Client provides a written statement of material non‑conformities, Vendor shall correct such non‑conformities and re‑submit the Extended Deliverable in accordance with **Sections 2.7.3 and 2.7.4** of the Parent SOW, with the same limitations on the number of re‑submissions.

Extended Services may be performed at Vendor’s premises, Client’s premises, remotely, or at such other locations as the Parties may agree in writing, consistent with **Section 2.8 (Location of Services)** of the Parent SOW. ## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Work Order Term.**  

This Work Order shall commence on the WO Effective Date and continue until **September 15, 2027** (the “**Work Order Term**” or “**WO Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or as a result of Parent Termination as described in **Section 4.5** below.

4.2 **Renewal.**  

Any extension or renewal of this Work Order beyond the WO Expiration Date shall be subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties, which may include updated scope, pricing, and Service Levels, and shall be documented as an amendment to this Work Order and, if applicable, the Parent SOW in accordance with **Section 16.2 (Amendments)** of the Parent SOW.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor. This is consistent with Client’s termination rights under **Section 4.3.1** of the Parent SOW.

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Work Order for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client and only after completion of any active critical Milestones mutually identified as such for this Work Order, consistent with **Section 4.3.2** of the Parent SOW.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for cause if the other Party materially breaches this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (ten (10) days for payment breaches) after receiving written notice specifying the breach, in alignment with **Section 4.4 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent SOW.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (c) ceases to do business in the ordinary course, as described in **Section 4.4.2** of the Parent SOW.

4.5 **Effect of Parent Termination.**  

4.5.1 **Automatic Alignment.** In the event of any Parent Termination under **Article IV** of the Parent SOW, this Work Order shall automatically terminate on the effective date of such Parent Termination, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue this Work Order as a standalone agreement.

4.5.2 **Payment Obligations.** Upon Parent Termination (and resulting termination of this Work Order), Client shall pay Vendor for all undisputed Fees and approved expenses incurred under this Work Order up to the effective date of termination, consistent with **Section 4.5.1** of the Parent SOW. In case of termination due to Vendor’s uncured material breach, Client shall have no obligation to pay Fees for materially non‑conforming Extended Services or Extended Deliverables not accepted, consistent with **Section 4.5.2** of the Parent SOW.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order (other than due to Client’s uncured material breach), Vendor shall, upon Client’s request, provide reasonable transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days to facilitate the orderly transfer of Extended Services to Client or a third party, at Vendor’s then‑current time‑and‑materials rates, consistent with **Section 4.6 (Transition Assistance)** of the Parent SOW. Such assistance shall include, as applicable, knowledge transfer regarding the Enhanced Analytics Platform and Observability Framework.

4.7 **Survival.**  

The following provisions of this Work Order shall survive any expiration or termination: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.
