## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Phoenix shall supply to Contoso, and Contoso shall purchase from Phoenix, the Deliverables and Services described in this Article II, in each Statement of Work and in each Purchase Order issued under this Agreement. The Parties agree that the high-level scope includes:

  a. Design, manufacture, and supply of industrial-grade equipment and related hardware components;  
  b. Supply and configuration of embedded software and associated monitoring and control systems;  
  c. On-site installation, integration, and commissioning of the Deliverables;  
  d. Preventive and corrective maintenance, technical support, and spare parts provision; and  
  e. Training of Contoso’s personnel in the operation and maintenance of the Deliverables.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, Phoenix shall deliver the following categories of Deliverables (the exact models, specifications, and quantities to be further detailed in each relevant SOW or PO):

  a. **Industrial Equipment and Hardware.**  
  - Automated assembly line modules;  
  - Robotic handling units;  
  - Sensors, controllers, and programmable logic controllers (PLCs);  
  - Power units, cabling, and mounting hardware;  
  - Replacement and spare parts as agreed.

  b. **Software and Systems.**  
  - Equipment control software (object code) for Phoenix-supplied equipment;  
  - Monitoring dashboards and data collection modules;  
  - Integration connectors for interfacing with Contoso’s existing enterprise resource planning (ERP) and manufacturing execution system (MES);  
  - Standard user manuals, operating procedures, and technical documentation.

  c. **Documentation.**  
  - Installation and commissioning reports;  
  - System configuration diagrams and network topology;  
  - Maintenance schedules, checklists, and recommended spare parts lists;  
  - Training materials and standard operating procedures tailored to Contoso’s environment.

2.3 **Services.**  
Phoenix shall provide the following Services:

  a. **Project Management and Design.**  
  Phoenix shall assign qualified project managers and engineers to work with Contoso to develop detailed specifications, layout, and integration plans for the Deliverables. Phoenix shall provide a project plan, including key Milestones, resource allocation, and risk mitigation measures.

  b. **Installation and Commissioning.**  
  Phoenix shall install the Deliverables at the Contoso facilities identified in the relevant SOW or PO, configure the equipment and software according to agreed specifications, integrate the same with Contoso’s systems where applicable, and carry out all necessary testing and commissioning activities. Phoenix shall notify Contoso upon completion of commissioning and facilitate acceptance testing in accordance with Section 2.6.

  c. **Maintenance and Support.**  
  Phoenix shall provide maintenance and support in accordance with Article XII, including:
   - Preventive maintenance visits at the frequency specified in the applicable SOW;  
   - Corrective maintenance in response to hardware or software faults;  
   - Remote diagnostics and troubleshooting where feasible;  
   - Supply and replacement of spare parts as specified.

  d. **Training.**  
  Phoenix shall conduct training sessions (on-site or remote, as agreed) for Contoso’s designated personnel, covering operation, safety, and first-level maintenance of the Deliverables. Training shall be conducted in English and shall include training materials that Contoso may reproduce internally for its staff.

  e. **Additional Professional Services.**  
  At Contoso’s request and subject to a mutually agreed SOW or Change Order, Phoenix may provide additional consulting, optimization, customization, or engineering services. Such services shall be billed as set forth in Article III.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Phoenix shall perform all Services:

  a. with due skill, care, prudence, and diligence, in a professional and workmanlike manner, and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards;  
  b. in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and safety standards in the Territory;  
  c. in accordance with the timelines, Milestones, and Service Levels agreed in this Agreement and in any SOW; and  
  d. using personnel who are properly trained, qualified, and experienced in the relevant tasks.

Phoenix acknowledges that time is of the essence with respect to delivery of the Deliverables and completion of the Services, particularly where any delays may materially impact Contoso’s business operations.

2.5 **Delivery and Installation Timelines.**  
Specific delivery dates and installation timelines shall be set forth in the relevant SOW or PO. Unless otherwise agreed:

  a. Phoenix shall deliver hardware Deliverables DDP (Delivered Duty Paid, Incoterms 2020) to Contoso’s designated facility in Singapore;  
  b. Phoenix shall provide at least ten (10) Business Days’ notice of intended delivery and installation;  
  c. Any anticipated delay shall be notified to Contoso in writing as soon as reasonably practicable, together with a proposed recovery plan; and  
  d. If Phoenix fails to meet a Milestone or delivery date by more than fifteen (15) Business Days for reasons not attributable to Contoso or a Force Majeure Event, Contoso may, without prejudice to its other rights, require Phoenix to implement an expedited recovery plan at Phoenix’s cost, and any associated Service Credits (as defined in Article XII) shall apply.

2.6 **Acceptance.**  

  a. **Acceptance Testing.**  
  Upon completion of installation and commissioning of any Deliverable, the Parties shall conduct mutually agreed acceptance tests to verify conformity with the specifications in the applicable SOW or PO. Acceptance criteria and test procedures shall be documented prior to or at the time of installation.

  b. **Acceptance Period.**  
  Contoso shall have thirty (30) calendar days from completion of acceptance testing (the **“Acceptance Period”**) to either:
   - issue a written acceptance notice; or  
   - notify Phoenix in writing of any material non-conformities.

  c. **Remedy of Non-Conformities.**  
  Where non-conformities are notified, Phoenix shall promptly, and at its own cost, correct the non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverables for acceptance testing. The Acceptance Period shall reset only with respect to the corrected aspects.

  d. **Deemed Acceptance.**  
  If Contoso neither issues an acceptance notice nor a notice of material non-conformities within the Acceptance Period, and if the Deliverables are substantively operational and in use by Contoso in a production environment, the Deliverables may be deemed accepted, provided that such deemed acceptance shall not limit Contoso’s warranty rights under Article VII.

2.7 **Statements of Work and Purchase Orders.**  
The Parties may, from time to time, execute additional SOWs and issue POs under this Agreement. Each SOW and PO shall:

  a. reference this Agreement and Contract Reference Number **PUR-PHO-202407-135**;  
  b. describe the relevant Deliverables and Services, quantities, specifications, Milestones, and timelines; and  
  c. set out the applicable Fees and payment schedule consistent with Article III.

In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and any SOW or PO, the following order of precedence shall apply: (i) this Agreement; then (ii) the SOW; then (iii) the PO, unless the SOW or PO expressly states that it is intended to vary a specific provision of this Agreement and clearly identifies such provision.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the estimated total contract value for all Deliverables and Services contemplated as of the Effective Date is **Three Million Thirty-Nine Thousand Eight Hundred Fifteen United States Dollars (US$3,039,815)** (the **“Contract Value”**). This amount may be adjusted by mutual agreement through additional POs, SOWs, or Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall comprise:

  a. **Fixed Fees for Deliverables.**  
  Fixed unit prices or lump sum amounts for hardware, software licenses (if any), and related Deliverables, as set forth in the applicable SOW or PO.

  b. **Implementation and Professional Services Fees.**  
  Either a fixed fee or time-and-materials rates (daily or hourly rates) for implementation, integration, consulting, and training Services, as specified in the relevant SOW.

  c. **Maintenance and Support Fees.**  
  Annual or periodic fees for maintenance and support, as detailed in Article XII and the relevant SOW, which may be invoiced annually in advance unless otherwise agreed.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
Unless otherwise specified in a SOW or PO:

  a. Phoenix shall invoice Contoso in accordance with the Milestones defined in the relevant SOW or, where no Milestones are specified, monthly in arrears for Services rendered and Deliverables delivered;  
  b. All invoices shall be payable by Contoso within **thirty (30) days** of the date of the invoice (**“Net 30”**);  
  c. Invoices shall reference the applicable SOW or PO, itemize the Deliverables and Services, and state the Contract Reference Number **PUR-PHO-202407-135**.

3.4 **Payment Milestones (Illustrative).**  
For major implementation projects, Milestones may include:

  a. Ten percent (10%) of the applicable project Fees upon execution of the SOW;  
  b. Forty percent (40%) upon delivery of major hardware Deliverables to Contoso’s site;  
  c. Thirty percent (30%) upon successful completion of installation and commissioning;  
  d. Twenty percent (20%) upon final acceptance in accordance with Section 2.6.

Such Milestones shall be specifically defined in each SOW.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
If Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amount by the due date:

  a. Phoenix may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate not exceeding one percent (1%) per month (or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, if lower), accruing daily from the due date until actual payment; and  
  b. Phoenix shall provide written notice of late payment and allow Contoso an additional ten (10) Business Days to cure before taking any action to suspend Services under Section 3.6.

3.6 **Suspension for Non-Payment.**  
Subject to Section 3.5, if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid more than forty-five (45) days after its due date, Phoenix may, upon at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice to Contoso, suspend the provision of Services and further deliveries of Deliverables until such overdue amounts are paid in full. Phoenix shall resume performance promptly upon receipt of the overdue payments. Suspension shall not relieve Contoso of its obligation to pay all outstanding amounts.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.**  
Contoso may, in good faith, dispute any portion of an invoice by providing written notice to Phoenix within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the reasons for the dispute. Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice when due and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the disputed portion promptly. No interest shall accrue on disputed amounts unless and until resolved in favor of Phoenix.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of applicable taxes, including any goods and services tax (GST), value added tax (VAT), sales tax, or similar taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for any such taxes (other than taxes based on Phoenix’s income, property, or employees), provided that Phoenix shall itemize such taxes clearly on its invoices. Phoenix shall cooperate with Contoso to lawfully minimize any applicable taxes, including providing resale or exemption certificates where applicable.

3.9 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Phoenix shall be responsible for its own overhead and ordinary business costs. Out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred by Phoenix in connection with on-site Services shall be reimbursable by Contoso only if:

  a. such expenses are reasonable and in line with Contoso’s travel and expense policy, as provided to Phoenix; and  
  b. such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Contoso.

Phoenix shall provide suitable documentation (e.g., receipts) for all reimbursable expenses.

3.10 **Currency and Method of Payment.**  
All payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD), unless otherwise agreed in writing. Payments shall be made by bank transfer to the account designated by Phoenix in writing. Each Party shall bear its own bank charges.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **November 08, 2028** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Term may be extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties for one or more additional periods, each not exceeding three (3) years, on terms and conditions to be agreed at the time of renewal.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk level and client-friendly terms intended by the Parties, Contoso may terminate this Agreement, any SOW, or any PO, in whole or in part, for convenience at any time upon at least **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Phoenix. Phoenix may terminate for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso. In the event of such termination:

  a. Contoso shall pay Phoenix for Deliverables properly delivered and accepted and for Services properly performed up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments reasonably incurred by Phoenix and approved in writing by Contoso; and  
  b. Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate its losses and minimize any non-cancellable costs.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  a. Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW or PO immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement which, if capable of remedy, is not cured within thirty (30) days (or such longer period as may be agreed in writing) after receipt of written notice specifying the breach.

  b. Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation or administration (other than for purposes of a solvent restructuring), makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver or similar officer appointed over its assets.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  a. All outstanding unpaid invoices (other than those properly disputed) shall become immediately due and payable;  
  b. Phoenix shall promptly cease all work relating to the terminated SOW or PO, except as necessary to protect Contoso’s assets and facilitate a smooth transition;  
  c. Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5;  
  d. Phoenix shall cooperate reasonably with Contoso to transition the Services and Deliverables to Contoso or a replacement supplier, subject to payment of Phoenix’s reasonable transition fees (if any), to be agreed in good faith.

4.6 **Survival.**  
Any provision of this Agreement which by its nature is intended to survive expiration or termination, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

  a. use Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving Services and Deliverables under this Agreement;  
  b. not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent;  
  c. restrict access to Confidential Information to its employees, officers, contractors, and professional advisers who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those contained herein; and  
  d. protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than reasonable care.

The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  a. is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
  b. was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  c. is lawfully received from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation; or  
  d. is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for record-keeping purposes and for compliance with legal or regulatory obligations.

5.6 **Survival Period.**  
The confidentiality obligations under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except that obligations relating to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under applicable law.

SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Where Phoenix provides software systems or remote monitoring Services, Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such systems are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and agreed outages.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Phoenix may perform scheduled maintenance during off-peak hours, with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice to Contoso. Scheduled Maintenance shall not exceed eight (8) hours per month and shall be excluded from the availability calculation.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Phoenix shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times for support incidents reported by Contoso:

  a. **Priority 1 – Critical (system down, production halted):**  
     - Response time: within 1 hour;  
     - Target resolution or workaround: within 8 hours.

  b. **Priority 2 – High (major functionality impaired, no workaround):**  
     - Response time: within 4 hours;  
     - Target resolution or workaround: within 24 hours.

  c. **Priority 3 – Medium (non-critical issue, workaround available):**  
     - Response time: within 1 Business Day;  
     - Target resolution: within 5 Business Days.

  d. **Priority 4 – Low (minor issue, cosmetic, enhancement request):**  
     - Response time: within 2 Business Days;  
     - Target resolution: as reasonably scheduled in future releases.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If Phoenix fails to meet the Service Levels set forth in this Article XII in any calendar month, Contoso may be entitled to Service Credits as follows (subject to the limitations in Article IX):

  a. Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: Service Credit equal to 5% of the monthly maintenance and support Fees for the affected system;  
  b. Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: Service Credit equal to 10% of such Fees;  
  c. Availability below 98.0%: Service Credit equal to 20% of such Fees.

Service Credits shall be applied as a credit against the next invoice(s) issued by Phoenix. Service Credits are Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the Service Levels, without prejudice to Contoso’s right to terminate for repeated failures under Section 4.4.

12.5 **Chronic SLA Failures.**  
If Phoenix fails to meet the availability commitment in three (3) or more months in any rolling twelve (12)-month period, Contoso may, at its option, treat such repeated failures as a material breach and terminate the relevant SOW or PO in accordance with Section 4.4.
