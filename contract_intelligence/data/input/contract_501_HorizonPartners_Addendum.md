## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Horizon shall provide to Contoso, on a non-exclusive basis, certain enterprise technology implementation, integration, and managed support services as more fully described in this Article II and any attached Statement of Work or similar schedule (collectively, the **“Services”**). The Services shall include, without limitation:

- Design, configuration, and deployment of a cloud-based analytics platform for Contoso’s internal operations and customer-facing services;
- Data integration from multiple Contoso systems and third-party data sources into the analytics platform;
- Ongoing monitoring, maintenance, and support of such platform in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII;
- Provision of periodic performance, usage, and incident reports; and
- Training and knowledge transfer to Contoso’s personnel.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Services will be performed in the following high-level phases, each of which may contain multiple sub-tasks and milestones:

2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (Estimated 3 Months).**  
Horizon shall conduct discovery workshops with Contoso stakeholders, document requirements, and deliver a detailed solution design document. Activities shall include:

- Requirements gathering sessions with key business and technical stakeholders;
- Current-state systems assessment and data landscape analysis;
- Definition of target-state architecture, data flows, and integration points;
- Security and compliance requirements mapping; and
- Delivery of a **Solution Design and Implementation Plan** (a Deliverable).

2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Implementation and Integration (Estimated 9–12 Months).**  
Horizon shall configure, build, and integrate the platform in accordance with the approved design. Activities shall include:

- Configuration of core analytics platform components;
- Development of custom connectors and APIs for systems integration;
- Data modeling, ETL/ELT workflow development, and transformation rules;
- Implementation of role-based access controls and logging; and
- Delivery of **Configuration Documentation**, **Data Integration Specifications**, and initial **Test Reports** (Deliverables).

2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Testing, User Acceptance, and Go-Live (Estimated 3 Months).**  
Horizon will support Contoso in comprehensive testing and transition to production. Activities shall include:

- System integration testing, performance testing, and security validation;
- Support of Contoso’s user acceptance testing;
- Remediation of defects identified in agreed-upon test cycles;
- Preparation of go-live cutover plan and rollback procedures; and
- Production cutover and stabilization period of at least thirty (30) days post go-live.

2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Managed Services and Optimization (Remainder of Term).**  
Following go-live, Horizon shall provide ongoing managed services, including:

- 24x7 monitoring of platform uptime and key performance indicators in accordance with SLAs;
- Incident management and issue resolution pursuant to the response times in Article XII;
- Regular patching, minor upgrades, and security updates as reasonably required;
- Quarterly performance and capacity reviews with recommendations for optimization;
- Provision of training updates and knowledge transfer sessions as requested by Contoso (not to exceed 40 hours per quarter, unless otherwise agreed in a Change Order).

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the foregoing, the following items shall be deemed Deliverables under this Addendum:

(a) **Solution Design and Implementation Plan** (including architecture diagrams and a detailed project plan);  
(b) **Configuration Documentation** describing environment setup, parameter configurations, and system dependencies;  
(c) **Data Integration Specifications** detailing data sources, mapping, transformation rules, and schedules;  
(d) **Test Plans and Test Reports** summarizing completed tests, results, and defect logs;  
(e) **Operations Runbook** outlining standard operating procedures for incident response, monitoring, and maintenance;  
(f) **User Guides and Training Materials** for Contoso’s end-users and administrators; and  
(g) **Quarterly Service and Performance Reports**.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Horizon shall perform the Services:

- In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  
- In accordance with the Specifications, Service Levels, and timelines described in this Addendum;  
- Using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications; and  
- In compliance with all Applicable Laws, including laws governing data protection and information security.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties acknowledge that time is of the essence with respect to Horizon’s performance under this Addendum. Within thirty (30) days after the Effective Date, Horizon shall provide a detailed project plan (including milestones, dependencies, and resource allocations) for review and approval by Contoso. Once approved, the project plan shall be incorporated into this Addendum by reference. Any changes to the agreed-upon timelines or milestones shall be managed in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management).

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Contoso shall:

(a) Provide Horizon with timely access to appropriate personnel, systems, and data reasonably necessary for Horizon to perform the Services;  
(b) Designate a primary project manager who shall serve as the main point of contact for Horizon;  
(c) Review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of receipt, unless another period is expressly agreed in writing;  
(d) Obtain any necessary third-party consents for Horizon’s access to and use of third-party systems or data used in connection with the Services; and  
(e) Perform any other responsibilities expressly assigned to Contoso in this Addendum or reasonably necessary for the successful completion of the Services.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the aggregate value of this Addendum is **US$3,168,785** (the **“Contract Value”**), exclusive of any applicable taxes, subject to any increases or decreases pursuant to mutually executed Change Orders in accordance with Article XIII.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Contract Value shall be allocated approximately as follows:

(a) **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design:** US$350,000 (fixed fee);  
(b) **Phase 2 – Implementation and Integration:** US$1,500,000 (time and materials with a not-to-exceed cap);  
(c) **Phase 3 – Testing, UAT, and Go-Live:** US$618,785 (milestone-based);  
(d) **Phase 4 – Managed Services (through Expiration Date):** US$700,000 (recurring monthly support fees over the remaining Term).

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  

3.3.1 **Phase 1 Payments.**  
Fifty percent (50%) of the Phase 1 fee shall be invoiced upon commencement of Phase 1, and the remaining fifty percent (50%) shall be invoiced upon delivery of the Solution Design and Implementation Plan, regardless of whether Contoso’s formal sign-off is delayed due to review processes exceeding fifteen (15) Business Days without material objection.

3.3.2 **Phase 2 Payments.**  
Phase 2 shall be invoiced monthly in arrears based on actual time and materials incurred, subject to the not-to-exceed cap of US$1,500,000, unless increased by a Change Order. Each invoice shall include a detailed breakdown of hours by role and task. Horizon shall not exceed the cap without Contoso’s prior written approval.

3.3.3 **Phase 3 Payments.**  
Phase 3 fees shall be invoiced in three milestones:

- 40% upon completion of system integration testing;  
- 40% upon completion of user acceptance testing;  
- 20% upon successful production go-live.

3.3.4 **Phase 4 Payments.**  
Managed services fees shall be invoiced monthly in advance, based on a monthly recurring charge equal to US$700,000 divided by the number of months in the managed services period. Horizon shall provide a breakdown of the services and resources covered by the monthly charge upon request.

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
Horizon shall issue invoices to Contoso in accordance with Section 3.3. All undisputed amounts shall be payable by Contoso within **sixty (60) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 60”**). Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by electronic funds transfer to the account designated in writing by Horizon. Contoso may withhold payment of any disputed amounts, provided that Contoso: (i) pays all undisputed amounts; and (ii) provides written notice of the basis for the dispute within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the applicable invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any such disputes.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one percent (1%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, starting from the date payment was due until the date payment is received. Horizon shall not suspend or withhold Services solely due to late payment unless: (i) such amounts are more than thirty (30) days overdue; (ii) Horizon has provided at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice; and (iii) Contoso has not made payment or cured the breach within such notice period.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
The fees set forth in this Addendum are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority. Contoso shall be responsible for such taxes (other than taxes based on Horizon’s net income, property, or employees), provided that Horizon includes such taxes on its invoices and that such taxes are properly documented and invoiced in accordance with Applicable Law.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Horizon in performing the Services (including travel, lodging, and related expenses) shall be reimbursable by Contoso. Horizon shall obtain Contoso’s written approval (email sufficient) for any individual expense expected to exceed US$1,500. Reimbursable expenses shall be itemized and supported by appropriate documentation. Contoso shall reimburse such expenses in accordance with the payment terms in Section 3.4.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Addendum shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk classification of this engagement, Contoso may terminate this Addendum, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause, upon **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Horizon. Horizon may terminate this Addendum for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Contoso, provided that such termination by Horizon shall not become effective until completion of any then-active critical production cutover activities, as reasonably determined by Contoso.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.3.1 **Material Breach.**  
Either Party may terminate this Addendum immediately upon written notice to the other Party if such other Party materially breaches this Addendum and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail. If the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have an additional reasonable period (not to exceed sixty (60) days in total) to cure, provided that the breaching Party commences cure within the initial thirty (30) day period and diligently pursues such cure.

4.3.2 **Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Addendum immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they become due; (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or takes any similar action under Applicable Law.

4.4 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Addendum for any reason:

(a) Horizon shall promptly cease performing the terminated Services;  
(b) Contoso shall pay Horizon for all Services properly performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to any good-faith disputes and contingent upon receipt of proper invoices;  
(c) Horizon shall deliver to Contoso any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which Contoso has paid or will pay in accordance with this Section 4.4;  
(d) To the extent requested by Contoso, Horizon shall reasonably cooperate in transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days post-termination at Horizon’s then-current time and materials rates, unless the termination results from Horizon’s uncured material breach, in which case such transition assistance shall be provided at no additional cost to Contoso; and  
(e) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5.

4.5 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections 3.5, 3.6, 4.4, and under Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions) shall survive any expiration or termination of this Addendum, as shall any other provision which by its nature is intended to survive.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Horizon shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production analytics platform and related Services covered by this Addendum are available **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding permitted downtime as described in Section 12.2 (the **“Availability Commitment”**).

12.2 **Permitted Downtime.**  
“Permitted Downtime” means:

(a) Scheduled maintenance, provided that Horizon provides Contoso with at least seventy-two (72) hours’ advance notice of such maintenance and schedules it during off-peak hours where reasonably possible;  
(b) Emergency maintenance required to address a critical security or performance issue, provided that Horizon uses commercially reasonable efforts to notify Contoso as soon as practicable; and  
(c) Unavailability caused by factors outside Horizon’s reasonable control, including but not limited to Force Majeure Events and failures attributable to Contoso’s own systems, networks, or third-party services not controlled by Horizon.

12.3 **Measurement and Reporting.**  
Horizon shall monitor Service availability and provide Contoso with monthly availability reports upon request. The Parties shall meet at least quarterly to review Service performance, incidents, and any remediation plans.

12.4 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
Horizon shall respond to and resolve incidents as follows (measured from the time Contoso logs the incident with Horizon’s designated support channel):

- **Priority 1 (Critical):**  
  - Impact: Complete loss of Service or a critical function affecting a substantial number of users with no workaround.  
  - Response Time: Within 1 hour (24x7).  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: Within 4 hours.

- **Priority 2 (High):**  
  - Impact: Significant degradation of Service or function with a temporary workaround.  
  - Response Time: Within 4 hours (Business Days).  
  - Target Resolution: Within 1 Business Day.

- **Priority 3 (Medium):**  
  - Impact: Partial loss of Service, minor degradation, or non-critical function impacted.  
  - Response Time: Within 1 Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution: Within 5 Business Days.

- **Priority 4 (Low):**  
  - Impact: Cosmetic issues or minor inconveniences.  
  - Response Time: Within 2 Business Days.  
  - Target Resolution: Within the next scheduled maintenance release or as mutually agreed.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If the Service availability in a given calendar month falls below the Availability Commitment (excluding Permitted Downtime), Contoso shall be entitled to the following service credits, applied against the next monthly invoice for managed services:

- Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% credit of the monthly managed services fee;  
- Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% credit of the monthly managed services fee;  
- Availability below 98.0%: 15% credit of the monthly managed services fee.

Service credits shall not exceed 25% of the monthly managed services fee in any calendar month. Service credits are Contoso’s exclusive monetary remedy for Horizon’s failure to meet the Availability Commitment, without limiting Contoso’s right to terminate for material breach in accordance with Article IV.

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding strikes or labor disputes involving the affected Party’s own employees if such strikes are reasonably foreseeable), governmental actions, or failures of telecommunications or internet services not under the affected Party’s control.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature and expected duration of the event and the steps being taken to mitigate its impact. The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume full performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the obligations of the affected Party under this Addendum shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that performance is prevented or materially hindered by the Force Majeure Event, except for obligations to make payments that accrued prior to the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period of more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate this Addendum, in whole or in part, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for payment obligations for Services performed prior to the effective date of termination.
