## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.** The Vendor shall provide to the Client a comprehensive suite of professional services and managed IT services in support of the Client’s digital transformation and operations optimization program (the **"Project"**). The Services shall encompass discovery, design, implementation, transition, and steady-state managed services, as well as associated advisory and support services, all as further described in this **Article II**.

2.2 **Primary Service Components.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services shall include the following key components:

2.2.1 **Discovery and Assessment.**  
(a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and surveys to understand the Client’s existing IT landscape, business processes, applications, and pain points.  
(b) Perform an assessment of the Client’s current infrastructure, including network topology, server estate, cloud usage, application inventory, and security posture.  
(c) Review existing IT service management processes, including incident management, change management, problem management, asset management, and service request fulfillment.  
(d) Produce a **Discovery Report** summarizing the current state, key risks, and recommended areas of improvement.

2.2.2 **Solution Design and Roadmap.**  
(a) Develop a target-state architecture and operating model for the Client’s IT services, including on-premises and cloud components, integration layers, monitoring, and security controls.  
(b) Design an IT service management framework aligned with ITIL (or similar industry standards), including process flows, roles, responsibilities, and governance structures.  
(c) Prepare a phased implementation roadmap with clearly defined Milestones, dependencies, and resource requirements.  
(d) Deliver a **Solution Design Document** and **Implementation Roadmap**, which shall form part of the Deliverables and be subject to the Client’s review and approval in accordance with **Section 2.6**.

2.2.3 **Implementation and Configuration Services.**  
(a) Configure selected IT service management tools (such as ticketing, monitoring, and knowledge management systems) in accordance with the approved Solution Design.  
(b) Implement integrations between core systems (e.g., HR, finance, CRM, and existing IT tools) using APIs or other standard mechanisms, as identified and agreed by the Parties.  
(c) Migrate relevant data, including configuration data, documentation, and historical incident records, in accordance with a mutually agreed data migration plan.  
(d) Perform system tests, integration tests, and, where applicable, user acceptance testing (UAT) support, including defect remediation.

2.2.4 **Transition and Knowledge Transfer.**  
(a) Develop and deliver training materials for Client personnel regarding new or updated tools, processes, and procedures.  
(b) Conduct up to **six (6)** training workshops for designated Client employees, which may be conducted on-site or remotely by mutual agreement.  
(c) Prepare and deliver operational runbooks, standard operating procedures (SOPs), escalation matrices, and knowledge base articles.  
(d) Support a defined transition period during which the Vendor’s team will work alongside the Client’s team to stabilize operations under the new model.

2.2.5 **Managed Services.**  
(a) Provide ongoing Level 2 and Level 3 support for the configured IT service management tools and integrated systems during the Term, subject to the Service Levels in **Article XII**.  
(b) Monitor system health, capacity, and performance, and provide regular reports and recommendations for optimization.  
(c) Manage incidents, problems, changes, and service requests related to the scope of supported systems, in accordance with agreed process documentation.  
(d) Provide periodic service reviews (at least quarterly) with the Client to discuss performance, improvements, and upcoming changes.

2.3 **Deliverables.** The Vendor shall provide, at a minimum, the following Deliverables:

2.3.1 **Discovery Report** (including environment inventory, gaps, and recommendations).  
2.3.2 **Solution Design Document**, including:  
   - Target architecture diagrams;  
   - Process designs;  
   - RACI charts and governance model.  
2.3.3 **Implementation Roadmap** with Milestones, estimated effort, and dependencies.  
2.3.4 **Configured ITSM Toolset**, with agreed workflows, forms, queues, and user roles.  
2.3.5 **Integration Configurations and Documentation** for all interfaces within the agreed scope.  
2.3.6 **Data Migration Plan and Completion Report**.  
2.3.7 **Training Materials** and **Training Completion Log**.  
2.3.8 **Operational Runbooks, SOPs, and Knowledge Base Articles**.  
2.3.9 **Monthly Performance and Service Level Reports** during the managed services phase.  
2.3.10 **Project Closure Report** upon completion or termination of the Project, summarizing work performed, Deliverables provided, and outstanding items.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.** The Parties acknowledge the Term of this Agreement runs from the Effective Date through the **Expiration Date of November 28, 2029**, subject to earlier termination as provided in **Article IV**. The Parties shall agree on a more detailed project schedule within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, which may be attached as **Schedule 1 – Project Plan**. At a high level:

2.4.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Assessment:**  
Estimated duration: 8–12 weeks from Effective Date.  
Key Milestone: Delivery and Client approval of the Discovery Report.

2.4.2 **Phase 2 – Design and Roadmap:**  
Estimated duration: 8–10 weeks after completion of Phase 1.  
Key Milestone: Delivery and Client approval of Solution Design Document and Implementation Roadmap.

2.4.3 **Phase 3 – Implementation and Configuration:**  
Estimated duration: 16–24 weeks after completion of Phase 2.  
Key Milestones: Completion of configured ITSM toolset, completion of system/integration testing, UAT sign-off.

2.4.4 **Phase 4 – Transition and Stabilization:**  
Estimated duration: 8–12 weeks after Phase 3 Milestones.  
Key Milestone: Completion of knowledge transfer and transition sign-off.

2.4.5 **Phase 5 – Managed Services:**  
Commencing upon transition sign-off and continuing until the Expiration Date, unless terminated earlier in accordance with **Article IV**.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** The Vendor shall perform the Services:

2.5.1 With due care, skill, and diligence, using personnel with appropriate experience, qualifications, and training;  
2.5.2 In accordance with generally accepted industry standards and best practices for similar services;  
2.5.3 In compliance with Applicable Laws and the Client’s reasonable written policies provided in advance and agreed to by the Vendor; and  
2.5.4 In accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII** for the managed services component.

2.6 **Delivery, Review, and Acceptance.**

2.6.1 For each Deliverable identified in **Section 2.3**, the Vendor shall submit the Deliverable to the Client in electronic form (and hard copy if reasonably requested).  
2.6.2 The Client shall have **fifteen (15) Business Days** (or such other period as agreed in writing) from receipt of a Deliverable (the **"Review Period"**) to review and either:

2.6.4 Upon receiving a notice of non-conformity, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformity and re-submit the Deliverable within a reasonable time. The Client shall have a further Review Period to assess the corrected Deliverable.  
2.6.5 If, after two (2) re-submissions, the Deliverable still materially fails to conform to the Specifications, the Client may:  
(a) Accept the Deliverable with a mutually agreed reduction or credit; or  
(b) Treat such failure as a material breach and exercise its rights under **Article IV**, subject to any cure periods therein.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.** The Client shall:

2.7.1 Provide timely access to relevant systems, facilities, data, and personnel as reasonably required for the Vendor to perform the Services;  
2.7.2 Designate a project manager and key stakeholders authorized to provide approvals and decisions;

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **November 28, 2029** (the **"Expiration Date"**).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend this Agreement for additional periods and on such terms as the Parties may agree.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience, without cause, by giving the other Party not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice**. During such notice period, the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to wind down or transition the Services in an orderly manner.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement in whole or in part, effective upon written notice, if the other Party:

4.4.1 Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or  
4.4.2 Becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, has a receiver appointed, or is subject to any bankruptcy or insolvency proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Persistent Service Level Failures.** In addition to the foregoing, the Client may terminate the managed services portion of this Agreement (Phase 5) upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice if the Vendor fails to meet the critical Service Levels (as defined in **Article XII**) for three (3) consecutive months or any five (5) months in a rolling twelve (12)-month period, provided that the Vendor has been given notice and a reasonable opportunity to implement remedial action plans.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement:

4.6.1 The Vendor shall cease performing the Services (other than any transition services agreed by the Parties) and shall promptly deliver to the Client all completed or partially completed Deliverables for which the Client has paid;  
4.6.2 The Client shall pay the Vendor for:  
(a) All Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted (or deemed accepted) up to the effective date of termination; and  
(b) Any non-cancellable third-party costs or commitments reasonably incurred by the Vendor on behalf of the Client, provided such costs were pre-approved in writing;  
4.6.3 Any prepaid fees for Services not yet performed shall be refunded to the Client on a pro-rata basis;  
4.6.4 Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s written request, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information, subject to **Section 5.4**; and  
4.6.5 Termination shall not affect any rights, remedies, obligations, or liabilities of either Party that have accrued as of the effective date of termination, including any rights relating to breaches occurring prior to such date.

4.7 **Survival.** The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: **Sections 3.4–3.9**, **4.6–4.7**, and **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions)**, as well as any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

12.1 **Service Availability.** For the managed services described in **Section 2.2.5**, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the supported production systems under its direct control achieve **monthly uptime of not less than 99.9%**, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and downtime caused by Force Majeure Events or the Client’s acts or omissions.

12.2.1 The Vendor gives the Client at least **five (5) Business Days’** prior notice of planned maintenance that is expected to materially impact service availability; and  
12.2.2 Scheduled maintenance does not exceed **eight (8) hours per month**, unless otherwise agreed.

12.3 **Incident Response Times.** The Vendor shall meet the following initial response times for incidents reported by the Client:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Production system down or severe business impact – initial response within **1 hour**, 24x7.  
- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation or major functionality impaired – initial response within **4 hours** during Business Days.  
- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues or partial loss of functionality – initial response within **1 Business Day**.  
- **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries – initial response within **2 Business Days**.

12.4 **Incident Resolution Targets.** Resolution or workaround targets (non-binding but used for performance measurement) are as follows:

- Priority 1: 8 hours  
- Priority 2: 2 Business Days  
- Priority 3: 5 Business Days  
- Priority 4: To be addressed in next planned maintenance release or as otherwise agreed

12.5 **Service Credits.** If the Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitments in **Section 12.1** for a given calendar month (excluding exclusions), the Client shall be entitled to the following service credits against the managed services fees for the following month:

- Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: **5%** credit of monthly managed services fees  
- Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: **10%** credit  
- Uptime below 98.0%: **15%** credit

Service credits:

12.5.1 Shall not exceed **25%** of the monthly managed services fees for any given month;  
12.5.2 Shall be the Client’s sole monetary remedy for SLA breaches, without prejudice to the Client’s right to terminate for persistent failures under **Section 4.5**.

12.6 **Reporting.** The Vendor shall provide monthly Service Level reports summarizing uptime, incident volumes, response and resolution times, and any SLA breaches.

12.7 **Exclusions.** SLA commitments shall not apply to:

12.7.1 Issues caused by the Client’s equipment, software, or network not under the Vendor’s control;  
12.7.2 Unauthorized modifications or misuse of the systems by the Client;  
12.7.3 Outages due to Force Majeure Events; or  
12.7.4 Scheduled maintenance notified in accordance with **Section 12.2**.
