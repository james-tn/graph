## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.** Vendor shall provide Client with end-to-end consulting, implementation, and managed support services to design, deploy, and support an integrated cloud-based analytics and workflow automation platform (the **“Solution”**). The Solution will support Client’s internal operations, reporting, and decision-making processes across finance, operations, and customer service functions.

2.2 **Project Phases.** The Services shall be organized into the following major phases. Each phase may contain multiple milestones, as detailed in Sections 2.3 and 2.4:

  a. **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Estimated: March 15, 2024 – July 31, 2024).**  
  Vendor shall conduct stakeholder workshops, system assessments, and process mapping sessions to document business and technical requirements, use cases, and integration points. Vendor shall produce a comprehensive Requirements and Solution Architecture Document.

  b. **Phase 2 – Design and Architecture (Estimated: August 1, 2024 – November 30, 2024).**  
  Vendor shall design the Solution architecture, data models, API integration patterns, and security framework, aligned with Client’s IT policies. Vendor shall deliver detailed design specifications for Client review and approval.

  c. **Phase 3 – Build and Configuration (Estimated: December 1, 2024 – July 31, 2025).**  
  Vendor shall configure the cloud analytics platform, develop necessary integrations, implement data pipelines, and create standard dashboards, reports, and workflow automations as defined in the Specifications.

  d. **Phase 4 – Testing and User Acceptance (Estimated: August 1, 2025 – November 30, 2025).**  
  Vendor shall perform unit, integration, and performance testing. Client shall conduct User Acceptance Testing (**“UAT”**) with Vendor’s support. Vendor shall remedy material defects identified during UAT.

  e. **Phase 5 – Go-Live and Stabilization (Estimated: December 1, 2025 – February 28, 2026).**  
  Vendor shall support production deployment, data migration, hypercare support, and performance tuning during initial go-live and stabilization.

  f. **Phase 6 – Managed Support and Optimization (Estimated: March 1, 2026 – April 30, 2027).**  
  Vendor shall provide ongoing support, maintenance, minor enhancements, and optimization services for the Solution, consistent with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

2.3 **Detailed Description of Services.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services shall include:

  a. **Project Management.** Vendor shall assign a qualified project manager as the primary point of contact for Client, who shall be responsible for project planning, status reporting, coordination of resources, risk management, escalation of issues, and adherence to timelines and budget.

  b. **Business Analysis and Requirements Gathering.** Vendor shall conduct interviews, workshops, and data analysis to identify Client’s key business processes, reporting requirements, data sources, and performance metrics. Vendor shall document detailed functional and non-functional requirements, including data quality, security, performance, and auditability.

  c. **Solution Architecture and Design.** Vendor shall design a scalable, secure, and resilient architecture for the Solution, including data ingestion layers, data warehouses/data lakes, analytics tools, and workflow engines. Architecture shall comply with Client’s security and compliance policies, including role-based access control, encryption standards, and audit logging.

  d. **System Configuration and Development.** Vendor shall configure the selected cloud analytics and workflow platforms (e.g., data warehouses, ETL/ELT tools, BI tools, and orchestration services) and develop custom integrations with Client’s existing ERP, CRM, and other core systems. Vendor shall follow industry best practices, including source control, code review, and continuous integration processes.

  e. **Data Integration and Migration.** Vendor shall design and implement data pipelines to extract data from identified source systems, transform and cleanse such data according to agreed rules, and load it into the target data models. Vendor shall support one-time data migration for historical data as agreed, including validation checks and reconciliation reports.

  f. **Reporting, Dashboards, and Analytics.** Vendor shall develop a set of standard reports and dashboards, including (by way of example) financial performance dashboards, operational KPIs, and customer service metrics, as specified in the Requirements Document. Vendor shall provide configuration to enable authorized Client users to create additional self-service analytics artifacts.

  g. **Workflow Automation.** Vendor shall implement workflow automations for selected processes (e.g., approvals, notifications, exception handling), leveraging the workflow capabilities of the chosen platform(s), and will document such workflows for operational transparency and support.

  h. **Testing and Quality Assurance.** Vendor shall develop test plans and test cases and perform unit, integration, system, and performance testing. Vendor shall support Client in developing UAT test scripts and shall remediate defects categorized as Severity 1 or Severity 2 prior to go-live, subject to mutually agreed acceptance criteria.

  i. **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall provide training sessions (remote or onsite as agreed) for Client’s key users, administrators, and support personnel. Vendor shall prepare user guides and technical runbooks that outline operational procedures, support processes, and troubleshooting steps.

  j. **Support and Maintenance Services.** During Phase 6, Vendor shall provide support services in accordance with Article XII, including incident management, bug fixes, minor enhancements (as defined in the Service Levels), environment monitoring, and periodic health checks.

2.4 **Deliverables.** Vendor shall produce and deliver the following core Deliverables, at a minimum:

  a. **Requirements and Solution Architecture Document** (including business requirements, data sources, integration points, and high-level architecture diagrams).

  b. **Detailed Design Documentation** (including entity-relationship diagrams, data flow diagrams, interface specifications, and security design).

  c. **Configured Platform Environments** (development, test, and production environments configured according to Client’s standards).

  d. **Integration Components** (APIs, connectors, data pipelines, and related configuration scripts).

  e. **Standard Reports and Dashboards** as specified by Client and documented in the Requirements Phase.

  f. **Workflow Configuration Documentation** for each process automation implemented.

  g. **Test Plans, Test Cases, and Test Summary Reports** (including defect logs and resolution status).

  h. **User and Administrator Training Materials** (slide decks, reference guides, and quick-start guides).

  i. **Operational Runbooks** (covering monitoring, routine maintenance, escalation procedures, and backup/restore procedures).

  j. **Support and Maintenance Reports** (including monthly incident metrics, root cause analyses for Severity 1 and Severity 2 incidents, and Service Level performance reports).

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

  a. in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards;  
  b. in compliance with Applicable Laws and Client’s written policies provided to Vendor in advance;  
  c. using personnel who are appropriately qualified, experienced, and supervised; and  
  d. in a manner reasonably designed to achieve the milestones and timelines set forth in this Article II.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.** Client shall:

  a. provide timely access to Client personnel, systems, and Client Materials as reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  
  b. designate a project sponsor and primary business lead with decision-making authority to facilitate timely approvals and guidance;  
  c. review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of receipt, unless otherwise agreed; and  
  d. be responsible for procuring and maintaining any third-party licenses for platforms selected by Client that are not expressly included within Vendor’s scope.

2.7 **Assumptions.** The Services and Fees are based on the following key assumptions:

  a. Client will provide accurate and complete information reasonably required to perform the Services;  
  b. no material change in Client’s core system landscape or business requirements will occur without an agreed Change Order; and  
  c. the volume of data and number of users specified at the Effective Date will not increase by more than twenty percent (20%) without adjustment to Fees through a Change Order.

To the extent any of the above assumptions proves to be inaccurate or changes materially, the Parties shall address any necessary modifications through the change management process in Article XIII.

  b. **Phase 2 – Design and Architecture:**  
  - Twenty percent (20%) (US$143,296) upon Client’s written approval of the Detailed Design Documentation.

  c. **Phase 3 – Build and Configuration:**  
  - Thirty percent (30%) (US$214,944) upon completion of initial configuration and integration development and Vendor’s provision of a build completion report.

  - Fifteen percent (15%) (US$107,472) upon successful completion of UAT, evidenced by Client’s written acceptance.

  e. **Phase 5 – Go-Live and Stabilization:**  
  - Fifteen percent (15%) (US$107,472) upon completion of go-live and stabilization period (defined as sixty (60) days after production deployment) and delivery of a stabilization summary report.

3.4 **Payment of Managed Support Fees.** The managed support portion (US$180,000) for Phase 6 shall be invoiced in six (6) equal quarterly installments of **US$30,000** each, in advance of each quarter during the support period, starting March 1, 2026.

Vendor shall not suspend Services solely due to late payment unless: (a) such payment is more than thirty (30) days overdue, and (b) Vendor has provided Client with at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice and an opportunity to cure.

Vendor shall comply with Client’s travel and expense policies provided in advance. Vendor shall submit itemized expense reports with supporting receipts, and Client shall reimburse approved expenses within **forty-five (45) days** of receipt.

3.9 **Disputed Amounts.** Client may withhold payment of any portion of an invoice that Client reasonably and in good faith disputes, provided that Client: (a) pays the undisputed portion when due; and (b) notifies Vendor in writing of the specific reasons for the dispute within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice. ## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Scope.** This Article XII sets forth the Service Levels applicable to the managed support Services provided by Vendor during Phase 6.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Solution (to the extent under Vendor’s direct control) is available to Client with an uptime of at least **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure events.

12.3 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may schedule maintenance windows during which the Solution may be unavailable, provided that:  
  a. Scheduled Maintenance shall not exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month; and  
  b. Vendor shall provide Client with at least five (5) Business Days’ advance notice of any Scheduled Maintenance expected to materially impact availability.

12.4 **Incident Severity Levels and Response Times.** Vendor shall provide incident support as follows:

  a. **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or major functionality unavailable with no workaround, significantly impacting Client’s critical business operations.  
     - Initial Response: within one (1) hour during support hours.  
     - Target Resolution or Workaround: within eight (8) hours.

  b. **Severity 2 (High):** Significant loss of functionality or performance degradation with a workaround available, impacting many users or key operations.  
     - Initial Response: within two (2) hours during support hours.  
     - Target Resolution: within twenty-four (24) hours.

  c. **Severity 3 (Medium):** Limited impact issues with acceptable workaround; non-critical functionality affected.  
     - Initial Response: within one (1) Business Day.  
     - Target Resolution: within five (5) Business Days.

  d. **Severity 4 (Low):** Cosmetic issues, minor defects, or general inquiries.  
     - Initial Response: within two (2) Business Days.  
     - Target Resolution: as mutually agreed in backlog.

12.5 **Service Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.2 for a given calendar month (excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure events), Client shall be entitled to a service credit equal to:

  - 5% of the monthly managed support Fees for uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%;  
  - 10% of the monthly managed support Fees for uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%;  
  - 15% of the monthly managed support Fees for uptime below 98.0%.

Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) for managed support Fees and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for Service Level failures, subject to Client’s termination rights under Section 4.5.

12.6 **Reporting.** Vendor shall provide Client with monthly Service Level reports summarizing uptime, incident volumes, response and resolution times, and any Service Level failures.
