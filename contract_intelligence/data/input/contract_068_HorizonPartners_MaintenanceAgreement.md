## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall provide the Company with comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Equipment and systems identified in one or more SOWs. Such Services may include, without limitation:

  2.1.1 **Preventive Maintenance.** Scheduled inspections, testing, calibration, cleaning, lubrication, adjustments, replacement of consumables, firmware and software updates, and related activities intended to prevent failures and extend Equipment life.

  2.1.2 **Corrective Maintenance.** Diagnosis, troubleshooting, repair, and restoration activities in response to Incidents, malfunctions, performance degradation, or failures of Equipment or systems covered under this Agreement.

  2.1.3 **Emergency Support.** Support and remediation services for critical or high-severity Incidents that materially impair the Company’s ability to operate critical business functions, subject to the Response Times and escalation procedures set forth in Article XII.

  2.1.4 **Monitoring and Remote Support.** Where specified in a SOW, remote monitoring of system performance metrics, log analysis, proactive alerts, and remote troubleshooting using secure connectivity tools.

  2.1.5 **Spare Parts and Replacement Components.** Subject to the terms of the applicable SOW, the Service Provider shall maintain or arrange access to an inventory of spare parts and replacement components for Equipment in order to meet Restoration Time commitments.

  2.1.6 **Documentation and Reporting.** Preparation and delivery of maintenance logs, service records, incident reports, root cause analyses, and performance summaries, including periodic reports detailing Services performed, trends observed, and recommendations for improvements.

  2.1.7 **Consultation and Advisory Services.** Upon the Company’s reasonable request, the Service Provider shall provide advisory services regarding lifecycle planning, upgrades, capacity planning, and best practices for maintaining Equipment and associated systems.

2.2 **Detailed Description of Services.**  
The specific scope, nature, and technical details of the Services shall be set forth in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. Each SOW shall, at a minimum:

  (a) identify the covered Equipment and locations;  
  (b) set forth the schedule for Planned Maintenance (e.g., monthly, quarterly, annual);  
  (c) define service hours (e.g., 8x5 standard support, 24x7 emergency support);  
  (d) describe the applicable Response Times and Restoration Times;  
  (e) specify any Deliverables (e.g., quarterly maintenance reports, incident logs); and  
  (f) allocate any responsibilities between the Parties (e.g., access, safety, permits).

In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern, except where the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede specific provisions of this Agreement, in which case such specifically identified provisions shall be superseded for that SOW only.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  2.3.1 **Professional Manner.** In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for maintenance of the type of Equipment covered hereunder, and with due skill, care, and diligence.

  2.3.2 **Compliance with Laws.** In compliance with all Applicable Laws, including occupational safety and health regulations and applicable environmental regulations.

  2.3.3 **Qualified Personnel.** Using appropriately trained, experienced, and qualified personnel, each of whom will comply with the Company’s reasonable site rules, security policies, safety procedures, and access requirements provided in advance to the Service Provider.

  2.3.4 **Minimization of Disruption.** In a manner designed to minimize disruption to the Company’s normal business operations, including, where commercially reasonable, scheduling Planned Maintenance during agreed maintenance windows or non-peak periods.

  2.3.5 **Service Levels.** In accordance with the Service Level Agreement set forth in Article XII, including Response Times, Restoration Times, uptime targets, and associated remedies.

2.4 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall:

  2.4.1 Provide the Service Provider with reasonable access to the Company’s premises, Equipment, systems, and relevant personnel as necessary for the performance of the Services.

  2.4.2 Ensure that all necessary permits, clearances, and authorizations for access to facilities and Equipment are obtained, except where expressly stated to be the responsibility of the Service Provider in an applicable SOW.

  2.4.3 Provide timely notice to the Service Provider of any Incidents, anomalies, or conditions that may require maintenance or repair, providing sufficient detail to enable initial triage.

  2.4.4 Maintain adequate environmental and operating conditions for the Equipment, including power, cooling, and ventilation, consistent with the manufacturer’s specifications and any guidelines provided by the Service Provider.

  2.4.5 Not modify, move, or allow third parties to service the Equipment in a manner that materially interferes with the Service Provider’s ability to perform the Services, except as expressly permitted in writing.

2.5 **Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly set forth in an SOW or otherwise agreed in writing:

  2.5.1 The Services shall not include repairs necessitated by (a) misuse, abuse, neglect, or improper operation of the Equipment; (b) modifications or repairs not authorized by the Service Provider; (c) natural disasters or Force Majeure Events; or (d) use of non-approved parts or supplies.

  2.5.2 The Service Provider shall have no obligation to provide Services relating to Equipment that is end-of-life, unsupported by the original manufacturer, or otherwise beyond reasonably supportable condition; provided that the Service Provider shall notify the Company and, if requested, propose alternatives.

Any excluded services requested by the Company may be provided by the Service Provider subject to mutual agreement and execution of a change order under Article XIII, and shall be billed at the Service Provider’s then-current time and materials rates, unless otherwise agreed.

2.6 **Subcontracting.**  
The Service Provider may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that (a) the Service Provider remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors; (b) any subcontractor that will have access to the Company’s Confidential Information or Personal Data is bound by written confidentiality and data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth herein; and (c) the Service Provider remains the single point of contact and accountability to the Company.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until **May 24, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for additional periods under mutually agreed terms and fees. Any such renewal shall be documented in a written amendment or renewal SOW executed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile of this engagement and subject to the terms herein, either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any individual SOW), for convenience upon **not less than thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. In the event of such termination:

  (a) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination; and  
  (b) With respect to any prepaid Fees applicable to periods after the effective date of termination, the Service Provider shall refund the pro-rata portion of such prepaid Fees within thirty (30) days after termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  4.4.1 Materially breaches any material term of this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; provided that, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have such additional time as may be reasonably necessary to cure, not to exceed sixty (60) days from receipt of notice, so long as it commences cure within the initial thirty (30) days and diligently pursues such cure; or  

  4.4.2 Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Partial Termination.**  
Termination of any SOW shall not automatically terminate this Agreement or any other SOW, unless expressly stated in the notice of termination or unless the basis for termination also applies to the Agreement as a whole. In case of termination of one or more SOWs, the Agreement shall remain in effect for the remaining SOWs.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  4.6.1 The Service Provider shall cease performing the affected Services, except to the extent reasonably necessary to effect an orderly transition, as described in Section 4.7.

  4.6.2 Each Party shall promptly return or, at the disclosing Party’s written instruction, securely destroy any Confidential Information of the other Party relating to the terminated portion of the Agreement, subject to Section 5.4.

  4.6.3 The Company shall pay the Service Provider all outstanding, undisputed Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued through the effective date of termination.

  4.6.4 Any rights and obligations that by their nature are intended to survive (including without limitation payment obligations, confidentiality, indemnities, IP ownership, limitations of liability, dispute resolution) shall survive in accordance with Section 16.10.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement (other than termination by the Service Provider due to the Company’s uncured material breach where the Service Provider elects not to provide transition services), the Service Provider shall, at the Company’s request and expense, provide reasonable transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days after termination to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to the Company or a third-party provider. Such assistance may include knowledge transfer, documentation handover, and coordination with successor service providers.

  (c) Is received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Where the Services include ongoing support and maintenance of systems that impact service availability, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the covered systems meet a **monthly uptime target of 99.9%**, excluding scheduled Planned Maintenance, approved outage windows, and downtime attributable to Force Majeure Events or the Company’s actions or omissions.

12.2 **Response Times.**  
The Service Provider shall meet the following Response Time targets for Incidents reported by the Company:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of a critical business function or widespread system outage.  
    - Response Time: within **1 hour**, 24x7.  

  - **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation impacting important business functions with no readily available workaround.  
    - Response Time: within **4 hours**, during business hours (with best efforts outside business hours).  

  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial degradation with workaround available; limited impact.  
    - Response Time: within **1 Business Day**.  

  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
    - Response Time: within **3 Business Days**.

12.3 **Restoration Targets.**  
The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the following Restoration Time targets:

  - Severity 1: Restore service to an operational state within **8 hours** of initial response.  
  - Severity 2: Restore service within **2 Business Days** of initial response.  
  - Severity 3: Restore service within **5 Business Days** of initial response.  
  - Severity 4: As mutually agreed, based on prioritization and resource availability.

12.4 **Maintenance Windows.**  
Planned Maintenance that may cause service interruption shall be scheduled during agreed maintenance windows, and the Service Provider shall provide the Company with at least **five (5) Business Days’** advance notice of such maintenance, except in emergency situations where shorter notice may be acceptable.

12.5 **Reporting and Measurement.**  
The Service Provider shall maintain records sufficient to measure compliance with the SLA, including Incident logs and uptime reports, and shall provide such reports to the Company on at least a **quarterly** basis or upon request.

12.6 **SLA Remedies.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime target or Restoration Time targets for a given calendar month, and such failure materially impacts the Company’s operations, the Company shall be entitled, as its sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures (absent willful misconduct or gross negligence), to service credits as follows:

  - For each full 0.5% below the 99.9% uptime target in a given month (excluding permitted downtime as described in Section 12.1), the Company shall receive a service credit equal to **2%** of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services, up to a maximum of **10%** of such monthly recurring Fees for that month.

Service credits shall be applied to future invoices or, if no further invoices are due, refunded to the Company. The Company must request service credits in writing within sixty (60) days after the end of the month in which the SLA failure occurred.

### FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond a Party’s reasonable control that renders its performance under this Agreement impossible or substantially impracticable, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes, labor disputes (excluding those involving the non-performing Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, power outages not caused by the non-performing Party, and widespread failures of the public internet or telecommunications networks.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event; and  

  (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended to the extent prevented or delayed by the Force Majeure Event, and the time for performance shall be extended accordingly.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially prevents performance of a material obligation under this Agreement, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or this Agreement upon written notice, without liability other than payment for Services performed and expenses incurred up to the date of termination.
