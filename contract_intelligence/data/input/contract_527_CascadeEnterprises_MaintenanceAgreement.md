## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

**2.1 General.** Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Equipment and related systems at the facilities designated by Client in one or more SOWs. The Services shall include, without limitation, the activities described in this Article II, which may be further specified in any SOW. In the event of conflict between this Article II and an SOW, the SOW shall control solely with respect to the subject matter of such SOW, provided that the SOW expressly references and supersedes the conflicting provision.

**2.2 Preventive Maintenance.**  

2.2.1 Vendor shall perform scheduled preventive maintenance on the Equipment in accordance with (a) manufacturers’ recommended service intervals, (b) applicable industry standards (including, where applicable, ASHRAE, NFPA, and IEEE guidelines), and (c) any additional schedules agreed upon by the Parties in writing.

2.2.2 Preventive maintenance shall include, as appropriate for the particular Equipment: inspection, cleaning, lubrication, calibration, adjustment, replacement of consumables (filters, belts, fluids, seals, etc.), testing of safety and control systems, firmware updates, and performance verification.

2.2.3 Vendor shall prepare and maintain a written Preventive Maintenance Schedule for each covered site, indicating the tasks, frequency, necessary mobilization, and expected downtime. Vendor shall provide Client with updated copies of such schedule at least quarterly and upon reasonable request.

**2.3 Corrective and Remedial Maintenance.**  

2.3.1 Vendor shall provide corrective maintenance to diagnose and repair faults, failures, or degradation of the Equipment or supported systems. Corrective maintenance may be initiated by Client’s request, Vendor’s monitoring alerts, or automated system notifications.

2.3.2 Vendor shall respond to and resolve service incidents in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII. Vendor shall assign qualified personnel and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to Client’s operations.

2.3.3 Corrective maintenance includes troubleshooting, replacement or repair of faulty components, firmware or software updates to supported systems, recalibration, reconfiguration, and verification testing to confirm restoration of normal operation.

**2.4 Emergency Maintenance.**  

2.4.1 Vendor shall provide 24x7x365 emergency maintenance services for critical failures that materially impact Client’s ability to operate essential business functions at a covered facility (for example, complete loss of HVAC in critical rooms, major power distribution failure, or immediate safety hazards).

2.4.2 Vendor shall maintain an on-call team capable of remote diagnostics and dispatch of field technicians. Response and escalation times for emergency incidents shall be governed by the Service Levels in Article XII.

2.4.3 Vendor shall coordinate emergency activities with Client’s designated contact(s) and shall promptly communicate status updates, interim workarounds, and estimated time to resolution.

**2.5 Monitoring and Remote Support.**  

2.5.1 Where Client’s systems and network access allow, Vendor may provide remote monitoring of certain Equipment via building automation systems, SCADA, or other monitoring tools. Vendor shall use such monitoring solely for the purpose of delivering the Services and shall comply with the data protection obligations in Article XI.

2.5.2 Vendor shall remotely review alarms, trends, and performance metrics; identify anomalies; and recommend or implement corrective actions consistent with Client’s change control requirements.

2.5.3 Remote access credentials and permissions shall be governed by Client’s security policies. Vendor shall not circumvent security controls or introduce tools/software without Client’s prior written approval.

**2.6 Documentation, Reporting, and Deliverables.**  

2.6.1 Vendor shall maintain accurate, complete, and up-to-date maintenance records for all Services performed, including date and time, description of work, parts used, labor hours, test results, and responsible personnel.

2.6.2 Vendor shall provide Client with the following Deliverables, at minimum:

  (a) **Monthly Maintenance Reports** summarizing preventive and corrective activities, open issues, and recommendations;

  (b) **Incident Tickets and Logs** documenting all service calls and emergencies, including root-cause analysis where appropriate;

  (c) **Quarterly Performance Reports** comparing Equipment performance to Specifications, Service Levels, and historical baselines, along with recommendations for improvements or capital replacement;

  (d) **Annual Asset Condition Assessment**, including estimated remaining useful life, priority scoring, and budgetary recommendations for upgrades or replacements.

2.6.3 All Deliverables shall be provided in electronic format (PDF and/or spreadsheet or such format agreed between the Parties) and shall meet reasonable professional standards of clarity, accuracy, and completeness.

**2.7 Performance Standards.**  

2.7.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, with the degree of skill, care, and diligence ordinarily exercised by qualified professionals performing similar services under similar conditions, and in accordance with (a) this Agreement, (b) the Specifications and Service Levels, and (c) all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards.

2.7.2 Vendor shall ensure that all personnel assigned to the Services are properly trained, licensed (where applicable), and competent to perform their assigned tasks. Vendor shall be responsible for the acts and omissions of its personnel, subcontractors, and agents.

2.7.3 Vendor shall coordinate scheduling of on-site work with Client, use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid disruption of Client’s critical operations, and comply with Client’s site-access, safety, and security policies, as communicated in writing to Vendor.

**2.8 Exclusions.**  

2.8.1 Unless explicitly stated in an SOW or Change Order, the Services do not include: major capital replacements of Equipment (e.g., full chiller, generator, or switchgear replacement), structural building repairs, design/engineering of new systems, construction, or services unrelated to the Equipment.

2.8.2 Vendor shall promptly notify Client when Vendor reasonably determines that Equipment has reached the end of its useful life or that a major capital replacement, upgrade, or redesign is recommended. Any such capital projects shall be subject to separate agreement or SOW.

**2.9 Timeline and Milestones.**  

2.9.1 Vendor shall commence provision of the Services on the Effective Date and shall continue through the Expiration Date, subject to Article IV.

2.9.2 Within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, Vendor shall complete an initial site survey and baseline assessment of the Equipment, and deliver an initial Preventive Maintenance Schedule and Asset Condition Report.

2.9.3 Recurring milestones, including monthly reports, quarterly reviews, and annual assessments, shall be delivered in accordance with Sections 2.6 and 2.7 and any more detailed timeline set forth in an SOW.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

**3.1 Contract Value.** The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables under this Agreement is **US$3,707,869** (the “Contract Value”), exclusive of any applicable taxes, unless otherwise stated in this Article III or an SOW.

**3.2 Fee Structure.**  

3.2.1 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Contract Value shall be allocated as follows:

  (a) **Fixed Annual Maintenance Fees.** A fixed fee of US$2,900,000 allocated over the Term, to cover standard preventive and corrective maintenance, remote monitoring, reporting, and management services;

  (b) **Contingency and Variable Services.** A not-to-exceed amount of US$700,000 to cover emergency maintenance, non-standard corrective work, and other variable services performed on a time-and-materials basis, as approved by Client in advance;

  (c) **Implementation and Transition.** A one-time fee of US$107,869 for initial site assessment, onboarding, asset registry creation, and initial configuration of monitoring tools.

3.2.2 Any additional services requested by Client beyond the defined scope, or any changes to the scope or Service Levels, shall be subject to the Change Order process described in Article XIII, including adjustments to fees and schedule.

**3.3 Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for (a) one-twelfth (1/12) of the applicable fixed annual maintenance fees; (b) any time-and-materials or contingency services performed in the preceding month; and (c) reimbursable expenses, if any, in accordance with Section 3.6.

3.3.2 All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable **Net 45** days from Client’s receipt of the invoice.

3.3.3 Invoices shall reference this Agreement, the contract number **MNT-CAS-202502-527**, the relevant SOW (if applicable), and shall include sufficient supporting documentation to reasonably substantiate the charges (e.g., time logs, parts lists, and receipts).

**3.4 Late Payment.**  

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the date due until the date paid.

3.4.2 Vendor shall provide written notice to Client of any delinquent payment. If any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the due date, Vendor may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, suspend provision of non-critical Services until such amounts are paid in full; provided, however, that Vendor shall continue to provide emergency maintenance to preserve safety and prevent material damage to property.

**3.5 Disputed Amounts.**  

3.5.1 Client may in good faith dispute an invoice or any portion thereof by providing written notice to Vendor within thirty (30) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute.

3.5.2 Client shall pay all undisputed portions of an invoice in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts promptly. Vendor shall not exercise its suspension or other remedies with respect to any amount that is the subject of a reasonable and documented dispute, provided Client is cooperating in good faith to resolve the dispute.

**3.6 Expenses and Reimbursements.**  

3.6.1 Routine travel and ordinary tools and consumables necessary to perform the Services at Client’s facilities within the continental United States are included in the Fees and shall not be separately invoiced.

3.6.2 Extraordinary or out-of-pocket expenses (such as long-distance travel requested by Client, expedited shipping of major components, or third-party specialist services) shall be reimbursed by Client at actual cost, without markup, provided that: (a) such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Client; and (b) Vendor provides reasonable supporting documentation (receipts, vendor invoices, etc.).

**3.7 Taxes.**  

3.7.1 The Fees do not include any sales, use, gross receipts, value added, or other similar transaction taxes (“Transaction Taxes”). Client shall be responsible for any applicable Transaction Taxes properly imposed on the Services, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employment.

3.7.2 If Vendor is required by law to collect Transaction Taxes from Client, Vendor shall include such taxes on its invoices, and Client shall pay such amounts unless Client timely provides a valid exemption certificate.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

**4.1 Term.** The term of this Agreement (the “Term”) shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **June 16, 2026** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

**4.2 Renewal.**  

4.2.1 The Parties may mutually agree in writing, no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, to renew this Agreement for one or more additional periods of twelve (12) months each, subject to mutually agreed fees and terms at the time of renewal.

4.2.2 Unless and until renewed in writing, this Agreement shall automatically expire on the Expiration Date, with no obligation on either Party to renew.

**4.3 Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 **Client-Favorable Right.** Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience at any time upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor.

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client, and only after completion of any critical ongoing maintenance activities that, if abandoned, would pose a material risk of damage or safety issues.

**4.4 Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement in its entirety upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of nonpayment) after receipt of written notice specifying the nature of the breach.

4.4.2 Either Party may immediately terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (d) ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

**4.5 Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  

4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) Vendor shall promptly stop work, except to the extent reasonably necessary to protect persons and property, and shall cooperate with Client in an orderly transition of the Services to Client or another service provider;

  (b) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and reimbursable expenses properly incurred through the effective date of termination (the “Termination Date”), subject to Client’s right to withhold payment for defective or non-conforming Services;

  (c) Vendor shall deliver to Client, within thirty (30) days after the Termination Date, all completed and in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid, all maintenance records, and any Client Data or Client Confidential Information in Vendor’s possession, in accordance with Sections 5.4 and 11.8;

  (d) Any pre-paid but unearned Fees (for Services not performed) shall be promptly refunded to Client on a pro rata basis.

4.5.2 Termination or expiration shall not relieve either Party of any obligation that has accrued as of the Termination Date, nor shall it waive any rights or remedies available to either Party for any breach occurring prior to termination.

**4.6 Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and Sections 3.4, 3.5, and 4.5.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

**12.1 Uptime Commitment.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the critical systems and Equipment under Vendor’s direct maintenance and monitoring achieve a minimum uptime of **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding:

12.1.1 Planned Downtime agreed in advance with Client; and

12.1.2 Downtime caused by events outside Vendor’s reasonable control, including Force Majeure Events, failures of third-party utilities, or failures of equipment not under Vendor’s maintenance responsibility.

**12.2 Response and Resolution Times.**  

12.2.1 For service requests and incidents, Vendor shall meet the following initial response times (measured from the time of notification by Client or alert by monitoring system):

  (a) **Critical Incidents** (complete failure of critical systems, safety hazards): response within one (1) hour, 24x7x365;

  (b) **High Priority** (significant degradation affecting key operations): response within four (4) hours during Business Days, with best efforts outside Business Days;

  (c) **Medium Priority** (non-critical, but impacting comfort or non-essential operations): response within one (1) Business Day;

  (d) **Low Priority** (minor issues, cosmetic or informational): response within three (3) Business Days.

12.2.2 Resolution times shall be commercially reasonable and shall depend on the nature and severity of the incident, availability of parts, and access constraints. Vendor shall provide Client with an estimated time to repair and regular updates for all Critical and High Priority incidents.

**12.3 Service Credits.**  

12.3.1 If Vendor fails to achieve the monthly uptime commitment in Section 12.1 for systems within Vendor’s maintenance scope, and such failure is attributable to Vendor, Client shall be entitled to a service credit equal to a percentage of the monthly fixed Fees for the affected systems, as follows:

  (a) Uptime between 99.5% and 99.9%: 2% credit;  
  (b) Uptime between 99.0% and 99.5%: 5% credit;  
  (c) Uptime below 99.0%: 10% credit.

12.3.2 Client must request Service Credits in writing within thirty (30) days following the month in which the Service Level failure occurred, providing reasonable detail. Service Credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash.

12.3.3 Service Credits shall be Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet Service Levels, subject to Article IX and Vendor’s obligations to correct deficiencies.

The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.
