## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Engagement.**  
During the Term, Summit Tech shall provide the Services to the Company as an independent provider of professional and employment-related services. The Services shall consist of (a) staffing of specialized technical, engineering, and project management personnel; (b) software development and systems integration; (c) support and maintenance services; (d) consulting, training, and knowledge transfer; and (e) such other related services as may be described in one or more SOWs executed under this Agreement.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) Each Project shall be described in a separate SOW that references this Agreement by its reference number **AGR-SUM-202312-508**.  
(b) Each SOW shall set forth, at a minimum: (i) a description of the Services and Deliverables; (ii) the Project schedule and milestones; (iii) applicable Service Levels; (iv) the fees and payment schedule; and (v) any additional terms and conditions specific to such SOW.  
(c) In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and the terms of an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern, unless an SOW expressly states that it overrides specified provisions of this Agreement.

2.3 **Nature of Services.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services may include:

  2.3.1 **Technical Staffing and Employment Services.**  
  Provision of Vendor Personnel with skills in software engineering, data science, DevOps, cybersecurity, UI/UX design, quality assurance, cloud infrastructure, and related disciplines. Vendor Personnel may be assigned on a full-time, part-time, or project basis, as mutually agreed. Summit Tech shall be responsible for all employment-related obligations with respect to Vendor Personnel, including wages, benefits, payroll taxes, workers’ compensation insurance, and compliance with Applicable Law, except as expressly otherwise provided in this Agreement or a specific SOW.

  2.3.2 **Software Development and Integration.**  
  Design, development, configuration, customization, and integration of software applications, microservices, APIs, and related technology components to support the Company’s business operations, including but not limited to enterprise systems, customer-facing applications, internal tools, and integrations with third-party platforms.

  2.3.3 **Advisory and Consulting Services.**  
  Technical and strategic consulting regarding solution architecture, technology stack selection, security best practices, DevOps pipelines, and related subjects. Summit Tech may conduct assessments of the Company’s existing systems and provide written recommendations.

  2.3.4 **Support and Maintenance.**  
  Ongoing support services including bug fixing, incident response, performance tuning, routine maintenance, and periodic enhancements, in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and any applicable SOW.

  2.3.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
  Development and delivery of training programs, workshops, documentation, and knowledge transfer sessions to the Company’s personnel to enable effective use, operation, and maintenance of the Deliverables.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Summit Tech shall perform the Services (i) in a timely, diligent, and professional manner; (ii) in accordance with the standards of care, skill, and diligence customarily exercised by experienced professionals performing services of a similar nature; and (iii) in material compliance with Applicable Law and the terms of this Agreement and any applicable SOW.  

(b) Summit Tech shall use reasonable efforts to meet any schedules and milestones specified in an SOW. If Summit Tech becomes aware that it is unlikely to meet a milestone or deadline, Summit Tech shall promptly notify the Company in writing, explaining the reasons for the delay and proposing a corrective action plan.  

(c) The Company may review and accept or reject Deliverables pursuant to acceptance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW. If no acceptance procedure is specified, the Company shall have thirty (30) days after delivery to review each Deliverable and either (i) provide written notice of acceptance, or (ii) provide a written explanation of any material nonconformities. Summit Tech shall use commercially reasonable efforts to promptly cure such nonconformities, after which the Company shall have a further fifteen (15) days to re-test and accept. If the Company fails to respond within the applicable review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, except where such non-response is due to Summit Tech’s failure to deliver or other material breach.

2.5 **Vendor Personnel; Replacement.**  
(a) Summit Tech shall assign Vendor Personnel who are appropriately qualified and experienced to perform the Services. Summit Tech shall be responsible for the supervision, direction, and control of all Vendor Personnel.  

(b) The Company shall have the right, for reasonable cause and upon written notice, to request the removal or replacement of any Vendor Personnel whose performance or conduct materially interferes with the provision of the Services or the Company’s operations. Summit Tech shall use reasonable efforts to promptly replace such Vendor Personnel with a suitable resource, subject to availability and mutual agreement on any impact to fees or schedule.  

(c) Vendor Personnel shall, when on the Company’s premises or accessing the Company’s systems, comply with all security, safety, and conduct policies of the Company communicated to Summit Tech in writing.

2.6 **Locations and Remote Work.**  
The Services may be performed at the Company’s premises, at Summit Tech’s facilities, or remotely, as specified in an SOW or as otherwise agreed in writing by the Parties. Summit Tech shall ensure that any remote access is secure and complies with the Company’s security requirements and Data Protection Laws.

2.7 **Tools, Equipment, and Facilities.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Summit Tech shall provide and be responsible for its own tools, equipment, and facilities necessary to perform the Services. If the Company elects to provide any equipment, access, or facilities, such items shall remain the property of the Company and shall be used solely for the performance of the Services.

2.8 **Compliance with Policies and Laws.**  
Summit Tech shall perform the Services in compliance with all Applicable Laws and with the Company’s internal policies and procedures (including security, privacy, and code of conduct policies) that are provided or made available to Summit Tech.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The aggregate value of this Agreement, inclusive of all Services and Deliverables under all SOWs executed hereunder, shall not exceed **Four Million Six Hundred Sixty-Six Thousand Three Hundred Three U.S. Dollars (US$4,666,303)** (the “**Contract Value**”), unless the Parties expressly agree in a written amendment or Change Order.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees may be structured as one or a combination of the following, as specified in each SOW:

  (a) **Time and Materials (T&M).** Hourly or daily rates for Vendor Personnel, multiplied by the actual number of hours or days worked.  

  (b) **Fixed Price.** A fixed amount for the completion of defined Deliverables or milestones.  

  (c) **Retainer or Monthly Service Fees.** A recurring monthly fee for specified capacity, support, or services.

3.3 **Sample Fee Schedule.**  
Without limiting the foregoing and by way of illustration, an SOW may specify rates substantially similar to the following (subject to final agreement in each SOW):

  - Senior Software Engineer: US$175 per hour  
  - DevOps Engineer: US$165 per hour  
  - Data Scientist: US$185 per hour  
  - Project Manager: US$150 per hour  
  - QA Engineer: US$135 per hour  

3.4 **Invoicing.**  
(a) Summit Tech shall invoice the Company for Services performed and Deliverables accepted in accordance with the billing schedule set forth in each SOW. If no schedule is specified, Summit Tech may invoice monthly in arrears.  

(b) Each invoice shall reference the Agreement number **AGR-SUM-202312-508**, identify the applicable SOW, describe the Services and Deliverables provided, specify the billing period, and provide any supporting documentation reasonably required by the Company (e.g., time sheets for T&M work).  

(c) Invoices shall be sent electronically to the Company’s designated accounts payable contact, or such other address as the Company may designate in writing.

3.5 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) The Company shall pay undisputed amounts on each invoice within **sixty (60) days** of the Company’s receipt of such invoice (“**Net 60**” payment terms).  

(b) If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Company shall notify Summit Tech in writing within thirty (30) days of receipt, specifying the nature of the dispute. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. The Company shall pay all undisputed portions of the invoice in accordance with this Section 3.5, and payment of any disputed amounts shall be deferred until resolution of the dispute.

3.6 **Late Payment.**  
Undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law; provided that Summit Tech may not charge interest (and interest shall not accrue) on any amounts disputed in good faith by the Company.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes (“**Transaction Taxes**”). The Company shall be responsible for any Transaction Taxes that are imposed on the Services or Deliverables, other than taxes based on Summit Tech’s net income, property, or employees.  

(b) Summit Tech shall separately state any applicable Transaction Taxes on its invoices and shall cooperate with the Company in providing documentation to support any exemptions or tax credits.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
(a) Summit Tech shall be responsible for its own normal business expenses (including overhead, equipment, and standard administrative costs).  

(b) The Company shall reimburse Summit Tech for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred in connection with the Services, in accordance with the Company’s travel and expense policy provided to Summit Tech. Summit Tech shall provide original receipts or other documentation reasonably required by the Company.  

(c) Any single expense exceeding US$1,000 shall require the Company’s prior written approval.

3.9 **No Withholding of Services.**  
Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement, Summit Tech shall not suspend or withhold Services due to a payment dispute, provided that the Company is in material compliance with its payment obligations for undisputed amounts and the Parties are actively and in good faith working to resolve any such dispute.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 22, 2025** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be extended or renewed for additional periods only by written agreement of the Parties executed prior to the Expiration Date. Absent such written agreement, this Agreement shall automatically expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Summit Tech. Summit Tech may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Company. This Section 4.3 is intended to be client-friendly consistent with the “Low” risk designation.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof (or, if such breach is not capable of cure within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period); or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets.

4.5 **Termination of SOWs Only.**  
Unless otherwise specified in the notice of termination, termination of a particular SOW shall not terminate any other SOW or this Agreement. Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall, however, automatically terminate all then-active SOWs, subject to the obligations in Section 4.6.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon any termination or expiration of this Agreement or an SOW:

  (a) Summit Tech shall immediately cease performance of the terminated Services and shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate costs and expenses.  

  (b) The Company shall pay Summit Tech for (i) all Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted through the effective date of termination, and (ii) any non-cancellable, pre-approved expenses incurred by Summit Tech prior to the effective date of termination, subject to the Contract Value and the Company’s right to dispute invoiced amounts.  

  (c) Summit Tech shall promptly deliver to the Company all completed Deliverables and any partially completed work, as well as any Client Materials and other Company property in its possession or control.  

  (d) At the Company’s request, Summit Tech shall cooperate in good faith and for a reasonable period (not to exceed ninety (90) days, unless otherwise agreed) to transition the Services to the Company or a successor vendor, at Summit Tech’s then-current T&M rates or as otherwise agreed.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties that, by their nature, should survive termination or expiration of this Agreement shall so survive, including, without limitation, provisions regarding payment obligations (for Services already rendered), confidentiality (Article V), intellectual property (Article VI), indemnification (Article VIII), limitation of liability (Article IX), data protection and privacy (Article XI), dispute resolution (Article XIV), and general provisions (Article XVI).

(a) The Receiving Party shall: (i) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement; (ii) restrict disclosure of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, agents, and contractors who have a need to know for such purpose and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality at least as protective as those set forth herein; and (iii) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information from unauthorized use, access, or disclosure using at least a reasonable degree of care and no less than the care it uses to protect its own similar information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the earlier of (a) the Disclosing Party’s written request, or (b) termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s option) all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for purposes of compliance with Applicable Law or its internal compliance policies, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence as of the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided that, with respect to trade secrets, such obligations shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.
