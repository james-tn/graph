## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide the Services to Client in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement, including the project phases, Deliverables, and timelines set forth herein. The Services are intended to support the design, build, and deployment of an integrated cloud-based business operations platform for Client, including order management, inventory, financial reporting, and analytics modules, as well as related systems integration, data migration, and training.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Project will be executed in the following phases, which may overlap as mutually agreed:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements Gathering.**  
  During Phase 1, Vendor shall:
  - Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and discovery sessions with Client’s designated personnel;
  - Document current business processes, systems architecture, data flows, and integration points;
  - Identify functional and technical requirements, constraints, and priorities;
  - Produce a **Requirements Specification Document** summarizing the outcomes, including high-level use cases, process maps, and an integration inventory.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Solution Design and Architecture.**  
  During Phase 2, Vendor shall:
  - Design the target-state architecture for the platform, including hosting topology, security model, and integration approach;
  - Produce a **Solution Design Document** providing detailed functional design, data model, interface specifications, and security/roles matrix;
  - Align the design with Client’s compliance requirements, including data protection obligations as referenced in Article XI;
  - Obtain Client’s written sign-off on the Solution Design Document (such sign-off not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed).

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Configuration, Development, and Integration.**  
  During Phase 3, Vendor shall:
  - Configure the platform in accordance with the approved Solution Design Document;
  - Develop custom components, extensions, and integration connectors with Client’s designated systems (e.g., ERP, CRM, and financial systems);
  - Implement security configurations, user roles, and access controls;
  - Create technical documentation for custom integrations and configurations;
  - Coordinate with Client’s IT team for any required on-premises connectivity or firewall configuration.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Data Migration and Testing.**  
  During Phase 4, Vendor shall:
  - Define data migration strategy and mapping from legacy systems to the new platform;
  - Execute data extraction, transformation, loading (ETL) processes for at least two test cycles and one final production cutover;
  - Plan and conduct unit testing, system integration testing (SIT), and user acceptance testing (UAT) in collaboration with Client;
  - Document test plans, test cases, and test results;
  - Address defects or issues identified during testing within timeframes consistent with the Service Levels described in Article XII.

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Training and Go-Live Support.**  
  During Phase 5, Vendor shall:
  - Develop training materials, including user guides, quick reference cards, and slide presentations;
  - Deliver up to [X] instructor-led training sessions (remote or onsite, as mutually agreed) for end users and system administrators;
  - Support Client’s go-live event, including hypercare support for a period of not less than thirty (30) days following go-live;
  - Transition ongoing support responsibilities to Client’s internal teams or to Vendor’s managed services team, as agreed.

  2.2.6 **Phase 6 – Ongoing Support and Maintenance (Optional).**  
  If elected by Client and documented in a Change Order or annex, Vendor shall provide ongoing support and maintenance services, including:
  - Tier 2 and Tier 3 technical support;
  - Application monitoring and patch management;
  - Minor enhancements and configuration changes; and
  - Continued adherence to the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables shall include, without limitation, the following:

  - Requirements Specification Document;
  - Solution Design Document;
  - Configured platform instance(s) and environment configuration documentation;
  - Custom integration components and related technical documentation;
  - Data migration scripts and mapping documentation;
  - Test plans, test cases, and summarized test results;
  - Training materials and session attendance records;
  - Go-live checklist and cutover runbook;
  - Support and maintenance runbook (if applicable).

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards and in accordance with the Specifications. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing:

  - Vendor shall assign personnel with qualifications, experience, and skill levels appropriate for the tasks assigned;
  - Vendor shall adhere to the timelines set forth in this Article II, subject to reasonable adjustments by mutual agreement or as required by Change Orders;
  - Vendor shall comply with all applicable Client policies and procedures communicated in writing to Vendor, including security and data handling requirements, to the extent they do not conflict with this Agreement.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Client’s timely performance of the following:

  - Providing access to relevant systems, personnel, facilities, and data as reasonably required;
  - Designating a primary project manager and key stakeholders who are authorized to make decisions and provide approvals;
  - Reviewing and providing feedback on Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of submission, unless otherwise stated herein;
  - Ensuring the accuracy and completeness of Client Data provided to Vendor;
  - Promptly notifying Vendor of any issues or concerns related to the Services or Deliverables.

To the extent Client fails to fulfill its responsibilities, Vendor shall be relieved from performance obligations affected by such failure, and timelines and Fees may be adjusted accordingly via Change Order.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**
