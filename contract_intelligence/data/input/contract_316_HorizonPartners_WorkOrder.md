## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order is executed as a **Statement of Work under the Parent Agreement**, within the meaning of **Section 2.2 (Statements of Work)** of the Parent Agreement. The Services to be performed and the Deliverables to be provided hereunder constitute “Services” and “Deliverables” as defined in **Sections 1.15 and 1.9** of the Parent Agreement.  

2.1.2 Unless expressly modified by this Work Order, all provisions of **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement (including, without limitation, **Sections 2.4 (Performance Standards)**, 2.5 (Personnel and Subcontractors), 2.6 (Company Responsibilities), 2.7 (On‑Site and Remote Work), 2.8 (Risk Level and Reliance), and 2.9 (Dependencies and Delays)) shall apply in full to the Services performed under this Work Order.

2.2 **Overview of the Project.**  

The Services under this Work Order collectively comprise the **“Enterprise Transformation Acceleration Program”** for Contoso’s enterprise software business. Building on the strategic, digital transformation, and data analytics service categories described in **Sections 2.1(a)–(g)** of the Parent Agreement, Horizon shall:  

(a) refine and update Contoso’s existing strategic roadmap;  
(b) define a target operating model and technical architecture for priority platforms and data domains;  
(c) mobilize execution through a program management office (“PMO”); and  
(d) perform structured knowledge transfer to Contoso personnel.

2.3 **Project Phases and Major Activities.**  

The Project will be organized into the following four (4) Project Phases. Timeframes are estimates and may be adjusted in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement and this Work Order.

2.3.1 **Phase 1 – Diagnostic & Strategy Refresh (Estimated Duration: 10–12 weeks).**  

Key activities include:

- Review of existing strategic materials, Deliverables, and Work Product produced under prior SOWs or work orders under the Parent Agreement (if any), including strategic assessment reports and technology roadmaps contemplated in **Section 2.3(a)** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Conduct executive interviews and stakeholder workshops with Contoso’s leadership across product, sales, marketing, operations, finance, and IT.  
- Assess current performance against key performance indicators (“KPIs”) developed under prior engagements or to be newly defined, consistent with **Section 2.1(c) (Data Analytics and Business Intelligence)** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Identify gaps in go‑to‑market strategy, product positioning, and organizational design, consistent with **Section 2.1(a) (Strategic Advisory Services)**.  
- Produce a **Strategic Diagnostic Report** summarizing findings, prioritizing initiatives, and recommending a refined 24–36‑month roadmap.

**Phase 1 Deliverables:**
- Strategic Diagnostic Report (PDF and editable format).  
- Executive presentation for the Board or C‑suite summarizing key insights and recommendations.  
- Prioritized initiative backlog with indicative business case assumptions.

2.3.2 **Phase 2 – Target Operating Model & Architecture (Estimated Duration: 12–16 weeks).**  

Key activities include:

- Design of a **Target Operating Model** for the enterprise software business, covering organizational structure, key roles, RACI matrices, and process flows, consistent with **Section 2.1(d) (Process Optimization and Operational Excellence)**.  
- Development of a **Target Technology Architecture** spanning core platform, customer‑facing applications, integration layer, and data platform, consistent with **Section 2.1(b) (Digital Transformation and Technology Consulting)**.  
- Detailed **Data Model and Analytics Framework** for key subject areas (e.g., customer, product, usage, support), including metrics and KPIs, consistent with **Section 2.1(c)** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Recommendations for third‑party technology selection and sequencing of implementation.

**Phase 2 Deliverables:**
- Target Operating Model documentation (process maps, role descriptions, responsibility matrices).  
- Enterprise technology architecture diagrams, including logical, application, and data views.  
- Data and analytics framework including KPI dictionary and reporting blueprint.  
- Implementation roadmap with high‑level milestones and dependencies.

2.3.3 **Phase 3 – Execution Mobilization & PMO Stand‑Up (Estimated Duration: 16–20 weeks).**  

Key activities include:

- Stand‑up of a **Program Management Office (PMO)** in accordance with **Section 2.1(e) (Program and Project Management)** of the Parent Agreement, including governance forums, status reporting cadence, and risk/issue management processes.  
- Development of detailed project plans, including timelines, resource requirements, risk registers, and communication plans.  
- Support for initial implementation sprints or workstreams (e.g., data platform build‑out, process redesign pilots).  
- Establishment of performance dashboards and reporting mechanisms, consistent with **Section 2.1(c)** (Data Analytics and Business Intelligence).

**Phase 3 Deliverables:**
- PMO charter, governance framework, and operating procedures.  
- Integrated master project plan with dependencies and critical path highlighted.  
- Risk and issue registers with mitigation plans.  
- Executive dashboard templates for tracking progress and value realization.

2.3.4 **Phase 4 – Knowledge Transfer & Transition (Estimated Duration: 8–10 weeks).**  

Key activities include:

- Formal training sessions and workshops consistent with **Section 2.1(f) (Training and Knowledge Transfer)** of the Parent Agreement.  
- Development of playbooks, runbooks, and standard operating procedures (SOPs) for key processes and tools.  
- Shadowing and reverse‑shadowing arrangements for Contoso personnel to progressively assume PMO and analytics responsibilities.  
- Creation of a final **Lessons Learned and Transition Report**.

**Phase 4 Deliverables:**
- Training materials (slide decks, handouts, and recordings where feasible).  
- Standard operating procedures and process documentation.  
- Lessons Learned and Transition Report summarizing outcomes, residual risks, and recommendations for future enhancements.

2.4.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services under this Work Order in accordance with **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, namely, in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards.

2.4.2 The Parties acknowledge that the Services are **advisory in nature**, and that Horizon does not guarantee any particular business outcome or financial result, consistent with **Section 2.4(b)** and **Section 7.4 (Disclaimer)** of the Parent Agreement.
