## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.** Vendor shall provide a comprehensive suite of services to design, implement, migrate, secure, and operate a hybrid cloud infrastructure and associated systems for Client. The Services include, without limitation:

- Strategic assessment and architecture design;  
- Cloud migration planning and execution;  
- Deployment and configuration of infrastructure and security controls;  
- Ongoing 24x7 monitoring, incident management, and support;  
- Capacity planning, optimization and cost management; and  
- Periodic reporting, governance, and continuous improvement activities.

2.2 **Phased Approach.** The Project shall be organized into the following phases. The Parties may refine the details of each phase in a mutually agreed written project plan; however, such refinements shall not diminish Vendor’s obligations under this SOW.

2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Assessment and Architecture (Months 1–4).**  
(a) Vendor shall perform a detailed discovery and assessment of Client’s existing on-premises and cloud environments, including network topology, application inventory, security posture, identity and access management, data classification, and compliance requirements.  
(b) Vendor shall prepare a comprehensive **Current-State Assessment Report** (“CSAR”) detailing identified issues, risks, dependencies, and recommended remediation steps.  
(c) Vendor shall design a **Target-State Hybrid Cloud Architecture** incorporating at least:  
   - Multi-region cloud deployment across leading cloud providers;  
   - High-availability and disaster recovery design;  
   - Network segmentation and zero-trust security principles;  
   - Identity and access management strategy;  
   - Logging, monitoring, SIEM integration and incident response framework.  
(d) Vendor shall conduct up to three (3) workshops with key Client stakeholders to review and refine the architecture and operating model.

2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Implementation and Migration (Months 5–18).**  
(a) Vendor shall implement the approved target architecture, including provisioning of cloud infrastructure (IaaS, PaaS components), configuration of virtual networks, security groups, firewalls, VPN/Direct Connect links, and related components.  
(b) Vendor shall design and implement an automated Infrastructure-as-Code (IaC) framework for environment provisioning and configuration management.  
(c) Vendor shall plan and execute migration of designated workloads from on-premises data centers to the hybrid cloud environment, including application servers, databases, storage, and associated middleware.  
(d) Vendor shall perform test migrations, cutover planning, rollback planning, and conduct user acceptance testing (UAT) in coordination with Client.  
(e) Vendor shall provide migration runbooks, detailed cutover plans, and post-migration validation reports for each major migration wave.  
(f) Vendor shall deliver training sessions (up to five (5) sessions) for Client’s technical staff on the operation and governance of the new environment.

2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Managed Services and Optimization (Months 19–End of Term).**  
(a) Vendor shall provide 24x7 monitoring, incident detection, and incident response services for the hybrid cloud environment, including infrastructure, operating systems, and agreed application components.  
(b) Vendor shall provide Level 1, Level 2, and coordination for Level 3 support, including incident management, problem management, change management (in coordination with Client), and service request fulfillment.  
(c) Vendor shall perform routine maintenance tasks, patch management for managed components, backup administration, capacity management, and ongoing security operations.  
(d) Vendor shall conduct monthly service reviews including performance metrics, incident trends, capacity usage, security incidents, and continuous improvement recommendations.  
(e) Vendor shall perform quarterly architecture and cost optimization reviews and provide written recommendations to improve performance, resiliency, security, and cost-efficiency.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Without limiting Vendor’s obligations under this SOW, the Vendor shall deliver at least the following Deliverables to Client:

2.3.1 **Current-State Assessment Report (CSAR)** – comprehensive documentation of existing environment, including diagrams, inventories, risk analysis, and recommendations.

2.3.2 **Target-State Architecture Design Document** – detailed system architecture, network diagrams, data flows, security controls, identity model, and infrastructure bill of materials.

2.3.3 **Migration Strategy and Detailed Migration Plan** – including workload prioritization, dependency mapping, timelines, resource requirements, and contingency plans.

2.3.4 **Infrastructure-as-Code Artifacts** – scripts, templates, and configuration files required to provision and manage the cloud infrastructure, subject to IP provisions in Article VI.

2.3.5 **Runbooks and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)** – for incident management, change management, backup and restore, patching, security incident response, and routine operational tasks.

2.3.6 **Test and UAT Reports** – documentation of test cases, test results, defects identified and resolved, and sign-off records.

2.3.7 **Training Materials** – slide decks, user guides, and recorded sessions (if applicable) used in Client training.

2.3.8 **Monthly Service Reports** – summarizing service levels, incidents, changes, capacity and performance, security events, and recommendations.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
(b) in compliance with the Performance Standards and Service Levels described in Article XII;  
(c) using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications; and  
(d) in compliance with all Applicable Laws.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.** Vendor’s performance is contingent upon Client fulfilling the following responsibilities in a timely manner:

(a) providing Vendor with access to systems, facilities, documentation, and personnel as reasonably required;  
(b) appointing a primary point of contact with authority to make decisions and approvals;  
(c) ensuring that any data or materials provided to Vendor are accurate, complete, and do not infringe the rights of third parties; and  
(d) performing any tasks expressly identified as Client responsibilities in the project plan or as reasonably requested by Vendor to perform the Services.

2.6 **Assumptions and Dependencies.** The Parties acknowledge that the Project is subject to certain assumptions and dependencies, including but not limited to:

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Scope.** This Article XII sets forth the Service Levels applicable to the managed Services provided by Vendor under Phase 3 of the Project.

12.2 **Service Availability.**  

(a) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the managed infrastructure and services for which Vendor is responsible are available **99.9% of the time** in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Downtime and Excluded Causes.  

(b) “**Scheduled Downtime**” means planned maintenance notified to Client at least seventy-two (72) hours in advance and occurring during agreed maintenance windows.  

(c) “**Excluded Causes**” include downtime or service unavailability caused by:  
   - factors outside Vendor’s reasonable control (including Force Majeure Events);  
   - failures or issues caused by Client systems or third-party services not under Vendor’s control;  
   - Client’s misuse or unauthorized modifications; or  
   - any suspension of Services in accordance with this SOW.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall respond to and work to resolve incidents in accordance with the following target timeframes:

- **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Severe outage or major business impact:  
  - Response Time: within 30 minutes, 24x7  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant degradation or high business impact:  
  - Response Time: within 1 hour, 24x7  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 8 hours  

- **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Moderate impact issues:  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 2 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues or service requests:  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 5 Business Days

12.4 **Service Credits.**  

(a) If Vendor fails to meet the 99.9% availability Service Level in a given calendar month, Client shall be eligible for a Service Credit equal to **5% of the monthly managed services Fees** for that month for each 0.5% (or part thereof) of unavailability below 99.9%, up to a maximum of 25% of the monthly Fees.  

(b) Service Credits shall be issued as a credit against the next invoice(s) due from Client. Service Credits shall be Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the availability Service Levels, without prejudice to any other remedies available for material breach.

12.5 **SLA Exclusions.** Service Levels do not apply to:

(a) any services provided on a time-and-materials basis that are not part of the managed services scope;  
(b) issues caused by Client’s failure to comply with Vendor’s instructions or to implement recommended configurations; or  
(c) any period during which Vendor’s performance is excused under Article XV (Force Majeure).

## ARTICLE XIV – DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14.1 **Negotiation and Escalation.** In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this SOW (a “**Dispute**”), the Parties shall first attempt to resolve the Dispute through good-faith negotiations between their respective project managers. If the Dispute is not resolved within fifteen (15) Business Days, either Party may escalate the Dispute to senior executives of each Party, who shall meet (in person or by teleconference) within ten (10) Business Days and attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute.

14.2 **Mediation.** If the Dispute remains unresolved thirty (30) days after escalation to senior executives, either Party may request that the Parties participate in non-binding mediation conducted under the auspices of a mutually agreed mediation provider in Singapore. The Parties shall share the mediator’s fees and expenses equally.

14.3 **Arbitration.** Any Dispute not resolved through negotiation or mediation within sixty (60) days after the commencement of mediation shall be finally settled by arbitration administered by the **Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC)** in accordance with the SIAC Rules then in force, which rules are deemed to be incorporated by reference into this clause. The seat of arbitration shall be **Singapore**. The arbitration shall be conducted by **three (3) arbitrators**. The language of the arbitration shall be **English**. The arbitrators’ decision shall be final and binding, and judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

14.4 **Injunctive Relief.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek interim or provisional injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to protect its Intellectual Property Rights or Confidential Information or to prevent irreparable harm pending the outcome of the arbitration.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.** A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including, but not limited to, acts of God, natural disasters, epidemic or pandemic, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving the non-performing Party’s workforce only), governmental actions, embargoes, shortages of materials or utilities, or failures of telecommunications or internet service providers that are not within a Party’s reasonable control.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.** The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, providing reasonable details. The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** During the period of the Force Majeure Event, the obligations of the affected Party under this SOW (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended to the extent such obligations are directly prevented or hindered by the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Prolonged Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than **ninety (90) consecutive days**, either Party may, upon written notice, terminate this SOW without further liability, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
