## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Engagement.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Company hereby engages Vendor, and Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described herein and in any SOW entered into by the Parties during the Term. Vendor shall provide qualified Assigned Personnel to perform professional services that may include, without limitation: software engineering; systems administration; project management; business analysis; quality assurance; technical support; data analytics; human resources advisory; and other professional and technical roles requested by Company and agreed in writing.

2.2 **Staffing and Assignment of Personnel.**  
(a) Vendor shall recruit, screen, and present to Company candidates for Assigned Personnel roles that meet the qualifications, experience, and skill sets specified by Company. Company shall have the right to interview and either accept or reject candidates in its discretion, provided that such decision is not in violation of applicable law.  
(b) Assigned Personnel shall, at all times, remain employees, contractors, or agents of Vendor (or its permitted subcontractors) and shall not be considered employees or agents of Company for any purpose. Vendor shall be solely responsible for all employment-related obligations with respect to Assigned Personnel, including payment of salaries, wages, benefits, payroll taxes, workers’ compensation, and compliance with all employment laws, except as expressly stated in this Agreement.  
(c) Vendor shall replace any Assigned Personnel who, in Company’s reasonable opinion, fails to perform the Services in a satisfactory manner, violates Company policies, or otherwise proves unsuitable for the assignment. Company may request removal of Assigned Personnel upon written notice to Vendor, and Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to remove and replace such personnel within ten (10) Business Days, or such other period as the Parties may agree in writing.

2.3 **Description of Services.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor shall perform the following categories of Services, as applicable to a particular SOW:

(a) **Technical Services.** Assigned Personnel may be engaged to:  
  - Design, develop, test, and maintain software applications, systems, and tools using programming languages and platforms designated by Company;  
  - Provide DevOps, infrastructure, and cloud operations services, including configuration, monitoring, and incident response for Company’s environments;  
  - Perform systems integration, data migration, and deployment activities in accordance with Company’s project plans and security standards; and  
  - Prepare technical documentation, including architecture diagrams, configuration guides, test plans, and user manuals.

(b) **Business and Project Services.** Assigned Personnel may perform:  
  - Project management, including project planning, coordination of tasks, status reporting, risk management, and stakeholder communication;  
  - Business analysis, requirements gathering, process mapping, and documentation of functional and non-functional requirements;  
  - Quality assurance and testing, including test case design, test execution, defect tracking, and regression testing; and  
  - Operational support functions, such as customer support, internal help desk support, training, and onboarding assistance for Company’s personnel.

(c) **Advisory and Strategic Services.** Where agreed in an SOW, Vendor may provide senior-level consultants to support:  
  - Technology strategy, roadmap development, and architecture reviews;  
  - Organizational change management and process improvement initiatives;  
  - Compliance, security, and governance assessments related to IT or HR operations; and  
  - Other advisory services mutually agreed by the Parties.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide Deliverables as specified in each SOW, which may include:  
  - Written reports, analyses, and recommendations;  
  - Software code, scripts, configurations, and related documentation;  
  - Training materials, presentations, and user guides;  
  - Project plans, schedules, and status reports; and  
  - Any other tangible or intangible outputs expressly identified in a SOW.  

(b) Each SOW shall specify the applicable acceptance criteria, format, and method of delivery for each Deliverable. Company shall have a reasonable period, not less than ten (10) Business Days unless otherwise stated in the SOW, to review Deliverables and either accept them in writing or provide Vendor with a written notice identifying any material non-conformity with the acceptance criteria. Vendor shall, at no additional cost to Company (except as expressly provided in an approved Change Order), re-perform or correct any non-conforming Deliverables within a reasonable time period.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform the Services and provide the Deliverables (i) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards; (ii) in accordance with the terms of this Agreement, the applicable SOW, and the Service Levels set forth in Article XII; and (iii) in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including those related to employment, anti-discrimination, health and safety, and data protection.  
(b) Assigned Personnel shall, while on Company premises (or in remote work for Company), comply with all Company workplace policies, security policies, code of conduct, and safety rules that are communicated to Vendor or Assigned Personnel in writing, including any information security and confidentiality policies.  
(c) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure continuity of Assigned Personnel and to avoid unnecessary turnover. Vendor shall provide adequate supervision and oversight of its Assigned Personnel as may be reasonably required to ensure quality and timeliness of the Services.

2.6 **Project Planning and Reporting.**  
(a) For major projects or long-term assignments, Vendor shall cooperate with Company’s project managers to develop and maintain project plans, including milestones, dependencies, and resource allocations.  
(b) Vendor shall provide regular status reports at intervals agreed in the applicable SOW, which may include progress updates, risks and issues, staffing changes, and any deviations from the project plan.  
(c) Upon Company’s reasonable request, Vendor shall participate in status meetings, whether in person or via teleconference or video conference, to review progress, address issues, and plan upcoming work.

2.7 **Service Locations and Remote Work.**  
(a) Services may be performed at Company’s offices, at Vendor’s facilities, at consultant home offices, or at other mutually agreed locations.  
(b) Where Services are performed remotely, Vendor shall ensure that Assigned Personnel have appropriate equipment, secure network connectivity, and working conditions that comply with applicable laws and Company security requirements.  
(c) Vendor shall ensure that any remote access to Company systems or data is performed in accordance with Company’s security protocols, including multi-factor authentication, VPN use, and device management requirements.

2.8 **No Guarantee of Minimum Volume.**  
Unless expressly stated in an SOW, Company does not guarantee any minimum number of Assigned Personnel, minimum hours, or minimum fees under this Agreement. Staffing levels, scope, and duration of assignments shall be determined by mutual agreement as documented in SOWs or written work orders.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees and Rates.**  
(a) Company shall pay Vendor for the Services and Deliverables on a time-and-materials and/or milestone basis, as specified in the applicable SOW.  
(b) The aggregate fees payable under this Agreement shall not exceed the **Contract Value of USD $3,758,672** without a written Change Order or amendment.  
(c) Each SOW shall set forth the applicable hourly or daily rates for Assigned Personnel categories (e.g., Senior Engineer, Project Manager, Business Analyst), any fixed fees for specific Deliverables or milestones, and any volume discounts or rate adjustments.

3.2 **Invoices.**  
(a) Vendor shall invoice Company **monthly in arrears** for Services rendered and Deliverables accepted during the preceding calendar month, unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW.  
(b) Each invoice shall include reasonable supporting detail, including:  
  - Names and roles of Assigned Personnel;  
  - Hours or days worked per Assigned Personnel (if time-based billing applies);  
  - Milestones achieved or Deliverables accepted (if milestone-based billing applies);  
  - Approved expenses and supporting documentation;  
  - Reference to the relevant SOW(s); and  
  - The Contract Reference Number **AGR-ACM-202412-608**.  

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) Company shall pay undisputed amounts on each properly submitted invoice within **sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt by Company (“**Net 60**”), subject to Section 3.4.  
(b) Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method. Vendor shall provide valid banking and remittance instructions.  

3.4 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If Company, in good faith, disputes any portion of an invoice, Company shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Company may withhold payment of the disputed portion while still paying the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall work in good faith to promptly resolve the dispute. Upon resolution, any amounts determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days of such resolution.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid by Company when due shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate allowed by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment. Vendor’s right to interest shall be the sole remedy for late payment, except that Vendor may exercise its suspension rights under Section 4.4, subject to prior written notice and a reasonable cure period.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
(a) Vendor shall be responsible for all ordinary business expenses incurred by Assigned Personnel, including commuting costs and regular office expenses.  
(b) Company shall reimburse Vendor for **pre-approved, reasonable, and necessary out-of-pocket expenses** incurred by Assigned Personnel in connection with the Services (such as travel, lodging, meals, and materials), provided that:  
  - such expenses are authorized in advance in writing by Company;  
  - Vendor submits appropriate receipts or documentation; and  
  - such expenses comply with Company’s then-current travel and expense policy, as communicated to Vendor.  

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) Fees under this Agreement are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes that may be imposed by applicable law (other than taxes based on Vendor’s net income or payroll). Company shall be responsible for any such taxes, if applicable, and Vendor shall itemize such taxes on the invoice.  
(b) Vendor shall be solely responsible for all payroll taxes, social security contributions, unemployment insurance, and other employment-related taxes and obligations with respect to Assigned Personnel.

3.8 **Set-Off.**  
Company may not set off any amounts owed by Vendor against payments due to Vendor under this Agreement, except where (i) such set-off is agreed in writing by Vendor; or (ii) Company has obtained a final, non-appealable judgment against Vendor for such amounts.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement (the “**Term**”) shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **June 15, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV. Any extension or renewal of the Term shall be subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW **for convenience**, without cause, upon providing the other Party at least **forty-five (45) days’ prior written notice**. The Parties acknowledge this period is intended as a balanced notice period given the medium risk profile of the Engagement.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach.  
(b) Company may terminate immediately upon written notice if:  
  - Vendor becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy (which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days);  
  - Vendor or any Assigned Personnel engages in fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence in connection with the Services; or  
  - Vendor materially violates Company’s information security requirements or causes a material data breach impacting Company’s Personal Data.  

4.4 **Suspension of Services.**  
In addition to its termination rights, Vendor may suspend the Services, in whole or in part, upon fifteen (15) days’ prior written notice to Company if undisputed invoices remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after their due date, provided that Vendor has engaged in good faith discussions with Company to resolve the payment issues. Vendor shall promptly resume Services once the overdue undisputed amounts are paid.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement or any SOW:  
(a) Vendor shall immediately cease performing the applicable Services, except to the extent reasonably requested by Company to assist in an orderly transition, which may be further documented in a separate transition plan.  
(b) Company shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, plus any approved expenses incurred prior to such date, subject to the not-to-exceed Contract Value and any applicable SOW limits.  
(c) Each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5, subject to any retention obligations under law.  
(d) The rights and obligations of the Parties under those provisions of this Agreement that, by their nature, should survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, Sections 3.5, 4.5, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 14, 15 and 16) shall so survive.

4.6 **No Penalty for Termination.**  
Except as expressly provided herein or in an applicable SOW (e.g., early termination fees negotiated for specific projects), neither Party shall be liable to the other for any termination of this Agreement or any SOW in accordance with its terms, and termination shall be without prejudice to any rights or remedies that may have accrued prior to such termination.

CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”) in connection with this Agreement, whether oral, written, electronic, visual, or in any other form, including but not limited to: business plans, strategies, pricing, financial information, customer lists, technical data, software, documentation, designs, know-how, trade secrets, Personal Data, security processes, and any information that is identified as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
(b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted under this Agreement;  
(c) restrict access to Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisers who need to know such information for the permitted purpose, and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  
(d) use at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction at the time of disclosure;  
(b) becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
(c) is received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party and cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If disclosure is required, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of Confidential Information that is legally required.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain:  
(a) copies of Confidential Information required to be retained under applicable law; and  
(b) one archival copy solely for purposes of compliance, dispute resolution, or audit, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall remain confidential for so long as such information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law or remains Personal Data.
