## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW.**  

  a. This Work Order **adds scope to the Statement of Work dated March 15, 2024 (Ref: SOW-STE-202403-422, identifier contract_422)** by defining Additional Services and Additional Deliverables that extend and enhance the Solution described in **Section 2.1 (Overview)** and **Section 2.2 (Project Phases)** of the Parent SOW.  

  b. For clarity, the general nature of Services described in **Section 2.3 (Detailed Description of Services)** of the Parent SOW (including project management, business analysis, architecture, configuration, integration, data migration, reporting, workflow automation, testing, training, and support) shall apply to this Work Order, but shall be expanded and customized as set forth in this **Article II**.

2.2 **Overall Objectives.** Vendor shall provide end-to-end Additional Services to:

  a. Extend the Solution to additional business units and geographic regions (including but not limited to EMEA and APAC entities of Client);  
  b. Increase data volume and user concurrency beyond the assumptions in Section 2.7(c) of the Parent SOW by up to an additional 200% of the initial baseline;  
  c. Implement advanced analytics capabilities, including predictive models, machine learning-driven anomaly detection, and advanced self-service analytics;  
  d. Introduce further workflow automations for cross-functional processes spanning finance, operations, customer service, and supply chain; and  
  e. Provide enhanced, higher-touch managed support and optimization services overlaying the Service Levels described in **Article XII of the Parent SOW** and supplemented by **Article XII of this Work Order**.

2.3 **Additional Services.** Without limiting the generality of the Services defined in Section 1.14 of the Parent SOW, the Additional Services under this Work Order include:

  a. **Global Rollout and Localization.**  
     i. Assess regional requirements, compliance constraints, localization needs (including currency, language, time zone, and regulatory reporting) for EMEA and APAC affiliates.  
     ii. Extend the data models and configuration described in Sections 2.2(b) and 2.3(c)-(d) of the Parent SOW to support local chart-of-accounts structures, tax and regulatory fields, and regional KPIs.  
     iii. Configure and validate region-specific integration with local ERP and CRM instances, where such systems are technically compatible with the Solution.

  b. **Advanced Analytics and Data Science Enablement.**  
     i. Design and implement additional data models and pipelines to support predictive forecasting, churn analysis, and operational optimization.  
     ii. Integrate third-party or native machine learning platforms (as agreed in writing by the Parties) with the configured cloud analytics platform referenced in Section 2.3(d) of the Parent SOW.  
     iii. Develop, test, and deploy a minimum of six (6) advanced analytics models and associated dashboards, to be documented under the Additional Deliverables as “Advanced Analytics Model Documentation.”

  c. **Expanded Workflow Automation.**  
     i. Identify at least ten (10) additional cross-functional processes suitable for automation, beyond those contemplated in Section 2.3(g) of the Parent SOW.  
     ii. Configure, test, and deploy automated workflows, approval chains, and exception-handling logic; document these in updated workflow configuration documentation.  
     iii. Coordinate with Client’s security teams to ensure role-based access is consistent with the security framework described in Section 2.3(c) of the Parent SOW.

  d. **Performance and Scalability Enhancements.**  
     i. Analyze current performance and capacity against the expanded usage scenarios, including data volume and user concurrency.  
     ii. Implement architectural optimizations, including partitioning, indexing, caching, and resource scaling, to maintain or exceed the performance standards contemplated in Section 2.5 (Performance Standards) of the Parent SOW for the expanded scope.  
     iii. Conduct performance and load testing at revised benchmarks specified by Client and documented as part of the Supplemental Architecture.

  e. **Enhanced Training and Change Management.**  
     i. Develop additional training materials tailored for regional users, including localization where appropriate, supplementing the training materials described in Section 2.4(h) of the Parent SOW.  
     ii. Deliver additional training sessions (remote or onsite) targeting new user groups to support global adoption.  
     iii. Provide advisory support to Client’s internal change management function regarding communication plans, adoption metrics, and stakeholder engagement.

  f. **Expanded Managed Support and Optimization.**  
     i. Provide extended coverage hours, proactive monitoring, and periodic optimization workshops, supplementing Vendor’s obligations in Phase 6 of the Parent SOW and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** therein.  
     ii. Support additional regional environments and integrations, including localized incident triage and resolution.  
     iii. Implement a quarterly optimization review to identify and execute agreed minor enhancements and performance improvements.

2.4 **Additional Deliverables.** Vendor shall produce and deliver, in addition to the Deliverables specified in Section 2.4 of the Parent SOW, at minimum:

  a. **Global Requirements Addendum and Localization Matrix** detailing regional and functional localization needs, specific compliance requirements, language/currency configurations, and mapping to the existing architecture.  
  b. **Supplemental Architecture and Design Documentation** including updated data models, cross-region data flows, integration specifications for regional systems, and revised security and access control models.  
  c. **Advanced Analytics Model Documentation** including model objectives, input features, training methodologies, performance metrics, limitations, and operational runbooks for each deployed model.  
  d. **Expanded Workflow Design and Configuration Documents** detailing each new process automation, including process flow diagrams, roles, decision logic, exception paths, and integration points.  
  e. **Performance and Load Test Plans and Summary Reports** specific to the Program Expansion, with clear benchmarks, test data profiles, results, issues, and remediation actions.  
  f. **Regional Training Materials** (slide decks, quick reference guides, localized user guides) for each major region and user persona.  
  g. **Quarterly Optimization Review Reports** summarizing incident trends, performance metrics, enhancement backlog, and actions taken during each quarter of the Expanded Support Period.  
  h. **Updated Operational Runbooks** reflecting global deployment, additional integrations, revised monitoring procedures, and updated escalation protocols.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

  a. The Additional Services under this Work Order shall be organized into the following phases, which overlay the Project phases in Section 2.2 of the Parent SOW:

   - **Phase A – Global Discovery and Localization Design** (Estimated: May 15, 2024 – September 30, 2024)  
   - **Phase B – Build and Integration of Global and Advanced Analytics** (Estimated: October 1, 2024 – June 30, 2025)  
   - **Phase C – Global Testing, UAT, and Performance Validation** (Estimated: July 1, 2025 – November 30, 2025)  
   - **Phase D – Global Go-Live, Hypercare, and Stabilization** (Estimated: December 1, 2025 – March 31, 2026)  
   - **Phase E – Expanded Managed Support and Optimization** (Estimated: April 1, 2026 – September 18, 2027)

  b. Each phase shall be tied to Milestones and Deliverables, with target dates and Acceptance procedures specified in a Milestone Schedule annexed hereto (which shall be deemed incorporated by reference). Acceptance of each Milestone shall be structured consistently with the approval and acceptance mechanisms in Section 2.6(c) and Section 3.3 of the Parent SOW.

2.6 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Additional Services:

  a. in accordance with the performance standards set forth in Section 2.5 of the Parent SOW and this Work Order;  
  b. in a manner that, after full deployment, maintains Solution performance at or above the levels demonstrated at the conclusion of Phase 3 and Phase 4 of the Parent SOW, notwithstanding the Program Expansion;  
  c. using personnel with expertise in global deployments and advanced analytics, consistent with the warranties regarding personnel in Section 7.2(d) of the Parent SOW; and  
  d. with the objective of meeting or exceeding the Enhanced Service Levels defined in **Article XII** of this Work Order.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities (Supplemental).** In addition to Client’s responsibilities set forth in Section 2.6 of the Parent SOW, Client shall:

  a. Provide timely access to regional stakeholders, systems, and Client Materials necessary for the global deployment and advanced analytics enablement.  
  b. Ensure that local regulatory, privacy, and data residency constraints are clearly communicated to Vendor in writing.  
  c. Allocate internal change management and training resources to support adoption and to coordinate with Vendor’s training activities.  
  d. Assume responsibility for any third-party license costs and fees associated with optional advanced analytics tools or platforms that are not expressly included within Vendor’s scope, consistent with Section 2.6(d) of the Parent SOW.

2.8 **Assumptions.** The Contract Value of this Work Order is based on the assumptions in Section 2.7 of the Parent SOW as well as the following:

  a. Client will provide timely approvals of Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days, consistent with Section 2.6(c) of the Parent SOW.  
  b. The number of additional regions does not exceed six (6), and additional data volume does not exceed a 200% increase over the baseline.  
  c. No major re-platforming of Client’s core systems occurs during the WO Term.  
If any assumption proves inaccurate or changes, the Parties shall follow the change management process in **Article XIII of the Parent SOW** and **Article XIII of this Work Order**.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value for this Work Order.** The total **Contract Value (WO)** for the Additional Services and Additional Deliverables described in this Work Order is **US$2,865,747** (Two Million Eight Hundred Sixty-Five Thousand Seven Hundred Forty-Seven U.S. Dollars), exclusive of Taxes as defined in Section 3.7 of the Parent SOW.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

  a. **Fixed-Price Components for Expansion (US$1,985,747).**  
  The following phases shall be delivered on a fixed-fee basis:

   i. Phase A – Global Discovery and Localization Design: **US$395,000**  
   ii. Phase B – Build and Integration of Global and Advanced Analytics: **US$895,000**  
   iii. Phase C – Global Testing, UAT, and Performance Validation: **US$395,747**  
   iv. Phase D – Global Go-Live, Hypercare, and Stabilization: **US$300,000**

  b. **Expanded Managed Support Fees (US$880,000).**  
  The Expanded Support Period under Phase E shall be provided on a recurring fee basis of **US$40,000 per calendar quarter** for twenty-two (22) quarters-equivalent months within the WO Term (prorated as necessary to align to the Expiration Date of **September 18, 2027**). The total for Phase E is **US$880,000**, invoiced as set forth in Section 3.4 below.

3.3 **Payment Milestones – Fixed-Price Components.** The fixed-fee portion (US$1,985,747) shall be invoiced upon achievement and Acceptance of the following Milestones, consistent with the acceptance and invoicing structure in Section 3.3 of the Parent SOW:

  a. **Phase A – Global Discovery and Localization Design (US$395,000):**  
     - Fifty percent (50%) (US$197,500) upon Client’s Acceptance of the Global Requirements Addendum and Localization Matrix.  
     - Fifty percent (50%) (US$197,500) upon Client’s Acceptance of the Supplemental Architecture and Design Documentation.

  b. **Phase B – Build and Integration (US$895,000):**  
     - Forty percent (40%) (US$358,000) upon completion of initial global configuration and integration development and delivery of a Global Build Completion Report.  
     - Thirty percent (30%) (US$268,500) upon successful deployment of all planned regional integrations into the test environment.  
     - Thirty percent (30%) (US$268,500) upon Client’s confirmation that all planned advanced analytics models have been deployed to the staging environment in accordance with the agreed Specifications.

  c. **Phase C – Global Testing, UAT, and Performance Validation (US$395,747):**  
     - Fifty percent (50%) (US$197,873.50) upon successful completion of global integration and performance testing, evidenced by a Test Summary Report with all Severity 1 and Severity 2 defects (as defined in Section 2.3(h) of the Parent SOW) closed or mitigated with Client-approved workarounds.  
     - Fifty percent (50%) (US$197,873.50) upon Client’s UAT Acceptance for at least four (4) regional deployments.

  d. **Phase D – Global Go-Live, Hypercare, and Stabilization (US$300,000):**  
     - Forty percent (40%) (US$120,000) upon completion of initial global go-live and commencement of hypercare.  
     - Sixty percent (60%) (US$180,000) upon completion of a ninety (90) day global stabilization period and delivery of a Global Stabilization Summary Report acceptable to Client.

3.4 **Payment of Expanded Managed Support Fees.** The Expanded Support Fees (US$880,000) shall be invoiced **quarterly in advance**, beginning April 1, 2026, at **US$40,000 per quarter** (or prorated portion thereof for partial quarters) through the Expiration Date. These fees are in addition to the managed support fees specified in Section 3.4 of the Parent SOW and shall apply to the enhanced and expanded scope described in this Work Order.

3.5 **Payment Terms.** All undisputed amounts invoiced by Vendor under this Work Order shall be due and payable by Client within **forty-five (45) days (Net 45)** of Client’s receipt of the applicable invoice, in U.S. dollars, and paid by wire transfer or another method agreed by the Parties. The payment terms in Section 3.5 of the Parent SOW are hereby **reaffirmed and incorporated**.

3.6 **Late Payments.** Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest per Section 3.6 of the Parent SOW (one percent (1%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law). Vendor may, consistent with Section 3.6 of the Parent SOW, suspend Additional Services under this Work Order if payment is more than thirty (30) days overdue and Client fails to cure within ten (10) Business Days after written notice.

3.7 **Taxes.** Fees under this Work Order are exclusive of all Taxes, which shall be handled in accordance with Section 3.7 of the Parent SOW.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.** Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred in connection with onsite Services under this Work Order in accordance with Section 3.8 of the Parent SOW.

3.9 **Disputed Amounts.** Client may withhold payment of any disputed amounts consistent with Section 3.9 of the Parent SOW, provided that Client pays the undisputed portion on time and provides written notice of the basis for the dispute.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **WO Term.** This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated as provided herein, shall remain in full force and effect until **September 18, 2027** (the **“WO Expiration Date”** or **“Expiration Date”**).

4.2 **Relationship to Parent SOW Term.** The Term of the Parent SOW (Section 4.1) currently extends through April 30, 2027. The Parties hereby agree that, solely for purposes of supporting the Expanded Managed Support and other continuing obligations in this Work Order, the Parent SOW shall be deemed **extended through September 18, 2027** with respect to the Services and Deliverables under this Work Order, unless the Parties enter into a separate written extension or replacement agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  a. Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. This longer notice period supersedes Section 4.3 of the Parent SOW with respect to this Work Order only.  

  b. Upon termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor for: (i) all Additional Services performed and Additional Deliverables provided and Accepted up to the effective date of termination; (ii) any proportionate portion of fixed fees for partially completed Milestones based on the percentage of completion agreed in good faith; and (iii) any non-cancellable commitments incurred by Vendor with Client’s prior written consent.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  a. materially breaches this Work Order or the Parent SOW with respect to the Additional Services and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach; or  
  b. becomes subject to an insolvency event as described in Section 4.4(b) of the Parent SOW.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent SOW.**  

  a. If the Parent SOW is terminated for any reason, this Work Order shall automatically **terminate concurrently**, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue some or all of the Additional Services under a separate agreement.  

  b. Upon such termination, the effect-of-termination provisions in Section 4.6 of the Parent SOW shall apply to this Work Order mutatis mutandis, including cessation of Services, payment of Fees earned, return or destruction of Confidential Information, and continuation of any perpetual licenses.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of this Work Order.** Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:

  a. Vendor shall cease performing the Additional Services, except as necessary to provide any requested transition assistance under Section 4.7;  
  b. Client shall pay Vendor all Fees due and payable for Services, Deliverables, and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to any good faith disputes;  
  c. The obligations concerning Confidential Information, Data Protection, Intellectual Property, and other surviving provisions identified in Section 4.8 shall remain in effect.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.** Upon Client’s request, Vendor shall provide transition assistance in accordance with Section 4.7 of the Parent SOW for up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration, at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates (or the rates specified herein).

4.8 **Survival.** The following provisions of this Work Order shall survive expiration or termination: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any accrued payment obligations.
