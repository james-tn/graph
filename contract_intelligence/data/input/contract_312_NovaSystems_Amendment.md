## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.** Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of technology and professional services, including implementation, configuration, hosting, ongoing support, and optimization of Vendor’s proprietary cloud-based enterprise management platform (the **“Platform”**), along with related consulting and training services. The Services shall be provided in accordance with this **Article II**, the Service Levels in **Article XII**, and the Original Agreement, as amended hereby.

2.2 **Implementation and Configuration Services.**

2.2.1 Vendor shall perform an initial discovery and requirements-gathering phase, including stakeholder interviews, workshops, and documentation review, to define Client’s business, technical, and security requirements for the Platform.

2.2.2 Based on the discovery phase, Vendor shall prepare a written **Implementation Plan** setting forth, at a minimum: (a) project milestones and timelines; (b) responsible Parties and resources; (c) environments (e.g., development, test, production); (d) data migration approach; and (e) acceptance criteria. The Implementation Plan shall be subject to Client’s review and approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed.

2.2.3 Vendor shall configure the Platform to support Client’s business processes, including but not limited to: user roles and permissions, workflows, approval hierarchies, notifications, integration endpoints, data fields, and custom reports, as further detailed in any applicable SOWs.

2.2.4 Vendor shall assist Client with data mapping and migration from Client’s legacy systems to the Platform. Vendor shall implement commercially reasonable data validation and reconciliation procedures to minimize data quality issues. Client shall remain responsible for providing accurate and complete source data and for reviewing and approving test migration results.

2.3 **Hosting and Managed Services.**

2.3.1 Vendor shall host the production instance of the Platform in a secure, industry-standard cloud infrastructure environment. Vendor shall be responsible for provisioning and managing the underlying infrastructure (e.g., servers, storage, databases) necessary to support the agreed Service Levels.

2.3.2 Vendor shall perform routine maintenance activities, including system monitoring, capacity management, performance tuning, application of Updates and security patches, backups, and disaster recovery preparations in a manner that minimizes disruption to Client’s operations.

2.3.3 Vendor shall maintain and implement a disaster recovery plan designed to achieve, at a minimum, the recovery time objective (RTO) and recovery point objective (RPO) levels agreed in writing by the Parties. Vendor shall perform periodic testing of its disaster recovery procedures and provide summaries of such tests upon Client’s reasonable request.

2.4 **Support Services.**

2.4.1 Vendor shall provide technical support services to Client during the Term, including incident management, troubleshooting, and resolution of issues relating to the use and operation of the Platform.

2.4.2 Vendor’s standard support hours shall be 8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Central Time on Business Days, with 24x7 availability for Severity 1 (critical) incidents, as more fully detailed in **Article XII**.

2.4.3 Vendor shall provide Client with a designated support portal, email address, and telephone number for logging and tracking support requests. Vendor shall assign unique ticket numbers to each issue and maintain records of support actions taken.

2.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**

2.5.1 Vendor shall provide initial training to Client’s key users, administrators, and support personnel on the use and configuration of the Platform. Such training may include remote sessions, on-site workshops, or self-paced online modules, as mutually agreed.

2.5.2 Vendor shall provide updated training materials and release notes in connection with significant Updates or new features of the Platform that materially affect Client’s use of the Services.

2.5.3 Vendor shall reasonably cooperate with Client’s internal training team to enable Client to develop and deliver internal training programs for its employees and contractors.

2.6 **Deliverables and Milestones.**

2.6.1 Vendor shall deliver to Client the following primary Deliverables (without limitation):

  a. A finalized and Client-approved Implementation Plan;  
  b. Configured Platform instances (development, test, and production) aligned with Client’s requirements;  
  c. Data migration scripts, mapping documents, and migration reports;  
  d. System and configuration documentation;  
  e. Training materials and user guides tailored to Client’s deployment; and  
  f. Periodic performance and usage reports as agreed in writing.

2.6.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to complete the initial implementation and go-live of the production Platform within [●] weeks of the Amendment Effective Date, subject to Client’s timely provision of necessary information, resources, and approvals.

2.6.3 Each major project milestone shall be documented and subject to Client’s acceptance pursuant to mutually agreed acceptance criteria. If Client reasonably rejects a Deliverable, Vendor shall promptly re-perform or correct the Deliverable at no additional charge and resubmit it for Client’s review.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**

2.7.1 Vendor shall perform all Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices, and with at least the same degree of care and skill as exercised by reputable providers of similar services.

2.7.2 Vendor shall ensure that the Platform and Deliverables materially conform to the specifications set forth in the Documentation, the Implementation Plan, and any applicable SOWs.

2.7.3 Vendor shall comply with all Applicable Laws in the performance of the Services, including without limitation Data Protection Laws, export control laws, and employment laws.

2.7.4 Vendor shall take commercially reasonable measures to prevent unauthorized access to or use of the Platform and Customer Data, including the implementation of administrative, technical, and physical safeguards.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.**

2.8.1 Client shall designate a project manager and key stakeholders who shall be responsible for coordinating with Vendor, providing necessary information, and making timely decisions to facilitate performance of the Services.

2.8.2 Client shall provide Vendor with reasonable access to its systems, facilities, and personnel, as necessary for Vendor to perform the Services, subject to Client’s security and access control policies.

2.8.3 Client shall be responsible for the accuracy and completeness of all information and data provided to Vendor and for obtaining any necessary consents from end users or data subjects, where required by applicable law.

2.8.4 Client shall use the Platform and Deliverables in accordance with the Documentation and any reasonable usage guidelines provided by Vendor, and shall not use the Services in a manner that violates Applicable Law or infringes the rights of third parties.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Amendment is **Four Million Three Hundred Sixteen Thousand Nine Hundred Twenty-One U.S. Dollars (US$4,316,921)** (the **“Contract Value”**), exclusive of any applicable taxes.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** The Contract Value shall be comprised of the following components (illustrative allocation):

3.2.1 **Implementation and Professional Services Fees:** US$1,200,000, payable based on project milestones as set forth in Section 3.3.

3.2.2 **Subscription / License Fees:** US$2,800,000, covering access to the Platform for the Term, payable in equal quarterly installments in advance, unless otherwise stated in an applicable SOW.

3.2.3 **Training and Knowledge Transfer Fees:** US$150,000, payable upon completion of the agreed training sessions and acceptance by Client.

3.2.4 **Contingency and Reserved Services Pool:** US$166,921, to be utilized for approved Change Orders, additional consulting, or other mutually agreed incremental services; such amounts shall be invoiced upon execution of applicable Change Orders.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**

3.3.1 **Implementation Milestones.** Implementation and professional services fees shall be invoiced as follows:

  a. Twenty-five percent (25%) upon Client’s approval of the Implementation Plan;  
  b. Thirty-five percent (35%) upon completion of system configuration and successful completion of initial integration testing;  
  c. Twenty-five percent (25%) upon production go-live; and  
  d. Fifteen percent (15%) upon completion of a thirty (30) day post-go-live stabilization period and written acceptance by Client (such acceptance not to be unreasonably withheld).

3.3.2 **Subscription Fees.** Subscription fees shall be invoiced quarterly in advance, beginning on the earlier of (i) the production go-live date, or (ii) ninety (90) days after the Amendment Effective Date, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

3.3.3 **Training Fees.** Training fees shall be invoiced upon completion of agreed initial training sessions, supported by evidence of delivery and attendance records.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**

3.4.1 All undisputed amounts invoiced by Vendor shall be due and payable by Client within **thirty (30) days** from the date of the applicable invoice (**“Net 30”** terms).

3.4.2 Client may withhold payment of any disputed amount by providing written notice to Vendor within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute and the amount in dispute. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve any such disputes promptly. Client shall pay all undisputed portions of any invoice in accordance with this Section.

3.5 **Late Payment.**

3.5.1 Any undisputed invoices not paid within ten (10) days after the due date may accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the date due until the date paid in full.

3.5.2 Vendor shall not exercise any suspension rights for non-payment unless (a) amounts remain undisputed and unpaid for more than forty-five (45) days after the invoice due date, and (b) Vendor has provided Client with written notice and a further ten (10) day cure period.

3.6 **Taxes.**

3.6.1 All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, or other similar taxes (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Client shall be responsible for all Taxes imposed in connection with this Amendment, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employment.

3.6.2 If Vendor is required to collect or remit Taxes on behalf of Client, such Taxes shall be included on the invoice, and Client shall pay such amounts in accordance with this Article III, unless Client provides a valid tax exemption certificate.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

3.7.1 Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with on-site services (such as travel, lodging, and meals) shall be reimbursable by Client, provided that Vendor supplies appropriate supporting documentation.

3.7.2 Vendor shall comply with Client’s then-current travel and expense policies, as provided by Client to Vendor in writing, for any Client-funded travel.

3.8 **No Setoff.** Except as explicitly permitted under Section 3.4.2 with respect to disputed amounts, Client shall not set off, withhold, or offset any amounts due to Vendor under this Amendment against any amounts owed by Vendor to Client, unless otherwise agreed in writing or ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The term of this Amendment shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date of July 2, 2026**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.** Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, the Term may be extended or renewed only upon mutual written agreement of the Parties under a subsequent amendment or renewal order form.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor.  

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience only upon at least **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client and only after the initial twelve (12) months of the Term, in order to avoid undue disruption to Client’s operations.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Amendment upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Amendment or the Original Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days for non-payment) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Amendment immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed into receivership, or ceases to carry on its business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment for any reason:  
  a. All undisputed Fees for Services performed and Deliverables accepted prior to the termination effective date shall become due and payable in accordance with **Article III**;  
  b. All licenses granted to Client under **Article VI** shall terminate, except for any perpetual licenses expressly granted herein;  
  c. Vendor shall promptly cease performing the Services, except as reasonably necessary to effect an orderly transition.

4.5.2 Upon Client’s request made within thirty (30) days after the effective date of termination or expiration, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates (unless otherwise agreed), to facilitate migration of Client’s data and operations to another system or provider.

4.6 **Data Return and Deletion.**  

4.6.1 Within thirty (30) days following expiration or termination, Vendor shall, at Client’s option, either (a) provide Client with a one-time export of Customer Data in a commonly used machine-readable format, or (b) make Customer Data available for secure download by Client.  

4.6.2 Subject to any legal retention requirements, Vendor shall securely delete or anonymize all remaining Customer Data in its possession or control within ninety (90) days after expiration or termination, except for backup copies that will be securely destroyed in the ordinary course of Vendor’s backup rotation and retention policies.  

4.6.3 Upon Client’s written request, Vendor shall certify in writing its compliance with this Section 4.6.

4.7 **Survival.** The provisions of **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions)** and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Amendment.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **System Availability.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Platform’s production environment achieves a minimum **System Availability** of **99.9%** during each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime.

12.2 **Permitted Downtime.** System Availability calculations shall exclude: (a) scheduled maintenance notified to Client at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance, occurring during pre-agreed maintenance windows; (b) emergency maintenance required to address critical security issues, provided that Vendor uses reasonable efforts to minimize disruption; (c) downtime caused by Client-provided equipment or third-party systems not under Vendor’s control; and (d) downtime caused by Force Majeure Events.

12.3 **Response Times.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the following initial response times for support tickets:

- Severity 1 (Critical – system down, no workaround): response within one (1) hour, 24x7.  
- Severity 2 (High – major functionality impacted, workaround available): response within two (2) Business Hours.  
- Severity 3 (Medium – minor functionality or general issues): response within one (1) Business Day.  
- Severity 4 (Low – informational, enhancement requests): response within two (2) Business Days.

12.4 **Remedies for SLA Breach.** If Vendor fails to meet the System Availability commitment in Section 12.1 in a given calendar month, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows (to be applied against future subscription Fees):

- System Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit of 5% of the monthly subscription Fee;  
- System Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit of 10% of the monthly subscription Fee;  
- System Availability below 98.0%: credit of 20% of the monthly subscription Fee.

Service credits shall be Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches, except where repeated failures constitute a material breach under **Article IV**.
