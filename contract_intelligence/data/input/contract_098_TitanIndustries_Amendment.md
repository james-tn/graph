# AMENDMENT TO SOFTWARE LICENSE AGREEMENT  
**Contract Reference Number: AMD-TIT-202404-098**

---

## PREAMBLE & RECITALS

This AMENDMENT TO SOFTWARE LICENSE AGREEMENT (this “**Amendment**”), reference number **AMD-TIT-202404-098**, is made and entered into as of **September 28, 2024** (the “**Amendment Effective Date**”), by and between:

- **Contoso Enterprises, Inc.**, a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of Washington, with its principal place of business at 1200 Fifth Avenue, Suite 2400, Seattle, WA 98101 (“**Contoso**”, the “**Client**” or the “**Company**”); and  
- **Titan Industries, LLC**, a limited liability company organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware, with its principal place of business at 300 Titan Plaza, Floor 10, Bellevue, WA 98004 (“**Titan**”, the “**Vendor**” or the “**Service Provider**”).

Contoso and Titan are each referred to herein individually as a “**Party**” and collectively as the “**Parties**.”

This Amendment relates to and modifies that certain **Software License Agreement** between the Parties titled “SOFTWARE LICENSE AGREEMENT,” reference number **SLA-TIT-202404-088**, file identifier **contract_088**, dated **April 10, 2024** (the “Software License Agreement” or the “Parent Agreement”).

---

### RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties entered into the **Software License Agreement**, reference number **SLA‑TIT‑202404‑088** (file identifier **contract_088**), effective as of **April 10, 2024** (the “Effective Date” of the Parent Agreement), pursuant to which Titan grants to Contoso certain license rights to use the **TitanCore Enterprise Suite** Software (as defined in Article I, Section 1.18 of the Parent Agreement) and provides related Services, all on the terms and conditions set forth therein;

WHEREAS, Article XVI, Section 16.2 of the Parent Agreement (“**Amendments**”) provides that no amendment or modification of the Parent Agreement shall be valid or binding unless in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to expand the scope of the Software modules, Services, and Service Levels provided under Article II (“Scope of Services and License”) and Article XII (“Service Level Agreement (SLA)”) of the Parent Agreement, adjust the fees and payment terms previously set forth in Article III (“Fees and Payment Terms”), extend the overall contractual relationship beyond the Initial Term defined in Section 1.12 and Section 4.1 of the Parent Agreement, and otherwise modify certain provisions of the Parent Agreement;

WHEREAS, the Parties acknowledge that the total contract value for the Initial Term under the Parent Agreement is **US $1,945,996** as set forth in the Preamble and Article III, Section 3.1 of the Parent Agreement (the “Contract Value” thereunder), and now desire to increase and restate the aggregate contract value applicable to the extended term and additional scope described herein;

WHEREAS, the Parties now agree that the revised **Contract Value** for the extended term and expanded scope contemplated by this Amendment shall be **Three Million Seven Hundred Forty‑Four Thousand Nine Hundred Fifty‑Eight U.S. Dollars (US $3,744,958)** for the period from the Amendment Effective Date through **October 17, 2027** (the “Amendment Term”), subject to adjustments in accordance with the Parent Agreement as amended hereby;

WHEREAS, the Parties further desire to adjust the payment terms from **Net 30** as provided in Section 3.5 of the Parent Agreement to **Net 60** under this Amendment, while preserving all other payment mechanics and dispute procedures in Sections 3.5–3.7 of the Parent Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, terms, and conditions contained herein and in the Parent Agreement, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.** This Article II supplements and, to the extent expressly stated, modifies Article II of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly amended herein, all provisions of Article II of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect. The Parties agree that all Extended Software Modules and Extended Services provided pursuant to this Amendment constitute “Software” and “Services” for purposes of the Parent Agreement and shall be subject to all applicable terms therein, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), and XII (Service Level Agreement).

2.2 **Extended Software Modules.** In addition to the core modules and functionalities listed in Section 2.2 of the Parent Agreement, Titan shall make available to the Company the following Extended Software Modules within the TitanCore Enterprise Suite:

  (a) **Advanced Analytics & Forecasting Pack.** Enhanced analytics dashboards with machine‑learning‑based demand forecasting, anomaly detection, and executive‑level KPI scorecards integrated with the ERP and CRM modules referenced in Sections 2.2(a)–(c) of the Parent Agreement.  

  (b) **Customer 360 & Engagement Module.** An extension to the CRM module referenced in Section 2.2(b) of the Parent Agreement, providing unified customer profiles, engagement scoring, and campaign performance tracking, with full role‑based access control consistent with Section 2.2(d) of the Parent Agreement.  

  (c) **Extended Integration Hub.** Additional Integration APIs beyond those described in Section 2.2(e) of the Parent Agreement, including pre‑built connectors for Contoso’s designated third‑party applications (e.g., external data warehouses, marketing automation tools, and payment gateways), subject to availability of appropriate APIs and documentation as referenced in Section 2.3(d) of the Parent Agreement.  

  (d) **Workflow Orchestration & Automation Pack.** Advanced workflow tools that extend the administrative configuration tools referenced in Section 2.2(f) of the Parent Agreement, including multi‑step approval chains, conditional logic, and templated process libraries.

The precise quantities of Authorized Users, environments, and any per‑module licensing metrics shall be specified in one or more Statements of Work executed under the Parent Agreement and referencing this Amendment by Contract Reference Number **AMD‑TIT‑202404‑098**.

2.3 **Extended Services.** Titan shall provide Extended Services that build upon Section 2.3 (Implementation and Configuration Services) and Section 2.9 (Project Timeline) of the Parent Agreement, including:

  (a) **Global Rollout and Multi‑Region Deployment.** Planning, configuration, and deployment of the Extended Software Modules across Contoso’s U.S., EMEA, and APAC business units, including tenant configuration, localization, and time‑zone‑aware processing;  

  (b) **Data Model Consolidation and Migration.** Design and implementation of a consolidated enterprise data model, migration of additional historical data sets into the TitanCore platform, and validation of cross‑module data consistency, expanding on the data migration assistance contemplated in Section 2.3(c) of the Parent Agreement;  

  (c) **Integration Design and Implementation.** Design, development, and testing of integrations between the Extended Integration Hub and Contoso’s designated external systems, in alignment with the integration development processes described in Section 2.3(d) of the Parent Agreement;  

  (d) **Training and Change Management Support.** Delivery of instructor‑led training sessions (remote and on‑site, as agreed) for business users, administrators, and IT staff, including the creation of training materials that supplement the Deliverables referenced in Section 2.4(d) of the Parent Agreement; and  

  (e) **Optimization and Performance Tuning.** Post‑go‑live optimization services, including performance tuning, configuration refinement based on production usage, and periodic health checks.

2.4 **Deliverables under this Amendment.** In addition to the Deliverables listed in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement, Titan shall provide the following specific Deliverables, which shall be governed by Section 2.4 (including acceptance criteria) of the Parent Agreement unless expressly modified herein:

  (a) A **Global Implementation Blueprint**, defining process design, solution architecture, data flows, and integration specifications across all regions;  

  (b) A **Data Migration Plan and Validation Reports**, documenting source‑to‑target mappings, migration procedures, and post‑migration reconciliation outcomes for the extended data sets;  

  (c) **Integration Specification Documents and Test Artifacts**, including interface control documents, API specifications, and test cases/test results for the Extended Integration Hub;  

  (d) **Advanced Analytics Configuration Guides**, including definitions of KPIs, dashboards, and forecasting models; and  

  (e) **Operational Runbooks**, including incident response procedures specific to the Extended Software Modules, alignment with the Enhanced SLA, and administrative tasks.

Deliverables shall be deemed accepted in accordance with the acceptance mechanism set forth in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement, except that for critical path Deliverables expressly identified in the applicable SOW as “Milestone Deliverables,” the acceptance period shall be **fifteen (15) Business Days** instead of ten (10) Business Days.

2.5 **Project Timeline and Milestones.** The Parties shall document the detailed project timeline and milestones in one or more SOWs executed pursuant to Section 1.19 and Article II of the Parent Agreement. At a minimum, the following milestone categories shall apply to the Extended Services:

  (a) **Initiation & Design Milestone.** Completion of project initiation, stakeholder alignment, and Global Implementation Blueprint, expected within ninety (90) days following the Amendment Effective Date;  

  (b) **Configuration & Integration Milestone.** Completion of core configuration of Extended Software Modules and build of priority integrations, expected within six (6) months following the Amendment Effective Date;  

  (c) **Data Migration & UAT Milestone.** Completion of data migration cycles and user acceptance testing for each major region; and  

  (d) **Go‑Live & Stabilization Milestone.** Successful go‑live for all in‑scope regions and transition to steady‑state operations, including hand‑over of Operational Runbooks, expected within eighteen (18) months following the Amendment Effective Date, subject to Contoso’s timely cooperation as contemplated in Section 2.10 of the Parent Agreement.

2.6 **Performance Standards.** The performance standards described in Section 2.8 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the Extended Services. Without limiting the generality of Section 2.8, Titan shall:

  (a) assign appropriately skilled and experienced personnel with knowledge of enterprise‑grade ERP/CRM implementations;  

  (b) coordinate with Contoso’s designated project manager as described in Section 2.9 of the Parent Agreement; and  

  (c) re‑perform, at no additional charge, any Extended Services that fail to meet the agreed performance standards, as Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for such failures, subject to the overall limitation of liability and remedies set forth in Article IX of the Parent Agreement and Article IX of this Amendment.

2.7 **Company Responsibilities.** In addition to the responsibilities set forth in Section 2.10 of the Parent Agreement, the Company shall:

  (a) identify and make available regional business owners for each major functional area to validate requirements and participate in testing;  

  (b) provide timely access to third‑party vendors and documentation necessary for integration work; and  

  (c) ensure that appropriate change management and end‑user training activities are implemented within Contoso’s organization, with Titan providing support as defined herein.

2.8 **Relationship to Existing SOWs.** All SOWs executed under the Parent Agreement prior to the Amendment Effective Date shall remain in effect in accordance with their terms and the Parent Agreement. Any new SOWs referencing this Amendment shall clearly indicate when Extended Software Modules, Extended Services, or Enhanced SLA terms apply.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Aggregate Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that:

  (a) The **Contract Value** for the Initial Term under the Parent Agreement, as defined in Section 3.1 thereof, is **US $1,945,996**.  

  (b) The total aggregate contract value for the combined scope of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, for the period ending on **October 17, 2027**, is hereby set and agreed at **US $3,744,958**.  

  (c) The difference between these amounts (the “Additional Contract Value”) represents the fees associated with the Extended Software Modules, Extended Services, and Enhanced SLA introduced by this Amendment.

3.2 **Fee Components.** The Additional Contract Value shall be comprised of:

  (a) **Additional License Fees** for Extended Software Modules, calculated based on Authorized User counts, environments, and module‑specific pricing metrics as defined in the applicable SOWs;  

  (b) **Extended Professional Services Fees** for implementation, configuration, integration, migration, training, and optimization Services described in Article II of this Amendment, billed on a fixed‑fee and/or time‑and‑materials basis as specified in the applicable SOWs; and  

  (c) **Enhanced Maintenance and Support Fees** and, where applicable, **Hosting Fees** associated with the Enhanced SLA, billed annually in advance, consistent with Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement, as modified by this Amendment.

3.3 **Fee Schedules and Milestones.** Each SOW executed under this Amendment shall include a fee schedule specifying:

  (a) fixed‑fee amounts associated with defined milestones (e.g., completion of Initiation & Design Milestone, Go‑Live & Stabilization Milestone);  

  (b) hourly or daily rates for time‑and‑materials Services, including any premium rates for after‑hours or expedited work; and  

  (c) annual or multi‑year license and maintenance fees for Extended Software Modules, broken down by module, region, and environment.

3.4 **Invoicing.** Invoicing shall follow the general framework set forth in Sections 3.2–3.4 of the Parent Agreement, subject to the following clarifications:

  (a) **Additional License Fees** shall be invoiced annually in advance for the relevant license period, in accordance with Section 3.2 of the Parent Agreement;  

  (b) **Time‑and‑Materials Services** shall be invoiced monthly in arrears, consistent with Section 3.3 of the Parent Agreement;  

  (c) **Fixed‑Fee Milestones** shall be invoiced upon achievement of the applicable milestone as defined in the SOW; and  

  (d) **Maintenance, Support, and Hosting Fees** related to the Extended Software Modules and Enhanced SLA shall be invoiced annually in advance, consistent with Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement.

3.5 **Amended Payment Terms (Net 60).** Section 3.5 of the Parent Agreement is hereby amended **solely with respect to invoices issued on or after the Amendment Effective Date** so that all such invoices shall be due and payable within **sixty (60) days** of the invoice date (“**Net 60**”), unless otherwise expressly specified in an applicable SOW referencing this Amendment. All other provisions of Section 3.5 (including currency and payment methods) remain in effect.

3.6 **Late Payment.** Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement (Late Payment) shall continue to apply to all undisputed amounts not paid when due under this Amendment. For clarity, interest shall accrue from the due date established by the Net 60 terms set forth in Section 3.5 of this Amendment.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.** Section 3.7 of the Parent Agreement (Disputed Amounts) applies to any disputed amounts arising under this Amendment. The Parties shall employ the same good faith dispute resolution mechanisms for fee disputes as they do for the Parent Agreement.

3.8 **Taxes and Withholding.** The provisions of Section 3.8 of the Parent Agreement (Taxes) shall apply equally to all fees invoiced under this Amendment.

3.9 **Expenses.** Section 3.9 of the Parent Agreement (Expenses) shall apply to on‑site Extended Services, provided that:

  (a) Titan shall obtain Contoso’s prior written approval for any individual expense exceeding **US $500**, as already required in Section 3.9 of the Parent Agreement; and  

  (b) where practicable, Titan shall align travel and expenses with Contoso’s then‑current travel policies as supplied to Titan in advance.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Amendment Term.** This Amendment shall become effective as of the Amendment Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **October 17, 2027**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or Article IV of the Parent Agreement. The Amendment Term shall be deemed a “Renewal Term” for purposes of Section 4.1 of the Parent Agreement, and, together with the Initial Term, shall be part of the overall “Term” as defined therein.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.** The Parent Agreement remains in full force and effect through the Term defined in Section 4.1 thereof and as extended or renewed by mutual agreement. This Amendment shall not be construed as automatically extending the Parent Agreement beyond the Amendment Term; rather, the Parent Agreement and this Amendment shall be co‑terminous unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in a subsequent written instrument.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement (Termination for Convenience) is incorporated by reference and shall apply to this Amendment; provided, however, that either Party may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. Any termination of this Amendment for convenience shall be subject to the payment obligations specified in Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement, as applied to the fees incurred or committed under this Amendment up to the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement (Termination for Cause) shall apply equally to this Amendment. In the event of a material breach relating specifically to Extended Software Modules or Extended Services, the non‑breaching Party may elect to terminate only this Amendment (and any directly related SOWs) while preserving the Parent Agreement, except where the breach also constitutes a material breach of the Parent Agreement as a whole.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon any expiration or termination of this Amendment:

  (a) Titan’s obligation to provide Extended Software Modules, Extended Services, and Enhanced SLA commitments shall cease as of the effective date of termination, subject to the Transition Period described below;  

  (b) the Company shall cease all access to and use of any Extended Software Modules and related Documentation, and shall, upon Titan’s request, certify in writing that it has removed and destroyed all copies thereof, except as permitted for backup purposes in accordance with Section 4.4(b) of the Parent Agreement;  

  (c) the Parties shall cooperate in good faith during the **Transition Period** (up to ninety (90) days) to effect an orderly transition of the Extended Services, during which Titan may provide reasonable assistance at its then‑current time‑and‑materials rates unless the termination is due to Titan’s uncured material breach, in which case Titan shall provide reasonable transition assistance at no additional cost for up to thirty (30) days; and  

  (d) the obligations in Section 4.5 of the Parent Agreement (Survival) shall apply, and, in addition, any provisions of this Amendment that by their nature should survive (including payment obligations accrued prior to termination, confidentiality, IP ownership, limitations of liability, indemnities, and dispute resolution) shall continue in full force and effect.

4.6 **Termination of Parent Agreement.** If the Parent Agreement terminates or expires for any reason, this Amendment shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, without the need for additional notice, and the consequences set forth in Section 4.4 of the Parent Agreement and Section 4.5 of this Amendment shall apply.
