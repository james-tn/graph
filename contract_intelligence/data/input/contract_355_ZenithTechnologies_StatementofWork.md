## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.** Pursuant to **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Master Services Agreement, Vendor shall provide Client with a comprehensive Program encompassing (a) technology implementation services, (b) systems integration, (c) data migration and transformation, (d) managed services and support, and (e) consulting and advisory services, with the objective of enabling a unified, digitized enterprise operations platform across Client’s global business units.

2.2 **Relation to Master Services Agreement.**  
The Services under this SOW are a specific instantiation of the categories of Services contemplated under **Section 2.2** of the Master Services Agreement, including:

- **Technology Implementation Services** (Section 2.2.1), including requirements gathering, solution design, configuration, integration, testing, UAT support, and go-live activities;  
- **Managed Services and Support** (Section 2.2.2), including ongoing application maintenance, incident management, performance tuning, and periodic health checks;  
- **Consulting and Advisory Services** (Section 2.2.3), including business process analysis, IT strategy roadmapping, training, and project management; and  
- **Other Services** (Section 2.2.4) as mutually agreed and described herein, including development of bespoke dashboards and integration accelerators.

Except as expressly modified or supplemented in this SOW, the obligations and performance standards described in **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** and **Section 2.6 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Master Services Agreement shall apply in full to the Services performed under this SOW.

2.3 **Program Description and Project Phases.**  
Vendor shall deliver the Program in the following **Project Phases**, each consisting of specified activities and Deliverables:

2.3.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery & Assessment (Estimated: 16 weeks).**  
Vendor shall perform:

- Stakeholder interviews across Client’s regional business units (APAC, EMEA, Americas);  
- Current state assessment of Client’s enterprise resource planning (ERP), customer relationship management (CRM), and custom line-of-business systems;  
- Data landscape mapping, including identification of key Client Data sources and interfaces;  
- High-level risk assessment (technical, operational, and data privacy) in accordance with Vendor’s obligations under **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of the Master Services Agreement;  
- Drafting of a **Discovery and Assessment Report** (Deliverable D1) including pain points, process bottlenecks, and proposed target-state architecture options.

2.3.2 **Phase 2 – Design & Architecture (Estimated: 14 weeks).**  
Based on Phase 1 outputs, Vendor shall:

- Develop detailed **Functional Design Specifications** (Deliverable D2) and **Technical Architecture Documents** (Deliverable D3);  
- Define integration interfaces, data transformation rules, and master data governance models;  
- Propose security and access control model consistent with Client’s security requirements and Vendor’s obligations under **Section 11.3 (Security Measures)** of the Master Services Agreement;  
- Conduct design workshops and walkthroughs with Client’s designated representatives;  
- Finalize design sign-off package (Deliverable D4) including documented Acceptance Criteria for subsequent phases.

2.3.3 **Phase 3 – Build, Integration & Migration (Estimated: 32 weeks).**  
Vendor shall:

- Configure and customize the selected platform(s) in accordance with the approved design documents;  
- Develop required custom modules, integration services, and APIs leveraging Vendor Pre-Existing IP where appropriate, in line with **Section 6.3 (Vendor Pre-Existing IP)** of the Master Services Agreement;  
- Build automated data migration scripts and conduct iterative data loads (Deliverable D5 – Data Migration Toolkit);  
- Prepare and execute system integration tests and performance tests;  
- Provide interim technical documentation (Deliverable D6 – Technical Configuration and Integration Documentation).

2.3.4 **Phase 4 – User Acceptance Testing & Go-Live (Estimated: 18 weeks).**  
Vendor shall:

- Support Client in developing UAT scenarios and scripts, aligned with **Section 2.2.1 (UAT support and defect remediation)** of the Master Services Agreement;  
- Facilitate UAT execution, track defects, and perform remediation cycles;  
- Prepare cutover plan and go-live runbook (Deliverable D7);  
- Support production cutover and hypercare stabilization period (minimum four (4) weeks post go-live);  
- Provide training sessions and materials (Deliverable D8 – Training Materials and Knowledge Base Articles) consistent with the knowledge transfer expectations under **Section 2.2.3** of the Master Services Agreement.

2.3.5 **Phase 5 – Managed Services & Continuous Improvement (Estimated: Remainder of SOW Term).**  
From the initial go-live date through the SOW Expiration Date:

- Vendor shall provide Level 1, 2, and 3 support for the implemented solutions as specified in Article XII of this SOW and **Article XII** of the Master Services Agreement;  
- Perform periodic health checks, capacity planning, and performance tuning (Deliverable D9 – Quarterly Operations and Performance Reports);  
- Implement minor enhancements and optimizations mutually agreed via Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII** of the Master Services Agreement and this SOW;  
- Maintain compliance with applicable Service Levels, including uptime commitments for hosted components, as set forth in Section 12.3 of this SOW.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  
In addition to the Deliverables referenced above, Vendor shall deliver at a minimum the following:

- **D1** – Discovery and Assessment Report;  
- **D2** – Functional Design Specifications;  
- **D3** – Technical Architecture Documents;  
- **D4** – Design Sign-Off Package and Confirmed Acceptance Criteria;  
- **D5** – Data Migration Toolkit (scripts, mappings, validation rules);  
- **D6** – Technical Configuration & Integration Documentation;  
- **D7** – Go-Live Runbook and Operational SOPs;  
- **D8** – Training Materials and Knowledge Base Articles;  
- **D9** – Quarterly Operations and Performance Reports;  
- **D10** – Annual Strategic Roadmap and Improvement Recommendations.

All Deliverables shall constitute “Deliverables” and “Work Product” within the meaning of **Sections 1.10 and 6.4** of the Master Services Agreement, and shall be subject to the IP and license provisions set forth in **Article VI** of the Master Services Agreement and Article VI of this SOW.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services under this SOW:

2.5.1 In accordance with the performance standards set forth in **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Master Services Agreement;  

2.5.2 Utilizing personnel with skills and experience appropriate to the complexity of the Program, including a dedicated project manager as required by **Section 2.4.2** of the Master Services Agreement;  

2.5.3 In compliance with the Service Levels defined in **Article XII** of the Master Services Agreement and Article XII of this SOW, including target uptime of 99.9% for any hosted Production Environment; and  

2.5.4 In coordination with Client’s responsibilities as set forth in **Section 2.6 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Master Services Agreement and in Section 2.9 of this SOW.

2.6 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The following key Milestones and target dates shall apply, subject to adjustment through the change management process under **Article XIII** of the Master Services Agreement and this SOW:

- **M1 – Completion of Phase 1 / D1 Delivery:** Target – 31 January 2025  
- **M2 – Completion of Phase 2 / D2 & D3 Delivery and Design Sign-Off (D4):** Target – 31 May 2025  
- **M3 – Completion of Build & Integration / D5 & D6 Delivery:** Target – 31 January 2026  
- **M4 – Completion of UAT and Go-Live / D7 & D8 Delivery:** Target – 30 June 2026  
- **M5 – End of Initial Hypercare and Transition to Steady-State Managed Services:** Target – 31 August 2026  
- **M6 – Annual Strategic Roadmap (D10) Each Year:** Delivered by 31 March of each calendar year from 2026 to 2028.

In accordance with **Section 2.5.1** of the Master Services Agreement, the Parties acknowledge that these timelines may be impacted by Client dependencies, third-party delays, and other external factors, and agree to cooperate in good faith to mitigate such impacts.

2.7 **Acceptance Process.**  
2.7.1 For each Deliverable tied to a Milestone, Vendor shall submit the Deliverable to Client for review and acceptance. Client shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery to either (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide a written rejection specifying in reasonable detail any material non-conformities with the applicable Acceptance Criteria.

2.7.2 If Client fails to respond within such fifteen (15) Business Day period, acceptance shall be **deemed to have occurred**, consistent with the deemed acceptance mechanism contemplated in **Section 2.5.2** of the Master Services Agreement.

2.7.3 In the event of a rejection, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to remedy the identified material non-conformities and re-submit the Deliverable. The Parties shall repeat the acceptance process until the Deliverable is accepted or deemed accepted.

2.8 **Key Personnel and Governance.**  
2.8.1 Vendor shall designate a **Program Director** and a **Project Manager**, who shall act as the primary points of contact and who shall be responsible for coordination, reporting, and escalation, in line with **Section 2.4.2** of the Master Services Agreement.

2.8.2 The Parties shall establish a joint **Steering Committee** composed of senior representatives from each Party, which shall meet at least quarterly to review Program status, key risks, and strategic direction.

2.9 **Client Responsibilities for this SOW.**  
In addition to the general responsibilities outlined in **Section 2.6** of the Master Services Agreement, Client shall:

- Provide timely access to appropriate subject matter experts across business units;  
- Ensure that necessary Client systems and environments are available for integration, testing, and migration;  
- Provide accurate and complete data extracts as reasonably requested by Vendor;  
- Obtain all necessary internal approvals for process changes, integrations, and adoption of new tools.

Vendor’s obligations are conditioned on Client’s fulfillment of the responsibilities set forth herein and in **Section 2.6** of the Master Services Agreement.
