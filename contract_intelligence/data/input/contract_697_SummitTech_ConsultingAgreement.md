2.1 **Engagement of Service Provider.**  
The Company hereby engages the Service Provider, and the Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to perform the Consulting Services and provide the Deliverables in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement, including any applicable SOWs executed hereunder.

The Services to be provided by the Service Provider under this Agreement shall include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 Strategic technology consulting, including digital transformation roadmap development, cloud migration strategy, and enterprise architecture advisory;

  2.2.2 Business process assessment and optimization relating to the Company’s core operational processes, including but not limited to finance, procurement, customer relationship management, and supply chain;

  2.2.3 Systems integration consulting, including integration of existing systems with new platforms, middleware configuration, data migration planning, and interface design;

  2.2.4 Implementation support for selected technology platforms, including configuration guidance, test planning support, and go-live readiness assessment;

  2.2.5 Project and program management services, including project planning, status reporting, risk and issue management, and stakeholder communication support;

  2.2.6 Knowledge transfer, training, and documentation services to facilitate the Company’s ongoing operation and maintenance of implemented solutions;

  2.2.7 Any additional services specifically described in an SOW executed by the Parties pursuant to this Agreement.

2.3 **Statements of Work.**  

  2.3.1 The Parties may, from time to time, enter into one or more SOWs that reference this Agreement and describe in reasonable detail the specific Services to be performed and Deliverables to be provided. Each SOW shall include, as applicable:  
  (a) a description of the Services and Deliverables;

  (c) acceptance criteria;  
  (d) applicable Service Levels (if different or additional to Article XII);  
  (e) assumptions and dependencies;  
  (f) the fees and payment schedule; and  
  (g) any special terms, provided they do not conflict with this Agreement.

  2.3.2 In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to modify a specific provision of this Agreement and is signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

2.4 **Deliverables and Milestones.**  

  2.4.1 The Service Provider shall produce the Deliverables as specified in the SOW(s). By way of example and without limitation, Deliverables may include:  
  (a) current-state assessment reports;  
  (b) future-state solution designs;  
  (c) migration and implementation plans;  
  (d) configuration documents;  
  (e) test scripts and test reports;  
  (f) training materials and user guides; and  
  (g) executive presentations and status reports.

  2.4.2 The Service Provider shall meet the milestones and timelines set forth in the Project Plan, subject to any adjustments made under Article XIII (Change Management) and events of Force Majeure under Article XV.

  2.4.3 The Company shall promptly review and either accept or reject Deliverables in writing within ten (10) Business Days of receipt, unless another period is specified in the applicable SOW. If the Company fails to respond within such period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, provided that the Service Provider has given a reminder notice and a further five (5) Business Day period has elapsed without response.

The Service Provider shall, at its own cost, promptly correct such deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance within a mutually agreed timeframe.   2.5.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services:  
  (a) with due care, skill, and diligence and in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services;  
  (b) in accordance with the Project Plan and Service Levels; and

  2.5.3 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid any material disruption to the Company’s business operations in performing the Services and shall coordinate with the Company’s designated project manager regarding scheduling of key activities.

  2.6.1 The Company shall:

  (b) designate a project manager to act as primary point of contact for the Service Provider;

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until **21 May 2027** (the **"Expiration Date"**) (the **"Term"**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be renewed for additional periods upon mutual written agreement of the Parties, executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date. Any renewal shall specify the renewed term, revised fees (if any), and any updated SOWs.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Client-Friendly).**  

  4.3.1 The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for its convenience at any time upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider.

  4.3.2 The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Company, provided that such termination shall not be effective prior to the completion of any critical milestone then in progress, as reasonably determined by the Company, unless the Company agrees otherwise.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any SOW) immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (a) commits a material breach of this Agreement or the SOW, and such breach is not cured within thirty (30) days after written notice specifying the breach; or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation, has a receiver or administrator appointed, or takes any analogous action under Applicable Law indicating financial distress.

  4.4.2 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, repeated failure to meet Service Levels (Article XII), significant data protection breaches (Article XI), or material violations of confidentiality obligations (Article V) may constitute material breaches.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
  (a) the Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the Services (except to the extent reasonably necessary to effect an orderly wind-down);  
  (b) the Company shall pay the Service Provider all undisputed fees for Services properly performed and accepted Deliverables delivered through the effective date of termination, plus any approved expenses; and  
  (c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.

  4.5.2 If the Company terminates this Agreement or an SOW for convenience pursuant to Section 4.3.1, the Company shall not be liable for any early termination fees, lost profits, or similar charges, but shall pay for:  
  (a) Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination; and  
  (b) any non-cancellable third-party commitments incurred by the Service Provider in reliance on this Agreement, provided such commitments were pre-approved in writing by the Company.

  4.5.3 If the Company terminates for cause under Section 4.4.1, the Company shall have no obligation to pay for Services not yet performed or Deliverables not accepted, and the Service Provider shall refund any prepaid amounts for Services not provided as of the date of termination.

  4.5.4 The provisions of this Section 4.5 are without prejudice to any other rights or remedies available to either Party under this Agreement or Applicable Law, including, without limitation, the Company’s rights under Articles VII, VIII, IX, and XI.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: Articles I, III (to the extent of unpaid amounts and audit rights), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
"Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **"Disclosing Party"**) to the other Party (the **"Receiving Party"**), whether oral, visual, or written, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:  
(a) business plans, strategies, and financial information;  
(b) customer, vendor, and partner information;  
(c) technical information, including software, specifications, designs, and architectures;  
(d) Personal Data; and  
(e) the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  

  5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
  (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein; and  
  (c) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar importance, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

  5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose the Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisers who have a need to know for the purpose of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Article V. The Receiving Party shall remain liable for any breach of this Article V by such persons.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
The obligations in Section 5.2 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party, without restriction, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
(d) is rightfully received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permissible:  
(a) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and  
(b) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required, using reasonable efforts to preserve its confidential nature.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except to the extent that the Receiving Party is required to retain copies under Applicable Law or for legitimate archival purposes. Any retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations set forth herein.

5.6 **Survival Period.**  
The obligations of confidentiality and non-use under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets and Personal Data shall be kept confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets or Personal Data under Applicable Law.

  15.2.1 promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature, start date, and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event and the impact on its obligations; and

  15.2.2 use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
Subject to compliance with Section 15.2, the obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event to the extent such obligations are impacted.
