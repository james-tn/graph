## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES  

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client strategic, advisory, business development, implementation, and related technology support services in connection with Client’s enterprise solutions and platforms (the “**Services**”). The Services are intended to support Client’s market expansion, customer acquisition, product deployment, and operational efficiency initiatives. The Services shall be further specified in one or more SOWs executed by both Parties and referencing this Agreement.

2.2 **Strategic Advisory and Planning Services.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide strategic advisory services to assist Client in developing and refining its market strategy, including:  
  (i) market analysis and segmentation;  
  (ii) competitive positioning;  
  (iii) go‑to‑market planning;  
  (iv) identification of potential strategic partners and key accounts; and  
  (v) recommendations regarding product roadmap and enhancement priorities.  

(b) Vendor shall deliver written strategic assessment reports and periodic executive summaries (each a “**Strategy Deliverable**”) outlining findings, recommended actions, and measurable objectives, as specified in the applicable SOW.

2.3 **Business Development and Pipeline Services.**  
(a) Vendor shall support Client’s business development efforts by:  
  (i) identifying prospective customers and partners;  
  (ii) conducting outreach and preliminary qualification;  
  (iii) facilitating introductions and meetings; and  
  (iv) assisting Client in shaping proposals and solution designs for identified opportunities.  

(b) Vendor shall maintain a business development pipeline tracker, updated no less than monthly, outlining prospects, deal stages, and key next steps. Vendor shall provide such tracker to Client and review it in regularly scheduled status meetings.

2.4 **Implementation and Technology Support Services.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide implementation services for Client’s solutions, which may include:  
  (i) requirements gathering and solution design;  
  (ii) configuration and customization of Client’s platforms;  
  (iii) integration with Client’s and/or customer systems;  
  (iv) data migration and validation;  
  (v) user acceptance testing support; and  
  (vi) go‑live planning and execution.  

(b) Vendor shall provide post‑implementation support as mutually agreed, which may include:  
  (i) troubleshooting and issue resolution;  
  (ii) performance optimization;  
  (iii) minor configuration updates; and  
  (iv) knowledge transfer and training sessions for Client’s personnel.

2.5 **Deliverables and Milestones.**  
(a) Each SOW shall describe the specific Deliverables to be provided by Vendor, including at a minimum:  
  (i) a description of each Deliverable;  
  (ii) the format (e.g., electronic document, spreadsheet, presentation, configuration package);  
  (iii) acceptance criteria, including quality standards and completion metrics; and  
  (iv) delivery dates or milestone deadlines.  

(b) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to complete each Deliverable in accordance with the timelines specified in the applicable SOW. Vendor acknowledges that timely delivery is a critical performance requirement under this Agreement.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform the Services:  
  (i) in a professional and workmanlike manner;  
  (ii) in accordance with industry standards and best practices applicable to strategic advisory and implementation services of similar scope and complexity;  
  (iii) in compliance with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations; and  
  (iv) in compliance with Client’s written policies and procedures that have been provided to Vendor in advance, including security and data protection requirements.  

(b) Vendor shall assign qualified personnel with appropriate experience and skills to perform the Services. Vendor shall ensure that all such personnel are properly supervised and receive adequate guidance with respect to Client’s requirements.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
(a) Client shall:  
  (i) provide Vendor with access to relevant information, systems, personnel, and facilities reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  
  (ii) designate a project manager or primary point of contact to coordinate with Vendor;  
  (iii) review and provide timely feedback on Deliverables and key project decisions; and  
  (iv) fulfill any other responsibilities set forth in the applicable SOW.  

(b) Vendor shall not be liable for delays, increased costs, or failures in performance resulting from Client’s failure to perform its responsibilities under this Agreement, provided that Vendor has promptly notified Client in writing of any such failure and the resulting impact on the Services.

2.8 **Subcontracting.**  
Vendor may, with Client’s prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed), subcontract portions of the Services to third parties, provided that:  
  (i) Vendor remains fully responsible for the performance of such subcontractors;  
  (ii) Vendor ensures that each subcontractor is bound by written obligations of confidentiality, data protection, and security no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; and  
  (iii) any processing of Personal Data by subcontractors complies with **Article XI**.  

2.9 **Service Reviews and Reporting.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide regular status reports in the form and frequency set forth in the applicable SOW, which may include:  
  (i) progress against milestones;  
  (ii) key risks and issues;  
  (iii) planned activities for the next reporting period; and  
  (iv) any change requests or scope clarifications.  

(b) The Parties shall hold periodic review meetings (at least quarterly unless otherwise stated in an SOW) to review performance, address issues, discuss potential improvements, and consider changes to the Services in accordance with **Article XIII**.

2.10 **Compliance with Service Levels.**  
To the extent any Services are subject to Service Levels under **Article XII**, Vendor shall perform the Services in compliance with such Service Levels and shall provide Client with sufficient data and reports to verify Vendor’s performance. Remedies for Service Level failures shall be as set forth in **Article XII** and the applicable SOW.

## ARTICLE III

3.1 **Fees and Contract Value.**  
(a) In consideration for the performance of the Services and provision of Deliverables, Client shall pay Vendor fees in an aggregate amount not to exceed the **Contract Value** of **US$2,868,905**, subject to adjustments due to Change Orders, additional SOWs, or agreed expansions of scope.

  (i) fixed fees for defined Deliverables or milestones;  
  (ii) time-and-materials rates for advisory and support Services; and

3.2 **Payment Terms; Invoices.**  
(a) Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the schedule set forth in each SOW, which may include milestone-based billing, monthly billing for time and materials, or other agreed schedules.  

(b) All undisputed amounts invoiced by Vendor shall be due and payable by Client within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the date of receipt of the invoice (“**Net 30**”), as required by the Contract Parameters.  

(c) Invoices shall include:  
  (i) reference to this Agreement (AGR-ATL-202402-381) and the relevant SOW;  
  (ii) a description of Services and Deliverables provided;  
  (iii) itemized fees and expenses; and  
  (iv) any applicable taxes.  

3.3 **Late Payments.**  
(a) Any undisputed amount not paid by Client within ten (10) days following written notice of non‑payment after the Net 30 period has expired shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of:  
  (i) one percent (1%) per month; or  
  (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law,  
calculated from the original due date until paid in full.  

(b) Vendor’s acceptance of late or partial payments shall not constitute a waiver of any of Vendor’s rights under this Agreement.  

3.4 **Disputed Amounts.**  
(a) If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the amount in dispute and the reasons therefor. Client shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with **Section 3.2**.  

(b) The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any dispute regarding invoiced amounts within thirty (30) days of Client’s notice. Any portion of the invoice subsequently agreed or determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of resolution.  

3.5 **Taxes.**  
(a) All fees and other amounts payable by Client under this Agreement are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employment.  

(b) Vendor shall cooperate with Client to provide reasonable documentation necessary for Client to fulfill its tax obligations, including valid tax invoices where required.  

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable and pre‑approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred by Vendor in performing the Services, including travel, lodging, and necessary materials, provided that:  
  (i) such expenses are consistent with Client’s travel and expense policy (as provided to Vendor); and  
  (ii) Vendor provides appropriate receipts and documentation.  

(b) Vendor shall obtain Client’s written approval (email sufficient) in advance for any single expense exceeding US$1,000 or such other threshold as specified in the relevant SOW.  

3.7 **No Additional Charges.**  
Except as expressly provided in this Agreement or an SOW, Vendor shall not charge Client any additional fees, costs, or expenses without Client’s prior written consent.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION  

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article, continue in full force and effect until **March 24, 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, upon mutual written agreement at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Agreement for additional one‑year renewal terms upon such terms and conditions as the Parties may then agree in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile of this engagement, Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, for any reason or no reason, upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor. Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (i) materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail (or, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue a cure within such period); or  
  (ii) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed for its assets, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.  

4.5 **Termination of Individual SOWs.**  
Any SOW may be terminated separately from this Agreement in accordance with the terms of such SOW and the provisions of this Article IV. Termination of a particular SOW shall not automatically terminate this Agreement or any other SOW, unless expressly stated in the termination notice.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:  
  (i) Vendor shall immediately cease all affected Services (except as necessary for an orderly transition as set forth below);  
  (ii) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and Deliverables accepted through the effective date of termination, and for any non-cancellable commitments made with Client’s prior written approval;  
  (iii) Vendor shall promptly return or, at Client’s option, securely destroy Client’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**; and  
  (iv) the Parties shall reasonably cooperate to transition the Services to Client or to a third party designated by Client, subject to mutually agreed fees for any transition assistance beyond what is expressly included in the SOW.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including without limitation **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure)**, and **XVI (General Provisions)**, as well as any accrued payment obligations, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

Confidential Information includes the terms and conditions of this Agreement but does not include information that falls within one of the exceptions in **Section 5.3**.

5.2 **Confidentiality Obligations.**  
(a) The Receiving Party shall:  
  (i) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services and exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
  (ii) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care;

  (iv) limit access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, and Affiliates who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Article V.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:  
  (i) was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  (ii) becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (iii) is received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
  (iv) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that (to the extent legally permitted) the Receiving Party:  
  (i) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement;  
  (ii) cooperates with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking protective orders or other appropriate remedies; and  
  (iii) discloses only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement or upon the Disclosing Party’s earlier written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly (a) return or destroy (at Disclosing Party’s election) all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, and (b) certify in writing that it has complied with this obligation; provided, however, that the Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for purposes of legal or compliance obligations, subject to the continuing confidentiality obligations of this Article V.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations of confidentiality under this Article V shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall be kept confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets or Personal Data protected by applicable law.

  (i) access controls;  
  (ii) encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest where appropriate;  
  (iii) regular vulnerability assessments and patch management;  
  (iv) secure development and change management practices; and  
  (v) incident detection and response procedures.

  (i) notify Client without undue delay, and in any event within seventy‑two (72) hours after becoming aware of the Security Incident;  
  (ii) provide Client with sufficient information to enable Client to comply with its own notification and reporting obligations, including a description of the nature of the incident, the categories and approximate number of affected data subjects, and the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the incident; and  
  (iii) cooperate with Client in investigating the incident, mitigating its adverse effects, and implementing reasonable remedial measures.

11.7 **Data Transfers.**  
To the extent Personal Data is transferred across national borders in connection with the Services, the Parties shall ensure that such transfers comply with Data Protection Laws, including, where required, the execution of Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms.

11.8 **Data Retention and Deletion.**  
Vendor shall retain Personal Data only for as long as necessary to perform the Services or comply with applicable legal obligations. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon Client’s written request, Vendor shall promptly delete or return all Personal Data in accordance with **Section 5.5**, unless retention is required by law.     - Initial Response: within 4 Business Hours  
    - Targeted Resolution/Workaround: within 2 Business Days  

  - **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Non-critical issue or workaround available:  
    - Initial Response: within 8 Business Hours  
    - Targeted Resolution/Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

  - **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues or general inquiries:  
    - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
    - Targeted Resolution: as mutually agreed.

12.6 **Measurement and Reporting.**  
Vendor shall monitor the performance of the Services and maintain records sufficient to verify compliance with the Service Levels. Vendor shall provide periodic reports to Client upon request and shall participate in Service Level review meetings as reasonably requested by Client.

## ARTICLE XV  
## FORCE MAJEURE  

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to: acts of God; natural disasters; epidemics and pandemics; war, terrorism, riots, or civil disturbances; strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce); government actions or orders; and failures of telecommunications or internet service providers not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Effect of Force Majeure.**  
If a Party is prevented from or delayed in performing its obligations under this Agreement due to a Force Majeure Event, such Party shall be excused from performance for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that:  
  (i) the affected Party promptly notifies the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event, including a reasonable description of the event and its expected duration;  
  (ii) the affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance; and  
  (iii) the affected Party continues to provide periodic updates on the status of the Force Majeure Event.

15.3 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially impairs performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected Services or SOW upon written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination (except for payment for Services performed prior to termination).
