## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **Engagement.** Client hereby engages Service Provider, and Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement. The Parties anticipate that the Services will include strategic consulting, operational assessments, technology and systems advisory, process optimization, and implementation support, as more fully described herein and in any applicable SOW.

2.2 **General Description of Services.** Without limiting the foregoing, and subject to the specific requirements of each SOW, the Services may include:

  (a) **Strategic Assessment and Advisory.** Conducting a comprehensive assessment of Client’s existing business processes, systems, and organizational structure in the areas of operations, supply chain, information technology, customer relationship management, and financial reporting. Service Provider will analyze current-state capabilities, identify key gaps and inefficiencies, benchmark against industry best practices, and provide recommendations for improvement, including a prioritized roadmap with estimated timelines and resource requirements.

  (b) **Process Design and Optimization.** Designing and documenting target-state business processes, workflows, and operating models to improve Client’s operational efficiency, scalability, and risk management. This may include process mapping (current and future state), standard operating procedures, performance metrics, and change impact assessments.

  (c) **Technology and Systems Consulting.** Providing advisory and planning services related to the selection, configuration, and integration of enterprise software solutions (e.g., ERP, CRM, data analytics platforms), including vendor evaluation, requirements definition, solution architecture recommendations, and high-level implementation planning. Service Provider’s role is consultative; any actual software licensing or development is outside the scope unless expressly included in an SOW.

  (d) **Implementation Support and Project Management.** Providing project management support, implementation oversight, and coordination with Client’s internal teams and third-party vendors. This may include developing project plans, tracking milestones, managing risks and issues, facilitating status meetings, preparing communications, and training materials, and advising on organizational change management.

  (e) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Developing and delivering training sessions, workshops, and supporting materials to enable Client’s personnel to understand, adopt, and sustain the recommended processes and tools. Service Provider will reasonably cooperate to ensure that key knowledge is transferred to designated Client personnel.

  (f) **Reporting and Executive Communication.** Preparing periodic reports, dashboards, and executive presentations summarizing progress, findings, recommendations, and key decisions. Service Provider will participate in governance meetings or steering committees as reasonably requested by Client.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Service Provider shall produce Deliverables in accordance with the applicable SOW, which may include, without limitation:

  (a) Written assessments, diagnostic reports, and gap analyses;

  (b) Process maps, operating models, and RACI matrices;

  (c) Strategy documents, roadmaps, and implementation plans;

  (d) Training curricula, user guides, and reference materials;

  (e) Project plans, status reports, risk and issue logs, and stakeholder communication plans; and

  (f) Any other documentation or materials mutually agreed upon in writing by the Parties.

All Deliverables shall be prepared in a professional manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for consulting services of similar scope and complexity, and shall be provided in such formats as are reasonably requested by Client (e.g., Microsoft Office formats, PDF).

2.4 **Statements of Work.**  

  (a) From time to time, the Parties may execute one or more SOWs that reference this Agreement. Each SOW shall describe the specific Services and Deliverables to be provided, the applicable timeline, milestones, acceptance criteria, Fees, and any other terms and conditions relevant to such Services and Deliverables.

  (b) Each duly executed SOW shall be deemed incorporated into and made a part of this Agreement. In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and the terms of any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that a specific provision is intended to supersede this Agreement and identifies such provision with reasonable specificity.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, using Personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications;

  (b) in accordance with generally accepted standards in the consulting industry and any specific performance standards set forth in this Agreement or an SOW;

  (c) in material compliance with Applicable Law, including Data Protection Laws; and

  (d) in cooperation with Client’s designated personnel, and in accordance with Client’s reasonable policies and procedures furnished in advance in writing.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.** Client shall:

  (a) Provide Service Provider with timely access to Client Materials, facilities, systems, and key personnel as reasonably necessary for Service Provider to perform the Services;

  (b) Designate a primary contact and project sponsor with appropriate authority to make decisions, provide approvals, and resolve issues on behalf of Client;

  (c) Review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within reasonable timeframes as set forth in the applicable SOW or, if not specified, within ten (10) Business Days of receipt; and

  (d) Be responsible for the accuracy and completeness of information and data provided to Service Provider, it being understood that Service Provider will rely on such information without independent verification unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  (a) Each SOW may specify acceptance criteria and procedures for the Deliverables. In the absence of such specifics, Client shall have ten (10) Business Days following delivery of a Deliverable to review and either (i) provide written acceptance, or (ii) provide a written notice of material non-conformity with the applicable SOW or this Agreement.  

  (b) If Client timely notifies Service Provider of material non-conformity, Service Provider shall, at no additional cost to Client, promptly correct the identified deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for review. Client shall have an additional ten (10) Business Days to review the resubmitted Deliverable.

Service Provider shall have no liability for any decisions made by Client in reliance on the Services to the extent Client fails to obtain appropriate professional advice where reasonably required.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees; Contract Value.** In consideration for the performance of the Services and delivery of the Deliverables, Client shall pay to Service Provider Fees in the total contract value of Four Million Eight Hundred Sixty Thousand Eight Hundred Twelve U.S. Dollars (US$4,860,812), subject to any adjustments pursuant to this Agreement or applicable SOWs. The Fees may include fixed-fee components, time-and-materials components, or milestone-based fees, as further described in each SOW.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  (a) **Fixed Fees.** Certain phases or Deliverables may be billed on a fixed-fee basis, with payment tied to the achievement of specified milestones or completion of Deliverables.  

  (b) **Time and Materials.** Where Services are provided on a time-and-materials basis, Service Provider shall invoice based on the actual hours worked by its Personnel multiplied by the applicable hourly or daily rates specified in the SOW, plus approved expenses.  

  (c) **Milestone Billing.** The Parties anticipate that the Fees will be allocated to major project phases, such as assessment, design, implementation planning, and implementation support, with milestone payments due upon completion and acceptance of the corresponding Deliverables.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Net 45.**  

  (a) Service Provider shall invoice Client in accordance with the billing schedule specified in each SOW. Invoices shall be itemized and shall reasonably describe the Services performed, the Deliverables provided, the applicable milestone (if any), the period covered, and any approved expenses.  

  (b) Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within forty-five (45) calendar days after the date of receipt of a properly issued invoice (**“Net 45”**). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer or such other method as may be agreed in writing by the Parties.  

  (c) Client may withhold payment of any disputed portion of an invoice, provided that Client pays the undisputed portion by the due date and notifies Service Provider in writing of the dispute, including a reasonably detailed description of the basis for the dispute, within fifteen (15) calendar days of receipt of the invoice. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve any such dispute promptly. Any amounts subsequently agreed or determined to be payable shall be paid by Client within fifteen (15) calendar days after resolution.

3.4 **Late Payment.** Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted under Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment. Service Provider shall not charge interest on any amounts subject to a good faith dispute in accordance with Section 3.3(c).

3.5 **Expenses.**  

  (a) Client shall reimburse Service Provider for reasonable, necessary, and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Service Provider in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other expenses, in accordance with Client’s travel and expense policies provided to Service Provider in advance in writing.  

  (b) Service Provider shall provide reasonable supporting documentation (e.g., receipts) for all reimbursable expenses upon request.  

  (c) Any single expense in excess of US$1,500 and any international travel expense shall require Client’s prior written approval (email sufficient).

3.6 **Taxes.** All Fees and other amounts payable under this Agreement are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, or other similar taxes or duties, however designated or levied (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Client shall be responsible for all such Taxes (other than taxes based on Service Provider’s net income, property, or employment-related taxes for its Personnel). If Service Provider is required to collect or pay Taxes on behalf of Client, such Taxes shall be separately stated on the invoice and paid by Client, unless Client provides a valid tax exemption certificate.

3.7 **No Setoff.** Except for amounts subject to a good faith dispute under Section 3.3(c), Client shall not set off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from payments due to Service Provider under this Agreement, except as required by law or as expressly authorized in writing by Service Provider.

4.1 **Term.** The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until August 27, 2029 (the **“Expiration Date”**) (the **“Term”**).

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  (a) **By Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Service Provider.  

  (b) **By Service Provider.** Service Provider may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon not less than sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Client.  

Any such termination shall not relieve either Party of any obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  (a) **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for cause if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.  

  (b) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (i) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or ceases conducting business in the ordinary course; (ii) becomes subject to a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (iii) is subject to the appointment of a receiver, trustee, or similar officer for substantially all of its assets that is not discharged within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) Client shall pay Service Provider for all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, including any applicable milestone payments for Deliverables completed and accepted (or deemed accepted) and any non-cancellable commitments reasonably incurred prior to notice of termination, subject to Client’s right to dispute amounts in good faith.  

  (b) Service Provider shall promptly return or, at Client’s option, securely destroy all Client Materials and Confidential Information in its possession or control, except as required to be retained by Applicable Law, and shall certify such destruction upon Client’s written request.  

  (c) Upon Client’s request, Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to thirty (30) days after termination (or such other period as the Parties may agree), at Service Provider’s then-applicable time-and-materials rates, to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Client or a replacement vendor.  

  (d) The termination of any individual SOW shall not, by itself, result in the termination of this Agreement or any other SOW then in effect, unless expressly stated in the termination notice or otherwise agreed by the Parties.

4.6 **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Sections and Articles concerning Fees and Payment (to the extent of accrued but unpaid amounts), Confidentiality, Intellectual Property Rights, Representations and Warranties (to the extent stated), Indemnification, Limitation of Liability, Data Protection and Privacy, Dispute Resolution, and General Provisions.
