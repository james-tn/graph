## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF PRODUCTS, SERVICES, AND WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Gamma shall manufacture, supply, and deliver the Products and shall perform the Services for Contoso, and Contoso shall purchase the Products and procure the Services from Gamma. The Products and Services to be provided hereunder are further detailed in one or more SOWs and/or purchase orders referencing this Agreement and the Contract Reference No. PUR-GAM-202408-035.

2.2 **Products.**  
(a) Gamma shall supply to Contoso specialized enterprise-grade hardware components, including but not limited to high-performance compute nodes, storage appliances, network interface modules, and related power and rack-mount accessories (collectively, the **“Products”**).  
(b) Each Product shall conform in all material respects to the specifications, technical documentation, and performance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW or purchase order (the **“Specifications”**).  
(c) Gamma shall ensure that all Products are new or equivalent to new (not used or refurbished, unless expressly agreed in writing), free from material defects in materials and workmanship, and suitable for use in production enterprise environments.

2.3 **Embedded Software and Firmware.**  
(a) Certain Products may include embedded software or firmware (collectively, **“Embedded Software”**) required for the proper operation of such Products.  
(b) Gamma shall provide Contoso with a non-exclusive, worldwide license to use such Embedded Software solely as embedded in, and for the operation of, the Products, subject to the terms of Article VI.  
(c) Gamma shall provide updates, patches, and security fixes for Embedded Software as part of the maintenance and support Services, as further described in Section 2.6 and Article XII.

2.4 **Implementation and Integration Services.**  
(a) Gamma shall provide implementation, installation, and configuration Services to integrate the Products into Contoso’s existing infrastructure, including:  
   (i) technical assessment of Contoso’s current environment;  
   (ii) development of an implementation plan and deployment schedule;  
   (iii) installation of Products at Contoso-designated facilities;  
   (iv) configuration of Embedded Software, network parameters, and security settings; and  
   (v) integration of Products with Contoso’s existing software platforms and management tools.  
(b) Gamma shall perform such Services in accordance with generally accepted industry standards, using personnel who are appropriately trained, qualified, and experienced for the tasks to which they are assigned.

2.5 **Professional Services and Customization.**  
(a) Upon Contoso’s request and as described in an applicable SOW, Gamma shall provide professional services, which may include solution design, performance optimization, custom scripting, interface development, and other consulting services.  
(b) Any custom configurations, scripts, or interfaces created specifically for Contoso shall be Deliverables subject to Article VI.  
(c) Gamma shall cooperate with Contoso’s designated project managers, engineers, and other personnel to ensure timely and effective delivery of the professional Services.

2.6 **Support and Maintenance Services.**  
(a) During the Term, and subject to the payment of applicable Fees, Gamma shall provide maintenance and support Services for the Products and Embedded Software, including:  
   (i) remote and on-site technical support;  
   (ii) correction of reproducible defects;  
   (iii) provision of updates, upgrades, and patches;  
   (iv) replacement of defective hardware components in accordance with the warranty described in Article VII.  
(b) Gamma shall provide support Services in a manner consistent with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII.  
(c) Support shall be available, at a minimum, during normal Business Days and business hours in the relevant jurisdiction, with escalation procedures for critical incidents as defined in Article XII.

2.7 **Project Governance and Timelines.**  
(a) The Parties shall designate project managers to serve as primary points of contact for all communications related to the implementation, integration, and delivery of Products and Services.  
(b) The Parties shall collaborate to prepare a project plan (which may be attached to an SOW) setting forth key milestones, dependencies, and timelines.  
(c) Gamma shall use commercially reasonable efforts to perform the Services and deliver Products in accordance with the agreed timelines. Time is of the essence with respect to critical milestones expressly identified as such in an SOW.  
(d) If Gamma anticipates any delay that may materially affect a critical milestone, Gamma shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, explain the reasons for the delay, and propose a remediation plan. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate any adverse impact on the project schedule.

2.8 **Acceptance of Products and Deliverables.**  
(a) Upon delivery of Products or completion of Deliverables, Contoso shall have a period of thirty (30) days (the **“Acceptance Period”**) to inspect and test the Products and Deliverables for material conformance to the Specifications and other applicable requirements.  
(b) If the Products or Deliverables materially conform to the Specifications, Contoso shall be deemed to have accepted them upon expiration of the Acceptance Period or earlier written acceptance.  
(c) If Contoso reasonably determines that any Product or Deliverable does not materially conform, Contoso shall provide Gamma with a written notice of non-conformance before the end of the Acceptance Period, specifying the nature of the defect or non-conformance.  
(d) Upon receipt of such notice, Gamma shall, at its cost and as Contoso’s exclusive remedy for non-acceptance (except as otherwise provided in Article VII), promptly correct, repair, or replace the non-conforming Product or Deliverable and resubmit it for re-testing within a mutually agreed timeframe.  
(e) If Gamma is unable to remedy the non-conformance in a commercially reasonable time, Contoso may, at its election: (i) reject the non-conforming Product or Deliverable and receive a refund of any Fees paid for such Product or Deliverable; or (ii) accept the Product or Deliverable with an appropriate equitable adjustment to Fees.

2.9 **Location of Services.**  
Gamma shall perform the Services at Contoso’s designated facilities, remotely from Gamma’s facilities, or at such other locations as may be agreed by the Parties in an applicable SOW. Gamma shall comply with all reasonable security, safety, and site policies provided by Contoso to Gamma in writing in advance.

2.10 **Subcontracting.**  
Gamma may engage subcontractors to perform certain Services, provided that: (a) Gamma remains fully responsible and liable for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were Gamma’s own; and (b) any subcontractor with access to Contoso’s Confidential Information or Personal Data is bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement.

2.11 **Changes to Scope.**  
Any changes to the scope of Products, Services, or Deliverables, including any changes requested by Contoso, shall be governed by the Change Management process in Article XIII. No change shall be binding unless documented in a Change Order executed by both Parties.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the estimated aggregate value of the Products and Services to be procured under this Agreement is **US $4,017,596** (the **“Contract Value”**). The Contract Value is based on the pricing assumptions, quantities, and scope set forth in the initial SOW(s) and may be adjusted in accordance with this Agreement and any Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure and Schedule.**  
(a) Fees for Products shall be as set forth in the applicable SOW or purchase order and may be based on unit pricing per Product type, volume-based discounts, and any applicable installation or freight charges.  
(b) Fees for Services, including implementation, integration, professional services, and support, may be set forth as:  
   (i) fixed-price amounts for defined Deliverables or milestones;  
   (ii) time-and-materials rates based on hourly or daily rates for specified categories of personnel; or  
   (iii) recurring fees (e.g., annual support and maintenance fees).  
(c) Each SOW or purchase order shall specify the applicable fee model and the payment schedule, which may include milestone-based payments linked to delivery, acceptance, or time-based intervals.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, all undisputed invoices issued by Gamma under this Agreement shall be due and payable within **thirty (30) days** from the date of invoice (**“Net 30”**).  
(b) All payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method to the account designated by Gamma on the invoice.  
(c) Contoso shall reference the Contract Reference No. **PUR-GAM-202408-035** and any applicable SOW or purchase order number on each payment.

3.4 **Invoicing.**  
(a) Gamma shall invoice Contoso in accordance with the payment schedule set forth in the relevant SOW or purchase order.  
(b) Milestone-based invoices shall be issued upon completion of the applicable milestone and, where acceptance is required, upon deemed or actual acceptance pursuant to Section 2.8.  
(c) Each invoice shall provide reasonable detail of the Products delivered and/or Services performed, including dates of performance, quantities, unit prices, applicable taxes, and any approved reimbursable expenses.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
(a) All Fees are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes, duties, or levies imposed by any governmental authority (collectively, **“Taxes”**).  
(b) Contoso shall be responsible for all such Taxes (other than taxes based on Gamma’s net income, property, or employment), provided that Gamma shall include such Taxes as separate line items on each invoice.  
(c) If Contoso is exempt from any Taxes, it shall provide Gamma with appropriate exemption certificates. Gamma shall reasonably cooperate with Contoso to minimize Taxes to the extent permitted by law.

3.6 **Late Payments.**  
(a) If Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amount by the due date, Gamma may charge interest on the overdue amount at the lesser of: (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.  
(b) Gamma may, upon written notice to Contoso, suspend performance of Services and/or withhold delivery of Products in the event that any undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than sixty (60) days following the due date, provided that Gamma has provided Contoso with at least ten (10) Business Days’ written notice and an opportunity to cure such non-payment.  
(c) Gamma’s right to suspend shall be without prejudice to any other rights or remedies it may have under this Agreement or at law.

3.7 **Disputed Invoices.**  
(a) If Contoso in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice, Contoso shall notify Gamma in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the amount and basis of the dispute.  
(b) Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the disputed portion promptly.  
(c) Non-payment of a disputed portion of an invoice in accordance with this Section shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement or a basis for suspension under Section 3.6, provided Contoso complies with the procedures above.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
(a) Out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Gamma in connection with the performance of the Services (such as travel, lodging, and meal expenses) shall be reimbursable by Contoso only if:  
   (i) such expenses are reasonable and necessary; and  
   (ii) Gamma obtains prior written approval from Contoso where any single trip or event is expected to exceed US $2,500 in expenses.  
(b) Gamma shall provide reasonable documentation (such as receipts or expense reports) to support any request for reimbursement.  
(c) Any reimbursement of expenses shall be invoiced separately and shall be payable in accordance with Section 3.3.

3.9 **No Set-off.**  
Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or required by law, neither Party shall have the right to set off, withhold, or deduct any amounts owed to the other Party under this Agreement against any amounts payable by the other Party, without the prior written consent of such other Party.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 12, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, upon mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend this Agreement for additional periods on terms and conditions to be negotiated in good faith.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any SOW), for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. In the event of termination for convenience by Contoso:  
(a) Contoso shall pay Gamma for all Products delivered and accepted and all Services performed and accepted as of the effective date of termination;  
(b) For any active fixed-fee projects, Contoso shall pay Gamma on a pro-rata basis for work-in-progress, based on the percentage of completion as reasonably determined by the Parties; and  
(c) Gamma shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate any costs and expenses associated with such termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for cause if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the nature of the breach; provided, however, that for breaches that are not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have such additional time (not exceeding sixty (60) days in total) as is reasonably necessary to cure, so long as it commences cure within the initial thirty (30) days and diligently pursues such cure.  
(b) Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party:  
   (i) becomes insolvent or is generally unable to pay its debts as they become due;  
   (ii) becomes the subject of any voluntary or involuntary bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceeding, which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or  
   (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or ceases to do business.

4.5 **Termination for Change in Law or Regulatory Requirement.**  
If any applicable law, regulation, or governmental requirement makes performance of a material portion of this Agreement illegal or impossible, either Party may terminate the affected portion upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, after engaging in good faith discussions to modify the Agreement to comply with such change.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) All rights and licenses granted hereunder shall terminate, except as expressly provided otherwise in this Agreement;  
(b) Contoso shall pay Gamma for all conforming Products delivered and accepted, and Services performed and accepted, as of the effective date of termination, subject to any applicable credits or offsets specifically provided herein;  
(c) Each Party shall promptly return or, at the other Party’s written request, securely destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party, in accordance with Section 5.4;  
(d) Termination shall not affect any rights or obligations that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including any right to claim damages for breach; and  
(e) Those provisions that, by their nature or express terms, are intended to survive termination or expiration (including Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI) shall so survive.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
In the event of termination or expiration, Gamma shall, upon Contoso’s request and at Contoso’s expense (unless termination is due to Gamma’s uncured material breach, in which case such assistance shall be provided at no additional charge), provide reasonable transition cooperation for up to ninety (90) days to enable Contoso to transition the Products and Services to an alternative supplier or internal solution.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
For purposes of this Agreement, **“Confidential Information”** means all non-public information disclosed by a Party or its Affiliates (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party or its Affiliates (the **“Receiving Party”**), whether orally, visually, in writing, electronically, or otherwise, that is designated as confidential or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to: technical information, product designs, specifications, roadmaps, software, source code, business plans, financial information, pricing, customer lists, marketing plans, trade secrets, and know-how. Confidential Information includes the terms and conditions of this Agreement and any SOW.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
(a) The Receiving Party shall:  
   (i) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
   (ii) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any Third Party except as expressly permitted in this Agreement; and  
   (iii) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.  
(b) The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein.

5.3 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is received from a Third Party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (to the extent legally permitted) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice and cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If disclosure is ultimately required, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s earlier written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s election) all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except for copies retained in routine archival or backup systems, which shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein until destroyed in the ordinary course.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall be protected for so long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
For any hosted or remotely managed components of the Products or Services (if applicable), Gamma shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such services are available with a minimum uptime of **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Gamma may schedule routine maintenance windows, provided that:  
(a) Gamma shall provide Contoso with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance expected to cause service unavailability; and  
(b) scheduled maintenance shall, where feasible, be performed outside of Contoso’s normal business hours as reasonably designated by Contoso.

12.3 **Incident Classification and Response Times.**  
Gamma shall classify and respond to service incidents as follows:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or severe impact on production operations, no workaround.  
  - Initial response within **1 hour**, 24x7.  
  - Continuous efforts to resolve until service is restored or workaround is implemented.

- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant impairment of key functionality, workaround available.  
  - Initial response within **4 hours** during Business Days.  
  - Work to resolve during normal support hours with escalation as needed.

- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Moderate impact, non-critical functionality impaired.  
  - Initial response within **1 Business Day**.  

- **Priority 4 (Low):** Non-urgent issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
  - Initial response within **3 Business Days**.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If Gamma fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 for any calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits as follows (if there is a recurring services fee component):  
(a) Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring services fee;  
(b) Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring services fee;  
(c) Uptime below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly recurring services fee.  
Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice for recurring services. Service credits are Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet uptime Service Levels, without prejudice to Contoso’s rights in case of persistent or material breaches.

12.5 **Chronic SLA Failures.**  
If Gamma fails to meet the uptime commitment or Priority 1 response/resolution commitments for three (3) consecutive months or for any four (4) months in any rolling twelve (12) month period, Contoso may, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice and opportunity to cure, terminate the affected Services without penalty.
