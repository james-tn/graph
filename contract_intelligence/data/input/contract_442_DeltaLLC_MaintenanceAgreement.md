## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Equipment located at the Sites, as more fully described in this **Article II** and in each applicable SOW. The Services shall include, at a minimum, preventive maintenance, corrective maintenance, emergency support, and technical advisory services.

2.2 **Preventive Maintenance.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform preventive maintenance on the Equipment in accordance with: (i) the manufacturer’s recommendations; (ii) industry best practices; and (iii) any additional standards specified by Client in the applicable SOW, provided such standards are reasonable and communicated in writing.  
(b) Preventive maintenance shall include periodic inspections, calibration, cleaning, lubrication, adjustments, functional testing, and replacement of consumables or minor parts, as necessary to keep the Equipment in safe and efficient operating condition.  
(c) Vendor shall prepare and maintain preventive maintenance schedules for all covered Equipment, subject to Client’s review and approval. Such schedules shall identify the frequency of maintenance activities (e.g., monthly, quarterly, annually) and shall be updated as needed based on Equipment condition and operating history.  
(d) Vendor shall provide at least ten (10) Business Days’ advance written notice to Client of any planned preventive maintenance activity that requires Equipment shutdown, interruption of Client’s operations, or access to restricted areas of the Site.

2.3 **Corrective Maintenance.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide corrective maintenance services to diagnose and repair malfunctions or failures in the Equipment. Corrective maintenance includes troubleshooting, replacement of failed components, and restoration of the Equipment to normal operation.  
(b) Vendor shall respond to Client’s service requests in accordance with the response times and Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.  
(c) Vendor shall maintain an adequate inventory of commonly required spare parts, consumables, and tools to minimize downtime, as described in the applicable SOW.  
(d) Vendor shall not replace any major component or undertake any repair involving material cost or potential operational impact without Client’s prior written authorization, except in cases of imminent risk to health, safety, or property, in which case Vendor shall notify Client as soon as reasonably practicable.

2.4 **Emergency and After-Hours Support.**  
(a) Vendor shall provide 24x7 emergency maintenance support for critical Equipment, as identified in the applicable SOW.  
(b) For emergency incidents, Vendor shall meet the response and resolution times stated in the Service Levels (see **Section 12.3**). Vendor shall prioritize emergency requests over non-critical requests.  
(c) Vendor shall establish a dedicated telephone number and email address for Client personnel to submit emergency and after-hours service requests. Vendor shall ensure that qualified technical staff are available to respond.

2.5 **Remote Monitoring (If Applicable).**  
To the extent specified in an SOW, Vendor may implement remote monitoring of the Equipment. In such event:  
(a) Vendor shall ensure secure configuration of any remote monitoring software or devices, in accordance with **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.  
(b) Vendor shall provide periodic performance and status reports generated from remote monitoring systems.  
(c) Any Personal Data collected through remote monitoring shall be processed strictly in accordance with Data Protection Laws and Client’s instructions.

2.6 **Documentation and Reporting.**  
(a) Vendor shall maintain complete and accurate records of all Services performed, including: (i) date and time of service; (ii) description of work performed; (iii) parts used; (iv) measurements, readings, and test results; and (v) name and qualifications of the technician.  
(b) Vendor shall provide to Client: (i) monthly maintenance summary reports; (ii) quarterly performance reviews comparing actual performance against Service Levels; and (iii) annual maintenance assessments with recommendations for improvements, replacements, or upgrades.  
(c) All such reports shall be delivered in electronic format in a manner reasonably acceptable to Client and shall be considered Deliverables for purposes of **Article VI**.

2.7 **Personnel and Subcontractors.**  
(a) Vendor shall assign adequately trained, qualified, and experienced personnel to perform the Services. All such personnel shall comply with Client’s site rules, safety policies, security requirements, and reasonable instructions.  
(b) Vendor shall remain fully responsible and liable for the acts and omissions of its employees, agents, and subcontractors as if they were Vendor’s own.  
(c) Vendor shall not subcontract any material portion of the Services without Client’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed. Any approved subcontract shall be in writing and shall impose obligations on the subcontractor that are no less protective of Client than the terms of this Agreement.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.**  
(a) Client shall provide Vendor with reasonable access to the Sites, Equipment, and relevant documentation necessary for Vendor to perform the Services.  
(b) Client shall timely notify Vendor of any known defects, unusual conditions, or operational changes that may affect the Equipment or the performance of the Services.  
(c) Client shall not unreasonably interfere with Vendor’s performance of the Services and shall ensure that all Client personnel cooperate as reasonably necessary.  
(d) Client shall be responsible for maintaining appropriate environmental conditions (e.g., temperature, humidity, power quality) at the Sites, as specified by the Equipment manufacturer, except to the extent such conditions are specifically within Vendor’s control under this Agreement.

2.9 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with: (i) generally accepted industry standards; (ii) applicable laws and regulations; (iii) manufacturer specifications; and (iv) the Service Levels described in **Article XII**.  
(b) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to Client’s operations during the performance of the Services. Unless otherwise agreed, preventive maintenance shall, where practicable, be scheduled outside of Client’s business-critical operating hours.  
(c) Vendor shall promptly correct, at its own cost and without delay, any Services or Deliverables that do not conform to the requirements of this Agreement or the applicable SOW, as further described in **Section 7.4** (Warranties).

2.10 **Exclusions.**  
Unless expressly stated in an SOW or otherwise agreed in writing, the Services do not include:  
(a) replacement of Equipment due to obsolescence or capacity upgrades;  
(b) repairs necessitated by Client’s gross negligence, intentional misconduct, or unauthorized modifications by Client or third parties;  
(c) structural building repairs unrelated to the Equipment; or  
(d) services outside the agreed Sites or geographical scope.  

Any such excluded services may be provided by Vendor pursuant to a separate Change Order under **Article XIII**, subject to mutually agreed fees and terms.

(b) Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion of the invoice. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the disputed amount promptly. 3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
(a) Vendor shall be responsible for all ordinary overhead and operating expenses required to perform the Services (including salaries, benefits, tools, ordinary travel, and lodging) unless otherwise expressly stated in an SOW.  
(b) Client shall reimburse Vendor for pre-approved, reasonable, and documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Services, including major travel or third-party fees, provided that Vendor supplies appropriate receipts or documentation.  
(c) Vendor shall comply with Client’s travel and expense policies as communicated in writing prior to incurring any reimbursable expense.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) The Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, excise, value-added, or similar taxes imposed by any governmental authority in connection with the Services, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s income, property, or employment obligations.  
(b) Client shall be responsible for any such applicable taxes, unless Client provides a valid exemption certificate. Vendor shall itemize any such taxes on its invoices.

Except as expressly permitted under **Section 3.5** regarding disputed amounts, Client shall not withhold payment due to unrelated claims or disputes. Nothing in this Section limits Client’s rights to seek recovery or credits in accordance with **Article VIII** or **Article XII**.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **August 24, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
(a) Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement may be renewed for successive periods of twelve (12) months (each, a “**Renewal Term**”), upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date or the end of the then-current Renewal Term.  
(b) Any Renewal Term shall be subject to mutually agreed adjustments in Fees and Service Levels.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
(a) Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any SOW), for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor.  
(b) Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client, and only after completion of any critical maintenance activities reasonably identified by Client.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
   (i) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
   (ii) becomes the subject of any voluntary or involuntary insolvency, receivership, or bankruptcy proceeding which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or otherwise ceases to do business.  
(b) Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon notice if Vendor:  
   (i) commits a material violation of applicable law in connection with the Services;  
   (ii) materially fails to meet the Service Levels in a sustained or repeated manner, as further addressed in **Section 12.6**; or  
   (iii) engages in fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence that adversely affects Client.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the terminated Services and shall take all reasonable steps to ensure an orderly transition, including cooperating with Client and any replacement service provider.  
(b) Client shall pay Vendor for all undisputed Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, in accordance with **Article III**.  
(c) Vendor shall promptly return to Client (or, at Client’s written instruction, securely destroy) all Client property, including keys, access cards, documents, and equipment, subject to **Article V (Confidentiality)** and **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.  
(d) Vendor shall deliver to Client all completed and in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid or will pay, and all maintenance logs, records, and other documentation reasonably necessary for Client to continue maintenance of the Equipment.  
(e) Any license granted to Client under **Article VI** that is stated to be perpetual or that pertains to Deliverables fully paid for by Client shall survive termination.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive expiration or termination, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**, and Sections relating to payment obligations accrued as of termination, shall survive.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **General.**  
Vendor shall provide the Services in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in this **Article XII** and any SOW. The Service Levels are intended to ensure high availability, responsiveness, and reliability of maintenance and support.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment.**  
Where Vendor provides ongoing operational support or remote monitoring critical to the functioning of the Equipment, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain an uptime of at least **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding downtime due to: (a) scheduled maintenance agreed in advance with Client; (b) Client-caused issues; or (c) Force Majeure Events as described in **Article XV**.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
(a) Vendor shall meet the following target response times from the time of receipt of a service request:  

- **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Equipment failure causing complete outage of critical systems with significant impact on Client operations:  
  - Response Time: within **1 hour**  
  - Onsite Presence (if required): within **4 hours**  
  - Target Resolution Time: within **8 hours** or continuous work until resolved  

- **Severity 2 (High)** – Major degradation of performance or partial outage affecting important but not mission-critical systems:  
  - Response Time: within **4 hours**  
  - Onsite Presence (if required): within **8 hours**  
  - Target Resolution Time: within **24 hours**

- **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Non-critical issues, minor performance degradation, or non-urgent repairs:  
  - Response Time: within **1 Business Day**  
  - Target Resolution Time: within **5 Business Days**

- **Severity 4 (Low)** – Routine maintenance requests, cosmetic issues, or general inquiries:  
  - Response Time: within **2 Business Days**  
  - Target Resolution Time: within **10 Business Days**

(b) Vendor shall prioritize Severity 1 and 2 incidents over lower-severity incidents.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
(a) If Vendor fails to meet the Service Levels for response or resolution times in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows:  
   (i) For each Severity 1 incident where response or resolution targets are not met, a credit equal to **5%** of the monthly recurring Fee for the affected Site;  
   (ii) For each calendar month in which more than two (2) Severity 2 incidents fail to meet the resolution time, an additional credit equal to **3%** of the monthly recurring Fee for the affected Site;  
   (iii) For chronic failure (defined as three (3) consecutive months of Service Level breaches for the same Site), an additional monthly credit of **5%** of the recurring Fee for such Site until remediation.  
(b) Service credits shall be applied against subsequent invoices. In no event shall total service credits in a calendar year exceed ten percent (10%) of the total annual Fees for the affected Services.

12.5 **Exclusions from Service Levels.**  
Service Levels shall not apply to failures or delays caused by:  
(a) Client’s failure to provide required access or approvals;  
(b) Client’s modification or misuse of Equipment contrary to Vendor’s or manufacturer’s instructions;  
(c) issues with third-party networks or utilities beyond Vendor’s control; or  
(d) Force Majeure Events.

12.6 **Persistent Service Level Failures.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the Service Levels in a material and persistent manner (including chronic failure as defined in **Section 12.4(b)(iii)**), Client may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice and opportunity to cure, terminate the affected portion of the Services or the Agreement for cause pursuant to **Section 4.4(b)(ii)**.
