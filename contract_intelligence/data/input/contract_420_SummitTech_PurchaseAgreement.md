## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Summit Tech agrees to provide to Contoso, and Contoso agrees to purchase from Summit Tech, the Services and Deliverables described in this Article II and in the applicable SOWs. The Parties intend for Summit Tech to act as a primary technology solutions provider with respect to the infrastructure and services described herein.

2.2 **Core Deliverables.** Without limiting any specific SOW, the Deliverables contemplated under this Agreement may include, but are not limited to:

  2.2.1 **Hardware Procurement and Supply.** Summit Tech shall source, procure, and deliver to Contoso various server, networking, storage, and related hardware components (“**Hardware**”) as specified in an SOW, including but not limited to:
  
  - Rack-mounted servers with specified CPU, RAM, and storage configurations;  
  - Network switches, routers, and firewalls meeting performance and security standards established by Contoso;  
  - Storage arrays and backup appliances; and  
  - Necessary cables, racks, and peripheral equipment.

  2.2.2 **Software and Licensing.** Summit Tech shall provide licenses or sublicenses (as applicable) for software identified in the SOW, which may include:
  
  - Operating systems, virtualization platforms, and database software;  
  - Monitoring, logging, and security software;  
  - Management consoles and orchestration tools; and  
  - Any related client access licenses or user subscriptions.

  2.2.3 **Implementation and Integration Services.** Summit Tech shall perform Services to install, configure, and integrate the Hardware and software into Contoso’s existing environment, including:
  
  - Environmental and compatibility assessments;  
  - Installation of Hardware in Contoso’s data center(s) or hosting environment(s);  
  - Configuration of operating systems, hypervisors, and network settings in accordance with the Specifications;  
  - Integration with Contoso’s authentication, logging, monitoring, and backup systems; and  
  - Performance tuning and validation of system performance against agreed benchmarks.

  2.2.4 **Migration and Cutover Support.** Where specified in an SOW, Summit Tech shall provide migration planning and execution support, including:
  
  - Data migration strategy, test plans, and migration runbooks;  
  - Test migrations and validation, with documented test reports;  
  - Final cutover activities performed during agreed maintenance windows; and  
  - Rollback plans and execution if required.

  2.2.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Summit Tech shall provide training for Contoso’s designated personnel on the operation, administration, and maintenance of the Deliverables, which may include:
  
  - On-site or virtual training sessions;  
  - Training materials and documentation; and  
  - Knowledge transfer workshops at project milestones or completion.

2.3 **Managed Services and Support.** If specified in an applicable SOW, Summit Tech shall provide ongoing managed services, which may include:

  2.3.1 **Monitoring and Incident Management.** Continuous or scheduled monitoring of systems covered by this Agreement, with alerts and incident response as defined in Article XII and the relevant SOW.

  2.3.2 **Maintenance and Updates.** Application of patches, firmware updates, and minor version upgrades in accordance with vendor recommendations and Contoso’s maintenance policies.

  2.3.3 **Service Desk and Technical Support.** Provision of technical support during Business Hours (and, if applicable, 24x7 support for critical incidents) with defined response and resolution targets.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Summit Tech shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  
  (b) using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications;  
  (c) in accordance with the Specifications and applicable Service Levels; and  
  (d) in compliance with Applicable Law.

If Summit Tech fails to meet any performance standard or Service Level, Summit Tech shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, promptly re-perform the affected Services and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence, without prejudice to Contoso’s other rights and remedies.

2.5 **Project Management and Reporting.** For any significant project-based SOW, Summit Tech shall:

  2.5.1 Designate a project manager as the primary point of contact for Contoso.  
  2.5.2 Develop and maintain a project plan with milestones, dependencies, and resource allocations.  
  2.5.3 Provide regular status reports (e.g., weekly or bi-weekly), including progress against milestones, risk and issue logs, and upcoming activities.  
  2.5.4 Conduct periodic project review meetings with Contoso to discuss status, issues, and change requests.

2.6 **Timelines and Milestones.** Each SOW shall set forth timelines and key milestones, which may include:

  - Hardware delivery and acceptance dates;  
  - Installation and configuration completion dates;  
  - Testing and user acceptance testing (“**UAT**”) periods;  
  - Go-live dates and/or phased rollout schedules; and  
  - Transition-to-support dates.

Summit Tech shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet such timelines and milestones. In the event of any anticipated delay, Summit Tech shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, explain the cause of the delay, and propose a mitigation plan. If delays are caused by Summit Tech and materially impact Contoso’s operations, the Parties shall negotiate appropriate remedies, which may include service credits or schedule adjustments as set forth in Article XII.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.** Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  2.7.1 Summit Tech shall notify Contoso in writing when a Deliverable or milestone is ready for review (“**Delivery Notice**”).  
  2.7.2 Contoso shall have a reasonable period, not less than ten (10) Business Days following receipt of the Delivery Notice, to review and either accept the Deliverable in writing or provide a written list of deficiencies describing how it fails to conform to the Specifications.  
  2.7.3 If Contoso notifies Summit Tech of deficiencies, Summit Tech shall, at no additional cost, promptly correct the deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance.

  (a) designate a primary contact for project coordination;

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in effect until **June 25, 2025** (the “**Initial Term**” or “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for additional terms upon mutually agreed pricing and conditions. Any such renewal shall be documented by an amendment or new agreement executed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Given the low risk profile of this engagement, Contoso shall have the right to terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Summit Tech. Summit Tech may terminate for convenience only upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso and only with respect to SOWs not yet commenced, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they become due, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets, to the extent permitted by Applicable Law.

4.5 **Termination for Persistent Service Failures.** Without limiting Section 4.4, if Summit Tech fails to meet critical Service Levels (as defined in Article XII or an SOW) in three (3) or more months in any rolling six (6)-month period, Contoso may, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, terminate the affected SOW for cause if such failures are not cured and prevented from recurring within such notice period.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:

  4.6.1 Summit Tech shall cease performing the affected Services, except as necessary to perform any agreed transition services.  
  4.6.2 Contoso shall pay Summit Tech for (a) Services actually and satisfactorily performed through the effective date of termination, and (b) any non-cancellable third-party commitments incurred by Summit Tech on Contoso’s behalf, provided that Summit Tech used commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such commitments.  
  4.6.3 Any licenses granted to Contoso that are designated as perpetual shall survive termination, subject to continued compliance with applicable license terms.  
  4.6.4 Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.  
  4.6.5 The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination shall so survive, including, without limitation, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI, and Sections 3.4, 3.5, 4.6, and 15.3.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.** At Contoso’s request made prior to or within thirty (30) days after termination or expiration, Summit Tech shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days to enable an orderly transition of the Services to Contoso or a successor provider. Such services shall be provided at Summit Tech’s then-current T&M rates or as otherwise agreed, except where termination is due to Summit Tech’s material breach, in which case reasonable transition assistance shall be provided at no additional cost for a mutually agreed period, not less than thirty (30) days.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.** For any managed services or hosted solutions provided under this Agreement, Summit Tech shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the covered systems are available **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows agreed in advance with Contoso.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Summit Tech shall:

  (a) provide Contoso with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance that may affect system availability;  
  (b) perform such maintenance during agreed maintenance windows, preferably outside Contoso’s standard business hours; and  
  (c) minimize disruption to Contoso’s operations.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.** Summit Tech shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times for support requests, as applicable:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical)** – System or service outage materially impacting Contoso’s core business:  
    - Initial response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
    - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours

  - **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant impairment with no practical workaround:  
    - Initial response: within 4 Business Hours  
    - Target resolution or workaround: within 1 Business Day

  - **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact, workaround available:  
    - Initial response: within 1 Business Day  
    - Target resolution: within 5 Business Days

  - **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, requests for information:  
    - Initial response: within 2 Business Days  
    - Target resolution: as agreed case-by-case.

12.4 **Service Credits.** If Summit Tech fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in a given calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly service fee for the affected service;  
  - Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly service fee;  
  - Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly service fee.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices for the same service. Service credits are Contoso’s exclusive remedy for failure to meet uptime commitments, without prejudice to Contoso’s rights to terminate under Section 4.5 for persistent failures.

12.5 **Exclusions.** Uptime calculations shall exclude:

  (a) scheduled maintenance performed in accordance with Section 12.2;  
  (b) downtime caused by Contoso’s acts or omissions, or by third-party systems outside Summit Tech’s reasonable control; and  
  (c) Force Majeure Events as defined in Article XV.

12.6 **SLA Reporting.** Summit Tech shall provide Contoso with monthly reports detailing uptime performance, incident statistics, and response/resolution times. At Contoso’s request, Summit Tech shall participate in regular service review meetings to discuss SLA performance and continuous improvement measures.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.** The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event on its performance; and  
  (c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension and Termination.** Performance of the affected obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than thirty (30) consecutive days and materially affects performance, either Party may terminate the affected SOW upon written notice, without liability (other than obligations accrued prior to the Force Majeure Event and any payment for work performed prior to termination).
