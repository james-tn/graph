## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF LEASE AND SERVICES

2.1 **Grant of Lease.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Vendor hereby leases to Client and Client hereby takes from Vendor the Premises for the Term. The Lease includes the non‑exclusive right (together with Vendor, Vendor’s other tenants and clients, and their respective invitees) to use common areas, lifts, stairwells, corridors, restrooms, entrances, loading areas, and other shared facilities (collectively, “**Common Areas**”) as designated by Vendor from time to time, subject always to Vendor’s rules, regulations, and reasonable directions.

2.2 **Permitted Use.** Client shall use the Premises solely for general commercial office use, corporate administration, technology operations, and associated activities, including the operation of data processing equipment and light technical installations, and for no other purpose without Vendor’s prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed, where the proposed use is compatible with building regulations and insurance requirements). Client shall not use the Premises for any illegal, immoral, or hazardous activities, nor for residential, manufacturing, or heavy industrial purposes.

2.3 **Services Provided.** During the Term, Vendor shall provide, or cause to be provided, the following Services (collectively, the “**Core Services**”):

2.3.1 **Premises Access and Security.**  
(a) 24x7 secure access to the Premises for authorised personnel of Client;  
(b) Physical access control systems, visitor registration procedures, and security patrols consistent with good industry practice; and  
(c) CCTV monitoring of Common Areas and building entrances.

2.3.2 **Facilities Management and Maintenance.**  
(a) Routine maintenance, inspection, and repair of structural elements, roof, foundations, external walls, common mechanical and electrical systems, and central HVAC;  
(b) Cleaning of Common Areas and exterior glazing;  
(c) Waste disposal arrangements for common waste streams; and  
(d) Landscaping and external grounds maintenance.

2.3.3 **Building Services and Utilities.**  
(a) Provision of electrical power capacity as specified in Schedule 1, with redundancy as set out in Article XII;  
(b) Provision of chilled water and/or air conditioning sufficient for typical office usage, not accounting for extraordinary heat loads from specialised equipment unless expressly agreed;  
(c) Basic water and sewer services; and  
(d) Fire detection and suppression systems as required by applicable laws and standards.

2.3.4 **IT and Network Services.**  
(a) Access to Vendor‑managed network infrastructure, including structured cabling, patch panels, and equipment racks as specified in Schedule 2;  
(b) Internet connectivity services with minimum committed bandwidth and uptime commitments set out in Section 12.2; and  
(c) Optional managed services such as firewall management, network monitoring, and remote hands support, where ordered by Client under a Change Order.

2.3.5 **Support and Helpdesk Services.**  
(a) A helpdesk reachable via email and telephone, available during Business Hours (09:00–18:00 UK time, Monday–Friday, excluding UK public holidays) for routine support requests and incident logging;  
(b) Incident response and escalation in accordance with the response times specified in Section 12.3; and  
(c) Coordination of planned maintenance windows and notification to Client in accordance with Section 12.5.

2.4 **Client Obligations in Relation to Premises.** Client shall:

2.4.1 Keep the interior of the Premises clean, tidy, and in good repair (fair wear and tear excepted), and promptly notify Vendor of any damage, defect, or need for repair affecting the Premises or the building systems serving the Premises.

2.4.2 Not make any structural alterations to the Premises, nor install major fixtures, cabling, or equipment that may impact building infrastructure or fire safety, without Vendor’s prior written consent (which may be conditioned upon Client obtaining approvals, using approved contractors, and complying with building standards).

2.4.3 Comply with all reasonable building rules and regulations issued by Vendor from time to time, provided that Vendor shall provide reasonable written notice of any material changes that could materially affect Client’s operations.

2.4.4 Ensure that its employees, contractors, and visitors adhere to all security procedures, health and safety regulations, and access rules notified by Vendor.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

2.5.1 In a timely, professional, and workmanlike manner in accordance with the Work Standards;  

2.5.2 In compliance with all applicable laws, building codes, fire safety regulations, and health and safety legislation; and  

2.5.3 In a manner designed to ensure that the Service Levels set out in Article XII are achieved or exceeded.

2.6 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

2.6.1 **Premises Readiness.** Vendor shall ensure that the Premises are made available for Client’s beneficial occupation no later than the Commencement Date. Prior to such date, Vendor shall complete any agreed fit‑out works set forth in Schedule 3, including base lighting, raised flooring, and standard office finishes.

2.6.2 **Installation of IT and Network Infrastructure.** Vendor shall, by no later than thirty (30) days after the Commencement Date, provision the core network connectivity, rack space, and necessary cabling described in Schedule 2. Any delays arising from Client’s failure to provide required information, approvals, or equipment shall extend the timeline accordingly.

2.6.3 **Acceptance.** Within ten (10) Business Days after Vendor notifies Client that the Premises and initial Services are ready for use, Client shall conduct a walk‑through and functional verification. If Client reasonably identifies any material deficiencies in meeting the agreed specifications, Client shall notify Vendor in writing detailing such deficiencies. Vendor shall promptly remedy such deficiencies within a reasonable period. In the absence of such written notice within the ten (10) Business Day period, the Premises and initial Services shall be deemed accepted as of the Commencement Date.

2.7 **Additional Services.** Client may, from time to time, request additional services (such as dedicated security guards, extended cleaning, enhanced redundancy, or bespoke fit‑out works). Any such services shall be subject to the Change Management procedure set out in Article XIII and shall be documented in a Change Order specifying scope, fees, and any impact on the Service Levels.

2.8 **Vendor’s Control of Building.** Notwithstanding the Lease granted herein, Vendor retains full control, management, and operation of the building in which the Premises are located. Vendor may from time to time reconfigure Common Areas, implement security upgrades, perform repairs, or undertake other works, provided that Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to minimise disruption to Client’s business and shall comply with the notice obligations in Section 12.5.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value and Fee Structure.** The Parties acknowledge that the estimated Contract Value for the Term is **US$2,400,651**, based on the fee components described in this Article III. The actual amounts payable by Client may vary based on usage, Change Orders, and other provisions of this Agreement.

3.2 **Base Rent.** Client shall pay to Vendor an annual base rent (the “**Base Rent**”) of **US$520,000** for the Premises, payable in equal quarterly instalments in advance. The Base Rent shall be subject to an annual review on each anniversary of the Effective Date, at which time Vendor may increase the Base Rent by the lesser of: (a) 4% per annum; or (b) the percentage increase in the UK Consumer Price Index (CPI) over the preceding twelve (12) months.

3.3 **Service Charges and Operating Expenses.**  

3.3.1 In addition to Base Rent, Client shall pay a service charge (“**Service Charge**”) estimated at **US$120,000** per annum, representing Client’s proportionate share of building operating expenses, including but not limited to: common utilities, building insurance, security, cleaning of Common Areas, maintenance contracts, and management fees.

3.3.2 Vendor shall provide an annual reconciliation of actual operating expenses versus Service Charge estimates and shall either invoice Client for any shortfall or credit Client for any overpayment in the following billing period.

3.4 **IT and Network Service Fees.** Fees for IT and network services (such as dedicated bandwidth, rack space, managed firewall, network monitoring, etc.) shall be set forth in Schedule 2. Unless otherwise stated, such fees are billed monthly in arrears based on the agreed capacity or usage tiers.

3.5 **One‑Time Charges.** Client shall pay Vendor the following one‑time charges:

3.5.1 **Initial Fit‑Out Contribution.** A fit‑out and setup contribution of **US$250,000**, payable fifty percent (50%) on the Effective Date and fifty percent (50%) within thirty (30) days after the Commencement Date.

3.5.2 **Installation Fees.** Any specific installation or configuration fees set out in Schedule 3 or approved via Change Order.

3.6 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**

3.6.1 Vendor shall issue invoices to Client for all amounts due under this Agreement. Unless expressly stated otherwise, all invoices shall be due and payable by Client within **sixty (60) days** following the date of the invoice (“**Net 60**” terms).

3.6.2 All payments shall be made in US dollars (USD) by electronic bank transfer to the account designated in writing by Vendor. Client shall be responsible for all bank charges, transfer fees, and similar charges incurred in making payment to Vendor, other than those imposed by Vendor’s own bank.

3.6.3 Client shall not be entitled to set off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from payments due to Vendor except as may be required by law or expressly permitted in this Agreement.

3.7 **Taxes.** All fees and charges are exclusive of any applicable Taxes. Client shall be responsible for and shall pay all applicable Taxes arising from or relating to the payments made under this Agreement, except for Taxes based on Vendor’s net income. If Client is required by law to withhold any Taxes from payments to Vendor, Client shall provide reasonable documentation and cooperate with Vendor to seek any available relief.

3.8 **Late Payment.**

3.8.1 Any undisputed amounts not received by Vendor by the due date shall accrue interest at a rate of **4% per annum above the Bank of England base rate**, calculated daily and compounded monthly, from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.8.2 If any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after the due date, Vendor may, after giving Client written notice and a further ten (10) Business Day cure period, suspend the provision of Services (including access to certain non‑critical facilities and network services) until all outstanding amounts and applicable interest are paid in full. Suspension of Services in accordance with this Section shall not be deemed a breach of this Agreement by Vendor.

3.9 **Disputed Invoices.** If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of the invoice date, specifying the reasons for the dispute. Client shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.6. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve any disputed amounts promptly. Any amounts ultimately determined to be payable shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days of resolution, together with any applicable interest.

3.10 **Reimbursable Expenses.** Where Vendor incurs out‑of‑pocket expenses at Client’s written request (e.g., special equipment procurement, additional third‑party services), Client shall reimburse Vendor at cost plus a ten percent (10%) administrative markup, provided such expenses were pre‑approved in writing by Client (email sufficing).

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **9 January 2029** (the “**Initial Term**”), unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Article IV. Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of twelve (12) months each (each a “**Renewal Term**”), unless either Party provides written notice of non‑renewal at least twelve (12) months prior to the end of the Initial Term or the then‑current Renewal Term.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience by Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience at any time upon providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to Client.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing at least one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Vendor, provided that:

4.3.1 Client shall remain liable for all fees and charges accrued up to the effective date of termination; and  

4.3.2 Client shall pay an early termination fee equal to six (6) months of the average monthly Total Fees (Base Rent plus Service Charges plus average IT and network fees) payable in the twelve (12) months immediately prior to the termination notice (or, if less than twelve (12) months have elapsed, the average since Commencement Date).

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause with immediate effect by written notice if:

4.4.1 The other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement which, if capable of remedy, is not remedied within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach and requiring it to be remedied; or  

4.4.2 The other Party becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation (other than for the purposes of solvent reorganisation), has a receiver or administrator appointed over any of its assets, enters into any composition or arrangement with its creditors, or suffers any similar event under the laws of its jurisdiction.

4.5 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice if a Force Majeure Event affecting the other Party continues for a continuous period of more than ninety (90) days and substantially prevents performance of the obligations under this Agreement, in accordance with Article XV.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiry.** Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement for any reason:

4.6.1 Client shall promptly vacate the Premises and return to Vendor vacant possession in substantially the same condition as at the Commencement Date, fair wear and tear excepted, and remove any of Client’s property, equipment, and signage, at Client’s cost.

4.6.2 Client shall pay all outstanding amounts due to Vendor up to the effective date of termination, including any early termination fees, interest, and reimbursable expenses.

4.6.3 Vendor shall cease provision of Services, save for any transitional services agreed in writing by the Parties, which shall be subject to mutually agreed fees and terms.

4.6.4 Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and any other provision which by its nature is intended to survive termination or expiry shall survive and remain in full force and effect.

4.7 **No Automatic Renewal of Sub‑Tenancies.** Client shall not create any sub‑tenancy or occupancy rights that extend beyond the Term. Any such agreement with third parties shall terminate no later than the effective date of termination or expiry of this Agreement.

12.2.2 Network connectivity (including Internet access provided by Vendor) is available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month; and  

12.2.3 Building access and core facility services (e.g., security, access control) are available at least **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times for incidents:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of Services or substantial unavailability of Premises.  
  - Response: within 30 minutes  
  - Target Resolution: within 4 hours

- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation of Services affecting multiple users.  
  - Response: within 1 hour  
  - Target Resolution: within 8 hours

- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non‑critical issues affecting limited functions.  
  - Response: within 4 hours  
  - Target Resolution: within 24 hours

- **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues or service requests.  
  - Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution: within 3 Business Days

12.4 **Service Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitments in Section 12.2 in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to the following service credits:

12.4.1 For uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly IT and network service fees;  

12.4.2 For uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of such fees;  

12.4.3 For uptime below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of such fees.

Service credits shall be applied as a credit against the next invoice(s) and are Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for Service Level failures, subject always to Article IX (which includes uncapped liability).

12.5 **Planned Maintenance.** Vendor may schedule planned maintenance that may affect the Services or access to the Premises. Vendor shall:

12.5.1 Provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for maintenance expected to cause material disruption; and  

12.5.2 Use reasonable efforts to schedule maintenance outside Business Hours and to minimise any disruption to Client’s operations.

Planned maintenance properly notified under this Section shall not count as downtime for purposes of the uptime calculations.
