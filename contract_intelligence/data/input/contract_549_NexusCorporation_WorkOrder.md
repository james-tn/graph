## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of technology and professional services, including but not limited to:

  (a) **Managed Cloud Services.** Design, implementation, and ongoing management of cloud-based environments supporting Client’s enterprise applications and data, including infrastructure provisioning, configuration, monitoring, backup, and security management across agreed cloud platforms (e.g., Azure, AWS, or other specified platforms).

  (b) **Software Development and Integration Services.** Custom development of software components, APIs, microservices, integrations with third-party systems, and user interfaces as described in this Agreement, including requirements gathering, design, coding, unit testing, integration testing, and deployment support.

  (c) **Application Support and Maintenance.** Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 application support, bug fixes, minor enhancements, patch management, and release management for designated Client applications, subject to the Service Levels in **Article XII**.

  (d) **Consulting and Advisory Services.** Technology roadmap planning, architecture consulting, performance optimization, security assessments, and related activities as may be requested by Client and accepted by Vendor pursuant to the Change Management processes in **Article XIII**.

  (e) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Delivery of technical and user training sessions, documentation, and knowledge transfer workshops to Client personnel, in accordance with the training schedule to be mutually agreed by the Parties.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.1**, Vendor shall prepare and deliver to Client the following Deliverables (collectively, the **“Deliverables”**), which may be further detailed in schedules or Change Orders:

  (a) **Cloud Architecture Design Documents**, including high‑level and detailed architecture diagrams, network topology, security models, and capacity planning documents.

  (b) **Configuration and Deployment Scripts**, including Infrastructure-as-Code templates (e.g., Terraform, ARM templates, or other agreed tools), deployment scripts, and runbooks.

  (c) **Custom Software Components**, including source code, compiled binaries, API endpoints, configuration files, and related documentation.

  (d) **Integration Specifications and Interfaces**, including data mapping documents, interface specifications, and test cases for integrations with third-party or legacy systems.

  (e) **Operational Runbooks and Procedures**, including incident management procedures, escalation workflows, maintenance checklists, and periodic reporting templates.

  (f) **Training Materials**, including slide decks, manuals, quick reference guides, and recorded training sessions (if applicable).

Each Deliverable shall conform to the Specifications and acceptance criteria agreed by the Parties. Where acceptance criteria are not expressly specified, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted if Client does not reject them, in writing and with reasonable specificity as to deficiencies, within fifteen (15) Business Days of delivery.

2.3 **Project Phases and Timeline.**  
The Services shall be organized into the following phases, each with its own objectives and indicative timelines. The Parties acknowledge that specific dates may be refined by mutual written agreement:

  (a) **Phase 1 – Discovery and Planning (Approx. 60 days from Effective Date).** Vendor shall conduct workshops, interviews, and assessments to understand Client’s current environment, business requirements, and constraints. Deliverables in this phase include initial requirements documentation, risk assessments, and a detailed project plan.

  (b) **Phase 2 – Design and Prototyping (Approx. 90–120 days following Phase 1).** Vendor shall produce detailed architecture designs, prototypes, and proof-of-concept implementations. Client shall provide timely feedback and approvals. Any changes to scope identified during this phase will be managed via **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

  (c) **Phase 3 – Implementation and Migration (Approx. 6–12 months following Phase 2).** Vendor shall implement the agreed solutions, configure environments, develop custom components, and migrate workloads as per the agreed migration plan. Periodic status reports shall be provided to Client at least bi-weekly.

  (d) **Phase 4 – Stabilization and Optimization (Approx. 3–6 months following Phase 3).** Vendor shall monitor performance, address defects, optimize configurations, and ensure the environment meets the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

  (e) **Phase 5 – Ongoing Operations and Support (Remainder of Term).** Vendor shall perform ongoing managed services, maintenance, and support through the Expiration Date, subject to the Service Levels and other provisions of this Agreement.

The Parties shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the above timelines; however, such timelines shall be extended to account for delays attributable to (i) Client’s failure to provide timely approvals, information, or access; (ii) Change Orders; or (iii) Force Majeure Events, as provided in **Article XV**.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**

  (a) Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and the Service Levels in **Article XII**.

  (b) Vendor shall ensure that its personnel assigned to the Services possess the appropriate skills, training, and experience to perform the Services competently.

  (c) Vendor shall comply with all Applicable Laws in the performance of the Services, including but not limited to Data Protection Laws as further detailed in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.

  (d) Vendor shall maintain appropriate internal quality assurance processes to verify that Deliverables conform to the Specifications and are free from material defects.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services and deliver the Deliverables in accordance with this Agreement is dependent on Client’s timely performance of the following responsibilities, without limitation:

  (a) Provide Vendor with reasonable access (virtual or physical, as applicable) to Client’s relevant systems, networks, facilities, and personnel, subject to Client’s security policies.

  (b) Designate a project manager or primary point of contact who shall be authorized to make decisions on behalf of Client and provide approvals required under this Agreement.

  (c) Provide accurate and complete information, requirements, and feedback in a timely manner.

  (d) Review and respond to Deliverables and other materials submitted by Vendor for approval within the periods set forth herein or otherwise agreed.

  (e) Obtain any third‑party consents or licenses necessary for Vendor to perform the Services on or in conjunction with Client’s systems or Third‑Party Products controlled by Client.

2.6 **Subcontractors.**  
Vendor may use subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that (i) Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were Vendor’s own acts and omissions; (ii) any subcontractors who may have access to Client’s Confidential Information or Personal Data are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those contained herein; and (iii) Vendor will not subcontract material portions of the Services outside the United States without Client’s prior written consent, not to be unreasonably withheld.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total not‑to‑exceed contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **Three Million Nine Hundred Seventy-One Thousand Four Hundred Seventy-Six U.S. Dollars (US$3,971,476)** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustments only as expressly provided for in this Agreement (including approved Change Orders).

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees payable by Client to Vendor shall consist of the following components:

  (a) **Fixed Implementation Fees.** An aggregate fixed fee of **US$1,650,000** for Phases 1–3 (Discovery & Planning, Design & Prototyping, and Implementation & Migration), invoiced in accordance with **Section 3.3(a)**.

  (b) **Managed Services Fees.** A recurring monthly fee of **US$45,000** for managed services, operations, and support during Phases 4 and 5, commencing upon successful completion of Phase 3 and Client’s acceptance thereof. Assuming a 48‑month managed services period, the projected managed services total is **US$2,160,000** (subject to actual commencement date and any Change Orders).

  (c) **Contingency and Miscellaneous Services.** A contingency budget of **US$161,476** reserved for additional consulting, development, or support services to be performed only upon mutual agreement and issuance of a Change Order.

3.3 **Invoicing Schedule.**

  (a) **Fixed Implementation Fees.** Vendor shall invoice Client for the fixed implementation fees as follows:
  
   - Twenty percent (20%) of US$1,650,000 (US$330,000) upon Effective Date;
   - Thirty percent (30%) (US$495,000) upon completion of Phase 1 and acceptance of the corresponding Deliverables;
   - Thirty percent (30%) (US$495,000) upon completion of Phase 2 and acceptance of the corresponding Deliverables;
   - Twenty percent (20%) (US$330,000) upon completion of Phase 3 and acceptance of the corresponding Deliverables.

  (b) **Managed Services Fees.** Vendor shall invoice the monthly managed services fees in advance on the first Business Day of each calendar month during the period in which managed services are provided.

  (c) **Contingency and Miscellaneous Services.** Vendor shall invoice for any contingency and miscellaneous services upon completion of such services, in accordance with the applicable Change Order.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
Client shall pay all undisputed amounts due under this Agreement within **forty‑five (45) days** from the date of Vendor’s invoice (**“Net 45”**). Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method to the account specified on Vendor’s invoice.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, compounded monthly, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full. Vendor shall be entitled to suspend performance of the Services, following at least ten (10) Business Days’ written notice to Client, if any undisputed amount remains unpaid more than thirty (30) days after the due date, provided that Vendor resumes the Services promptly upon receipt of payment in full.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client disputes any portion of any invoice in good faith, Client shall provide written notice to Vendor within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, identifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Client shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with **Section 3.4**, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute as promptly as reasonably practicable in accordance with **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**. No interest shall accrue on disputed amounts while the dispute is being resolved.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value‑added, goods and services, or other similar taxes, duties, or assessments imposed by any governmental authority (collectively, **“Taxes”**), except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees. Client shall be responsible for all Taxes associated with the Services and Deliverables, and Vendor shall itemize such Taxes on its invoices where practicable. If Client is exempt from certain Taxes, Client shall provide Vendor with a valid exemption certificate.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Vendor shall be responsible for its own ordinary business expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, except that Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre‑approved travel and out‑of‑pocket expenses (such as airfare, lodging, and ground transportation) incurred at Client’s written request or in connection with on‑site services at Client’s facilities. Vendor shall provide copies of receipts or other documentation reasonably required by Client for such reimbursable expenses. Reimbursable expenses shall be invoiced monthly in arrears and shall be payable in accordance with **Section 3.4**.

This Agreement may be extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties for additional renewal terms, each not to exceed two (2) years, on terms and conditions to be negotiated in good faith. 4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience, without cause, by providing not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. For any such termination initiated by Client, Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted through the effective date of termination, plus any non‑cancelable commitments incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf, provided that Vendor uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such commitments.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

  (a) **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof (or, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period).

  (b) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they become due, (ii) files a petition for bankruptcy or has a petition for bankruptcy filed against it that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**

  (a) Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Vendor shall cease work on all Services (except as necessary to wind down operations), and Client shall pay Vendor any outstanding and undisputed Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued through the effective date of termination or expiration.

  (b) Vendor shall deliver to Client, within thirty (30) days after the effective date of termination or expiration, all completed and in‑progress Deliverables for which Client has paid or will be obligated to pay under this Agreement, together with any Client Materials in Vendor’s possession or control.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties that by their nature should survive termination or expiration of this Agreement shall so survive, including but not limited to **Article III (with respect to outstanding payment obligations)**, **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)** (to the extent applicable post‑termination), **Article VII (Representations and Warranties)**, **Article VIII (Indemnification)**, **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)**, **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**, **Article XV (Force Majeure)**, and **Article XVI (General Provisions)**.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability (Uptime).**  
Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the managed services environment (excluding scheduled maintenance windows and excused downtime due to Client or third‑party causes or Force Majeure Events) is available no less than **99.9%** of each calendar month (the **“Uptime Commitment”**).

Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance during agreed maintenance windows, which shall, to the extent practicable, occur outside of Client’s core business hours. Vendor shall provide Client with at least seventy‑two (72) hours’ prior written notice of scheduled maintenance that is reasonably expected to impact availability.

12.3 **Incident Response Times.**  
Vendor shall respond to incidents reported by Client in accordance with the following target response times, measured from the time of Client’s initial notification:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Major outage or service unavailability affecting production; response within one (1) hour.
  - **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation or impairment of services; response within four (4) hours.
  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Functional issues with limited impact; response within one (1) Business Day.
  - **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, informational requests, or cosmetic defects; response within three (3) Business Days.

12.4 **Remedies for SLA Breaches – Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to Service Credits as follows:

  - Uptime ≥ 99.0% but < 99.9%: Credit equal to 5% of the monthly managed services Fees for the affected month.
  - Uptime ≥ 98.0% but < 99.0%: Credit equal to 10% of the monthly managed services Fees for the affected month.
  - Uptime < 98.0%: Credit equal to 20% of the monthly managed services Fees for the affected month.

Service Credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) for managed services. Service Credits shall not entitle Client to any refund or cash payment, except upon termination of this Agreement where no future invoices remain.

12.5 **Limitations.**  
Service Credits are Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for Vendor’s failure to meet the Service Levels set forth in this **Article XII**, subject to **Article IX**. Vendor shall have no liability under the Service Levels to the extent performance failures result from:

  (a) Client’s misuse or unauthorized modification of the Services;

  (b) failures or issues caused by Client’s systems, Third‑Party Products under Client’s control, or Client‑provided network connectivity; or

  (c) Force Majeure Events in accordance with **Article XV**.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
The obligations of the affected Party (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party continues to use commercially reasonable efforts to perform its obligations to the extent possible.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of material obligations under this Agreement, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party, without liability except for payment of Fees and expenses accrued through the effective date of termination.
