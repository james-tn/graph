## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES

### 2.1 General

2.1.1 During the Term, Vendor shall supply to Company, and Company shall purchase from Vendor, the Deliverables and Services described in this **Article II** and any applicable Schedules or Statements of Work. The Parties acknowledge and agree that this Agreement establishes the general terms and conditions applicable to all such purchases and that specific transactions shall be initiated by Company’s issuance of Purchase Orders referencing this Agreement.

2.1.2 Vendor shall perform all Services and provide all Deliverables in a diligent, professional, and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for high-end enterprise hardware and systems integration providers, and in accordance with the Specifications and Service Levels.

### 2.2 Deliverables

2.2.1 **Hardware Equipment.** Vendor shall manufacture, configure, and supply to Company a suite of industrial-grade, network-connected hardware devices (the “**Equipment**”), including but not limited to:

- Edge computing gateway units designed for high-throughput data collection and processing;  
- Rack-mounted server appliances pre-configured with Vendor’s proprietary system software;  
- Associated power supplies, cables, mounting brackets, and accessories; and  
- Redundant components for high availability, as specified in applicable Purchase Orders.

2.2.2 **Embedded Software and Firmware.** Each Equipment unit shall be delivered with Vendor’s proprietary embedded software and firmware (collectively, the “**Embedded Software**”) pre-installed. The Embedded Software shall include:

- System-level firmware enabling secure boot, device-level encryption, and remote update capability;  
- Device management agents that facilitate monitoring, diagnostics, and configuration; and  
- Communication modules supporting secure transport protocols and integration with Company’s network infrastructure.

2.2.3 **Configuration and Integration Deliverables.** Vendor shall provide configuration templates, scripts, and related documentation necessary for integration of the Equipment into Company’s existing operational environment. Such Deliverables shall include:

- Standard configuration images optimized for Company’s network topology;  
- Integration guides and playbooks for deployment in Company’s data centers and remote sites;  
- Test plans and validation scripts to verify proper operation and interoperability.

2.2.4 **Documentation.** Vendor shall provide user manuals, installation guides, maintenance instructions, and technical reference materials in electronic format (PDF or equivalent). Documentation shall be updated as necessary to reflect changes in the Deliverables and shall be made available to Company via a secure online portal or other mutually agreed method.

### 2.3 Services

2.3.1 **Implementation and Deployment Services.** Vendor shall provide implementation Services, which shall include:

(a) Reviewing Company’s existing infrastructure and deployment requirements;  
(b) Developing an implementation plan, including milestones, resource allocation, and dependencies;  
(c) Assisting with physical installation and logical configuration of the Equipment at Company’s designated locations; and  
(d) Supporting initial integration and validation testing with Company’s systems.

2.3.2 **Training Services.** Vendor shall provide training to Company’s designated personnel, which may include:

(a) On-site or remote training sessions addressing operation, monitoring, and maintenance of the Equipment;  
(b) Administrative and technical training for Company’s IT and operations staff; and  
(c) Access to Vendor’s standard training materials and recorded sessions.

2.3.3 **Maintenance and Support Services.** Vendor shall provide ongoing maintenance and support Services, comprising:

(a) Remote technical support during the hours defined in **Article XII**;  
(b) Software and firmware updates, bug fixes, and security patches;  
(c) Replacement of defective Equipment in accordance with the warranty provisions set forth in **Article VII**; and  
(d) Incident management and escalation in accordance with the Service Levels in **Article XII**.

### 2.4 Timelines and Milestones

2.4.1 **Initial Deployment Window.** The Parties anticipate that the initial deployment of Equipment under this Agreement shall substantially occur within twelve (12) months from the Effective Date, subject to timely issuance of Purchase Orders by Company and timely availability of Company resources necessary for deployment.

2.4.2 **Delivery and Lead Times.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the delivery dates specified in Purchase Orders. Unless otherwise agreed in a Purchase Order, standard lead time for Equipment shall be eight (8) to twelve (12) weeks from Vendor’s written acceptance of the relevant Purchase Order. The Parties acknowledge that lead time may be affected by supply chain constraints, and Company accepts that such constraints may extend delivery dates provided Vendor gives timely notice in accordance with **Section 15.2**.

2.4.3 **Performance Milestones.** The Parties may define specific project milestones (e.g., completion of pilot deployment, regional rollouts, full production deployment) in Statements of Work. Each milestone shall have associated acceptance criteria and, where applicable, related milestone payments in accordance with **Article III**. Vendor shall notify Company in writing when a milestone has been achieved and provide supporting documentation reasonably necessary for Company’s review.

### 2.5 Performance Standards

2.5.1 Vendor shall ensure that the Deliverables, when properly installed and used in accordance with the Documentation, conform in all material respects to the Specifications. Vendor shall remedy material non-conformities in accordance with the warranty provisions in **Article VII** and the Service Levels in **Article XII**.

2.5.2 Vendor shall use personnel with appropriate training, experience, and qualifications to perform the Services. Vendor shall be solely responsible for the direction, control, and compensation of its personnel and for any subcontractors engaged in accordance with **Section 16.7**.

2.5.3 Vendor shall perform the Services in compliance with Applicable Law and with reasonable care to avoid interfering with Company’s normal business operations. Company shall provide Vendor with reasonable access to its facilities, systems, and personnel as necessary for Vendor to perform the Services, subject to Company’s security and access policies.

### 2.6 Acceptance

2.6.1 Unless otherwise set forth in an applicable Statement of Work or Purchase Order, Company shall have fifteen (15) Business Days following receipt of each shipment of Equipment or completion of a milestone (the “**Acceptance Period**”) to inspect and test the Deliverables to determine whether they materially conform to the Specifications and applicable Documentation.

2.6.2 If Company determines in good faith that the Deliverables contain material defects or do not materially conform to the Specifications, Company shall notify Vendor in writing within the Acceptance Period (a “**Rejection Notice**”), specifying the non-conformities in reasonable detail. Vendor shall, at its own cost and expense, use commercially reasonable efforts to promptly correct such deficiencies by repair, replacement, or re-performance.

2.6.3 If Company fails to issue a Rejection Notice within the applicable Acceptance Period, the Deliverables shall be deemed accepted as of the expiration of such period, provided that such deemed acceptance shall not limit Company’s rights with respect to latent defects or warranties under **Article VII**.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **August 14, 2025** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**.

4.1.2 Any extension or renewal of the Term beyond the Expiration Date shall require a written amendment executed by both Parties pursuant to **Section 16.2**.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Given the high-risk nature of the engagement, either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.  

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Company, Company shall:

(a) Pay Vendor for all Deliverables accepted and Services properly performed up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) Pay any non-cancellable third-party commitments incurred by Vendor in reliance on this Agreement, provided Vendor uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such commitments; and  
(c) Pay any agreed early termination or demobilization fees expressly set forth in an applicable Statement of Work.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.3.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (d) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) All rights and licenses granted to Company under **Article VI** (other than any perpetual licenses expressly granted therein) shall terminate;  
(b) Company shall immediately cease use of any Deliverables and Services that are subject to term-limited licenses;  
(c) Company shall pay Vendor all undisputed Fees accrued but unpaid through the effective date of termination; and  
(d) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5**.

4.4.2 Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of any obligation that arose prior to the effective date of such termination or expiration, including any payment obligations, indemnification obligations under **Article VIII**, and any other obligations that expressly or by their nature survive termination (see **Section 16.10**).

15.1.1 A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, riots, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), epidemics, pandemics, governmental actions, embargoes, fires, floods, earthquakes, and widespread internet or telecommunications outages.

### 15.2 Notice

15.2.1 The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of such event, describing its nature, expected duration, and anticipated impact on the Party’s performance under this Agreement, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event.

### 15.3 Suspension

15.3.1 Performance of the affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, to the extent such obligations are affected. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to develop a plan for resuming performance as soon as reasonably practicable after the Force Majeure Event ceases.

### 15.4 Termination for Extended Force Majeure

15.4.1 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially impacts the Parties’ ability to perform their obligations, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice.
