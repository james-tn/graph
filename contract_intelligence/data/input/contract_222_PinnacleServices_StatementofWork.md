## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Overview.** The Vendor shall provide to the Client a comprehensive suite of professional services, implementation services, and managed support services in relation to the Client’s enterprise business systems, including but not limited to customer relationship management (CRM), enterprise resource planning (ERP), data integration, and infrastructure monitoring. The Services are designed to support the Client’s digital transformation and operational excellence objectives for the Term of this Agreement.

2.2 **Service Components.** The Services shall, at a minimum, comprise the following components:

2.2.1 **Discovery and Assessment.**  
(a) Conduct an initial discovery phase to review the Client’s current IT landscape, business processes, and key pain points.  
(b) Perform stakeholder interviews with up to ten (10) designated Client personnel to document current-state workflows and requirements.  
(c) Produce a **Discovery and Assessment Report** summarizing findings, gaps, risks, and recommended remediation or optimization initiatives.  
(d) Deliver a prioritized roadmap proposing phases, timelines, and resource estimates for implementation.

2.2.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.**  
(a) Develop a detailed solution architecture for the Client’s target-state environment, including application architecture, integration architecture, and high-level data flows.  
(b) Prepare detailed design documents, including functional specifications, technical design specifications, and interface control documents for up to five (5) core systems (e.g., CRM, ERP, Data Warehouse, Customer Portal, and Identity Management).  
(c) Conduct up to three (3) design review workshops with the Client to validate and refine the proposed architecture.  
(d) Obtain written sign-off from the Client on the final design documents, which shall form part of the Deliverables.

2.2.3 **Implementation and Configuration Services.**  
(a) Configure and customize the Client’s selected platforms (for example, but not limited to, Microsoft Dynamics 365, SAP S/4HANA, or equivalent) in accordance with the approved design documents.  
(b) Develop up to twenty (20) integration workflows between the Client’s core systems using the Vendor’s integration tools or third-party middleware, as agreed in writing.  
(c) Create or modify up to thirty (30) reports and dashboards to support operational and management reporting requirements.  
(d) Implement role-based access control and security configurations in accordance with the Client’s policies and applicable Data Protection Laws.  
(e) Execute system, integration, and user acceptance testing (UAT) in collaboration with the Client, including preparation of test plans, test scripts, and defect logs.

2.2.4 **Data Migration Services.**  
(a) Assess the Client’s legacy data quality and provide recommendations for data cleansing.  
(b) Design and implement ETL (extract-transform-load) processes to migrate data from identified legacy systems into the target platforms.  
(c) Perform up to two (2) full migration cycles (test and production) for in-scope data entities, as agreed with the Client.  
(d) Provide a Data Migration Summary Report, including migration statistics, reconciliation results, and any residual data quality considerations.

2.2.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
(a) Develop end-user training materials, including user guides, quick reference sheets, and video tutorials (where agreed) for key modules.  
(b) Conduct up to six (6) instructor-led training sessions (on-site or remote) for end-users and administrators, each session not exceeding half a day, unless otherwise agreed in writing.  
(c) Provide an administrative handover pack, including system configuration documentation, runbooks, and operational procedures, to enable the Client to effectively manage the solution.

2.2.6 **Managed Support and Maintenance Services.**  
(a) Provide ongoing application support and incident management services during Business Hours (unless enhanced support hours are separately agreed in writing) for the systems and integrations implemented under this Agreement.  
(b) Monitor system availability and performance for designated in-scope systems, in accordance with the Service Levels set out in Article XII.  
(c) Manage incidents and service requests through a centralized service desk, including categorization, prioritization, and escalation.  
(d) Provide regular maintenance activities, including patch management, minor configuration changes, and performance tuning, subject to agreed change control procedures.  
(e) Issue monthly Service Performance Reports summarizing incidents, resolutions, root cause analyses for high-severity incidents, and SLA performance.

2.3 **Project Management and Governance.**  
2.3.1 The Vendor shall assign a qualified **Project Manager** who will be responsible for day-to-day project coordination, status reporting, and issue resolution.  
2.3.2 The Client shall appoint a **Project Sponsor** and a **Project Lead** to act as primary contacts, provide timely decisions, and facilitate access to relevant Client resources.  
2.3.3 The Parties shall hold regular governance meetings (not less than monthly during implementation phases and quarterly during steady-state support) to review progress, risks, issues, and upcoming activities.

2.4 **Milestones and High-Level Timelines.**  
The Parties agree to the following high-level Milestones and indicative timelines, subject to adjustment through the Change Management process under Article XIII:

- **Milestone 1 – Discovery and Assessment Completed:** within sixty (60) days from the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 2 – Solution Design and Architecture Sign-Off:** within one hundred twenty (120) days from the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 3 – Core Implementation and Initial Integrations Completed:** within nine (9) months from the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 4 – Data Migration and Go-Live:** within twelve (12) months from the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 5 – Transition to Steady-State Support:** upon completion of at least sixty (60) days of post go-live hypercare.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
2.5.1 The Vendor shall perform the Services with all due skill, care, and diligence, in a professional and workmanlike manner, and in accordance with (a) generally accepted industry standards, (b) the Service Levels in Article XII, and (c) Applicable Law.  
2.5.2 The Deliverables shall conform in all material respects to the specifications and requirements set out in this Agreement, and the Vendor shall promptly correct any non-conformities at its own cost, subject to the warranty and limitation provisions in Articles VII and IX.  
2.5.3 Time shall not be of the essence except where explicitly stated; however, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet agreed target dates and Milestones, and shall promptly notify the Client in writing of any known or anticipated delays, their causes, and proposed remediation.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
In addition to any other responsibilities specified in this Agreement, the Client shall:  
(a) Provide timely access to relevant systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably required for the Vendor to perform the Services;  
(b) Ensure that all Client Data provided to the Vendor is accurate and complete to the best of the Client’s knowledge;  
(c) Review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables and Change Requests within a reasonable timeframe, not to exceed ten (10) Business Days unless otherwise agreed; and

2.7 **Out-of-Scope Services.**

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and such breach (if remediable) is not cured within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach.  
4.3.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters into administration or liquidation, or is subject to any analogous insolvency-related proceedings under Applicable Law.

Without limiting Section 4.3, the Client may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect if the Vendor suffers a material data breach or persistent Service Level failures that materially compromise the security or integrity of Client Data and fails to implement corrective measures to the Client’s reasonable satisfaction within a timeframe agreed in writing, acting reasonably.

4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) The Vendor shall cease performing the Services (except to the extent reasonably requested by the Client for transition assistance);  
(b) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information and Client Data in accordance with Article V and Article XI; and

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For the managed support and monitoring Services described in Section 2.2.6, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable endeavours to ensure that in-scope systems are available at least **99.9% of the time** in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and agreed downtime.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
12.2.1 The Vendor may schedule maintenance windows during which Services may be unavailable, provided that the Vendor:  
(a) Gives the Client at least three (3) Business Days’ prior notice of any Scheduled Maintenance that is reasonably expected to impact Service availability; and  
(b) Schedules such maintenance outside of the Client’s core business hours where reasonably practicable.

12.3 **Incident Management and Response Times.**  
12.3.1 The Vendor shall classify incidents in accordance with the following severity levels:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or major impact on business-critical functionality with no available workaround.  
- **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of service with limited workaround; business operations are affected but not halted.  
- **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial loss of non-critical functionality; workaround is available.  
- **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, requests for information, or cosmetic defects.

12.3.2 The Vendor shall meet the following target response times (measured during Business Hours unless otherwise agreed):

- Severity 1: Initial response within **1 hour**, status updates every 2 hours until resolution or workaround.  
- Severity 2: Initial response within **4 hours**, status updates daily until resolution.  
- Severity 3: Initial response within **1 Business Day**.  
- Severity 4: Initial response within **2 Business Days**.

12.4 **Resolution Targets.**  
Resolution times shall depend on complexity and root cause; however, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve:

- Severity 1 incidents within **8 Business Hours**;  
- Severity 2 incidents within **2 Business Days**;  
- Severity 3 incidents within **5 Business Days**; and  
- Severity 4 incidents within the next scheduled release or maintenance window.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
12.5.1 If the Vendor fails to meet the monthly availability target of 99.9% (excluding permitted downtime) or repeatedly fails to meet response/resolution targets for Severity 1 and 2 incidents, the Client shall be entitled to **service credits** as follows:

- Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: service credit equal to 3% of the monthly Support Fees.  
- Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly Support Fees.  
- Availability below 98.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly Support Fees.

12.5.2 Service credits shall be applied as a deduction from the next invoice for Support Fees. Service credits are the Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the Service Levels, without prejudice to the Client’s rights to terminate under Article IV and to claim other remedies for material breach.

12.6 **Reporting.**  
The Vendor shall provide monthly Service Performance Reports detailing availability, incident statistics, SLA compliance, and any root cause analyses for Severity 1 incidents.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall:

15.2.1 Promptly notify the other Party in writing upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event that is likely to prevent or delay its performance;

15.2.2 Use reasonable endeavours to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and

15.2.3 Keep the other Party reasonably informed of the status and anticipated duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent they are affected by the Force Majeure Event, and the time for performance shall be extended accordingly.

15.4 **Termination for Prolonged Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period of more than ninety (90) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party, without liability, save for payment of Fees due and payable for Services performed prior to the effective date of termination.
