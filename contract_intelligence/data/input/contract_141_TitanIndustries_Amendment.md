# AMENDMENT TO SOFTWARE LICENSE AGREEMENT  
**Contract Reference Number: AMD-TIT-202404-141**

This AMENDMENT TO SOFTWARE LICENSE AGREEMENT (this “**Amendment**”), reference number **AMD-TIT-202404-141**, is made and entered into as of **July 14, 2024** (the “**Amendment Effective Date**”), by and between:

- **Contoso Enterprises, Inc.**, a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of Washington, with its principal place of business at 1200 Fifth Avenue, Suite 2400, Seattle, WA 98101 (“**Contoso**”, the “**Client**” or the “**Company**”); and  
- **Titan Industries, LLC**, a limited liability company organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware, with its principal place of business at 300 Titan Plaza, Floor 10, Bellevue, WA 98004 (“**Titan**”, the “**Vendor**” or the “**Service Provider**”).

Contoso and Titan may be referred to herein individually as a “**Party**” and collectively as the “**Parties**.”

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties entered into that certain **Software License Agreement** dated **April 10, 2024**, reference number **SLA‑TIT‑202404‑088**, internal identifier **contract_088** (the “Software License Agreement” or the “Parent Agreement”), pursuant to which Titan licenses to Contoso the “TitanCore Enterprise Suite” software and provides related implementation, configuration, integration, hosting, and Maintenance and Support Services;

WHEREAS, the Parent Agreement, as defined in Section 1.2 therein, includes all exhibits, schedules, and Statements of Work, and may be amended from time to time in accordance with **Article XVI (General Provisions)**, specifically **Section 16.2 (Amendments)**, which requires that any amendment be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties;

WHEREAS, under **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties acknowledged a total Contract Value for the Initial Term in the amount of **US $1,945,996**, and established payment terms of **Net 30** in **Section 3.5 (Payment Terms)**;

WHEREAS, under **Article IV (Term and Termination)** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties agreed to an Initial Term ending **April 15, 2026**, and provided for renewal upon mutual written agreement;

WHEREAS, the Parties now desire (i) to expand the scope of the Software and Services originally described in **Article II (Scope of Services and License)** of the Parent Agreement, including additional modules, enhanced hosting and SLA commitments, and additional professional services, (ii) to extend certain commercial and temporal parameters beyond the Initial Term, (iii) to increase the overall contract value, and (iv) to vary certain financial and risk allocation provisions, all as more fully set forth in this Amendment;

WHEREAS, the Parties acknowledge that this Amendment **modifies, supplements, and partially supersedes** the Software License Agreement dated April 10, 2024 (Ref: **SLA‑TIT‑202404‑088**, identifier **contract_088**), but does not replace it in its entirety;

WHEREAS, in consideration of the expanded scope, higher service levels, and additional risk assumed by Titan, the Parties have agreed to a revised total **Contract Value** for the combined arrangements, and to modified **Payment Terms** of **Net 45**, and to certain **high‑risk, Vendor‑favorable provisions**, including broader indemnification by the Company and a modified liability regime;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, terms, and conditions contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties **hereby agree as follows**:

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.** This Amendment supplements **Article II (Scope of Services and License)** of the Parent Agreement (SLA‑TIT‑202404‑088 / contract_088). Unless expressly modified herein, all provisions of Article II of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect and shall apply to the Additional Modules, Enhanced SLA, and Expanded Services. In the event of a direct conflict between this Article II and Article II of the Parent Agreement with respect to the Additional Modules or Expanded Services, this Article II shall control.

2.2 **Additional License Grant.** Subject to the terms and conditions of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, including timely payment of all applicable fees, Titan hereby grants to the Company, and the Company hereby accepts, a **non‑exclusive, non‑transferable (except as permitted in Section 16.6 of the Parent Agreement), non‑sublicensable**, worldwide license during the Amendment Term to:

  (a) access and use the **Additional Modules** of the TitanCore Enterprise Suite in object code form only, solely for the internal business operations of the Company and its Affiliates, consistent with the license grant in **Section 2.1** of the Parent Agreement;  

  (b) permit Authorized Users (as defined in **Section 1.3** of the Parent Agreement) to access and use the Additional Modules up to the number and types of licenses specified in SOW‑Amendment‑01 and any subsequent SOWs executed under this Amendment; and  

  (c) use the associated Documentation (as defined in **Section 1.8** of the Parent Agreement) solely in connection with the authorized use of the Additional Modules.

2.3 **Description of Additional Modules.** The Additional Modules licensed under this Amendment shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **TitanCore Advanced Analytics Module**, providing enhanced data visualization, predictive analytics, and KPI dashboards that extend the “Analytics and reporting dashboards” functionality originally referenced in **Section 2.2(c)** of the Parent Agreement;  

  (b) **TitanCore Supply Chain Optimization Module**, enabling advanced planning, demand forecasting, supplier performance monitoring, and logistics optimization, complementing the “ERP core module” described in **Section 2.2(a)** of the Parent Agreement;  

  (c) **TitanCore Compliance & Audit Trails Module**, offering configurable compliance frameworks, automated audit logs, and regulatory reporting for industries identified by the Company, further enhancing the access controls referenced in **Section 2.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement; and  

  (d) any other modules or add‑ons mutually agreed and documented in SOW‑Amendment‑01, each of which shall be considered part of the “Software” as defined in **Section 1.18** of the Parent Agreement, subject to the ownership provisions in **Article VI** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

2.4 **Expanded Services and Project Milestones.** Titan shall provide **Expanded Services** in addition to those described in **Section 2.3 (Implementation and Configuration Services)** and **Section 2.9 (Project Timeline)** of the Parent Agreement. The Expanded Services shall include at least the following:

  (a) **Enterprise‑Wide Rollout Planning.** Titan shall conduct joint planning workshops with the Company’s designated project manager (as referenced in **Section 2.9** of the Parent Agreement) to define a phased rollout of the Additional Modules across the Company’s global operations. Deliverables include a detailed rollout plan, dependency mapping, and risk register.  

  (b) **Advanced Configuration and Customization.** Titan shall configure the Additional Modules to align with the Company’s specific business processes, including multi‑currency and multi‑entity financial structures, complex supply chain workflows, and compliance reporting for identified regulatory regimes. Customizations shall be documented as Deliverables, consistent with **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement, and any custom code shall be treated as “Titan Materials” under **Section 6.1** of the Parent Agreement unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing.  

  (c) **Integration Enhancements.** Building on the “integration APIs” and integration Services described in **Sections 2.2(e)** and **2.3(d)** of the Parent Agreement, Titan shall design, develop, and implement integrations between the Additional Modules and the Company’s designated third‑party systems (e.g., logistics platforms, tax engines, industry‑specific compliance tools), as specified in SOW‑Amendment‑01.  

  (d) **Data Migration and Enrichment.** Titan shall assist the Company with migration of historical and in‑flight transactional data relevant to the Additional Modules, including data mapping, cleansing, and enrichment. The Company acknowledges its continued responsibility under **Section 2.10(b)** of the Parent Agreement for the accuracy and completeness of Client Data.  

  (e) **Performance Tuning and Scalability Testing.** Titan shall perform load and scalability testing in the Hosted Environment to validate performance targets agreed by the Parties, and shall optimize configurations accordingly.  

  (f) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Titan shall provide enhanced training to the Company’s super‑users and administrators, including materials and workshops specifically focused on the Additional Modules and Enhanced SLA processes, in addition to any training referenced in **Sections 2.4(d)** and 2.3 of the Parent Agreement.  

  (g) **Project Milestones.** The Parties shall document in SOW‑Amendment‑01 a set of key Milestones, including (i) completion of solution design for Additional Modules, (ii) completion of configuration and integrations, (iii) completion of data migration and validation, (iv) completion of user acceptance testing for the Additional Modules, and (v) production go‑live and stabilization. Acceptance of Milestones shall follow the acceptance procedures for Deliverables in **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement, unless otherwise specified in SOW‑Amendment‑01.

2.5 **Service Levels and Hosting.** For the Additional Modules and any Hosted Environment explicitly covered by this Amendment:

  (a) Titan shall host the Additional Modules in accordance with **Section 2.5 (Hosting (If Applicable))** of the Parent Agreement and the Enhanced SLA in **Article XII** of this Amendment.  

  (b) The uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** of the Parent Agreement is superseded with respect to the Additional Modules by the Enhanced SLA uptime commitment in Section 12.1 of this Amendment.  

  (c) Titan’s monitoring, redundancy, and security obligations outlined in **Section 2.5** and **Article XI** of the Parent Agreement remain applicable and are supplemented by the more detailed security and data protection provisions in **Article XI** of this Amendment.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.** In addition to its obligations under **Section 2.10** of the Parent Agreement, the Company shall:

  (a) designate executive sponsors and functional leads for each major business area impacted by the Additional Modules;  

  (b) provide timely access to subject matter experts, data sets, and test cases necessary for Titan to perform the Expanded Services;  

  (c) validate and approve configuration decisions, data mappings, and integration specifications within the timeframes set forth in SOW‑Amendment‑01; and  

  (d) participate actively in change management and training initiatives to support internal adoption of the Additional Modules.

2.7 **Restrictions on Use.** The usage restrictions set forth in **Section 2.7 (Restrictions on Use)** of the Parent Agreement apply fully to the Additional Modules and any customizations or enhancements provided under this Amendment. For the avoidance of doubt, any attempt by the Company to provide the Additional Modules as a service bureau or to process third‑party data for commercial gain without Titan’s prior written consent shall be deemed a material breach of this Amendment and the Parent Agreement.

2.8 **Performance Standards.** Titan shall perform the Expanded Services with due skill, care, and diligence in accordance with **Section 2.8 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement. In light of the High‑Risk Allocation, the Parties agree that the Company’s sole and exclusive remedy for any failure to meet the performance standards for professional services under this Amendment shall remain re‑performance of the non‑conforming Services at no additional charge, as stated in Section 2.8 of the Parent Agreement, subject to the broader limitation/expansion of liability and indemnification provisions in **Articles VIII and IX** of this Amendment.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Amended Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that, as a result of the Additional Modules, Enhanced SLA, and Expanded Services contemplated by this Amendment, the total estimated fees payable under the Parent Agreement as amended for the applicable term are **US $3,785,369** (the “**Amended Contract Value**”), which supersedes the Contract Value of US $1,945,996 referenced in **Section 3.1 (Contract Value)** of the Parent Agreement. The Amended Contract Value includes, without limitation:

  (a) incremental license fees for the Additional Modules;  

  (b) incremental hosting fees associated with increased capacity and availability commitments;  

  (c) professional services fees for implementation, configuration, integration, training, and related Expanded Services; and  

  (d) additional maintenance and support fees for the Additional Modules.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** Fees under this Amendment shall be structured as follows:

  (a) **License Fees for Additional Modules.** Payable annually in advance, consistent with **Section 3.2** of the Parent Agreement, but at the rates and license metrics described in SOW‑Amendment‑01. Any increases in Authorized Users or additional module entitlements during the Amendment Term shall be prorated for the remainder of the then‑current license period and included in subsequent annual invoices.  

  (b) **Implementation and Professional Services Fees.** Billed on a blended time‑and‑materials basis or fixed‑fee basis, as specified in SOW‑Amendment‑01, consistent with **Section 3.3 (Professional Services Fees)** of the Parent Agreement. For fixed‑fee portions, Titan may invoice upon completion of Milestones; for time‑and‑materials, Titan may invoice monthly in arrears.  

  (c) **Maintenance and Support Fees.** For the Additional Modules, maintenance and support fees shall be billed annually in advance, as provided in **Section 3.4** of the Parent Agreement, and may be adjusted upon renewal of the Amendment Term in accordance with the percentage increase limitations stated therein, unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing.  

  (d) **Hosting Fees.** Titan shall charge hosting fees for the Hosted Environment supporting the Additional Modules based on resource consumption, uptime commitments, and data volumes, as specified in SOW‑Amendment‑01. Hosting fees shall be billed monthly in advance unless otherwise specified.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.** Without limiting the generality of Section 3.2:

  (a) **Initial License and Setup Fees.** A one‑time upfront fee, as specified in SOW‑Amendment‑01, shall be invoiced on or promptly after the Amendment Effective Date and shall be payable in accordance with the Revised Payment Terms (Net 45).  

  (b) **Milestone‑Based Payments.** For those Expanded Services structured on a milestone basis, Titan shall invoice upon the Company’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance pursuant to Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement) of each Milestone.  

  (c) **Annual Renewals.** License and maintenance fees for each subsequent annual period within the Amendment Term shall be invoiced at least thirty (30) days prior to the start of such period.

3.4 **Revised Payment Terms (Net 45).** Notwithstanding **Section 3.5 (Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement (Net 30), all invoices issued under or expressly referencing this Amendment shall be due and payable within **forty‑five (45) days** of the invoice date (“**Net 45**”). All payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method.

3.5 **Late Payment and Suspension Rights.** The provisions of **Section 3.6 (Late Payment)** of the Parent Agreement apply to this Amendment, except that Titan may suspend access to the Additional Modules or Expanded Services if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after the due date under the Revised Payment Terms. Titan shall resume Services promptly upon receipt of all past‑due amounts and any applicable interest.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.** The dispute mechanism in **Section 3.7 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to invoices issued under this Amendment. The Company shall pay all undisputed amounts and provide written notice and reasonable supporting documentation for any disputed amounts prior to the applicable due date.

3.7 **Taxes.** The tax provisions in **Section 3.8 (Taxes)** of the Parent Agreement apply fully to all fees under this Amendment. The Amended Contract Value is exclusive of Taxes, and the Company shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes, except those based on Titan’s net income, property, or employees.

3.8 **Expenses.** Reimbursement of Titan’s out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred in connection with on‑site performance of Expanded Services shall be governed by **Section 3.9 (Expenses)** of the Parent Agreement, including the requirement for the Company’s prior written approval for any individual expense exceeding US $500, and compliance with the Company’s travel policies provided in advance.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Amendment Term.** This Amendment shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date (July 14, 2024) and shall remain in effect until **February 1, 2027** (the “Amendment Term”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV. For clarity, the Amendment Term may extend beyond the **Initial Term** of the Parent Agreement, which ends on April 15, 2026, and the Parties intend that the Parent Agreement shall be deemed renewed or extended as necessary to give full effect to this Amendment through the Amendment Term, subject to Section 4.5 below.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.** The **Term** defined in **Section 4.1** of the Parent Agreement is hereby modified for purposes of this Amendment only, such that, with respect to the Additional Modules and Expanded Services, the Parties’ rights and obligations shall continue through the Amendment Term. To the extent required, the Parties agree that the Parent Agreement is hereby renewed or extended through at least February 1, 2027, solely to enable performance and enforcement of this Amendment.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Notwithstanding **Section 4.2 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent Agreement, which permits termination on sixty (60) days’ prior written notice, the Parties agree that, with respect to this Amendment and any SOW‑Amendment‑01:

  (a) Either Party may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.  

  (b) In the event of such termination by the Company for convenience, the Company shall pay Titan for (i) all fees for Services performed and Software licensed up to the effective date of termination, and (ii) any non‑cancellable expenses or commitments incurred by Titan, consistent with Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement, plus any early termination charges expressly stated in SOW‑Amendment‑01.  

  (c) Termination of this Amendment for convenience shall not, by itself, terminate the Parent Agreement with respect to the original scope unless expressly stated in the termination notice and accepted by Titan.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** The termination for cause provisions in **Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement apply to this Amendment. In addition, a material breach by the Company of its payment obligations under this Amendment, or of its license restrictions under Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement with respect to the Additional Modules, shall constitute grounds for termination of this Amendment for cause by Titan upon written notice if not cured within the applicable cure period (ten (10) days for non‑payment, thirty (30) days for other breaches).

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.** If the Parent Agreement is terminated or expires for any reason:

  (a) This Amendment shall automatically terminate on the effective date of such termination or expiration, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue certain obligations (e.g., payment of outstanding fees, data return, confidentiality) for a limited period.  

  (b) Upon such termination, all rights and licenses granted to the Company under this Amendment with respect to the Additional Modules shall immediately cease, consistent with **Section 4.4(a)** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (c) The Company shall cease all access to and use of the Additional Modules and associated Documentation and shall certify destruction or removal of all related Software components in accordance with **Section 4.4(b)** of the Parent Agreement.  

  (d) The Parties’ obligations regarding Confidential Information, data protection, and any accrued payment obligations shall survive in accordance with **Section 4.5 (Survival)** of the Parent Agreement and Section 4.7 of this Amendment.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of this Amendment Only.** If this Amendment is terminated in whole or in part (e.g., only with respect to certain Additional Modules) without terminating the Parent Agreement:

  (a) The Parent Agreement shall continue in full force and effect with respect to the remaining Software and Services as originally agreed.  

  (b) The Company shall remain obligated to pay any outstanding fees related to the terminated portion, as well as any early termination fees specified in SOW‑Amendment‑01.  

  (c) Titan’s obligations to provide hosting, maintenance, and support for the Additional Modules shall cease as of the effective date of termination, subject to any Transition Services agreed under Section 4.7.

4.7 **Transition Services.** Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment for any reason, and upon the Company’s written request made no later than thirty (30) days prior to the effective date of termination (or within ten (10) days thereafter if termination is for cause by Titan), Titan shall provide Transition Services for a period of up to ninety (90) days, subject to:

  (a) the Parties’ agreement on scope, fees (time‑and‑materials at Titan’s then‑current professional services rates), and a transition plan; and  

  (b) the Company’s continued compliance with its confidentiality and data protection obligations, as well as prompt payment of all fees for Transition Services.

4.8 **Survival.** Any provisions of this Amendment that by their nature should survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions), and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination, shall survive such termination or expiration, together with the applicable survival provisions of the Parent Agreement.
