## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Article II supplements **Article II (Scope of Services/Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly modified in this Addendum, the description of Services, Deliverables, Milestones, and Client Responsibilities in the Parent Agreement shall continue to apply.  

2.1.2 For clarity, all definitions of **“Services,” “Deliverables,” “Milestones,” “Work Product,”** and **“Vendor’s Pre-Existing Materials”** set out in Article I and Article II of the Parent Agreement shall apply mutatis mutandis to the Enhanced Services and Enhanced Deliverables under this Addendum.

2.2 **Overview of Enhanced Services.**  

2.2.1 The Vendor shall provide an expanded set of professional, implementation, and managed services designed to (a) extend the solution implemented under the Parent Agreement to the Extended Territory, (b) implement a High Availability Architecture, (c) enable advanced analytics and reporting, and (d) provide Premium Support on a 24x7 basis.  

2.2.2 The Enhanced Services are intended to build upon the Milestones established in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement, specifically following the completion of **Milestone 4 – Data Migration and Go-Live** and **Milestone 5 – Transition to Steady-State Support**. This Addendum assumes that the core implementation and initial integrations described in Section 2.2.3 of the Parent Agreement have reached at least conditional acceptance by the Client.

2.3 **Global Rollout and Master Data Harmonization.**  

2.3.1 The Vendor shall design and execute a phased rollout of the solutions implemented under the Parent Agreement to the Extended Territory. This shall include:  
(a) A **Global Rollout Plan**, including country-by-country timelines, dependencies, and resource plans;  
(b) Localization analysis for each in-scope jurisdiction, addressing language, currency, regulatory, tax, and data residency requirements; and  
(c) Integration of local systems into the global architecture designed under Section 2.2.2 of the Parent Agreement.

2.3.2 The Vendor shall perform **Master Data Harmonization**, including:  
(a) Assessment of master data inconsistencies across existing systems and regions;  
(b) Definition of global master data standards and governance rules;  
(c) Design and implementation of data consolidation and synchronization processes, including integration workflows in addition to the twenty (20) integration workflows contemplated in Section 2.2.3(b) of the Parent Agreement; and  
(d) Delivery of a **Master Data Harmonization Report** and updated data dictionaries as Enhanced Deliverables.

2.4 **High Availability Architecture and Resilience.**  

2.4.1 The Vendor shall extend the solution design and architecture developed under Section 2.2.2 of the Parent Agreement to include a High Availability Architecture. This shall include:  
(a) Design of failover and redundancy mechanisms at the application, database, and integration layers;  
(b) Implementation of load balancing and clustering where supported by the Client’s selected platforms (e.g., Microsoft Dynamics 365, SAP S/4HANA, or equivalent);  
(c) Design and testing of disaster recovery procedures, including a documented Recovery Time Objective (RTO) and Recovery Point Objective (RPO) agreed by the Parties; and  
(d) Execution of at least one (1) full-scale Disaster Recovery test and delivery of a **Disaster Recovery Test Summary Report**.

2.4.2 The Vendor shall update and expand the architectural design documents referred to in Section 2.2.2(b) of the Parent Agreement, and such updated documents shall constitute Enhanced Deliverables under this Addendum.

2.5 **Advanced Analytics and Reporting.**  

2.5.1 In addition to the thirty (30) reports and dashboards contemplated in Section 2.2.3(c) of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall design and implement a suite of advanced analytics solutions, including:  
(a) Up to thirty-five (35) additional dashboards and analytical reports, leveraging data from CRM, ERP, Data Warehouse, and other in-scope systems;  
(b) Design of analytical data models and semantic layers to support self-service reporting by the Client’s business users;  
(c) Implementation of role-based access control consistent with the security configuration obligations under Section 2.2.3(d) of the Parent Agreement; and  
(d) Delivery of end-user and analyst training materials specifically for the analytics capabilities.

2.5.2 The Vendor shall also design a **Major Release and Minor Release** strategy for analytics enhancements, aligned with the change management processes in Article XIII of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum.

2.6 **Premium 24x7 Managed Support and Enhanced SLAs.**  

2.6.1 The Vendor shall provide Premium Support for in-scope systems, which shall supersede the “Business Hours” scope of support defined in Section 2.2.6(a) of the Parent Agreement, for the duration of the Addendum Term. Premium Support shall:  
(a) Operate on a **24x7x365** basis for Severity 1 and Severity 2 incidents (as defined in Section 12.3.1 of the Parent Agreement);  
(b) Apply enhanced response and resolution targets as specified in Article XII of this Addendum; and  
(c) Include proactive monitoring, capacity planning, and performance optimization activities beyond those specified in Section 2.2.6(b) of the Parent Agreement.

2.6.2 The Vendor shall maintain and regularly update an **Operational Runbook** covering all systems and integrations in scope of Premium Support, including:  
(a) Standard operating procedures for routine tasks;  
(b) Incident classification, escalation paths, and communication templates;  
(c) Checklists for releases, patches, and scheduled maintenance.

2.6.3 A **Governance Board** shall be established, comprising representatives of both Parties, which shall meet at least monthly (and more frequently as reasonably requested by the Client) to review SLA performance, risk register, upcoming Major Releases, and any escalated issues.

2.7 **Security Hardening and Compliance.**  

2.7.1 Building upon Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy) of the Parent Agreement, the Vendor shall perform security hardening services, including:  
(a) Periodic vulnerability scanning of the in-scope environments;  
(b) Remediation of identified high and critical vulnerabilities within timeframes agreed by the Parties;  
(c) Review and optimization of access controls, logging, and audit trails; and  
(d) Delivery of **Security Hardening Deliverables**, including a security configuration baseline, exception register, and test evidence.

2.7.2 The Vendor shall ensure that all Enhanced Services are performed in compliance with Data Protection Laws as defined in Section 1.1.7 of the Parent Agreement and further elaborated in Article XI of this Addendum.

2.8 **Client Responsibilities.**  

2.8.1 The Client’s responsibilities under Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to the Enhanced Services, and the Client shall additionally:  
(a) Provide timely approvals for global rollout plans and localization requirements;  
(b) Allocate business subject matter experts in each country covered by the Extended Territory to participate in workshops, testing, and training;  
(c) Provide access to relevant legacy systems, data stores, and networks required for Master Data Harmonization and analytics; and  
(d) Provide prompt decisions on any design or scope questions escalated by the Vendor.

2.8.2 Delays or failures by the Client to perform its responsibilities shall be managed in accordance with the Change Management framework in Article XIII of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum, and may impact timelines, Fees, and SLA performance.

2.9 **Performance Standards.**  

2.9.1 The performance standards in Section 2.5 of the Parent Agreement remain in effect. In addition, the Vendor shall perform the Enhanced Services in accordance with:  
(a) The High Availability and uptime objectives in Article XII of this Addendum;  
(b) The security and compliance requirements in Article XI of both the Parent Agreement and this Addendum; and  
(c) Any additional performance metrics agreed by the Governance Board and documented in writing.

2.9.2 Time shall not be of the essence unless specifically stated in this Addendum; however, the Vendor acknowledges that global rollout timelines and Premium Support Service Levels are critical to the Client’s business, and the Vendor shall prioritize resources accordingly.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Addendum Contract Value.**  

3.1.1 In consideration for the Enhanced Services and Enhanced Deliverables, the Client shall pay the Vendor aggregate **Addendum Fees** in the amount of **USD 3,837,562** (the **“Addendum Contract Value”**), exclusive of applicable taxes, duties, and levies.

The Addendum Fees shall be allocated as follows:

An aggregate amount of **USD 1,350,000** shall be allocated to the design and execution of the global rollout and Master Data Harmonization described in Sections 2.3 and 2.4, including associated testing and training.

An aggregate amount of **USD 985,000** shall be allocated to the design, implementation, and testing of the High Availability Architecture and Security Hardening Deliverables described in Sections 2.4 and 2.7.

3.2.3 **Advanced Analytics Fees.**  
An aggregate amount of **USD 702,562** shall be allocated to the advanced analytics, reporting, and related training described in Section 2.5.

3.2.4 **Premium Support Fees.**  
An aggregate amount of **USD 800,000** shall be allocated to Premium Support services for the Addendum Term, based on an assumed scope of systems and users set forth in a schedule to this Addendum. Any substantive expansion of that scope shall be handled under Article XIII (Change Management).

3.3.1 The Addendum Contract Value shall be invoiced in accordance with the following milestones, which shall be deemed **Addendum Milestones**:

(a) **Addendum Milestone 1 – Addendum Project Initiation (10% of Addendum Contract Value).**  
Upon execution of this Addendum, the Vendor shall invoice the Client **USD 383,756.20**.

(b) **Addendum Milestone 2 – Approval of Global Rollout Plan and Master Data Design (20% of Addendum Contract Value).**

Upon delivery and Client’s acceptance (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed) of the initial advanced analytics suite, the Vendor shall invoice **USD 767,512.40**.

(e) **Addendum Milestone 5 – Completion of Global Rollout, DR Test, and Security Hardening (15% of Addendum Contract Value).**  
Upon: (i) completion of global rollout to all agreed regions, (ii) successful completion of the Disaster Recovery test, and (iii) delivery of Security Hardening Deliverables, the Vendor shall invoice **USD 575,634.30**.

(f) **Premium Support Fees – Recurring Invoicing (Remaining 10%).**  
The portion of the Addendum Contract Value attributable to ongoing Premium Support shall be invoiced quarterly in advance during the Addendum Term, in four (4) equal instalments of **USD 95,939.05**, or as otherwise agreed in writing to align with the Addendum Effective Date.

3.4 **Payment Terms; Invoicing.**  

3.4.1 For all invoices issued under this Addendum, the Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) calendar days** of the invoice date (**“Net 30”**), which constitutes a modification of the **Net 45** terms set out in Section 3.4.1 of the Parent Agreement solely for invoices under this Addendum.

3.4.2 All invoices shall reference both:  
(a) This Addendum’s contract number **ADD-PIN-202509-332**; and  
(b) The Parent Agreement number **SOW-PIN-202509-222 (contract_222)**,  
and shall be sent to the Client’s billing contact as specified in Section 16.7 of the Parent Agreement, unless the Client notifies the Vendor in writing of updated billing details.

3.4.3 The Client’s rights to dispute invoices, and the procedures relating thereto, shall be in accordance with Section 3.4.3 of the Parent Agreement, except that the time to notify disputes for invoices under this Addendum shall be **twenty (20) Business Days** from receipt.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  

3.5.1 Section 3.5 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to late payment of invoices under this Addendum. For clarity, undisputed invoices not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate defined in Section 3.5.1 of the Parent Agreement.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.6.1 The expenses and reimbursement provisions in Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the Enhanced Services under this Addendum. For extended global rollout activities, travel and lodging expenses for on-site deployment and training shall be considered reimbursable, provided they are pre-approved in writing by the Client.

3.7 **Taxes.**  

3.7.1 Section 3.7 (Taxes) of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to the Addendum Fees and any other amounts payable hereunder.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Addendum Term.**  

4.1.1 The term of this Addendum shall commence on the Addendum Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **4 August 2027**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Addendum Term”**).

4.1.2 For clarity, the Addendum Term shall operate **within** the overall Term of the Parent Agreement as defined in Section 1.1.18 and Article IV of the Parent Agreement, and shall not extend the Expiration Date of the Parent Agreement (24 December 2028), unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing.

4.2 **Renewal.**  

4.2.1 The Parties may mutually agree in writing, no less than ninety (90) days prior to the Addendum Expiration Date, to renew or extend this Addendum for additional periods, including continuation of Premium Support and related Enhanced Services. Any such renewal shall be on terms to be negotiated in good faith, including revised Fees and Service Levels.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Notwithstanding Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement, either Party may terminate this Addendum for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party.  

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Client under this Section 4.3, the Client shall pay the Vendor:  
(a) All Fees earned and expenses incurred under this Addendum up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) Any non-cancellable costs reasonably and properly incurred by the Vendor in reliance on this Addendum, provided such costs are documented and were pre-approved by the Client; and  
(c) An early termination fee equal to **ten percent (10%)** of the remaining unpaid portion of the Addendum Contract Value that would have been payable had this Addendum continued through the Addendum Term, in recognition of the Vendor’s resource commitments and pricing concessions.

4.3.3 If the Vendor terminates this Addendum for convenience, it shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate adverse impacts on the Client, including providing Transition Assistance as described in Section 4.6, and shall not be entitled to the early termination fee in Section 4.3.2(c).

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 The termination for cause provisions in Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Addendum. Either Party may terminate this Addendum with immediate effect by written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Addendum or the Parent Agreement, which materially affects the Enhanced Services, and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach.

4.5 **Effect of Parent Agreement Termination.**  

4.5.1 If the Parent Agreement is terminated for any reason, this Addendum shall automatically terminate on the same effective date of termination, without the need for further notice, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to maintain certain aspects of the Enhanced Services for a limited period.

4.5.2 Upon automatic termination under Section 4.5.1, the Client shall pay the Vendor all Fees accrued and unpaid under this Addendum up to the effective date of termination, subject to the Client’s dispute rights under Section 3.4 of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum.

4.6 **Effect of Termination; Transition Assistance.**  

4.6.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Addendum for any reason:  
(a) The Vendor shall cease performing the Enhanced Services, except as reasonably requested by the Client for Transition Assistance;  
(b) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Article V of the Parent Agreement and this Addendum; and  
(c) All accrued but unpaid Fees and approved expenses shall become immediately due and payable.

4.6.2 At the Client’s written request, the Vendor shall provide Transition Assistance for a period of up to six (6) months following termination or expiration of this Addendum, at the then-current time-and-materials rates or at rates agreed in writing, to assist in the orderly transition of the Enhanced Services to the Client or its designee.

4.7 **Survival.**  

4.7.1 The provisions of Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), and XVI (General Provisions) of this Addendum, together with any relevant surviving provisions of the Parent Agreement, shall survive termination or expiration of this Addendum.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (ENHANCED SLA)

12.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement SLA.**  

12.1.1 Article XII of the Parent Agreement shall continue to apply to managed support and monitoring services; however, for systems and services covered by Premium Support under this Addendum, the Enhanced SLA in this Article XII shall apply and, where inconsistent, shall supersede the Parent Agreement SLA.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment.**  

12.2.1 For in-scope production systems under Premium Support, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure availability of at least **99.95%** in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and agreed downtime.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Targets.**  

12.3.1 Severity levels defined in Section 12.3.1 of the Parent Agreement remain in effect. For Premium Support, the following enhanced targets apply (24x7 measurement for Severity 1 and 2):

- Severity 1 (Critical):  
  - Initial response within **15 minutes** (24x7)  
  - Target resolution or workaround within **4 hours**

- Severity 2 (High):  
  - Initial response within **30 minutes** (24x7)  
  - Target resolution within **1 Business Day**

- Severity 3 (Medium):  
  - Initial response within **4 Business Hours**  
  - Target resolution within **3 Business Days**

- Severity 4 (Low):  
  - Initial response within **1 Business Day**  
  - Target resolution within **10 Business Days** or in the next scheduled Minor Release.

12.4 **Service Credits and Remedies.**  

12.4.1 If the Vendor fails to meet the monthly availability target of 99.95% or repeatedly fails to meet Severity 1 and 2 targets, the Client shall be entitled to **service credits** calculated as follows (on the Premium Support portion of the Addendum Fees only):

- Availability between 99.5% and 99.95%: 5% of the monthly Premium Support Fees;  
- Availability between 99.0% and 99.5%: 10% of the monthly Premium Support Fees;  
- Availability below 99.0%: 20% of the monthly Premium Support Fees.

12.4.2 If, for three (3) consecutive months, the Vendor fails to meet the Severity 1 resolution targets or the monthly availability target, the Client shall have the right to initiate a remediation plan through the Governance Board. Persistent failures over six (6) consecutive months may constitute a material breach for purposes of Section 4.4 of this Addendum and Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement.
