## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Service Provider shall provide to Client professional consulting services in the nature of:

  (a) strategic management consulting related to Client’s corporate growth initiatives, market expansion, and operational optimization;

  (b) technology and digital transformation consulting, including assessment of Client’s existing technology stack, recommendations regarding modernization, and support in implementing selected initiatives;

  (c) process assessment and business process re‑engineering, including mapping current processes, identifying bottlenecks, recommending improvements, and supporting implementation;

  (d) organizational change management, including stakeholder analysis, communications planning, training, and adoption support; and

  (e) such other related consulting, advisory, or professional services as may be agreed in one or more SOWs.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) From time to time, the Parties may execute one or more SOWs that incorporate this Agreement by reference and specify the particular Services and Deliverables to be provided. No SOW shall be binding unless executed by authorized representatives of both Parties.  

(b) Each SOW shall, at a minimum, set forth: (i) a description of the Services and Deliverables; (ii) project milestones and schedule; (iii) Fees and payment terms (consistent with **Article III**); (iv) any assumptions and dependencies; (v) any Service Levels specific to that SOW; and (vi) any special terms or conditions that apply solely to that SOW.

2.3 **Description of Core Services and Deliverables.**  
Without limiting any SOW, the Parties currently anticipate that the consulting engagement will involve the following workstreams and Deliverables:

  (a) **Diagnostic and Assessment Phase (Months 1–3).**  
  - Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and data analysis to assess Client’s current operating model, technology environment, and process landscape.  
  - Deliverable: a comprehensive **Current State Assessment Report** summarizing findings, including gap analysis, risk assessment, and recommendations.  
  - Deliverable: a **Prioritized Opportunity Matrix** identifying high-impact initiatives, estimated benefits, and implementation complexity.

  (b) **Strategy and Roadmap Phase (Months 3–6).**  
  - Collaborate with Client to develop a multi‑year strategic roadmap for operational and digital transformation.  
  - Deliverable: a **Strategic Transformation Roadmap** outlining initiatives, timelines, resource requirements, and key performance indicators (“**KPIs**”).  
  - Deliverable: an **Executive Presentation Deck** for Client’s leadership team and board-level stakeholders.

  (c) **Implementation Support Phase (Months 6–18).**  
  - Provide project management and advisory support to Client teams responsible for executing priority initiatives.  
  - Deliverable: **Monthly Progress Reports** documenting status, issues, risks, dependencies, and recommended mitigation actions.  
  - Deliverable: **Updated Roadmap and Benefit Realization Tracking** documents on at least a quarterly basis, reflecting changes in scope, timing, or benefit forecasts.

  (d) **Change Management and Training (Throughout Term).**  
  - Develop and deliver training content, communications plans, and change impact assessments.  
  - Deliverable: **Change Management Plan**, including stakeholder mapping, communication templates, and training calendar.  
  - Deliverable: training materials (e.g., slide decks, user guides) customized for different stakeholder groups.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) Service Provider shall perform the Services (i) in a diligent, professional, and workmanlike manner, (ii) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for consulting services of a similar nature, and (iii) in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.  

(b) Service Provider shall use personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications to perform the Services. Upon Client’s reasonable request and for good cause (such as persistent performance issues or misconduct), Service Provider shall promptly replace any personnel assigned to the Services with personnel of comparable qualifications, as further provided in any applicable SOW.

(c) Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the timelines, milestones, and delivery dates set forth in this Agreement and any SOW. Time is of the essence with respect to milestone dates explicitly identified as critical in an SOW, provided that Client has fulfilled its dependencies and responsibilities described therein.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Service Provider’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Client’s timely performance of its obligations and cooperation, including:  

  (a) timely provision of Client Materials, data, systems access, and subject matter experts;  

  (b) making decisions and approvals in a timely manner;  

  (c) providing a primary point of contact with decision‑making authority for the project; and  

  (d) ensuring that Client’s internal policies (including for security, privacy, and compliance) are communicated to Service Provider in writing to the extent they materially affect the Services.

Service Provider shall not be responsible for delays, increased costs, or failures in performance to the extent caused by Client’s failure to comply with the foregoing. Any such impacts shall be addressed pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.6 **Subcontracting.**  
Service Provider may subcontract portions of the Services to third parties, provided that (a) Service Provider remains fully responsible for the performance of all Services and compliance with this Agreement; (b) any subcontractor is bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection that are no less protective than those set forth herein; and (c) Service Provider remains Client’s sole point of contact regarding the Services.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
(a) Each SOW shall specify acceptance criteria and any applicable review period for Deliverables. Unless otherwise stated, Client shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery of a Deliverable to review and either (i) provide written acceptance or (ii) provide a written notice of non‑conformity describing how the Deliverable fails to conform to the applicable requirements.  

(b) If Client provides a timely notice of non‑conformity, Service Provider shall, at no additional cost to Client, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the non-conformity and resubmit the Deliverable for Client’s review. 3.1 **Contract Value.**

  (b) **Time-and-Materials Fees.** Other Services (such as ongoing advisory support) may be billed on a time-and-materials basis at the hourly or daily rates specified in the applicable SOW.

  - Strategy & Roadmap Phase: US $200,000 (fixed fee), payable 30% on phase commencement, 40% at delivery of draft Roadmap, and 30% upon acceptance of final Roadmap.  
  - Implementation Support & Change Management: US $353,673, billed on a time‑and‑materials basis monthly in arrears, at rates to be defined in the SOW (e.g., partner, senior consultant, consultant).

3.4 **Invoices and Payment Terms.**  
(a) Service Provider shall invoice Client in accordance with the billing schedule set forth in the relevant SOW and this **Article III**. Unless otherwise specified, invoices shall be issued monthly in arrears.  

(b) All properly issued and undisputed invoices shall be due and payable within **forty‑five (45) days** from the date of Client’s receipt thereof (“**Net 45**”).  

(c) Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method. Bank account details shall be provided by Service Provider in writing.

Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. 3.6 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month (12% per annum) or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the due date until paid in full. Service Provider may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice and provided that any undisputed and overdue amounts remain unpaid after such period, suspend performance of the Services until such overdue amount is paid, without liability to Client, provided such suspension is proportionate and consistent with **Section 4.5**.

(a) Client shall reimburse Service Provider for reasonable, pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket expenses necessarily incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and materials, in accordance with Client’s travel and expense policies as communicated in writing to Service Provider.  

(b) Service Provider shall submit itemized expense reports with appropriate supporting documentation, and any such expenses shall be invoiced in accordance with **Section 3.4**.  

(c) Service Provider shall not mark up reimbursable expenses and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize costs.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
All Fees and other amounts payable under this Agreement are exclusive of Taxes, unless expressly stated otherwise. Client shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes (other than taxes on Service Provider’s net income, property, or payroll). If Service Provider is required to collect and remit any such Taxes, such Taxes shall be itemized on the invoice, and Client shall pay them in addition to the Fees. ## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated pursuant to this **Article IV**, shall continue in effect until **June 2, 2025** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may extend or renew this Agreement beyond the Expiration Date only by a written amendment or new agreement signed by authorized representatives of both Parties. There shall be no automatic renewal.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low‑risk profile of this engagement, Client shall have the right to terminate this Agreement, any SOW, or any portion thereof for convenience, in whole or in part, upon **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Service Provider. Service Provider may terminate for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Client, Limited to periods when no critical milestones are due under an active SOW, and provided that Service Provider reasonably cooperates in transitioning Services to Client or another provider.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for cause upon written notice if:

  (a) the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail (or, if the breach is not capable of cure within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period); or  

  (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is subject to any similar proceeding.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the applicable Services (except to the extent Client requests reasonable transition assistance as set forth below).  

  (b) Client shall pay Service Provider for all Services performed and approved expenses incurred through the effective date of termination (and any additional Services expressly requested by Client in writing during the transition period), subject to Client’s rights regarding non‑conforming Services and Deliverables.  

  (c) At Client’s request, Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide transition services for up to sixty (60) days after termination (or such longer period as the Parties may agree in writing) at the rates specified in the applicable SOW, to facilitate a smooth transfer of Services and Deliverables to Client or a new provider.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement, along with any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive: **Sections 3.6, 3.8, 4.5, 4.6, Article V (Confidentiality), Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights), Article VII (Representations and Warranties), Article VIII (Indemnification), Article IX (Limitation of Liability), Article X (Insurance), Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy), Article XIV (Dispute Resolution), Article XV (Force Majeure), and Article XVI (General Provisions)**.

  (c) is received from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or

5.4 **Return or Destruction.**

5.5 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue during the Term and for a period of **five (5) years** following expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets and Personal Data shall be kept confidential for so long as such information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law or as required by Data Protection Laws, respectively.
