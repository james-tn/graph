## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Acme shall provide to Contoso professional technology consulting, software development, systems integration, configuration, testing, and related advisory and support services (collectively, the “Services”) to support Contoso’s enterprise software initiatives, including but not limited to the design, implementation, and optimization of Contoso’s internal business applications and customer-facing platforms.

2.1.2 The Services shall be provided in a phased manner, consisting of: (a) discovery and requirements gathering; (b) solution design and architecture; (c) development and configuration; (d) testing and quality assurance; (e) deployment and go-live support; and (f) post-deployment stabilization and ongoing support.

### 2.2 Specific Deliverables

2.2.1 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Acme shall create and deliver to Contoso the following Deliverables, which shall be further detailed in one or more written statements of work or schedules agreed by the Parties (each, an “SOW”):

  (a) **Requirements and Design Documentation.** Detailed requirements documents, business process maps, functional specifications, and technical design documents for the target solution, including integration points with Contoso’s existing systems;

  (b) **Software Components.** Custom-developed software modules, configuration scripts, APIs, integration adapters, and other code-based components required to implement the solution;

  (c) **Configuration and Setup.** System configurations within agreed environments, including development, test, staging, and production environments, in accordance with Contoso’s security and governance policies;

  (d) **Testing Artifacts.** Test plans, test cases, test scripts, test data sets, defect logs, and test reports, demonstrating that the Deliverables meet the agreed acceptance criteria;

  (e) **Operational Documentation.** User manuals, administrator guides, runbooks, operational procedures, and training materials sufficient to enable Contoso’s personnel to use and manage the solution;

  (f) **Reports.** Status reports, project governance dashboards, risk and issue logs, and post-implementation review reports.

2.2.2 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all Deliverables shall be provided in English and in electronic form, in file formats reasonably requested by Contoso (e.g., Microsoft Office formats, PDF, or industry-standard text-based formats for code).

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 Acme shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  

  (b) in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including Data Protection Laws as specified in **Article XI**;  

  (c) using personnel who are appropriately skilled, trained, and experienced for the tasks assigned to them; and  

  (d) in accordance with the project plans, timelines, and Service Levels agreed by the Parties in writing, including any applicable SOWs and Service Level Agreements under **Article XII**.

2.3.2 Acme shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet all milestones and delivery dates specified in the applicable SOWs. Any expected delay shall be promptly communicated to Contoso in writing, together with a remediation plan to minimize any adverse impact on the project schedule.

2.3.3 In performing the Services, Acme shall comply with Contoso’s reasonable policies, including information security and site access requirements, that are made available to Acme in writing prior to or during the Term, to the extent such policies are generally applicable to third-party vendors.

### 2.4 Cooperation and Client Responsibilities

2.4.1 Contoso shall provide Acme with reasonable cooperation, information, and access to systems, facilities, and personnel necessary for Acme to perform the Services, subject to Contoso’s security, confidentiality, and safety policies.

2.4.2 Contoso shall designate a project manager or primary contact who shall be responsible for coordinating with Acme, reviewing and providing feedback on Deliverables, and providing necessary approvals or sign-offs.

2.4.3 Contoso shall be responsible for the accuracy and completeness of all information and materials provided to Acme for use in connection with the Services, and Acme shall be entitled to rely on such information and materials without independent verification, unless otherwise expressly stated in an SOW.

### 2.5 Acceptance of Deliverables

2.5.1 Each Deliverable shall be subject to review and acceptance by Contoso in accordance with acceptance criteria and procedures set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, the following default process shall apply:

  (a) Upon delivery of a Deliverable, Acme shall provide written notice to Contoso that such Deliverable is ready for review. Contoso shall have a period of fifteen (15) Business Days (the “Review Period”) to review the Deliverable.

  (b) If Contoso determines that the Deliverable materially fails to conform to the applicable acceptance criteria, Contoso shall provide written notice to Acme before the end of the Review Period, specifying in reasonable detail the non-conformities.

  (c) Acme shall promptly correct the reported non-conformities and re-submit the Deliverable. A subsequent Review Period shall apply to the corrected Deliverable.

  (d) If Contoso does not notify Acme of any material non-conformity within the applicable Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

2.5.2 Acceptance of a Deliverable shall not relieve Acme of its continuing warranties and obligations under this Agreement, including those set forth in **Article VII (Representations and Warranties)**.

3.2.1 Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Acme shall invoice Contoso for:

  (a) fixed-price milestone Fees upon Contoso’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the applicable Deliverable; and  

  (b) time-and-materials Fees monthly in arrears, based on actual hours worked multiplied by the applicable rates.

3.2.2 All invoices shall be accompanied by reasonable supporting documentation, including references to milestone completions and, for time-and-materials Fees, detailed timesheets.

3.2.3 Payment of undisputed invoices shall be due within **thirty (30) days** from the date of receipt of the correct invoice (“**Net 30**”), as aligned with the Payment Terms specified by the Parties.

3.2.4 All payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method, to the account designated in writing by Acme.

### 3.3 Late Payments

3.3.1 Any undisputed amount not paid by Contoso when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: 

  (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or  

  (b) the maximum rate allowed by applicable law,

from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.3.2 Before imposing interest or exercising any remedies for late payment (including suspension of Services), Acme shall provide Contoso with written notice specifying the overdue amounts and provide Contoso with a cure period of fifteen (15) Business Days to remedy the non-payment.

### 3.4 Disputed Amounts

3.4.1 Contoso may in good faith dispute any portion of an invoice by notifying Acme in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the amount in dispute and the reasons therefor.

3.4.2 Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts in accordance with **Section 3.2**, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts promptly. No interest shall accrue on disputed amounts while under good-faith dispute.

### 3.5 Expenses and Reimbursement

3.5.1 Contoso shall reimburse Acme for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred by Acme in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, provided that such expenses are:

  (a) consistent with Contoso’s then-current travel and expense policies (provided to Acme in writing); and  

  (b) supported by appropriate receipts and documentation.

3.5.2 Any single expense item in excess of US$1,000 and any international travel expenses shall require Contoso’s prior written approval.

### 3.6 Taxes

3.6.1 All Fees and other amounts payable under this Agreement are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes (other than taxes based on Acme’s net income), which shall be borne by Contoso if applicable.

3.6.2 Acme shall include any applicable taxes as separate line items on an invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to minimize taxes to the extent legally permissible, including by providing valid tax exemption certificates where applicable.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date of February 26, 2029** (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.1.2 The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Agreement for additional period(s) (each, a “**Renewal Term**”, and together with the Initial Term, the “**Term**”).

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience, without cause, upon providing the other Party with not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice**. This period reflects a balanced, medium-risk allocation between the Parties.

4.2.2 During the notice period, Acme shall cooperate with Contoso to wind down the Services in an orderly manner and, if requested by Contoso, assist in transitioning the Services and Deliverables to Contoso or a replacement vendor, subject to agreed Fees for transition services.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or similar proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, ceases to carry on its business in the ordinary course, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

4.3.2 Without limiting the foregoing, persistent or material failure by Acme to meet the agreed Service Levels under **Article XII** may constitute a material breach, subject to any cure or service credit mechanisms specified therein.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) Acme shall immediately cease performing the Services (except for any transition services requested by Contoso and agreed in writing);  

  (b) each Party shall promptly return or, at the Disclosing Party’s written request, securely destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession or control, subject to **Section 5.4** and any applicable legal retention obligations; and  

  (c) all licenses granted under this Agreement shall terminate, except as explicitly provided otherwise in **Article VI** or **Article XVI**.

4.4.2 Contoso shall pay Acme for:

  (a) all Services duly performed and Deliverables accepted (or deemed accepted) through the effective date of termination; and  

  (b) any reasonable, non-cancellable, pre-approved expenses incurred prior to the termination date.

4.4.3 In no event shall Contoso be responsible for any loss of anticipated profits or unperformed work following the effective date of termination, except as expressly set forth in an SOW.

### 4.5 Survival

4.5.1 The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination.
