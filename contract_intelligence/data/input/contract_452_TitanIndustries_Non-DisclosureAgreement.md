## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Titan shall provide Contoso with high-value professional services related to enterprise systems integration, data analytics implementation, and associated operational support (the “Services”). The Services shall include, without limitation:

  a. Assessment and design of Contoso’s target technical architecture for data analytics and reporting;  
  b. Design, configuration, and implementation of integration interfaces between Contoso’s existing enterprise systems and a new analytics platform;  
  c. Development, customization, and deployment of data pipelines, dashboards, and reporting templates;  
  d. Provision of operational support and maintenance services for the integrated solution, including incident management, patching, and performance optimization; and  
  e. Provision of project management, training, and knowledge transfer services to Contoso’s internal stakeholders.

2.1.2 The exact scope, milestones, Deliverables, and assumptions for particular phases or projects may be further defined in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern, unless the SOW expressly states otherwise and specifically references the provision of this Agreement it is superseding.

### 2.2 Deliverables

2.2.1 Titan shall prepare and deliver to Contoso the Deliverables described herein and in any applicable SOW, which may include, by way of example and without limitation:

  a. **Architecture and Design Documents**: system architecture diagrams, interface specifications, data models, technical design documents, configuration standards, and related documentation;  
  b. **Integration Components**: application programming interfaces (APIs), connectors, scripts, and configuration files necessary for integration between Contoso’s systems and the analytics platform;  
  c. **Analytics Assets**: preconfigured dashboards, reports, KPIs, data visualizations, and related analytics artifacts;  
  d. **Operational Documentation**: runbooks, operating procedures, incident handling guidelines, and maintenance playbooks;  
  e. **Training Materials**: user guides, presentations, and training manuals tailored for Contoso’s personnel; and  
  f. Any additional Deliverables identified in the relevant SOW.

2.2.2 Deliverables shall conform in all material respects to the Specifications set forth in this Agreement and in any applicable SOW. Titan shall use commercially reasonable efforts, consistent with industry best practices, to ensure that Deliverables are accurate, complete, and suitable for their intended purpose as described in the applicable SOW.

### 2.3 Timelines and Milestones

2.3.1 The Parties acknowledge that the timely performance of the Services and delivery of Deliverables is of material importance. Titan shall perform the Services in accordance with the timelines and milestones set forth in the applicable SOW, including any phased implementation schedule.

2.3.2 If Titan reasonably believes that any milestone or delivery date may not be met, Titan shall promptly notify Contoso in writing, explaining the reasons for the anticipated delay and proposing a revised schedule. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate any delay and to agree on an updated schedule, which shall be documented in writing. Delays caused by Contoso (including delays in providing information, approvals, or access) shall entitle Titan to a reasonable extension of the applicable deadlines and may give rise to additional fees under Article III.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Titan shall perform the Services:

  a. With due professional care, skill, and diligence;  
  b. In accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  c. In compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including but not limited to Data Protection Laws, export controls, and anti-bribery laws; and

2.4.2 Titan shall assign suitably experienced and qualified personnel to perform the Services.   a. Provide Titan with access to Contoso’s systems, infrastructure, and personnel as reasonably necessary to perform the Services;  
  b. Designate a primary point of contact for coordination and approvals; and  
  c. Respond promptly to Titan’s requests for information and decisions, and provide all required data in a complete and accurate manner.

3.2.1 Titan shall invoice Contoso in accordance with the milestones or billing schedule defined in the applicable SOW. In the absence of any specified schedule, Titan may invoice monthly in arrears for Services performed and Deliverables provided during the preceding month.

3.3.1 Any amount not paid when due and not subject to a good-faith dispute shall accrue interest at a rate of one percent (1%) per month, or the maximum rate allowed by applicable law, whichever is lower, from the due date until the date of payment.

Any suspension shall not relieve Contoso of its obligation to pay the outstanding amounts and may result in schedule delays and additional costs.

3.4.1 Fees under this Agreement are exclusive of any applicable taxes, duties, or levies, including without limitation value-added tax (VAT), goods and services tax (GST), sales tax, use tax, and withholding tax (collectively, “Taxes”). 3.5.1 Contoso shall reimburse Titan for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred by Titan in connection with the performance of the Services, including but not limited to travel, lodging, meals, and third-party tools or services required for the project.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until the Expiration Date (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.1.2 The Parties may, upon mutual written agreement, extend or renew the Agreement for one or more additional periods (each, a “**Renewal Term**”). Any such Renewal Term shall be documented in writing and signed by both Parties.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause by providing the other Party with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**.

4.2.2 During the notice period, the Parties shall continue to perform their respective obligations under this Agreement. Contoso shall pay Titan for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments and reasonable wind-down costs incurred by Titan as a direct result of the termination.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party:

  a. Commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days (or such longer period as the Parties may agree in writing) after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  b. Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed under judicial management, or has a receiver or similar officer appointed over its assets, and such proceeding is not dismissed within sixty (60) calendar days.

4.3.2 Notwithstanding Section 4.3.1, Contoso shall endeavor to provide Titan with a reasonable opportunity to cure any non-material breaches before exercising its termination rights, provided that such breaches do not pose an imminent risk to Contoso’s data, operations, or legal compliance.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  a. All rights and licenses granted hereunder shall immediately cease, except as expressly stated otherwise in this Agreement;  
  b. Contoso shall pay Titan all fees and reimbursable expenses accrued through the effective date of termination, including any amounts set forth in Section 4.2.2;  
  c. Each Party shall, upon written request of the other Party, return or destroy the requesting Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5; and  
  d. The rights and obligations of the Parties that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration (including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination) shall survive.

4.4.2 Termination shall be without prejudice to any rights or remedies that may have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including any claims for damages arising from a breach.

### 5.2 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:

  a. Use the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Services and fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement;  
  b. Not disclose the Confidential Information to any Third Party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement;  
  c. Protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care; and  
  d. Limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

### 5.3 Exceptions

5.3.1 Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  a. Was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction at the time of disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  b. Becomes publicly known through no wrongful act or omission of the Receiving Party;  
  c. Is received from a Third Party who did not acquire or disclose such information in breach of any legal obligation; or  
  d. Was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

### 5.4 Mandatory Disclosure

5.4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, court order, or other governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party may do so, provided that, to the extent legally permissible:

  a. The Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of the requirement to disclose, so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and  
  b. The Receiving Party discloses only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required and uses reasonable efforts to obtain assurances that such information will be treated confidentially.

### 5.5 Return or Destruction

5.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Confidential Information in its possession or control, including any copies on backup media, subject to:

  a. The Receiving Party’s right to retain Confidential Information as required by applicable law or for legitimate record-keeping purposes; and  
  b. The retention of copies in electronic archives maintained in accordance with automated backup systems, provided that such archived copies shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement until destroyed in the ordinary course.

### 5.6 Survival

5.6.1 The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall be maintained confidential for so long as they qualify as such under applicable law.

### 12.1 Uptime and Availability

12.1.1 To the extent Titan provides any hosted or managed services under this Agreement, Titan shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such services maintain an uptime availability of not less than **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and excused downtime (as defined below).

12.1.2 “Excused Downtime” shall include:

  a. Scheduled maintenance, provided that Titan gives Contoso at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice and schedules such maintenance during off-peak hours where reasonably practicable;  
  b. Downtime caused by Contoso’s systems, networks, or third-party services outside Titan’s reasonable control; and  
  c. Downtime resulting from Force Majeure Events.

### 12.2 Response and Resolution Times

12.2.1 Titan shall provide incident response and resolution in accordance with the following guidelines, unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical)**: Complete loss of service or a severe issue affecting a majority of users.  
    - Target Response Time: within one (1) hour.  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within eight (8) hours.

  - **Priority 2 (High)**: Significant impairment of service with no reasonable workaround.  
    - Target Response Time: within four (4) hours.  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within two (2) Business Days.

  - **Priority 3 (Medium)**: Non-critical issue with a viable workaround.  
    - Target Response Time: within one (1) Business Day.  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within five (5) Business Days.

  - **Priority 4 (Low)**: Minor issues or general inquiries.  
    - Target Response Time: within two (2) Business Days.  
    - Target Resolution: to be addressed on a reasonable efforts basis.

### 12.3 SLA Remedies

12.3.1 In the event Titan fails to meet the uptime or response time targets in this Article XII for two or more consecutive calendar months, Titan shall, as Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for such SLA failures (subject to Article IX):

  a. Provide Contoso with service credits equal to a percentage of the monthly fees for the affected services, as specified in the applicable SOW; or  
  b. Implement a mutually agreed remediation plan at Titan’s expense to restore service performance.

12.3.2 Service credits, if applicable, shall be applied against subsequent invoices and shall not be refundable in cash.

### 15.1 Definition

15.1.1 A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including, but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, riots, civil commotion, labor disputes, governmental actions, pandemics, epidemics, failures or interruptions of telecommunications or power supplies not attributable to the Party, and any other similar events that significantly impair the Party’s ability to perform its obligations.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event (“Affected Party”) shall:

  a. Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event and provide reasonable details of its expected duration and impact on performance; and  
  b. Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension of Performance

15.3.1 The Affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, to the extent that such obligations cannot be performed due to the Force Majeure Event.

15.3.2 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive calendar days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) calendar days’ written notice to the other Party.
