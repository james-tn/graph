4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article, shall continue in full force and effect until **September 28, 2026** (the **“Expiration Date”**), constituting the **initial term** (the **“Initial Term”**).

Any renewal shall be subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor. Vendor may terminate for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client. In the event of such termination:

  (a) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services rendered and Software usage up to the effective date of termination; and  

  (b) For any prepaid Fees for periods after the effective date of termination, Vendor shall refund to Client the pro rata portion of such prepaid Fees.

  (a) The other Party materially breaches any of its obligations under this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or fifteen (15) days in the case of non-payment) after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) The other Party becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) All licenses granted to Client under this Agreement shall immediately terminate, and Client shall cease all use of the Software, except as otherwise expressly permitted under **Section 4.6** or **Section 6.6** (if applicable);  

  (b) Client shall promptly return or certify destruction of all copies of the Software and Documentation in its possession or under its control, except for copies retained for archival or legal compliance purposes;  

  (c) Vendor shall promptly return or, at Client’s written request, securely destroy all Client Data in its possession or control in accordance with **Section 11.7**; and  

  (d) The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive (including, without limitation, **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI**, and any outstanding payment obligations) shall survive such expiration or termination.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.** Upon Client’s request made at least thirty (30) days prior to the termination or expiration of this Agreement, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance, including export of Client Data in a commonly used machine-readable format and cooperation in transition to a successor solution. Such assistance shall be provided at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates, unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach, in which case such assistance shall be provided at no additional charge for a period of up to thirty (30) days.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Software is available to Client with a monthly uptime percentage of at least **99.9%**, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Downtime as defined below.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance during designated maintenance windows, which shall be communicated to Client at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance. Scheduled maintenance shall not exceed eight (8) hours per month and shall be excluded from the uptime calculation.

12.3 **Excluded Downtime.** “Excluded Downtime” means any unavailability of the Software caused by:

  (a) Client’s breach of this Agreement or misuse of the Software;  

  (b) Failure of Client’s systems, network, or internet connectivity;  

  (c) Force Majeure Events as defined in **Article XV**; or  

  (d) Any third-party service or infrastructure not under Vendor’s reasonable control.

12.4 **Incident Classification and Response Times.** Vendor shall respond to support incidents in accordance with the following priority levels:

- **Priority 1 – Critical:** Software is unavailable or severely impacted, causing a complete business outage with no workaround.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour (Business Days)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours  

- **Priority 2 – High:** Significant functionality is impaired, with major impact on business operations, but a workaround may exist.  
  - Initial Response: within 4 hours (Business Days)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 24 hours  

- **Priority 3 – Medium:** Partial, non-critical loss of functionality or performance issues; business impact is moderate.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 – Low:** General questions, cosmetic issues, or minor defects with minimal business impact.  
  - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
  - Target Resolution: as agreed, typically in a future release  

12.5 **SLA Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** in any calendar month (a **“SLA Failure”**):

  (a) Client shall be entitled to service credits equal to a percentage of the monthly Fees for that month, calculated as follows:  

  - Uptime 99.0% - 99.9%: 5% credit  
  - Uptime 98.0% - 99.0%: 10% credit  
  - Uptime < 98.0%: 20% credit  

  (b) Service credits shall be applied against the invoice for the subsequent month or refunded at Client’s request if no further invoices are due;  

  (c) Service credits shall be Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA Failures, without prejudice to Client’s right to terminate for persistent SLA Failures as provided in **Section 4.4**.

12.6 **Reporting.** Vendor shall provide Client, upon request and not more than once per calendar quarter, with a report summarizing uptime performance and incident statistics for the preceding period.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent such obligations are affected by the Force Majeure Event.
