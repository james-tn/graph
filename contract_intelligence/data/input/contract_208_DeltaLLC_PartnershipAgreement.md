## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Appointment of Vendor.** Client hereby appoints Vendor, and Vendor hereby accepts such appointment, as Client’s primary service provider for the Project during the Term, in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement and the applicable SOWs.

2.2 **General Description of Services.** Vendor shall provide Client with the following categories of Services, as further detailed in each SOW:

2.2.1 **Advisory and Consulting Services.** Vendor shall provide strategic and operational consulting services to Client, including:

  (a) assessment of Client’s existing technology environment, systems, processes, and controls;

  (b) development of a multi-year technology roadmap aligned with Client’s business objectives;

  (c) recommendations on system architecture, security, scalability, and cost optimization; and

  (d) preparation of reports, presentations, and workshops for Client’s management.

2.2.2 **Implementation and Integration Services.** Vendor shall design, configure, implement, and integrate specified software platforms, tools, and systems, including but not limited to:

  (a) system design and solution architecture;

  (b) configuration of application modules and features;

  (c) integration with Client’s existing systems and third-party platforms via APIs, middleware, or other mechanisms;

  (d) migration of data from legacy systems to the new solution, including mapping, cleansing, and validation; and

  (e) performance, security, and user acceptance testing.

2.2.3 **Managed Services and Support.** Following go-live of the implemented solution, Vendor shall provide managed services and operational support, which may include:

  (a) system monitoring, incident management, and problem resolution;

  (b) user support (e.g., help desk, ticketing systems) during agreed service windows;

  (c) ongoing maintenance, patching, and updates as required;

  (d) periodic performance optimization and capacity planning; and

  (e) support for regulatory or compliance-related changes impacting the systems under Vendor’s management.

2.2.4 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall design and deliver training programs to Client’s personnel, which may include:

  (a) onboarding and orientation sessions;

  (b) administrator and super-user training;

  (c) end-user training tailored to different user roles; and

  (d) provision of training materials, user guides, and documentation.

2.3 **Deliverables.** Vendor shall prepare and deliver to Client various Deliverables as specified in each SOW, which may include, without limitation:

  (a) requirements and design documents;

  (b) configuration and integration documentation;

  (c) test plans, test scripts, and test results;

  (d) deployment and cutover plans;

  (e) operating procedures, run-books, and support documentation; and

  (f) periodic performance reports, incident reports, and SLA reports.

Each Deliverable shall conform in all material respects to the applicable specifications and acceptance criteria set forth in the relevant SOW.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.** The Parties shall agree on detailed project timelines and Milestones in each SOW. Vendor shall perform the Services in a timely and professional manner in accordance with such timelines. The Parties acknowledge, however, that timelines and Milestones may be subject to change based on dependencies on Client resources, third-party providers, regulatory changes, or other factors outside Vendor’s reasonable control, as further addressed in Article XIII (Change Management) and Article XV (Force Majeure).

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall:

  (a) perform the Services with due skill, care, and diligence, in a professional and workmanlike manner, and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for comparable services;

  (b) assign suitably qualified and experienced personnel to perform the Services;

  (c) comply with all applicable laws and regulations in performing the Services; and

  (d) use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruptions to Client’s business operations.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.** Vendor’s performance of the Services is expressly conditioned upon the timely performance by Client of its responsibilities, which include:

  (a) providing Vendor with timely access to Client’s personnel, systems, facilities, and relevant information;

  (b) designating a project manager or primary point of contact with sufficient authority to make decisions on behalf of Client;

  (c) reviewing and providing feedback or approvals on Deliverables within the timelines specified in the applicable SOW;

  (d) ensuring that all Client Materials are complete, accurate, and current; and

  (e) securing any third-party consents or licenses necessary for Vendor to access, use, or interface with third-party systems or data.

If Client fails to perform any of its responsibilities, Vendor shall be entitled to a reasonable extension of time to perform its obligations, and may be entitled to additional fees and costs as mutually agreed in a Change Order.

2.7 **Subcontractors.** Vendor may, in its discretion, engage subcontractors or Affiliates to perform portions of the Services; provided that Vendor shall remain responsible for the performance of such subcontractors and Affiliates and for compliance with this Agreement. Upon Client’s reasonable request, Vendor shall inform Client of the identity and role of any material subcontractors.

2.8 **Statements of Work.** Each SOW shall be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties. In the event of a conflict between the main body of this Agreement and a SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states that it intends to override specific provisions of this Agreement, in which case such overridden provisions shall apply only for that SOW.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

  (d) **Pass-Through Costs** and reimbursable expenses as set forth in Section 3.5.

  (a) Vendor shall invoice Client upon the completion of each Milestone;

  (b) Completion shall be deemed to occur when Vendor has fulfilled the objective of the Milestone in all material respects and has made all associated Deliverables available to Client; and

  (c) Client shall promptly review and, if applicable, raise any bona fide, documented objections within ten (10) Business Days. In the absence of such objections, the Milestone shall be deemed accepted.

Client shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that any withholding tax is minimized under applicable tax treaties.

3.7 **Late Payments.** Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of actual payment at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower. In addition, if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after the due date, Vendor may, upon written notice to Client, suspend performance of the Services (including access to Deliverables or systems) until all overdue amounts and applicable interest are paid in full, without liability to Client and without prejudice to any other rights or remedies.

3.8 **Disputed Amounts.** If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within ten (10) Business Days of the invoice date, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute in reasonable detail. Client shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.4. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any such dispute promptly. Any amount subsequently determined to be payable shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days after resolution.

3.9 **Price Review.** For multi-year Services, Vendor may review and adjust its standard rates on an annual basis, provided that (a) any increase does not exceed five percent (5%) per annum unless justified by significant changes in scope or market conditions, and (b) Vendor provides at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Client. Any such adjustments shall apply only to Services performed after the effective date of the adjustment and shall not affect previously agreed fixed fees unless expressly agreed in a Change Order.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **August 19, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**) (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for additional periods on terms and conditions to be negotiated in good faith, taking into account the then-current scope of Services and pricing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for its convenience, without cause, by providing Vendor with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** specifying the effective date of termination. Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience by providing Client with at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement (in whole or in part) or any SOW for cause upon written notice to the other Party if:

  (a) the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice detailing the breach; or

  (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into liquidation, is placed under judicial management, or any analogous event occurs under applicable law.

4.5 **Termination for Non-Payment.** Notwithstanding Section 4.4, Vendor may terminate this Agreement or the applicable SOW upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Client if any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after its due date and Client fails to cure such non-payment within such notice period.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:

  (a) Client shall pay Vendor all fees and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, including (i) all amounts due for Services and Deliverables completed or in progress, (ii) all non-cancellable third-party costs incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf, and (iii) any reasonable wind-down costs directly resulting from termination;

  (b) Vendor shall cease performance of the terminated Services and shall cooperate with Client in an orderly transition of Services to Client or its designee, for a period and on terms (including fees) to be agreed in good faith;

  (c) each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s election, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to the retention rights in Section 5.4 and any applicable legal record-keeping requirements; and

  (d) any licenses granted to Client under Article VI shall terminate in accordance with their terms, except for any perpetual licenses expressly stated in a SOW.

4.7 **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Articles III (to the extent of accrued payment obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (as to post-termination reporting and remedies for prior failures), XIII (to the extent of pending Change Orders), XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provisions stated to survive.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.** For Services involving hosted or managed systems, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such systems achieve a minimum monthly availability of **99.9%** (excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Force Majeure Events), as measured over each calendar month.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance during agreed maintenance windows, which shall not count as downtime for purposes of calculating availability. Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to notify Client at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance of any scheduled maintenance which is expected to cause material service interruption.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to respond to and resolve incidents in accordance with the following target Service Levels, unless otherwise specified in a SOW:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Major system outage or critical business impact.  
    - Target Response Time: within 1 hour of notification (24x7).  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within 4 hours.

  - **Severity 2 (High):** Significant functionality or performance issue.  
    - Target Response Time: within 4 Business Hours.  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within 1 Business Day.

  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issue with limited business impact.  
    - Target Response Time: within 1 Business Day.  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: within 5 Business Days.

  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issue or general question.  
    - Target Response Time: within 2 Business Days.  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: on a best-efforts basis.

12.4 **SLA Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the availability targets in Section 12.1 for any given calendar month, Client may be entitled to service credits as follows (unless otherwise specified in a SOW):

  (a) Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly fees for the affected Service.

  (b) Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly fees for the affected Service.

  (c) Availability below 98.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly fees for the affected Service.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the availability targets, without prejudice to any other remedies for material breaches.

12.5 **Exclusions.** SLA calculations shall exclude any downtime or service degradation caused by:

  (a) Client’s acts or omissions or those of its third-party providers;

  (b) Client’s failure to comply with this Agreement or Vendor’s reasonable instructions;

  (c) Scheduled Maintenance; or

  (d) Force Majeure Events.

15.1 **Definition.** A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those affecting the affected Party’s own workforce), government actions, embargoes, denial-of-service attacks or other large-scale cyber incidents affecting infrastructure providers, or failure of utilities or telecommunications not reasonably within the Party’s control.

15.2 **Notice.** The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, describing its nature, expected duration, and the steps being taken to mitigate its impact.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** The obligations of the affected Party (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, to the extent such obligations are affected by the Force Majeure Event, and the time for performance shall be extended accordingly.

15.4 **Mitigation.** The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.5 **Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period of more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or, if the entire Agreement is materially affected, this Agreement, by providing thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
