## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General.** The Service Provider shall perform the Services and provide the Deliverables for the benefit of the Client in accordance with the terms of this Agreement and each applicable SOW. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify: (a) a description of the Services and Deliverables; (b) the applicable Project Plan, including key milestones and delivery dates; (c) acceptance criteria; (d) Fees and payment schedule; and (e) any special terms or conditions unique to that SOW.

2.2 **Nature of Services.** The Services may include, without limitation:

2.2.1 Technology strategy and advisory services, including assessments, roadmaps, and solution architecture to support the Client’s commercial operations;

2.2.2 Implementation, configuration, and integration of software platforms, enterprise systems, and related infrastructure, whether hosted on-premises, in the cloud, or in hybrid environments;

2.2.3 Development of custom software components, integrations, and scripts, including configuration of APIs, user interfaces, and data flows, to meet the functional and technical requirements specified in an SOW;

2.2.4 Provision of managed support services, including Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 support and maintenance, incident management, problem resolution, monitoring, and periodic system health checks;

2.2.5 Training, knowledge transfer, and documentation services, including preparation of user manuals, technical documentation, training materials, and conduct of training sessions for the Client’s personnel;

2.2.6 Project management and governance, including periodic status reporting, risk and issue management, change tracking, and coordination with the Client’s project team and third-party vendors.

2.3 **Deliverables and Acceptance.**

2.3.1 For each SOW, the Parties shall define Deliverables and associated acceptance criteria. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW, the Client shall have ten (10) Business Days from delivery of a Deliverable (the **“Review Period”**) to review and either: (a) provide written acceptance; or (b) provide written notice of rejection specifying material non-conformities with the acceptance criteria.

2.3.2 If the Client does not provide any written response within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the last day of the Review Period, provided that the Service Provider has given the Client a written reminder at least two (2) Business Days prior to the expiration of the Review Period.

2.3.3 If the Client rejects a Deliverable in accordance with **Section 2.3.1**, the Service Provider shall, at no additional cost to the Client, promptly correct the identified non-conformities and re-submit the Deliverable. The Client shall then have an additional Review Period to review the corrected Deliverable. If the Deliverable fails to conform after two (2) good faith re-submissions, the Parties shall escalate the matter in accordance with **Article XIV** and may agree to a remedial plan, fee adjustment, or termination of the relevant SOW.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**

2.4.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services: (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner; (b) with due skill, care, and diligence; (c) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and best practices; (d) in compliance with Applicable Law; and (e) in accordance with the Project Plan and Service Levels.

2.4.2 The Service Provider shall designate a project manager who shall serve as the primary contact for coordination and communications with the Client’s designated project manager. The Parties shall hold regular status meetings or calls as specified in the SOW.

2.4.3 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet all milestones and delivery dates set forth in the applicable Project Plan. Any anticipated delay shall be promptly notified to the Client, including the reasons for the delay and a proposed mitigation plan.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**

2.5.1 The Client shall provide, in a timely manner and at no cost to the Service Provider, such access to its premises, systems, personnel, and information as reasonably required for the Service Provider to perform the Services.

2.5.2 The Client shall designate a project sponsor and project manager with authority to make decisions on behalf of the Client regarding the Services and to facilitate coordination with Client stakeholders.

2.5.3 The Client shall be responsible for obtaining all necessary licenses, consents, and approvals from third parties (including licensors and other vendors) required for the Service Provider to perform the Services on or in connection with such third-party systems or software, unless otherwise explicitly provided in an SOW.

2.5.4 The Service Provider shall be entitled to rely on all instructions, decisions, approvals, and information provided by the Client as accurate and complete. The Service Provider shall not be liable for delays or additional costs resulting from incomplete or inaccurate Client information or failures by the Client to perform its obligations.

2.6 **Subcontracting.** The Service Provider may use Subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (a) the Service Provider remains fully responsible for the acts, omissions, and performance of any Subcontractor as if performed by the Service Provider itself; and (b) where the Subcontractor will have access to Client Confidential Information or Personal Data, the Client’s prior written consent shall be obtained (such consent not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed). The Service Provider shall ensure that any Subcontractor is bound by written obligations of confidentiality, data protection, and IP ownership that are no less protective of the Client than those set forth in this Agreement.

2.7 **Non-Exclusivity.** The Services are provided on a non-exclusive basis, and nothing in this Agreement shall prevent the Client from engaging any other service provider to perform services similar to or competitive with the Services, nor shall it prevent the Service Provider from providing services to any other clients, provided that the Service Provider complies with its obligations under **Article V** (Confidentiality) and **Article XI** (Data Protection and Privacy).

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total aggregate contract value under this Agreement shall be **Eight Hundred Fifty-Six Thousand Two Hundred Seventy United States Dollars (US$856,270)**, subject to adjustment in accordance with approved Change Orders and additional SOWs. Individual SOWs shall allocate portions of this contract value to specific projects or Service phases.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** Fees may consist of one or more of the following components, as set forth in each SOW:

3.2.1 **Fixed Fees.** Fixed-price amounts for clearly defined Deliverables or phases of the project, payable upon completion of milestones or acceptance of Deliverables.

3.2.2 **Time and Materials (T&M).** Hourly or daily rates for personnel categories, subject to an estimated budget. The Service Provider shall notify the Client if it anticipates exceeding any T&M estimate by more than ten percent (10%) and obtain the Client’s written approval before proceeding.

3.2.3 **Recurring Fees.** Monthly, quarterly, or annual fees for managed services, support, or maintenance, billed in advance or in arrears as specified in the SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**

3.3.1 The Service Provider shall invoice the Client in accordance with the milestone schedule and billing frequency set out in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, invoices for fixed-fee Deliverables shall be issued upon acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the relevant Deliverable, and invoices for recurring services shall be issued monthly in arrears.

3.3.2 All undisputed amounts invoiced by the Service Provider shall be due and payable by the Client within **sixty (60) days** from the date of invoice (**“Net 60”** payment terms). Payment shall be made in the currency specified in the SOW, which, absent contrary indication, shall be United States Dollars.

3.3.3 The Client shall notify the Service Provider in writing of any disputed amounts within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of an invoice, specifying the basis for the dispute. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute. The Client shall pay the undisputed portion of any invoice within the Net 60 payment term.

3.4 **Late Payment.**

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate equal to the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law. The accrual or receipt of interest shall not limit the Service Provider’s other rights and remedies.

3.4.2 If undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of late payment, the Service Provider may suspend performance of the Services until such amounts are paid, provided that the Service Provider has given the Client at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice of its intent to suspend in accordance with this **Section 3.4.2**.

3.5 **Taxes.** Fees are exclusive of any value-added tax, sales tax, or similar indirect taxes, which shall be added to the invoice and paid by the Client where applicable. Each Party shall be responsible for its own income taxes. The Service Provider shall cooperate with the Client to provide reasonable documentation to support any tax exemptions or withholdings as required by Applicable Law.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

3.6.1 The Client shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and subsistence, in accordance with the Client’s travel and expense policy as communicated to the Service Provider in writing.

3.6.2 Expense reimbursement claims shall be supported by appropriate receipts and documentation. The Service Provider shall invoice approved expenses together with or separately from the Services and clearly distinguish them as reimbursable expenses.

3.7 **Set-Off.** Except to the extent required by Applicable Law or as expressly permitted under this Agreement, neither Party shall set off any amounts owed to the other Party under this Agreement against any other amounts that may be owed by such other Party.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in effect until **19 July 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**), collectively the **“Term”**.

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Agreement for one or more additional periods on terms and conditions to be agreed. In the absence of a written renewal, this Agreement shall expire on the Expiration Date, except that any SOW then in effect shall continue until completion or earlier termination in accordance with its terms.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience by providing the other Party not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice.

4.3.2 Upon termination for convenience by the Client, the Client shall pay the Service Provider: (a) all Fees for Services performed and Deliverables accepted (or deemed accepted) up to the effective date of termination; (b) any non-cancellable commitments properly incurred by the Service Provider for the benefit of the Client; and (c) any agreed termination fees set forth in the applicable SOW, if any.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment) after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they fall due; (b) enters into liquidation (other than for the purpose of solvent amalgamation or reconstruction); (c) has a receiver, administrator, or similar officer appointed over all or substantially all of its assets; or (d) ceases or threatens to cease to carry on business.

4.5 **Effects of Termination.**

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason: (a) all rights and licenses granted hereunder (other than those expressly stated to survive) shall immediately terminate; (b) each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**; and (c) each Party shall pay all undisputed amounts then due and payable.

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not automatically terminate any SOW then in effect, unless such SOW is expressly terminated pursuant to this **Article IV**. In the absence of such termination, each SOW shall continue in full force and effect in accordance with its terms, and this Agreement shall remain in effect solely for purposes of such SOW.

4.5.3 The expiry or termination of this Agreement (or any SOW) shall be without prejudice to any rights or remedies that may have accrued to either Party up to the effective date of such expiry or termination.

4.6 **Survival.** The provisions of **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.** “Confidential Information” means any and all information disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**), whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, strategies, financial information, technical data, trade secrets, Client Materials, Personal Data, and the terms and conditions of this Agreement (but not its existence).

5.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was lawfully known by the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party; (c) is lawfully received from a third party not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Confidentiality Obligations.**

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall: (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement; (b) restrict disclosure of the Confidential Information to those of its employees, officers, directors, Affiliates, Subcontractors, and professional advisers who have a need to know for such purposes and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less protective than those in this Agreement; and (c) protect such Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party shall promptly notify the Disclosing Party upon discovery of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information and shall cooperate with the Disclosing Party in every reasonable way to help regain possession of the Confidential Information and prevent further unauthorized use or disclosure.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.** If the Receiving Party is required by Applicable Law, court order, or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information, it may do so provided that, to the extent legally permissible, it gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in any effort to obtain a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain: (a) one copy solely for archival purposes; and (b) any Confidential Information that it is required to retain under Applicable Law, which shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.** The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or is subject to Data Protection Laws, respectively.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.** If the Services include hosting, managed services, or systems support, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure the availability of the covered systems at least **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and downtime caused by a Force Majeure Event or the Client’s acts or omissions.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** The Service Provider may perform scheduled maintenance during maintenance windows agreed in the SOW. The Service Provider shall provide the Client with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice of scheduled maintenance expected to cause system unavailability.

12.3 **Incident Response Times.** The Service Provider shall respond to incidents in accordance with the following priority levels (unless otherwise specified in the SOW):

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete system outage or critical business impact. Response within one (1) hour, work continuously until resolution or workaround.
- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation with high business impact. Response within four (4) hours, work during Business Hours until resolution.
- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Limited impact issue, workaround exists. Response within one (1) Business Day.
- **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, general inquiries. Response within three (3) Business Days.

12.4 **SLA Credits.** If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** in any calendar month, the Client shall be entitled to receive service credits as follows, to be applied against future invoices:

- Uptime 99.0% to < 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services;
- Uptime 98.0% to < 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services;
- Uptime < 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services.

Service credits are the Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitments, without prejudice to the Client’s rights under **Section 4.4** in the event of persistent failures.

12.5 **Reporting.** The Service Provider shall provide the Client with monthly SLA reports summarising system availability, incidents, and performance against the agreed Service Levels.
