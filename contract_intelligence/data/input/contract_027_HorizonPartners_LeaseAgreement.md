# MASTER COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT  

This MASTER COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT (this **“Lease”** or **“Agreement”**) is made and entered into as of September 10, 2025 (the **“Effective Date”**), by and between:

- **Horizon Partners**, a [●] organized and existing under the laws of the State of California, having its principal place of business at 2450 Market Street, Suite 1800, San Francisco, California 94103 (**“Horizon Partners”**, the **“Landlord”**, **“Vendor”** or **“Service Provider”**); and  
- **Contoso Enterprises**, a [●] organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware, authorized to do business in California, having its principal place of business at 1000 Innovation Drive, Suite 500, San Jose, California 95134 (**“Contoso Enterprises”**, the **“Tenant”** or **“Client”** or **“Company”**).

Landlord and Tenant may hereinafter be referred to individually as a **“Party”** and collectively as the **“Parties.”**

## PREAMBLE AND RECITALS

WHEREAS, Landlord is the fee simple owner, ground lessee, or master lessee, as applicable, of that certain office building and related improvements commonly known as **Horizon Tech Tower**, located at 3000 Bayfront Plaza, San Jose, California 95134 (the **“Building”**), together with the underlying real property and associated common areas (the **“Property”**);

WHEREAS, Tenant desires to lease from Landlord, and Landlord desires to lease to Tenant, certain office premises in the Building for general office, administrative, and other lawful business purposes consistent with a high-quality multi-tenant office building, all pursuant to the terms and conditions set forth herein;

WHEREAS, Landlord has represented to Tenant that the Building and the Premises (as defined below) are suitable for Tenant’s intended business use, subject to Tenant’s obligations to comply with all applicable Legal Requirements (as defined below) and to obtain any required governmental approvals;

WHEREAS, the Parties intend that this Lease shall be a full-service commercial lease including, without limitation, the provision of certain building services, access to Common Areas (as defined below), and the allocation of costs and responsibilities between the Parties;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to set forth in this Lease their mutual agreements with respect to the lease of the Premises, the payment of Rent (as defined below), the provision of building services, risk allocation, and related matters;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, agreements, representations, warranties, and undertakings contained herein, and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties, intending to be legally bound, hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF LEASED PREMISES, SERVICES, AND USE

2.1 **Demise of Premises.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Lease, Landlord hereby leases to Tenant, and Tenant hereby leases from Landlord, the Premises, together with the non-exclusive right to use the Common Areas (subject to reasonable rules and regulations promulgated by Landlord from time to time), for the Term and in consideration of the Rent reserved herein.

2.2 **Use of Premises.**

2.2.1 **Permitted Use.** Tenant shall use the Premises solely for general office, administrative, research and development (non-wet lab, non-hazardous), software development, meeting rooms, training, and ancillary uses customarily incident thereto (such as employee break rooms, storage areas, and server rooms), and for no other purpose without Landlord’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed.

2.2.2 **Compliance with Legal Requirements.** Tenant shall, at its sole cost and expense, comply with all Legal Requirements now or hereafter in effect relating to Tenant’s use and occupancy of the Premises, including all fire and safety codes, ADA accessibility obligations arising from Tenant’s specific use or alterations, and data protection laws applicable to Tenant’s operations.

2.2.3 **Prohibited Uses.** Tenant shall not use or permit the Premises to be used for (a) any illegal or immoral purpose; (b) any use that would cause the Building or any part thereof to be in violation of any Legal Requirements or Building insurance requirements; (c) any heavy industrial, medical, or laboratory use involving Hazardous Materials (except for de minimis amounts customarily used in normal office operations, such as cleaning supplies or printer toner, and used and stored in compliance with applicable law); (d) residential purposes; or (e) any purpose that unreasonably generates excessive noise, vibration, odor, or traffic inconsistent with a first-class office building.

2.3 **Landlord’s Work and Condition of Premises.**

2.3.1 **Delivery Condition.** Landlord shall deliver the Premises to Tenant in a commercially reasonable “warm shell” condition, with base building systems (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, life safety, and vertical transportation) in good working order and code compliant as of the Commencement Date, subject to ordinary wear and tear and any punch list items agreed upon in writing by the Parties.

2.3.2 **Tenant Improvements.** If a Work Letter is attached as Exhibit B, it shall set forth the scope, allocation of costs, and schedule for Tenant-specific improvements to the Premises (**“Tenant Improvements”**). Tenant Improvements shall, unless expressly stated otherwise, become part of the Premises and shall remain upon expiration or termination, except for Tenant’s trade fixtures and personal property.

2.3.3 **Acceptance of Premises.** Subject to Landlord’s express obligations in this Lease (including any Landlord’s Work), Tenant’s taking possession of all or any portion of the Premises shall constitute Tenant’s acknowledgment that the Premises are in the condition required for Tenant’s occupancy, except for latent defects and any items listed on a written punch list delivered by Tenant to Landlord within thirty (30) days after Tenant’s first taking of possession of the Premises.

2.4 **Services Provided by Landlord.**

2.4.1 **Base Building Services.** During the Term, Landlord shall provide, or cause to be provided, the following Services in a manner consistent with first-class multi-tenant office buildings in the San Jose, California market:

   (a) **HVAC.** Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (**“HVAC”**) sufficient to maintain reasonable and customary office comfort levels during Business Hours (8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Monday through Friday, excluding Building holidays), subject to Tenant’s reasonable cooperation in maintaining a balanced load and not exceeding design capacities.

   (b) **Electricity.** Electrical service suitable for normal office use, including lighting and standard office equipment, but not including, without limitation, extraordinary power requirements for server rooms or specialized equipment, which shall require Landlord’s prior written approval and may be subject to additional charges as Additional Rent.

   (c) **Water and Sewer.** Reasonable quantities of domestic water for restrooms and break rooms, and sewer service, as required for ordinary office use.

   (d) **Janitorial Services.** Nightly weekday janitorial services for the Premises (excluding specialized cleaning of Tenant’s equipment), as well as regular cleaning of Common Areas.

   (e) **Security and Access.** Reasonable security measures for Building entrances and Common Areas, including remote monitoring, keycard access or similar systems, and after-hours access for Tenant’s employees in accordance with Landlord’s standard access control policies. Landlord does not, however, guaranty security for Tenant’s persons or property and is not an insurer of Tenant’s security risks.

   (f) **Elevator Service.** Passenger elevator service during Business Hours and as otherwise reasonably required for Tenant’s ingress and egress, as well as freight elevator access by appointment for move-ins, move-outs, and deliveries.

2.4.2 **Operating Standards.** Landlord shall operate and maintain the Building, Common Areas, and Building systems in a manner consistent with comparable first-class office buildings in the region, subject to reasonable wear and tear, casualty, and Force Majeure Events. Landlord may modify the nature, scope, or manner of providing Services as reasonably required for Building operations or Legal Requirements, provided that such changes do not materially and adversely affect Tenant’s use of the Premises in a manner disproportionate to other similarly situated tenants.

2.4.3 **Service Interruptions.** In the event of an interruption, curtailment, or suspension of any Services that materially interferes with Tenant’s ability to use the Premises for more than three (3) consecutive Business Days and is caused by Landlord’s negligence or failure to maintain the Building systems (and is not caused by Tenant, a Force Majeure Event, or a utility provider beyond Landlord’s reasonable control), then, as Tenant’s sole remedy (subject to Section 9.3), Base Rent shall equitably abate with respect to the affected portion of the Premises for the period of such material interference continuing beyond such three (3) Business Day period.

2.5 **Tenant’s Obligations Concerning the Premises.**

2.5.1 **Maintenance by Tenant.** Tenant shall keep the interior non-structural portions of the Premises, including interior walls, doors, finishes, fixtures, and Tenant’s own equipment, in a clean, safe, and sanitary condition, and in good order and repair, except for reasonable wear and tear and damage by casualty or condemnation (addressed in Section 4.7).

2.5.2 **Alterations by Tenant.** Subject to Landlord’s prior written consent (not unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed), Tenant may make non-structural alterations and improvements to the Premises consistent with its permitted use, provided that such work shall comply with all Legal Requirements, using licensed contractors, and shall not adversely affect Building systems or the structural integrity of the Building. All alterations shall, at Landlord’s option, either remain with the Premises upon Lease termination or be removed by Tenant at Tenant’s expense, with Tenant restoring the affected areas to a condition reasonably acceptable to Landlord, except as otherwise agreed in writing.

2.6 **Parking.** Landlord shall make available to Tenant, during the Term, the right to use up to one (1) unreserved parking stall per 300 rentable square feet of the Premises (or such other parking ratio as is customary for the Building), at then-standard Building rates if applicable. Tenant’s parking rights shall be subject to Landlord’s reasonable parking rules and any parking covenants or restrictions affecting the Property.

2.7 **Contract Value.** The Parties acknowledge that the estimated aggregate economic value associated with this Lease, including Base Rent, Additional Rent projections, and related charges, is approximately **Three Million One Hundred Eighty-One Thousand Four Hundred Sixty-Five U.S. Dollars (US$3,181,465)** over the Term, subject to reconciliation of Operating Expenses and Taxes and any adjustments or abatements under this Lease.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES, RENT, AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Rent Generally.** Tenant shall pay to Landlord, without notice, demand, setoff, deduction, or counterclaim (except as expressly permitted herein), the Base Rent and all Additional Rent due under this Lease, in lawful money of the United States of America, in immediately available funds, to the address specified by Landlord in Section 16.8, or by electronic funds transfer to an account designated by Landlord in writing.

3.2 **Base Rent; Schedule.**

3.2.1 **Base Rent Amount.** Base Rent for the Premises shall be payable in equal monthly installments, in advance on or before the first (1st) day of each calendar month during the Term. For purposes of this Lease, the Parties agree that the Base Rent schedule is as follows (illustrative and subject to final space measurement):

- From the Commencement Date through the first anniversary thereof: US$95,000 per month;  
- From the first anniversary through the second anniversary: US$100,000 per month;  
- From the second anniversary through the Expiration Date: US$105,000 per month;

subject to a proportionate adjustment if the actual rentable square footage of the Premises differs from 35,000 rentable square feet based on Landlord’s BOMA measurement.

3.2.2 **Partial Month.** If the Commencement Date is other than the first day of a month, or if the Term expires or terminates on a day other than the last day of a month, then the Base Rent for such partial month shall be prorated on a per diem basis based on a thirty (30)-day month.

3.3 **Additional Rent – Operating Expenses and Taxes.**

3.3.1 **Tenant’s Proportionate Share.** Tenant shall pay, as Additional Rent, Tenant’s proportionate share of Operating Expenses and Taxes incurred by Landlord with respect to the Building and the Property. Tenant’s **“Proportionate Share”** shall be the ratio of the rentable square footage of the Premises to the total rentable square footage of the Building, as reasonably determined by Landlord in accordance with BOMA standards.

3.3.2 **Estimates and Reconciliation.** Landlord may estimate Tenant’s share of Operating Expenses and Taxes on an annual basis and require Tenant to pay such estimated share in equal monthly installments together with Base Rent. Within a reasonable period following the end of each calendar year, Landlord shall deliver to Tenant a statement of actual Operating Expenses and Taxes and Tenant’s share thereof. If Tenant has overpaid, Landlord shall either refund such excess or credit it against future Rent. If Tenant has underpaid, Tenant shall pay the deficiency within thirty (30) days after receipt of such statement.

3.3.3 **Exclusions.** Operating Expenses shall not include (a) Landlord’s federal or state income or franchise taxes; (b) costs of capital improvements (except to the extent such improvements reduce Operating Expenses, in which case such costs may be amortized); (c) costs for which Landlord is reimbursed by insurance or other tenants; (d) leasing commissions; and (e) fines or penalties arising from Landlord’s violation of Legal Requirements (except to the extent caused by Tenant).

3.4 **Payment Terms; Net 60.**

3.4.1 **Due Dates.** Unless otherwise expressly provided, all amounts owed by Tenant under this Lease (including any reimbursements, additional charges, or other sums invoiced by Landlord) shall be paid within **sixty (60) days** after Tenant’s receipt of Landlord’s invoice (**“Net 60”**), provided that Base Rent shall remain due and payable in advance as set forth in Section 3.2 unless Landlord invoices on a consolidated basis.

3.4.2 **Manner of Payment.** Tenant shall make payments by check, ACH, wire transfer, or other method acceptable to Landlord. Tenant shall bear any bank or transaction fees associated with payment methodology (other than fees imposed directly on Landlord by its bank).

3.5 **Late Payment; Interest and Fees.**

3.5.1 **Late Charge.** If any installment of Rent or other sum due hereunder is not received by Landlord within ten (10) days after the date due, Tenant shall pay to Landlord a late charge equal to five percent (5%) of the overdue amount as a reasonable estimate of Landlord’s administrative and processing costs, not as a penalty.

3.5.2 **Interest.** In addition to the late charge, any unpaid amounts shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at the lesser of (a) ten percent (10%) per annum, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

3.6 **Security Deposit.** [If applicable] Upon execution of this Lease, Tenant shall deposit with Landlord a security deposit in the amount of [●] (the **“Security Deposit”**) as security for the full and faithful performance by Tenant of Tenant’s obligations hereunder. Landlord may apply all or any portion of the Security Deposit to cure Tenant’s defaults. If Landlord so applies any portion, Tenant shall replenish the Security Deposit within fifteen (15) days of written demand.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.** Except as otherwise expressly provided herein, each Party shall bear its own expenses relative to this Lease, including legal, consulting, and due diligence costs. Any reimbursable expenses incurred by Landlord on Tenant’s behalf (such as after-hours HVAC at Tenant’s request, additional cleaning services, or special security arrangements) shall be invoiced to Tenant and paid in accordance with the Net 60 terms set forth in Section 3.4.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM, RENEWAL, AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term; Commencement and Expiration.** The term of this Lease (the **“Term”**) shall commence on the **Commencement Date**, which shall be deemed to be the Effective Date (September 10, 2025), unless otherwise specified in a mutually executed commencement memorandum, and shall continue in full force and effect until **November 18, 2027** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

4.2 **Option to Renew.** Provided that Tenant is not then in default beyond any applicable notice and cure period, Tenant shall have one (1) option to renew the Term for an additional period of three (3) years (the **“Renewal Term”**) on terms and conditions substantially similar to those set forth herein, except that (a) Base Rent for the Renewal Term shall be at the then-current fair market rental rate for comparable space in the Building (or comparable buildings), as reasonably determined by Landlord, subject to good faith negotiations; and (b) Tenant shall exercise such renewal option by providing Landlord with written notice at least nine (9) months before the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**

4.3.1 **Tenant Termination for Convenience.** Tenant may, at its sole discretion, terminate this Lease for convenience effective any time on or after the first anniversary of the Commencement Date by providing Landlord with not less than ninety (90) days’ prior written notice. As a condition to such early termination, Tenant shall pay to Landlord, on or before the effective termination date, an early termination fee equal to three (3) months of the then-current Base Rent plus unamortized transaction costs (if any) as set forth in a separate side letter or exhibit, if applicable.

4.3.2 **Landlord Termination for Convenience.** Landlord shall have no general right to terminate this Lease for convenience, except as expressly permitted in connection with casualty, condemnation, or as otherwise authorized in this Lease.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 **Events of Default by Tenant.** Each of the following shall constitute an **Event of Default** by Tenant:

   (a) Tenant fails to pay any installment of Base Rent, Additional Rent, or other sum due hereunder within ten (10) days after written notice from Landlord that such amount is past due (provided that Landlord shall not be required to provide more than two (2) such notices in any twelve (12)-month period, after which Tenant shall be in default automatically if any further payments are not made when due);

   (b) Tenant fails to perform any non-monetary obligation under this Lease and such failure continues for thirty (30) days after written notice from Landlord, or such longer period (not to exceed ninety (90) days) as may be reasonably required to cure, provided Tenant commences cure within thirty (30) days and diligently pursues completion;

   (c) Tenant files, or has filed against it, any petition in bankruptcy or insolvency, or for reorganization, or for the appointment of a receiver or trustee for all or a substantial part of its assets, and such proceeding is not dismissed within ninety (90) days;

   (d) Tenant abandons the Premises for more than thirty (30) consecutive days without reasonable notice to Landlord and continued payment of Rent; or

   (e) Tenant assigns this Lease or sublets all or substantially all of the Premises in violation of Section 16.10.

4.4.2 **Remedies for Tenant Default.** Upon an Event of Default by Tenant, Landlord may, subject to applicable law, in addition to any other rights or remedies available at law or in equity:

   (a) Terminate this Lease by written notice and recover possession of the Premises, without prejudice to Landlord’s rights to recover Rent and damages;  

   (b) Enter the Premises and relet all or any portion thereof for Tenant’s account, applying any rent collected first to reletting expenses and then to Tenant’s outstanding obligations;  

   (c) Accelerate the remaining Rent due for the balance of the Term (discounted to present value at a reasonable discount rate) to the extent permitted by law; and  

   (d) Exercise any other remedies available under California law, subject to the limitations in Article IX.

4.4.3 **Events of Default by Landlord.** Landlord shall be in default if it fails to perform any of its material obligations under this Lease and such failure continues for thirty (30) days after written notice from Tenant, or such longer period (not to exceed ninety (90) days) as may be reasonably required to cure, provided Landlord commences cure within thirty (30) days and diligently pursues completion.

4.4.4 **Tenant Remedies for Landlord Default.** If Landlord is in default, Tenant may, subject to applicable law and the terms of this Lease:

   (a) Seek specific performance or injunctive relief;  

   (b) Recover actual direct damages subject to the limitations in Article IX; and  

   (c) In the case of material, continuing failure of critical building services that materially and adversely affect Tenant’s use of the Premises, terminate this Lease upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice if Landlord fails to cure within such period.

4.5 **Holdover.** If Tenant remains in possession of the Premises after the Expiration Date or earlier termination without Landlord’s written consent, such possession shall be deemed a month-to-month tenancy on the same terms and conditions herein, except that Base Rent shall be increased to one hundred fifty percent (150%) of the last monthly Base Rent, plus Additional Rent. Either Party may terminate such month-to-month tenancy on thirty (30) days’ written notice.

4.6 **Effects of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Lease for any reason:

4.6.1 Tenant shall immediately vacate the Premises, remove its personal property and trade fixtures (unless otherwise agreed), repair any damage caused by such removal, and surrender the Premises to Landlord in broom-clean condition, reasonable wear and tear excepted.

4.6.2 All obligations of the Parties shall cease as of the effective date of termination, except for those provisions that expressly or by their nature survive termination, including without limitation Sections 3.5, 4.6, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 12 (as applicable), 14, 15, and 16.

4.6.3 Tenant shall pay all Rent accrued through the date of termination and any other amounts then due or coming due as a result of such termination, subject to applicable law and Article IX.

4.7 **Casualty and Condemnation.** In the event of a casualty or condemnation affecting the Premises or Building, the rights and obligations of the Parties shall be determined in accordance with applicable California law and customary casualty/condemnation provisions to be set forth in a separate exhibit or as reasonably agreed by the Parties, including potential termination rights, rent abatement, and repair obligations.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.** For purposes of this Lease, **“Confidential Information”** means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party to the other Party, whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential, including, without limitation: financial information; business plans; building security systems and protocols; tenant lists; proprietary technology; data center locations and configurations; and any personal data or client information processed by Tenant in the Premises. Confidential Information includes the terms and conditions of this Lease, except as otherwise required by law.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.** Each Party (the **“Receiving Party”**) agrees:

5.2.1 To protect the Confidential Information of the other Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care;

5.2.2 Not to use the Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance or enforcement of this Lease;

5.2.3 Not to disclose the Confidential Information to any third party except to Affiliates, employees, contractors, advisors, lenders, or investors who have a need to know such information and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

5.3 **Exceptions.** The obligations in this Article V shall not apply to information that:

5.3.1 Is or becomes generally known to the public through no breach by the Receiving Party;

5.3.2 Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records;

5.3.3 Is received by the Receiving Party from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality to the Disclosing Party; or

5.3.4 Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Required Disclosure.** If the Receiving Party is required by law, court order, or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permitted) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information as is legally required.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon expiration or termination of this Lease, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the Disclosing Party’s option) all tangible forms of Confidential Information, except to the extent retention is required by law or necessary for enforcement of rights under this Lease. Electronic copies stored in standard backup systems may be retained in accordance with normal retention cycles, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.** The obligations under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive termination or expiration of this Lease for a period of **five (5) years**, except for trade secrets, which shall remain confidential for so long as they retain trade secret status under applicable law.

## ARTICLE VI  
## INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

6.1 **No Transfer of IP Ownership.** This Lease does not transfer ownership of either Party’s Intellectual Property Rights. Each Party retains all right, title, and interest in and to its pre-existing intellectual property and any enhancements or derivatives thereof, except as expressly provided herein.

Tenant shall not reverse engineer, copy, modify, or create derivative works based upon such systems or materials, except as necessary for Tenant’s ordinary use of the Premises and Building services in accordance with this Lease.

6.3 **Tenant’s Business Operations.** Landlord acknowledges that Tenant may, within the Premises, create, develop, or use proprietary software, content, inventions, and other intellectual property (**“Tenant IP”**). Tenant IP shall remain the sole and exclusive property of Tenant (or its licensors), and Landlord shall have no rights therein except as expressly granted by Tenant in writing.

6.4 **License Grants.**

6.4.1 **Landlord License to Tenant.** Landlord hereby grants to Tenant a non-exclusive, non-transferable (except as permitted under Section 16.10), revocable license during the Term to use the Building’s management systems, access control systems, and any related software or documentation solely as necessary for Tenant’s use and occupancy of the Premises under this Lease.

6.4.2 **Tenant License to Landlord.** Tenant grants Landlord a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use Tenant’s name, logo, and trademarks solely for Building directories, signage, emergency planning, and other customary property management purposes, subject to Tenant’s brand guidelines as reasonably provided.

6.5 **Restrictions.** Tenant shall not (and shall not permit any third party to) remove, alter, or obscure any proprietary notices on any Building systems or materials, or use any Landlord IP for any purpose outside the scope of this Lease. 15.2.2 Uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably possible.

15.3 **Limitations.** Force Majeure Events shall not excuse or delay Tenant’s obligation to pay Rent, except as expressly provided elsewhere in this Lease (e.g., rent abatement following casualty) or as may be required by applicable law.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than one hundred eighty (180) consecutive days and materially prevents Tenant from using the Premises for its intended purpose, either Party may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, terminate this Lease without further liability (except for obligations accrued prior to termination).
