## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Company comprehensive Maintenance Services in relation to the Client Systems, which shall include, without limitation: (a) preventive and corrective maintenance; (b) remote and on-site technical support; (c) monitoring and performance optimization; (d) patch management and updates; (e) incident management and problem resolution; and (f) reporting and documentation, all in accordance with this Article II, the SLA in Article XII, and any applicable SOW.

2.2 **Covered Client Systems.**  
(a) The Client Systems covered under this Agreement shall include, at a minimum:  

   (i) Core application servers, file servers, and database servers located at the Company’s primary data centers in Texas;  
   (ii) Network infrastructure components, including switches, routers, firewalls, and load balancers;  
   (iii) Up to five hundred (500) employee workstations and associated peripheral devices;  
   (iv) Core enterprise applications and middleware components identified in the initial SOW; and  
   (v) Any additional systems mutually agreed upon by the Parties in writing via a Change Order or subsequent SOW.

(b) The specific inventory of Client Systems may be refined and updated from time to time by mutual written agreement. Updates to the inventory that significantly alter the volume or complexity of the Services may result in corresponding adjustments to Fees pursuant to Article XIII.

2.3 **Preventive Maintenance.**  
The Service Provider shall perform scheduled preventive maintenance on the Client Systems, including, but not limited to:  

   (a) Regular health checks on servers and network devices;  
   (b) Reviewing system logs and performance metrics;  
   (c) Applying recommended patches and firmware updates, subject to the Company’s change management policies;  
   (d) Performing backup verification tests and checking storage integrity; and  
   (e) Providing written summaries of preventive activities performed, at least on a monthly basis.

Preventive maintenance shall be scheduled during non-peak hours (as reasonably defined by the Company) and shall be coordinated with the Company’s designated contact in accordance with Section 12.3 regarding Planned Downtime.

2.4 **Corrective Maintenance and Incident Management.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall provide corrective maintenance to address Incidents, errors, failures, or performance degradations affecting the Client Systems.  

(b) The Service Provider shall classify Incidents into severity levels (e.g., Critical, High, Medium, Low) and shall respond and resolve such Incidents within the timeframes set forth in the SLA (Article XII).  

(c) Corrective maintenance activities shall include: diagnostics, troubleshooting, repair or replacement of hardware components (where within the Service Provider’s scope), reconfiguration, and liaison with third-party vendors as necessary.

2.5 **Monitoring and Proactive Services.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall implement monitoring tools and processes to provide 24x7 monitoring of critical components of the Client Systems, including system availability, CPU load, memory usage, storage capacity, and network performance.  

(b) The Service Provider shall implement alerts and thresholds to detect anomalies and potential failures and shall take proactive steps to mitigate issues before they impact business operations.  

(c) The Service Provider shall provide the Company with access to monitoring dashboards or periodic reports summarizing system performance, incident trends, and recommendations for improvements.

2.6 **Updates, Patches, and Upgrades.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall identify necessary security patches, bug fixes, and minor updates for hardware, operating systems, and covered software as part of the Maintenance Services.  

(b) The Service Provider shall obtain the Company’s prior written approval for major version upgrades or updates that may materially impact system functionality or introduce significant changes.  

(c) The Service Provider shall ensure that patches and updates are tested in a controlled environment (where reasonably practicable) before deployment to production systems.

2.7 **Documentation and Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall maintain appropriate documentation of all Services performed, including maintenance logs, incident reports, change records, and configuration documentation. The Service Provider shall provide the Company with:

   (a) Monthly summary reports detailing Services performed, Incidents logged and resolved, SLA performance metrics, and planned upcoming maintenance;  
   (b) Incident-specific reports upon request by the Company for Critical or High severity Incidents;  
   (c) Quarterly review reports and participation in quarterly service review meetings to discuss performance, issues, and continuous improvement opportunities.

2.8 **Personnel and Subcontractors.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall assign appropriately qualified and trained personnel to perform the Services, and such personnel shall have the necessary skills and experience to meet the performance standards set forth in this Agreement.  

(b) The Service Provider may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (i) the Service Provider remains fully responsible and liable for the acts and omissions of such subcontractors; (ii) such subcontractors are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection consistent with this Agreement; and (iii) no subcontractor shall be engaged for Services involving access to sensitive Client Data without the Company’s prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld).

2.9 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall perform the Services:  
   (i) in a timely, professional, and workmanlike manner, using qualified personnel and in accordance with industry best practices;  
   (ii) in compliance with all applicable Laws, including data protection and information security requirements; and  
   (iii) in accordance with the service levels, response times, and resolution times set forth in the SLA (Article XII).

(b) Any failure to meet the performance standards or SLA metrics shall be addressed in accordance with Article XII, including the provision of service credits or other remedies as specified therein.

2.10 **Excluded Services.**  
Unless explicitly stated in this Agreement or an SOW, the following services are excluded and, if requested, shall be subject to a separate written agreement or Change Order and may be billed at the Service Provider’s then-current time and materials rates:  

   (a) Major system implementations or migrations;  
   (b) Custom software development;  
   (c) Hardware procurement and capital expenditures;  
   (d) End-user training beyond standard support and knowledge transfer;  
   (e) Any services outside the geographic scope or technical scope agreed in the SOW.

2.11 **Cooperation by the Company.**  
The Company shall reasonably cooperate with the Service Provider in the performance of the Services, including by:  

   (a) granting necessary access (physical and logical) to the Client Systems and facilities;  
   (b) providing accurate and up-to-date information regarding the Client Systems;  
   (c) designating a primary point of contact for operational and technical coordination; and  
   (d) timely reviewing and approving proposed changes, upgrades, and maintenance schedules.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value for the Services to be provided during the Initial Term, as defined in Section 4.1, is **US$2,750,454** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to adjustments for approved Change Orders and any additional SOWs.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
(a) The Contract Value shall be comprised of:  
   (i) A fixed annual base maintenance fee for standard Maintenance Services; and  
   (ii) Variable fees for additional services performed on a time and materials basis, where such services are requested by the Company and agreed to in writing.

(b) The Parties may further detail the allocation of the Contract Value across years, service components, and locations in an attached fee schedule or SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall invoice the Company in advance on a **quarterly** basis for the fixed portion of the Fees and in arrears for any variable or time and materials services.  

(b) Each invoice shall itemize the services provided, the billing period, and any applicable taxes, and shall reference this Agreement and the Contract Reference No. MNT-STE-202405-118.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) All undisputed amounts invoiced by the Service Provider shall be due and payable by the Company within **sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt of the invoice (“**Net 60**”), unless otherwise agreed in the applicable SOW.  

(b) Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method to the account specified in the Service Provider’s invoice.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  
(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of the lesser of: (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (ii) the maximum rate permitted under applicable Law, from the due date until the date of payment.  

(b) Prior to assessing interest or suspending Services for non-payment, the Service Provider shall provide the Company with written notice and a ten (10) Business Day cure period.  

(c) The Service Provider shall not suspend Services for non-payment if the Company is disputing the relevant charges in good faith and has provided written notice of such dispute in accordance with Section 3.7.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
(a) The Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes, duties, or assessments (“**Taxes**”), except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or employees.  

(b) The Company shall be responsible for paying all applicable Taxes arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, provided that the Service Provider properly itemizes such Taxes on its invoices.

3.7 **Disputed Charges.**  
If the Company disputes any amount invoiced, it shall notify the Service Provider in writing within thirty (30) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve such disputes promptly. The Company shall pay any undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with this Article III. Failure to dispute an invoice within such thirty (30) day period shall not waive the Company’s rights, but may be considered in evaluating the good faith nature of the dispute.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall bear all ordinary business expenses incurred in performing the Services, including internal overhead, employee salaries, and standard tools.  

(b) The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for pre-approved reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses, including travel, lodging, and meals, incurred in connection with on-site Services, in accordance with the Company’s then-current travel and expense policy, which shall be provided to the Service Provider upon request.  

(c) The Service Provider shall submit supporting documentation (receipts, itineraries) with any expense reimbursement requests.

3.9 **No Set-Off.**  
Except for amounts disputed in good faith and subject to Section 3.7, neither Party shall have the right to set off, withhold, or deduct from any amounts owed to the other Party under this Agreement, except as required by Law or expressly permitted herein.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 24, 2029** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement may be renewed for additional periods of one (1) year each (each, a “**Renewal Term**”) by mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date or the end of any Renewal Term, as applicable.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider. The Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the Company. Upon such termination, the Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, but shall have no further obligations regarding future Fees, except as expressly stated in any SOW or as required by Law.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.  

(b) Without limiting the foregoing, the following shall be considered material breaches:  
   (i) Failure by the Service Provider to meet critical SLA obligations on a repeated basis, after notice and opportunity to cure;  
   (ii) Failure by the Company to pay undisputed Fees when due, after notice and an additional ten (10) Business Day cure period; and  
   (iii) Repeated or serious violations of data protection or confidentiality obligations.

4.5 **Termination for Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) ceases to carry on business in the ordinary course.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
(a) Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:  
   (i) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all undisputed Services performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination;  
   (ii) Each Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the other Party’s election) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession, in accordance with Section 5.5; and  
   (iii) The Service Provider shall provide reasonable cooperation to facilitate a smooth transition of the Services to the Company or a successor provider, as further described in Section 4.7.

(b) Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or obligations of either Party that have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, nor any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination, as specified in Section 16.11.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall, upon the Company’s written request, provide transition assistance for a period of up to ninety (90) days to facilitate the orderly transfer of the Services to the Company or another service provider. Such assistance may include the transfer of documentation, knowledge transfer sessions, and reasonable support for data migration. The Parties shall mutually agree upon any Fees payable to the Service Provider for transition assistance, which shall be at fair and reasonable rates.

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, visually, in writing, or in other tangible form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:  

   (a) Client Data, including personal data;  
   (b) business plans, strategies, pricing, financial information, and forecasts;  
   (c) technical information, system configurations, and security procedures;  
   (d) this Agreement and its terms; and  
   (e) any information derived from Confidential Information.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
   (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
   (b) was rightfully known by the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
   (c) is received from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation; or  
   (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
   (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  
   (b) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent; and  
   (c) protect the confidentiality of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:  
   (a) to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  
   (b) to the extent required by Law or by order of a court or governmental authority, provided that the Receiving Party (where legally permitted) gives prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party and cooperates with the Disclosing Party’s reasonable efforts to seek protective measures.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for the purposes of compliance with Law and enforcement of its rights under this Agreement, and any automatically generated backup copies maintained in the ordinary course of business, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that, with respect to trade secrets and Client Data, such obligations shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Client Data under applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the core Services supporting the availability and operation of the Client Systems are available at least **99.9%** of the time on a monthly basis, excluding Planned Downtime and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall meet the following response and resolution time targets for Incidents, measured from the time the Incident is properly reported by the Company:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):**  
  - Response Time: within 1 hour (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Severity 2 (High):**  
  - Response Time: within 2 hours (Business Days)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 Business Hours  

- **Severity 3 (Medium):**  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 2 Business Days  

- **Severity 4 (Low):**  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

The Parties may further define severity levels and examples in an attached SLA schedule.

12.3 **Planned Downtime.**  
(a) The Service Provider shall provide the Company with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice of any Planned Downtime that is expected to impact the availability of critical Client Systems.  

(b) Planned Downtime shall be scheduled outside the Company’s peak business hours wherever reasonably practicable.  

(c) Planned Downtime of up to eight (8) hours per month shall be excluded from uptime calculations; any additional Planned Downtime may be subject to review and mutual agreement.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the monthly uptime commitment in Section 12.1 or repeatedly fails to meet response or resolution times in Section 12.2, the Company shall be entitled to service credits, calculated as a percentage of the monthly Fees for the affected Services. 12.5 **Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall provide monthly SLA reports, summarizing uptime performance, response/resolution times, Incident volumes, and service credits (if any) due to the Company.
