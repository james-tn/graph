## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services  
The Service Provider agrees to perform, and the Client agrees to purchase, the Services described in this **Article II** and in each applicable Statement of Work executed under this Agreement. The Services shall include, without limitation:

1. The provision of highly skilled technology professionals, including software engineers, architects, DevOps engineers, business analysts, project managers, and quality assurance personnel, to work on the Client’s enterprise software initiatives;  
2. Design, development, customization, configuration, integration, and implementation of software components, platforms, and tools for the Client’s internal and customer-facing systems;  
3. Ongoing support, maintenance, monitoring, and enhancement of existing Client systems and applications; and  
4. Related consulting and advisory services to support technology strategy, architecture, security, performance, and scalability.

The Parties acknowledge that the Services may be further detailed in one or more Statements of Work, which shall be incorporated by reference into this Agreement upon execution.

### 2.2 Specific Deliverables  
Without limiting the generality of **Section 2.1**, the Service Provider shall provide the following types of Deliverables, as may be specified in the applicable SOW:

a. **Requirements and Design Documents:** Functional and technical requirements, system design specifications, architecture diagrams, and related documentation;  

b. **Software Components:** Source code, compiled code, scripts, APIs, integrations, and configuration files for enterprise applications and system integrations;  

c. **Test Artifacts:** Test plans, test cases, automated test scripts, test data, and test reports;  

d. **Operational Documentation:** User guides, administrator manuals, runbooks, deployment instructions, and troubleshooting guides;  

e. **Status and Governance Reports:** Regular status reports, project dashboards, risk logs, issue logs, and other governance documents required by the Client; and  

f. **Training Materials:** Training curricula, presentations, and materials for knowledge transfer sessions with the Client’s personnel.

All Deliverables shall conform to the requirements, acceptance criteria, and format specified in the applicable SOW and shall be subject to the acceptance procedures set forth in **Section 2.6**.

### 2.3 Performance Standards  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

1. In a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and best practices for similar services;  
2. In compliance with all applicable laws, ordinances, regulations, and professional standards, including applicable Data Protection Laws and export control regulations;  
3. Using personnel with appropriate skill, experience, and qualifications to perform the Services to the satisfaction of the Client; and  
4. In a manner designed to meet or exceed the Service Levels and timelines set forth in this Agreement and the applicable SOW.

The Service Provider shall maintain and enforce policies and procedures reasonably designed to ensure high-quality delivery, including quality assurance, change control, and risk management processes.

### 2.4 Project Management and Reporting  
2.4.1 **Project Managers.** Each Party shall designate a project manager to act as the primary point of contact with respect to the Services (each a “Project Manager”). The Project Managers shall meet, as needed, to review progress, resolve issues, and coordinate activities.

2.4.2 **Status Reporting.** The Service Provider shall provide the Client with periodic status reports, in the format and frequency specified in the applicable SOW (e.g., weekly or bi-weekly), describing:

- Tasks completed and in progress;  
- Risks, issues, and mitigation plans;  
- Progress against milestones and timelines; and  
- Any requested changes or deviations from the SOW.

2.4.3 **Meetings.** The Service Provider shall attend regular project governance meetings, stand-ups, and stakeholder reviews as reasonably requested by the Client, which may be conducted in-person or remotely.

### 2.5 Timelines and Milestones  
The Parties shall agree on specific milestones, deliverable due dates, and overall project timelines in each SOW. Time is of the essence with respect to the Service Provider’s obligations. If the Service Provider becomes aware of any circumstance that may delay performance, it shall promptly notify the Client in writing and propose a corrective action plan. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to adjust the schedule where appropriate, provided that any material change shall be documented in a Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

### 2.6 Acceptance of Deliverables  
2.6.1 **Review Period.** Unless otherwise set forth in an SOW, upon delivery of a Deliverable, the Client shall have a period of **ten (10) Business Days** (the “Review Period”) to review and test the Deliverable for conformity with the requirements and acceptance criteria set forth in the SOW.

2.6.2 **Acceptance or Rejection.** Within the Review Period, the Client shall either:

- Provide written notice of acceptance of the Deliverable; or  
- Provide written notice of rejection, specifying in reasonable detail the deficiencies in the Deliverable.

If the Client fails to provide such notice within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, except in the case of latent defects.

2.6.3 **Correction of Deficiencies.** If the Client rejects a Deliverable, the Service Provider shall, at no additional cost to the Client, promptly correct the deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for review. The Client shall then have another Review Period to accept or reject the corrected Deliverable. If after two (2) correction attempts the Deliverable still fails to conform, the Parties shall escalate the issue in accordance with **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**.

### 2.7 Location and Tools  
The Services may be performed at the Client’s premises, the Service Provider’s premises, remotely, or at such other locations as may be agreed upon in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise stated, the Service Provider shall be responsible for providing its own equipment, tools, and software necessary to perform the Services, except for any Client-provided systems or access required by the Client.

### 2.8 Non-Exclusivity  
This Agreement is non-exclusive. The Client may engage other service providers for similar services, and the Service Provider may provide services to other clients, provided that the Service Provider complies with its confidentiality obligations under **Article V** and conflict of interest obligations under **Article VII**.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value and Fee Structure  
3.1.1 **Contract Value.** The total aggregate value of this Agreement, including all SOWs and Change Orders, shall not exceed **US $2,094,901** (Two Million Ninety-Four Thousand Nine Hundred One Dollars), unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to an increase.

3.1.2 **Fee Types.** Fees may consist of one or more of the following, as specified in the applicable SOW:

- Time-and-materials fees based on hourly or daily rates;  
- Fixed-price fees for specific Deliverables, milestones, or Projects; and  
- Retainer or monthly recurring fees for ongoing support and maintenance services.

3.1.3 **Rate Card.** The Service Provider’s standard resource rate card (which may vary by role, seniority, and location) shall be set forth in the applicable SOW or an attached schedule. Any changes to rates during the Term shall be subject to mutual written agreement and may be implemented via Change Order.

### 3.2 Invoicing  
3.2.1 **Invoice Frequency.** Unless otherwise provided in the relevant SOW, the Service Provider shall invoice the Client **monthly in arrears** for Services and Deliverables completed and accepted (or deemed accepted) during the preceding month, as well as any reimbursable expenses incurred.

3.2.2 **Invoice Details.** Each invoice shall include:

- Reference to this Agreement and the relevant SOW;  
- The billing period;  
- A breakdown of Services performed (e.g., resource name/role, hours worked, rates, tasks);  
- A listing of Deliverables delivered and accepted;  
- Details and receipts for any approved reimbursable expenses; and  
- The total amount due in U.S. dollars.

### 3.3 Payment Terms  
3.3.1 **Payment Due Date.** The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** from the date of receipt of a valid invoice (“**Net 60**”).

3.3.2 **Method of Payment.** Payments shall be made by wire transfer, ACH, or such other method as may be agreed by the Parties, to the account designated by the Service Provider in writing.

3.3.3 **Disputed Amounts.** If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice, the Client shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. The Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts promptly.

### 3.4 Late Payment and Interest  
Any undisputed amount not paid by the Client when due shall accrue interest at a rate of the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the date due until the date paid. The imposition or payment of interest shall not excuse or cure any breach of or default under this Agreement.

### 3.5 Taxes  
3.5.1 **Responsibility for Taxes.** The Fees are exclusive of all sales, use, value-added, goods and services, and other similar taxes. The Client shall be responsible for all such taxes (except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property, or payroll), which shall be itemized on the invoices, where applicable.

3.5.2 **Withholding Taxes.** If the Client is required by law to withhold any taxes from payments due to the Service Provider, the Client shall: (a) deduct such taxes from the amount owed; (b) remit the withheld taxes to the appropriate taxing authorities; and (c) provide the Service Provider with official receipts or other documentation evidencing such payment.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursement  
3.6.1 **Pre-Approval.** The Service Provider shall obtain the Client’s prior written approval before incurring any travel or other extraordinary out-of-pocket expenses to be reimbursed by the Client.

3.6.2 **Reimbursement.** Approved expenses shall be reimbursed at cost, without markup, upon submission of itemized expense reports accompanied by reasonable supporting documentation (e.g., receipts, invoices). The Client shall reimburse approved expenses together with the applicable monthly invoice.

### 3.7 No Additional Compensation  
Except as expressly provided in this Agreement or an SOW, the Service Provider shall not be entitled to any additional compensation, bonuses, or benefits, and the Service Provider is solely responsible for all compensation and benefits payable to its Employment Resources.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article, shall remain in effect until **February 8, 2028** (the “Expiration Date”), constituting the initial term (the “Initial Term”).

### 4.2 Renewal  
Upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement may be renewed for one or more additional terms, each as agreed in writing (each a “Renewal Term”). If the Parties do not so agree, this Agreement shall automatically expire on the Expiration Date, subject to the surviving provisions in **Section 16.10**.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for any reason or no reason upon providing the other Party at least **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice**. During such notice period, the Service Provider shall continue to perform the Services in an orderly manner, and the Client shall continue to pay for Services performed and expenses incurred in accordance with this Agreement.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause  
4.4.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or seeking reorganization that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) has a receiver appointed for substantially all of its assets.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination or Expiration  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:

1. The Service Provider shall promptly cease all Services, except as otherwise directed by the Client for transition purposes;  
2. The Client shall pay the Service Provider all undisputed Fees and approved expenses accrued through the effective date of termination, including for any completed Deliverables accepted (or deemed accepted) by the Client;  
3. Each Party shall return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession, as provided in **Section 5.4**; and  
4. The Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate with the Client, at the Client’s request and expense, to transition the Services to the Client or to a third-party service provider, for a period not to exceed sixty (60) days, unless otherwise agreed.

### 4.6 Survival  
The termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not affect the survival of those provisions which, by their nature, are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions)**.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information  
“Confidential Information” means any non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (the “Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (the “Receiving Party”), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or in any other form, including but not limited to business plans, financial information, pricing, technical data, software, specifications, know-how, trade secrets, Client Materials, Personal Data, and information concerning the Disclosing Party’s employees, clients, vendors, and partners. Confidential Information shall also include the terms and conditions of this Agreement, except where disclosure is required by law or as permitted under **Section 5.3**.

### 5.2 Obligations of Confidentiality  
The Receiving Party shall:

1. Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
2. Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted under this Agreement;  
3. Limit access to the Confidential Information to its and its Affiliates’ employees, contractors, and agents who have a need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  
4. Use at least the same degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

### 5.3 Exceptions  
The obligations in **Section 5.2** shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written evidence:

1. Was in the public domain at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
2. Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party, without restriction, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
3. Is lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party who is not under any obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or  
4. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, specific information shall not be deemed to be within the foregoing exceptions merely because it is embraced by more general information within the exceptions.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permissible) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

### 5.5 Survival of Confidentiality Obligations  
The confidentiality obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that with respect to trade secrets, such obligations shall continue for so long as such information constitutes a trade secret under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

To the extent the Services include ongoing system support, monitoring, or managed services, the following Service Levels shall apply, unless superseded by more specific SLA terms in an SOW.

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment  
For systems or services operated or managed by the Service Provider under this Agreement, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve a **monthly uptime percentage of 99.9%**, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events.

### 12.2 Response and Resolution Times  
The Service Provider shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times for incidents reported by the Client:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** System down or major business impact.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant functionality degraded, but workarounds exist.  
  - Initial Response: within 4 hours  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 1 Business Day  

- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues or minor functionality impact.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low):** General questions or minor requests.  
  - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
  - Target Resolution: as mutually agreed

### 12.3 Remedies for SLA Breach  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** for a given calendar month, the Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows (as a percentage of the monthly Fees for the affected service):

- Uptime 99.0% – 99.89%: 5% credit  
- Uptime 98.0% – 98.99%: 10% credit  
- Uptime below 98.0%: 20% credit

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall be the Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitment, without limiting any other remedies for material breach as provided in this Agreement.

### 15.1 Definition

### 15.2 Suspension of Performance  
If a Party is prevented from or delayed in performing its obligations under this Agreement due to a Force Majeure Event, such obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that:

1. The affected Party promptly notifies the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event and its expected duration; and  
2. The affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Termination for Extended Force Majeure  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW upon written notice to the other Party, without liability, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
