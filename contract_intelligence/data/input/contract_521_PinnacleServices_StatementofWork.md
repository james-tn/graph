## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
The Vendor shall provide integrated IT consulting, software development, application management, infrastructure support, and related professional services to the Client under this Agreement. The primary objectives of the Project are to:

(a) Design and implement a unified enterprise application platform to support the Client’s core business operations;  
(b) Migrate certain legacy systems and data to the new platform;  
(c) Provide ongoing Level 1–3 support, maintenance, and enhancement services; and  
(d) Ensure availability, performance, and security in accordance with the Service Levels set out in Article XII.

2.2 **Detailed Description of Services.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Services shall include the following workstreams:

2.2.1 **Solution Design and Architecture.**  
(a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, requirements workshops, and system assessments to document functional and technical requirements;  
(b) Produce a comprehensive Solution Design Document ("SDD") describing system architecture, integrations, data flows, security controls, and infrastructure topology;  
(c) Develop a detailed implementation roadmap including sequencing of releases, cutover strategy, and risk mitigation plans; and  
(d) Present the SDD and roadmap to the Client’s steering committee for review and approval.

2.2.2 **Application Development and Configuration.**  
(a) Configure and customize the enterprise application platform in accordance with the approved SDD and Specifications;  
(b) Develop custom modules, APIs, and integration components to connect with the Client’s existing systems (including but not limited to CRM, ERP, and data warehouse solutions);  
(c) Implement role-based access control, audit logging, and security features consistent with the Client’s internal policies and Applicable Law; and  
(d) Prepare technical documentation, configuration guides, and release notes for each major release.

2.2.3 **Data Migration and Integration.**  
(a) Analyze legacy databases and data sources to identify data quality, mapping, and transformation requirements;  
(b) Design and develop migration scripts and ETL (extract-transform-load) processes;  
(c) Execute data migration test cycles, including mock runs and reconciliations; and  
(d) Perform final production data migration in coordination with the Client’s IT team, minimizing downtime and data inconsistency.

2.2.4 **Testing, Quality Assurance, and Acceptance Support.**  
(a) Develop test plans, test cases, and test scripts covering unit, integration, system, performance, and user acceptance testing;  
(b) Conduct testing cycles and provide test result reports, defect logs, and remediation plans;  
(c) Support Client-led user acceptance testing ("UAT") and address defects within agreed resolution timelines; and  
(d) Assist the Client in performing go-live readiness assessments and sign-off processes.

2.2.5 **Deployment, Cutover, and Hypercare.**  
(a) Plan and execute deployment activities, including environment preparation, configuration promotion, and rollback planning;  
(b) Coordinate cutover activities with the Client and manage transition from legacy systems;  
(c) Provide "hypercare" support for a minimum period of sixty (60) days after each major go-live, including increased support coverage, rapid defect resolution, and daily status reporting; and  
(d) Stabilize the solution and transition to steady-state support as described in Section 2.2.6.

2.2.6 **Managed Services & Application Support.**  
(a) Provide Level 1 (help desk), Level 2 (technical support), and Level 3 (advanced engineering) support for the covered applications;  
(b) Monitor system performance, capacity, and availability, and proactively address incidents and potential issues;  
(c) Apply patches, upgrades, and minor enhancements in accordance with change control procedures set out in Article XIII;  
(d) Maintain configuration documentation and a knowledgebase to support efficient incident resolution; and  
(e) Provide monthly operational reports detailing incidents, resolutions, trends, SLAs, and improvement recommendations.

2.2.7 **Infrastructure and Hosting Support (If Applicable).**  
If the Client elects to utilize Vendor-managed infrastructure, the Vendor shall:  
(a) Provision, configure, and manage cloud or data center resources as agreed;  
(b) Implement backup, disaster recovery, and security controls in line with the Specifications and SLAs; and  
(c) Coordinate with any third-party hosting providers as necessary.

2.2.8 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
(a) Develop training materials, including user guides, quick reference cards, and e-learning modules;  
(b) Conduct training sessions for end users, administrators, and support staff;  
(c) Perform knowledge transfer sessions for the Client’s internal IT team prior to handover; and  
(d) Provide refresher training and training for new functionalities as mutually agreed.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Vendor shall provide, at a minimum, the following Deliverables, which shall be subject to acceptance in accordance with Section 2.5:

(a) Requirements Documentation and Business Process Maps;  
(b) Solution Design Document (SDD) and Technical Architecture Diagrams;  
(c) Configured Application Modules and Integration Components;  
(d) Data Migration Scripts, Mapping Documents, and Reconciliation Reports;  
(e) Test Plans, Test Cases, Test Summary Reports, and Defect Logs;  
(f) Deployment and Cutover Plan;  
(g) Operational Runbooks and Support Procedures;  
(h) Training Materials and Records of Training Sessions;  
(i) Monthly Operational and SLA Performance Reports; and  
(j) Other documents, code repositories, and artifacts as identified in project plans.

2.4 **Project Timeline and Milestones.**  
The Project shall substantially commence on the Effective Date. The key Milestones and indicative target dates (which may be refined by mutual agreement) are:

(a) **Milestone 1 – Requirements & Design Completion:** within ninety (90) days of the Effective Date;  
(b) **Milestone 2 – Core Platform Configuration & Initial Development Completed:** within one hundred eighty (180) days of the Effective Date;  
(c) **Milestone 3 – UAT Completion and Production Go-Live for Phase 1:** within two hundred seventy (270) days of the Effective Date;  
(d) **Milestone 4 – Completion of Phase 2 Enhancements and Secondary Go-Live:** within four hundred fifty (450) days of the Effective Date;  
(e) **Milestone 5 – Transition to Steady-State Support:** no later than twelve (12) months after the first production go-live.

The Parties may adjust the Milestones and target dates through the Change Management process under Article XIII, provided that any adjustments having a material impact on fees, timelines, or resource commitments shall be documented in a Change Order.

2.5 **Acceptance Criteria and Procedures.**  
(a) Each Deliverable shall conform to the applicable Specifications, requirements, and quality standards set forth in this Agreement.  
(b) Upon delivery of a Deliverable, the Client shall have a review period of fifteen (15) Business Days (or such other period as may be agreed for complex Deliverables) to evaluate the Deliverable against the acceptance criteria.

(d) If the Client rejects a Deliverable, the Vendor shall, at no additional cost to the Client (except where the rejection is due to Client-provided materials or scope changes), promptly correct the deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable.

(b) In compliance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII;  
(c) In accordance with all Applicable Law; and  
(d) With appropriate due care to avoid disruption to the Client’s business operations.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall:

(a) Provide timely access to relevant personnel, systems, and facilities as reasonably required for the Vendor to perform the Services;  
(b) Provide accurate and complete information and Client Materials;  
(c) Nominate a primary project manager as a single point of contact; and

(a) If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Client shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the disputed amount and the reasons for the dispute.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **January 20, 2027** (the "**Expiration Date**"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the "**Term**").

Subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, the Term may be extended for one or more renewal periods on terms and conditions to be agreed in writing, including any revised fees and Service Levels.

(a) The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, plus any non-cancellable commitments properly incurred in reliance on this Agreement; and

(a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
(b) The other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed under judicial management or liquidation, or ceases to carry on business in the ordinary course.

In addition to Section 4.4, the Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if the Vendor fails to meet critical Service Levels (as defined in Article XII) for three (3) consecutive months or for any four (4) months in any rolling twelve (12)-month period, provided that the Client has given the Vendor reasonable opportunity to cure such failures.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Availability/Uptime Commitment.**  
For hosted or managed components under the Vendor’s control, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure system availability of at least **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events, as measured at the primary production environment level.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance that may impact availability, subject to:

(a) Providing at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for planned maintenance windows; and  
(b) Scheduling such maintenance during off-peak hours where reasonably possible.

Scheduled maintenance shall not count against the uptime commitment.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Vendor shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** System outage or major functionality unavailable  
  - Response: within 1 hour  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation or functionality impacted, but workaround available  
  - Response: within 2 hours  
  - Target resolution: within 1 Business Day  

- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issue, minor impairment  
  - Response: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target resolution: within 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low):** Cosmetic issues or general inquiries  
  - Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target resolution: in next scheduled release or as mutually agreed

12.4 **SLA Measurement and Reporting.**  
The Vendor shall monitor Service Levels on an ongoing basis and provide monthly SLA reports to the Client, including incident logs, response and resolution metrics, and uptime statistics.

12.5 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
If the Vendor fails to meet the availability commitment or other critical Service Levels in a given calendar month, the Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

(a) For each 0.1% shortfall in availability below 99.9% (down to 99.0%), a service credit equal to 2% of the monthly Managed Services Fees;  
(b) For availability below 99.0%, an additional service credit of 10% of the monthly Managed Services Fees;  
(c) For repeated failures as described in Section 4.5, the Client may exercise its termination rights.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall be the Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to the Client’s termination rights and other rights under this Agreement.
