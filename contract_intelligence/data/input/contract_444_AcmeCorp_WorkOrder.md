## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order is issued under, supplements, and is subject to the terms and conditions of the Parent Agreement in accordance with **Section 1.2, Section 2.2 (Statements of Work)**, and **Section 16.11 (Order of Precedence)** of the Parent Agreement.  

2.1.2 Except as expressly modified in this Work Order, the obligations, rights, and standards applicable to “Services” and “Deliverables” under **Article II (Scope of Services/Work)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the Services and Deliverables described in this Work Order.  

2.1.3 In the event of a conflict between this Work Order and the Parent Agreement, the Parent Agreement shall control unless this Work Order expressly states that a specific provision of the Parent Agreement is being superseded with respect solely to this Work Order, as permitted under **Section 2.2.2** and **Section 16.11** of the Parent Agreement.

2.2 **Overview of Services.**  

Vendor shall provide the following categories of Services to Client under this Work Order (collectively, the “Services”), consistent with the general categories set forth in **Section 2.1** of the Parent Agreement:

(a) **Solution Design and Architecture.** Vendor shall perform business analysis, requirements gathering, and solution architecture for Client’s enterprise analytics and operations platform, including:
   - Detailed current-state assessment of Client’s existing systems and data flows;
   - Definition of target-state architecture, integration patterns, and data governance;
   - Documentation of functional and technical Specifications, in alignment with **Section 2.4.1** of the Parent Agreement.

(b) **Implementation and Configuration.** Vendor shall configure, customize (to the extent agreed), and implement the agreed solution components, which may include data integration tools, ETL pipelines, dashboards, and workflow automation, including:
   - Configuration of up to 20 data source connectors;
   - Development of up to 50 standard reports and dashboards;
   - Implementation of role-based access controls and audit logging.

(c) **Integration and Data Migration.** Vendor shall design and build integrations between Client’s systems (ERP, CRM, HRIS, and operational systems) and the target platform, and perform initial and incremental data migrations, including:
   - Data mapping and transformation rules;
   - Execution of test migrations and validation scripts;
   - Production cutover migration support.

(d) **Testing and Acceptance Support.** Vendor shall develop test plans and scripts, support Client’s user acceptance testing (“UAT”), and remediate issues identified during the Acceptance Period, in accordance with **Section 2.4.2–2.4.4** of the Parent Agreement.

(e) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Vendor shall provide training sessions and documentation for Client’s administrators, power users, and end users, including:
   - Up to 10 remote training sessions (up to 2 hours each);
   - Administrator runbooks and configuration guides;
   - End-user quick reference materials.

(f) **Managed Services and Support.** Following initial go-live, Vendor shall provide ongoing managed services, monitoring, maintenance, and support, consistent with **Section 2.1(f)** and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement, including:
   - Proactive monitoring of integrations and data pipelines;
   - Incident management and issue resolution within the Service Levels defined in **Article XII** of this Work Order;
   - Periodic health checks and optimization recommendations.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  

Specific Deliverables under this Work Order shall include, without limitation:

(a) **Architecture and Design Deliverables.**
   - Current-state systems inventory and integration map;
   - Target-state architecture diagram and narrative;
   - Detailed functional and technical Specifications (Exhibit B).

(b) **Configuration and Integration Deliverables.**
   - Configured platform environments (dev, test, and production);
   - Developed integrations and interfaces (source/target mappings, ETL jobs);
   - Configuration documentation and deployment scripts.

(c) **Data Migration Deliverables.**
   - Data mapping documents;
   - Data validation and reconciliation reports;
   - Migration runbooks and rollback procedures.

(d) **Testing Deliverables.**
   - Master test plan;
   - Test cases and scripts;
   - UAT results and defect logs.

(e) **Training and Documentation Deliverables.**
   - Training curriculum and materials;
   - Administrator guides and runbooks;
   - End-user reference guides.

(f) **Managed Services Deliverables.**
   - Monthly system performance and incident reports;
   - Quarterly optimization recommendation reports;
   - Updated knowledge base entries reflecting incident resolutions.

All Deliverables shall be provided in commonly used electronic formats (e.g., PDF, Word, Excel, or mutually agreed alternatives) and shall meet the Specifications set forth in **Exhibit B** and **Section 2.4** of this Work Order.

2.4 **Specifications and Performance Standards.**  

2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Services and provide the Deliverables in accordance with the Specifications, Service Levels, and project schedule set forth in this Work Order, including **Exhibit A (Project Plan and Milestones)** and **Exhibit B (Specifications)**, and in accordance with the performance standards in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** and **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement.  

2.4.2 The solution’s technical design shall be consistent with industry best practices and shall provide reasonable scalability to support at least:
   - 500 concurrent users accessing dashboards;
   - Daily processing of up to 2 million records across all data sources;
   - A minimum of 99.9% availability for the managed services components, as referenced in **Section 12.1** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 12.1** of this Work Order.

2.4.3 Vendor shall ensure that all personnel performing Services are appropriately trained and qualified, as required by **Section 2.5.2** of the Parent Agreement, and shall assign a dedicated Project Manager as described in **Section 2.3.1** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4.4 Vendor shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including Data Protection Laws, in performing the Services, consistent with **Sections 2.5.1(c)** and **7.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.5 **Project Management and Governance.**  

2.5.1 **Project Managers.** In accordance with **Section 2.3.1** of the Parent Agreement, each Party shall designate a Project Manager. The Project Managers shall:
   - Act as primary day-to-day points of contact;
   - Coordinate tasks, dependencies, and resource allocations;
   - Address issues and escalate matters as necessary.

2.5.2 **Steering Committee.** Because of the material size of the WO Contract Value and multi-year WO Term, the Parties shall establish a steering committee as contemplated in **Section 2.3.2** of the Parent Agreement. The steering committee shall:

   - Review overall Project status, risks, and issues;
   - Approve significant scope changes and strategic decisions.

Client’s obligations under **Section 2.6** of the Parent Agreement apply to this Work Order. Without limiting the foregoing, Client shall:

(a) Provide timely access to Client Materials, systems, environments, and key personnel necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  

(b) Provide accurate and complete information regarding requirements, data sources, and business processes;

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees and WO Contract Value.**  

3.1.1 In consideration for the Services and Deliverables provided under this Work Order, Client shall pay Vendor fees in an aggregate amount not to exceed the **WO Contract Value** of **US $1,474,824**, exclusive of Taxes and reimbursable expenses, subject to adjustment by mutually executed Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and this Work Order.

3.1.2 The fee structure for this Work Order shall be a combination of:
   - **Fixed-fee** amounts for defined phases and Milestones; and
   - **Recurring fees** for ongoing managed services.

3.2 **Fee Schedule.**  

3.2.1 The Parties agree to the following high-level fee allocation (further detailed in **Exhibit C**):

(a) **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (Fixed Fee):** US $225,000  
(b) **Phase 2 – Implementation and Integration (Fixed Fee):** US $550,000  
(c) **Phase 3 – Data Migration and Go-Live (Fixed Fee):** US $299,824  
(d) **Phase 4 – Training and Knowledge Transfer (Fixed Fee):** US $100,000  
(e) **Managed Services – Years 1–4 (Recurring):** US $75,000 per year (total US $300,000)  

Total: US $1,474,824

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  

3.3.1 Fixed-fee components shall be invoiced upon completion and acceptance of associated Milestones in accordance with **Section 2.4** of the Parent Agreement, as follows (illustrative):

(a) 20% of Phase 1 fee upon delivery and Client’s acceptance of the current-state assessment;  
(b) 80% of Phase 1 fee upon delivery and acceptance of the final architecture and design documentation;  
(c) 40% of Phase 2 fee upon completion of environment setup and core configuration;  
(d) 60% of Phase 2 fee upon acceptance of all configured integrations and reports;  
(e) 50% of Phase 3 fee upon completion of successful test migration;  
(f) 50% of Phase 3 fee upon go-live completion and acceptance;  
(g) 50% of Phase 4 fee upon completion of administrator training;  
(h) 50% of Phase 4 fee upon completion of end-user training.

3.3.2 Managed services fees shall be invoiced **monthly in arrears** beginning on the first full calendar month after the production go-live date, in accordance with **Section 3.2.1** of the Parent Agreement.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  

3.4.1 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of receipt of a valid invoice (“**Net 45**”), consistent with **Section 3.2.2** of the Parent Agreement.  

3.4.2 All payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method, in accordance with **Section 3.2.3** of the Parent Agreement.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.5.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred in connection with the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with **Section 3.4** of the Parent Agreement.  

3.5.2 Any single expense in excess of **US $1,000** must be pre-approved in writing by Client, consistent with **Section 3.4.2** of the Parent Agreement.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.5** of the Parent Agreement. All fees in this Work Order are exclusive of Taxes; Client shall be responsible for applicable Taxes other than Vendor’s income, property, or employment-related taxes.

3.7 **Disputed Invoices and Late Payments.**  

3.7.1 Any invoice dispute shall be handled pursuant to **Section 3.6 (Disputed Invoices)** of the Parent Agreement. Client may withhold only the disputed portion and shall pay the undisputed portion when due.

4.1 **WO Term.**

The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend the managed services portion of this Work Order for additional periods, on terms to be negotiated and documented in a written amendment or new work order, consistent with **Section 4.2 (Renewal)** of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Notwithstanding **Section 4.3.1** of the Parent Agreement (which permits termination of the Parent Agreement or any SOW upon at least forty-five (45) days’ prior written notice), either Party may terminate this Work Order in whole or in part for convenience upon providing at least **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. This provision is intended to supplement and clarify the application of **Section 4.3** of the Parent Agreement solely with respect to this Work Order.

4.3.2 Upon termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor:  
(a) fees for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) approved, non-cancellable expenses actually incurred; and  
(c) mutually agreed reasonable wind-down costs, consistent with **Section 4.3.2** of the Parent Agreement.

4.3.3 Upon termination for convenience by Vendor, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption and cooperate in transition activities in accordance with **Section 4.3.3** and **Section 4.6** of the Parent Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order for cause in accordance with **Section 4.4** of the Parent Agreement if the other Party materially breaches this Work Order or the Parent Agreement with respect to this Work Order and fails to cure within thirty (30) days after written notice.  

4.4.2 The insolvency-related termination rights in **Section 4.4.2** of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to this Work Order.

4.5 **Effect of Termination; Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

4.5.1 Termination of this Work Order shall not, by itself, result in termination of the Parent Agreement or any other work orders or SOWs, unless expressly stated in the notice of termination. However, termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement shall automatically terminate this Work Order in accordance with **Section 4.5** of the Parent Agreement.  

4.5.2 Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:  
(a) Vendor shall cease performance of the Services under this Work Order;  
(b) Client shall pay all undisputed fees and reimbursable expenses incurred through the effective date of termination; and  
(c) each Party shall return or destroy Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4** of the Parent Agreement and **Article V** of this Work Order.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  

At Client’s request, and at Client’s cost (unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach), Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to **ninety (90) days** following termination, consistent with **Section 4.6.2** of the Parent Agreement.

4.7 **Survival.**  

Notwithstanding any termination or expiration, the provisions of this Work Order that by their nature should survive, including but not limited to payment obligations, confidentiality, intellectual property, data protection, limitation of liability, and dispute resolution, shall survive, together with those provisions of the Parent Agreement identified in **Section 4.7** thereof.
