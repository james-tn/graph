## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF LEASE AND SERVICES

### 2.1 Premises and Grant of Lease

(a) **Lease Grant.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Cascade hereby leases to Contoso, and Contoso hereby leases from Cascade, the Premises for the Lease Term, together with (i) the non-exclusive right to use the building’s common areas, including lobbies, corridors, elevators, stairwells, restrooms, and parking areas designated for tenant use, and (ii) the right of ingress and egress over the Property as reasonably necessary for Contoso’s use of the Premises.

(b) **Use of Premises.** Contoso shall use the Premises solely for general office, administrative, and related lawful business purposes consistent with a first-class office building (the **“Permitted Use”**) and for no other purpose without the prior written consent of Cascade, which consent may be withheld in Cascade’s reasonable discretion.

(c) **Condition of Premises.** Except as expressly set forth in this Agreement, Contoso acknowledges that it has had the opportunity to inspect the Premises and agrees to accept the Premises in their **“AS IS, WHERE IS”** condition as of the Commencement Date, with all faults, latent or patent. Cascade makes no representation or warranty, express or implied, regarding the condition, suitability, or fitness of the Premises except as expressly set forth in Article VII.

### 2.2 Services Provided by Cascade

(a) **Building Operations and Maintenance.** Cascade shall provide the following Services in accordance with the Service Levels in Article XII and industry standards for comparable Class A commercial office buildings:

  1. **HVAC Services.** Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning during normal building hours (8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Business Days), sufficient to maintain commercially reasonable temperature and air quality. After-hours HVAC may be provided at Cascade’s then-current rates upon reasonable advance request by Contoso.

  2. **Elevator Services.** Operation, maintenance, and repair of passenger and freight elevators serving the Premises, with reasonable uptime consistent with Service Levels.

  3. **Electrical and Mechanical Systems.** Maintenance and repair of building electrical, plumbing, fire protection, life safety, and mechanical systems serving the Premises, excluding internal systems exclusively installed by or for Contoso’s unique business needs (e.g., special server room equipment).

  4. **Janitorial Services.** Routine cleaning of the Premises and common areas on Business Days, including vacuuming, trash removal, and restroom cleaning, in accordance with building standards. Special or above-standard cleaning (e.g., construction clean-up, event-related clean-up) may be provided at Contoso’s expense.

  5. **Security Services.** Building security, access control, and surveillance of common areas as described in Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy) and consistent with Cascade’s building security policies. Cascade does not guarantee prevention of unauthorized access but shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain security consistent with similarly situated buildings.

  6. **Common Area Maintenance.** Maintenance, repair, and replacement of common areas, including lobbies, corridors, restrooms, and parking areas, and exterior areas such as landscaping, sidewalks, and exterior lighting.

(b) **Utilities.** Cascade shall provide or arrange for the provision of standard utilities to the Premises, including electricity, water, and sewage. Unless otherwise specified in Exhibit B, (i) base utility consumption is included in the Rent, and (ii) extraordinary or above-standard utility usage by Contoso, as reasonably determined by Cascade, may be separately metered or allocated and charged to Contoso as additional Rent at Cascade’s then-current rates.

(c) **Telecommunications and Data.** Cascade shall provide access to risers, conduits, and cable paths necessary for Contoso’s telecommunications and data services. Contoso shall contract directly with third-party telecommunications providers at its own expense. Cascade shall reasonably cooperate with such providers, provided that such cooperation does not materially interfere with building operations or other tenants.

### 2.3 Contoso’s Use and Obligations

(a) **Compliance with Law and Rules.** Contoso shall:

  1. Comply with all Applicable Laws in its use and occupancy of the Premises;
  2. Comply with Cascade’s reasonable building rules and regulations, as may be amended from time to time with notice to Contoso, provided such rules are enforced in a non-discriminatory manner among tenants;
  3. Not use the Premises for any unlawful or hazardous purpose, nor permit any nuisance or waste.

(b) **Tenant Improvements and Alterations.** Any alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises by or on behalf of Contoso (collectively, **“Alterations”**) shall be subject to Cascade’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed, provided that:

  1. Contoso obtains all necessary permits and approvals;
  2. All work is performed in a good and workmanlike manner, using contractors reasonably approved by Cascade and compliant with building standards;
  3. Contoso restores, upon Cascade’s written request, the Premises to their original condition (reasonable wear and tear excepted) at the end of the Lease Term, with respect to any specialized or non-standard Alterations.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Alterations shall become the property of Cascade upon installation and shall not be removed except as required by Cascade as stated above.

(c) **Maintenance by Contoso.** Contoso shall, at its sole cost and expense, maintain in good order and condition (reasonable wear and tear excepted): (i) all interior, non-structural components of the Premises, including Contoso’s fixtures, furnishings, equipment, cabling, and IT infrastructure; and (ii) any Alterations installed by or for Contoso.

### 2.4 Deliverables and Documentation

Cascade shall provide and maintain, and make available to Contoso upon reasonable request:

1. **Maintenance Logs and Reports.** Periodic reports detailing material maintenance activities, service outages, and repairs affecting the Premises;
2. **Compliance Certificates.** Copies of building inspection reports, fire and life safety certifications, and code compliance certificates applicable to the Premises, to the extent reasonably available;
3. **Access and Security Documentation.** Building access procedures, emergency evacuation plans, and security policies applicable to tenants.

Such Deliverables shall be deemed part of the Services under this Agreement.

### 2.5 Performance Standards

(a) **General Standard.** Cascade shall perform the Services in accordance with: (i) the Service Levels specified in Article XII; (ii) generally accepted industry standards for first-class office buildings in the Seattle central business district; and (iii) all Applicable Laws.

(b) **Service Interruptions.** Cascade shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize interruptions to Services and to provide advance notice (when reasonably practicable) of any scheduled outages, maintenance, or repairs that may materially impact Contoso’s operations. Emergency work may be performed without advance notice, but Cascade shall notify Contoso as soon as reasonably practicable.

(c) **Remedies.** Contoso’s remedies for Service failures shall be as specified in Article XII (Service Level Agreements), Article VIII (Indemnification), and Article IX (Limitation of Liability).

## ARTICLE III  
FEES, RENT, AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Rent and Contract Value

(a) **Base Rent.** In consideration for the lease of the Premises and the Services provided, Contoso shall pay to Cascade base rent in the aggregate amount of **US$4,769,549** over the Lease Term, allocated as set forth in Section 3.2.

(b) **Additional Rent.** In addition to the base rent, Contoso shall pay as **“Additional Rent”** all amounts due under this Agreement including, without limitation: (i) common area maintenance charges, if any; (ii) above-standard utilities; (iii) charges for additional Services requested by Contoso; (iv) Taxes payable by Contoso as provided in Section 3.7; and (v) any other reimbursable expenses.

(c) **Contract Value.** The total of base rent plus the estimated Additional Rent (excluding contingent charges) is collectively referred to herein as the **Contract Value**, which the Parties acknowledge as **US$4,769,549** for budgeting and approval purposes. Such figure is not a cap or limitation on amounts otherwise payable by Contoso under this Agreement.

### 3.2 Fee Schedule and Payment Milestones

(a) **Periodic Payments.** Unless otherwise specified in Exhibit C, base rent shall be payable in equal monthly installments in advance on or before the first (1st) day of each calendar month during the Lease Term. For any partial month at the beginning or end of the Lease Term, Rent shall be prorated based on a 30-day month.

(b) **Invoicing.** Cascade shall invoice Contoso monthly in arrears (or as otherwise agreed) for Additional Rent and any other amounts then due. Each invoice shall reasonably itemize charges, including utility overages, additional services, and applicable taxes.

(c) **Milestone Payments.** If the Parties agree to specific project-related work or build-outs for which milestone payments are applicable, such milestones and payments shall be set forth in a separate written schedule or Statement of Work, which shall be incorporated by reference into this Agreement.

### 3.3 Payment Terms; Net 60

All undisputed amounts due under this Agreement shall be payable in accordance with **Net 60** terms. Contoso shall pay the full amount of any undisputed invoice within sixty (60) calendar days from the date of such invoice.

### 3.4 Disputed Amounts

If Contoso disputes any invoice in good faith, Contoso shall:

1. Pay all undisputed portions of the invoice within the Net 60 period; and  
2. Provide written notice to Cascade within thirty (30) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature and basis of the dispute.

The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve disputed amounts within thirty (30) days following Cascade’s receipt of such notice. Any amounts ultimately determined to be valid shall be payable within thirty (30) days after resolution, without prejudice to any applicable late charges accruing during the dispute period as provided in Section 3.5.

### 3.5 Late Payments

Any amount not paid when due (other than amounts disputed in good faith) shall accrue interest from the date due until the date paid at the lesser of: (i) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law. In addition to interest, Cascade may assess a one-time late fee in a reasonable amount to cover administrative costs for each past due invoice, provided such fee is not prohibited by Applicable Law.

### 3.6 Payment Method

Unless otherwise directed by Cascade in writing:

- Payments shall be made by wire transfer or ACH to the account designated by Cascade in writing; and  
- Each payment shall reference the Contract Reference No. **LSE-CAS-202409-633** and the relevant invoice numbers.

### 3.7 Taxes

(a) **General.** Contoso shall be responsible for and shall pay all Taxes imposed on or incident to the leasehold interest, the use or occupancy of the Premises by Contoso, and the Services provided under this Agreement, excluding Cascade’s income, franchise, or net worth taxes.

(b) **Sales and Use Taxes.** To the extent that any sales, use, or similar taxes are required by law to be collected by Cascade from Contoso with respect to Rent or Services, Cascade shall itemize such taxes on the invoice and Contoso shall remit such taxes together with payment of the applicable invoice.

### 3.8 Expenses and Reimbursement

Where Cascade incurs reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses at Contoso’s request (e.g., special build-out, after-hours services, special cleaning), Contoso shall reimburse Cascade for such expenses as Additional Rent, provided that:

1. Cascade obtains Contoso’s prior written approval for any single expense exceeding [●] US dollars; and  
2. Cascade provides reasonable supporting documentation for such expenses upon request.

### 3.9 Security Deposit

Contoso shall, upon execution of this Agreement, deposit with Cascade a Security Deposit in the amount of **[insert amount or formula]** as security for the performance of Contoso’s obligations hereunder. Cascade may apply all or any part of the Security Deposit to cure any Event of Default (as defined in Section 4.4). If Cascade applies any portion of the Security Deposit, Contoso shall promptly replenish the same upon written notice. Any unused portion of the Security Deposit shall be returned to Contoso within sixty (60) days after the later of: (i) expiration or termination of this Agreement; and (ii) Contoso’s complete vacation of the Premises, subject to any lawful deductions.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

The Lease Term shall commence on the Effective Date (or Commencement Date if different and specified in writing) and shall continue in full force and effect until the Expiration Date of **July 26, 2026**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

### 4.2 Renewal Options

Subject to Contoso not being in material default beyond any applicable cure period at the time of exercise, Contoso may have the option, if expressly granted in a separate schedule or amendment, to renew the Lease for one or more additional terms on terms to be negotiated in good faith. Unless otherwise specified, any renewal shall be subject to:

1. Written notice of exercise by Contoso at least nine (9) months prior to the then-current Expiration Date; and  
2. Mutual written agreement on Rent and other economic terms for the renewal period.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience by delivering at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. Any termination for convenience shall not relieve Contoso of (i) its obligation to pay Rent through the effective date of termination, or (ii) any other obligations accrued prior to such effective date. The Parties acknowledge that this long notice period is a material condition of this Agreement and favors the Vendor consistent with the high-risk allocation contemplated herein.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause

(a) **By Cascade.** Cascade may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to Contoso if any of the following events occur (each, an **“Event of Default”**):

  1. Contoso fails to pay any Rent or other amount due hereunder within fifteen (15) days after written notice of non-payment;  
  2. Contoso materially breaches any non-monetary term or condition of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice, or, if such breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period;  
  3. Contoso becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it any petition in bankruptcy or for reorganization, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, and such proceeding is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or  
  4. Contoso abandons the Premises for more than thirty (30) consecutive days without payment of Rent.

(b) **By Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to Cascade if:

  1. Cascade materially fails to provide essential Services (such as basic utilities, building access, or life safety systems) in accordance with Article II and Article XII, resulting in a material and persistent disruption of Contoso’s use of the Premises for more than thirty (30) consecutive days, and Cascade fails to cure such failure within thirty (30) days after written notice; or  
  2. Cascade materially breaches any other material term of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice, or, if such breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination or Expiration

Upon expiration or any termination of this Agreement:

1. Contoso shall vacate and surrender the Premises to Cascade in good order and condition, reasonable wear and tear excepted, and in compliance with any restoration obligations relating to Alterations;  
2. All keys, access cards, and security credentials shall be returned to Cascade;  
3. All amounts accrued and owing as of the effective date of expiration or termination (including any applicable late fees and interest) shall become immediately due and payable;  
4. Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights) to the extent applicable, VII (Representations and Warranties) to the extent intended to survive, VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive shall survive such expiration or termination.

### 4.6 Holding Over

If Contoso remains in possession of the Premises after the Expiration Date or any earlier termination without a written extension, such occupancy shall be deemed a month-to-month tenancy terminable by either Party upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, and Rent shall be increased to one hundred fifty percent (150%) of the last applicable monthly Rent, plus any Additional Rent. All other terms of this Agreement shall continue to apply during such holdover period.

The Receiving Party shall:

1. Use the Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving obligations under this Agreement;  
2. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein;  
3. 4. Is approved for release by written authorization of the Disclosing Party.

### 5.5 Survival

The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**, except for trade secrets which shall be maintained as confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII

### 12.1 Uptime Guarantees

Cascade shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that:

1. **Building Access Systems** (e.g., entry badges, lobby security) are available at least **99.9%** of the time on a monthly basis, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events;  
2. **Elevators and Essential Building Systems** (HVAC, electrical distribution) achieve at least **99.0%** uptime on a monthly basis, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

### 12.2 Response and Resolution Times

Cascade shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times for Service incidents reported by Contoso:

1. **Critical Incidents (e.g., complete loss of access, life safety issues):**  
   - Response Time: within 1 hour;  
   - Resolution/Workaround: within 4 hours, or if not feasible, continuous efforts until resolved.

2. **High Priority Incidents (e.g., major HVAC issues affecting productivity):**  
   - Response Time: within 4 hours;  
   - Resolution: within 24 hours where reasonably practicable.

3. **Normal Priority Incidents (e.g., minor maintenance):**  
   - Response Time: within 1 Business Day;  
   - Resolution: within 3 Business Days where reasonably practicable.

### 12.3 Reporting

Cascade shall, upon reasonable request, provide Contoso with periodic reports summarizing significant incidents, uptime statistics, and maintenance activities.

### 12.4 Remedies for SLA Breaches

If Cascade fails to meet the Service Levels in a material and repeated manner (e.g., in two or more consecutive months), Contoso shall be entitled to:

1. Request a corrective action plan from Cascade specifying remedial measures and timelines;  
2. Receive SLA Credits, if any are specified in Exhibit D; and  
3. In the case of chronic failures that materially impair the Premises’ usability, terminate this Agreement for cause under Section 4.4(b)(1), after giving Cascade the opportunity to cure in accordance with that section.

SLA Credits, if applicable, shall constitute Contoso’s primary remedy for Service Level failures, without limiting Contoso’s rights under Articles VIII and IX.

## ARTICLE XV  
FORCE MAJEURE

### 15.1 Definition of Force Majeure Event

A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party that prevents or materially delays the performance of its obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to: acts of God; natural disasters; fire; flood; earthquake; explosion; epidemic, pandemic, or public health emergency; war; terrorism; civil unrest; strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce, except where such strike is beyond its reasonable control); acts of government; or failure of public utilities or transportation networks.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

The affected Party shall:

1. Promptly notify the other Party in writing upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event that materially affects its ability to perform;  
2. Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of such event and to resume performance as soon as practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event:

1. The affected Party’s obligations (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the delay caused by the Force Majeure Event;  
2. Contoso shall remain responsible for payment of Rent, except to the extent the Force Majeure Event results in total and continuous impossibility of occupancy of the Premises for more than thirty (30) consecutive days, in which case Rent may be equitably abated, subject to mutual agreement.

### 15.4 Extended Force Majeure

If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially impairs the Parties’ ability to perform their core obligations under this Agreement, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without liability other than for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
