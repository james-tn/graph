## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall perform for the Client a combination of systems integration, cloud infrastructure management, application support, and ongoing managed services (collectively, the **“Services”**). The Services shall be rendered in a professional and workmanlike manner substantially in accordance with industry best practices for projects of similar size, scope, and complexity, subject to the specific terms of this Agreement.

2.2 **Project Components.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services shall include, at a minimum, the following components:

  2.2.1 **Systems Integration and Implementation.**  
  (a) Assessment of the Client’s existing enterprise architecture, including on-premise and cloud-based systems, data repositories, and network topology;  
  (b) Design of an integrated solution architecture leveraging a hybrid cloud environment (including, but not limited to, major public cloud providers used by the Client);  
  (c) Configuration of integration middleware, APIs, and data pipelines to enable secure data exchange between the Client’s legacy systems and new platforms;  
  (d) Implementation of standard and custom integrations to support the Client’s core business processes, including order management, billing, customer relationship management (CRM), and internal analytics;  
  (e) Unit and system testing, quality assurance, and validation of the integrated solution against agreed-upon acceptance criteria.

  2.2.2 **Cloud Infrastructure Management.**  
  (a) provisioning, configuration, and ongoing management of virtual machines, storage, networking components, and security controls in the Client’s designated cloud accounts;  
  (b) implementation of monitoring and alerting tools to track system performance, availability, and capacity utilization;  
  (c) application of security hardening measures, including, without limitation, access control policies, encryption configurations, and logging;  
  (d) periodic patching, updates, and maintenance of the managed environment, coordinated with the Client to minimize downtime;  
  (e) participation in capacity planning, including recommendations for scaling resources and optimizing cost-efficiency.

  2.2.3 **Application Support and Managed Services.**  
  (a) Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 support services for designated applications and components, including incident triage, troubleshooting, and resolution;  
  (b) service desk operations available during agreed support windows, including ticket intake, categorization, and initial diagnosis;  
  (c) problem management and root cause analysis for recurring or high-severity incidents;  
  (d) minor enhancements, configuration changes, and updates within the scope of agreed support parameters;  
  (e) preparation of periodic performance and incident reports, including analysis and recommendations for continuous improvement.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Service Provider shall produce and deliver to the Client the following **Deliverables**, which shall constitute **Work Product** under this Agreement:

  2.3.1 **Project Governance and Planning Deliverables.**  
  (a) Project charter detailing objectives, scope, stakeholders, and success criteria;  
  (b) Detailed project plan and schedule including Milestones, resource allocations, and critical path analysis;  
  (c) Communication plan specifying meeting cadence, reporting mechanisms, and escalation paths.

  2.3.2 **Design and Architecture Deliverables.**  
  (a) Current-state assessment documentation of the Client’s environment;  
  (b) Target-state architecture diagrams and technical specifications;  
  (c) Data mapping and integration design documents;  
  (d) Security and compliance design documentation addressing key regulatory requirements applicable to the Client.

  2.3.3 **Implementation and Testing Deliverables.**  
  (a) Configured integration components, scripts, and deployment artifacts;  
  (b) Test plans, test cases, and test results reports;  
  (c) Cutover and rollback plans for go-live events;  
  (d) Acceptance test summary and sign-off documentation.

  2.3.4 **Operational and Support Deliverables.**  
  (a) Runbooks, operational procedures, and knowledge base articles for ongoing support;  
  (b) Monitoring dashboards and standard reports;  
  (c) Monthly service performance reports, including SLA attainment metrics and incident summaries.

2.4 **Timeline and Milestones.**  
The Services shall be performed over the period from the Effective Date through the Expiration Date, subject to earlier termination as provided in **Article IV**. The preliminary project phases shall be substantially as follows (subject to refinement in an SOW):

  (a) **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (Approx. 3 months)**: completion of current-state assessment, requirements gathering, and production of design and architecture deliverables;  
  (b) **Phase 2 – Implementation and Integration (Approx. 6–9 months)**: configuration, development, integration, and testing activities leading to initial go-live;  
  (c) **Phase 3 – Stabilization and Optimization (Approx. 3–4 months)**: performance tuning, issue remediation, and operational handover;  
  (d) **Phase 4 – Ongoing Managed Services (through Expiration Date)**: continuous operations, monitoring, support, and periodic enhancements.

Milestone completion dates and acceptance criteria shall be documented in writing and may be amended only through a Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII**.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services:

  (a) using personnel with suitable qualification, experience, and expertise;  
  (b) in compliance with Applicable Laws and the Client’s written policies provided in advance;  
  (c) in a manner designed to meet or exceed the Service Levels specified in **Article XII**; and  
  (d) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for service providers performing similar services for enterprise clients.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client acknowledges that the Service Provider’s performance is dependent upon the timely and effective performance of the Client’s responsibilities, including:

  (a) providing timely access to Client Materials, systems, personnel, and facilities;  
  (b) assigning a project sponsor and designated points of contact to make approvals and decisions;  
  (c) performing any tasks or activities assigned to the Client in the project plan;  
  (d) reviewing and providing feedback or approvals within reasonable timeframes; and  
  (e) ensuring that any third-party systems or vendors controlled by the Client cooperate reasonably with the Service Provider.

Delays or failures by the Client in performing such responsibilities may result in schedule adjustments and additional fees, as further addressed in **Article XIII**.

3.1 **Contract Value and Fee Structure.**  
The Client shall pay the Service Provider fees for the Services and Deliverables in an amount not to exceed an aggregate **Contract Value** of **US$3,794,474**, subject to adjustments as provided in this Agreement. The fee structure shall consist of:

  (a) **Fixed Implementation Fees** for Phases 1–3, estimated at US$2,200,000;  
  (b) **Recurring Managed Services Fees** for Phase 4 (ongoing support), estimated at US$1,400,000 over the Term; and  
  (c) **Contingency and Additional Services Allowance** of US$194,474 for approved out-of-scope work requested by the Client through the Change Management process.

3.2 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise specified in a Statement of Work, the Client shall pay the fixed implementation fees according to the following milestones:

  (a) **Milestone 1 – Project Initiation:** 20% of fixed implementation fees upon execution of this Agreement and issuance of a valid purchase order, if applicable;  
  (b) **Milestone 2 – Completion of Design Phase:** 25% upon acceptance by the Client (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed) of the principal design and architecture Deliverables;  
  (c) **Milestone 3 – Completion of Integration Build:** 30% upon completion of primary integration development and successful completion of system testing;  
  (d) **Milestone 4 – Go-Live and Stabilization:** 25% upon successful go-live and completion of a thirty (30) day stabilization period with no Severity 1 defects open.

Recurring managed services fees shall be invoiced **monthly in arrears** based on the agreed rate card and the actual Services provided, unless otherwise specified in an SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
All invoices shall be submitted electronically to the Client’s designated billing contact. Each invoice shall reference the **Contract Reference Number WO-BET-202403-031**, the applicable Milestone or billing period, and any supporting documentation reasonably required by the Client. The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 45”**).

3.4 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Client shall notify the Service Provider within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the amount disputed and the basis for the dispute. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such disputes promptly. The Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice. Non-payment of a disputed amount shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full. In addition, if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the due date, the Service Provider may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Services until such amounts are paid, without liability to the Client, and such suspension shall not be deemed a breach by the Service Provider.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
For any hosting or managed services components provided by the Service Provider under this Agreement, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve a minimum **monthly uptime availability of 99.9%** for the production environment, excluding scheduled maintenance windows and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Incidents reported by the Client shall be classified and handled in accordance with the following target response and resolution times:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical)**: total loss of core service or major security incident.  
    - Initial Response: within 1 hour, 24x7  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours  

  - **Severity 2 (High)**: significant degradation impacting critical functions.  
    - Initial Response: within 4 hours, during Business Days  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 24 hours  

  - **Severity 3 (Medium)**: partial degradation or non-critical functions impacted.  
    - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
    - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

  - **Severity 4 (Low)**: minor issue, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
    - Initial Response: within 2 Business Days  
    - Target Resolution: as reasonably agreed.

12.3 **SLA Measurement and Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall track and measure uptime and response/resolution times using its monitoring tools and incident management system. Monthly SLA reports shall be provided to the Client within ten (10) Business Days following the end of each calendar month.

12.4 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
In the event that the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in a given calendar month, the Client shall be entitled to **service credits** calculated as a percentage of the monthly managed services fees for the affected service, as follows:

  - Availability 99.0% to < 99.9%: 5% credit  
  - Availability 95.0% to < 99.0%: 10% credit  
  - Availability < 95.0%: 20% credit  

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice. Service credits are the Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the uptime or response time commitments, without limiting any other non-monetary remedies under this Agreement.
