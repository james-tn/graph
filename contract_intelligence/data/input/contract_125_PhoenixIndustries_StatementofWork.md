## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Engagement.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Company a comprehensive suite of professional and managed services to support the design, implementation, and operation of an integrated enterprise resource planning (“ERP”) and customer relationship management (“CRM”) solution for the Company’s U.S.-based operations. The Services will include, without limitation: (a) discovery and requirements gathering; (b) solution design and architecture; (c) configuration and customization; (d) data migration and validation; (e) integration with existing systems; (f) user acceptance testing support; (g) training and knowledge transfer; (h) go-live support; and (i) post go-live managed services, including monitoring, incident management, and performance reporting.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Services will be performed in the following major phases, each with associated Deliverables:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Planning (Estimated Duration: 8 Weeks).**  
  (a) Conduct stakeholder interviews and workshops to identify business requirements, pain points, and success criteria.  
  (b) Review existing systems, data sources, interfaces, and business processes.  
  (c) Assess current IT infrastructure, security requirements, and compliance considerations.  
  (d) Deliverables for Phase 1 shall include:  
  - (i) **Requirements Specification Document** detailing functional and non-functional requirements, regulatory considerations, and integration points;  
  - (ii) **Current State Assessment Report** summarizing the Company’s existing systems and processes;  
  - (iii) **High-Level Solution Architecture Document** proposing an overall technical design; and  
  - (iv) **Project Plan and Schedule**, including milestones, resource assignments, and dependencies.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Design and Configuration (Estimated Duration: 14 Weeks).**  
  (a) Develop detailed solution design documents based on approved requirements.  
  (b) Configure core ERP and CRM modules to align with the Company’s processes (including finance, procurement, inventory, sales, and customer support).  
  (c) Develop necessary customizations, extensions, and automations strictly in accordance with agreed Specifications.  
  (d) Deliverables for Phase 2 shall include:  
  - (i) **Detailed Design Specification** for each configured module;  
  - (ii) **Configuration Workbook** documenting configured parameters and options;  
  - (iii) **Customization and Extension Documentation**, including technical design and code-level documentation; and  
  - (iv) **Integration Design Document** detailing interfaces with legacy systems and third-party applications.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Integration and Data Migration (Estimated Duration: 12 Weeks).**  
  (a) Develop and test integration components and APIs to connect the new solution with the Company’s existing applications (including the Company’s data warehouse, HR system, and external partner portals).  
  (b) Design and execute data migration strategy, including data mapping, cleansing, transformation, and loading from legacy systems into the new environment.  
  (c) Implement data validation processes and reconciliation to ensure accuracy and completeness.  
  (d) Deliverables for Phase 3 shall include:  
  - (i) **Integration Components and Documentation**;  
  - (ii) **Data Migration Plan and Scripts**;  
  - (iii) **Data Mapping and Transformation Specifications**; and  
  - (iv) **Data Validation and Reconciliation Report** summarizing outcomes and exceptions.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Testing and User Acceptance (Estimated Duration: 10 Weeks).**  
  (a) Develop test strategy and test plans, including unit, system, integration, performance, and user acceptance testing (“UAT”).  
  (b) Support the Company in the execution of UAT, including defect logging, triage, and resolution.  
  (c) Perform performance and load testing for key processes.  
  (d) Deliverables for Phase 4 shall include:  
  - (i) **Test Strategy and Plan**;  
  - (ii) **Test Cases and Scripts**;  
  - (iii) **Test Execution and Defect Reports**; and  
  - (iv) **Go-Live Readiness Assessment** summarizing residual risks and readiness criteria.

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Training, Go-Live, and Transition (Estimated Duration: 8 Weeks).**  
  (a) Develop and deliver training materials and sessions (in-person or remote as agreed) for end-users, administrators, and support teams.  
  (b) Support cutover activities, including final data migration, configuration validation, and go-live support.  
  (c) Transition the solution to steady-state operations and the managed services team.  
  (d) Deliverables for Phase 5 shall include:  
  - (i) **Training Materials** (presentations, user guides, quick reference cards);  
  - (ii) **Training Completion Report**;  
  - (iii) **Cutover Plan and Runbook**; and  
  - (iv) **Post-Implementation Review Report**.

  2.2.6 **Phase 6 – Managed Services (from Go-Live through Expiration Date).**  
  (a) Provide ongoing application and technical support, including incident management, problem resolution, and minor configuration adjustments within agreed Service Levels.  
  (b) Monitor solution performance and availability, perform routine maintenance, and implement security patches and updates in coordination with the Company.  
  (c) Provide monthly service reporting and quarterly service review meetings.  
  (d) Deliverables for Phase 6 shall include:  
  - (i) **Monthly Service Performance Reports**;  
  - (ii) **Incident and Problem Logs**;  
  - (iii) **Change and Release Notes**; and  
  - (iv) **Quarterly Service Review Reports**.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall perform the Services: (a) in a timely, professional, and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and best practices for similar services; (b) in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards, including Data Protection Laws; and (c) using personnel who are appropriately skilled, trained, and experienced for the tasks to which they are assigned. The Service Provider shall devote sufficient resources to meet the project schedule and Service Levels. Failure to meet Service Levels shall give rise to remedies set forth in Article XII.

2.4 **Project Management and Governance.**  
Each Party shall designate a project manager who will be the primary point of contact and responsible for day-to-day coordination. The Parties shall conduct regular project status meetings (no less than bi-weekly during implementation phases and monthly during managed services), either in-person or virtually. The Service Provider shall provide updated status reports prior to each meeting, including progress against plan, issues, risks, and mitigation actions. Escalation procedures will be established in accordance with Article XIV.

2.5 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall:  
  (a) Provide timely access to relevant personnel, facilities, systems, and information reasonably necessary for the Service Provider to perform the Services;  
  (b) Review Deliverables and provide feedback or Acceptance decisions within the periods specified in this SOW or, if not specified, within ten (10) Business Days of receipt;  
  (c) Obtain any third-party consents or licenses required for integration with or access to third-party systems and data controlled by the Company; and  
  (d) Fulfill any other obligations specifically assigned to the Company in this SOW or agreed through Change Orders.  
The Service Provider’s performance is contingent upon the Company’s timely fulfillment of these responsibilities.

2.6 **Acceptance Procedures.**  
For each Deliverable subject to Acceptance:  
  (a) The Service Provider shall notify the Company in writing when such Deliverable is ready for review;  
  (b) The Company shall have the review period specified in the applicable phase description or, if none is specified, ten (10) Business Days from delivery to test and review the Deliverable against the Acceptance Criteria;  
  (c) The Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing of Acceptance or rejection, describing in reasonable detail any material nonconformities;  
  (d) If rejected for material nonconformity, the Service Provider shall promptly remedy the nonconformities and resubmit the Deliverable for re-testing, and the review process shall repeat; and  
  (e) A Deliverable will be deemed Accepted if the Company fails to respond within the applicable review period, provided that the Service Provider’s notice clearly stated that the Deliverable would be deemed Accepted if no response is received within such period.  

Acceptance of a Deliverable shall not limit the Company’s rights with respect to latent defects or warranty claims under Article VII.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value and Fee Structure.**  
The total Contract Value for the Services and Deliverables under this SOW shall not exceed **US $2,310,799**, except as may be expressly increased by a mutually executed Change Order. The Fees shall be split between: (a) fixed-fee amounts for implementation phases (Phases 1–5); and (b) time-and-materials and recurring fees for managed services during Phase 6, subject to the overall Contract Value cap, absent a Change Order.

3.2 **Implementation Fees (Phases 1–5).**  
Implementation phases shall be billed on a fixed-fee milestone basis as follows:

- Phase 1 (Discovery and Planning): US $310,000  
- Phase 2 (Design and Configuration): US $580,000  
- Phase 3 (Integration and Data Migration): US $510,000  
- Phase 4 (Testing and User Acceptance): US $420,000  
- Phase 5 (Training, Go-Live, and Transition): US $290,000  

Total Implementation Fees: **US $2,110,000**.

Each phase fee shall be invoiced upon achievement of the corresponding milestone as follows:

  (a) 40% of the phase fee upon commencement of the phase;  
  (b) 40% upon delivery of all primary phase Deliverables (as identified in Article II); and  
  (c) 20% upon Acceptance of such Deliverables or deemed Acceptance in accordance with Section 2.6.

3.3 **Managed Services Fees (Phase 6).**  
Managed services shall be billed as follows:

  (a) **Base Monthly Service Fee.** A base monthly fee of **US $25,000** covering up to 200 hours of support, monitoring, minor enhancements, and maintenance activities per month.  
  (b) **Overage Rate.** Any hours beyond 200 hours per month shall be billed on a time-and-materials basis at the blended rate of **US $185 per hour**, subject to prior written approval by the Company for any overage exceeding twenty (20) hours in a given month.  
  (c) **Service Credits.** Any service credits arising from SLA breaches under Article XII shall be applied as a credit against the Base Monthly Service Fee in the month following the month in which the SLA breach occurred, unless otherwise agreed.

The aggregate of all Managed Services Fees, together with Implementation Fees, shall not exceed the Contract Value without a Change Order.

3.4 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred by the Service Provider in connection with onsite Services at the Company’s locations shall be reimbursed by the Company at cost, subject to the following:  
  (a) The Service Provider shall obtain the Company’s written approval prior to incurring any travel and lodging expenses;  
  (b) All reimbursable expenses shall be in compliance with the Company’s travel and expense policy, as provided to the Service Provider; and  
  (c) The Service Provider shall submit reasonably detailed documentation and receipts with each invoice for expenses.  
Unless otherwise agreed in writing, expenses shall be in addition to the Contract Value but capped at **US $75,000** in the aggregate.

3.5 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
The Service Provider shall invoice the Company in accordance with the milestones and monthly schedules described herein. All invoices shall reference Contract Reference Number **SOW-PHO-202502-125**, and shall include sufficient detail to enable the Company to verify the accuracy and basis of the charges. The Company shall pay undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** from the date of receipt of an undisputed invoice (“**Net 30**”). If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice, it shall notify the Service Provider in writing, describing the nature of the dispute, within fifteen (15) days of receipt. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve disputed amounts, and the Company shall pay all undisputed amounts when due.

3.6 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted under applicable law, from the due date until payment is made in full. The imposition of late payment interest shall not limit the Service Provider’s other rights or remedies under this SOW.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees and charges are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. The Company shall be responsible for all such taxes related to the Services, excluding taxes based on the Service Provider’s income, property, or employment of personnel. The Service Provider shall itemize any applicable taxes on its invoices and cooperate with the Company in any reasonable efforts to claim exemptions or recover such taxes where permitted by law.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This SOW shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until the earlier of: (a) completion of all Services and Deliverables and the conclusion of the managed services period; or (b) **March 17, 2026** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend the Term for one or more additional periods (each a “Renewal Term”), subject to negotiation of applicable fees, Service Levels, and other terms. Any such renewal shall be documented by a written amendment or new statement of work signed by both Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. Upon such termination for convenience by the Company, the Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and accepted and all expenses incurred and reimbursable through the effective date of termination, plus any non-cancellable commitments made by the Service Provider specifically in support of the Project, provided such commitments were pre-approved in writing by the Company. Upon termination for convenience by the Service Provider, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption and cooperate in an orderly transition of the Services.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (a) Materially breaches any provision of this SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days for payment-related breaches) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) Becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy (which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days), ceases to conduct business in the normal course, or is subject to any other insolvency, receivership, or similar proceeding.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this SOW for any reason:  
  (a) The Service Provider shall promptly cease performance of the Services (except as may be required for transition assistance under Section 4.6);  
  (b) The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services properly performed and accepted and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to any offset for amounts previously paid or any amounts owed by the Service Provider to the Company;  
  (c) All rights and licenses granted to the Company under Article VI that are designated as perpetual or that by their nature should survive shall continue in effect; and  
  (d) Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s request, destroy Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.4.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
Upon the Company’s request in connection with expiration or termination (other than termination by the Service Provider for the Company’s uncured material breach), the Service Provider shall, for up to ninety (90) days following the effective date of termination, provide reasonable transition assistance to facilitate the orderly transfer of the Services to the Company or a third-party service provider designated by the Company. Such assistance shall be provided at the Service Provider’s then-current time-and-materials rates, unless termination results from the Service Provider’s material breach, in which case the Parties shall discuss appropriate equitable adjustments.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this SOW that by their nature should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including, without limitation, Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), as well as any accrued payment obligations.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
During Phase 6 (Managed Services), the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment for the Deliverables has a monthly uptime of at least **99.9%**, measured on a calendar-month basis, excluding Scheduled Maintenance windows and any downtime attributable to: (a) the Company’s systems or networks; (b) Force Majeure Events; or (c) acts or omissions of the Company or third parties not under the Service Provider’s control.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Service Provider may schedule maintenance windows during which the Services may be unavailable, provided that: (a) such maintenance is, where feasible, conducted during off-peak hours; and (b) the Service Provider provides the Company with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice of any maintenance expected to result in more than thirty (30) minutes of downtime. Scheduled Maintenance shall not count against the uptime commitment.

12.3 **Support and Response Times.**  
The Service Provider shall provide incident support during the hours agreed by the Parties (which shall, at a minimum, include standard Business Hours in the Company’s primary U.S. time zone). Incidents shall be categorized and responded to as follows:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of a mission-critical function with no workaround.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation or partial loss of key functionality with no feasible workaround.  
  - Initial Response: within 2 hours  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 8 hours  

- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Limited degradation or non-critical issue with a reasonable workaround.  
  - Initial Response: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution: within 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or requests for information.  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution: as scheduled in regular releases or service cycles.

12.4 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in any given calendar month, the Company shall be entitled to **service credits** determined as follows:

- Uptime ≥ 99.0% and < 99.9%: 5% of the Base Monthly Service Fee for that month;  
- Uptime ≥ 98.0% and < 99.0%: 10% of the Base Monthly Service Fee for that month;  
- Uptime < 98.0%: 20% of the Base Monthly Service Fee for that month.

Service credits shall be applied to invoices for the month following the month in which the SLA breach occurred. Service credits are the Company’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches, except to the extent such breaches also constitute a material breach of this SOW subject to other remedies, and in all cases subject to the limitations in Article IX.

12.5 **Reporting.**  
The Service Provider shall provide the Company with monthly SLA performance reports summarizing uptime, incident volumes, response and resolution times, and any SLA breaches, as well as planned improvement actions where applicable.
