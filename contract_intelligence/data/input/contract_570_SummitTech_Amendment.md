## PREAMBLE

This AMENDMENT TO MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT (this “**Amendment**”), Contract Reference No. **AMD-SUM-202507-570**, is made and entered into as of **March 22, 2026** (the “**Amendment Effective Date**”), by and between:

- **Contoso Enterprises, Inc.**, a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware, with its principal place of business at **1200 Market Plaza, Suite 2200, Wilmington, DE 19801** (“**Contoso**”, the “**Client**” or the “**Company**”); and  

- **Summit Tech Solutions LLC**, a limited liability company organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware, with its principal place of business at **4550 Summit Ridge Parkway, Floor 9, Newark, DE 19711** (“**Summit Tech**”, the “**Vendor**” or the “**Service Provider**”).

Contoso and Summit Tech are sometimes hereinafter referred to individually as a “**Party**” and collectively as the “**Parties**.”

This Amendment is entered into **pursuant to and in amendment and supplementation of** that certain **Master Services Agreement**, Reference No. **MSA-SUM-202507-473**, dated **July 12, 2025** (the “**Master Services Agreement**” or the “**MSA**”), by and between the Parties, which is internally identified by the Company as **“contract_473”**.

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties entered into the Master Services Agreement, Reference No. **MSA-SUM-202507-473** (internal identifier **contract_473**), effective as of **July 12, 2025** (the “**Original Effective Date**”), pursuant to which Vendor agreed to provide certain technology consulting, software development, systems integration, managed services, cloud services, and related professional services to Client, as more fully set forth therein, including in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the MSA;

WHEREAS, this Amendment is designated as **Contract Reference No. AMD-SUM-202507-570** and is intended to **modify, amend, and supplement the Master Services Agreement dated July 12, 2025 (Ref: MSA-SUM-202507-473, internal identifier contract_473)**;

WHEREAS, the Parties intend that, except as expressly amended herein, all of the terms and conditions of the MSA shall remain in full force and effect, and that the MSA, together with this Amendment, shall continue to govern the Parties’ relationship with respect to all Services and Deliverables;

## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.** This **Article II** supplements and, where expressly indicated, modifies **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the MSA. Except as expressly modified herein, all terms of **Article II** of the MSA remain in full force and effect. The Parties acknowledge that this Amendment is intended to expand and formalize the ongoing relationship under the MSA by defining **Extended Services** and **Program Expansion Work** that will be delivered under New SOWs executed pursuant to the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

2.2 **Extended Services Overview.** In addition to the Services described in **Section 2.3** of the MSA, Vendor shall provide the following Extended Services, as may be further detailed in applicable New SOWs:

  (a) **Enterprise Platform Modernization.** Vendor shall design, develop, and implement enhancements and new modules to Client’s enterprise applications (including ERP, CRM, and data integration platforms) leveraging modern cloud-native architectures. This includes refactoring legacy components, designing microservices, updating APIs, and implementing advanced integration patterns with third-party systems consistent with **Section 2.3(b) (Software Development and Integration)** of the MSA.

  (b) **Expanded Managed Services and Operations.** Building upon **Section 2.3(c) (Managed Services and Support)** of the MSA, Vendor shall expand its managed services to cover 24x7 monitoring, incident response, problem management, capacity management, and performance optimization for additional Client environments, including production, staging, and disaster recovery instances.

  (c) **Enhanced Cloud and Hosting Services.** Consistent with **Section 2.3(d) (Cloud and Hosting Services)** of the MSA, Vendor shall expand cloud hosting to additional regions and environments, including high-availability configurations and geo-redundancy, and shall perform ongoing cost optimization, security hardening, and compliance support for Client’s workloads hosted on third-party cloud providers (e.g., AWS, Azure, GCP).

  (d) **Cybersecurity and Compliance Enhancements.** Vendor shall provide advisory services and implementation support to improve Client’s security posture, including threat modeling, vulnerability management, security orchestration, logging and monitoring enhancements, and assistance with compliance (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR/CCPA) in alignment with **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of the MSA.

  (e) **Training, Enablement, and Knowledge Transfer.** In addition to the training described in **Section 2.3(e)** of the MSA, Vendor shall conduct ongoing enablement programs, including regular workshops, deep-dive technical sessions, runbooks, and updated documentation to ensure that Client’s operations and development teams can effectively operate and maintain the modernized environments.

  (f) **Transition Assistance and Program Governance.** Vendor shall provide Transition Assistance Services as contemplated by **Section 4.6(d)** of the MSA and expanded program governance support, including participation in steering committee meetings, roadmap planning sessions, and periodic executive reviews.

2.3 **Deliverables.** The Extended Services shall result in one or more **Deliverables** (as defined in **Section 1.7** of the MSA) that may include, without limitation:

  (a) technical and functional design documents, architecture diagrams, and security models;

  (b) software artifacts, including source code (to the extent expressly provided in a New SOW), compiled binaries, configuration scripts, infrastructure-as-code templates, and automation pipelines;

  (c) test plans, test cases, test reports, and performance benchmarks;

  (d) updated runbooks, standard operating procedures, user manuals, and administrative guides;

  (e) project plans, milestone reports, risk and issue logs, and status reports as referenced in **Section 2.5 (Project Management and Reporting)** of the MSA;

  (f) training materials, recorded sessions, and knowledge transfer documentation.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  

  (a) Vendor shall perform the Extended Services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with **Section 2.4(a)** of the MSA and prevailing industry standards for similar complex enterprise programs.

  (b) For managed and hosted services, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed the **99.9% monthly uptime** target specified in **Section 12.2** of the MSA, as further expanded and detailed in **Article XII** of this Amendment. Any New SLA Commitments set forth herein shall apply to the Extended Services and shall be read together with **Article XII** of the MSA.

  (c) Vendor’s performance is dependent on Client’s timely performance of its responsibilities under **Section 2.6 (Client Responsibilities and Dependencies)** of the MSA. Delays or failures by Client may entitle Vendor to schedule extensions and fee adjustments pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the MSA and **Article XIII** of this Amendment.

2.5 **Project Management and Governance.**  

  (a) The Parties reaffirm the project management framework in **Section 2.5** of the MSA. For Program Expansion Work, each New SOW shall designate a **Project Manager** from each Party. 

  (b) Vendor shall provide periodic status reports as specified in the applicable New SOW, which at a minimum shall include: status against milestones; key deliverables completed; upcoming activities; identified risks and issues; Change Requests; and resource allocation summaries.

  (c) The Parties shall engage in regular governance meetings (weekly or bi-weekly, unless otherwise agreed) to review program performance, address escalations, and align on strategic priorities, consistent with **Section 2.5(c)** of the MSA.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities and Dependencies.**  

The Parties reaffirm **Section 2.6 (Client Responsibilities and Dependencies)** of the MSA. In addition, for the Extended Services, Client shall:

  (a) designate appropriate technical and business stakeholders to participate in requirements definition, design reviews, testing, and acceptance activities as set out in **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the MSA;

  (b) provide timely access to test environments, production systems (where necessary), data sets, and other infrastructure, including VPN or secure connectivity, necessary for Vendor to perform the Extended Services;

  (c) ensure that third-party software vendors, licensors, and service providers cooperate with Vendor where such cooperation is reasonably required for integration, migration, or troubleshooting activities;

  (d) perform user acceptance testing within the agreed review timeframes (generally consistent with the **fifteen (15) Business Days** review period in **Section 2.7(b)** of the MSA, unless modified in a New SOW).

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

The acceptance process in **Section 2.7** of the MSA shall apply to Extended Services Deliverables, unless otherwise specified in a New SOW. The Parties may define more detailed acceptance criteria, test cases, and defect severity classifications in such SOWs. If a Deliverable fails to meet the agreed acceptance criteria, Vendor shall remedy the deficiencies in accordance with **Section 2.7(c)** of the MSA.

2.8 **Subcontracting.**  

The Parties reaffirm **Section 2.8 (Subcontracting)** of the MSA. For clarity, Client hereby acknowledges that Vendor may extend its use of subcontractors, including offshore resources, to support the Extended Services, provided that Vendor remains responsible for their performance and complies with the confidentiality and data protection obligations in **Articles V and XI** of the MSA and this Amendment.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Revised Contract Value; Relationship to MSA Section 3.1.**  

Pursuant to and amending **Section 3.1 (Contract Value)** of the MSA, the Parties agree that, taking into account the Extended Services, the aggregate estimated value of all Services and Deliverables under the MSA and this Amendment, including all SOWs executed as of the Amendment Effective Date and all New SOWs reasonably anticipated under this Amendment, is **Four Million Nine Hundred Fifty-One Thousand Eight Hundred Fifteen U.S. Dollars (US$4,951,815)** (the “**Revised Contract Value**”). The Parties acknowledge that the Revised Contract Value remains an estimate, and that additional SOWs or Change Orders may increase or decrease the total fees payable.

3.2 **Fee Structures.**  

The fee structures described in **Section 3.2** of the MSA (Time-and-Materials, Fixed-Price, Milestone-Based, and Recurring Fees) shall continue to apply. New SOWs issued under this Amendment may employ any of these structures, alone or in combination, and shall specify:

  (a) hourly or daily rates for Vendor personnel by role or skill set (for T&M work);

  (b) fixed fees for defined phases, Deliverables, or work packages;

  (c) milestone-based payment triggers tied to agreed acceptance events; and/or

  (d) recurring monthly or annual fees for managed services, hosting, and support.

3.3 **Rate Adjustments.**  

The Parties reaffirm **Section 3.3** of the MSA. For clarity:

  (a) Vendor may adjust its standard rates not more than once per calendar year upon at least **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Client, unless otherwise agreed in a New SOW.

  (b) For long-term managed services introduced under this Amendment, recurring fees may be subject to annual adjustment, as set forth in the relevant SOW, typically by reference to the U.S. Consumer Price Index or a mutually agreed index, consistent with **Section 3.3(b)** of the MSA.

3.4 **Invoices and Payment Terms; Confirmation of Net 45.**  

  (a) Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the billing schedule defined in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated in a New SOW, T&M Services shall be invoiced **monthly in arrears**, fixed-fee or milestone-based work shall be invoiced upon completion and acceptance of milestones, and recurring fees shall be invoiced monthly or annually in advance.

  (b) All undisputed amounts shall be due and payable within **forty-five (45) days** of the invoice date (“**Net 45**”), reaffirming **Section 3.4(b)** of the MSA.

  (c) Client shall make all payments in U.S. Dollars via wire transfer or other method specified on the invoice, consistent with **Section 3.4(c)** of the MSA.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  

The late payment provisions in **Section 3.5** of the MSA remain in effect. Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest as provided therein, and Vendor may, after notice, suspend performance or require advance payment as permitted by that section.

3.6 **Disputed Invoices.**  

The invoice dispute process in **Section 3.6** of the MSA shall apply. Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion of any invoice and provide written notice with reasonable detail for any disputed amount within thirty (30) days of the invoice date, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve such disputes.

3.7 **Taxes.**  

The tax allocation in **Section 3.7** of the MSA remains unchanged and applies to all fees payable under the Extended Services. Fees are exclusive of Taxes, and Client is responsible for applicable Taxes, except as otherwise specified in the MSA.

3.8 **Expenses.**  

Reimbursement of out-of-pocket expenses shall be in accordance with **Section 3.8** of the MSA. Vendor shall obtain Client’s prior written approval for extraordinary expenses and shall follow Client’s then-current travel and expense policies as provided in writing.

3.9 **No Set-off.**  

The no set-off provision in **Section 3.9** of the MSA remains in full force and effect. Client may not set off or withhold amounts due except as expressly permitted under the invoice dispute provisions.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Amended Term; Relationship to MSA Section 4.1.**  

**Section 4.1 (Term)** of the MSA provides that the Agreement remains in effect until **October 7, 2029**. Notwithstanding the Expiration Date in the MSA, the Parties hereby agree that, solely with respect to the Extended Services and any New SOWs executed under this Amendment, the term is extended and shall continue in full force and effect until **October 5, 2030** (the “**Extended Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with the MSA as modified herein. This creates an **Amended Term** for the Extended Services, provided that if the MSA is not otherwise renewed for general purposes, the MSA shall nevertheless remain in effect solely for the limited purpose of governing the Extended Services and New SOWs through the Extended Expiration Date.

4.2 **Renewal.**  

The renewal provisions in **Section 4.2** of the MSA shall apply to any further extension beyond the Extended Expiration Date, and any such renewal shall be subject to mutual written agreement of the Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Modified Notice Period for Amendment).**  

The Parties agree that with respect to Program Expansion Work and the Extended Services only, **Section 4.3 (Termination for Convenience)** of the MSA is modified as follows:

  (a) **Client Termination for Convenience.** Client may terminate any or all New SOWs entered into under this Amendment, or the Extended Services portion of the MSA, for convenience upon at least **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor.

  (b) **Vendor Termination for Convenience.** Vendor may terminate any or all New SOWs under this Amendment for convenience upon at least **ninety (90) days’ prior written notice** to Client, consistent with the Vendor notice period in **Section 4.3(b)** of the MSA.

  (c) The payment obligations upon termination for convenience under **Section 4.3(c)** of the MSA shall apply to the Extended Services, including payment for Services performed, non-cancellable third-party costs, and any agreed early termination fees in the applicable SOW.

4.4 **Termination for Cause; Insolvency.**  

The termination for cause provisions in **Section 4.4 (Termination for Cause)** and **Section 4.5 (Insolvency)** of the MSA remain in full force and effect with respect to this Amendment and the Extended Services.

4.5 **Effect of Termination on Extended Services.**  

Upon expiration or termination of the Extended Services or any New SOW:

  (a) Client shall pay Vendor all amounts due for Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, plus any approved wind-down or Transition Assistance Services requested by Client, consistent with **Section 4.6(b)** of the MSA.

  (b) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4** of the MSA and **Article V** of this Amendment, and Vendor shall handle Client Data and Personal Data in accordance with **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)** of both the MSA and this Amendment.

  (c) At Client’s request, Vendor shall provide Transition Assistance Services for up to ninety (90) days following termination, at Vendor’s then-current rates, unless otherwise agreed in the applicable SOW, as contemplated in **Section 4.6(d)** of the MSA.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  

If the Parent Agreement (MSA-SUM-202507-473 / contract_473) is terminated in its entirety:

  (a) This Amendment and all New SOWs shall terminate concurrently, except to the extent necessary to wind down the Extended Services and perform Transition Assistance Services;

  (b) The survival provisions in **Section 4.7** of the MSA and **Article XVI** of this Amendment shall govern continuing rights and obligations; and

  (c) The Revised Liability Cap in **Article IX** of this Amendment shall continue to apply to claims arising from acts or omissions during the Amended Term.

## ARTICLE IX  
LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

9.1 **Modification of MSA Article IX (Risk Profile Adjustment).**  

The Parties acknowledge that **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)** of the MSA established a high-risk profile favorable to Vendor, including **no contractual cap** on liability (see **Section 9.1** of the MSA). In light of the expanded scope and mutual interests, the Parties agree to adopt a **medium-risk** allocation for claims arising after the Amendment Effective Date and relating to this Amendment and the Extended Services.

Accordingly, solely with respect to claims arising out of or relating to this Amendment and the Extended Services (including New SOWs), **Article IX of the MSA is hereby superseded and replaced** by the following provisions. Article IX of the MSA shall continue to apply to claims that arise solely from activities prior to the Amendment Effective Date and that are not related to the Extended Services.

9.2 **Liability Cap.**  

Except as expressly provided in **Section 9.4** below, in no event shall either Party’s **aggregate liability** arising out of or relating to this Amendment and the Extended Services, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), warranty, or otherwise, exceed an amount equal to **three (3) times the total fees paid or payable by Client to Vendor under the MSA and this Amendment for the Services giving rise to the claim during the twelve (12) month period immediately preceding the event giving rise to such claim** (the “**Revised Liability Cap**”).

9.3 **Exclusion of Certain Damages.**  

Except as provided in **Section 9.4**, in no event shall either Party be liable to the other for any **indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages**, or for any loss of profits, loss of revenue, loss of data, or loss of goodwill, arising out of or relating to this Amendment or the Extended Services, even if such Party has been advised of the possibility of such damages and regardless of the legal or equitable theory (contract, tort, or otherwise).

9.4 **Exceptions.**  

The limitations and exclusions in Sections 9.2 and 9.3 shall **not** apply to:

  (a) a Party’s indemnification obligations under **Article VIII** of the MSA and this Amendment, **to the extent such obligations relate to third-party claims alleging bodily injury, death, or tangible property damage**;
