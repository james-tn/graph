## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Engagement.** Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of professional services related to the design, implementation, integration, and ongoing managed support of Client’s enterprise cloud platform and associated business applications (the **“Project”**). The Project is intended to enable Client to migrate its core business systems from on‑premises infrastructure to a cloud‑based architecture, enhance scalability and reliability, improve data analytics capabilities, and establish long‑term managed services support.

2.2 **Phases of Services.** The Services shall be performed in multiple phases, as set forth below. The Parties acknowledge that the specifics of each phase may be further refined via Change Orders in accordance with Article XIII:

  (a) **Phase 1 – Discovery and Assessment (Estimated 3 Months).**  
  Vendor shall perform detailed discovery and assessment activities, including:  
  (i) conducting stakeholder interviews and workshops to gather business, technical, and security requirements;  
  (ii) reviewing Client’s existing systems, applications, databases, integrations, and network infrastructure;  
  (iii) assessing current performance, scalability, security posture, and operational processes;  
  (iv) identifying gaps, risks, and dependencies related to cloud migration and integration; and  
  (v) preparing and delivering a comprehensive **Assessment Report** and **Migration Roadmap**.

  (b) **Phase 2 – Solution Design (Estimated 3 Months).**  
  Based on the findings from Phase 1, Vendor shall design the target cloud architecture and solution components, including:  
  (i) high‑level and detailed solution architecture diagrams;  
  (ii) data migration strategy and mapping;  
  (iii) integration design for connecting Client’s systems with selected Third‑Party Products and internal applications;  
  (iv) security and compliance design, including identity management, access controls, logging, and monitoring; and  
  (v) high‑level testing and cutover plans. Vendor shall deliver a **Solution Design Document** and associated technical specifications.

  (c) **Phase 3 – Implementation and Migration (Estimated 12 Months).**  
  Vendor shall implement the designed solution, including:  
  (i) provisioning and configuring cloud infrastructure and platform services;  
  (ii) installing, configuring, and customizing applicable enterprise applications;  
  (iii) developing custom integrations, extensions, and scripts as required;  
  (iv) executing data migration in accordance with the approved migration plan;  
  (v) performing system, integration, performance, and security testing; and  
  (vi) providing support during go‑live and stabilization. Vendor shall deliver configuration documentation, migration artifacts, and test reports.

  (d) **Phase 4 – Managed Services and Support (through Expiration Date).**  
  From completion of Phase 3 through the Expiration Date, Vendor shall provide ongoing managed services, including:  
  (i) monitoring system performance and availability;  
  (ii) managing incidents, service requests, and change requests;  
  (iii) performing routine maintenance, patches, and updates;  
  (iv) providing Level 2 and Level 3 support for the implemented solutions; and  
  (v) generating monthly performance, incident, and capacity reports.

2.3 **Specific Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor shall provide the following Deliverables, which shall be deemed accepted unless Client provides written notice of rejection specifying material non‑conformities within ten (10) Business Days of delivery:

  (a) **Assessment Report** – A detailed report summarizing the current environment, including system inventory, application dependencies, security and compliance posture, and risk assessment.

  (b) **Migration Roadmap** – A prioritized roadmap outlining migration waves, dependencies, estimated timelines, and resource assumptions.

  (c) **Solution Design Document** – Comprehensive architecture and design specifications, including infrastructure topology, network segmentation, data models, integration patterns, security controls, and backup/DR strategy.

  (d) **Data Migration Plan and Mapping** – Detailed plan and mapping from legacy data structures to the target environment, including transformation rules.

  (e) **Configuration and Integration Artifacts** – Configuration scripts, integration connectors, APIs, and related technical documentation.

  (f) **Test Plans and Reports** – System, integration, performance, and security test plans and corresponding test results.

  (g) **Operations Runbook** – Documentation describing standard operating procedures, escalation paths, maintenance procedures, and monitoring practices.

  (h) **Training Materials** – End‑user and administrator training guides, quick reference documents, and, if requested, recorded training sessions.

  (i) **Monthly Service Reports** – Summaries of system availability, incidents, problem trends, changes implemented, and capacity metrics.

2.4 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  

  (b) using personnel with the skills, experience, and qualifications reasonably necessary to perform the assigned tasks;  

  (c) in accordance with the Service Levels specified in Article XII for the managed services portion of the engagement; and  

  (d) in material conformity with the requirements of this SOW and any applicable Change Orders.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.** Client shall, at no cost to Vendor (unless otherwise expressly stated), timely perform the following obligations, which are material to Vendor’s performance:

  (a) provide Vendor with accurate and complete information, documentation, and access to systems, facilities, personnel, and data as reasonably required to perform the Services;  

  (b) designate a project manager and key stakeholders with authority to make decisions, provide approvals, and resolve issues;  

  (c) obtain and maintain all necessary consents, licenses, and approvals from third parties (including relevant licensors and service providers) to allow Vendor to perform the Services;  

  (d) ensure that its systems and infrastructure meet any pre‑requisites or minimum requirements notified by Vendor; and  

  (e) review and respond to Vendor’s submissions, Deliverables, and requests for information or approvals in a timely manner and, in any event, within ten (10) Business Days unless otherwise specified.

Vendor shall not be liable for any failure to meet timelines, Service Levels, or other obligations to the extent caused by Client’s acts or omissions or failure to comply with this Section 2.5.

2.6 **Timeline.** The Parties anticipate that Phase 1 and Phase 2 shall be completed within the first six (6) to eight (8) months following the Effective Date, Phase 3 shall be completed within approximately twenty (20) months following the Effective Date, and Phase 4 shall continue thereafter through the Expiration Date (December 23, 2028), subject to modifications as may be agreed under Article XIII.   (a) **Priority 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of essential functionality impacting production:

  - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours.

  (b) **Priority 2 (High)** – Significant degradation with major impact on business operations:  
  - Initial response: within 4 hours during Business Days.  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 1 Business Day.

  (c) **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Partial, non‑critical loss of functionality; workaround available:
