## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview and Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  
2.1.1 Vendor shall provide the **Additional Services** and **Additional Deliverables** described in this Article II in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Work Order and the Parent Agreement. The Services described herein supplement and extend the Project described in **Section 1.14 (“Project”)** and **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.1.2 Except as expressly modified in this Work Order, all obligations, performance standards, acceptance mechanics and Client responsibilities set forth in **Sections 2.4 (Performance Standards), 2.5 (Client Responsibilities)** and **2.6 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the Additional Services and Additional Deliverables provided under this Work Order. For the avoidance of doubt, acceptance of Additional Deliverables shall be governed by the **Review Period** and “deemed acceptance” concepts set forth in **Section 2.6** of the Parent Agreement.

2.2 **Description of Additional Services.**  
Vendor shall perform the following Additional Services, which may overlap and be performed in parallel, as mutually agreed in the project plan:

2.2.1 **Expansion Rollout Services.**

(a) **Scope.** Vendor shall plan and execute a structured rollout of the platform implemented under the Parent Agreement to up to **six (6)** additional business units and/or geographic regions designated by Client.

(b) **Key Activities.**  
Vendor shall:

- Conduct up to **twelve (12)** additional stakeholder workshops focused on local requirements, regulatory considerations and process variances for the new business units, building upon the discovery work completed in **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements Gathering** under **Section 2.2.1** of the Parent Agreement;
- Produce **Localized Process Maps** and **Gap Analysis Reports** for each new business unit, identifying variances from the baseline processes documented in the original **Requirements Specification Document** (a Deliverable under **Section 2.2.1** and **Section 2.3** of the Parent Agreement);
- Configure environment-specific parameters (e.g., currencies, tax codes, local chart-of-accounts mappings) in alignment with the approved **Solution Design Document** produced under **Section 2.2.2** of the Parent Agreement, and update such design as necessary via mutually agreed WO Change Orders;
- Execute data migration planning and support for each rollout, leveraging the data migration strategies originally defined under **Section 2.2.4 (Phase 4 – Data Migration and Testing)** of the Parent Agreement, including up to one (1) test cycle and one (1) production cutover per additional business unit;
- Support localized User Acceptance Testing (UAT) for each rollout, coordinating with Client project teams and applying the same test planning and defect remediation standards as set forth under **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent Agreement.

2.2.2 **Advanced Analytics Services.**

Vendor shall design and implement an advanced analytics layer to extend the reporting capabilities of the platform delivered under the Parent Agreement.

Key tasks:

- Develop a **Data Warehouse / Data Mart** model aggregating transactional data from the platform and relevant external systems (ERP, CRM, financials) integrated under **Phase 3 (Section 2.2.3)** of the Parent Agreement;
- Define and document a **KPI and Metrics Library** (minimum of 50 KPIs) aligned with Client executive requirements, including financial, operational, and customer metrics, together with calculation logic and data lineage;
- Design and configure **Executive Dashboards** and **Operational Reports** (minimum of 20 dashboards and 40 tabular reports) in an agreed analytics tool (which may constitute **Third-Party Materials** as defined in **Section 1.19** of the Parent Agreement);
- Provide **Analytics Training Sessions** (not less than four (4) instructor-led remote or onsite sessions) for business analysts and power users, in addition to the training contemplated in **Phase 5 – Training and Go-Live Support** (Section 2.2.5 of the Parent Agreement);
- Deliver technical documentation of the analytics architecture, data model, and report catalogue, constituting Additional Deliverables under this Work Order.

2.2.3 **Integration Enhancement Services.**

Vendor shall design, develop, test, and deploy additional integration interfaces and enhancements to existing integrations beyond those originally contemplated in the Parent Agreement.

Activities include:

- Designing and implementing up to **eight (8)** new **integration connectors** with Client-approved systems (e.g., HRIS, procurement portals, third-party logistics providers), following the technical documentation practices specified in **Section 2.2.3 (Phase 3 – Configuration, Development, and Integration)** of the Parent Agreement;
- Enhancing performance, security and error handling of existing integrations implemented pursuant to the Parent Agreement, including implementation of message queuing, dead-letter queues, and improved logging where required;
- Providing end-to-end integration testing, including mock services and test harnesses where target systems are not continuously available;
- Updating the **integration inventory** originally produced under **Section 2.2.1** of the Parent Agreement to reflect the additional integration landscape.

2.2.4 **Managed Support Services (Extended Phase 6).**

Building on the optional services described in **Section 2.2.6 (Phase 6 – Ongoing Support and Maintenance (Optional))** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide **Managed Support Services** for the production platform and associated integrations and analytics components.

These services shall include:

- **Tier 2 and Tier 3 Support** for incidents, problems and service requests, with response targets aligned to the **Incident Response Times** in **Section 12.3** of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented by **Article XII** of this Work Order;
- **Application Monitoring and Proactive Health Checks**, including daily monitoring of key system metrics, integrations and job schedules, and monthly health-check reports to Client;
- **Minor Enhancements and Configuration Changes** (up to a monthly cap of **80 hours** of effort on a time-allocation basis) to accommodate small process changes, configuration tweaks, and incremental reports, with larger enhancements to be governed by WO Change Orders;
- Coordination with Client’s internal IT teams for **on-premises connectivity** issues as described in **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent Agreement.

2.3 **Additional Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the definition of Deliverables in **Section 1.8** and **Section 2.3** of the Parent Agreement, the Additional Deliverables under this Work Order shall include at least the following:

- Localized Process Maps and Gap Analysis Reports for each new business unit;
- Updated Solution Design Addendum(s) reflecting rollout and integration extensions;
- Data migration mapping addenda and cutover runbooks for each additional rollout wave;
- KPI and Metrics Library documentation;
- Data warehouse/data mart schema documentation and ETL specifications;
- Catalogue of dashboards and reports, with description and business owner information;
- Integration specifications and technical documentation (for new and enhanced integrations);
- Monthly health-check and incident summary reports for Managed Support Services;
- Updated platform **Support and Maintenance Runbook** supplementing any runbook produced under **Section 2.2.6** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Additional Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards and in accordance with the Specifications, applying the obligations from **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement. In particular:

- Vendor shall assign personnel with the requisite qualifications and experience in enterprise cloud platforms, integration, and analytics;
- Vendor shall adhere to the mutually agreed project plan and WO Milestones, subject to adjustments via WO Change Orders;
- Vendor shall comply with applicable Client policies communicated in writing, provided they do not conflict with the Parent Agreement or this Work Order.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities (WO-Specific).**  
In addition to its responsibilities set forth in **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

- Provide designated business owners and subject matter experts for each new business unit involved in the Expansion Rollout Services;
- Ensure timely availability of test data and access to third-party systems for Integration Enhancement Services;
- Assign data governance representatives to approve KPI definitions and analytics outputs;
- Provide timely approvals or comments on Additional Deliverables, consistent with the **Review Period** in **Section 2.6** of the Parent Agreement.

2.6 **Acceptance of Additional Deliverables.**  
The acceptance procedure in **Section 2.6 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to all Additional Deliverables, provided that the Parties may agree in writing (e.g., in a project plan) to longer review periods for particularly complex Deliverables, such as overall analytics architecture, without modifying the underlying acceptance principles.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **WO Service Term.**  
This Work Order shall commence on the **WO Effective Date** and shall continue in full force and effect until **December 22, 2026** (the **WO Expiration Date**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or automatically upon the termination or expiration of the Parent Agreement as provided in Section 4.5.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience (WO).**  
Notwithstanding the **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** period for Client’s termination for convenience set forth in **Section 4.2** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties agree that, solely with respect to this Work Order:

- **Client** may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason upon not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor; and  
- **Vendor** may terminate this Work Order, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason upon not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Client.

For the avoidance of doubt, the Parent Agreement itself (contract_439) shall remain subject to its own termination provisions unless separately amended in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  
The termination for cause provisions in **Section 4.4 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Work Order, such that either Party may terminate this Work Order for material breach by the other Party, subject to the cure periods specified therein. A material breach of this Work Order shall constitute a material breach of the Parent Agreement and vice versa, if such breach materially affects the Project as a whole.

4.4 **Termination for Insolvency.**  
The provisions of **Section 4.5 (Termination for Insolvency)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to this Work Order. Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party experiences an insolvency event described in that section.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration of Parent Agreement.**  
4.5.1 This Work Order is **executed under and subject to** the Parent Agreement. Accordingly, if the Parent Agreement is terminated or expires for any reason:

- This Work Order shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, without further action by either Party;  
- Vendor shall cease performance of the Additional Services, except to the extent Client requests Transition Assistance in accordance with Section 4.6; and  
- The Parties’ rights and obligations shall be as described in **Section 4.6 (Effect of Termination)** of the Parent Agreement and Section 4.6 of this Work Order.

4.5.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, if the Parent Agreement is renewed or extended, this Work Order shall continue in effect through the WO Expiration Date unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of this Work Order.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:

- Client shall pay Vendor all Fees and expenses accrued and unpaid through the effective date of termination, including any third-party termination/cancellation charges that Vendor cannot mitigate with commercially reasonable efforts, as contemplated in **Section 4.6** of the Parent Agreement;  
- Upon Client’s written request, Vendor shall provide **Transition Assistance** for up to thirty (30) days following termination, at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates, consistent with the transition obligations set forth in **Section 4.6** of the Parent Agreement;  
- The Parties shall return or destroy Confidential Information consistent with **Section 5.5** of the Parent Agreement and Section 5.5 of this Work Order; and  
- Any licenses granted under this Work Order shall terminate except to the extent expressly specified as surviving termination in **Article VI** herein and **Article VI** of the Parent Agreement.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Those provisions of this Work Order which by their nature should survive termination or expiration, including without limitation **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**, and Sections 3.5, 3.7, 4.6, 5.5, and 11.6, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Work Order, together with the surviving provisions of the Parent Agreement referenced therein.
