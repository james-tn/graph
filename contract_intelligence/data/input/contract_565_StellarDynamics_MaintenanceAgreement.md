## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

(a) This Maintenance Agreement **supports the Software License dated January 06, 2024 (Ref: SLA-STE-202401-487, contract_487)**.  

(b) This Maintenance Agreement **supplements and is entered into pursuant to** the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly modified or supplemented herein, all terms and conditions of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect and apply to the Maintenance Services.  

(c) For clarity, the Maintenance Services provided hereunder are **Services** within the meaning of Section 1.12 and Article II of the Parent Agreement and are subject to all applicable provisions of the Parent Agreement, including but not limited to **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**, **Article VIII (Indemnification)**, **Article IX (Limitation of Liability)**, and **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.

2.2 **General Description of Maintenance Services.**  

Vendor shall provide the following Maintenance Services for the Supported Software during the Maintenance Term, in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards, as set forth in Section 2.1 of the Parent Agreement:

(a) **Incident Management and Technical Support.** Vendor shall provide technical support to Client’s designated support contacts for the reporting, triage, and resolution of Incidents and Errors affecting the Supported Software. This includes:

  i. Logging and tracking of all Incidents and Service Requests in Vendor’s ticketing system;  
  ii. Classification of Incidents by severity level consistent with Section 12.4 of the Parent Agreement (Severity 1–4);  
  iii. Provision of workarounds, Error Corrections, or other resolutions within the Response and Target Resolution Times described in Article XII herein; and  
  iv. Regular status updates to Client regarding critical and high-severity Incidents.

(b) **Updates and Patches.** In addition to Vendor’s existing obligation under Section 2.7(d) and Section 1.18 of the Parent Agreement to apply security patches and Updates, Vendor shall, as part of the Maintenance Services:

  i. Provide and deploy all generally available **Updates** (bug fixes, error corrections, patches, minor enhancements) to the Supported Software at no additional charge;  
  ii. Coordinate with Client regarding the scheduling of Updates in non-disruptive windows, giving reasonable notice consistent with Scheduled Maintenance procedures; and  
  iii. Inform Client of material changes or deprecations introduced by Updates that may impact Client’s use or integrations.

(c) **Upgrades.** As further clarification of Section 1.18 of the Parent Agreement:

  i. Vendor shall make new major versions or **Upgrades** of the Supported Software available to Client when released to Vendor’s enterprise customers generally;  
  ii. Upgrades designated by Vendor as being included in maintenance shall be deployed at no additional license fee, subject to any agreed implementation services;  
  iii. Upgrades that Vendor designates as separately chargeable may be implemented under a separate SOW and may entail additional fees, but Maintenance Services shall continue to apply to the prior supported version until end-of-life dates communicated with at least six (6) months prior written notice.

(d) **Configuration and Optimization Support.** Building on the configuration services described in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall:

  i. Provide remote guidance and best practice recommendations to optimize workflows, dashboards, and reporting configurations within the Supported Software;  
  ii. Assist with minor configuration changes that do not constitute custom development or a Change Request under Article XIII; and  
  iii. Provide up to a mutually agreed number of hours per calendar quarter of consultative support for performance tuning and capacity planning.

(e) **Proactive Monitoring and Health Checks.** Vendor shall implement reasonable monitoring tools to:

  i. Track uptime and performance in line with the Uptime Commitment in Article XII herein and Section 12.1 of the Parent Agreement;  
  ii. Detect anomalies that may signal emerging issues and take remedial actions; and  
  iii. Conduct at least one (1) annual system health review, providing a written summary to Client with recommendations.

2.3 **Support Channels and Contact Points.**  

Vendor shall provide Client with dedicated support contact information, including:

(a) An email address and web-based ticketing portal for non-Severity 1 Incidents and Service Requests;  

(b) A 24x7 phone hotline for Severity 1 (Critical) Incidents, consistent with **Section 12.4(a)** of the Parent Agreement, as enhanced under this Maintenance Agreement; and  

(c) Identification of a **Technical Account Manager** (“TAM”) who will serve as Client’s primary point of contact for recurring maintenance matters, periodic reviews, and escalation.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  

In addition to the responsibilities set forth in Section 2.8 of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

(a) Designate up to three (3) authorized support contacts who are trained in the Supported Software and are the primary liaison with Vendor;  

(b) Use reasonable efforts to provide sufficient information and documentation when reporting Incidents (including screenshots, logs, steps to reproduce, and impact assessment);  

(c) Use the Supported Software in accordance with the Documentation and the use restrictions in Section 6.3 of the Parent Agreement;  

(d) Maintain Client-side infrastructure and connectivity required to access the Supported Software, as referenced in Section 2.3 and Section 2.8(c) of the Parent Agreement; and  

(e) Implement Updates and Upgrades as reasonably recommended by Vendor, especially security-related Updates, and not unreasonably refuse or delay such implementations.

2.5 **Supported Environments.**  

The Maintenance Services are provided solely for the Supported Software as hosted by Vendor in accordance with Section 2.3 of the Parent Agreement. Maintenance Services do not extend to:

(a) Client’s on-premises systems, networking equipment, or third-party applications not supplied or managed by Vendor (except to the limited extent specified in integration support provisions);  

(b) Third-party software or services that interface with the Supported Software, except where such support is expressly agreed in a separate SOW; or  

(c) Versions of the Supported Software that have reached end-of-support or end-of-life as communicated in writing by Vendor with reasonable advance notice.

2.6 **Maintenance Service Levels and Performance Standards.**  

Vendor shall perform the Maintenance Services:

(a) Using personnel with skills and experience reasonably commensurate with the complexity of the tasks, consistent with Section 2.7(a) of the Parent Agreement;  

(b) In good faith and in a commercially reasonable manner designed to meet or exceed the Uptime and Response Time commitments specified in **Article XII** herein, which enhance and are complementary to the commitments in **Article XII of the Parent Agreement**; and  

(c) In compliance with applicable laws and regulations, consistent with the representations in **Section 7.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **Deliverables under this Maintenance Agreement.**  

Vendor’s Deliverables (as that term is defined in Section 1.5 of the Parent Agreement) under this Maintenance Agreement shall include, without limitation:

(a) Access to the Supported Software in accordance with the Service Levels herein;  

(b) Incident and support tickets with documented resolutions;  

(c) Periodic maintenance reports, including uptime statistics, incident summaries, and status of major Updates/Upgrades;  

(d) Annual system health check reports and recommended improvement actions; and  

(e) Any additional documentation, scripts, configuration artifacts, or knowledge base materials developed specifically for Client’s environment in connection with the Maintenance Services.

2.8 **Exclusions from Maintenance Services.**  

The following are excluded from the Maintenance Services unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing (e.g., via SOW under Section 1.15 of the Parent Agreement):

(a) Major functional enhancements or new modules not currently licensed by Client under the Parent Agreement;  

(b) Custom development work (including custom integrations beyond the two integrations contemplated in Section 2.4(d) of the Parent Agreement);  

(c) On-site services at Client’s premises, unless pre-approved and compensated as expenses under **Section 3.8 of the Parent Agreement** and this Maintenance Agreement;  

(d) Issues caused by Client’s use of the Supported Software in violation of the Parent Agreement, this Maintenance Agreement, or the Documentation; and  

(e) Data restoration or reconstruction outside what is required under standard backup and recovery procedures, except where the incident stems from Vendor’s failure to perform those procedures.

2.9 **Priority of Documents.**  

In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Maintenance Agreement and the Parent Agreement with respect to Maintenance Services, the terms of this Maintenance Agreement shall govern solely with respect to the Maintenance Services; otherwise, the Parent Agreement controls. For all other matters not specifically addressed herein, the Parent Agreement shall apply.

(a) Client shall pay each undisputed invoice within **thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 30”**), consistent with Section 3.4(a) of the Parent Agreement.

(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest as described in Section 3.5(a) of the Parent Agreement.  

(b) Without limiting Vendor’s rights under Section 3.5(b) and Section 4.5 of the Parent Agreement, if any undisputed Maintenance Fees remain unpaid more than thirty (30) days past the due date, Vendor may, after giving Client at least ten (10) days’ prior written notice and opportunity to cure, suspend Maintenance Services until all overdue amounts and applicable interest are paid.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  

Client may dispute invoices in good faith in accordance with Section 3.6 of the Parent Agreement. During the pendency of a bona fide dispute, Vendor shall not suspend Maintenance Services with respect to the disputed amounts, provided Client timely pays all undisputed amounts.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Maintenance Term.**  

The term of this Maintenance Agreement shall commence on the Maintenance Effective Date, **September 17, 2024**, and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 04, 2027**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Maintenance Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  

(a) This Maintenance Agreement may be renewed upon mutual written agreement of the Parties, including agreement on renewal term(s) and Maintenance Fees.  

(b) For clarity, there is no automatic renewal. Renewal is subject to the Parties’ mutual written agreement, similar to Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

(a) Client may terminate this Maintenance Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor.  

(b) Vendor may terminate this Maintenance Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client.  

(c) Upon termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor for (i) any accrued but unpaid Maintenance Fees up to the effective date of termination; and (ii) any non-cancellable commitments reasonably incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf with Client’s prior written approval, consistent with Section 4.3 of the Parent Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

Either Party may terminate this Maintenance Agreement upon written notice if:

(a) The other Party materially breaches this Maintenance Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or  

(b) Any of the insolvency or bankruptcy events set forth in Section 4.4(b) of the Parent Agreement occur with respect to the other Party.

4.5 **Termination for Non-Payment.**  

Vendor may terminate this Maintenance Agreement if Client fails to pay any undisputed Maintenance Fees within forty-five (45) days after its due date and does not cure such non-payment within ten (10) days after written notice, consistent with Section 4.5 of the Parent Agreement.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Maintenance Agreement for any reason:

(a) Vendor’s obligation to provide Maintenance Services shall cease as of the effective date of termination;  

(b) Client shall promptly pay all accrued but unpaid Maintenance Fees and approved expenses through the effective date of termination;  

(c) The rights and licenses to the Software governed by the Parent Agreement shall be unaffected by termination of this Maintenance Agreement, except that Vendor shall have no further maintenance or support obligations under this Maintenance Agreement (but any support obligations under the Parent Agreement that survive shall continue, if applicable);  

(d) Each Party shall handle Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4 of the Parent Agreement**; and  

(e) Those provisions of this Maintenance Agreement that by their nature should survive (including, without limitation, payment obligations, confidentiality, IP protections, indemnification, limitations of liability, and dispute resolution) shall survive.

4.7 **Effect of Termination or Expiration of Parent Agreement.**  

(a) If the Parent Agreement is terminated or expires and the Parties do not enter into a replacement license agreement, this Maintenance Agreement shall automatically terminate effective as of the termination or expiration date of the Parent Agreement, and Section 4.6 shall apply mutatis mutandis.  

(b) If the Parties extend or renew the Parent Agreement for continued use of the Software and explicitly agree in writing to continue Maintenance Services under this Maintenance Agreement (or a successor maintenance agreement), then Maintenance Services may continue under such terms as agreed.

4.8 **No Liability for Termination.**  

Except as expressly provided herein, neither Party shall be liable to the other for any damages arising solely from a termination effected in accordance with this Article IV, including loss of prospective profits or investments, consistent with Section 4.7 of the Parent Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement SLA.**  

This Article XII refines and supplements the **Service Level Agreements** set forth in **Article XII of the Parent Agreement** for the duration of the Maintenance Term. If there is any inconsistency between this Article XII and Article XII of the Parent Agreement, this Article XII shall govern with respect to Maintenance Services.

12.2 **Service Availability (Uptime Commitment).**  

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to make the production instance of the Supported Software available **99.95%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Downtime, an enhancement over the 99.9% commitment in Section 12.1 of the Parent Agreement.

12.3 **Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Downtime.**  

Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Downtime shall be as defined in **Section 12.2 of the Parent Agreement**, provided that:

(a) Vendor will provide at least **five (5) Business Days’** notice for any Scheduled Maintenance that is expected to cause more than thirty (30) minutes of downtime; and  

(b) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to conduct Scheduled Maintenance during off-peak hours relevant to Client’s primary operating time zones.

12.4 **Support Response and Target Resolution Times.**  

Vendor shall respond to Incidents according to the severity levels defined below (which build upon Section 12.4 of the Parent Agreement):

(a) **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of the Supported Software or critical functionality for a significant number of Users, with no workaround.

  - Initial Response: within **1 hour**, 24x7, including weekends and holidays;  
  - Target Resolution or effective workaround: within **4 hours** of initial response.

(b) **Severity 2 (High):** Significant impairment of core functionality with limited workaround, affecting business-critical operations.

  - Initial Response: within **2 Business Hours** during Support Hours;  
  - Target Resolution or effective workaround: within **1 Business Day**.

(c) **Severity 3 (Medium):** Moderate impact with available workaround; non-critical functionality issues.

  - Initial Response: within **8 Business Hours**;  
  - Target Resolution: within **5 Business Days**, or included in next scheduled Update.

(d) **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, and general questions or requests.

  - Initial Response: within **2 Business Days**;  
  - Target Resolution: as mutually agreed, generally within next scheduled release or maintenance window.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the enhanced Uptime Commitment in Section 12.2 in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to service credits calculated as follows (superseding Section 12.5 of the Parent Agreement for the Maintenance Term):

- Uptime between 99.0% and 99.95%: **5%** of the applicable monthly portion of Maintenance Fees;  
- Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: **10%** of the applicable monthly portion of Maintenance Fees;  
- Uptime below 98.0%: **25%** of the applicable monthly portion of Maintenance Fees.

Service credits are Vendor’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the Uptime Commitment, but do not limit Client’s rights to terminate for repeated material breaches as provided in Article IV.

12.6 **Reporting.**  

Vendor shall provide monthly or quarterly (as requested) reports consistent with **Section 12.6 of the Parent Agreement**, including uptime statistics, incident summaries, and SLA performance.
