## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Description of the Project.**  
Vendor shall perform for Client a series of IT consulting, implementation, integration, and managed support services designed to support Client’s enterprise customer engagement platform, analytics infrastructure, and internal business applications (the **“Project”**). The Project includes, without limitation:

  a. Assessment and design of a scalable, cloud-based integration architecture supporting Client’s CRM, ERP, and data warehouse platforms;  
  b. Configuration and customization of designated software tools selected by Client;  
  c. Implementation of data pipelines, APIs, and middleware to enable near real-time data synchronization;  
  d. Development and delivery of appropriate documentation and training materials; and  
  e. Provision of ongoing managed support and maintenance services throughout the Term, subject to the Service Levels in **Article XII**.

2.2 **Core Services.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor’s Services include the following major workstreams:

  2.2.1 **Discovery and Requirements Gathering.**  
  Vendor shall conduct workshops with Client’s business and technical stakeholders to document detailed requirements, including future-state business processes, data flows, reporting needs, performance targets, and security requirements. Vendor shall:

  - Conduct up to ten (10) on-site or virtual discovery sessions;  
  - Perform a gap analysis between Client’s current systems and desired future state;  
  - Produce a comprehensive **Requirements Specification Document** (RSD); and  
  - Obtain written confirmation from Client that the RSD accurately reflects Client’s requirements.

  2.2.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.**  
  Based on the RSD, Vendor shall design a solution architecture including:

  - High-level and detailed architecture diagrams;  
  - Interface control documents for each system integration;  
  - Data model and data mapping documentation;  
  - Security and access control design; and  
  - High-availability and disaster recovery strategy aligned with the Service Levels.

  Vendor shall present the proposed design to Client for review and comment, incorporate mutually agreed revisions, and provide a final **Solution Design Document** (SDD) for Client’s Acceptance in accordance with **Section 2.4**.

  2.2.3 **Configuration, Development, and Integration.**  
  Vendor shall configure and, where applicable, customize designated software tools and integration components to implement the approved SDD. Such efforts shall include:

  - Configuration of workflow rules, business logic, and user interface elements;  
  - Development of integration components, APIs, and data connectors;  
  - Implementation of data validation and transformation rules;  
  - Coordination with Client’s internal IT teams to ensure environment readiness; and  
  - Adherence to Client’s secure coding and change management policies if provided.

  Vendor shall maintain a configuration log and source code repository, as applicable, and shall ensure that all configuration and code are version-controlled and documented.

  2.2.4 **Testing and Quality Assurance.**  
  Vendor shall plan and execute test activities, including:

  - Unit testing of all configuration and code components;  
  - Integration testing of end-to-end data flows;  
  - Performance and load testing to validate Service Levels;  
  - Security testing consistent with Client’s security guidelines (as provided); and  
  - User acceptance testing (UAT) support, including preparation of test scripts and assistance in defect triage.

  Vendor shall remediate defects identified during testing in a timely manner and shall not seek Acceptance of a Deliverable until critical and high-severity defects have been resolved or otherwise accepted in writing by Client pursuant to a documented risk acceptance process.

  2.2.5 **Deployment and Transition to Operations.**  
  Vendor shall plan and execute deployment into Client’s production environment in close coordination with Client. This includes:

  - Preparing a cutover plan and rollback procedures;  
  - Coordinating deployment windows and approvals;  
  - Providing on-site or remote support during go-live; and  
  - Providing a **Runbook** detailing operational procedures, monitoring, and support processes.

  2.2.6 **Managed Support and Maintenance.**  
  During the Term, Vendor shall provide ongoing support and maintenance services, including:

  - Level 2 and Level 3 support for the implemented solution;  
  - Incident management, including response and resolution within agreed Service Levels;  
  - Routine maintenance, updates, and patching of components implemented by Vendor;  
  - Root cause analysis and problem management for recurring issues; and  
  - Monthly service reports summarizing incidents, performance, and Improvement recommendations.

2.3 **Milestones and Deliverables.**  
The Project shall be structured into the following indicative Milestones, each associated with Deliverables and Acceptance Criteria:

  a. **Milestone 1 – Discovery Completion**  
     - Deliverables: Requirements Specification Document (RSD).  
     - Target Completion: Within sixty (60) days following the Effective Date.  

  b. **Milestone 2 – Design Completion**  
     - Deliverables: Solution Design Document (SDD), Architecture diagrams, Data mapping.  
     - Target Completion: Within one hundred twenty (120) days following the Effective Date.  

  c. **Milestone 3 – Build and Integration Completion**  
     - Deliverables: Configured environments, Integration components, Test plans.  
     - Target Completion: Within two hundred forty (240) days following the Effective Date.  

  d. **Milestone 4 – Testing and Go-Live**  
     - Deliverables: Test reports, UAT sign-off, Runbook, Production deployment.  
     - Target Completion: Within three hundred (300) days following the Effective Date.  

  e. **Milestone 5 – Ongoing Managed Services**  
     - Deliverables: Monthly service reports, Incident logs, Change logs.  
     - Duration: From go-live through the Expiration Date, unless terminated earlier.

The Parties may refine and supplement the Milestones, Deliverables, and timelines through a mutually executed Change Order.

2.4 **Acceptance Procedures.**  
  2.4.1 **Submission for Acceptance.**  
  Upon completion of a Deliverable or Milestone, Vendor shall submit the Deliverable to Client for review. Vendor shall provide all documentation reasonably necessary for Client to verify compliance with the Acceptance Criteria.

  2.4.2 **Review Period.**  
  Unless otherwise specified, Client shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt of a Deliverable to: (a) provide written Acceptance; or (b) provide a written rejection identifying in reasonable detail the non-conformities to the Acceptance Criteria.

  2.4.3 **Deemed Acceptance.**  
  If Client neither accepts nor rejects a Deliverable in writing within the review period, the Deliverable shall not be deemed Accepted by default; instead, Vendor may request escalation pursuant to **Article XIV**, and the Parties shall promptly meet to resolve the status of such Deliverable in good faith.

  2.4.4 **Correction of Defects.**  
  If Client rejects a Deliverable, Vendor shall, at no additional cost, promptly correct the deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for Acceptance. Client shall have an additional review period of ten (10) Business Days for each resubmission.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  a. In a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  b. In compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and Data Protection Laws;  
  c. In accordance with the timelines, Service Levels, and Specifications set forth in this Work Order; and  
  d. Using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and training for their assigned roles.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client shall:

  a. Provide Vendor with access to Client’s relevant systems, facilities, data, and personnel as reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  
  b. Designate a project manager and key stakeholders to coordinate with Vendor;  
  c. Timely review and respond to Vendor’s requests for information, approvals, and decisions; and  
  d. Fulfill any other responsibilities expressly described in this Work Order or in any Change Order.

Vendor’s obligations hereunder shall be conditioned upon Client’s timely performance of its responsibilities. Vendor shall promptly notify Client in writing if Vendor reasonably believes Client’s delay or failure to perform materially impairs Vendor’s ability to meet its obligations, and the Parties shall cooperate to mitigate such impact.

3.10 **No Withholding of Services.**  
Except as expressly provided in **Section 3.7**, Vendor shall not suspend or withhold any Services solely as a result of a payment dispute that is being handled in accordance with **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)**.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until **September 22, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience without cause upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. Upon such termination:

  a. Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and approved expenses incurred through the effective date of termination;  
  b. For fixed-fee components, the Parties shall reasonably apportion fees based on work performed and value received, taking into account Milestones achieved and partially completed activities; and  
  c. Any prepaid but unearned Managed Services Fees shall be prorated and refunded to Client within thirty (30) days.

4.3 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Work Order for cause if the other Party:

  a. Materially breaches this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days for non-payment) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  b. Becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, or becomes the subject of any bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.4 **Termination for Persistent Service Level Failures.**  
Client shall have the right to terminate this Work Order upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if Vendor fails to meet critical Service Levels, as defined in **Article XII**, at a severity or frequency that materially impacts Client’s operations and such failures are not cured or mitigated pursuant to a mutually agreed remediation plan.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon any expiration or termination of this Work Order:

  a. Vendor shall promptly cease performing Services, except as necessary to provide any agreed transition assistance;  
  b. Vendor shall deliver to Client all completed and in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid or will pay in accordance with this Work Order;  
  c. Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5**; and  
  d. Any licenses granted pursuant to **Article VI** shall survive to the extent expressly provided therein.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Work Order: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

Before initiating arbitration or litigation, the Parties shall attempt to resolve the Dispute through non-binding mediation conducted by a mutually agreed mediator in Seattle, Washington. Mediation shall commence within thirty (30) days after one Party’s written request for mediation.

14.4 **Arbitration / Litigation.**  
If the Parties are unable to resolve the Dispute through mediation within sixty (60) days of commencement of mediation, either Party may submit the Dispute to binding arbitration before a single arbitrator under the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association (**AAA**). The arbitration shall be held in Seattle, Washington, and conducted in English. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek interim injunctive or equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to protect its Confidential Information or Intellectual Property rights.

During the pendency of any Dispute, Vendor shall continue to perform the Services, and Client shall continue to pay undisputed amounts, in accordance with this Work Order.

## FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, riots, civil disturbances, labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own employees), epidemics, pandemics, governmental actions, power or Internet outages not caused by the affected Party, or other events of similar nature.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of such event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party continues to use reasonable efforts to perform its obligations to the extent possible.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects a Party’s performance, either Party may terminate this Work Order upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability for such termination, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
