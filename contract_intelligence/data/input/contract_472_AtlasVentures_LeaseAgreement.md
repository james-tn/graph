## RECITALS

## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF LEASED PREMISES AND SERVICES

### 2.1 Grant of Lease

2.1.1 Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Landlord hereby leases to Tenant, and Tenant hereby leases from Landlord, the **Premises** located in the commercial office building commonly known as **Atlas Tower**, situated at **1200 Harbor Point, Wilmington, DE 19801** (the “**Building**”).

2.1.2 The Premises shall consist of approximately **22,000 rentable square feet** on the 9th and 10th floors of the Building, as more particularly described and outlined on the floor plan attached hereto as **Exhibit A**. The rentable square footage is agreed upon for all purposes of this Agreement, including the calculation of Rent and Operating Expenses, irrespective of actual measurement variations.

2.1.3 The lease of the Premises includes the right, in common with other tenants of the Building and their invitees, to use the Building’s **Common Areas**, including lobbies, corridors, elevators, restrooms, stairways, loading docks, parking areas (if any), and other common facilities, all as designated from time to time by Landlord, subject to reasonable rules and regulations promulgated by Landlord pursuant to Section 2.9.

### 2.2 Intended Use and Permitted Use

2.2.1 Tenant shall use the Premises solely for general office, administrative, and related commercial business operations consistent with a first-class office building and for no other purpose without Landlord’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed.

2.2.2 Tenant shall not use or permit the Premises to be used for any unlawful purpose, any purpose that would violate applicable zoning or building codes, any purpose that would materially increase the risk of fire or other casualty, or any purpose that would cause a nuisance or unreasonable disturbance to other tenants of the Building.

2.2.3 Tenant shall be solely responsible for obtaining and maintaining, at its cost, any governmental approvals, permits, licenses, or consents necessary for its particular business operations, provided that Landlord shall reasonably cooperate (without material cost to Landlord) in executing any required building owner documents.

### 2.3 Delivery Condition; Landlord Work

2.3.1 Landlord shall deliver the Premises to Tenant in **broom clean** condition, with all Building systems serving the Premises (including HVAC, electrical, and plumbing) in good working order as of the Commencement Date and compliant with applicable laws, ordinances, and regulations as of such date.

2.3.2 Any initial improvements to be performed by Landlord prior to Tenant’s occupancy (“**Landlord Work**”) shall be described in **Exhibit B** (if applicable). Landlord shall use commercially reasonable efforts to substantially complete the Landlord Work by the Commencement Date and shall notify Tenant in writing upon substantial completion.

2.3.3 Tenant shall have the right to inspect the Premises prior to occupancy and shall promptly notify Landlord in writing of any material deficiencies in the Landlord Work or Building systems. Landlord shall remedy any such deficiencies within a reasonable period, taking into account the nature and complexity of the work.

### 2.4 Services and Utilities

2.4.1 Landlord shall provide or cause to be provided to the Premises the following **Services** and **Utilities**, subject to the SLAs set forth in Article XII:

   (a) **HVAC** services during Business Hours (typically 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday through Friday, excluding federal holidays), sufficient to maintain a commercially reasonable temperature and air quality for standard office use;  
   (b) **Electricity** for normal office lighting and standard office equipment consumption;  
   (c) **Water and Sewer** for restrooms and basic office use;  
   (d) **Janitorial Services** to the Premises on Business Days in accordance with building standards;  
   (e) **Security and Access Control** for the Building, including controlled access outside of Business Hours; and  
   (f) **Maintenance and Repairs** of the Building’s structural components, common areas, and central mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems.

2.4.2 To the extent practicable, Landlord shall separately meter or submeter electricity and other Utilities serving the Premises. If separate metering is not feasible, Tenant’s proportional share of such Utilities shall be reasonably allocated based on rentable square footage or other equitable allocation methodologies and shall be included in Operating Expenses.

2.4.3 Tenant may request **after-hours HVAC** or other off-schedule Services, subject to availability of building staff and systems. Any such additional Services shall be billed to Tenant at Landlord’s then-current reasonable rates, which shall be disclosed upon Tenant’s written request and may be updated by Landlord from time to time.

### 2.5 Maintenance and Repairs

2.5.1 Landlord shall, at its expense (subject to inclusion in Operating Expenses where applicable), maintain in good order and repair the **Building structure**, including roof, exterior walls, foundation, main electrical and mechanical rooms and risers, plumbing risers, elevators, and Common Areas, except to the extent damage is caused by Tenant’s negligence or willful misconduct, in which case Tenant shall reimburse Landlord for the cost of such repairs.

2.5.2 Tenant shall, at its sole cost and expense, maintain in good order and repair all **non-structural** portions of the Premises, including interior walls, doors, windows (interior side), floor coverings, ceilings, Tenant’s fixtures, equipment, and any Tenant Improvements, except to the extent such repairs are due to Landlord’s negligence or willful misconduct.

2.5.3 Tenant shall promptly notify Landlord of any condition in the Premises or Building that requires Landlord’s repair or maintenance, and Landlord shall use commercially reasonable efforts to effect such repairs within the Response Times set forth in the SLAs. Tenant shall not perform structural or Building system repairs without Landlord’s prior written consent.

### 2.6 Tenant Improvements and Alterations

2.6.1 Tenant shall not make any alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises (“**Alterations**”) without Landlord’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed for non-structural Alterations that do not adversely affect Building systems or other tenants.

2.6.2 All Alterations shall be performed at Tenant’s sole cost and expense, in a good and workmanlike manner, in compliance with all applicable laws and building rules, and using contractors reasonably approved by Landlord. Tenant shall obtain all necessary permits and approvals.

2.6.3 Except as otherwise provided in a written consent or work letter, all Alterations shall become the property of Landlord upon installation and shall remain with the Premises upon expiration or earlier termination of this Agreement, without any obligation by Landlord to reimburse Tenant, unless Landlord specifically requires Tenant in writing to remove certain Alterations and restore the Premises to its prior condition (reasonable wear and tear excepted).

### 2.7 Access and Security

2.7.1 Tenant and its employees, contractors, and invitees shall have reasonable access to the Premises **24 hours per day, 7 days per week**, subject to Building security procedures and emergency closures.

2.7.2 Landlord shall implement commercially reasonable security measures for the Building, which may include access cards, security personnel, and monitoring systems. Landlord does not guarantee absolute security, and Tenant remains responsible for the security of its own property and data within the Premises, subject to Article XI.

### 2.8 Compliance with Laws

2.8.1 Landlord shall ensure that the Building, including the Premises (excluding Tenant Improvements and Alterations), complies in all material respects with all applicable building codes, fire codes, accessibility laws, and other laws, rules, and regulations as of the Commencement Date and during the Term, subject to customary capital expenditure limitations.

2.8.2 Tenant shall comply with all laws, rules, and regulations applicable to its business operations and use of the Premises, including environmental, health, and safety laws, and shall not store or use hazardous materials except in de minimis quantities customarily used in general office environments.

### 2.9 Rules and Regulations

2.9.1 Landlord may from time to time adopt reasonable, non-discriminatory **rules and regulations** for the safety, care, and cleanliness of the Building and the Common Areas, and for the preservation of good order therein. Landlord shall provide Tenant with a written copy of all such rules and regulations and any amendments thereto.

2.9.2 Tenant shall comply, and shall cause its employees, contractors, visitors, and invitees to comply, with such rules and regulations, provided that such rules do not materially and adversely impair Tenant’s rights expressly granted under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value; Base Rent

3.1.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the **Contract Value** of this Agreement over the Term is **US$1,649,371**, representing the aggregate of Base Rent, estimated Operating Expenses, Taxes, and other anticipated charges.

3.1.2 Tenant shall pay to Landlord, without offset or deduction except as expressly provided in this Agreement, **Base Rent** in the amounts and according to the schedule set forth in **Exhibit C** (Rent Schedule). By way of example, Base Rent may be structured on a monthly basis as follows:

- February 25, 2025 – February 24, 2026: US$94,000 per month  
- February 25, 2026 – May 24, 2026: US$97,000 per month  

(These figures are illustrative and may be adjusted in Exhibit C to align with the Contract Value and actual negotiations.)

3.1.3 Base Rent shall be due and payable in advance on or before the **first (1st) day of each calendar month** during the Term, except that Base Rent for any partial month at the beginning or end of the Term shall be prorated on a per diem basis.

### 3.2 Additional Rent; Operating Expenses and Taxes

3.2.1 In addition to Base Rent, Tenant shall pay Tenant’s **Proportionate Share** of Operating Expenses and Taxes as **Additional Rent**. Tenant’s Proportionate Share shall be the fraction, expressed as a percentage, the numerator of which is the rentable square footage of the Premises and the denominator of which is the total rentable square footage of the Building, as reasonably determined by Landlord.

3.2.2 Landlord shall provide Tenant with an annual **Operating Expense and Tax Estimate** for each Lease Year. Tenant shall pay one-twelfth (1/12) of its estimated share of such amounts monthly, together with the payment of Base Rent.

3.2.3 Within a reasonable period after each Lease Year, Landlord shall provide Tenant with a **statement of actual Operating Expenses and Taxes** for such Lease Year. If Tenant’s estimated payments exceeded its actual share, Landlord shall credit the overpayment against future Rent or refund such excess if this Agreement has expired. If Tenant’s estimated payments were less than its actual share, Tenant shall pay the shortfall within forty-five (45) days after receipt of the statement.

### 3.3 Definition of Operating Expenses

3.3.1 “Operating Expenses” shall include all reasonable and customary operating and maintenance expenses incurred by Landlord in connection with the Building and Common Areas, including but not limited to:

   (a) utilities for Common Areas;  
   (b) maintenance and repair of Building systems;  
   (c) janitorial and cleaning services for Common Areas;  
   (d) security services;  
   (e) insurance premiums as contemplated in Article X;  
   (f) property management fees (not to exceed a commercially reasonable rate);  
   (g) replacement of Building standard lighting and finishes;  
   (h) landscaping, snow removal, and exterior maintenance; and  
   (i) capital expenditures that (i) are required by changes in law after the Effective Date or (ii) reduce Operating Expenses or improve building efficiency, amortized over their useful life in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP).

3.3.2 Operating Expenses shall exclude, without limitation: (i) Landlord’s income taxes; (ii) depreciation except as allowed in Section 3.3.1(i); (iii) costs of capital improvements not included in Operating Expenses by this Agreement; (iv) leasing commissions; and (v) costs attributable solely to other tenants.

### 3.4 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.4.1 All amounts due from Tenant to Landlord under this Agreement shall be invoiced by Landlord and shall be payable by Tenant within **forty-five (45) days** after Tenant’s receipt of each invoice (“**Net 45**”), unless otherwise expressly stated.

3.4.2 Invoices shall be sent electronically to:

> **Contoso Enterprises**  
> Accounts Payable Department  
> 600 Innovation Drive, 9th Floor  
> Newark, DE 19711  
> Email: ap@contosoenterprises.com  

Tenant may update its billing contact information by written notice in accordance with Section 16.7.

### 3.5 Late Payments; Interest

3.5.1 Any amount not paid by Tenant when due under this Agreement (and not subject to a bona fide dispute pursuant to Section 14.2) shall accrue interest from the date due until paid at the lesser of: (a) **one percent (1%) per month** (12% per annum), or (b) the maximum rate allowed by applicable law.

3.5.2 In addition to interest, Landlord may impose a **late fee** of up to five percent (5%) of the overdue amount to cover administrative and collection costs, provided that such late fee may be charged only once per delinquent invoice.

3.5.3 Landlord’s acceptance of late or partial payments shall not be deemed a waiver of any rights or remedies for Tenant’s default, nor an accord and satisfaction, unless expressly agreed in writing.

### 3.6 Disputed Amounts

3.6.1 Tenant may in good faith dispute any portion of an invoice by providing written notice to Landlord within thirty (30) days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the nature and amount of the dispute in reasonable detail.

3.6.2 Tenant shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the disputed portion in accordance with the dispute resolution procedures in Article XIV. If it is determined that Tenant was required to pay the disputed amount, such amount shall be paid promptly, together with interest accrued as provided herein.

### 3.7 Security Deposit (If Applicable)

3.7.1 Landlord may require Tenant to provide a **security deposit** or **letter of credit** in an amount and on terms set forth in **Exhibit D**, which may be applied by Landlord to cure any Event of Default by Tenant, including unpaid Rent and repair costs, subject to any legal limitations.

3.7.2 Provided Tenant is not then in default, Landlord shall return any remaining cash security deposit or cause the release of the letter of credit within sixty (60) days after expiration or earlier lawful termination of this Agreement and surrender of the Premises by Tenant.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Commencement Date and shall continue until the **Expiration Date of May 24, 2026**, unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

### 4.2 Renewal Options

4.2.1 Tenant may have one (1) option to renew this Lease for an additional period of **two (2) years** (each, a “**Renewal Term**”), upon written notice to Landlord given not less than twelve (12) months prior to the Expiration Date.

4.2.2 The Renewal Term shall be on substantially the same terms and conditions as this Agreement, except that Base Rent shall be adjusted to the then-prevailing fair market rental rate for comparable premises in the Building or similar buildings in Wilmington, Delaware, as determined in good faith by Landlord and subject to a mutual market-based negotiation.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement **for convenience** upon written notice to the other Party given at least **sixty (60) days** prior to the desired termination date.

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Tenant, Tenant shall: (a) pay all Rent and other amounts accrued and unpaid as of the effective date of termination, (b) surrender the Premises in accordance with Section 4.6, and (c) pay any agreed-upon early termination fee set forth in **Exhibit E** (if any).

4.3.3 In the event of termination for convenience by Landlord, Landlord shall provide Tenant with reasonable assistance in transitioning out of the Premises and shall refund any prepaid amounts for Services not yet performed, except for non-refundable items expressly identified in this Agreement.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause; Events of Default

4.4.1 A Party may terminate this Agreement for **cause** if the other Party commits an **Event of Default** and fails to cure such Event of Default within the applicable cure period.

4.4.2 The following shall constitute Events of Default by Tenant:

   (a) Failure to pay any Rent or other amounts due hereunder within fifteen (15) days after written notice of non-payment;  
   (b) Failure to perform any non-monetary obligation under this Agreement, which failure continues for thirty (30) days after written notice specifying the default, or, if the default cannot reasonably be cured within thirty (30) days, failure to commence cure within such period and diligently complete such cure;  
   (c) Tenant’s abandonment of the Premises;  
   (d) Tenant’s insolvency, assignment for the benefit of creditors, or commencement of bankruptcy or receivership proceedings, subject to applicable law; or  
   (e) Tenant’s use of the Premises in material violation of applicable law, which violation is not cured within the applicable cure period.

4.4.3 The following shall constitute Events of Default by Landlord:

   (a) Failure to provide essential Services (HVAC, electricity, water, or Building access) for an extended period that materially and adversely affects Tenant’s use of the Premises, and failure to cure such failure within thirty (30) days after written notice, subject to Force Majeure and necessary repair times;  
   (b) Failure to perform any other material obligation under this Agreement, which failure continues beyond thirty (30) days after written notice, or if not curable within thirty (30) days, failure to diligently commence and pursue cure; or  
   (c) Landlord’s material breach of confidentiality or data protection obligations under Articles V and XI.

### 4.5 Remedies; Early Termination

4.5.1 If an Event of Default by Tenant occurs and continues beyond the applicable cure period, Landlord may, in addition to any other rights or remedies available at law or in equity, terminate this Agreement upon written notice to Tenant and pursue any lawful remedies, including recovery of unpaid Rent, damages, and, where permitted, reasonable attorneys’ fees.

4.5.2 If an Event of Default by Landlord occurs and continues beyond the applicable cure period, Tenant may terminate this Agreement upon written notice and pursue any lawful remedies, including recovery of direct damages as limited by Article IX.

### 4.6 Surrender of Premises; Holding Over

4.6.1 Upon expiration or earlier termination of this Agreement, Tenant shall vacate and surrender the Premises to Landlord in a neat, clean, and orderly condition, reasonable wear and tear excepted, and shall remove all of Tenant’s personal property and any Alterations required by Landlord to be removed.

4.6.2 If Tenant fails to vacate the Premises by the expiration or termination date, such occupancy shall be deemed a **month-to-month tenancy** on the same terms and conditions as this Agreement, except that Rent shall be increased to **150%** of the then-current Base Rent plus Additional Rent, until such time as Tenant vacates.

## ARTICLE V. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 “**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information disclosed by one Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that the Receiving Party ought reasonably to understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including without limitation: business plans, financial information, tenant lists, security protocols, building plans, proprietary processes, and any personal data protected by Article XI.

### 5.2 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:

   (a) use Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
   (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted herein; and  
   (c) protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than a reasonable standard of care.

5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, affiliates, contractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

### 5.3 Exceptions

5.3.1 Confidential Information shall not include information that:

   (a) is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
   (b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession before disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
   (c) is received by the Receiving Party from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality to the Disclosing Party; or  
   (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

### 5.4 Legally Required Disclosure

5.4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it may do so, provided that, to the extent legally permitted, it provides the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice and cooperates reasonably (at the Disclosing Party’s expense) in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

### 5.5 Survival

5.5.1 The obligations of confidentiality under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**, except with respect to trade secrets and personal data, which shall be protected for so long as such information remains a trade secret or personal data under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

### 12.1 General

12.1.1 Landlord shall perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and timely manner consistent with industry standards for first-class office buildings. The SLAs set forth in this Article XII represent agreed minimum performance levels.

### 12.2 Uptime and Availability

12.2.1 Landlord shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that essential building systems (electricity, HVAC, water, and Building access control) are available **99.9%** of the time during Business Hours, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

### 12.3 Response and Resolution Times

12.3.1 Landlord shall respond to service requests as follows:

   (a) **Critical Issues** (e.g., complete loss of power, HVAC, water, or Building access): response within **1 hour** during Business Hours;  
   (b) **High Priority Issues** (e.g., partial service disruption): response within **4 hours**;  
   (c) **Routine Issues** (e.g., minor repairs, janitorial issues): response within **24 hours**.

12.3.2 Landlord shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve:

   (a) Critical Issues within **8 hours**;  
   (b) High Priority Issues within **24 hours**;  
   (c) Routine Issues within **five (5) Business Days**.

### 12.4 Remedies for SLA Breach

12.4.1 If Landlord fails to meet the uptime commitment or repeatedly fails to meet response and resolution times (e.g., in three (3) or more instances in a calendar quarter) in a manner that materially impacts Tenant’s business operations, Tenant shall be entitled, as its sole and exclusive financial remedy for such SLA failures (subject to Article IX), to **service credits** equal to a reasonable portion of the Rent allocable to the affected period, as detailed in **Exhibit F**.

12.4.2 Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) due from Tenant and shall not, in total, exceed ten percent (10%) of the monthly Base Rent for any month.

## ARTICLE XV. FORCE MAJEURE

### 15.1 Definition

15.1.1 A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, fire, flood, earthquake, epidemic or pandemic, war, terrorism, civil unrest, governmental actions or orders, labor strikes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own employees, except where prohibited by law), supply chain disruptions, and failures of public utilities or telecommunications networks not caused by the affected Party.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature of the event, its expected duration, and the anticipated impact on the Party’s performance.

15.2.2 The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

15.3.1 The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that Rent obligations may be equitably adjusted if the Premises becomes substantially unusable for Tenant’s business operations for an extended period, as determined by mutual agreement or dispute resolution under Article XIV.

### 15.4 Extended Force Majeure

15.4.1 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially prevents either Party from performing its material obligations under this Agreement, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
