## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties entered into that certain **Master Services Agreement** dated as of **June 1, 2022** (the “**Master Agreement**”), pursuant to which Vendor agreed to provide certain technology implementation, integration, and managed support services to Client, as more fully described in various statements of work, purchase orders, and service schedules executed thereunder (collectively, the “**Existing Engagements**”);

WHEREAS, the Parties now desire to expand and refine the scope of services and deliverables to be provided by Vendor to Client, including enhanced cloud-based platform services, professional services, data management services, and associated service level commitments, subject to the terms of this Amendment;

WHEREAS, the Parties further desire to confirm that, except as expressly modified by this Amendment, the Master Agreement remains in full force and effect, and that this Amendment shall be incorporated into and form part of the Master Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and promises contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the sufficiency and receipt of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

## ARTICLE II  
### SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.** Vendor shall perform, and Client shall receive, the Services described in this Article II and in any SOWs executed by the Parties under the Master Agreement as modified by this Amendment. The Services shall include, without limitation:

- Implementation and configuration of Vendor’s cloud-based enterprise solutions for Client’s internal business operations;
- Integration of the System with Client’s existing enterprise resource planning (ERP), customer relationship management (CRM), and data warehouse systems;
- Ongoing managed services, support, maintenance, monitoring, and technical assistance;
- Data migration and transformation services;
- Training, knowledge transfer, and documentation; and
- Any other related services reasonably necessary for the successful operation of the System by Client.

2.2 **Implementation and Configuration Services.**  

2.2.1 Vendor shall design, configure, and implement the System in accordance with industry best practices and Client’s documented business requirements, as set forth in the applicable SOW. This includes configuring workflows, user roles and permissions, interfaces, and data schemas to support Client’s use cases.

2.2.2 Vendor shall develop and document a detailed project plan, including milestones, timelines, dependencies, and acceptance criteria, subject to Client’s review and approval. The Parties shall collaborate in good faith to update the project plan as necessary through the Change Management process described in Article XIII.

2.2.3 Vendor shall provide qualified personnel with appropriate expertise and experience in enterprise software implementation, system integration, and data management. Vendor shall ensure that such personnel are adequately supervised and that there is continuity of key project resources to the extent commercially reasonable.

2.3 **Integration Services.**  

2.3.1 Vendor shall integrate the System with Client’s designated applications, databases, and infrastructure as specified in the SOW. This may include APIs, middleware, data connectors, and batch or real-time data interfaces.

2.3.2 Vendor shall be responsible for designing and documenting integration specifications, data mapping, error handling procedures, and security controls relating to the integrations.

2.3.3 Vendor shall conduct integration testing, including unit, system, and user acceptance tests, in cooperation with Client. Vendor shall remediate any integration defects identified during testing within commercially reasonable timeframes consistent with the Service Levels in Article XII.

2.4 **Managed Services and Support.**  

2.4.1 Throughout the Amendment Term, Vendor shall provide ongoing managed services for the System, including monitoring, patching, performance tuning, incident management, and routine maintenance. Vendor shall manage and operate the System to meet or exceed the Service Levels.

2.4.2 Vendor shall provide a help desk function accessible by email, web portal, and during Business Hours by telephone. Vendor shall assign incident priority levels based on impact and urgency, and shall meet the response and resolution times specified in Article XII.

2.4.3 Vendor shall provide periodic maintenance windows for upgrades, patches, and changes. Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to schedule maintenance during agreed low-usage periods and shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of any planned maintenance that may materially impact the availability of the Services.

2.4.4 Vendor shall provide Client with monthly service reports detailing system availability, incident statistics, SLA performance, and any significant events affecting the Services.

2.5 **Data Migration and Transformation.**  

2.5.1 Vendor shall assist Client in migrating Client Data from Client’s legacy systems into the System, including data extraction, cleansing, transformation, validation, and loading, as described in the applicable SOW.

2.5.2 Vendor shall perform data migration activities in a secure manner, using appropriate encryption and access controls. Vendor shall not use Client Data for any purpose other than performing the Services.

2.5.3 Vendor shall conduct test migrations and data validation exercises and shall cooperate with Client to resolve any data quality issues prior to production cutover.

2.6 **Training and Documentation.**  

2.6.1 Vendor shall prepare and deliver training materials, user guides, technical documentation, and administration manuals reasonably necessary for Client to use and administer the System and Services effectively.

2.6.2 Vendor shall conduct training sessions (in-person or virtual, as agreed) for Client’s designated users and administrators. The number of training hours and sessions shall be specified in the SOW.

2.7 **Deliverables and Acceptance.**  

2.7.1 The specific Deliverables (including configurations, integrations, documentation, and reports) shall be enumerated in the SOW, together with milestones and acceptance criteria.

2.7.2 Upon completion of each milestone or Deliverable, Vendor shall notify Client in writing and provide all necessary artifacts for review and testing. Client shall have a reasonable acceptance period, typically not less than ten (10) Business Days, to review and either (a) accept the Deliverable in writing; or (b) reject it by providing a written description of any material non-conformities with the agreed specifications.

2.7.3 If Client timely rejects a Deliverable for material non‑conformity, Vendor shall promptly correct the non‑conformity at no additional cost and resubmit the Deliverable. The Parties shall repeat the acceptance process until the Deliverable is accepted. If Client neither accepts nor rejects a Deliverable within the acceptance period, the Deliverable shall not be deemed automatically accepted; instead, Vendor shall notify Client of the lapse, and the Parties shall confer in good faith to complete acceptance within a further mutually agreed period.

2.8 **Performance Standards.**  

2.8.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry standards, the Master Agreement, this Amendment, and any SOW.

2.8.2 Vendor shall ensure that the Services and Deliverables conform to the specifications set forth in the SOW and comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including data protection laws as referenced in Article XI.

2.8.3 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to Client’s business operations, and shall coordinate major implementation activities, migrations, and changes with Client’s designated project manager.

2.9 **Client Responsibilities.**  

2.9.1 Client shall provide timely access to relevant systems, facilities, personnel, and information reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services.

2.9.2 Client shall assign a project manager with appropriate authority to make decisions, review Deliverables, and provide approvals or feedback in a timely manner.

2.9.3 Vendor’s performance of the Services is contingent upon Client fulfilling its obligations under this Section 2.9; however, Vendor shall provide notice to Client of any material delays or impacts arising from Client’s failure to meet its obligations, and the Parties shall work collaboratively to adjust the timelines through the Change Management process in Article XIII.

## ARTICLE III  
### FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Amendment and the associated SOWs shall be **US$3,801,577** (the “Contract Value”), exclusive of Taxes, subject to approved Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** The Fees shall be allocated approximately as follows, with exact amounts specified in the applicable SOW(s):

3.2.1 **Implementation and Integration Services:** US$1,600,000  
3.2.2 **Data Migration and Transformation Services:** US$500,000  
3.2.3 **Managed Services and Support (Amendment Term):** US$1,300,000  
3.2.4 **Training and Documentation:** US$151,577  
3.2.5 **Contingency/Change Budget (if used via approved Change Orders):** US$250,000 (not to be incurred without Client’s prior written approval).

3.3 **Payment Milestones.** Unless otherwise specified in the SOW, the Fees shall be invoiced according to the following milestone schedule:

3.3.1 **Project Initiation:** Ten percent (10%) of the implementation and integration portion upon Effective Date and execution of the initial SOW.

3.3.2 **Design and Configuration Completion:** Twenty-five percent (25%) of the implementation and integration portion upon Client’s acceptance of design and configuration Deliverables.

3.3.3 **Integration and Data Migration Completion:** Twenty-five percent (25%) of the combined implementation and data migration portions upon successful completion of integration testing and data migration acceptance.

3.3.4 **Production Go-Live:** Twenty percent (20%) of the implementation, integration, and data migration portions upon production go-live and initial Service Level monitoring commencement.

3.3.5 **Remainder / Managed Services:** The remaining Fees, including managed services and support, shall be invoiced in equal monthly installments over the remainder of the Amendment Term, commencing on the first day of the month following production go-live, unless otherwise specified in the SOW.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  

3.4.1 Invoices shall be issued by Vendor in accordance with the milestones or periodic schedule described above and in the SOW. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all undisputed amounts on each invoice shall be payable by Client **Net 30** days from the date of Client’s receipt of such invoice.

3.4.2 Client may withhold payment of any disputed amounts, provided that Client (a) pays all undisputed amounts when due; and (b) provides written notice to Vendor within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any such disputes promptly. Upon resolution, any amounts determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of such resolution.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  

3.5.1 Any undisputed amount not paid by Client within the applicable payment terms shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one percent (1.0%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.5.2 Vendor’s entitlement to interest shall be Vendor’s sole remedy for late payment, provided that nothing herein shall limit Vendor’s rights under Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause) in the event of persistent or material non‑payment.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.6.1 Travel and out‑of‑pocket expenses shall be reimbursable by Client only if (a) they are reasonable and customary; (b) pre‑approved in writing by Client; and (c) supported by appropriate receipts and documentation.

3.6.2 Vendor shall comply with Client’s travel and expense policies as provided in writing to Vendor from time to time. Any expenditure not aligned with such policies shall not be reimbursable unless expressly authorized in writing by Client.

3.7 **Taxes.**  

3.7.1 The Fees do not include Taxes. Client shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees.

3.7.2 If Vendor is required to collect any Taxes from Client, Vendor shall itemize such Taxes on its invoices and Client shall pay such Taxes in accordance with this Article III, unless Client provides a valid exemption certificate.

## ARTICLE IV  
### TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Amendment Term.** This Amendment shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until **December 16, 2025**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.** Any renewal or extension of the Services beyond the Amendment Term shall be subject to written agreement of the Parties and may be documented by a subsequent amendment, renewal order, or SOW.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for its convenience at any time upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor.  

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client and only after completion of any critical milestones mutually identified in writing as non-deferrable.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Amendment immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Amendment or the Master Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (ten (10) days in the case of non‑payment) after receiving written notice specifying the nature of the breach.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Amendment immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) admits in writing its inability to pay its debts as they mature, (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (d) becomes subject to any bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

4.5.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Amendment:  
(a) Vendor shall promptly cease performing the terminated Services;  
(b) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and authorized expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to Client’s rights under Section 3.4 with respect to disputed amounts; and  
(c) any prepaid Fees for Services not yet performed as of the effective date of termination shall be refunded to Client on a pro-rated basis within thirty (30) days.

4.5.2 At Client’s request and expense (except where termination results from Vendor’s uncured material breach, in which case such assistance shall be at Vendor’s cost), Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance to facilitate the orderly transfer of the Services to Client or a replacement provider, for a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration.

4.5.3 Sections that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including without limitation Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), shall survive termination or expiration of this Amendment.

## ARTICLE V  
### CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.** “Confidential Information” means all non‑public or proprietary information disclosed by a Party (the “Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (the “Receiving Party”), whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that, given the nature of the information or circumstances surrounding its disclosure, should reasonably be understood to be confidential. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, Client Data, business plans, financial information, technical data, product roadmaps, software, source code, trade secrets, and security information.

5.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Amendment by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party without confidentiality obligations prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under the Master Agreement and this Amendment;  
(b) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care; and  
(c) limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know such information and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

5.3.2 Vendor acknowledges that Client Data is the Confidential Information of Client and shall be subject to heightened protection as further described in Article XI.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that (to the extent legally permissible) the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement and cooperates reasonably with the Disclosing Party’s lawful efforts to limit or contest the disclosure.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) copies required to comply with legal or regulatory obligations; and (b) archival copies maintained in accordance with its standard backup policies, which shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.** The confidentiality obligations in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for **five (5) years** following the expiration or termination of this Amendment; provided that trade secrets and Client Data shall be maintained as confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets or Client Data, respectively.

11.3.4 Incident response procedures designed to detect, investigate, and remediate security incidents.

(b) provide Client with information known to Vendor regarding the nature and scope of the Data Breach, the categories and approximate number of data subjects and records affected (if applicable), and the likely consequences; and  
(c) cooperate with Client in investigating, containing, and remediating the Data Breach, and in complying with any applicable notification or regulatory requirements.

## ARTICLE XII  
### SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.** Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the System is available not less than **99.9%** of the time, measured on a monthly basis, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and emergency maintenance as agreed by the Parties. “Scheduled Maintenance” means planned maintenance that is communicated in advance in accordance with Section 2.4.3.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.** Vendor shall provide the following incident response and resolution times during the Amendment Term:

- **Priority 1 (Critical)** – System outage or severe degradation impacting a material portion of Client’s users:  
  - Initial response within 1 hour (24x7);  
  - Target resolution or workaround within 4 hours.

- **Priority 2 (High)** – Major functionality impaired with no reasonable workaround:  
  - Initial response within 4 Business Hours;  
  - Target resolution or workaround within 1 Business Day.

- **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Non‑critical functionality issues with a reasonable workaround:  
  - Initial response within 1 Business Day;  
  - Target resolution within 5 Business Days.

- **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries:  
  - Initial response within 2 Business Days;  
  - Target resolution in a future scheduled maintenance release or as mutually agreed.

12.3 **SLA Credits.**  

12.3.1 If Vendor fails to meet the availability commitment in Section 12.1 in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows, calculated as a percentage of the monthly managed services Fees for the affected month:

- Availability < 99.9% and ≥ 99.5%: 5% credit  
- Availability < 99.5% and ≥ 99.0%: 10% credit  
- Availability < 99.0%: 15% credit

12.3.2 SLA credits shall be applied against future invoices or, if no further invoices are due, refunded to Client. SLA credits shall be Vendor’s sole monetary obligation for failure to meet the availability SLA, but shall not limit Client’s right to terminate for chronic SLA failures as provided in Section 12.4.

12.4 **Chronic SLA Failure.** If Vendor fails to meet the availability SLA in Section 12.1 for three (3) or more consecutive months, or for any four (4) or more months in a rolling twelve‑month period, Client may, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, terminate the affected Services without early termination charges, in addition to any other remedies available under this Amendment.

12.5 **Exclusions.** The SLA commitments shall not apply to failures or downtime attributable to: (a) Client’s acts or omissions; (b) Client’s failure to use supported configurations; (c) Third‑Party Materials not under Vendor’s direct control; or (d) a Force Majeure Event; provided that Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of such events.

### FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.** A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, war, terrorism, riots, embargoes, labor disputes (other than those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), power or telecommunications outages, or acts of government.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.** The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate its effects and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** The obligations of the affected Party (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended during the period of the Force Majeure Event to the extent they are affected by such event. The time for performance shall be extended for a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portions of this Amendment upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without liability for such termination (except for payment of Fees for Services performed prior to the effective date of termination).
