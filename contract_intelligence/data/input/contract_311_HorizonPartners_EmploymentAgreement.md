## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall provide to the Company comprehensive **employment-style professional services**, including:

  2.1.1 Identification, recruitment, and selection of candidates to be deployed as Assigned Personnel in various functions of the Company’s operations, including but not limited to software engineering, data analysis, project management, product management, customer support, and administrative functions;

  2.1.2 Engagement and employment (or subcontracting) of such candidates by the Service Provider, such that the Service Provider remains the legal employer or contracting party with respect to Assigned Personnel, unless otherwise explicitly agreed in a separate written agreement;

  2.1.3 Deployment and assignment of such personnel to carry out daily tasks, projects, and responsibilities as designated by the Company, provided that supervision of day-to-day activities may be exercised by the Company as permitted under Applicable Law, while the Service Provider remains responsible for overall employment administration and compliance;

  2.1.4 Provision of HR administration, including payroll, statutory contributions, benefits management (if any), leave administration, and other personnel-related services for Assigned Personnel in accordance with Applicable Law;

  2.1.5 Provision of periodic advisory services related to workforce planning, performance management of Assigned Personnel, and optimization recommendations for staffing structures, as reasonably requested by the Company.

2.2 **Detailed Responsibilities of the Service Provider.**  
Without prejudice to the generality of Section 2.1, the Service Provider shall:

  2.2.1 Conduct screening, reference checks, and, where agreed, basic background checks on potential Assigned Personnel;

  2.2.2 Ensure that Assigned Personnel possess the agreed minimum qualifications, technical skills, and experience relevant to the roles for which they are deployed, as set out in the applicable SOW;

  2.2.3 Provide each Assigned Personnel with a written agreement or engagement letter specifying employment terms, remuneration, and obligations, and ensure that such terms comply with Applicable Law and do not conflict with this Agreement;

  2.2.4 Manage and pay all wages, salaries, bonuses (if any), allowances, statutory contributions (including but not limited to CPF or equivalent), and other benefits due to Assigned Personnel, except to the extent that certain cost elements are expressly stated to be reimbursable by the Company under Article III;

  2.2.5 Handle disciplinary actions, grievances, and termination of Assigned Personnel, in consultation with the Company where such matters affect performance under this Agreement; and

  2.2.6 Provide replacement personnel, within a commercially reasonable period and at no additional cost to the Company (other than agreed Fees), where an Assigned Personnel leaves or is removed for performance or disciplinary reasons within a probationary period as defined in the applicable SOW.

2.3 **Detailed Responsibilities of the Company.**  

The Company shall:

  2.3.1 Provide accurate descriptions of job roles, skills requirements, and anticipated project needs to the Service Provider to facilitate appropriate candidate sourcing;

  2.3.2 Provide necessary access to the Company’s premises, systems, tools, and information to enable Assigned Personnel to perform their functions, subject always to the Company’s internal policies, security protocols, and Applicable Law;

  2.3.3 Provide day-to-day work instructions, task assignments, and operational supervision of Assigned Personnel, consistent with the nature of the Services, provided such supervision does not convert the legal status of Assigned Personnel into employees of the Company under Applicable Law unless the Parties expressly agree otherwise in writing;

  2.3.4 Notify the Service Provider promptly of any performance issues, misconduct, or other concerns relating to any Assigned Personnel, and cooperate with the Service Provider in addressing such concerns; and

  2.3.5 Review and approve timesheets, work logs, or other records documenting the Services provided, for purposes of invoicing and performance evaluation.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  

The Services shall produce the following key Deliverables, without limitation:

  2.4.1 A defined number of Assigned Personnel in designated roles, as specified in each SOW, deployed within agreed timelines;

  2.4.2 Periodic staffing and performance reports (e.g., monthly or quarterly) summarizing Assigned Personnel allocation, utilization, attendance, key performance indicators, and any significant issues;

  2.4.3 Documentation of policies and procedures applicable to Assigned Personnel, including HR guidelines, escalation procedures, and points of contact, where reasonably requested by the Company;

  2.4.4 Advisory reports, presentations, or recommendations regarding workforce planning, productivity improvements, and skill development opportunities, to the extent included in the applicable SOW;

  2.4.5 Any specific project-based deliverables (e.g., software modules, analytical reports, training materials) that Assigned Personnel develop for the Company, as further specified in an SOW.

2.5 **Timelines and Service Levels.**  

  2.5.1 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to deploy Assigned Personnel within the timelines agreed in the applicable SOW, taking into account market conditions and availability of suitable candidates.

  2.5.2 For new role requests, the Service Provider shall acknowledge receipt within two (2) Business Days and provide an indicative timeline for candidate shortlisting.

  2.5.3 Time is of the essence in relation to critical project allocations expressly identified as such in an SOW. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate delays, including by adjusting role requirements or deployment sequencing where necessary.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  

The Service Provider shall:

  2.6.1 Perform the Services in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for personnel and professional services providers;

  2.6.2 Ensure that Assigned Personnel comply with all applicable Company policies relating to confidentiality, security, health and safety, and workplace conduct, as notified to the Service Provider or the Assigned Personnel;

  2.6.3 Respond to the Company’s reasonable requests for information on Assigned Personnel, including qualifications and performance history, subject to Applicable Law and data protection obligations; and

  2.6.4 Implement corrective measures promptly where performance standards are not met, including coaching, retraining, or replacement of Assigned Personnel as appropriate.

2.7 **Statements of Work.**  

  2.7.1 From time to time, the Parties may execute one or more SOWs under this Agreement to specify particular Services, Deliverables, timelines, and Fees.

  2.7.2 Each SOW shall reference this Agreement, be in writing, and be signed by authorized representatives of both Parties. In the event of conflict between the terms of this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail, unless the SOW expressly states otherwise with reference to the specific provision being varied.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  

The total estimated contract value of this Agreement is **Six Hundred Seventy-Five Thousand One Hundred and Four United States Dollars (US$675,104)** (the “**Contract Value**”). This Contract Value represents the anticipated Fees for Services and Deliverables to be provided during the Term, subject to adjustment based on actual utilization and any additional work agreed in Change Orders or SOWs.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

The Fees shall consist of the following components:

  3.2.1 **Base Service Fees.** Monthly or project-based fees for each Assigned Personnel, as specified in the applicable SOW, which shall typically include the Assigned Personnel’s wages, statutory costs, and the Service Provider’s margin;

  3.2.2 **One-Time Recruitment Fees.** Where applicable, a placement or onboarding fee per Assigned Personnel, as agreed in the SOW, particularly for specialized roles requiring significant sourcing effort;

  3.2.3 **Advisory Fees.** Fees for workforce consulting or strategic advisory services, if any, calculated on a time-and-materials basis (e.g., daily or hourly rates) or a fixed project fee as specified in the SOW;

  3.2.4 **Approved Additional Services.** Fees arising from Change Orders or new SOWs, which may be billed separately or integrated into monthly invoices.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  

  3.3.1 The Company shall pay all undisputed invoices within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of invoice (“**Net 60**”), in accordance with the payment instructions specified therein.

  3.3.2 Invoices shall be issued monthly in arrears, unless otherwise stated in an SOW, and shall include reasonable supporting documentation, such as timesheets and summary reports, where applicable.

  3.3.3 All fees and charges are exclusive of any applicable taxes (including GST or VAT), which shall be added to the invoice and borne by the Company, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

  3.4.1 Any amount not paid by the Company when due (and not subject to a bona fide dispute) shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.

  3.4.2 The Service Provider shall provide written notice of late payment and allow a grace period of ten (10) Business Days before applying interest or taking further action.

  3.4.3 Persistent non-payment (for more than ninety (90) days past due) may constitute a material breach and shall entitle the Service Provider to suspend Services in accordance with Section 4.4, subject to prior written notice and a cure period.

3.5 **Disputed Invoices.**  

  3.5.1 If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the basis of the dispute.

  3.5.2 The Company shall timely pay the undisputed portion of such invoice and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the disputed amount within thirty (30) Business Days.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  

  3.6.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable and properly documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Assigned Personnel in the performance of the Services (e.g., business travel, accommodation, and related expenses), provided such expenses are pre-approved in writing by the Company.

  3.6.2 All expense claims shall be supported by receipts or other documentation reasonably acceptable to the Company.

3.7 **Currency and Method of Payment.**  

All payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) or such other currency as may be mutually agreed in writing, by wire transfer or other electronic funds transfer to the account designated by the Service Provider.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date of December 10, 2029**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  

The Parties may agree in writing to renew or extend the Term for one or more additional periods, on terms and conditions as may be mutually agreed in a written amendment or new agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

  4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

  4.3.2 During the notice period, the Parties shall cooperate to wind down or transition the Services in an orderly manner. The Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and authorized expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination for convenience.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days (or such longer period as may be reasonably necessary, if the breach is capable of cure and the breaching Party is diligently pursuing such cure) after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or  

  (b) becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into liquidation (other than for a solvent restructuring), or has a receiver or similar officer appointed over any substantial part of its assets.

  4.4.2 The Service Provider may, in addition, suspend performance of Services if the Company fails to pay any undisputed amount within ninety (90) days following its due date, after giving at least fifteen (15) Business Days’ prior written notice and an opportunity to cure.

4.5 **Termination of Specific SOWs.**  

The Parties may agree that specific SOWs may be terminated independently of this Agreement, under the same termination provisions or additional termination provisions specified in such SOWs.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  4.6.1 The Service Provider shall promptly cease performance of the Services (except as reasonably necessary for transition), and shall remove Assigned Personnel from the Company’s premises, systems, and networks;

  4.6.2 The Company shall pay the Service Provider all Fees and authorized expenses accrued and outstanding up to the effective date of termination, subject to Article III;

  4.6.3 Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Article V and Article XI; and

  4.6.4 Those provisions which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any payment obligations accrued, shall survive and continue in full force and effect.

15.1 **Definition.**

The affected Party shall notify the other Party in writing as soon as reasonably practicable upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event that may materially affect its ability to perform its obligations under this Agreement, providing details of the nature, expected duration, and likely impact of the Force Majeure Event.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  

To the extent and for the duration that the affected Party is prevented from performing its obligations due to a Force Majeure Event, such obligations shall be suspended, and the affected Party shall not be deemed in breach of this Agreement.

15.4 **Mitigation.**  

The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably possible.

15.5 **Prolonged Force Majeure.**  

If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially affects performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement (or the affected part of the Services) upon thirty (30) calendar days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
