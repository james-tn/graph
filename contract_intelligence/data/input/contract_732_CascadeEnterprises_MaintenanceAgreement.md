## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive Maintenance Services relating to Client’s designated Equipment, systems, and facilities, which may include, without limitation:

  (a) scheduled preventive maintenance of identified Equipment, including inspection, testing, cleaning, lubrication, calibration, and replacement of routine consumable parts;

  (b) corrective maintenance and repair services, including diagnostic work, component replacement, and restoration of Equipment to operational status;

  (c) remote monitoring and alerting services for certain systems, networks, and devices as described in the applicable SOW;

  (d) on-site support services at Client’s designated locations during agreed service windows;

  (e) emergency and unscheduled maintenance response for critical incidents;

  (f) maintenance-related documentation, recordkeeping, and reporting; and

  (g) related professional services reasonably required for the proper performance of the foregoing items.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
The specific description of the Services, including the particular Equipment, locations, Service Levels, Deliverables, schedules, and Fees, shall be set forth in one or more Statements of Work executed by the Parties. Each SOW shall:

  (a) reference this Agreement by its title and Contract Reference Number **MNT-CAS-202407-732**;  

  (b) describe the Services and Deliverables to be provided;  

  (c) specify the applicable Service Levels;  

  (d) identify the applicable Fees and payment schedule; and  

  (e) set forth any additional terms and conditions specific to the Services covered by that SOW.

In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern, unless the SOW expressly and specifically states that it is intended to supersede a particular provision of this Agreement.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for maintenance services of a similar nature;

  (b) in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and the applicable SOW;

  (c) in compliance with Applicable Law; and  

  (d) using personnel who are appropriately trained, qualified, and experienced to perform the Services assigned to them.

Client acknowledges that maintenance by its own personnel or by third parties not authorized or approved in writing by Vendor may interfere with Vendor’s ability to meet the Service Levels. To the extent Client or such third parties perform unauthorized maintenance or alterations, Vendor shall be relieved of Service Level obligations for the affected Equipment and shall have no liability for any degradation or failure arising therefrom.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities and Dependencies.**  
Vendor’s performance of the Services is expressly conditioned upon Client’s timely fulfillment of its responsibilities, including, without limitation:

  (a) providing Vendor with reasonable access to the applicable Equipment, facilities, systems, and relevant personnel, during agreed service windows;

  (b) ensuring that Equipment and environments comply with manufacturer specifications and applicable safety standards;

  (c) providing accurate and complete information regarding Equipment, its configuration, and any prior maintenance or modifications;

  (d) promptly notifying Vendor of any faults, incidents, or conditions affecting the Equipment or Services; and

  (e) implementing Vendor’s reasonable recommendations to mitigate recurring issues or systemic risks.

Vendor shall not be responsible for delays, failures, or non-conformities to the extent caused by Client’s failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement. Any additional time or costs incurred by Vendor as a result of such failure shall be deemed an additional Service subject to additional Fees.

2.5 **Exclusions from Scope.**  
Unless expressly included in an SOW, the Services shall not include:

  (a) repair or replacement of Equipment that has reached the end of its useful life or is deemed by Vendor, acting reasonably, to be beyond economical repair;

  (b) maintenance required due to misuse, neglect, accident, modification, or improper handling by Client or third parties;

  (c) upgrades to newer models, major system overhauls, or capacity expansions;

  (d) services related to third-party software or hardware for which Vendor is not an authorized service provider;

  (e) any construction, civil, or electrical work unrelated to maintenance of the identified Equipment.

If Client requests Vendor to perform any services falling within the foregoing exclusions, such services shall be subject to a separate written agreement or a Change Order under Article XIII and shall be billed at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates, unless otherwise agreed.

2.6 **Deliverables.**  
Vendor shall provide such Deliverables as are specified in the applicable SOW, which may include, without limitation:

  (a) maintenance logs and service reports summarizing work performed, parts used, and any identified issues;

  (b) incident and root-cause analysis reports for certain critical incidents;

  (c) periodic performance and reliability analyses, including trend data where available;

  (d) recommended maintenance schedules or upgrade plans.

Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Deliverables are for Client’s internal business use only and are subject to the intellectual property terms set forth in Article VI.

2.7 **Transition and Onboarding.**  
As applicable, Vendor shall perform transition and onboarding activities to commence the Services, including:

  (a) initial assessment of Equipment and facilities;  

  (b) baseline inventory of Equipment, including serial numbers, locations, and status;  

  (c) establishment of communication protocols, incident reporting procedures, and escalation paths; and  

  (d) initial configuration of any monitoring tools to be used by Vendor.

Client shall cooperate fully with Vendor in completing all onboarding activities and acknowledges that delays in onboarding caused by Client may impact Vendor’s ability to achieve certain Service Levels during an initial stabilization period, as may be defined in the applicable SOW.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The aggregate not-to-exceed contract value payable by Client to Vendor under this Agreement, inclusive of all SOWs and before applicable taxes, shall be **Four Million Seven Hundred Ninety-Three Thousand Fifty-One United States Dollars (US $4,793,051)**, unless increased by written amendment or Change Order executed by the Parties.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees for the Services shall be set forth in each SOW and may include one or more of the following components:

  (a) **Fixed Fees** for specified preventive maintenance and scheduled services;  

  (b) **Time and Materials Fees** for unscheduled or out-of-scope work, billed at Vendor’s then-current hourly rates;  

  (c) **Per-Incident Fees** for emergency or priority incident response, as specified in the applicable SOW;  

  (d) **Pass-Through Costs** for replacement parts, third-party services, or licenses.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
Except as otherwise set forth in the applicable SOW, Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for all Fees incurred in the prior month. All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable within **thirty (30) days** from the invoice date (“**Net 30**”).

3.4 **Late Payment; Interest.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of:

  (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or  

  (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law,

from the due date until paid in full. Vendor’s acceptance of late or partial payments shall not waive any of its rights or remedies with respect to such late payment.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
Client may withhold payment of any portion of an invoice that Client reasonably and in good faith disputes, provided that Client:

  (a) pays all undisputed amounts by the due date;  

  (b) notifies Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of the invoice date, specifying the amount in dispute and the reasons therefor; and  

  (c) cooperates with Vendor in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly.

Upon resolution of the dispute, any amounts determined to be due shall be paid within ten (10) Business Days, together with any applicable interest under Section 3.4.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, or other taxes (collectively, “**Taxes**”). Client shall be responsible for all Taxes associated with the Services, except taxes imposed on Vendor’s income, net worth, or employment of personnel. If Vendor is required to collect or pay any Taxes on Client’s behalf, Vendor shall itemize such Taxes on its invoices and Client shall reimburse Vendor for the same.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with the performance of the Services, including without limitation travel, lodging, meals, and shipping, provided that:

  (a) such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Client where required by the applicable SOW; and  

  (b) Vendor submits appropriate supporting documentation along with the applicable invoice.

3.8 **Price Adjustments.**  
Vendor may adjust its standard time and materials rates no more than once in any twelve (12) month period, upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Client. Such adjusted rates shall apply only to Services performed after the effective date of the rate change and shall not affect any fixed Fees agreed in an SOW, unless the SOW expressly permits such adjustments.

3.9 **Setoff.**  
Client shall have no right of setoff, deduction, or withholding against any amounts due to Vendor under this Agreement, except as required by Applicable Law or as expressly permitted under Section 3.5 with respect to disputed amounts.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date**, namely **August 23, 2026**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for one or more additional periods, on the same or modified terms as may be set forth in a written amendment executed by the Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing Vendor with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice of termination. Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience by providing Client with at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  (a) **By Vendor.** Vendor may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, upon written notice to Client if:

   (i) Client fails to pay any undisputed amount due under this Agreement within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of non-payment from Vendor; or  

   (ii) Client materially breaches any other provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice from Vendor describing the breach in reasonable detail.

  (b) **By Client.** Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice to Vendor if Vendor materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice from Client describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.5 **Termination for Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they become due;  

  (b) files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or any similar proceeding; or  

  (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

  (a) Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement or any SOW, Client shall promptly pay Vendor all undisputed Fees and reimbursable expenses accrued up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf.

  (b) Vendor shall cease performing the terminated Services as of the effective date of termination and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to assist Client in transitioning the Services to Client or a replacement service provider, at Client’s written request and subject to Client’s payment of Vendor’s then-current rates for such transition assistance.

  (c) Except as otherwise expressly provided herein, all rights and licenses granted to Client under this Agreement with respect to Work Product shall cease upon termination, subject to Client’s payment of all amounts then due.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under this Agreement which by their nature should survive termination or expiration of this Agreement, including but not limited to those under Sections 3.4, 3.6, 4.6, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI, shall survive any such termination or expiration.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
For those Services involving continuous system or network monitoring or management, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that applicable systems maintained under this Agreement are available at least **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance windows, emergency maintenance, and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Vendor may schedule planned maintenance activities that may impact system availability, provided that Vendor:

  (a) gives Client at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance expected to affect availability; and  

  (b) schedules such maintenance, where reasonably possible, during off-peak hours.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Vendor shall respond to and seek to resolve Incidents in accordance with the following target metrics, unless otherwise specified in an applicable SOW:

  (a) **Priority 1 (Critical)** – System or Equipment outage severely impacting Client’s core operations with no workaround:  

   - Target Response Time: within **1 hour** of Incident notification.  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround: within **8 hours** of Incident notification.

  (b) **Priority 2 (High)** – Major degradation of service or functionality with significant business impact:  

   - Target Response Time: within **2 hours**.  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround: within **24 hours**.

  (c) **Priority 3 (Medium)** – Limited degradation, partial functionality issues, or non-critical failures:  

   - Target Response Time: within **4 Business Hours**.  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround: within **3 Business Days**.

  (d) **Priority 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or informational requests:  

   - Target Response Time: within **1 Business Day**.  
   - Target Resolution/Workaround: within **7 Business Days**.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 or any response/resolution commitments expressly designated as firm Service Levels in an applicable SOW, Client may be eligible for service credits as specified in the applicable SOW. Service credits:

  (a) are not automatic and must be requested by Client in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the non-compliance occurred;  

  (b) shall be calculated as a percentage of the monthly Fees for the affected Services; and  

  (c) shall be applied as a credit against future invoices.

Service credits shall be Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for any failure to meet the Service Levels.

12.5 **Exclusions from SLA Calculations.**  
Uptime and other SLA metrics shall exclude downtime or delays:

  (a) caused by Client’s acts or omissions, or those of its agents or third-party providers;  

  (b) resulting from Client’s failure to comply with Vendor’s reasonable instructions or recommendations;  

  (c) due to Equipment or services not provided or controlled by Vendor;  

  (d) due to scheduled maintenance or emergency maintenance; or  

  (e) due to Force Majeure Events.
