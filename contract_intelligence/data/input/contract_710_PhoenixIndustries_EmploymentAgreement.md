## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Company comprehensive professional services relating to the sourcing, selection, engagement, administration, and management of personnel to perform various functional roles within Company’s operations (the “**Employment Services**”). These Services shall include, without limitation:

- Recruitment, pre-screening, and recommendation of candidates for roles identified by Company;  
- Engagement of such candidates as employees or contractors of Vendor, as appropriate;  
- Assignment of such personnel (“Assigned Personnel”) to perform services for Company at Company’s facilities or remotely;  
- Management of payroll, benefits (if applicable), tax withholdings, and other employment administration for Assigned Personnel employed by Vendor;  
- Performance monitoring, reporting, and standard HR administration with respect to Assigned Personnel; and  
- Additional consulting, workforce planning, and related support as may be requested by Company and agreed in writing.

2.1.2 For the avoidance of doubt, nothing herein shall obligate Company to award any minimum number of positions or hours to Vendor, unless a specific SOW expressly states otherwise. Company may use other vendors for similar services, subject to any exclusivity provisions set forth in a particular SOW (if any).

### 2.2 Specific Deliverables

2.2.1 Vendor shall furnish the following categories of Deliverables, as may be described more fully in one or more SOWs:

(a) **Staffing Plans and Resource Proposals.** Vendor shall prepare written staffing plans that identify the number and type of personnel required, proposed job descriptions, required skills and experience, and estimated compensation ranges. These plans will form the basis for Company’s evaluation of staffing needs.

(b) **Candidate Shortlists.** For each role requested by Company, Vendor shall provide a shortlist of qualified candidates meeting the requirements described by Company. Each shortlist shall include candidate resumes, background verification status (as permitted by law), and proposed hourly or salary rates.

(c) **Onboarding Documentation.** Vendor shall provide all necessary onboarding documentation for Assigned Personnel, including signed employment or engagement agreements with Vendor, tax forms, acknowledgments of policies, and any Company-specific forms as reasonably requested.

(d) **Periodic Reports.** Vendor shall provide monthly or other periodic reports summarizing: (i) Assigned Personnel deployed; (ii) hours worked or services rendered; (iii) turnover rates; (iv) key performance metrics; and (v) any compliance matters or incidents.

(e) **Knowledge Transfer & Training Materials.** Where requested, Vendor shall prepare and deliver training materials, process documentation, and knowledge transfer documents to facilitate continuity of services and effective management of Assigned Personnel.

(f) **Exit and Transition Documentation.** Upon termination or reassignment of Assigned Personnel or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, Vendor shall provide exit checklists, status summaries, and transition assistance documentation as may be reasonably required for an orderly transition, pursuant to Section 4.5.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional, timely, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with (a) generally accepted industry standards for staffing and employment administration services, (b) applicable laws and regulations, and (c) the Service Levels specified in Article XII and any applicable SOW.

2.3.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that Assigned Personnel:

- Possess the skills, qualifications, and experience described in the applicable job description provided by Company;  
- Comply with all applicable Company policies, including but not limited to security, confidentiality, anti-harassment, and workplace safety policies, provided that such policies have been made available to Vendor and communicated to Assigned Personnel; and  
- Perform their duties in a diligent, competent, and professional manner.

2.3.3 Company shall have the right, acting reasonably and in good faith, to request removal or replacement of any Assigned Personnel whose performance, conduct, or qualifications are unsatisfactory or who fail to meet Company’s requirements. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to promptly replace such Assigned Personnel with individuals possessing comparable qualifications.

### 2.4 Timelines and Milestones

2.4.1 The Parties agree that time is of the essence with respect to Vendor’s obligations under this Agreement.

2.4.2 Specific timelines and Milestones, such as candidate submission deadlines, onboarding completion dates, and reporting deadlines, may be set forth in one or more SOWs. By way of example, a typical workflow may include:

- Within five (5) Business Days after Company’s request for a particular role, Vendor shall submit an initial candidate shortlist;  
- Within ten (10) Business Days after Company’s selection of a candidate, Vendor shall complete onboarding and make the Assigned Personnel available for work, subject to any relocation or notice period constraints;  
- Monthly performance and utilization reports shall be delivered within five (5) Business Days following the end of each calendar month.

2.4.3 If Vendor anticipates that it will be unable to meet any Milestone or deadline, Vendor shall notify Company in writing as soon as reasonably practicable, stating the reasons for the delay and its proposed corrective action plan. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate the impact of any such delay.

### 2.5 Cooperation and Dependencies

2.5.1 Company acknowledges that Vendor’s performance of the Services is dependent on Company’s timely provision of information, decisions, access to systems and facilities, and other assistance as may be reasonably required. Vendor shall not be liable for delays or failures in performance to the extent caused by Company’s failure to fulfill such responsibilities.

2.5.2 Company shall designate a primary contact person or project manager to coordinate with Vendor’s designated account manager. Both Parties shall ensure that their respective representatives have the authority to make day-to-day decisions and approvals necessary for execution of the Services.

### 2.6 Subcontracting

2.6.1 Vendor may use subcontractors or Affiliates to perform portions of the Services, provided that Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of such subcontractors as if they were Vendor’s own acts and omissions.

2.6.2 Vendor shall ensure that any subcontractors comply with obligations materially similar to those imposed on Vendor under this Agreement, including confidentiality, data protection, and security obligations.

3.2.1 Each SOW shall specify the applicable rate structure, including:

- Hourly or daily billing rates by role or level;  
- Any overtime rates, where permitted by law;  
- Any applicable volume discounts or tiered pricing.

3.2.2 Where project Milestones are defined, the payment schedule shall be tied to the completion and acceptance (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed) of such Milestones. For example:

- 20% of project fees upon execution of the SOW;  
- 40% upon completion and acceptance of Phase 1 Deliverables;  
- 30% upon completion and acceptance of Phase 2 Deliverables;  
- 10% upon final completion and acceptance of all Deliverables.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Company on a **Net 60** basis. Invoices shall be issued monthly in arrears, unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW, and shall reference the Agreement number (AGR-PHO-202501-710) and related SOW numbers.

3.3.2 Company shall pay undisputed amounts within sixty (60) days after its receipt of a complete and accurate invoice. Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer or other agreed electronic payment method to the account designated by Vendor.

3.3.3 If Company disputes any portion of an invoice, Company shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the nature and amount of the dispute. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve the dispute promptly. Company shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3.2.

### 3.4 Late Payment

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at a rate equal to the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

3.4.2 Vendor’s right to charge interest shall be without prejudice to any other rights or remedies available under this Agreement or at law, including the right, after reasonable prior written notice, to suspend performance of Services for material non-payment, subject to Section 4.4.

### 3.5 Taxes

3.5.1 Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, excise, value-added, or similar taxes. Company shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees), and Vendor shall itemize such taxes on its invoices where applicable.

3.6.1 Company shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with Company’s then-current travel and expense policy, provided that such policy has been communicated to Vendor in writing.

3.6.2 Vendor shall submit itemized expense reports with supporting documentation, and Company shall reimburse such expenses in accordance with the payment terms in Section 3.3.

### 4.1 Term

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, in whole or in part, upon not less than **forty-five (45) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party.

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Company, Company shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, together with any non-cancellable commitments expressly authorized by Company.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for cause if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the nature of the breach. 

4.4.2 Notwithstanding Section 4.4.1, the following events shall be considered immediate grounds for termination for cause, without a cure period, upon written notice:

(a) The other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets;  
(b) A material violation of applicable law that materially compromises the terminating Party’s interests;  
(c) A material breach of confidentiality or data protection obligations under Articles V and XI.

### 4.5 Effects of Termination

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) Vendor shall promptly cease performance of the Services (except for any transition assistance agreed pursuant to Section 4.5.3);  
(b) Company shall pay Vendor all undisputed amounts due and payable for Services rendered and approved expenses incurred through the effective date of termination;  
(c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5; and  
(d) All licenses granted under this Agreement shall terminate, except as otherwise expressly provided in Article VI.

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not automatically terminate individual SOWs, unless the termination notice expressly states that such SOWs are terminated or unless the basis for termination affects all Services.

4.5.3 Upon Company’s request, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to sixty (60) days after termination (or such shorter period as specified by Company) to facilitate the orderly transfer of services to Company or a replacement provider, at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates, subject to any negotiated transition plan.

### 4.6 Survival

4.6.1 The rights and obligations of the Parties under Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions), and any other provisions that by their nature should survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## FORCE MAJEURE

### 15.1 Definition

15.1.1 A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, epidemic or pandemic, terrorism, war, civil unrest, strikes (other than strikes by the affected Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, or interruptions in telecommunications or power services not caused by the affected Party.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The affected Party shall:

(a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, including reasonable details of its impact; and  
(b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension and Termination

15.3.1 Performance of the affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, to the extent such obligations cannot be performed due to the Force Majeure Event.

15.3.2 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement or the affected SOW for convenience upon written notice, without liability other than obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
