## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of professional services in connection with the design, configuration, deployment, and ongoing support of a cloud-based enterprise data and analytics platform (the “**Platform**”). The Services shall include, without limitation:  

(a) discovery and requirements gathering;  
(b) solution architecture and design;  
(c) system configuration and customization;  
(d) data migration and integration;  
(e) testing and quality assurance;  
(f) user training and enablement;  
(g) transition to operations; and  
(h) ongoing managed services and support.

2.1.2 The Services will be performed in multiple phases, as further detailed in **Section 2.3** (Phases of Work) and **Section 2.4** (Milestones and Timeline). Vendor shall perform the Services in accordance with the Service Levels, performance standards, and timelines set forth herein.

### 2.2 Objectives

2.2.1 The primary objectives of the Services are to:

(a) Implement a scalable, secure, and resilient cloud-based Platform to support Client’s enterprise analytics and reporting needs;  
(b) Consolidate Client’s disparate data sources into a unified data repository with governed access and robust data quality controls;  
(c) Deliver standardized and ad-hoc analytics capabilities to Client’s business users and data analysts; and  
(d) Establish an operational support framework to maintain and enhance the Platform over the Term.

2.2.2 Vendor acknowledges that timely delivery, quality of work, and adherence to Client’s security, compliance, and data protection requirements are critical to achieving these objectives.

### 2.3 Phases of Work

2.3.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (Months 1–3)**  
(a) Conduct stakeholder interviews, workshops, and technical assessments to document business and technical requirements.  
(b) Prepare and deliver a **Solution Design Document** detailing the architecture, data model, integration points, security model, and high-level implementation plan.  
(c) Define detailed **Use Cases** and **User Stories** to guide configuration and development work.  
(d) Identify any required Third-Party Materials and associated licensing or subscription requirements.

2.3.2 **Phase 2 – Build and Configuration (Months 3–9)**  
(a) Configure the Platform in accordance with the Solution Design Document and agreed Specifications.  
(b) Develop custom integrations with Client’s source systems (e.g., ERP, CRM, HR, and operational systems) using approved integration patterns and tools.  
(c) Implement data ingestion pipelines, transformation logic, and data quality rules as defined in collaboration with Client.  
(d) Configure user roles, permissions, and security controls in alignment with Client’s security policies.  
(e) Develop initial analytics content, including standardized dashboards, reports, and data models for at least three priority business domains (e.g., Sales, Finance, Operations).

2.3.3 **Phase 3 – Testing, Training, and Go-Live (Months 9–12)**  
(a) Execute unit, integration, performance, and user acceptance testing in cooperation with Client.  
(b) Remediate identified defects and deficiencies in accordance with the acceptance criteria in **Section 2.6**.  
(c) Develop and deliver training materials, including user guides, reference documents, and recorded training sessions.  
(d) Conduct train-the-trainer sessions and live end-user training workshops, as mutually agreed.  
(e) Support cutover and go-live activities, including final data loads, validation, and readiness checks.

2.3.4 **Phase 4 – Managed Services and Enhancements (Months 13–24)**  
(a) Provide ongoing Level 2 and Level 3 support for the Platform in accordance with the Service Levels in **Article XII**.  
(b) Monitor system performance, capacity, and availability, and provide periodic reports to Client.  
(c) Implement minor enhancements, configuration changes, and optimizations as requested by Client and agreed under Change Orders, where applicable.  
(d) Provide quarterly roadmap and improvement recommendations based on usage analytics and feedback from Client.

### 2.4 Milestones and Timeline

2.4.1 Vendor and Client agree to the following key Milestones and target completion dates (subject to adjustment through Change Orders as set forth in **Article XIII**):

- **Milestone 1** – Completion of Discovery & Requirements Sign-Off: Within 60 days of the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 2** – Delivery of Solution Design Document: Within 90 days of the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 3** – Completion of Core Platform Configuration & Initial Integrations: Within 210 days of the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 4** – Completion of User Acceptance Testing & Go-Live: Within 365 days of the Effective Date.  
- **Milestone 5** – Transition to Steady-State Managed Services: No later than 395 days after the Effective Date.

2.4.2 The Parties shall collaborate in good faith to refine and maintain a detailed project plan, identifying activities, dependencies, and resources. Vendor shall update the project plan at least monthly, or more frequently upon Client’s reasonable request.

### 2.5 Performance Standards

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Services:  

(a) in a timely, professional, and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications;  
(b) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards;  
(c) in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and Client’s written policies made available to Vendor; and  
(d) in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

2.5.2 Vendor shall devote adequate resources, including Key Personnel, to ensure that the Services and Deliverables are completed in accordance with the Milestones and performance standards described in this Article II.

### 2.6 Deliverables and Acceptance

2.6.1 Vendor shall deliver the Deliverables specified in this Article II and in any attached schedules. Key Deliverables include, without limitation:

(a) Solution Design Document;  
(b) Data Integration Specifications and Mapping Documents;  
(c) Configured Platform Environments (development, test, and production);  
(d) Standard dashboards and reports for agreed business domains;  
(e) User and technical documentation;  
(f) Training materials and recordings; and  
(g) Operational runbooks and support procedures.

2.6.2 Upon delivery of each Deliverable, Vendor shall notify Client in writing. Client shall have a reasonable period, not to exceed **twenty (20) Business Days** (unless otherwise agreed in writing), to review and test the Deliverable for conformity with the Specifications and applicable acceptance criteria.  

2.6.3 A Deliverable shall be deemed accepted when:

(a) Client provides written confirmation of acceptance; or  
(b) Client fails to provide written notice of rejection within the review period, provided that Vendor’s delivery notice clearly stated that the Deliverable would be deemed accepted absent timely rejection.

2.6.4 If Client reasonably determines that a Deliverable does not conform to the Specifications or acceptance criteria, Client shall provide Vendor with a written notice specifying the deficiencies. 12.3.1 Vendor shall adhere to the following incident response and resolution targets during the Managed Services phase:

- **Priority 1 (Critical):** Platform unavailable or severely degraded with no workaround.  
  - Response Time: 1 hour  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired with limited workaround.  
  - Response Time: 2 hours  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: 1 Business Day  

- **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issue; functionality impaired but workaround available.  
  - Response Time: 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issue, cosmetic defect, or general inquiry.

(c) Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly Managed Services fee.
