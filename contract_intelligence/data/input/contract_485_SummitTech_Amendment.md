- **Contoso Enterprises**, a company duly organized and existing under the laws of its jurisdiction of incorporation, with its principal place of business at 10 Fleet Place, London EC4M 7RB, United Kingdom (hereinafter referred to as “**Contoso**”, the “**Client**” or the “**Company**”); and

## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  
2.1.1 The Services provided pursuant to this Amendment are **in addition to and, where expressly stated, in modification of** the Services described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly modified herein, all terms and conditions of Article II of the Parent Agreement shall continue to apply, including the phased approach, Deliverables, Client Responsibilities under **Section 2.5**, and Performance Standards under **Section 2.6**.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that the Vendor has, or will have, substantially completed Phases 1–5 (Discovery and Requirements, Architecture and Design, Build/Configuration/Integration, Data Migration and UAT, and Go‑Live and Transition) and transitioned into Phase 6 (Managed Support and Optimization) as described in **Sections 2.3.1–2.3.6** of the Parent Agreement, prior to the commencement of the Extended Term. This Amendment is intended to (a) extend and expand Phase 6 activities, and (b) introduce additional Optimization Services and Additional Integration Services.

2.2 **Overall Objectives of Amendment Services.**  
The primary objectives of the Services to be provided under this Amendment are to:

  2.2.1 Extend the managed support, incident management, and operational services for the Platform beyond the original Expiration Date of **10 June 2025**, through the Extended Term ending **19 May 2028**, in order to ensure continuity of operations and to maintain the Service Levels described in the Parent Agreement and this Amendment.

  2.2.2 Provide ongoing Optimization Services to enhance the performance, scalability, and functionality of the Platform originally implemented under the Parent Agreement, including periodic release management, performance tuning, and user‑driven functional refinements.

  2.2.3 Design, implement, and support Additional Integration Services to connect the Platform to additional Client systems (including, but not limited to, new CRM instances, regional ERPs, and third‑party logistics providers), utilizing the integration framework defined under **Section 2.3.2 (Architecture and Design)** and **Section 2.3.3 (Build, Configuration, and Integration)** of the Parent Agreement.

  2.2.4 Provide enhanced reporting, analytics, and operational dashboards extending the Deliverables listed in **Section 2.4 (Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, including updated as‑built documentation and updated Operational Run Books for the Extended Term.

2.3 **Optimization Services.**  
2.3.1 During the Extended Term, the Vendor shall provide recurring Optimization Services, including:

  (a) Quarterly performance assessments of the Platform’s application, data, and integration components, leveraging the Architecture Diagrams and High‑Level Design Document delivered pursuant to **Section 2.4.2** of the Parent Agreement.

  (b) Identification and implementation (subject to the Change Management process in **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XIII** of this Amendment where materially beyond existing scope) of configuration changes, workflow optimizations, and query tuning aimed at improving throughput, response times, and system stability.

  (c) Annual scalability reviews to ensure the Platform remains capable of handling projected transaction volumes across the Client’s EMEA business units, consistent with the key objectives outlined in **Section 2.2 (Key Objectives)** of the Parent Agreement.

  (d) Coordination with the Client’s internal IT and business stakeholders, including the project sponsor and project manager designated pursuant to **Section 2.5.2 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement, to prioritize optimization activities and to document agreed acceptance criteria.

2.3.2 Optimization Services shall be planned and tracked via an annually updated **Optimization Plan**, which shall be considered part of the Project Plan referenced in **Section 2.3.1(d)** of the Parent Agreement. The Optimization Plan will set out:

  (a) A prioritized list of optimization initiatives and associated expected outcomes;  
  (b) Estimated effort and timelines for each initiative; and  
  (c) Any anticipated impact on Fees, to be formalized via Change Orders where applicable.

2.3.3 Deliverables specific to the Optimization Services shall include, at a minimum:

  (a) Quarterly Performance and Optimization Reports;  
  (b) Updated configuration documentation;  
  (c) Revised Operational Run Books and Support Procedures supplementing those described in **Section 2.4.8** of the Parent Agreement; and  
  (d) Updated As‑built Documentation reflecting changes made during the Extended Term, supplementing **Section 2.4.10** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Additional Integration Services.**  
2.4.1 The Vendor shall design and implement Additional Integration Services connecting the Platform to up to **five (5)** additional systems identified by the Client in writing (which may include new ERP instances, CRM platforms, third‑party logistics providers, or data warehouses), subject to mutual agreement through the Change Management process.

2.4.2 For each new integration, the Vendor shall:

  (a) Perform technical and functional analysis of the target system and its data structures;  
  (b) Produce **Integration Specifications and API Documentation**, extending the Deliverable described in **Section 2.4.3** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (c) Implement, configure, and test the required APIs, message queues, ETL jobs, or other interface mechanisms, utilizing similar design principles to those defined in **Section 2.3.2 (Architecture and Design)** and **Section 2.3.3 (Build, Configuration, and Integration)** of the Parent Agreement;  
  (d) Conduct unit, integration, and system testing for each integration; and  
  (e) Provide updated integration diagrams and interface control documents as part of the As‑built Documentation.

2.4.3 The Additional Integration Services shall be delivered in phases analogous to Phase 3 and Phase 4 of the Parent Agreement, namely:

  (a) **Integration Design Sub‑Phase:** Requirements validation, interface design, and specification;  
  (b) **Integration Build & Test Sub‑Phase:** Development, configuration, and testing;  
  (c) **Integration UAT & Go‑Live Sub‑Phase:** Support for Client‑led UAT (in line with **Section 2.3.4 (Phase 4 – Data Migration and User Acceptance Testing)** of the Parent Agreement), remediation of identified defects, and go‑live assistance.

2.4.4 Each integration will be subject to acceptance in accordance with **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, including the ten (10) Business Day review period and the criteria for deemed acceptance.

2.5 **Extended Managed Support Services.**  
2.5.1 During the Extended Term, the Vendor shall continue to provide Extended Managed Support Services that are broadly consistent with **Phase 6 – Managed Support and Optimization** described in **Section 2.3.6** of the Parent Agreement, including:

  (a) Level 2 and Level 3 support for incidents and problems relating to the Platform;  
  (b) Routine monitoring of the Platform’s health, performance, and integrations;  
  (c) Patch deployment, minor release management, and configuration changes within scope; and  
  (d) Monthly service reports, including key statistics, incidents, and recommendations.

2.5.2 The Extended Managed Support Services shall be governed by the Parent Service Levels in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement, as modified and supplemented by **Article XII** of this Amendment (including Extended Service Levels and revised service credit mechanisms).

2.5.3 The Parties acknowledge and agree that Extended Managed Support Services will be delivered **remotely** by default, except where the Parties expressly agree in writing (via Change Order) that on‑site support is required, in which case additional travel and related expenses shall be subject to reimbursement under **Section 3.9 (Expenses and Reimbursement)** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 3.8** of this Amendment.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities Under Amendment.**  
2.6.1 The Client’s responsibilities set forth in **Section 2.5 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement remain fully applicable to the Services under this Amendment. In particular, the Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to relevant personnel and systems for optimization and integration work;  
  (b) Nominate, and keep in place throughout the Extended Term, a project sponsor and project manager with authority to make decisions regarding Optimization Services, Additional Integration Services, and Extended Managed Support Services;  
  (c) Validate design documents, specifications, test plans, and Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of submission, or such other period as agreed in the applicable Project Plan or Optimization Plan;  
  (d) Provide test data, environments, and relevant Client Data as needed; and  
  (e) Provide consolidated feedback and approvals as necessary to meet agreed timelines.

2.6.2 Failure of the Client to perform its obligations under this **Section 2.6** (and under **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement) may entitle the Vendor to equitable adjustments to timelines and to additional Fees made via Change Order, consistent with **Section 2.5 (last paragraph)** and **Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services under this Amendment in accordance with the Performance Standards described in **Section 2.6 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, including:

  (a) Professional and workmanlike performance with reasonable skill and care;  
  (b) Compliance with Applicable Law and Client policies provided in advance;  
  (c) Use of suitably skilled, experienced, and qualified personnel; and  
  (d) Adherence to the Service Levels (as extended herein).

2.8 **Out of Scope.**  
2.8.1 For clarity, and in line with **Section 2.8 (Out of Scope)** of the Parent Agreement, the following remain out of scope under this Amendment unless specifically agreed in a Change Order:

  (a) Custom software development beyond configurations, scripts, and light customizations expressly described in this Amendment;  
  (b) Hardware procurement, on‑premises infrastructure acquisition, or end‑user device support;  
  (c) Comprehensive end‑user training programs beyond “train the trainer” sessions;  
  (d) Any large‑scale re‑architecture of the Platform that fundamentally alters the solution design agreed in the Parent Agreement.

2.8.2 Any requested work that falls outside the Amendment Effective Scope shall be managed via the Change Management process described in **Article XIII** of both the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, and may result in additional Fees and extended timelines.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Amendment Contract Value.**  
In addition to the Contract Value of **USD $3,201,187** set forth in **Section 3.1 (Contract Value)** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties agree that the total estimated Contract Value for the Services to be performed under this Amendment is **USD $1,001,200** (the “**Amendment Contract Value**”), exclusive of Taxes.

3.2 **Fee Components Under Amendment.**  
The Amendment Contract Value consists of the following components:

  3.2.1 **Extended Managed Support (T&M with Annual Ceiling):**  
  An estimated amount of **USD $660,000** for Extended Managed Support Services for the Extended Term (approximately three years), billed monthly in arrears on a time‑and‑materials (“**T&M**”) basis, with an annual not‑to‑exceed ceiling of **USD $220,000** per twelve‑month period of the Extended Term. Actual billing will be based on agreed rate cards consistent with **Section 3.4 (T&M Support Billing)** of the Parent Agreement, with updated rates to be mutually agreed and appended to this Amendment.

  3.2.2 **Optimization Services (Fixed‑Price Packages):**  
  A fixed‑price allocation of **USD $201,200** for Optimization Services over the Extended Term, to be structured into six (6) semi‑annual optimization packages of **USD $33,533.33** each, tied to the delivery of agreed Optimization Plans and associated Deliverables.

  3.2.3 **Additional Integration Services (Fixed‑Price per Integration):**  
  A fixed‑price allocation of **USD $140,000** for Additional Integration Services, based on up to five (5) integrations, each priced at **USD $28,000**, covering design, build, testing, and go‑live support.

3.3 **Milestone Payment Schedule (Amendment Fixed‑Price Portions).**  
3.3.1 **Optimization Services Milestones.** Payments for Optimization Services shall be made as follows:

  (a) 1/6 of the Optimization Services amount (**USD $33,533.33**) upon Client’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the first Optimization Plan and associated initial optimization Deliverables;  
  (b) 1/6 of such amount upon acceptance of each subsequent semi‑annual optimization Deliverable set, for a total of six (6) optimization Milestones.

3.3.2 **Additional Integration Services Milestones.** For each Additional Integration:

  (a) 30% (USD $8,400) upon completion and acceptance of the integration design and Integration Specifications;  
  (b) 40% (USD $11,200) upon completion of build and successful system/integration testing;  
  (c) 30% (USD $8,400) upon completion of UAT support and go‑live, and acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the integration by the Client in accordance with **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement.

3.4 **T&M Support Billing Under Amendment.**  
Consistent with **Section 3.4 (T&M Support Billing)** of the Parent Agreement:

  3.4.1 The Vendor shall invoice the Client monthly in arrears for Extended Managed Support Services based on actual hours incurred at the agreed rate card;  

  3.4.2 Each invoice shall include a reasonably detailed breakdown of hours by resource role and activity (e.g., incident resolution, monitoring, optimization support), and shall indicate whether the annual not‑to‑exceed ceiling is projected to be reached, in which case a Change Request may be initiated for additional scope.

3.5 **Payment Terms.**  
3.5.1 Notwithstanding the **Net 45** payment terms in **Section 3.5 (Payment Terms)** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties hereby agree that **all Fees payable under this Amendment** shall be subject to **Net 60** payment terms. Accordingly, the Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** from the date of the Vendor’s invoice.

3.5.2 For the avoidance of doubt, the Net 45 terms in the Parent Agreement shall continue to apply to Fees invoiced solely under the Parent Agreement and not under this Amendment, unless the Parties mutually agree to update such terms in writing.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  
The disputed amounts process described in **Section 3.6 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to invoices issued under this Amendment, provided that references therein to Sections 3.5 shall be read to include **Section 3.5** of this Amendment.

3.7 **Late Payment.**  
Late payment provisions in **Section 3.7 (Late Payment)** of the Parent Agreement (including interest at 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, and the Vendor’s suspension rights) shall apply to any undisputed Fees due under this Amendment.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
The provisions of **Section 3.8 (Taxes)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the Fees payable under this Amendment. All Fees herein are exclusive of Taxes.

3.9 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
3.9.1 The expense reimbursement principles described in **Section 3.9 (Expenses and Reimbursement)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply to the Services under this Amendment. Any travel or on‑site work requested by the Client in connection with Optimization Services, Additional Integration Services, or Extended Managed Support Services will be subject to prior Client approval and shall be invoiced at cost.

3.10 **No Withholdings or Set‑off.**  
Except for disputed amounts under **Section 3.6** of the Parent Agreement and this Amendment, and required tax withholdings, the Client shall not withhold, set‑off, or deduct any amounts from payments due under this Amendment without Vendor’s prior written consent, consistent with **Section 3.10 (No Withholdings or Set‑off)** of the Parent Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Extended Term.**  
Notwithstanding **Section 4.1 (Term)** of the Parent Agreement, the Parties hereby agree that, solely for the purposes of the Services and obligations under this Amendment, the term shall commence on the Amendment Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **19 May 2028** (the “**Extended Term**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV and the Parent Agreement.

4.2 **Effect on Parent Agreement Term.**  
4.2.1 This Amendment does **not** by itself automatically extend the Term of the Parent Agreement for all purposes. Rather, it **extends and supplements** the Services and obligations related to the Platform support, optimization, and integration as described herein. The Parent Agreement shall remain in full force and effect during the Extended Term **to the extent necessary** to give full effect to this Amendment.

4.2.2 To the extent there is any inconsistency between the Term applicable to the Services under this Amendment and the Term in **Section 4.1** of the Parent Agreement, the Extended Term set out in this **Section 4.1** shall prevail for the Services and obligations governed by this Amendment.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
4.3.1 The Client’s right to terminate for convenience as described in **Section 4.3 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent Agreement shall apply equally to this Amendment. Accordingly, the Client may terminate this Amendment (in whole or in part) for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Vendor.

4.3.2 In such event, the Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered under this Amendment up to the effective date of termination, plus any non‑cancellable commitments and reasonable wind‑down costs, and pro rata amounts for incomplete fixed‑price Milestones as determined under **Section 4.3.2** of the Parent Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
4.4.1 The termination for cause provisions set out in **Section 4.4 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent Agreement are incorporated herein by reference and shall apply to this Amendment. Either Party may terminate this Amendment for material breach not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice, or upon insolvency events as described in the Parent Agreement.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  
4.5.1 If the Parent Agreement is terminated in its entirety (whether for convenience or cause), this Amendment shall automatically terminate on the same effective date, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue certain Extended Managed Support Services under standalone terms.

4.5.2 In such case, the Client shall remain liable for all accrued Fees and reimbursable expenses under this Amendment up to the effective date of termination, and the effect of termination provisions in **Section 4.6 (Effect of Termination)** of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented by **Section 4.6** below, shall apply.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of This Amendment.**  
4.6.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Amendment:

  (a) The Vendor shall cease performance of the Services contemplated herein, except as reasonably necessary to wind down or provide Transition Assistance Services as mutually agreed;  
  (b) The Client shall pay all accrued and outstanding Fees and approved expenses under this Amendment;  
  (c) All licenses granted under this Amendment (e.g., with respect to optimization Deliverables or additional integration components) shall continue under the same terms and conditions as licenses granted under **Article VI** of the Parent Agreement, unless expressly stated otherwise in this Amendment.

4.6.2 The confidentiality, data protection, IP ownership, and limitation of liability provisions of the Parent Agreement, as supplemented herein, shall survive termination of this Amendment in accordance with **Section 16.12 (Survival)** of the Parent Agreement and **Section 16.8** of this Amendment.

4.6.3 If the Client requests Transition Assistance Services following termination or expiry of this Amendment, the Vendor shall provide such services at its then‑current T&M rates, consistent with **Section 4.6.2** of the Parent Agreement, and the Parties shall document the scope and Fees via a Change Order.
