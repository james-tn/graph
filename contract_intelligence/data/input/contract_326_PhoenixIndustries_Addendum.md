## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF EXTENDED PRODUCTS AND ENHANCED SERVICES

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Scope.**  
This Addendum is executed **under and subject to** the overarching scope of Products and Services described in **Article II (Scope of Products and Services)** of the Parent Agreement, including Sections 2.1 through 2.9. For clarity:

  (a) The Extended Products shall constitute **“Products”** within the meaning of Section 1.12 and Article II of the Parent Agreement and shall be ordered via **Purchase Orders** as described in Section 2.3 (Ordering Procedure) of the Parent Agreement.  

  (b) The Enhanced Services shall constitute **“Services”** within the meaning of Section 1.13 and Article II of the Parent Agreement and shall be governed by Statements of Work consistent with Section 1.16 of the Parent Agreement, as further detailed herein.  

  (c) Except as expressly supplemented or modified in this Addendum, all obligations concerning quality control, conformance to **Specifications**, and performance standards under Sections 2.2, 2.4, 2.5, 2.6, and 2.7 of the Parent Agreement shall apply in full to the Extended Products and Enhanced Services.

2.2 **Extended Products – Description.**  
Phoenix shall design, manufacture, configure, and supply to Contoso the Extended Products, which include, at a minimum, the following categories:

  (a) **High-Density Compute Chassis.** Rack-mounted chassis (19" form factor) supporting high-density compute blades, redundant power modules, and hot-swappable fans, with integrated environmental monitoring, compatible with Contoso’s existing racks and power distribution as specified in the Addendum Specifications.  

  (b) **Advanced Interconnect Assemblies.** Network backplanes, fiber-optic patch harnesses, and high-speed copper assemblies designed for data rates up to 400 Gbps, with labeling standards and cable management tailored to Contoso’s data center layouts.  

  (c) **Edge Deployment Kits.** Ruggedized enclosures, shock-resistant mounts, and environmental sealing suitable for remote and edge environments (e.g., industrial or outdoor-adjacent settings) with specified operating temperature and humidity ranges.  

  (d) **Lifecycle Spare and Upgrade Kits.** Spare power modules, fans, controllers, monitoring boards, and upgrade kits (e.g., increased capacity or additional monitoring features) necessary to support a five-year lifecycle at Contoso’s forecast deployment levels.

All Extended Products shall be **new, free from defects in materials and workmanship**, and not refurbished or reconditioned, consistent with Section 2.2(c) and Section 7.2(b) of the Parent Agreement.

2.3 **Enhanced Services – Description.**  
Phoenix shall provide the following Enhanced Services, which build upon and extend the Services described in Section 2.4 of the Parent Agreement:

  (a) **Advanced Engineering and Design Optimization.** Phoenix shall collaborate with Contoso’s engineering teams to perform detailed thermal, power, and signal integrity analysis for the Extended Products, including:  
   - validation of chassis air flow and temperature profiles;  
   - optimization of backplane trace routing for signal integrity; and  
   - recommendations for rack layout and power distribution to maximize system reliability.  

  (b) **Pre-Staging and Integration Services.** Prior to shipment, Phoenix shall provide:  
   - pre-assembly of Extended Products into racks or sub-racks;  
   - installation of firmware, baseline configurations, labels, and barcodes;  
   - functional and burn-in testing per Addendum Specifications; and  
   - generation of staging records and test results as Addendum Deliverables.  

  (c) **Extended Commissioning Support.** Phoenix shall provide on-site and remote commissioning support to assist Contoso’s teams in bringing Extended Products into service at Critical Deployment Sites, including:  
   - supervision of installation and power-up;  
   - participation in integrated system testing; and  
   - joint validation of acceptance criteria documented in applicable SOWs.  

  (d) **Lifecycle Reliability and Failure Analysis Program.** Phoenix shall track FFR metrics for Extended Products and shall:  
   - provide quarterly reliability reports;  
   - perform root cause analysis for field failures; and  
   - recommend corrective actions or design improvements in consultation with Contoso.  

  (e) **Enhanced Technical Support and Training.** In addition to the training under Section 2.4(e) of the Parent Agreement, Phoenix shall provide:  
   - periodic advanced training sessions for Contoso’s engineering, operations, and field staff;  
   - updated technical documentation and knowledge base articles; and  
   - participation in periodic technical review boards to address product enhancements.

2.4 **Deliverables, Milestones, and Documentation.**  
Consistent with Section 2.5 of the Parent Agreement, each SOW under this Addendum shall:

  (a) Describe the specific Addendum Deliverables, including design documents, test plans, staging records, commissioning reports, training materials, and reliability reports;  

  (b) Set forth milestones for design freeze, prototype delivery, first article approval, pilot deployment, and volume ramp;  

  (c) Define acceptance criteria for Extended Products and Enhanced Services, including specific performance metrics (e.g., maximum allowable FFR for defined periods) and required documentation; and  

  (d) Specify the format and timing of progress reports, which shall be no less frequent than monthly during active deployment phases.

Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the default acceptance procedure in Section 2.5(c) of the Parent Agreement (10 Business Day review period) shall apply to Addendum Deliverables, subject to the right of Contoso to provide notice of material non-conformity.

2.5 **Performance Standards and FFR Targets.**  
In addition to the performance standards in Section 2.6 of the Parent Agreement, the Parties agree that:

  (a) Phoenix shall implement process controls and reliability testing such that the Extended Products achieve an initial FFR not exceeding the target specified in Schedule 3 for each product family during the first Program Year;  

  (b) The Parties will review FFR data quarterly and agree on continuous improvement plans where FFR exceeds the target thresholds;  

  (c) Persistent failure to meet the FFR targets for two (2) consecutive quarters shall trigger a joint corrective action plan. While this Addendum is **Vendor‑favorable**, such failures shall not, by themselves, constitute a material breach permitting immediate termination, but may be considered in Contoso’s future volume decisions and in negotiating Change Orders.

2.6 **Ordering Procedure Under Addendum.**  
The ordering procedure established in Section 2.3 of the Parent Agreement applies to all Extended Products and Enhanced Services. In addition:

  (a) Each Purchase Order issued under this Addendum shall **reference both** the Parent Agreement (**PUR-PHO-202402-266 / contract_266**) and this Addendum (**ADD-PHO-202402-326**);  

  (b) Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to acknowledge or reject Purchase Orders within **three (3) Business Days**, rather than five (5) Business Days, where the orders relate to Critical Deployment Sites, provided that failure to respond within such period shall still be deemed acceptance under Section 2.3(b) of the Parent Agreement;  

  (c) Any deviation from the terms of a Purchase Order shall be effective only if agreed in writing by both Parties, consistent with Section 2.3(c) of the Parent Agreement.

2.7 **Subcontractors and Supply Chain.**  
Phoenix may use subcontractors consistent with Section 2.8 of the Parent Agreement. Given the larger scope and extended Term of this Addendum, Phoenix shall, upon Contoso’s reasonable request, provide supply chain transparency for critical components of the Extended Products and promptly notify Contoso of any anticipated supply disruptions.

2.8 **Non-Exclusivity.**  
Consistent with Section 2.9 of the Parent Agreement, this Addendum is non-exclusive. However, Contoso acknowledges that certain Extended Products may be proprietary designs of Phoenix, and nothing herein shall be construed to grant Contoso exclusivity in such designs unless explicitly stated in a specific SOW.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Addendum Term.**  
This Addendum shall commence on the Addendum Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 12, 2029**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent Agreement.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.**  
Notwithstanding Section 4.1 of the Parent Agreement (Initial Term ending February 17, 2028), the Parties expressly agree that:

  (a) This Addendum’s Term extends beyond the Initial Term of the Parent Agreement to **February 12, 2029** to support extended lifecycle commitments for Extended Products;  

  (b) The Parties intend, and hereby agree, that the Parent Agreement shall be renewed or extended, pursuant to Section 4.2 of the Parent Agreement, as necessary to ensure that the Parent Agreement remains in effect and governs this Addendum through its expiration date; and  

  (c) If the Parties do not execute a formal renewal of the Parent Agreement, they nonetheless agree that the **relevant provisions of the Parent Agreement** (including Articles II–XVI) shall be deemed automatically extended and applicable solely to govern this Addendum through the Addendum Term, unless and until otherwise agreed in writing.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Vendor‑Favorable).**  

  (a) **By Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Addendum, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to Phoenix.  

  (b) **By Phoenix.** Phoenix may terminate this Addendum for convenience upon not less than **ninety (90) days’** prior written notice to Contoso, provided that such termination shall not relieve Phoenix from fulfilling any firm, accepted Purchase Orders scheduled for delivery within ninety (90) days after the termination effective date, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
The termination for cause rights in Section 4.4 of the Parent Agreement apply to this Addendum. For clarity:

  (a) A material breach of this Addendum shall be deemed a material breach of the Parent Agreement, and vice versa, to the extent such breach affects the Parties’ obligations with respect to the Extended Products and Enhanced Services.  

  (b) Failure to meet FFR targets or Addendum SLAs, by itself, shall not constitute a material breach unless accompanied by failure to implement and follow an agreed corrective action plan and such failure continues uncured for at least ninety (90) days after written notice.

4.5 **Effect of Termination of Parent Agreement.**  

  (a) If the Parent Agreement is terminated in its entirety in accordance with Article IV thereof, this Addendum shall automatically terminate concurrently, **unless** the Parties expressly agree in writing to continue this Addendum under mutually agreed modified terms.  

  (b) Termination of individual Purchase Orders or SOWs under Section 4.5 of the Parent Agreement shall not automatically terminate this Addendum, except to the extent this Addendum contains no remaining active Orders or SOWs and the Parties agree there is no further need for this Addendum.

4.6 **Effect of Termination of Addendum.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Addendum:

  (a) Phoenix shall promptly cease work on the terminated portions and use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate costs, consistent with Section 4.6(a) of the Parent Agreement;  

  (b) Contoso shall pay Phoenix for Extended Products delivered and accepted and Enhanced Services performed and accepted up to the effective date of termination, including any reasonable, documented, and non‑cancellable commitments pre‑approved in writing by Contoso, consistent with Section 4.6(b) of the Parent Agreement;  

  (c) Phoenix shall return or destroy Contoso’s Confidential Information and Client Data as required by Section 4.6(c) and Section 11.7 of the Parent Agreement;  

  (d) Any licenses granted under Article VI of this Addendum shall continue only to the extent expressly stated therein; and  

  (e) The survival provisions in Section 4.7 of the Parent Agreement shall apply, together with the survival terms stated in Article XVI of this Addendum.

12.2 **Support Hours and Severity Levels.**  
Phoenix shall provide technical support for Extended Products and Enhanced Services during standard support hours and for escalations as follows:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of system functionality at a Critical Deployment Site affecting production operations:  
   - Response Time: within **30 minutes** of ticket opening (24x7).  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within **4 hours**.  

  (b) **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation of functionality with no acceptable workaround, potentially impacting upcoming production operations:  
   - Response Time: within **2 hours** during Business Days.  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within **12 hours**.  

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact with a reasonable workaround available:  
   - Response Time: within **1 Business Day**.  
   - Target Resolution or Workaround: within **5 Business Days**.  

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low)** – General questions, minor issues, or enhancement requests:  
   - Response Time: within **3 Business Days**.  
   - Resolution: as mutually agreed.

12.3 **Emergency Onsite Support.**
