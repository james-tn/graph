## ARTICLE I – DEFINITIONS

1.6 **"Controller"** (or **"Business"** under CCPA, as the context requires) means the entity which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data.

1.7 **"Data Breach"** or **"Security Incident"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed by Delta in connection with the Services under this Agreement.

1.8 **"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom the Personal Data relates, as such term is defined under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

1.9 **"Deliverables"** means any reports, analyses, dashboards, processed datasets, software configurations, documentation, or other work product (whether in tangible or intangible form) generated or provided by Delta to Contoso under this Agreement, as further described in Article II.

1.11 **"Force Majeure Event"** has the meaning set forth in Article XV and includes events beyond a Party’s reasonable control which prevent or delay the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

1.12 **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person and, for the purposes of CCPA, includes "Personal Information" as defined therein, which is processed by Delta on behalf of Contoso under this Agreement.

1.13 **"Processing"** (and **"Process"**, **"Processed"**) means any operation or set of operations which is performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.14 **"Processor"** (or **"Service Provider"** under CCPA, as the context requires) means the entity which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller, as further specified under Applicable Data Protection Laws. ## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **Description of Services.**  
Delta shall provide to Contoso a comprehensive set of managed data processing and related services, including, without limitation:

  2.1.1 Secure ingestion and aggregation of raw data sets from Contoso systems and third-party sources designated by Contoso, using secure APIs, file transfer mechanisms, or other agreed technical interfaces;

  2.1.2 Normalization, cleansing, enrichment, anonymization or pseudonymization (where instructed by Contoso), and transformation of such data for the purpose of analytics, reporting, and operational usage;

  2.1.3 Hosting of Contoso’s processed data and related metadata on Delta’s managed cloud infrastructure, including provisioning of storage, compute, and networking resources necessary to support agreed workloads;

  2.1.4 Provision of advanced analytics and business intelligence services, including statistical analysis, machine learning model training and execution, creation of dashboards, and generation of scheduled and ad hoc reports for use by Contoso;

  2.1.5 Configuration and maintenance of application programming interfaces (APIs) and dedicated interfaces to enable Contoso to access, extract, and integrate processed data with Contoso’s internal systems and third-party applications;

  2.1.6 Implementation of agreed data retention, archiving, and deletion policies under the direction of Contoso and in compliance with Applicable Data Protection Laws, as further detailed in Article XI;

  2.1.7 Provision of technical support, monitoring, incident response, and service-level management to ensure the performance and availability of the hosted environment, as described in Article XII;

  2.1.8 Professional services, including data migration, platform configuration, integration services, and training for Contoso’s designated personnel, as may be agreed in one or more written Statements of Work ("SOWs") executed by the Parties.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
Delta shall provide Deliverables as may be specified in written SOWs or order forms issued under this Agreement and accepted by Delta. Such Deliverables may include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 Data analytics reports, dashboards, and visualizations tailored to Contoso’s business requirements;

  2.2.2 Documented data processing workflows, schemas, and data dictionaries;

  2.2.3 Configuration files, scripts, or code snippets necessary for automation of data ingestion or transformation processes;

  2.2.4 Deployment and configuration documentation for the hosted environment and integrations with Contoso’s systems;

  2.2.5 Training materials, user guides, or standard operating procedures relating to the use of the Services.

Unless otherwise expressly stated in an applicable SOW, all Deliverables shall be deemed accepted by Contoso five (5) Business Days after delivery if Contoso has not provided written notice of material non-conformance within such period, specifying in reasonable detail the nature of such non-conformance.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Delta shall perform the Services:

  2.3.1 In a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for high-availability managed data services;

  2.3.2 In material compliance with this Agreement, the applicable SOWs, and any documented processing instructions from Contoso that are consistent with this Agreement and Applicable Data Protection Laws;

  2.3.3 Using personnel who are appropriately trained, qualified, and subject to confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth in Article V;

  2.3.4 With reasonable efforts to meet the Service Levels described in Article XII and any additional service levels set forth in an SOW.

2.4 **Timeline and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall agree upon the specific timelines, phases, and milestones for the implementation and ongoing delivery of the Services under each SOW. Unless otherwise set forth therein:

  2.4.1 Delta shall commence the onboarding and initial configuration of the Services within thirty (30) days following the Effective Date or the applicable SOW effective date, subject to Contoso’s timely provision of necessary access, data, and cooperation;

  2.4.2 Key phases may include: (a) discovery and requirements gathering; (b) environment setup and connectivity; (c) data ingestion and validation; (d) configuration of analytics and reporting; (e) user acceptance testing; and (f) go-live and stabilization;

  2.4.3 Delays caused by Contoso’s failure to timely provide data, access, personnel, or approvals shall extend the applicable milestones on a day-for-day basis and may result in additional Fees as set forth in Article III and Article XIII.

2.5 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges and agrees that Delta’s performance of the Services depends on Contoso’s timely cooperation, including:

  2.5.1 Providing accurate, complete, and lawful data to be processed, and ensuring that Contoso has obtained all necessary rights, consents, and authorizations to permit the processing by Delta as contemplated herein;

  2.5.2 Designating qualified personnel to serve as points of contact for project management, technical coordination, and escalation;

  2.5.3 Maintaining and operating its own systems, networks, and applications that interface with the Services, including the security and integrity of such systems;

  2.5.4 Reviewing and approving Deliverables and providing feedback within reasonable timeframes.

Any failure by Contoso to fulfill its obligations may be deemed a material breach of this Agreement and may relieve Delta from corresponding performance obligations to the extent impacted.

2.6 **Nature and Purpose of Processing.**  
For purposes of Article XI and Applicable Data Protection Laws:

  2.6.1 **Nature of Processing:** Collection, storage, organization, structuring, retrieval, consultation, use, analysis, transmission, disclosure (only as authorized), alteration, restriction, deletion, and destruction of Personal Data.

  2.6.2 **Purpose of Processing:** To provide the Services described in this Article II, including data hosting, analytics, reporting, optimization of Contoso’s operations, and such other purposes as are specified in the applicable SOWs and consistent with Contoso’s documented instructions.

  2.6.3 **Types of Personal Data:** May include, without limitation, identifiers (e.g., name, email address, IP address, account identifiers), commercial information, usage data, contact details, and other categories of Personal Data as may be further described in an SOW.

  2.6.4 **Categories of Data Subjects:** May include Contoso’s employees, contractors, customers, prospects, users, and other individuals whose Personal Data is submitted to the Services by or at the direction of Contoso.

  3.3.1 At a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the due date until paid in full;

  3.4.3 The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Any part of the disputed amount finally agreed or determined to be payable shall be due within fifteen (15) days after resolution.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**

  3.6.1 Such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Contoso (email being sufficient for this purpose, unless otherwise agreed); and

3.7 **No Withholding.**  
Except as required by applicable law, Contoso shall not withhold, set off, or deduct any amounts from payments due to Delta under this Agreement. 4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until **July 06, 2027** (the "**Expiration Date**"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may agree in writing to renew this Agreement for one or more additional periods on such terms and conditions as the Parties may mutually agree. Absent a written renewal agreement executed prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement shall expire automatically on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Contoso.**  
Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, by providing Delta with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days** prior written notice. Contoso shall remain liable for all Fees and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination and any non-cancellable commitments entered into by Delta in reliance on this Agreement.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Delta.**  
Delta may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, by providing Contoso with at least ninety (90) days prior written notice, provided that such termination shall not affect any SOW then in effect unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Parties.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.5.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

  4.5.2 Delta may terminate or suspend the Services immediately, in whole or in part, upon written notice if: (a) Contoso fails to pay undisputed amounts due under this Agreement within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of non-payment, as set forth in Section 3.3; (b) Contoso’s use of the Services, in Delta’s reasonable judgment, violates applicable law or poses a security or operational risk; or (c) Contoso becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, is placed under receivership, or undergoes a similar event under applicable bankruptcy or insolvency laws.

4.6 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:

  4.6.1 All rights and licenses granted by Delta to Contoso hereunder shall cease, except as expressly stated otherwise in this Agreement;

  4.6.2 Contoso shall promptly pay all outstanding Fees and expenses due through the effective date of termination or expiration;

  4.6.3 Delta shall, at Contoso’s written request and subject to payment of applicable Fees, provide reasonable transition assistance for a period not to exceed ninety (90) days to facilitate the orderly transition of Services and data back to Contoso or to a successor provider;

  4.6.4 Delta shall, in accordance with Section 11.7, return or delete Personal Data processed on behalf of Contoso, except to the extent retention is required by applicable law;

  4.6.5 The provisions of this Agreement that, by their nature, are intended to survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and all payment obligations accrued prior to termination) shall so survive.

4.7 **No Liability for Termination.**  
Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, neither Party shall be liable to the other for any damages or compensation of any kind solely by reason of the expiration or valid termination of this Agreement in accordance with its terms.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**") in connection with this Agreement, whether disclosed orally, visually, in writing, electronically, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information or the circumstances of disclosure. 5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  5.2.1 Is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

  5.2.2 Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without restriction on use or disclosure;

  5.2.3 Is lawfully received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

  5.2.4 Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  5.3.1 Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations and exercising its rights under this Agreement;

  5.3.2 Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement;

  5.3.3 Limit access to the Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein;

  5.3.4 Exercise at least the same degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted:

  5.4.1 Provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement so that the Disclosing Party may seek an appropriate protective order or other remedy; and

  5.4.2 Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that the Receiving Party is legally required to disclose, and cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in any efforts to limit or protect such disclosure.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible embodiments of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, except that the Receiving Party may retain copies as required by law or for archival and compliance purposes, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XI – DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

11.1 **Roles of the Parties.**

  11.1.1 Contoso shall be deemed the Controller (or Business under CCPA) with respect to Personal Data processed under this Agreement;

  11.1.2 Delta shall be deemed the Processor (or Service Provider under CCPA), and shall process Personal Data solely on behalf of Contoso and in accordance with Contoso’s documented instructions and this Agreement.

11.2 **Processing Instructions.**  
Delta shall process Personal Data only:

  11.2.1 For the purposes described in Article II and in applicable SOWs;

  11.2.2 In accordance with Contoso’s documented instructions, unless otherwise required by applicable law, in which case Delta shall, to the extent legally permitted, inform Contoso of such legal requirement prior to the Processing.

If Delta believes that an instruction from Contoso violates Applicable Data Protection Laws, Delta shall promptly notify Contoso and may suspend the performance of such instruction until the Parties agree on appropriate alternative instructions.

Each Party shall comply with Applicable Data Protection Laws applicable to its role and activities under this Agreement. Without limitation:

  11.3.1 Delta shall provide assistance reasonably requested by Contoso to enable Contoso to comply with its obligations under GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable laws, including, where applicable, responding to Data Subject requests and carrying out data protection impact assessments;

  11.3.2 Under CCPA, Delta shall not (a) sell or share Personal Information; (b) retain, use, or disclose Personal Information for any purpose other than for the specific purpose of performing the Services or as otherwise permitted by CCPA; or (c) retain, use, or disclose Personal Information outside the direct business relationship between the Parties.

11.4 **Security Measures.**  
Delta shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, such data, including, as appropriate:

  11.4.1 Encryption of Personal Data at rest and in transit using industry standard or stronger encryption technologies;

  11.4.2 Access controls, including role-based access restrictions and authentication mechanisms;

  11.4.3 Regular vulnerability assessments, patch management, and security monitoring;

  11.4.4 Business continuity and disaster recovery procedures designed to ensure the availability and resilience of processing systems and services;

  11.4.5 Employee training and awareness programs on data protection and information security.

11.5 **Data Breach Notification.**  
In the event of a Data Breach:

  11.5.1 Delta shall notify Contoso without undue delay, and in any event within **forty-eight (48) hours** after becoming aware of the Data Breach;

  11.5.2 Such notice shall describe, to the extent known: (a) the nature of the Data Breach, including the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records affected; (b) the likely consequences of the Data Breach; and (c) the measures taken or proposed to be taken by Delta to address the Data Breach and mitigate its possible adverse effects;

  11.5.3 Delta shall cooperate with Contoso and provide reasonable assistance in investigating, remedying, and mitigating the Data Breach, including providing information necessary for Contoso to comply with any notification obligations to regulators or Data Subjects.

11.6 **Sub-processors.**  
Delta may engage Sub-processors to process Personal Data on its behalf, provided that:

  11.6.1 Delta enters into a written agreement with each Sub-processor imposing data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement;

  11.6.2 Delta remains fully liable for the acts and omissions of any Sub-processor to the same extent as if performed by Delta;

  11.6.3 Upon Contoso’s written request, Delta shall provide a list of current Sub-processors involved in the provision of the Services, which Delta may update from time to time.

11.7 **Data Retention and Deletion.**  
Delta shall not retain Personal Data for longer than necessary to perform the Services or as required by applicable law. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon Contoso’s written request:

  11.7.1 Delta shall, at Contoso’s choice, return to Contoso or securely delete all Personal Data (including all copies) processed on behalf of Contoso, except to the extent Delta is required by law to retain such data;

  11.7.2 If Contoso does not elect return of Personal Data within thirty (30) days after termination or expiration, Delta may proceed to delete such Personal Data in accordance with its standard data deletion procedures.

11.8 **Data Subject Rights.**  
Delta shall assist Contoso, to the extent reasonably practicable and at Contoso’s expense, in responding to requests from Data Subjects to exercise their rights under Applicable Data Protection Laws (e.g., rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection), where such requests relate to Personal Data processed by Delta on behalf of Contoso.

11.9 **Audits.**  
Subject to reasonable confidentiality and security controls:

  11.9.1 Delta shall make available to Contoso information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Article XI;

  11.9.2 Contoso may, no more than once in any twelve (12) month period, and upon at least thirty (30) days prior written notice, conduct (or have a third-party auditor conduct) an audit of Delta’s processing facilities, systems, and records to verify Delta’s compliance with this Agreement, provided that such audit shall: (a) be conducted during normal business hours; (b) not unreasonably interfere with Delta’s business operations; and (c) be limited in scope to information relevant to the Services.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Delta shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the hosted Services are available for use by Contoso at least **99.9% of the time** during each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime.

12.2 **Permitted Downtime.**  
"Permitted Downtime" includes:

  12.2.1 Scheduled maintenance windows, provided that Delta provides at least forty-eight (48) hours prior notice and schedules such maintenance, where possible, during off-peak hours;

  12.2.2 Emergency maintenance required to address critical security vulnerabilities or operational issues, for which Delta shall provide as much advance notice as practicable;

  12.2.3 Downtime attributable to Contoso’s systems, networks, or third-party services not under Delta’s control.

12.3 **Response Times.**  
Delta shall provide technical support according to the following target response times from the time a support ticket is logged:

  12.3.1 **Severity 1 (Critical – Service down or severely degraded):** Initial response within one (1) hour; work continuously until resolution or workaround;

  12.3.2 **Severity 2 (High – Significant impact, no workaround available):** Initial response within four (4) hours; reasonable efforts for resolution during Business Hours;

  12.3.3 **Severity 3 (Medium – Limited impact, workaround available):** Initial response within one (1) Business Day;

  12.3.4 **Severity 4 (Low – Minor issues, general questions):** Initial response within two (2) Business Days.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  
If the uptime for the hosted Services falls below 99.9% in any calendar month (excluding Permitted Downtime), Contoso shall be eligible for service credits as follows:

  12.4.1 Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services;

  12.4.2 Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% of the monthly recurring Fees;

  12.4.3 Uptime below 98.0%: 20% of the monthly recurring Fees.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall be Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the uptime Service Levels, without prejudice to any other rights or remedies under this Agreement (including non-monetary obligations).

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
Delta shall not be responsible for failure to meet Service Levels to the extent caused by:

  12.5.1 Contoso’s misuse of the Services or failure to comply with documentation or instructions;

  12.5.2 Force Majeure Events as described in Article XV;

  12.5.3 Third-party services, networks, or infrastructure not under Delta’s control.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

A "**Force Majeure Event**" means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including, without limitation, acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, embargoes, disruptions to transportation or utilities, widespread network or telecommunications failures, or other similar events.

15.2 **Notice.**  
If a Party is prevented or delayed from performing its obligations under this Agreement due to a Force Majeure Event, such Party shall:

  15.2.1 Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, including reasonable details of its impact on performance; and

  15.2.2 Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such performance is prevented or materially delayed.

15.4 **Termination.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party, without liability to the other Party, except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
