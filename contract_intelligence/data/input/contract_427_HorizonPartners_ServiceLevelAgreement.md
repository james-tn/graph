## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Vendor shall provide to Client the following categories of Services:

  (a) **Managed Infrastructure and Hosting Services.** Vendor shall provision and operate a secure, scalable cloud hosting environment for Client’s applications and data, including virtual servers, storage, backup, and network connectivity. Vendor shall be responsible for operating system installation, patching, capacity management, and routine maintenance of the hosted infrastructure.

  (b) **Application Support and Maintenance.** Vendor shall provide support and maintenance services for specified applications identified in the applicable SOW, including incident management, bug fixes, performance tuning, and related remedial services. Vendor shall coordinate planned maintenance windows with Client, as described in Section 12.4.

  (c) **Monitoring and Incident Management.** Vendor shall continuously monitor the availability and performance of the Systems supporting the Services. Vendor shall detect, log, categorize, prioritize, and remediate Incidents in accordance with the Service Levels defined in Article XII. Vendor shall maintain a helpdesk or ticketing system available to Client during the hours specified in Section 12.2.

  (d) **Security and Compliance Support.** Vendor shall implement and maintain industry-standard security controls, including firewall configurations, access controls, malware protection, encryption-at-rest and in-transit (where commercially reasonable and agreed), and vulnerability patching. Vendor shall support Client’s compliance obligations under Applicable Law as further set forth in Article XI.

  (e) **Professional and Consulting Services.** Vendor shall perform professional services, including planning, configuration, integration, migration, and other consulting services as may be described in SOWs. These services may involve the design of architecture diagrams, migration plans, deployment runbooks, and other Deliverables.

2.2 **Specific Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, Vendor shall deliver to Client at least the following key Deliverables, subject to further elaboration in applicable SOWs:

  (a) **Implementation Plan.** Within thirty (30) days following the Effective Date, Vendor shall prepare and deliver to Client a detailed Implementation Plan describing the steps, timelines, responsibilities, and dependencies necessary to transition Services into production. The Implementation Plan shall include a risk register and mitigation strategies.

  (b) **Architecture and Configuration Documentation.** Vendor shall provide system architecture diagrams, configuration details for key components (e.g., virtual machines, security groups, load balancers, databases), and network topology documentation.

  (c) **Operational Runbooks.** Vendor shall prepare runbooks describing routine operational procedures, backup and restore processes, Incident escalation paths, and maintenance windows.

  (d) **Monthly Service Reports.** Vendor shall deliver monthly reports detailing uptime, Incidents, root cause analyses for Severity 1 and Severity 2 Incidents, capacity utilization, security events of interest, and any Service Level credits applicable under Article XII.

  (e) **Change Records.** For significant changes implemented under Article XIII, Vendor shall provide change approval records, impact assessments, and post-implementation review documentation.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) In a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards for similar services;

  (b) In compliance with the Service Levels set forth in Article XII;

  (c) In compliance with Applicable Law and Client’s reasonable written security and usage policies provided to Vendor in advance;

  (d) Using personnel with appropriate skill, experience, and qualifications to perform the assigned tasks.

Vendor shall assign a service delivery manager as a primary point of contact for Client and shall reasonably maintain continuity of key personnel, subject to Vendor’s existing employment policies.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that its cooperation is essential to Vendor’s provision of the Services. Client shall:

  (a) Provide Vendor with timely access to required facilities, systems, and personnel;

  (b) Designate a primary contact and escalation contacts for communications relating to the Services;

  (c) Promptly review and provide feedback on Deliverables and change requests;

  (d) Ensure that its systems and applications that interface with the Services meet the technical requirements provided by Vendor;

  (e) Be solely responsible for the accuracy, quality, and legality of Client Data and obtaining all necessary rights and consents for Vendor’s processing thereof as contemplated by this Agreement.

2.5 **Location of Services.**  
Services may be performed from Vendor’s or its subcontractors’ facilities, Client’s sites, or remote locations, as reasonably determined by Vendor, provided that Vendor maintains any agreed data residency and security requirements specified in an SOW or data protection addendum.

2.6 **Statements of Work.**  
From time to time, the Parties may enter into SOWs referencing this Agreement. Each SOW shall describe the specific Services, Deliverables, timelines, acceptance criteria, and fees applicable thereto. In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control, unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a particular provision of this Agreement.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless an SOW specifies alternative acceptance criteria or procedures, Client shall have ten (10) Business Days after delivery of a Deliverable to review and either accept or reject such Deliverable in a written notice specifying the reasons for any rejection. If Client does not provide such notice within the review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct any nonconformities identified in a rejection notice and resubmit the Deliverable for Client’s review, whereupon the review cycle shall repeat.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **Three Million Three Hundred Forty-Two Thousand Eighty-Two U.S. Dollars (US$3,342,082)** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustments for changes in scope under Article XIII, Service Level credits under Article XII, and any additional SOWs.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Contract Value shall be comprised of the following components, as further detailed in the applicable SOW(s):

  (a) **Recurring Service Fees.** Fixed monthly fees for core managed and hosting Services, estimated at **US$58,000 per month** for the initial thirty-six (36) months, subject to adjustments as set forth herein.

  (b) **Usage-Based Fees.** Variable fees for usage beyond baseline capacity, such as additional storage, data transfer, or compute instances, invoiced monthly in arrears based on actual measured usage.

  (c) **Professional Services Fees.** Time-and-materials fees for professional and consulting services not included in the recurring fees, billed at Vendor’s then-current rate card as specified in the applicable SOW.

  (d) **Pass-Through Costs.** Third-party license, cloud infrastructure, or other external costs that Vendor incurs specifically on Client’s behalf, as approved by Client in writing, which shall be passed through at cost or with a markup, as specified in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

  (a) Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in advance for recurring Service fees and monthly in arrears for usage-based fees, pass-through costs, and professional services performed in the preceding month.

  (b) Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 30”**). Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other agreed electronic method.

  (c) Client shall notify Vendor in writing of any disputed charges within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the applicable invoice, specifying the details and basis of the dispute. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve invoice disputes under Article XIV. Client shall timely pay all undisputed amounts.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until paid in full. Vendor may, after providing Client with written notice and a ten (10) Business Day cure period, suspend performance of the Services for continued failure to pay undisputed amounts, provided that Vendor resumes performance promptly upon receipt of all overdue amounts plus any applicable interest.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
All fees are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes arising from the transactions under this Agreement, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees. Vendor shall itemize any such taxes on its invoices. If Client is exempt from certain taxes, it shall provide Vendor with a valid exemption certificate.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses (e.g., travel, lodging, per diem, shipping) incurred by Vendor in connection with on-site Services at Client’s request shall be reimbursed by Client at cost. Vendor shall provide reasonable supporting documentation for such expenses upon request.

3.7 **Price Adjustments.**  
After the first twelve (12) months of the Term, Vendor may adjust recurring Service fees annually upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice, provided that any increase shall not exceed the greater of (i) three percent (3%) per year, or (ii) the percentage increase in the U.S. Consumer Price Index (CPI-U) for the prior calendar year, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **July 24, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**), which period constitutes the **“Initial Term.”**

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Following the Initial Term, the Agreement may be renewed for successive one (1) year periods (each, a **“Renewal Term”**) upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date or the then-current Renewal Term, as applicable, including agreement on any revised pricing or Service Levels.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, without cause by providing the other Party with at least **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice of termination. Termination for convenience shall not relieve Client of its obligation to pay for Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred through the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

  (a) **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail. If such breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall commence cure within such period and diligently pursue full cure within a reasonable time thereafter.

  (b) **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they become due, (ii) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or for reorganization, (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (iv) has a receiver appointed for a substantial part of its assets and such proceeding is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Chronic SLA Failure.**  
Client may terminate this Agreement for cause upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice if Vendor materially and repeatedly fails to meet critical Service Levels (such as uptime or Response Time) in any three (3) consecutive calendar months or any five (5) calendar months in a rolling twelve (12) month period, provided that Vendor has been notified of such failures and has not implemented a mutually agreed remedial plan.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

  (a) **Payment Obligations.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed fees and reimbursable expenses accrued but unpaid through the effective date of termination, including fees for work-in-progress on a time-and-materials basis, subject to Client’s rights in respect of defaults by Vendor.

  (b) **Transition Assistance.** Upon Client’s request made before or within thirty (30) days after termination or expiration, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition services for up to ninety (90) days (or such other period as the Parties may agree) at Vendor’s then-current rates, to facilitate migration of Services to Client or a third-party provider.

  (c) **Return or Deletion of Data.** Subject to Article XI, Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request, return to Client a copy of Client Data in a mutually agreed format or provide for secure export of such data. Within ninety (90) days after termination or expiration, Vendor shall delete or anonymize Client Data from its active Systems, subject to any legal retention requirements, and upon request shall confirm such deletion in writing.

  (d) **Survival.** The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration (including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI and payment obligations accrued as of termination) shall so survive.

4.7 **Suspension of Services.**  
Vendor may suspend the Services, in whole or in part, upon written notice to Client, if: (i) Client fails to pay undisputed fees within ten (10) Business Days after Vendor’s written notice of non-payment as described in Section 3.4; or (ii) Vendor reasonably determines that Client’s use of the Services materially violates Applicable Law or creates a security risk to the Services or any third party. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to limit the scope and duration of any suspension and shall promptly resume Services once the underlying issue is resolved.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production Services are available 24 hours per day, 7 days per week, excluding Scheduled Maintenance (as defined in Section 12.4), with a **monthly uptime** of at least **99.9%** (the **“Availability Commitment”**). Uptime shall be measured as follows:

> Uptime % = 100% × (Total Minutes in Month – Downtime) / (Total Minutes in Month)

Where “Downtime” means minutes when the production Services are unavailable due to unplanned outages caused by Vendor’s infrastructure or Services, excluding:

  (a) Scheduled Maintenance;  
  (b) Force Majeure Events;  
  (c) outages caused by Client systems or third-party providers not under Vendor’s control; or  
  (d) outages due to Client’s misuse or breach of this Agreement.

12.2 **Support Hours and Contact.**  
Vendor shall provide support via email and ticketing system 24x7 for Severity 1 (critical) Incidents and during normal Business Days between 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. Pacific Time for non-critical requests. Vendor shall provide a documented escalation process and contact information to Client.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the following **target** Response and Resolution Times:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):**  
    - Definition: Complete loss of Service or severe impact on critical business operations with no workaround.  
    - Response Time: 1 hour.  
    - Resolution Time Target: 8 hours or provision of a reasonable workaround.

  - **Severity 2 (High):**  
    - Definition: Significant degradation of Service impacting important processes with limited workaround.  
    - Response Time: 2 hours.  
    - Resolution Time Target: 24 hours or provision of a reasonable workaround.

  - **Severity 3 (Medium):**  
    - Definition: Partial loss of functionality or moderate impact with an acceptable workaround.  
    - Response Time: 1 Business Day.  
    - Resolution Time Target: 5 Business Days.

  - **Severity 4 (Low):**  
    - Definition: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
    - Response Time: 2 Business Days.  
    - Resolution Time Target: As agreed in the normal course.

12.4 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Vendor may perform Scheduled Maintenance during non-peak hours, generally between 10:00 p.m. and 4:00 a.m. Pacific Time. Vendor shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for maintenance expected to cause material Service disruption and shall endeavor to minimize downtime. Scheduled Maintenance shall not count as Downtime for purposes of the Availability Commitment.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If Vendor fails to meet the Availability Commitment in a calendar month, Client shall be entitled to the following **service credits**:

  - Uptime 99.9% to 99.0%: Service credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring Service fees for the affected Service.  
  - Uptime 99.0% to 98.0%: Service credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring Service fees for the affected Service.  
  - Uptime below 98.0%: Service credit equal to 20% of the monthly recurring Service fees for the affected Service.

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice(s) and shall be Client’s exclusive monetary remedy for failure to meet the Availability Commitment, subject to Client’s termination rights under Section 4.5.

12.6 **Exclusions from SLA Calculation.**  
Service Levels shall not apply to: (a) beta or evaluation features; (b) services provided under separate terms; or (c) any period during which Client is in material breach of this Agreement (e.g., non-payment), if such breach directly causes or contributes to the SLA failure.

15.1 **Definition.**

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the delay caused by the event, provided that the unaffected Party’s payment obligations for Services actually rendered shall not be excused.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event prevents a Party from substantially performing its obligations for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability (except for accrued payment obligations), subject to the obligations under Section 4.6.
