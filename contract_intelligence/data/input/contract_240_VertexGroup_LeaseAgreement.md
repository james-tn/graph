## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF LEASE AND SERVICES

2.1 **Lease of Premises.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Vertex hereby leases to Contoso, and Contoso hereby leases from Vertex, the Premises for the Term. The Premises shall be delivered to Contoso in a commercially reasonable condition as of the Effective Date, with base building systems (including HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and fire safety) in good working order and compliant with applicable Laws.

2.2 **Use of Premises.**  
(a) **Permitted Use.** Contoso shall use the Premises solely for general office purposes, including administrative, managerial, research and development, and other lawful business activities reasonably related to Contoso’s corporate operations, and for no other purpose without the prior written consent of Vertex (such consent not to be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed).  

(b) **Compliance with Laws.** Contoso shall not use or occupy the Premises in any manner that violates any Laws or Building Rules. Vertex shall likewise operate and maintain the building, including common areas and base building systems, in compliance with all applicable Laws.

2.3 **Services to be Provided by Vertex.**  
During the Term, Vertex shall provide the following Services to Contoso in a manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for first-class commercial office buildings in Houston, Texas, and in accordance with the Service Levels in Article XII:

(a) **Building Operations and Maintenance.**  
i. Operation, maintenance, repair, and replacement (as necessary) of the building structure, roof, exterior façades, lobbies, elevators, stairwells, common corridors, parking structures, and base building mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems.  
ii. Regular HVAC services providing reasonable heating and cooling during Business Hours (as defined by the Building Rules) and any additional after-hours HVAC services requested by Contoso, subject to applicable Service Fees as set forth in Section 3.2.  
iii. Housekeeping and janitorial services for common areas and standard janitorial services for the Premises, including trash removal and periodic deep cleaning, at frequencies consistent with comparable buildings.

(b) **Security Services.**  
i. Building-wide security services, including reception desk coverage, access control systems (e.g., key cards), security monitoring of entrances and exits, and periodic patrols of common areas.  
ii. Coordination with local law enforcement and emergency services as reasonably required for security incidents, provided that Vertex shall not be responsible for Contoso’s internal security measures, proprietary equipment protection, or data security, except as specifically stated in this Agreement.

(c) **Utilities Coordination.**  
i. Provision, coordination, or procurement of standard building utilities for the Premises, including electricity, water, sewer, and basic HVAC. Vertex may bill Contoso separately for electricity and other utilities consumed within the Premises based on sub-metering or a reasonable allocation formula, as outlined in Article III.  
ii. Maintenance and monitoring of utility infrastructure up to the point of entry into the Premises.

(d) **Facilities Management and Support.**  
i. Onsite or on-call facilities management personnel available during standard Business Hours to respond to maintenance requests, facility-related incidents, and other operational needs of Contoso.  
ii. Management of building vendors and subcontractors providing cleaning, landscaping, waste disposal, and other facility-related services.

(e) **IT and Infrastructure Support (Building Systems).**  
Vertex shall provide support and maintenance for building-controlled IT infrastructure related to the Premises, including building access systems, shared conference room audiovisual systems (if any), and building-wide internet connectivity where such services are provided as bundled amenities. Any dedicated IT services for Contoso’s internal systems shall be governed by separate agreements unless expressly set forth herein.

2.4 **Fit-Out and Initial Improvements.**  
(a) **Landlord Work.** Vertex shall, at its cost (unless otherwise agreed in writing), perform the initial fit-out work described in Exhibit A (if any), which may include basic partitioning, standard lighting, carpeting, and painting of the Premises. Vertex shall use commercially reasonable efforts to complete such work by the Effective Date or within such timeframe as mutually agreed in writing.  

(b) **Tenant Work.** Any additional improvements requested by Contoso beyond the standard fit-out shall be treated as Tenant Work. Tenant Work shall be at Contoso’s cost and performed either by Vertex or Contoso’s contractors, subject to Vertex’s reasonable approval and coordination requirements, consistent with Section 2.7.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Vertex shall perform the Services:  
(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner;  
(b) in accordance with the Service Levels in Article XII;  
(c) consistent with standards of first-class, professionally managed office buildings in the Houston, Texas central business district; and  
(d) in compliance with all applicable Laws, including building, fire, safety, and occupational health regulations.

2.6 **Contoso’s Obligations Regarding Premises.**  
Contoso shall:  
(a) maintain the interior portions of the Premises (excluding structural elements and base building systems) in a clean and orderly condition, reasonable wear and tear and damage from casualty excepted;  
(b) promptly notify Vertex of any damage, malfunction, or defect in the Premises or building systems of which Contoso becomes aware;  
(c) not permit the storage or use of hazardous materials at the Premises except for commercially reasonable quantities of standard office supplies and cleaning agents, in compliance with applicable Laws; and  
(d) comply with all Building Rules, provided that such rules are applied in a non-discriminatory manner to all tenants in the building.

2.7 **Improvements and Alterations.**  
(a) Contoso shall not make structural alterations to the Premises without Vertex’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Non-structural interior alterations that do not affect building systems and do not exceed a reasonable spending threshold (to be mutually agreed or as provided in Building Rules) may be made by Contoso upon notice to Vertex.  
(b) All Improvements made by or on behalf of Contoso shall comply with applicable Laws, building permits, and Vertex’s construction requirements.  
(c) Unless otherwise agreed in writing, non-removable Improvements shall become the property of Vertex upon installation and shall remain with the Premises upon expiration or termination of this Agreement. Removable trade fixtures, furniture, and equipment owned by Contoso shall remain Contoso’s property and may be removed by Contoso in accordance with Section 4.5.

2.8 **Subleasing and Sharing of Space.**  
Contoso may not sublease, license, or otherwise permit third parties to use or occupy the Premises or any portion thereof without Vertex’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed; provided, however, that Contoso may, without Vertex’s consent, permit its Affiliates and contractors performing services for Contoso to use the Premises solely for Contoso’s internal business purposes, subject to Contoso’s continued responsibility for compliance with this Agreement.

2.9 **Service Adjustments.**  
The Parties acknowledge that Contoso’s business needs may evolve during the Term. Any material change to the Services, including increased or decreased service levels, additional amenities, or reconfiguration of space, shall be handled in accordance with the change management procedures in Article XIII, and may result in an adjustment to Fees and applicable timelines.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Lease Fees and Contract Value.**  
(a) The total contract value for the Term shall be **Eight Hundred Forty-Five Thousand One Hundred Ninety-Eight U.S. Dollars (US$845,198)** (the **“Contract Value”**), inclusive of Lease Fees and base-level Service Fees, but exclusive of pass-through utilities, extraordinary services, taxes, and other reimbursable expenses as described herein.  

(b) The Parties agree that the Lease Fees shall be payable in equal monthly installments during the Term, allocated from the Contract Value, as set forth in Exhibit B. For clarity, the aggregate amount of Lease Fees and base Service Fees over the Term shall not exceed the Contract Value unless a change order is executed pursuant to Article XIII.

3.2 **Service Fees and Additional Charges.**  
(a) **Included Services.** Standard building operations, maintenance, basic security, and standard janitorial services described in Section 2.3(a)–(c) are included in the Lease Fees.  

(b) **Additional Services.** Additional or non-standard services, such as:  
   i. after-hours HVAC beyond standard Business Hours;  
   ii. dedicated security personnel assigned exclusively to the Premises;  
   iii. additional or specialized cleaning;  
   iv. modifications to building IT infrastructure to support Contoso’s unique needs;  
shall be charged as Service Fees at the rates set forth in Exhibit B or as otherwise agreed in writing through a change order.  

(c) **Utilities.** Electricity for the Premises will be separately metered or reasonably allocated. Contoso shall reimburse Vertex monthly for actual utility consumption and any associated surcharges, taxes, or fees, as itemized on the invoice. Water and sewer charges for the building shall be equitably allocated among tenants, with Contoso’s share reflected on monthly invoices.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms (Net 30).**  
(a) Vertex shall issue invoices to Contoso on a monthly basis in arrears for Lease Fees, base Service Fees, utilities, and any other Fees incurred during the preceding month.  

(b) All undisputed amounts shall be due and payable by Contoso within **thirty (30) calendar days** after Contoso’s receipt of the applicable invoice (**“Net 30”**). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by ACH, wire transfer, or other method agreed by the Parties.  

(c) If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall (i) notify Vertex in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the disputed amounts and reasons; and (ii) timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts within thirty (30) days of such notice.

3.4 **Late Payment and Interest.**  
(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month; or (ii) the maximum interest rate permitted by applicable Law, calculated from the day following the due date until the date payment is received.  

(b) Vertex shall provide written notice to Contoso of any late payment before imposing late fees or interest, and Contoso shall have a five (5) Business Day cure period following receipt of such notice before interest begins to accrue.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
(a) Contoso shall be responsible for all sales, use, occupancy, or similar taxes imposed on the Fees, other than taxes based on Vertex’s net income. Such taxes shall be separately stated on invoices.  

(b) Vertex shall be responsible for all real property taxes and assessments on the building and land; provided that Vertex may equitably allocate and pass through to tenants, including Contoso, the tenant’s proportionate share of increases in such taxes during the Term, as expressly described in Exhibit B.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursable Costs.**  
(a) To the extent that Contoso requests extraordinary services or work beyond the standard Services, and Vertex incurs out-of-pocket expenses (e.g., third-party contractor fees, special equipment rentals), Contoso shall reimburse Vertex for such expenses at actual cost, without markup, provided such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Contoso (email sufficient).  

(b) Vertex shall provide reasonable supporting documentation for reimbursable expenses, including vendor invoices and receipts upon request.

3.7 **Set-Off.**  
Except as expressly permitted by Law or upon a final, non-appealable order of a court or arbitrator, neither Party shall have the right to set off any amounts owed by the other Party under this Agreement against any amounts payable by such Party, without the prior written consent of the other Party; provided that Contoso may withhold payment of disputed amounts in accordance with Section 3.3(c).

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal Options.**  
Provided Contoso is not in material default beyond applicable cure periods, Contoso shall have the option to renew this Agreement for one (1) additional period of twelve (12) months (the **“Renewal Term”**), upon written notice to Vertex not less than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith the Lease Fees and Service Fees for the Renewal Term based on then-current market rates for comparable properties, but with a preference for reasonable and modest increases not exceeding [x]% per annum, unless otherwise justified by market data.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Client-Friendly).**  
(a) **By Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, upon at least **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Vertex. In such event, Contoso shall (i) pay all undisputed Fees accrued and payable up to the effective date of termination; and (ii) reimburse Vertex for any non-cancellable, pre-approved, documented expenses directly incurred on Contoso’s behalf. No early termination fee shall be owed by Contoso, except as expressly set forth in an exhibit.  

(b) **By Vertex.** Vertex may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon at least **one hundred twenty (120) days’ prior written notice** to Contoso and only after first offering Contoso substantially comparable alternative premises within the same building or a comparable building, on terms no less favorable than those herein. This provision is intended to be client-friendly and shall be strictly construed.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party:  
   (i) materially breaches any term of this Agreement, and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice specifying the breach; provided that if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have a reasonable additional period (not to exceed sixty (60) days) to cure, provided it is diligently pursuing cure; or  
   (ii) becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is subject to any bankruptcy or insolvency proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.  

(b) Without limiting the foregoing, persistent failure by Vertex to meet Service Levels in Article XII, where such failure materially and adversely impacts Contoso’s use of the Premises and is not cured after notice and cure periods, shall constitute cause for termination by Contoso.

4.5 **Effects of Termination or Expiration.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Contoso shall vacate the Premises on or before the effective date of termination, leaving the Premises in broom-clean condition, ordinary wear and tear and casualty damage excepted.  

(b) Contoso shall remove all of its personal property and trade fixtures and shall repair any material damage caused by such removal, reasonable wear and tear excepted. Any property not removed within thirty (30) days after expiration or termination may be deemed abandoned, and Vertex may dispose of it at Contoso’s expense, after giving Contoso at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice.  

(c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.6.  

(d) Any provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration (including without limitation payment obligations accruing prior to termination, confidentiality, intellectual property, indemnification, limitation of liability, and dispute resolution) shall so survive, as further set forth in Section 16.9.

4.6 **Holdover.**  
If Contoso remains in possession of the Premises after expiration or termination of this Agreement without a written extension, such possession shall be deemed a month-to-month tenancy under the same terms and conditions, except that Vertex may charge a holdover rent not exceeding 125% of the then-current monthly Lease Fees. Vertex shall notify Contoso in writing before imposing such holdover rate and shall cooperate in good faith with Contoso to facilitate an orderly transition.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“Confidential Information” means all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**), whether orally or in writing, and whether before or after the Effective Date, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to: business plans, financial information, pricing, technical data, security procedures, building systems specifications, tenant lists, and personal data of employees or visitors.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records;  
(c) is received by the Receiving Party from a third party who is not under a duty of confidentiality with respect to such information; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Confidentiality Obligations.**  
Each Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of exercising its rights and fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement;  
(b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party, except as permitted in Section 5.4; and  
(c) protect such Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.**  
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:  
(a) to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, contractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein; and  
(b) to the extent required by Law, regulation, or court order, provided that (i) the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice to the extent legally permissible, and (ii) cooperates reasonably with the Disclosing Party in seeking confidential treatment or protective orders.

5.5 **Data Protection Obligations.**  
To the extent Confidential Information includes personal data or personal information (as defined in Article XI), the Parties shall comply with the additional data protection and privacy obligations set forth in Article XI.

5.6 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except to the extent retention is required by Law or for archival, audit, or dispute resolution purposes, in which case such retained information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.7 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**, except with respect to trade secrets, which shall be protected for so long as they remain trade secrets under applicable Law.

12.1 **Uptime and Availability.**  
Vertex shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that building systems critical to Contoso’s occupancy (including HVAC, elevators, access control, and standard lighting) are available at least **99.9%** of the time during Business Hours, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

(a) **Critical Issues** (e.g., building access failure, major HVAC outage): Response within **1 hour**, targeted resolution within **4 hours**.  
(b) **High Priority Issues** (e.g., localized power outage in Premises, water leaks): Response within **2 hours**, targeted resolution within **8 hours**.  
(c) **Standard Issues** (e.g., minor repairs, non-urgent maintenance): Response within **1 Business Day**, targeted resolution within **5 Business Days**.

12.3 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Vertex shall provide Contoso with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance that may materially impact Contoso’s use of the Premises. Vertex shall use commercially reasonable efforts to schedule such maintenance outside of peak Business Hours.

14.2 **Escalation.**  
If the Parties are unable to resolve the Dispute within fifteen (15) Business Days, either Party may escalate the Dispute to senior executives of each Party, who shall meet (in person or virtually) and attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute within an additional fifteen (15) Business Days.

If the Dispute remains unresolved after escalation, the Parties agree to submit the Dispute to non-binding mediation administered by a mutually agreed mediator in **Dallas, Texas**, with each Party bearing its own costs and sharing the mediator’s fees equally. Mediation shall be initiated within thirty (30) days of a written request by either Party.

14.4 **Litigation or Arbitration.**  
If the Dispute is not resolved by mediation within sixty (60) days of the appointment of the mediator, either Party may pursue its rights and remedies in a court of competent jurisdiction as provided in Section 16.3. The Parties may, by mutual written agreement, elect binding arbitration instead of litigation.

## ARTICLE XV  
FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor strikes (excluding strikes solely by the affected Party’s own employees), acts of government, or failures of public utilities or telecommunications networks not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Effect of Force Majeure.**  
Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement (excluding payment obligations) to the extent directly caused by a Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party:  
(a) promptly notifies the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event and its expected duration;  
(b) uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact; and  
(c) resumes performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than thirty (30) consecutive days and materially prevents Contoso’s use of the Premises, Contoso may, upon written notice to Vertex, either:  
(a) suspend payment of Lease Fees proportionally to the degree of lost use of the Premises; or  
(b) terminate this Agreement without penalty, effective upon notice, provided that Contoso vacates the Premises within a reasonable period.
