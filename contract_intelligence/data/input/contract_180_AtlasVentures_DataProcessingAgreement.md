## ARTICLE I  
## DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below. Capitalized terms used but not otherwise defined herein shall have the meanings ascribed to them in the Master Agreement, if any.

“**Applicable Data Protection Laws**” means all privacy, data protection, and data security laws and regulations applicable to the Processing of Personal Data under this Agreement, including, without limitation, the CCPA, GDPR (to the extent applicable), and any implementing regulations or related guidelines issued by competent authorities, as each may be amended, superseded, or replaced from time to time.

“**Data Breach**” means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data transmitted, stored or otherwise Processed by Vendor or any Subprocessor, as further addressed in Section 11.5.

### 1.7 “Data Subject”

### 1.10 “Force Majeure Event”

### 1.11 “Personal Data”

“**Processing**” or “**Process**” means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client certain **data processing and analytics services**, including but not limited to:  
(a) secure hosting and storage of Client Data and Personal Data on Vendor’s cloud-based or co-located infrastructure;  
(b) data ingestion, normalization, transformation, and aggregation;  
(c) development and maintenance of analytics dashboards, visualization tools, and reporting mechanisms;  
(d) batch and real-time data processing workflows; and  
(e) related support, maintenance, monitoring, and incident management.

2.1.2 Vendor’s Services shall be performed in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and the Service Levels specified in Article XII. Vendor shall ensure that all personnel assigned to the Services possess appropriate skills, training, and experience.

### 2.2 Detailed Deliverables

2.2.1 **Initial Implementation Deliverables.** During the initial onboarding phase (the “Implementation Phase”), Vendor shall provide:

- A detailed **implementation plan** including timelines, milestones, resource assignments, and dependencies;  
- A **data mapping and classification report**, identifying categories of Personal Data, data flows, and storage locations;  
- A **security and privacy configuration document**, outlining encryption, access controls, and retention configurations applicable to Client’s environment within Vendor’s platform;  
- Integration of the Services with Client’s designated systems (e.g., CRM, ERP, marketing platforms, customer support systems) through APIs, connectors, or other agreed integration methods; and  
- An initial **set of dashboards and reports** agreed upon by the Parties, which may include operational metrics, customer behavior analytics, and performance indicators.

2.2.2 **Ongoing Operational Deliverables.** Following completion of the Implementation Phase, Vendor shall provide, on an ongoing basis:

- Continuous **data ingestion and processing** of Client-provided or Client-collected datasets;  
- **Scheduled reports** delivered at frequencies agreed in writing (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly), in formats mutually agreed upon (e.g., PDF, CSV, or via secure portal);  
- **Ad hoc analysis and reporting** upon Client’s reasonable written request, subject to any applicable fee schedule;  
- **System health and security reports**, including uptime statistics, security incident summaries (if any), and performance measurements against Service Levels; and  
- Documentation updates and release notes for any system changes that materially impact Client’s use of the Services.

### 2.3 Timelines and Performance Standards

2.3.1 Vendor shall commence the Implementation Phase within **ten (10) Business Days** following the Effective Date, subject to timely receipt of necessary information, access, and cooperation from Client as described in Section 2.7.

2.3.2 The Parties shall mutually agree in writing on an **Implementation Completion Date**, which is expected (but not guaranteed) to occur within **ninety (90) days** after the Effective Date. Delays caused by Client’s failure to provide timely information, approvals, or access shall extend the Implementation Completion Date on a day-for-day basis.

2.3.3 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed the Service Levels in Article XII, including targeted **99.9% monthly uptime** for production systems, excluding scheduled maintenance and permitted downtime. Vendor shall proactively monitor system performance, provide notification of outages or material degradations, and promptly remediate issues in accordance with the response times specified in Article XII.

2.3.4 Vendor shall process data in accordance with processing instructions provided by Client in this Agreement, the Master Agreement, and in any documented instructions that are reasonably and lawfully issued by Client from time to time. Vendor shall promptly inform Client if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes Applicable Data Protection Laws.

### 2.4 Location of Services and Data Hosting

2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Services primarily from facilities located in the **United States**, unless otherwise agreed in writing. Client acknowledges that Vendor may utilize data centers operated by reputable third-party hosting providers (e.g., cloud infrastructure providers) acting as Subprocessors.

2.4.2 Vendor shall not transfer Personal Data outside of the United States (including to any country outside the jurisdiction where the data was originally collected) without Client’s prior written authorization and without ensuring appropriate safeguards, such as standard contractual clauses or other transfer mechanisms recognized under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

### 2.5 Use of Subprocessors

2.5.1 Client grants Vendor a general authorization to engage Subprocessors for the provision of the Services, subject to Vendor’s obligations in Article XI and Article VIII. Vendor shall ensure that any Subprocessor is bound by written terms that are no less protective of Personal Data than those set forth in this Agreement.

2.5.2 Vendor shall maintain an up-to-date list of Subprocessors involved in the Processing of Personal Data on behalf of Client and shall make such list available to Client upon request. Client may reasonably object, on privacy or data security grounds, to any new Subprocessor by providing written notice within **fifteen (15) days** of receiving notice of such Subprocessor. In such event, the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the objection, which may include Vendor proposing alternative solutions or Subprocessors.

### 2.6 Restrictions on Vendor’s Use of Personal Data

2.6.1 Vendor shall Process Personal Data solely for the purpose of providing the Services and Deliverables to Client as described herein, and shall not:  
(a) sell or share Personal Data;  
(b) retain, use, or disclose Personal Data for any purpose other than for the specific purpose of performing the Services, including for Vendor’s own commercial purposes; or  
(c) retain, use, or disclose Personal Data outside the direct business relationship between Vendor and Client.

2.6.2 Vendor certifies that it understands and shall comply with the restrictions set forth in Section 2.6.1 in its capacity as a “service provider” under the CCPA.

### 2.7 Client Responsibilities

2.7.1 Client shall provide Vendor with timely access to accurate and complete data, information, systems, and personnel required for the performance of the Services.

2.7.2 Client shall be responsible for obtaining any consents, permissions, or authorizations from Data Subjects or third parties as may be required for Vendor to lawfully Process Personal Data under this Agreement.

2.7.3 Client shall promptly notify Vendor of any changes in Applicable Data Protection Laws or any regulatory requirements that materially impact Vendor’s Processing activities and shall cooperate with Vendor to implement any required changes to the Services.

3.3.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of:  
(a) one percent (1.0%) per month, or  
(b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.4.2 Client shall pay all undisputed portions of an invoice in accordance with Section 3.2.1, and the payment due date for any disputed portion shall be tolled until the dispute is resolved. No interest shall accrue on disputed amounts while the dispute is being resolved in good faith.

3.5.1 Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, or other similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes (excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income) that are legally imposed in connection with the Services, provided that Vendor shall itemize any such taxes on the applicable invoice.

### 3.6 Expenses

3.6.1 Out-of-pocket expenses (e.g., travel, lodging, meals) reasonably incurred by Vendor in the performance of onsite services shall be reimbursable by Client, provided that:  
(a) such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Client; and  
(b) Vendor provides reasonable supporting documentation (e.g., receipts).

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, remain in full force and effect until **December 06, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”).

4.1.2 Upon mutual written agreement of the Parties, this Agreement may be renewed for additional periods, each not to exceed two (2) years, under then-current terms or as otherwise agreed in writing.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience at any time by providing Vendor with at least **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice**.

4.2.2 Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience only upon at least **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Client, and only after the initial twelve (12) months of the Term, in order to minimize disruption to Client’s business.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.3.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, Client shall have the right to terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:  
(a) Vendor suffers a Data Breach that materially impacts Client’s Personal Data and fails to implement reasonable remedial measures in a timely manner; or  
(b) Vendor violates Applicable Data Protection Laws in a manner that materially adversely affects Client.

### 4.4 Insolvency

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
(a) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they become due;  
(b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy; or  
(c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or has a receiver, trustee, or similar officer appointed over all or substantially all of its assets.

### 4.5 Effects of Termination

4.5.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Vendor shall promptly cease Processing Personal Data on behalf of Client, except to the extent necessary to perform any post-termination obligations;  
(b) Vendor shall, at Client’s option and in accordance with Section 11.7, return or securely delete all Personal Data and Client Confidential Information;  
(c) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed fees and expenses accrued and payable through the effective date of termination; and  
(d) any licenses granted to Client in respect of Deliverables under Article VI shall survive, unless expressly stated otherwise.

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or obligations which have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including without limitation payment obligations and indemnification obligations.

4.5.3 Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI, and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 “Confidential Information” means any non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (the “Receiving Party”), whether orally or in writing, and whether or not marked or designated as confidential, including, without limitation: business plans, financial information, technical data, product roadmaps, trade secrets, security controls, customer lists, and Personal Data.

### 5.2 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use Confidential Information solely to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;  
(b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted hereunder; and  
(c) protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, advisors, and Affiliates who have a need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein.

### 5.3 Exceptions

5.3.1 Confidential Information shall not include any information that:  
(a) is or becomes generally known to the public without breach of this Agreement;  
(b) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;  
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
(d) was already known to the Receiving Party free of any confidentiality obligation at the time of disclosure.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure

5.4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permitted) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. The Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

### 5.5 Survival

5.5.1 The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations relating to trade secrets and Personal Data shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret or Personal Data under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

## ARTICLE XI  
## DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

### 11.1 Roles of the Parties

11.1.1 For purposes of Applicable Data Protection Laws, Client is the Controller (or “business” under CCPA) and Vendor is the Processor (or “service provider” under CCPA) with respect to the Personal Data Processed under this Agreement.

### 11.2 Compliance with Data Protection Laws

11.2.1 Vendor shall comply with all Applicable Data Protection Laws in connection with its Processing of Personal Data, including implementing data subject rights requests and assisting Client with impact assessments as reasonably requested.

### 11.3 Data Security Measures

11.3.1 Vendor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access. Such measures shall include, at a minimum:  
(a) encryption of Personal Data at rest and in transit using industry-standard encryption protocols;  
(b) logical access controls, including unique user IDs, strong passwords, and role-based access;  
(c) regular vulnerability assessments and security patching;  
(d) monitoring and logging of access to Personal Data;  
(e) data backup and disaster recovery procedures; and  
(f) employee training regarding data protection and security.

### 11.4 Subprocessing

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

12.1.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment for the Services is available **99.9% of the time**, measured on a calendar-month basis, excluding scheduled maintenance and permitted downtime.

### 12.2 Scheduled Maintenance

12.2.1 Vendor may schedule maintenance windows for upgrades, patches, and other necessary activities, and shall provide Client with at least **five (5) Business Days’ prior notice** for any scheduled maintenance expected to cause downtime. Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to schedule maintenance during off-peak hours.

### 12.3 Incident Response and Resolution Times

12.3.1 Vendor shall respond to and resolve incidents in accordance with the following guidelines:

- **Priority 1 (Critical)**: Production system down or severely impacted.  
  - Response time: within 1 hour  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Priority 2 (High)**: Major functionality impaired, significant business impact.  
  - Response time: within 4 hours  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 1 Business Day  

- **Priority 3 (Medium)**: Moderate impact, partial loss of functionality.  
  - Response time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low)**: Minor issues or general inquiries.  
  - Response time: within 3 Business Days  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 10 Business Days  

### 12.4 SLA Remedies

12.4.1 If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment or response times set forth in this Article XII, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows (client-friendly):

- If monthly uptime falls below 99.9% but is at least 99.5%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly Recurring Service Fees.  
- If monthly uptime falls below 99.5% but is at least 99.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly Recurring Service Fees.  
- If monthly uptime falls below 99.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly Recurring Service Fees.

12.4.2 Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice(s) issued by Vendor. Service credits are Client’s exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures; however, repeated or material SLA failures may constitute a material breach under Section 4.3.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party in writing upon becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event on its performance.

### 15.3 Suspension and Termination

15.3.1 Performance of the affected obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. If the Force Majeure Event continues for more than **sixty (60) consecutive days**, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice, without liability, except for obligations accrued prior to the date of termination.
