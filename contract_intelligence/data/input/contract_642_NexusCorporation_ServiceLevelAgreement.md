## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide to Client a suite of managed IT services, cloud hosting, infrastructure management, application maintenance, monitoring, incident management, and related professional services (collectively, the **“Services”**). The Services are intended to support Client’s production and non-production environments used for internal operations and external customer-facing applications.

2.2 **Core Hosting and Infrastructure Services.**  
Vendor shall:

2.2.1 Provision and manage virtualized server resources, including compute, storage, and network capabilities, sufficient to support Client’s workloads as specified in the applicable SOW;

2.2.2 Provide Operating System (OS) installation, configuration, patching, and maintenance for all servers directly managed by Vendor for Client;

2.2.3 Implement and maintain network connectivity, including firewalls, load balancers, VPN gateways, and other necessary network components, to ensure secure and reliable access to Client’s applications and data;

2.2.4 Provide data backup services, including daily incremental backups and weekly full backups, with a minimum retention period of thirty (30) days unless otherwise specified in an SOW;

2.2.5 Implement standard security controls, including access controls, encryption in transit (e.g., TLS) and at rest where applicable, and antivirus/anti-malware solutions in accordance with industry best practices and Article XI.

2.3 **Application Support and Maintenance.**  
Vendor shall provide Level 1 and Level 2 support for designated Client applications as identified in the SOW, including:

2.3.1 Monitoring application availability and performance metrics, including response times and error rates;

2.3.2 Managing application deployments, configuration changes, and patch releases, subject to the change management procedures in Article XIII;

2.3.3 Performing root cause analysis for critical Incidents and recurring issues, and providing Client with written incident reports not later than five (5) Business Days following resolution of severity-1 Incidents;

2.3.4 Providing bug-fix support for application issues within Vendor’s control, and coordinating with third-party vendors for issues related to Third-Party Materials.

2.4 **Service Desk and Support.**  
Vendor shall provide a service desk (helpdesk) as the primary contact point for Client for all Incidents, requests, and inquiries related to the Services:

2.4.1 Support shall be available on a **24x7** basis for severity-1 and severity-2 Incidents, and during Business Hours (9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Singapore time, Monday through Friday) for lower severity tickets, unless otherwise agreed in an SOW;

2.4.2 Vendor shall maintain and provide to Client a ticketing system through which Client may log and track Incidents and service requests, and Vendor shall assign each Incident a unique identifier, priority, and severity level in accordance with Article XII;

2.4.3 Vendor shall provide periodic reporting (monthly or as otherwise agreed) detailing ticket volumes, response and resolution times, system availability, and any SLA breaches and Service Credits issued.

2.5 **Professional and Consulting Services.**  
Upon Client’s request and subject to execution of a mutually agreed SOW, Vendor may provide professional services such as system architecture design, performance tuning, migration assistance, and project-based work. Such professional services will be governed by this Agreement and the applicable SOW, including clearly defined Deliverables, milestones, and Fees.

2.6 **Service Performance Standards.**  
Vendor shall perform the Services:

2.6.1 In a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services;

2.6.2 In compliance with all Applicable Laws, including data protection and privacy laws as described in Article XI;

2.6.3 In compliance with Client’s reasonable written security, compliance, and governance policies provided to Vendor in advance, to the extent such policies are not inconsistent with this Agreement or Applicable Law;

2.6.4 Using personnel who are appropriately skilled and experienced for the tasks assigned.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services may depend on Client’s timely performance of its responsibilities, which shall include:

2.7.1 Providing Vendor with accurate and complete information regarding Client’s technical environment, application requirements, and service needs;

2.7.2 Providing Vendor with necessary access credentials, permissions, and network connectivity to enable Vendor to perform the Services;

2.7.3 Designating one or more primary points of contact (and backups) for communication with Vendor regarding the Services;

2.7.4 Performing Client-specific responsibilities identified in any SOW (e.g., user acceptance testing, business continuity plans, data validation);

2.7.5 Maintaining appropriate backup copies of Client Data where Client elects to maintain additional backups beyond those included in the Services.

2.8 **Deliverables.**  
Vendor shall deliver the Deliverables expressly identified in this Agreement or in any SOW, which may include:

2.8.1 System configuration documents, network diagrams, and infrastructure topology documents;

2.8.2 Operational runbooks, standard operating procedures (SOPs), escalation matrices, and incident response plans;

2.8.3 Monthly service reports and incident reports;

2.8.4 Project-specific deliverables such as migration plans, test plans, results of performance tests, and post-implementation review documents.

2.9 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall agree in each SOW on key dates, milestones, and timelines. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet such timelines and milestones, subject to:

2.9.1 Client’s timely provision of information, approvals, and resources;

2.9.2 Dependencies on third-party vendors or service providers not under Vendor’s control;

2.9.3 Force Majeure Events as set forth in Article XV.

Any delays attributable to Client or third parties shall extend the corresponding Vendor timelines on a day-for-day basis.

2.10 **Risk Level and Client-Friendly Posture.**  
Given that the Parties have designated the risk level of this engagement as “Low,” the Parties agree that this Agreement is structured to be reasonably client-friendly, including:

2.10.1 Reasonable termination rights in favor of Client as set forth in Article IV;

2.10.2 Reasonable caps and exclusions in Article IX favoring Client;

2.10.3 Balanced but protective provisions for Client in Articles V, VI, VIII, XI, and XII.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total estimated contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **One Million Two Hundred Ninety-Five Thousand Six Hundred Fifty-Five U.S. Dollars (US$1,295,655)** (the **“Contract Value”**), inclusive of all recurring and non-recurring Fees described in this Agreement and any initial SOW, but exclusive of any future SOWs or optional services not yet specified.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, the Fees shall consist of:

3.2.1 **Recurring Monthly Service Fees.** Fixed monthly Fees for hosting, infrastructure, monitoring, and standard support services;

3.2.2 **Usage-Based Fees.** Variable Fees based on usage (e.g., storage overages, additional bandwidth, or additional user licenses) as specified in an SOW;

3.2.3 **Professional Services Fees.** Time-and-materials or fixed-fee charges for project-based work, as agreed in the relevant SOW;

3.2.4 **Onboarding Fees.** One-time transition/onboarding fees, if any, as specified in an SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for recurring Fees and usage-based Fees, and either in advance or upon completion of milestones for professional services, as specified in the SOW;

3.3.2 All undisputed invoices shall be due and payable **Net 60** days from the date of invoice;

3.3.3 Client shall remit payment in U.S. Dollars (USD) via wire transfer or other mutually agreed method to the account designated by Vendor in writing.

3.4 **Late Payments.**  

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1.0%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until the date of payment;

3.4.2 Vendor shall provide Client with written notice of any past due amounts and a cure period of fifteen (15) Business Days before taking any action to suspend Services for non-payment;

3.4.3 Vendor shall not suspend Services for amounts that are under good faith dispute in accordance with Section 3.5.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall:

3.5.1 Notify Vendor in writing within thirty (30) days from the invoice date, specifying the amount in dispute and the reasons for such dispute;

3.5.2 Pay the undisputed portion by the due date;

3.5.3 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. If the dispute is resolved in Vendor’s favor, Client shall pay the disputed amount within thirty (30) days of resolution, together with any applicable interest as provided in Section 3.4.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
Unless expressly stated otherwise, Fees are exclusive of all taxes, duties, levies, or similar governmental assessments (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Client shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes imposed by any governmental authority with respect to the Services, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employees. If Vendor is required to pay any such Taxes on Client’s behalf, Client shall promptly reimburse Vendor upon presentation of appropriate documentation.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses (such as travel, lodging, and per diem) incurred by Vendor in connection with on-site services or agreed activities shall be reimbursed by Client at cost, provided that:

3.7.1 Vendor obtains Client’s written approval prior to incurring any material travel or related expenses;

3.7.2 Vendor provides proper receipts or documentation for reimbursable expenses;

3.7.3 Vendor complies with Client’s travel and expense policies made available in writing.

3.8 **Fee Adjustments.**  
For any renewal term (if agreed in writing), Vendor may propose adjustments to Fees by providing Client with at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice prior to the expiration of the then-current Term, provided that any such adjustments shall be reasonable, market-aligned, and subject to mutual agreement. ## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **February 20, 2026** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be renewed for additional periods as mutually agreed in writing by the Parties. Any renewal shall be documented by a written amendment or new agreement, signed by both Parties, specifying the renewal term and any adjustments to the Fees or other terms.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause, upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor;

4.3.2 Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client, subject to Vendor’s obligations under Section 4.7 (Transition Assistance).

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach, except that breaches involving non-payment shall have a fifteen (15) day cure period;

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) files for or is placed into bankruptcy or receivership, or (c) ceases to carry on business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Termination for Repeated SLA Failures.**  
Without limiting Client’s other rights or remedies, Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for cause upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if:

4.5.1 Vendor fails to meet the monthly uptime commitment in Article XII in any three (3) months (whether consecutive or not) in any rolling twelve (12) month period; or

4.5.2 Vendor incurs cumulative Service Credits exceeding ten percent (10%) of the annual Fees in any rolling twelve (12) month period.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement:

4.6.1 All rights and licenses granted hereunder shall cease, except as expressly provided otherwise;

4.6.2 Client shall pay to Vendor all undisputed Fees and approved expenses accrued and unpaid up to the effective date of termination, along with any agreed termination assistance Fees for services rendered under Section 4.7;

4.6.3 Vendor shall promptly return or, at Client’s written election, securely destroy all Client Data and Confidential Information in accordance with Article XI and Article V, subject to any mandatory legal retention requirements;

4.6.4 Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s tangible property and materials in its possession.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  

4.7.1 For a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration (or such shorter period as Client may designate), Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance to enable an orderly transfer of the Services to Client or to a new service provider designated by Client;

4.7.2 Such transition assistance may include providing data exports in a mutually agreed format, technical documentation, and cooperation with Client’s personnel and third-party providers;

4.7.3 Transition assistance shall be provided at Vendor’s then-current time-and-materials rates, unless termination is due to Vendor’s uncured material breach, in which case reasonable, standard transition assistance for up to thirty (30) days shall be provided at no additional charge.

4.8 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination or expiration (including but not limited to Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XVI, and Section 4.7) shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environments for the Services have a **Monthly Uptime Percentage** of at least **99.9%** (the **“Uptime Commitment”**), excluding downtime caused by:

12.1.1 Scheduled Maintenance Windows, not to exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month, with at least seventy-two (72) hours prior notice to Client;

12.1.2 Force Majeure Events in accordance with Article XV;

12.1.3 Outages attributable to Client’s acts or omissions, including misuse of the Services, unauthorized changes by Client, or failures in Client-controlled systems;

12.1.4 Failures of the public internet or third-party networks not under Vendor’s control.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Vendor shall adhere to the following response and initial resolution targets for Incidents:

- **Severity 1 (Critical):**  
  - Definition: Complete loss of production service or severe business impact with no workaround.  
  - Response Time: within 30 minutes (24x7).  
  - Initial Resolution / Workaround Target: within 4 hours.

- **Severity 2 (High):**  
  - Definition: Significant degradation affecting critical functionality with limited workaround.  
  - Response Time: within 1 hour (24x7).  
  - Initial Resolution / Workaround Target: within 8 hours.

- **Severity 3 (Medium):**  
  - Definition: Moderate impact, partial loss of non-critical functionality.  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours.  
  - Initial Resolution / Workaround Target: within 3 Business Days.

- **Severity 4 (Low):**  
  - Definition: Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries.  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day.  
  - Initial Resolution / Workaround Target: next planned maintenance or release.

12.3 **Service Credits for Uptime Failures.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in a given calendar month, Client shall be entitled to Service Credits as follows:

- Monthly Uptime 99.0% to <99.9%: **5%** of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services;  
- Monthly Uptime 97.0% to <99.0%: **10%** of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services;  
- Monthly Uptime <97.0%: **20%** of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services.

12.4 **Service Credits for Response Time Failures.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the response time targets for more than three (3) Severity 1 Incidents in any calendar quarter, Client shall be entitled to an additional Service Credit of **5%** of the monthly recurring Fees for the affected Services for that quarter.

12.5 **Conditions for Service Credits.**  

Service Credits:

12.5.1 Shall be Client’s sole monetary remedy for SLA breaches, subject to Client’s termination rights in Section 4.5 and Vendor’s overall liability cap in Article IX;

12.5.2 Shall be applied against future invoices and may not be converted into cash, unless there are no further invoices due, in which case Vendor shall refund the outstanding credit amount;

12.5.3 Must be requested by Client in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month or quarter in which the SLA breach occurred, including reasonable supporting information.

12.6 **Service Reporting.**  
Vendor shall provide monthly reports to Client detailing:

12.6.1 Uptime statistics and any incidents of unplanned downtime;

12.6.2 A summary of Incidents by severity, response times, and resolution times;

12.6.3 Any Service Credits earned by Client for the reporting period.
