## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK  

2.1 **Overview of Services.** Nova shall provide to Contoso a suite of technology integration, software customization, and managed support services designed to support Contoso’s enterprise operations, including but not limited to: (a) assessment and design of system architectures; (b) development and configuration of custom software components; (c) integration of Contoso’s existing enterprise systems with third‑party platforms; (d) implementation of monitoring and reporting tools; and (e) ongoing maintenance and support.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  

2.2.1 The Parties anticipate that the Services will be further described in one or more SOWs referencing this Agreement. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify: (a) a description of the Services and Deliverables; (b) applicable milestones and timelines; (c) acceptance criteria; (d) applicable Service Levels (if any, in addition to those in **Article XII**); (e) fees and payment schedule; and (f) any project‑specific assumptions or dependencies.  

2.2.2 In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern, except where the SOW explicitly states that it is intended to override specific provisions of this Agreement, in which case such SOW provisions shall apply solely for the purposes of that SOW.

2.3 **Detailed Description of Services.**  

2.3.1 **Discovery and Requirements Analysis.**  
Nova shall conduct an initial discovery phase, which may include stakeholder interviews, review of existing documentation, system audits, and workshops with Contoso’s designated personnel. Nova shall:  
(a) analyze Contoso’s current architecture, workflows, data flows, and security posture;  
(b) document business requirements, technical requirements, and constraints; and  
(c) prepare a written Requirements and Solution Design Document (“**RSDD**”) for review and approval by Contoso.  

Contoso shall provide timely access to knowledgeable personnel, systems, and documentation as reasonably requested by Nova. Delays in Contoso’s cooperation may impact the timelines set forth in the applicable SOW.

2.3.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.**  
Based on the approved RSDD, Nova shall design a solution architecture that may include:  
(a) integration of Contoso’s internal systems (e.g., ERP, CRM, data warehouse) via APIs and middleware;  
(b) implementation of secure data exchange mechanisms;  
(c) design of dashboards and reporting tools for performance monitoring; and  
(d) definition of system configurations, roles, and access controls.  

Nova shall deliver a Solution Architecture Document (“**SAD**”), describing the proposed architecture, integration points, data models, security controls, and high‑level implementation plan. The SAD shall be subject to Contoso’s written approval (not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed). Any material deviations from the agreed architecture must be processed via **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.3.3 **Development, Configuration, and Integration.**  
Nova shall perform software development, configuration, and integration activities as specified in the applicable SOW, which may include:  
(a) developing custom modules, scripts, or connectors;  
(b) configuring off‑the‑shelf or third‑party applications to match Contoso’s requirements;  
(c) integrating Contoso’s systems with cloud‑based services or external APIs;  
(d) implementing data transformations, mapping, and validation logic; and  
(e) setting up test environments and performing unit and integration tests.  

Nova shall adhere to industry‑standard development methodologies and coding practices, including version control, documentation, and peer reviews, as appropriate. Nova shall also comply with any reasonable Contoso coding standards or security guidelines provided in advance in writing.

2.3.4 **Testing and User Acceptance.**  
Nova shall conduct system testing, integration testing, and performance testing in accordance with test plans agreed upon by the Parties. Following Nova’s internal testing, Nova shall support Contoso in conducting user acceptance testing (“**UAT**”). The Parties shall agree upon UAT acceptance criteria in the relevant SOW or in the SAD.  

Contoso shall, within the period specified in the applicable SOW (or, if not specified, within ten (10) Business Days) after receipt of a Deliverable, review and either: (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide written notice of any material deficiencies or non‑conformities with the applicable specifications. Nova shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such deficiencies within a mutually agreed timeframe and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance. Acceptance shall be deemed to occur upon the earlier of (i) Contoso’s written acceptance, or (ii) Contoso’s failure to provide written rejection within the applicable review period.

2.3.5 **Deployment and Go‑Live Support.**  
Upon acceptance of the relevant Deliverables, Nova shall assist Contoso with deployment to production environments, including:  
(a) preparation of deployment plans and rollback procedures;  
(b) execution of deployment activities during scheduled maintenance windows;  
(c) validation of system performance post‑deployment; and  
(d) availability of Nova personnel for a mutually agreed “hypercare” period to address any critical issues arising immediately after go‑live.  

2.3.6 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
Nova shall provide training sessions for Contoso’s technical and business users as reasonably required to enable effective use and administration of the Deliverables. Such training may include: on‑site sessions, virtual sessions, user manuals, technical documentation, and recorded tutorials. Nova shall also conduct knowledge transfer workshops with Contoso’s designated technical staff prior to completion of major project phases, as specified in the applicable SOW.

2.3.7 **Ongoing Support and Maintenance.**  
During the Term, and subject to Contoso’s timely payment of all fees, Nova shall provide support and maintenance services as described in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** and the applicable SOW. Such services may include: incident management, bug fixes, minor enhancements (as agreed), performance monitoring, and periodic reporting. Nova shall maintain a helpdesk or support contact center available during the hours specified in the SOW, with defined response and resolution targets.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  

2.4.1 Nova shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications, and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices, as well as applicable laws and regulations.  

2.4.2 Nova shall devote such time and resources as are reasonably necessary to meet the milestones, delivery dates, and Service Levels specified in this Agreement and any SOW. Where delays are caused or contributed to by Contoso’s acts or omissions, the Parties shall, acting reasonably and in good faith, adjust the relevant timelines in writing.

2.5 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that Nova’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Contoso’s timely cooperation. Accordingly, Contoso shall:  
(a) designate a project manager and key stakeholders with authority to make decisions;  
(b) provide timely access to required systems, facilities, data, and personnel;  
(c) review and provide feedback on Deliverables, documentation, and Change Orders within a reasonable timeframe; and  
(d) fulfill any other responsibilities specified in this Agreement or the applicable SOW.  

Any failure by Contoso to perform its responsibilities that materially impacts Nova’s ability to meet agreed timelines shall be addressed through the change management process in **Article XIII**.

3.1 **Fees and Contract Value.**

3.2.1 Nova shall invoice Contoso in accordance with the billing schedule set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise specified, Nova may invoice: (a) up to thirty percent (30%) of the total SOW value upon SOW execution; (b) a further forty percent (40%) upon completion of key milestones; and (c) the remaining thirty percent (30%) upon final acceptance of the Deliverables under that SOW.  

3.2.2 Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty‑five (45) calendar days** from the date of receipt of a properly issued invoice (“**Net 45**”). Payment shall be made by bank transfer or other mutually agreed method to the account designated by Nova in writing.

The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve such dispute promptly. Contoso shall pay any undisputed portion of the invoice within the Net 45 period. Failure to provide timely notice of dispute shall be deemed acceptance of the invoice.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

3.4.1 Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.  

3.4.2 If any undisputed invoice remains unpaid more than thirty (30) days after the due date, Nova may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Services (in whole or in part) until all overdue amounts and applicable interest are paid, provided that such suspension shall not relieve Contoso of its payment obligations.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Contoso shall reimburse Nova for reasonable, pre‑approved, out‑of‑pocket expenses actually incurred in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and subsistence expenses, in accordance with Contoso’s then‑current travel and expense policies provided to Nova in advance. Nova shall provide appropriate receipts or documentation with any expense invoice. Expense reimbursement shall be subject to the same Net 45 terms.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value‑added, withholding, or similar taxes, duties, or levies (collectively, “**Taxes**”), except for taxes based on Nova’s net income. Contoso shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes arising from the provision of Services or Deliverables under this Agreement. If Contoso is required by applicable law to withhold Taxes from amounts payable to Nova, Contoso shall: (a) withhold and remit such Taxes to the appropriate tax authority; (b) pay to Nova the net amount after such withholding; and (c) provide Nova with appropriate documentation evidencing the remittance of such Taxes.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION  

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in full force and effect until **May 11, 2027** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for one or more additional periods under such terms and conditions as the Parties may negotiate in good faith, including any adjustments to fees, Service Levels, and other key provisions.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or with respect to any applicable SOW, for convenience by providing not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. For clarity, termination for convenience of one SOW shall not automatically terminate other SOWs or this Agreement unless expressly stated in such notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the nature of the breach.  

4.4.2 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they become due; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) has a receiver, trustee, or similar officer appointed for a substantial part of its property or business.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
(a) Contoso shall promptly pay Nova all fees and reimbursable expenses accrued and unpaid under this Agreement and all SOWs up to the effective date of termination, including any non‑cancelable commitments approved by Contoso;  
(b) Nova shall cease performance of the terminated Services and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate any further costs; and  
(c) subject to **Section 4.5.2**, each Party shall return or, at the other Party’s written election, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information, except as required to be retained by law or for record‑keeping purposes as set forth in **Section 5.4**.

4.5.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Contoso, Contoso shall pay Nova: (a) for all Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination; and (b) any reasonable and documented non‑recoverable costs or commitments incurred by Nova in reliance on this Agreement or the applicable SOW, provided such commitments were pre‑approved in writing by Contoso.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including without limitation **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure)**, and **XVI (General Provisions)**, and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination, shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except that the Receiving Party may retain: (a) one archival copy for legal or compliance purposes; and (b) any electronic copies stored in routine backup systems, which shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein until destroyed in the ordinary course.

5.6 **Survival Period.**  
The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

15.2 **Excused Performance.**  
Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) to the extent such failure or delay is caused by a Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party: (a) promptly notifies the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event; (b) uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the event; and (c) resumes performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected Services or SOW upon written notice to the other Party, without liability except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
