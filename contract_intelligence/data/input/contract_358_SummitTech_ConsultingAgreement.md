## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Service Provider.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Client hereby engages Service Provider, on a non-exclusive basis, to provide the Services, and Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement and agrees to diligently perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices for technology consulting services of similar scope and complexity.

2.2 **General Description of Services.**  
The Services to be provided by Service Provider may include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 Technology strategy and roadmap consulting, including assessment of Client’s current technology environment, application portfolio, infrastructure, and operational processes, and the development of strategic recommendations for modernization, optimization, and cost reduction.

  2.2.2 Systems architecture and design services, including the design of cloud-based architectures (e.g., on Microsoft Azure, AWS, Google Cloud), microservices architectures, data architecture, and integration frameworks for Client’s systems and applications.

  2.2.3 Implementation and integration services, including configuration, customization, and deployment of selected software platforms and tools; data migration; and integration with Client’s existing systems and third-party applications.

  2.2.4 Project and program management services, including the provision of a dedicated project manager, establishment of governance structures, risk and issue management, and reporting on project status, milestones, and deliverables.

  2.2.5 Quality assurance, testing, and validation services, including development and execution of test plans, test scripts, and acceptance criteria, as well as support for user acceptance testing (UAT) and performance testing.

  2.2.6 Training and knowledge transfer services, including development and delivery of training materials, workshops, and documentation to enable Client’s personnel to operate, maintain, and support the implemented systems and processes.

  2.2.7 Ongoing advisory and support services, which may include post-implementation support, optimization consulting, and periodic health checks or performance reviews, as further specified in applicable SOWs.

2.3 **Statements of Work.**  
The specific details of the Services, including scope, Deliverables, timelines, assumptions, dependencies, and pricing, shall be set forth in one or more SOWs executed by both Parties and made a part of this Agreement. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify:

  (a) a description of the Services and Deliverables;  
  (b) the Project Plan and key milestones;  
  (c) applicable Service Levels, if any;  
  (d) Fees and payment schedule;  
  (e) Client’s responsibilities and dependencies; and  
  (f) any additional terms applicable to that SOW.

In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and the terms of any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern unless the SOW expressly states the intent to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Service Provider shall:

  2.4.1 Perform the Services in a diligent, timely, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with this Agreement, the applicable SOW, and generally accepted industry standards;

  2.4.2 Use personnel who are suitably qualified, experienced, and skilled to perform the Services, and shall, upon Client’s reasonable request, replace any personnel who fail to meet reasonable performance or conduct standards;

  2.4.3 Comply with all Applicable Laws and Client’s written policies and procedures provided in advance, including those relating to security, data protection, and facility access;

  2.4.4 Cooperate reasonably and in good faith with Client’s personnel, third-party vendors, and other stakeholders as necessary to perform the Services.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client shall:

  2.5.1 Provide Service Provider with timely access to Client personnel, systems, facilities, and Client Materials as reasonably required for Service Provider to perform the Services;

  2.5.2 Review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within the timeframes set forth in the applicable SOW, and in any event not unreasonably delay or withhold approval;

  2.5.3 Perform any Client responsibilities and dependencies identified in an SOW, and acknowledge that Service Provider’s performance is contingent upon Client’s timely and proper fulfillment of its obligations.

2.6 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Each SOW may specify acceptance criteria for Deliverables. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW:

  2.6.1 On delivery of a Deliverable, Service Provider shall notify Client in writing (which may be via email) and provide Client with sufficient documentation to enable Client to verify whether the Deliverable meets the acceptance criteria.

  2.6.2 Client shall have a period of **fifteen (15) Business Days** (the “**Acceptance Period**”) from receipt of the Deliverable to review and either:  
  (a) provide written acceptance; or  
  (b) provide written notice of non-conformities detailing how the Deliverable fails to meet the acceptance criteria.

  2.6.3 If Client does not provide such notice during the Acceptance Period, the Deliverable will be deemed accepted as of the last day of the Acceptance Period.

  2.6.4 Upon receipt of a valid notice of non-conformity, Service Provider shall, at no additional cost, promptly correct the non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for re-testing and acceptance. The re-submitted Deliverable will be subject to a new Acceptance Period of ten (10) Business Days.

2.7 **Service Location and Tools.**  
Services may be performed at Client’s premises, Service Provider’s facilities, or remotely, as set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise agreed, Service Provider shall be responsible for providing its own tools, equipment, software development environments, and communication facilities necessary to perform the Services.

2.8 **No Exclusivity.**  
Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an exclusive relationship between the Parties. Client may engage other vendors to provide services similar to or competitive with the Services, and Service Provider may provide services to other clients, provided that Service Provider complies with its confidentiality and conflict of interest obligations under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The aggregate maximum contract value under this Agreement shall not exceed **US $4,504,734** (the “**Contract Value**”), inclusive of all Fees for Services and Deliverables under all SOWs but excluding reimbursable expenses, unless otherwise agreed in a written amendment pursuant to **Section 16.2 (Amendments)**.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees under this Agreement may include one or more of the following, as specified in each SOW:

  3.2.1 **Time and Materials (T&M) Fees** – calculated based on the actual hours worked by Service Provider’s personnel at the hourly or daily rates specified in the SOW.

  3.2.2 **Fixed-Price Fees** – a fixed fee for specified Deliverables or milestones, payable upon achievement of defined milestones or acceptance of Deliverables as set forth in the SOW.

  3.2.3 **Retainer Fees** – recurring fees for a specified allocation of Service Provider resources or advisory capacity over a defined period.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
Unless otherwise specified in the SOW:

  3.3.1 Service Provider shall invoice Client **monthly in arrears** for T&M Services and reimbursable expenses, and upon achievement of milestones or acceptance of Deliverables for fixed-price components.

  3.3.2 Each invoice shall include reasonable detail, including (as applicable) the SOW reference, description of Services performed, dates and hours worked by each resource, rates applied, milestone achievements, and itemized expenses.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
Subject to **Section 3.6 (Disputed Amounts)**, Client shall pay all undisputed amounts due under each invoice within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of Client’s receipt of such invoice (“**Net 60**”). Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed payment method.

3.5 **Expenses.**  

  3.5.1 Client shall reimburse Service Provider for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses actually incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and third-party fees, provided that such expenses are consistent with Client’s travel and expense policies provided in advance.

  3.5.2 Service Provider shall provide receipts or other appropriate documentation for all reimbursable expenses in accordance with Client’s policies.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.**  

  3.6.1 If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute and the amount in dispute.

  3.6.2 Client may withhold payment only for the disputed portion of the invoice and shall timely pay the undisputed portion in accordance with **Section 3.4**.

  3.6.3 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any such dispute promptly. Upon resolution, Client shall pay any amounts determined to be payable within fifteen (15) Business Days of such resolution.

3.7 **Late Payment.**  

  3.7.1 Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1.0%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, from the due date until the date of payment.

  3.7.2 Before suspending Services for non-payment, Service Provider shall provide Client with written notice and a cure period of at least fifteen (15) Business Days. Suspension shall not relieve Client of its obligation to pay overdue amounts.

3.8 **Taxes.**  

  3.8.1 The Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes (“**Taxes**”). Client shall be responsible for paying all Taxes arising from this Agreement, excluding taxes based on Service Provider’s income, property, or employment.

  3.8.2 If Service Provider is required to collect Taxes, such Taxes shall be separately stated on the invoice, and Client shall pay such Taxes unless Client provides a valid exemption certificate.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Initial Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until **January 12, 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Agreement may be renewed for additional terms only by mutual written agreement of the Parties executed no later than thirty (30) days prior to the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Client).**  
Given the low risk profile and to provide flexibility to Client, Client may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Service Provider.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience (Service Provider).**  
Service Provider may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Client, provided that Service Provider shall continue to perform all Services during such notice period and shall cooperate with Client to effect an orderly transition.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.5.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (a) materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail (or such longer cure period as is reasonably required, provided the breaching Party has commenced cure within such thirty (30)-day period and diligently pursues cure); or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.6.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:  
  (a) all SOWs shall automatically terminate, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing to the contrary;  
  (b) Client shall pay Service Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination, including any Fees that have accrued under milestone-based or fixed-fee arrangements in proportion to the work performed;  
  (c) Service Provider shall cease performing the Services (except as necessary for transition under **Section 4.6.2**) and shall promptly deliver to Client all completed and in-progress Deliverables for which Client has paid, and all Client Materials.

  4.6.2 At Client’s request, Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to sixty (60) days after termination (or such longer period as the Parties may agree), at Service Provider’s then current T&M rates, to facilitate the orderly transition of the Services to Client or a third party.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under **Sections 3.4 (Payment Terms)**, 3.7, 4.6, and **Articles V (Confidentiality)**, VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XII (Service Level Agreements, to the extent applicable), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions)** shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including acts of God; natural disasters; war; terrorism; civil unrest; strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving a Party’s own employees); epidemics or pandemics; governmental actions or orders; failures of telecommunications or internet services not caused by the Party; or shortages of materials or energy, in each case to the extent such event prevents, delays, or impairs the performance of the Party’s obligations under this Agreement.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, describing its nature, expected duration, and impact on performance; and  

  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such obligations are prevented or delayed by the Force Majeure Event. The affected Party shall not be liable for any failure or delay in performance caused by a Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects performance under this Agreement, either Party may terminate the affected SOW(s) or this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party, without liability (except for amounts accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
