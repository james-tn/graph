## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES/WORK

2.1 **Overview.** Vendor shall provide a combination of consulting, design, implementation, migration, integration, testing, knowledge transfer, and ongoing managed services to support Client’s modernization of its enterprise applications and infrastructure. The Services shall be rendered in a professional and workmanlike manner and in accordance with this Agreement, the Specifications, and industry best practices.

2.2 **Project Phases.** The Project shall be organized into the following phases, each containing specific Deliverables and Milestones:

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Assessment and Planning (Weeks 1–6).**  
  (a) **Current-State Assessment:** Vendor shall perform a comprehensive assessment of Client’s existing on-premises applications, databases, middleware, and infrastructure, including:  
  - Application inventory and dependency mapping;  
  - Network topology review;  
  - Security posture review;  
  - Performance and capacity assessment; and  
  - Identification of integration points and third-party systems.

  (b) **Deliverables – Phase 1:**  
  - Current-State Assessment Report;  
  - Risk and Gap Analysis Document;  
  - Preliminary Migration Strategy;  
  - High-Level Project Plan and Resource Plan.

  (c) **Milestone:** Completion and Client’s written acceptance (which may be by email) of the Phase 1 deliverables shall constitute **Milestone 1**.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Solution Design (Weeks 7–14).**  
  (a) **Target Architecture Design:** Vendor shall design the target cloud-enabled architecture, including application tiers, data layer, integration middleware, security controls, and monitoring capabilities.

  (b) **Integration and Data Migration Design:** Vendor shall define integration patterns, data migration strategy (including cut-over and rollback procedures), and alignment with Client’s regulatory and compliance obligations (including GDPR/CCPA where applicable).

  (c) **Deliverables – Phase 2:**  
  - Target Architecture Design Document;  
  - Integration Design Specifications;  
  - Data Migration Plan;  
  - Security and Compliance Design Document;  
  - Updated Detailed Project Plan.

  (d) **Milestone:** Delivery and Client’s acceptance of the Phase 2 deliverables shall constitute **Milestone 2**.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Implementation and Migration (Weeks 15–40).**  
  (a) **Build and Configuration:** Vendor shall build, configure, and/or customize the agreed cloud and on-premises components, including:  
  - Provisioning of cloud resources (in Client’s cloud accounts or as otherwise agreed);  
  - Configuration of virtual networks, storage, and computing resources;  
  - Implementation of security controls, identity and access management, and logging.

  (b) **Application and Data Migration:** Vendor shall perform iterative migrations of applications and data, including:  
  - Pilot migrations to non-production environments;  
  - Validation and performance tuning;  
  - Final production migration and cut-over, with minimal downtime as reasonably practicable.

  (c) **Testing:** Vendor shall conduct unit, integration, system, performance, and security testing in accordance with the testing plan agreed by the Parties. Client shall participate in user acceptance testing (“**UAT**”) and shall provide timely feedback.

  (d) **Deliverables – Phase 3:**  
  - Configured Environment(s) as per the Target Architecture;  
  - Migration Scripts and Configurations;  
  - Test Plans and Test Reports;  
  - UAT Support and Defect Resolution Logs.

  (e) **Milestones:**  
  - Completion of non-production environment migration and testing: **Milestone 3**;  
  - Completion of production environment migration and successful cut-over: **Milestone 4**.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Stabilization and Knowledge Transfer (Weeks 41–48).**  
  (a) **Stabilization:** Vendor shall provide hypercare support post go-live, including enhanced monitoring, incident triage, and performance tuning.

  (b) **Knowledge Transfer and Training:** Vendor shall deliver training sessions and documentation for Client’s technical and operations teams covering:  
  - System architecture and configuration;  
  - Operational procedures and run books;  
  - Incident management and escalation.

  (c) **Deliverables – Phase 4:**  
  - Operations Runbook;  
  - Knowledge Transfer Materials (presentations, recordings, manuals);  
  - Training Completion Report.

  (d) **Milestone:** Completion of stabilization period and acceptance of Phase 4 deliverables: **Milestone 5**.

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Managed Services (Months 12–13 of Term and through Expiration Date).**  
  (a) **Ongoing Support:** Vendor shall provide ongoing support and managed services for the migrated environment, including:  
  - 24x7 monitoring of production systems;  
  - Incident management and resolution per the Service Levels in **Article XII**;  
  - Routine maintenance, patching, and backup verification.

  (b) **Enhancement Requests:** Subject to Change Orders, Vendor shall implement minor enhancements and optimizations requested by Client.

  (c) **Deliverables – Phase 5:**  
  - Monthly Service Performance Reports;  
  - Incident and Problem Management Reports;  
  - Capacity and Performance Reports.

  (d) **Milestones:**  
  - Commencement of managed services: **Milestone 6**;  
  - Completion of managed services at Expiration Date: **Milestone 7**.

2.3 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

  2.3.1 In accordance with the Service Levels and performance metrics specified in **Article XII**;  
  2.3.2 With due skill, care, and diligence consistent with generally accepted industry standards for providers of similar services;  
  2.3.3 In compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including those referenced in **Article VII** and **Article XI**.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.** Client acknowledges that timely performance by Vendor may be dependent upon Client fulfilling its responsibilities, including:

  2.4.1 Providing Vendor with timely access to Client’s premises, systems, networks, documentation, and subject matter experts as reasonably required;  
  2.4.2 Reviewing and providing written feedback or approvals on Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of receipt, unless otherwise specified;  
  2.4.3 Obtaining and maintaining all necessary third-party licenses, consents, and approvals for systems not provided by Vendor that are required for Vendor to perform the Services.

2.5 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**

  2.5.1 **Delivery and Review.** Upon delivery of a Deliverable, Vendor shall notify Client in writing. Client shall have ten (10) Business Days (“**Review Period**”) to review and either:  
  (a) provide written notice of acceptance; or  
  (b) provide written notice of rejection, specifying in reasonable detail the non-conformities with the Specifications.

  2.5.2 **Deemed Acceptance.** If Client fails to provide any written notice within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted, provided that Vendor has expressly notified Client that the Review Period has commenced.

  2.5.3 **Remedy of Non-Conformities.** If Client rejects a Deliverable, Vendor shall promptly correct the identified non-conformities at no additional charge and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance in accordance with this Section 2.5.

2.6 **Subcontractors.** Vendor may use subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that:

  2.6.1 Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors; and  
  2.6.2 Any subcontractor with access to Client Data or Confidential Information is bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less stringent than those set forth in this Agreement.

2.7 **Project Governance.** The Parties shall establish a project governance framework, including:

  2.7.1 A steering committee comprised of representatives of both Parties that shall meet at least monthly to review status, risks, issues, and upcoming milestones;  
  2.7.2 Designation of a Project Manager for each Party, responsible for day-to-day coordination and communication;

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.** For managed services under Phase 5, Vendor shall ensure that the production environment hosting the Deliverables achieves a monthly availability of at least **99.9%** (“**Uptime Commitment**”), excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Vendor may schedule maintenance windows during which the services may be unavailable, provided that:

  12.2.1 Vendor provides at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice;  
  12.2.2 Scheduled maintenance occurs outside of Client’s core business hours where reasonably practicable; and  
  12.2.3 Total scheduled maintenance time shall not exceed eight (8) hours per month.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution.** Vendor shall adhere to the following incident management SLAs:

  12.3.1 **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of a production system or severe impact on a mission-critical function:  
  - Response time: within 1 hour;  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours.

  12.3.2 **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation of performance or functionality with no reasonable workaround:  
  - Response time: within 2 hours;  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 8 hours.

  12.3.3 **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Moderate impact on non-critical functionality or performance:  
  - Response time: within 4 Business Hours;  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within 3 Business Days.

  12.3.4 **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries:  
  - Response time: within 1 Business Day;  
  - Target resolution: next scheduled release or maintenance cycle.

12.4 **Service Credits.** If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in a given calendar month, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  12.4.1 Uptime < 99.9% and ≥ 99.5%: Credit equal to 5% of the monthly managed services fees;  
  12.4.2 Uptime < 99.5% and ≥ 99.0%: Credit equal to 10% of the monthly managed services fees;  
  12.4.3 Uptime < 99.0%: Credit equal to 20% of the monthly managed services fees.

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice(s) and shall not exceed 20% of the monthly managed services fees for the applicable month. Service credits are Client’s sole monetary remedy for failure to meet the Uptime Commitment, without prejudice to Client’s rights to terminate under Section 4.5.

12.5 **Reporting.** Vendor shall provide monthly reports detailing Service availability, incidents, response and resolution times, and any SLA deviations.

12.6 **Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery.** Vendor shall maintain and periodically test a Business Continuity Plan and disaster recovery procedures that support the Uptime Commitment. Upon Client’s request, Vendor shall provide a high-level summary of its BCP and evidence of recent testing.
