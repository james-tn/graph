## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
The Service Provider shall design, develop, implement, deploy, and provide ongoing managed services for a global, cloud-based enterprise analytics and workflow Platform for the Company’s multi-regional operations. The Platform will integrate data from multiple internal and external systems, provide advanced reporting and analytics capabilities, enable configurable workflow automation, and support role-based access for users across multiple business units and geographic regions.

2.2 **Phased Approach.**  
The Services shall be performed in the following phases, each including specific Deliverables and Milestones, with further details to be set forth in Exhibit A (Project Plan) and Exhibit B (Deliverables & Milestones):

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (Estimated: 3 Months).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Conduct workshops, interviews, and data-gathering sessions with the Company’s stakeholders;
  - Document business requirements, technical requirements, integration points, data sources, and compliance constraints;
  - Produce a comprehensive **Requirements Specification Document** and **Solution Architecture Document**;
  - Identify Key Personnel, governance structure, communication plan, and risk register for the Project.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Design and Architecture (Estimated: 4 Months).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Develop detailed solution designs, including data model, integration architecture, user interface designs, security architecture, and workflow logic;
  - Provide a **High-Level Design (HLD)** and **Low-Level Design (LLD)** document;
  - Define non-functional requirements, including performance, scalability, availability, and disaster recovery;
  - Validate designs with the Company through formal design review sessions and sign-off.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Development and Integration (Estimated: 8 Months).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Develop and configure the Platform in accordance with the approved design documents;
  - Build integrations with the Company’s ERP systems, CRM, HR systems, and external data sources using secure APIs and ETL processes;
  - Implement role-based access control, audit logging, and data classification mechanisms;
  - Prepare developer, system, and user documentation, including **Technical Implementation Guides**.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Testing, User Acceptance Testing (UAT), and Deployment (Estimated: 5 Months).**  
  The Service Provider shall:
  - Develop and execute test plans, including unit, integration, performance, security, and regression tests;
  - Support the Company in executing UAT cycles, including defect triage and remediation;
  - Conduct load and performance testing to meet or exceed the Service Levels defined in Article XII;
  - Deploy the Platform into the production environment following a mutually agreed cutover plan;
  - Provide initial hypercare support for not less than ninety (90) days after go-live.

  2.2.5 **Phase 5 – Managed Support and Enhancements (Remainder of Term).**  
  Following production go-live, the Service Provider shall:
  - Provide Level 2 and Level 3 support for the Platform and related integrations;
  - Perform routine maintenance, patching, and upgrades;
  - Implement minor enhancements and continuous improvements as requested by the Company and approved via Change Orders;
  - Monitor system performance and availability and provide regular reports to the Company.

2.3 **Key Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Service Provider shall be responsible for providing at least the following Deliverables:

  (a) Requirements Specification Document;  
  (b) Solution Architecture Document;  
  (c) High-Level Design (HLD) and Low-Level Design (LLD);  
  (d) Data Model and Integration Specifications;  
  (e) Configured and Developed Platform Components (including workflows and dashboards);  
  (f) Integration Components and APIs;  
  (g) Security, Access Control, and Audit Configurations;  
  (h) Test Plans, Test Cases, and Test Reports;  
  (i) UAT Support Documentation and Defect Logs;  
  (j) Deployment and Cutover Runbooks;  
  (k) User Training Materials and Administrator Guides;  
  (l) Operational Runbooks and Support Procedures;  
  (m) Periodic Service Level Performance Reports.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall, within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, mutually agree in writing on a detailed project plan specifying Milestones, Deliverable due dates, dependencies, and resource allocations. Time is of the essence with respect to the Company’s strategic initiatives; however, the Parties acknowledge that timelines may be adjusted by mutual agreement via Change Order as described in Article XIII.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
The Service Provider shall:

  (a) perform the Services with due skill, care, and diligence, in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services and in accordance with the Specifications;

  (b) assign adequately qualified and trained personnel, including Key Personnel, to perform the Services, and ensure that such personnel maintain appropriate certifications and expertise;

  (c) comply with all Company policies and procedures reasonably communicated to the Service Provider in writing, including security, data protection, and code of conduct policies;

  (d) meet or exceed the Service Levels set forth in Article XII for the production environment, recognizing that failure to meet Service Levels may give rise to Service Level Credits and other remedies;

  (e) manage and mitigate project risks and issues, maintain accurate project documentation, and regularly communicate status, risks, and dependencies to the Company’s designated project manager.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.**  
The Company shall:

  (a) provide timely access to relevant systems, data, documentation, and personnel as reasonably required for the Service Provider to perform the Services;

  (b) designate a competent project manager authorized to make decisions and provide approvals on behalf of the Company;

  (c) review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within the timeframes specified in the project plan, failing which such Deliverables may be deemed accepted as set forth in the applicable acceptance procedures;

The Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, by providing not less than one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to the Service Provider. In the event of such termination for convenience:

  (a) the Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination;

The Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate to provide Transition Assistance as set forth in Section 4.6.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.5.1 **By Either Party.**  
  Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof (or within such longer cure period as may be agreed by the Parties in writing), provided that the notice of breach reasonably describes the nature of the breach.

  4.5.2 **Insolvency.**  
  Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent or generally unable to pay its debts as they become due; (b) files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (c) is placed under judicial management, receivership, or similar proceedings; or (d) ceases to carry on its business in the ordinary course.

4.6 **Effects of Termination; Transition Assistance.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) the Company shall pay the Service Provider all undisputed Fees and reimbursable expenses due and payable as of the effective date of termination;  

  (b) the Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the Services (except as necessary for Transition Assistance) and deliver to the Company any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which the Company has paid;

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Availability.**  
The Service Provider shall ensure that the production environment for the Platform is available not less than **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and approved downtime. Availability shall be measured based on the total minutes in a month minus the minutes of unplanned downtime, divided by the total minutes in the month.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Service Provider may schedule maintenance windows for upgrades, patches, and routine maintenance, provided that:

  (a) maintenance is, where feasible, performed during off-peak hours;  

  (b) the Service Provider provides at least seventy-two (72) hours’ prior notice to the Company;  

  (c) scheduled maintenance windows shall not exceed eight (8) hours per month, unless otherwise agreed.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Service Provider shall respond to and resolve incidents according to the following priority levels:

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or major business impact (e.g., Platform unavailable for all users).  
    - Initial response: within 30 minutes (24x7).  
    - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours.

  - **Priority 2 (High):** Major functionality impaired, significant impact, but core operations continue.  
    - Initial response: within 2 hours during Business Days.  
    - Target resolution or workaround: within 1 Business Day.

  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Minor functionality impaired, moderate impact, workaround available.  
    - Initial response: within 4 Business Hours.  
    - Target resolution or workaround: within 5 Business Days.

  - **Priority 4 (Low):** Cosmetic issues, non-critical enhancements, general queries.  
    - Initial response: within 1 Business Day.  
    - Target resolution: as agreed in backlog planning.

12.4 **Service Level Credits.**  
If the Service Provider fails to meet the availability SLA in Section 12.1 or the incident response and resolution targets in Section 12.3 in any calendar month, the Company shall be entitled to service level credits ("**Service Credits**") as follows:

  (a) For each 0.1% below 99.9% monthly availability (e.g., 99.8%, 99.7%, etc.), a credit equal to 2% of the monthly Managed Services Fees;  

  (b) For recurrent failure (three (3) consecutive months) to meet Priority 1 incident resolution targets, an additional one-time credit of 10% of the monthly Managed Services Fees;  

  (c) The total Service Credits in any calendar month shall not exceed 30% of the monthly Managed Services Fees.

Service Credits shall be applied as a credit against future invoices. Service Credits are the Company’s primary monetary remedy for Service Level failures, without prejudice to other contractual remedies.

  (b) it uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable;
