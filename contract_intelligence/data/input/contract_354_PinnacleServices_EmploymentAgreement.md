## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Service Provider shall provide to Client professional employment and staffing services, including but not limited to: recruitment, screening, selection, hiring or engagement, onboarding, HR administration, payroll processing (if applicable), benefits administration support (if applicable), performance monitoring, and offboarding of Assigned Personnel, as more particularly described in this Article II and in any SOW entered into by the Parties.

2.2 **Recruitment and Staffing Services.**  
(a) Service Provider shall, upon receipt of a written requisition from Client specifying job description, skill requirements, experience level, compensation range, work location, and other relevant criteria, use commercially reasonable efforts to source, recruit, and present qualified Candidates to Client.  

(b) Service Provider shall conduct initial screening, including résumé review, preliminary interviews (telephonic or virtual), verification of qualifications, and such background checks as may be mutually agreed upon and compliant with applicable law.  

(c) Service Provider shall present to Client, within a mutually agreed timeframe (typically within five (5) to ten (10) Business Days from requisition), a shortlist of Candidates that meet the criteria specified by Client, together with candidate profiles, resumes, and rate or salary expectations.  

(d) Client shall have the sole discretion to interview, approve, reject, or request additional Candidates. Client’s approval of a Candidate shall be made in writing (including email) and shall authorize Service Provider to proceed with hiring or engaging such Candidate as Assigned Personnel.

2.3 **Onboarding and Assignment.**  
(a) Following Client’s approval of a Candidate, Service Provider shall, as applicable:  
   (i) extend an offer of employment or engagement to the Candidate on terms materially consistent with Client’s requirements;  
   (ii) manage the Candidate’s onboarding process, including completion of required forms (e.g., I‑9, tax forms), verification of eligibility to work, and collection of any necessary documentation; and  
   (iii) coordinate with Client regarding start date, work location, reporting manager, and initial assignment details.  

(b) Assigned Personnel shall perform services for Client under the direction of Client’s designated managers or supervisors, within the scope of responsibilities described in the applicable requisition or SOW. The Parties acknowledge that while Assigned Personnel may be employees of Service Provider, Client shall retain the right to direct and control the day-to-day activities of Assigned Personnel with respect to the work performed for Client.

2.4 **HR Administration and Payroll (if applicable).**  
(a) Except as otherwise stated in a specific SOW, Service Provider shall be the employer of record for Assigned Personnel and shall:  
   (i) process payroll for Assigned Personnel, including withholdings for taxes and other statutory deductions;  
   (ii) be responsible for the payment of wages, employer payroll taxes, and any mandatory benefits as required by applicable law; and  
   (iii) maintain all employment records required by law for Assigned Personnel employed by Service Provider.  

(b) Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Client shall not be responsible for payment of wages, payroll taxes, or statutory benefits directly to Assigned Personnel employed by Service Provider; instead, Client shall pay Service Provider for Services rendered by Assigned Personnel as invoiced under Article III.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Service Provider shall perform the Services (including the provision of Assigned Personnel) in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with (i) generally accepted industry standards for staffing and employment services; (ii) applicable laws and regulations; (iii) Client’s written policies and procedures provided in advance to Service Provider or Assigned Personnel (including workplace safety and security policies); and (iv) the Service Levels set forth in Article XII and any applicable SOW.

2.6 **Timelines and Service Levels.**  
(a) Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the timelines for candidate submission, onboarding, and assignment commencement set forth in the applicable requisition or SOW.  

(b) Specific Service Levels, including candidate response times, time-to-fill metrics, escalation procedures, and quality targets, shall be set forth in Article XII and any applicable SOW.  

(c) Repeated failure by Service Provider to meet agreed Service Levels may constitute a material breach of this Agreement, subject to the cure provisions in Section 4.4.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  
Service Provider may not subcontract any material portion of the Services without the prior written consent of Client, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld. Where subcontractors are approved, Service Provider shall remain fully responsible and liable for all acts and omissions of such subcontractors as if they were acts and omissions of Service Provider.

2.8 **Exclusions.**  
Unless explicitly stated in this Agreement or in a SOW:  
(a) Service Provider shall not provide legal advice, tax advisory services, or benefits design services to Client; and  
(b) Service Provider shall not be responsible for managing Client’s internal employees who are not Assigned Personnel.

2.9 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) From time to time, the Parties may enter into SOWs that reference this Agreement. Each SOW shall describe: (i) the specific Services and Deliverables; (ii) timelines and milestones; (iii) Service Levels; (iv) fees and payment terms; and (v) any other project-specific terms.  

(b) In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and those of an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control, except where the SOW expressly states the Parties’ intent to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.10 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Client shall:  
(a) provide Service Provider with accurate and complete job descriptions, requirements, and other information reasonably necessary for Service Provider to perform the Services;  
(b) provide Assigned Personnel with access to facilities, systems, tools, and information necessary to perform their duties, subject to Client’s security policies;  
(c) ensure a safe working environment for Assigned Personnel while on Client’s premises; and  
(d) promptly notify Service Provider of any performance or conduct issues relating to Assigned Personnel.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The aggregate value of this Agreement, including all Services to be performed during the Term, is **One Million Seven Hundred Forty-Four Thousand Five Dollars (USD $1,744,005)** (the **“Contract Value”**), subject to adjustments based on mutually executed SOWs and any approved change orders in accordance with Article XIII.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees under this Agreement may include one or more of the following components, as specified in the applicable SOW:  
(a) **Hourly or Daily Rates** for Assigned Personnel;  
(b) **Fixed Fees** for specific projects or engagements;  
(c) **Placement Fees** for direct-hire conversions; and  
(d) **Recurring Management Fees** for ongoing program management services.

3.3 **Sample Fee Schedule.**  
By way of illustration, and without limitation, the Parties anticipate the following fee categories (actual amounts to be set forth in each SOW):  
(a) **Professional-Level Assigned Personnel:** Hourly billing rates ranging from USD $80.00 to USD $175.00 per hour, depending on role and experience.  
(b) **Administrative-Level Assigned Personnel:** Hourly billing rates ranging from USD $35.00 to USD $65.00 per hour.  
(c) **Placement/Conversion Fees:** In the event Client elects to hire an Assigned Personnel as a direct employee, Service Provider may charge a one-time placement fee equal to a mutually agreed percentage of the employee’s first-year base salary, as set forth in the applicable SOW, subject to any minimum engagement period or conversion terms.  

3.4 **Invoices and Payment Terms.**  
(a) Service Provider shall invoice Client on a monthly basis, in arrears, for Services rendered during the preceding calendar month, unless otherwise stated in a SOW.  

(b) Invoices shall itemize Services provided, including: (i) names of Assigned Personnel; (ii) hours or days worked; (iii) applicable rates; (iv) any authorized expenses; and (v) any taxes or other charges permitted under this Agreement.  

(c) Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** from the date of receipt of a proper invoice (**“Net 30”** payment terms). Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed method.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the reasons for the dispute. The Parties shall use good-faith efforts to resolve the dispute promptly. Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice. Upon resolution, any amounts determined due shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days.

3.6 **Late Payments.**  
Any undisputed invoice amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate equal to the lesser of (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the date due until the date paid. Service Provider shall not suspend Services for non-payment without first providing written notice and a ten (10) Business Day cure period.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Service Provider shall be responsible for all ordinary business expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including local travel and administrative costs, unless otherwise specified in an SOW.  

(b) Client shall reimburse Service Provider for pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses that are reasonable, documented, and directly related to the performance of the Services, such as long-distance travel, lodging, and certain equipment costs.  

(c) Service Provider shall provide receipts or other documentation as reasonably requested by Client to substantiate reimbursable expenses.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
(a) Each Party shall be responsible for its own income, franchise, and similar taxes.  

(b) If applicable law requires Client to withhold taxes from payments to Service Provider, Client shall provide Service Provider with documentation of such withholding and cooperate reasonably to minimize such taxes, including providing any applicable tax forms or certificates.  

(c) The fees stated in this Agreement do not include sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. If any such taxes apply to the Services (excluding taxes on Service Provider’s income), Client shall be responsible for payment of such taxes or reimbursement to Service Provider.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **April 12, 2029** (the **“Expiration Date”**) (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, mutually agree in writing to renew or extend this Agreement for one or more additional periods on such terms as they may negotiate.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
In light of the low risk profile of this engagement, Client shall have the right to terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience, without cause, upon no less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Service Provider. Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon no less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party:  
(a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
(b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

4.5 **Termination of Specific SOWs.**  
Client may terminate an individual SOW for convenience upon written notice, subject to any specific notice periods or termination fees set forth in such SOW. Termination of a SOW shall not automatically terminate this Agreement, which shall remain in effect with respect to other SOWs.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:  
(a) Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the affected Services and shall take all reasonable steps to minimize any disruption to Client’s business.  

(b) Service Provider shall submit a final invoice for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination, and Client shall pay all undisputed amounts in accordance with Article III.  

(c) At Client’s request, Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate in the orderly transition of Services to Client or to a replacement service provider, including transfer of relevant documents, records, and information.  

(d) All rights and licenses granted to Client with respect to Deliverables and Work Product shall survive in accordance with Article VI and Article XVI.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance) (with respect to occurrences during the Term), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XVI (General Provisions), and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

(b) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;  
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
(d) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records.

5.2 **Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for purposes of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;

(b) to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that (to the extent legally permissible) the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.4 **Return or Destruction.**

5.5 **Duration.**  
The confidentiality obligations under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue during the Term and for a period of **five (5) years** after expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets shall be maintained as confidential for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition of Force Majeure.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, governmental actions, labor disputes (excluding those involving only the affected Party’s own employees), epidemic, pandemic, failure of utilities or telecommunications networks not under the control of the affected Party, or other events commonly recognized as force majeure events.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:  
(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature and expected duration of the event;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event; and  
(c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended during the period of the Force Majeure Event to the extent performance is prevented or delayed. The Parties shall cooperate to develop a contingency plan for resuming Services.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and substantially prevents performance of material obligations under this Agreement, either Party may terminate this Agreement, or the affected SOW, upon written notice to the other Party, without liability (except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
