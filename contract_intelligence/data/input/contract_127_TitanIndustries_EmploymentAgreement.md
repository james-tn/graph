## PREAMBLE AND RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company desires to obtain from the Service Provider certain professional services relating to workforce augmentation, including the provision, management, and oversight of personnel and associated employment‑type services for the benefit of the Company, on the terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement;

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Vendor shall provide the Company with professional, employment‑type services consisting primarily of (a) the provision of qualified personnel (“Assigned Personnel”) to perform services for the Company; (b) oversight, management, and administration of such Assigned Personnel; and (c) related consulting, reporting, and support activities, as further described in this Article II and any SOW. The Parties acknowledge that this is a business‑to‑business services arrangement, and nothing herein shall be construed as creating an employer‑employee relationship between the Company and Vendor’s Assigned Personnel.

2.2 **Assigned Personnel and Roles.**  
(a) Vendor shall identify and assign personnel with appropriate qualifications, skills, and experience to perform the Services, including but not limited to the following categories:  

(i) Technical staff (e.g., software engineers, systems analysts, QA professionals);  
(ii) Operational and administrative staff (e.g., project coordinators, administrative assistants);  
(iii) Project management staff (e.g., project managers, team leads); and  
(iv) Other specialized personnel as mutually agreed in an SOW.  

(b) Vendor shall remain the sole employer (or, as applicable, principal) of all Assigned Personnel and shall be responsible for all employment‑related obligations with respect to Assigned Personnel, including but not limited to wages, benefits, taxes, insurance, and compliance with employment laws, except as otherwise expressly stated in an SOW.  

(c) Vendor shall ensure that all Assigned Personnel comply with the Company’s reasonable workplace policies, codes of conduct, information security policies, and other policies provided in writing to Vendor, to the extent such policies are applicable to Assigned Personnel’s performance of the Services.

2.3 **Specific Services and Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services shall include:

(a) Workforce Planning and Onboarding. Vendor shall, in collaboration with the Company, plan and implement staffing solutions to meet the Company’s project and operational needs, including sourcing, screening, and onboarding of Assigned Personnel. Vendor shall ensure that Assigned Personnel are properly oriented to the Company’s work environment, project requirements, and applicable policies.  

(b) Day‑to‑Day Performance of Services. Assigned Personnel shall perform the tasks, duties, and responsibilities described in the applicable SOWs or role descriptions approved by the Company, including but not limited to:  

- Development, maintenance, and support of the Company’s technical systems, applications, and infrastructure;  
- Operational support, administrative assistance, and project coordination;  
- Analysis, documentation, and reporting related to the Company’s business operations; and  
- Any other professional services mutually agreed upon in writing by the Parties.  

(c) Deliverables. Vendor shall produce and deliver to the Company certain Deliverables as specified in the applicable SOWs. Such Deliverables may include, without limitation, project plans, technical specifications, software code, test plans and results, configuration documents, training materials, and periodic performance reports. Each Deliverable shall meet the acceptance criteria and delivery timelines specified in the applicable SOW.

(d) Management and Reporting. Vendor shall provide oversight, supervision, and coordination of Assigned Personnel. Vendor shall designate a project manager or engagement lead as the primary point of contact with the Company. Vendor shall provide periodic status reports, including but not limited to updates on staffing levels, progress against milestones, issues and risks, and key performance indicators. The frequency and format of such reports shall be as set forth in the applicable SOW or as mutually agreed by the Parties.

2.4 **Timelines and Performance Standards.**  
(a) Timelines. The Parties shall agree to detailed schedules and milestones in each SOW. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to comply with the timelines and milestones specified therein. Where Vendor reasonably anticipates any delay, Vendor shall promptly notify the Company in writing, stating the cause of the delay and proposed corrective actions.  

(b) Performance Standards. Vendor shall perform the Services in a timely, professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with (i) generally accepted industry standards; (ii) the Service Levels set forth in Article XII; (iii) the applicable SOW; and (iv) Applicable Law. Vendor shall ensure that Assigned Personnel possess the requisite skills and qualifications to perform their duties. Upon the Company’s reasonable request and where consistent with Applicable Law, Vendor shall provide high‑level profiles or resumes of Assigned Personnel.  

(c) Replacement of Personnel. If the Company reasonably determines that any Assigned Personnel materially fail to perform their duties, violate the Company’s policies, or otherwise adversely affect the Company’s operations, the Company may request Vendor, in writing, to remove such Assigned Personnel from the Services. Vendor shall promptly remove such individual and, where appropriate, provide a suitable replacement within a reasonable period, subject to availability and the Company’s reasonable approval.  

(d) Location of Services. Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Services may be performed at (i) the Company’s facilities, (ii) Vendor’s facilities, (iii) remote locations, or (iv) a combination thereof. Vendor shall ensure that, where Services are performed at the Company’s premises, Assigned Personnel comply with all security, safety, and access requirements.

2.5 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) The Parties may execute one or more SOWs referencing this Agreement. Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify: (i) a description of the Services and Deliverables; (ii) timelines and milestones; (iii) applicable Service Levels, if any; (iv) Fees and payment terms (subject to Article III); and (v) any other specific terms and conditions as agreed by the Parties.  

(b) In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement, in which case the SOW shall control solely with respect to the subject matter therein.

2.6 **No Employment Relationship with Company.**  
The Parties expressly agree that: (a) Vendor and its Assigned Personnel are independent contractors of the Company; (b) nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, or employer‑employee relationship between the Company and any Assigned Personnel; and (c) Assigned Personnel shall not be entitled to any benefits provided by the Company to its employees. Vendor shall be solely responsible for all matters relating to compensation, benefits, tax withholding, workers’ compensation, and other employment obligations regarding Assigned Personnel.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total aggregate value of this Agreement, inclusive of all Fees under all SOWs, shall not exceed **US $1,254,939** (the “Contract Value”) without a written amendment signed by both Parties.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees may be structured using one or more of the following models, as specified in each SOW:  

(a) **Time and Materials (T&M).** Hourly or daily rates for Assigned Personnel, multiplied by actual hours or days worked. The SOW shall specify the applicable rates for each role (e.g., software engineer, project manager).  

(b) **Fixed Fee.** A fixed amount for specified Deliverables or defined phases of work, payable upon completion of milestones or acceptance of Deliverables, as set forth in the SOW.  

(c) **Blended or Retainer Model.** A flat monthly or periodic fee for a defined scope of Services, levels of effort, or number of Assigned Personnel.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
(a) Vendor shall invoice the Company in accordance with the invoicing schedule set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Vendor shall invoice monthly in arrears for T&M Fees and upon achievement of milestones for fixed‑fee components.  

(b) All undisputed invoices shall be payable **Net 30** days from the date of receipt by the Company.  

(c) Invoices shall be sent electronically to the Company’s designated accounts payable contact or portal, as notified in writing by the Company. Each invoice shall reference this Agreement, the relevant SOW, and the applicable purchase order number, if any, and shall provide reasonable detail to substantiate the Fees charged.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
If any undisputed amount is not paid by the Company within the applicable payment term, Vendor may charge interest on the overdue amount at a rate of the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the date such amount became due until the date of actual payment. Vendor’s right to charge interest shall be without prejudice to any other rights or remedies available to Vendor under this Agreement or at law.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Company shall: (a) notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute; and (b) pay all undisputed portions of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any such dispute promptly. Once resolved, any previously withheld amounts determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of resolution.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
(a) Out‑of‑Pocket Expenses. The Company shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable and pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, such as travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with the Company’s then‑current travel and expense policies, provided such policies are communicated to Vendor in advance.  

(b) Documentation. Vendor shall provide reasonable documentation (e.g., receipts, itineraries) to substantiate reimbursable expenses.  

(c) No Mark‑Up. Unless otherwise agreed in an SOW, reimbursable expenses shall be passed through at cost without markup.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) Fees are exclusive of all applicable sales, use, value‑added, goods and services, and similar taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority (collectively, “Taxes”). The Company shall be responsible for all such Taxes (excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employment‑related taxes) resulting from the Services or Deliverables.  

(b) If Vendor is required to collect Taxes from the Company, Vendor shall include such Taxes as a separate line item on the invoice, and the Company shall pay such Taxes in accordance with the payment terms herein.

3.8 **Set‑Off.**  
The Company may not set off or withhold any amounts owed to Vendor under this Agreement except for amounts disputed in good faith pursuant to Section 3.5, or as required by Applicable Law or a final court order.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **May 31, 2029** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “Term”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be renewed or extended upon mutual written agreement of the Parties executed prior to the Expiration Date. Unless and until such renewal or extension is executed, neither Party shall be obligated to renew or extend this Agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any or all SOWs), for convenience without cause by providing not less than **forty‑five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. The Parties acknowledge that this notice period represents a balanced approach consistent with the Medium risk level.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any affected SOW, effective upon written notice, if the other Party:  

(a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

(b) becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy or for reorganization, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver appointed for substantially all of its assets, and such petition or appointment is not dismissed or discharged within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination of SOWs Only.**  
Termination of an individual SOW shall not automatically terminate the Agreement, but termination of the Agreement shall, unless otherwise stated, automatically terminate all then‑active SOWs.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:  

(a) The Company shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and approved, and reimbursable expenses incurred, up to the effective date of termination, in accordance with Article III, subject to any bona fide disputes.  

(b) Each Party shall promptly return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession or control, subject to Section 5.4 regarding archival copies.  

(c) Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance, at then‑current or mutually agreed rates, for up to sixty (60) days following the effective date of termination, if requested by the Company, to transition the Services to the Company or a third‑party provider, provided the Company is not in material breach of its payment obligations.  

(d) All licenses, rights, and access granted by the Company to Vendor shall terminate, except to the extent reasonably necessary for Vendor to wind down or transition the Services.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Sections and provisions that by their nature should survive termination or expiration of this Agreement shall so survive, including without limitation Articles III (to the extent of unpaid Fees and expenses), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and this Section 4.7.

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics (including COVID‑19 and similar outbreaks), war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), government actions, or failures of telecommunications or internet service providers not caused by the affected Party.

15.2 **Notice.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence, nature, and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate its effects.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that such Force Majeure Event prevents or materially delays performance. The affected Party shall resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable after the Force Majeure Event ceases.

15.4 **Termination for Prolonged Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially prevents performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or, if the entire Agreement is affected, this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice.
