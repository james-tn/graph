## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.** Under this Amendment AMD-VAN-202404-087, Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of technology consulting, systems integration, and managed support Services in connection with Client’s enterprise resource planning (ERP) environment, customer relationship management (CRM) platform, and underlying infrastructure. The Services are intended to support Client’s business operations, enhance system reliability and performance, and facilitate ongoing digital transformation initiatives.

2.2 **Description of Services.** The Services to be provided by Vendor shall include, but are not limited to, the following:

2.2.1 **Assessment and Design.**  
(a) Conduct detailed business and technical assessments of Client’s existing ERP and CRM systems, including integrations, customizations, data flows, and security controls.  
(b) Prepare design documentation, including high-level and low-level architecture diagrams, interface specifications, mapping documents, and migration strategies.  
(c) Facilitate workshops with Client’s key stakeholders to validate requirements and obtain sign-off on the proposed solution design.  

2.2.2 **Implementation and Integration Services.**  
(a) Configure and customize the relevant software platforms in accordance with the approved Specifications.  
(b) Develop integrations between Client’s ERP, CRM, data warehouse, and ancillary systems using mutually agreed middleware or integration platforms.  
(c) Perform data migration activities, including data cleansing, transformation, validation, and load, in accordance with mutually agreed data quality standards.  
(d) Conduct functional, integration, security, and user acceptance testing (UAT) in collaboration with Client, and remedy defects identified during testing in a timely manner.  

2.2.3 **Managed Services and Support.**  
(a) Provide ongoing Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 support for the covered systems as set forth in Article XII, including incident management, problem management, and service request fulfillment.  
(b) Monitor system performance, availability, and capacity, and proactively address threshold breaches or impending issues.  
(c) Perform routine maintenance activities, including patching, updates, configuration management, and performance tuning, following mutually agreed maintenance windows.  
(d) Provide a helpdesk function accessible via telephone, email, and ticketing portal during agreed service hours, as detailed in Article XII.  

2.2.4 **Enhancements and Continuous Improvement.**  
(a) Implement minor enhancements, change requests, and optimizations approved by Client pursuant to Article XIII (Change Management).  
(b) Propose improvements to system usability, performance, and reliability on at least a quarterly basis, including recommendations for process automation and cost optimization.  
(c) Conduct periodic knowledge transfer sessions with Client’s internal team to ensure adequate knowledge retention and continuity of operations.  

2.2.5 **Security and Compliance Support.**  
(a) Assist Client in aligning the Services and Deliverables with applicable Data Protection Laws and Client’s internal security policies.  
(b) Implement agreed security controls including role-based access, encryption (in transit and at rest where applicable), and logging and monitoring mechanisms.  
(c) Participate in security reviews, audits, and risk assessments relating to the Services, and promptly remediate identified vulnerabilities within agreed timeframes commensurate with severity.  

2.3 **Deliverables.** Vendor shall provide the following core Deliverables, without limitation:

2.3.1 Detailed **Assessment Report** summarizing current state, findings, and recommendations.  
2.3.2 **Solution Design Documents**, including architecture, integration specifications, and data models.  
2.3.3 **Configuration and Customization Artifacts**, including source code, configuration files, scripts, and related technical documentation.  
2.3.4 **Testing Artefacts**, including test plans, test cases, test scripts, and test summary reports.  
2.3.5 **Operations Runbook**, including support procedures, escalation paths, maintenance schedules, and incident response guidelines.  
2.3.6 **User and Administrator Manuals**, including step-by-step guides for key processes.  
2.3.7 **Training Materials**, including slide decks, recorded sessions (if agreed), and reference documentation.  
2.3.8 Periodic **Service Performance Reports**, as further detailed in Article XII.  

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.** The Parties shall document specific project phases, timelines, and milestones in one or more SOWs referencing this Amendment. At a minimum:

2.4.1 An initial **Assessment and Design Phase** shall be completed within ninety (90) calendar days following the Effective Date, subject to Client’s timely cooperation and provision of necessary information and access.

2.4.2 An **Implementation and Integration Phase** shall commence upon Client’s sign-off of the Solution Design and shall be executed in mutually agreed sprints or work packages, each with clearly defined acceptance criteria.

2.4.3 A **Transition to Steady-State Support** shall occur upon completion of the Implementation Phase, at which time ongoing managed services shall be provided through the remainder of the Term.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Without limiting the Service Levels in Article XII, Vendor shall:

2.5.1 Perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner and in accordance with generally accepted industry standards, the Master Agreement, and this Amendment.

2.5.2 Ensure that its personnel assigned to the Services are suitably skilled, experienced, and qualified.

2.5.3 Use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed all timelines, milestones, and Service Levels agreed under this Amendment.

2.5.4 Maintain adequate project governance and reporting, including regular status meetings (at least monthly, or more frequently if agreed), risk and issue logs, and action item tracking.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.** Client shall:

2.6.1 Provide Vendor with timely access to relevant systems, facilities, documentation, and personnel, to the extent reasonably required for Vendor to perform the Services.

2.6.2 Designate a project manager or primary point of contact to coordinate with Vendor and facilitate approvals and decision-making.

2.6.3 Review and provide feedback on Deliverables within commercially reasonable timeframes, not to exceed ten (10) Business Days unless otherwise agreed.

2.6.4 Ensure that any third-party vendors or systems under Client’s control are reasonably available for integration and testing activities.

2.7 **Priority.** In the event of any conflict between this Article II and a specific SOW, the SOW shall prevail with respect to the Services and Deliverables described therein, unless expressly stated otherwise. In case of conflict between this Amendment and the Master Agreement, this Amendment shall prevail with respect to the subject matter described herein.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total not-to-exceed contract value for this Amendment AMD-VAN-202404-087 is **USD $2,678,117** (two million six hundred seventy-eight thousand one hundred seventeen United States dollars), inclusive of all Fees for Services and Deliverables described herein and in any SOW referencing this Amendment, but exclusive of any applicable taxes.

3.2 **Fee Structure.** The Fees shall be comprised of the following components:

3.2.1 **Fixed Project Fees.** Certain phases (e.g., Assessment and Design, Initial Implementation) shall be charged on a fixed-fee basis, with payment milestones tied to deliverable acceptance. The fixed project portion shall not exceed forty percent (40%) of the Contract Value.

3.2.2 **Time and Materials (T&M).** Enhancements, change requests, and certain consulting services may be charged on a T&M basis, at the daily or hourly rates set forth in the applicable SOW or rate card. Any increase to the T&M rate card shall be subject to Client’s prior written consent.

3.2.3 **Managed Services Fees.** Ongoing support and managed services shall be charged as recurring monthly fees, as agreed in the applicable SOW, comprising the remainder of the Contract Value.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.** Unless otherwise set forth in an SOW, the following milestone payment structure shall apply to fixed-fee components:

3.3.1 **Milestone 1 – Project Initiation (15%).** Payable upon execution of this Amendment and issuance of a valid invoice.

3.3.2 **Milestone 2 – Completion of Assessment & Design (25%).** Payable upon Client’s written acceptance of the assessment report and solution design documents.

3.3.3 **Milestone 3 – Completion of Core Implementation (30%).** Payable upon Client’s written acceptance of the core implementation deliverables, including successful completion of UAT for the defined scope.

3.3.4 **Milestone 4 – Transition to Steady State (30%).** Payable upon commencement of steady-state managed services and delivery of the operations runbook, subject to Client’s confirmation.

3.4 **Invoicing.** Vendor shall invoice Client:

3.4.1 For fixed-fee milestones, upon achievement of the relevant milestone and Client’s acceptance (which shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed).  

3.4.2 For T&M services, monthly in arrears, based on approved timesheets or similar documentation reasonably acceptable to Client.  

3.4.3 For managed services, monthly in advance, unless otherwise agreed in writing.  

3.5 **Payment Terms.** Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of receipt of a valid and correct invoice ("**Net 45**" terms). Payment shall be made in United States dollars (USD) by bank transfer or other mutually agreed method.

3.6 **Disputed Amounts.** If Client disputes any invoice or portion thereof in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt of the invoice, specifying the reasons for the dispute. Client shall pay all undisputed portions of the invoice by the due date. The Parties shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve any such dispute promptly and, upon resolution, Client shall pay any subsequently agreed amounts.

3.7 **Late Payment.** For any undisputed amount not paid when due, Vendor may charge interest at the lesser of: (i) one percent (1%) per month, or (ii) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the day after the due date until the date of actual payment. Vendor’s right to interest shall be Vendor’s sole monetary remedy for late payment, without prejudice to its other rights under this Amendment in case of persistent non-payment.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursements.** 

3.8.1 **Pre-Approval.** Out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with the Services (such as travel, accommodation, and meals) shall be reimbursable only to the extent: (a) they are reasonable and necessary; and (b) pre-approved in writing by Client.

3.8.2 **Supporting Documentation.** Vendor shall provide receipts or other customary documentation for all reimbursable expenses together with the relevant invoice.

3.9 **Taxes.** The Fees do not include any applicable sales, use, value-added, goods and services, withholding, or other taxes, duties, or governmental charges (collectively, "**Taxes**"). Client shall be responsible for all Taxes arising from this Amendment, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, property, or employment-related taxes for Vendor’s personnel. If Client is required by law to withhold taxes from payments due to Vendor, Client shall provide Vendor with official receipts or other evidence of such withholding and shall reasonably cooperate with Vendor to obtain any available tax exemptions or reductions.

3.10 **Most Favored Customer.** Given the Low risk profile and strategic nature of the relationship, Vendor represents that the pricing and commercial terms offered herein are competitive. Vendor agrees, during the Term, to notify Client if Vendor offers substantially similar services to another customer of comparable size and scope at materially more favorable rates, and to discuss in good faith an equitable adjustment to the Fees.

- **Priority 2 (High):**  
  - Response Time: within 2 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution Time: within 8 Business Hours  

- **Priority 3 (Medium):**  
  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution Time: within 3 Business Days  

- **Priority 4 (Low):**  
  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution Time: within 5 Business Days

12.3.3 Service Credits shall be applied as a credit against the next invoice(s) due from Client. Service Credits are Client’s primary financial remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to Client’s right to terminate for cause under Article IV in case of persistent material breaches.

12.4 **Reporting.** Vendor shall provide monthly Service performance reports, including metrics on availability, Incident volumes, response and resolution times, and Service Credits (if any).

12.5 **Exclusions.** Availability calculations shall exclude downtime caused by: (a) Scheduled Maintenance agreed in advance with Client; (b) Client’s acts or omissions; (c) failures of systems or services not under Vendor’s reasonable control; or (d) Force Majeure Events.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.** To the extent performance by a Party is prevented or delayed by a Force Majeure Event, such Party’s obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the Party continues to use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.
