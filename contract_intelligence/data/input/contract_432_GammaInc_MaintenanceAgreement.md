## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services  
2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive Maintenance Services for the Equipment and associated systems identified in one or more SOWs executed pursuant to this Agreement. The Services shall include, but are not limited to:  

  (a) Preventive maintenance inspections and scheduled servicing;  
  (b) Corrective and remedial maintenance;  
  (c) Emergency maintenance and on-call support;  
  (d) Spare parts recommendation and assistance with procurement (as agreed);  
  (e) Configuration adjustments, calibration, and optimization services;  
  (f) Remote monitoring (if applicable and specified in an SOW); and  
  (g) Documentation, reporting, and maintenance record keeping.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that the Equipment may include, without limitation, mechanical systems (HVAC, pumps, compressors), electrical distribution systems, backup power systems, IT server racks, and related infrastructure components. The specific Equipment and locations covered shall be listed in one or more SOWs, which shall form an integral part of this Agreement.

### 2.2 Preventive Maintenance  
2.2.1 Vendor shall perform preventive maintenance at intervals specified in the applicable SOW, which shall be based on manufacturer recommendations, industry best practices, and Client’s operational requirements.  

2.2.2 Preventive maintenance shall include, as applicable:  

  (a) Visual inspection of Equipment for signs of wear, damage, or improper operation;  
  (b) Testing of controls, sensors, safety interlocks, and alarms;  
  (c) Cleaning, lubrication, and adjustment of mechanical components;  
  (d) Firmware and software updates, patches, or configuration changes (subject to Client approval where required);  
  (e) Replacement of consumables such as filters, belts, and seals;  
  (f) Verification of system logs and operating parameters; and  
  (g) Documentation of completed tasks in maintenance logs.

2.2.3 All preventive maintenance activities shall be scheduled in advance with Client and coordinated so as to minimize disruption to Client’s operations. Vendor shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for scheduled preventive maintenance that may affect system availability.

### 2.3 Corrective and Emergency Maintenance  
2.3.1 In the event of an Incident or Equipment malfunction, Client shall notify Vendor in accordance with the incident reporting procedures described in the applicable SLA.  

2.3.2 Vendor shall respond to Incidents within the Response Times specified in **Article XII**, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to diagnose and correct the problem as promptly as practicable.  

2.3.3 Emergency maintenance shall be provided on a 24x7 basis if expressly stated in the SOW. Otherwise, Emergency maintenance shall be provided during standard support hours as specified in the SOW. Additional fees may apply for after-hours or holiday services, as set forth in **Article III**.

2.3.4 Vendor shall prioritize Incidents based on impact and severity, in accordance with the SLA. Vendor shall keep Client reasonably informed of progress and estimated time to resolution.

### 2.4 Performance Standards  
2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with:  

  (a) Generally accepted industry standards;  
  (b) Applicable manufacturers’ specifications and guidelines;  
  (c) Applicable Law; and  
  (d) The requirements of this Agreement and each SOW.

2.4.2 Vendor shall ensure that all personnel assigned to perform the Services are properly trained, qualified, and licensed (where required) to perform the tasks assigned to them.  

2.4.3 Vendor shall comply with all reasonable safety, security, and access requirements established by Client for its facilities, provided that such requirements are communicated in writing to Vendor in advance.

### 2.5 Deliverables  
2.5.1 As part of the Services, Vendor shall provide the following Deliverables, as applicable and further detailed in each SOW:  

  (a) Maintenance reports summarizing each preventive or corrective visit, including tasks completed, parts used, and recommendations;  
  (b) Incident reports detailing the root cause, remedial action taken, and preventive measures;  
  (c) Updated asset inventories and maintenance schedules;  
  (d) Configuration documentation, including backup copies of configuration files where applicable; and  
  (e) Periodic performance and SLA reports (e.g., monthly or quarterly).

2.5.2 Unless otherwise agreed in an SOW, Deliverables shall be delivered electronically in a commonly used format (such as PDF, Excel, or Word) and shall be deemed accepted by Client if no written rejection or request for correction is provided within ten (10) Business Days after delivery, specifying the reasons for such rejection. Vendor shall promptly correct any material deficiencies identified by Client.

### 2.6 Exclusions  
2.6.1 The Services do not include:  

  (a) Major upgrades or expansions of Equipment not expressly covered by an SOW;  
  (b) Replacement of major capital components unless explicitly identified;  
  (c) Repairs required due to misuse, abuse, negligence, or unauthorized modifications by Client or third parties;  
  (d) Work required to correct design flaws introduced by third-party contractors, unless otherwise agreed; and  
  (e) Services outside the agreed scope in an SOW.

2.6.2 Any services beyond the scope described herein or in an SOW shall be subject to the **Change Management** provisions set forth in **Article XIII** and may require separate written authorization and additional Fees.

### 2.7 Cooperation and Client Responsibilities  
2.7.1 Client shall provide Vendor with reasonable access to its facilities, Equipment, systems, and relevant documentation necessary for Vendor to perform the Services.  

2.7.2 Client shall designate a primary contact person for coordination with Vendor and shall ensure that Vendor personnel are accompanied by authorized Client personnel where required for site access or security.  

2.7.3 Client shall promptly notify Vendor of any known or suspected problems with the Equipment and shall not attempt to repair or modify Equipment covered under this Agreement in a manner that might interfere with Vendor’s obligations, unless necessary in an emergency to protect persons or property.

  (a) **Fixed Annual Maintenance Fee**: Covering scheduled preventive maintenance, routine inspections, and standard reporting;  
  (b) **Time and Materials (T&M) Fees**: For corrective maintenance, emergency interventions, and unscheduled work not covered by the fixed fee;  
  (c) **Parts and Materials Costs**: For replacement parts, consumables, and materials not included in the fixed fee;  
  (d) **Optional Services Fees**: For optional or specialized services requested by Client (e.g., advanced diagnostics, extended training).

3.2.2 The Parties may agree upon a payment schedule that allocates the Contract Value across the Term, and such schedule shall be specified in the relevant SOW(s).

### 3.3 Payment Milestones  
3.3.1 Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Client shall pay the Fixed Annual Maintenance Fee in equal quarterly installments, invoiced in advance.  

3.3.2 T&M and parts-related Fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears, based on actual Services performed and parts supplied during the preceding month.  

3.3.3 Any milestone-based payment structure for significant projects or major overhaul activities shall be set forth in the applicable SOW, including specific deliverable-based or event-based milestones.

### 3.4 Invoicing and Net 30 Terms  
3.4.1 Vendor shall issue invoices to Client in accordance with this **Article III** and the applicable SOW(s). Each invoice shall:  

  (a) Reference this Agreement and Contract Ref. No. MNT-GAM-202503-432;  
  (b) Identify the applicable SOW;  
  (c) Itemize the Services performed, hours worked (if T&M), and parts provided; and  
  (d) Specify any applicable taxes as a separate line item.

3.4.2 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the date of the invoice (“**Net 30**”).  

3.4.3 If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the basis of the dispute. Client shall pay the undisputed portion on or before the due date. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any invoice disputes promptly.

### 3.5 Late Payment  
3.5.1 Any undisputed invoice amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of **one percent (1%) per month**, or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, from the due date until paid in full.  

3.5.2 Vendor shall have the right, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice and provided that Client has failed to cure such non-payment within such notice period, to suspend the performance of non-critical Services until all overdue amounts (including interest) are paid. Vendor shall not suspend Services if such suspension would reasonably be expected to create an imminent risk to safety or cause substantial damage to Client’s property.

### 3.6 Taxes  
3.6.1 Unless otherwise expressly stated, the Fees are exclusive of all sales, use, value-added, or other similar taxes. Client shall be responsible for all such taxes (except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income) and shall pay or reimburse Vendor for any such taxes that Vendor is required to collect or remit.

### 3.7 Expenses  
3.7.1 Vendor shall be reimbursed for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with Client’s travel and expense policies communicated in advance.  

3.7.2 Vendor shall provide reasonable supporting documentation (e.g., receipts) for any reimbursable expenses.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term  
4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall continue in full force and effect until **December 16, 2029** (the “**Expiration Date**”).

### 4.2 Renewal  
4.2.1 The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend this Agreement for an additional term, subject to negotiation of applicable fees and any revised terms.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience  
4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience, without cause, upon providing at least **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party.  

4.3.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Vendor for:  

  (a) All Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination;  
  (b) Any non-cancellable commitments made by Vendor for the benefit of Client, to the extent documented and reasonable; and  
  (c) Any applicable early termination charges explicitly set forth in a relevant SOW (if any).

### 4.4 Termination for Cause  
4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  

  (a) Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  
  (b) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed over a substantial portion of its assets.

4.4.2 Without limiting the foregoing, material breaches by Client may include failure to pay undisputed fees when due, subject to the cure opportunity described above. Material breaches by Vendor may include repeated failure to meet critical SLAs without remedy as described in **Article XII**.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination  
4.5.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:  

  (a) Vendor shall cease performing the Services, except as mutually agreed in writing for transition assistance;  
  (b) Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed amounts due and payable for Services performed and Deliverables accepted as of the effective date of termination; and  
  (c) Each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s written request, destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession, subject to **Section 5.5** (Survival of Confidentiality).

4.5.2 Termination of this Agreement shall automatically terminate all SOWs, unless the Parties expressly agree in writing that a particular SOW shall survive.  

4.5.3 Termination or expiration shall not relieve either Party of obligations that, by their nature, are intended to survive, including but not limited to payment obligations, confidentiality, IP rights, indemnification, limitations of liability, and dispute resolution provisions.

### 4.6 Transition Assistance  
4.6.1 At Client’s written request made prior to the effective date of termination or expiration, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance (for a period not to exceed ninety (90) days unless otherwise agreed) to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Client or a third-party provider.  

4.6.2 Transition assistance shall be provided on a T&M basis at Vendor’s then-current rates, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations
