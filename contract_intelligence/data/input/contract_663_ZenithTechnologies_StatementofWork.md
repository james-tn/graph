## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview.**  
Pursuant to **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement, the Client hereby engages the Vendor, and the Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to perform the Services and provide the Deliverables described in this SOW in support of the Client’s global technology infrastructure, business analytics, and digital transformation initiatives. This SOW constitutes a specific “Statement of Work” as defined in **Section 1.17** of the Parent Agreement and is incorporated into and governed by the Parent Agreement.

2.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  
The Services under this SOW are a concrete instantiation of the general service categories described in **Section 2.2** of the Parent Agreement:

- The technology consulting and assessment activities under this SOW implement **Section 2.2(a)** of the Parent Agreement.
- The systems integration and implementation activities implement **Section 2.2(b)**.
- The data analytics and business intelligence activities implement **Section 2.2(c)**.
- The project management and governance activities implement **Section 2.2(d)**.
- The training and knowledge transfer activities implement **Section 2.2(e)**.
- The support and maintenance services described herein implement **Section 2.2(f)** and are subject to **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement.

Any references in this SOW to acceptance, timelines, subcontracting, Client responsibilities, and change management shall be construed in harmony with **Sections 2.4–2.9** and **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement.

2.3 **Project Phases.**  
The Vendor shall deliver the Program in the following Project Phases. Each Phase shall comprise specific tasks and Deliverables, and shall be associated with Milestones for acceptance and invoicing, as further detailed in **Schedule 1 (Project Plan and Milestones)**:

2.3.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery & Assessment (Months 1–4).**  
a. Conduct workshops with key Client stakeholders across commercial, finance, and IT functions.  
b. Assess the Client’s existing technology landscape, including infrastructure, applications, and data architecture, in line with **Section 2.2(a)** of the Parent Agreement.  
c. Perform a data quality and data governance assessment over at least three (3) major data domains (e.g., sales, customer, product).  
d. Produce a **Current State Assessment Report** and **Gap Analysis** identifying modernization, cloud migration, security enhancements, and data governance opportunities.  
**Key Deliverables:**  
- D1.1 Current State Architecture & Systems Inventory  
- D1.2 Data Quality and Governance Assessment Report  
- D1.3 Gap Analysis and Recommendations Report  

2.3.2 **Phase 2 – Solution Design (Months 5–8).**  
a. Design a target data platform architecture (cloud-based, vendor-neutral unless otherwise agreed).  
b. Define integration patterns, data pipelines, data models, semantic layers, and reporting structures.  
c. Develop a **Target Operating Model** for analytics, including roles, processes, and governance bodies.  
d. Prepare a prioritized **3-year Roadmap** for delivery of analytics and digital capabilities.  
**Key Deliverables:**  
- D2.1 Target Data Platform Architecture Document  
- D2.2 Logical and Physical Data Models for Priority Domains  
- D2.3 Integration Specifications and Data Mapping Documents  
- D2.4 Analytics Roadmap and Implementation Plan  

2.3.3 **Phase 3 – Implementation & Integration (Months 9–24).**  
a. Configure and implement the target data platform and associated tools.  
b. Develop and test data ingestion pipelines and integration workflows consistent with **Section 2.2(b)** of the Parent Agreement.  
c. Implement data governance controls and security model aligned with the Client’s policies and Applicable Law.  
d. Conduct system, integration, and performance testing; support Client user acceptance testing (UAT).  
**Key Deliverables:**  
- D3.1 Configured Data Platform and Core Services  
- D3.2 Implemented ETL/ELT Pipelines and Integration Workflows  
- D3.3 Security and Access Control Configuration Documentation  
- D3.4 Test Plans, Test Results, and UAT Support Logs  

2.3.4 **Phase 4 – Data Analytics Enablement (Months 18–30).**  
a. Design and build at least ten (10) strategic dashboards and associated analytical data models.  
b. Implement self-service analytics capabilities for business users in alignment with **Section 2.2(c)** of the Parent Agreement.  
c. Optimize queries, performance, and data refresh schedules.  
**Key Deliverables:**  
- D4.1 Dashboard and Report Catalogue  
- D4.2 Configured Dashboards and Reports in Client’s Selected Tools  
- D4.3 Performance Benchmarking and Tuning Report  

2.3.5 **Phase 5 – Training & Knowledge Transfer (Months 12–34).**  
a. Prepare detailed operational runbooks and administrator guides.  
b. Deliver training sessions and workshops for technical, data governance, and business analytics users as contemplated in **Section 2.2(e)** of the Parent Agreement.  
c. Provide “train-the-trainer” sessions enabling the Client to conduct internal training thereafter.  
**Key Deliverables:**  
- D5.1 Training Curriculum and Materials  
- D5.2 Runbooks and Administration Guides  
- D5.3 Completion Report of Training Sessions and Attendance Records  

2.3.6 **Phase 6 – Transition to Steady-State Support (Months 30–44).**  
a. Establish support processes, incident ticketing, and escalation paths consistent with **Article XII (SLAs)** of the Parent Agreement.  
b. Provide Support Services focusing on incident resolution, minor enhancements, and operational monitoring.  
c. Conduct knowledge transfer to Client support teams or designated third parties.  
**Key Deliverables:**  
- D6.1 Support Operating Model and RACI Matrix  
- D6.2 Monthly Service Performance and Incident Reports  
- D6.3 Transition & Handover Report  

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services in accordance with **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, using due skill, care, and diligence, consistent with good industry practice, and in compliance with Applicable Law and Client policies communicated in advance.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties agree to the high-level timelines in Section 2.3 above and the detailed Milestones defined in Schedule 1. In line with **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement, if the Vendor becomes aware of circumstances that may materially delay performance, it shall promptly notify the Client and propose a revised timeline. Any material adjustments to scope, Milestones, or Fees shall be handled via the Change Management process under **Article XIII** of the Parent Agreement and this SOW.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall fulfil the responsibilities set forth in **Section 2.6** of the Parent Agreement, including:  
a. Providing timely access to Client personnel, facilities, systems, and Client Materials, including test environments and representative data sets;  
b. Designating subject matter experts for each functional domain;  
c. Reviewing Deliverables within the timeframes stated in **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement (ten Business Days plus reminder grace period); and  
d. Ensuring the accuracy and completeness of Client Materials.

Delays or additional work resulting from Client’s failure to comply with these responsibilities may be subject to schedule and Fee adjustments via a Change Order.

2.7 **Support and Maintenance Services.**  
During Phase 6, the Vendor shall provide Support Services, including:  
a. Incident management and resolution in accordance with **Section 12.4** of the Parent Agreement and **Article XII** of this SOW;  
b. Minor configuration changes and enhancements (e.g., report adjustments, minor data pipeline changes) within an agreed monthly capacity;  
c. Proactive monitoring of data loads and system health.  

Support Services shall be delivered during Business Hours unless otherwise agreed in writing. Any 24x7 or extended-hour coverage shall require a Change Order.

2.8 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Deliverables shall be subject to the acceptance procedure in **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement:  
- The Vendor shall submit Deliverables in accordance with the Milestones in Schedule 1;  
- The Client shall review and either accept or provide written notice of material non-conformity within ten (10) Business Days;  
- The Vendor shall remediate and resubmit any non-conforming Deliverables; and  
- Deemed acceptance shall apply as described in **Section 2.7(d)** of the Parent Agreement, following the reminder process.

2.9 **Project Governance and Key Personnel.**  
a. The Client shall appoint a **Client Project Manager**, initially the Client’s Director of Data & Analytics, or such replacement as notified in writing.  
b. The Vendor shall appoint a **Vendor Project Manager**, initially the Vendor’s Engagement Director or similar senior role.  
c. The Parties shall hold regular project governance meetings (weekly operational status, monthly steering committees) as contemplated by **Section 2.2(d)** of the Parent Agreement.  
d. Replacement of key Vendor personnel shall be subject to prior consultation with the Client; the Vendor shall ensure that replacement personnel possess skills and experience equivalent to those being replaced.

a. The Vendor shall issue invoices in accordance with the Milestones and monthly billing schedules described above and in Schedule 2.  
b. **Notwithstanding Section 3.4(a) of the Parent Agreement (Net 30), the Parties agree that for this SOW all invoices shall be due and payable within forty-five (45) days of the invoice date (“Net 45”). This modifies the payment term solely with respect to this SOW in accordance with Section 2.1 of the Parent Agreement.**

All other payment-related terms (including handling of disputed amounts) shall follow **Sections 3.4 and 3.9** of the Parent Agreement, except as expressly modified above.

- The Vendor’s right, after prior written notice and opportunity to cure, to suspend Services (excluding critical safety-related Services) until overdue amounts are paid, provided there is no bona fide dispute.

The Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses in accordance with **Section 3.6** of the Parent Agreement, including:  
a. Travel, lodging, subsistence for on-site work;  
b. Special materials or third-party facilities required for workshops or training; provided that:  
- All expenses are pre-approved in writing (email sufficient);  
- Supporting documentation is provided with expense claims; and  
- Any single expense in excess of USD 1,000 is separately authorized.

3.8 **Taxes and Withholding.**  
Taxes shall be handled in accordance with **Sections 3.7 and 3.8** of the Parent Agreement. Fees stated herein are exclusive of VAT and other applicable taxes.

3.9 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Client in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice, the procedure in **Section 3.9** of the Parent Agreement shall apply. The Client shall notify the Vendor within fifteen (15) Business Days, pay the undisputed portion, and the Parties shall resolve the dispute per **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)** of the Parent Agreement and this SOW.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **SOW Term.**  
This SOW shall commence on the SOW Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **December 28, 2027** (the “**SOW Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV or the Parent Agreement.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent Agreement Term.**  
Notwithstanding the SOW Expiration Date, this SOW cannot extend beyond the Term of the Parent Agreement as defined in **Section 4.1** thereof (currently through November 6, 2025), unless the Parent Agreement is renewed or extended in accordance with **Section 4.2** of the Parent Agreement. The Parties anticipate extending or renewing the Parent Agreement to cover the SOW Term; if not extended, the Parties shall enter into an appropriate successor agreement to allow continued performance, or mutually agree on an orderly wind-down of this SOW.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
In accordance with **Section 4.3** of the Parent Agreement, either Party may terminate this SOW (without necessarily terminating the Parent Agreement or other SOWs) for convenience upon not less than **forty-five (45) days’** prior written notice to the other Party. This notice period is within the 30–60 day range contemplated by the Parties for a medium risk engagement and supersedes the 30-day minimum in the Parent Agreement solely with respect to this SOW.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this SOW immediately upon written notice if:

a. The other Party commits a material breach of this SOW or the Parent Agreement as it applies to this SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach; or  
b. The other Party becomes insolvent or is subject to insolvency events described in **Section 4.4(b)** of the Parent Agreement.

4.5 **Termination Linked to Parent Agreement.**  
a. If the Parent Agreement is terminated for any reason, this SOW shall **automatically terminate** effective on the same date, without separate notice, except as expressly agreed otherwise in writing.  
b. In the event of termination of the Parent Agreement for the Vendor’s uncured material breach, the Client shall have no obligation to pay for non-conforming Services or Deliverables under this SOW that cannot be remedied, consistent with **Section 4.6(d)** of the Parent Agreement.  

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this SOW:

a. The Vendor shall promptly cease performing the terminated Services, except for any Transition Services requested by the Client.  
b. Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5** of the Parent Agreement.  
c. The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services properly performed and accepted Deliverables up to the effective date of termination, plus approved expenses, consistent with **Section 4.6(c)** of the Parent Agreement.  
d. At the Client’s written request, the Vendor shall provide Transition Services (e.g., handover of documentation, knowledge transfer to a new provider) on a time and materials basis at the rates set forth in Schedule 2 for a period not exceeding six (6) months, unless otherwise agreed.  

4.7 **Survival.**  
Provisions of this SOW that by their nature should survive expiration or termination shall so survive, including, without limitation, obligations relating to Fees accrued but unpaid, confidentiality, IP rights, data protection, limitations of liability, indemnification, dispute resolution, and governing law and jurisdiction. This is consistent with **Section 4.7** and **Article XVI** of the Parent Agreement.
