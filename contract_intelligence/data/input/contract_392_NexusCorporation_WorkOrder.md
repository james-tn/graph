## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
The Vendor shall provide to the Client a comprehensive set of professional services and deliverables to design, implement, configure, integrate, and support a cloud-based enterprise platform (the “**Platform**”) for the Client’s internal business operations. The Services include, without limitation:

  (a) technology consulting and solution design;  
  (b) system architecture definition and documentation;  
  (c) configuration and customization of a cloud-based enterprise resource planning (ERP) and customer relationship management (CRM) solution;  
  (d) integration with designated third-party systems and internal Client systems;  
  (e) data migration planning, extraction, transformation, and loading;  
  (f) testing (functional, integration, performance, and user acceptance testing support);  
  (g) go-live preparation and cutover support;  
  (h) post go-live hypercare and stabilization support; and  
  (i) ongoing managed services and Level 1–3 support in accordance with Article XII.

2.2 **Project Phases.**  
The Services shall be organized into the following phases, each with associated Deliverables and Milestones:

  (a) **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements (August 21, 2024 – November 30, 2024).**  
  The Vendor shall conduct workshops, interviews, and analysis sessions with Client stakeholders to gather business, functional, and technical requirements. Deliverables in this phase include:  
  (i) Requirements Traceability Matrix;  
  (ii) High-Level Solution Design Document;  
  (iii) Project Plan and Milestone Schedule; and  
  (iv) Data Migration Strategy Document.  

  (b) **Phase 2 – Design and Architecture (December 1, 2024 – March 31, 2025).**  
  The Vendor shall define the detailed solution architecture and system design. Deliverables include:  
  (i) Detailed Solution Architecture Document;  
  (ii) Integration Design Specifications;  
  (iii) Security and Access Control Design;  
  (iv) Test Strategy and Test Plan.  

  (c) **Phase 3 – Configuration, Development, and Integration (April 1, 2025 – December 31, 2025).**  
  The Vendor shall configure the Platform, perform required customizations, develop interfaces and integrations, and implement data migration routines. Deliverables include:  
  (i) Configured ERP and CRM modules per Specifications;  
  (ii) Custom components (e.g., scripts, workflows, reports) as defined in the Requirements Traceability Matrix;  
  (iii) Implemented integrations with up to five (5) named third-party systems;  
  (iv) Data migration scripts and transformation rules; and  
  (v) Technical Runbooks for deployed components.  

  (d) **Phase 4 – Testing, UAT Support, and Go-Live (January 1, 2026 – June 30, 2026).**  
  The Vendor shall support system, integration, and performance testing and provide assistance during user acceptance testing (UAT) led by the Client. Vendor shall remediate defects within agreed response/resolution times as per Article XII. Deliverables include:  
  (i) Test Execution Reports;  
  (ii) Defect Logs and Resolution Reports;  
  (iii) Go-Live Cutover Plan;  
  (iv) Training Materials and Conduct of up to ten (10) virtual training sessions for Client end-users;  
  (v) Go-Live Completion Report.  

  (e) **Phase 5 – Hypercare and Stabilization (July 1, 2026 – December 31, 2026).**  
  The Vendor shall provide intensified support post go-live, including monitoring, incident management, and minor enhancements necessary for stabilization. Deliverables include:  
  (i) Monthly Hypercare Status Reports;  
  (ii) Knowledge Transfer Sessions to Client’s internal support team;  
  (iii) Stabilization Completion Report.  

  (f) **Phase 6 – Managed Services and Support (January 1, 2027 – November 30, 2028).**  
  For the remainder of the Term, the Vendor shall provide managed services for the Platform, including:  
  (i) Incident and problem management;  
  (ii) Service requests for configuration changes within a defined scope;  
  (iii) Patch and release management for the Platform components under Vendor’s remit;  
  (iv) Continuous monitoring and reporting in line with the Service Levels in Article XII.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  (b) using personnel with appropriate skills and experience;  
  (c) in accordance with the Milestones and timelines set forth above, subject to delays caused by the Client or Force Majeure Events as provided in Article XV; and  
  (d) in compliance with Applicable Law.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client acknowledges that the Vendor’s ability to perform the Services and meet Milestones is dependent on the timely and effective performance of the Client’s responsibilities, including:

  (a) providing timely access to Client systems, facilities, networks, and relevant personnel;  
  (b) providing, validating, and maintaining Client Materials and data necessary for the Services;  
  (c) designating a project manager and subject-matter experts to provide approvals, feedback, and decisions within reasonable timeframes;  
  (d) performing Client-led tasks such as user acceptance testing and internal change management; and  
  (e) obtaining any required licenses, approvals, or consents from third parties where Client systems or data are involved.

Any delay or failure by the Client to discharge its responsibilities under this Section 2.4 shall entitle the Vendor to an equitable extension of time for performance and, where such delay materially impacts Vendor’s costs or resource allocation, an equitable adjustment to Fees as mutually agreed through the Change Management process in Article XIII.

2.5 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise set out in a specific schedule or Change Order:

  (a) For each Deliverable, the Vendor shall notify the Client in writing upon completion and provide the Deliverable for review.  
  (b) The Client shall have fifteen (15) Business Days (the “**Review Period**”) from receipt to review each Deliverable and either:  
   (i) provide written acceptance; or  
   (ii) provide a written rejection with specific reasons and defects.  
  (c) If the Client does not respond within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.  
  (d) Upon written or deemed acceptance, the Deliverable shall be considered finally accepted and any further changes shall be subject to Article XIII (Change Management).  
  (e) In the event of a justified rejection, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified defects within a reasonable period and resubmit the Deliverable for a further Review Period.

2.6 **Subcontracting.**  
The Vendor may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that:

  (a) the Vendor remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services and compliance with this Agreement; and  
  (b) any subcontractor engaged to perform material elements of the Services is bound by written obligations no less protective of the Client than those set forth in this Agreement with respect to confidentiality and data protection.

  (f) **Phase 6 – Managed Services and Support (Jan 1, 2027 – Nov 30, 2028):** USD $70,000

Any amounts listed above are estimates for allocation purposes; however, the Client remains obligated to pay all Fees as incurred in accordance with the milestones and actual effort, subject always to the total contract value and any duly executed Change Orders.

3.3 **Milestone-Based Payments.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in writing:

  (a) **Phase 1 Payment:** 100% of Phase 1 Fees upon Client’s acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the Phase 1 Deliverables.  
  (b) **Phase 2 Payment:** 50% of Phase 2 Fees upon commencement of Phase 2; 50% upon acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of Phase 2 Deliverables.  
  (c) **Phase 3 Payment:** 40% of Phase 3 Fees upon commencement of Phase 3; 40% upon completion of initial configuration; 20% upon acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of Phase 3 Deliverables.  
  (d) **Phase 4 Payment:** 50% of Phase 4 Fees upon commencement of Phase 4; 50% upon go-live date, as documented in the Go-Live Completion Report.  
  (e) **Phase 5 Payment:** 50% of Phase 5 Fees upon commencement of Phase 5; 50% upon completion of hypercare, evidenced by the Stabilization Completion Report.  
  (f) **Phase 6 Payment:** Fees for Managed Services shall be invoiced quarterly in advance based on an annualized fee schedule, except as adjusted by any Change Orders.

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
The Vendor shall issue invoices in accordance with the allocation above. All invoices are due and payable within **thirty (30) days net** from the invoice date (“**Net 30**”), without set-off or deduction, except as expressly permitted under this Agreement. Payments shall be made in **United States dollars (USD)** by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method to the account designated by the Vendor in writing.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of:  
  (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or  
  (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law,  
from the due date until the date of payment in full. The Vendor shall be entitled, upon written notice to the Client, to suspend performance of the Services (including access to any Deliverables under Vendor’s control) if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than forty-five (45) days after the due date, without prejudice to any other right or remedy of Vendor.

3.6 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, the Client shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days after receipt of such invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute in reasonable detail. The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts in accordance with Section 3.4. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts promptly. Any resolved disputed amount shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days after the dispute is resolved.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, goods and services, withholding, or other taxes (“**Taxes**”). The Client shall be responsible for all Taxes arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, employment, or property.   (a) withhold only the minimum amount required;  
  (b) promptly pay such Taxes to the relevant authority; and  
  (c) provide Vendor with an official receipt or other appropriate evidence of such payment.

3.8 **Expenses.**  
Reasonable, out-of-pocket travel and living expenses incurred by the Vendor in performing on-site Services at the Client’s locations shall be reimbursed by the Client, provided that such expenses are in accordance with the Client’s then-current travel policy (if provided to Vendor in writing) and pre-approved in writing by the Client. ## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **November 30, 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Any extension or renewal of this Agreement beyond the Expiration Date shall be subject to the mutual written agreement of the Parties, including agreement on applicable Fees, Service Levels, and scope of services.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience (Client).**  
The Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for its convenience, without cause, upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Vendor. In such event:

  (a) The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables delivered up to the effective date of termination, and for all non-cancellable commitments and reasonable wind-down costs incurred by the Vendor as a direct result of such termination.  
  (b) Any phase-based or milestone-based Fees that have not yet been fully earned shall be pro-rated based on the work actually performed, as reasonably determined by the Vendor.  
  (c) The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to cease work in an orderly manner and to cooperate with the Client for transition, subject to payment of applicable Fees and expenses.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience (Vendor).**  
The Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Client, provided that:

  (a) such termination shall not be effective prior to completion of any critical Milestone that the Parties expressly identify as non-terminable prior to completion in a subsequent written agreement; and  
  (b) the Vendor shall provide reasonable cooperation and transition assistance during the notice period, subject to Client’s payment of applicable Fees and expenses.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**

  (a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.  
  (b) Without prejudice to any other rights or remedies, the Vendor may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to the Client if:  
   (i) the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount due within sixty (60) days following the due date; or  
   (ii) the Client or its representatives materially interfere with Vendor’s performance of the Services and fail to remedy such interference within fifteen (15) days after written notice.

4.6 **Insolvency.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, files for bankruptcy, enters into receivership, liquidation, or similar proceedings, or ceases operating in the ordinary course of business.

4.7 **Effects of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) The Client shall immediately pay all outstanding invoices and any accrued Fees and reimbursable expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination.  
  (b) The Vendor shall cease providing the Services, except for any mutually agreed transition services under a separate written agreement, which shall be subject to additional Fees.  
  (c) Each Party shall, upon request, return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, destroy any Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession, subject to any retention obligations under Applicable Law or Section 11.7.  
  (d) All rights and licenses granted to the Client under Article VI with respect to Deliverables accepted and fully paid for as of the effective date of termination shall remain in effect, subject to the applicable terms of this Agreement.  
  (e) Any provisions which, by their nature, are intended to survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI) shall so survive.

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For managed services provided during Phase 6, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve a monthly service availability of **99.9%** for the Platform components under Vendor’s direct control, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.3 **Incident Classification and Response/Resolution Times.**  
Incidents reported by the Client shall be categorized and addressed as follows (all times are during Business Days unless otherwise agreed):

  - **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or critical function unavailable with no workaround.  
    - Target Response Time: 1 hour  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: 8 hours

  - **Priority 2 (High):** Major functionality severely degraded; workaround available but not sustainable.  
    - Target Response Time: 4 hours  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: 2 Business Days

  - **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical defect; business impact moderate; workaround available.  
    - Target Response Time: 1 Business Day  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: 5 Business Days

  - **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issue; cosmetic or no significant business impact.  
    - Target Response Time: 2 Business Days  
    - Target Resolution/Workaround Time: Next scheduled release or as mutually agreed.

12.4 **SLA Credits.**  
If the Vendor fails to meet the service availability commitment in Section 12.1 (excluding permitted downtime) in any calendar month, the Client shall be entitled, upon written request within thirty (30) days after the end of such month, to service credits as follows:

  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: Credit equal to 5% of the monthly managed services fee.  
  - Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: Credit equal to 10% of the monthly managed services fee.  
  - Availability below 98.0%: Credit equal to 15% of the monthly managed services fee.

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices for managed services and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service credits are the Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the Service Levels, without limiting other remedies for material breach under Article IV.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics or pandemics, war, terrorism, riots, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those involving a Party’s own workforce), acts of government or regulatory authorities, embargoes, or failure of utilities or telecommunications networks (not attributable to the Party).

15.2 **Notification and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event on its performance and to resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party (other than payment obligations) shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event to the extent that such obligations cannot be performed due to such event. The time for performance shall be extended by a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and materially prevents performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to the other Party, without liability (except for amounts payable for Services performed prior to the effective date of termination and other accrued obligations).
