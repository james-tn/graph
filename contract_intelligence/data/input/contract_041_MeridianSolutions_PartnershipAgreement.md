## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Meridian shall provide to Contoso a comprehensive suite of professional and managed services designed to support Contoso’s enterprise operations, including but not limited to: (a) technology consulting and advisory services; (b) systems integration for Contoso’s ERP, CRM, and data analytics platforms; (c) design, configuration, and deployment of cloud-based infrastructure; (d) managed application and infrastructure support; and (e) project management and governance services. The Parties anticipate that specific projects and initiatives will be defined in Statements of Work, which shall be subject to and governed by the terms of this Agreement.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) Each SOW shall reference this Agreement and shall at a minimum set forth: (i) a description of the Services to be performed and Deliverables to be provided; (ii) project milestones and timelines; (iii) acceptance criteria; (iv) Service Levels (if any) applicable to such SOW; and (v) the Fees and invoicing schedule.  
(b) In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states that a specific provision is intended to supersede this Agreement and such SOW is executed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

2.3 **Project Phases.**  
The Services will be organized into phases, which may include, without limitation:

  2.3.1 **Discovery and Assessment.** Meridian shall conduct stakeholder interviews, current-state assessments, data and infrastructure inventories, and requirements-gathering workshops. Meridian shall prepare and deliver an Assessment Report, including recommended solutions, proposed architecture, and a prioritized roadmap for implementation.

  2.3.2 **Design and Planning.** Meridian shall design target-state architectures, integration patterns, data models, and process flows. Meridian shall produce Solution Design Documents, implementation plans, risk assessments, and a migration strategy. Meridian shall work closely with Contoso’s designated representatives to validate assumptions and refine the designs.

  2.3.3 **Implementation and Integration.** Meridian shall configure, develop, and integrate systems and applications in accordance with the approved Solution Design Documents and SOWs. This may include configuration of ERP and CRM modules, development of integration services and APIs, implementation of data pipelines, and deployment of cloud resources. Meridian will use commercially reasonable efforts to follow industry best practices and Contoso’s architecture and security standards, as provided in writing to Meridian.

  2.3.4 **Testing and Acceptance.** Meridian shall conduct unit testing, system testing, integration testing, and user acceptance testing support as described in the applicable SOW. Meridian shall remedy, at its cost, any defects or non-conformities identified during the testing period that are attributable to Meridian’s failure to comply with the applicable SOW or this Agreement. Formal acceptance criteria and processes shall be specified in each SOW.

  2.3.5 **Transition to Operations and Managed Services.** Upon successful completion of implementation activities, Meridian shall transition the solution to steady-state operations, which may include: (a) 24x7 monitoring of critical systems; (b) incident management; (c) problem and change management; (d) performance tuning; and (e) periodic reporting to Contoso. Service Levels applicable to managed services shall be defined in the relevant SOW and **Article XII**.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Deliverables may include:

  (a) Assessment Reports and business requirements documents;  
  (b) Solution Design Documents, architecture diagrams, and configuration guides;  
  (c) Configured instances of enterprise applications and platforms;  
  (d) Custom code, scripts, integration components, and deployment artifacts;  
  (e) Operational runbooks, user guides, and training materials; and  
  (f) Periodic performance, incident, and SLA reports.

Deliverables shall be provided in electronic form (e.g., PDF, Word, Excel, source code repositories) unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Meridian shall:  

  (a) Perform the Services in a professional, workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and in compliance with applicable laws and regulations;  

  (b) Utilize personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications to perform the Services;  

  (c) Use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the timelines, milestones, and Service Levels set forth in this Agreement and the applicable SOWs; and  

  (d) Cooperate in good faith with Contoso’s personnel, third-party vendors, and other stakeholders designated by Contoso.

2.6 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges that its timely cooperation and performance of responsibilities is critical to Meridian’s performance of the Services. Without limiting the foregoing, Contoso shall:

  (a) Provide Meridian with timely access to appropriate personnel, systems, facilities, and information reasonably required for Meridian to perform the Services;  

  (b) Designate a primary project sponsor and a project manager who will be authorized to make or obtain decisions on behalf of Contoso;  

  (c) Review and provide feedback on Deliverables within the timeframes specified in the SOW;  

  (d) Obtain and maintain, at its own cost, any necessary consents, licenses, or approvals from third parties (unless otherwise specified in a SOW) for Meridian’s access to and use of third-party systems or data; and  

  (e) Ensure that all Client Data provided to Meridian is accurate and complete to the best of Contoso’s knowledge.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  
Meridian may, upon prior written notice to Contoso, engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services; provided that (a) Meridian remains fully responsible for the performance of the Services and compliance with this Agreement; and (b) any subcontractor who will have access to Confidential Information or Personal Data shall be bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection consistent with this Agreement.

2.8 **No Exclusivity.**  
Unless expressly stated otherwise in an SOW, this Agreement does not create an exclusive relationship between the Parties, and each Party is free to enter into similar arrangements with third parties, provided that neither Party shall take actions that would materially interfere with or undermine the performance of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties agree that the total estimated value of this Agreement, inclusive of all SOWs anticipated at the Effective Date, shall be **One Million Seven Hundred Eighty-Nine Thousand Sixty-Eight U.S. Dollars (US $1,789,068)** (the “**Contract Value**”), subject to adjustment in accordance with this Agreement and any Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Unless otherwise set forth in a particular SOW, the Fees shall be composed of:

  (a) **Fixed-Price Components.** Certain phases (e.g., Discovery, Design, and specified implementation work) may be charged on a fixed-fee basis, as detailed in the applicable SOW.  

  (b) **Time-and-Materials Components.** Certain Services, such as ongoing managed services, change requests, or additional consulting, may be charged on a time-and-materials basis at Meridian’s then-current standard rates or rates agreed in an SOW.  

  (c) **Recurring Fees.** Managed services and support Services may be priced as recurring monthly or annual Fees, as specified in the applicable SOW.

3.3 **Payment Terms; Invoices.**  
(a) Meridian shall invoice Contoso for Fees in accordance with the schedule set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise specified, invoicing shall occur monthly in arrears.  
(b) Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of each valid invoice (“**Net 60**” payment terms).  
(c) Payment shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other mutually agreed electronic payment method to the account designated by Meridian.  

3.4 **Milestone Payments.**  
Where an SOW specifies milestone-based billing, Meridian shall be entitled to invoice the milestone amount upon completion of the milestone and submission of the corresponding Deliverables, regardless of whether Contoso has completed internal testing or commenced production use, provided such Deliverables materially conform to the agreed acceptance criteria. Acceptance procedures shall be as specified in the SOW.

3.5 **Late Payments.**  
Any undisputed invoice amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate allowed by applicable law. Meridian may, upon written notice, suspend the Services in the event any undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment, provided Meridian resumes Services promptly upon receipt of all overdue amounts and applicable interest.

3.6 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If Contoso disputes any invoiced amount in good faith, Contoso shall notify Meridian in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature of the dispute and the disputed amount. The Parties shall promptly seek to resolve the dispute pursuant to **Article XIV**. Contoso shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice. Failure to timely dispute an invoice shall be deemed acceptance of the invoice.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, GST, withholding, or similar taxes imposed by any governmental authority (collectively, “**Taxes**”). Contoso shall be responsible for all Taxes associated with this Agreement, excluding taxes based on Meridian’s net income, property, or employees. If Contoso is required by law to withhold any Taxes from the amounts payable to Meridian, Contoso shall provide Meridian with official receipts or other evidence of such withholding.

3.8 **Expenses.**  
Contoso shall reimburse Meridian for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket travel and living expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, in accordance with Contoso’s travel and expense policy as communicated in writing to Meridian. Meridian shall submit appropriate documentation for such expenses with its invoices.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **November 24, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, by mutual written agreement executed not less than sixty (60) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew this Agreement for additional periods on terms and conditions to be negotiated in good faith.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any or all SOWs), for convenience upon not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. Termination of the Agreement shall automatically terminate all SOWs unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, effective upon written notice to the other Party, if:

  (a) The other Party materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail; or  

  (b) The other Party (i) becomes insolvent or is unable to pay its debts as they become due; (ii) files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or similar relief that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (iv) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Termination of Individual SOWs.**  
The termination of any SOW shall not, by itself, terminate this Agreement. However, termination of this Agreement shall automatically terminate all SOWs, unless otherwise expressly provided in a written agreement between the Parties.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) Contoso shall pay Meridian for all Services performed and Deliverables provided through the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments, in accordance with the applicable SOW and **Article III**;  

  (b) Meridian shall promptly deliver to Contoso any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which Contoso has paid or is obligated to pay;  

  (c) Each Party shall promptly return or, at the disclosing Party’s option, securely destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to **Section 5.4**; and  

  (d) Meridian shall provide reasonable transition assistance to Contoso, at Contoso’s request and expense (on a time-and-materials basis), to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Contoso or a replacement provider.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties under **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, as well as any payment obligations accrued prior to termination, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party (“**Receiving Party**”), whether oral, written, electronic, visual, or in any other form, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, financial data, pricing, technology, software, source code, algorithms, trade secrets, Client Data, Personal Data, and the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault or breach of the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is received from a third party who is not under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Services and fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement;  
(b) protect the confidentiality of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care;  
(c) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or with the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent; and  
(d) limit access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to its employees, officers, directors, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted, provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice and cooperate reasonably with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If disclosure is ultimately required, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required and shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that such information is treated confidentially.

5.5 **Confidentiality Period.**  
The obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue during the Term and for a period of **five (5) years** following the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except that trade secrets and Personal Data shall be kept confidential for so long as they retain their status as trade secrets or Personal Data under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Applicability.**  
This **Article XII** applies to any managed services, support, or operational Services described in an SOW that specifies Service Levels.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment.**  
For any hosted or managed systems under Meridian’s operational control, Meridian shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such systems achieve at least **99.9%** uptime per calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and Force Majeure Events.

12.3 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Meridian may perform scheduled maintenance during windows mutually agreed with Contoso. Meridian shall provide Contoso with at least five (5) Business Days’ advance notice of any scheduled maintenance expected to cause material service interruption. Time during scheduled maintenance windows shall not count as downtime for purposes of the uptime calculation.

12.4 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
Meridian shall respond to and work to resolve incidents in accordance with the following target timeframes (unless different times are specified in an SOW):

- **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Production system outage or major incident with severe business impact:  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 4 hours  

- **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation with high business impact:  
  - Initial Response: within 2 hours (Business Days)  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 1 Business Day  

- **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact, workarounds available:  
  - Initial Response: within 4 hours (Business Days)  
  - Target Resolution/Workaround: within 3 Business Days  

- **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or informational requests:  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution: as reasonably scheduled

12.5 **SLA Credits.**  
If Meridian fails to meet the uptime or response time Service Levels in a given calendar month, Contoso may be entitled to service credits (“**SLA Credits**”) as specified in the applicable SOW. SLA Credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not, under any circumstances, be redeemable for cash. SLA Credits shall be Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for Meridian’s failure to meet the Service Levels, without prejudice to Contoso’s termination rights under **Article IV**.

## ARTICLE XIV – DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14.1 **Good Faith Negotiations.**  
In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the Services, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof (a “**Dispute**”), the Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute through negotiation between senior executives of each Party.

14.2 **Escalation.**  
If the Parties are unable to resolve the Dispute within thirty (30) days after one Party provides written notice of the Dispute to the other Party, either Party may escalate the Dispute to executive management. The executive representatives shall meet (in person or via videoconference) within fifteen (15) days thereafter to attempt to resolve the Dispute.

14.3 **Mediation.**  
If the Dispute is not resolved within thirty (30) days after escalation to executive management, the Parties agree to attempt to resolve the Dispute by non-binding mediation before a mutually agreed mediator in New York, New York. 14.4 **Arbitration or Litigation.**  
If the Dispute is not resolved through mediation within sixty (60) days after the appointment of a mediator, either Party may pursue its rights and remedies in a court of competent jurisdiction as provided below. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek interim or injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to protect its Confidential Information or Intellectual Property Rights, or to prevent irreparable harm, without first complying with the negotiation or mediation procedures in this **Article XIV**.

14.6 **Governing Law; Venue.**

Subject to the foregoing dispute resolution procedures, the Parties agree that the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York, shall have exclusive jurisdiction and venue over any Dispute that is not subject to arbitration, and each Party hereby submits to the personal jurisdiction of such courts.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “**Force Majeure Event**” means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including, but not limited to, acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving the affected Party’s own workforce), pandemics, epidemics, public health emergencies, governmental actions, power or telecommunications outages, or failures of third-party hosting providers.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall: (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature and anticipated duration of the event; (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event; and (c) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement to the extent affected by the Force Majeure Event shall be suspended, provided that the affected Party complies with **Section 15.2**. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith to adjust any affected timelines or milestones.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this Agreement or the applicable SOW upon written notice to the other Party, without liability (other than for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination).
