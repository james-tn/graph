## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES AND LEASED PREMISES

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 The Vendor hereby leases to the Client, and the Client hereby leases from the Vendor, the Premises (as further described in Section 2.2) and engages the Vendor to provide the Services described herein. The Vendor shall provide the Services on a non‑exclusive basis, except as expressly stated otherwise in this Agreement.

2.1.2 The primary purpose of this Agreement is to enable the Client to operate its corporate and technology operations within the Premises and to rely upon the Vendor for facilities management, critical infrastructure, and related managed services.

2.1.3 The Parties acknowledge that the Services may evolve over time. Changes to the Services, including expansions or reductions in space or service scope, shall follow the Change Management procedures in Article XIII.

### 2.2 Description of Premises

2.2.1 The Premises shall consist of:

- Approximately **15,000 square feet** of Grade A office space located on Levels 20 and 21 of the property at **88 Raffles Quay, Singapore 048952** (the “Building”);
- Access to shared common areas including lobby, restrooms, pantries, meeting rooms, and other facilities customarily made available to tenants of the Building;
- A dedicated secure server room of approximately **300 square feet**, inclusive of raised flooring, structured cabling, power distribution units, and adequate cooling capacity; and
- Allocated rights to parking bays and storage spaces as may be mutually agreed in writing.

2.2.2 The Client shall have the right to use the Premises solely for lawful corporate, administrative, professional, and technology‑related operations consistent with the classification of the Building and Applicable Law.

2.2.3 The Vendor shall provide to the Client quiet enjoyment of the Premises, subject to the Client’s timely payment of Fees and compliance with the terms of this Agreement.

### 2.3 Services to be Provided

2.3.1 **Facility Operation and Maintenance Services.** The Vendor shall provide:

(a) Operation and preventive maintenance of HVAC systems, electrical systems, fire detection and suppression systems, elevators serving the Premises, and other base‑building systems;  
(b) Regular cleaning of common areas and base‑building facilities, and periodic cleaning of the Client’s Premises as mutually agreed;  
(c) Security services including building access control, CCTV monitoring in common areas, and access badges for Client personnel;  
(d) Coordination of building management services, including liaison with building owner(s), municipal authorities, and utility providers.

2.3.2 **Fit‑Out and Configuration Services.** Subject to a separate mutually approved fit‑out plan and budget, the Vendor shall:

(a) Design and implement interior layouts, partitioning, lighting, cabling, and furniture setup;  
(b) Coordinate with approved contractors and ensure work is performed in accordance with Applicable Law, building regulations, and safety standards;  
(c) Deliver an acceptance report and as‑built documentation upon completion of fit‑out works.

2.3.3 **Managed IT and Infrastructure Support (If Ordered).** Where the Client elects to procure such services by written order or annex:

(a) Provide and maintain racks, power distribution (including UPS), structured cabling, and cooling in the dedicated server room;  
(b) Monitor environmental conditions (temperature, humidity) and power utilization;  
(c) Provide first‑level technical support to assist the Client in accessing and using the provided infrastructure, in accordance with Article XII.

2.3.4 **Ancillary Services.** The Vendor may offer additional services including reception, mail handling, on‑site support staff, and conference room services. Such services may be requested by the Client and shall be subject to additional Fees, as agreed in writing.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 The Vendor shall perform the Services with due skill, care, and diligence, in a professional manner consistent with best practices of reputable providers of similar services in Singapore, and in compliance with Applicable Law.

2.4.2 The Vendor shall ensure that building systems and utilities necessary for occupancy and business operations are maintained at levels sufficient for normal business use, subject to the Service Levels and Force Majeure provisions.

2.4.3 The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize disruptions to the Client’s use and occupancy of the Premises, provided that the Client acknowledges that occasional disruptions (e.g., due to maintenance, repairs, or upgrades) may be necessary and shall not constitute a breach if carried out in accordance with Article XII.

### 2.5 Client Responsibilities

2.5.1 The Client shall:

(a) Use the Premises and Services only in accordance with this Agreement and Applicable Law;  
(b) Promptly notify the Vendor of any defects, damages, or safety issues relating to the Premises or Services;  
(c) Maintain the interior of the Premises in good condition and repair, fair wear and tear excepted;  
(d) Comply with all building rules, regulations, and security policies provided by the Vendor from time to time.

2.5.2 The Client shall not:

(a) Use the Premises for any immoral, illegal, or hazardous activities;  
(b) Make structural alterations or material changes to the Premises without the Vendor’s prior written consent;  
(c) Interfere with or impair the proper functioning of any building systems or equipment.

### 2.6 Service Commencement and Transition

2.6.1 The Services and the lease of the Premises shall commence on the Effective Date, or if the Premises are not fit for occupation as of the Effective Date due solely to the Vendor’s delay, upon written notice from the Vendor that the Premises are ready for occupation. Any delay and its consequences shall be addressed via Change Management under Article XIII.

2.6.2 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Vendor shall, during the Transition Period (if any), provide reasonable assistance to enable an orderly handover, as further set forth in Section 4.6.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The total estimated contract value for the Term is **USD $2,672,056** (the **“Contract Value”**), which includes base rent, standard facility services, and anticipated ancillary services as detailed herein. The Contract Value is an estimate and may vary based on actual utilization, Change Orders, and other variable Fees.

### 3.2 Fee Components

3.2.1 **Base Rent.** The Client shall pay to the Vendor a monthly base rent for the Premises, which shall be allocated per square foot and specified in a fee schedule attached as **Annex A** (Fee Schedule). Base rent shall be payable monthly in advance.

3.2.2 **Service and Maintenance Fees.** In addition to base rent, the Client shall pay:

(a) Monthly facility service fees for cleaning, security, and base‑building maintenance;  
(b) Monthly infrastructure maintenance fees for the dedicated server room and related systems;  
(c) Additional support or managed services fees as mutually agreed and set forth in Annex A or any subsequent Change Order.

3.2.3 **Utilities and Pass‑Through Charges.** The Client shall be responsible for its proportionate share of utilities (electricity, water, gas, if any), as measured by sub‑meters or allocated on a reasonable basis. Such amounts shall be invoiced monthly in arrears.

3.2.4 **Ancillary Services.** Fees for ancillary services (e.g., meeting room bookings above allocation, additional security passes, temporary space, special cleaning) shall be charged as incurred and at the rates specified in Annex A or otherwise notified to the Client.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.3.1 The Vendor shall issue invoices to the Client on a monthly basis, or as otherwise specified for particular components, detailing the applicable Fees, Taxes, and any other charges.

3.3.2 Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all undisputed amounts shall be payable by the Client within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of the invoice (**“Net 60”**).

3.3.3 Payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) or such other currency as may be agreed, by bank transfer to the account designated by the Vendor in writing. All bank charges or transfer fees shall be borne by the Client.

3.3.4 If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice, the Client shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the disputed amount and reasons. The Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputed amount; no withholding of undisputed amounts shall be permitted.

### 3.4 Late Payment

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of actual payment at a rate of **1.5% per month** or the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, whichever is lower.

3.4.2 In addition to interest, the Vendor shall have the right, upon fifteen (15) Business Days’ written notice of default and failure by the Client to cure such non‑payment, to suspend all or part of the Services and, in severe cases, restrict access to non‑essential areas of the Premises, without prejudice to its other rights or remedies.

3.4.3 Persistent non‑payment (defined as failure to pay any undisputed amount within thirty (30) days after receipt of a written notice of default) shall constitute grounds for termination for cause under Section 4.3.

### 3.5 Taxes

3.5.1 All Fees are exclusive of Taxes unless expressly stated otherwise. The Client shall be responsible for all applicable Taxes arising from or in connection with this Agreement, other than taxes imposed on the Vendor’s net income.

3.5.2 If the Client is required by Applicable Law to withhold Tax from any payment, the Client shall:

(a) Deduct only the minimum amount of such withholding tax required;  
(b) Remit such withholding tax to the appropriate authority; and  
(c) Provide the Vendor with official receipts or other documentation evidencing such payment, enabling the Vendor to claim any available tax credit.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursements

3.6.1 Except as otherwise expressly stated, each Party shall bear its own internal costs of performing its obligations hereunder.

3.6.2 Out‑of‑pocket expenses reasonably incurred by the Vendor in connection with extraordinary requests or Client‑specific requirements (e.g., customized security systems, special permits, additional fit‑out) shall be reimbursable by the Client, provided that the Vendor has obtained the Client’s prior written approval for such expenses.

### 3.7 Adjustments

3.7.1 The Vendor may, upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to the Client, adjust the Fees annually to account for inflation, increases in operating costs, or changes in scope of Services, provided that any such adjustment is reasonable and consistent with market conditions.

3.7.2 If the Parties cannot agree on the adjustment, the matter shall be addressed under the Change Management and Dispute Resolution provisions (Articles XIII and XIV), and the prevailing Fees shall remain in effect until resolution.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **November 13, 2028** (the **“Expiration Date”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.1.2 The period between the Effective Date and the Expiration Date shall constitute the **Initial Lease Term**.

### 4.2 Renewal

4.2.1 The Parties may, no later than six (6) months prior to the Expiration Date, enter into good faith discussions regarding renewal or extension of this Agreement. Any renewal shall be subject to mutual written agreement, including revised Fees and terms, and shall be formalized via written amendment or new agreement.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for cause by giving written notice to the other Party, if:

(a) The other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; or  
(b) The other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver, administrator, or similar officer appointed in respect of its assets.

4.3.2 For the avoidance of doubt, persistent non‑payment by the Client as described in Section 3.4.3 shall be deemed a material breach.

### 4.4 Termination for Convenience

4.4.1 The Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, by providing not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Vendor.

4.4.2 The Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing not less than one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to the Client, provided that such termination shall not take effect earlier than the second anniversary of the Effective Date, unless otherwise mutually agreed.

4.4.3 Upon termination for convenience by the Client, the Client shall:

(a) Remain liable for all Fees accrued up to the effective date of termination;  
(b) Pay any agreed early termination fee or unamortized fit‑out costs as specified in Annex A or in the relevant fit‑out agreement; and  
(c) Pay all reasonable costs incurred by the Vendor in connection with demobilization and transition.

### 4.5 Immediate Termination in Certain Circumstances

4.5.1 Notwithstanding any other provision, the Vendor may, upon written notice, immediately terminate this Agreement or suspend performance if:

(a) The Client engages in illegal activities on the Premises;  
(b) The Client’s use of the Premises creates an imminent safety or security risk; or  
(c) A governmental authority orders cessation of the Client’s operations at the Premises for reasons attributable to the Client.

### 4.6 Effects of Termination; Transition

4.6.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) The Client shall promptly vacate the Premises and return possession to the Vendor in substantially the same condition as at the Effective Date, reasonable wear and tear excepted, subject to any approved alterations;  
(b) The Client shall remove its property, equipment, and data from the Premises, and repair any damage caused by such removal;  
(c) The Vendor shall have the right to retain any Client property left on the Premises after thirty (30) days from termination, and may dispose of such property at the Client’s cost, without liability.

4.6.2 Upon the Client’s request made at least sixty (60) days prior to the termination date, the Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period not exceeding ninety (90) days after termination (the **“Transition Period”**). Such assistance shall be provided at the Vendor’s then‑current rates, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4.6.3 Termination of this Agreement shall be without prejudice to any rights or obligations which have accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including payment obligations, indemnities, and confidentiality obligations.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

### 15.1 Definition

15.1.1 A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics or pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (other than those involving that Party’s own workforce), acts of government, or failure of utilities or telecommunications not caused by such Party.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The affected Party shall:

(a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the Force Majeure Event, providing reasonable details;  
(b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event on its performance;  
(c) Resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

15.3.1 To the extent a Party is prevented from performing its obligations due to a Force Majeure Event, such obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.3.2 If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days and substantially prevents performance of a material obligation, either Party may terminate the affected portion of the Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice.
