## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Description of Services.**  
Service Provider shall provide to Company the Services described in this Article II and any attached exhibits or statements of work (“SOWs”), which are hereby incorporated by reference. The Services shall include, without limitation:

  (a) **Data Hosting and Storage.** Provision of secure hosting, storage, backup, and retrieval of Company Data and Personal Data within a secure cloud environment maintained by Service Provider or its authorized Subprocessors, in accordance with Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy) and Article XII (Service Level Agreements).

  (b) **Software-as-a-Service Platform.** Access to and use of Service Provider’s proprietary multi-tenant software platform (the **“Platform”**) for data intake, processing, analytics, reporting, and visualization, accessible by Company via secure web interface, APIs, or other mutually agreed technical means.

  (c) **Data Processing and Integration.** Automated and manual data-processing services, including transformation, validation, normalization, encryption, pseudonymization (where applicable), and integration with Company’s internal systems (e.g., CRM, HRIS, ERP) via APIs or secure file transfer.

  (d) **User Management and Access Control.** Functionality enabling Company to create, manage, and deactivate user accounts, define user roles and permissions, and control access to Company Data and Personal Data in accordance with the minimum necessary principle and Article XI.

  (e) **Support and Maintenance.** Provision of technical support, maintenance, monitoring, and incident management, including troubleshooting, bug fixes, security patches, and updates, as further described in Article XII.

  (f) **Professional Services.** Implementation, configuration, training, and consulting services reasonably necessary to enable Company’s effective use of the Platform and Services, including initial onboarding and periodic optimization workshops.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, Service Provider shall provide the following Deliverables to Company:

  (a) **Implementation Plan.** A written implementation plan, including a detailed project schedule, milestones, resource assignments, and a data migration strategy, to be delivered within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date.

  (b) **Configuration Documentation.** Documentation describing Company-specific configurations, integrations, and workflows implemented within the Platform, to be updated upon material changes.

  (c) **Periodic Reports.** Periodic service performance and usage reports, including uptime statistics, incident summaries, and support ticket metrics, delivered on at least a quarterly basis.

  (d) **Training Materials.** Standard user guides, FAQs, and recorded or live training sessions sufficient to enable Company’s personnel to make productive use of the Services.

  (e) **Security and Compliance Materials.** Upon reasonable request, copies or summaries of relevant security policies, third-party audit reports (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001, if available), and data protection impact assessments (to the extent they relate to the Services).

2.3 **Timelines and Milestones.**  

  (a) **Initial Implementation.** Service Provider shall commence implementation activities promptly upon the Effective Date and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to complete the initial implementation and enable production use of the Services within ninety (90) days thereafter, subject to Company’s timely cooperation and provision of necessary information.

  (b) **Project Milestones.** The Parties shall mutually agree in writing (via SOW or project plan) on key milestones (e.g., completion of data mapping, integration testing, user acceptance testing, go‑live). Service Provider shall provide periodic status updates at least bi‑weekly during implementation and monthly thereafter.

  (c) **Service Commencement.** The Services shall be deemed to commence on the earlier of: (i) the date Company first uses the Services in a production environment; or (ii) ninety (90) days after the Effective Date, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  

Service Provider shall:

  (a) perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices for similar services;

  (b) devote such suitably skilled and experienced personnel, and such time and resources, as are reasonably required to perform the Services and meet the Service Levels in Article XII;

  (c) comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including Data Protection Laws, in connection with its performance of the Services and processing of Personal Data;

  (d) implement and maintain the technical and organizational security measures set forth in Article XI to protect Company Data and Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access;

  (e) promptly notify Company of any material issues that may reasonably be expected to adversely affect Service performance or jeopardize the security or integrity of Personal Data; and  

  (f) cooperate reasonably with Company’s audits, assessments, and requests for information relating to security and data protection, subject to the limitations in Article XI.

2.5 **Company Responsibilities.**  

Company shall:

  (a) provide Service Provider with accurate, complete, and timely information, assistance, and cooperation reasonably necessary for Service Provider to perform the Services;

  (b) ensure that its use of the Services, and its instructions to Service Provider regarding the Processing of Personal Data, comply with applicable laws and Data Protection Laws;

  (c) maintain and enforce appropriate internal policies and procedures for access control, password management, and secure use of the Services by its personnel;

  (d) be responsible for the accuracy, quality, and legality of Company Data and the means by which Company acquired such data; and  

  (e) promptly notify Service Provider of any unauthorized access or misuse of the Services or Company’s account credentials that it becomes aware of.

2.6 **Subprocessors.**  

Service Provider may engage Subprocessors to assist in providing the Services, provided that:

  (a) Service Provider shall impose data protection obligations on such Subprocessors offering at least the same level of protection as those set forth in this Agreement, including with respect to confidentiality, security, and data breach notification;

  (b) Service Provider shall remain fully liable to Company for the performance of any Subprocessor to the same extent as if the acts or omissions were those of Service Provider;

  (c) Service Provider shall maintain an up-to-date list of Subprocessors used for the Services and make such list reasonably available to Company upon request; and  

  (d) where required under Data Protection Laws, Service Provider shall provide Company with prior notice of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Subprocessors and shall allow Company to object on reasonable, data-protection-related grounds, in which case the Parties will work in good faith to resolve the objection.

2.7 **Service Modifications.**  

Service Provider may modify or update the Services from time to time, including to improve functionality, usability, security, or to comply with applicable laws, provided that such modifications do not materially degrade the overall functionality or security of the Services. Any material change to the scope of Services, fees, or Service Levels shall be handled in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management).

Service Provider shall not exercise its remedies under Section 3.3 with respect to the disputed portion of any invoice that is the subject of a bona fide dispute.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

Reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses (such as travel, lodging, and meals) incurred by Service Provider in connection with on-site services requested by Company shall be reimbursed by Company at actual cost, provided that:

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until **October 31, 2027** (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  

The Term may be extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties, signed by authorized representatives, at least sixty (60) days prior to the then-current expiration date. Absent such agreement, this Agreement shall automatically terminate at the expiration of the Term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

Given the low risk profile and client-friendly allocation of risk, Company may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, upon **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Service Provider. Service Provider may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Company, provided that Service Provider shall reasonably cooperate to facilitate an orderly transition as set forth in Section 4.6.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:

  (a) the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof (or such longer cure period as the non-breaching Party may grant in writing); or  

  (b) the other Party becomes the subject of a petition in bankruptcy or any other proceeding relating to insolvency, receivership, liquidation, or assignment for the benefit of creditors that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Data Protection or Security Concerns.**  

Company may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, immediately upon written notice to Service Provider if:

  (a) Service Provider experiences a material Personal Data Breach and fails to promptly and adequately remediate such breach in a manner reasonably acceptable to Company; or  

  (b) Service Provider repeatedly fails to comply with the data protection and security obligations set forth in Article XI, resulting in a material risk to the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects or to Company’s compliance with Data Protection Laws.

4.6 **Effects of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) **Cessation of Services.** Service Provider shall cease providing the Services on the effective date of termination, except to the extent necessary to perform post-termination obligations set forth herein.

  (b) **Return or Deletion of Data.** Service Provider shall, at Company’s written direction and subject to applicable law, either return to Company or securely delete all Company Data and Personal Data in its possession or control, in accordance with Section 11.10.

  (c) **Transition Assistance.** At Company’s request made prior to or within thirty (30) days following termination, Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance and cooperation to migrate Company Data to Company or its designated third party. Such assistance shall be provided at Service Provider’s then-current time and materials rates, unless termination was due to Service Provider’s uncured material breach, in which case reasonable transition assistance (up to a commercially reasonable cap to be agreed) shall be provided at no additional charge.

  (d) **Fees Upon Termination.** Company shall pay Service Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination. If Company terminates for Service Provider’s uncured material breach, Company shall have no obligation to pay for any Services not actually performed or for any unearned prepaid amounts, and Service Provider shall promptly refund any such prepaid but unearned Fees.

  (e) **Survival.** All provisions that by their nature should survive termination (including but not limited to Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI and Sections 3.3, 3.5, 4.6) shall so survive.

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  

“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**), whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information or the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business and marketing plans, pricing, financial information, product designs, technology and technical information, security reports, customer and supplier lists, and, in the case of Company, all Company Data and Personal Data.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  

The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving the Services under this Agreement;  

  (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement; and  

  (c) protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3 **Permitted Disclosures.**  

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:

  (a) to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, contractors, professional advisors, and Subprocessors who have a legitimate need to know such information for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  

  (b) as required by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided that, where legally permissible, the Receiving Party provides the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice and cooperates reasonably with any efforts to limit or challenge the required disclosure.

5.4 **Exceptions.**  

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  

  (b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party, without confidentiality restriction, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  

  (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  

  (d) is lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligation.

5.5 **Injunctive Relief.**  

The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief to prevent or curtail any actual or threatened breach of this Article V, without the necessity of posting bond or proving actual damages.

5.6 **Survival.**  

The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and remain in effect during the Term and for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that trade secrets and Personal Data shall remain subject to confidentiality obligations for as long as such information qualifies as a trade secret or as Personal Data under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XI – DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

11.1 **Roles of the Parties.**  

For purposes of Data Protection Laws, the Parties acknowledge and agree that:

  (a) Company is the “controller” (or equivalent term) with respect to Personal Data; and  

  (b) Service Provider acts as Company’s “processor” (or equivalent term) when Processing Personal Data on Company’s behalf in providing the Services.

11.2 **Processing Instructions.**  

Service Provider shall Process Personal Data only:

  (a) on documented instructions from Company, including with respect to cross-border transfers, unless required to do so by applicable law; and  

  (b) as necessary to provide the Services and perform its obligations under this Agreement.

If Service Provider is required by law to Process Personal Data beyond Company’s instructions, Service Provider shall inform Company of that legal requirement before Processing (unless prohibited by law).

11.3 **Compliance with Data Protection Laws.**  

Service Provider shall comply with all Data Protection Laws applicable to its performance of the Services. Company shall ensure that its use of the Services and its instructions to Service Provider comply with applicable law.

11.4 **Security Measures.**  

Taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, the nature, scope, context, and purposes of Processing, and the risks to the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects, Service Provider shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including as appropriate:

  (a) pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data;  

  (b) ensuring ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems and services;  

  (c) the ability to restore the availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident; and  

  (d) a process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures.

11.5 **Confidentiality of Personnel.**  

Service Provider shall ensure that persons authorized to Process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality and receive appropriate training on data protection and information security.

11.6 **Subprocessors & International Transfers.**  

Service Provider shall comply with Section 2.6 regarding Subprocessors. To the extent Personal Data is transferred from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland to a jurisdiction that does not provide an adequate level of data protection, the Parties shall implement appropriate data transfer mechanisms (such as standard contractual clauses) as required under Data Protection Laws.

11.7 **Data Subject Requests.**  

Taking into account the nature of the Processing, Service Provider shall assist Company by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as possible, in fulfilling Company’s obligations to respond to requests from Data Subjects to exercise their rights under Data Protection Laws (e.g., rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability, and objection). Service Provider shall promptly notify Company if it receives a request directly from a Data Subject relating to Personal Data Processed on Company’s behalf and shall not respond to such request except on Company’s documented instructions or as required by law.

11.8 **Regulatory Cooperation.**  

Service Provider shall, upon Company’s reasonable request, assist Company in fulfilling its obligations under Data Protection Laws relating to security, Personal Data Breaches, data protection impact assessments, and prior consultations with supervisory authorities, taking into account the nature of Processing and the information available to Service Provider.

11.9 **Personal Data Breach Notification.**  

In the event of a confirmed Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data Processed by Service Provider on behalf of Company, Service Provider shall:

  (a) without undue delay and in any event within forty-eight (48) hours of becoming aware of the Personal Data Breach, notify Company in writing, providing known details of the breach;  

  (b) promptly investigate the Personal Data Breach and take all reasonable steps to mitigate its adverse effects and to prevent recurrence;  

  (c) cooperate with Company and provide all reasonably requested information to enable Company to comply with its obligations under Data Protection Laws, including obligations to notify supervisory authorities and affected Data Subjects; and  

  (d) not notify any third party (other than Service Provider’s advisors or insurers) or Data Subjects of the Personal Data Breach unless expressly authorized by Company or required by applicable law (in which case Service Provider shall, where legally permitted, consult with Company prior to such notification).

11.10 **Data Retention and Deletion.**  

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or at any time upon Company’s written request, Service Provider shall, at Company’s choice and subject to applicable law:

  (a) return all Personal Data and Company Data to Company in a commonly used electronic format; and/or  

  (b) securely delete or anonymize all copies of Personal Data and Company Data in Service Provider’s possession or control, unless retention is required by applicable law or necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

Service Provider shall certify to Company in writing that such deletion or anonymization has occurred, upon request.

11.11 **Audit Rights.**  

Upon at least thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, and no more than once annually (unless required more frequently by a supervisory authority or in the aftermath of a material security incident), Company or its designated independent auditor may conduct a reasonable audit of Service Provider’s relevant facilities, systems, and documentation to verify compliance with this Article XI and applicable Data Protection Laws.   (a) be conducted during normal business hours;  

  (b) not unreasonably interfere with Service Provider’s business operations;  

  (c) be subject to reasonable confidentiality protections; and  

  (d) to the extent possible, rely in the first instance on existing third-party audit reports (e.g., SOC 2) and certifications made available by Service Provider.

Company shall bear its own costs and expenses of any such audit, unless the audit reveals a material breach of this Article XI, in which case Service Provider shall reimburse Company’s reasonable out-of-pocket audit expenses.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  

Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to make the Platform available with a **Monthly Uptime Percentage** of at least **99.9%**, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and excused downtime due to Force Majeure Events, as defined in Article XV.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  

Service Provider may perform scheduled maintenance on the Services, provided that:

  (a) Service Provider shall provide Company with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance expected to cause service unavailability;  

  (b) scheduled maintenance shall be conducted during off-peak hours where reasonably practicable; and  

  (c) Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize downtime and disruption.

12.3 **Incident Response Times.**  

Service Provider shall adhere to the following incident response targets:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of core service functionality or major security incident. Target initial response within **1 hour** and continuous efforts until resolution.  
  - **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of core services with no reasonable workaround. Target initial response within **4 hours** and resolution or workaround within **24 hours**.  
  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Moderate impact issues with a reasonable workaround. Target initial response within **1 Business Day** and resolution within **5 Business Days**.  
  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries. Target initial response within **2 Business Days** and resolution in a future release or as reasonably scheduled.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  

If Service Provider fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month, Company shall be entitled, upon written request within thirty (30) days after the end of such month, to the following service credits (as a percentage of the monthly subscription Fees applicable to the affected Services):

  - Monthly Uptime Percentage between 99.0% and 99.9%: **5%** credit  
  - Monthly Uptime Percentage between 98.0% and 98.99%: **10%** credit  
  - Monthly Uptime Percentage below 98.0%: **20%** credit

Service credits shall be applied to future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash. Service credits shall be Company’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the Uptime Commitment, without prejudice to any other rights arising from Service Provider’s material breach of this Agreement.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  

The Service Levels shall not apply to any unavailability or performance issues caused by:

  (a) factors outside Service Provider’s reasonable control, including Force Majeure Events;  

  (b) Company’s equipment, software, or network connections;  

  (c) misuse of the Services by Company or its users in violation of this Agreement; or  

  (d) Scheduled Maintenance conducted in accordance with Section 12.2.

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  

A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, epidemics, pandemics, terrorism, war, civil unrest, strikes or labor disputes (excluding those of the affected Party’s own employees), government actions, power failures, Internet or telecommunications outages (not caused by the affected Party), or other events of a similar nature.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  

The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  

  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  

  (c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of the progress of remediation.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  

During the continuation of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration impacted by the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party continues to comply with Section 15.2.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  

If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially prevents performance of a material part of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected portion of this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice, without liability (except for payment of Fees incurred prior to the effective date of termination).
