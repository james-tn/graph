## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vertex shall provide to Contoso comprehensive maintenance, support, and related professional services for the systems, equipment, and infrastructure identified by the Parties from time to time in applicable SOWs (collectively, the “**Supported Systems**”). These Supported Systems may include, without limitation:

- Data center servers, storage systems, and networking equipment;  
- Building management systems (BMS), HVAC units, electrical panels, UPS systems, and associated facilities infrastructure;  
- Enterprise software environments, including monitoring tools, maintenance management systems, and automation scripts; and  
- Any additional systems as may be agreed by the Parties in writing.

2.1.2 The Services shall be provided in a manner consistent with industry best practices for maintenance services, using appropriately skilled personnel, and in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**.

### 2.2 Preventive Maintenance

2.2.1 Vertex shall perform regularly scheduled preventive maintenance on the Supported Systems in accordance with maintenance schedules mutually agreed between the Parties and documented in applicable SOWs or maintenance plans. Preventive maintenance shall include, as applicable:

- Inspection of physical equipment for signs of wear, corrosion, overheating, or other damage;  
- Firmware and software patching, updates, and configuration reviews;  
- Cleaning, calibration, and performance optimization;  
- Validation of backups, redundancy, and failover mechanisms; and  
- Generation of preventive maintenance reports and recommendations.

2.2.2 Preventive maintenance activities shall be scheduled during agreed Maintenance Windows to minimize disruption to Contoso’s operations. Vertex shall provide written notice of scheduled preventive maintenance in accordance with **Section 12.4**.

### 2.3 Corrective Maintenance and Incident Response

2.3.1 Vertex shall provide corrective maintenance services to diagnose and resolve incidents affecting the Supported Systems, including failures, malfunctions, and performance degradation.

2.3.2 Vertex shall maintain an incident reporting and ticketing process through which Contoso may report issues. Each incident shall be classified according to severity levels (e.g., Critical, High, Medium, Low) as defined in the SLA schedule under **Article XII**.

2.3.3 Vertex shall meet or exceed the response and resolution times corresponding to each severity level, as set forth in the SLA. Response shall include acknowledgement of the incident and assignment of resources; resolution shall mean restoration of the affected Service to normal operating conditions or implementation of a workaround that materially mitigates the impact.

2.3.4 Where replacement parts, components, or Third-Party Materials are required to complete corrective maintenance, Vertex shall coordinate procurement in accordance with **Section 3.4 (Expenses and Reimbursements)** and any applicable SOW.

### 2.4 Predictive Maintenance and Monitoring

2.4.1 Where specified in an SOW, Vertex shall implement monitoring and predictive maintenance capabilities on the Supported Systems, including:

- Installation and configuration of monitoring agents, sensors, and dashboards;  
- Threshold-based and anomaly-based alerts for key performance and health indicators;  
- Periodic trend analysis to anticipate potential failures or capacity issues; and  
- Recommendations for upgrades, replacements, or configuration changes.

2.4.2 Vertex shall provide Contoso with periodic monitoring reports that include observed incidents, trends, risk assessments, and proposed remedial actions. Such reports shall be delivered at least quarterly, unless otherwise specified in an SOW.

### 2.5 Documentation and Reporting

2.5.1 Vertex shall maintain accurate and complete records of all maintenance activities, including preventive, corrective, and predictive maintenance, as well as any configuration changes, patches, or upgrades applied to the Supported Systems.

2.5.2 Vertex shall provide Contoso with maintenance reports on a monthly basis, unless otherwise agreed, containing at minimum:

- Summary of maintenance activities performed;  
- Incident logs with classification, cause analysis, and resolution details;  
- Changes implemented and configuration baselines;  
- Open issues and recommended next steps; and  
- SLA performance metrics, including uptime and response times.

2.5.3 Vertex shall make maintenance logs and related documentation available for audit by Contoso, upon reasonable advance notice and during normal Business Hours, in accordance with **Section 7.4 (Compliance with Laws and Standards)**.

### 2.6 Service Locations and Remote Support

2.6.1 Services shall be provided at Contoso’s designated facilities and data centers, as identified in applicable SOWs, and remotely from Vertex’s facilities as appropriate.

2.6.2 Vertex shall provide remote support using secure communication channels and tools that comply with Contoso’s security requirements and industry best practices. Remote access shall be strictly controlled and logged.

### 2.7 Service Exclusions

2.7.1 Unless expressly included in an SOW or agreed in a Change Order, the following are excluded from the scope of Services:

- Major system upgrades, migrations, or redesigns not described as maintenance in the applicable SOW;  
- Custom software development unrelated to maintenance;  
- Replacement of hardware or components where such costs are not expressly included in the Fees;  
- Support for third-party equipment or software not identified as part of the Supported Systems; and  
- On-site support outside of agreed Business Hours, except as specifically provided for emergency incidents in the SLA.

2.7.2 Any work performed outside the defined scope shall be subject to the Change Management procedure described in **Article XIII**, and may result in additional Fees and adjustments to timelines.

### 2.8 Performance Standards

2.8.1 Vertex shall perform the Services:

- With due care, skill, and diligence, and in a professional and workmanlike manner;  
- Using personnel with appropriate training, experience, and qualifications; and  
- In accordance with all Applicable Laws, industry standards, and Contoso’s reasonable policies communicated in writing to Vertex.

2.8.2 Failure to meet the Service Levels or performance standards shall entitle Contoso to the remedies set forth in **Article XII (SLA Remedies)** and, where applicable, the termination rights under **Article IV**.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **May 22, 2030** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “**Initial Term**”).

### 4.2 Renewal

4.2.1 The Agreement **shall not automatically renew**. Any extension or renewal beyond the Expiration Date shall require a written amendment or a new agreement signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

### 4.3 Termination for Convenience

4.3.1 Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part (including any SOW), for convenience, without cause, by providing at least **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vertex.

4.3.2 Vertex may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing at least **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Contoso; provided, however, that Vertex shall complete all critical or in-flight maintenance activities reasonably necessary to prevent material disruption to Contoso’s operations during such notice period.

4.3.3 In the event of termination for convenience, Contoso shall pay Vertex:

- All undisputed Fees for Services performed up to the effective date of termination; and  
- Any approved reimbursable expenses incurred up to such date.

No early termination penalties or minimum fees shall apply except as expressly agreed in an SOW for specific hardware or non-cancellable third-party commitments.

### 4.4 Termination for Cause

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any affected SOW) for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 Material breach by Vertex shall include, without limitation:

- Repeated failure to meet critical Service Levels under **Article XII**, resulting in significant adverse impact on Contoso’s operations;  
- Failure to maintain required insurance under **Article X**; or  
- Material violation of data protection obligations under **Article XI**.

4.4.3 Material breach by Contoso shall include, without limitation:

- Failure to pay undisputed Fees within thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment; or  
- Material breach of Confidentiality obligations under **Article V**.

### 4.5 Termination for Insolvency

4.5.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

- Becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, or is the subject of any proceeding related to its liquidation or insolvency that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or  
- Ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business.

### 4.6 Effect of Termination

4.6.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

- Vertex shall promptly cease performance of the Services, except as requested by Contoso for transition assistance under **Section 4.7**;  
- Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.5**; and  
- All unpaid Fees and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination shall become immediately due and payable, subject to Contoso’s right to dispute in good faith.

4.6.2 Any prepaid Fees for Services not yet performed as of the effective date of termination (other than termination for cause by Vertex due to Contoso’s uncured material breach) shall be refunded by Vertex to Contoso on a pro rata basis within thirty (30) days.

### 4.7 Transition Assistance

4.7.1 Upon Contoso’s request, Vertex shall provide reasonable transition assistance, for a period not to exceed ninety (90) days after termination or expiration, to facilitate the orderly transfer of Services to Contoso or a replacement vendor. Such assistance may include:

- Transfer of maintenance documentation and configurations;  
- Knowledge transfer sessions; and  
- Coordination with Contoso’s designated personnel or successor providers.

4.7.2 Transition assistance shall be provided at Vertex’s then-current time-and-materials rates, unless termination is due to Vertex’s uncured material breach, in which case a reasonable amount of transition assistance (up to forty (40) hours) shall be provided at no additional cost.

### 4.8 Survival

4.8.1 The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature should survive expiration or termination shall so survive, including without limitation: **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination.

5.6.1 The confidentiality obligations under this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**, except for trade secrets, which shall be protected for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

12.1.1 Vertex shall ensure that the Supported Systems, to the extent under Vertex’s direct control, achieve a monthly uptime of at least **99.9%**, excluding agreed Maintenance Windows and Force Majeure Events.

12.1.2 Uptime shall be calculated as:

> Uptime % = (Total Minutes in Month – Unscheduled Downtime) / Total Minutes in Month × 100

### 12.2 Response and Resolution Times

12.2.1 Vertex shall meet the following target response and resolution times:

- **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete outage or severe impact on critical operations  
  - Initial Response: within 1 hour (24x7)  
  - Target Resolution or Workaround: within 4 hours

- **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant impact with no feasible workaround  
  - Initial Response: within 2 hours (Business Hours)  
  - Target Resolution: within 1 Business Day

- **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact; workaround available  
  - Initial Response: within 4 Business Hours  
  - Target Resolution: within 3 Business Days

- **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor impact or informational requests  
  - Initial Response: within 1 Business Day  
  - Target Resolution: within 5 Business Days

### 12.3 SLA Credits and Remedies

12.3.1 If Vertex fails to meet the uptime commitment or response/resolution targets in any given calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits as follows, applied to the next invoice:

- Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: 3% of the monthly Fee for the affected Services;  
- Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: 7% of the monthly Fee;  
- Uptime below 98.0%: 12% of the monthly Fee.

12.3.2 Total service credits in any month shall not exceed 15% of the monthly Fees for the affected Services.

12.3.3 Service credits shall be Contoso’s exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to Contoso’s rights to terminate for chronic SLA failures under **Section 4.4.2**.

### 12.4 Maintenance Windows and Scheduled Downtime

12.4.1 Vertex may schedule planned maintenance during agreed Maintenance Windows, typically outside of Contoso’s peak business hours.

12.4.2 Vertex shall provide Contoso with at least five (5) Business Days’ prior notice for non-emergency maintenance that may impact availability.

12.4.3 Scheduled downtime within Maintenance Windows shall not count as Unscheduled Downtime for SLA calculations.
