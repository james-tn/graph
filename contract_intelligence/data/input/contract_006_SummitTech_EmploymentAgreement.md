## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Summit Tech.**  
Contoso hereby engages Summit Tech, and Summit Tech hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services described herein and in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. Each SOW shall be deemed incorporated into this Agreement by reference upon execution.

2.2 **Nature of Services.**  
The Services may include, without limitation:

  (a) **Professional Staffing and “Employment”-Type Services.** Provision of qualified technology professionals (including software engineers, systems analysts, project managers, DevOps engineers, cloud architects, and related roles) to perform work assignments for the benefit of Contoso, under Contoso’s day-to-day direction regarding business requirements, but under the general oversight of Summit Tech as their employer/contractor of record.

  (b) **Software Development and Integration.** Design, development, configuration, testing, and deployment of software components, applications, APIs, and system integrations that support Contoso’s business, including enhancements and maintenance of existing systems.

  (c) **Implementation and Migration.** Planning and execution of technology implementations, system rollouts, cloud migrations, and related transition tasks, including data mapping, cutover strategy, and post-go-live stabilization.

  (d) **Managed Services and Support.** Ongoing operational support, monitoring, and maintenance of designated systems or applications, including incident response, problem management, and performance optimization in accordance with the Service Levels.

  (e) **Consulting and Advisory Services.** Technology and strategic advisory services, including architecture review, security and compliance assessments, process improvement, and training of Contoso’s personnel on new systems or processes.

2.3 **Statements of Work.**  
Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify:

  (a) a detailed description of the Services and Deliverables;  
  (b) the applicable project plan, including key milestones and associated timelines;  
  (c) any dependencies, assumptions, or prerequisites for the Services;  
  (d) applicable Service Levels, if different from or additional to those in Article XII;  
  (e) the Fees, rate cards, and payment schedule; and  
  (f) acceptance criteria for Deliverables.

In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail, unless the SOW expressly states an intent to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Summit Tech shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services;  
  (b) in accordance with the specifications and timelines set forth in the applicable SOW;  
  (c) in compliance with Applicable Law and Contoso’s written policies provided in advance to Summit Tech, including security and workplace conduct policies applicable to contractors; and  
  (d) using personnel with appropriate expertise, experience, and qualifications.

Contoso acknowledges that project timelines may depend upon Contoso’s timely provision of Client Materials, information, approvals, and access to systems. Summit Tech shall promptly notify Contoso in writing if it reasonably believes that any delay or failure of Contoso to meet its obligations is likely to impact any timeline or Service Level.

2.5 **Personnel and Supervision.**  
Summit Tech shall have sole responsibility for the recruitment, hiring, compensation, benefits, supervision, and, where applicable, termination of its personnel performing the Services, including any subcontractors approved by Contoso in accordance with Section 16.8. Summit Tech shall:

  (a) ensure that all personnel assigned to perform the Services are legally authorized to work in the jurisdiction in which the Services are performed;  
  (b) cause its personnel to comply with all applicable security, safety, and access policies of Contoso while at any Worksite;  
  (c) promptly remove and replace, at Contoso’s reasonable request, any Summit Tech personnel whose conduct or performance materially fails to meet Contoso’s reasonable expectations, provided that Contoso provides a written explanation of such failure; and  
  (d) ensure that all personnel execute appropriate confidentiality and IP assignment agreements as necessary to effectuate the terms of this Agreement.

2.6 **Deliverables; Acceptance.**  
For each Deliverable identified in an SOW:

  (a) **Delivery.** Summit Tech shall deliver the Deliverable in accordance with the schedule set forth in the applicable SOW.  
  (b) **Review Period.** Unless otherwise provided in the SOW, Contoso shall have thirty (30) days from delivery (the **“Review Period”**) to review each Deliverable against the applicable specifications.  
  (c) **Acceptance or Rejection.** Within the Review Period, Contoso shall either (i) accept the Deliverable in writing, or (ii) provide Summit Tech with written notice of rejection, specifying in reasonable detail the basis for rejection. Failure to provide such written notice within the Review Period shall be deemed acceptance.  
  (d) **Correction.** If Contoso rejects a Deliverable, Summit Tech shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the deficiencies and re-deliver the Deliverable within a mutually agreed timeframe. Contoso shall then have a new Review Period to evaluate the corrected Deliverable. If, after at least two (2) correction attempts, the Deliverable still materially fails to conform to the specifications for reasons attributable to Summit Tech, Contoso may, as its sole and exclusive remedy (subject to Article IX), (x) accept the Deliverable subject to an equitable reduction in the corresponding Fee, or (y) reject the Deliverable and terminate the applicable portion of the SOW upon written notice, with a refund of any pre-paid Fees for the non-conforming Deliverable.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Contoso shall:

  (a) designate a project manager who shall be responsible for providing direction and decisions on behalf of Contoso;  
  (b) provide timely access to Client Materials, facilities, systems, and personnel as reasonably necessary for Summit Tech to perform the Services;  
  (c) review and respond to Summit Tech’s inquiries and requests for information within a reasonable time; and  
  (d) perform any other obligations expressly assigned to Contoso in the applicable SOW.

2.8 **No Minimum Commitment.**  
Unless expressly stated in an SOW, nothing in this Agreement obligates Contoso to order any minimum amount of Services or Deliverables. Contoso may engage other providers to perform services similar to the Services.

## ARTICLE III. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge and agree that the estimated aggregate value of this Agreement, including all SOWs anticipated as of the Effective Date, is Three Million Four Hundred Seventy-Five Thousand Two Hundred Ninety-Six U.S. Dollars (US $3,475,296) (the **“Contract Value”**). This Contract Value is an estimate and not a guarantee of any particular volume of Services.

3.2 **Fee Structures.**  
Fees under this Agreement may be structured as:

  (a) **Time-and-Materials (“T&M”).** Billing based on hourly or daily rates for Summit Tech personnel, as set forth in the applicable SOW;  
  (b) **Fixed Fee.** A fixed price for specified Deliverables or project phases;  
  (c) **Milestone-Based.** Payments triggered by completion and acceptance of defined milestones; or  
  (d) **Retainer or Monthly Recurring Fees.** Regular monthly or periodic charges for managed services or ongoing staffing.

Each SOW shall specify the applicable Fee structure.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  (a) Summit Tech shall invoice Contoso monthly in arrears for T&M Services and monthly or upon completion of applicable milestones for fixed-fee or milestone-based Services;  
  (b) Each invoice shall include reasonable detail, including SOW reference, period of performance, names or roles of personnel, hours worked (for T&M), and a description of the Services and Deliverables provided; and  
  (c) Invoices shall be sent electronically to the billing contact designated by Contoso or to such other address as Contoso may designate in writing.

3.4 **Payment Terms (Net 60).**  
Contoso shall pay undisputed amounts due under each invoice within sixty (60) days after Contoso’s receipt of a proper invoice (**“Net 60”**). Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by electronic funds transfer or other mutually agreed method.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Contoso reasonably disputes any portion of an invoice, Contoso shall provide written notice of the dispute within thirty (30) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the nature and amount of the dispute. Contoso shall timely pay any undisputed portion of such invoice. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts. Failure to dispute an invoice within such thirty (30)-day period shall not be deemed a waiver of any rights but may be considered evidence against any subsequent claim that such invoice was improper.

3.6 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law. Summit Tech shall not be entitled to suspend the Services for late payment unless: (i) the undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment; and (ii) Summit Tech has provided a further ten (10) days’ written notice of its intent to suspend for non-payment. Any suspension shall be limited to the Services affected by the overdue invoice.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Contoso shall reimburse Summit Tech for actual, reasonable, and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with Contoso’s travel and expense policy provided to Summit Tech in advance. Summit Tech shall provide receipts and reasonably detailed documentation for any expenses exceeding twenty-five dollars (US $25.00) or such other threshold agreed in writing.

3.8 **Taxes.**  
Fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Contoso shall be responsible for all Taxes imposed on the Services, except for taxes based on Summit Tech’s income, property, or employment-related taxes. If Summit Tech is required to collect Taxes from Contoso, Summit Tech shall itemize such Taxes on the applicable invoice and Contoso shall pay such amounts in accordance with this Article III.

3.9 **No Withholding.**  
Payments under this Agreement shall be made without withholding or deduction for any taxes, charges, or withholdings, except as required by Applicable Law. If any such withholding is required, Contoso shall gross-up the payment such that Summit Tech receives the full amount it would have received absent such withholding.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may extend the Term by mutual written agreement executed prior to the Expiration Date. Any such extension shall be on terms mutually agreed and set forth in a written amendment.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Given the low risk profile and client-preferred terms, Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Summit Tech. Summit Tech may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Contoso.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if:

  (a) the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach; or  
  (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

  (a) Summit Tech shall promptly cease performing the affected Services, except as necessary for an orderly wind-down if requested by Contoso;  
  (b) Contoso shall pay Summit Tech for all Services properly performed and accepted, and for reimbursable expenses incurred, through the effective date of termination, subject to Contoso’s rights under Section 3.5;  
  (c) each Party shall return or, at the disclosing Party’s written election, destroy (and certify such destruction) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to Section 5.6 and Section 11.9; and  
  (d) the rights and licenses granted under Article VI shall continue or terminate as expressly provided therein.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
At Contoso’s request, Summit Tech shall provide reasonable transition services for up to sixty (60) days following termination or expiration of this Agreement or an applicable SOW, at Summit Tech’s then-current T&M rates, to ensure an orderly transfer of Services to Contoso or a successor provider. The Parties shall agree in writing on the scope and Fees for such transition services.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The following provisions shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement: Sections 3.4–3.9, 4.5–4.7, and Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (with respect to accrued credits or remedies), XIV, XV, and XVI, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE V. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“**Confidential Information**” means any non-public information, in whatever form, disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**) that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business plans, financial information, pricing, trade secrets, customer lists, marketing strategies, technical data, software (including source code), specifications, designs, security information, and the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party, without confidentiality restriction, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  (c) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Obligations of Confidentiality.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely as necessary to perform its obligations or exercise its rights under this Agreement;  
  (b) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care;  
  (c) not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted herein; and  
  (d) limit access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by Applicable Law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted, promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing and cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

5.5 **Injunctive Relief.**  
The Receiving Party acknowledges that any unauthorized use or disclosure of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief, without the necessity of posting bond or proving actual damages, in the event of any such unauthorized use or disclosure.

5.6 **Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.**  
The obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of five (5) years after the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that Confidential Information constituting trade secrets shall be maintained as confidential for so long as such information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XII. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

12.1 **General.**  
Where Summit Tech provides ongoing managed services, hosting, or support Services under an SOW, the Service Levels set forth in this Article XII and any applicable SOW shall apply. In the event of a conflict, the SOW shall prevail.

12.2 **Uptime Commitment.**  
For hosted or managed systems specified in an SOW, Summit Tech shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the covered services are available at least ninety-nine point nine percent (99.9%) of the time during each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Permitted Downtime (as defined in the SOW). Availability shall be measured based on external monitoring or other mutually agreed metrics.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Summit Tech shall adhere to the following incident response and resolution targets for production-impacting issues, unless otherwise agreed in an SOW:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of service or major functionality affecting all users.  
    - Initial Response: within one (1) hour  
    - Workaround or Restoration Target: within four (4) hours  

  - **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation affecting a subset of users or key functionality.  
    - Initial Response: within two (2) hours  
    - Workaround or Restoration Target: within one (1) Business Day  

  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues, partial loss of functionality with acceptable workaround.  
    - Initial Response: within one (1) Business Day  
    - Resolution Target: within five (5) Business Days  

  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, general inquiries.  
    - Initial Response: within two (2) Business Days  
    - Resolution Target: as scheduled in the normal release cycle.

12.4 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
If Summit Tech fails to meet the uptime commitment or other Service Levels in a calendar month, Contoso may be eligible for service credits as follows (unless a different credit schedule is set out in an SOW):

  (a) Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly Fees for the affected Service;  
  (b) Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly Fees;  
  (c) Availability below 98.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly Fees.

Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice and shall constitute Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the applicable Service Levels, subject to Contoso’s termination rights under Article IV and any recurring or material breaches.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
Service Levels shall not apply to, and Summit Tech shall have no liability for, failures caused by: (a) Contoso’s misuse or unauthorized modification of the Services; (b) failures of Contoso’s equipment, networks, or third-party services not under Summit Tech’s control; or (c) Force Majeure Events.

## ARTICLE XV. FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event beyond a Party’s reasonable control that prevents or delays such Party’s performance under this Agreement, including but not limited to: acts of God; natural disasters; acts of war, terrorism, civil unrest, or riots; strikes, labor disputes, or industrial disturbances (excluding those involving the non-performing Party’s own employees where such disputes are reasonably within its control); pandemics, epidemics, or public health emergencies; government orders or restrictions; and failures or interruptions of utilities or telecommunications not caused by the non-performing Party.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
A Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  (a) promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature and expected duration of the event and the anticipated impact on its performance; and  
  (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the Party continues to comply with Section 15.2. The time for performance shall be extended by a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially affects the performance of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or, if the entire Agreement is impacted, this Agreement, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without liability other than payment for Services properly performed prior to the effective date of termination.
