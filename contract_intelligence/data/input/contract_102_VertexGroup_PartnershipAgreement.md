## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Vertex shall provide to Contoso a combination of professional consulting, systems integration, and managed support services designed to support Contoso’s technology operations and digital initiatives (the “Services”). The Services shall include, without limitation:

  a. **Technology Assessment and Consulting.** Vertex shall conduct an initial discovery and assessment of Contoso’s existing technology environment, applications, infrastructure, and processes. This includes stakeholder interviews, system inventory, architecture review, and gap analysis. Vertex shall prepare written recommendations, prioritization of initiatives, and a high-level roadmap.

  b. **System Integration and Implementation.** Vertex shall design, configure, and implement identified software and integration solutions as set forth in the applicable SOW(s). This may include middleware implementation, API development, data migration, and integration with existing platforms used by Contoso.

  c. **Custom Configuration and Development.** Where specified in a SOW, Vertex shall perform limited customization or configuration of software platforms, including, but not limited to, building custom workflows, reports, dashboards, and scripts consistent with Contoso’s requirements.

  d. **Managed Support Services.** Vertex shall provide ongoing support, incident management, and maintenance services for the implemented solutions in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**. This shall include troubleshooting, patch management, routine monitoring, and escalation management.

  e. **Knowledge Transfer and Training.** Vertex shall provide training sessions (remote and/or on-site as mutually agreed), documentation, and knowledge transfer to Contoso personnel to enable effective use and day-to-day administration of implemented solutions.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vertex shall deliver to Contoso the following Deliverables, as further detailed in applicable SOW(s):

  a. **Assessment Report and Roadmap.** A written report summarizing the findings of the initial technology assessment, including current-state architecture diagrams, identified risks, and a prioritized roadmap of recommended improvements.

  b. **Solution Design Documentation.** Detailed technical and functional design documents, including system architecture diagrams, integration specifications, data flow diagrams, and configuration specifications.

  c. **Configured and Integrated Systems.** Deployed and configured instances of the identified solutions, integrated with Contoso’s designated systems and data sources, in a non-production environment and subsequently in production as mutually agreed.

  d. **Testing Artifacts.** Test plans, test cases, and test results related to system integration, user acceptance testing (UAT), performance testing, and security testing, as applicable.

  e. **Operational Runbooks and User Guides.** Documentation describing operational procedures, incident handling workflows, escalation paths, backup and recovery processes, and user-facing instructions for the implemented systems.

  f. **Training Materials.** Presentations, manuals, and other materials used for training Contoso’s staff.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
Vertex shall perform the Services:

  a. in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards for similar services;

  b. in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including data protection laws as set forth in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**;

  c. in accordance with the timeframes, milestones, and Service Levels specified in this Agreement and any SOW; and

  d. using personnel with appropriate skill, experience, and qualifications.

Failure to meet any Service Levels shall entitle Contoso to the remedies set forth in **Article XII**, without prejudice to any other rights or remedies under this Agreement.

2.4 **Project Plan and Milestones.**  
Within fifteen (15) Business Days following the Effective Date, or as stated in the applicable SOW, Vertex shall develop and submit to Contoso a detailed project plan outlining the key tasks, milestones, dependencies, and indicative timelines for the Services (the “Project Plan”). The Project Plan shall:

  a. define major phases such as discovery, design, build, testing, deployment, and stabilization;

  b. identify milestone acceptance points at which Contoso shall review and accept or reasonably reject Deliverables, such acceptance not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed;

  c. specify Contoso responsibilities (e.g., provision of subject matter experts, timely approvals, access to systems, and data).

The Parties shall mutually agree in writing on the Project Plan, which may be updated by mutual written agreement or pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.5 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  

  a. **Submission.** Upon completion of a Deliverable or milestone, Vertex shall notify Contoso in writing and provide all associated materials, documentation, and access necessary to review and test such Deliverable.

  b. **Review Period.** Unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW, Contoso shall have fifteen (15) Business Days (the “Review Period”) to review and test the Deliverable and either (i) provide written acceptance, or (ii) provide a written notice of rejection specifying in reasonable detail the deficiencies and the applicable requirements that such Deliverable failed to meet.

  c. **Correction of Deficiencies.** If Contoso rejects a Deliverable, Vertex shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, promptly correct the identified deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable for review. Contoso shall then have an additional Review Period to review the corrected Deliverable.

  d. **Deemed Acceptance.** If Contoso fails to respond within the Review Period and does not request an extension in writing, the Deliverable will be deemed accepted, provided that Vertex has supplied the Deliverable in accordance with the requirements of the applicable SOW.

2.6 **Contoso Responsibilities.**  
Contoso shall:

  a. provide Vertex with reasonable access, during normal business hours and upon reasonable notice, to Contoso’s relevant personnel, systems, and facilities necessary for the performance of the Services;

  b. supply accurate and complete information and data reasonably required by Vertex to perform the Services, and timely review and provide feedback on Deliverables;

  c. designate a project manager or primary contact to coordinate with Vertex and facilitate communication, approvals, and escalations;

  d. be responsible for obtaining any necessary licenses for third-party software or systems not expressly provided by Vertex under this Agreement, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.7 **Subcontractors.**  
Vertex may use subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that (a) Vertex remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of such subcontractors, (b) any subcontractors with access to Confidential Information or Customer Data are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less restrictive than those set forth in this Agreement, and (c) Vertex shall remain Contoso’s sole point of contact and shall be liable for fulfillment of all obligations under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until the **Expiration Date of July 04, 2026**, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV (the “Term”).

The Parties may mutually agree in writing to renew this Agreement for additional one-year terms or other mutually agreed periods. Any renewal shall be subject to agreed revisions to Fees, scope, and other terms as set forth in a written amendment executed by both Parties.

  a. **By Contoso.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, upon **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Vertex.

  b. **By Vertex.** Vertex may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Contoso, provided that Vertex shall complete any critical transition activities as reasonably requested by Contoso.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  a. **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice of such breach, specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable detail.

  b. **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent, (ii) admits in writing its inability to pay debts as they become due, (iii) makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, (iv) files or has filed against it any bankruptcy, reorganization, or similar proceeding which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or (v) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  a. Vertex shall promptly cease all work (except as necessary for agreed transition assistance) and shall deliver to Contoso all completed Deliverables and any in-progress Work Product that Contoso has paid for or is obligated to pay for;

  b. Contoso shall pay Vertex for all Services properly performed, Deliverables accepted, and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to any valid offsets or credits;

  c. All licenses granted by Vertex to Contoso under this Agreement shall continue in effect in accordance with **Article VI**, except in case of Contoso’s uncured material breach where Vertex may terminate such licenses to the extent permitted under Section 6.2;

  d. Each Party shall promptly return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**;

  e. The provisions of this Agreement that, by their nature, should survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI**.

4.6 **Transition Assistance.**  
For up to thirty (30) days following expiration or termination (or such longer period as the Parties may reasonably agree), Vertex shall provide Contoso with transition assistance, including reasonable cooperation to transfer knowledge, documentation, and access, at Vertex’s then-applicable time-and-materials rates, unless such termination results from Vertex’s uncured material breach, in which case reasonable transition assistance shall be provided at no additional charge to Contoso.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
For any managed services or hosted solutions operated by Vertex on Contoso’s behalf, Vertex shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such services are available at least **99.9% of the time** during each calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and permitted downtime as set forth herein (“Availability Commitment”).

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Vertex may perform scheduled maintenance that may affect service availability, provided that:

  a. Vertex shall provide Contoso with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance window expected to result in service unavailability;

  b. Scheduled maintenance shall, where commercially reasonable, be performed outside Contoso’s normal business hours in the relevant time zone, or as otherwise agreed by the Parties.

12.3 **Incident Response and Resolution Times.**  
Vertex shall respond to and use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents within the following target timeframes:

  a. **Priority 1 (Critical):** Service is unavailable or severely impacted.

  - Response Time: within 1 hour of notification (24x7).  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 4 hours.

  b. **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation of service with major functionality impacted, but a workaround may exist.

  - Response Time: within 2 hours during Business Days.  
  - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 1 Business Day.

  c. **Priority 3 (Medium):** Limited impact with minor degradation; service remains operational.

  - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours.  
  - Target Resolution: within 3 Business Days.

  d. **Priority 4 (Low):** Non-critical requests, general inquiries, or minor issues.

  - Response Time: within 1 Business Day.  
  - Target Resolution: as agreed, in a future maintenance release.

12.4 **SLA Credits.**  
If Vertex fails to meet the Availability Commitment in any calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits as follows:

  - Availability between 99.0% and 99.9%: credit equal to 5% of the monthly managed services Fees for the affected services;  
  - Availability between 98.0% and 99.0%: credit equal to 10% of the monthly managed services Fees;  
  - Availability below 98.0%: credit equal to 20% of the monthly managed services Fees.

Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) issued by Vertex. Service credits shall be Contoso’s sole and exclusive remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to Contoso’s rights to terminate for repeated SLA breaches pursuant to **Article IV**.

12.5 **Exclusions.**  
SLA commitments shall not apply to unavailability or service degradation due to: (a) Scheduled Maintenance; (b) factors outside Vertex’s reasonable control, including Force Majeure Events; (c) Contoso’s or its third-party providers’ acts or omissions; (d) Customer’s failure to comply with this Agreement; or (e) beta or trial services expressly identified as such.

## FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A “Force Majeure Event” means an event or circumstance beyond a Party’s reasonable control that prevents or materially delays its performance under this Agreement, including, but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, earthquakes, floods, fires, epidemics, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes, labor disputes (excluding those involving the non-performing Party’s employees), embargoes, or governmental actions or orders.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

  a. promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;

  b. use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event to the extent performance is prevented or materially delayed. The time for performance of such obligations shall be extended for a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement or the affected SOW upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without further liability, except for payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
