## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client a hosted, cloud-based data analytics platform (the **“Platform”**) and related professional and support services as described in this **Article II** and any applicable SOWs. Such Services shall include, without limitation: (a) access to Vendor’s proprietary analytics platform for processing and analyzing Client’s data; (b) integration of the Platform with Client’s designated systems and data sources; (c) configuration and customization as mutually agreed; (d) ongoing platform maintenance, monitoring, and updates; and (e) technical support in accordance with **Article XII**.

2.1.2 The Services shall be provided remotely through Vendor’s data centers or approved third-party cloud infrastructure providers, unless an SOW provides otherwise. Vendor shall be responsible for managing the hosting environment required to deliver the Services, including hardware, network, and operating system infrastructure, subject to the Service Levels specified herein.

### 2.2 Specific Deliverables

2.2.1 Initial Implementation Deliverables. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor shall, during the initial implementation phase (which is expected to be completed within one hundred twenty (120) days of the Effective Date, subject to Client’s timely cooperation):

- (a) Perform an initial requirements and architecture assessment of Client’s data sources and existing systems;
- (b) Configure the Platform to receive and process data from Client’s specified systems via APIs, secure file transfer, or other agreed methods;
- (c) Develop and deliver up to ten (10) standardized analytics dashboards and associated reporting templates tailored to Client’s specified business use cases;
- (d) Provide user and administrator documentation, including an online knowledge base, sufficient to permit trained Client users to access and utilize the Platform;
- (e) Conduct remote training sessions (up to sixteen (16) hours) for designated Client personnel on use of the Platform and dashboards; and
- (f) Migrate and validate initial data sets provided by Client, and conduct a user acceptance testing (UAT) cycle pursuant to **Section 2.5**.

2.2.2 Ongoing Service Deliverables. During the Term, Vendor shall:

- (a) Maintain the availability of the Platform in accordance with the uptime commitments in **Section 12.2**;
- (b) Provide technical support to Client’s designated administrators and power users per **Section 12.3**;
- (c) Provide periodic performance and usage reports, including uptime metrics, reported Incidents, and key system performance indicators, on at least a quarterly basis;
- (d) Implement mutually agreed minor enhancements and configuration changes through the Change Management procedures set forth in **Article XIII**; and
- (e) Provide security patches, bug fixes, and upgrades to the Platform as part of routine maintenance, subject to **Section 12.4**.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices for providers of similar services, and consistent with the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

2.3.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Platform is free of material defects that would prevent the Platform from functioning in accordance with the documentation provided to Client. Vendor shall promptly correct reproducible errors reported by Client in accordance with the Incident classification and response times set forth in **Section 12.3**.

2.3.3 Vendor shall implement and maintain reasonable administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect the security, confidentiality, and integrity of Client Data, including Personal Data, as more fully described in **Article XI**.

### 2.4 Client Responsibilities

2.4.1 Client shall provide Vendor with timely access to relevant information, personnel, systems, and data as reasonably required for Vendor to perform the Services. Client shall be responsible for ensuring that such information and data are accurate and complete to the best of Client’s knowledge.

2.4.2 Client shall designate at least one (1) primary contact and one (1) backup contact to serve as Client’s project manager and point of contact for coordination with Vendor. Client shall ensure that its personnel attend scheduled meetings, training sessions, and review sessions as reasonably requested.

2.4.3 Client shall be responsible for (a) procuring and maintaining its own Internet connectivity and end-user devices; (b) maintaining the security of its user credentials, passwords, and access methods; and (c) complying with all reasonable usage guidelines and security requirements provided by Vendor.

2.4.4 Client shall not, and shall not permit any third party to, (a) access or use the Platform in a manner that violates Applicable Law; (b) reverse engineer, decompile, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code of the Platform, except to the extent permitted by Applicable Law; or (c) use the Services to store or transmit malicious code.

### 2.5 Acceptance of Implementation Deliverables

2.5.1 For Deliverables provided as part of initial implementation or any project-based SOW, Client shall have fourteen (14) Business Days following Vendor’s delivery of each Deliverable (the **“Acceptance Period”**) to review and test the Deliverable for material conformity with the applicable specifications set forth in the SOW.

2.5.2 If Client, in good faith, determines that a Deliverable does not materially conform to the applicable specifications, Client shall notify Vendor in writing prior to the end of the Acceptance Period, specifying in reasonable detail the nature of the non-conformity. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to remedy such non-conformity within a reasonable time, and thereafter resubmit the Deliverable for re-testing. The Parties shall follow a similar process for re-testing, provided that Client shall not unreasonably withhold or delay acceptance.

2.5.3 Any Deliverable shall be deemed accepted if (a) Client provides written acceptance; or (b) Client fails to provide Vendor with a written rejection stating material non-conformities prior to the expiration of the applicable Acceptance Period.

### 2.6 Service Modifications and Roadmap

2.6.1 Vendor may, from time to time, modify the Services (including by adding, removing, or altering features or functionalities) as part of its ongoing product roadmap, provided that such modifications do not materially degrade the overall functionality of the Services as they exist as of the Effective Date.

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 The term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **June 29, 2029** (the **“Initial Term”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party (the **“Termination for Convenience Notice Period”**).

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall remain liable for: (a) all Fees accrued and payable through the effective date of termination; and (b) any non-cancellable commitments expressly identified in the applicable SOW or order form, provided that Vendor uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate such commitments.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement in its entirety upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment) after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

4.3.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or is the subject of any liquidation or insolvency proceeding (that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days); or (b) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination or Expiration

4.4.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason: (a) all rights of Client to access and use the Platform shall immediately cease, except as expressly provided in **Section 4.4.3**; and (b) Client shall promptly cease all access and use of the Services and any non-Client-owned Deliverables.

4.4.2 Client shall pay Vendor all undisputed amounts due and payable as of the effective date of termination or expiration, including any amounts owed under **Section 4.2.2** and any Fees for Services performed up to such date.

4.4.3 Data Return and Export. For a period of thirty (30) days following the effective date of termination or expiration (the **“Data Retrieval Period”**), Vendor shall make available to Client, upon request, a copy of Client’s data stored in the Platform, in a commercially reasonable format. After the Data Retrieval Period, Vendor may delete Client’s data in accordance with **Article XI**, except for any data retained by Vendor as required by Applicable Law or for archival or backup purposes subject to confidentiality obligations.

4.4.4 Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not affect any accrued rights or liabilities of either Party, nor any provision of this Agreement that, by its nature, should reasonably be understood to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI** and all payment obligations under **Article III** that accrued prior to termination.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

### 12.1 General

12.1.1 Vendor shall provide the Services in accordance with the Service Levels set forth in this **Article XII**. Except as expressly provided herein, Client’s sole and exclusive remedy for any failure to meet the Service Levels (a **“Service Level Failure”**) shall be the award of Service Credits as described in **Section 12.4**, without prejudice to Client’s other rights in the event of repeated or material failures.

### 12.2 Uptime Commitment

12.2.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Platform is available **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding Permitted Downtime (the **“Uptime Commitment”**).

12.2.2 “Permitted Downtime” includes: (a) scheduled maintenance windows (not to exceed four (4) hours per calendar month, with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice to Client, where feasible); (b) emergency maintenance required to address critical security or operational issues; (c) downtime caused by Force Majeure Events; and (d) downtime attributable to Client’s systems, networks, or third-party providers not under Vendor’s control.

### 12.3 Incident Classification and Response

12.3.1 Vendor shall categorize Incidents reported by Client as follows:

- **Priority 1 (Critical)**: Complete outage or significant impact to core functionality affecting the majority of users.  
  - Initial Response: within one (1) hour, 24x7.  
  - Work Continuity: continuous work until resolution or workaround.

- **Priority 2 (High)**: Major functionality impaired with no reasonable workaround, but platform still accessible.  
  - Initial Response: within four (4) Business Hours.  
  - Work Continuity: business hours until resolution.

- **Priority 3 (Medium)**: Partial loss of non-critical functionality or minor performance issues.  
  - Initial Response: within one (1) Business Day.  
  - Work Continuity: reasonable efforts during business hours.

- **Priority 4 (Low)**: Cosmetic issues, general inquiries, or requests for enhancements.  
  - Initial Response: within two (2) Business Days.

### 12.4 Service Credits

12.4.1 In the event Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in a given calendar month (excluding Permitted Downtime), Client shall be entitled to Service Credits calculated as a percentage of the monthly subscription Fees for the affected Services, as follows:

- Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: **5%** credit  
- Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: **10%** credit  
- Uptime below 98.0%: **20%** credit

12.4.2 To receive Service Credits, Client must submit a written request within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the Service Level Failure occurred. Service Credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash.

12.4.3 The total Service Credits in any calendar quarter shall not exceed twenty-five percent (25%) of the subscription Fees payable by Client for that quarter.

12.4.4 Repeated Service Level Failures resulting in Uptime below 98% for three (3) consecutive months shall constitute a material breach by Vendor, entitling Client to pursue its remedies under **Section 4.3** and **Article XIV**.
