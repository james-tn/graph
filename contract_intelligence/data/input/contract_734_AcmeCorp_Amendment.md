## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK  

2.1 **Overview of Services.** Vendor shall provide to Client comprehensive technology implementation and managed services for Client’s enterprise business systems, including but not limited to application integration, cloud environment setup, ongoing monitoring and support, and related consulting. The Services are intended to support Client’s internal operations and external customer-facing platforms across North America and, where agreed in writing, globally.

2.2 **Implementation Services.**  

2.2.1 **Planning and Design.** Vendor shall, in collaboration with Client, develop a detailed Project Plan describing major phases, milestones, dependencies, and responsibilities. The Project Plan shall include, at a minimum:

- Business requirements and system requirements documentation;  
- High-level and detailed solution architecture;  
- Integration design with Client’s existing systems;  
- Data migration strategy and cutover plan; and  
- Risk register and mitigation strategies.

Vendor shall submit the initial draft Project Plan to Client for review and approval within thirty (30) days after the Effective Date. No material deviation from an approved Project Plan shall be implemented except pursuant to a Change Order under **Article XIII**.

2.2.2 **Configuration and Integration.** Vendor shall configure the agreed software platforms and tools to meet the requirements provided by Client, including:  

- Setting up and configuring cloud environments (e.g., production, staging, test);  
- Implementing integration interfaces to Client’s ERP, CRM, identity management, and data warehouse systems; and  
- Establishing monitoring, logging, and alerting mechanisms consistent with industry best practices.

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid downtime in production environments during configuration activities and shall coordinate any necessary maintenance windows with Client in advance.

2.2.3 **Testing and Acceptance.** Vendor shall perform unit, integration, system, and performance testing as appropriate. Upon completion of each major phase identified in the Project Plan, Vendor shall deliver to Client:

- Test plans and test cases;  
- Test results and defect logs; and  
- A written certification that the relevant Deliverables conform in all material respects to the agreed specifications.

Client shall have a reasonable period, not less than ten (10) Business Days, to review and either accept or reject the Deliverables associated with each phase. If Client identifies material non-conformities, Vendor shall promptly correct such non-conformities at no additional charge and resubmit the Deliverables for Client’s review, in accordance with the acceptance and cure processes described in the Master Agreement.

2.3 **Managed Services and Support.**

2.3.1 **Operational Support.** Following completion of the initial implementation and any agreed stabilization period, Vendor shall provide ongoing managed services for the supported systems, including, at a minimum:

- Monitoring system health and performance on a 24x7 basis;  
- Applying agreed patches and updates in coordination with Client;  
- Managing user access requests and role-based access controls;  
- Performing routine backups and disaster recovery readiness checks; and  
- Providing second-level support for incidents escalated by Client’s help desk.

2.3.2 **Service Desk and Incident Management.** Vendor shall operate a ticketing and incident management process with clearly defined severity levels and response/resolution times as further set forth in **Article XII**. Vendor shall provide Client with access to a ticketing portal or equivalent mechanism for logging and tracking incidents and service requests.

2.3.3 **Maintenance and Upgrades.** Vendor shall perform scheduled maintenance and upgrades of the managed environments, coordinating with Client to:

- Minimize disruption to business operations;  
- Notify Client at least five (5) Business Days in advance of any planned downtime, except in emergencies; and  
- Provide post-maintenance reports upon Client’s request.

2.4 **Deliverables.**  

Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor’s Deliverables under this Amendment shall include, as applicable:

- Project Plan and implementation schedule;  
- Solution architecture diagrams;  
- Configuration documentation, including parameter settings and integrations;  
- User guides and standard operating procedures;  
- Test plans, test results, and acceptance documentation;  
- Operational run books and incident response playbooks;  
- Monthly performance reports, including uptime, incident statistics, SLA performance, and trends.

Each Deliverable shall be prepared in a professional and workmanlike manner and in accordance with industry standards and the specifications set out in the Master Agreement, this Amendment, and any applicable statement of work.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform all Services in a diligent, professional, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with (a) generally accepted industry standards, (b) the Service Levels, (c) the Project Plan, and (d) all Applicable Laws. 

2.5.2 Vendor shall ensure that personnel assigned to perform the Services possess the requisite skills, experience, and qualifications.

2.5.3 Vendor shall comply with Client’s written policies and procedures provided to Vendor in advance, including information security and acceptable use policies, when accessing Client systems or facilities.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  

Client shall:

- Provide Vendor with timely access to relevant systems, data, and personnel reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;  
- Review and provide feedback on Deliverables and Project Plans within a reasonable timeframe;  
- Timely perform any Client responsibilities identified in the Project Plan or applicable statement of work.

Vendor’s obligations that are expressly dependent on Client’s performance of its responsibilities shall be excused to the extent Vendor is materially prevented from performing solely due to Client’s failure to perform such responsibilities, provided Vendor has given Client prompt written notice of such failure.

2.7 **No Exclusivity.**  

Unless otherwise expressly stated in a statement of work, this Amendment does not create exclusive rights to Vendor for the types of services described herein, and Client may obtain similar services from other providers, provided that Client does not disclose Vendor’s Confidential Information contrary to **Article V**.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS  

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables under this Amendment is **US $4,565,433** (the **“Contract Value”**), inclusive of all fees for implementation, managed services, and support, but exclusive of any approved out-of-pocket expenses and applicable taxes.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  

3.2.1 **Fixed Implementation Fees.** A portion of the Contract Value, equal to **US $1,750,000**, is allocated to fixed fees for implementation Services, payable upon completion of milestones as follows:

- **Milestone 1 – Project Plan and Design Completion:** US $350,000  
- **Milestone 2 – Core Configuration and Integration Completion:** US $700,000  
- **Milestone 3 – System Testing and User Acceptance Completion:** US $350,000  
- **Milestone 4 – Go-Live and Stabilization (30 days post go-live):** US $350,000  

Achievement of each milestone shall be determined in accordance with the acceptance procedures under **Section 2.2.3** and the applicable statement of work.

3.2.2 **Managed Services Fees.** The remaining portion of the Contract Value, equal to **US $2,815,433**, shall be allocated to recurring managed services fees to be invoiced in equal monthly installments over the Term, unless otherwise agreed in writing. 

3.2.3 **Time and Materials (Optional Work).** Any additional Services requested by Client outside the defined scope and not covered under the fixed or recurring fees may be performed on a time-and-materials basis at the rates set forth in the applicable statement of work or Change Order, subject to Client’s prior written approval in accordance with **Article XIII**.

3.3 **Invoicing and Payment.**  

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Client for (a) milestone-based fees upon completion and acceptance of each milestone, and (b) recurring managed services fees monthly in advance, unless otherwise specified in a statement of work.

3.3.2 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **Net 30** days of the invoice date. Payment shall be made via ACH, wire transfer, or other mutually agreed method.

3.3.3 Client may withhold payment of disputed amounts, provided that (a) Client pays the undisputed portion of the invoice, and (b) Client provides Vendor with written notice of the dispute, specifying the nature and amount of the dispute, within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve such disputes promptly.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  

If Client fails to pay any undisputed amount when due, Vendor may charge interest on the overdue amount from the due date until paid at a rate not exceeding the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law. Vendor shall not suspend the Services for late payment unless (i) amounts are more than forty-five (45) days overdue, (ii) Vendor has provided at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, and (iii) Client has not cured such non-payment in the interim.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.5.1 Vendor shall be responsible for its own ordinary business expenses incurred in performing the Services, including overhead, general administrative costs, and ordinary travel within the greater Seattle metropolitan area.

3.5.2 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred outside the greater Seattle metropolitan area, provided that (a) such travel is expressly requested or approved in advance in writing by Client, and (b) Vendor submits appropriate documentation and receipts in accordance with Client’s expense policies.

3.6 **Taxes.**  

All fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes that may be imposed by Applicable Law on the Services, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, employment, or property. Client shall be responsible for such taxes, if any, and Vendor shall itemize any applicable taxes on its invoices.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION  

4.1 **Term.** This Amendment shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or as otherwise provided in the Master Agreement.

4.2 **Renewal.**  

Unless otherwise specified in a subsequent written agreement between the Parties, this Amendment shall not automatically renew beyond the Expiration Date. Any extension or renewal of the Term shall be subject to the Parties’ mutual written agreement.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

Client may terminate this Amendment, in whole or in part, for convenience upon not less than **thirty (30) days’** prior written notice to Vendor. Vendor may terminate this Amendment for convenience only upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to Client, and only with Client’s written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld) if such termination would materially disrupt Client’s critical business operations.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Amendment for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Amendment or the Master Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.4.2 Either Party may terminate this Amendment immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent, (b) files for bankruptcy or has a bankruptcy petition filed against it (that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days), (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (d) ceases to do business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

Upon expiration or termination of this Amendment:

4.5.1 Client shall pay Vendor for all Services properly performed and accepted Deliverables provided through the effective date of termination, subject to Client’s rights of set-off and withholding of disputed amounts.

4.5.2 Any prepaid but unearned fees for Services that Vendor has not yet performed shall be promptly refunded to Client on a pro rata basis.

4.5.3 Vendor shall promptly cease performing the Services (except to the extent reasonably requested by Client to facilitate an orderly transition) and shall return or, at Client’s direction, securely destroy Client’s Confidential Information and Customer Data in accordance with **Article XI**.

4.5.4 Vendor shall, if requested by Client, provide Transition Services for up to ninety (90) days following termination or expiration (or such longer period as the Parties may agree in writing), at the rates set forth in the applicable statement of work or as otherwise agreed, and subject to the restrictions and obligations herein.

4.6 **Survival.**  

The provisions of this Amendment which by their nature should survive expiration or termination (including, without limitation, **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and any payment obligations accrued prior to termination) shall survive such expiration or termination.

“**Confidential Information**” means all non-public information, in any form or medium, disclosed or made available by one Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**), whether before or after the Effective Date, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. 5.2 **Exclusions.**  

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:  
(a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Amendment by the Receiving Party;  
(b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(c) is received from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.3 **Use and Disclosure.**  

The Receiving Party shall:  
(a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under the Master Agreement and this Amendment;  
(b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein; and  
(c) protect such Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.**  

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:  
(a) to its employees, officers, directors, affiliates, Subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  
(b) to the extent required by Applicable Law or court order, provided that the Receiving Party (i) gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted) so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and (ii) limits disclosure to the minimum amount required.

5.5 **Customer Data and Personal Data.**  

Customer Data and Personal Data shall be deemed Confidential Information of Client and subject to the enhanced protections set forth in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.

5.6 **Return or Destruction.**  

Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon expiration or termination of this Amendment, the Receiving Party shall promptly (a) return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, and (b) certify in writing its compliance with this Section, except for one archival copy that may be retained solely for evidentiary or compliance purposes.

5.7 **Survival.**  

The obligations of confidentiality under this **Article V** shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Amendment, except with respect to trade secrets and Customer Data, which shall remain confidential for so long as such information constitutes a trade secret or while Client continues to maintain such Customer Data as confidential.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS  

12.1 **Service Availability.**  

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environments of the managed systems are available not less than **99.9%** of the time in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and permitted downtime agreed with Client (**“Uptime Commitment”**).

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  

Vendor shall meet the following target response and resolution times for incidents:

- **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of service or major business impact:  
  - Initial response: within **1 hour** (24x7)  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within **4 hours**

- **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation with moderate business impact:  
  - Initial response: within **4 hours** (Business Days)  
  - Target resolution or workaround: within **1 Business Day**

- **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Limited impact, partial loss of functionality:  
  - Initial response: within **1 Business Day**  
  - Target resolution: within **5 Business Days**

- **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, non-critical:  
  - Initial response: within **2 Business Days**  
  - Target resolution: as mutually agreed.

12.3 **Measurement and Reporting.**  

Vendor shall measure its performance against the Service Levels on a monthly basis and provide Client with a written or electronic report within fifteen (15) days after the end of each month, detailing:

- Uptime percentage;  
- Incident volumes by severity;  
- Response and resolution time performance;  
- Any SLA Credits due to Client.

12.4 **Service Credits.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month, Client shall be entitled to SLA Credits as follows:

- Uptime < 99.9% but ≥ 99.0%: credit equal to **5%** of the monthly managed services fees;  
- Uptime < 99.0% but ≥ 98.0%: credit equal to **10%** of the monthly managed services fees;  
- Uptime < 98.0%: credit equal to **20%** of the monthly managed services fees.

SLA Credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) issued by Vendor and shall not, in themselves, constitute a waiver of Client’s right to claim damages or terminate for repeated failures.

12.5 **Chronic Failure.**  

If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in three (3) or more months in any rolling six (6) month period, such repeated failure shall constitute a material breach, and Client may, at its option, terminate the affected Services upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without penalty, in addition to other remedies available under this Amendment.
