## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Vendor.** The Client hereby engages the Vendor, and the Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described in this Agreement and in one or more SOWs executed by the Parties. The Vendor shall perform the Services as an independent contractor, and nothing contained herein shall be construed to create an employer-employee, joint venture, or partnership relationship between the Parties (see **Section 16.8**).

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) Each SOW shall, at a minimum, identify:  
  (i) the applicable project or engagement;  
  (ii) a detailed description of the Services and Deliverables;  
  (iii) the project schedule, milestones, and any acceptance criteria;  
  (iv) the fees and payment terms specific to the SOW (consistent with **Article III**);  
  (v) any key assumptions, Dependencies, and responsibilities of each Party; and  
  (vi) any special terms or deviations from this Agreement (which shall apply solely to such SOW unless expressly stated otherwise).  

(b) In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and those of an SOW, the terms of the SOW shall control solely with respect to that SOW, but only to the extent such SOW explicitly states that it is intended to modify the relevant provision of this Agreement.

2.3 **General Description of Services.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services may include, as specified in the SOW(s):

(a) **Consulting and Advisory Services.** Technology strategy, solution architecture, business analysis, process reengineering, and related advisory services, including the evaluation of the Client’s IT landscape, infrastructure, applications, security posture, and digital transformation initiatives.

(b) **Software Development and Integration.** Design, development, configuration, customization, and testing of software applications, platforms, and integrations, including APIs, middleware, interfaces to third-party systems, and data migration services. This may include both custom-developed solutions and configuration of Vendor’s proprietary products or Third-Party Materials.

(c) **Managed Services and Support.** Ongoing managed services for hosted or on-premises solutions, including system monitoring, incident management, problem resolution, patching, upgrades, performance tuning, capacity planning, and help desk or technical support, subject to the SLA set forth in **Article XII** and/or any SOW.

(d) **Cloud and Hosting Services.** Provision of cloud-based infrastructure, platform, or software services, whether hosted by Vendor or by a third-party cloud provider (such as AWS, Azure, or GCP), including provisioning, configuration, security management, back-up and recovery, and related operational Services.

(e) **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Delivery of training sessions, workshops, and knowledge transfer activities to the Client’s personnel relating to the Solutions, Deliverables, processes, or systems implemented under the applicable SOW.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) The Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for services of a similar nature, and will use personnel with appropriate skills and experience.  

(b) The Vendor does not guarantee error-free or uninterrupted operation of any Deliverables or hosted systems, but shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the SLA targets described in **Article XII** and any applicable SOW.  

(c) The Client acknowledges that the quality, timeliness and accuracy of the Services and Deliverables are dependent in part upon the Client’s timely performance of its responsibilities and obligations hereunder, including as set forth in **Section 2.6**.

2.5 **Project Management and Reporting.**  
(a) Each Party shall designate a project manager (a “**Project Manager**”) for each SOW who shall act as the primary point of contact for day-to-day communications.  

(b) The Vendor shall provide periodic status reports at intervals specified in the applicable SOW, which may include updates on progress against milestones, risks and issues, change requests, and upcoming activities.  

(c) The Parties shall conduct regular project governance meetings (e.g., weekly or bi-weekly) to review status, address risks, and make decisions, as described in the applicable SOW.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities and Dependencies.**  
(a) The Client shall:  
  (i) provide the Vendor with timely access to Client personnel, systems, facilities, information, and Client Data necessary for the performance of the Services;  
  (ii) obtain all necessary internal approvals and decisions, and provide timely feedback and sign-offs as reasonably requested by the Vendor;  
  (iii) ensure that its systems, software, infrastructure and third-party service providers are configured and operating in a manner that supports the Services and Deliverables; and  
  (iv) perform any specific tasks or obligations assigned to the Client in the applicable SOW.  

(b) The Vendor’s obligations may be subject to certain dependencies, assumptions, or prerequisites (“**Dependencies**”), including those specified in the SOW. To the extent any delay or failure by the Client to satisfy its responsibilities or the Dependencies affects the Vendor’s performance, the Vendor shall be entitled to a reasonable schedule extension and an equitable adjustment in fees pursuant to **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

2.7 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
(a) Each SOW shall define specific acceptance criteria and procedures for Deliverables, including any testing to be performed by the Client.  

(b) Unless an SOW provides otherwise, the Client shall review each Deliverable within fifteen (15) Business Days after delivery. If the Client reasonably determines that the Deliverable does not conform to the acceptance criteria, the Client shall provide a written rejection notice specifying the deficiencies.  

(c) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct material non-conformities and resubmit the Deliverable for re-testing within a mutually agreed period.  

(d) A Deliverable shall be deemed accepted upon the earliest of: (i) Client’s written acceptance; (ii) the expiration of the review period without written rejection; or (iii) the Client’s use of the Deliverable in a production environment (other than for testing), provided that any non-conformity materially impacting production may still be addressed under warranty pursuant to **Article VII**.

2.8 **Subcontracting.** The Vendor may, in its sole discretion, engage subcontractors to perform any portion of the Services, provided that the Vendor shall remain responsible for the performance of such subcontractors and shall ensure that such subcontractors are bound by obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement. The Client hereby consents to the use of subcontractors, including offshore resources, provided that cross-border transfer of Personal Data is governed by **Article XI**.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The aggregate estimated value of all Services and Deliverables under this Agreement, including all SOWs executed as of the Effective Date, is **One Million Eight Hundred Seventy-One Thousand Four Hundred Seventy-Three U.S. Dollars (US$1,871,473)** (the “**Contract Value**”). The Parties acknowledge that the Contract Value is an estimate and that additional SOWs or Change Orders may increase the total fees payable.

3.2 **Fee Structures.** Fees under this Agreement may be structured as set forth in the applicable SOW(s), including:  
(a) **Time-and-Materials (T&M)** based on hourly or daily rates for Vendor personnel;  
(b) **Fixed-Price** for specific Deliverables or phases;  
(c) **Milestone-Based** payments tied to specified achievements; and/or  
(d) **Recurring Fees** for managed services or subscriptions.  

Each SOW shall specify the applicable fee structure.

3.3 **Rate Cards and Adjustments.**  
(a) The Vendor’s standard rate card for personnel categories may be attached to an SOW. Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, Vendor may adjust its standard rates no more than once per calendar year upon at least sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to Client.  

(b) For long-term managed services, recurring fees may be subject to annual adjustment based on the U.S. Consumer Price Index or a mutually agreed index, as specified in the relevant SOW.

3.4 **Invoices and Payment Terms.**  
(a) Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the billing schedule set forth in each SOW. Invoices may be issued monthly in arrears for T&M Services, upon completion or acceptance of milestones for fixed-fee work, or in advance for recurring fees.  

(b) All undisputed amounts invoiced by the Vendor shall be due and payable within **forty-five (45) days** of the date of invoice (“**Net 45**”), unless another payment term is specified in the SOW.  

(c) Payments shall be made in U.S. Dollars by wire transfer or other method specified on the invoice. Client shall be responsible for any bank charges associated with international transfers.

3.5 **Late Payments.** Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable Law, from the due date until the date of payment. In addition, if the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within fifteen (15) days after receiving written notice of late payment, the Vendor may, at its option and without liability: (i) suspend performance of the Services and any Service Levels until all overdue amounts are paid; and/or (ii) condition future performance on shortened payment terms, including payment in advance.

3.6 **Disputed Invoices.**  
(a) If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall pay the undisputed portion by the due date and provide written notice of the dispute with reasonable detail within thirty (30) days of the invoice date.  

(b) The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days. Upon resolution, any amounts agreed or determined to be payable shall be paid promptly, together with any applicable late payment interest as provided in **Section 3.5**.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) All fees are exclusive of any sales, use, value-added, goods and services, excise, withholding, or similar taxes (“**Taxes**”).  

(b) Client shall be responsible for all Taxes levied on the Services or Deliverables, except for taxes based on Vendor’s net income, employment taxes, or taxes for which Vendor is solely legally responsible. If Vendor is required to collect Taxes, such Taxes shall be included on the invoice and paid by Client.  

(c) If Client is required by Law to withhold any Taxes on payments to Vendor, Client shall provide Vendor with official receipts or other documentation to enable Vendor to claim tax credits or refunds.

3.8 **Expenses.** The Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and third-party costs, in accordance with the Client’s then-current travel and expense policy provided to Vendor in writing. Vendor shall provide documentation of expenses as reasonably requested. Any extraordinary expenses shall require the Client’s prior written approval.

3.9 **No Set-off.** Except for amounts the Client is permitted to withhold pursuant to a bona fide invoice dispute under **Section 3.6**, the Client may not set-off, withhold, or deduct any amounts from the fees due to Vendor under this Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in full force and effect until **October 7, 2029** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, any SOW in effect as of the Expiration Date shall continue to be governed by this Agreement until completion or earlier termination of such SOW.

4.2 **Renewal.** Upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, the term of this Agreement may be extended for additional renewal terms on terms and conditions to be agreed by the Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
(a) The Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, including any or all SOWs, for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to the Vendor.  

(b) The Vendor may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, including any or all SOWs, for convenience upon at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to the Client.  

(c) In the event of termination for convenience by the Client, the Client shall pay Vendor: (i) all fees for Services performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination; (ii) all non-cancellable third-party costs incurred; and (iii) any early termination fees expressly set forth in the applicable SOW. For any fixed-fee SOW, the Parties shall equitably allocate the fee based on work performed and committed costs.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or the applicable SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer period as may be reasonably necessary if the breach is not capable of cure within thirty (30) days, provided the breaching Party begins to cure within that period and diligently completes such cure) after receipt of written notice describing the breach.  

(b) Notwithstanding subsection (a), Vendor may immediately suspend performance or terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice if: (i) Client fails to pay any undisputed sum when due, and such failure continues for more than fifteen (15) days after written notice; or (ii) Client materially violates Vendor’s acceptable use or security policies, if any, resulting in a material security risk.

4.5 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) becomes insolvent or is generally unable to pay its debts as they become due; (b) makes a general assignment for the benefit of its creditors; (c) files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or reorganization that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or (d) has a receiver, trustee, or similar officer appointed for its business or assets.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.** Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:  
(a) All rights and obligations of the Parties under the terminated portions shall cease, except as provided in this **Section 4.6** and **Section 16.10** (Survival).  

(b) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed, Deliverables delivered, and reimbursable expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, plus any approved wind-down services requested by Client.  

(c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**, subject to any retention obligations under **Article XI** or applicable Law.  

(d) Upon Client’s written request and at Client’s expense, Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance services at Vendor’s then-current rates for up to ninety (90) days, unless otherwise agreed in an SOW.

4.7 **Survival.** The following provisions shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement: **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII (to the extent relating to accrued credits or remedies), XIII (with respect to outstanding Change Orders), XIV, XV, and XVI**, and any other provisions which by their nature should survive.

5.7 **Survival.** The obligations of confidentiality under this **Article V** shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided, however, that with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, such obligations shall survive for so long as such information constitutes a trade secret under applicable Law or is required by data protection Laws.

(a) promptly notify the other Party in writing of the nature and expected duration of the Force Majeure Event;  
(b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable; and  
(c) keep the other Party reasonably informed of progress.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.** The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event. The time for performance shall be extended by a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event, plus a reasonable recovery period.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.** If a Force Majeure Event prevents a Party from substantially performing its obligations under this Agreement for a continuous period of more than ninety (90) days, the other Party may terminate the affected SOW(s) or this Agreement, in whole or in part, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice.
