## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF LEASE AND SERVICES

2.1 **General.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, during the Lease Term Nexus hereby leases to Contoso, and Contoso hereby leases from Nexus, the Premises described in **Section 2.2**, and Nexus shall provide the Services described in this **Article II** and any applicable SOW(s). The Parties acknowledge that Nexus shall have broad discretion in the manner of performing the Services, provided that the Service Levels and other express obligations set forth herein are satisfied.

2.2 **Premises Description.**

2.2.1 **Location.** The Premises consist of approximately **28,500 rentable square feet** of commercial space located on the 7th and 8th floors of the building known as “Nexus Plaza,” with a street address of **2500 Nexus Plaza, Bellevue, Washington 98004**. The detailed floor plan and space allocation may be set forth in an attached Exhibit A (Floor Plan) or in an SOW.

2.2.2 **Use of Premises.** The Premises shall be used by Contoso solely for general office, light technical, software development, IT operations, and related lawful business purposes. Contoso shall not use the Premises for any purpose prohibited by applicable law, building rules, or any provision of this Agreement, nor for industrial manufacturing, hazardous materials storage (beyond ordinary office use), or public-facing retail.

2.2.3 **Common Areas.** Contoso shall have non-exclusive access, in common with other tenants and licensees of Nexus Plaza, to lobbies, hallways, elevators, stairwells, restrooms, parking facilities (to the extent allocated), and other common areas provided by Nexus, subject to rules and regulations reasonably determined by Nexus and communicated to Contoso.

2.3 **Core Services.** Nexus shall provide the following core Services to Contoso during the Lease Term:

2.3.1 **Facilities Management.** Nexus shall manage and operate the Premises, including:

- Routine cleaning and janitorial services (excluding specialized laboratory or high-security cleaning);
- Maintenance and repair of structural elements (roof, foundations, exterior walls) and building systems (HVAC, elevators, electrical distribution, plumbing) serving the Premises;
- Building security and access control, including front-desk reception during standard business hours and electronic key-card access control.

2.3.2 **Utilities and Infrastructure.** Subject to **Section 2.7**, Nexus shall:

- Provide electricity, water, heating, ventilation, and air conditioning sufficient for typical office and light technical uses;
- Provide shared access to building backup power (e.g., generators or UPS systems), where installed, in accordance with the capacity allocations described in applicable SOW(s);
- Provide basic data connectivity from the building demarcation point to the Premises, which may include dedicated fiber, shared bandwidth, or other connectivity specified in an SOW.

2.3.3 **Technical Services.** Nexus shall provide, as described in the applicable SOW:

- Rack space and power in designated areas (if any);
- Facilities for equipment staging and secure storage, if specified;
- Managed network services, including monitoring, incident response, and escalation, subject to the Service Levels set forth in **Article XII**.

2.4 **Supplemental Services.** Upon Contoso’s written request and Nexus’s acceptance (which may be in an SOW), Nexus may provide supplemental services, which may include:

- Custom build-out or reconfiguration of Premises, including cabling, walls, office layout, and security enhancements;
- Additional security measures (e.g., enhanced access control, CCTV coverage, security personnel);
- Dedicated technical staff for on-site support at the Premises;
- Special cleaning, waste management, or handling of sensitive or regulated materials.

All supplemental services are subject to additional Fees, as set forth in **Article III** and any applicable SOW.

2.5 **Performance Standards and Service Levels.**

2.5.1 **General Standard of Performance.** Nexus shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted standards for comparable commercial facilities and managed services providers in the greater Seattle/Bellevue market, subject to the limitations and allocations of responsibility set forth herein.

2.5.2 **Service Levels.** Specific Service Levels, including uptime commitments, response times, and resolution times, are set forth in **Article XII** and may be further elaborated in an SOW. In the event of a conflict between an SOW and **Article XII**, the SOW shall prevail with respect to the services described therein, unless the SOW expressly states otherwise.

2.5.3 **Measurement and Reporting.** Nexus shall measure performance against the Service Levels using its standard tools and methodologies and shall provide Contoso with periodic reports (e.g., monthly or quarterly) upon request or as specified in an SOW.

2.6 **Company Responsibilities.** Contoso acknowledges and agrees that the proper performance of certain Services and the safe and lawful use of the Premises require Contoso’s cooperation and responsibilities, including:

2.6.1 **Compliance with Rules and Laws.** Contoso shall at all times comply with (a) all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances; (b) building rules and regulations provided by Nexus; and (c) all reasonable policies and procedures issued by Nexus for the safety and security of the Premises and Facility.

2.6.2 **Equipment and Systems.** Contoso shall be solely responsible for the procurement, installation, maintenance, and security of its own equipment, systems, and software located at the Premises, except to the extent expressly provided otherwise in an SOW. Contoso shall ensure that such equipment and systems do not overload or interfere with the building’s electrical, mechanical, or connectivity infrastructure.

2.6.3 **Personnel and Access Control.** Contoso shall ensure that its employees, contractors, and visitors comply with access control procedures. Contoso shall promptly notify Nexus of personnel terminations or other changes affecting access rights and shall be responsible for the actions and omissions of its personnel, contractors, and visitors at the Premises.

2.7 **Limitations on Services.** Notwithstanding anything to the contrary:

2.7.1 Nexus does not guarantee uninterrupted or error-free operation of utilities or connectivity, and outages may occur due to maintenance, repairs, third-party failures, power disruptions, or Force Majeure Events, subject to the remedies set forth in **Article XII**.

2.7.2 Nexus shall not be responsible for loss, damage, or unavailability caused by (a) misuse or negligent operation by Contoso; (b) Contoso’s equipment failure; (c) acts or omissions of third parties not under Nexus’s control; or (d) Force Majeure Events.

2.8 **High-Risk Operations.** If Contoso intends to conduct any operations at the Premises that involve heightened risk (e.g., 24x7 trading platforms, mission-critical data center functions), Contoso shall notify Nexus in writing and the Parties shall document any additional controls, responsibilities, or Fees in an SOW. Nexus’s obligations remain limited to those expressly stated in this Agreement and such SOWs.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The total estimated contract value for the Lease Term is **One Million Eight Hundred Sixty-One Thousand Two Hundred Twenty-Eight U.S. Dollars (US$1,861,228)** (“**Contract Value**”), which includes base rent, standard Services, and the anticipated standard charges as of the Effective Date, but excludes Taxes, pass-through expenses, and any supplemental services requested by Contoso.

3.2 **Base Rent and Service Fees.**

3.2.1 **Base Rent.** Contoso shall pay to Nexus a monthly base rent of **US$52,000.00** for the Premises, subject to annual escalations of **3%** on each anniversary of the Effective Date during the Lease Term.

3.2.2 **Standard Services Fee.** In addition to Base Rent, Contoso shall pay a monthly standard services fee of **US$9,500.00** for building services, standard connectivity, and facilities management as described in **Article II**.

3.2.3 **Supplemental Services Fees.** Fees for any supplemental services shall be as set forth in the applicable SOW or as otherwise agreed in writing by the Parties.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**

3.3.1 **Invoicing.** Nexus shall issue invoices to Contoso on a monthly basis in advance for Base Rent and standard Service Fees, and in arrears for variable or usage-based charges and supplemental services, unless otherwise specified in an SOW.

3.3.2 **Payment Due Date (Net 45).** Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **forty-five (45) days** from the invoice date (“**Net 45**”), in U.S. dollars, by electronic funds transfer to the account designated by Nexus or by other payment method approved by Nexus in writing.

3.3.3 **Disputed Amounts.** If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion by the due date and provide written notice of the dispute, with reasonable supporting details, within **fifteen (15) days** of the invoice date. The Parties shall use good-faith efforts to resolve such disputes promptly. Failure to dispute within such period shall be deemed acceptance of the invoice.

3.4 **Late Payments.**

3.4.1 **Interest.** Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid at a rate of the lesser of (a) **1.5% per month** or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

3.4.2 **Suspension Rights.** If any undisputed amount remains unpaid for more than **sixty (60) days** after the invoice date, Nexus may, upon at least ten (10) days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of non-critical Services and restrict access to certain non-essential features until all overdue amounts (including interest) are paid, without liability to Contoso, provided such suspension does not violate applicable law or building safety requirements.

3.5 **Taxes.** All Fees are exclusive of Taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all Taxes associated with the Fees and the use of the Premises and Services, except for taxes based on Nexus’s net income. Nexus may invoice applicable Taxes, which Contoso shall pay in accordance with **Section 3.3**, unless Contoso provides a valid tax exemption certificate acceptable to the relevant taxing authority.

3.6 **Expenses and Reimbursement.** If Nexus incurs reasonable out-of-pocket expenses on behalf of Contoso (e.g., third-party contractor charges for supplemental build-out requested by Contoso), Contoso shall reimburse Nexus for such expenses at cost plus a **10%** administrative fee, provided that (a) such expenses are agreed in writing in advance by Contoso, and (b) Nexus provides reasonable documentation and receipts upon request.

3.7 **No Withholding.** Contoso shall make all payments under this Agreement free and clear of, and without deduction or withholding for, any taxes or other charges, except as required by law. If any such deduction or withholding is required, Contoso shall pay such additional amounts as necessary so that Nexus receives the full amount it would have received absent such deduction or withholding.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Initial Term.** The initial term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until the Expiration Date, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** (the “**Initial Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.** Subject to Nexus’s prior written consent (which may be granted or withheld in Nexus’s reasonable discretion), Contoso may request to renew the Lease Term for one or more additional periods of **twelve (12) months** each. Renewal terms, including Base Rent, Service Fees, and other terms, shall be as agreed by the Parties in writing at least **nine (9) months** prior to the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 **By Company.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days** prior written notice to Nexus, provided that (a) such termination shall not become effective prior to **March 31, 2026**, and (b) Contoso remains fully liable for all Fees and other amounts accrued through the effective date of termination, plus any early termination Fees expressly agreed in an applicable SOW.

4.3.2 **By Vendor.** Nexus may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon at least **one hundred eighty (180) days** prior written notice to Contoso, in which case Nexus shall reasonably cooperate with Contoso to facilitate an orderly transition and, at Contoso’s request, negotiate in good faith a short-term extension on mutually acceptable terms, subject to space and resource availability.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**

4.4.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; provided that if the breach is not reasonably capable of cure within such thirty (30) day period, the breaching Party shall have an additional **thirty (30) days** to cure, so long as the breaching Party diligently commences and continuously pursues such cure.

4.4.2 **Non-Payment.** Notwithstanding **Section 4.4.1**, Nexus may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written notice if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than **ninety (90) days** after the invoice date and Contoso fails to make such payment within ten (10) days after receipt of a final written notice of default.

4.4.3 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent; (b) makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors; (c) applies for or consents to the appointment of a receiver, trustee, or similar official for a substantial part of its assets; or (d) becomes subject to any bankruptcy or insolvency proceeding that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Effect of Termination or Expiration.**

4.5.1 **Cessation of Services.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Nexus shall cease providing the Services (except as may be required during a transition period as expressly agreed in writing), and Contoso shall cease using the Services and shall vacate the Premises in accordance with **Section 4.5.2**.

4.5.2 **Surrender of Premises.** Contoso shall surrender the Premises to Nexus on or before the effective date of termination or expiration, in substantially the same condition as received, reasonable wear and tear excepted, and shall remove all of its personal property and equipment. Any property not removed within thirty (30) days after termination/expiration may be deemed abandoned and disposed of by Nexus at Contoso’s expense.

4.5.3 **Survival of Payment Obligations.** Termination or expiration shall not relieve Contoso of its obligation to pay all Fees, Taxes, and other amounts accrued or payable prior to the effective date of termination/expiration, including any early termination Fees expressly provided in an SOW.

4.5.4 **Surviving Provisions.** The rights and obligations of the Parties under **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure)**, and **XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions that by their nature should survive, shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement.

5.4 **Permitted Disclosures.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information: (a) to its and its Affiliates’ employees, contractors, professional advisors, and agents who have a legitimate need to know such information and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement; and (b) as required by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party (to the extent legally permissible) provides prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.7 **Injunctive Relief.** The Parties acknowledge that a breach of this **Article V** may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief and specific performance without the necessity of posting bond or proving actual damages.

5.8 **Survival.** The confidentiality obligations set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** after termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets, which shall be maintained as confidential for so long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Applicability.** This **Article XII** sets forth the general Service Levels applicable to core Services provided under this Agreement. Specific or additional SLAs may be set forth in an SOW, which shall govern in the event of a conflict.

12.2 **Uptime Commitments.**

12.2.1 **Facility Uptime.** Nexus shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Premises and building systems (excluding scheduled maintenance) are available and operational **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month (“**Facility Uptime**”).

12.2.2 **Network Uptime.** For any managed connectivity services provided by Nexus, Nexus shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain network connectivity from the building demarcation point to the Premises at **99.9%** uptime each calendar month (“**Network Uptime**”).

12.3 **Scheduled Maintenance.** Nexus may schedule maintenance windows for repairs, upgrades, or testing. Nexus shall endeavor to conduct routine maintenance outside of normal Business Hours and shall provide Contoso with at least **five (5) Business Days** prior written notice of any planned maintenance expected to result in material service interruption.

12.4 **Response and Resolution Times.**

12.4.1 **Incident Severity Levels.** Nexus shall classify incidents as follows:  
(a) **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage of Premises or critical building systems materially impairing Contoso’s operations;  
(b) **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation or partial outage impacting important operations;  
(c) **Severity 3 (Medium/Low):** Non-critical issues or cosmetic defects.

12.4.2 **Response Times.** Nexus shall acknowledge incident reports from Contoso within the following target timeframes:

- Severity 1: within **1 hour**, 24x7;  
- Severity 2: within **4 hours**, during Business Days;  
- Severity 3: within **1 Business Day**.

12.4.3 **Resolution Targets.** Nexus shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents within the following target timeframes (which are goals, not guarantees):

- Severity 1: within **8 hours** of acknowledgment;  
- Severity 2: within **2 Business Days** of acknowledgment;  
- Severity 3: within **10 Business Days** of acknowledgment.

12.5 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  

12.5.1 If Facility Uptime or Network Uptime falls below 99.9% in any calendar month due to reasons within Nexus’s control, Contoso may be eligible for service credits as follows:

- Uptime 99.0% – 99.89%: 5% credit on the affected month’s Service Fees;  
- Uptime 98.0% – 98.99%: 10% credit;  
- Uptime below 98.0%: 15% credit.

12.5.2 Service credits shall be Contoso’s sole monetary remedy for SLA failures, provided that this limitation does not restrict Contoso’s rights to pursue other remedies for persistent or material breaches under **Article XIV** and **Article IX**.
