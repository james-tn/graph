## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND WORK

2.1 **General Scope.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Vendor shall supply to the Client the Deliverables and perform the Services described in this Article II and in any attached Schedules or Statements of Work, which are incorporated herein by reference. The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement establishes a framework for multiple Purchase Orders and/or Statements of Work during the Term.

2.2 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables to be provided by the Vendor shall include, without limitation:

  2.2.1 **Core Platform Software.** A proprietary enterprise-grade software platform, inclusive of server-side components, web-based user interfaces, APIs, integration middleware, and associated configuration tools (the **"Platform"**), to enable the Client to manage and automate its internal procurement and supply chain workflows.

  2.2.2 **Hardware Appliances (if applicable).** Any physical hardware appliances, dedicated servers, or edge devices required for optimal operation of the Platform within the Client’s environment, as further detailed in the hardware schedule agreed by the Parties.

  2.2.3 **Documentation.** User guides, technical documentation, installation manuals, configuration guides, and release notes, delivered electronically, sufficient to enable reasonably skilled personnel of the Client to use and, where applicable, administer the Platform.

  2.2.4 **Integration Components.** Custom integration modules, connectors, and data migration scripts to interface the Platform with the Client’s existing systems, including ERP systems, financial systems, and vendor management tools, as mutually agreed.

  2.2.5 **Training Materials.** Standard and customized training materials, including slide decks, recorded sessions, and hands-on exercises, prepared by the Vendor for the Client’s designated users and administrators.

  2.2.6 **Updates and Upgrades.** During the Term and subject to Article III and Article VI, the Vendor shall make available to the Client such updates, bug fixes, and minor version upgrades to the Platform as the Vendor generally makes available to its other customers with similar support subscriptions.

2.3 **Services.**  
The Vendor shall perform the following Services:

  2.3.1 **Project Management and Planning.** The Vendor shall assign a project manager to coordinate the delivery of the Deliverables and Services, engage in project planning workshops with the Client, and prepare a mutually agreed high-level implementation plan with key milestones.

  2.3.2 **Implementation and Configuration Services.** The Vendor shall implement and configure the Platform in accordance with the agreed Specifications and the implementation plan. Such Services include environment setup, configuration of workflows, roles, access permissions, and basic customization as mutually agreed.

  2.3.3 **Integration and Migration Services.** The Vendor shall perform the necessary integration and data migration work as described in Section 2.2.4. The Client acknowledges that integration and migration outcomes are dependent on the quality and completeness of the Client’s data and systems, and the Client shall provide all required access, information, and resources in a timely manner.

  2.3.4 **Testing and Acceptance Support.** The Vendor shall support the Client’s user acceptance testing (UAT) by providing test plans, sample test cases, and reasonable technical support. Acceptance procedures shall be as set out in Section 2.8.

  2.3.5 **Training Services.** The Vendor shall conduct initial administrator and end-user training sessions, either remotely or onsite, as mutually agreed, to enable the Client’s personnel to operate the Platform effectively.

  2.3.6 **Support and Maintenance Services.** During the Term, the Vendor shall provide support and maintenance in accordance with the Service Levels described in Article XII, including incident management, problem resolution, and access to updates.

  2.3.7 **Professional Services.** At the Client’s request and subject to additional fees under Article III, the Vendor may provide additional professional services such as advanced customization, additional integrations, reporting and analytics configuration, or dedicated consultancy.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties shall agree in writing (via Schedule or Statement of Work) on a detailed implementation timeline and phased milestones. By way of example, and without limitation, the following indicative phases may apply:

  - **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (0–60 days from Effective Date).** Requirements workshops, system assessment, and solution design.
  - **Phase 2 – Configuration and Development (60–150 days).** Platform configuration, integration development, and initial data migration.
  - **Phase 3 – UAT and Pilot (150–210 days).** UAT execution, remediation, and pilot deployment.
  - **Phase 4 – Production Go-Live (210–270 days).** Final data migration and full production launch.

Time for performance shall not be of the essence unless explicitly stated otherwise in a specific Statement of Work. The Vendor shall not be liable for delays caused by the Client’s acts or omissions, including failure to provide required information, approvals, or access.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall:

  2.5.1 Provide timely access to its premises, systems, personnel, and data as reasonably required by the Vendor to perform the Services.

  2.5.2 Designate a primary contact and project lead to liaise with the Vendor and coordinate internal activities.

  2.5.3 Ensure that its systems, network, and infrastructure meet the minimum technical requirements specified by the Vendor.

  2.5.4 Obtain any third-party consents or licenses necessary to allow integration with or access to the Client’s systems.

  2.5.5 Be solely responsible for its internal change management, user adoption, and organizational processes needed to successfully utilize the Deliverables.

2.6 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for comparable services. The Vendor does not warrant that the Deliverables will be error-free or uninterrupted, but shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct reproducible material errors in accordance with the Service Levels. The Client acknowledges that complex software may contain inherent defects and that no vendor can guarantee perfect performance.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  
The Vendor may use subcontractors to perform any part of the Services, provided that the Vendor shall remain responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were the Vendor’s own. Upon written request, the Vendor shall identify its material subcontractors involved in providing the Services.

2.8 **Delivery, Testing and Acceptance.**  

  2.8.1 **Delivery.** Unless otherwise agreed, Deliverables shall be delivered electronically. Hardware, if any, shall be delivered FCA (Free Carrier, Incoterms 2020) at the Vendor’s designated facility, risk passing upon delivery to the carrier.

  2.8.2 **Acceptance Testing.** For major Deliverables specified in a Statement of Work, the Client shall have an agreed acceptance period (typically thirty (30) days) from delivery to test such Deliverables against the applicable Specifications.

  2.8.3 **Acceptance Criteria.** Deliverables shall be deemed accepted upon the earlier of (a) the Client’s written notice of acceptance; (b) the Client’s use of the Deliverables in production; or (c) the expiration of the acceptance period without the Client providing a written rejection specifying material non-conformities.

  2.8.4 **Rejection and Remediation.** If the Client validly rejects a Deliverable due to material non-conformity with the Specifications, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to remedy such non-conformity within a reasonable time. Thereafter, the Deliverable shall be resubmitted for acceptance in accordance with this Section.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value and Fee Structure.**  
The total Contract Value under this Agreement is **USD 3,677,445**, exclusive of applicable taxes. The Contract Value shall be allocated as follows, unless otherwise agreed in a specific Purchase Order:

  3.1.1 **License / Subscription Fees:** USD 2,200,000 for the right to use the Platform and associated software components during the Term.

  3.1.2 **Implementation and Integration Fees:** USD 850,000 for implementation, configuration, integration, and data migration Services.

  3.1.3 **Support and Maintenance Fees:** USD 427,445 for standard support and maintenance during the Term.

  3.1.4 **Training and Professional Services:** USD 200,000 for initial training and agreed professional services.

3.2 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise set forth in an applicable Purchase Order or Statement of Work, the Client shall pay the Contract Value in accordance with the following schedule:

  3.2.1 **Initial Payment:** Thirty percent (30%) of the Contract Value (USD 1,103,233.50) within sixty (60) days of the Effective Date.

  3.2.2 **Implementation Progress Payment:** Thirty percent (30%) of the Contract Value (USD 1,103,233.50) within sixty (60) days following completion of Phase 2 (Configuration and Development) as confirmed by the Vendor’s written milestone completion notice.

  3.2.3 **Go-Live Payment:** Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value (USD 735,489) within sixty (60) days following the production go-live of the Platform (Phase 4) or deemed acceptance pursuant to Section 2.8, whichever occurs first.

  3.2.4 **Final Payment:** Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value (USD 735,489) within sixty (60) days of the earlier of (a) issuance of the final acceptance certificate for all Deliverables, or (b) twelve (12) months after the Effective Date, provided the Platform is operational in a production environment.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
The Vendor shall issue invoices corresponding to the payment milestones and any other fees payable under this Agreement. All invoices shall reference Contract Reference Number **PUR-DEL-202403-307** and any applicable Purchase Order number.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
All payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) by wire transfer to the bank account designated by the Vendor in the invoice. **Payment terms are Net 60** from the date of the relevant invoice, unless otherwise expressly stated in a Purchase Order.

3.5 **Taxes.**  
All fees and prices are exclusive of any applicable value-added tax (VAT), sales tax, withholding tax, or other governmental charges. The Client shall be responsible for all such taxes, except for taxes based on the Vendor’s net income. If withholding tax is required by law, the Client shall gross up the payment so that the Vendor receives the full amount it would have received absent such withholding.

3.6 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at an annual rate equal to the lesser of (a) eight percent (8%) per annum above the Bank of England base rate, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law. The Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Services and/or access to the Deliverables if the Client is in default of payment of any undisputed amount by more than thirty (30) days.

3.7 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, it shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the nature of the dispute and the amount in question. The Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion. The Parties shall seek to resolve any invoice disputes promptly.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
The Client shall reimburse the Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by the Vendor in connection with providing the Services, including travel, lodging, and subsistence costs, in accordance with the Vendor’s then-current expense policy, provided such expenses are supported by appropriate receipts or documentation.

3.9 **Price Adjustments.**  
For multi-year components of the Services, the Vendor may increase recurring fees (e.g., support and maintenance fees) annually by up to five percent (5%) or in line with the increase in the UK Consumer Price Index (CPI), whichever is higher, upon at least sixty (60) days’ written notice prior to the start of the relevant renewal period.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated pursuant to this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **20 July 2028** (the **"Expiration Date"**) (the **"Term"**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Upon the Expiration Date, this Agreement may be renewed by mutual written agreement of the Parties. Any renewal shall be on terms to be agreed, including pricing and scope of Services.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience by Client.**  
The Client may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, at any time by providing the Vendor with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**. In such event, the Client shall remain liable for all fees incurred up to the effective date of termination and for any non-cancellable commitments entered into by the Vendor in reliance on this Agreement. For the avoidance of doubt, any termination for convenience by the Client shall not entitle the Client to any refund of prepaid fees.

4.4 **Termination for Convenience by Vendor.**  
The Vendor may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing the Client with at least ninety (90) days’ prior written notice, provided that the Vendor shall, where reasonably practicable, cooperate with the Client to facilitate an orderly transition as described in Section 4.7.

4.5 **Termination for Cause.**

  4.5.1 **Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or, if the breach is not reasonably curable within such thirty (30) days, fails to commence and diligently pursue cure within such period) after receiving written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

  4.5.2 **Insolvency.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into liquidation (other than for the purposes of a bona fide solvent amalgamation or reconstruction), has a receiver or administrator appointed over any of its assets, or enters into any arrangement or composition with its creditors.

4.6 **Termination for Non-Payment.**  
Notwithstanding Section 4.5, the Vendor may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the Client fails to pay any undisputed amount within sixty (60) days after the due date and fails to cure such non-payment within ten (10) Business Days after receiving written notice thereof.

4.7 **Effect of Termination.**  

  4.7.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:  
  (a) All licenses and rights granted to the Client under this Agreement shall automatically terminate, except as expressly provided otherwise in Article VI;  
  (b) The Client shall immediately cease use of the Deliverables and Services, except to the extent expressly permitted under a surviving license; and  
  (c) The Client shall pay all outstanding fees and charges due under this Agreement up to the effective date of termination, including any early termination charges expressly agreed in writing.

  4.7.2 Upon the Client’s written request made within thirty (30) days after the effective date of termination or expiration, the Vendor shall make commercially reasonable efforts to provide the Client with a final export of the Client’s data in a standard industry format, subject to payment of reasonable professional services fees. After such period, the Vendor may delete or anonymize the Client’s data in accordance with its data retention policies and Article XI.

4.8 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration (including Articles III, V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any payment obligations accrued but unpaid) shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the Platform is available 24 hours per day, 7 days per week, with a minimum monthly uptime of **99.9%**, excluding scheduled maintenance and downtime caused by factors outside the Vendor’s reasonable control.

The Vendor may perform scheduled maintenance that may affect the availability of the Platform. The Vendor shall respond to support requests according to the severity of the incident, as follows:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of service with no workaround. Target initial response time: within one (1) hour of receipt, 24x7.  
  - **Severity 2 (High):** Significant degradation of service with limited workaround. Target initial response time: within four (4) hours during Business Days.  
  - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Partial, non-critical loss of functionality or intermittent issues. Target initial response time: within one (1) Business Day.  
  - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries. Target initial response time: within three (3) Business Days.

The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents in accordance with internal procedures and priorities but does not guarantee resolution within any specific timeframe. The Client acknowledges that some issues may require workarounds or future releases.

12.5 **Remedies for SLA Breaches.**  
In the event the Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in any calendar month, the Client may be eligible for service credits as follows:

  - Uptime < 99.9% and ≥ 99.0%: 2% of the monthly subscription fee.  
  - Uptime < 99.0% and ≥ 98.0%: 5% of the monthly subscription fee.  
  - Uptime < 98.0%: 10% of the monthly subscription fee.

Service credits shall be the Client’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA breaches. Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall not be redeemable for cash. The Client must request service credits in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the SLA breach occurred, providing reasonable details.

15.3 **Suspension of Performance.**
