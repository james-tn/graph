## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide to Client a comprehensive suite of **managed technology services**, **enterprise software implementation**, **systems integration**, and **ongoing support and maintenance services** (collectively, the “Services”), in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Addendum and any applicable SOW.

2.1.2 The Services include, at a minimum, the following components:

- (a) Assessment and planning of Client’s existing IT environment and business requirements;  
- (b) Design and configuration of the System to support Client’s operational, financial, and customer-facing processes;  
- (c) Implementation and integration of Vendor’s proprietary platforms and Third-Party Materials into Client’s environment;  
- (d) Data migration and validation services;  
- (e) Provision of hosting, infrastructure, and managed services to operate the System;  
- (f) Ongoing monitoring, maintenance, patching, and upgrades; and  
- (g) Training and knowledge transfer to Client personnel as reasonably necessary to enable effective use of the Deliverables.

### 2.2 Deliverables

2.2.1 Vendor shall create, configure, and deliver the Deliverables as further described in the initial SOW, which shall be deemed incorporated into this Addendum by reference. Such Deliverables shall include, without limitation:

- (a) A detailed solution design document outlining the functional and technical architecture of the System;  
- (b) Configured software instances tailored to Client’s business processes, including workflows, approval chains, reporting dashboards, and user roles;  
- (c) Integration components and APIs necessary to interoperate with Client’s existing systems, including but not limited to CRM, ERP, HRIS, and billing platforms;  
- (d) Data migration scripts, mapping documents, and validation reports evidencing successful migration of data from legacy systems;  
- (e) Operational runbooks, standard operating procedures, and escalation matrices for ongoing managed services; and  
- (f) Training materials, user guides, and administrative documentation.

2.2.2 For avoidance of doubt, the Services are structured primarily as **managed services** and do not constitute a sale or transfer of Vendor’s underlying platforms or proprietary tools, except as explicitly stated otherwise in **Article VI (Intellectual Property Rights)**.

### 2.3 Timelines and Milestones

2.3.1 The Parties shall, within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, finalize and execute an initial SOW that includes detailed project timelines and key milestones. Sample milestones include:

- (a) Completion of discovery and requirements gathering;  
- (b) Delivery of initial solution design;  
- (c) Completion of configuration and unit testing;  
- (d) Completion of system integration testing and user acceptance testing (UAT);  
- (e) Go-live of production environment; and  
- (f) Transition to steady-state managed services.

2.3.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed the agreed milestones. Vendor shall promptly notify Client in writing if Vendor anticipates any material delay and shall propose a remediation or recovery plan. Client’s failure to provide timely approvals, information, or access as required shall be deemed a Client-caused delay and shall entitle Vendor to schedule extensions and equitable adjustments in Fees, as further described in **Article XIII (Change Management)**.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards for high-availability managed services, and in accordance with the Service Levels specified in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)**.

2.4.2 Vendor shall assign personnel with appropriate expertise and experience to perform the Services. Vendor shall have the right, in its sole discretion, to replace or reassign its personnel, provided that Vendor remains responsible for performance of the Services.

2.4.3 Vendor shall maintain adequate security controls and practices in operating the System and providing the Services, including, where applicable, encryption, access controls, and vulnerability management, as further detailed in **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.

### 2.5 Client Responsibilities

2.5.1 Client acknowledges that Vendor’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Client’s timely and effective performance of its responsibilities, which shall include, without limitation:

- (a) Providing Vendor with timely access to Client’s personnel, systems, environments, facilities, and data as reasonably necessary;  
- (b) Furnishing complete and accurate information, decisions, and approvals in a timely manner;  
- (c) Ensuring that Client’s hardware, software, and network environment meet the minimum requirements specified by Vendor;  
- (d) Designating a primary point of contact with authority to make binding decisions on behalf of Client; and  
- (e) Complying with all applicable laws, including Data Protection Laws, in connection with its use of the Services.

2.5.2 Any failure by Client to fulfill its responsibilities may result in delays, additional Fees, or degradation of Service Levels, for which Vendor shall not be liable. Vendor shall be entitled to charge Client for any additional work or resources required to address such failure at Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates, pursuant to **Article III (Fees and Payment Terms)**.

### 2.6 Subcontractors

2.6.1 Vendor may utilize Subcontractors in the performance of the Services, provided that Vendor shall remain responsible for the acts and omissions of such Subcontractors as if they were Vendor’s own.

2.6.2 Vendor shall require any Subcontractor that processes Personal Data to be bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection consistent with **Article V (Confidentiality)** and **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**.

### 2.7 Changes to Scope

2.7.1 Any change to the scope of Services or Deliverables shall be managed strictly in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**. Client acknowledges that any material changes requested by Client may impact the timelines, Service Levels, and Fees.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Contract Value

3.1.1 The total contract value for this Addendum, inclusive of all Services and Deliverables described herein and any initial SOW executed on or around the Effective Date, is **Two Million Two Hundred Eight Thousand One Hundred Eighty-Four U.S. Dollars (US$2,208,184)** (the “**Contract Value**”).

3.1.2 The Contract Value may be increased by mutual written agreement of the Parties through duly executed Change Orders or additional SOWs.

### 3.2 Fee Structure

3.2.1 The Fees shall consist of:

- (a) **Implementation Fees**: One-time fees for design, configuration, integration, and go-live services;  
- (b) **Recurring Managed Services Fees**: Monthly or annual fees for ongoing hosting, support, and managed services; and  
- (c) **Time and Materials Fees**: Fees for out-of-scope or ad hoc services requested by Client and approved via a Change Order.

3.2.2 A sample fee schedule may be set forth in an exhibit to the initial SOW and shall detail the allocation of the Contract Value across implementation and recurring services.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment Terms

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Client in accordance with the following high-level schedule:

- (a) Twenty percent (20%) of the implementation portion of the Contract Value upon Effective Date;  
- (b) Thirty percent (30%) of the implementation portion upon completion of solution design;  
- (c) Fifty percent (50%) of the implementation portion upon go-live; and  
- (d) Recurring managed services Fees monthly in advance, commencing on the first day of the month following the go-live date, unless otherwise stated in an SOW.

3.3.2 All invoices shall be due and payable by Client within **sixty (60) calendar days** from the date of invoice (“**Net 60**”), without any set-off or deduction, except as expressly permitted in **Section 3.7**.

### 3.4 Late Payment

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid by Client when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.

3.4.2 In addition, if any undisputed invoice remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after the due date, Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ written notice to Client, suspend performance of the Services (including access to the System) until all past due amounts, including interest, are paid in full. Such suspension shall not constitute a breach by Vendor, and all Service Levels shall be deemed waived during the suspension period.

### 3.5 Taxes

3.5.1 All Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, excise, value-added, or similar taxes (excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income), which shall be borne by Client. Vendor shall itemize any such taxes on its invoices.

3.5.2 Client shall provide Vendor with any applicable exemption certificates or other documentation necessary to substantiate any exemption from such taxes.

### 3.6 Expenses

3.6.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket travel and related expenses incurred by Vendor in connection with the performance of the Services, consistent with Client’s reasonable travel policies provided to Vendor in writing.

3.6.2 Vendor shall provide reasonable supporting documentation for reimbursable expenses. Expenses shall be invoiced monthly and payable in accordance with **Section 3.3**.

### 3.7 Disputed Amounts

3.7.1 If Client disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, Client shall notify Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the reasons for the dispute and the amount in dispute. Client shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice by the due date.

3.7.2 The Parties shall work diligently and in good faith to resolve any disputed amounts. Any resolution requiring an adjustment shall be reflected in a subsequent invoice or credit memo.

### 3.8 No Withholding

Except to the extent required by law, Client shall not withhold, offset, or deduct any amount from Vendor’s invoices for any reason, including alleged non-performance or breach, unless expressly authorized under this Addendum or the Master Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Addendum shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 17, 2027** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**.

4.1.2 Unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Addendum shall automatically renew for additional one (1) year periods under the same terms and conditions, subject to Vendor’s standard annual price increases, not to exceed seven percent (7%) per renewal term, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Client may terminate this Addendum, in whole or in part, for convenience, without cause, upon no less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice** to Vendor.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client:

- (a) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables provided through the effective date of termination;  
- (b) Client shall pay any non-cancellable third-party commitments and early termination fees incurred by Vendor on Client’s behalf; and  
- (c) Any implementation Fees that are fixed-price and associated with milestones that have been partially completed shall be due on a pro rata basis, plus a wind-down fee equal to five percent (5%) of the remaining unpaid Contract Value to compensate Vendor for lost opportunity and allocation of resources.

4.2.3 Vendor may terminate this Addendum for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice to Client, provided that Vendor shall cooperate reasonably with Client to transition the Services in an orderly manner.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Addendum immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

- (a) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, is placed in receivership, or ceases to operate in the ordinary course of business; or  
- (b) Commits a material breach of this Addendum, which breach remains uncured thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice thereof (or ten (10) days in the case of non-payment).

4.3.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, Vendor may terminate this Addendum or suspend Services immediately upon written notice if Client materially violates **Article V (Confidentiality)**, **Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy)**, or fails to cease any unlawful use of the Services following Vendor’s notice.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Addendum:

- (a) All rights and licenses granted to Client under this Addendum shall immediately cease, except as expressly stated otherwise in **Article VI**;  
- (b) Client shall immediately discontinue all use of the Services and Deliverables, except for any Deliverables expressly designated as “Client-owned work product” under **Section 6.2**;  
- (c) Client shall pay all undisputed amounts due and owing to Vendor as of the effective date of termination; and  
- (d) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**.

4.4.2 Vendor shall, upon Client’s written request received no later than thirty (30) days after the effective date of termination, provide reasonable transition assistance at Vendor’s then-current professional services rates, subject to an agreed SOW.

### 4.5 Survival

The provisions of **Articles III (Fees and Payment Terms), V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions)**, and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Addendum.

## CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 “Confidential Information” means all non-public information, whether oral, written, electronic, or otherwise, disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (the “Receiving Party”) that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

5.1.2 Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business and marketing plans, technology and technical information, product designs, business processes, pricing, financial information, trade secrets, and Personal Data.

### 5.2 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:

- (a) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Addendum;  
- (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein; and  
- (c) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, Affiliates, Subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a need to know such information for purposes consistent with this Addendum, provided that such persons are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those set forth herein.

### 5.3 Exclusions

Confidential Information shall not include information that:

- (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault or breach of the Receiving Party;  
- (b) Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
- (c) Is lawfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
- (d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party and cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order is not obtained, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

### 5.5 Survival

The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following the expiration or termination of this Addendum; provided, however, that trade secrets and Personal Data shall be maintained in confidence for so long as they qualify as trade secrets or remain subject to Data Protection Laws, respectively.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the System is available **99.9% of the time** in each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance, emergency maintenance, and downtime caused by Client, third parties, or Force Majeure Events.

### 12.2 Scheduled Maintenance

Vendor may schedule maintenance windows during which the System may be unavailable. Vendor shall use reasonable efforts to perform scheduled maintenance during off-peak hours and shall provide Client with at least forty-eight (48) hours’ prior notice of any scheduled maintenance expected to result in material downtime.

### 12.3 Incident Response Times

Vendor shall respond to incidents in accordance with the following target response times from the time Client properly logs a support ticket:

- **Severity 1 (Critical – System down)**: Response within one (1) hour, continuous work until workaround or resolution is provided.  
- **Severity 2 (High – Major functionality impaired)**: Response within four (4) hours, work during Business Days until workaround or resolution.  
- **Severity 3 (Medium – Non-critical issue)**: Response within one (1) Business Day.  
- **Severity 4 (Low – Informational / minor issue)**: Response within three (3) Business Days.

### 12.4 SLA Remedies

12.4.1 If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in **Section 12.1** in any calendar month, Client may be eligible for service credits as follows:

- Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: Credit equal to 5% of the monthly recurring managed services Fees.  
- Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: Credit equal to 10% of the monthly recurring managed services Fees.  
- Uptime below 98.0%: Credit equal to 15% of the monthly recurring managed services Fees.

12.4.2 Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute Client’s sole and exclusive remedy, and Vendor’s entire liability, for failure to meet Service Levels, except in cases of Vendor’s gross negligence or willful misconduct.
