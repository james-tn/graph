## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 Service Provider shall provide to Client the Services and Deliverables described in this Agreement and each applicable SOW executed hereunder. Each SOW shall (a) reference this Agreement; (b) describe the Services and Deliverables; (c) identify any Milestones and timelines; (d) set forth the Fees, payment schedule, and payment method; and (e) specify any applicable Service Levels, acceptance criteria, and dependencies.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that the Services may include, without limitation:  
(a) IT consulting and advisory services;  
(b) requirements analysis and solution design;  
(c) software implementation, integration, configuration, and customization;  
(d) project management, change management, and training;  
(e) managed services and ongoing operational support; and  
(f) maintenance and enhancement of existing systems and applications.

### 2.2 Detailed Service Description

2.2.1 Without limiting any SOW, the Services to be performed by Service Provider may include:

- **Discovery & Assessment.** Conducting discovery sessions, workshops, and interviews with Client’s stakeholders to assess existing systems, processes, and requirements; documenting current-state architecture, pain points, and business objectives.

- **Solution Design.** Developing detailed solution designs, including system architectures, data models, integrations, workflow configurations, and security models, consistent with industry best practices and Client’s technical standards as reasonably communicated to Service Provider.

- **Implementation & Configuration.** Installing, configuring, and integrating software components and related infrastructure within Client’s environments (on-premises and/or cloud-based), including customizing standard modules to align with documented requirements, subject to any limitations specified in an SOW.

- **Data Migration.** Planning and executing data migrations, including data cleansing, mapping, transformation, and validation, in coordination with Client’s teams. Service Provider shall propose data migration strategies, testing procedures, and fallback plans.

- **Testing & Quality Assurance.** Designing and executing test plans, including unit testing, system testing, integration testing, and supporting Client’s user acceptance testing (“UAT”); documenting test results; remediating identified defects in a timely manner as set forth in the applicable SOW.

- **Training & Knowledge Transfer.** Providing end-user and administrator training, including training materials, manuals, and knowledge-base articles; conducting train-the-trainer sessions and handover workshops to Client’s support and operations personnel.

- **Managed Services & Support.** Providing ongoing support, monitoring, incident management, and maintenance services, which may include application support, performance monitoring, patch management, and minor enhancements, as further defined in the SOW and subject to the Service Levels.

2.2.2 Any Services not expressly listed in an SOW shall be deemed out of scope and shall require a Change Order pursuant to **Article XIII** before Service Provider is obligated to perform such additional services.

### 2.3 Deliverables

2.3.1 For each SOW, Service Provider shall provide a clear list of Deliverables, which may include:

- Detailed design documents and architecture diagrams;  
- Configuration documentation and migration runbooks;  
- Custom-developed code, scripts, and integration components;  
- User guides, training materials, and knowledge-base articles;  
- Operational runbooks and support procedures;  
- Status reports and project documentation.

2.3.2 Each Deliverable shall conform in all material respects to the specifications and acceptance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW. Client shall have the right to review and test Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.4**.

### 2.4 Acceptance of Deliverables

2.4.1 Unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW, Client shall have a period of **fifteen (15) Business Days** from the date of delivery of a Deliverable (the **“Review Period”**) to review, test, and either (a) accept the Deliverable in writing, or (b) reject the Deliverable by providing a written notice specifying in reasonable detail any material non-conformities with the applicable specifications or acceptance criteria.

2.4.2 If Client rejects a Deliverable, Service Provider shall, at its own cost, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified non-conformities and re-submit the Deliverable to Client for re-testing within a reasonable time, not to exceed the timeframes (if any) set forth in the SOW. The Parties shall follow the same acceptance procedure for the corrected Deliverable.

2.4.3 If Client fails to provide written acceptance or a written rejection within the Review Period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted as of the last day of the Review Period, provided that Service Provider provided Client with written notice of such Review Period at the time of delivery.

2.4.4 Acceptance of any Deliverable shall not be deemed a waiver of any warranty rights under **Article VII** or any other rights or remedies available to Client under this Agreement or applicable Law.

### 2.5 Performance Standards

2.5.1 Service Provider shall perform all Services:  
(a) in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;  
(b) using personnel with appropriate skill, experience, and qualifications;  
(c) in compliance with all applicable Laws; and  
(d) in accordance with the terms of this Agreement and the applicable SOW.

2.5.2 Service Provider shall designate a project manager (the **“Service Provider Project Manager”**) who shall be responsible for the overall coordination, management, and delivery of the Services. Client shall designate a corresponding project manager (the **“Client Project Manager”**). The project managers shall serve as principal points of contact for operational matters.

2.5.3 Service Provider shall comply with Client’s reasonable written policies and procedures communicated to Service Provider in advance, including with respect to security, safety, and acceptable use, when performing Services on Client’s premises or accessing Client’s systems.

2.5.4 Service Provider shall promptly notify Client in writing if Service Provider becomes aware of any circumstances that may materially delay the performance of the Services or the delivery of any Deliverable, and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to mitigate such delay, including, where appropriate, the issuance of a Change Order.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Fees and Contract Value

3.1.1 In consideration of the Services and Deliverables provided by Service Provider under this Agreement, Client shall pay Service Provider the Fees set forth in each SOW. ### 3.2 Payment Terms

3.2.1 Except as otherwise stated in an SOW, Service Provider shall invoice Client on a monthly basis in arrears for Services performed and Deliverables accepted (or deemed accepted) during the preceding month.

3.2.2 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts due under each invoice within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of Client’s receipt of such invoice (**“Net 45”**), by wire transfer or other mutually agreed payment method.

3.2.3 Each invoice shall (a) reference this Agreement and the applicable SOW; (b) describe the Services rendered, the period covered, and any Deliverables provided; and (c) provide reasonable supporting documentation, including timesheets for time-and-materials engagements if requested by Client.

### 3.3 Late Payments

3.3.1 Any undisputed invoice amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full, at a rate equal to the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable Law.

3.3.2 Service Provider shall not suspend the performance of the Services for Client’s failure to pay amounts due unless (a) such amounts are not subject to a good-faith dispute, (b) such amounts remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment, and (c) Service Provider has provided at least ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice of its intent to suspend. Any suspension shall be in accordance with **Section 4.3** (if applicable) and shall not relieve Client of its obligation to pay undisputed Fees.

### 3.4 Disputed Amounts

3.4.1 Client may in good faith dispute any portion of an invoice by providing written notice to Service Provider within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, identifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Client shall pay any undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with **Section 3.2**.

3.4.2 The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any invoice disputes within thirty (30) days. Upon resolution, Client shall promptly pay any amounts determined to be payable.

### 3.5 Expenses and Reimbursements

3.5.1 Out-of-pocket expenses reasonably incurred by Service Provider in the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other related expenditures, shall be reimbursed by Client at actual cost, provided that:  
(a) such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Client, except for minor incidental expenses under a threshold agreed in the SOW; and  
(b) Service Provider submits receipts or other reasonable documentation.

3.5.2 Service Provider shall comply with Client’s travel and expense policies as communicated in writing to Service Provider from time to time.

### 3.6 Taxes

3.6.1 Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes, duties, or other governmental charges (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Client shall be responsible for any Taxes imposed on the provision of Services or Deliverables under this Agreement, excluding taxes based on Service Provider’s net income, property, or employment.

3.6.2 If Service Provider is required to collect any Taxes, Service Provider shall itemize such Taxes on the invoice, and Client shall pay such Taxes unless Client provides a valid tax exemption certificate.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall remain in effect until **May 02, 2028** (the **“Expiration Date”**).

4.1.2 Upon mutual written agreement of the Parties at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, this Agreement may be renewed for one or more additional periods of one (1) year each (each, a **“Renewal Term”**), on such terms as the Parties may agree in writing.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, for convenience and without cause upon not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Service Provider (a) Fees for all Services properly performed and Deliverables provided and accepted (or deemed accepted) through the effective date of termination, and (b) any non-cancellable commitments reasonably incurred in reliance on this Agreement, provided such commitments were pre-approved in writing by Client.

4.2.3 In the event of termination for convenience by Service Provider, Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize any disruption to Client’s business and cooperate in transition activities as reasonably requested by Client under **Section 4.5**.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW, in whole or in part, immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** (or, if the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, within such additional period not to exceed sixty (60) days, provided the breaching Party has commenced and is diligently pursuing a cure) after receiving written notice of the breach.

4.3.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
(a) becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed for a substantial portion of its assets, or has a petition in bankruptcy filed against it that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or  
(b) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

### 4.4 Termination for Legal or Regulatory Reasons

Client may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice if Client reasonably determines that (a) continued performance would violate applicable Law or a regulatory requirement applicable to Client; or (b) a governmental authority issues an order that renders continued performance unlawful. In such case, Client shall pay Service Provider for Services and Deliverables properly provided up to the effective date of termination.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination; Transition Assistance

4.5.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

(a) Service Provider shall promptly cease performing the terminated Services;  
(b) Client shall pay all undisputed Fees and approved expenses due for Services properly performed and Deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination; and  
(c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**.

4.5.2 At Client’s request, for a period not to exceed ninety (90) days following termination or expiration (or such other period as the Parties may agree in writing), Service Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance to Client or to a replacement service provider designated by Client, at Service Provider’s then-current time-and-materials rates (unless otherwise agreed in an SOW), to facilitate an orderly transition of the Services.

### 4.6 Survival

Any provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should reasonably be understood to survive termination or expiration (including, without limitation, **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII, XIV, XV, XVI**, and those provisions relating to payment obligations accrued prior to termination) shall so survive.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 For purposes of this Agreement, **“Confidential Information”** means all non-public information, whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, that is disclosed or made available by one Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**) in connection with this Agreement and is either (a) marked or identified as confidential or proprietary at the time of disclosure, or (b) should reasonably be understood by the Receiving Party to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

5.1.2 Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, customer lists, financial information, product roadmaps, pricing, technical data, system configurations, source code, trade secrets, know-how, Client Materials, Personal Data, and the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

### 5.2 Obligations

5.2.1 The Receiving Party shall:

(a) protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;  
(b) use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or exercising its rights and obligations under this Agreement; and  
(c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement.

5.2.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement, provided that such recipients are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein. The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this **Article V** by its recipients.

### 5.3 Exceptions

The obligations in **Section 5.2** shall not apply to any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) was already known to the Receiving Party without obligation of confidentiality prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
(b) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;  
(c) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction on disclosure; or  
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure

If the Receiving Party is required by Law or by a valid subpoena or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall (a) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of the requirement (to the extent legally permitted), (b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and (c) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose.

### 5.5 Return or Destruction

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party (including all copies, summaries, and extracts), except that the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal and compliance purposes and copies stored in automatic backup systems in the ordinary course of business, subject in all cases to the confidentiality obligations herein.

### 5.6 Survival

The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided that trade secrets and Personal Data shall be kept confidential for so long as they constitute trade secrets or Personal Data under applicable Law.
