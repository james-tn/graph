  (d) Provision of help desk, support, and incident management services;  
  (e) Consulting and advisory services relating to digital transformation, optimization, and strategy; and  
  (f) Any other services mutually agreed in writing and described in an SOW.

2.2.1 Each SOW shall, at a minimum, specify: (i) a description of the Services and Deliverables; (ii) applicable Specifications; (iii) the project schedule and key milestones; (iv) any dependencies or assumptions; (v) the Fees and payment schedule; and (vi) any Service Levels applicable to the Services under such SOW.

2.2.2 In the event of any conflict between the terms of this Agreement and the terms of any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall govern and control, unless expressly stated otherwise in such SOW with specific reference to the conflicting section of this Agreement.

### 2.3 Performance Standards

2.3.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for high-quality service providers of similar services, and in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations.

2.3.2 The Service Provider shall use personnel who are properly trained, qualified, and experienced to perform the Services. The Service Provider shall be solely responsible for the direction, control, and compensation of its personnel and shall, at its discretion, be entitled to subcontract portions of the Services, subject to Section 16.9.

2.3.3 The Service Provider shall comply in all material respects with the project schedules and milestones described in the applicable SOW(s). If the Service Provider anticipates any material delay, it shall promptly notify the Company in writing, specifying the cause of the delay, the anticipated impact on the schedule, and proposed mitigation steps.

2.3.4 The Parties acknowledge that the performance of the Services may depend on the timely cooperation and performance of the Company, including the provision of access to necessary systems, personnel, facilities, and information. The Company shall use commercially reasonable efforts to provide all such access and cooperation. The Service Provider shall not be liable for delays or failures caused by the Company’s acts or omissions, and any such delays shall entitle the Service Provider to an equitable adjustment in the schedule and Fees through the Change Management process in Article XIII.

### 2.4 Deliverables and Acceptance

2.4.1 The Service Provider shall deliver the Deliverables in accordance with the applicable SOW. Each SOW may include detailed acceptance criteria and procedures.

2.4.2 Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, upon delivery of any Deliverable, the Company shall have a period of **ten (10) Business Days** (the “**Acceptance Period**”) to review and either (i) accept the Deliverable in writing, or (ii) reject the Deliverable by providing a written notice of rejection specifying in reasonable detail the deficiencies based on the applicable Specifications.

2.4.3 If the Company timely rejects a Deliverable, the Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified deficiencies and re-deliver the corrected Deliverable within a reasonable time. The Company shall then have a further Acceptance Period to review such corrected Deliverable.

2.4.4 A Deliverable shall be deemed accepted if: (i) the Company provides written notice of acceptance; or (ii) the Company fails to provide a written notice of acceptance or rejection within the applicable Acceptance Period.

2.4.5 Following acceptance of any Deliverable, the Service Provider shall have no further obligation to modify, correct, or enhance such Deliverable unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing or covered under any warranty or support obligations set forth in this Agreement or the applicable SOW.

### 2.5 Company Responsibilities

2.5.1 The Company shall timely provide the Service Provider with all information, materials, and access to systems and facilities reasonably required for the Service Provider to perform the Services.

2.5.2 The Company shall be responsible for: (i) the accuracy and completeness of information provided to the Service Provider; (ii) making timely decisions and approvals as reasonably required; and (iii) obtaining any necessary third-party consents or licenses required for the Service Provider’s performance of the Services, unless otherwise expressly stated in an SOW.

2.5.3 The Company acknowledges that any failure to perform its responsibilities may adversely affect the Service Provider’s ability to perform the Services, and may result in delays, increased costs, or changes in scope, for which the Service Provider shall be entitled to seek equitable adjustments through the Change Management process.

### 2.6 High-Risk Nature of Engagement

2.6.1 The Parties acknowledge and agree that the engagement under this Agreement has been designated as **High Risk**, due to the complexity and critical nature of the Services and the potential impact of failures or delays.

2.6.2 In light of such high-risk designation, the Parties expressly agree to the risk allocation provisions set forth herein, including the broad indemnification obligations of the Company in Article VIII and the uncapped liability framework in Article IX, and acknowledge that the Fees and other commercial terms reflect such allocation of risk.

3.4.1 Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest from the due date until paid in full at a rate of **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month** or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less.

3.6.1 The Fees are exclusive of all applicable sales, use, value-added, and other taxes, duties, or similar charges (other than taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income), which shall be the responsibility of the Company.

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **July 20, 2026** (the “**Expiration Date**”).

4.2.1 The Company may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for convenience and without cause upon **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the Service Provider.

4.2.2 In the event of such termination for convenience by the Company, the Company shall pay the Service Provider: (i) all Fees for Services performed and Deliverables delivered (and accepted or deemed accepted) through the effective date of termination; (ii) all non-cancellable commitments and third-party costs incurred by the Service Provider; and (iii) any early termination fees or wind-down costs specified in the applicable SOW.

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any SOW, for cause if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.3.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Service Provider shall have the right to terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:  
  (a) the Company fails to pay any undisputed amount within sixty (60) days after the due date; or  
  (b) the Company becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or becomes subject to any bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceeding.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:  
  (a) all outstanding SOWs shall automatically terminate, except as otherwise agreed in writing;  
  (b) the Company shall immediately pay all amounts due and owing for Services performed and Deliverables delivered through the effective date of termination, including any additional amounts owed under Section 4.2.2 (if applicable); and  
  (c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.6.

4.4.2 Termination of this Agreement shall be without prejudice to any rights or remedies that may have accrued to either Party prior to the effective date of termination.

### 4.5 Survival

4.5.1 The following provisions shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement: Articles III (to the extent of outstanding payment obligations), V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI, and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive.

### 15.1 Force Majeure Events

15.1.1 A “Force Majeure Event” means any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, labor disputes, epidemics, pandemics, governmental actions, failure or interruption of electrical or telecommunications services, or other similar events.

### 15.2 Notice and Mitigation

15.2.1 The affected Party shall promptly notify the other Party of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of such event on its performance.

### 15.3 Suspension of Performance

15.3.1 The affected Party’s obligations shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that if the Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) days, either Party may terminate the affected SOW upon written notice.
