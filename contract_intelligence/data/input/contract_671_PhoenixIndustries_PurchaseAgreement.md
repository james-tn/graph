## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES

2.1 **General.** Phoenix shall supply to Contoso, and Contoso shall purchase from Phoenix, the Deliverables and Services described in this **Article II** and in any applicable SOW or PO issued hereunder. Phoenix shall perform the Services and deliver the Deliverables in a professional, timely, and workmanlike manner, in accordance with this Agreement, the Specifications, and generally accepted industry standards.

2.2 **Deliverables.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Deliverables to be supplied under this Agreement are expected to include, at a minimum:

  2.2.1 **Hardware Systems.**  
  (a) A minimum of twenty (20) rack-mounted high-availability server units, each meeting or exceeding the agreed Specifications, including specified CPU, RAM, storage, network interfaces, redundancy features, and power supplies.  
  (b) Ancillary networking and infrastructure components required to integrate the servers into Contoso’s existing data center environment, including switches, cabling, racks, and power distribution units as mutually agreed.  

  2.2.2 **Software Components.**  
  (a) Base operating system licenses as specified in the SOW (e.g., enterprise Linux distributions or equivalent), properly licensed for the intended production environment.  
  (b) Phoenix’s proprietary monitoring and management tools (if any) necessary for ongoing support and Service Level monitoring.  

  2.2.3 **Documentation.**  
  Comprehensive Documentation, including installation and configuration guides, operational manuals, support handbooks, and maintenance procedures, in English, provided in electronic format and, upon Contoso’s request, up to two (2) printed sets.

2.3 **Services.** Phoenix shall provide the following Services:

  2.3.1 **Project Planning and Design.**  
  Phoenix shall collaborate with Contoso’s designated project team to perform a detailed requirements analysis, confirm the Specifications, and prepare a mutually agreed implementation plan outlining milestones, resource allocation, and key dependencies.

  2.3.2 **Installation and Configuration.**  
  Phoenix shall install the hardware at Contoso’s designated facilities in Singapore (or such other locations as specified in a SOW), perform initial configuration of the hardware and software, integrate the Deliverables with Contoso’s existing infrastructure, and perform initial system testing.

  2.3.3 **Integration and Testing.**  
  Phoenix shall conduct integration testing, performance testing, and acceptance testing in accordance with the test plan agreed in writing by the Parties. Phoenix shall promptly remedy any material non-conformities or defects identified during such testing.

  2.3.4 **Training.**  
  Phoenix shall provide up to forty (40) hours of on-site or remote training sessions to Contoso’s technical personnel, covering system administration, monitoring, troubleshooting, and routine maintenance procedures.

  2.3.5 **Maintenance and Support.**  
  Phoenix shall provide ongoing maintenance and support for the Deliverables during the Term, including incident management, problem resolution, and hardware replacement, in accordance with the Service Levels specified in **Article XII**.

2.4 **Project Timeline and Milestones.**  
The Parties anticipate that the core deployment phase will be completed within a period of approximately six (6) months from the Effective Date, subject to timely cooperation by Contoso and any dependencies on third parties. Without limitation:

  2.4.1 **Milestone 1 – Design Finalization (within 30 days of Effective Date).**  
  Completion of requirements analysis, architectural design, and approval of the final Specifications and project plan by both Parties.

  2.4.2 **Milestone 2 – Delivery of Hardware (within 90 days of Effective Date).**  
  Delivery of the hardware Deliverables to Contoso’s designated site(s), with associated Documentation and packing lists.

  2.4.3 **Milestone 3 – Installation & Initial Configuration (within 150 days of Effective Date).**  
  Completion of installation and initial configuration, including basic connectivity testing and environment validation.

  2.4.4 **Milestone 4 – System Acceptance (within 180 days of Effective Date).**  
  Completion of agreed acceptance tests, documented acceptance sign-off by Contoso (which shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed), or deemed acceptance in accordance with mutually agreed acceptance criteria.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  
Phoenix shall:

  (a) Perform the Services with due skill, care, and diligence, using personnel who are appropriately qualified, experienced, and trained.  
  (b) Ensure that all Deliverables materially conform to the Specifications and are free from material defects in materials and workmanship for the applicable warranty period set out in this Agreement.  
  (c) Deliver hardware and software that is new (not used or refurbished unless explicitly agreed in writing) and suitable for deployment in a production environment.  
  (d) Comply with all Applicable Laws and relevant industry standards in performing the Services and delivering the Deliverables.  

2.6 **Acceptance Procedure.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in a SOW:

  2.6.1 Contoso shall have a period of thirty (30) days following delivery and installation of the Deliverables (the **“Acceptance Period”**) to test the Deliverables for material conformity with the Specifications.

  2.6.2 If the Deliverables materially conform, Contoso shall issue a written acceptance confirmation. Failure to provide written notice of rejection specifying material non-conformities within the Acceptance Period shall constitute deemed acceptance, provided that Phoenix has provided reasonable assistance during the Acceptance Period.

  2.6.3 In the event of a justified rejection, Phoenix shall, at no additional cost to Contoso, use commercially reasonable efforts to promptly correct the identified deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverables for acceptance. The Acceptance Period shall restart for the corrected Deliverables for a further fifteen (15) Business Days.

2.7 **Cooperation and Dependencies.**  
Contoso shall provide timely access to its premises, systems, data, and personnel as reasonably necessary for Phoenix to perform the Services. Phoenix shall not be responsible for delays or failures arising from Contoso’s or third parties’ acts or omissions, provided that Phoenix has promptly notified Contoso in writing of such dependencies and impacts.

2.8 **Changes to Scope.**  
Any change to the Scope of Services, Deliverables, specifications, or project timeline shall be managed under **Article XIII (Change Management)**. Phoenix shall have no obligation to implement any change requested by Contoso unless and until a written Change Order is executed by both Parties.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
Subject to the terms of this Agreement, and based on the scope described herein, the total estimated Contract Value payable by Contoso to Phoenix is **US$651,544**, exclusive of applicable taxes and duties, unless expressly stated otherwise.

  (d) Maintenance and support Services for the Term: US$50,000  

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise specified in a SOW, Phoenix shall invoice Contoso according to the following milestones:

  3.3.1 **Milestone 1 – Upon Effective Date:**  
  Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value (US$130,308).

  3.3.2 **Milestone 2 – Upon Delivery of Hardware:**  
  Forty percent (40%) of the Contract Value (US$260,618), upon confirmed delivery of the hardware to Contoso’s designated site(s).

  3.3.3 **Milestone 3 – Upon Completion of Installation & Initial Configuration:**  
  Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value (US$130,309).

  3.3.4 **Milestone 4 – Upon System Acceptance or deemed acceptance:**  
  Twenty percent (20%) of the Contract Value (US$130,309).

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
Phoenix shall issue invoices upon completion of each payment milestone. Contoso shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** of the invoice date (**“Net 30”**). Payments shall be made in US Dollars by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method to the account designated by Phoenix.

3.5 **Disputed Invoices.**  
If Contoso disputes any portion of an invoice in good faith, it shall notify Phoenix in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall timely pay any undisputed portion. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any dispute promptly. Failure to dispute an invoice within the aforementioned period shall not waive Contoso’s rights, but the invoice shall be presumed accurate absent manifest error.

3.6 **Late Payment.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until payment is received in full. Phoenix may, upon written notice and after providing at least ten (10) Business Days’ cure period, suspend performance of the Services for material non-payment, provided such suspension is proportionate and Phoenix continues essential support to maintain system stability.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
All Fees are exclusive of any applicable sales, use, value-added, goods and services, withholding, or other similar taxes, duties, or charges imposed by any governmental authority (collectively, **“Taxes”**). Contoso shall be responsible for payment of all such Taxes arising from this Agreement, except for taxes based on Phoenix’s net income, property, or employment.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
Reasonable and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Phoenix in the performance of the Services (including travel, accommodation, and subsistence) shall be reimbursed by Contoso at cost, provided that Phoenix submits appropriate supporting documentation. Any anticipated expenses exceeding US$5,000 in the aggregate shall be subject to Contoso’s prior written approval.

3.9 **No Set-Off.**  
Except as required by Applicable Law or expressly permitted in this Agreement, each Party shall make payments under this Agreement without set-off, deduction, or counterclaim. This provision does not limit Contoso’s right to withhold disputed amounts under **Section 3.5**.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV**, shall remain in force until **September 24, 2026** (the **“Expiration Date”**) (the **“Term”**).

4.2 **Renewal.**  
The Parties may, by mutual written agreement at least ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, renew or extend this Agreement for additional periods on terms to be agreed.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience, in whole or in part, by providing the other Party with not less than **sixty (60) days’** prior written notice. In such event:

  (a) Contoso shall pay Phoenix for all Deliverables delivered and accepted and all Services performed up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments reasonably incurred with Contoso’s prior written approval.  
  (b) Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate any expenses and costs.  

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  4.4.1 **Material Breach.**  
  Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect by written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

  4.4.2 **Insolvency.**  
  Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, has a receiver appointed over its assets, or is the subject of any petition, proceeding, or other action relating to bankruptcy, insolvency, or liquidation that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

  (a) Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement, Phoenix shall promptly cease performance of the Services and, upon Contoso’s request, deliver any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which Contoso has paid.  
  (b) Contoso shall pay all Fees due and payable up to the effective date of termination, subject to any good faith disputes.  
  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4**.  
  (d) Termination shall not affect any rights or obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including rights to payment, indemnification, and damages.

4.6 **Survival.**  
The provisions of this Agreement which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure)** and **XVI (General Provisions)**, shall so survive.

  (c) Protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own similar confidential information, and in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:

  (a) To its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, professional advisors, and subcontractors who have a legitimate need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those set forth herein; and  
  (b) As required by Applicable Law, court order, or governmental authority, provided that (to the extent legally permissible) the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement and cooperates with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5.5 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:

  (a) Is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;  
  (c) Is received from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation; or  
  (d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this **Article V** shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets and Personal Data, which shall be kept confidential for so long as they remain trade secrets or as required by Data Protection Laws, respectively.

## ARTICLE XII  
## SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLAs)

12.1 **Service Availability.**  
Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the critical support services and monitoring tools provided in connection with the Deliverables achieve an average monthly uptime of **99.9%**, excluding scheduled maintenance windows as agreed in advance with Contoso.

12.2 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Phoenix shall respond to and work towards resolving support requests in accordance with the following target Service Levels:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete system outage or major impact on mission-critical operations:  
      - Response Time: within 1 hour (24x7)  
      - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 8 hours  

  (b) **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation of performance or functionality, but system still operational:  
      - Response Time: within 4 Business Hours  
      - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 2 Business Days  

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Non-critical issues, performance concerns, or limited impact:  
      - Response Time: within 1 Business Day  
      - Target Resolution / Workaround: within 5 Business Days  

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues, cosmetic defects, or general inquiries:  
      - Response Time: within 3 Business Days  
      - Resolution: as reasonably scheduled in future maintenance releases.

12.3 **Remedies for SLA Breach.**  
If Phoenix fails to meet the uptime Service Level in **Section 12.1** in any calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to receive a service credit as follows:

  (a) Uptime between 99.5% and 99.9%: service credit equal to 5% of the monthly support Fee;  
  (b) Uptime between 99.0% and 99.5%: service credit equal to 10% of the monthly support Fee;  
  (c) Uptime below 99.0%: service credit equal to 20% of the monthly support Fee.

Service credits shall be applied against the next invoice(s) issued by Phoenix. Service credits shall not, in aggregate, exceed twenty percent (20%) of the total monthly support Fees for the month in which the SLA breach occurred.

12.4 **Exclusions from SLA Calculations.**  
The uptime and Service Level calculations shall exclude periods of unavailability caused by:

  (a) Scheduled maintenance agreed in advance with Contoso;  
  (b) Contoso’s or third-party systems, networks, or acts or omissions;  
  (c) Force Majeure Events as described in **Article XV**; or  
  (d) Contoso’s breach of this Agreement.

12.5 **Sole Remedies.**  
Service credits under this **Article XII** are Contoso’s sole and exclusive monetary remedy for Phoenix’s failure to meet the Service Levels, without prejudice to Contoso’s rights to terminate this Agreement for material breach or to seek equitable relief.

## ARTICLE XV  
## FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those involving a Party’s own workforce), governmental actions, embargoes, or failure of telecommunications or power infrastructure, provided that such event could not have been avoided by the exercise of reasonable care and diligence.

15.2 **Notice and Mitigation.**  
The affected Party shall:

  (a) Promptly notify the other Party in writing of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, providing reasonable details of its nature, expected duration, and impact on performance;  
  (b) Use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration that they are prevented by the Force Majeure Event. The time for performance shall be extended by a period equal to the duration of the Force Majeure Event.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period exceeding ninety (90) consecutive days and materially prevents performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party, without liability except for obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination.
