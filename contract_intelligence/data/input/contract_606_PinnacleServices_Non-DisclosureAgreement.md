## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Scope

2.1.1 Pinnacle shall perform the Services for Contoso as described in this Article II and any applicable SOWs. The Services will generally consist of:

- Business analysis and requirements gathering for Contoso’s internal systems and customer-facing platforms;  
- Design and implementation of integrations between Contoso’s existing systems and certain cloud-based platforms designated by Contoso;  
- Development of data pipelines, dashboards, and reporting tools to enable enhanced analytics and performance monitoring;  
- Configuration and deployment of selected software tools, subject to applicable third-party licenses;  
- Ongoing support, maintenance, and optimization of the implemented solutions; and  
- Project management, knowledge transfer, and training for Contoso’s personnel.

2.1.2 The Parties acknowledge that further details of the Services, including specific Deliverables, deadlines, and milestones, may be set forth in one or more SOWs or Change Orders, which shall be incorporated by reference into this Agreement, provided such SOWs or Change Orders are executed by authorized representatives of both Parties in accordance with Article XIII.

### 2.2 Detailed Description of Services

2.2.1 **Discovery and Requirements Gathering.**  
Pinnacle shall conduct an initial discovery phase, during which it will:

- Interview key stakeholders designated by Contoso;  
- Review existing documentation, architectures, process flows, and data models;  
- Assess Contoso’s business objectives, performance metrics, and constraints;  
- Identify gaps, dependencies, and risks in the current environment; and  
- Propose a detailed solution approach, including a high-level architecture, integration strategy, and phased implementation plan.

Pinnacle will produce a **Discovery Report** summarizing its findings, recommendations, preliminary timelines, and any assumptions or prerequisites for Contoso’s review and approval.

2.2.2 **Solution Design and Architecture.**  
Based on the approved Discovery Report, Pinnacle shall:

- Design the target technical architecture, including key components, integrations, and data flows;  
- Define detailed functional and technical specifications for each major feature, integration, or module;  
- Identify required Third-Party Materials and any associated licensing requirements;  
- Develop a security and data protection approach consistent with Article XI, including access controls, encryption, and logging; and  
- Provide Contoso with a **Solution Design Document** for approval prior to commencing development or implementation activities.

2.2.3 **Implementation, Integration, and Configuration.**  
Upon approval of the Solution Design Document, Pinnacle shall:

- Configure relevant platforms and tools in accordance with approved specifications;  
- Implement APIs, ETL processes, or other integration mechanisms as described in the SOW;  
- Develop custom scripts, connectors, dashboards, reports, and analytics as specified in writing;  
- Conduct unit testing, integration testing, and system testing; and  
- Coordinate with Contoso’s designated technical personnel to ensure alignment with Contoso’s internal standards and change management procedures.

Pinnacle shall maintain detailed records of configuration and customizations to facilitate troubleshooting, maintenance, and future enhancements.

2.2.4 **User Acceptance Testing (UAT) and Go-Live.**  
Pinnacle shall support Contoso’s UAT activities by:

- Providing UAT test plans and scripts aligned with business requirements;  
- Assisting in preparing and maintaining UAT environments;  
- Analyzing and documenting defects or issues identified during UAT; and  
- Implementing fixes, enhancements, or reconfigurations required for UAT success.

Following successful completion of UAT and Contoso’s written acceptance (which may be via email from an authorized representative), Pinnacle shall assist with production deployment, cutover planning, and go-live support.

2.2.5 **Support and Maintenance.**  
During the term of this Agreement and any applicable SOW, Pinnacle shall provide support services that may include:

- Incident management, help desk support, and troubleshooting;  
- Corrective maintenance for defects attributable to Pinnacle’s work;  
- Configuration adjustments and minor enhancements reasonably necessary to maintain system performance;  
- Performance monitoring and reporting against agreed Service Levels (Article XII); and  
- Periodic status meetings and written status reports.

Specific support hours, escalation procedures, and Service Levels shall be as set forth in Article XII and any applicable SOW.

2.2.6 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.**  
Pinnacle shall provide training sessions (onsite or remote, as mutually agreed) for Contoso personnel to enable effective use and administration of the implemented solutions. Training may include:

- User training for business users;  
- Technical training for system administrators and support staff;  
- Documentation, including user manuals, configuration guides, and operational runbooks; and  
- Train-the-trainer sessions, if requested by Contoso.

### 2.3 Deliverables and Milestones

2.3.1 Pinnacle shall produce Deliverables as described in Section 2.2 and any applicable SOW, which may include, without limitation:

- Discovery Report;  
- Solution Design Document;  
- Configuration and Customization Documentation;  
- Integration and Data Flow Specifications;  
- Test Plans, Test Scripts, and Test Reports;  
- User Manuals and Training Materials; and  
- Periodic Performance and Service Level Reports.

2.3.2 Each SOW shall define specific milestones and associated Deliverables. Milestones may include, by way of example:

- Completion of discovery and requirements gathering;  
- Approval of Solution Design Document;  
- Completion of development/configuration;  
- Completion of integration and system testing;  
- Successful completion of UAT; and  
- Production go-live and transition to steady-state support.

2.3.3 Unless otherwise agreed in an SOW, Contoso shall have a reasonable period, not to exceed fifteen (15) Business Days after receipt of a Deliverable, to review and either accept the Deliverable or provide written notice of deficiencies. Pinnacle shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct any identified deficiencies in a timely manner and resubmit the Deliverable for acceptance.

### 2.4 Performance Standards

2.4.1 Pinnacle shall perform the Services:

- In a timely, professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner;  
- In accordance with generally accepted industry standards and practices;  
- By personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and qualifications; and  
- In compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and professional standards.

2.4.2 Pinnacle shall designate a project manager who will serve as the primary point of contact for Contoso, coordinate resources, attend regular status meetings, and ensure adherence to the agreed schedule and Service Levels.

2.4.3 Contoso shall reasonably cooperate with Pinnacle, including providing necessary access to systems, data, and personnel, subject to appropriate security and confidentiality measures. Delays caused by Contoso’s failure to cooperate or provide timely approvals may be subject to reasonable schedule adjustments and, if materially impactful, may require a Change Order in accordance with Article XIII.

### 2.5 Subcontractors

2.5.1 Pinnacle may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that:

- Pinnacle remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors;  
- Any subcontractor accessing Contoso’s Confidential Information or Personal Data is bound by written confidentiality and data protection obligations at least as protective as those set forth in this Agreement; and  
- Pinnacle provides Contoso with notice of any material subcontractors performing core Services.

2.5.2 Contoso may reasonably object to a proposed core subcontractor on legitimate grounds related to security, conflict of interest, or regulatory constraints; in such case, the Parties shall work in good faith to identify an alternative resource or mitigation.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until the **Expiration Date of November 14, 2026** (the **“Term”**).

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend the Term beyond the Expiration Date, in which case such extension shall be considered an amendment in accordance with Section 16.2.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

4.2.1 **Client-Friendly Termination Right.** Contoso may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience, in whole or in part, upon **thirty (30) days’ prior written notice** to Pinnacle.

4.2.2 Pinnacle may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience upon **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to Contoso, provided that Pinnacle shall complete any then-in-progress critical milestones reasonably identified by Contoso and cooperate in a professional transition of Services, unless Contoso agrees otherwise in writing.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

- Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach; or  
- Becomes insolvent, files or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy (which is not dismissed within sixty (60) days), makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial part of its assets.

4.3.2 If the breach is not reasonably curable within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall commence cure within such period and diligently pursue completion thereof, and the non-breaching Party may extend the cure period in its reasonable discretion.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

4.4.1 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason:

- Each Party shall promptly cease any further use of the other Party’s Confidential Information, except as necessary to wind down activities;  
- Pinnacle shall promptly return or, at Contoso’s option, securely destroy Contoso’s Confidential Information and Personal Data in accordance with Sections 5.4 and 11.7;  
- Contoso shall pay Pinnacle for all Services properly performed and approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, subject to any bona fide disputes; and  
- Pinnacle shall deliver to Contoso any completed or in-progress Deliverables for which Contoso has paid or is obligated to pay.

4.4.2 In the event of termination by Contoso for Pinnacle’s uncured material breach, Contoso shall have no obligation to pay for any Services that are not satisfactorily performed or Deliverables not accepted, and Contoso may exercise its rights under Article VIII (Indemnification).

### 4.5 Survival

4.5.1 The following provisions shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason: Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance) (with respect to periods of required coverage), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), XVI (General Provisions), and any other provisions which by their nature are intended to survive.

## ARTICLE V

### 5.1 Definition of Confidential Information

5.1.1 **“Confidential Information”** means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Disclosing Party to a Receiving Party, directly or indirectly, in any form (including oral, written, electronic, or visual), that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

5.1.2 Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- Business plans, strategies, forecasts, and financial information;  
- Customer, supplier, and partner information;  
- Product roadmaps, technical data, trade secrets, know-how, and inventions;  
- System architectures, configurations, and security procedures;  
- Personal Data and proprietary data sets; and  
- The terms and conditions of this Agreement, including pricing and Contract Value.

### 5.2 Exceptions

5.2.1 Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

- Is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
- Was lawfully in the Receiving Party’s possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party and not subject to any duty of confidentiality;  
- Is lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

### 5.3 Obligations of Confidentiality

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:

- Use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;  
- Not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein; and  
- Protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its and its Affiliates’ employees, officers, directors, contractors, and professional advisors (collectively, **“Representatives”**) who have a legitimate need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Article V.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure

5.4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall:

- Provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice, to the extent legally permissible, so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and  
- Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required, and use reasonable efforts to obtain assurance that such information will be treated confidentially.

### 5.5 Return or Destruction

5.5.1 Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

- Return to the Disclosing Party all tangible materials containing Confidential Information; or  
- Destroy such materials and certify in writing that destruction has occurred, except that the Receiving Party may retain copies to the extent required by applicable law or for compliance, archival, or back-up purposes, subject to continued confidentiality obligations.

### 5.6 Survival

5.6.1 The obligations in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect during the Term and for **five (5) years** after the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except that trade secrets and Personal Data shall be kept confidential for so long as they qualify as trade secrets or as required by Data Protection Laws, respectively.
