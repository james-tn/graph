## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

12.1.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the Software is available **99.9%** of the time during each calendar month, excluding Downtime as defined in Section 12.1.2.

12.1.2 “Downtime” means any period during which the production environment of the Software is not accessible by Users, excluding:

  (a) Scheduled maintenance (for which Vendor provides at least forty-eight (48) hours’ advance notice);  
  (b) Emergency maintenance required to address critical security vulnerabilities;  
  (c) Outages caused by Client’s systems, networks, or third-party services under Client’s control; or  
  (d) Force Majeure Events.

### 12.2 Response and Resolution Times

12.2.1 Vendor shall respond to support requests in accordance with the following target response times:

  - **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Production outage or complete loss of core functionality: Initial response within one (1) hour during Business Days; continuous efforts until resolution or workaround;  
  - **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation with no reasonable workaround: Initial response within four (4) hours during Business Days;  
  - **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Partial loss of functionality with a workaround: Initial response within one (1) Business Day;  
  - **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issues or general inquiries: Initial response within two (2) Business Days.

12.2.2 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve incidents promptly but does not guarantee resolution times, recognizing that complex issues may require extended investigation.

### 12.3 Service Credits

12.3.1 If Vendor fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1.1 in any calendar month, Client may be eligible for service credits as follows:

  (a) Availability < 99.9% but ≥ 99.0%: Service credit equal to 5% of the monthly subscription fee;  
  (b) Availability < 99.0% but ≥ 98.0%: Service credit equal to 10% of the monthly subscription fee;  
  (c) Availability < 98.0%: Service credit equal to 15% of the monthly subscription fee.

12.3.2 Service credits shall be applied against future subscription fees and shall be the sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitment, without limiting the broader liability provisions in Article IX.

12.3.3 To receive a service credit, Client must submit a written request to Vendor within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the downtime occurred, detailing the dates and times of perceived Downtime.
