## ARTICLE II. SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent SOW.**  

2.1.1 This Work Order is executed under and subject to the terms and conditions of the Parent SOW **SOW-ZEN-202401-226 (contract_226)** and **adds scope** to the existing Project described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent SOW. Except as expressly provided herein, nothing in this Work Order shall be construed to limit or reduce Vendor’s obligations under the Parent SOW, including but not limited to Vendor’s obligations regarding **Performance Standards (Section 2.5)**, **Deliverable Review and Acceptance (Section 2.6)**, **Client Responsibilities (Section 2.7)**, and **Key Personnel (Section 2.8)**.

2.1.2 All Services and Deliverables under this Work Order shall be deemed part of the “Project” as defined in Section 1.14 of the Parent SOW and shall be performed in accordance with the same professional standards set out in **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW, unless expressly modified herein.

2.2 **Overview of Project Expansion.**  

Vendor shall provide Client with **Expanded Services** to extend the Solution to additional regions, enhance analytics and automation capabilities, and strengthen managed support. These Expanded Services are organized into the following workstreams, which build upon the Phases described in **Section 2.2** of the Parent SOW:

- **Workstream A – Global Rollout & Localization**  
- **Workstream B – Advanced Analytics & Data Warehouse Enhancement**  
- **Workstream C – Automation & Orchestration**  
- **Workstream D – Enhanced Support Services & Operational Excellence**

2.2.1 **Workstream A – Global Rollout & Localization (Estimated: Sep 2024 – Jun 2026).** Vendor shall:

(a) Perform a global requirements discovery for Client’s non-U.S. entities, aligned with the approach in **Section 2.2.1 (Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements)** of the Parent SOW, including:

- Workshops with regional finance, operations, and IT stakeholders.  
- Documentation of country‑specific tax, compliance, and reporting requirements.  
- Identification of localization requirements (languages, currencies, chart of accounts, fiscal calendars).

(b) Design and configure localized Solution instances for at least three (3) non-U.S. jurisdictions, including:

- Multi‑currency and multi‑entity configuration consistent with the Solution’s architecture designed under **Section 2.2.2 (Phase 2 – Solution Design and Configuration)** of the Parent SOW.  
- Localized tax rules and invoice templates.  
- Country‑specific reporting packs.

(c) Deliver the following **Additional Deliverables**:

- **Global Rollout Requirements Addendum** – supplementing the original Requirements Specification Document (RSD) delivered under the Parent SOW, clearly indicating incremental scope.  
- **Localization Design Pack** – detailed configuration design for each jurisdiction, consistent with the existing FDS and TDS framework.  
- **Regional Cutover & Deployment Plan** – aligned with the Go‑Live Cutover Plan under **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent SOW.

(d) Target Milestones:

- **A1:** Completion and Client acceptance (per Section 2.6 of the Parent SOW) of the Global Rollout Requirements Addendum.  
- **A2:** Completion and acceptance of Localization Design Pack.  
- **A3:** Successful deployment and go‑live of first non-U.S. jurisdiction and 30‑day stabilization period.

2.2.2 **Workstream B – Advanced Analytics & Data Warehouse Enhancement (Estimated: Jan 2025 – Dec 2026).** Vendor shall:

(a) Extend Client’s existing reporting and analytics, originally scoped under **Section 2.2.2 (Design and Configuration)** and **Section 2.2.3 (Development, Integration & Data Migration)** of the Parent SOW, by:

- Designing and implementing an **Advanced Analytics Module**, leveraging a cloud data warehouse and BI tools agreed by the Parties.  
- Creating at least twelve (12) executive dashboards covering financial performance, supply chain KPIs, and customer metrics.  
- Implementing a semantic data model and standardized data definitions.

(b) Develop ETL and data pipelines extending the **Data Migration Runbook** delivered under the Parent SOW, including:

- Historical data loading for up to seven (7) years of financial and sales data, subject to Client’s data availability.  
- Near real‑time data ingestion from the Solution and selected third‑party systems.

(c) Deliver the following Additional Deliverables:

- **Analytics Solution Blueprint** – architecture and design of the Advanced Analytics Module.  
- **Data Warehouse Schema & Documentation** – including entity relationship diagrams and data dictionaries.  
- **Analytics Dashboard Catalog** – listing and describing each dashboard and its metrics.  

(d) Target Milestones:

- **B1:** Acceptance of Analytics Solution Blueprint.  
- **B2:** Acceptance of initial data warehouse deployment and successful test data load.  
- **B3:** Acceptance of production-ready dashboards after UAT, following acceptance procedures consistent with **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW.

2.2.3 **Workstream C – Automation & Orchestration (Estimated: Jul 2025 – Oct 2027).** Vendor shall:

(a) Identify automation opportunities across Client’s finance, order management, and customer service processes by conducting workshops and process assessments, building upon the business analysis activities in **Section 2.3(b)** of the Parent SOW.

(b) Design and implement at least:

- Eight (8) end‑to‑end workflow automations leveraging the Solution’s native workflow engine.  
- Four (4) RPA (Robotic Process Automation) bots or equivalent automation scripts for repetitive back‑office tasks.  
- Automated exception handling and alerting for integrations originally developed under **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent SOW.

(c) Deliver:

- **Automation Design Specifications** – similar in structure to the Functional Design Specifications (FDS) under the Parent SOW.  
- **Orchestration Runbook** – operating procedures, trigger conditions, and fallback mechanisms.  
- **Automation Performance Report** – baseline vs. post‑implementation metrics.

(d) Milestones:

- **C1:** Completion and acceptance of Automation Design Specifications.  
- **C2:** Successful deployment of initial wave of automations and completion of SIT/UAT.  
- **C3:** Acceptance of Orchestration Runbook and sign‑off by Client.

2.2.4 **Workstream D – Enhanced Support Services & Operational Excellence (Estimated: Jan 2026 – Jan 2028).** Vendor shall:

(a) Provide **Enhanced Support Services** that augment Phase 5 services under **Section 2.2.5** of the Parent SOW by:

- Extending Level 2 and Level 3 support coverage to include Global Rollout entities and the Advanced Analytics Module.  
- Providing proactive monitoring of critical integrations and automations.  
- Offering a dedicated Technical Account Manager and quarterly executive reviews.

(b) Meet or exceed the Parent SLA commitments in **Article XII** of the Parent SOW and the **Supplemental SLA** set forth in **Article XII** of this Work Order.

(c) Provide Additional Deliverables:

- **Quarterly Global System Health & Analytics Reports** – supplementing the “System Health Reports and Optimization Recommendations” referenced in Section 2.2.5 of the Parent SOW.  
- **Annual Capacity and Performance Assessment** – detailing system load and scaling recommendations.  

2.3 **In-Scope Services under this Work Order.** In addition to the “In‑Scope Services” described in **Section 2.3** of the Parent SOW, the following are expressly in scope for this Work Order:

(a) Global rollout design, configuration, testing, and deployment activities for the jurisdictions listed in the mutually agreed Global Rollout Requirements Addendum.

(b) Design, build, and deployment of the Advanced Analytics Module, data warehouse extensions, and associated dashboards.

(c) Automation & Orchestration Workstream, including RPA or equivalent automation, subject to tooling agreed in writing.

(d) Enhanced Support Services as described in **Section 2.2.4** above and **Article XII** below.

(e) Project management specific to the Project Expansion, including integrated planning with the existing Project Plan established under **Section 2.2.1** of the Parent SOW.

2.4 **Out-of-Scope Services under this Work Order.**  

Without limiting **Section 2.4 (Out‑of‑Scope Services)** of the Parent SOW, the following are expressly out of scope for this Work Order unless incorporated via a Change Order under **Article XIII** of the Parent SOW and this Work Order:

(a) Any new ERP or CRM modules not already contemplated in the Parent SOW or this Work Order (e.g., HR, Payroll core system replacement).  

(b) Custom mobile applications beyond interfaces and responsive UI adjustments necessary to access the Solution.  

(c) Development of any new third‑party systems unrelated to the Solution or the Advanced Analytics Module.  

(d) Large‑scale business process re‑engineering beyond optimization directly related to the Solution.  

(e) Migration of data other than the data expressly identified in the extended Data Migration Runbook or Analytics Solution Blueprint.

2.5 **Performance Standards and Acceptance.**  

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Expanded Services in accordance with **Section 2.5 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent SOW and with the same degree of skill, care, and diligence customarily exercised by leading providers of enterprise systems implementation and support services.

2.5.2 Additional Deliverables under this Work Order shall be subject to review and acceptance by Client in accordance with **Section 2.6 (Deliverable Review and Acceptance)** of the Parent SOW, except that the Parties may agree to adjusted Review Periods for specific Deliverables in the Milestone table in **Section 3.2** of this Work Order. Any adjustments shall be expressly documented.

2.5.3 Acceptance of any Deliverable under this Work Order shall not constitute a waiver of Client’s rights under **Article VII (Representations and Warranties)** of the Parent SOW, nor any rights relating to latent defects, all of which are preserved.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities Specific to This Work Order.**  

In addition to the responsibilities set out in **Section 2.7** of the Parent SOW, Client shall:

(a) Provide timely access to non-U.S. business units and local subject matter experts required for the Global Rollout and localization efforts.

(b) Ensure that necessary licenses and access rights to any third‑party analytics or RPA tools selected for the Advanced Analytics Module and Automation & Orchestration Workstream are obtained and maintained.

(c) Provide historical and operational data needed for analytics and data warehouse activities in reasonably usable formats, acknowledging that Vendor may rely on the accuracy and completeness of such Client Materials in accordance with **Section 2.7.3** of the Parent SOW.

(d) Assign a Global Program Manager to coordinate with Vendor’s project manager for integrated planning and risk management.

## ARTICLE III. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  

3.1.1 The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables under this Work Order shall not exceed **Four Million Eight Hundred Seventy‑Nine Thousand Seven Hundred Ninety U.S. Dollars (US$4,879,790)**, exclusive of any applicable taxes.

3.1.2 The foregoing amount is **incremental** to, and does not amend or supersede, the total contract value of **US$1,792,213** specified in **Section 3.1** of the Parent SOW. For clarity, the combined not‑to‑exceed aggregate for the Parent SOW and this Work Order (excluding taxes) is **US$6,672,003**, subject to increases via duly executed Change Orders pursuant to **Article XIII** of the Parent SOW and this Work Order.

3.2.1 Fees under this Work Order shall be structured as follows:

(a) **Fixed-Price Components:**  

- Workstream A – Global Rollout & Localization: **US$1,450,000**  
- Workstream B – Advanced Analytics & Data Warehouse Enhancement: **US$1,580,000**  
- Workstream C – Automation & Orchestration: **US$1,120,000**

(b) **Time-and-Materials Components (Enhanced Support Services):**  

- Workstream D – Enhanced Support Services & Operational Excellence shall be billed on a time‑and‑materials (“**T&M**”) basis at Vendor’s standard rate card for this Work Order:  
  - Senior Architect: **US$245/hour**  
  - Senior Consultant: **US$205/hour**  
  - Consultant: **US$165/hour**  
  - Support Engineer: **US$135/hour**  
  - Technical Account Manager: **US$225/hour**  

The T&M fees for Workstream D are subject to a not‑to‑exceed (“**NTE**”) amount of **US$729,790** over the Term of this Work Order, unless increased by a written Change Order.

3.2.2 **Milestone Payments – Fixed-Price Components.**

Fixed‑price fees shall be invoiced upon achievement of the following Milestones and Client’s acceptance of the related Deliverables, in accordance with **Section 2.6** of the Parent SOW:

- **Milestone A1:** Completion and Client acceptance of Global Rollout Requirements Addendum – **30% of Workstream A fees** (US$435,000).

- **Milestone A3:** First non-U.S. jurisdiction go‑live and completion of 30‑day stabilization – **30% of Workstream A fees** (US$435,000).

- **Milestone B1:** Acceptance of Analytics Solution Blueprint – **25% of Workstream B fees** (US$395,000).  
- **Milestone B2:** Acceptance of initial data warehouse deployment and successful test data load – **35% of Workstream B fees** (US$553,000).  
- **Milestone B3:** Acceptance of production-ready dashboards post‑UAT – **40% of Workstream B fees** (US$632,000).

- **Milestone C1:** Completion and acceptance of Automation Design Specifications – **30% of Workstream C fees** (US$336,000).  
- **Milestone C2:** Deployment of initial automations and completion of SIT/UAT – **40% of Workstream C fees** (US$448,000).  
- **Milestone C3:** Acceptance of Orchestration Runbook and Production Handover – **30% of Workstream C fees** (US$336,000).

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  

3.3.1 Vendor shall invoice Client:

(a) For fixed‑price components, upon achievement of the applicable Milestone as described in **Section 3.2.2** above; and  

(b) For T&M Services under Workstream D, on a **monthly** basis in arrears, detailing hours worked by role and the Services performed.

3.3.2 Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **thirty (30) days** from the date of Client’s receipt of invoice (“**Net 30**”), consistent with **Section 3.3.2** of the Parent SOW.

3.3.3 Disputed amounts shall be handled in accordance with **Section 3.6 (Disputed Amounts)** of the Parent SOW, which is incorporated herein by reference.

Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, from the date such payment was due until paid in full, as provided in **Section 3.4** of the Parent SOW. Vendor’s rights to suspend Services for non‑payment shall be as stated in **Section 3.4** of the Parent SOW and shall apply to this Work Order.

3.5 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  

3.5.1 Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket travel and lodging expenses incurred in connection with this Work Order, consistent with **Section 3.5** of the Parent SOW and Client’s then‑current travel and expense policy.

## ARTICLE IV. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  

The term of this Work Order shall commence on the Effective Date (**August 09, 2024**) and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 23, 2028** (the “**Expiration Date**”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or the Parent SOW.

4.2 **Relationship to Parent SOW Term.**  

4.2.1 The Term of this Work Order falls within, and is intended to be co‑terminous with, the Term of the Parent SOW which, pursuant to **Section 4.1** of the Parent SOW, expires on **October 16, 2028**, unless earlier terminated.

4.2.2 In the event the Parent SOW is terminated for any reason, this Work Order shall automatically terminate on the effective date of such termination, without the need for additional notice, except as otherwise expressly agreed in writing by the Parties.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  

4.3.1 Notwithstanding **Section 4.2 (Termination for Convenience)** of the Parent SOW, either Party may terminate this Work Order for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

4.3.2 In the event of such termination for convenience:

(a) Client shall pay Vendor for (i) all Services performed and Deliverables delivered and accepted through the effective date of termination; (ii) any completed Milestones not yet invoiced but achieved by the effective date; and (iii) any non‑cancellable, pre‑approved expenses incurred in reliance on this Work Order; and  

(b) Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to effect an orderly wind‑down of the Expanded Services and, at Client’s request and cost, provide transition assistance for up to thirty (30) days following termination, consistent with **Section 4.2** of the Parent SOW.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

4.4.1 Either Party may terminate this Work Order immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach of this Work Order and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days following written notice thereof, consistent with **Section 4.3 (Termination for Cause)** of the Parent SOW.

4.4.2 For purposes of this Work Order, material breach by Client includes, in addition to the examples in **Section 4.3.3** of the Parent SOW, repeated failure to pay undisputed fees related to this Work Order. Material breach by Vendor includes, in addition to the examples in **Section 4.3.2** of the Parent SOW, persistent failure to meet the Supplemental SLA targets in **Article XII** of this Work Order that materially impact the Enhanced Support Services.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.**  

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Work Order for any reason:

(a) Vendor shall promptly cease performing the Expanded Services, except as reasonably necessary to wind down activities or provide agreed transition assistance;  

(b) Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 5.4** of the Parent SOW and **Section 5.4** of this Work Order; and  

(c) Any licenses to Work Product or Deliverables already granted under the Parent SOW shall continue, subject to payment in full of applicable fees, and the IP framework in **Article VI** of the Parent SOW and **Article VI** of this Work Order shall remain in effect.

4.5.2 Termination of this Work Order shall not relieve Client of any payment obligations accrued prior to the effective date of termination, nor shall it limit either Party’s rights or remedies for any breach occurring prior to such termination.

4.6 **Survival.**  

The provisions of this Work Order which by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration, including but not limited to **Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV, XVI**, and any applicable provisions of the Parent SOW incorporated herein, shall so survive.
