## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 Engagement of Service Provider  

2.1.1 The Company hereby engages the Service Provider, and the Service Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services and Deliverables described in this Agreement and in one or more Statements of Work executed by the Parties during the Term.  

2.1.2 Each SOW shall form an integral part of this Agreement and shall be subject to all of the terms and conditions set forth herein, except to the extent that such SOW expressly states otherwise with specific reference to the provision to be modified.

### 2.2 General Description of Services  

2.2.1 The Services shall include, without limitation:

  a. **Technology Consulting and Advisory Services**, including assessment of the Company’s existing IT infrastructure, applications and processes; development of target-state architectures; roadmap planning; and strategic recommendations for modernization, migration, security and optimization.

  b. **Software Design, Development and Integration**, including design of system architectures; development of custom applications and modules; configuration and customization of third-party platforms; application programming interface (API) development; and integration with the Company’s existing systems, databases and third-party services.

  c. **Data Analytics and Business Intelligence**, including data ingestion, data modeling, data warehousing, dashboard and report development, advanced analytics, and related services intended to enable the Company to derive insights from its operational and customer data.

  d. **Managed Services and Hosting**, including, where applicable and as specified in a SOW, hosting of applications and data in data centers or cloud environments operated by or on behalf of the Service Provider; system monitoring; incident management; capacity planning; and routine operational support.

  e. **Maintenance and Support Services**, including provision of helpdesk support, bug fixes, patches, updates, upgrades and enhancements for the Deliverables and any related systems, at the support levels and within the response and resolution times specified in the applicable SOW and Service Level commitments.

  f. **Training and Knowledge Transfer**, including training sessions, workshops, documentation and other forms of knowledge transfer to enable the Company’s personnel to effectively use, administer and support the Deliverables and Services.

2.2.2 The Parties acknowledge that the foregoing description provides a general overview of the types of Services that may be provided under this Agreement. The specific Services and Deliverables, including their scope, technical details, acceptance criteria, and any assumptions or dependencies, shall be set forth in one or more SOWs.

### 2.3 Statements of Work  

2.3.1 Prior to the commencement of any particular project or workstream, the Parties shall negotiate and execute a SOW. Each SOW shall, to the extent applicable, specify:

  a. the detailed description of the Services and Deliverables;  
  b. the project plan, milestones, and estimated schedule;  
  c. the roles and responsibilities of each Party;  
  d. acceptance criteria and testing procedures;  
  e. any applicable Service Levels;  
  f. the fees, expenses and payment terms (consistent with Article III); and  
  g. any project-specific terms, assumptions or dependencies.

2.3.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between the terms of this Agreement and the terms of a SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede a specific provision of this Agreement, in which case such SOW provision shall govern solely for purposes of that SOW.

### 2.4 Performance Standards  

2.4.1 The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for similar services, and using personnel who are suitably skilled, experienced and qualified.

2.4.2 The Service Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the schedules, milestones and delivery dates specified in each SOW. The Parties acknowledge that certain schedules may be estimates and that timely performance may depend upon the Company’s provision of information, approvals, access and other Client Materials.

2.4.3 The Company shall provide timely access to Client Materials, systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably required by the Service Provider to perform the Services. The Service Provider shall not be liable for any delay or failure in performance to the extent caused by the Company’s failure to comply with its obligations under this Agreement.

2.4.4 Unless specifically set forth in a SOW, time shall not be of the essence for any obligations of the Service Provider; provided that the Service Provider shall exercise commercially reasonable efforts to comply with the timelines and Service Levels agreed by the Parties.

### 2.5 Acceptance of Deliverables  

2.5.1 Each SOW may set forth procedures and timeframes for the Company to review and accept or reject Deliverables. In the absence of specific procedures in a SOW, the following default process shall apply:

  a. Upon delivery of a Deliverable, the Service Provider shall notify the Company in writing.  
  b. The Company shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt of such Deliverable to review and either (i) provide written acceptance, or (ii) provide a written notice of material non-conformity describing in reasonable detail the manner in which the Deliverable fails to meet the agreed specifications or acceptance criteria.  
  c. If the Company fails to respond within such fifteen (15) Business Day period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

2.5.2 Upon receipt of a timely and valid rejection notice, the Service Provider shall, at its own cost, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the identified material non-conformities and re-deliver the corrected Deliverable within a reasonable period. The Company shall then have a further review period as set forth in Section 2.5.1.  

2.5.3 Acceptance of a Deliverable shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned or delayed. Acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of a Deliverable shall not limit the Company’s rights with respect to latent defects that could not reasonably have been discovered during the acceptance testing period.

### 2.6 Subcontracting  

2.6.1 The Service Provider may subcontract portions of the Services to Third Parties; provided that the Service Provider shall remain fully responsible for the performance of such subcontractors and for compliance with this Agreement.  

2.6.2 The Service Provider shall ensure that any subcontractor that will have access to the Company’s Confidential Information or Personal Data is bound by contractual obligations of confidentiality and data protection no less protective than those set forth herein.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Fees and Contract Value  

3.1.1 In consideration for the Services and Deliverables provided under this Agreement and the applicable SOWs, the Company shall pay the Service Provider the fees set forth in each SOW, which shall be structured in accordance with one or more of the following models:

  a. **Fixed Fee:** A fixed amount for completion of defined Deliverables or project phases;  
  b. **Time and Materials:** Fees based on actual time spent and materials used at the rates specified in the applicable SOW; and/or  
  c. **Retainer / Managed Services Fee:** A recurring monthly or annual fee for ongoing Services as defined in the SOW.

3.1.2 As of the Effective Date, the Parties estimate that the aggregate Contract Value for the initial set of SOWs under this Agreement shall be approximately **USD $1,928,271**, subject to adjustment for Change Orders, new SOWs, or other modifications in accordance with this Agreement.

### 3.2 Payment Terms  

3.2.1 Unless otherwise provided in the applicable SOW, the Service Provider shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears for Services performed and expenses incurred during the preceding month.

3.2.2 The Company shall pay all undisputed amounts set forth in each invoice within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of receipt of such invoice (**“Net 45”**). Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars (USD) by wire transfer or other method agreed by the Parties.

3.2.3 If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice, the Company shall notify the Service Provider in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of such invoice, specifying the amounts in dispute and the reasons therefor. The Company shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.2.2. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the disputed portion, and, upon resolution, any amount determined to be payable shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days.

### 3.3 Payment Milestones  

3.3.1 Where a SOW specifies payment milestones, the relevant portion of the fees shall become due and payable upon achievement of the applicable milestone, which may be linked to the delivery and acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of certain Deliverables or completion of certain phases of the Services.

3.3.2 The SOW may include, without limitation, milestones such as:

  a. project initiation and mobilization;  
  b. completion of design and specification documents;  
  c. completion of development and internal testing;  
  d. completion of user acceptance testing;  
  e. go-live or production deployment; and  
  f. expiry of a post-implementation support period.

### 3.4 Late Payment  

3.4.1 Any amount not paid when due (other than amounts validly disputed in accordance with Section 3.2.3) shall accrue interest from the due date until the date of payment at a rate of one percent (1%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower.

3.4.2 In addition, if any undisputed payment remains overdue for more than thirty (30) days, the Service Provider may, upon written notice to the Company, suspend performance of some or all of the Services until all overdue amounts (including interest) are paid in full. Any such suspension shall not be deemed a breach by the Service Provider.

### 3.5 Taxes  

3.5.1 All fees and charges under this Agreement are exclusive of any applicable taxes, duties, levies, or similar governmental assessments, including value-added tax (VAT), goods and services tax (GST), sales tax or withholding tax (collectively, **“Taxes”**).  

3.5.2 The Company shall be responsible for payment of all Taxes associated with the Services and Deliverables, except for taxes based on the Service Provider’s net income, property or employees. If the Company is required by law to withhold Taxes from any payment, the Company shall pay such Taxes to the appropriate tax authority and provide the Service Provider with an official receipt or other appropriate documentation.

### 3.6 Expenses and Reimbursement  

3.6.1 The Company shall reimburse the Service Provider for reasonable and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other expenses, in accordance with the Company’s then-current expense reimbursement policies, if provided in writing to the Service Provider.

3.6.2 The Service Provider shall provide supporting documentation, such as receipts, for expenses exceeding the threshold specified in the applicable SOW or the Company’s policy.

3.6.3 Any extraordinary or unusual expenses (such as international travel, specialized equipment or third-party licenses not otherwise contemplated in the SOW) shall require the Company’s prior written approval.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Term  

4.1.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall continue in full force and effect until **6 February 2028** (the **“Expiration Date”**) (the **“Term”**).

4.1.2 The Parties may mutually agree in writing to extend the Term for additional periods, subject to such terms and conditions as the Parties may negotiate.

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience  

4.2.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience and without cause by providing at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’** prior written notice to the other Party.

4.2.2 In the event of termination for convenience by the Company:

  a. the Company shall pay the Service Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination;  
  b. to the extent a SOW provides for minimum fees, early termination fees or non-cancellable commitments, the Company shall pay the Service Provider the amounts specified therein; and  
  c. any pre-paid but unearned fees shall be handled in accordance with the applicable SOW, it being understood that the Service Provider shall be entitled to retain any amounts that are non-refundable or constitute a minimum commitment.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause  

4.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days (or such longer period as may be reasonably necessary if the breach is not capable of being cured within thirty (30) days, provided that the breaching Party commences cure during such period and diligently pursues such cure) after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

4.3.2 Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Service Provider shall have the right to terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the Company (a) fails to pay any undisputed amount due under this Agreement within sixty (60) days after the due date, or (b) breaches its confidentiality obligations or infringes the Service Provider’s Intellectual Property Rights in a manner that is not curable.

### 4.4 Insolvency  

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) becomes insolvent or unable to pay its debts as they become due, (b) files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or similar relief that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days, (c) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or (d) has a receiver or similar officer appointed over all or substantially all of its assets.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination  

4.5.1 Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  a. all SOWs then in effect shall automatically terminate (unless the Parties agree in writing that any particular SOW shall continue under the terms of this Agreement as if in effect solely for that purpose);  
  b. the Company shall immediately pay all amounts due and payable for Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, including any applicable termination or early cancellation fees;  
  c. each Party shall, upon written request, return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, subject to Section 5.6 and any record retention obligations under applicable law.

4.5.2 Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not relieve either Party of any obligation or liability accrued prior to the effective date of termination, nor shall it affect any provision of this Agreement which, by its nature or express terms, is intended to survive termination or expiration, including without limitation Articles V, VI, VII, VIII, IX, XI, XIV, XV and XVI.

5.1.1 **“Confidential Information”** means any non-public, proprietary or confidential information, in any form or medium, disclosed or made available by or on behalf of a Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**) in connection with this Agreement, whether before or after the Effective Date, including without limitation:

  b. product plans, product designs, inventions (whether patentable or not), know-how, Trade Secrets, technical data, software (including source code and object code), documentation, and research and development activities;  
  c. operational, administrative, and organizational information;  
  d. any data, information or materials relating to the Company’s customers or end users; and  
  e. the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

5.1.2 Confidential Information shall include information disclosed by a Party’s Affiliates, employees, contractors, or advisors, and information disclosed prior to the Effective Date in connection with the contemplated relationship.

### 5.2 Exclusions  

Confidential Information shall not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:

  a. is or becomes generally known to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  b. was rightfully in the Receiving Party’s possession without restriction on use or disclosure prior to receipt from the Disclosing Party;  
  c. is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a Third Party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or  
  d. is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

### 5.3 Obligations of Confidentiality  

5.3.1 The Receiving Party shall:

  a. use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  
  b. restrict access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know such information for such purpose and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less protective than those set forth herein;  
  c. protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own similar information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.3.2 The Receiving Party shall not, without the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent, disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any Third Party, except as expressly permitted in this Agreement.

### 5.4 Compelled Disclosure  

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or legal process (including subpoena or court order) to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permissible, promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing and cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective measures are not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose, and shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that such information is treated as confidential.

### 5.5 Survival  

The obligations of confidentiality and restrictions on use set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in effect for **five (5) years** following the expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that, with respect to Trade Secrets, such obligations shall survive for so long as such Confidential Information qualifies as a Trade Secret under applicable law.

### 5.6 Residuals  

Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party shall be free to use for any purpose the general knowledge, skills and experience, and any ideas, concepts, know-how or techniques that are retained in the unaided memory of its personnel who have had access to Confidential Information in the course of performing this Agreement, provided that such personnel have not intentionally memorized such Confidential Information for the purpose of retaining and using it in such manner.
