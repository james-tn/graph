## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General

2.1.1 Delta shall provide to Contoso the Maintenance Services described in this Article II and in any SOW executed pursuant to this Agreement. The Services are intended to maintain, support, and enhance the performance, reliability, and availability of the Equipment and associated systems.

2.1.2 The Services covered by this Agreement include, without limitation: (a) preventive maintenance; (b) corrective maintenance and repairs; (c) remote and on-site support; (d) monitoring and alerts; (e) patching and firmware updates for supported systems; (f) documentation and reporting; and (g) related consulting and advisory services in support of the foregoing.

### 2.2 Preventive Maintenance

2.2.1 Delta shall perform preventive maintenance activities on the Equipment in accordance with industry best practices, manufacturer recommendations, and the schedules set forth in the applicable SOWs. Such activities may include, as applicable:

- Inspection, cleaning, lubrication, and calibration of mechanical components;  
- Testing of system functionality and performance;  
- Installation of manufacturer-approved firmware and software updates;  
- Backups of system configuration files and relevant data;  
- Verification of security settings, user access controls, and logging configurations.

2.2.2 Preventive maintenance will be scheduled in coordination with Contoso so as to minimize disruption to Contoso’s operations. Delta shall provide at least five (5) Business Days’ prior written notice of Scheduled Downtime for preventive maintenance, except in cases where a shorter notice period is mutually agreed or where urgent action is required to prevent imminent system failure or security risk.

### 2.3 Corrective Maintenance and Incident Response

2.3.1 Delta shall provide corrective maintenance to diagnose, troubleshoot, and remediate Incidents affecting the Equipment. Corrective maintenance shall include reasonable efforts to restore the system to normal operation and to identify root causes where feasible.

2.3.2 Contoso shall report Incidents through the designated support channels identified by Delta (e.g., support portal, phone number, email address). Delta will classify Incidents by severity and respond in accordance with the Service Levels stated in Article XII.

2.3.3 Corrective maintenance may be performed remotely or on-site, as determined by Delta in consultation with Contoso based on the nature and severity of the Incident. Where on-site presence is required, Delta shall dispatch qualified Personnel to the relevant Contoso location in accordance with the Response Times and availability commitments set forth in Article XII.

### 2.4 Monitoring and Alerting

2.4.1 Subject to any network and security constraints specified by Contoso, Delta may implement remote monitoring tools to observe system performance, availability, and health metrics for the Equipment.

2.4.2 Delta shall configure alert thresholds and escalation paths, in consultation with Contoso, to ensure timely notification to both Parties in the event of system anomalies, failures, or security-related events. Delta will take reasonable steps to promptly address alerts that indicate a potential Incident.

2.4.3 Delta’s monitoring activities shall be conducted in accordance with Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy), and shall not unreasonably interfere with Contoso’s system usage or compromise Contoso’s security policies.

### 2.5 Exclusions

2.5.1 Unless expressly set forth in an SOW, the following are excluded from the Services and may be provided by Delta only upon separate written agreement and, where applicable, additional fees:

- Major system upgrades, migrations, or re-platforming projects;  
- Design or implementation of new infrastructure or systems not identified as Equipment;  
- Repair or replacement of Equipment damaged due to misuse, negligence, accidents, unauthorized modifications, or environmental conditions outside manufacturer specifications;  
- Support of third-party software or hardware not expressly listed as covered Equipment;  
- Development of custom software applications unrelated to maintenance of the Equipment.

2.5.2 The Parties may agree in writing to modify the list of exclusions through the change management process described in Article XIII.

### 2.6 Performance Standards

2.6.1 Delta shall perform the Services (a) in a timely and professional manner; (b) in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for maintenance services of a similar nature; (c) with Personnel who are appropriately trained, qualified, and supervised; and (d) in compliance with all Applicable Laws.

2.6.2 Delta shall make commercially reasonable efforts to meet or exceed the Service Levels set forth in Article XII. Where Delta becomes aware of circumstances that may prevent it from meeting any Service Level, Delta shall promptly notify Contoso and work diligently to mitigate any adverse impact.

2.6.3 Delta shall periodically (at least quarterly, unless otherwise agreed) provide Contoso with maintenance reports summarizing Services performed, Incidents handled, system performance metrics, and any observed patterns or recommendations for improvement.

### 2.7 Cooperation by Contoso

2.7.1 Contoso acknowledges that Delta’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon Contoso’s timely cooperation and provision of accurate and complete information. Accordingly, Contoso shall:

- Provide Delta with access (remote and/or on-site) to the Equipment, systems, facilities, and relevant documentation as reasonably necessary for Delta to perform the Services;  
- Designate qualified Contoso Personnel as primary points of contact for coordination of maintenance activities;  
- Promptly report Incidents and provide relevant information to facilitate diagnosis and resolution;  
- Refrain from making unauthorized modifications to the Equipment that would interfere with Delta’s ability to provide the Services.

2.7.2 If Delta’s performance of the Services is materially delayed or prevented due to Contoso’s failure to fulfill its obligations under this Section 2.7, Delta shall not be liable for such delays or any resulting failure to meet Service Levels, and Delta shall be entitled to a reasonable extension of time and, where applicable, an equitable adjustment to Fees as addressed under Article XIII.

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

12.1.1 For any systems or services for which Delta provides ongoing monitoring and support under this Agreement, Delta shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve **99.9%** availability per calendar month, excluding Scheduled Downtime and events outside Delta’s reasonable control.

### 12.2 Response and Resolution Times

12.2.1 Delta shall adhere to the following target Response Times for Incidents reported by Contoso or detected by Delta:

- **Severity 1 (Critical)**: Complete loss of a mission-critical system or function with no workaround. Target Response Time: **1 hour**, 24x7.  
- **Severity 2 (High)**: Significant degradation of critical functionality with a workaround. Target Response Time: **4 hours**, during Business Days.  
- **Severity 3 (Medium)**: Limited impact on operations; non-critical functions affected. Target Response Time: **1 Business Day**.  
- **Severity 4 (Low)**: General inquiries, minor issues, or cosmetic defects. Target Response Time: **3 Business Days**.

12.2.2 Resolution times shall depend on the nature and complexity of the Incident. Delta shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve or provide a viable workaround for Severity 1 Incidents within twenty-four (24) hours of Response, recognizing that some issues may require longer periods.

### 12.3 Remedies for SLA Breaches

12.3.1 In the event Delta materially fails to meet the uptime or Response Time commitments in this Article XII for a calendar month, Contoso shall be entitled to service credits, calculated as follows:

(a) If monthly availability falls below 99.9% but is at least 99.5%: service credit of 2% of the monthly recurring Fees for affected Services;  

(b) If monthly availability falls below 99.5%: service credit of 5% of the monthly recurring Fees for affected Services.

12.3.2 Service credits shall be the sole and exclusive monetary remedy for SLA failures, subject to the overall liability limitations in Article IX. Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice(s) issued by Delta.

12.3.3 To receive service credits, Contoso must submit a written request within thirty (30) days after the end of the month in which the SLA failure occurred, identifying the dates and nature of the alleged failure.
