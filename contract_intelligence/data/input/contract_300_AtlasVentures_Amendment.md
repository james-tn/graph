## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK (AMENDED AND EXTENDED)

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.**  

2.1.1 This Amendment **modifies and supplements** the scope of Services described in **Article II (Scope of Services / Work)** of the Parent Agreement. Except as expressly provided herein, all provisions of **Article II** of the Parent Agreement remain in full force and effect.

2.1.2 This Amendment is entered into **pursuant to and in accordance with Article XIII (Change Management)** of the Parent Agreement and constitutes a formal, mutually agreed extension to the Project defined in **Section 1.15 (Project)** and the Program described in the Recitals of the Parent Agreement.

2.1.3 The Extended Services contemplated herein are intended to (a) expand Phase 3 – Implementation & Migration activities; (b) add new Analytics Use Cases and related Deliverables; (c) extend Phase 4 – Stabilization & Knowledge Transfer into an Extended Support Period; and (d) add an Optimization Services workstream and enhanced governance mechanisms.

2.2 **Overview of Extended Services.**  

Vendor shall, in addition to the Services described in **Section 2.2** of the Parent Agreement, perform the following Extended Services for Client under the Program:

  (a) Complete the migration of additional applications and data sets not originally within scope of Phase 3, including legacy line-of-business systems and selected third-party SaaS integrations;  

  (b) Design, implement, and operationalize additional Analytics Use Cases leveraging the cloud data platform, including cross-functional dashboards for finance, sales, customer success, and operations;  

  (c) Provide an Extended Support Period that ensures continuity of operations, proactive monitoring, incident response, and problem management for the migrated systems and analytics workloads;  

  (d) Deliver Optimization Services focused on cost management, performance tuning, architectural refactoring, and automation; and  

  (e) Implement an enhanced Program Governance framework, including steering committee participation, quarterly business reviews, and expanded reporting.

2.3 **Detailed Description of Extended Services.**  

2.3.1 **Extended Phase 3 – Implementation & Migration.**  
In addition to the tasks specified in **Section 2.2.3** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall:

  (a) Identify, prioritize, and migrate up to **twelve (12)** additional business-critical applications and associated data sets to the target cloud environment, including performing application assessment, remediation, and re-platforming where necessary.

  (b) Design and execute migration waves for such applications, aligning with Client’s change windows and risk tolerances, and updating the **Migration Runbooks and Cutover Plans (Deliverable D7)** to reflect the additional scope.

  (c) Extend integration activities to include additional on-premise systems and third-party platforms, ensuring end-to-end data flows and process continuity across hybrid environments.

  (d) Implement enhanced security controls and monitoring for the newly migrated systems consistent with the **Security and Compliance Framework Documentation (Deliverable D6)** under the Parent Agreement, including fine-grained access controls, encryption key management, and logging/alerting.

  (e) Conduct additional cycles of system integration testing, regression testing, and user acceptance testing support, updating the **Testing Strategy, Test Plans, and Test Reports (Deliverable D10)** to incorporate the extended scope.

2.3.2 **Additional Analytics Use Cases and Data Products.**  
Building on Deliverables **D9 – Data Models, Data Pipelines, and Analytics Dashboard Specifications**, Vendor shall design, develop, and deploy the following Analytics Use Cases:

  (a) **Revenue and Margin Analytics:** Dashboards and underlying data models providing multi-dimensional views of revenue, gross margin, and product performance, with drill-down by region, channel, and product line.

  (b) **Customer Experience and Churn Prediction:** Machine learning-based propensity models and dashboards to identify at-risk customers, key drivers of churn, and recommended actions; including operational integration into Client’s CRM for front-line usage.

  (c) **Operations and Capacity Management:** Dashboards and reports providing visibility into system performance, incident trends, support volume, and resource utilization, to support capacity planning and continuous improvement.

  (d) **Regulatory Reporting Dashboards:** Data models and dashboards aggregating data needed for compliance reporting (e.g., SOC2-related metrics for system uptime and access management, privacy metrics under CCPA/CPRA, and internal risk metrics), leveraging the **Security and Compliance Framework** established under Phase 2.

  (e) For each Analytics Use Case, Vendor shall:  
   - Define detailed functional and technical Specifications;  
   - Implement the necessary data ingestion pipelines, transformations, data quality checks, and semantic layers;  
   - Design and build user-facing dashboards or reports using Client-approved BI tools; and  
   - Conduct knowledge transfer sessions to relevant Client stakeholders, extending **Deliverables D11 and D12**.

2.3.3 **Extended Support Period – Managed Services.**  
In addition to the Stabilization & Knowledge Transfer activities described in **Section 2.2.4** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide Extended Support for a period commencing upon completion of the initial Phase 4 as defined in the Parent Agreement and continuing through the Amendment Term, subject to **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of both the Parent Agreement and this Amendment. Such Extended Support shall include:

  (a) 24x7 monitoring (for Severity 1 and 2 incidents) of critical production environments related to the migrated systems and Analytics Use Cases;

  (b) Incident management, including triage, root cause analysis for Severity 1 and 2 incidents, and coordination with Client and third parties, consistent with the response and resolution targets in **Section 12.4** of the Parent Agreement as modified herein;

  (c) Routine maintenance, including patching, configuration updates, and minor enhancements, as agreed in the Program Governance Addendum;

  (d) Monthly operational health reports summarizing incidents, performance metrics, changes implemented, and recommendations.

2.3.4 **Optimization Services.**  
Vendor shall perform Optimization Services intended to improve cost efficiency, performance, reliability, and maintainability of the cloud-based environment and analytics platform, including:

  (a) Periodic cost-optimization assessments, with recommendations around scaling, resource rightsizing, storage tiering, and data lifecycle management;

  (b) Performance tuning of data pipelines, query workloads, and dashboards, including indexing strategies, caching, and concurrency optimization;

  (c) Architectural refactoring proposals for high-value services and applications to align with cloud-native best practices, microservices, and managed services where appropriate;

  (d) Automation and DevOps enablement (e.g., infrastructure as code, CI/CD pipelines) to reduce manual operations and risk.

2.3.5 **Updated Transition and Knowledge Transfer.**  
Vendor shall extend the knowledge transfer and transition activities described in **Section 2.2.4** and **Deliverables D11 and D12** of the Parent Agreement, including:

  (a) Updated operational runbooks and support procedures covering the Extended Services, Analytics Use Cases, and optimization processes, forming part of the Amendment Deliverables;

  (b) Additional training sessions for Client’s internal teams and any third-party managed service providers designated by Client, with focus on ongoing operations, troubleshooting, monitoring, and enhancement of the analytics platform;

  (c) A comprehensive **Transition-Out Plan (Amended)** to support possible transition at the end of the Amendment Term or upon early termination, aligned with **Section 4.6 (Transition Assistance)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Amendment Deliverables.**  

Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Vendor shall provide the following additional Deliverables, each subject to Client’s review and acceptance in accordance with **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement:

- **AD1 – Extended Application Migration Inventory and Plan**, detailing the additional systems in scope under this Amendment and their migration strategy.  
- **AD2 – Updated Migration Runbooks and Cutover Plans**, superseding prior versions of Deliverable D7 where explicitly identified.  
- **AD3 – Analytics Use Case Specifications Pack**, including detailed design documents for each Use Case in **Section 2.3.2**.  
- **AD4 – Deployed Analytics Dashboards and Reports**, with accompanying configuration and user guides.  
- **AD5 – Extended Support Operating Model Document**, describing processes, roles, escalation paths, and tooling for the Extended Support Period.  
- **AD6 – Optimization Assessments and Recommendations Reports**, delivered on at least a quarterly basis.  
- **AD7 – Program Governance Addendum and Reporting Templates**, including steering committee charters, KPI scorecards, and risk/issue logs.  
- **AD8 – Updated Operational Runbooks and Support Procedures**, extending Deliverable D11.  
- **AD9 – Training Curriculum and Session Materials**, extending Deliverable D12.  
- **AD10 – Transition-Out Plan (Amended)** with detailed roles, responsibilities, timelines, and knowledge transfer activities.

2.5 **Performance Standards.**  

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Extended Services in accordance with the performance standards in **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, and in no event less than a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards for projects of similar size and complexity.

2.5.2 Vendor acknowledges that failure to meet the service levels described in **Article XII** of the Parent Agreement (as modified in **Article XII** of this Amendment) may entitle Client to service credits and/or termination rights as described therein.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities (Amended/Extended).**  

In addition to the responsibilities set out in **Section 2.5** of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to additional systems, data sources, and subject matter experts necessary to implement the Extended Services and Analytics Use Cases;  

  (b) Participate in the enhanced Program Governance structure, including attending steering committee meetings and quarterly business reviews;  

  (c) Provide timely review, feedback, and approval for Amendment Deliverables in accordance with **Section 2.7** of the Parent Agreement, acknowledging that delays may require schedule adjustments under **Article XIII (Change Management)**.
