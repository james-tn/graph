## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.1 General Description of Services

2.1.1 Vendor shall provide Client with enterprise consulting, technology integration, and managed support services designed to support Client’s core business operations, digital transformation initiatives, and enterprise applications (the **"Services"**). The Services shall include, at a minimum:

(a) assessment and analysis of Client’s existing IT and operational environments;  
(b) development of solution architectures and implementation roadmaps;  
(c) configuration, integration, and customization of agreed technology platforms;  
(d) migration of specified data and workloads;  
(e) ongoing application support and maintenance; and  
(f) related project management, training, and knowledge transfer.

2.1.2 The Services will be performed primarily remotely from Vendor’s facilities; however, Vendor may, upon mutual agreement and subject to reimbursement of travel and related expenses as set forth in Section 3.5, perform Services on Client’s premises.

### 2.2 Specific Deliverables

2.2.1 Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Parties agree that Vendor shall deliver at least the following Deliverables, subject to refinement in one or more SOWs:

(a) **Discovery and Assessment Report** – a detailed report documenting Client’s current-state architecture, systems, business processes, and risk areas, including recommended remediation steps and a prioritized roadmap.

(b) **Solution Design Documentation** – comprehensive solution architecture documentation, including technical design, data flows, integration points, security considerations, scalability plans, and dependencies.

(c) **Implementation Plan and Project Schedule** – a detailed project plan outlining phases, milestones, resource allocations, dependencies, and estimated completion dates.

(d) **Configured and Integrated Environment** – an operational, configured, and integrated environment in Client’s designated production, staging, and/or test environments, in accordance with the Solution Design Documentation.

(e) **Migration Scripts and Tools** – scripts, tools, or configurations used to migrate data and workloads from legacy systems to the new environment.

(f) **User and Administrator Documentation** – user manuals, administrator guides, and operational run-books sufficient to enable Client’s personnel to use and administer the implemented solutions.

(g) **Training Materials and Sessions** – training curriculum, presentation materials, and training sessions (virtual or in-person as agreed) for Client’s designated personnel.

(h) **Support and Maintenance Procedures** – documented procedures for incident management, escalation, change control, release management, and performance monitoring.

### 2.3 Timelines and Milestones

2.3.1 Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to perform the Services and provide the Deliverables in accordance with the project schedule and milestones set forth in the applicable SOW(s). Unless otherwise specified, the following indicative timeline shall apply:

(a) Discovery and Assessment Phase – commencement within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date and completion within sixty (60) days thereafter;  
(b) Design and Planning Phase – completion within ninety (90) days following completion of the Discovery and Assessment Phase;  
(c) Implementation and Integration Phase – staged phases to be mutually agreed, with initial go-live targeted within twelve (12) months of the Effective Date;  
(d) Post-Go-Live Support and Optimization – ongoing until the Expiration Date, subject to Articles III and IV.

2.3.2 The Parties acknowledge that the timelines and milestones are estimates based on information currently available and may be adjusted through the Change Management process described in Article XIII if Client requirements change, if Client fails to fulfill its obligations, or if unforeseen technical complexities arise.

### 2.4 Client Responsibilities

2.4.1 Client shall:

(a) provide timely access to Client Materials, systems, personnel, and facilities reasonably required by Vendor to perform the Services;  
(b) designate a project manager and appropriate stakeholders to act as primary points of contact for coordination, decisions, and approvals;  
(c) review and provide feedback or approvals on Deliverables within ten (10) Business Days of receipt, unless otherwise stated in an SOW; and  
(d) ensure that all information and materials it provides to Vendor are accurate, complete, and lawful to use for the intended purposes.

2.4.2 Any delay or failure by Client to meet its responsibilities under this Agreement may result in schedule slippage, additional Fees, or both, and Vendor shall not be liable for any failure to meet timelines or Service Levels to the extent caused by Client’s delay or non-performance.

### 2.5 Performance Standards

2.5.1 Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards for providers of similar services, and shall use personnel with appropriate skills and experience.

2.5.2 Vendor does not guarantee that the Services or Deliverables will be error-free or that all defects can or will be remedied. However, Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to promptly correct any material non-conformities in the Deliverables identified by Client in writing within thirty (30) days after delivery.

2.5.3 The Parties acknowledge that the Services may involve complex integrations with third-party systems and that Vendor’s ability to meet certain performance targets may depend on the availability and performance of such third-party systems, for which Vendor is not responsible.

### 15.3 Suspension of Obligations

15.3.1 During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the affected Party’s obligations under this Agreement shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration impacted by the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party continues to use commercially reasonable efforts to perform.
