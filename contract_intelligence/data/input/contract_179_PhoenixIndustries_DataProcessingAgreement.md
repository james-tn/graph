## ARTICLE I – DEFINITIONS

1.2 **“Agreement”** means this Data Processing Agreement, contract ref. **DPA-PHO-202504-179**, including all annexes, schedules, and any written amendments hereto.

1.4 **“Business Day”** means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in England on which banks are open for general business in London.

1.6 **“Controller”** means the entity which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data, and for purposes of this Agreement, Contoso shall generally be considered the Controller (or “Business” under CCPA) and Phoenix shall generally be considered the Processor (or “Service Provider” under CCPA).

1.7 **“Data Breach” or “Personal Data Breach”** means any breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data transmitted, stored or otherwise Processed by Phoenix or its Sub-Processors.

1.8 **“Data Subject”** means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom the Personal Data relates.

1.9 **“Deliverables”** means all reports, documents, software configurations, dashboards, analytics outputs, and other work product (excluding Vendor Background IP) created or developed by Phoenix for Contoso in the course of providing the Services under this Agreement.

1.11 **“Personal Data”** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, and shall include “personal data”, “personal information”, or “personally identifiable information” as such terms are defined in Applicable Data Protection Law, that is Processed by Phoenix on behalf of Contoso.

1.12 **“Processing”** or **“Process”** means any operation or set of operations performed upon Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.13 **“Services”** means the data processing, hosting, analytics, support, maintenance, advisory, and related professional services to be provided by Phoenix to Contoso as further described in Article II and in any applicable statements of work (“SOWs”) executed by the Parties.

1.17 **“Term”** has the meaning set forth in Section 4.1 of this Agreement.

1.18 **“Vendor Background IP”** means all intellectual property, including software, methodologies, tools, processes, know-how, and documentation owned by or licensed to Phoenix prior to the Effective Date or developed by Phoenix independently of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Phoenix shall provide to Contoso the Services described in this Article II and in any SOWs or ordering documents executed by the Parties. The Services shall include, without limitation:

  a. **Data Ingestion and Integration.** Phoenix shall design, implement, and manage data ingestion pipelines to collect data from Contoso’s internal systems, third-party platforms, and external data sources, including but not limited to CRM systems, ERP systems, customer support platforms, and cloud storage environments.

  b. **Data Hosting and Storage.** Phoenix shall host and store Personal Data and other Client Data in secure data centers located within the United Kingdom, the European Economic Area, or other locations as agreed in writing by the Parties and compliant with Applicable Data Protection Law. Phoenix shall maintain logically segregated environments to ensure that Client Data is segregated from data of other customers.

  c. **Data Processing and Analytics.** Phoenix shall perform data transformation, cleansing, enrichment, aggregation, and analytics on Client Data, including Personal Data, to produce dashboards, reports, predictive models, and other analytical outputs as required by Contoso.

  d. **Application Layer Services.** Phoenix shall provide web-based portals, APIs, and dashboards through which authorized users of Contoso may access, view, query, export, and manage Client Data, subject to access controls defined by Contoso and implemented by Phoenix.

  e. **Support and Maintenance.** Phoenix shall provide technical support, incident management, and maintenance for the platform and Services, including troubleshooting, patching, updates, and upgrades, in accordance with the SLA in Article XII.

  f. **Professional and Advisory Services.** Upon request by Contoso, and subject to separate SOWs if required, Phoenix shall provide professional services including data governance consulting, data model design, custom report building, integration design, and training of Contoso personnel.

2.2 **Specific Deliverables.**  
Without limiting the generality of Section 2.1, the Parties anticipate that Phoenix shall deliver, among other items:

  a. A **secure multi-tenant cloud-based data platform** configured for Contoso, including user accounts and role-based access controls;

  b. **Integration connectors** for at least five (5) core Contoso systems identified in writing by Contoso within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date;

  c. A suite of **standard analytics dashboards** and **custom reporting templates** for Contoso’s sales, operations, and compliance teams;

  d. **Documentation** describing data flows, data schemas, security controls, and standard operating procedures for onboarding new data sources;

  e. **Training materials** and up to three (3) remote training sessions per year of the Term for Contoso’s designated personnel.

2.3 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
The Parties agree that the Services will be performed in phases and in accordance with the following indicative milestones, subject to adjustment by mutual written agreement or individual SOWs:

  a. **Phase 1 – Discovery and Design (First 60 Days).** Phoenix shall conduct workshops with Contoso stakeholders to document requirements, define data sources, and design the target architecture. Phoenix shall deliver a written **Solution Design Document** by Day 45.

  b. **Phase 2 – Initial Implementation (Days 61–150).** Phoenix shall configure the platform, implement initial data connectors, and deliver at least three (3) core dashboards. A **Go-Live** for the initial environment is targeted by Day 150.

  c. **Phase 3 – Optimization and Expansion (Days 151–365).** Phoenix shall refine analytics models, extend to additional data sources, and implement custom reports identified by Contoso. Ongoing adjustments shall be made based on user feedback.

  d. **Phase 4 – Ongoing Operations (Remainder of Term).** Phoenix shall operate, maintain, and support the platform, implement minor enhancements, and ensure compliance with applicable SLAs and security requirements.

These milestones are indicative and not strictly binding unless expressly stated in a specific SOW. Failure to meet indicative timelines shall not, in and of itself, constitute a material breach, provided Phoenix uses commercially reasonable efforts to minimize delays.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
Phoenix shall perform the Services:

  a. with the degree of skill, diligence, prudence, and foresight which would reasonably be expected from a professional provider of similar services under similar circumstances;  

  b. in accordance with this Agreement, the SLA in Article XII, and any applicable SOWs;  

  c. in compliance with Applicable Data Protection Law and other applicable laws and regulations; and  

  d. using personnel who are appropriately qualified, experienced, and trained in information security and data protection.

2.5 **Client Responsibilities.**  
Contoso acknowledges and agrees that Phoenix’s ability to perform the Services depends on Contoso’s timely performance of the following obligations:

  a. providing accurate and complete information regarding data sources, systems, and requirements;  

  b. obtaining and maintaining all necessary rights, consents, and permissions for Phoenix to Process Personal Data as contemplated under this Agreement;  

  c. making available relevant systems, environments, and personnel for integration and testing activities;  

  d. designating authorized users and managing internal user access policies.

Phoenix shall have no liability for any failure to meet performance standards to the extent such failure is caused by Contoso’s failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement.

2.6 **Sub-Processors.**  
Phoenix may engage Sub-Processors to perform parts of the Services that involve the Processing of Personal Data, provided that:

  a. Phoenix imposes on such Sub-Processors data protection obligations substantially similar to those set forth in this Agreement; and  

  b. Phoenix remains fully liable to Contoso for the performance of such Sub-Processors.

A current list of Sub-Processors shall be made available to Contoso upon request. Contoso may object on reasonable data protection grounds to a new Sub-Processor by providing written notice within ten (10) Business Days of being informed. Phoenix shall use good faith efforts to address such objection, including by proposing alternative arrangements, but shall not be obliged to terminate the Sub-Processor if doing so would materially impair the Services or be commercially unreasonable.

## ARTICLE III – FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Fees.**  
In consideration of the Services, Contoso shall pay to Phoenix total fees not to exceed **USD $3,802,807** over the Term, subject to the following structure (unless superseded by an applicable SOW):

  a. **Platform Subscription Fees.** An annual platform subscription fee of USD $1,200,000 per contract year, billed in advance, covering hosting, standard support, maintenance, and use of the platform for up to a mutually agreed data volume and user count;

  b. **Professional Services Fees.** Time-and-materials fees at Phoenix’s standard rates (currently anticipated to be between USD $180–$260 per hour for consultants and USD $280–$400 per hour for senior architects), for implementation and advisory services beyond the baseline implementation;

  c. **Overage Fees.** Additional fees for data volumes, API calls, or user counts exceeding agreed thresholds, as set out in a pricing schedule or SOW;

  d. **Change Request Fees.** Fees for changes requested by Contoso and approved pursuant to Article XIII.

3.2 **Invoicing.**  
Phoenix shall invoice Contoso as follows:

  a. Annual platform subscription fees shall be invoiced on or shortly after the Effective Date and each anniversary thereof during the Term;  

  b. Professional services fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears;  

  c. Overage and change request fees shall be invoiced monthly or as otherwise specified in an applicable SOW.

3.3 **Payment Terms.**  
All undisputed invoices shall be payable by Contoso within **sixty (60) calendar days** of the invoice date (**“Net 60”**). Payments shall be made in United States Dollars (USD) by bank transfer or such other method as agreed in writing by the Parties.

3.4 **Late Payment.**  
If Contoso fails to pay any undisputed amount when due:

  a. Phoenix may charge interest at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower, on the overdue amount, calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment; and  

  b. Phoenix may, after providing written notice to Contoso and a ten (10) Business Day cure period, suspend the provision of Services (including access to the platform) until all overdue amounts and applicable interest are paid in full, without liability for any resulting impact on Contoso.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If Contoso reasonably and in good faith disputes any portion of an invoice, Contoso shall notify Phoenix in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Contoso shall pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 3.3. The Parties shall use good faith efforts to resolve the dispute promptly. Any resolved disputed amounts shall be paid within fifteen (15) days of resolution.

3.6 **Taxes.**  
All fees are exclusive of any applicable value added tax (VAT), sales tax, use tax, or similar taxes. Contoso shall be responsible for all such taxes, other than taxes based on Phoenix’s net income or employment-related taxes owed by Phoenix.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  
Reasonable travel and out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Phoenix in performing on-site services at Contoso’s request shall be reimbursed by Contoso at cost, provided that:

  a. such expenses are pre-approved in writing by Contoso (email sufficient); and  

  b. Phoenix provides appropriate documentation, such as receipts or invoices.  

Reimbursable expenses shall be invoiced monthly and paid in accordance with Section 3.3.

## ARTICLE IV – TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **15 August 2027** (the **“Term”**), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
Any renewal of this Agreement shall be subject to mutual written agreement between the Parties no later than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date. Renewal may include revised fees, SLAs, or other terms.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Contoso may terminate this Agreement, in whole or in part, for convenience by providing Phoenix with at least **one hundred eighty (180) days’ prior written notice**. Phoenix may likewise terminate this Agreement for convenience on one hundred eighty (180) days’ written notice. Given the high-risk and complexity of the Services, the Parties acknowledge that this extended notice period is necessary to facilitate an orderly transition.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect by written notice to the other Party if:

  a. the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and, if such breach is capable of remedy, fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach; or  

  b. the other Party becomes insolvent, is unable to pay its debts as they fall due, enters into liquidation, or has a receiver or administrator appointed over any of its assets.

4.5 **Termination for Data Protection Breach.**  
Without prejudice to any other rights, Contoso may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Phoenix is in material, persistent, or incurable breach of its obligations under Article XI (Data Protection and Privacy) or if a Supervisory Authority issues a final decision prohibiting the Processing of Personal Data by Phoenix in the manner contemplated under this Agreement.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:

  a. Phoenix shall promptly cease all Processing of Personal Data on behalf of Contoso, except to the extent continued Processing is required by Applicable Data Protection Law;  

  b. in accordance with Section 11.10, Phoenix shall, at Contoso’s option and subject to payment of applicable fees, return or securely delete all Personal Data and Client Data;  

  c. all amounts due and payable, or accrued but not yet invoiced, shall become immediately due and payable;  

  d. any licenses granted under this Agreement shall terminate, except as expressly stated otherwise; and  

  e. the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to ensure an orderly transition of the Services, for which Contoso shall pay Phoenix on a time-and-materials basis at Phoenix’s then-current rates, unless otherwise agreed.

4.7 **Survival.**  
Any provisions of this Agreement that by their nature are intended to survive termination or expiration shall so survive, including without limitation Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions).

## ARTICLE V – CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Confidential Information.**  
For purposes of this Agreement, **“Confidential Information”** means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **“Disclosing Party”**) to the other Party (the **“Receiving Party”**) in connection with this Agreement, whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would consider confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, Personal Data, Client Data, trade secrets, technical information, security documentation, business plans, pricing, and customer information.

5.2 **Exclusions.**  
Confidential Information does not include information that:

  a. is or becomes publicly available through no act or omission of the Receiving Party;  

  b. was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records;  

  c. is lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation; or  

  d. is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records.

5.3 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  a. use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing or receiving the Services and fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement;  

  b. not disclose the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement;  

  c. limit access to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information to those of its employees, contractors, and Sub-Processors who have a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein; and  

  d. protect the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify the Disclosing Party and cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If such protective order or other remedy is not obtained, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information which it is legally required to disclose, and shall use reasonable efforts to ensure that confidential treatment is afforded to such disclosed information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon termination of this Agreement or upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all Confidential Information (including all copies) in its possession or control, except to the extent retention is required by law or for the purposes of defending legal claims, in which case such information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following termination or expiration of this Agreement. Confidential Information constituting trade secrets shall be kept confidential for so long as it constitutes a trade secret under applicable law.

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Uptime Commitment.**  
Phoenix shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that the production environment of the platform is available to Contoso 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, with a **monthly uptime percentage of at least 99.9%**, excluding Scheduled Maintenance, Emergency Maintenance, and other permitted downtime specified herein.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
Phoenix may schedule maintenance windows during which the platform may be unavailable, provided that:

  a. Phoenix provides Contoso with at least seventy-two (72) hours’ prior notice;  

  b. such maintenance is scheduled outside core business hours in the UK where reasonably practicable; and  

  c. the total scheduled maintenance does not exceed eight (8) hours per calendar month under normal circumstances.

12.3 **Emergency Maintenance.**  
Phoenix may perform emergency maintenance without prior notice where necessary to address critical security vulnerabilities, system failures, or other urgent issues. Phoenix shall use reasonable efforts to inform Contoso as soon as practicable.

12.4 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
Phoenix shall adhere to the following target response and resolution times for support requests:

  a. **Priority 1 (Critical):** Complete loss of Service or severe impact on critical business functions with no workaround.  
     - Initial response: within 1 hour  
     - Target resolution or workaround: within 4 hours  

  b. **Priority 2 (High):** Significant degradation of Service or impact on important business functions, workaround available but limited.  
     - Initial response: within 4 hours  
     - Target resolution or workaround: within 1 Business Day  

  c. **Priority 3 (Medium):** Non-critical issues, partial loss of functionality, or user queries.  
     - Initial response: within 1 Business Day  
     - Target resolution: within 5 Business Days  

  d. **Priority 4 (Low):** Minor issues or requests for information not affecting normal operations.  
     - Initial response: within 2 Business Days  
     - Target resolution: as mutually agreed.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
In the event that Phoenix fails to meet the uptime commitment in Section 12.1 in any calendar month, Contoso may be eligible for service credits, calculated as a percentage of the monthly platform subscription fees for that month, as follows:

  - Uptime 99.0% – <99.9%: 5% credit  
  - Uptime 98.0% – <99.0%: 10% credit  
  - Uptime <98.0%: 20% credit  

Service credits:

  a. shall be Contoso’s exclusive financial remedy for failure to meet the uptime commitment;  

  b. shall be applied to future invoices (no cash refunds);  

  c. must be requested in writing by Contoso within thirty (30) days of the end of the month in which the shortfall occurred.

12.6 **Exclusions from SLA.**  
The SLA shall not apply to any downtime or service degradation:

  a. caused by factors outside Phoenix’s reasonable control (including Force Majeure Events);  

  b. resulting from any action or inaction of Contoso or its users, or from Contoso’s failure to comply with this Agreement;  

  c. resulting from Contoso’s systems, networks, or third-party services not under Phoenix’s control;  

  d. resulting from Scheduled Maintenance or Emergency Maintenance.

In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve the matter through negotiations between senior representatives of each Party.

14.5 **Injunctive Relief.**

## ARTICLE XV – FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
A **“Force Majeure Event”** means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of a Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, strikes or other labor disputes (excluding those limited to the Party’s own workforce), epidemics or pandemics, government acts or regulations, failure of telecommunications or utilities, or widespread cyber-attacks not attributable to a Party’s failure to maintain industry-standard security.

15.2 **Notification.**  
If a Party is prevented or delayed in performing any of its obligations under this Agreement by a Force Majeure Event, it shall:

  a. promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature of the event, its expected duration, and the affected obligations;  

  b. use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
The obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected Party continues to use commercially reasonable efforts to overcome the event.

15.4 **Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than ninety (90) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice, without liability, except for payment of fees due for Services rendered prior to the effective date of termination.
