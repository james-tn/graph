## ARTICLE II – SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

### 2.2 Covered Software and Environments

2.2.1 The Maintenance and Support Services apply to the **Acme Enterprise Suite** modules and environments licensed and deployed under the Parent Agreement, including both:

  (a) Hosted or SaaS environments operated by the Vendor, consistent with Section 2.2.2 of the Parent Agreement; and  

  (b) On‑premises instances installed on Client Systems, where applicable.

2.2.2 The specific list of covered modules, environments (production, non‑production), and any exclusions shall be documented in one or more **Maintenance Schedules** agreed in writing by the Parties and referencing this Agreement and the Parent Agreement. Such schedules, once executed, shall be incorporated herein by reference.

### 2.3 Core Maintenance and Support Obligations

2.3.1 During the Maintenance Term, the Vendor shall provide the following core Maintenance and Support Services:

  (a) **Incident Management & Error Correction** – Logging, triage, diagnosis, and remediation of Errors, performance issues, and service interruptions reported via Support Requests, in accordance with the severity classifications and response times set forth in Article XII and consistent with Section 12.3 of the Parent Agreement.

  (b) **Updates and Patches** – Provision and deployment (for hosted components) or provision and guidance (for on‑premises components) of Updates, patches, and minor enhancements intended to correct Defects, address security vulnerabilities, or improve performance, at no additional license fee.

  (c) **Upgrades** – Provision of major releases as part of the maintenance scope, including coordination of Upgrade projects, change notes, and backward compatibility considerations. For hosted deployments, Vendor shall implement Upgrades; for on‑premises deployments, Vendor shall provide instructions and reasonable assistance.

  (d) **Configuration Assistance** – Reasonable assistance related to standard configuration settings, user management, and use of out-of-the-box features, but excluding custom development or material re‑implementation services (which shall be subject to a separate SOW under the Parent Agreement).

  (e) **Technical Support** – Availability of Vendor support staff during agreed support hours (at a minimum Business Days, during normal business hours in Client’s primary time zone, unless otherwise specified) to address Support Requests, provide guidance, and engage in troubleshooting.

  (f) **Knowledge Transfer and Documentation** – Provision of updated Documentation, release notes, FAQs, and best practice guidance related to Updates and Upgrades, aligned with Section 2.5.1(d) of the Parent Agreement.

### 2.4 Service Levels and Performance Standards

2.4.1 The Vendor shall perform the Services in a **professional and workmanlike manner**, using personnel with appropriate skills and experience, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards and consistent with **Section 2.6.1 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4.2 The Vendor shall adhere to the **Service Levels** set forth in **Article XII** of this Agreement, which are intended to be consistent with, and in some respects more specific than, the service levels set forth in **Article XII (Service Level Agreements)** of the Parent Agreement.

2.4.3 The Vendor shall maintain **production uptime of at least 99.9%** per calendar month, measured as described in Section 12.1 below, and shall respond to incidents according to the severity definitions in Section 12.3.

### 2.5 Client Responsibilities

2.5.1 The Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely access to relevant personnel, systems, and information as reasonably required for the Vendor to perform the Services, consistent with Section 2.7.1 of the Parent Agreement;

  (b) Maintain its Client Systems, including network connectivity, hardware, and third‑party software components, at levels sufficient to support the Software and allow the Vendor to perform the Services;

  (c) Use reasonable efforts to document and communicate to the Vendor sufficient detail regarding each Support Request to facilitate effective diagnosis and resolution;

### 2.6 Out-of-Scope Services

2.6.1 The following activities are not included in the standard scope of Maintenance and Support Services, but may be provided under a separate SOW or Change Order under **Article XIII**:

  (a) Major re‑implementation, re‑configuration, or new integration projects beyond routine maintenance, as contemplated in Sections 2.3 and 2.4 of the Parent Agreement;

## ARTICLE XII – SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

### 12.1 Uptime Commitment

12.1.1 For SaaS or hosted components of the Software, Vendor shall maintain a production uptime of at least **99.9%** per calendar month, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Permitted Downtime, consistent with Section 12.1.1 of the Parent Agreement.

12.1.2 Uptime shall be measured as:

> (Total Minutes in Month – Downtime Minutes) / (Total Minutes in Month – Excluded Minutes)

where “Excluded Minutes” include Scheduled Maintenance, Emergency Maintenance (as defined below), and any periods of unavailability primarily caused by Client Systems, third‑party providers under Client’s control, or Force Majeure Events under Article XV.

### 12.2 Maintenance Windows and Emergency Maintenance

12.2.1 Vendor may schedule routine maintenance during Maintenance Windows, with at least **forty‑eight (48) hours’** prior notice for any maintenance expected to result in downtime of more than fifteen (15) minutes, consistent with Section 12.2.1 of the Parent Agreement.

12.2.2 Scheduled Maintenance shall not exceed **eight (8) hours per month** and shall not count against the Uptime Commitment, consistent with the Parent Agreement.

12.2.3 Vendor may perform Emergency Maintenance when reasonably necessary to address critical security or stability issues. Vendor will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide prior notice (which may be short) and to minimize disruption.

### 12.3 Incident Severity and Response Targets

12.3.1 The Vendor shall classify incidents and respond in accordance with the following targets (aligning with Section 12.3.1 of the Parent Agreement):

- **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Complete loss of production service or severe business impact with no workaround: initial response within **one (1) hour**; Vendor shall work continuously (24x7) until a workaround or resolution is provided or the severity is downgraded by mutual agreement.

- **Severity 2 (High)** – Significant degradation of service with limited workaround: initial response within **four (4) hours** during support hours; Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to resolve or provide a workaround within **two (2) Business Days**, subject to complexity.

- **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Partial, non-critical functionality impacted: initial response within **one (1) Business Day**; Vendor shall target resolution or workaround in a future Update consistent with its release schedule.

- **Severity 4 (Low)** – General questions, enhancement requests, or cosmetic issues: initial response within **two (2) Business Days**; resolution may be addressed in the Vendor’s standard roadmap or knowledge base.

12.3.2 The Vendor shall track and report adherence to response targets upon reasonable request in accordance with Section 2.7.2.

### 12.4 Service Credits

12.4.1 If Vendor fails to meet the Uptime Commitment in any calendar month, Client shall be eligible for service credits as follows (aligned with Section 12.4.1 of the Parent Agreement):

- Uptime between 99.0% and 99.9%: 5% of the monthly Maintenance Fees allocable to the affected hosted components;  
- Uptime between 98.0% and 99.0%: 10% of the monthly Maintenance Fees allocable to the affected hosted components;  
- Uptime below 98.0%: 20% of the monthly Maintenance Fees allocable to the affected hosted components.

12.4.2 Client must request service credits in writing within thirty (30) days following the end of the month in which the failure occurred. Service credits:

  (a) Shall be applied to future invoices;
