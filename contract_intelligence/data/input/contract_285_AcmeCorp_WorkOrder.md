## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Overview of Services.**  
During the Term, the Vendor shall provide to the Client a suite of professional information technology services, including but not limited to: (a) design, development, and customization of enterprise software solutions for the Client’s internal operations and customer platforms; (b) integration of Client’s existing systems with third-party platforms and cloud infrastructure; (c) ongoing application management and support services; and (d) associated project management, testing, training, documentation, and knowledge transfer (collectively, the “Services”).

2.2 **Project Phases and Deliverables.**  
The Services will be executed in multiple phases. Each phase shall culminate in specified Deliverables and Milestones, as set forth below. The Parties may further detail these phases in an attached **Schedule A (Project Plan and Deliverables)**, which, upon execution, shall be incorporated herein by reference.

  2.2.1 **Phase 1 – Discovery and Requirements Analysis (Months 1–3).**  
  (a) The Vendor shall conduct discovery workshops and stakeholder interviews to assess the Client’s current systems, business processes, and strategic objectives.  
  (b) Key Deliverables for Phase 1 shall include:
  - A comprehensive **Requirements Specification Document** detailing functional and non-functional requirements, data flows, user roles, and integration points.
  - A **Solution Architecture Blueprint** outlining the proposed technical architecture, including infrastructure, cloud services, data storage, security controls, and system interfaces.
  - A detailed **Project Plan** with timelines, dependencies, Milestones, and resource allocations.

  2.2.2 **Phase 2 – Design and Development (Months 4–15).**  
  (a) Based on the Requirements Specification Document, the Vendor shall design and develop the software components and integrations necessary to satisfy the Specifications.  
  (b) Key Deliverables for Phase 2 shall include:
  - Detailed **Technical Design Documents** for each major module.  
  - Iterative **Software Builds** and **Integration Components** delivered in sprints or iterations agreed by the Parties.  
  - **Test Plans** and **Test Cases** for unit, integration, system, and user acceptance testing.  
  - Updated **Configuration and Deployment Documentation**.

  2.2.3 **Phase 3 – Testing, User Acceptance, and Deployment (Months 16–20).**  
  (a) The Vendor shall perform unit, integration, and system testing prior to making the system available for user acceptance testing (“UAT”).  
  (b) The Client shall participate in UAT, supported by the Vendor, and shall provide written acceptance or a written list of defects within a reasonable period, not to exceed fifteen (15) Business Days from delivery of each major Deliverable.  
  (c) Key Deliverables for Phase 3 shall include:
  - Completed and tested **Application Components** that meet the Specifications.  
  - **User Acceptance Test Reports**, including defect logs and resolution status.  
  - **Production Deployment Plan** and execution of deployment to the Client’s production environment, subject to Client’s approval.

  2.2.4 **Phase 4 – Managed Services and Ongoing Support (Months 21–End of Term).**  
  Following final production deployment, the Vendor shall provide ongoing managed services and support, including but not limited to:
  - Application monitoring, incident response, and resolution in accordance with the Service Levels.  
  - Routine maintenance, including updates, patches, and minor enhancements agreed upon by the Parties.  
  - A **Support and Maintenance Guide** describing procedures for incident reporting, escalation, and resolution.

2.3 **Performance Standards.**  
The Vendor shall perform the Services: (a) in a professional, diligent, and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards for similar services; (b) in compliance with all Applicable Laws; and (c) in accordance with the timelines, Milestones, and quality standards set forth in this Agreement. The Vendor shall assign suitably qualified personnel with appropriate expertise and experience. If the Client reasonably believes that any Vendor personnel are not performing in accordance with these standards, the Client may request replacement, and the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to accommodate such request.

2.4 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client acknowledges that the Vendor’s ability to perform the Services is dependent upon the timely cooperation of the Client. Accordingly, the Client shall:
  (a) Provide the Vendor with timely access to relevant Client personnel, systems, facilities, and Client Materials reasonably required for the Vendor to perform the Services.  
  (b) Designate a primary project manager who will serve as the Client’s main point of contact and will be responsible for coordination and decision-making.  
  (c) Review and provide feedback on Deliverables within the review periods specified in this Agreement, and respond promptly to Vendor inquiries and requests for approvals.  
  (d) Ensure that Client-provided information is accurate, complete, and not misleading to the best of the Client’s knowledge.

2.5 **Acceptance of Deliverables.**  
Unless otherwise specified in an applicable schedule, the Client shall have fifteen (15) Business Days from receipt of a Deliverable to review and either: (a) provide written acceptance; or (b) issue a written notice of rejection identifying specific material nonconformities with the Specifications. The Vendor shall promptly correct such nonconformities and resubmit the Deliverable for review. If the Client fails to provide written acceptance or a written rejection with specific reasons within the review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted. Acceptance of a Deliverable shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed.

2.6 **Change Management.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the scope of the Services may evolve over time. Any changes to the scope, Deliverables, Milestones, or Fees shall be managed in accordance with **Article XIII (Change Management)**, and shall be documented in a mutually executed Change Order.

2.7 **Subcontracting.**  
The Vendor may use subcontractors to perform portions of the Services, provided that: (a) the Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors; (b) any subcontractors who will have access to Confidential Information or Personal Data are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection at least as protective as those set forth in this Agreement; and (c) the Vendor shall not subcontract material portions of the Services to a direct competitor of the Client without the Client’s prior written consent.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The total contract value for the Services and Deliverables to be provided under this Agreement is **US$3,719,369** (the “Contract Value”), subject to adjustments for approved Change Orders and reimbursable expenses as described herein.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
The Fees shall be allocated as follows:

  (a) **Phase 1 (Discovery and Requirements):** US$519,369  
  (b) **Phase 2 (Design and Development):** US$1,900,000  
  (c) **Phase 3 (Testing, UAT, Deployment):** US$700,000  
  (d) **Phase 4 (Managed Services and Support for Remaining Term):** US$600,000

Each phase’s fee may be further detailed in **Schedule B (Fee Schedule)**, if attached.

3.3 **Payment Milestones.**  
Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the Client shall pay the Vendor according to the following Milestones:

  (a) **Milestone 1:** Forty percent (40%) of Phase 1 fees upon delivery of the Requirements Specification Document and Solution Architecture Blueprint, and their acceptance (or deemed acceptance) in accordance with **Section 2.5**.  
  (b) **Milestone 2:** Remaining sixty percent (60%) of Phase 1 fees upon acceptance of the final Project Plan.  
  (c) **Milestone 3:** Fifty percent (50%) of Phase 2 fees payable in equal monthly installments over the duration of Phase 2, upon submission of monthly invoices by the Vendor.  
  (d) **Milestone 4:** Remaining fifty percent (50%) of Phase 2 fees upon completion and acceptance of the major development Deliverables identified in the Project Plan.  
  (e) **Milestone 5:** One hundred percent (100%) of Phase 3 fees payable as follows: (i) fifty percent (50%) upon commencement of UAT; and (ii) fifty percent (50%) upon successful production deployment and Client’s written confirmation.  
  (f) **Milestone 6:** Phase 4 fees payable in equal quarterly installments in advance, beginning upon commencement of managed services.

3.4 **Invoicing and Payment Terms.**  
The Vendor shall submit invoices to the Client upon achievement of the applicable Milestones or at such other intervals as specified herein. The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within **sixty (60) days** from the date of the Vendor’s invoice (“Net 60”). All payments shall be made in U.S. dollars, by wire transfer or other mutually agreed method, to the account designated in writing by the Vendor.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.**  
If the Client, in good faith, disputes any portion of an invoice, the Client shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt, specifying the reasons for the dispute and the amount in dispute. The Client shall pay all undisputed portions of the invoice in accordance with **Section 3.4**, and the Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly. Any amount determined to be payable upon resolution of the dispute shall be paid within fifteen (15) Business Days thereafter.

3.6 **Late Payments.**  
Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one percent (1.0%) per month or the maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, from the due date until the date of payment. The Vendor shall provide written notice of any late payment and may, upon at least fifteen (15) Business Days’ prior written notice to the Client and subject to **Article IV**, suspend performance of the Services if undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than thirty (30) days after the due date.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
The Fees are exclusive of all sales, use, value-added, and other taxes, duties, or governmental charges (other than taxes based on the Vendor’s net income). The Client shall be responsible for all such taxes arising from the payments under this Agreement, provided that the Vendor shall cooperate with the Client in seeking any applicable exemptions or reductions.

3.8 **Expenses and Reimbursement.**  
The Client shall reimburse the Vendor for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, and meals, in accordance with the Client’s then-current travel and expense policies provided to the Vendor. The Vendor shall submit appropriate receipts and documentation as a condition to reimbursement. No markup or overhead shall be applied to reimbursable expenses unless expressly agreed in writing.

3.9 **No Withholding or Setoff.**  
Except for good-faith disputes raised in accordance with **Section 3.5**, the Client shall not withhold or set off any amounts due under this Agreement against any amounts owed or alleged to be owed by the Vendor under any other agreement or claim, without the Vendor’s prior written consent.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect until **January 03, 2027** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this **Article IV** or extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
No automatic renewal shall apply. Any extension of the Term shall be subject to mutual written agreement between the Parties, which may include revised Fees, Service Levels, and other terms.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience, without cause, upon not less than **sixty (60) days** prior written notice to the other Party. During such notice period, both Parties shall continue to perform their obligations hereunder, and the Client shall remain responsible for payment of all Fees and expenses accrued or committed prior to the effective date of termination.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party:

  (a) materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice thereof from the non-breaching Party; or  
  (b) becomes insolvent, makes a general assignment for the benefit of creditors, suffers or permits the appointment of a receiver for its business or assets, or becomes subject to any proceeding under any bankruptcy or insolvency law that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days.

4.5 **Termination for Persistent SLA Failures.**  
In addition to the rights set forth in **Section 4.4**, the Client may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice in the event that the Vendor fails to achieve the minimum Service Levels specified in **Article XII** for three (3) consecutive calendar months or five (5) months in any rolling twelve (12)-month period, provided that the Client’s notice specifies the basis of such termination and the Parties have engaged in good-faith remediation efforts.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement for any reason:

  (a) The Vendor shall cease performance of the Services (except as may be reasonably necessary to effect an orderly transition as described in **Section 4.7**).  
  (b) The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination, including any non-cancellable commitments made with the Client’s written approval.  
  (c) Each Party shall return or destroy (at the disclosing Party’s election) the other Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, in accordance with **Section 5.4**.  
  (d) All licenses granted by the Vendor to the Client and vice versa shall terminate, except as expressly provided in **Article VI**.

4.7 **Transition Assistance.**  
If requested by the Client in connection with any expiration or termination (other than termination by the Vendor for the Client’s uncured material breach), the Vendor shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days following termination, at the Vendor’s then-current time and materials rates or such other rates as the Parties may agree. Such assistance may include knowledge transfer, data export, and cooperation with any replacement service provider.

4.8 **Survival.**  
The provisions of **Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure)**, and **XVI (General Provisions)**, together with any other provisions that by their nature should survive, shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE XII  
SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (SLA)

12.1 **Availability/Uptime.**  
For any production systems managed by the Vendor under Phase 4 Managed Services, the Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure that such systems are available at least **99.9%** of the time each calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance and downtime caused by Force Majeure Events or the Client’s acts or omissions.

12.2 **Scheduled Maintenance.**  
The Vendor may schedule maintenance windows during which the system may be unavailable, provided that: (a) such maintenance is scheduled during off-peak hours where reasonably practicable; and (b) the Vendor provides the Client with at least seventy-two (72) hours’ prior notice of planned maintenance that is expected to cause material downtime.

12.3 **Response and Resolution Times.**  
The Vendor shall meet the following incident response and resolution targets:

  (a) **Severity 1 (Critical)** – Production system is down or severely impacted with no workaround:  
  - Initial response within one (1) hour during 24x7 coverage;  
  - Target resolution or workaround within four (4) hours.

  (b) **Severity 2 (High)** – Major functionality impaired; workaround available:  
  - Initial response within two (2) hours during Business Days;  
  - Target resolution or workaround within one (1) Business Day.

  (c) **Severity 3 (Medium)** – Non-critical issue with limited impact:  
  - Initial response within one (1) Business Day;  
  - Target resolution or workaround within five (5) Business Days.

  (d) **Severity 4 (Low)** – Minor issue or cosmetic defect:  
  - Initial response within two (2) Business Days;  
  - Target resolution as part of next scheduled release or mutually agreed timeline.

12.4 **SLA Measurement and Reporting.**  
The Vendor shall monitor performance against the Service Levels and provide monthly SLA reports to the Client, including availability, incident statistics, and response/resolution times. Upon the Client’s request, the Parties shall meet to review SLA performance and discuss any remediation plans for recurring issues.

12.5 **Service Credits.**  
If the Vendor fails to meet the availability or response time Service Levels in a given month, the Client shall be entitled to service credits, calculated as a percentage of the monthly managed services fees attributable to the affected systems, as follows:

  (a) Availability 99.0%–99.89%: 5% credit;  
  (b) Availability 98.0%–98.99%: 10% credit;  
  (c) Availability below 98.0%: 15% credit.  

Service credits shall be applied against future invoices and shall constitute the Client’s sole monetary remedy for SLA failures, without prejudice to the Client’s rights under **Section 4.5** in the event of persistent failures.
