## ARTICLE II  
SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **General Description of Services.**  
Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, the Vendor shall provide to the Client professional staffing, employment administration, and workforce management services, which may include, without limitation:

  (a) recruitment, screening, interviewing, and selection of qualified personnel to fulfill roles requested by the Client;  
  (b) engagement and employment of such personnel by the Vendor as employer of record, including administration of payroll, benefits (where applicable), tax withholdings, and other statutory obligations;  
  (c) assignment of such personnel to provide services to the Client at Client’s locations or remotely, as specified in the applicable SOW;  
  (d) ongoing management, supervision, and performance monitoring of Assigned Personnel, in coordination with the Client’s designated managers;  
  (e) consulting and advisory services related to staffing strategies, workforce planning, and optimization; and  
  (f) such additional related services as may be expressly described in one or more SOWs executed under this Agreement.

2.2 **Statements of Work.**  
(a) The detailed description of each engagement, including the number and type of positions, job descriptions, work location, schedule, compensation band, Deliverables, milestones, and any applicable Service Levels, shall be set out in one or more SOWs.  

(b) No SOW shall be binding unless it is in writing and executed by authorized representatives of both Parties. Each SOW shall be deemed an integral part of this Agreement, and in the event of conflict between an SOW and this Agreement, this Agreement shall govern, except where the SOW expressly states that its terms supersede a specified provision of this Agreement.

2.3 **Deliverables.**  
The Deliverables under this Agreement may include, by way of example and without limitation:

  (a) staffing fulfillment reports identifying roles filled, time-to-fill metrics, candidate sources, and retention statistics;  
  (b) workforce analytics and recommendations related to labor utilization, productivity benchmarks, and cost optimization;  
  (c) compliance documentation relating to employment eligibility, background checks (if requested and legally permissible), and regulatory filings;  
  (d) training materials and orientation content for Assigned Personnel specific to Client policies and procedures; and  
  (e) any other tangible or intangible work product identified in the applicable SOW.

All Deliverables shall conform to the specifications, formats, and acceptance criteria set forth in the applicable SOW.

2.4 **Performance Standards.**  
(a) The Vendor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, using personnel with the requisite skill, experience, and qualifications, and shall devote adequate resources to meet its obligations under this Agreement and each SOW.  

(b) The Vendor shall comply with all Applicable Laws, including but not limited to wage and hour laws, employment eligibility verification requirements, anti-discrimination laws, and occupational health and safety regulations, as applicable to the Vendor’s role as employer of record.  

(c) The Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to adhere to the Service Levels described in **Article XII** and in any applicable SOW, including target time-to-fill for open positions, responsiveness to Client inquiries, and accuracy of payroll and administrative services.

2.5 **Timelines and Milestones.**  
(a) Each SOW shall set forth the anticipated timelines for recruiting, onboarding, and assigning personnel, as well as for any specific Deliverables.  

(b) Unless otherwise stated in the SOW, the Vendor shall acknowledge receipt of a staffing request from the Client within one (1) Business Day and shall provide an initial shortlist of candidates (where applicable) within five (5) Business Days, subject to market conditions and the complexity of the role.  

(c) The Parties acknowledge that timelines may be impacted by factors beyond the Vendor’s reasonable control, including labor market conditions, specific skill requirements, and delays in Client feedback or approvals. The Vendor shall promptly notify the Client in writing of any anticipated delays and shall propose a revised timeline.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.**  
The Client shall:

  (a) provide timely, accurate, and complete information regarding job requirements, duties, qualifications, and work conditions for each requested role;  
  (b) designate a primary point of contact to coordinate with the Vendor regarding staffing needs, performance feedback, and issue resolution;  
  (c) provide Assigned Personnel with appropriate access to premises, systems, tools, and information necessary to perform their duties, subject to Client’s security policies;  
  (d) notify the Vendor promptly of any performance or conduct issues involving Assigned Personnel;  
  (e) comply with all Applicable Laws applicable to the Client’s role, including providing a safe work environment, and not requesting the Vendor to engage in any unlawful employment practices.

2.7 **Control and Supervision.**  
(a) The Vendor shall retain sole and exclusive responsibility as the employer of record for Assigned Personnel with respect to payroll, benefits (if any), tax withholdings, and compliance with employment laws, except to the extent otherwise expressly allocated to the Client under Applicable Law or in an SOW.  

(b) The Client shall be responsible for day-to-day direction of Assigned Personnel as it relates to the Client’s business operations, work assignments, and conduct at the Client’s facilities.  

(c) Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship between the Client and Assigned Personnel. The Parties acknowledge and agree that Assigned Personnel shall remain employees or contractors of the Vendor (or its approved subcontractors) and not of the Client.

2.8 **Background Checks and Screenings.**  
If specified in an SOW or requested in writing by the Client and permitted by Applicable Law, the Vendor will conduct background checks, drug screenings, or other pre-engagement screenings of candidates, in accordance with Client requirements and Applicable Law (including fair credit reporting, anti-discrimination, and data protection laws). The scope, cost, and timing of such screenings shall be specified in the applicable SOW.

2.9 **Acceptance of Services and Deliverables.**  
(a) Unless otherwise stated in an SOW, the Client shall have ten (10) Business Days following delivery of any Deliverable to review and either accept or reject it in writing based on non-conformity with the documented specifications.  

(b) If the Client rejects a Deliverable, the Client shall provide a reasonably detailed written explanation of the deficiencies. The Vendor shall, at no additional charge, use commercially reasonable efforts to correct such deficiencies and resubmit the Deliverable within a mutually agreed timeframe.  

(c) If the Client fails to provide written notice of acceptance or rejection within the review period, the Deliverable shall be deemed accepted.

## ARTICLE III  
FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.**  
The Parties acknowledge that the estimated total contract value for the Term of this Agreement is **US $1,661,846** (the “Contract Value”), based on anticipated volumes and rates set forth in the applicable SOWs. The actual amounts payable by the Client will be determined by the Services actually performed, subject to the Contract Value unless otherwise agreed in writing.

3.2 **Fee Structure.**  
Fees payable under this Agreement may include, as specified in the applicable SOW:

  (a) hourly or daily rates for Assigned Personnel;  
  (b) fixed-price fees for specific Deliverables or projects;  
  (c) success-based or placement fees for permanent hires (if applicable);  
  (d) retainers or minimum monthly fees for managed services; and  
  (e) reimbursement of approved expenses in accordance with **Section 3.6**.

All rates and pricing shall be set forth in the applicable SOW or fee schedule attached hereto.

3.3 **Invoicing.**  
(a) Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the Vendor shall invoice the Client monthly in arrears for Services performed and Deliverables accepted during the preceding month.  

(b) Each invoice shall reasonably detail the basis for the amounts invoiced, including the names of Assigned Personnel, hours or days worked, applicable rates, and itemized expenses where relevant.  

(c) Invoices shall be sent electronically to the billing contact designated by the Client in **Section 16.9 (Notices)** or as otherwise specified in writing by the Client.

3.4 **Payment Terms.**  
(a) The Client shall pay all undisputed amounts on each invoice within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of invoice (“Net 45”), unless otherwise stated in the applicable SOW.  

(b) Payments shall be made in U.S. dollars by wire transfer, ACH, or other method mutually agreed by the Parties.  

(c) If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice, the Client shall notify the Vendor in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt, specifying the basis of the dispute in reasonable detail. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute. The Client shall timely pay all undisputed portions of the invoice.

3.5 **Late Payment.**  
(a) Any undisputed amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of one percent (1%) per month, or the maximum rate allowed by Applicable Law, whichever is lower, from the due date until paid in full.  

(b) If any undisputed amounts remain unpaid for more than sixty (60) days after the due date, the Vendor may, upon ten (10) Business Days’ prior written notice, suspend performance of the Services until all outstanding undisputed amounts and applicable interest are paid. Suspension of Services shall not relieve the Client of its payment obligations.

3.6 **Expenses.**  
(a) The Client shall reimburse the Vendor for reasonable, documented, and pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with performing the Services, such as travel, lodging, and specialized materials, in accordance with the Client’s then-current travel and expense policies, provided such policies are communicated in writing to the Vendor.  

(b) The Vendor shall provide receipts or other documentation reasonably requested by the Client for any reimbursable expenses.

3.7 **Taxes.**  
(a) Fees under this Agreement are exclusive of all applicable sales, use, value-added, withholding, and other taxes, duties, or governmental charges (collectively, “Taxes”). The Client shall be responsible for paying all Taxes applicable to the Services, excluding taxes based on the Vendor’s net income or employment taxes for the Vendor’s Employees.  

(b) If the Client is required by Applicable Law to withhold Taxes from any payment to the Vendor, the Client shall provide the Vendor with appropriate documentation, and the Parties shall reasonably cooperate to minimize such withholding Taxes in accordance with Applicable Law.

3.8 **No Set-Off.**  
Except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or as required by Applicable Law, the Client shall not set off, counterclaim, withhold, or otherwise deduct any amount from any payments due to the Vendor without the Vendor’s prior written consent.

## ARTICLE IV  
TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.**  
The Term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until **September 30, 2025** (the “Expiration Date”), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV.

4.2 **Renewal.**  
This Agreement may be renewed by mutual written agreement of the Parties for additional periods, which may include revised pricing or terms. Unless and until such written renewal is executed, this Agreement shall expire on the Expiration Date.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW under this Agreement, for convenience and without cause upon not less than **sixty (60) days’ prior written notice** to the other Party. The Parties agree that a 60-day notice period balances the Client’s need for flexibility with the Vendor’s need for reasonable planning and transition.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  
(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any affected SOW, by written notice to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.  

(b) Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:  
  (i) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or is the subject of any bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceeding;  
  (ii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or  
  (iii) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

4.5 **Termination for Regulatory or Legal Reasons.**  
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if continued performance would violate Applicable Law, a court order, or a governmental directive, provided that such termination is limited to the affected Services or SOW to the extent reasonably practicable.

4.6 **Effect of Termination.**  
Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement or any SOW for any reason:

  (a) The Vendor shall cease performance of the Services under the terminated portions and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to assist in transitioning the Services to the Client or to a successor service provider, as reasonably requested by the Client and at the Client’s expense.  

  (b) The Client shall pay the Vendor for all Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the effective date of termination, including any approved expenses incurred, and any agreed termination or transition fees specified in the applicable SOW.  

  (c) Unless otherwise directed by the Client or required by Applicable Law, the Vendor shall promptly return or securely destroy all Client Confidential Information, in accordance with **Section 5.4** and **Article XI**.  

  (d) Assigned Personnel shall be removed from Client assignments in an orderly manner consistent with Applicable Law. The Vendor shall be solely responsible for any severance or other employment-related obligations owed to such personnel, except as otherwise required by Applicable Law or expressly agreed in writing.

4.7 **Survival.**  
The rights and obligations of the Parties that by their nature should survive termination or expiration of this Agreement (including but not limited to payment obligations accrued as of termination, confidentiality, intellectual property, indemnification, limitations of liability, data protection, and dispute resolution) shall survive in accordance with **Section 16.10**.

## ARTICLE V  
CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.**  
“Confidential Information” means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the “Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (the “Receiving Party”), whether orally or in writing, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to business plans, financial information, pricing, client or employee data, trade secrets, technical data, methodologies, software, and any information relating to the Services and Deliverables.

5.2 **Obligations.**  
The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing or receiving Services under this Agreement;  
  (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted herein;  
  (c) limit access to such Confidential Information to its employees, officers, agents, subcontractors, and professional advisors who have a legitimate need to know for purposes of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein;  
  (d) protect such Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

5.3 **Exceptions.**  
Confidential Information shall not include information that:

  (a) is or becomes generally known to the public without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;  
  (b) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;  
  (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or  
  (d) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.**  
If the Receiving Party is required by Applicable Law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permissible) provide prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If disclosure is ultimately required, the Receiving Party shall disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information which it is legally required to disclose, and shall use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment of the disclosed information.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.**  
Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all copies of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information in its possession or control, except where retention is required by Applicable Law or for ordinary course back-up and archival purposes, in which case the Receiving Party shall continue to protect such information in accordance with this Article V.

5.6 **Survival.**  
The obligations in this Article V shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** after termination or expiration of this Agreement; provided, however, that with respect to trade secrets, such obligations shall survive for so long as the information remains a trade secret under Applicable Law.

## ARTICLE XV  
FORCE MAJEURE

15.1 **Definition.**  
Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) to the extent that such delay or failure is caused by a **Force Majeure Event**, which includes, but is not limited to: acts of God; natural disasters; war, terrorism, or armed conflict; labor disputes or strikes (excluding a Party’s own workforce where such disputes are not industry-wide); epidemics or pandemics; governmental actions or restrictions; or failures of third-party communications or utility services not caused by the Party.

15.2 **Notice.**  
The Party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall promptly notify the other Party in writing, describing the nature of the event, its expected duration, and the anticipated impact on performance. The affected Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event.

15.3 **Suspension of Obligations.**  
During the continuance of a Force Majeure Event, the obligations of the affected Party shall be suspended to the extent and for the duration affected by the Force Majeure Event. The affected Party shall resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable after the Force Majeure Event ceases.

15.4 **Termination for Extended Force Majeure.**  
If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days and materially impairs the performance of a material portion of the Services, either Party may terminate the affected SOW or, if the entire Agreement is affected, this Agreement, upon written notice to the other Party.
