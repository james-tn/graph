## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Relationship to Parent Agreement.** This Work Order is executed as a **Statement of Work** under and subject to the Parent Agreement (CSA-NOV-202312-100 / contract_100). In accordance with **Section 1.18** and **Article II** of the Parent Agreement:

- The WO Services described herein constitute “Services” as defined in Section 1.16 of the Parent Agreement.
- The WO Deliverables described herein constitute “Deliverables” as defined in Section 1.9 of the Parent Agreement.
- Acceptance, performance standards, and other obligations will be governed by **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)**, **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)**, and related provisions of the Parent Agreement, except as expressly modified herein.

2.2 **General Description of WO Services.** Vendor shall provide a comprehensive program of consulting, implementation, and support services to expand and industrialize Client’s enterprise systems landscape. The WO Services shall include, without limitation:

2.2.1 **Enterprise Architecture Expansion and Roadmap Realization.** Building upon any assessment work already performed under prior SOWs, Vendor shall:

  (a) Refine and finalize the target enterprise architecture for Client’s core business domains, including finance, supply chain, sales, customer service, and analytics;  
  (b) Develop detailed architecture blueprints, integration patterns, and reference models for cloud-based infrastructure, microservices, and data platforms;  
  (c) Define and prioritize a phased multi-release roadmap covering the WO Term, including dependencies, risk assessment, and resource forecasting.

2.2.2 **Multi-Region ERP and CRM Rollout.** Vendor shall design, configure, and implement Client’s selected ERP and CRM solutions (as identified by Client) across at least three (3) major business units and up to six (6) geographic regions, including:

  (a) Localization and configuration of finance, order management, procurement, inventory, and customer engagement modules;  
  (b) Design and development of required integrations with existing legacy systems, external partners, payment gateways, and logistics providers;  
  (c) Data migration, including profiling, cleansing, mapping, test migrations, and cutover activities;  
  (d) End-to-end testing (unit, system, integration, user acceptance) and production deployment support.

2.2.3 **Data Lake, Analytics, and Reporting Enablement.** Vendor shall implement a cloud-based data lake and analytics layer to support enterprise reporting and advanced analytics, including:

  (a) Design of data ingestion pipelines from ERP, CRM, and other source systems;  
  (b) Implementation of data models, metadata, and governance structures;  
  (c) Configuration of dashboards, KPIs, and reports for finance, operations, and executive stakeholders;  
  (d) Enablement of self-service analytics capabilities where feasible, including training for Client’s analytics team.

2.2.4 **Project Management and Governance.** Vendor shall provide project management and governance services as described in Section 2.2.6 of the Parent Agreement, including:

  (a) A dedicated project manager responsible for planning, coordination, risk tracking, and reporting;  
  (b) Bi-weekly status reports covering progress, issues, risks, and upcoming milestones;  
  (c) Participation in Project Governance Committee meetings (at least monthly) to review progress, decisions, and escalations;  
  (d) Maintenance of a centralized project repository containing project plans, RAID logs (risks, assumptions, issues, dependencies), and decisions.

2.2.5 **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Consistent with Sections 2.2.7 and 2.3.6 of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall:

  (a) Conduct “train-the-trainer” sessions for Client’s designated super users on ERP, CRM, and analytics solutions;  
  (b) Prepare user guides, standard operating procedures, quick reference cards, and e-learning content as part of the WO Deliverables;  
  (c) Provide knowledge transfer sessions to Client’s IT team covering solution architecture, configuration, integrations, and operational procedures.

2.2.6 **Post-Implementation Support for WO Scope.** Consistent with Section 2.2.8 and Article XII of the Parent Agreement, Vendor shall provide post-implementation support for the systems implemented under this Work Order, including:

  (a) Incident management and troubleshooting for configuration and integration issues;  
  (b) Minor enhancements, configuration adjustments, and defect remediation within a mutually agreed backlog;  
  (c) Monitoring and reporting on system performance and uptime in accordance with **WO Service Levels**.

2.3 **WO Deliverables.** Without limiting the definition of “Deliverables” in Section 1.9 of the Parent Agreement, the WO Deliverables shall include, at a minimum:

2.3.1 **Architecture and Design Documentation.**  
  - Enterprise architecture target state diagrams;  
  - Solution blueprints for ERP, CRM, and data lake;  
  - Integration design specifications and interface catalog;  
  - Security and access control models.

2.3.2 **Configuration and Code Artifacts.**  
  - Configured instances of ERP and CRM modules for in-scope business units;  
  - Integration components, including APIs, middleware configurations, and data transformation scripts;  
  - Data ingestion pipelines, ETL/ELT jobs, and orchestration workflows.

2.3.3 **Data Migration and Testing Artifacts.**  
  - Source-to-target mapping documents;  
  - Data cleansing and validation reports;  
  - Test plans, test cases, test scripts, and test execution summaries;  
  - Cutover plans and run books.

2.3.4 **Training and Operational Materials.**  
  - User manuals and SOPs;  
  - Training decks, e-learning content, and recordings of key training sessions;  
  - Administration guides for Client’s IT operations.

2.3.5 **Service and Performance Reports.**  
  - Go-live readiness assessments;  
  - Monthly service performance and incident reports, including uptime metrics, response times, and root cause analyses for major incidents.

All WO Deliverables shall be subject to the acceptance procedures set forth in **Section 2.7 (Acceptance of Deliverables)** of the Parent Agreement, except that the review period for major Milestone Deliverables under this Work Order shall be **fifteen (15) Business Days** instead of ten (10) Business Days; all other acceptance mechanics remain as stated in Section 2.7 of the Parent Agreement.

2.4 **Timelines and Milestones.** The Parties acknowledge that timelines are estimates and depend on Client’s timely cooperation as noted in **Section 2.5 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement. Subject to change via Change Order, the indicative milestones are:

- **Milestone 1 – Architecture Finalization & Roadmap**  
  Target Date: June 30, 2024  
  Key Deliverables: Target architecture, integration patterns, multi-phase roadmap.

- **Milestone 2 – ERP/CRM Design & Prototype Completion**  
  Target Date: October 31, 2024  
  Key Deliverables: Detailed design documents; configured prototype environments; initial data model.

- **Milestone 3 – First Wave Go-Live (2 Business Units)**  
  Target Date: March 31, 2025  
  Key Deliverables: Production go-live for first two business units; cutover completion; post-go-live stabilization plan.

- **Milestone 4 – Remaining Rollout & Data Lake Launch**  
  Target Date: October 31, 2025  
  Key Deliverables: Go-live for remaining business units; data lake in production; analytics dashboards deployed.

- **Milestone 5 – Final Acceptance & Transition**  
  Target Date: January 17, 2026  
  Key Deliverables: Transition to steady-state support; final documentation; formal project closure report.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the WO Services in accordance with the performance standards stated in **Section 2.4 (Performance Standards)** of the Parent Agreement, including professional and workmanlike performance consistent with industry standards, and shall adhere to the Milestones and WO Service Levels.

2.6 **Client Responsibilities.** In addition to the responsibilities in **Section 2.5 (Client Responsibilities)** of the Parent Agreement, Client shall:

  (a) Provide timely decisions, approvals, and clarifications;  
  (b) Assign knowledgeable subject-matter experts for each in-scope business process;  
  (c) Ensure that required third-party licenses, consents, and access rights are in place for systems interfaced with or impacted by the WO Services;  
  (d) Provide reasonably skilled internal resources to participate in testing and change management activities.

2.7 **Subcontractors and Key Personnel.** Consistent with **Section 2.8 (Subcontractors)** of the Parent Agreement, Vendor may use subcontractors for portions of the WO Services, provided that Vendor remains responsible for their performance. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain continuity of Key Personnel; any replacement of Key Personnel shall be made with resources of equivalent skills and experience and communicated to Client in advance.

2.8 **Project Governance Committee.** The Parties shall establish a Project Governance Committee to oversee the WO Services, comprising at least one senior executive and one project manager from each Party. The Committee shall meet at least monthly (or more frequently as agreed) to review status, risks, and escalations. Escalation of disputes within the Committee shall follow the process set forth in **Article XIV (Dispute Resolution)** of the Parent Agreement.
