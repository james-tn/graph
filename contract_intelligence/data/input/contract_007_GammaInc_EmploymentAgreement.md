## PREAMBLE AND RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client is engaged in the business of technology product development, sales, and related enterprise services and desires to retain a qualified professional service provider to supply certain personnel and related employment‑type services in support of Client’s operations;

## ARTICLE II  
## SCOPE OF SERVICES / WORK

2.1 **Engagement of Vendor.** Client hereby engages Vendor, and Vendor hereby accepts such engagement, to provide the Services described in this Article II and as may be further specified in one or more SOWs entered into by the Parties from time to time under this Agreement. The Parties acknowledge that the Services generally consist of Vendor’s provision and management of Personnel who will perform various professional roles for Client on a time‑and‑materials or fixed‑fee basis, as designated in the applicable SOW.

2.2 **Nature of Services.** Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services may include, individually or in combination, the following:

  (a) **Staff Augmentation and Employment Services.** Vendor shall recruit, screen, hire, employ (or engage as independent contractors where lawful and appropriate), train as needed, and assign qualified Personnel to perform services for Client, which may include software engineering, quality assurance, DevOps, product management, project management, data analytics, technical writing, customer support, IT administration, and other professional functions requested by Client.

  (b) **Project‑Based Services.** Vendor may, at Client’s request and as detailed in an SOW, assemble project teams to deliver project‑based outcomes, such as design, development, and implementation of software modules; migration of applications or data to cloud environments; creation of data analytics dashboards; or other specifically defined projects.

  (c) **Managed Services.** Vendor may provide ongoing managed services such as help‑desk operations, application maintenance and support, monitoring and incident management, or similar operational support functions, subject to the Service Levels and performance metrics set forth in Article XII.

  (d) **Ancillary Services.** Vendor may provide ancillary services reasonably necessary to support the foregoing, including knowledge transfer, documentation, training of Client’s personnel, attendance at Client’s meetings, and periodic reporting.

2.3 **Deliverables.** As part of the Services, Vendor shall create and deliver certain Deliverables, which may include:

  (a) written reports, status updates, and documentation describing work performed, findings, recommendations, and project status;

  (b) software source code, executable code, scripts, configuration files, test cases, test results, and related technical artifacts;

  (c) design documents, architectural diagrams, data models, process flows, and user interface designs;

  (d) operational process documentation, run books, standard operating procedures, and knowledge‑base articles;

  (e) training materials, user guides, and reference manuals for Client’s personnel,

all as more specifically defined in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise expressly stated in an SOW, Deliverables shall be deemed accepted upon the earlier of (i) Client’s written acceptance, or (ii) ten (10) Business Days after delivery if Client has not provided written notice of a material non‑conformity with the applicable SOW or specifications.

2.4 **Service Locations; Remote Work.** The Services may be performed at Client’s premises, at Vendor’s premises, at remote locations, or in a hybrid manner as specified in the applicable SOW or as otherwise agreed by the Parties in writing. Vendor shall ensure that any remote work complies with Client’s security requirements and policies provided to Vendor in writing. Where Services are performed at Client’s premises, Vendor’s Personnel shall comply with all applicable Client workplace rules and safety policies made known to Vendor.

2.5 **Performance Standards.** Vendor shall perform the Services:

  (a) in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with generally accepted industry standards for services of a similar nature;

  (b) with suitably qualified and experienced Personnel who possess the necessary skills, training, and background to perform their assigned tasks;

  (c) in accordance with the timelines, milestones, and Service Levels specified in this Agreement and any applicable SOW;

  (d) in compliance with Applicable Law and Client’s written policies and procedures, to the extent such policies and procedures are provided to Vendor in advance and relate to the performance of the Services.

2.6 **Personnel Management.** Vendor shall be solely responsible for:

  (a) hiring, compensation, benefits, payroll, taxes, discipline, and termination of its Personnel;

  (b) ensuring that its Personnel have valid work authorization, where applicable, and meet any background, security, or other checks required under Applicable Law or agreed by the Parties in writing;

  (c) replacing any Personnel who are unable or unwilling to perform the Services or who, in Client’s reasonable judgment and upon written notice to Vendor, perform unsatisfactorily or violate Client’s policies. Vendor shall use commercially reasonable efforts to promptly replace such Personnel with suitably qualified individuals to avoid material disruption to the Services.

2.7 **Client Responsibilities.** Client shall:

  (a) provide Vendor with timely access to Client Materials, systems, facilities, and personnel as reasonably necessary for Vendor to perform the Services;

  (b) designate a project manager or primary point of contact to coordinate with Vendor regarding the Services and to make timely decisions and approvals;

  (c) review Deliverables and provide approvals or feedback in a timely manner, and respond to Vendor’s requests for information, decisions, or clarifications as reasonably necessary;

  (d) ensure that any equipment, networks, software, or environments provided or controlled by Client and used by Vendor’s Personnel are properly maintained and compliant with Applicable Law.

Vendor’s performance is contingent upon Client’s timely fulfillment of its responsibilities. Vendor shall not be liable for delays or failures in performance to the extent caused by Client’s failure to discharge its obligations under this Section 2.7.

2.8 **Statements of Work.**  

  (a) From time to time, the Parties may agree to SOWs that describe specific projects or assignments. Each SOW shall be in writing, reference this Agreement, and set forth the applicable scope, Deliverables, Personnel requirements, fees, project schedule, milestones, Service Levels (if any), and any project‑specific assumptions or dependencies.

  (b) In the event of a conflict between the terms of this Agreement and any SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it is intended to supersede specified provisions of this Agreement with respect to the subject matter of such SOW.

2.9 **Subcontracting.** Vendor may subcontract portions of the Services to third‑party subcontractors, provided that Vendor remains fully responsible for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were Vendor’s own acts and omissions, and that any subcontractors who will access Client’s Confidential Information or systems are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and data protection substantially similar to those set forth in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III  
## FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Contract Value.** The Parties agree that the estimated total contract value for all Services to be performed under this Agreement during the Term is Four Million One Hundred Ninety‑One Thousand Eight Hundred Seventy‑Seven U.S. Dollars (US $4,191,877) (the “**Contract Value**”). The actual fees payable by Client shall be determined pursuant to this Article III and the applicable SOWs and may be more or less than the Contract Value, subject to mutually agreed Change Orders.

3.2 **Fee Structures.** Fees for the Services may be structured as one or more of the following, as specified in the applicable SOW:

  (a) **Time and Materials (“T&M”).** Hourly, daily, or monthly rates for Personnel, billed based on actual hours or days worked, subject to any minimum commitments or rate cards set forth in the SOW.

  (b) **Fixed Fee.** A fixed amount for defined Deliverables or project phases, payable upon completion of milestones, Deliverables, or as otherwise set forth in the SOW.

  (c) **Retainer or Managed Services Fees.** Recurring monthly or quarterly fees for ongoing managed services, support, or staffing, subject to volume or usage assumptions in the SOW.

3.3 **Invoicing.** Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:

  (a) Vendor shall invoice Client monthly in arrears for T&M Services and recurring fees, and upon completion and acceptance of applicable milestones or Deliverables for fixed‑fee projects.

  (b) Each invoice shall reference this Agreement, the applicable SOW, and include a reasonably detailed description of the Services provided, Personnel deployed, hours or days worked (where applicable), and any approved reimbursable expenses.

  (c) All invoices shall be sent electronically to the following address (or such other address as Client may designate in writing):  
  **Email:** ap@contoso.com  
  **Mail:** Accounts Payable, Contoso Enterprises, 1234 Market Street, Suite 2500, San Francisco, CA 94105.

3.4 **Payment Terms.** Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within sixty (60) days of the date of Vendor’s valid and properly documented invoice (“**Net 60**”). Payment shall be made in U.S. Dollars by electronic funds transfer, wire, ACH, or such other method as the Parties may mutually agree.

3.5 **Disputed Amounts.** If Client disputes any portion of an invoice, Client shall, within fifteen (15) Business Days of receipt of the invoice, provide written notice identifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. Client shall pay the undisputed portion of the invoice in accordance with Section 3.4. The Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve any disputes as promptly as practicable. Any resolved disputed amount shall be paid within thirty (30) days after resolution.

3.6 **Late Payment.** Any undisputed invoice amount not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of (a) one percent (1%) per month, or (b) the maximum rate permitted by Applicable Law, calculated from the due date until paid in full. Vendor shall provide written notice of any overdue amounts prior to assessing interest. Vendor’s right to interest shall be in addition to any other rights and remedies it may have under this Agreement or at law.

3.7 **Expenses and Reimbursements.**  

  (a) Client shall reimburse Vendor for reasonable, pre‑approved out‑of‑pocket expenses incurred by Vendor or its Personnel in connection with the performance of the Services, including travel, lodging, meals, and other related expenses, in accordance with Client’s standard expense reimbursement policies provided to Vendor in advance.

  (b) Vendor shall submit documentation (such as receipts or expense reports) reasonably necessary for Client to verify reimbursable expenses.

3.8 **Taxes.**  

  (a) Fees payable under this Agreement do not include any applicable sales, use, value‑added, or similar taxes, which shall be separately stated on Vendor’s invoices. Client shall be responsible for all such Taxes, excluding taxes based on Vendor’s net income or gross receipts.

  (b) If Client is required by Applicable Law to withhold taxes from payments to Vendor, Client shall (i) withhold and remit such taxes as required by law; (ii) provide Vendor with appropriate documentation evidencing such withholding; and (iii) reasonably cooperate with Vendor to obtain any available reductions or exemptions.

3.9 **No Set‑Off.** Except as expressly provided in this Agreement or as required by law, neither Party shall set off or withhold amounts due under this Agreement based on amounts owed under any other agreement, claim, or dispute.

## ARTICLE IV  
## TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article IV, shall remain in effect until April 15, 2028 (the “**Expiration Date**”) (the “**Term**”).

4.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may, by mutual written agreement not less than ninety (90) days prior to the Expiration Date, extend or renew this Agreement for additional periods on terms and conditions to be negotiated in good faith.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement, or any individual SOW, for convenience and without cause upon not less than sixty (60) days’ prior written notice to the other Party. The Parties may agree in an SOW to a different notice period for termination of that SOW, provided that such SOW expressly references and modifies this Section 4.3.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.**  

  (a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement or such SOW and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

  (b) Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party (i) becomes insolvent or is generally unable to pay its debts as they become due; (ii) files or has filed against it a petition for bankruptcy or similar relief that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; (iii) makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (iv) is subject to the appointment of a receiver, trustee, or similar officer over a material portion of its assets.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon any expiration or termination of this Agreement:

  (a) Vendor shall promptly cease performing the Services (except as necessary to wind down in an orderly manner or as otherwise requested in writing by Client).

  (b) Client shall pay Vendor for all Services performed and approved expenses incurred through the effective date of termination, including any non‑cancellable commitments entered into by Vendor in reliance on this Agreement with Client’s written approval.

  (c) Vendor shall promptly deliver to Client all Deliverables (whether completed or in‑progress) for which Client has paid or is obligated to pay, together with any Client Materials and any other property of Client in Vendor’s possession or control.

  (d) Each Party shall, at the request of the other, return or destroy Confidential Information in accordance with Section 5.5.

4.6 **Survival.** The rights and obligations of the Parties under Sections 3.4–3.9, 4.5, and Articles V (Confidentiality), VI (Intellectual Property Rights), VII (Representations and Warranties), VIII (Indemnification), IX (Limitation of Liability), X (Insurance), XI (Data Protection and Privacy), XII (Service Level Agreements), XIV (Dispute Resolution), XV (Force Majeure), and XVI (General Provisions), as well as any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive, shall survive any expiration or termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE V  
## CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 **Definition of Confidential Information.** “**Confidential Information**” means all non‑public information disclosed, directly or indirectly, by or on behalf of a Party or its Affiliates (“**Disclosing Party**”) to the other Party or its Affiliates (“**Receiving Party**”), whether orally, visually, electronically, or in writing, that is identified as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

  (a) business plans, strategies, budgets, forecasts, and financial information;  

  (b) customer and supplier identities, contact information, and terms of contracts;  

  (c) trade secrets, know‑how, processes, techniques, and methodologies;  

  (d) product roadmaps, software designs, system architectures, and technical documentation;  

  (e) Personal Data; and  

  (f) the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

Confidential Information does not include information that falls within one of the exceptions in Section 5.3.

5.2 **Obligations of Confidentiality.** The Receiving Party shall:

  (a) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement;

  (b) protect the Confidential Information from unauthorized use, access, or disclosure using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in no event less than reasonable care;

  (c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted under this Agreement; and

  (d) limit access to Confidential Information to those of its Personnel and subcontractors who (i) have a legitimate need to know such information for the purposes of this Agreement; and (ii) are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and non‑use no less protective than those set forth herein.

5.3 **Exceptions.** Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

  (a) is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

  (b) was lawfully known to the Receiving Party, without restriction on use or disclosure, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

  (c) is received from a third party who was not, to the Receiving Party’s knowledge, under an obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

  (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

5.4 **Compelled Disclosure.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that (to the extent legally permissible) the Receiving Party (a) promptly notifies the Disclosing Party of the requirement to disclose, (b) cooperates, at the Disclosing Party’s expense, in seeking confidential treatment or a protective order, and (c) discloses only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required.

5.5 **Return or Destruction.** Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in its possession or control, including all copies, extracts, and summaries thereof, except that the Receiving Party may retain (a) one archival copy solely for purposes of legal compliance, and (b) Confidential Information that is stored in routine backup systems and is not readily accessible in the ordinary course of business, subject in each case to the ongoing confidentiality obligations herein.

5.6 **Injunctive Relief.** The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, in the event of any actual or threatened breach of this Article V, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief, in addition to any other rights and remedies available at law or in equity, without the necessity of posting bond or proving actual damages.

5.7 **Survival.** The obligations of confidentiality and non‑use in this Article V shall commence on the Effective Date and survive for a period of five (5) years following expiration or termination of this Agreement; provided that, for trade secrets, such obligations shall survive for so long as such information qualifies as a trade secret under Applicable Law.
